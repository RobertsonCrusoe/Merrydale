I. The House of Whitegate

The Whitegate family descends from Ardelusk Whitegate, a former Zhentarim slave liberated by Saint Maturin’s Liberators. Ardelusk led the famous Whitegate Epiphany that established the church of Amaunator in the dale, and founded the settlement at Liberty Hill. The family has held the highest ecclesiastical and civil offices in Daggerdale ever since, though by the early 1480s the surname is nearing extinction, the surviving bloodline having dispersed into the Thunderstorm family (through Radiance) and the Blackmane family (through Aestis).

II. The House of Thunderstorm

The Thunderstorm family saw a decline after the Whitegate Epiphany and the rise of Amaunator, but Karrigan’s marriage to Radiance Whitegate in 1425 reversed their fortunes. The union produced a large number of family members that have multiplied to a couple of dozen in the present generation. Many still live at Foothills Farm, while others have entered the Church of Amaunator and military service. The Thunderstorms have produced three Sunlords. Some members of the Whitegate family resent the Thunderstorm side, whom they perceive as ambitious and scheming. No Thunderstorm has ever been admitted to the Order of the Lambent Shield.

III. The Blackmane Family

The Blackmanes are an old Daggerdale family; Decker’s grandfather designed and built the settlement at Tesh Valley Farm. They have long been worshippers of Chauntea and Torm, placing them outside the Amaunatorite establishment. Decker’s marriage to Aestis Whitegate (daughter of Zenith Lord Argent) created a blood link to the ruling house, but Decker has been openly hostile to the Whitegate family, resenting what he calls the “Whitegate Entitlement.” The pedigree notes that “the Blackmane side of the family is also prolific,” but only Decker and Hasting are named in the record. The other children have “tried to infiltrate the power structure of the Dale, mostly unsuccessful.”

        • Hasting Blackmane (b. c. 1452)
          Steward of Loedegan Close
          Estranged from her father. Took the position of Steward against his wishes at age 25 and has not spoken to him since. Refuses to use the Blackmane surname, calling herself by her mother’s name: “I am my mother’s daughter, am I not?” Referred to as “Lady Blackmane” by the White Cloaks.
        • “Many children”—others unnamed in the record. “Tried to infiltrate the power structure of the Dale, mostly unsuccessful.”

IV. Cross-Family Connections

The three families are bound together through two marriages in the generation of Loedegan Whitegate’s children and grandchildren. Together, these unions mean that by the 1480s, the Whitegate bloodline persists almost entirely through the Thunderstorm and Blackmane surnames.

V. The Extinction of the Whitegate Name

By the early 1480s, the Whitegate surname is nearing extinction. Two catastrophes, a generation apart, destroyed the family.

The first was the massacre of Kythorn 1, 1455 DR—the attack on the wedding of Perspicuus and Lanya—which killed Loedegan, Madigan, Solis, Ardelusk II, Dorabell, Taryara, and many others. This left only four Whitegate men: Luminous (widowed since 1429), Argent, Blaze (widowed since 1455), and the young Perspicuus, who was left in charge of Liberty Hill.

The second was the Ritual of Corruption at Liberty Hill in Mirtul 1480. “Every member of the Whitegate family that resided at the farm perished.” Perspicuus had died in 1477; his wife Lanya was tortured to death in the chapel. Their children Lumen (b. 1457) and Luminosus (b. 1458) were “fed to the Ritual of Corruption, dying unthinkable deaths,” alongside their cousin Lucidus and his three small children (unnamed, ages 2, 3, and 5). Only Lumina survived—she was in Dagger Falls at the time.

The living Whitegate men, Luminous (elderly), Argent (elderly, widowed since 1475), and Blaze, have no further heirs. Lumina, granddaughter of Argent, is the only Whitegate of child-bearing age, but she has no children as of 1481.

The Whitegate bloodline persists through the Thunderstorm family (via Radiance) and the Blackmane family (via Aestis), but no one in either line bears the Whitegate surname. Hasting Blackmane has claimed her mother’s name, but she remains officially a Blackmane. Luminous, the patriarch, “is the patriarch of a family that is almost gone, ruling from an inner keep he rarely leaves.”
