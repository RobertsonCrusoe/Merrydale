The Daggerdale Concordat

Excerpts from the Concordat of Elesias, 1482 DR

15 Eleasias 1482 DR

I. Preamble & Superseding Rights

In any matters addressed by and prescribed for in this Concordat, The Concordat shall be held to be the governing document, superseding any provision in other agreements of any variety, on those specific matters. The Concordat is not a prescription for how the Signatories and their people are to carry out their daily lives, but rather, what obligations and limit to obligations they owe to each other. In all respects save Religious Pluralism, the Concordat neither prescribes nor proscribes specific behaviours.

II. Signatories

As of 15 Elesias, 1482 DR.

There being two classifications of Signatory to the Concordat:

Voting Signatories

Which shall have the right to attend the Governors’ Assembly and vote upon matters brought before it, which now includes:

Other documents in the archive indicate that the Governors’ Assembly includes governors from each farm, the Bishop of Dagger Falls, and ambassadors from Myth Drannor, Cormanthor, Tethyamar, and Cormyr, among others. A complete enumeration has not been located.

Non-Voting Signatories

Which shall have the right to attend the General Assembly and vote upon matters brought before it, which shall, if passed by the General Assembly, be brought to the Governors’ Assembly for ratification, which now includes:

  • The General of the Sun BladesAs above, only the first entry of the Non-Voting Signatories survives in this source text.

III. Consul of the Governors’ Assembly

The Voting Signatories shall elect a Consul of the Governors’ Assembly at the beginning of this agreement, and every Shieldmeet thereafter. The Consul shall be chosen from any citizen of the Dale, but shall not be a Voting Signatory or Non-Voting Signatory to the Concordat. The Consul shall call the Assembly, maintain order during proceedings, and act as the head of the Sun Blades.

The first Consul of the Governors’ Assembly, elected by unanimous agreement by all Voting Signatories, shall be Captain Kesh Oakdale (ret.) late of the 16th Company Sun Blades, of Dwimmerholme Farm, whose first term shall commence on 15 Elesias, 1482 DR, and end on Shieldmeet, 1484 DR.

For his service as Consul, Captain Oakdale shall be provided with apartments in Governor’s Hall (Dagger Falls), a yearly salary of 300 gp, 100 gp in discretionary administrative funds each year, and one aide to be paid 182 gp 5 sp per year, of his choosing.

IV. Provisions for the Common Defence

i. The Sun Blades

All settlements, nations, and other organizations represented by the Voting Signatories agree that the defence of their holdings and people is primarily their responsibility, and further, that a common defence strategy with a Standing Army deployed strategically throughout the dale will be to the mutual benefit of all. Therefore, the Voting Signatories now support the reorganization of the Sun Blades along the following framework:

Chain of Command

  1. Consul of the Governors’ Assembly
    1. General of the Sun Blades

Captains and Lieutenants may be placed at the direction of Governors in such circumstances as may warrant this arrangement. This shall not supersede any orders of the General, but is an arrangement of convenience for seeing to the local needs of specific settlements.

ii. Order of the Lambent Shield & Other Holy Orders

For the greatest clarity, the holy orders of knights, paladins, and other warriors, along with their priests and staff, shall be maintained at the sole expense of their Churches, and shall extract no mandatory payments from the people of Daggerdale. Holy orders may be granted a Charter Militant by four-fifths majority vote in the Governors’ Assembly as needed from time to time. The following Holy Orders are hereby granted a Charter Militant at the outset of the Concordat:

  • The Order of the Lambent Shield (Amaunator)
  • The Order of the Golden Lion (Torm)
  • The Order of the Lambent Rose (Ilmater)
  • The Knights of Justice (Tyr)

iii. The Free Companies

For the greatest clarity, the companies of independent soldiers, scholars, adventurers, and others who would commit themselves to the defence of the Dale, at their own expense and peril, shall extract no mandatory payments from the people of Daggerdale. However, they shall be granted by four-fifths majority vote of the Governors’ Assembly, their Charter Militant, which provides them with the following rights and privileges, so long as they keep their Charter Militant in good order:

  • To recruit, arm, and train individuals whom the Governors’ Assembly finds to be of suitable character and fibre, for the purposes of defending the Dale and of making a living and a profit
  • To hold such properties needed to conduct their business as the Governors’ Assembly finds suitable, and declares the specifics of such properties in the Charter Militant
  • To hold a seat on the General Assembly

Each Charter Militant shall be reviewed in advance of each Shieldmeet, whereupon it shall require approval by three-fifths majority of the Governors’ Assembly for the next four years, until the next Shieldmeet.

iv. The Militia

It being in the best interest of all Signatories to the Concordat that every Signatory provides defence in adequate measure to avoid becoming a liability to the Common Defence, all Signatories agree that:

  • All fighting-aged and capable people of their settlements are trained in their defence, with drills and training and other such suitable preparations to be conducted at least four times per year for two full days

And further that each farm shall raise, whether by conscription or by volunteers, a Militia composed of fighting-aged and capable people with the following considerations:

  • That one-fifth of all fighting-aged and capable people shall be enrolled into the Militia at any one time for a period of not less than four years per person
  • That members of the Militia drill and train for at least two hours per tenday either under local martial leaders or alongside the Sun Blades
  • That the militia may engage in their vocational duties with a minimum of disruption caused by their martial duties
  • That at least one full-time Captain of Militia is chosen to lead the Militia, whose duties are exclusively martial rather than vocational, and paid for by the settlement

The signatories of Dagger Falls, Castle Radiance, Charity, and all ally-nations (those currently being Tethyamar, Cormyr, Myth Drannor, and Cormanthor) agree to maintain such suitable defences, standing armies, and militia required for their own defence, and for the provision of mutual defence of the Dale.

An attack on one Signatory is to be considered an attack on all Signatories, with due consequences, which may include the deployment of Militia to neighbouring Signatories to aid in the Common Defence.

V. Religious Pluralism

The Signatories of the Concordat acknowledge that the worship of many gods in Daggerdale shall be permitted, and that the list of permitted gods may change from time to time. To that end, the Signatories shall maintain two lists of gods:

Approbata Deorum

Being the gods that all citizens of the Dale are entitled to worship free of restraint, which shall be edited from time to time by the Voting Signatories with four-fifths majority, and which at the outset of the Concordat includes:

  • All such elven deities as approved by the Ambassador from Myth Drannor
  • All such dwarven deities as approved by the Ambassador from Tethyamar
  • The gods of men and their exarchs including: Amaunator, Chauntea, Torm, Ilmater, Mielikki, Waukeen, Selûne, Sune, Tymora, Oghma, Gond, and TempusAmaunator and Chauntea are removable only by unanimous vote of the Voting Signatories.

Reprobata Deorum

Being the gods that are forbidden from worship in the Dale, which shall be edited from time to time by the Voting Signatories with four-fifths majority, and which at the outset of the Concordat includes:

  • All such elven deities as disapproved by the Ambassador from Myth Drannor
  • All such dwarven deities as disapproved by the Ambassador from Tethyamar
  • The gods of men and their exarchs including: Shar, Bane, Lolth, Ghaunadaur, Asmodeus, Cyric, and GruumshShar is removable only by unanimous vote of the Voting Signatories.

With respect to all other gods and exarchs, the Voting Signatories grant the ecumenical authorities of the Dale the right to bring forth such gods and exarchs for consideration, whether for the Approbata Deorum or Reprobata Deorum, from time to time.
