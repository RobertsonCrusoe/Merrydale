⚔ THE FREE DEFENCE LEAGUE ⚔

Chartered Company of Daggerdale — Summary & Roster, c. 1479–1483 DR

About This Document

The Free Defence League (FDL) is an independent bounty-hunter and volunteer force headquartered at Castle Radiance. Under the Daggerdale Concordat of Elesias 1482, the FDL Captain holds a seat on the Governors’ Assembly. This document catalogues the League’s known structure, membership, and operations.
I. Summary

The Free Defence League operates from Winter Tower at Castle Radiance, fielding organized adventuring teams that take bounties posted at the castle. General Bargle Battamond founded and leads the League, which numbered nearly 40 members as of the early 1480s. The FDL emphasizes mutual aid and brotherhood among adventurers: all members share supplies and intelligence, provide mandatory assistance to fellow League members in trouble, and return one-third of bounty money to the League treasury.

The League maintains at least three adventuring teams: Gryphon Company, Dragon Company, and a third team (initially designated “Mule Company,” later known by that name independently). The FDL also employs a garrison of eight hired soldiers at Winter Tower. “Ser” Reginald Boxfray commands the 14th Company, 1st Platoon “Dawnshields” (4 squads), indicating the FDL adopted more formal military organization over time or in parallel with its bounty operations.

The FDL fought alongside the Sun Blades at Thistle Ridge in 1483.

II. Command & Leadership
General & Founder

Bargle Battamond

“General Bargle of the Free Defence League” • “Bargle Battlemaster”
Winter Tower, Castle Radiance
Old warrior. Addresses his men “like old friends.” Realistic assessor of talent—rates new recruits candidly as being “at the start of their careers” and emphasizes that many adventurers die in the mountains without organization. His philosophy centres on team structure, shared resources, and mutual protection. Also rendered “Captain” informally. Holds a seat on the Governors’ Assembly under the Concordat.
Commander, 14th Company

“Ser” Reginald Boxfray

Commander, 1st Platoon “Dawnshields” (4 squads)
Field / Castle Radiance
Author of the Memoire of Ser Reginald Boxfray, a primary source for the Thistle Ridge campaign (1483). The “Ser” honorific and formal company/platoon structure suggests the FDL adopted or was assigned a more military organization by this period.
III. Headquarters — Winter Tower
Winter Tower
South end of outer wall, outer bailey, Castle Radiance
Six levels plus a dungeon level. Approximately 60’ high with an irregular 80’×80’ footprint. The south gate location makes it a defensible bulwark.
Layout
Ground floor: Huge hall with 20’ ceiling. Trophy collection from League bounties. Practice area with weapons racks.
2nd level: 12 rooms for garrison and team members. Kitchens and dining room.
3rd level: Gryphon Company quarters and armoury.
4th level: War room for Bargle’s meetings. Connection to outer wall battlements.
5th level: Dragon Company private quarters, armoury, dining, lounge, library.
6th level: Bargle’s private quarters and his assistant’s (Mr. Acorn) office.

One of the League’s original adventuring teams. Has its own quarters and armoury on the third level of Winter Tower. Previously had a bounty interfered with by the Redjacks (1478), leading to the Redjacks’ temporary ban from Castle Radiance.

14th Company, 1st Platoon “Dawnshields”
4 squads
Under the command of “Ser” Reginald Boxfray. Fought at Thistle Ridge (1483). The company/platoon numbering suggests a formal military structure either adopted by the FDL or imposed by the Concordat.
V. Recruitment Terms (c. Greengrass 1479)
As offered to Mule Company at recruitment:

• Free licensing and room accommodation at League tower
• All meals and bounty supplies provided
• Loan of starter gear (kept after 2 successful bounties)
• One-third of bounty money returned to the League
• Share of magical/artifact finds for League surplus gear pool
• Mandatory assistance to other League members in trouble
• Benefit of having a dozen or more seasoned adventurers as backup
