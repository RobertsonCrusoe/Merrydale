⚔ THE WHITE CLOAKS ⚔

Chartered Company of Daggerdale — Summary & Roster, c. 1479–1483 DR

About This Document

The White Cloaks are the most prominent chartered adventuring company in Daggerdale. Founded in the early summer of 1479 DR, they grew from a handful of bounty-hunters into a significant military and political force within the dale. This document catalogues their known membership, command structure, operational history, and relationship to the broader Daggerdale military establishment. Where source material permits, individual biographical details are preserved. This is a roster document with contextual summary, not a full organizational chart—the White Cloaks’ internal structure evolved organically and was never as rigid as the Sun Blades’.
I. Summary

The White Cloaks were founded at Castle Radiance in the early summer of 1479 DR by Lumina Whitegate (granddaughter of Zenith Sunlord Argent Whitegate), Dorian Dale, Tuugan Swift, and Alystair Dretch. The group took their name from the shimmering white cloaks gifted to them by the Zenith Lord himself—magical garments bearing the holy symbol of Amaunator in embroidery, which “shimmer and reflect light in a conspicuous and dazzling fashion when worn inside out.”

Their first mission—the assault on the orc fortress of Gang’Dar to free enslaved masons—cost them Dorian Dale, their first casualty, but bonded the survivors and drew Iolus Warburton and Troia Trustar into the company. Over the following months the White Cloaks grew through recruitment at their hostel in White Cloak Tower and through the reputation won in battles against Shadovar assassins, werewolf clans, krinth war-bands, and the agents of the Dark Garden.

By mid-1480 DR the company operated three teams: a field intelligence team (Dretch, Swift, Trustar), a “difficult mission” team (Warburton, Altair, Harbaugh, Sterling, Sorashana, Rhuun of Thunder), and a novice team of newer recruits led by veterans. The Daggerdale Concordat of Elesias 1482 granted the White Cloaks a Charter Militant, recognizing them as a formal part of the dale’s defence and granting their Captain a seat on the Governors’ Assembly.

The company was also marked by significant internal tensions. A crisis meeting in 1480 saw Banitt Harbaugh resign his cloak over the question of military command versus individual conscience, though he continued to fight alongside the company as “Guardian of the Tomb, First Pilgrim.” Captain Lumina Whitegate’s leadership was questioned both from within (Warburton’s private doubts about her decisions at Gang’Dar and in the Shadowfell) and from without.

II. Command Structure
The White Cloaks maintain a military chain of command, though this has been contentious. Lumina Whitegate holds the rank of Captain by founding right. Iolus Warburton serves as Sergeant and field commander for most operations. The company is organized into operational sub-teams designated by letter (Alpha, Bravo, etc.), though these evolved over time.
Leadership
Captain & Founder

Lumina Whitegate

Captain of the White Cloaks
White Cloak Tower, Castle Radiance
Granddaughter of Zenith Sunlord Argent Whitegate. Priestess of Amaunator. Co-founded the company in early summer 1479 with Dorian Dale, Tuugan Swift, and Alystair Dretch. Led the assault on Gang’Dar. Her experience in the Shadowfell left her deeply affected—“something cracked or died inside her on that ill-fated journey.” Recovered partially after intervention by her grandfather. Procured White Cloak Tower and established the ground-floor hostel for pilgrims. Holds a seat on the Governors’ Assembly under the Concordat. Candidate for the Order of the Lambent Shield (declined).
Banitt Harbaugh to Lumina: “You are ‘Captain’ by birthright in the dales, not by merit… Lumina.”
Sergeant & Field Commander

Iolus Warburton

Sergeant of the White Cloaks • Soldier of Amaunator
Field operations / Castle Radiance
Descendant of a long and troubled Daggerdale family. Great-great-grandson of Tiberius Warburton (paladin of Lathander) and grandson of Gode Warburton (Zhent collaborator, executed). Left his family’s failing farm at twelve to join the Church of Amaunator. Possesses a natural, intuitive connection to Amaunator that does not require prayer. Freed from captivity at Gang’Dar by the founding party. Led the Deadnettle task force. Made the controversial deal with the dragon Kruhwahll. Candidate for the Order of the Lambent Shield, “which he regularly turns down.”
III. Operational Teams (c. Mid-1480 DR)
By mid-summer 1480 DR, the White Cloaks operated three distinct teams, reflecting the growth and specialization of the company.
Field Intelligence Team
The “Alpha” team. Responsible for scouting, reconnaissance, and covert operations. Operates independently for extended periods, often in Cormanthor and the Dagger Hills.
Alystair Dretch — Sergeant. Avenger of Amaunator. “The Avenger.” Brutal interrogator, relentless hunter. Single-handedly defended a pilgrim column across two nights of continuous combat. Investigated Dwimmerholme Farm and the Black Hand of Gaebril. Dipped his armour in the blood of slain werewolf infants as a vow of vengeance against the Shadovar.
Tuugan Swift — Co-founder. Ranger and tracker of peerless skill; “misses no track.” Chauntean. Nephew of Sgt. Fortune Swift (Tesh Valley militia). Severely wounded in the Shadowfell—a sword was found protruding from his abdomen; healed by Lumina but bears permanent venous scars. Twin brother of Alaric Swift (Sun Blades captain).
Troia Trustar — Priestess of Sune. Freed herself from captivity at the top of Gang’Dar keep and leapt across the bailey to land among the party. Possessed of “grace and dignity that is unexplainable if not witnessed.” Soothed the werewolf Braxus and negotiated his relocation to Gang’Dar. Deeply affected by the Shadowfell ordeal.
Difficult Mission Team
The “Bravo” team. Handles the most dangerous bounties and direct combat operations.
Iolus Warburton — Team leader. See Command section above.
Corran Altair — Paladin of Torm. Former Sun Blade, discharged after the “Mystral affair.” Served years of labour at Castle Radiance, Fort Last Sun, and Fort Sansum on the Plaguelands frontier. Returned as a paladin bearing antiquated Tormite arms and armour. Recruited by Banitt Harbaugh in mid-winter 1479–80. Also Sergeant of the Order of the Golden Lion. Always carries an enchanted scrollcase.
Banitt Harbaugh — “The Pilgrim” • “Guardian of the Tomb, First Pilgrim.” Resigned his cloak over the question of military vs. divine authority but continued to fight alongside the company. Endorsed by Zenith Lord Argent Whitegate. Recruited Corran Altair from Fort Sansum. Sought the Black Saint. Has “a blade upon his back” of uncertain origin. Resigned cloak but not company.
Drake Sterling — Soldier. “Pretty sure he was happy enough being told where to go.” A straightforward fighter content with orders. Relishes physical dominance. Imagined rendering everyone he looked upon helpless—“but he did that with everyone.”
Sorashana — Recruited through the White Cloak Tower hostel, autumn 1479.
Rhuun of Thunder — Recruited through the White Cloak Tower hostel, autumn 1479.
Novice Team
Newest recruits, led by one or more veterans on rotation. Handles lower-risk bounties and training operations.

VI. Headquarters & Assets
White Cloak Tower
Outer wall, Castle Radiance
Multi-storey tower on the outer wall of Castle Radiance. Ground floor converted to a hostel for pilgrims and travellers (free lodging and one meal per day), which also served as a recruitment venue. Third floor houses the armoury (thick metal door, keys held by Lumina and the Quartermaster). Third floor also contains a conference room (outgrown by the time of the Crisis meeting, when the company had to use the second-floor training room).
Armoury Inventory (Selected)
The armoury is provisioned for extended field operations: 25 sections of hempen rope (50’ each), 10 spare backpacks, 5 prepared adventurer kits, 5 prepared climbing kits, 300 arrows, 200 quarrels, 5 daggers, 2 longswords, 3 maces, and an ornate Gondsman chest containing six pale sacks of 500 gold pieces each. The chest is secured by an ornate gold lock with two key slots; one key is kept in a lantern behind the desk, the second inside the desk itself, accessed by a third key in the Quartermaster’s possession.
VII. Relationships & Cross-References
Connections to Other Organizations
Sun Blades: Several White Cloaks are former Sun Blades. Corran Altair was discharged; Sammus Weaver served his term; Tara Hilltop left to join White Cloak leadership; Zelia Oakbough is a retired Sun Blade forester. Tuugan Swift’s twin brother Alaric is a Sun Blades captain.

Order of the Lambent Shield: Iolus Warburton is a recurring candidate who “regularly turns down” admission. The Order provided security at the Shadowfell cave (Knight Commander Pauldron). Lumina is granddaughter of the Zenith Lord.

Order of the Golden Lion: Corran Altair is simultaneously a Sergeant of the Golden Lion, a White Cloak, and a member of Mule Company—a career arc from Sun Blade conscript to paladin of Torm.

Free Defence League: Banitt Harbaugh had “recent trouble with the Free Defense League” before joining the White Cloaks. The FDL’s Mule Company includes Corran Altair as a shared member.

Church of Amaunator: The White Cloaks are closely tied to the Church but Lumina explicitly stated the company “is not now, nor will it ever be under my leadership, an extension of the Church of Amaunator.”

Mule Company: Corran Altair serves in both companies. Mule Company originated as a Free Defence League team.
