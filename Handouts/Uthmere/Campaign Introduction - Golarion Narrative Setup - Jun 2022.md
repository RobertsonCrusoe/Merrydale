So I filled you guys in on how things potentially start...

Here's a better descriptor of the current situation your characters start
in when they are children between the ages of 10 to 16.

After the first session.  We will be doing a twelve year time jump to
"current day" Golarion.

I'm not a writer...so my apologies in advance.

*An elderly looking man of medium height, slender with a long grey beard
that appeared to be gathered together in a series of leather ties and then
tucked inside his leather breeches stood before all of you.  He wore a
large, floppy brimmed hat, tilted far enough back on his head that you
could see his dancing blue eyes.  His face looked like…well…what you might
describe as kind. The old man carried a small wooden flute in his left hand
and what appeared to be a small journal tucked under his right arm.

Looking down at all of you, each dressed differently to your natures, he
smiled knowingly and spoke in a voice that could best be described as warm
and perhaps just a hint of something else. Something that made the hair on
the back of your necks stand up, as if you were in the presence of
something that transcended the mundane world around you.

"So...you are the ones my brother sent to me. I was wondering if you were
going to show up."

And with that, the old man turns and begins to wander deeper into the
cavern, calling over his shoulder as he walks.

"Follow me. There is much to discuss and far less time than you would think
to discuss it."

Oddly, the moment his feet touched the ground, the cavern was suddenly
filled with a soft blue light that emanated from what appeared to be tiny
motes of luminescence that floated from his clothing and swirled around
him like a small galaxy.

As the old man moved deeper into the cavern, he began to play a soft melody
on his flute, and it seemed to echo off the cavern walls in a strange way…
as if his flute was somehow playing in harmony with itself. The melody was
beautiful, haunting, and seemed to have a strange effect on all of you.

Time seemed to slow ever so slightly, and your thoughts became clear and
focused. Your emotions, which had seemed so chaotic and out of control, now
seemed to have purpose and meaning. Your fears seemed to melt away, and
your hopes seemed to crystallize into focus.

For just a moment, the world seemed to be at peace, and all the suffering
that you had endured up to this point seemed to be worth something.

And then, just as quickly as it began, the moment was over.

The old man walked into a large chamber and stopped, setting himself down
on a large stone that was covered in strange symbols and runes. Around him
in a circle lay seven other similar stones, each also covered in strange
symbols and runes of their own.

He gestured to the stones around him and spoke again.

"Please sit. We have much to discuss and far less time than you would think
to discuss it."

For the first time, his expression changed. His warm, friendly demeanor
seemed to fall away, and in its place was the weathered expression of a man
who had seen far too much.

"My name is Eragon. I have been sent here by my brother, to teach and
prepare you for what is to come. I do not know the full details of what my
brother has planned for you, but I know this much; your lives as you knew
them are over. You will never again walk the streets of your childhood homes
and see them as you once did. The weight of knowing what you now know will
forever change your perception of the world around you."

He paused and looked each of you in the eyes.

"The question is not whether your lives will change. That is inevitable. The
question is whether you will change with them, or whether you will be broken
by them."
