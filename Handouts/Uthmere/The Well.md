A disturbing missive

Midsummer, 1466 DR

May this find you in good health,

By the thaw of 1451 DR, word had spread throughout the region that the we were reputable and worth seeking aid from should the need arise and demonology suspect.  A missive arrived at the Bastion one day detailed the recent events of a chain of villages that dotted the foothills of north-western Impiltur.  It described how over the course of a few weeks several young children had started to disappear during the night.  No amount of searching turned up any useful tracks or information and the village elders blamed the increasing presence of foulspawn for the disappearances.  Watchers were dispatched and after two days of augury they arrived at a small lonely house made of rough stone and timber deep in the foothills.

Living there was a lone tiefling who asserted his complete innocence.  He was living a quite life far from the oppression he faced from Impilturan society.  He was questioned with force, the extent of which is somewhat hazy in this retelling, but his story held true, that is, until we found the hidden door to the subbasement.  We climbed into the dark earthen room and discovered a four-foot rough-stone well situated in the centre of the room.  We also found, to our horror, wooden tables lining the room stacked with piles of neatly-folded children's clothes.  I regret to inform that the tiefling did not survive the second round of questioning.  What happened to those children is better left to speculation and not for my retelling here, in hopes that these terrible events will be forgotten with time.

Before that monster spoke its last we were informed as to the magical properties of the Well.  It was not secured to the ground by any means other than its weight, which happened to be unusually light, so we lashed it to a wagon and brought it with us.  We informed the village elders as to the fate of their children and spent a time mourning their loss.  We then returned home and spent several years studying the artifact.

Its history is clouded from us, both through divination and research, it is unknown from whence it came or when it was constructed - an anomaly of history apparently.  Some of the Keepers have their theories, but those would be better left for another conversation.  What we do know for sure is that the Well contains a portal in its depths that ties itself every 483 days to a seemingly random plane of the Elemental Chaos, in the 11 times we have seen it shift they have all been portals to some layer of the Abyss.  The Keepers assume that during the Spellplague the portal magic woven into the Well was destabilized.  If it could permanently tie to one exit portal, it can no longer.  If the destination portal could be chosen, it can no longer.  They are currently looking in to the Well's potential dangers and possible uses, but that is for a subsequent report.

One more thing, those who were there with me, in that basement, were sworn to secrecy and I know my men are fiercely loyal and would never disobey a direct order.  But what I find disturbing, what shakes my very soul, is the name some of the other Watchers have given the Well.  Given its immediate history and what went on in those hills, I would request you make known that the Well not be referred to as, the 'Wishing Well'.

Knowledge through truth, strength through knowledge, self through strength.

Long stand the Watchtower,
— Chief Watcher Morros
