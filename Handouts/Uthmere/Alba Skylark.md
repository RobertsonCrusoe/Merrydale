== Tome of Knowledge: Alba Skylark ==

Daughter of the once wealthy Skylark family who smithed most of the weaponry needed by the New Sarshel militia. Alba, a wayfaring cleric of Oghma grew up in the small town of Brossel just north of New Sarshel. Her grandfather, Feinrich Skylark, was one of the senior strategists involved in dealing with the emerging demonic threat in that eastern region of Impiltur. With this looming threat, her family sends her to apprentice under Loremaster Nestrius, leader of a small militant faction of Oghma known as The Watchtower.

The Watchtower's chief concern is with that of creatures of demonic and aberrant origin. The Abyss, its denizens, layers, and demigods are studied and researched there in order to better protect the mortals of Faerûn. You were taught over a period of years a vast assortment of knowledge pertaining to the foul arcana employed by those of the Abyss. Armed with her expertise in demonic affairs and combat training she ventured out with small adventuring groups and other Watchtower members (called \"Watchers\") in order to protect her city and nation from threats beyond the realms.

More recently, she has since left her native country and ventured eastward. stopping in Uthmere on her way to see the druids of Yeshelmaar. She was sent by Loremaster Nestrius, to acquire a series of writings from the vast libraries of Yeshelmaar. The writings are called, \"The Slates of Obsidian\" and are a collection of ancient legends, fables, prophecy, and history. Believed to be written by several authors over 2000 years, only fragments of some remain while others have been almost completely eroded by the sands of time.

He also expressed that your journey is of extreme importance and emphasized secrecy. You are their most successful Watcher and have had years of training, this task must be kept within the Watchtower as he believes no other can be trusted. He also provided a note addressed to \"Druid of the Second Order, Master Tomlin Swiftmend\". It is in a sealed scroll case made of ivory, bound be powerful magics, you implicitly know that scrying and other forms of prying will most assuredly fail.
