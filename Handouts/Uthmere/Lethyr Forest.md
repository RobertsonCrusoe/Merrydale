The Forest of Lethyr occupies the southern half of the Great Dale and provides a natural boundary between the Great Dale and Thesk.  This great area of ancient forest lies within the borders of the Great Dale, although its southern tip (that part south of the Lethyr Stream) belongs to Thesk.  The forest abounds with wildlife, including bears, wolves, big cats, deer, rabbits, and all manner of birds, squirrels, and other treeloving animals and it is carefully forested and maintained by those who dwell within its confines.  The wood found here is generally employed for shipbuilding, spear shafts, bows, and arrows.  Weapons common to the inhabitants include the longbow, battleaxe, and short spear.

Druids, rangers, wardens and other folk of the woodlands form the druidic order called the Circle of Leth and answer to the Nentyarch (the most powerful druid in northeastern Faerûn).  They guard the boundaries of the Forest of Lethyr, protect its inhabitants, and patrol both the Forest of Lethyr and the Dunwood Forest to the north. Several treants who serve the druids also roam the wood, dedicated to finding and dispatching demonic infections from the north. A few secretive villages of elves exist in the woodland. Human settlements are limited to the forest's southern edges, in Theskian land.

In the western part of the woodlands stands Mauberg's Oak, a massive tree 300 ft tall, 50ft in diameter serving as an academy of sorts for druids and Circle initiates.  In more recent years it has served as the staging area for several forays into the northwestern Dunwood to purge any and all threats of demonic nature.

In the northeastern part of the forest, lies a clearing called the Mucklestones. These three stones once served as portals leading to other places in Faerûn, one of which lead to the Sunglade deep in the Yuirwood (in Aglarond).  Since their communication with the Masters of Yuirwood has been severed, the Circle of Leth now employ less immediate ways means of communing with their brothers to the south.

The Yeshelmaar also serves as the informal capital of the Great Dale, where the Nentyarch resides.
