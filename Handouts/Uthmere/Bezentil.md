== Tome of Knowledge: Bezentil ==

Bezentil is the only real community between the Great Road's western and eastern ends, and the only town worthy of the name for at least a hundred miles in any direction.  Here a series of ten dwarf-high stones are spaced along the road in 10-foot intervals.  The site has been used as a meeting place for untold years, but the settlement itself is relatively new.

The sole industry in Bezentil is trading with people passing through on the Road.  Caravans commonly use the place as a waymeet.  The locals bring out locally made or grown goods such as fine wooden items, rare herbs, produce, pelts, and leather goods, bartering for manufactured items such as tools and weapons.  The people of the Great Dale are wonderful foresters and farmers, but they lack skill in mining and working metals.

Bezentil is nestled in a small valley of sorts.  If one were to approach from the west, the road would take you through the wooden palisade straight into the town proper and they would see the two large hills that rise over the village to the east, atop each stands a small wooden watchtower buffeted by the strong winds that sweep the dale.  Approaching from the east one would see the two hills and two towers as the road leads down between the hills to the palisade and into the village.  The hills help to shield the village from the blistering winds and several buildings are built into their westward slopes.

While the villagers have only rudimentary defences and would never be able to hold off a demon on their own, luckily there are enough Daelen clansmen or Nentyar hunters either passing through or patrolling within a short distance of the village to keep it secure from any minor incursions.

== Major Businesses ==

Only a handful of businesses flourish in Bezentil.  Their proprietors are the friendliest and most open-minded people east of Uthmere.

The largest building is Ol' Strick's Trading Post, a general store run by the now elderly, Lorando Strick, son of the original owners, Abrihando and Gennia Strick.  Travelers can buy and sell most mundane items worth 25 gp or less here, although Strick's prices are double normal.  He's literally the only shop for hundreds of miles around, and he knows it.

There wasn't much left of the Windblown Goat, a small inn and tap house, after the terrible fires that followed the Spell Plague and has since been was rebuilt and renamed by it's new owner, Tamor Plainson.  It is now called the Middledale Inn and can charitably be described as \"rustic\".  There are a half-dozen rooms available at a reasonable rate, and on a winter night, a dirt-floored room with a fire is much more comfortable than a blanket on a frigid hillside.  Plainson's kitchen serves a hearty meal and excellent homemade ale, but the speciality of the house is a precious liquor known as goldenleaf.

An old livery stable is a stone's throw from the Middledale Inn.  Weary travelers can rest their mounts and have them groomed and fed for a few silver pieces each.  Cassilla Moore, the owner, is also a skilled cartwright, capable of repairing all but the most damaged wagons.  She has been a godsend to the caravans that have begun to pass through Bezentil with increased frequency over the last twenty years.
