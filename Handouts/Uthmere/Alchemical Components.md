A list of plants, herbs, mosses, and other components used in the art of making oils, salves, and potions.  These are commonly found throughout the plains and forests of the Great Dale.  All of these ingredients can be used by anyone proficient in the art of alchemy, be it a player or an NPC, to a varying degree of success.  

(DM note: This information has been taken and adapted from Dragon Magazine: Issue 301 & 317.)

== Organic ==

Cotsbalm

This fleshy-leafed plant, characterized by clusters of small yellow flowers, grows to a height of 1 foot.  A hardy plant, it can be found throughout temperate and subtropical forests.  Cotsbalm sap is extracted and used as a base for a clear, syrupy substance called purebalm.  Purebalm grants a +2 power bonus to the saving throw against the on-going poison damage to the imbiber.

Wittlewort

A very fine herb with gossamer-like green fronds, wittlewort has a rapid growth cycle.  Partly because of this rapid growth, it can be found only during spring months in temperate, subtropical, and tropical areas.  Once it has been dried, treated, and powdered, the alchemist dissolves the wittlewort in hot water to complete the alchemical process.  Wortleaf tea is useful for someone under arcane-induced conditions (blind, immobilized, weakened, etc.) by providing them an immediate saving throw against such conditions, provided they can drink the potion.

Musk Muddle

A brown, dead-looking plant with wide leaves and an unpleasant aroma, musk muddle can be found in nearly any swamp or marsh.  Musk muddle resembles the dock plant, a foul-smelling weed, and when boiled prevents an integral part of burn salve.  Burn salves help those afflicted with ongoing fire damage and grant them a +2 power bonus to the saving throw against the on-going fire damage, on a success the victim heals an additional 5 hit points.

Tyrant's Sword

A coarse, broad-leafed grass with sharp edges, tyrant's sword grows to a height of about 2 feet, distinguished only by the silvery edges of its leaves.  Although difficult to find, it grows sporadically in both temperate plains and tundra regions.  Tyrant's sword (named for its appearance) grows slowly and has a difficult time competing with other plants for survival.  Because of this, it is usually found away from other plants.  The silvery edges of tyrant's sword can be boiled and used to make a warm, porridge-like substance called frost lotion.  Frost lotion grants a victim of ongoing cold damage a +2 power bonus to the saving throw against on-going cold damage, on a success the victim heals an additional 5 hit points.

Prickly Tea

A small thorn bush with gray-green leaves, prickly tea rarely grows over 3 feet in height.  Although it is almost never found in large groups, the thorny bush can be found throughout temperate and subtropical plains and forests.  An alchemist who knows the proper distillation techniques can create a powerful effect by refining normal prickly tea into a stronger, fouler-tasting substance known as senses.  Senses is a tea that heightens the drinker's five senses, and grants the individual a +2 power bonus to Perception checks for 1 hour.

Elven Willow

The elven willow is a small tree (maximum height 5 feet) that grows by the banks of rivers in temperate forests.  The willow has a golden sheen to its bark and produces golden coloured buds in the spring.  There is much superstition concerning the elven willow.  It is said that each elven willow bears the spirit of a love-lorn elf who drowned in a river near the tree.  The sap of the elven willow is the main component in a fluid called elf hazel.  When applied to any scar (from a now-healed wound) the scar slowly vanishes.  It takes a week of daily applications for the scar to vanish completely.

Dwarven Oak

Dwarven oak is a stunted, gnarled affair with thick, iron-hard roots and heavily wrinkled bark that grows on the lower slopes of temperate mountains.  From a distance, the tree looks somewhat like a sitting dwarf, and goblin arrows occasionally jut from lone trees.  The bark of the dwarven oak can be used to create a viscous brown liquid called oakdeath, that can be added to any liquid poison to increase its potency (+1 to the required saving throw).

Wild Fireclover

Wild fireclover is a summer plant with a brilliant red and orange flower that blooms in temperate plains and farmland.  The unmistakable red blooms of fireclover are usually found in small clusters of three to ten.  The petals of wild fireclover are often crushed and used by peasants to provide pleasant scents.  Crushed petals provide a beautiful aroma for up to a week.  What is not commonly known is that the stem of the wild fireclover is capable of producing a powerful mind-clouding addition to a normal poison.  When combined with an ingested poison, mindfire leaves the imbiber confused and disoriented (on the first failed saving throw the target is dazed until the start of its next turn).
