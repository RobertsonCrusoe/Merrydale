The founding and more recent history of Uthmere, its people, and the events of the western Great Dale.

Return to: Uthmere

== Founding ==

Uthmere was built as a small trading and fishing outpost on the mouth of the Dalestream over 800 years ago by Lord Uthmere of Dilpur, a wealthy Dilpuran sea captain who believed he had discovered the perfect gateway to eastern Faerûn.  The trade post grew slowly yet steadily over the years, maturing into a small bustling hub of commerce for those who seek the Great Dale's rich raw materials.  Its existence is a thorn in the side of the Great Dale's original inhabitants, the Daelen and Circle of Leth.

== Timeline ==

1385 DR
* Mystra’s death and the creation of the Spellplague.
* Uthmere’s population sits at around 6,000 within the city and 8,000 outside.

1387 DR
* Two of Reginald Uthlain’s stone giant guards suffer terrible deaths from the Spellplague, a third disappears without a trace. 
* The Uthlain converts a small park into three graves for the stone giants.

1388 DR
* The Years of Sorrow begin.
* The last caravan arrives in Uthmere as trade with the outside world comes to a silent end: caravans, wagons, and ships aren’t seen for years.

1390-1398 DR
* Periodic earthquakes slowly destroy the city and its farmlands. The city walls are devastated and most of the city lies in ruin.
* The lands surrounding Uthmere to the south and north-east shift and raise, transforming the small hills into plateaus and mesas.
* The first mass exodus of the Uthmaar to Impiltur, most of the city dwellers leave as they have no place to live.

1399-1403 DR
* Strange and demonic creatures start venturing from the Dunwood Forest. Ravaging the country side.
* The second mass exodus to Impiltur by [Uthmere]’s remaining farmers.

1404 DR
* Hundreds starve that winter as the last of the eatable stored grain is consumed. Hundreds more are slaughtered by demonic creatures from the woods to the north east.
* Many flee to Uthmere and whatever safety it can provide. Uthmere’s population sits, scared and hungry, at around 1,200 people.

1405 DR
* Lord [Reginald Uthlain] commits suicide by leaping from a battlement.
* His son, Richan Uthlain is crowned Lord of Uthmere at the age of 28, becoming the 9th Uthlain to rule the city.
* Everyone still living in the city who is not part of the militia is tasked at hunting and gathering what little they can to keep food stores up.

1407 DR
* An envoy is sent from the Circle of Leth, seeking a military alliance and unified force against the creatures of the Dunwood.
* Lord Richan Uthlain accepts and the Nentyar hunters and Uthmere militia are stationed in the jagged plains north-east of the city and along the Great Road.

1408 DR
* The Years of Sorrow End.
* Construction of the Richan Wall is started. An ambitious plan to construct a defensive bulwark of earth and logs to help protect the country side and form a choke point into the western plains.

1409 DR
* With the land in such chaos, several foolhardy individuals call themselves “adventurers” and stop in Uthmere before heading off into the woods seeking treasure, glory, honour—few return.

1411 DR
* First ships start to sail into the ports, trade routes are painstakingly re-established. If the sea aberrations don’t destroy the cargo ships, the pirates are all too eager to pillage and sink them.

1414 DR
* The adventurers known as the Graywater Gang become prominent figures in the region. Uthlain sends them on several expeditions into the Dunwood Forest.

1418 DR
* Construction of the Richan Wall is finished. A forty-mile long earthen bulwark that stands 10 feet tall, with an additional 10 feet of timber palisade. The wall itself is 18 miles long but uses several outward facing plateau walls to help span the distance.

1421 DR
* Attacks on Uthmere and the surrounding countryside are significantly reduced.

1422 DR
* Farmlands are ploughed once again in relative safety. Food shortages cease.

1423 DR
* Tales of Uthmere’s safety reach Damaran and Impiltur and twenty years after most of the Uthmaar fled some start to return. Mostly farming families and tradesmen looking to reclaim their lost land, or make a fresh start.

1429 DR
* Richard Uthlain is born.

1431 DR
* Wide scale reconstruction plans are made to restore Uthmere to its former glory.
* Pressure from the Circle of Leth hampers these plans, reducing the scale of the proposed city by almost half.  Richan Uthlain concedes to these new plans, not wanting to risk the safety and protection provided by the Circle.

1435 DR
* The brother’s Silverstone arrive in Uthmere and almost immediately take over the reconstruction of the city’s walls, streets, and buildings.

1446 DR
* It has been 6 years since the last attack against the Richan Wall or any farmstead in the region. It is assumed the creatures returned to wherever they came from, or have learned the danger of venturing too far from the forest.
* The Nentyarch informs Richan of further danger in the greater region of the Great Dale to the east and requests the assistance of his militia.  Richan declines, claiming he cannot spare the men.

1447 DR
* The military alliance between Uthmere and the Circle is quietly dissolved on somewhat good terms. The forces of the Circle of Leth return to the east to protect the Daelen and the Forest of Lethyr from the remaining demonic forces.  This event is known as the \"Disbanding\".

1448 DR
* With the threat of the Dunwood Forest no longer looming, combined with the removal of the Circle’s reinforcements, Richan decides to pull his men from the wall. He keeps a small detachment there as scouts, but the majority return to Uthmere to help rebuild.

1450 DR
* A city council is formed and authority is given over matters of policy, taxes, justice, trade, etc. Collectively they are called, “lordlain”.

1451 DR
* A merchant’s guild, \"The Five Coins\", is formed now that trade has resumed to such an extent to warrant one.

1452 DR
* Lord Richan Uthlain dies of old age. Hailed as the Uthmere’s saviour and its greatest ruler.
* Richard Uthlain is crowned Lord of Uthmere at the age of 23, becoming the 10th Uthlain to rule the city.

1475 DR
* The Year of the New Dawn.
* The city, while smaller in size, is more or less restored to its pre-Spellplague way of life.
* In honour of the great work and dedication shown by the dwarven brothers, Richard Uthlain named the final constructed district, “Silverstone” in their honour.

1477 DR
* First presence of the Shadowmasters is seen, they moved up from the southern Theskian city of Telflamm.

1485 DR
* Present Day Uthmere. Population: 3,500 in the city and 5,000 surrounding.
