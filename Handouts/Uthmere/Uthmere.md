== Present Day ==

Uthmere is loosely defined as the port city and all land surrounding it within 30 miles. It’s a quiet, tidy, and relatively safe port with 2,500 souls within its walls and an additional 5,000 living in the surrounding countryside. With a regular patrol of city guard in the city that levy strict fines against ruffians, litterers, and disturbers of the peace. Most of the citizenry are content and law-abiding, although there are more than enough that frequent the lock-ups.

Streets are cobbled and buildings are made of fieldstone with roofs fashioned from slate or tile. All buildings are at least three stories tall, as only shallow cellars are available due to the swampy silt footings under most buildings. Roofs are steeply pitched to shed snow and rain and balconies start on the third floor of most buildings.

The Dalestream runs through Uthmere dividing the city into Northstream and Southgate — the former being everything north of it and the latter, everything south of it up to the southern walls of the city. The Keep and everything east of it as well as all of Southgate are at a slightly higher elevation compared to the rest of the city. Those two regions manage to peak above the nightly fog but the lower lying central region of Uthmere is soaked in eight feet of it every night.

The central square holds a bell tower that houses six bells of various sizes and tones and stands 80 feet tall. Towering over the rest of the city, this tower can be seen from almost anywhere in Uthmere. Surrounding this tower is a bustling allmarket that sells every manner of goods and equipment. East at the Dalegate Square livestock, workhorses, carts and other means of transport are traded and at the Southgate Square is a large food produce market from the local farmers.

== Atmosphere ==

Uthmere is a small city-state that has rebuilt itself and grown steadily during the aftermath of the Spellplague.  The populous is well-fed, sheltered, and generally content with a strong sense of pride in their leaders, Lord Uthlain and the Lordlain.

In the last few years there has been a wavering feeling of safety due to the war between the criminal Shadowmasters and Captain Ashar Jerra-kai of the Cudgels.

The Uthmaar feel safe behind her unrivalled discipline, training, and quest for order that has moulded the guard into a capable and commanding force.  Safety is almost assured when the guard are on duty and patrolling frequently.

However, there are threats looming in the shadows and hidden places of this city.  Darkness and corruption permeate every aspect of Uthmere, slowly rotting it to its core.  Certain names aren't spoken, streets aren't walked, and topics are quickly hushed.  Paranoia is beginning to take root, evident in the stalwart belief that the Syndicate are everywhere and are ever watchful.

During the day Uthmere is like any other city, bustling with commerce, events, and merriment.  Most city squares are crowded with people seeking entertainment, food, and stores.  The streets, depending on the district, are filled with carriages, horses, messengers, wagons, couriers, and children playing.  The troublesome Shadowmasters are barely an afterthought, the people trust the guard and feel safe so long as the sun is in the sky.

After the fourth braze the city completely changes.  People return to their homes and stay indoors after dark and rarely walk the streets.  When they do go out at night they stay very close to the armed Cudgel patrols and avoid the Shadowstreets at all costs.

== Lands Surrounding Uthmere ==

400px|thumb|A drawing of the properties and landscape surrounding Uthmere.
The earthquakes that nearly destroyed Uthmere caused extensive upheaval in the lands immediately surrounding and centred on the city. The rolling plains of the western Dale were broken by the sheer force of nature almost overnight, scattering plateaus and small mesas up to 80 miles away. The soil beneath the plateaus and remaining flatlands is just as fertile as it was before the Spellplague, only now some of it towers some 50-200 feet over the rest. Much of the land north-east of Uthmere is now referred to as the Tablelands and given its history as farmland, the crops that went untouched for decades have grown into a dense sea of wild grain, corn, and wheat fields that have taken over the tops of most of these highlands while leaving the shaded valleys and gullies bare.

When people started to return shortly after the Richan Wall was finished many found their lands scattered and split across several hundreds of feet. Buildings sat in valleys while their fields topped mesas. Most agreed to divided or exchange portions of land as evenly as they could so each family could have suitable acreage to support themselves.  Years have passed, and these awkwardly situated farmers continue the way of life their parents and grandparents had started before them.

In the highlands to the north and south of Uthmere farmers grow crops of all sorts, but prefer the hardier vegetables and grains like potatoes, wheat and corn. Depending on the type of crop, fenced in fields tend to be dug either on top of these mesas (potatoes, radishes, etc.) or down in the valleys (grain, corn, wheat) while free range livestock roam about.  Lost animals are rarely a problem, as most of these farms are bordered by hills, mesas, wood thickets, or all three, preventing most kinds of domesticated animals from roaming too far.  Livestock ranches for the most part, occupy the eastern plateaus and hills of Uthmere where cows, pigs, sheep, and horses are raised and sold back at the Dalegate Square.

A village has recently (1464 DR) formed some 30-35 miles out from Uthmere in the centre of a large basin. Middleton is a little farming community that travellers and caravans make a quick stop at before heading further east into the Dale. This town is the exception to the eastern-livestock farmers, the land surrounding the village is reserved for an abundant supply of vegetables and grains.

A lot of Uthmere's population live in small hamlets made up of a number of neighbouring families and workers, some are small others are quite large, almost considered villages themselves.  They surround the city and are usually found next to main roads, they are what keep Uthmere's belly full, for it is their crops and livestock that is sold in the streets of Uthmere.  Aside from food, they can at times provide other basic services such as tailoring, blacksmithing, and hunting - they often have excess work animals like riding or draft horses.  A few of these hamlets can be seen on the adjacent map, they include: Mivehaven, Centerfield Farms, Farhill Farms, and the Bailey Homestead.  These hamlets can be found along most major roadways up until the Richan Wall, the further you travel from Uthmere the smaller the generally are - as most folk don't like the risk of ranching that far from civilization.

For the most part, the lands up to about 30 miles from the city are owned by those in or around the City of Uthmere. Uthmaar farmsteaders enjoy the expansive land of the north-east up to about the Richan Wall, as well as east up to the Lethyr Gate, anything passed is considered Daelen. The Uthmaar are descendants of Impiltur who emigrated here for the fertile lands and rich natural resources of the Dale. Their expansion has been a slow but steady march toward their eastern Daelen neighbours and tension rises as they move further east into the Dale-proper. The land passed the Richan Wall and Lethyr Gate is either empty or belongs to Daelen farmers and clansmen.

Outside the city walls along the main roads leading west into Damaran and Impiltur, east into the Great Dale, and south toward Thesk are small pockets of urban expansion. Several urban-style houses, shops, and taverns have started popping up over the recent years as the small city’s population is returning back toward its pre-Spellplague levels. The problem stems from the reconstruction effort that reduced Uthmere to less than half of its original size, forcing these entrepreneurs to build outside the walls yet close enough to benefit from the urban economy.

Following the Easting River north from Uthmere are several logging camps along the north-western edge of Dunwood. Many of whom are owned and operated by merchants or craftsmen back in Uthmere, such as Lady Aymelia Murove. The loggers don’t clear-cut the land outright but they are felling acres of the dense forest every year. Stripping the logs and sending them down river to the timber mills near the outlet into the bay.

These logging operations have not gone unnoticed and many of them have been razed and their labourers scattered by the Nentyar hunters and druids of the Circle. Occasionally wild animals and dangerous creatures venture out of the forest to these small outlying settlements endangering the workers and industry. The Uthlain has taken action by establishing an outpost of Cudgels to guard the small region so the timber trade can continue relatively undisturbed.

One interesting landmark is the local clay pit called, the Splitearth Quarry.  The spellplague caused a massive chasm to split open north-east of the city that was first properly utilized by the Silverstones to excavate some of the raw materials to reconstruct the city.  The clay and shale found in the Splitearth Quarry are what many of the streets and buildings of Uthmere are constructed with.  Most of the quarry workers live in the aptly named, Minehaven, west of the quarry.
