Inside inn is a large 50' x 30' common room filled with manner of square, circular, and long tables with chairs and benches filled with rowdy Uthmaar laughing and shouting. Fitted into the centre of both the southern and northern walls are large fireplaces. About 20 feet away from the front doors are a large flight of stairs that lead up to the second level where the sleeping quarters and rooms for rent are located. Behind the common room are the kitchens and there is a door leading back there in the northwest corner of the common area.

This popular establishment is full just about every night.  The second week of Mirtul also sees a large influx of patrons as the owner promotes a festivities to honour those who explore the world and seek out adventure.  For seven days tales of dark dungeons, faraway lands and forgotten treasures are told and adventurer's drink for free.  It's a profitable gimmick because the tavern is usually packed for the duration of the event.  

The inn keeper is a man by the name of Edward Smith and has been the proprietor for nearly six years now.  His rates are 7 silver pieces per room per day, and does not provide lock box services.  This inn is much safer due to its proximity to the North Gate, so there are constant guards on patrol but a dozen yards away.

*Note:  This place was burnt to the ground on Mirtul 8, 1485 DR...
