'She's not worth the effort,' Blue hissed from behind him.
 
Basch merely stared down the dark road before him, unmoving and entirely unsure as to why. Why hesitate? The dragonborn crossed his broad arms and tried to ignore the voices hissing in his ear.
 
'She's dead. No one could have survived that ordeal,' Black drawled.
 
'No one but mighty Basch,' chimed Green, rubbing its scaled head against the sorcerer's shoulder. He pushed it aside, annoyance riding his face.
 
'Indeed, mighty Basch,' chorused the disembodied dragon heads in unison.
 
\"Silence your chattering, you lot.\" Basch snarled, rubbing his chin with one hand. Not an hour had passed since himself and the Oghman had been set upon by those abominations. Undead beasts festering in a ruined forest, Basch and Alba had torn their way through the host of monstrousities until they were well into the wilderness of the acursed wood. The sorcerer himself had only recently found the road, but the cleric was nowhere to be found. 
 
Damnable trees.
 
'Burn it down, burn it all!' cackled White frantically.
 
Basch considered it for a moment, but quickly turned the thought aside. These dragons were insane. The last thing he should do is listen to anything they have to say.
 
Alba was still in there somewhere. What was this he was feeling? This strange emotion concerning the woman, that he should be doing something.
 
'Guilt,' drawled Blue, her tongue flicking mockingly in Basch's face. He clawed at the apparition.
 
Guilt. She did hire him to escort her to Yeshelmarr. Of course, it wasn't Basch's idea to take a route so close to the Great Barrow. To be fair, this entire region was gripped in the throes of chaos. Demons and undead and monstrousities running rampant as though they owned every patch of land they set foot on. Still. Terrible route-planning on her part. She deserved her fate.
 
'True, but then you won't get paid. That won't do at all,' Red pointed out.
 
\"Indeed,\" Basch mused aloud, stroking his chin. He glanced down the road leading west, back to Uthmere.
 
'Are you going to let more of your companions die, Baschrek Tymodes? You abandon her now, you consign the Oghman to death,' purred Black.
 
\"Silence, worm. You tread on dangerous ground.\"
 
'Oh my, she's as good as dead indeed! You've killed so many of your companions through action, this will certainly be the first you've killed through inaction!\" cackled Black.
 
'Nonsense, Basch is no coward!' exclaimed Green.
 
'Oh my, certainly not,' Black mocked.
 
'Kill kill kill, it's all he knows!' screamed White.
 
'He is quite selfish, yes,' Red noted.
 
\"I SAID QUIET!\" Baschrek screamed, flailing his limbs around his head, grasping at the dragon's sinuous necks even as they slid between his clutching fingers like smoke, laughing all the while. Stomping his feet in sheer rage, spiting acid and curses, he lunged at the nearest tree and wrapped his powerful jaws around its thick trunk, chewing and ripping like a frenzied animal.
 
After several minutes of desecrating the local fauna with his claws and teeth, Basch slumped onto his back, panting with exhaustion.
 
They were right, the voices. He was selfish. He was a coward.
 
'Just a bit,' Black said.
 
Just a bit. A little bit cowardly, he conceeded.
 
'But you are strong, so strong mighty Basch,' hissed Green once more.
 
He was strong.
 
'And brave. Much more brave than cowardly,' Green said.
 
That was true as well.
 
'Truly, those destined for greatness must know humility,' muttered Blue.
 
Indeed. Basch nodded his head.
 
\"Power tempered by humility. Modesty is a virtue, after all,\" the dragonborn laughed as he crawled to his feet. After dusting himself off, he passed a final glance at the dark forest behind him. He spat a chunk of bark into the trees, and turned to leave.
 
\"I will be the most modest of all. One man cannot seek to prevail against overwhelming odds. With strength must come wisdom. I will seek out assistance from Uthmere, and save young Alba from the clutches of this god-forsaken forest.\" Basch proclaimed loudly to himself and his companions.
 
They hissed their agreement.
 
It was not a mere retreat, oh no. A calculated withdrawal. Basch was not about to get himself killed over his due payment. He needed rubes and minions to do that for him, and Basch knew of three rubes with hearts that bled more than enough for a pretty damsel in need of rescuing. He set off westward along the road at a steady jog.
 
It was a long way to Uthmere, and there was no telling what those smooth-skins had gotten themselves into while he was away.
 
'BLEEDING HEARTS,' cackled White.
 
Tiamat's tits, these things were going to drive him crazy.
