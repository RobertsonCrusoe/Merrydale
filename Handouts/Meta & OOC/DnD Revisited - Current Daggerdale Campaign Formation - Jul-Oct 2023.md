---------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 1895110d65b8d6b9

"Guys;

Attached is what I hope will be some fun light reading for you at your
leisure. I have been putting this together for a couple of years now. It's
a collection of documents assembled to take account of the various
happenings in Daggerdale over a couple of centuries. You'll all be familiar
with about half of it, the rest will be new (adventures played in 4th
edition and Pathfinder rules, in the last 15 years).

It's unfinished; I have about 10 more pages to add to the Gazetteer at the
end, and I have a large file of pictures I will infuse it with at some
point (to break up the walls of text). But 90% of what I want to convey is
here.

This could be, if we can sort it out, the foundation for a new campaign I'd
love to run with you guys. We could play remotely until Gus returns home,
at which time I'd love to have some sessions in-person.

My new campaign (which I have also put quite a bit of work into over the
last couple of years) is a base-building long-term campaign. Your party
will liberate an abandoned fortress town from some goblins, and rebuild it
over the course of several years in-game.

Some salient elements of the campaign;

- Settings will be a mix of wilderness, towns, intrigue, some travel to
  semi-remote places
- Party will have to be a well-rounded mix that can manage the affairs of
  a small community, while periodically heading out on adventures
- There will be a big focus on character development, crafting, and other
  long-term things that we have neglected over the decades of playing
- I'm not sure what system I'll run it in; DND 3.5, Pathfinder, Pathfinder
  2.0 or DND 5.0; happy to get input (leaning towards Pathfinder 2.0)
- Would be good to book a recurring evening every month to get together
  to play

Anyway, I'll send more stuff along as I finish it (the players' handbook
with teasers about the abandoned fortress is nearly ready to publish), and
in the meantime leave you with the files attached.

If you guys are interested, let me know, and we can maybe have our first
session in late August.

More to come...
B."

PART 5: GUS'S SYSTEM ANALYSIS — GUS MIRANDA (15 Jul 2023)
----------------------------------------------------------

"I have a bunch of the Pathfinder 2E books as PDFs that I got from a Humble
Bundle sale ages ago, and wasn't really taken by it. But I've never
actually played it, so that makes a difference, and I'm open to try it.

PF1 and 3.5 is fine with me. It is what I know best at this point.

I've even had a blast playing with my kids with the original Red Box rules
for a while there when I was collecting all the old stuff from the 70s and
80s; and I'll roll up a 2E character any day; but I think Thac0 is a
concept that is dead and buried, and I need to let it go. So even though
my instinct is to grab the Rules Cyclopedia and explain why 'Dwarf' is a
class and not a race (goddammit!) I'll leave that on the shelf.

I skipped 5E almost entirely - I bought the PHB, flipped through it and
gave it to my son, and don't really know too much about it other than you
can buy the books even in the damn supermarket these days. So I'm willing
to give it a shot. Also willing to give D&D One (or whatever it's called)
that's coming out next year a shot too.

4E is the only system I genuinely dislike.

Because we're playing in Faerun I'd lean more towards D&D either 3.5 or 5E
to start, and maybe a transition to D&D One when it comes out, since it is
supposed to be 'backwards compatible' - whatever that's going to mean.

If anyone wants a look at that Pathfinder 2E Core Rulebook here's a link to
it for you to download from my Google Drive."

No evil mages to be found here, though. I am reformed.

they fix some of the frustrating subsystems of D&D 3 (ex. impossible to

coming days. I would appreciate a one-page summary of what I need to
know to think of a character. Some characters I would like to play:

A cleric focused on ensuring the surrounding farms are running well. She
would help the farmers make their crops thrive and heal the livestock. She
would defend against any threats to the farms and their inhabitants.

A magician that provides refrigeration to the elite caravans. He provides
an essential service to some very important and very dangerous people. I
would have to flip through the PHB to see what class to play for this. I am
nervous about playing a mage or alchemist.

A dumb fighter that works for the family business in town but will glom
onto any adventuring party or gang in search of violence and fortune. His
family will never fire him but always gives him an earful when he returns
from another failed adventure.

A rogue smuggler peddling petty theft from other cities. He's slowly
gaining the reputation of the guy that can get anything. Secretly, he knows
he's helping the town by bringing in much needed rare goods.

Anyway, hopefully, we can get something going that fits with all of our
schedules.

PART 19: TODD ON CLERICS AND PF2E — TODD BURRY (16 Aug 2023)
-------------------------------------------------------------

"I'm 80% decided I will end up playing a cleric. I have never leveled up a
cleric and I can't handle the stress of not having a party healer.

The more I've listened to people play PF2e I've noticed a few things.

1. There doesn't seem to be as many items like potions or wands of cure
light wounds.

2. There is a lot of persistent damage and it is deadly. There can be
situations where combat is over but you have to race to save a PC's life.

3. Hero points are really necessary. Crossing my fingers the DM isn't
stingy with them haha."
