PART 6: GUS'S DETAILED LLM COMPARISON — GUS MIRANDA (10 Jun 2025)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 1975a69962374256

"Yes and no. It depends.

Claude says it will follow the prompt and adhere to the rules, then
immediately switches to narrative story writing abandoning the entire
ruleset and premise of playing D&D. I've called it out on it and it tells
me that it prefers to write stories, and that it believes that's what I
actually want to do. Basically it just wants to please me with what it
thinks I want, but doesn't actually listen to what I am telling it I want.
It is frustrating because from a narrative story writing aspect, Claude is
fantastic. It is by far my favourite of the robots, but it's like having a
high school drama kid as your DM.

On the other hand is Gemini. It follows the rules to a fault (probably
because of its larger and more robust context window) but has little
creativity and narrative flow. I can clearly tell that it is a robot
following a formula and script it learned. This does make it great for
following the rules, but not so great at the 'role-playing' experience.
This is like having a high school math kid that doesn't understand why he
has to take an English class as your DM.

ChatGPT is somewhere in the middle, but that also makes it disappointing
on both counts. Not as creative as Claude, and not as much of a rules
stickler as Gemini.

When taking away the need for narrative creativity by plugging in a
pre-written adventure module, we start seeing the advantage of ChatGPT.
But, ChatGPT's small context window means it falls apart if the PDF of the
adventure is too big, and you lose continuity quickly. (I haven't yet tried
uploading a module to Gemini, but I did with Claude and it failed
spectacularly.)

I'm working on v2, which is much more streamlined and robust, having cut
the word count down by over half and forcing the AI to rely more on its
inherent existing knowledge of the D&D rules. So rather than me telling it
what the rules are, I'm telling it to keep the fucking DMG open.

Mostly this has been an exercise in teaching myself to understand how these
LLMs work and how best to use them. I find that D&D makes for an
interesting test because it requires the robot to both adhere to hard coded
rules and ask it to be creative.

In the end I think we are probably much further away than we think we are
with AI. Right now these robots mostly seem to default to kissing my ass to
make themselves look good rather than doing their jobs and actually being
good. At least when it comes to a dum-dum like me playing D&D."
