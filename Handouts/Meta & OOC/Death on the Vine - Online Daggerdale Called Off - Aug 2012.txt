Thread Subject: "Death on the Vine"
Gmail Thread ID: 1390df63a12893c7
Date Range: 9 Aug 2012 – 12 Aug 2012
Authors: Les Blackwell, Gus Miranda, Steve Hicks, Scott McLaren
CC: Stephen Robertson
Campaign: Meta / Cross-Campaign — Online Daggerdale hiatus
Content Type: Campaign management, group discussion, alternative proposals
Extracted: 2026-04-06 from Gmail

NOTE: This is a pivotal meta-history thread. Les officially calls off the
Online Daggerdale campaign (the original Daggerdale, run on Vanilla Forums
with a MediaWiki wiki, using Hamachi/MapTools/Skype for sessions). The
thread captures the group's frustration with the writing-heavy online
format, Gus's proposal for d20Modern or Pathfinder in Waterdeep as an
alternative, Steve Hicks's declaration that he wants to play but won't
write, and Scott's memorable summary: "A distraction from reality. Not a
commitment to an alternate one." The thread ends with Les offering to
revive Daggerdale with a minimized online component. The group would not
successfully reconvene until Steve Hicks's PF2E Golarion campaign in 2022,
followed by Les's current Daggerdale campaign starting in late 2023.

PART 1: LES CALLS IT OFF — LES BLACKWELL (9 Aug 2012)
------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 1390df63a12893c7

"Guys;

So I guess DND is dead again. We try to put this together every 6 months
or so, but I guess everyone is getting too busy or some such.

At any rate, I'm officially calling off my online campaign.

For the record, any time we get a group of guys in proximity for some
table DND, I'm all up for it. I'd run a DND camp (self-contained,
no-internet-component) next summer, or anytime in fact, if you guys are
interested.

I am actually going to spend my creative energy this fall on settling down
to do some writing. I have never left Avilund, in my head, and will get
back to some serious writing starting in September.

By the way, for those of you that don't know, I am officially done clerking
at the hospital at the end of August. I will be diving into business full
time. My new company has tonnes of business already, and our prospects are
even better.

I am hoping that ditching night shifts and all the other craziness of
shift-work will facilitate writing and a slimmer waistline.

Word.
Blacky."


PART 2: GUS'S COUNTER-PROPOSALS — GUS MIRANDA (10 Aug 2012)
------------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 139108b131e2e354

"A couple of points:

- Good on you for striking out on your own Blacky! Its been a long time
coming, and I'm glad to see you taking that step. Congrats and good luck,
buddy!

- Our time here in the Southern Hemisphere is coming to an end. I can
guarantee with 100% certainty that I will be in Canada next summer. We
will be arriving home on Monday, July 1st, 2013. Let us schedule some D&D
camp for July.

After that is anyone's guess, for us. We may be going to Ottawa. We may
be sent on another overseas assignment. Or the wife may call it quits, and
we'll be making the long-delayed move to Alberta.

But for now, you can chisel it in stone: Canada Day, 2013, Gussy & Family
come back north. Let us have a big ol' D&D Camp next summer.

- It is too bad for Online Daggerdale. But it should not be the end of
Online D&D.

- I am willing to give it one more shot, doing it my way: no forum/story
commitments. I think that is where things get bogged down, in trying to
craft a detailed story and world in between sessions.

So what I'd like to do: d20Modern (or Pathfinder Modern Path; whatever we
can load onto HeroLab, but it is basically still the Pathfinder system
we've been using lately) where there is no real commitment other than
logging on to Hamachi/MapTools/Skype on a Friday night and shoot some guns
at some bad guys. You don't have to worry about posting online, or even
making detailed character motivations. The only motivation is staying alive.

The basic premise would be for you guys to make idealised (almost
comic-book) versions of yourselves at age 25, and then I toss you into
dangerous and fun situations all over Toronto.

If not Modern, then we can do the same with Pathfinder characters and
Waterdeep.

I can run it with as few as 1 of you, or as many as all of you. The only
commitment would be sitting down at your computer on a semi-regular basis
like we were doing with Loudwater.

I'm still willing to try."


PART 3: STEVE — JUST LET ME ROLL DICE — STEVE HICKS (10 Aug 2012)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 139110559646daf3

"I thought I had made it clear but maybe I didn't.

I am fully...fully ready to roll dice be it real or virtual but the
prospect of pouring out tonnes and tonnes of writing has zero appeal for
me. (at the moment)

D&D doesn't have to die because there isn't any writing.

So with that said...if and when it ever comes together, let me know. I am
in. I just don't have the bandwidth, creativity or inclination at the
moment to write out a characters thoughts, speech or actions.

I guaran-damn-tee that there are plenty of dudes playing something weekly
or even biweekly and they're not writing a lick of anything on any forums.

So...I'm not saying I won't get back into writing out things but for the
moment...I'm tapped.

I was ready to play Daggerdale. I thought I had made that pretty clear and
I thought my lack of desire to write was also clear.

I hope it's clear now.

Love;
S."
