From: Stephen Robertson
Date: Friday, February 7, 2025

Thanks (mostly) for an amazing adventure last night, Todd. These modules are so gonzo, it's great. You are doing a great job capturing the fantastic feel of Howard, Leiber, and Moorcock. It's refreshing not having much lore or background to ground the PCs in. Disorienting as well - but that's par for the course for old-school sword and sorcery.

Liltariel. This crap never would have happened in the Fickwood. Humans. So corruptible. So weak. So nasty. All because that idiot girl went and drowned herself.

Another problem with this character, in particular, is that - much like my other elf characters, Axel, Rabbite, and the Waterdeep drowslayer whose name escapes me - I never quite got a handle on playing her.

When I created Liltariel for Gus's foray "In Search of the Unknown," I intended her to play against type as a tank. She was supposed to be a very aggressive and angry elf who wore armour and took out her frustrations on monsters with her hand axe. That idea fell by the wayside in Gonzadrel, and she ended up as a harrier. Then, when we retooled Liltariel as a wood elf, I forgot about the armour restriction. Once the armour restriction was imposed, she became a complete glass cannon, but in my mind she was still at least partly a warrior.

I was hoping she would live out her exile, but oh well. I had fun designing the wood elf spell lists and writing her background. I'm not sure if there's another elf on the horizon, but we'll see.
