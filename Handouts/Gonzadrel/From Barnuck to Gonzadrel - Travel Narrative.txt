From Barnuck to Gonzadrel
Campaign: Gonzadrel / The Fringe
Source: Email thread, April 30, 2025
Author: Todd Burry (DM narrative)

---

You've spent two rowdy nights in the dwarven mining outpost of Barnuck. The folk there are rough-handed and soot-streaked, but they know how to welcome adventurers. Dwarven women are eager for revelry, and the ale flows like spring melt. You feast, sleep like the dead, and even slay a few trolls on the outskirts—for fun and a tidy bit of profit.

The Kragbuckle family takes your dragon ship off your hands, managing to fetch a handsome sum of 2,500 gold. They also arrange secure passage for you and the rescued villagers of Yert, all bound for Gonzadrel.

The return journey is long and lumbering. You ride in great iron-bound wagons hauled by giant aurochs, their breath steaming in the morning chill. The mountain peaks loom always nearby, dangerous but distant. Boulders tumble from high places, thrown by unseen giants. Vast winged shapes pass overhead, silhouetted against stars or stormclouds. Lightning cracks across the peaks. Sometimes, a falling star slams into the cliffs like a hurled spear. But the trade road is well-chosen—tucked low, hidden from the hungering eyes above.

Eventually, you arrive once more at the crooked streets of Gonzadrel. You part ways without ceremony, each returning to your own beds. Your bonds are stronger, if still wary. You feared you might never see home again, yet only two—perhaps three—weeks have passed. You haven't even missed your rent. Though, come to think of it, it's due tomorrow.
