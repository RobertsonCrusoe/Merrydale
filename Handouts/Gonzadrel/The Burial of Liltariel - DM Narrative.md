The Burial of Liltariel and the Tale of Molan and Felan
Campaign: Gonzadrel / The Fringe
Source: Email thread "Possible new addition to our party in The Fringe", February 8, 2025
Author: Todd Burry (DM narrative)

---

Sir Pellias wrapped the elf in his cloak with the care of a father swaddling a newborn. Her skin was cool, yet she smelled of spring earth, damp and rich. Though her breath had ceased, her eyelids fluttered. He lit the last of the incense—its scent, once cloying, now carried the solemn promise of release. Smoke curled toward the cavern's ceiling, weaving around the stalactites like spectral fingers, filling their path with the lingering fragrance of memory and endings.

The dragon ship drifted with the current, carried by the last remnants of the tidal wave that had borne them here. The cavern's ceiling pressed low, forcing them to stow the mast and draw in the oars. The runes, once pulsing with arcane light, had faded into the wood as though they had never been. Whatever power had guided them was gone, leaving them to the mercy of the underworld's silence.

Across the deck, Owen spoke to the others, his voice steady yet solemn, suited for the weight of their journey.

"Let me tell you the tale of the brothers, Molan and Felan, champions of Chaos. Vicious and cunning beyond measure. They led their bestial hordes in a tide of ruin, turning the land to barren moors. From the spoils of their conquest, they built a mighty keep, a bastion of terror that cast its shadow over all who dared defy them.

Yet their triumph was their doom. The kingdoms of men, divided by greed and pride, set aside their feuds to lay siege to the keep. For thirty-nine days, they battered its walls, and on the fortieth, King Grumgin's captain struck Felan down. Molan, knowing his fate was sealed, fled into the ancient caves beneath the keep. There, he cast off his mortal shell, surrendering his soul to the writhing embrace of the forces of Chaos.

But Molan asked for one thing in return—to rise again, when the world had forgotten, when the kingdoms had once more fallen to bickering and folly, when no great armies stood to stop him. And the kingdoms always do fall. When power is at stake, it is the serfs who are left to toil while kings scheme, and only scattered bands of the bold remain to hold the line against the darkness. Or so the old tales say."

Hurl stepped forward, dropping the only prize they had salvaged before the ziggurat's fall. A suit of blackened plate clattered onto the deck, gold and jewels spilling from its crevices—a mere fraction of the lost hoard, but a treasure nonetheless.

The armor, forged from the same star-fallen iron as the axe wrested from the frozen tomb, was dark as the void, swallowing all light. It bore no enchantment, yet its craftsmanship surpassed mortal hands. It would not dent, would not yield. It would guard its wearer better than plate.

Atop the pile rested a triple-headed flail, its spikes untouched by blood, its power yet unleashed. Hurl's master eyed the handle and nodded. "Those are command runes. Unlock them, and the weapon will reveal its true might."

No one spoke. They simply stared at the remnants of their conquest as the ship drifted onward, deeper into the unknown.
