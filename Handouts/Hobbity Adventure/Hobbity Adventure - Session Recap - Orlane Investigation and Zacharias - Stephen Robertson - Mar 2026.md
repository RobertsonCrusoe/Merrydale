THE ELM GROVE

The party visits the Elm Grove before heading to Zacharias's house. They
find a very old stone cottage, half-dug into the ground, clearly inhabited
but currently unoccupied. A weasel darts through. Wedge blows his magical
animal-calling whistle and summons a raven, which he befriends with food.
The party wisely decides NOT to break into this place and moves on.


MEETING ZACHARIAS

Olwyn intercepts them on the way and confirms Zacharias has returned. They
arrive at his house -- front door open, fishing gear piled in the entrance.
Zacharias greets them, takes the letter he himself wrote to Buford,
immediately tears it up and pockets the pieces, then starts loudly talking
about fishing. He's clearly paranoid about being overheard -- even in his
own home. He whispers that this isn't the time or place, then loudly
invites them to stay for dinner and see his fishing spots.

He takes them down to the lakeshore, as far from the house as possible, and
there finally speaks freely.


ZACHARIAS'S INTELLIGENCE BRIEFING

He confirms everything the party has observed and adds specifics. People
are disappearing, coming back changed, and everyone around them either
doesn't notice or actively rewrites the narrative. He identifies four
individuals he believes are compromised or complicit: the storekeeper
Wilbur Oldbuck, the blacksmith Maddie Snowvale and his family, and the
carpenter Quinn Finla (whom the party hasn't met). He's also convinced
the temple priests are the source of it all.

He's a well-traveled former adventurer who knows enough about religion to
be sure this Merikka operation is wrong. The problems started roughly a
year to a year and a half ago, correlating with the arrival of the priests
and the construction of the temple. The two priests came from the coast.
They used to come into the village regularly but have become increasingly
isolated this spring, only occasionally visiting the general store and the
smithy -- notably two of the places Zacharias identifies as compromised.

Zacharias trusts Grover -- he says Grover is the person he'd bring
evidence to, and that Grover has the manpower to act on it. This directly
contradicts the party's suspicions. He agrees to put in a good word with
Grover on the party's behalf.
