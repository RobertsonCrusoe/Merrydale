Previously on The Hobbity Adventure...
DM Recap by Gus Miranda
12 March 2026
Source: Email thread 19ce34be6a883c53

[Note: This is Gus's "Previously on..." recap sent before a session, summarizing the party's investigation in the village of Orlane. The party — Turnip (Stephen), Boffo (Todd), and Wedge (Les) — has arrived in Orlane on Buford's behalf to find Zaccarias, and has been getting into increasingly more trouble with the local constabulary.]

---

When we begin tonight, you will find yourselves right outside Matty Snowvale's Smithy, with Constable Grover and his men (Deputies Donovan and Bulbar) confronting you as you've exited the ruins of the Foaming Mug Inn.

A few refreshers for you:

- It was said that the Foaming Mug Inn was abandoned. You've heard a couple of different versions of the story, but the usual line is that the economic downturn of the village could no longer sustain three Inns, and the owners packed up and moved away. However, your visit to the ruined building revealed that it seemed to have been "Dripping Daggered." Not to mention a lair of Troglodytes in the basement (there were three sleeping pallets, but only two troglodytes present.)

- There have been several disappearances going on, and you've been told a variety of stories as well. Some say that people have left to find a better life elsewhere, others have moved to be closer to relatives, and some have disappeared in the middle of the night only to return "changed" (such as the blacksmith who has become quite violent since his disappearance and return.)

- Despite everyone claiming that the temple's reconstruction and this newfangled "religion" thing will bring back economic prosperity to Orlane any day now, you've also been told that the priests and devotees of this Merikka goddess no longer come into the village for supplies or to socialise. The walls surrounding the temple are an imposing 3m tall, and your attempt to gain entrance in the middle of the day was quickly rebuffed.

- You've been told this goddess Merikka is a bringer of light and illumination. She is a goddess of farming, fertility, home and the hearth, as well as knowledge and light. Ironically, not dissimilar to a combination of Chauntea and Amaunator. The original temple that stood in ruins on that spot was to a long and forgotten god.

- Orlane sits on a very flat agricultural plain, not dissimilar to the area out here in Eastern Ontario between the Ottawa River and the St Lawrence. This area is known as one of the richest and most fertile agricultural areas of this eastern shire. Hobbit-holes are difficult to build, so most buildings are above ground, but the temple stands atop one of the few hills in the area. You were told that early settlers tried to build homes into the hill, but gave up quickly.

A quick recap of your interactions with Constable Grover:

- You first met him as you arrived in the village. He and his men were lazily sitting on the porch of the Constabulary enjoying the pleasant warmth of a late spring evening. He struck you as odd because he and his men were armoured in mail, and Grover was repairing a crossbow on his lap. He greeted you quite warmly, and answered all your questions openly, though he did tell you to stay out of trouble. You were a little indignant about them being armoured, yet you yourselves were wearing leather armour - something extremely uncommon for hobbits to do, especially within the Shires. You told him about the dire wolves, news he took seriously, and which he followed up on by going searching for the dire wolves immediately the next day.

- The following day, while he was out of town looking for the dire wolves, you used magic on the blacksmith, knocking him out and subsequently greatly upsetting his wife who stormed off to file a complaint about you with Constable Grover. Later, as you ate supper on the front porch of the Slumbering Serpent Inn, Grover came to see you, but instead of confronting you about the blacksmith fiasco he tells you that they found no signs of the dire wolves you saw. You were quite dismissive of him, and so he then told you to conclude your business in town and head home as soon as that business is done. The implication being that one unmentioned fight with a respected member of the village is one too many, and perhaps you didn't listen the first time he told you to please stay out of trouble.

- The following morning, having spent the night skulking around in the shadows through peoples' backyards, breaking into Zaccarias' home and lighting fireplaces and candles all over the house to announce your presence, then skulking through the blacksmith's backyard and breaking into the ruins of the Foaming Mug, and now sneaking out from those same ruins, you've run into Constable Grover and his deputies at the foot of the bridge.

Think what you will about Constable Grover, but he might have one or two real concerns about the three of you now, and your inability to stay out of trouble in his quiet little village.
