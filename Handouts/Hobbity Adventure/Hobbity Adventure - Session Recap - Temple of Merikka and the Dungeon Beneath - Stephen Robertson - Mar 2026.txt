MONSTER STATS (as adapted for the campaign):

* Goblins: AC6 with 3hp
* Troglodytes: AC5 7hp
* Hobbit Monks: AC7 — Griff had 7hp as level 2, others had 4hp
* Skeletons: AC6 4hp
* Giant Gecko: AC5 with 12hp

All level 1 or level 2 monsters. The module is for 4-7 characters of
levels 1-3, but also meant for novice players. Because you guys are most
definitely not novices, I've toned things down a notch to compensate for
the smaller party size, but have tried to keep things challenging.

ON THE PARTY'S TACTICAL CHOICES:

You were very good at separating and dealing with the goblins in three
fights, instead of fighting them all at once, and using morale to your
advantage.

However, because you opted to go downstairs to Level 3 before going to
Level 2, your hit points and resources were whittled down by the goblins,
gecko and troglodytes before fighting the skeletons. In the more linear
progression of how this dungeon goes, the fight with the Skeletons is one
of the very first ones that happens. But without going to the Troglodyte
cave first, you wouldn't have had the Brooch of Shielding to absorb the
over 30 points of damage that Turnip took, so it balances out.

The eternal OSR lesson more than anything is that fighting in melee combat
sucks, and figuring out ways to avoid it if possible works best if you
want to survive.

ON THE SKELETON FIGHT (session after this recap):

Misha lost another spell when she got smacked between the eyes with a
sling stone, and she failed morale and ran away. You've basically
neutralised one of the two big clerics in the building, and left her
running around only in her nightgown.

When it came to the skeletons, the party moved 5 feet too far back, which
allowed two skeletons to enter the hallway simultaneously, rather than
making the bottleneck at the doorway itself to fight them only one at a
time. Those extra 5 feet of space cost you dearly.

There was a moment where you had 10,000gp+ worth of treasure and XP in
your packs, goblin and hobbit monk prisoners, and were standing at the
front doors with no threats around you. You could have taken all that loot
and the prisoners down to Grover and Zacharias, levelled up, rested and
healed, and then gone back in with Grover and a posse at your side to
clear out the rest of the temple. Classic D&D "let's explore just one more
dungeon room..." disease.
