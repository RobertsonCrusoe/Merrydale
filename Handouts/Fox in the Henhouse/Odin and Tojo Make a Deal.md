... Deputy Tallhollow approaches Tojo at the Hanged Hobgoblin, on the matter of investigating the Darkwater Brand Trading House in White Ford. 
----

 

Upon entering the ramshackle Hanged Hobgoblin House early one afternoon, Deputy Tallhallow won't have too much trouble spotting the gentlehobbit he came to see.

In a seat near one of the holes in the wall that pass for windows, a rather portly bespectacled halfling is heartily enjoying his lunch. A small gilded nameplate that reads \"Hollowbough And Quarterman, Investigators\" is prominently propped up on the table, though it is partly obscured by the several plates in front of the halfling sitting at it.

The halfling is undeniably of the stout family, and has shoulder-length hair knotted into a bun behind his head. Upon closer inspection (the halfling doesn't seem to notice Odin approach) the halfling is quite young, but has remarkable sideburns that are braided down the sides of his face. Every so often, the halfling will take a pause from his meal and lean back to take a few puffs from an elaborate pipe, tugging at his sideburns as if pondering some inscrutable problem.

== Tojo Introduces Himself ==

\"Good afternoon, Deputy,\" the halfling turns to look at Odin and stands to greet him before Odin can say a word. \"The name's Tomdril Jon, but most people call me Tojo\".

Tojo points at Odin's badge - \"Always a pleasure to see a lawman around. Not too many official types here in White Ford. By your clothes, I can see you're from Highdale - do you know my cousin Willow? No matter - it's a big place to be sure. Won't you sit down? What can I do for you? Hey Shal, can I get some more quiche over here? Thanks m'dear.\"

Tojo is dressed in a billowing cotton shirt under a rather tight leather vest with silver buttons. A small leather case overstuffed with papers is tucked under his chair. A pair of relatively well-cut brown cotton trousers are held up by a massive black belt with a huge steel buckle in the shape of a symbol of Selûne. Small wireframe spectacles give him an owlish look, though they don't appear to be helping him very much, as he squints at almost everything he looks at.

\"Business then? Let me see.\" Tojo reaches under his chair and pulls out a sheaf of well-marked parchment. An inkpot and quill appear in his hand, and he looks attentively at Odin.

\"Darkwater Brand Trading House? That's local - so you'll save there...\" he makes a few notations. \"Well, I'll treat this as a simple research mission, and charge you the standard rate. That'll be 5 sp a day, at a 2 day minimum. My initial quote, I think, will be 10 gp plus expenses. I'll need 5 gp and 5 sp up front, please.

\"Ah, ah, ah... I don't want to know what your suspicions are about the place - we'll compare notes after I've done some initial reconnaissance. If there's one thing I've learned it's that one should always start fresh. It may stretch the timelines here, but I think you'll find it will be worth it...\"
 
\"Now..., let's seal the deal with a meal, as I like to say.\"

== Deputy Odin Responds ==

\"Well met, fine fellow!\" Odin says somewhat cheerfully. He has all the good halfling-courtesy that humans expect, but no more than that. His handshake is brief, and his gaze seems to linger over you a little longer than is comfortable. But at the same time, his interest in the conversation that you have is divided, though subtly, with other events in the ramshackle place. Here is a hobbit that has likely been out-of-doors for longer than is sensible, and it has made him a fidgety fellow who is aloof and tense. 

\"Pleased to make your acquaintance, my good gentlehobbit. I am indeed a Deputy of High Dale, and I am on official business for Sheriff Diggs, investigating the disappearances of several deputies in the mountains to the west of use, here.

\"Now I can see clearly that you and I are talking business, and that you will expect some sort of payment for any help you can offer, and that suits me just fine. I shall make no remarks about any such topics as duty, capitalizing off of other's misfortunes, or being generally useful on your own behalf. No, my inscrutable detective, I shall mention no such things. We shall discuss services and fees, and later we will discuss your findings.\" With this he finally sits down, and helps himself to a half-empty glass of beer on your table.

\"Oh, pardon me, sir,\" he says after wiping some foam from his lips \"I have been out of doors for some time, and have nearly forgotten my manners, if I ever had any to begin with, and there's those as say I didn't. I ain't even warshed as yet, and here I am shaking your refined hand and stealing your beer with no leave. Well, good thing I'm the only lawhobbit in this austere place\" he blurts out, and then gives a laugh. He is quick to order some beer to replace yours, and much more. This is a strangely dressed hobbit who is clearly caught up in the affairs of larger men, but he is still a hobbit, and beer and food are enjoyed by the two of you together, as you discuss this business.

\"Right, then. Here is your first installment,” he says, handing over the coin. He has nearly emptied his purse to do so, and clearly does not have enough left to pay for the next 'installment'.

\"And I shall not trouble you with my predisposition towards these fellows. You know your business best, and my pa always said 'don't go telling a baker how bake', and so I'll leave you to it. Perhaps we could have some dinner together tomorrow to discuss your findings, eh? Good. Then lets see if we can't find the bottom of some of these glasses, and clean these plates off a bit!\"

== A Clarification of Terms ==

Around mouthfuls of meat-pies, Tojo sizes up his client. 

\"In my years, Constable, (though they are less than yours I deduce, no offense to yourself), I have observed that while it is admirable to mind one's duty to a fellow halfling, the weight and toil of arms cannot be borne without due care for the belly. Indeed a fair disservice would I do you were I not to charge you the meager sum I do. 

\"For what good would I be as an investigator were I to starve myself for lack of a few silver? My eyes wouldn't be as keen, my nose would confuse the scent of skullduggery with the scent of Shalhandra here's fresh baked bread, and my ears would hear only the rumbling of a poor, undernourished tummy. My mind would focus only on finding the odd apple, or be lost in a fantasy of how wonderful it would be to find a plump wheel of cheese – and I would thusly miss the significance of a crucial detail or a critical clue. In such a state, I would surely fail in my duty – and disappoint my fellow halflings. Never a help was a hungry hobbit, as my burrow-matron used to say.

\"No, far better for me to keep my hunger sated and my mind sharp to deal with the matter at hand. So you see, by charging you the price I do, my service is twofold – and so you should rightly feel that you are getting twice what you pay for.

\"So, with our business thus begun, I had best set to the case at hand.\"

Tojo solemnly clamps his teeth down on his pipe and salutes the Deputy. Grabbing his portmanteau from beneath the chair, he jumps to his feet and hollers into the kitchen.

\"SHAL! The good constable will be settling the bill! I'll see you on the morrow!\"

And without further ado, Tojo ambles out the door, and is out of sight.

== The Lawhobbit's Purse ==

As Tojo bolts out the door, the deputy simply reaches down to feel his coin pouch, notes a lack of jingling, and frowns. 

\"There goes a fellow who would not know the rumbling of a hungry belly if he were to have the abdomen of a dragon, freshly woken from a sleep of millennia, pressed against his ear. I wonder if the credit of the good civil servants of High Dale is any good here?\" Odin asks himself, then gives himself a smart slap on the top of the head; \" What am I thinking?! I am in Archendale!\"
