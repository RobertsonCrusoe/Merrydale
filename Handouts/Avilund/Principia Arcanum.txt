The Principia Arcanum, by Hesiod Isshal Walden, an undergraduate at Lord's University, is a logical, philosophical, mathematical, and scientific analysis of the principles of the Arcane, Divine and Ancient practices of magic in relation to the observable natural world. 

This work was Walden’s undergraduate thesis, presented to the fellows of the College of Mathematicians at Lord's University when he was just 22 years old. Its contents and theories are far removed from accepted dogma regarding the nature of how the world was formed, how it functions, how magic interacts with the world, and what the origin and explanations for the existence of magic are. It is a work of such complexity and far-reaching implications that it is said that fewer than a dozen people alive today are capable of understanding even part of what Walden has theorised. 

For his efforts, Walden was expelled from the university without obtaining his degree, tried and sentenced as a heretic by the Ecumenical Courts, and exiled from the City to the borderlands. The Principia has subsequently been published by an unknown group and widely circulated among the academics of the university. Since few truly understand the ideas and concepts which Walden wrote about, there have emerged many misconceptions and misunderstandings regarding his theories. 

The Principia has taken its place as the first work in what has been termed “The Humanist Movement” (or “Humanist Subversion”.) The Principia is a key work dealing with the scientific observations of the mechanics of both magic and the world as a whole.
