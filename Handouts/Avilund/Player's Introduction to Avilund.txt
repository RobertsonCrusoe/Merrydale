Dear Traveler,

We are delighted to be providing you with a new setting in which you can explore, adventure, and experience the joy of discovery.

Discovery. That is the crux of this setting. Avilund is a place that none of you have been to before; it is a new setting in which you will find some things common to the traditional fantasy role-playing genre, but with our own interpretations and usages. You will also find a great deal that is new, and it is this discovery of the new that we hope you will most appreciate. There are new maps, religions, magic systems, monsters, equipment, monetary systems and rules. All of this has been designed to make you feel like a visitor to a new place, and to make you wish to discover more, and figure things out for yourselves.

I recall that my favourite moments in my role-playing career all revolved around the discovery of the new. Setting foot in [http://greenronin.com/freeport/ Freeport] was the first time that I realized this; after years of exploring the [http://www.wizards.com/default.asp?x=dnd/fr/welcome Realms], our group had become too intimate with the setting. Freeport was a welcome change because we didn’t know what to expect. We didn’t even know the price of longsword or where to buy a 10’ pole. Going someplace new, and experiencing the unknown is what role-playing is all about.

The Avilund Player’s Manual is largely composed of a collection of documents that are from the setting; there are journal entries from travelers in the setting, newspaper clippings, textbook selections, religious texts, and many other odds and ends. The collection does not provide a third-person, or omniscient view of Avilund; the selections have each been written by inhabitants of Avilund who are often biased and sometimes overtly partisan. A number of the key selections are correspondence and journals from travelers from distant lands who find themselves in the starting region of the campaign setting; they give an account from the view of an outsider, with all the wonder, bias and disgust that any traveler might record in a strange land. This is appropriate, because you, too, are an outsider just now.

While we want your first glimpse of Avilund to be through the eyes and narratives of the locals, we understand that some traditional-style descriptions must be provided (given in the Synopsis). We won’t provide the player with dozens of pages of dry and factual detail, vis-à-vis countless other gaming products, but will provide a brief framework. We hope that your understanding and experience in out setting is as biased and coloured as that of the locals, and indeed, as your opinions and views of the world you really live in; there is no factual manual for Earth, nor is there one for Avilund. Of the truth, Galileo wrote “All truths are easy to understand once they are discovered; the point is to discover them”. Of the truth, Mark Twain also wrote “Truth is mighty and will prevail. There is nothing the matter with this, except that it ain't so”.

Enjoy Avilund.
