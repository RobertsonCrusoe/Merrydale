PART 1: STEPHEN — CAMPAIGN WITHDRAWAL — STEPHEN ROBERTSON (10 Jul 2008)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 11b0be419928722d
From: stephen.robertson@anthro.ox.ac.uk
To: Les Blackwell (les.blackwell@gmail.com)

"Hey Les,

I'm beginning to crack... going over old notes and e-mails about the
EOI campaign... bringing a tear to my eye at the fact that those days
are over.

I need to get out of the house, or I'll be spending the entire night
rewriting the summary of that adventure.

In otherworldly news, I will write to you more about Barbarossa. I've
been thinking about how to best make him as a low-level character, and
think I've got him down, but I don't think you're going to like what
I've done. However, before I do so, I'd like your thoughts on the
character concept, keeping in mind something you've said before:"

[Quoting Les, 24 Jan 2001:]
"Eastwitch is the antithesis of espionage. I made this setting as a
diversion from the Waterdeepish adventures to which we have become
accustomed. I have said many times that I didn't want a pile of 'out
of town' characters that ride in with all thier bells and whistles and
screw up the tone, mood and ecology of the setting."

"(I'm not kidding when I said I was poring over old e-mails - I ported
'em all into gmail, and there are some pretty interesting discussions
on many aspects of DND philosophy in there - all easily found thanks to
Gmail being awesome - imagine, type in 'Grumbar' and I find that
there's more time spent discussing Casey's one-off priest than ever he
spent adventuring with him!)

Anyway, that aside, it seems like I'm trying my best to make Barbarossa
an 'out of town' character, but this is only half of the story. The
crusades were all about the home team versus the out of towners, and
this in itself makes a foreigner character fit into a Crusading world.
Think of Azeem in Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves."

[Quoting Les, 22 Aug 2003:]
"I WILL SAY THIS; IT IS MORE FUN TO PLAY A SINGLE CHARACTER, AND IT IS
BETTER FOR THAT CHARACTER'S DEVELOPMENT TO GET ALL OF IT'S PLAYER'S
ATTENTION (LIKE AN ONLY CHILD...)."

"... but for the purposes of fleshing out the world, it's fun to make
characters for Waldheim, even if I won't play them.

Has anything been happening lately?

Stephen"

[Signature: D.Phil candidate, University of Oxford; Visiting Research
Student, University of Tokyo; St. Cross College]
