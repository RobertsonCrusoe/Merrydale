Ida is not really a Saint, though worshipped as such. Ida was the messenger angel dispatched from Heaven by Avitus to relate his tale to his son Severinius. Condemned to die on Earth as a result of her mission, Ida fell in love with Severinius (or so it is said. Some scholars maintain that her marriage to Severinius was part of her mission) and eventually married him, and bore him several children.

The worship of Ida has mostly fallen out of fashion, and is all but unknown outside the older areas of the Empire. Her soul was destroyed as a result of her exile and death on Earth, so she does not exist to be able to grant spells or powers. Still a cult or two in her name persists.

Ida's name is most commonly uttered in association with those who ply the world's oldest profession. The belief that Ida might have only borne Severinius children out of an obligation to Avitus is one that has taken root in the popular culture to an extent. In this fashion, Ida is often cited as patron of prostitutes.
