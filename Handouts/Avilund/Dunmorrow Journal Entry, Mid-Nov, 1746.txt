{{DunmorrowJournal|Mid-Nov, 1746|Oct 26-27?, 1746|Undated, 1746}}

I am having a lucid moment just now, and feel like writing. It is madness to be kept here in the darkness, eating one soft apple a day, and drinking some wine which once tasted so sweet. I would not be surprised if they have switched the brand, finding that it is the only thing that I consume regularly, and I would not be surprised if I am just plain sick of drinking it day after day, but it is now a noxious swill to me. And it is sweet, and leaves a pang in my broken teeth.
 
I took a look in a mirror the other night (for I shall ceased use of the word ‘day’ henceforth), and was terrified. I have lost a considerable amount of what little weight I began this charge with, and though I let out a chuckle at the thought, it is no funny matter that I now have Numble’s build. My wrist is a terrible swollen black and blue and blotchy white mess, and my eyes are sunken quite deep into my head. My garments are a reflection of my withered flesh. They are torn and sullied in a number of ways, and I am sure they smell terrible, though the smell of all but dust is forgotten to me.
 
While I think of it, I shall say that I hope that Janvermin has more wisdom than he does patience. I will not be delivering my message to the Scion, and I will not be leaving Valdenwail. I hope Vermin has left. 

I hope he is alive.
