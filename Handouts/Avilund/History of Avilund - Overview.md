History Primer for Students of the Humanities, Lord’s University.

Penned by Dr. Eustace Worthope in the Imperial Year of 1738.

== Preface ==

This volume is intended to make the Student of Humanities in the great University of Waldheim prepared for rudimentary discussions of History. The Knowledge contained herein is assumed knowledge for any Student of the Humanities, and will not be further illuminated in any lectures by the Professors at Lord’s. This volume is chiefly concerned with the History of the Early Middle Period (c. 1700 BC to 1400 BC) to the present day. There will be some discussion of Early History, which is often referred to as Elder History.

The Knowledge found herein is a compilation from a few significant sources that survived the Repurgo Imperialis. Chief amongst these is the Magnus Corpus Avitus which was provided by the Seminary of St. Ignatius, most generously and graciously. Other significant primary sources include the Treatise on the Historical Relevance of the “Tale of the Woodland King”, the Chronicle of Vermilund, and the remnants of the Corpus Officina Hominum per Gestalt Avarinius. Numerous other texts and sources are also cited, and are noted appropriately.

== On Calendars ==

For the most part, this volume will reference events using the Imperial Calendar (IC). The ‘zero’ year of this Calendar is marked by the conquest of the City of Waldheim by Saint Abaddon. The city was called Gulfziam by the Ferals that populated it at that time. It is important to note that the IC was started and restarted many times, and the City changed hands between the Ferals and Men for hundreds of years before Abaddon. The Imperial Calendar year zero also marks the beginning of the period of history known as the Saeculum Dominae Humanitas a Avilund, which persists to this day, according to some scholars (some rogues disagree, but that is an issue for modern Civics seminars). Time before year zero is noted as ‘Before Conquest’, or ‘BC’, and counts backwards from year zero.

Before the IC, the Men of Avilund marked a new chronicle of years which began with the landing of Sebald, Dunstan and Barnabas in the lands that are now known as the Imperial Ecclesiarchy of Valdenwail. What they called their own calendar is a fact lost to us in the Repurgo, but it shall herein, and in most lectures, be referred to as the SHA Calendar, after the period of history known as the Saeculum Humanitas a Avilund, which persists to this day, as per discussion above. It is likely that the conquest of Gulfziam by Abaddon was in, or close to, the year 1400 SHA. This puts us very close to the 3150th year of Men in Avilund.

What calendar, if any, was used by the Men that came before Sebald and his crew is a matter for speculation in the post-Repurgo world of academics.

The Elder Roll of Years (ERY) will be referenced from time to time. It is not known to modern scholars if the Elders counted years the same way we do, but it is assumed that they called the same revolution of the four seasons one year, as we do. The Seminary of St. Ignatius, after decades of research, insists that the Collegium Waldheim were made aware of the ERY in their historic visitation to Waldheim in the year 201 SHA. At this time, they record that the ERY was in its 39,999th year. This puts us at around the 42,900th year of the Elder Roll of Years. This number is, admittedly, overwhelming.

= Periods of History =

There are several conventions for the naming of periods of History, and there has been very little standardized procedure for this practice, even recently, in post-Repugo academics. Different groups with their own interests have identified dozens of different ways to break up our Historical Record. As one can well imagine, canonical texts break most of our History down by the activities of the Saints. Secular organizations choose more far-reaching and important landmarks in History to identify ‘periods’ or ‘eras’. Of course, many different periods overlap as a result of this. It is convention, however, in educated circles, to refer to the following periods of History as being the most definitive;

== Early History ==
Early History

This is the period of time before Men came to Avilund. It includes the activities of the Elders in Avilund, and the activities of Men on the Islands, though not much is now known about either of these groups and their doings.

== Middle History ==

This is the period of time in which the lives of the first Saints were lived, including the time of Men coming to Avilund, and up to the conquest of Gulfziam by Abaddon. Some antiquarians draw the curtain on ‘Middle History’ with the final destruction of the Elders, which was around 1199 BC (201 SHA). At Lord’s University, it is understood that an Educated Man is referring to the years circa 1700 BC to 0 IC as ‘Middle History’.

=== Early Middle History (1700 BC to 1400 BC) ===
Early Middle History

This is the most nebulous of historical periods, after Elder History. This period is chiefly concerned with the lives of Avitus, Gaul, and the Prime Saints. This period of history is now shrouded in complete obscurity after the Repurgo. No actual dates have survived to modern times about the events of this age, and of the events described in the Tale of the Woodland King. This age ends in 1400 BC, when Men arrive in Avilund.

=== Mezzo Middle History (1400 BC to 450 BC) ===
Mezzo Middle History

This age is sometimes called the First Age of Imperial Expansion. It begins with the arrival of Sebald, Dunstan and Barnabas in the legendary vessel known as Bram’Cairn. This age, the Saeculum Humanitas a Avilund, marks a tremendous expansion of Men on this continent. In this thousand or so years, Men came to Avilund, and managed to establish the Imperial Ecclesiarchy, and the Imperial boundaries that are, for all intents and purposes, the same as they are today. Some notable dates in this period of history include the following;

* 1250 BC	Birth of the Primus Deus Fidelis and the laying of the first stones at the Valdenwail.

* 1199 BC	Founding of the Colleguim Waldheim by Ignatius, Anselm and Gratus.

* 450 BC	Invasion of Brogard the Cruel. The ancient city of Tiberwail (the location of the modern day city of Lysander) is destroyed, and the Horde marches up to the walls of the Valdenwail. The Epic of Lazarus of Duneedin tells this tale pretty well, and the head of Brogard can still be viewed in the Cathedral of St.Lazarus in Duneedin today.

Although the huge Feral Horde lead by Brogard was defeated, the Empire had been crippled and nearly destroyed. The unchecked advance of Men in Avilund had come to a halt, and a period of slow rebuilding followed.

=== Late Middle History (450 BC to 0) ===
Late Middle History

This short bit of history is characterized by the worse times that Men in Avilund ever had to endure. The slow reclaiming of Imperial territories was difficult. Not only had the Ferals taken root in many lands, but the new inhabitants of the Eastern shores of Avilund, the Kells, had arrived. These warlike, robust and violent men of the Eastern Archipelago were very cunning and determined foes, and remain on this continent today, though in the far North. This is a brief period of history, but one which produced many heroes and saints. Chief among the heroes and saints of this time is Abaddon, the Liberator of Waldheim. Though he invaded the Feral-held City no less than 13 times, in the year 0, he took it, and it has not left the control of Men since. (See Late Middle History).

== Late History ==

With the conquest of Waldheim by Abaddon, historians mark the beginning of the Late Period. Late History is a time that is most associated with the slow expansions and contractions of the Empire, as it tries to expand into the Holy Theatres, and struggles with a new-found enemy. It is in Late History that the scholar will first read of the ‘Black Faith’, and witness the erosion of the Society of Man from the inside, which made his expansion and dominion difficult. While this period can be divided into dozens, even hundreds of significant periods of time, however, as in Middle History, there are three commonly acknowledged subdivisions.

=== Early Late History (0 to 750 IC) ===
Early Late History

This age is also known as the Second Age of Imperial Expansion. This age is typified by very strong Scions and Prelates with warlike personalities and the wills to match. Great Pilgrimages and Crusades happen during this time. Previous to the Repurgo, this was the most widely studied period of History. It is during this time that Men begin to unearth the libraries and relics of the Elders in Waldheim and other Elder Cities. New Magic was studied and employed by Men, and it is from this time that most Artifacts originate. This was a violent time, but a time of great advances for the culture of Men and acts of great heroism. Colonies were established in the Eastern Archipelago and in the lands beyond the Barrier Wood and the Sea of Sorrow.

This golden age of Men ended in 750 when the Black Faith first reared its hideous face. We know that the Scion Nicetus Avitus Vanderwail (700-804) revealed the Prospectus Imperialus ex Daemonica in November of 750. This document was a heavy handed policy that gave birth to the First Inquisition.

=== Middle Late History (750 IC to 1348 IC) ===
Middle Late History

With the birth of the Inquisition, the Empire of Men ceased its expansion immediately, and quickly lost ground to the Ferals, Kells, and other inhabitants of the lands in the new colonies. This age is typified by the tyranny of the Scions (with some exceptions), and the crumbling of the Society and Culture of Men. The hallmarks of this terrible time are illuminated most clearly during the Repurgo Imperialis. This 138-year tragedy begins in 1210 in the little-known hamlet of Sandalstone, where the Casparian Monk, David, emerged with his torch and hatred for all things academic. Though the Repurgo was started by a crazed vigilante, it soon had a powerful ally in the Second Inquisition, which was called for in 1295 by Baudri the Bloody, Baudri Avitus Vanderwail (1210-1350). This time is characterized by the horrible misdeeds of Men against Men, and though it was a time of conflagration for records, books, documents and all other forms of history, enough documents and tales about this time survived its own fires.

Middle Late History ends in 1348 with the Novae res Sanquinius. There is an entire sub-specialty of History that deals with the Revolution, from its causes to its effects upon the modern world, and this volume will not go into much of that detail. The Student of the Humanities is encouraged to read Dr. Carrington’s Cruentus Novae res Sanquinius Satus Compleo for a greater understanding of this monumental turning point in history. It must be understood at this point, though, that the Empire as it was came to an end during this Revolution, and the power of the Saintly Faith dwindled dramatically in many parts of what was the Empire. The Empire, as it had been, was changed forever, and some rogues actually insist that it came to an end outside the Hedge of the Valdenwail, when Baudri signed the Placitum a Terminus Potentia, on October 30, 1348. This document relinquished the control of the City of Avitus (modern day Waldheim) from the hands of the Vanderwails, and brought an end to the rule of the Saintly Faith in all but a few territories. If it had not been for the bloody polices of Baudri’s Inquisition, this might never have happened.

=== Modern History (1348 IC to Present Day) ===
Modern History

After the Placitum was signed, the Empire changed dramatically, and the whole face of Avilund was re-sculpted. This era is marked at its beginning by the end of the Revolution in 1348, but just as characteristic of this age is the renewed interest in the Holy Archipelago (the Eastern Archipelago) and the lost colonies in the West. The 3rd Crusade was launched in 1350 by Baudri, and in 1488, the Fourth Crusade was launched by Eustace Avitus Vanderwail, who is known as the Crusading Scion. Many old laws were repealed in many parts of Avilund (at this point, most scholars except the most proud use the term ‘Avilund’ rather than ‘Empire’ to refer to the lands of Men on the continent), and a new age of invention and discovery soon took hold, and was promptly labelled the Saeculum Ingenium Aurum by scholars here at Lord’s. In many places, the City of Waldheim especially, Institutions of Learning again sprung up, though this was a slow process; the long years of the Repurgo and its infection in the psyche of Avilund had made a lasting and durable obstacle to progress. This age is typified by such things as the great inventions of Gun Powder and the Printing Press, the Bank and the Corporation. Peace and prosperity had come, at long last, to most parts of Avilund, except some border nations, and, of course, the Crusading Theatres.

It is important to note that despite the modernization of Avilund, contact with the colonies in the Crusading Theatres was not fully established, and in 1510, with the death of Eustace in the Western Theatre, the last contact with the ancient colonies of the Empire was lost permanently. At the time of publication of this volume, it has been 228 years since Men entered either Theatre and returned.
