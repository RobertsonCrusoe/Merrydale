Kingston was a city on the west bank of the River Wen, across from Avitus. No longer called \"Kingston\", since the Revolution, the western districts have been formally annexed into the city. The four western districts are a tumultuous, virtually lawless place of stockyards, slaughterhouses, animal pens, factories, coach houses and seedy inns and brothels. 
* Hog's Market
Only the district of Hog's Market (site of both the city's live animal market, and the city's meat markets) holds any real degree of safety and security. This safety is brutally enforced by the district's Butchers' Guild, who fear that crime and lawlessness may drive the city's rulers to establish a new livestock market elsewhere. 
* King's
The central district, referred to as King's, is no more than an impoverished commercial centre full of slums and decaying buildings. 
*The Bride
The Bride, which is the northern suburb, is a mostly residential area not unlike the Northlund Road district on the opposite bank of the river. 
*Black's Road
Black's Road, which marks the westernmost part of the city, is the main point of entry for all who come from the western country. Named after a famous highwayman who terrorised the western road, the district of Black's Road is composed mostly of taverns, public houses, inns, coach-houses, caravan yards and animal pens servicing all who come in and out of the city.
