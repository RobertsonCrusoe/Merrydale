== St. Cardigan’s-Under-Cross Parish, Chapelton Ward ==

Chapelton is a large industrial parish that spans the Tine as it enters the City and pours into the Harbour. The Ward is known best for its black smoke, which it billows into the sky six days a week. There are many manufactories in this Ward that produce hundreds of different consumer goods. The people of this Ward are typically hard working and poor. There isn’t much unemployment in the Ward, but many people live on the brink of homelessness, as the manufactories don’t pay overly well. Charity in the Ward comes mostly from the many Royal Companies that provide workhouses and poor relief. The churches also provide for some poor relief. 

St. Cardigan’s-Under-Cross is known as the Crystal Parish by those that live in and around Chapelton. It is the best-off of the industrial parishes of Chapelton, Waverleigh and Pondluck, mostly owing to the presence of the Royal Company of Glaziers and Glassblowers,who have enjoyed tremendous prosperity for over 100 years. The parish is known for its stained glass, which is found in most of the homes and business of the parish, as well as all of the civic buildings, churches, and many street decorations. There are many lamps in the parish that have the colourful stained glass panes instead of the murky glass found in lamps in the rest of the City, and there are some glass arches over some streets that cast coloured rays in conspicuous parts of the parish.

The 1744 Census puts the population of the parish at 345 households accounting for 2005 citizens, and 3 workhouses accounting for another 188 souls. There is also a large jail known as Chapelton Tower, which serves as the Ward Jail, and accommodates as many as 150 criminals in the worst of times.

== Ward Offices ==

The Crystal Parish is one of three parishes in Chapelton; the other two are St. Verenus-of-Tine, and Middlegate. Many of the Ward’s governmental buildings are found in St. Cardigan’s, including the following;

* Chapelton Tower (Ward Jail)
* Tinehall (large, ancient building with the Exchequer, The Registrar, the Ward Court and the Ward Council Chambre)
* Wergild Hall (an old noble villa that is now used to house the Ward Garrison)

== Municipal Infrastructure ==

The main municipal buildings of the Crystal Parish include the following;

* Gamgee Hall (nice old building which contains the Parish Bench and the Parish Council Chambre, and the Parish Record Hall)
* Cardigan’s Keep (an ancient building that has the Justice Bench, the Office of the Constable, the Office of the Revenuer, and the Parish Coffers)
* Wergild Collegiate (a public school for boys aged 5-10)
* Chapel Street Dispensary (a small hospital with one doctor and a few beds)

== Ecclesiastical Presence ==

* Cathedral of St. Caanan (this is the centrepiece building for the Ward, and the ‘Chapel’ for which it was named; more on it below)
* St. Dunstan on the Tine (a large church on the river which has a large and faithful flock; this is the parish’s second largest faith, behind…)
* St. Cardigan on the Tine (a large church right beside Dunstan’s, on the river; this is the most popular cult in the Parish)
* St. Verenus Chapel (a smaller chapel for a regional saint that dates back to the 500’s, before the City engulfed Chapelton)
* St. Mairin’s Children (a large orphanage with a small chapel; it is a large, ancient building that sits under the shadow of Caanan’s)
* Canal Street Sorority (a villa with a church to St. Mairin, and buildings owned by the Society of St. Mairin, which is a woman’s club for most working and middling women)

== Noteworthy Industrial and Commercial Establishments ==

* Royal Company of Glaziers and Glass Blowers (this Company has a satellite guildhall on the canal; it is the most powerful Company in the parish, with over 200 men in its ranks)
* Royal Company of Clothworkers (this Company has a few men working in it, who all have dozens of female ‘employees’; these women aren’t apprentices, but rather the female version thereof; there are over 100 women involved in this trade in the parish)
* Royal Company of Dyers (the Dyers is run similar to the Clothworkers, and indeed, the two are intertwined; there are over 200 women in the Parish who produce coloured cloth for a man in the Company, and it is this activity which is most prevalent
