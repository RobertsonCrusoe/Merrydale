This neighbourhood is considered to be the latest casualty of the city's expansion. Prior to the Revolution, Saint Gideon was a prosperous town in its own right. These days, however, it has been swallowed up by the greater city of Avitus, and now forms one of its northern suburbs. Unlike many of the other neighbourhoods of the city, Saint Gideon retains much of its independent charm and character. It has its own town square, and its own hall, and retains its own town council. Many of Saint Gideon's wealthy elite have moved on to the nearby suburbs of Methwold and Newton, but many remain. Saint Gideon, in many ways, is as industrial an area as neighbouring Waverleigh, but it is decidedly cleaner and more prosperous in character.
