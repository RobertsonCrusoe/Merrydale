PART 1: GUS — FOUR OPTIONS — GUS MIRANDA (28 Sep 2011)
-------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 132b12b9c303260a

"Well, my attempt at getting some D&D online going has so far been a
failure. Here we are almost to October, and we never got to it. For shame.

Nevermind. Ever the optimist, I have high hopes for October. After that,
I'll start taking it personally. We have four options before us now:

1. My old-school D&D adventure which is still primed and ready
2. An online (email and message board and g+, but no maptool) Pathfinder or
   D&D 3.5 or D&D 4 set in a campaign setting I've been writing*
3. Blacky's Daggerdale in which my Drow and Robertson's Cormyrean conspire
   to take over the place**
4. Nothing.

I am currently looking at a nice, long easy stretch of vacation before me.

*The system matters not because there would be very little combat at all.
This would be almost entirely a sandbox and world-building campaign. The
way I've been making it and plotting it, it is about politics and diplomacy
and conspiracy and economics. The PCs are all individual actors who have
come to a Free City to exploit its opportunities for their own goals and
agendas."

PART 4: GUS'S WORLD SYNOPSIS — GUS MIRANDA (29 Sep 2011)
---------------------------------------------------------

"A scattershot synopsis. Better details later. Better writing too.

This is an old world.

It is the home world of two sentient races, who have been locked in mortal
combat for millennia upon millennia. On the one side are dragons, and their
draconic offspring. On the other, the elves and their legions of slaves.

About 10,000 years ago the Elves began dealing with dark forces they
shouldn't have been dealing with. They reached out to these eldritch
entities in a bid to gain the final upper hand against their draconic
enemies. But of course, they lost control. Most of the elven empire was
laid waste and the northern continent abandoned to the horrors that were
unleashed. The empire survived unscathed in their southern colonies, across
the Blood Sea.

Geographically, I want you to picture an area similar to the Mediterranean.
The Old Eladrin Empire used to span the entire area of what would be
Europe, as well as the northern coast of Africa. The dragon lands, for
their part, would lay in what is Russia.

[The Free City of Caledon] sits in an island chain in the Blood Sea, and
is a haven for escaped slaves, refugees, pirates, mercenaries, and
fortune-seekers of all stripes."

PART 8: GUS ON RELIGION IN CALEDON — GUS MIRANDA (2 Oct 2011)
--------------------------------------------------------------

"The major religion is elven. And this is a very sore point with many of
Caledon's inhabitants. The ideals behind the 'Free City' mean that anybody
is welcome. Well, there are many escaped/freed slaves who've arrived who
have brought to Caledon the one and only religion they have known.

While Caledon is a 'Free City' there is strong prejudice against anything
Elvish or Imperial. There are no known Eladrin on the island, and those
lesser Elves who have been found are not shown very much courtesy. Indeed
the history of Caledon is marked by more than one lynching of an Elf.

Yet, the elven religion has found a foothold. Mostly it is with recently
arrived human slaves who have nothing to their name, and are happy to cling
to the one familiar hand that guides them through their first arrival."
