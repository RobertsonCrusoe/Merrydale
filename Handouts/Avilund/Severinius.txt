SeveriniusExcerpted from An Account of The Lives of the Imperial Saints, by Dr. Archibald Beaumont. First Edition Published, 1698.

~

Saint Severinius, known also as Saint Sever (his real name), was the first Scion of Saint Avitus, and is held by most to have been Avitus' natural son.  Having escaped murder at the hands of his uncle, Sever was spirited away from his village by an old seeress named Mairin.  Saint Mairin raised Sever in the Great Wood until he was able to return to his village to assume the chieftainship of the tribe after his father's ascension (the story of Saint Sever's youth is told in the epic poem \"The Woodland King\"). Saint Sever is attributed with the foundation of the Primus Deus Fidelis, as well as with the creation of the first Canon (\"The Canon of Saint Avitus\"). His coupling with the Angel Ida resulted in the long line of the Vanderwail family, which has continued on to the present day, culminating with the present Scion, Sawl Avitus Vanderwail.

Severinius, today, continues to have one of the largest followings in the Empire. He is venerated by most common men, holding a special place among the administrators, magistrates and bureaucrats, and others of middling status. The Canon of Saint Severinius continues to be supported by a large, and well organised number of missionaries, both clerical and lay, as well as a handful of monasteries and one knightly order.

The Prelate of Saint Severinius was the first to hand over his saint's canon and relics to the Scion, and remains, to this day, the staunchest ally of the Scion within the Conclave.
