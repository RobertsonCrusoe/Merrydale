Officially known as the City's 'Civic Centre', this district, located directly across from the islands on the eastern shore, used to be called 'The Fleet', after its most storied resident, the Imperial Navy. Home to the original docks and shipyards of the Navy, this was a raucous, violent and impoverished neighbourhood for most of the city's history. With the coming of the Revolution, the new government chose The Fleet as the site of its new administrative and government centre, deciding to move it away from the church's influence in the Old City. It was hoped, as well, that by demolishing many of the slums and dark alleys of The Fleet, the neighbourhood might be invested with some semblance of respectability. In an attempt to buff up the image of the district, the city's new rulers renamed it 'Civic Centre' and eventually 'Civic' for short. However, there is not a man alive or dead in the city that calls this district by either of those names. This district has always been known as The Fleet, and continues to be referred to only by that name regardless of how the new maps have been labelled. It is of interest to note that, to date, virtually none of the improvements originally planned for this district have come to pass. Construction of the Parliament House and other government buildings has been slow and irregular throughout the centuries. Aside from the relative safety of the main thoroughfares, The Fleet remains one of the most vile, debauched, dangerous and dirty districts in the whole city.
