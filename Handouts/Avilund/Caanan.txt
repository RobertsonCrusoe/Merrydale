CaananExcerpted from An Account of The Lives of the Imperial Saints, by Dr. Archibald Beaumont. First Edition Published, 1698.

~

Little is known of Saint Caanan. Indeed, he is often referred to by scholars as \"The Forgotten Saint\". What is known of Cannan is this: he was a contemporary of Severinius and a member of his tribe; and his name is still freely invoked by the common man whenever and wherever misfortune strikes.

No known followers of Caanan are known to exist, but, given the mysterious nature of this saint, it is doubtful that any true followers of Caanan would identify themselves as such.

Because of his loose association with Severinius, and the fact that mention is made within Severinius's Canon of the existence of a \"Canon of Saint Caanan\", he is often labelled as a saint by scholars. However, given his deep associations with misfortune and catastrophe, it is little wonder that most would consider him a Hellion.
