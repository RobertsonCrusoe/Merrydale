Located south of the Severn Harbour on the appropriate spit of land, these neighbourhoods are old, crumbling, and hold a mixture of impoverished citizens and prosperous tradesmen. Landside is a cousin to The Harbour, holding many warehouses, along with the tenements and inns that hold the sailors and fishermen that work out of the Harbour. The Spit, however, is undergoing a bit of a renaissance. Linked to The Fleet and the islands by boatmen, barges and ferries, The Spit has become a fashionable district for the young and ambitious merchants of the city. Buying up property at a very cheap price in this district, many of these young men have turned this narrow strip of line into the financial centre of the city. Indeed, it is here that the Bank of Avitus is now located, and where the Exchange's building is under construction. With these young men and their businesses came the requisite service trades. New tea houses, taverns, restaurants and clubs have been opened up in remodelled buildings that had been left abandoned or condemned. Nowhere else in the city is there so much construction and optimism as in The Spit.
