The County of Norweald is due north of the City, a full day’s ride along the Tine River Road (a little used trail these days; most traffic goes up and down the Northlund Road). The County is ruled by Count Grumbledim Norweald, an ancient family of the ‘Old Blood’. Count Norweald is suspected in the uniformed noble circles of the County to be one of the Five Dukes. His castle in St. Sebald-upon-Tine is one of the largest and best appointed in Waldheim.

There are 43 parishes in the County, controlled by 13 barons, Earl Norweald, and Count Norweald. About 1/3 of the parishes are impoverished from the gradual diminishing of the population, and migration to the City and to the town of St. Sebald-upon-Tine (a large town of 3000 people). Many farms are completely vacant, and a few entire parishes are void of human inhabitants.

The predominant faith of the County is St. Sebald, which has been rigidly enforced by the Norweald nobles for centuries. About half of the parishes have churches to St. Sebald, with the rest having one or more churches to various other saints, including the agricultural saints Judith and Mairin, and a couple of regional saints as well.
