right|300pxAdjacent to Saints' Island, Sebald's Isle and Elder Isle have long been the residential neighbourhoods of the city. As the city grew out in size unto the mainland, these two islands traditionally had been the location of the palaces and villas of various prelates, nobles and wealthy merchants. However, with the continued expansion and growth of the city out of the islands and onto the mainland, many of these have moved their residences to larger estates in the newer suburbs to the north, particularly to Newton Hill where the floodwaters of the River Wen do not reach. Today, Sebald's and Elder remain as largely residential neighbourhoods, although many of the palaces have since been gutted and subdivided into smaller houses and tenements now populated by members of the growing middle class.
