'''Saint Mairin'''  is the maid and seeress who is credited with foreseeing the murder of [[Avitus]]' children, as well as the eventual rape and murder of [[Muriel]]. It is said that she also saw the eventual outcome of the struggle between Avitus and his brother [[Gaul]], and the foundation of the [[Primus Deus Fidelis]]. To this end, Mairin, a maid to Avitus and [[Ruth]], switched her own baby with [[Sever]] on the day of the brutal murders. When the deed had been done, and Mairin had fled into the Great Wood with the child Sever in her care, it was she who was blamed for the murder of the children of Avitus and Ruth. The tale of Mairin's abduction of Sever and their wanderings in the [[Great Wood]] is told in the epic poem "[[The Tale of the Woodland King]]", which forms the basis of both the [[Canon of Saint Mairin]] and the [[Canon of Saint Severinius]]. 

Mairin's is a large and well organised Canon. Composed largely of monasteries and convents spread throughout the Empire and beyond, her followers are an optimistic and hard-working lot. She is viewed as a mother-figure by most common men, and is regarded as somewhat of a patron of agriculture, families, and children, as well as a patron by those who are wrongly persecuted and those who seek to hide from evil. But, perhaps most importantly, the nuns and monks of Mairin are sought after for their advice as prognosticators and diviners.
