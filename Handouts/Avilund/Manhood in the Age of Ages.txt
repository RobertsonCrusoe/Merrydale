Manhood in the Age of Ages: a manual of behaviour, composure and etiquette for the modern courtier, by Lord Benjamin Ralston, nephew and heir to the Duke of Sarnstone.

Lord Ralston’s self-published work was initially expected to be no more than a superficial treatise on the ancient etiquette of the upper classes. It turned out to be, however, a revolutionary manifesto about the nature and possibilities of a secular humanity. Far from limiting itself to courtly manners, Age of Ages deals with the philosophical concept of the ideal Man and his place in the world. It is highly critical of the established government and religion, and brings forth the concept of a Humanist approach to existence.

In many ways, Lord Ralston’s book is more a manual for creating a new, primarily secular, society based on reason and philosophy than it is a treatise on etiquette. Age of Ages, originally dismissed as a vanity publication by a foppish aristocrat, has become the seminal work of the Humanist movement and the most detailed and intelligent treatise on the philosophical nature of human existence. Given Lord Ralston’s pedigree, wealth and position, he has yet to face any repercussions from this work. However, plenty of rumours and whispers suggest that such repercussions are not far.
