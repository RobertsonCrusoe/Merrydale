PART 1: STEPHEN — THE AVILUND MAP — STEPHEN ROBERTSON (17 Aug 2008)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 11bceb59cb91dc9f
From: stephen.robertson@anthro.ox.ac.uk
To: Les Blackwell (les.blackwell@gmail.com)

"Present for you:

http://www.robertsoncrusoe.com/avilund/images/9/9c/AVILUND_MAP_1.JPG

A lot of the text needs to be confirmed, since there were a bunch of
things that I just couldn't read on the original. When you have some
time, go over it, and make a note of the things I've screwed up or
messed with. Some of the colours were hard to interpret too - for
instance, is that a glacier above Castle Heirgallad, or a forest? I
interpreted as a forest around that alpine lake, but conceivably it's
above the treeline and is therefore a huge glacier. Some of the roads
were hard to see as well, so I may have drawn them in wrong. I was
hoping to stay faithful to your depiction of the mountain terrain, but
couldn't find a way to do it easily, so I just gauged the heights of
the mountains from your map and adjusted the size of the drawings
accordingly.

I also noticed that I forgot to put a scale in, so that's something
else.

So, there you go. I hope you like this. It was a lot of work, so I
want to make sure it gets done right, and now that I've put in the
effort, changes (at least to borders, names, roads, etc.) should be
pretty easy. It's a step up from that first map of the Island Wards of
Waldheim, I think, but I've still got a lot to learn. That said, I
think it looks pretty professional, if I do say so myself. I think
that if you ever do want to get to publishing Eastwitch, Avilund, or
something, we could put together a pretty nice product between the two
of us (assuming I learn to work more quickly).

I couldn't make something like this without your imagination to
produce the blueprint. I'm looking forward to continuing to explore
Avilund with you. I know it's just one of the many things you've got
going on these days, but we all appreciate your efforts.

Play through.

Stephen"

I think everything looks amazing, and much better than I would have

Changes;

The region north of Heirgallad is just more brown-field, but now that

Now that we have this is format where I can add/edit easily, I would

Anyway, again, that is fantastic work, sir, and I am most appreciative!

More to come..."
