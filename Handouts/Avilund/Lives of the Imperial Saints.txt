An Account of The Lives of the Imperial Saints, edited by Dr. Archibald Beaumont.
First Edition, Lord's Press, 1698.

This volume was originally penned by Sister Agatha of Maxton (nun of Saint Gratus), before its publication under Dr. Beaumont's editorial supervision.

== Volume One: On the Side of Heaven ==
=== Book One: The Early Saints===
* Avitus (\"The First Saint\", Fatherhood and Authority)
* Severinius (Tradition, Authority, Administrators, the Middle Class)
* Muriel (Rape and murder victims, Vengeance, Children)
* Mairin (Motherhood, Families, Agriculture, Children)
* Ida (Prostitution, Exiles)
* Dunstan (Craftsmen, Soldiers, Athletes, Artillery)
* Sebald (Nobility, Primogeniture, Authority)
* Ulric (Warriors, Mercenaries, Merchants, Finance, Ambition)
* Caspar (Fools, Doubt, Martyrs, Convictions)
* Caanan (Misfortune)
* Judith (Farmers, Ranchers, Agriculture, Breeders, Women) 

These are the saints who ascended to Sainthood in the early years after the foundation of the church (Primus Deus Fidelis). The First Saint is generally regarded as having been Saint Avitus, whose real name has been lost in time, and who is now known only by his Imperial name. It is said \"generally\", since, some scholars and theologians maintain that his younger sister, Muriel, having died many decades prior, should rightfully be given the position of honour. However, most do agree that Avitus was the first mortal to transcend this world for the next, and that it was he who was invited to dwell, and later rule, in Heaven by the Angels. It is through his divine grace that all later saints ascended to their position. 

Muriel, though, is regarded as the second saint, though her following has always been, and remains, quite small. Her name is known to most men and women, but few know that she does have active followers and several active convents. 

Severinius (or Sever), controversially described as the true son of Avitus, is considered to be the father of the church, and the third saint. While Avitus gained his position through feats and deeds of extraordinary magnitude, across the mortal and immortal realms, Severinius is credited with the competent rulership of his clan and setting down the rudimentary organisation and canons which would become the modern-day Primus Deus Fidelis. 

Mairin was Severinius' guardian during his time hidden away in the Great Wood. Though she died of old age prior to the ascension of Avitus and the formation of the church, Severinius, upon his death and ascension to sainthood, it is said, took it upon himself to locate Mairin in the Valley of Death and lead her to Heaven, where he pled her case for admittance, and eventually for Sainthood. Today, Mairin's Canon is one of the largest and most organised in the Empire, though it's largely domestic nature keeps it relatively innocuous. 

The other 'Early Saints': Ida, Dunstan, Sebald, Ulric, Caspar, Caanan, and Judith are rough contemporaries of Severinius, and considered his primary disciples. It was these seven saints who are credited with much of the early work in the creation and establishment of the church. It is also rumoured that these seven assisted, in unknown, or unverifiable, ways, Severinius in writing the Canon of Saint Avitus.
