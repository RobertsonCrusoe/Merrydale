Greengrass

Rumour 1:
An emerging Thieves' Guild – the Shadow Thieves

Rumour 2
A demon called ‘Magebane’ is roaming the streets of Waterdeep, coinciding with an increasingly organized anti-magic sentiment among the Waterdhavian populace.

Rumour 3
Contact between Waterdeep and the elves of Evermeet has been severed
[Murlyn]

2 crates packed with sugar cane will be shipped out of the Helmstar Warehouse at sundown after a Dragon Float distracts the nearby populace. Concealed within the sugar cane are 8 enchanted (in a less than legal fashion) blades.

Not considering stealing from smugglers and thieves a crime, a plan is hatched by Ellyth, Esperel, Garth, Tomas and a reluctant Gilthanas to distract or remove the men guarding the shipment and make a switch. Tomas is able to procure a wagon and mules, as well as crates to make the switch, and at sundown, the men are all knocked unconscious, the switch is made, and the wagon is brought back to Garth’s house.climbs up to the roof to keep watch. AS he pokes his head over the eaves, a voice yells ‘Stop’. Esperel shakes off some momentary sluggishness (probably a spell) and charges onto the roof in order to knock over a man standing up there. However, with seemingly superhuman strength, the se figure beast Esperel unconscious and somehow saves him from falling off of the roof.

Downstairs, the door bursts open and the tall dark man from Jannaxil’s office strides in with Teskul and for goons. He accuses the party of stealing from him and his men recover the blades from the crates. Accusing each one of us with very detailed knowledge of who we are, he insists that we owe him a favour and brooks no argument. Ellyth sees something scary drift by outside

The man reveals himself as Thagdul, head of Company Drurginz, an outwardly legitimate trading company, who most likely runs smuggled goods through Skullport, the City Beneath the City. Company Drurginz is at odds with a rival company, Company Ersgul, run by Yarkul. Both of these companies have extensive dealings with the Moonsea region. 

Rumour 4
An evil mage, variously known as Ravenblight and Doomface has been killing once a month at the full moon with a chilling touch over a period beginning 3 years ago until a few months previous to Greengrass. This coincides almost exactly with the period during which Tomas was away from Waterdeep.

Mirtul 10 (one week later)

A tenday later, Garth was contacted by a Calishite scholar looking for lodgings in Dockward. A consummate businessman, Garth made arrangements to have the wizard Jafar rent out one of the spare rooms. Tomas told me that in the three years he’d spent sailing around Calimshan and Amn, he’d never seen a Calishite that looked like him – which was true!  The fellow had as much I’ll never say wizards aren’t useful, but  never He joins the Watchful order of Magists and Protectors (Guild 42 – Castleward 15), sponsored by Orilar Thanas, and is given duties on the city firewatch.Rumour 5

An evil wizard named Halaster Blackcloack is the Keeper of the Undermountain, and sponsors illicit trade through the region beneath Waterdeep (maybe Skullport, too).

Rumour 6
Healing potions can be purchased pretty reasonably from Kappiyan Flurmastyr (though one wouldn’t want to bug him too often)

Jafar, a Calishite scholar (or so he says) newly arrived in Waterdeep, has taken up residence with Garth in his lodgings in Dockward. He joins the Watchful order of Magists and Protectors (Guild 42 – Castleward 15), sponsored by Orilar Thanas, and is given duties on the city firewatch.

Esperel joins the Finesmith’s Guild (Guild 36 – DW ), sponsored by the guildmaster Helthor Desmond.

A week after the previous encounter with Thagdul, Ellyth, Esperel, Garth and Tomas each receive a message from the dark gentleman, inviting them to dinner at the Thirsty Throat Tavern. Gilthanas, it is assumed, has found some other way to repay Thagdul. Jafar is summoned as well, having had other dealings with Thagdul in the south, and owing a favour to him from earlier. Thagdul wants the party to burn down a house in Northward, and offers us any loot we can take as incentive, as well as the possibility of clearing our debt. Ellyth suspects that Thagdul may be a dragon.

After dinner, Thagdul directs us to a large four-story building in Northward, to the block bounded by Sulmor St., the High Road, Delzorin St. and Vhezoar St. He tells us that a diversion will be engineered at 22h30 to keep the firewatch and guard busy so we can make good our escape once we’ve set the fires. He tells us a crate awaits us in the nearby sewer entrance.

Rumour 7
Crimes Against the Lords (impersonation, forgery or treason) are punishable by death. Crimes Against the city (bribery, etc.) are punished by death or exile..

Ellyth spends the intermediate time at the nearby Gentle Mermaid, a famous gambling house. There she meets Arsten Thunderstaff II and he succumbs to her charms. He gives her a cut of his winnings (50 gp) but she is unable to find out any information or anything of use.

Meanwhile, Esperel and Garth case out the joint while Tomas runs home to put on armour. The house seems to be 4 stories high, and movement is detectable on 2 of the 3 levels. When Tomas returns, he heads into the sewers with Garth and Esperel and discovers a box with five detonators and directions. Esperel finds several secret doors, and the rest of the party enters the sewers. It is now time to move. Several drow statues with seemingly deteriorating enchantments are found, and a possible entrance to the building is uncovered. As it is opened, however, the party is attacked from the rear by two watchspiders, and three figure come in from the other room – at least one seems to be a drow.
