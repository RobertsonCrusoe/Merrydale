Journal of Sanduskyn Vale - 22 Ches, 1483 DR
DM Handout (Les Blackwell)

---

Then came the afternoon of the 22nd, and here the people of Dagger Falls were reminded of the terrible beauty of the Gold Elves, and how they are just not the same as us mortals. Having already won their first match against the White Cloaks with seeming ease, the crowd was eager to see how Chrysalis fared against the brutal Redjacks. The murderous reputation of Buddy Gorehammer brought fear to the Chrysalis. I can say this with some confidence, as I considered them friends by this time, and hope they considered me the same, and we spoke on their prospects before the contest. Gorehammer was brutal and needed to be stopped, but his story didn't end on this afternoon, though very nearly.

As the first stone struck the gong, and both teams sprung into action, most of the crowd fell silent, holding their breath while the teams closed in about each other in a manufactured game of shifting blocks and towers. But the real game, as it always is, was violence and wrath, and the wrath of the Elves has no equal.

As the Dragonborn Grimaldous mounted one of the central towers in the arena, my friend and Gold Elf, Jeska, appeared as if out of nowhere (though talking with the team days later, and others that were watching her closely, I learned that she had, with great agility, climbed the nearby platform and bounded across to that which Grimaldous stood upon). Grimaldous, who was presently distracted by a localized rain cloud conjured by my imaginative friend and colleague, Jasper Hawkwinter, had no idea Jeska was behind him, and the magistrate in charge of protecting the Dragonborn was likewise caught off guard, as Jeska's rapier was thrust through clear through the heart of the brute, emerging from his chest. He was surely dead at that moment, and the fall to the ground below that followed sealed his fate if it wasn't already.

The battle stopped and the crowd went completely silent for a moment, before it erupted in cheers, screams and other chaos, and people in the crowd were calling for "Madame elf" to "kill the accursed dwarf next!" But Jeska silenced the entire arena as well as Bishop Whitegate might, with an imperious gesture for peace, and declared that Chrysalis would concede the round "in honour of the fallen."

At this point, some in the crowd, dwarves mostly, who hated their murderous kin, protested the concession, and seeing an opportunity to work the crowd a little, the clever Sembian Alavandor produced a wonderful speech which I hope I've captured accurately. As far as I remember, this is what he said;

"Most learned adjudicators, I invoke the Ancient Convention of Battle's Finality to contend that our Captain's unforeseen action upon yon errant Redjack cannot be judged forfeit nor breach. For by the self-same precedent permitting Accidental Slaying in the heat of fray, it must be acknowledged that one whose life is forfeit holds no claim to victory's laurels.

As our esteemed Bishop Whitegate well knows, just as a duelist in single combat concedes at the fatal wound, so too do the ranks arrayed behind a fallen champion. To permit otherwise would be to untie Finality's knot, unraveling the very foundation of these revered games.

Our hands were not stayed by treachery, but drove true in the passion of battle's crucible as all present embraced. Unfortunate though the outcome be, it stands as Finality's child, quickened by the same maternal conventions that lent this contest breath.

Just as in the solemn rites of Personal Combat by Code Duello, where a mortal wound etches the instant of decisive defeat - so too in this grander theatre of martial conflict between assembled companies, must the fall of one contestant be viewed as concluding forfeit for their whole.

I implore our Captain to lay down her noble burden. The deeds are done, the price of bravery's gambit exacted according to rules as immutable as the headsman's axe. We cannot forfeit what Finality has already bestowed - only humbly accept victory's mantle where rules anointed bind it to rest.

We merely administered Finality's rule, unlooked for though it was, and therefore cannot be judged wanting. Whereas our doomed adversaries are now wanting, and so must want for spoils fairly lost when first one of their number was carried from the field. By Ancient Convention, the round is ours!"

Whispers of agreement and approval began growing in the crowd, and the Bishop frowned down at the tournament ground from the Adjudicators' box. "What you say is sooth, young Alavandor, but we shall leave it to your Captain to make the call" came the Bishop's words. But Lucan retorted, cutting off the Elf, to the amazement of everyone in the stadium, except the Elf, who likely sensed that the Sembian was not quite finished.

"Alas, most learned bishop. I must protest. For while our esteemed captain's inclination towards magnanimity is as laudable as it is characteristic of true leadership, it is not a luxury that the tenets of this revered contest afford us.

We take no joy in this ruling, but the timeless precedents are clear - when an opponent is slain in the fray, striking that fatal blow immediately decides defeat for their full company. The combat's Finality is writ in the spilling of blood that can never be refunded.

For was it not by the self-same edicts of Battle's Finality - which you yourself invoked only days ago, your Grace - that our foes, harboring that wretched, rage-addled disgrace to dwarven honor in their ranks, avoided sanction after perpetrating a far more heinous transgression?

When their uncontrolled, base urges held sway, and the much-admired Bree Bridlestone—a dwarf whose camaraderie reached beyond the mere alliances of this tournament—fell to the reckless fury of one plying his veins with deviant, uncivilized concoctions, thereby permitting a cherished soul's life to be casually snuffed out like an errant candle flame... It was this very Convention that shielded them temporarily from the Maimed God's justice.

If such was their deliverance from accountability by rule of these hallowed grounds, then we who acted in the controlled furor of drills and disciplines sanctioned by the light of Aumanator and the support of the Triad cannot be bound to any further penance. Unbridled impulse and chemical mania were the Redjacks' vices, not ours.

A broken blade, indeed, cannot claim victory, and neither should a team that leans on the crutch of volatile remedies, hoping perhaps that the chaos of their onslaught might mask the deficiencies of honor and discipline. Such a party holds no further entitlement to the round's accolade and must recuse themselves in favour of their rightful opponents.

We merely administered Finality's rule, unlooked for though it was, and therefore cannot be judged wanting. Our hands, though marked by the grim task of battle, remain clean of treachery, guided by the immutable hand of Tyr and the unyielding structure of Law. Nay, it is our doomed adversaries who must now remain wanting for spoils fairly lost when first one of their number was carried from the field. By this Ancient Convention, the round is ours!"

Ambassador Whitmore motioned to quiet the crowd, and looked as though he would speak again, when he was cut off by Jeska. "Death among allies for good in a 'competition' is.. shameful".. she turned her eyes to the judge's tower. "From this moment, Team Chrysalis will use only non-lethal weaponry. If you agree, Master Redwater, we are willing to meet again for the second round."

Hassle glared up at the elf and looked quickly to the tower, where the Ambassador nodded assent. In a loud and rumbling voice, Hassle replied "We'd gladly meet you again, she-elf. You'll of course sit one of your team for the remainder of our two matches, I'm sure. You'll have the benefit of having your teammate after the match, which we will not enjoy..."

To this, Jeska declared that she would sit out herself so long as the new rules were adhered to, and a second round began. This round revolved around some of the members starting in cages, with one member turning over boxes to find keys to free them. It seems that the Redjacks planned to use Zellia's devilish familiar, Zensibarbus, to deliver keys quickly, but the unerring bow of the Sembian unmade the thing almost immediately into the round. In short, Chrysalis won the round, with none of the Redjacks getting freed from their cages (most importantly Gorehammer) and the third and most remarkable round began.

In the final round, the teams had to deliver large and heavy chests across the river to their opponent's side, and again, Jeska sat herself out, though she watched from one of the central towers, to see to the adherence of her demands for non-lethality. This round started with Gorehammer charging Chrysalis as the first stone sounded, and the brutality of the dwarf was terribly plain to see; he nearly eviscerated Mari and Lucan before the Gold Elf had seen enough. She jumped with astonishing grace from the tower, landing nimbly, and skewered the dwarf with a single thrust. Gorehammer fell to the ground, and the Elf jabbed a second time, this time at the prostrate form of the dwarf, and left her blade sticking from his back, swaying violently. She then strode brazenly across the arena, unarmed, to face the legendary fists of Livinia Leng. There were gasps and cries in the audience, as none expected what was about to happen.

The cruel monk smiled as the Elf closed in on her, and made a flourishing movement with her hands, elbows and knees, but Jeska, ignoring the show completely, threw one bare-fisted right cross that spun the jaw of the foreigner nearly right round, and dropped her instantly.

Seeing the fate of his team, the merciless captain Crasster Redwater made to close in to finish off any of the terribly wounded Chrysalis, but this is when the voice of Mari Thickwickle became the greatest weapon on the field. She amplified her voice through the power of her faith, and said "Crasster, me boy! We've got ye beat. Be reasonable now an' yield. Let's spare any more bloodshed fer yer lot." Amazingly, Crasster stopped, looking around the field at his defeated team, and to the edge of the arena where his father was shaking his head in disbelief. Saying nothing, Crasster sheathed his swords and walked off the field, and the day was won by Chrysalis.

The lasting impression that the people of our fair town were left with was that Chrysalis was a group of remarkably magnanimous heroes, who were as fair and kind as they were formidable. The impact especially of the halfling matron and the Gold Elf can't be understated; both women made an indelible entry into the chronicle of our time with their deeds and leadership.

The town was, by the end of that day, fully behind Chrysalis, and this support would grow in the days to come, as we saw.
