Marpenoth 5, 1481 - From the Journal of Nathan Gale 
Notes from GFD: 
I have been fortunate enough to have shared with me the journal of Nathan Gale, which was sent with a package of other White Cloaks possessions from Charity, after the unfortunate conclusion of events there. There is much to be said about Mr. Gale, 

and we hope against hope for his (and others) return. He was a wonderful writer, and it would be a treasure to have more of his hand. 
-GFD 
Marpenoth 5, 1481. 
For what seemed like an hour, we had been seated at the ornate table set in the second-floor dining room of the Close. Stern wooden benches supported our battle-weary bones as we bided our time. Two comfortable-looking chairs sat at either end of the table, suspiciously unoccupied. At the very least, the gradually warming scent of fresh dinner wafting in from distant halls, tempting our imaginations and stomachs, was enough to ease any anxious or impatient thoughts that may have crossed our minds. The proceedings directly following our victory were something of a strange blur. Disconcerting, to say in the least. 

Abruptly hurried inside, dried off, dressed in all manner of threaded finery by several servants sporting severely stressed countenances, and then hurried back again up an overlong flight of stairs to be seated for a dinner we hadn't expected with a host who had yet to arrive, despite his being in clear attendance at the conclusion of the tournament. Administrator Alen Reks had invited us to a celebration devoid of any attendees other than ourselves. Or himself. Or anyone. 

Silence lingered in the air, periodically disturbed by the awkward squawk of benches being adjusted, forks and knives being fiddled with, side-long small talk and discussion of the tournament battles prior and what, perhaps, has yet to come now that the lot of us stand victorious as the new White Cloaks Bravo team. 

Such discussion, however, was put on hold at the growing pitter-patter of tiny feet. A swelling ruckus of children's voices calling out in excitement, arrived in the dining hall, followed shortly thereafter by the children themselves, crashing like a wave in a vicious scramble against the rocky shore of the oaken table. Within moments, the benches were filled on either side of us by an assortment of youths ranging from toddler to teen, nearly twenty or so in total, all of them bickering amongst themselves over spots and utensils and other things of dire importance, including the attention of ourselves most importantly. 

(Before we went to sleep tonight, Lady Blackmane explained that many of the children were orphans from a place called Liberty Hill, which I'm vaguely familiar with, but which few want to discuss.) 

The clamour died down almost as quickly as it had begun, however, with a high-pitched whistle cutting through the shouting as though it were the sharpest of blades. The children quieted down and sat themselves on their hands and their bottoms, eyes turned towards the doorway they had just emerged from, faces puckered with anticipation. The abrupt silence was broken once more by the arrival of Administrator Reks and Lady Blackmane on his arm, which she seemed less than enthused about to those of us capable of noticing any hint of emotion hiding amongst the statuesque expression on her pretty face. Alen, in stark contrast, was smiling brightly as ever. The elf was well dressed and cut a dashing figure as he escorted the Lady to her seat, afterwards slowly making his way to the head of the 

table and his own cushioned seat, eyes rolling steadily over the accumulated faces on both sides of the table as he did so. 

Without a word, Administrator Reks reached into the folds of his jacket, withdrew what appeared to be a small bird, and tapped the top of its feathered head with two delicate fingers. The creature whistled once more, a different but equally high-pitched tone as the one from moments earlier, and within seconds servants were sweeping into the dining hall carrying trays and saucers laden with a variety of delectable meats, vegetables and breads still steaming from the oven, placing them one-by-one in front of the occupants of the table, with the sole exception of us five. 

The servants sweep out of the room like clockwork, and the Administrator quickly replaced the bird within the folds of his jacket, nodding at Lady Blackmane from across the table. 

"I would request a toast," Alen said in a smooth voice, "but with children present, such a thing would be most disagreeable. This rabble is more than enough to handle without liquor thrown into the mix, eh? Ah hah, yes." 

And without any further words, Alen speared a piece of potato with his fork and popped it into his mouth, chewing the morsel slowly and deliberately, eyes wandering over the accumulated faces. Hasting appeared to be disinterested as ever. The children were enthralled and horrified all at the same time, eyes flicking between the Administrator and their own plates, along with the empty ones occupying the places in front of Bravo Team. 

After an awkward minute of chewing, smile ever-widening, Alen swallowed and sighed contentedly, then waved a hand at the table. 

"Mhmm, mhmm, no poison tonight. It's delicious, proceed with your meal." And with that, the children set upon their food with great purpose, and the dining hall was filled with noise once again. 

For nearly fifteen minutes Alen and the children were at it, enjoying their dinner while we sat and watched, decidedly non-plussed and perhaps a bit irritated. At any mention, or attempt to do so, Alen would wave a hand at us and place one index finger over his food-stained lips, wagging it back and forth so as to insist on our patience. As soon as they finished, the children took up their plates and utensils and scampered out of the dining hall piece-meal, until all that remained was us, Administrator Reks and Lady Blackmane, to whom Alen passed a pleasant glance, before gazing about us. 

"I must offer you my sincerest congratulations, you've all done marvellously. Were I a better host, which I am, of course, disregarding current circumstances, I would offer you a meal, but you five, alas, will not be remaining here for long." 

And he affected a shocked expression. 

"But what do you mean, Administrator? Which is how I will be addressed from this point onwards, owing to the fact that you lot are now official auxiliary members of the White Cloaks, designated White 

Cloak Bravo team. The current state of the White Cloaks leaves much to be desired in terms of competent bodies. As such, you five have proven yourselves to both be competent in battle and in possession of said aforementioned bodies. Therefore, I consider this entire endeavour a success. You'll be walking in the footsteps of giants, my friends, and therefore I expect at least an ounce of professionalism and decorum when representing the White Cloaks. What we stand for, what we hope to accomplish, and the means we go about doing it -- all of these reflect not only individuals, but the company as a whole, and therefore I expect you lot to act in an... approvable fashion. But I digress. This has already been explained to you before the tournament. Pay and lodging and all that contractual nonsense will be hammered out in your absence." 

For a moment, Alen's eyes acquired a blank stare, as though he had fallen off his carefully laid path. He looked to Blackmane. Her heavy lids lowered a smidge as she regarded the Administrator, shuddering beneath a generous measure of disdain. Reks clapped his hands with delight, train of thought restored. 

"Ah, yes, you won't be here for long. Apologies, I honestly don't know why you all bothered getting so nicely dressed when you would be heading out so soon. I'm flattered, of course, but it was a waste of time. You see, you have a mission which you will be embarking upon immediately." 

With this statement, the Administrator's tone sharpened noticeably, and from somewhere beneath the table he withdrew three crisp letters, neatly sealed with a dab of red wax. With a sharp flick of his wrist, the letters slide across the table, coming to a rest before Sergeant Paros. 

"The one addressed to you is for your attention only, Paros. The other two will be explained in your letter. Share this information at your discretion, delegating as you see fit." 

The Administrator leaned forward and gazed at us over the smooth crest of his knuckles, green eyes examining us one-by-one. 

"At the conclusion of this briefing, you will make your way to the servants' quarters in the western wing of the Close. There you will change into appropriate attire for a group of individuals travelling by caravan. Outside you will find three carts laden with nothing of any particular interest to anyone. You will take these wagons north to Dwimmerholme Farm at a casual pace, and once you arrive you will acquire lodgings at the Amber Heart Inn and rest there. You will make contact with a liaison of mine and she will assist you in your... investigation." 

"I'm sure you're aware of the recent slew of disappearances and abductions taking place in the region. Dwimmerholme is the most recent lead we have on these happenings. Your objectives are two-fold. 

First, you will be investigating the disappearances of three families worth of townsfolk from Dwimmerholme Farm. Finding them and, if necessary, rescuing them is top priority. Their safety is paramount. Second, in the process of finding these individuals, you will be investigating the rumours of Zhentarim activities in the region. Encountering such individuals is a definite eventuality that you will have to be prepared for. Subdue and question them by any means available to you. The safety of the dale depends on it. For the good of Daggerdale, we need to know where they're operating out of, what they're doing, and how they're doing it. If you have any questions, now is the time." 

We basically packed our things, and prepared to leave before the break of dawn, and I now weary, must lay down my head

Note from Administrator Reks to Nathan Gale, 
5 Marpenoth, 1480 
Hello and Congratulations!!! 

By the time you read this letter, you should be well on your way towards Dwimmerholme farm. If not, what am I paying you for? Ha ha, but in all seriousness, please make sure you're keeping to the schedule. You are, for the most part, in charge of things on this expedition. 

Don't make me regret choosing you. 

Ha ha! I jest. You have my complete confidence. Where was I? 
Ah, yes. You have in your possession, in addition to this letter you (I hope!) are reading, two other letters. The larger letter is to be given to a certain Miss Andrea Stuard, proprietor proipriet owner of the Amber Heart Inn. She is a White Cloak liaison, and a trustworthy individual with much local knowledge that will no doubt be integral to your investigation. Once you establish contact with her, she will be your means of communication with myself, which I hope you will take advantage of quite thoroughly (keeping me abreast of the situation that is, not the lady herself ha ha!!!). 

I must stress the importance of keeping a low profile while on this investigation. The hart is quick to flee at the hunter's scent, eh? If word gets out that White Cloaks are investigating the rash of disappearances taking place in the area, who knows what problems may arise for you. We want to close in on these slavers without their being any the wiser, follow? So make sure you put on your clever boots for this one, if you please. Keep your people in line, and make sure that whenever you deal with Andrea, you're certain to keep her position safe. This is top priority. 

I trust you to go about your business intelligently. I'll leave the execution up to you. 

At the Amber Hart, order a Truestar Special. Stuard will take that as a sign that you're a White Cloak. It's been pre-arranged. Give her the letter. Be discreet. She will point you in the right direction. I've paid her enough to warrant that, at least. 

The smaller letter goes to the Mayor. It's a matter of fees. For services soon-to-be rendered, if you catch my drift. Right? You strike me as a catcher, yes. Alright. Again, I must stress discretion with how you go about delivering this. If you're frightened, give it to Andrea to deliver. This is imperative. The White Cloaks are a company first and foremost, and money is something we are most assuredly not made of (by the way, please make sure the carts and horses are taken care of, they are expensive and it'll come out of your pay if anything goes south regarding their condition, short of a dragon swooping down from the sky and making off with the lot of them, ha ha heavens forbid). 

We are not looking to milk Dwimmerholme of all their copper and silver, leaving the farmers to fling mud at each other by means of paying for their loaves and turnips. But some recompense will be required. I'll leave it to you to hammer out the details, although I'm certain as the quality of your pay share depends on the amount we receive from potential clients, your motivation on the subject is all but guaranteed. 

Finally, I suppose it's only fair to inform you that you and your team are actively acting as bait during your travel to Dwimmerholme. Keep this to yourself if you wish, I could not care less if it bothers any of you. You lot are rough and tough and you like to fight, so I imagine if you're accosted by slavers, you will be more than capable of giving them the old what-for. Why chase your prey when you can get them to chase you, eh? 

Ha ha. But seriously, mind the wagons. We're running on a budget here. 

What knowledge we have of the abductions is scattered and disorganized at best. 

From Dwimmerholme alone, three families have been abducted, and there have been skirmishes all over the dale, mostly on the roads and trails, in the last tenday. 

According to a reliable source, an entire caravan vanished from Charity Hill as it made its way to Liberty Hill. Eyewitness accounts from rescued individuals indicated that perhaps the slavers had an accent of a particularly Cormyrian bent, specifically from close to Marsember, which I find unlikely, but foreign mercenaries would indeed be concerned less with enslaving locals than natives to the Dale, I suppose. 

But I will cast no judgements, not yet at any rate. Keep me updated. I will keep an eye on things. I have many eyes. Many fingers in many pies... 

Oh, and keep Shae away from the money. Fanatics are notoriously open-pouched when it comes to group funding. We are not a charity. 

Cheers, and please do not get yourselves killed. Another tournament so soon... garish. 
Administrator Reks Marpenoth 5, 1481 DR
