The war room in Loedegan’s Hall is half again as busy this morning as it was yesterday.  The usual host of military and clergy are there.  There are twice as many knights here this morning as there were yesterday, as many companies of them arrived late in the day yesterday.  Bargle is there as well, with his Lieutenant of , Gamble Netherstorm, and you notice that Lumina is doing her best to avoid eye contact with them.  Fortunately, the dark tiers of benches in the room make that easy.  The Captain of the , Hastle Blackwater is also there, and he is doing his best to try to make the pair of you uncomfortable, fixing a glare on you from time to time.

==The King's Embassy==

As the opening prayer for the meeting is being delivered by Zenith Lord Whitegate, there is a jostling at one of the doors at the top of the amphitheatre, and a squad of Purple Dragons enters the room, with John Tunbridge, the Cormyeran Ambassador shuffling in close behind.  Everyone looks up at the late-comers, and the clergy of Amaunator, Argent especially, are visibly perturbed by the tardy knights.

“Forgive our late arrival, brothers” the large plate-clad knight in the lead bellows in a thick Cormyrean accent.  “We have ridden hard all night to get here, and just now got through your diligent gate wardens just now. I am General Stuart Angleton, and I come with an entire company of Purple Dragons for the common defence of good people against the aggression of Netheril!”

Lord Whitegate marches half way up the stairs to greet the man with a vigorous clasping of forearms and claps on the back.  “I know who you are, General!  You are always welcomed in this hall, and you may even come late, once!”

The Purple Dragons file into one of the tiers, and the meeting continues as usual.  The floor is opened up, also in the usual manner, but General Angleton is given the privilege of speaking first.

“Neighbours, brothers, sisters!  I come with grim tidings.  Or common enemy, the foul and treacherous Netheril, marches against you.  Our scouts have watched them pull away thousands of soldiers from their front in the south, which is our northern border.  Those troops are reported to be heading north, and we can only guess that they plan to escalate their aggression on your borders.  Thus, I come with aid, all that the King could spare.  I pledge my blade and those of the men who are mine to command at the will of Lord Whitegate and the people of Daggerdale, our neighbours and brothers!”  The big old knight drops to one knee in front of Lord Whitegate, who takes the kneeling knight’s offered blade.  They exchange a number of oaths and then embrace.

== The Farm Report ==

After thanks are offered to the General and the Ambassador from Cormyr, news is reported by various scouts and clerics.

“The news from Charity Hills farm is bad.  They lost 6 Sun Blades last week, and 10 militia.  Not surprisingly, it seems like straight-up orc raids from the hills, but their boldness is unusual.”

“At Crossed Blades, a pair of werewolves jumped the wall a few nights ago and killed a pair of priests right outside the cathedral.  There are also rumours of undead sightings towards the Old Castle, which is making a lot of people nervous.”

“A patrol on Foothills road (Between Foothills Farm and Tesh Valley Farm) claims they saw a small pack of black devils eating a dead stag right on the road.  They gave chase but didn’t catch the fleeing monsters, nor did they figure out what they were.”

“At Gribbon's, there was actually a small siege for half a day; a small army of gnolls, maybe 200 of them, ambushed a whole squad of Sun Dragoons on the Moonsea road; they killed half of them, and chased the other half to the farm.  The siege gain anything for the marauders, but...hell’s bells!  A siege?!  A few miles from town?!”

==The Dark Garden Bounty==
thumb

When all the reports are given, Luminous just nods his head gravely.  “We knew things were coming to this.” He says slow and quietly.  “We are sure the Dark Garden is at work in at least a few of these escalations.  Their timing is impeccable; just as we are about to see an increase in tempo on the Plains of Netheril, and maybe right here at our own doors, our heartland is put at risk.  For this reason, I am authorizing the largest common bounty we have ever endorsed.  The Dark Garden Bounty will see that enterprising and capable men and women turn their attention towards the heartland, and ferret out these villains.”

There is some muttering in the auditorium, and you can see the smiling face of Captain Blackwater of the Redjacks across the room; he winks at you and then starts to head out.

==Desperate Times==

“There is another announcement that I will share with you all here, which will be made across the Dale in town and in every farm at High Sun today; As of noon today, the minimum required service in the Sun Blades is increased from two years to three.  Those who are slated to be released from duty today will be offered remuneration above one rank if they choose to stay.  All new recruits and those at the end of their first year as of noon today, will have to serve for three years.”

There is more muttering in the auditorium, and a few red faces that dare not speak out against what they have just heard.

“These might be the hardest times we have had to face, brothers and sisters” Lord Whitegate continues.  “We are now in the teeth of a hungry lion, and we will need every bit of strength we can find to get out.  Tell your friends and family to eagerly accept the new terms of the conscription.  Encourage the retired to don his blade and tabard in service to the Sun Blades once more.  Find our enemies and destroy them!  The sun has risen on a darker day, but we will chase away shadow with light and with steel!”

Many of the soldiers, knights and clergy are energized by the brief but moving words of their commander-in-chief, and they are cheering and rapping their swords on the benches in front of them.  With this, the strategic meeting starts to let out.

==Agents of the White Cloak==

To Iolus, Lumina says “I am going to register for that Dark Garden Bounty at the Registrar’s right now.  Get your team ready.  You will be heading to Dagger Falls tomorrow morning to start looking for leads.  Alystair’s report recommended starting there.  However, see if you can turn up any intel here in the Castle before you go.  I’ll see you at supper time!”

Iolus watched Lumina take her leave.

\"Alystair...\" , he spoke quietly to himself as Lumina left his company. There had been a time where he had felt isolated from everyone and everything and upon meeting Alystair and seeing a similar light within that man, as he had within himself; he had fostered the hope that the two might become friends.

But that had never happened, Alystair was as distant a man from others as he was dangerous with that ungainly blade he wielded.

It seemed as if all of the original \"White Cloaks\" had scattered or died. He had not seen Tuugan or Malvau in months, and Troia even longer than that. He could not blame any of them. What they had gone through together was not something that could pull people closer. They had seen terrible things and had witnessed terrible things done to each other. It was only natural he supposed that the fellowship they were all once part of had shattered.

Now there was only him, and somehow the mantle of leadership had been placed upon him. He was to lead a disparate group of individuals into the new chaos of the lowlands and somehow make things right.

He'd had little time to get more than just the names of his companions.

*Corran Altair. A name that he knew from the archives. A one time up and comer of the Sun Blades, only to end up getting the wrong farmgirl with child and be dishonourably discharged from the Sun Blades. Now a paladin of Torm and member of a failed expedition into the deserts of Netheril, Iolus was saddled with the possibly broken man.

*An errant warrior by the name of Drake Sterling. Iolus put little stock into the ever-increasing amounts of mercenaries that were coming to the castle of late. He might be capable with a sword and shield but this Drake fellow struck him as a brigand of questionable moral quality.

*Rhuun. A giant. He had been corrected in his categorizing of the large man by one of the more worldly priests in the archives that Rhuun was a goliath and not a giant. It seems Rhuun was an emissary from the northern mountains and some manner of burgeoning treaty between the castle and Rhuun's people were forming. For Iolus, it did not matter what the giant preffered to identify himself as...he was a giant. The one small thing that seemed to be positive about Rhuun was that he appeared to be devout even if it was not Amaunator that Rhuun revered.

*Sorashana. A significant source of concern for Iolus as there is much talk over how she even got through the gates. There is the common talk that says as much, \"If they let a drow in, what makes ya think they'd stop some devil worshipper at the gate?\" Iolus is not so quick to judge but the woman is clearly an unquantifiable factor and one that Iolus feels will need to keep a close eye on.

*Banitt. A priest of Amaunator and the sole source of Iolus feeling positive about this task he has been given. Banitt is commonly referred to as The Pilgrim in the castle and although not from Daggerdale, he seems to carry the will of Amaunator about him at all times. Banitt seems to have caught the attention of the upper levels of the clergy in the castle and despite his ties to Corran Altair and the failed mission in Netheril a few months back, Banitt truly appears to be an individual that is capable of making a difference in Daggerdale's future.

He needed to speak to them all before they head east and time was the friend of no man. He left the chamber as quickly as possible, eager to be free of the politicking and boot-licking that always seemed to occur in Loedegan's Hall.

It was time to speak to his comrades and set upon a target.
