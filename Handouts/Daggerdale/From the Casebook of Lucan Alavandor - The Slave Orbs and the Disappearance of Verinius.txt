----From the Casebooks of Lucan Alavandor: The Slave Orbs and the Disappearance of Verinius, 9 Mirtul, 1483 DR

Reks was right, damn him. I've been asking the wrong questions. I think I've put it all together, though I'm not sure where it gets us.

PREMISE I: "VERINIUS ALONE CAN OPEN PORTALS" - REKS

Supporting Evidence:

- Reks suggested that Belladonna used the orbs to open the Shadowfell rift in the Dagger Hills, now guarded by the Shadowdam
- Estelmer's report discusses how the Soul of Corruption was "summoned" to Liberty Hill (implying it came from elsewhere)
- Netherese artifacts have intrinsic shadow-plane associations, and the Shar-Shadowfell connection points to the Soul's origins in the Shadowfell.

Hypothesis: Belladonna used Verinius to summon the Soul of Corruption from the Shadowfell by opening a temporary portal and pulling it through, just as she had used the orb to open the gate in the Dagger Hills. Accordingly, we can safely conclude that Verinius can be used to open a gate to the Shadowfell.

PREMISE II: THE ORBS FEED ON BLOOD SACRIFICE

- Jendwendle's testimony: Morganna (priestess of Tymora) and the caravan captain (likely a mage) had their throats cut and were "put into the terrible, terrible orb" in autumn 1482
- Coventina was a priestess of Sharess (a divine spellcaster)
- My direct observation at Thistle Ridge: The orb Loki grabbed after Coventina was killed was inky black
- Estelmer's report: The orbs were being used to power rituals sustained by constant blood sacrifice up until the moment of capture at Liberty Hill

Analysis: The orbs are glass spheres banded with brass around the circumference; when dark and viewed perpendicular to the band, they resemble a new moon, which is to say the holy symbol of Shar. Invictus confirmed the Sharran Inversion: charged orbs are dark, spent orbs glow. Notably, Vessuvian reported that Vortellius was glowing when he saw it in the Historic Wing (spent), and that Varros was cracked/damaged and glowing erratically (broken, cannot hold charge). Spellcaster blood charges the orbs. Coventina, Morganna, and the caravan captain all fit this pattern. When they were captured at Liberty Hill, the orbs had been powering weeks-long rituals sustained by constant blood sacrifice. Estelmer reports the Whitegates and others being eviscerated and hung from the Table of the Sun, their blood dripping to feed the Ritual of Corruption. Belladonna was thus wielding charged artifacts when Drake Sterling subdued her. Accordingly, we can surmise that Verinius was captured in a charged state.

PREMISE III: THE ORBS ARE "TIED TO" THE IMPERIOSUS ORBIS - ESTELMER'S REPORT

Evidence:

- Estelmer doesn't just say "powered by" but "tied to"
- Hoetmer's cryptic warning: "I would not transport it through any farm or Charity Hills"
- Verinius was held at the Castle for nearly a year before being moved to the Historic Wing

Analysis: Invictus interpreted Hoetmer's warning as concern about theft, but what if Hoetmer feared what the orb itself could do? A "tie" to the Imperiosus Orbis (IO) implies ongoing connection, not just one-way power flow. If the IO can send power through the Slave Orbs, it might also send commands. Remote activation would explain why the transport route mattered: a charged orb passing through populated areas could open a portal at any moment if triggered from afar.

CONCLUSION

If these three premises hold - portal capability, retained charge through blood sacrifice, and bidirectional communication - then Verinius could have been remotely activated to portal itself out of the Historic Wing. Sometime before autumn 1482, someone with access to the Imperiosus Orbis (almost certainly Loki) sent an activation command through the link between the IO and Verinius. The still-charged orb responded by opening a portal to the Shadowfell and passing through it. The orb would have arrived in the Shadowfell at the shadow-correspondence to the Cathedral in Dagger Falls, where Loki (or an agent) would have been waiting to retrieve it. No theft. No forced entry. The three "blessed" magistrates saw nothing because there was nothing to see: just a portal opening inside a locked box, and the orb going through. This explains why the box was empty with no sign of intrusion. And it explains why Hoetmer warned against populated transport routes. He wasn't worried about bandits stealing it. He understood that a charged orb vulnerable to remote activation could open a Shadowfell portal anywhere, anytime, putting farms and villages at risk of either losing the orb or having something come through from the other side. The year-long hold at the Castle suggests that the Aumanatori were waiting for something, maybe for the charge to dissipate naturally. When it didn't, they moved it to the Historic Wing. Still charged, still dangerous.

QUESTIONS REMAINING / QUESTIONS ARISING

1. Was Verinius glowing while under observation in the Historic Wing? This would be a partial confirmation or refutation. If it were glowing (spent), the theory would fail, and we would need an alternative explanation (e.g., could it have been charged while in the box through a nearby bloodletting/sacrifice?).
2. Why wait until autumn 1482 to recall it? Two years after capture. What changed? Invictus suggests that the date of the theft may be a clue, but if we accept that the theft was remote, we should consider the wider context, rather than just who had access to the Historic Wing.
3. Speaking of dates, when was Verinius last confirmed to be in the box? The gap between the last verification and Invictus's discovery defines the window of disappearance.
4. What else did the Estelmer-Hoetmer report contain? Reks claims never to have seen it, yet he somehow knows details about the orbs' natures and capabilities, Belladonna's use of them, and their current status. How? What's in that report that we haven't seen?
5. Who else knows about the orbs' capabilities? If Reks knows without seeing the report, who told him? Invictus? Or does he have another source?

One final consideration: What does this theory tell us about the fate of Phinneas Hoetmer?

Hoetmer died on 29 Flamerule, 1480 - two months after capturing three charged Slave Orbs "tied to" the Imperiosus Orbis, and two years, more or less, before Verinius vanished. He was examining the orbs with Estelmer. He understood enough to warn against transporting Verinius through populated areas, suggesting he grasped dangers that others did not. Estelmer says he "went to sleep and never woke up," exhausted from his Aetherial excursions. Hoetmer's death left Invictus without expert guidance. Was his death natural exhaustion, or was he silenced because he knew too much about vulnerabilities someone wanted kept secret?

We need to retrieve that orb.

-L.A.
