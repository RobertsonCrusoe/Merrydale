The Lord's Auction — Candlekeep Emissaries at Castle Radiance
Source: Email from Les Blackwell to the group, 5 July 2011. Subject: "Auction"
Set on 29th Ches 1481 DR. Les's narrative introduction to the Candlekeep Emissaries' Auction, held in Madigan's Hall in the Inner Bailey of Castle Radiance. Describes the Hall (Sun Blades and Knights barracks, capacity 400, typically half garrisoned), the Inner Bailey layout (Madigan's Hall, Loedgan's Hall, Fortress Cathedral), and the three Candlekeep auctioneers: Brother Neddle, Brother Desden, and Sister Caylam, with Brother Darred presiding. Lord Whitegate ordered the auction moved before the Green Grass Festival. Every company captain and many sergeants attend.

The Candlekeep Emissaries' Auctions are the biggest commercial events of the year. There are typically two, and the first is usually part way through the summer. This year, Lord Whitegate has ordered that the auction take place before the Green Grass Festival, and as such, it is taking place today, the 29th of Ches, 1481, and has been dubbed the Lord's Auction.

It isn't too fine a day for an auction; the sky is dark and there is something between wet flakes of snow and freezing rain drizzling to make things dreary outside. The auction itself is held in Madigan's Hall, high up in the Inner Bailey of the Castle. This hall is one of the three buildings that form the triangle of the impressive Inner Bailey, the others being Loedgan's Hall and the Fortress Cathedral which soars above the very peak of the mountain upon which the Castle rests.

Madigan's Hall is a barracks for the Sun Blades and the Knights of the Castle. It can accommodate as many as 400 soldiers if needed, though only half that many are typically garrisoned here. It is a 3-story stone building with 3 corresponding tiers of battlements on the outer wall. It is a thick and imposing building designed first and foremost for the defence of the Inner Bailey and the Fortress Cathedral adjacent to it.

Today, despite the weather, the Inner Bailey courtyard is choked with people waiting to get into the Lord's Auction, getting wet and cold all the while. There are many familiar faces in the crowd; every company captain and many sergeants are there. There are a score of merchants waiting to get a crack at the competition, and dozens of dozens of other men and women from the Castle who have come to either just look, or to pay their gold to get in on the bidding.

And there is a significant presence of Knights and soldiers arrayed about the courtyard and the hall to ensure the peace of the affair. There is absolutely no tolerance for nonsense at this event.

Admittance to the Hall is 1 silver piece. This buys standing room in the galleries; a pair of choir-like lofts that look down over the nave of the hall. For an additional gold piece, one gains admittance to the nave and a numbered paddle with which to bid. There are dozens of men and women who have paid their gold for this privilege and opportunity this morning.

The large nave is divided into two main areas by a red rope. The two areas are both about the same size. One contains all of the bidders who stand or sit on stools, while the other side has an impromptu stage made of tables pushed together, upon which the auctioneers ply their precocious trade. Most of the furniture of the lower level has been stacked and shoved off to the side to accommodate the affair; there are stacks of beds and chests lining two long walls. Most of the tables have been sequestered as a make-shift stage for the block.

Before the auction starts, every bidder is admitted into the back area to wander the tables full of items for auction. Each bidder is given exactly 10 minutes to inspect the things he or she wishes to bid on, and it is just not enough time. The 20 or so bidders that are doing so at any one time move frantically from table to table; some take their time looking at a couple particular items, but most take a fast look and then move on. There are hundreds of items to be auctioned today, and they will certainly go for the best possible market price.

By 9 AM, every bidder has been registered and has been through the stock, and hopefully has a plan for the day's dealings. As the bells in the Fortress Cathedral begin to chime outside, the Auction is brought to order. There is a very brief prayer offered by Brother Darred that half the people in the cramped hall talk through or overtly ignore. Following this, though, the auctioneers are introduced. It is commonly known that the most energetic of the Candlekeep staff are chosen for the job of auctioneer, and this year there are three of them, one more interesting than the others.

The first is Brother Neddle. This man is well known to many of the people in the Hall. He does about one third of the evaluations and histories of artefacts that are brought to their tower. He is genuinely excited about his craft and the objects he represents. His specialty is pre-plague mundane objects, of which his knowledge seems inexhaustible. He will be auctioning off all of these items, which accounts for nearly half of the collection today.

The second is Brother Desden. Desden is new to the Emissaries, and his specialty is in arcane writings; scrolls, spellbooks and the like. There are dozens of these on the block today, and he will be auctioning them off with his brand of fast wit and slicing humour. At last year's auction, he was able to drive up prices of some items through clever insults of some of the bidders; a feat that had the rest of the brethren scratching their heads, and earned him a spot on the block today.

Finally, there is Sister Caylam. Caylam has been at the Castle for 2 years, and like Desden, delivered her first auction in the fall last year. Caylam has the twin perils of beauty and wit, and does not shy away from suggestive discourse in her auctioneering. She is able raise the price of an item past reason with a pout or the angle of her thigh. And if that isn't enough, she makes cryptic and facetious promises that, for the long history of red-blooded males, part men from their money. Last year she displayed no profound knowledge of the items she was auctioning off. This did not seem to affect the outcome.

With the auctioneers introduced by Brother Darred, the auction begins. Each auctioneer takes turns about 30 minutes long, selling off those things under his or her purview. It is clear to see who is interested in what; the bidding for the mundane stuff is as heated as the magical wares; there are many professional merchants here who are fighting tooth and nail for the best deal, and watching these men carry out their business inspires no small amount of awe and respect; bidding wars between two or more merchants are intense and appear to be chess matches that are given the added dimension of complexity by a berating auctioneer. There are cheering victories and cursing defeats with nearly every item. In fact, the very first auction was for a Netherese rug that nearly ended in a brawl. This auction set the tone for the day; the event is fast paced, spirited and rowdy.

After Desden is finished his first shift, he helps Sister Caylam to mount the auction block; she puts one knee on the table and slowly slithers her way up, her ululating curves barely contained by the short silver dress that has come from gods-only-know-what-brothel's-tailor. She is handed a glass of water which she makes a show of drinking in the most suggestive manner. When a bit of water dribbles onto her chin and then in between her generous and conspicuous cleavage, there are audible groans from the few women in the room, and cheers from many of the men. "This is a priestess of Ohgma?" one nearby bidder is heard to say. "Had I known, I'd have learned my letters better!"

The first item that is handed to her by one of the many assistants on the floor is a 6' long hickory staff that has been advertised as a Staff of the War Mage. Just the sight of this thing is enough to start laughs among the crowd, and the surprised and excited face she makes as she takes it in hand, both hands, is just the start. "Sorry boys, I think I'll take this one home myself."

It begins.
