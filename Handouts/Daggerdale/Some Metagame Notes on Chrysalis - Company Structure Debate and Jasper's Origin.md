Some Metagame Notes on Chrysalis — Company Structure Debate and Jasper's Origin
Scott Mclaren (as Jeska), Gus Miranda (as Zephrin/Jasper), Stephen Robertson (as Lucan), Todd Burry (as Ser Rowan/Mari), Les Blackwell (DM)
Email thread: "Some metagame notes on Chrysalis", 16-17 Dec 2023
Thread ID: 18c72f3f049eb450

A foundational 43-message group thread in which the players negotiate the structure of the Chrysalis adventuring company. What begins as Scott's practical loot-sharing proposal spirals into an intense metagame debate about ownership, motivation, and character fit — culminating in Gus scrapping his original character Zephrin and creating Jasper as a replacement. Les weighs in with economic data and lore. This is one of the most consequential pre-campaign threads: it establishes the company structure, reveals deep character motivations, and directly causes the creation of Jasper.

KEY LORE POINTS:

CHRYSALIS COMPANY STRUCTURE (FINAL):
- 50/50 split: half to members, half to company coffers
- Jeska is sole owner/CEO; other PCs are "members" receiving profit shares
- Members become shareholders upon resettlement of Thistle Ridge
- Contract term: 1 year or reclamation of Thistle Ridge
- Ferguson (Godwin Ferguson, nephew of Lord Chancellor Ferguson of Charity) offered 1,000gp investment + trade route
- Ferguson to receive 1/8 share of Jeska's company half

CHARACTER ORIGINS:
- Zephrin (Gus's original character): wanted money to buy books and return to Deepingdale; had no personal connection to Thistle Ridge; 6 months of development made him "overdeveloped" with "no real room for growth"
- Jasper was created specifically to replace Zephrin as a better fit for the campaign — 23-year-old Human Level 1 Wizard, "clean slate"
- Stephen's hint "A wizard whose name starts with JA?" suggests he noticed the Jasper/Jafar echo
- Lucan characterized as "a 16-year-old Sembian looking to get rich quick" who will "grow as we play... or die trying"
- Scott's vision: "I have no intention for this party to be honest, scrupulous or good.... except with each other"

METAGAME DYNAMICS:
- Scott (Scun) draws on experience from multiple campaigns for loot management
- Todd's "Conspiracy of Paper" reference (David Liss novel) — the financial intrigue angle
- Tina (Scott's wife) calls Stephen "Toast Robertson"
- The debate reveals the tension between player autonomy and party structure that drives much of the early campaign roleplay
