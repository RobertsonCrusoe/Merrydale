# Sun Blades
# Source: The Sieges Perilous wiki (backup dated 2010-02-21)

The Sun Blades is the name of the standing army in Daggerdale.  This army was created in 1410 by the Whitegates and the Church to replace the patchwork military in the dale at the time.  The Sun Blades are ostensibly aligned with the faith of Amaunator, but some members worship other, acceptable gods, such as Chauntea or Tyr.  

The Sun Blades have a nearly spotless history; each company is assigned a chaplain from the Church, in order to tend to the spiritual needs and moral fortitude of the soldiers, and this has kept them from any mischief normally associated with young soldiers.  All men in the dale are required to serve 2 years as a Sun Blade, in their 18th year.  New cohorts of trainees are all inducted into the army on Greengrass in their 18th year, when they all begin training together.  Women may volunteer to serve, but are not obliged to.  Currently, about 9% of the Sun Blades are women, including many officers.

Soldiers' families are paid 10 gold/tenday while the soldier doing his 2 years of mandatory service, and single men with no family are paid 5 gold/tenday, which is held in trust until their 2 year of service is completed.  The soldiers live something of a monastic life in their first two years, having everything provided for them by the army, and having little access to extra resources.  They spend all but 10 days of the year in service, but are granted 10 days off during Solstice Festival.  Solders are prohibited from partaking in nearly any kind of the debauchery, including drinking or use of drugs, fornication, gambling and singing of bawdy songs.  It is a harsh and disciplined service, but despite that, more than half of the soldiers stay on longer than their minimum service.

Those that stay on longer than the minimum of 2 years are promoted immediately.  Most are given the rank of Corporal, but some very promising and accomplished soldiers are given the rank of Sergeant.  Soldiers of rank get higher rates of pay (see below) which is paid to them at the end of every other month.  They are not permitted to carry more than 10g on their person while on duty, however.

There are currently over 2000 men in the Dale who are retired from the Sun Blades, who can be summoned up within 24-48 hours to respond to any serious threat to the Dale.  All retirees from the army keep their weapons and armour, but have an obligation to answer any call-to-arms made by a captain or higher-ranking officer in the army.

Ranks and Pay

• Footman - 10g/tenday or 5g for men with no family

• Corporal - 15g/tenday

• Sergeant - 20g/tenday

• Lieutenant - 25g/tenday

• Captain - 30g/tenday

• General - 40g/tenday 

Structure

• Squad - 11 footmen/corporals, 1 sergeant

• Platoon - 3 squads under the command of 1 lieutenant

• Company - 3 platoons (or more or less) under the command of 1 captain

• Army - every company, all under the command of the general

The general is answerable ultimately to Zenith Lord Argent Whitegate, though he has orders to follow the commands of Sunlord Blaze Whitegate.  The current general is a seasoned veteran of a dozen campaigns known as General Thunderstorm.  The general is a member of the 'other side' of the Whitegate Family, but he is on excellent and amiable terms with all the Whitegates, his superiors especially.  This has actually put him at odds with other members of the Thunderstorm Family, but he carries on his duties as though there is no conflict.

There are currently 5 companies in the army, and each has it's own rich and storied past.  They are all numbered and named, and troops are indoctrinated into the loyalty of their company just as they are Amaunator.  In fact, many men who didn't previously worship Amaunator before joining the army would probably put company before faith, though there is little conflict between the two.

The 5 active companies include the following;

• 1st - "Dalehearth Company" - 4 platoons - Usually deployed in the town and patrolling nearby.

• 2nd - "Mountaineers" - 5 platoons - Always deployed to Castle Radiance, and on patrol in the Desertsmouth Mountains.

• 4th - "Foresters" - 3 platoons - A specialized company that is deployed to regions of recent conflict.  All officers speak elven, and the company has an attached adviser from Myth Drannor who can requisition elven reinforcements.  

• 7th - "Bright Spears" -  10 platoons, 1 at each [farm].

• 8th - "Sundragoons" - 3 platoons, all mounted and expert fighters from horseback.

Religious Structure

The Sun Blades are indoctrinated into the Church immediately upon entering their training, though more than half are already faithful members of the Church before they turn 18.  All the training exercises and daily curriculum of the soldiers are based around the movements of the sun and the associated prayers and activities of the Church.  There are no exceptions made ever, and no soldier is exempt from daily prayers, no matter what god(s) he worshiped before entry into service.  Soldiers are permitted to worship other, acceptable, gods while in service, but must partake in all rituals associated with Amaunator.

About half of the squads deployed in the field have priests of Amaunator attached to them.  These are priests that are training to become full-fledged chaplains, and they are typically brave young men and women who are as ready to jump into a fight as they are to pray over the troops.

Each Platoon in the army has a Chaplain attached to it, and this chaplain travels with the platoon no matter where they go.  The chaplains are all trained for battle as well as the troops, and most of them are also seasoned veterans of many battles. Chaplains are responsible for the spiritual well being of all their troops, and meet with each and every soldier in his Platoon at least once a month.  They live side-by-side with the lieutenant of their platoon, and are typically on good terms with this officer.

Each Company has a Sunlord attached to it.  This Sunlord lives side-by-side with the Captain of the Company, and all are on good terms with this officer.  These Sunlords, who often began their careers in the Church after successful military careers in the army, are all hardened men who are experts in the tactical and strategic aspects of warfare.  They always wear their plate armour while on duty, and more resemble the paladins of the Order of the Lambent Shield than they do 'men of the cloth'.  The Sunlord of each company is the heart of each company, and the man responsible for much of the decision making for the activities of the company.  He and the captain discuss all orders and plans together, usually arriving at consensus easily.

The head of the religious structure of the army is Zenith Lord Whitestone, who has a close and effective relationship with the head of the army, General Thunderstorm.  

Though there seems to be a two-sided command structure to the army, with the religious chain of command and the military chain of command, there is in fact no conflict.  From the top, orders ultimately come from the Zenith Lord, and the Sunlords attached to the companies know that the General has received his orders from their immediate superior.  There aren't two sets of orders being handed down the chain; all orders include both aspects of the army, and conflicts between the two are unknown.

Weapons and Tactics

All soldiers are trained extensively in the use of the longsword, longspear, and longbow.  They are given these weapons when they join, and keep them their whole life, though many seasoned soldiers replace them with upgrades, or add their their personal armoury.  They are trained to fight in shield walls, as well as from ramparts and other defensive positions.  All soldiers are given chain mail armour and a light shield which they keep for life.  Their shields have the crest of the army on them.  The crest of the company is found on their cloak.

All captains and many officers are armed with a magical sword known as a Radiant Blade.  These weapons are forged up in Castle Radiance,and are awarded to exceptional officers with distinguished service, as a form of medal. Many corporals and other seasoned soldiers may acquire better armour throughout their career, and they are encouraged to make use of the best gear they can deploy.  Each captain has a set of gear that he has acquired throughout a long and accomplished career that looks completely different from the rest of his troops; some have magical axes or great blades, and most have unique armour.

Each Sun Blade platoon is trained to fight in concert with their chaplain, who assists in the fighting and healing of the men.  Soldiers will aid wounded comrades in returning to the rear to be healed, and are trained to call for healing or other divine aid when it is needed, and most are disciplined enough to call only when urgently needing help.

All Sun Blades receive some training in fighting from horseback, and those that show exceptional promise are mustered into the Sundragoons Company.  Soldiers who show a talent for scouting and fighting in the woods of ever-expanding Cormanthor are typically mustered into the Foresters, especially if they can speak elven.

Sun Blades don't act without orders, though they usually have detailed orders with contingencies laid out.  This means that they are a reliable, but somewhat static force that might be slow to react to some situations.  As such, adventurers often find need for their talents in a pinch to assist the Dale and the army.
