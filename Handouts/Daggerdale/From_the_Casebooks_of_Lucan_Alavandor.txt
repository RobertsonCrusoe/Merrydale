From the Casebooks of Lucan Alavandor: The Slave Orbs and the Disappearance of Verinius

7 Mirtul, 1483 DR

Reks was right, damn him. I've been asking the wrong questions. I think I’ve put it all together, though I’m not sure where it gets us. Let’s lay it out.

First, let’s assume the following premises.

1)  Reks’s statement that “Verinius opens portals” is true

2)  The Sharran Inversion: A primed orb is dark, whereas a spent orb glows

From Vessuvian's account:

3)  Vortellius was glowing when Vessuvian saw it in the Historic Wing

4)  Varros was glowing chaotically and is cracked/damaged

5)  Verinius's box was empty when Vessuvian visited

From Invictus:

6)  Verinius went missing “sometime before last fall” (that is, before autumn 1482)

7)  Invictus indicated that “If you find out the exact date, we might be able to sort out how it happened”

From Sister Estelmer's report:

8)  The orbs are “tied to” the Imperiosus Orbis

9)  All three orbs were captured at Liberty Hill on Kythorn 1, 1480

10) The orbs were being used to power rituals sustained by constant blood sacrifice up until the moment of capture

11) Hoetmer warned: “I would not transport it through any farm or Charity Hills”

12) Verinius was held at the Castle for nearly a year before being moved to the Historic Wing

13) Hoetmer died Flamerule 29, 1480 (two months after the orbs were captured)

From Jendwendle's testimony:

14) Morganna and the caravan captain were “put into the terrible terrible orb” in autumn 1482

15) Their throats were cut as part of this process

From my own direct observation at Thistle Ridge:

16) The orb Loki grabbed after Coventina was killed was inky black (fully charged)

17) Coventina was a priestess of Sharess, which is to say, a divine spellcaster

The Orbs:

Three Slave Orbs to the Imperiosus Orbis, captured at Liberty Hill after the Saints' Battle, Kythorn 1, 1480:

Vortellius – From Vessuvian's account of his meeting with Invictus: “This is Vortellius. One of three, and the least of them...This one was used by the Black Druid, Deadnettle, to empower an aberration to produce unnatural spawn of troll and dragon.” Currently in the Historic Wing. When Vessuvian saw it, “the glowing object inside cast a light on its bottom and on Invictus' glowering face.” Glowing means that its charge is spent.

Varros – Middling power. Vessuvian: “Varros is the name of this orb, and it is of middling power. It was surrendered to us after the interdiction of the changeling, Lotus, by an unlikely ally in a moment of unlikely generosity. It was damaged in the process...this orb glowed with a chaotic and oscillating frequency, and that it was cracked and some bands of brass riven.” The damage appears permanent. From Vessuvian’s description, it cannot hold a charge. This one is functionally neutralized.

Verinius – The greatest of the three. Invictus, showing its empty case to Vessuvian, said: “Verinius was in this box. The greatest of three. It was taken from the hands of Belladonna after she and Drake Sterling plummeted from the Table of the Sun, ending the Saints' Battle.” Reks maintains that this orb can open portals between planes. Missing since before autumn 1482.

From the intelligence briefing and what I saw at Thistle Ridge: The Slave Orbs are remote amplifiers for the Imperiosus Orbis. The IO remains in Netherese hands. The Slave Orbs channel its tremendous power to distant locations—reportedly up to a kilometer, though I suspect farther.

They require sacrifice to charge. Blood, death, pain. At Liberty Hill, Estelmer reports: “The Ritual of Corruption needed not only the bolstering might of the Slave Orbs, but a constant supply of fresh blood, which was grotesquely provided by the prisoners that resisted the transcendence. These unfortunate souls were eviscerated and then hung, still living but in unthinkable agony, from the walls of the Table of the Sun.”

THE TIMELINE

Kythorn 1, 1480 – Saints' Battle. All three Slave Orbs captured.

At the moment of capture, we can presume that Verinius was fully charged. Estelmer's report makes this clear: the orbs had been channeling the power of the IO for weeks, sustained by constant blood sacrifice. The Ritual of Corruption was still active when the White Cloaks stormed the Table of the Sun. Belladonna was literally wielding charged artifacts when Drake Sterling grabbed her and went over the side.

Kythorn 1, 1480 onwards – Verinius held at the Castle.

Invictus to Vessuvian: “It was unharmed in the descent, and was secured by Magistrate Harbaugh, after the arrest of the Priestess of Shar who had led the Dark Garden plot. I will disclose that Brother Hoetmer, whom you will have read and read about, warned me about removing Verinius immediately. 'I would not transport it through any farm or Charity Hills' he said in his commonly-cryptic portents. That was enough for me; we had a brace of knights come down from the Castle and move it overland with the guidance of the ranger, Tuugan Swift. It came to us by way of the Castle, where it stayed nearly a year, before being brought to the Wing to be interred with its mates.”

Nearly a year at the Castle. Hoetmer's warning was specific about the transport route—avoid farms and populated areas. They took precautions: knights, careful routing, extended delay at the Castle before final transport to the Wing.

Flamerule 29, 1480 – Hoetmer dies.

Estelmer: “As we worked daily on our intelligences, Phin became gradually more exhausted. On the 29th of Flamerule, just days before the trial of Belladonna, and the conclusion of his travails, he went to sleep and never woke up.”

Two months after the orbs were captured. Two years before Verinius vanished. The man who understood how the orbs worked and warned about their dangers died in his sleep.

Sometime in 1481 – Verinius moved to the Historic Wing.

After nearly a year at the Castle, it was finally transported to what Invictus believed was the most secure location in the dale. Triple-locked boxes, three “blessed” magistrates who can barely see, hear, or speak between them, with rotating codes and keys.

Sometime before Autumn 1482 – Verinius vanishes.

Invictus: “It went missing sometime before last fall, though the date we do not know. If you find out the exact date, we might be able to sort out how it happened...”

Autumn 1482 – The Drurginz Coster caravan is ambushed.

Jendwendle was held captive over the winter. When we rescued him, he raved that Morganna (a priestess of Tymora) and the caravan captain (likely a Zhent wizard) were “put into the terrible terrible orb.” Their throats were cut, their deaths deliberate. This wasn't robbery. This was ritual sacrifice.

Ches 1483 – Purple Worm attack at the arena. Intelligence briefing confirmed a Slave Orb was used to direct the assault. Almost certainly Verinius.

Mirtul 1483 – Coventina dies at Thistle Ridge.

Deceived into betraying Jasper, corrupted into committing an impure deed, then sacrificed. I saw what Loki grabbed: an orb, one foot in diameter, inky black. Fully charged.

THE QUESTION REKS POSED

During our wine-soaked dinner, I asked: “How did Verinius get out? And how did it get to Loki?”

Reks: “The first question is entirely erroneous, and you've already answered it, if you really think hard. Maybe when you return to your casebooks and put this into your logical written words, you'll see the truth of it.”

He's right. I have answered it.

Verinius didn't “get out.”

THE THEORY

Here's what I believe happened, though I cannot prove all of it:

Assumption A: The communication between the Imperiosus Orbis and the Slave Orbs is bidirectional.

The orbs don't just channel power FROM the IO. The IO can send commands TO the orbs. Loki—or whoever controls the IO—can remotely activate a Slave Orb from anywhere.

Assumption B: Verinius was still charged when it was stored in the Historic Wing.

The orb had been feeding on weeks of blood sacrifice at Liberty Hill. Even after a year at the Castle, even after being moved to the Wing, it retained enough charge to function. The orbs may degrade slowly, but they don't discharge overnight.

Hoetmer's warning supports this. “I would not transport it through any farm or Charity Hills.” If the orb was inert, he wouldn't care about the route. If it couldn't be remotely activated, he wouldn't care about the route. But if it was charged AND vulnerable to remote activation, then every populated area it passed through was a potential disaster—either the orb vanishes mid-transport, or something comes through from the other side while the portal is open.

That's why they kept it at the Castle for nearly a year. They were waiting for it to cool down. It apparently didn't.

What likely happened: Verinius portaled itself to the Shadowfell.

Verinius can open portals between planes, not teleport around within a plane. Sometime before autumn 1482, someone with access to the Imperiosus Orbis—Loki, most likely—sent the command. Verinius woke up, opened a portal to the Shadowfell, and went through.

It would have arrived in the Shadowfell at the same geographical location—the shadow-correspondence to the Cathedral in Dagger Falls. Loki (or an agent) would have been waiting there to retrieve it. Once in her possession, she could use it to return to the Material Plane wherever she chose.

The Logic:

IF A + B ARE TRUE: The date of disappearance doesn't matter much. It just tells us when Loki decided to recall her property. No one died at the Cathedral to charge it. No one breached the Historic Wing to steal it. The three magistrates never saw an intruder because there wasn't one. Verinius simply opened a door and stepped through.

IF A IS TRUE BUT NOT B: Then bidirectional communication exists, but the orb had lost its charge from Liberty Hill. A sacrifice would have been required near the Cathedral to recharge it before it could be remotely activated. Finding the exact date and cross-referencing deaths near the Cathedral becomes critical.

IF NOT A: Then someone had to be physically present in the Historic Wing to activate it. Inside job. Whether it was still charged or needed a fresh sacrifice becomes secondary—either way, someone with access had to betray their trust.

WHAT I BELIEVE

I believe A and B are both true.

Hoetmer's warning is the linchpin. It only makes sense if the orb was charged and vulnerable to remote activation. He knew. He understood how these things worked, and he tried to warn them. They listened—they took precautions, they held it at the Castle for a year, they routed the transport carefully.

But you can't secure something that doesn't need doors to leave.

Estelmer wrote: “the Slave Orbs of the Imperialis Orbis were taken from the Table of the Sun. These were indeed brought by the Bishop right to Phin and I for examination. They are terrible, powerful artefacts that are tied to the Imperialis Orbis.”

Tied to the IO. Not just powered by it. Tied. Bound. Connected.

And if the connection runs both ways, then every Slave Orb in existence is just a phone waiting for someone to call.

IMPLICATIONS

If I'm right, then Vortellius and Varros are secure—not because of Invictus's elaborate security measures, but because they can't portal themselves out. Varros is broken and can't hold a charge anyway. Vortellius could theoretically be remotely activated for use, but it would still need to be physically retrieved afterward.

Only Verinius was both powerful enough and mobile enough to escape on its own.

Reks said Loki will use Verinius to “go home” and find another agent. “She'll use it to go home and find another lackey. She won't risk her neck directly, especially so long as Monarch is above the ground; the Netherese hate and fear the eldar, and Monarch has given them every reason to account their fears warranted.”

She's already gone home once—when she first took Verinius to the Shadowfell. She can do it again whenever she wants, as long as the orb stays charged.

REMAINING QUESTIONS

1.  When exactly did Verinius vanish? Reks suggested finding the date might reveal how it happened. I think he was testing me to see if I'd figure out the date only matters if I'm wrong about B.

2.  Why did the Netherese wait until 1482 to recall it? They could have called it home at any point after capture. Why wait two years?

3.  How many times can an orb be used before it needs recharging? Verinius was charged at Liberty Hill (1480), possibly used to escape (1482), used for the Purple Worm attack (1483), and recharged with Coventina's death (1483) before opening the portal to the Shadowfell. That's at least three major uses on one charge, possibly four.

4.  What do the 'Alavandors' and 'Clarendals' have to do with any of this? Reks put both names in air quotes. “'Alavandors' and 'Clarendals'?! What ho, my chum, but you've held something back, haven't you? Well, I won't press, because I'm the one singing tonight, but it will be your turn next time, if you live to profit by my loose tongue.”

CONCLUSION

The question was never “how did Verinius get out?”

The question is: “How do we fight an enemy whose weapons come when called, who can project power across hundreds of miles, and who only need to whisper to their stolen toys to make them walk back home?”

I don't have an answer yet.

But I'm working on it.

—L.A.

Verinius as portal

Verinius likely summoned the Soul of Corruption from the Shadowfell. Reks confirmed "Verinius alone can open portals." The Soul was "summoned" (Estelmer's word—it came from elsewhere). Reks also stated Belladonna opened the Shadowdam (a Shadowfell rift in the Dagger Hills), proving portal capability was employed during this period. The Sharran-Shadowfell connection and Netherese artifacts' shadow-plane associations point to Soul’s origins in the Shadowfell. Reks attested that Verinius was linked to the planar rent guarded by the Shadowdam.

Bidirectionality

The orbs are "tied to" the Imperiosus Orbis (Estelmer). Invictus interpreted Hoetmer's warning—"I would not transport it through any farm or Charity Hills"—as concern about theft or the orb falling into wrong hands. But what if Hoetmer feared what the orb itself could do? Not that it might be stolen, but that it might activate on its own—or be activated remotely. A "tie" to the IO implies connection, not just one-way power flow. If the IO can send power through the Slave Orbs, it might send commands as well. Remote activation would explain why the transport route mattered: a charged orb passing through populated areas could open a portal at any moment if triggered from afar. Hoetmer wasn't worried about bandits stealing it. He was worried it might open a door to the Shadowfell in the middle of someone's farm.

Charging:

The orbs are glass spheres banded with brass around the circumference. When dark and viewed perpendicular to the band, they resemble a new moon—Shar's holy symbol. This is the "Sharran Inversion": charged orbs are dark, spent orbs glow. Invictus confirmed this. When Lucan saw Verinius after Coventina's death (a priestess of Sharess, a divine spellcaster), the orb was inky black—fully charged. Jendwendle's account supports this: Morganna (priestess of Tymora) and the caravan captain (likely a mage) had their throats cut and were "put into the terrible terrible orb"—spellcaster blood charges the orbs. Vessuvian's report confirms Vortellius glows (spent) and Varros glows chaotically (broken, cannot hold charge).

Verinius was charged when captured at Liberty Hill. The orbs had been powering weeks-long rituals sustained by constant blood sacrifice—the Whitegates and others eviscerated and hung from the Table of the Sun, their blood dripping to feed the Ritual of Corruption. Belladonna was wielding charged artifacts when Drake Sterling took her down. Hoetmer's warning about avoiding populated transport routes is best explained if the orb was still hot and could be remotely activated—though other explanations are possible. The year-long hold at the Castle suggests they were waiting for the charge to dissipate naturally. When it didn't, they moved it to the Historic Wing—still charged, still dangerous.

Conclusion

If all three premises are true, then Verinius could have portaled itself out of the Historic Wing when remotely activated. Sometime before autumn 1482, someone with access to the Imperiosus Orbis—almost certainly Loki—sent an activation command through the "tie" between the IO and Verinius. The still-charged orb responded by opening a portal to the Shadowfell and passing through it. The orb would have arrived in the Shadowfell at the same geographical location—the shadow-correspondence to the Cathedral in Dagger Falls—where Loki (or an agent) was waiting to retrieve it. No theft. No forced entry. No sacrifice needed at the Cathedral. The three "blessed" magistrates saw nothing because there was nothing to see: just a portal opening inside a locked box, and the orb going through. This explains why the box was empty with no sign of intrusion. It explains why finding the exact date matters less than Invictus thinks—the date only tells us when Loki pushed the button, not how the mechanism worked. And it explains why Hoetmer warned against populated transport routes: he understood that a charged orb vulnerable to remote activation could open a Shadowfell portal anywhere, anytime, putting farms and villages at risk of either losing the orb or having something come through from the other side.

Questions Remaining:

1.  Was Verinius glowing while under observation in the Historic Wing? If it was glowing (spent), the theory fails and we need another explanation. Could it have been charged while in the box through a nearby bloodletting/sacrifice?

2.  Why wait until autumn 1482 to recall it? Two years after capture. What changed?

3.  When was Verinius last confirmed to be in the box? The gap between last verification and Vessuvian's discovery of the empty box defines the window of disappearance.

4.  What else did the Estelmer-Hoetmer report contain? Reks claims never to have seen it, yet he knows details about the orbs' capabilities, Belladonna's use of them, and their current status. How? What's in that report that we haven't seen?

5.  Who else knows about the orbs' capabilities? If Reks knows without seeing the report, who told him? Invictus? Or does he have another source?

One final consideration: What does this theory tell us about the fate of Phinneas Hoetmer?
Hoetmer died Flamerule 29, 1480—two months after capturing three charged Slave Orbs "tied to" the Imperiosus Orbis, two years before Verinius vanished. He was examining the orbs with Estelmer. He understood enough to warn against transporting Verinius through populated areas—suggesting he grasped that a charged orb vulnerable to remote activation through its IO connection posed dangers others didn't see. Estelmer says he "went to sleep and never woke up," exhausted from profound Aetherial journeying. He and Estelmer composed a report on the Slave Orbs that has yet to surface. Reks claims never to have seen it, yet somehow knows details about the orbs' natures and capabilities. Hoetmer's death left Invictus without expert guidance. Was his death natural exhaustion, or was he silenced because he knew too much about vulnerabilities someone wanted kept secret?
