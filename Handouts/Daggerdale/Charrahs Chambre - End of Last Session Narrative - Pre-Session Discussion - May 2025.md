Source: Message 196a212ad40fbc3b

"Mirtul 1, 1483 DR – Charrah's Chambre

The first thing you noticed as you mounted the landing to the third floor
of the Town Hall is that the sounds and smells of the building changed
dramatically. While you could still hear the battle raging at the doors
two floors down, and the hue and cry from the growing crowd of goblins in
the court outside, sounds become noticeably muffled as soon as you made the
landing. As you drew nearer the main doors to the central room, it became
almost entirely silent, until you threw open the doors.

The smell of the place changed, too. Though the first floor, which had
become a camp for a large group of goblin guards, was cleaner than most of
the settlement, it still stunk of goblin bodies and trash, if not outright
excrement. And it was crawling with rats, lice and meaner things besides.
The second floor was much cleaner than the first, and indeed, looked as
though the floors had been swept, and garbage taken out. The third floor
has been cleaned with some diligence, and the smell of goblin bodies
transitions into a very earthy, pungent odour that after a moment you liken
to a swamp. It isn't offensive but does seem out of place.

Before throwing open the doors, you take the chance to peek out a window or
arrow slit into the court below, and you can see that it has veritably
filled with goblins of all types; soldiers, females and children alike are
thronging in the open area, brandishing weapons, torches, and improvised
tools. They are making a great cacophony, with a mixture of screams and
chants, and you can distinctly hear them calling for the death and worse of
Jeska Monarch, with the most loathsome oaths and threats imaginable.

Throwing open the doors to the central room, Charrah's Chambre, refocuses
your senses in an instant. The smell of the swamp rolls out of the room,
and you can now hear various noises from inside that were completely absent
a second before; low moans and groans from nearly a dozen men and women in
cages much too small for them, the growling and grunting of an ogre-sized
goblin on the opposite side of the room, encouraged by the various soldiers
standing nearby, and of course, the voice of your enemy, whose presence
upon a wooden dais in the middle of the chambre dominates your attention.

'I'm glad you've come, Chrysalis' she begins. Her voice is slow and
confident and has an alluring mix of mature gravelly earthiness and smoky
seduction. She stands with black-gloved hands resting on voluptuous hips
that sway slowly while she addresses you. Her most conspicuous features
are her curves, which are ample and accentuated by an old black dress that
clings and reveals in all the right places. Her form nearly distracts from
her other dominant features; a slightly off-green hue, stark white hair,
very long and pointed ears, and mismatched eyes; one of the clear-grey that
you've seen in a couple of other changelings in the settlement, the other a
bright, intelligent and unmistakably blue.

She steps off the dais and approaches the group, walking in and among you
nonchalantly, with a brazen confidence that is disarming. To one side,
where she must sit to rest, are comfortable furnishings worthy of a lady of
a grand estate, with books, inks and papers, a sewing loom, personal
items and curiosities, and a polished makeup table. Everywhere you look
there are personal touches and items of culture, and the room is teeming
with trinkets, old world art, and exotic things.

There is also, unmistakably, a fey beauty in here who looks identical to
the one whom Jasper Hawkford has been courting from the Limestone Court. She
kneels by the side of the dais, eyes cast down. Her demeanor is one of
total servitude.

Charrah - whose human name is Lobelia, the name you've heard before - talks
to you at length. She is not hostile, and in fact seems thrilled that you
have fought your way through her defenses all the way up to the top of the
building to reach her, as though she has been waiting for you for a very
long time.

She seems to have intimate knowledge about each of you, and is particularly
attentive to Jasper, who she congratulates on 'uncovering the trail that
others have tried to bury'. She reveals that 'so many of the answers you
seek can be found beneath your very feet, in the old places where the
waters used to flow.'

On your history and lineage, she has this to say:

'I know far more about your bloodline than you do, Jasper Hawkford. I've
watched your family for generations. The Hawkfords of Merrydale - yes, I
know where your people hail from - have always been touched by something
old. Your grandmother had the sight, did she not? And her mother before
her. The gift skips, you see. It always skips. But it never truly leaves.'

'The Alavandors thought themselves clever, changing names and fleeing
Daerlun. But blood remembers what the mind tries to forget. You carry
threads of the same weave that built this place - and destroyed it.'

On the nature of the haunting and the girl ghosts, she says:

'Those poor girls... their spirits are bound to the Tenebrius Ostia - the
Shadow Gates. They were never meant to be sacrifices. They were meant to be
keys. My mother's ritual required pure souls who had committed impure acts
- and so the girls were tricked into cruelty against each other. Children
can be so easily turned, can't they? A whispered dare, a cruel game... and
suddenly innocent hands have done terrible things.'

'If you want to free them, you'll need to understand what binds them. The
Ostia aren't just doorways - they're anchors. Destroy the anchors without
understanding the mechanism, and you'll tear those children's souls apart.
But find the right resonance, the right counter-frequency... you might just
give them peace.'

On the wider conflict, she offers a troubling perspective:

'You think I'm the villain here? I'm the compromise. My mother Palus
wanted to raze Dagger Falls to the ground and open a permanent highway to
the Shadowfell. The Netherese wanted to use this valley as a breeding
ground for their dying bloodlines. The hobgoblins just wanted gold and
plunder. I've been holding all of these interests in check for years,
playing them against each other while trying to preserve something of what
this place once was.'

'Ask yourself - who benefits if I fall? Not the people in those cages - I
keep them alive because they're useful, yes, but also because wholesale
slaughter draws attention I can't afford. Not the settlers in the Dale -
without someone managing the goblin tribes, they'd pour out of these hills
like a plague. And certainly not you - because the moment I'm gone, the
things beneath this ridge will be free to pursue their own agenda without a
mediator.'

'But I'll make you a deal, Chrysalis. You want Thistle Ridge? Take it. I'm
tired of this place. I'm tired of managing ungrateful goblins and paranoid
Zhentarim and scheming Netherese who think they own everything their shadow
touches. Give me safe passage south - just me, and perhaps a few personal
effects - and I'll tell you everything. Every secret. Every trap. Every
hidden passage and ward that protects the dungeons below. I'll even tell
you where the real Coventina is.'

She pauses, letting that last sentence hang in the air.

'Yes. The real one.'"

PART 11: GUS'S PRE-SESSION TACTICAL PLAN AND RULES QUESTIONS — GUS MIRANDA (9 May 2025)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 196b6b8bdd3d5457

"Ok. So if the DM will permit, I'd like to retcon a few actions that Jasper
would have taken when walking up to the door of Charrah's throne room. Gus
was too tired at that point to remember to do this correctly.

(And maybe some of these I did but have since forgotten about it.)

1. He would have cast his innate Detect Magic as soon as he realized there
was something odd with the sound on the floor.

2. I wanted to roll Arcana (+11) and Occultism (+10) and use my Recognize
Spell Feat to try to identify the effect of that silence when we are in
the hallway.

3. Furthermore, I wanted to use the same skills and feat to identify
whether my spellcasting will be hampered by that effect while standing in
this hallway. Casting the Detect Magic alone might not be enough, since it
could return a false negative. I'll also cast a quick Light cantrip to test
the silence if my Arcana and Occultism are indeterminate.

4. While Charrah is speaking I want to Recall Knowledge (Perception +8) on
Coventina to see if there are any 'tells' that might lead me to believe
that she is not the same Coventina I've been cavorting with, or that she
might be some sort of hag-in-disguise. Is she looking at me, and does her
expression show recognition of me? Are her boobs bigger or smaller than I
remember?

5. I also want to use my Arcana (+11) to discern any illusions that we
might be seeing in the room. I know that seeing through illusions typically
requires a Seek action using Perception, but if you allow it, I want to
use my Arcana since I'm a wizard.

6. I also want to use my Arcana (+11) and Recognize Spell feat to figure
out if there are any nefarious effects from that encroaching darkness.

I am fine with taking negatives, or for the DC to be high, because Jasper
is demoralized. I can also accept that because of that mental state, Jasper
might not have the wherewithal to do these actions while Charrah speaks.


Now for a DM ruling:

Invisibility: the wording of 'hostile action' in the rules is very vague.
The consensus online seems to be that a 'hostile action' is one that causes
the enemy (or PC) to roll a die for a save, or it is blatantly an attack.

An example is an Invisible Jasper casting Grease. If an Invisible Jasper
casts Grease beneath Charrah, or on Charrah's weapon, this is a hostile
action because it will cause Charrah to roll a Reflex save directly. But if
Jasper casts Grease on the floor, and Charrah happens to walk on the Grease
and fall, this is NOT a hostile action because Charrah walked onto the
Grease out of her own accord.

An Invisible Jasper casting a Sleep spell on a goblin is a hostile action
because the goblin has to roll a save. But an Invisible Jasper casting Mist
on an area where goblin is standing is not a hostile action, because the
goblin is not rolling a die to save. He is simply having his vision
naturally obscured by a mist.

Similarly, an invisible Jasper summoning a creature does not reveal him as
the summoning spell itself is not a hostile action. However, the grey area
is if the summoned creature attacks the enemy because it was commanded to do
so by Jasper. I'd argue that if Jasper commands the creature to do something
hostile, it is then a hostile action that reveals him. But if Jasper just
commands the creature to stand beside an enemy and flank with Ser Rowan,
this does not reveal Jasper as the creature is not causing the enemy to roll
a die to defend itself.

Another grey area is an invisible Jasper trying to unlock a cage door. Is
that a hostile action? If the person inside the cage wants to be freed,
maybe it's not, but if the person in the cage wants to remain in the cage,
maybe it is?

I just think that as a wizard that has the invisibility spell, Jasper
should know what the limitations of that spell actually are.

I am all for the 'try it and find out' method for sorcerers and other
lesser spellcasters, but a wizard is supposed to know these things, and
knowing these things very well is a cornerstone of Jasper's build as a
prodigy.

Now that I have tipped my hand, I just want to know what to expect from
the rules."
