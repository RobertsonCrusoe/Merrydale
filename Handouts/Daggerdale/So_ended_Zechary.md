So ended Zechary’s brief stay in Waterdeep. Lady Fortune would see him back to its many-splendored streets, but for a time Zeke passed into the shadow of her dark sister Beshaba, who had designs of her own for the hapless pirate.

Despite Zeke’s colourful protestations and some initial fistwork which ended when the Cyclops casually knocked him to the ground with a gentle swipe of its massive arm, Coppercuff and Algernon – as his captors introduced themselves – were very civil with their charge. “Her Ladyship was clear, my salty friend,” said the former, his mechanical hand adjusting the locking mechanism of a manacle brace with which the pirate’s hands were secured behind his back and locked to a strange chain fastened tightly around Zeke’s waist. “She wants you in top condition – and we aim to please.” His mammoth partner, the Cyclops Algernon, smiled at Zeke, revealing a perfect set of what appeared to be mithril teeth inset with gleaming jewels. “No more trouble, now, oldster.” The monster cracked the giant knuckles of its immaculately manicured hands and then muttered an eldritch-sounding phrase, and Zeke heard – or rather, failed to hear – himself and the rest of the world fall silent.

Zeke’s mind, reeling with grog and the smoke of the pub from which he had been so rudely ejected, searched desperately for some memory. He was sure he had never seen these two characters before, and doubly sure that he didn’t know any ladies
