This is the second-largest building in the Outer Bailey.  It is 80’ long by 40’ wide.  It is the original stone stables, built in the early years of the construction of the Castle.  

A pair of barn doors at either end of the building are connected by a 10’ wide thoroughfare.  On either side of the thoroughfare are the large stalls.  Each stall has three stone walls, and a floor area of 14’ x 11’.  

The stalls are rented from the Castle for 5 gp/tenday.  Just about any kind of good that is pertinent to adventuring and warfare have come through these stalls, and it is no secret that Lord Whitegate allows merchants to come here to sell products that other merchants in more permanent establishments in the Castle already sell, in order to foster healthy competition.

It is in these stalls that the usual day-to-day supplies change hands.  There are usually farmers from the dale up here selling fresh fruit and vegetables, merchants with smoked meats, cheeses, grains and other consumables, tradesmen selling their manufactured goods such as boxes, barrels, tools, clothes, shoes, and so on.

Just about any common item and supply can be found in here during the summer months, for about the price listed in the PHB.

Some deals can be had as well; there is a stall that has been rented for 2 years now by a collective of hunters and trappers, who sell pelts of every variety imaginable for very good prices.  As well, a skilled cooper has been here for years who makes barrels and other wooden containers very inexpensively.  He claims that he could make more money in town or in a city, but he likes the mountain climate and the stories that the men up here tell.
