DDC – 1481 - 2: Excerpts from the Yellow Abbeye Booke of Measures and Surveyes 
1480 Edition - Dwimmerholme 
Notes from GFD: 
I have included this interesting excerpt, as it relates to the size and nature of Dwimmerholme on the eve of their problems both in 1380 and 1381. For the sake of keeping my own notes in order, I have included it herein as DDC-1481-2, as it was tucked into a pile of correspondence that Administrator Reks provided me regarding the Sons of Bel, and the events of 1481. 

It also illustrates the level of detail and bureaucratic aptitude that the Yellow Abbey is capable of. The 'Booke of Measures and Surveyes' is completed every year. 
-GFD 
Population: 333 
Non-Humans: 3 of partial elven descent, 6 dwarves (of one family) 
Governance 
Mayor Darius Winters, 4th Co. Sun Blades Foresters (retired), aged 44, council of 5 elders elected biannually. 

Magistrate Ankger Willems (c.f. Yellow Abbeye Grande Accounting of the Clergy, 1480 Edition), aged 51, 3 clerks in Rotation 
Military 
7th Sun Blade Bright Spears, 3rd Platoon; bolstered Platoon of 5 Squads, under command of Lt. 
Kesh Oakdale, aged 30. 
Militia under-strength; reported at only 35 men, 30 of which are retired from the Sun Blades 
Real Holdings 
Class IV fortification, barracks for 150 
Fortified holdings; 147 acres inside class IV perimeter, accounting for all inhabitants and ¾ of livestock 

Cultivated holdings; 664 acres, 1/3rd each in rotation of Wheat, Barley, fallow. 100 acres of private gardens with all known varieties of vegetable, fruits and herbs. 
Holdings 
Of Livestock; 46 dairy cows, 39 goats, 88 swine, 145 chickens, 140 (or so) rabbits, 43 sheep, 18 plough-oxen 

Of Common Wealth; town hall furnishings worth 955 gold (itemized in Appendix M/S-D4), common market accoutrements worth 245 gold, 

Of Ecclesiastic Wealth; chapel furnishings and accoutrement worth 2045 gold (itemized in Appendix M/S-DA4). 

Of Military Stores; 200 long spears, 30 cross bows, 2000 bolts, 200 wooden shields, 40 pikes, 26 maces, 24 long swords, 2 large military hammers. 

Of Mounts; 44 horses privately owned, 44 horses assigned to the garrison, 12 horses commonly owned. 
Tax Revenue for 1479 
Coin; 2980 gold to the State Coffers, 2980 to the Ecclesiastic Coffers. 
Kind; 10,000 measures each of Barley and Wheat, 4 dairy cows, 16 swine, 33 chickens, 35 rabbits, 5 sheep, in roughly equal measure to both coffers. 
Taxable Revenues for 1480 
Possibly 3330 gold each for the state and the church. Suggest increasing taxes for demihumans. 
Locations and People of Interest 
Locations 
The Old Stones – ½ mile south east of the village, just inside the forest at the edge of the fields. A place of Chauntean worship 

The Cathedral – 60' x 100' stone building; centre of town, extending to eastern wall (access from eastern edge of square) 

The Castle – 150' x 150' stone building, 2 stories, 3 story towers at northern edge which extends to the northern wall (access from the northern edge of the square) 
Amber Hart Inn – See map details; access from western edge of the square 
The Market – barn-like building 60' x 100' on the southern edge of the square; many businesses inside 

Bronze Hammers – small smithy, named for the dwarven family that owns and operates it 
Daily Bread – large bakery operated by part of the Weaver family 
Butcher's – busy, clean, unremarkable butcher run by a family with the same name. 

Barns – western wall is lined with 3 large barns where livestock are brought in most nights. 
People 
Mayor Darius Winters – Retired veteran of the 4th Company Sun Blades – Foresters. Young, capable. 
Magistrate Renfrew – new, young magistrate. 
Andrea Stuard – proprietor of the Amber Hart Inn 
Cultivar Marissa Dale – priestess of Chauntea, and aunt to some missing villagers 

Lt. Kesh Oakdale – Lt of the local platoon of Sun Blades. Young, capable, wracked with guilt/grief. 

Roger Dunland – Son of the last mayor, and one of the ring leaders of a failed cult of Shar. Reformed but now solitary.
