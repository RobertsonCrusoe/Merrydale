[PC Attribution: Rufus Larrigan (Stephen Robertson)]
Maturin's speech for the recovery of this letter.

\"The letter you keep for our friend Rufus may yet bear import to our mission. Like you, I grieve for his loss, and, I think, would not see his sacrifice made in vain. For though he died nobly, a hero, yea even a martyr, his cause is not yet won.  

The Brethren of Ilmater have long been friends and colleagues of Selûne's Children. You, Father, more than most should realize this. Beneath this very temple, are there not Ilmatari brothers treating the lunatics of Waterdeep - poor souls whose minds ventured too close to the Mother of the Night Sky, and lost their reason? Does Ilmater not also bear the suffering of those afflicted with lycanthropy until Selûne should take them to Her bosom? Is not Ilmater also the sworn enemy of Muir'Quaiggoth, who even now whispers poison into the spirits of Her children? Look upon this cloak, and tell me if I am worthy.

I pledge to you that though I knew Brother Rufus but briefly, his fight was noble, and it is mine. He fought, as I still fight, to end murder, to end tyranny, to end fear itself. This tear you see on my face is for the suffering he bore. This tear I weep openly, and without cease. I weep for Rufus, as I weep for all the Children of Selûne brought low by her enemies. I ask you in faith, to trust in Ilmater, and to trust in me.\"
