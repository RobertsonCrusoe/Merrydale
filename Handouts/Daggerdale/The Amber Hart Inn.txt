Narrative about the Inn from the autumn of 1481 DR;

800px|right

The Hart is decorated in the Chauntean seasonal decor as well; every wall and banister is hung with the leaves and other golden, brown, and orange organic crafts that you have seen all over the village.  There are glowing jack-o-lanterns on just about every table and the end of the bar.  As well, the stage has been turned into something of a makeshift shrine; there are sixteen dolls made of sticks and leaves strung together on the walls flanking the stage; they are miniatures, from a 1’ to 2’ tall, of families; children, husbands and wives.  There are sixteen of them in total, in three clusters.  A few villagers are in here with candles in their hands, praying quietly while tears streak down their faces.

The proprietor of this inn is Andrea Stuard.
