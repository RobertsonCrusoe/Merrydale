Excerpt from the Yellow Abbey Booke of Surveys and Measures, 1440 DR Edition—Thistle Ridge Farm

Preamble From Magistrate Blaze Whitegate

I have been to Thistle Ridge many times in my capacity as a young Magistrate. This settlement is strategically important; it commands both the Tesh River and the Moonsea Road from an unassailable limestone perch within bowshot of both, and it is only about 10 miles as the crow flies from Dagger Falls; the perfect distance to send near-instant communications and for next-day reinforcement if needed.

However, the origin of the settlement has nothing to do with trade, though this did attract some later migrants, which I'll discuss shortly. The original settlement dates to before the 13th century, though none now living know when exactly. The Thistle Family, who, as I understand it, take their name from the Ridge and not the other way 'round, have lived here for more than a century. They are private people who both give and expect respect at arm's length. The family have been gardeners (I don't think I'd call them 'farmers') and expert herbalists for as long as anyone can remember, and their skill at healing obscure ailments (both real and imagined) is legendary, bordering on heretical. They are a peaceful people, however, and though rarely seen in the Falls, they get into no quarrels when they are.

The Thistles follow the Ancient Faith, though they do so respectfully with regard to their Chauntean and Amaunitori countrymen, and they do not proselytize. Theirs is a Hereditary Faith, and we shall leave it at that for now. It should be noted that the Chaunteans insist that the settlement is upon 'sacred' ground, and the evidence of that can be seen in the productivity of the small gardens there, the fruit from their trees, the quality of their medicines, and indeed, their numerous healthy children.

In 1408, migrants from Sembia were settled in Thistle Ridge. These migrants are mostly from two Houses of Sembia and their attendants, namely the Alavandor and Clarandal Families. The former were successful merchants in their homeland, while the latter were of a highly disciplined military order. The previous mayor was from the Alavandor Family, namely Andarl Alavandor. After an unexpected death hunting, his younger sister, Senthia (married to a Clarandal), took over mayorship. She is slated to fill the role until Sheildmeet.

The Sembians started a completely new era at Thistle Ridge. They brought industry and commerce, as well as a new degree of security and much-needed improvements to agriculture and the overall subjugation of the landscape. They have established trading relationships with neighbours, as well as a reputation for some of the finest products in the dales (the Dale Burgundy Apple, Goldwin Wines, and the finest medicines, just to name a few).

They also attracted the strange Alavaxi people, Chessentans, or so I'm told. The Alavaxi make the curved blades that some of our military orders now favour, as well as other fine steel products. Obvious foreigners, they have brought some small discord to the Dale, though not in Thistle Ridge Farm, where they have been welcomed by the immigrants before them (the Sembians).

Thistle Ridge is an experiment in religious plurality and mixed cultural traditions that we are sure to keep an eye on in the future.

Population: 444
Non-Humans: 12 halflings (two families)

Governance

This settlement is run by a small council made up of a Mayor who is chosen by the Merchants' Guild and a non-secular leader chosen by the rest of the adults on the farm by vote. Both are elected every Shieldmeet unless misadventure requires it sooner.

Mayor Senthia Clarandal, sister of the previous Mayor, Andarl Alavandor, who was killed in a hunting accident in the northern reaches of the farm in 1439.

Nadia Thistle, Priestess of The Ancient Faith (Silvanus), matriarch of the Thistle family, is the current non-secular leader of the farm.

There is no Magistrate permanently stationed here, though there ought to be. I myself (Blaze Whitegate) administer to the farm through a clerk of local birth, one Daphne Dale (an unmarried girl).

Military

7th Company Sun Blade Bright Spears, 4th Platoon; understrength Platoon of 3 Squads, under the command of Lt. Tergus Clarandal, aged 39. Lt. Clarandal is an accomplished fighter of several campaigns, having risen to Sgt in the 2nd Company, Mountaineers, over five Terms of Service.

The robust militia is led by Cpt Claudius Clarandal of the 3rd Company, Mountaineers (Sgt, retired). The militia is one of the largest per capita, at 106 capable men and women, who undergo some of the best training in the dale. Claudius, like his brother, did five Terms of Service.

Real Holdings

Class II fortification, with Class IV fortified central edifice ('Town Hall').
Barracks for 100 ('Garrison,' Class III fortified).
Barracks for another 100 ('The Bastion,' Class IV fortified).
Fortified holdings: 19 acres inside Class II perimeter, accounting for 4/5 of inhabitants and ¾ of livestock.

Cultivated holdings: 126 acres, 1/3rd each in rotation of Wheat, Barley, and fallow. Forty-four acres of private gardens with all known varieties of vegetables, fruits and herbs. Of note is 12 acres known as the 'Apple Glade,' about 1 ½ miles north northeast of the town.

Holdings

Of Livestock: 16 dairy cows, 22 goats, 0 swine (they keep none apurpose), 220 chickens, 70 (or so) rabbits, 0 sheep (they keep none apurpose, having a trade arrangement for Charity Loom Wool), 12 plough-oxen.

Of Common Wealth: Town hall furnishings worth 2,175 gold (itemized in Appendix M/S-D7) and common market accoutrements worth 1,225 gold.

Of Ecclesiastic Wealth: Chapel furnishings and accoutrements worth 150 gold (itemized in Appendix M/S-DA7). Chapel furnishings in the Shrine of Silvanus not assessed.

Of Military Stores: 100 long spears, 100 long bows, 5,000 arrows, 100 wooden shields, 100 pikes, 24 maces, 24 long swords, and 24 large military hammers.

Of Mounts: 61 horses privately owned, 24 horses assigned to the garrison, and 4 horses commonly owned.

Tax Revenue for 1479
Coin: 4,000 gold to the State Coffers, 2,000 to the Ecclesiastic Coffers.
Kind: 14,000 measures each of Barley and Wheat, 6 dairy cows, 44 chickens, and 50 rabbits, in roughly equal measure to both coffers.

Taxable Revenues for 1480
Possibly 4,250 gold each for the state and 2,200 for the church. Suggest increasing taxes for demihumans.

Locations and People of Interest

Locations

Caravaner's Court—a large clearing at the edge of the River Tesh, straddling the Moonsea Road. This is where travellers can first request entry to the town or, more often, will set up tents in the open market to do business with the locals, who will come down from the ridge-top settlement. 'The Court' (as the locals call it, somewhat imprudently) is a busy pace, with permanent barns and paddocks for travellers and a few notable permanent businesses, namely;

Whistle's House—a public house run by the halfling of the same name. Whistle also owns the large double barn next to the public house, which he rents out for the stabling of mounts and a dry place to sleep for travellers.

The Broken Spoke—a small wagonwright capable of fixing wheels, barrels, and crates, and providing shoeing for mounts.

Milly's Mill—a small but very busy flower mill on the river. Milly (the halfling wife of Whistle) grinds most of the flour for the settlement, and her establishment provides much of the traffic that benefits traders set up in the Court.

The Fish Market—not really named this, but rather just called that, this is a small collection of tents and huts where the wives of a few local fishermen sell the day's catch. Usually, a good supply of fresh pygmy afanc, brownfin, bass, sunfish, trout and eels to be found.

All Faiths Shrine—found at the edge of Caravaner's Court. This small shrine is built of granite that is not local to the region and has been there since the earliest known records. What faith it was originally built for is unclear, and there are no permanent idols or other embellishments that suggest one god or another. Locals steer clear of it, but some travellers may use it to venerate their odd foreign gods.

Old Thistle Ridge Farm (Eastern Bailey)—the smaller of two baileys in the fortified portion of the farm, this area is where the Thistle and other older families live. The houses are a little smaller and lower than the newer, Sembian-influenced bailey beside it. This part of the farm is choked by lush gardens of every variety and is quite delightful to walk through.

Oak and Shield (Inn - Old Thistle Ridge Farm)—this is the oldest public establishment in the area. Run by the Sweetberry family, who have been here for generations, this is a quiet and respectable inn that has clean beds, wonderful food, and excellent stabling for mounts and other animals. The proprietress is Janus Sweetberry, widow and mother of a dozen (I think, or more) very healthy children who run the place with courtesy and attention to detail.

Shrine to Silvanus (Old Thistle Ridge Farm)—One can't miss the giant Cormanthor Oak that grows outside this small wooden shrine. The oak itself is the real centrepiece for the worship of Silvanus here, with the small building serving to hold some accoutrements specific to their faith, such as it is.

The Sembian Bailey (Western Bailey)—This is the more substantial of the two baileys, holding most of the commerce and people of the settlement. Although a bit more overgrown than other settlements, the roads are mostly cobbled with well-fitted limestone. There are vibrant gardens beside most buildings, and berries seem veritably to spring from the pavers themselves when not properly cleared.

Beneath the Sembian Bailey is a complicated network of sewers and tunnels that deliver fresh water to many buildings and take away waste from even more. A map of this network is known to exist in the Historic Wing of the Yellow Abbey, but I have not seen it.

Town Hall—A Class IV fortified building inside the Bailey itself, this is more a redoubt than a municipal building. While it does hold the offices of the Mayor, her clerks and other dignitaries, it was principally designed as a fortress and would be difficult to assault and easy to defend, with its thick straight walls and cantilevered battlements, atop which command of the streets below is possible.

The Bastion—A Class IV fortified building that oversees the main entrance to the town. This is a 20' limestone structure that was built in the 1420s and is in the Sembian style of the engineer that the Sembians brought with them a decade before.

The Garrison—A sprawling network of fortified annexes, this building could be considered a Class III fortified structure, but the Sembian disorganization of the place makes it slightly less than that. However, the main hall is outfitted with all the necessaries for training and lodging troops, and the Bright Spears stationed there seem perfectly comfortable, which is to say, neither too comfortable nor too uncomfortable.

Shrine to Amaunator—While one of the finest such shrines in our dale, it is certainly the most underutilized, as the size of the Flock here is small relative to similar settlements. The sole clerk here is Daphne Dale, a sensible local unmarried girl who holds an office in the Town Hall next door and can more usually be found there. While the Flock is small here, the Alavandor and Clarandal families undertook the expense of erecting this formidable Shrine themselves, no doubt in appreciation for the land deeded them here. The catacombs of the Shrine are nearly empty and, I'm told, connected to the larger network of tunnels beneath the Sembian Bailey.

The Phylactery—This is a small school and apothecary under one roof. Started by Aisling Alavandor in the early days of their arrival here, the medicines and students of medicine-making they produce are renowned about the dale and further abroad. Sisters from Charnel Priory often make the journey here for rare medicines and herbs, and the apothecaries here are known to have the most reliable medicines for fertility. As mentioned elsewhere, the quality of the products here and their results have bordered on heresy, but formal inquiries have turned up nothing occult or unsavoury.

Merchants' Hall—The second largest building in the settlement, the first floor of this 3-story structure is divided into many street-facing shops that can be rented by any merchant licensed by the town. The second floor is made up of apartments for the merchants, and the third is reserved for the Merchants' Guild of the farm, which is a strong political force here. The Hall has a large central stone tower that is a defensible structure in its own right, with the top floor serving as a counsel chambre of sorts for the Merchants Guild.

It is worth noting that the Merchants' Guild is headed by the Alavandor family and has been instrumental in the economic success of the settlement.

Artisans' Hall—Another large Sembian building with a cosmopolitan purpose, the Artisans' Guild of the farm owns and operates this facility. The main floor is a workshop used in the training of stone mason's apprentices, but the 2nd floor sees much more traffic. This floor is a large public house, complete with kitchens, a taproom, a stage (with live music a few nights every tenday), and a busy bar. It must be admitted that the best wine in the Dale is vinted at Thistle Ridge, no doubt due to the productive nature of the soil here and the presence of a Sembian family of Vintners that emigrated here in the '20s, the Goldwins. The matriarch of the family, Maura Goldwin, runs the bar and happily sells her wines and other local beverages to a thirsty patronage. The third floor of this large building is for apartments for young unattached masons, and a few are available for rent from the Festhall.

Brewery, Distillery, Vintner—With no formal name for this establishment, this is the name on Master Cartographer Vost's fine map of the area. This is a collection of businesses, really, with the most prominent being the Goldwins' vintner, which lends the fragrant aroma to the town square that many visitors enjoy. It is well known that the catacombs beneath this establishment have been extensively worked to square them up and provide storage space for hundreds of barrels and casks of product and thousands of bottles.

A brewer, Darla Dale, buys hops and other grains from local farmers to make a variety of delicious beers and ales, which she both sells locally and exports to the Falls. Her best customer is her close neighbour, the Artisans' Hall, who buys most of her product for sale in the Festhall.

A distillery here is owned by three brothers, the Kaskills, who produce a variety of creative spirits from their productive gardens nearby. Their most popular distillation is a corn spirit called Thistle Kiss, for which a dozen lewd pronunciations and variations of the name are equally popular.

The Alavandor Academy—One of the earliest public works taken on by the Alavandors after their settlement here, this establishment could safely be labelled a 'Grammar School,' in the Sembian sense of the word, or so I'm told. It is the largest building in town, has 3 floors and is also in the Sembian style of architecture (busy, convoluted, elaborate), but is a pleasing structure nonetheless, complete with a fine but small courtyard that the instructors make good use of. The Academy is equipped with a small but impressive observatory for the instruction of astronomy, and is the envy of most astronomers in the region.

The first and second floors of this large building are full of lecture halls, offices, a respectable library, and a well-appointed refectory. The third-floor acts as a dormitory for a few dozen children, some from the nearby outlying region, others from nearby farms (many families at Gribbons Farm choose to send their children here for instruction, rather than the Yellow Abbey, which bears discussion). It also has apartments for the Dean of the Academy, Franko Petrark and his family, as well as the small staff that operate the Academy.

At the present, almost all children of the farm aged 5-12 spend much of the year attending classes here and are given a traditional education in Grammar. Promising students might be invited to stay on until they are aged 15, and learn Logic, Rhetoric and Astronomy from the Dean himself. To date, all of the teachings of the Academy are in line with the general Moral Guidelines of the Yellow Abbey.

Alavaxi's (Blacksmith)—Owned by the patriarch of the family, Pharaxes, this establishment represents a consolidation of the various men and women who ply this most noble trade, under something that is akin to a guild structure one might find in Charity or larger cities. Alavaxi himself, although a newcomer (of dubious origin, it is worth mentioning, but seemingly harmless) is the head of this guild, and has operated it with efficiency and focus. With the abundance of Iron and Copper from the two mines outside the farm, there are plenty of raw materials to support the operation, and they are a net-exporter of finished goods in these materials.

Pharaxes himself is a Master Blacksmith and has forged several magical blades in the curved style of his home country. Pharaxes and his wife, Numestra, as well as a few others that arrived with them in the late 'teens all suffer from a condition of the skin that the best physicians and priests assure me is not contagious. Their children all seem free of the condition.

Sulphonti's (Herbalist)—Now operated by the daughters of the original Family Medic that accompanied the Alavandor's to the Dales and started this business, Sulphonti's is a peerless herbalist supply, the envy of many colleagues throughout the Dale. The women and girls who operate this business have small but remarkably productive gardens around the property, and under a small glass enclosure that produces even the most delicate herbs in the harshest of winters.

Sulphonti's stocks all of the herbs and spices expected for the region, but additionally stocks cubeb, nutmeg, saffron and turmeric, which they grow in their glass enclosure. It is well known that the Sisters of Charnel Priory spend a lot of time with the Sulphonti women when they come to town, and leave with many supplies.

Without the Walls—Hard by the walls of the farm are 60 or more acres under heavy cultivation, being about ¼ each of wheat, barley, fallow and vegetables. The nearby acreage surrounds the walls, neatly cleared out to 800' or so in each of three directions, with the limestone cliff providing the southern boundary of this part of the farm. These fields are exceptionally productive, and the animals that grow upon them fat and healthy.

Hinterland—The well-trained militia of Thistle Ridge has done an exemplary job of pacifying the 16 square miles (or so) around the walled town. Three additional farms with small fortified homesteads can be found less than 2 miles away, as well as a hunting lodge, a copper mine, a pair of towers that overlook the road and the river, and an iron mine a little further to the north.

Alavandor's Farm—This is the country home of the Alavandor family, and the main vineyard of the Goldwin family. Although I've never been to it, I am told that it is a well-fortified small holding with a stone, 2-storey castle, and many outbuildings. During the summer, most of the Alavandor family can be found on this estate. A good road leads from this farm to the main town, being perhaps 1 1/3 miles away (northeast of the town).

Clarandal's Farm—The road to Alavandor's continues to the northeast another 1 1/3 miles, where it finds Clarandal's farm; another Sembian-style summer estate. I am told that this smallholding is a single castle, small but sturdy, that stands on a good hill commanding the 4 square miles around it. While referred to as a 'farm', this is really a forward observation post for the settlement and a specialized training ground for the militia. Here, forest warcraft is taught to the militia, and a small but capable platoon of Foresters is stationed that patrols the northern extent of the hinterland.

Alavandor Lodge—Also known as the Autumn Estate, this is another small castle with mostly seasonal occupation. From mid-Eleint to mid-Uktar, most well-to-do men can arrange hunts out of the lodge, which are hosted by the Alavandor patriarch himself. Expeditions across the Solmen R. and into the Fog Hills usually produce a bounty of game, including white tailed deer, great harts, black and brown bears, and myriad fowl. The lodge is also a staging point for the Foresters when needed.

The Apple Glade—a mile and a half north of the town is the source of Thistle Ridge's most voluminous export, apples. Though more than a dozen species are cultivated here, the Dale Burgundy is the most popular eating apple in the region, owing to its sweet and crisp flesh, which preserves better than any other known variety. These can be found in the provision bags of soldiers in every corner of the dale and further abroad.

The Glade itself is owned in common by the farm, and just about anyone who wants to cultivate a row is permitted to do so. The Thistle family maintains more than a thousand trees, and these produce the bulk of their exports and profits each year.

There are no permanent structures here besides a couple of large barns for holding the animals that assist in transport of these goods, and their feed.

The Copper Hole—Copper Mine & Keep—Located 2 ½ miles north of the town, and accessed by roads that come from Clarandal's Farm and the Alavandor Lodge, this is a busy if small mine. I haven't visited it, but I am told it is a neatly ordered affair, exploiting some large veins of copper that have produced more than enough to keep the smiths busy, and exports of ingots healthy. The mine itself is accessed by a small keep that has been built atop it. Those that mine it are typically farmers that mine throughout the winter; apparently the mine is warm and not unpleasant during the coldest months. The limestone hill that contains the mine is riddled with caves, and it is widely rumoured that these caves connect with a broader network that extends beneath the entire region, and indeed, possibly to the Underdark. While no major threats have ever been encountered down here as far as I know, Knights of the Shield do make it a habit to poke around there every other year or so.

The Iron Hole—Iron Mine & Keep—This is the furthest regularly-used resource in the hinterland. About 6 ½ miles by trail from the town, this is another portion of the limestone cave network that permeates the area. A rich vein of iron has been supplying the retorts and smiths of the area for more than 20 years, since the arrival of the Alavaxi's and their people. This mine is a less-secure setting given its distance from the rest of the settlement, and the depth of the mine itself. The Alalvaxi's lead mining expeditions a few times each year that last for a few weeks; just enough time to get the materials needed to keep the journeymen busy. During this time, there is a heavy military presence here. Skirmishes with deep-things have happened a number of times over the years, and this remains a Location of Interest to the Church and the Military.

Families and People

Alavandor Family—Emigrating from Sembia in 1407, they were settled on Thistle Ridge in 1408, along with the Clarandal Family, that arrived with them. In Sembia, the Alavandors were accomplished merchants. The family acumen for the mercantile life is plainly visible in the fast growth of a thriving market and several trade agreements with their neighbours. The Matriarch of the Family is Susserah, widow of Andarl. Susserah runs the Merchant's guild, and by proxy, much of the decision-making in the settlement. This family owns several businesses, a fine home in town, and a sturdy castle estate not far from town. Additionally, they own and operate the Alavandor Academy, which provides free education for local children, and subsidized education for children from out of town.

The Clarandal Family—Also emigrating from Sembia and settled in Thistle Ridge in 1408, the Clarandal Family can be thought of as the military arm of the Sembian Families that settled here. Bringing with them a right martial tradition and discipline, the sons of this family have distinguished themselves in the pacification of the hinterland about the Farm, as well as professional soldiers and officers of the Sunblades. The current mayor is Senthia Clarandal, herself originally an Alavandor. Additionally, the brothers Claudius and Tergus are now full-time inhabitants of the Farm after long careers as Sunblades, mostly on the Netherese Front.

The Alavaxi—It's unclear to me if this is their surname, the name of the town they are from, or both, but this is a family of notable origin. Arriving in 1416 from distant Chessenta, this family (or group of families?) became the immediate source of an Investigation by my father and his senior Magistrates. Some of the elders of the family had a skin infection contracted on their long journey from Chessenta, and being so far from home (I have never met another Chessentan, and have only heard of them in books), the investigation was severe. In the end, most of the Alavaxi were permitted stay in Thistle Ridge (where they first arrived in Dagger Dale), but a couple of the eldest were sent to stay under guard in the Chapel Hospital at Castle Radiance, where they dwell today. The Alavaxi are accomplished artisans, especially of worked metals, and their bladesmithery seems to be without peer in the Dale.

The Thistles—As I mentioned in the preamble, the Thistles have been here for hundreds of years, and are peaceful and insular followers of Silvanus. They have never attempted to cement relationships with other nearby Dalesmen, and their faith might have stood in the way of proper diplomatic relations in any event. However, Nadia Thistle is respected by all who know her well enough as a thoroughly peaceful woman, unmatched in her generosity for strangers despite her adherence to the Ancient Faith. Her many daughters and grandchildren can be found bounding through their half of the settlement, usually barefoot and happy. They operate a few small businesses, but are mostly a self-sufficient people that buy and sell little, and cause no trouble.

The Sweetberry's—Also natives of Thistle Ridge, and originally followers of Silvanus, the Sweetberry family has embraced their new Sembian neighbours and the changes that have come to Thistle Ridge; the Matriarch of the Family, Janus, operates the Oak and Sheild, and seems to be making a very good living doing so for her and her family. She is a famously fertile woman of countless offspring (with more than one husband), who attend on visitors to Thistle Ridge with warmth and enthusiasm. They are a lovely family.

The Hillborns—Transplants from Charity Hills Farm, but originally from Hillsfar, this is another family that came to Daggerdale pursuing the prosperity that it enjoyed in the closing years of the Sundering and those chaotic times. While some Hillborns remain in Charity, the majority of that family moved to Thistle Ridge at the request of then-mayor Alvund Alavandor, who was looking for hard working men to help build walls, workshops, and other works needed to grow the settlement. Already doing business with many people in Charity, the Elder Alavandor liked the people of this settlement, and offered them fine properties and trade agreements to help the Sembian settlement grow. The Patriarch of this Family, Harrington Hillborn, is 'Free of the Guild of Joiners' in Charity (a caste-based title of some importance to those people), and is presumable not in exile from Charity. The family operates several of the businesses in Thistle Ridge, as well as being the foremen for the mining operations at the Iron Hole and the Copper Hole.

The Wheatbushels—One of two Halfling families at the Farm, the Wheatbushels are somewhat new to the area, having emigrated from Ashabenford about 20 years ago. The Matriarch of the family, Milly, runs the mill on the river, and is probably the friendliest and most popular citizen. She deals with many of the wives and handmaids of the settlement on a daily basis, as she grinds their wheat to flour, making pleasant conversation the while. Her two daughters work here alongside her. Her husband, Whistle, runs the Public House with his name nearby, where his three sons help him out.
