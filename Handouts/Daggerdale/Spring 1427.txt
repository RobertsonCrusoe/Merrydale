Spring, 1427 
From the barracks in Dagger Falls, I pen these words with a mind heavy with the weight of revelry and remorse. This day of the Muster, a time of celebration and solemnity, has found me succumbing to the former rather than adhering to the latter. The festive spirit of the week-long festival has swept me away, and I fear I have indulged in too much merrymaking, neglecting the gravitas of the occasion. 
For today is not merely a festival; it is the day when the Sun Blades assign us to our permanent units. My heart yearns to be placed in the 2nd Company, the illustrious Mountaineers, whose valor and prowess are renowned throughout these lands. Yet, I cannot shake the feeling that my lack of focus this day may have sullied my chances. 
As I wandered through the bustling streets, the sights and sounds of the festival rekindled memories of my home, Thistle Ridge. The faces of those who worship Sylvanus, the ancient god of nature, filled my mind. These families, with their deep reverence for the land, have thrived for centuries in the embrace of the wild. Their simple, harmonious existence stands in stark contrast to the fortified walls and martial discipline my family brought to the village. 
Two years of rigorous training with the Sun Blades, preceded by a lifetime at Fathers fighting school, have honed my skills in warfare. Yet, I find myself marveling at the people of Thistle Ridge, who, without sword or shield, have weathered the storms of time through their devotion to Sylvanus and the natural order. 
Have I, in my pursuit of martial excellence, strayed too far from the wisdom of those ancient ways? The people of Thistle Ridge live in harmony with the world around them, while I have sought to bend it to my will through steel and discipline. 
As the night deepens and the revelry dies down, I vow to redouble my focus. The path of the warrior is not one of excess and frivolity, but of dedication and honor. I shall strive to embody the spirit of the Mountaineers, to earn my place among them, and to honor both my familys legacy and the enduring strength of those who revere Sylvanus. 
For now, I must rest and prepare for the morrow. The Muster is upon us, and my fate within the Sun Blades shall soon be decided. 

Journal of Tergus Clarandal
