He writes with a shaky hand on a crumpled page of vellum torn from his depleted journal.  Precious inks stain his hands.  He hands me the note. 

Sparrow helps Corrina into the saddle. She lurches in the saddle.  Her face is sullen.  She glances at me, and blame, bitterness and anger spew from her eyes.  Her cheeks are sunken.  Her hair is lank.  She fears for her unborn child. 

Boregar pushes ahead.  He was withdrawn into himself.  Since Raven's death he has been distant and driven.  I cannot catch his eye.  His boots crunch the sand. 

Warburden marches beside me.  Even this most noble creature seems defeated.  He tolerates Dalak with no complaint.  He carries our supplies.  He carries our armour.  He carries the folly of my pride. 

The party carries the marks of my sin. 

O Lord Ilmater!  It was Your Will that I saw the evil in the noble-looking Lion.  It was Your Will that I fight It.  But the Creature did not act in evil!  Tribute for safe passage.  It did not seek war, but peace.  It is peace that I long for, and it is peace that You denied me! 

I remember fighting in the Bloodstone armies against the brigand forces of the Lich-King of Damara.  I remember the blood, the anger, the hatred, the pain and the sorrow.  I vowed to stop the wars.  I vowed to heal, to struggle to save lives, not take them.  I vowed to ease suffering wherever it existed in the Realms. 

But yesterday, my Lord, You showed me Evil.  Yesterday, my Lord, You asked for Blood.  Why did it sue for peace, O Lord?  Why did it seek to spare us?  I feel the blood course through my veins.  Lycanthropy.  I touch my tongue to my teeth.  Are they sharper?  My skin itches.  Tomorrow night is the full moon.  Is this Your Will? 

I feel the eyes of the party on me. 

A gust of wind.  Warburden rears up.  Dalak pitches to the ground.  I reach out a hand to restraint the mount, then reach down to help my comrade.  His wounds have opened.  I tend to them carefully, reassuring him.  He grunts stoically, but I see the worry in his eyes.  I know that my dread writhes equally within him. 

Ignorance and suspicion eat at our bellies.  Tomorrow night we'll know.  Tomorrow night we'll be tested. 

Ilmater, why have You forsaken me? 

The next night, the DE arrived at the first Zhent oasis-outpost, Vaerthyl.  Before they would leave this outpost, Dorian, Sparrow, Maturin and Boregar turn into were-lions and rip the place apart.  The battle rager also helped in the destruction, but in the end he was met by Maturin in his lion-man form, and ripped Dalak apart, nearly killing him. 

The next day dawned with little hope for all of the heroes.  The whirlwind of bloodshed, violence and destruction was but a foggy memory.  The four new lycanthropes found themselves alone, each waking up alone and naked in an unfamiliar part of the desert.  Most managed to find their way back to the oasis, and there they found a horrific scene of slaughter.  Men were ripped to shreds and many had clearly been feasted upon.  This caused the four infected members of the party to become sick to their stomachs.  Zhents and slaves alike had all been slaughtered.  Horses had been butchered, walls clawed through.  The entire place was in absolute ruin.  Corrina had gone missing, and never again was she heard from.  Her story ends in obscurity here. 

A great pyre was made to burn all the corpses, and great piles of weapons and such were made.  Dalak was found, and tended to by Maturin all day, but things did not look good for the battle rager.  Though the remainder of the company looked for their missing comrade, at length they all solemnly admitted that it was time to move on.  The road continued, and so did their quest.  They left behind a companion that day, and many of the items they treasured and needed for the journey.  But Maturin left behind everything.  Ilmater turned His back on Maturin that day, and the Paladin began his long lonesome trial, and went down the godless road that he was bound to travel. 

The remaining 4 members would have perished if not for a fortuitous meeting with a benign tribe of Neshaba.  These desert nomads were quick to help the DE, despite what all had heard about the people of this land.  It was not until much later that it became clear why.  This particular band bore the symbol of the Green Eye of Muir'Quaggoth, and they must have known about the infection in the veins of these men. 

After days of wandering the road, they come to the second oasis-outpost, Olomaa.  They decide not to tackle this one head on, but in their attempt to circumvent the place, they were intercepted by the 14th company of Fist mercenaries.  They managed to destroy this company nearly to the man, but they learned that these 20 men were of elite training, and quick thinking. 

Days later, as they trekked through the sands, the DE encountered another 'golden death' storm.  This was the worst one they would endure, and it was bad enough to sunder the company.  Dorian was blown away hopelessly in the storm, and the DE lost almost all of the valuable gear they had managed to retain after the slaughter in Vaerthyl.  When the storm finally subsided, Dorian and Sparrow were long gone, and no tracks or trails were there to be followed.  The desert had seemingly just swallowed the men up mercilessly.  

Three days later, and two nights before the full moon would rise again, a boon came from the desert, and hope was put into the hearts of all, but especially in the heart of the Forsaken Paladin.  A wanderer came from the desert.  A man who seemed a beggar came over the dunes, and he helped along the weakened Dorian, who he had found the day before.  Dorian had been staring at the sun and hallucinating, drawing his final breaths.  The wanderer followed the birds of carrion and rescued him. 

This man, Shildevan the Wanderer, was a holy man of Ilmater.  He was on a holy pilgrimage to harsh lands everywhere, and the hand of Ilmater guided him to the DE. 

The man had favour enough with Ilmater that he could cure the horrible lycanthropic sickness in the veins of the infected members, but all had to agree to a quest in order to receive this blessing. 

The three men were all given an individual quest that they would have to fulfill, lest the curse of Ilmater consume them. 

Dorian was told he had to eliminate the threat posed by the Beholders, and that he could not accept any pay for this mission.  Though the clever Sembian agreed to not accept a single 'copper piece' for the entire quest, he did not realize the scope of his challenge.  Indeed, his was the only quest that would bring him back to the streets of Waterdeep over a year later. 

Boregar was commanded to be true to his friends, but to return and set right all the wrong he had left behind.  This seemed like a great task as well, given all the evil they had turned their cheeks on during the journey out here. 

Maturin was commanded to free the slaves of Dagger Dale, and then to return to the desert to slay the Chosen of Muir'Quaggoth, Grandfather. 

Shildevan made his egress the next morning after lighting a fire for the sleeping companions. 

Back on the road, the DE began to make solid headway after their fortuitous encounter with the holy wanderer.  They came to Addas Babar, and secretly traded with some slaves for food and water.  A small fight ensued, and Boregar was taken down by the mage Farim, the only survivor of the Vaerthyl massacre.  Though he was later rescued by his companions, the Gond Pistola was not recovered. 

Days later the DE once again encounter the Nashaba, and this time their headman Kabina assigns a guide to them, so that they may go through the desert quicker.  After many more days of travel and after many more pounds had come off of the frames of the travelers, the Desert's Mouth Mountains came into view in the East, and the hearts of the travelers leapt. 

It should be said that all the obstacles, challenges, misery and atrophy of the desert have not been detailed in this brief summary.  The journey was one of constant loss and doubt, and in recorded history it was not known that four lonesome travelers could make such a remarkable journey and live to tell about it, until these four came out alive.  

While traveling in the bad lands between the desert and the mountains, the 4 encounter the ogre-mage Yin Xu.  They find they are unable to hurt the creature before he is able to escape using his gaseous form, but he is also overmatched to fight these 4. 

After crossing the mountains, the 4 head down into the fertile valley that is the border region between Dagger Dale and Shadow Dale.  The hearts of these men were renewed, though their bodies were just beginning to recover from their journey.  They traveled off the roads, as they were weakened and had insufficient tools for making war, and knew that the Fist would be close by. 

They encountered a group of Zhent scouts as they neared Shadow Dale, but they were rescued by a group of elves of the Region, who Boregar was familiar with.  The elves then escorted the DE to Shadow Dale, where they arrived on the 4th of Eleint. 

Even the Boregar was noticeably thin when the 4 turned up at the Old Skull Inn.  The others had the look of skeletons, and all were haggard, filthy and wretched.  After they were cleaned up, and donned some new clothing, they weren't long fitting in at the taproom.  Crowds flocked around them (despite their want for some peace) and asked many questions of the men who had come from the desert to liberate the slaves of Dagger Dale. 

One of the guests at the inn that night was the noble fop, William Cormaeril.  This man had come from Cormyr, and claimed that he was the rightful heir to rule Dagger Dale with the death of Randal Mourn.  He had a small retinue that included a half dozen Purple Dragons, and he boasted that he would liberate Dagger Dale from the treacherous Zhents, but he was not taken seriously by anyone who heard him. 

That night peace would not come easily.  Dragon entered the inn and made an attempt on Dorian's life, but was not successful.  She managed to escape before any harm could befall her.  Though she scorched and skewered Dorian, her most grievous wound was upon his heart.  The fool was beginning to fall for her. 

The next morning, the epic scale of the events in the Dales was made greater yet again.  The order of the Broken Blade, including 8 knights of Tempus and their retinue, rode into Shadow Dale with full pomp and ceremony.  They, too, announce that they will be heading to Dagger Dale to put an end to the villainy there, and that they will not wait a day.  Their leader Sir Durnbold, implores Cormaeril to ride with them and take his rightful seat after a valiant battle, but his cowardice was great, and the Broken Blade rode out that very morning without him. 

As the Paladins of Tempus went west out of town, all took it as a good sign.  But then, the heavens gave ill portent.  The green and black vortex was seen in the sky, and it inspires panic in the village.  As the sun was coming up that morning and vortex was swirling, the Church of Lathander began its bifurcation.  A fragment of the church lead by Rewel Thunderstorm, the second-in-command of the church in Shadow Dale walks out on High Dawnlord Cassimar's sermon.  Thunderstorm insists that the vortex is a sign, and that they should have sent all available Lathanderites with Sir Durnbold to help in their noble quest. 

By a reckoning put together by Kennyth much later, this was the morning that the slaves in the Dagger Hills mines uncovered the beholder tablets, and the vortex was likely the manifestation of some god associated with the beholders. 

That night, good news and bad both come to the DE in the inn.  Sparrow has emerged from the desert.  He explains that he was also helped by the nomads of the desert, and that magic sustained him.  He brings ill news though.  That day he passed by the site of an ambush, and reports that the Order of the Broken Blade have all been slain in ambush less than 20 miles from Shadow Dale.  As this news spreads, so does the bifurcation of the local church of Lathander. 

The next morning, all surviving DE members are together and recuperated.  They plan to re-supply today, and to begin their toil.  That morning, though, more trumpets are heard, and Shadow Dale once again seems to be the stage for a play of the Gods.  The holy trinity of Tyr, Torm and Ilmater march their brightest lances and blades into Shadow Dale that morning.  The following orders were present that morning; 

Order of the Golden Lion (Torm), led by Sir Gethard Soulbright 

Order of the Lambent Rose (Ilmater), led by Sir Lexinauth and Father Eisley of St.Collin's Abbey.  Both of these men refused to speak with Maturin. 

Knights of Justice (Tyr), led by Sir Wildermot Silverlance 

One of the men in the tent-train of the OLR was Revered Brother Turin, who was an old friend of Maturin's.  The two had served in the army together in Damara, fighting the Lich King of Vaasa.  Turin was glad to see his old friend, despite what politics where at work. 

Word that day comes to Boregar from the local wood elves that Yarkul was spotted at the Dagger Hills mine the day before, perhaps accompanied by a beholder.  They took something large and heavy from the mine, and marched away.  Sparrow insists that the trail least cold lies in that direction, but there were many questions about which task to start first.  There were 3 slave mines to free, and the town itself to liberate.  The choices were many, and there were many political considerations now as well, with nights of three orders in town.
