Tarsakh 21, 1434 

The quiet of the night finds me once again in the solitude of my quarters at castle radiance. Earlier today, while standing watch on the western battlement, I found myself in conversation with my captain. We were enjoying the sweet crisps taste of Dale Burgundy apples a cherished reminder of Thistle Ridge. The captain remarked on the unrivalled quality of these apples attributing their excellence to the fertile soil of the test valley. 

His comment, laced with a touch of humor, extended to the fertility of my dear wife, Senthia. It is true that despite my infrequent visits, she has blessed me with a wife child each year since our marriage in 1427. We shared a laugh over my apparent virility, but the captain's words lingered in my mind long after our watch had ended. 

As the night deepens, I find myself contemplating the peculiar abundance of life at this low Ridge. My father who had two wives before my mother was childless until he came to this valley. With my weather he, his third wife, he fathered me within the first year of their union in Thistle Ridge this pattern of fertility, repeated now in my own marriage, seems more than mere coincidence. 

Could there be something unique about Thistle Ridge, something that enhances the fruitfulness of those who dwell there? The thought stirs a mix of curiosity and reverence within me. The people of Thistle Ridge, with their ancient ties to the land and their worship of the old faith, might hold some ancient knowledge or blessing that influences this phenomenon. New line new line such musings lead me to ponder the broader implications of our connection to the land. We, the sun blades, pride ourselves on our discipline in martial prowess. But perhaps there are forces at work beyond our understanding forces that shape our lives in ways that we cannot readily see or measure. 

As I prepare for sleep, I resolve to delve deeper into the lower official Ridge when next I return home. Perhaps there is wisdom to be gleaned from the elders and worshippers of Sylvanus, insights that could illuminate the mystery mysteries of our fertile homeland. 

For now, duty calls come up and I must remain focused on my responsibilities here at castle radiance. Yet the seeds of these questions has been planted in my mind and I am certain they will grow and bear fruit in their own time 

Cassimar Tower 
Known as 'Brotherhood Tower' in the early 1370s, this building was the headquarters of a militant wing of the cult of Iyactu Xvim until 25 Eleint, 1372.  On that day, a group of men stormed the court outside the tower, and waged a battle against the evil knights and prevailed.  Today, the court in which the tower sits is called 'Harpers' Court', and a large round paving stone not far from the entrance of the tower reads "On this spot was the head of the Xvimite Half-Devil, Baron Drake, separated from his shoulders by the Axe of St. Maturin.  Maturin was accompanied that day by Dorian of Sembia, Boregar the Wanderer, and other heroes who desired justice more than fame."  
Today, the tower is owned by what is left of the Cassimar family.  The Cassimars were one of the most prominent families the Dales from the 1310's-1380's, having come to the Dales with Sembian gold and a knack for spreading the faith of Lathander.  In most large settlements, a Dawnlord with their surname could be found until the Spellplague, and the metamorphosis of Lathander into Amaunator. 
The faith of the Cassimar family did not survive the Spellplague, but their wealth did, and what few members of that once great family remain have all retired to Dagger Falls and reside in their now-opulent tower. 
After a fire in 1375 (supposedly an act of arson) gutted the building, it sat empty for decades, until the Cassimars moved to town and bought and renovated it.   
The tower is an imposing stone structure and one of the most conspicuous in town.  The main body of the building is 4 stories, while an additional set of towers built atop the main battlement in the 1460's adds another story and a half, making it the tallest structure in the town.  The Cassimars have been in the habit of painting the dark stones of the main tower in a brilliant lavender, and many lavender & yellow flags and banners with the family crest adorn the building, lending it a bright and pleasing countenance. 
The main body is 75' in diameter, with 14' ceilings on the first floor, 12' ceilings on the second, and 10' ceilings on the third and fourth floors.  A grand winding staircase in the middle of the main building connects the original 4 floors plus the larger of the two new towers.  Each level has a balcony encircling the stairs, surrounded by many rooms and small passageways.  The Cassimars favour a convoluted and detailed architecture, with many fine panelled rooms on every level, many private and intimate nooks filled with tapestries, cushions and carpets with rich colours from around the Realms. 
The largest room in the building is the dining hall on the 2nd floor, which serves the mayor as the focal point for her many parties, but guests are encouraged to wander the tower and explore.  Many of the finer rooms have magical lights with intimate tones, some with twinkling motes that give specific rooms a fey feeling. 
The large battlement at the top of the main building has well-kept gardens of fragrant flowers, and a small arboretum that connects the new tower to the open space. 
The Cassimars employ a small number of private guards, all originally orphan boys whom they have looked after for many years.  There are 8 of them in total, the oldest being Dorian Dale, aged 26, who like his namesake is a capable swordsman, and a former Sunblade. 
The Cassimars 

There are three remaining Cassimars in the Dales, and they all live at the Tower.  The eldest is Renley Cassimar, 75 years old, and father of Lilly.  Renley is a kind and quiet old man who is rarely in the public eye, and it is well known that he dislikes his daughter's parties. 
Lilly Cassimar is the Mayor of Dagger Falls, and a dedicated follower of Sune.  She is very close friends with Troia Truestar, the highest ranking Sunite in the Dale, and who serves as a spiritual mentor as well as a friends.  Troia always visits the Cassimars when she is in town, lending a politically useful angle to the friendship.  Lilly always looks her best, and delights in helping others to improve their presentation, whether through hygeine, clothing, language and manners, or some of the concoctions she purchases from Blossom Bellwether at The Phylactery.  Lilly is well connected in town, the Dale, and further abroad, and keeps her own messenger doves which support her many correspondents around the Dales. 
The youngest Cassimar is Coventina, the niece of Lilly.  Coventina's father was Lilly's older brother, Caedren.  Her mother died in child birth, and Caedren raised Coventina at the tower with the rest of his family.  When she was old enough to be conscrpited for military service a few years ago, Caedren took the place of his daughter and served her term.  He was a capable soldier and formidable on horseback, and was placed in the 9th Company, one of the Rides, rather than in town as he had hoped.  He was killed at Liberty Hill on the 1st of Kythorn, 1480 DR by the swarming madness known as the Soul of Corruption.  Coventina, like her aunt, is a dedicated Sunite, and considered the most stunning beauty in Daggerdale.  She is kind but aloof, and keeps not close friends or confidants, though some suspect she may have a secret relationship with the head of her household guard, Dorian. 

Local Rumours 
Temple of Bane in the Grotto 
The tower is built atop one of the oldest cave networks in the area, and was originally a smaller building used to conceal the caves and the temple to Bane within.  Randal Mourn himself rooted out the Temple, but was captured inside by the Zhents when he did, leading to his years-long imprisonment and ultimately his execution.  During the Zhent Occupation of the 1370's, the Tower was built to once again serve to control access to the caves.  In more recent times, sewers have been connected with the basements of the tower, and no caves have been discovered. 
Old Money 
It is unclear exactly what the Cassimars do to earn money, if they do at all.  They own no other properties or businesses, but run one of the most expensive households in the Dale.  Most assume that the family is still thriving off the generations of wealth accumulated by the family in the previous century, which has made the tower a periodic target for thieves, especially during the Mayor's frequent parties, where she invites many guests and encourages them to wander the fine labyrinth of her opulent home.

Dearest Sister: 
I hope you will destroy this letter after you read it. Part of the agreement with the new Alavandors is that my family and the other would take what we were given and vanish forever. The old patriarch made good on his end of the deal; he did indeed make our journey safe, even comfortable, although a trade route this long is something I had only read about before undertaking it myself. I shouldn't say any more, but if I'm going to break Terms (and I have with this letter), I might as well break them good, and give you all I'm sure you're interested in hearing. 
First, the trip across the Sea of Fallen Stars, even though I'm told we only crossed a small portion of it, was exciting but often terrifying. This is no life for me, or indeed any of the two families aboard. It wasn't the last time we'd be on a ship. 
We came to Turmish – what a strange land. They barely speak modern Chondathan there, and they are quite dusky. However, they had comfortable factories and delicacies I'd never dreamt of. We followed the coastline in a small but fast one-masted galley. We could stay quite close you see, avoiding the largest of the pirates that plague the coasts here. We rarely stopped until we came to Chessenta, and here we lingered for nearly a month. There is a great deal of political conflict between the Chessentans and the nation of our destination. 
I should say that despite the language barrier (they speak no Chondathan, but the trade tongue works well enough here), the foreign and almost drow-like look of these people, their strange gods (though I think their hawk-headed Ho-Ka-Tep is the same as our Amaunator), their strange customs (I was nearly jailed for kicking a stray cat out of my way), and bizarre architecture, I rather like these people. For starters, they keep elves as slaves, and I never heard that sort of thing was possible. I've had many a long night of drinking their spiced wines with owners of these slaves, and I still can't understand how they manage it. 
The city I was in, Luthcheq, was beautiful in its own way, though I'm told it was much finer 100 years ago (what wasn't?) They don't tolerate wizards here, which is sort of what I had expected. The factory here was the finest of the lot, and the factor had met the old patriarch a few times, and was fond of him, and treated us well. I think he knew more than he let on. Anyway, we were comfortable there and really didn't want to leave, and now I wish we hadn't. I could have spent the rest of my days there happy, but its an expensive place to live, and our money wouldn't last forever. 
The final leg of the journey was perilous, and now we were outside the reach of the old patriarch; these were lands he told us he had no friends in, and that if they got the slightest idea that we were friendly with the Chessentans, there'd be trouble. But this is the agreement we made, and knowing that the man's reach extends this far, I'm doubly determined to keep up my end of the bargain, and I know the others are too. 
From Luthcheq, we marched and marched and marched, and the Chessentans (whom I consider mostly 'our people' if you catch my meaning) gave way to the mean-faced Imaskari, who are kinder, I was later to learn, than the frightening people that have become my neighbours. I write this now from a fine apartment in our little villa, which our money bought cheaply here (the old patriarch was right about that, and his factor made the arrangements for us to our benefit). We have a couple of slaves, which is new for me. It isn't at all what you might expect, from the stories.
