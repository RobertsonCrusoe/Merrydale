Eleint 25, 1372 DR 
Things are changing here very quickly now, and it is difficult to keep track of it all. 
I was personally looking on yesterday when you and your friends marched those hundreds of slaves out of the Eastern Bailey and south to freedom. My heart leapt for the first time since I started this assignment, and later I wept, that our hard work has finally brought about some good. 
Well, there is more, though good or bad I cannot say. It is difficult these days, and with this type of enemy 
First of all, early this morning, William Cormaeril addressed the town, declaring the rule of the tyrant Yarkul to be at an end, and declaring himself the rightful ruler, by birth, of this town. He announced that Yarkul had been killed by a Harper assassin. I suppose this isn't true, but whoever did that job has my nomination for certain. 
At any rate, the garrison all donned new tabards of yellow, with a coat of arms of four fields: one a purple dragon, one the Daggerdale coat, one with the Cormaeril sigil, and the fourth quarter a tacky rising sun. The twenty or so men that surround Cormaeril wear purple tabards of the same style. 
Someone had to be expecting this to happen, as even the cleverest invoker would have had a difficult time conjuring up a couple hundred bright yellow tabards in one day. 
I heard a commoner in the tavern this evening remark that "at least the Zhents didn't make such a show of lording it up over the people." 
I have a source that sits on the council in the Tower who filled me in on some of the political changes that William is going to instigate: 
1) No religious oppression and no tolerance of religious quarrels. The town will be paying to build a temple to Lathander as soon as a capable priest is found. 

2) Absolutely zero slavery. Slave ownership and trade is punishable by hanging, or at the end of a sword 3) Establish a trade route to Cormyr. What they plan to do about Tilverton, I do not know. 

4) An alliance between the Dales is also desired by William, as WELL as with the town of Yûlash. I don't know how that can be done right now. 

That is all I know about the political changes that Cormaeril wants to make. A couple of other events here are worth mentioning as well. 
Today at about noon, a bunch of the Garrison dressed in yellow tabards marched into the temple of Bane and slaughtered the clergy to the last. 
This came about after a man in the town council accused the Banite church of retaining its slaves. I don't know who it was that made this accusation, but it is an interesting play; certainly all the Churches in town and likely the Tower itself still have slaves in them. Nothing would be done about this if not for the publicizing of this fact by a power-elite member. 
At any rate, 24 more slaves became free men and women today, and the Church of Bane in Dagger Falls is no more. 
Related to this, my informant on the Council tells me that he was told in confidence by William himself that he (William) is worried about the three demons living in Brotherhood Tower (the Xvimite knights' building). He knows he lacks the strength to overcome the Baron and his remaining men, and this keeps the voice of Hatemaster Dragkul a very powerful one on the council. Certainly, we shall not see the people who are enslaved in that horrible place freed anytime soon and no accusations have been made against the Xvimites. 
I feel this is something we shall have to deal with ourselves. 
The Fist may not be out of the picture yet. Riders came in this morning from the north road, reporting that they had been ambushed by a group of ogres and hill giants while trying to reach Addas Babar. From the report, ten men were cut down in seconds, and only two managed to escape. From what I have been told of the tactics used in this encounter, it sounds like elements of the 5th and 12th Companies are watching the road to ensure that no one will be able to either follow the 'Beholder Stones', trade west of Dagger Falls, or both! 
Things appear to be getting better, but our war is far from over. That is all for now.

It is nearly over, my friend. I trust that your health has improved under Brother Turan's conscientious care and Timfast's cookery. Dorian and I disagreed on the best course of action, but with the council of Boregar, a man named Gaebril Tremalkin – a holy warrior of a strange faith – and Garrick Shining, we decided to cleanse Dagger Falls once and for all. 
Following the council, we were to get some rest before heading north. I had barely caught my first wink of sleep in 48 hours when Dorian burst into my tent to tell me that Sparrow was both in grave danger, and was missing. I know not how the warning came, but Sparrow was nowhere to be found. A hurried search was organized, and with a presence of mind that still surprises me, I tracked Sparrow to the Old Skull Inn, where patrolling guards had seen him head in the company of a beautiful woman. 
En masse, our party charged into the inn and up the stairwell only to find ourselves confronting an obviously ensorcelled member of Shadowdale's emergency militia. Our friend Dorian, hardly the most level-headed of men these days, failed immediately and spectacularly in his attempts at negotiation and began to scuffle with the man. I ran downstairs with Boregar to enlist the aid of any spellcasters who 
considered themselves decent enough to come to our aid and I am surprised and sorry to say that only one soul in the bar saw his duty clearly. I came to the doorway just in time to see her figure sprout wings, let out an inhuman screech, and launch upwards, smashing through the skylight. 
While Garrick fired an arrow up at the quickly diminishing beast, I bade the spellcaster dispel whatever magicks may have lain upon Sparrow. I quickly jammed my index finger down his throat to induce vomiting, and, satisfied that he was safe, helped him down to the bar. By now, the inn had emptied to watch whatever succubus or harpy had been preying on our friend fly quickly north, towards Dagger Falls.

We have accomplished so much in the last months, and yet so much more is before us. Of the four of us who crossed the desert together, I am comforted that you, at least, have achieved peace, though at great cost. I wish you and Balak all the best and bid you take care. Boregar and Dorian have much to deal with, and I cannot escape the feeling that our destinies are still linked with Dagger Falls and Anauroch. For myself, doubt plagues me like a shadow, stronger now that the path seems clear. I had another vision from Torm yesterve, urging me again into the desert, away from Dagger Falls. There is yet to be a reckoning.

Departure - Harvestide 
By the time things cooled off in Daggerfalls, Harvestide was upon the Dales. Winter was coming, and the DE were overdue to leave. All signs indicated that the Tablets were weeks ahead of the DE, but they planned on catching them anyway. 
Daggerfalls had become a place of peace again in those days. Rewel Thunderstorm, though having made peace with Cassamar, still begat the church of Lathander in Daggerfalls. The three evil faiths were not heard from or seen in Daggerfalls for many years to come, and peace was upon the place.

Until the height of the Spellplague and the God Deaths, the faiths of Lathander and Shar were the most influential in the Dale and in the town itself. The temple to Mystra was also a very busy place, and it is well known that this attraction brought many practitioners of magic, and their money, to the town every year.

Addas Babar 
The DE was soon in the desert again. Just as the badlands begin to give way to sands and dunes, one finds the citadel of Addas Babar, perched high upon an island of rock overlooking the first oasis on the long road through the desert. 
The DE decided they would not circumnavigate the outposts on this return-journey. Boregar was on a quest to put to rights all those wrongs he had left behind, and the DE would have been without him if they would not fight his battle with him. 
The DE made short work of this fortress, and left it a grave of Zhents and a ruin. 

Bhaerlith 
Bhaerlith was a difficult conquest, given the heavily fortified and geographical nature of the place. At length, however, the DE were victorious, and the fortress was liberated. Of the bounty found in this fortress, the Gond Pistola was recovered and returned to Boregar. Many other powerful tools of war were also recovered.

To this day, Bhaerlith is an abbey dedicated to Ilmater. The wandering priest Shildevan came to the abbey not long after the DE had departed, and assumed leadership of the flock.

Apparently, this crazed priest knew something of Maturin, and although the Boregar attempted to subdue the man, and he makes off into the desert. His trail could not be followed. 
The DE stays on here for a day to burn bodies and desecrate the remains of the Temple. They ponder the visions from 'Torm' that were visited upon Maturin back in Daggerfalls, and conclude that Cyric was posing as Torm again (the 'Fool Knight' being his favourite god to impersonate) to motivate the DE to follow the Tablets, and leave the church of Cyric in Daggerfalls alone. 
That Maturin didn't follow the advice of Cyric in the guise of Torm is to his credit, as well as the credit of his friends and advisors, who saw their duty clearly. Cyric had been taken completely off the board with the cleansing of Olomaa. 

Maturin and the Lion 
Many weeks later, the DE arrives back at Vaerthyl. As Boregar had feared, winter had come and the DE where trapped. They prepare the ruined keep there for their occupation throughout the winter. 
The winter is cruel in Anauroch, to say the least. The remaining five men of the DE train hard all winter, but on New Year's Eve, the time of reckoning for Maturin and Grandfather is nigh. 
Late that night, as the full moon broke through the relentless clouds and snows, and the eye of Muir'Quaggoth was seen by the Grey Knight in its radiance. A great roar is heard off in the desert somewhere, and the DE do what they can to equip Maturin for the fight. Not long after, Grandfather lets out a roar just outside the doors. Maturin takes the floor, braced for battle, while the other 4 wait at the stairs. They know that this can be a fight between Grandfather and Maturin, alone. 
Grandfather crashes open the door and stands on the threshold while Maturin calls out the beast; 
Grandfather! 
In the name of Ilmater, I call you out and command you to lie down before me. I command you to repent your wrongs and forsake your evil god. 
I accuse you, scion of Muir-Quaggoth, of carnage immeasurable. Your legacy of pain and suffering lies heavily upon Faerun. 
I am here to cleanse it. In the name of Ilmater; 
In the name of those who have suffered; In the name of all that is holy and good; Repent and prepare to be destroyed. 
Grandfather growled low and strode into the room. The two god-fated combatants stared at each other for a moment, and then the Lion sprung! From the outset, things looked horrible for Maturin. He was knocked over and pinned right away, the giant lycanthrope poised above his prone form. The Lion bit deep into the knight, and tore and tore. All the while, however, Maturin was pulling the beast onto silver-plated spikes upon his breast plate, and making what attacks were allowed him with the Lathkule blade; this blade having been forged and enchanted in Waterdeep for the eradication of lycanthropes. The blade was thrust up through the beast's belly, and it gave a great roar before delivering one final bite to the knight. It tore deep into his neck, and then both combatants ceased their struggle. 
Maturin was hardly perceptible from beneath the giant beast, and neither were moving. When his companions came to his side however, and hefted the heavy monster off him, they found him still breathing. Though he was covered in blood, both from himself and the Lion, they could make out the shape of a gray tear under the Knights right eye. The knight breathed, and his companions helped him to his knees. Maturin stayed kneeling for what seemed like an eternity. His breathing caused him to heave, and he steadied himself with the bloodied blade that had slain the huge Lion that lay at his side. 
He looked up, and the grey tear sparkled for just a moment, and Maturin nodded wearily. At that moment, a scraping was heard at the wind-assaulted door. Warburden had returned, and stepped in to nuzzle his masters bloodied head. With what strength he had, the weary knight raised one bloodied gauntlet to stroke the beasts great face in silence, while his companions, silent as well, looked on. 
Dorian was heard to whisper "Ilmater be praised". 
This final portion of the Daggerdale Expedition is most eloquently told through the words of the Grey Knight, found in his memoirs years after these events; 

Ilmater's boon 'neath the green-eyed moon Did tame the were-lion's wroth; 
The desert trails had their secret tales For the sons of Muir-Quagoth; 
But a Damaran knight is an outlandish sight, To rise from Anauroch's haze 
And a night of fear in the Rogue Dragon Year Was Grandfather's end of days. 

The Story Ends and Continues 
Much more could be said of what happened to those heroes after the death of Grandfather, and their return to Waterdeep. But with regards to the role they played in Daggerdale, this chapter of the story is concluded. In the spring of 1373 they continued on to Waterdeep, hunting beholders, Zhents and Drow with their other companions whose company they parted in Phaulkonmere two years earlier. 
I wish I knew what happened to the tablets, to the wizard Thagdul, to the other Zhents and the drow, but I do not. The world shortly after was thrown into the chaos of the Spellplague, and only during my lifetime have we seen a reconnection between the Dales and the Western Heartlands (despite the efforts of Returned Netheril). 
I do know that Dorian returned to the Castle, and I think he's still there in some evil form or another. 
I know also that the Black Saint returned in secret in the early 1400's; his presence was whispered of enough to be more than a rumour, but he is dead now, though ironically not forgotten. 
Maturin too is dead. I am told that the remains of his body are at the abbey at Bhaerlith, which remains free of Netherese influence despite its proximity. 
Unfortunately, Sparrrow Flame-Handed, who served as the high priest of Mystra for decades even after her death, also vanished in the tumultuous years of the Spellplague, though his body is not accounted for. He was last seen with my father on the plains of Netheril, facing off against abominable agents of that evil empire. Wielding what magic he could, he held off a company of monsters while my father and his soldiers retreated from an impossible battle. I in fact owe my life to Sparrow, as this was years before my birth. 
The Daggerdale Expedition and its allies was made up of other mortal men whose names I cannot find in this age, though evidence of their passing remains. Of the Harpers, Boregar, Garrick & Aerin, there is no further mention I have found (and I have searched), but there are many young boys named Boregar in this Dale and Shadowdale today, and Harper's Court outside what was Brotherhood Tower, is named for them. 
I will return to an accounting of some of these loose ends someday when my duties are either lessened or shared. But for now, I remain, 
Luminous Whitegate, 
Lord of Castle Radiance and High Commander of the Order of the Lambent Shield
