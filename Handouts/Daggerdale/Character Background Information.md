The Myth Drannor Embassy 
The loneliest place in Dagger Falls. That is what the friendliest of the locals call the Embassy. Others call it a waste of space, or a blatant offence to the people of the Dale. This is because there are very few elves left in this part of the Dalelands, as they seem preoccupied with some trouble in their own land, not terribly far away. Still others call the Embassy The Tomb, as it was the site of the grisliest battle in the history of Dagger Falls. In Eleint, 1372 DR, The Eastern Storm, accompanied by William Cormaeril and his hearthguard assaulted what was then Dark Sun Temple, a large fortified temple dedicated to Cyric. Inside was a small army of undead, and a protracted battle lasted for many hours, as reinforcements were called in to help clear the temple out. 

For many years afterward, the building lay empty. Even though it had been thoroughly cleaned out and deconsecrated by many powerful priests. It wasnt until the late 1390s, when Faerun was just over the most tumultuous years of the Spell Plague, that the elves returned. The House Miratar, who often supported the Whitegates in the mountains and the growing plains of Netheril, purchased the building, and hired many local craftsmen to put it back into a serviceable condition. For nearly a decade, there were usually a group of elves at the Embassy, and they worked closely with the local government, helping with local development and defensive measures. However, by 1415, the elves all left, and for the most part havent returned. What is happening in Myth Drannor has them all occupied most of the time. The windows of this now-fine building are usually dark, and almost no one resides there. The Redjacks are under contract to provide two guards there at all times, and they have a local custodian, Merriadock Burgundy (a middle aged halfling male) who keeps the place tidy. 

The building itself is a fortified 3-story affair, integrated into the thick walls of the southern ramparts. Indeed, the second floor has a rampart encircling its perimeter around which guards can walk. There are many locked doors into the Embassy on this level, but few dare try the handles.

They are battling devils that are deep beneath the surface of their ancient settlement, as well as Prince Brennus Tanthul, one of the rulers of Netheril. Preamble From Magistrate Blaze Whitegate 
I have been to Thistle Ridge many times in my capacity as a young Magistrate.  This settlement is strategically important; it commands both the Tesh River and the Moonsea Road from an unassailable limestone perch within bowshot of both, and it is only about 10 miles as the crow flies from Daggerfalls; the perfect distance to send near-instant communications and for next-day reinforcement if needed. 

However, the origin of the settlement has nothing to do with trade, though this did attract some later migrants, which Ill discuss shortly. The original settlement dates to before the 13th century, though none now living know when exactly.  The Thistle Family, who as I understand it take their name from the Ridge, and not the other way round, have lived here for more than a century. They are private people, who both give and expect respect at arms length. The family have been gardeners (I dont think Id call them farmers) and expert herbalists for as long as anyone can remember, and their skill at healing obscure ailments (both real and imagined) is legendary, bordering on heretical.  They are a peaceful people, however, and though rarely seen in the Falls, they get into no quarrels when they are. 

The Thistles follow the Ancient Faith, though they do so respectfully with regards to their Chauntean and Amaunitori countrymen, and they do not proselytize.  Theirs is a Hereditary Faith, and we shall leave it at that for now. It should be noted that the Chaunteans insist that the settlement is upon sacred ground, and the evidence of that can be seen in the productivity of the small gardens there, the fruit from their trees, the quality of their medicines, and indeed, their numerous healthy children. 

In 1408, migrants from Sembia were settled in Thistle Ridge. These migrants are mostly from two Houses of Sembia and their attendants, namely the Alavandor and Clarandal Families. The former were successful merchants in their homeland, while the latter were of a highly disciplined military order. The previous mayor was from the Alavandor Family, namely Andarl Alavandor. After an unexpected death hunting, his younger sister, Senthia (married to a Clarandal) took over mayorship. She is slated to fill the role until Sheildmeet. 

The Sembians started a completely new era at Thistle Ridge.  They brought industry and commerce, as well as a new degree of security, and much-needed improvements to agriculture and the overall subjugation of the landscape.  They have established trading relationships with neighbours, as well as a reputation for some of the finest products in the dales (the Dale Burgundy Apple, Goldwin Wines, the finest medicines, just to name a few). 

They also attracted the strange Alavaxi people, Chessentans, or so Im told. The Alavaxi make the curved blades that some of our military orders now favour, as well as other fine steel products.  Obvious foreigners, they have brought some small discord to the Dale, though not in Thistle Ridge Farm where they have been welcomed by the immigrants before them (the Sembians). 

Thistle Ridge is an experiment in religious plurality and mixed cultural traditions that we are sure to keep an eye on in the future

Locations and People of Interest 
Locations 
Caravaners Court  a large clearing at the edge of the River Tesh, straddling the Moonsea Road.  This is where travelers can first request entry to the town, or, more often, will set up tents in the open market to do business with the locals, who will come down from the ridge-top settlement.  The Court (as the locals call it, somewhat imprudently) is a busy pace, with permanent barns and paddocks for travelers, and a few notable permanent businesses, namely; 

Whistles House  a public house run by the halfling of the same name. Whistle also owns the large double-barn next to the public house, which he rents out for the stabling of mounts and a dry place to sleep for travelers. 

The Broken Spoke  a small wagonwright capable of fixing wheels, barrels, crates, and providing shoeing for mounts. 

Millys Mill  a small but very busy flower mill on the river. Milly (halfling wife of Whistle) grinds most of the flower for the settlement, and her establishment provides much of the traffic that benefits traders set up in the Court 

The Fish Market  not really named this, but rather just called that, this is a small collection of tents and huts where the wives of a few local fishermen sell the days catch.  Usually a good supply of fresh pygmy afanc, brownfin, bass, sunfish, trout and eels to be found. 

All Faiths Shrine  found at the edge of Caravaners Court.  This small shrine is built of a granite not local to the region, and has been there since the earliest known records. What faith it was originally built for is unclear, and there are no permanent idols or other embellishments that suggest one god or another. Locals steer clear of it, but some travellers may use it to venerate their odd foreign gods. 

Old Thistle Ridge Farm (Eastern Bailey)  the smaller of two baileys in the fortified portion of the farm, this area is where the Thistle and other older families live. The houses are a little smaller and lower than the newer, Sembian-influenced bailey beside it.  This part of the farm is choked by lush gardens of every variety and is quite delightful to walk through. 

Oak and Shield (Inn -Old Thistle Ridge Farm), this is the oldest public establishment in the area. Run by the Sweetberry family, who have been here for generations, this is a quiet and respectable inn that has clean beds, wonderful food, and excellent stabling for mounts and other animals. The proprietress is Janus Sweetberry, widow and mother of a dozen (I think, or more) very healthy children who run the place with courtesy and attention to detail. 

Shrine to Silvanus (Old Thistle Ridge Farm)  One cant miss the giant Cormanthor Oak that grows outside this small wooden shrine. The oak itself is the real centrepiece for the worship of Silvanus here, with the small building serving to hold some accoutrements specific to their faith, such as it is. 

The Sembian Bailey (Western Bailey)  This is the more substantial of the two baileys, holding most of the commerce and people of the settlement.  Although a bit more overgrown than other settlements, the roads are mostly cobbled with well-fitted limestone.  There are vibrant gardens beside most buildings, and berries seem veritably to spring from the pavers themselves when not properly cleared. 

Beneath the Sembian Bailey is a complicated network of sewers and tunnels that deliver fresh water to many buildings and take away waste from even more.  A map of this network is known to exist in the Historic Wing of the Yellow Abbey, but I have not seen it. 

Town Hall  A Class IV fortified building inside the bailey itself, this is more a redoubt than a municipal building.  While it does hold the offices of the Mayor, her clerks and other dignitaries, it was principally designed as a fortress, and would be difficult to assault, easy to defend, with its thick straight walls, and cantilevered battlements atop which command of the streets below is possible.  

The Bastion  A Class IV fortified building that oversees the main entrance to the town. This is a 20 limestone structure that was built in the 1420s and is in the Sembian style of the engineer that the Sembians brought with them a decade before. 

The Garrison  A sprawling network of fortified annexes, this building could be considered a Class III fortified structure, but the Sembian disorganization of the place makes it slightly less than that. However, the main hall is outfitted with all the necessaries for training and lodging troops, and the Bright Spears stationed there seem perfectly comfortable, which is to say, neither too comfortable nor too uncomfortable. 

Shrine to Amaunator  While one of the finest such shrines in our dale, it is certainly the most underutilized, as the size of the Flock here is small relative to similar settlements. The sole clerk here is Daphne Dale, a sensible local unmarried girl who holds an office in the Town Hall next door, and can more usually be found there.  While the Flock is small here, the Alavandor and Clarandal families undertook the expense of erecting this formidable Shrine themselves, no doubt in appreciation for the land deeded them here. The catacombs of the Shrine are nearly empty, and, Im told, connected to the larger network of tunnels beneath the Sembian Bailey. 

The Phylactery  This is a small school and apothecary under one roof.  Started by Aisling Alavandor in the early days of their arrival here, the medicines and students of medicine-making they produce are renowned about the dale and further abroad.  Sisters from Charnel Priory often make the journey here for rare medicines and herbs, and the apothecaries here are known to have the most reliable medicines for fertility.  As mentioned elsewhere, the quality of the products here and their results have bordered on heresy, but formal inquiries have turned up nothing occult or unsavoury. 

Merchants Hall  The second largest building in the settlement, the first floor of this 3-story structure is divided into many street-facing shops that can be rented by any merchant licensed by the town. The second floor is made up of apartments for the merchants, and the third is reserved for the Merchants Guild of the farm which is a strong political force here. The Hall has a large central stone tower which is a defensible structure in its own right, the top floor of which serves as a counsel chambre of sorts for the Merchants Guild. 

It is worth noting that the Merchants Guild is headed by the Alavandor family, and has been instrumental in the economic success of the settlement. 

Artisans Hall  Another large Sembian building with a cosmopolitan purpose, the Artisans Guild of the farm owns and operates this facility.  The main floor is a workshop used in the training of stone masons apprentices, but the 2nd floor sees much more traffic. This floor is a large public house, complete with kitchens, a taproom, a stage (with live music a few nights every tenday), and a busy bar. It must be admitted that the best wine in the Dale is vinted at Thistle Ridge, no doubt due to the productive nature of the soil here and the presence of a Sembian family of Vintners that emigrated here in the 20s, the Goldwins. The matriarch of the family, Maura Goldwin, runs the bar and happily sells her wines and other local beverages to a thirsty patronage.  The third floor of this large building is for apartments for young unattached masons and a few are available for rent from the Festhall. 

Brewery, Distillery, Vintner  With no formal name for this establishment, this is the name on Master Cartographer Vosts fine map of the area.  This is a collection of businesses, really, with the most prominent being the Goldwins vintner, which lends the fragrant aroma to the town square that many visitors enjoy.  It is well known that the catacombs beneath this establishment have been extensively worked to square them up and provide storage space for hundreds of barrels and casks of product, and thousands of bottles. 

A brewer, Darla Dale, buys hops and other grains from local farmers to make a variety of delicious beers and ales, which she both sells locally and exports to the Falls.  Her best customer is her close neighbour, the Artisans Hall, who buys most of her product for sale in the Festhall. 

A distillery here is owned by three brothers, the Kaskills, who produce a variety of creative spirits from their productive gardens nearby. Their most popular distillation is a corn-spirit called Thistle Kiss, for which a dozen lewd pronunciations and variations of the name are equally popular. 

The Alavandor Academy  One of the earliest public works taken on by the Alavandors after their settlement here, this establishment could safely be labelled a Grammar School, in the Sembian sense of the word, or so Im told.  It is the largest building in town, has 3 floors and is also in the Sembian style of architecture (busy, convoluted, elaborate), but is a pleasing structure nonetheless, complete with a fine but small courtyard that the instructors make good use of. The Academy is equipped with a small but impressive observatory for the instruction of astronomy, and is the envy of most astronomers in the region. 

The first and second floors of this large building are full of lecture halls, offices, a respectable library, and a well-appointed refectory. The third-floor acts as a dormitory for a few dozen children, some from the nearby outlying region, others from nearby farms (many families at Gribbons Farm choose to send their children here for instruction, rather than the Yellow Abbey, which bears discussion).  It also has apartments for the Dean of the Academy, Franko Petrark and his family, as well as the small staff that operate the Academy. 

At the present, almost all children of the farm aged 5-12 spend much of the year attending classes here and are given a traditional education in Grammar. Promising students might be invited to stay on until they are aged 15, and learn Logic, Rhetoric and Astronomy from the Dean himself. To date, all of the teachings of the Academy are in line with the general Moral Guidelines of the Yellow Abbey. 

Alavaxis (Blacksmith)  Owned by the patriarch of the family, Pharaxes, this establishment represents a consolidation of the various men and women who ply this most noble trade, under something that is akin to a guild structure one might find in Charity or larger cities.  Alavaxi himself, although a newcomer (of dubious origin, it is worth mentioning, but seemingly harmless) is the head of this guild, and has operated it with efficiency and focus. With the abundance of Iron and Copper from the two mines outside the farm, there are plenty of raw materials to support the operation, and they are a net-exporter of finished goods in these materials. 

Pharaxes himself is a Master Blacksmith and has forged several magical blades in the curved style of his home country. Pharaxes and his wife, Numestra, as well as a few others that arrived with them in the late teens all suffer from a condition of the skin that the best physicians and priests assure me is not contagious.  Their children all seem free of the condition. 

Sulphontis (Herbalist)  Now operated by the daughters of the original Family Medic that accompanied the Alavandors to the Dales and started this business, Sulphontis is a peerless herbalist supply, the envy of many colleagues throughout the Dale.  The women and girls who operate this business have small but remarkably productive gardens around the property, and under a small glass enclosure that produces even the most delicate herbs in the harshest of winters. 

Sulphontis stocks all of the herbs and spices expected for the region, but additionally stocks cubeb, nutmeg, saffron and turmeric, which they grow in their glass enclosure. It is well known that the Sisters of Charnel Priory spend a lot of time with the Sulphonti women when they come to town, and leave with many supplies. 

Without the Walls  Hard by the walls of the farm are 60 or more acres under heavy cultivation, being about ¼ each of wheat, barley, fallow and vegetables. The nearby acreage surrounds the walls, neatly cleared out to 800 or so in each of three directions, with the limestone cliff providing the southern boundary of this part of the farm.  These fields are exceptionally productive, and the animals that grow upon them fat and healthy. 

Hinterland  The well-trained militia of Thistle Ridge has done an exemplary job of pacifying the 16 square miles (or so) around the walled town. Three additional farms with small fortified homesteads can be found less than 2 miles away, as well as a hunting lodge, a copper mine, a pair of towers that overlook the road and the river, and an iron mine a little further to the north. 

Alavandors Farm  This is the country home of the Alavandor family, and the main vineyard of the Goldwin family. Although Ive never been to it, I am told that it is a well-fortified small holding with a stone, 2-storey castle, and many outbuildings. During the summer, most of the Alavandor family can be found on this estate.  A good road leads from this farm to the main town, being perhaps 1 1/3 miles away (northeast of the town). 

Clarandals Farm  The road to Alavandors continues to the northeast another 1 1/3 miles, where it finds Clarandals farm; another Sembian-style summer estate.  I am told that this smallholding is a single castle, small but sturdy, that stands on a good hill commanding the 4 square miles around it. While referred to as a farm, this is really a forward observation post for the settlement and a specialized training ground for the militia.  Here, forest warcraft is taught to the militia, and a small but capable platoon of Foresters is stationed that patrols the northern extent of the hinterland. 

Alavandor Lodge  Also known as the Autumn Estate, this is another small castle with mostly seasonal occupation. From mid-Eleint to mid-Uktar, most well-to-do men can arrange hunts out of the lodge, which are hosted by the Alavandor patriarch himself. Expeditions across the Solmen R. and into the Fog Hills usually produce a bounty of game, including white tailed deer, great harts, black and brown bears, and myriad fowl. The lodge is also a staging point for the Foresters when needed. 

The Apple Glade  a mile and a half north of the town is the source of Thistle Ridges most voluminous export, apples.  Though more than a dozen species are cultivated here, the Dale Burgundy is the most popular eating apple in the region, owing to its sweet and crisp flesh, which preserves better than any other known variety. These can be found in the provision bags of soldiers in every corner of the dale and further abroad. 

The Glade itself is owned in common by the farm, and just about anyone who wants to cultivate a row is permitted to do so.   The Thistle family maintains more than a thousand trees, and these produce the bulk of their exports and profits each year. 

There are no permanent structures here besides a couple of large barns for holding the animals that assist in transport of these goods, and their feed. 

The Copper Hole  Copper Mine & Keep  Located 2 ½ miles north of the town, and accessed by roads that come from Clarandals Farm and the Alavandor Lodge, this is a busy if small mine.  I havent visited it, but I am told it is a neatly ordered affair, exploiting some large veins of copper that have produced more than enough to keep the smiths busy, and exports of ingots healthy.  The mine itself is accessed by a small keep that has been built atop it. Those that mine it are typically farmers that mine throughout the winter; apparently the mine is warm and not unpleasant during the coldest months. The limestone hill that contains the mine is riddled with caves, and it is widely rumoured that these caves connect with a broader network that extends beneath the entire region, and indeed, possibly to the Underdark.  While no major threats have ever been encountered down here as far as I know, Knights of the Shield do make it a habit to poke around there every other year or so. 

The Iron Hole  Iron Mine & Keep  This is the furthest regularly-used resource in the hinterland.  About 6 ½ miles by trail from the town, this is another portion of the limestone cave network that permeates the area. A rich vein of iron has been supplying the retorts and smiths of the area for more than 20 years, since the arrival of the Alavaxis and their people.  This mine is a less-secure setting given its distance from the rest of the settlement, and the depth of the mine itself. The Alalvaxis lead mining expeditions a few times each year that last for a few weeks; just enough time to get the materials needed to keep the journeymen busy.  During this time, there is a heavy military presence here.  Skirmishes with deep-things have happened a number of times over the years, and this remains a Location of Interest to the Church and the Military.

I submit this report to Ambassador Whitmore and would like to thank him and his Office for their consideration for my intelligence. My Background 
I, Malveau Greenleaf, am of partially-elven descent, my father being a Cormanthor Wood Elf born in the 12th century, my mother, Alexi Greenleaf (Im not sure this was her true surname), originally a stable maid at Anathars Dell, in the days before the great battles there in 1372. Ardelusk made the most of my experience; having lived among the mountain orcs for more than a decade, I knew more about their language, culture, religion, and other facets of life than anyone else.  I was cloistered at the Cathedral for more than a year, where I was brought to full health while reciting all I knew to a team of scribes that have turned my account of the mountain orcs into a volume you can find reprinted and dog-eared on many shelves in the Dale. 

By 1400, I started to meet the brave men and women that came and went from the Castle and became interested in the places they had come from, and the battles they were waging and, usually, winning, against the Netherese.  As a small-framed woman, I was taught the shortbow, which I took to quickly. Indeed, I served in the First Company Sun Blades when it was originally commissioned in 1403, and created the archery training curriculum still used to prepare our brave women of the Sun Blades for service today. 

After spending more than ten years at the Castle and ranging the Plains of Netheril, and many excursions into the Wastes, my focus began to wither.  I was in love with a Man, for starters.  He was a mercenary serving at the Castle, and after a cruel wound to his chest, was forced into retirement. I watched him grow weaker as I hardly aged at all, and it was more than I could bear. No longer young even by half elven standards, and finding it harder to keep up to the demands of the life I had made for myself, I came once again to Anathars Dell, to find it completely encompassed by Cormanthor. Her mother had business, and Zarra and I would play together while our mothers worked. Even as a young girl, Zarra was already entrenched in the Old Faith, and I found her fascinating and impressive. I hoped to meet her descendants to talk about her, but found out that her own daughter, Nadia Thistle, who had grown to be an impressive matriarch of Thistle Ridge, was murdered in the Tragedy of 1441.

Zarra was now of middle age, a revered time of life for us of the Old Faith, and her daughter, Nadia was an accomplished priestess. Then came the morning of the 27th of Tarsakh; a very light but very late snowfall gave a nice contrast to the budding trees and forest floor, but most of all to the smell of fresh bread. Yes, indeed!  The bread oven in what is now Ambershields Monastery was fired up for the first time this morning, and the warm smell filled our lungs and hearts with joy and optimism. For here we are, at the wild edge of civilization, trying to carve an existence out of a forest that seems determined to repel us. And this morning we ate fresh bread! 

I no longer use the term camp for our settlement. The carpenters and masons stood up the walls of the central farmhouse quickly, and there is already a shingled roof over it all.  With 2 floors, we have more than enough room to move in all the soldiers and supplies, and are grateful to be out of the elements, fine though this spring has been. We are now calling this New Town Hall. 

The settlers that came last week have proven incredibly industrious, especially compared to my soldiers. Without the need to keep one eye on the treeline for trouble, trusting that the Free Defense League has our affairs in order, the five families that have settled here have made astonishing progress; they all banded together to clear the rubble out of the old foundations of a collection of buildings, and with direction from Billy Mason and John Carpenter, they have stood up stone walls on the first floor, wooden walls complete with exterior plaster on the second, and a finished roof of slate shingles. They have, as I have already bragged, lit the old bread oven, and are now clearing the old foundations of several other buildings around the farm. 

It is worth noting our numbers on this late Tarsakh morning. We are the following; 
 
Free Defense League 14th Company 1st Platoon Dawnshields (20 men and myself) 

We are 58 souls and counting. 
Mayor Winters and the elders from Gryphons Farm allocated a squad of Sunblades and a crew of men to clear the old road as far as Greenwood, and we now have regular visits daily from that settlement. Mostly soldiers, but just yesterday a group of over 20 women, young and old, Chaunteans all, came to bless our farm. They brought a bounty with them, including the flour needed to make the bread which I have just watched my men enjoy this morning. It has taken less than a tenday to establish this route, and the small buildings we have here. I am eager to see what a month or a season brings! 

Of the enemy we have seen very little. We scout only a half-mile from the farm, and there are some thickets that I know the men avoid. We have chased off some very small scouting parties of goblins, but havent seen a single one in a few days. We have, however, run into our Fey allies who seem to be watching our border to the north. We suspect they have been playing mischief with the goblins, confounding and harrying them, if not outright attacking them, in their way. We are grateful for the ties that the Captain made with the Limestone Court. The cost of a quiet northern border has been a few jars of honey and jam up until today.  As I write this, a few of the sprites have turned up, attracted by the smell of the bakery, and just now they are chasing a few of the children (yes, children!) around the monastery, who wave buttered rolls teasingly at the small fey. It is a distraction from our work, but it is also the experience of living that we have all come here to build. Even those of us that have been paid gold to fight and deliver it.
