== 10 Kythorn - The Golden Death ==
On the 10th of Kythorn, the companions crossed into the desert of Anauroch, where their progress slowed drastically. Their mounts' hooves sank into the dunes, their cart often foundered, and they were forced to travel only at dawn and dusk, resting during the scorching midday heat. On the first nights, a lion was often seen atop distant dunes, hunting, the companions hoped, for distant game. Boregar, experienced in desert survival, helped them weather a small sandstorm on the 12th, but even the Wanderer's experience gave them no respite from the awe-inspiring terror of the Aziirla'Dahab - \"Golden Death\" in the language of the desert tribes of the  Bedine - that blew in on the 13th and lasted until the next day. Wearily taking up their journey once the storm had passed, they were disheartened to learn that they had traveled less than 20 miles in those four days - having misread the movements of the sun in the mornings and evenings, they had bun inscribed a tiny, circular path in the Western desert. It seemed as though the desert were not going to let them pass.

== 16 Kythorn - Grandfather ==
200px|thumb|Grandfather of the Desert

Having regained their bearings and found the likely location of the trade road, now swept clean by sandstorms, the companions finally began making slow but sure progress. Early on the morning of the 16th, however, a chance encounter with a fearsome denizen of the sands altered their destinies forever - the magnificent werelion known by the Bedine as the \"Grandfather of the Desert\". The companions were making their way along the roughly marked road when they came upon the great beast and his consorts, sunning themselves on a rocky outcropping jutting out of the golden dunes near the tradeway. The creature gracefully leaped down onto the path, its noble countenance framed by a luxurious mane of golden fur, and as the companions drew up short, struggling to calm their mounts, they heard - or, rather, felt - its demand for a tribute of horseflesh in exchange for safe passage. As Maturin stepped forward to answer the beast's demand, however, he very nearly doubled over from the wave of evil will that flashed from the creature's emerald eyes. Drawing himself up again, Maturin looked at the lion and drew his axe. \"Never.\"

Grandfather and his consorts attacked, and in the drawn-out battle that ensued in the growing desert heat, Maturin, Boregar, Dorian and Sparrow were all wounded by the great lycanthrope. Maturin, especially, took wounds numerous and grievous enough that infection was almost certain, but it was Dorian who perhaps felt the brunt of Grandfather's rage. The lion's great maw closed over the Sembian's neck, and the lion wrung him back and forth like a rag doll before flinging his limp body into the sands. That Dorian did not die of such injury can but be the work of Tymora alone. In the end, it was the dangerous dwarven battlerager, Dalak, who fought the beast off - for though it ripped into him with considerable ferocity, so too did it feel the bite of the dwarf's axe. In the end, Grandfather and his surviving mates were repelled, but the companions had been severely wounded in the process. The party rested for some hours before moving away from the outcropping, and then only far enough so as to be a safe distance away from the lions' haunt - if any distance were safe.

It was a difficult setback - both physically and spiritually. Maturin's journal entry from the following day records the atmosphere of doom that haunted the companions after this incident.

== 18-20 Kythorn - Nightmare at Vuerthyl ==
thumb|200px
thumb|400px|A map of the re-constructed Zhentarim outpost at Vuerthyl, following a bitter night of re-destruction.

After a forced march through the next day and night, their progress much slower now that they were without horses - save, of course, Maturin's trusty steed Warburden, the companions finally found themselves in sight of the first oasis on Garrik's map - Vuerthyl, which the Zhents had fortified as a desert outpost. As they approached, the doors of the compound opened, and a mounted troop of about twenty men galloped forth, and upon reaching them, demanded to know their business. When the companions tried to explain themselves, and asked to be allowed to shelter in the outpost, the captain of the riders offered little sympathy - only a great deal of suspicion. Indeed, the wounded and bleeding companions made a sorry spectacle, carrying only a fraction of the supplies that would be necessary for a desert-crossing expedition. The riders marched them into the compound, where a sharp-featured wizard named Farim oversaw the removal of their possessions and their confinement to individual holding cells, with bars that opened up and out onto an enclosed courtyard - and the cruel desert sun.

Alas, when the sun set that evening, it was replaced by the light of the full moon, and hell itself was unleashed. Dorian, Sparrow, Maturin and Boregar would later swear that when the moon rose that evening, it blazed like a great green eye, and that the light of that eye was the last thing they remembered before awakening the next morning to ruin and destruction. The four companions had indeed been infected by lycanthropy in their clash with Grandfather, and that cursed blood now brought doom to Vuerthyl. While Dalak and Corrina huddled warily in their cells, the four newly infected lycanthropes burst out of their cages to wreak destruction on the Zhent outpost, feasting on camels, horses, soldiers and slaves - before finally attacking each other. When Dalak managed to work himself free of his cell, he, too made short work of some of the disorganized Zhents, but was then tragically brought down by his comrade Maturin - in the form of a ravening half-man half-lion crature. Fortunately, Maturin was attacked by Dorian before he could finish off the dwarf, and Dorian had his feline eyes on other quarry once he had incapacitated his opponent. During the battle, the wizard that had been overseeing the operation quickly realized that flight was the only option. Abandoning his men, he magically flew eastwards into the desert night while chaos continued to erupt behind him.

=== Tainted and Forsaken ===

The next day dawned with little hope. The whirlwind of bloodshed, violence and destruction was but a foggy memory, and the four new lycanthropes awoke to find themselves alone, naked and lost in the desert. Fortunately - if it can be called fortune - most managed to find their way back to the oasis, where they found a grisly sight. Corpses were cast savagely about, ripped to shreds - many clearly half-eaten, a sight which brought the bile rising to their throats. Horses had been butchered, walls clawed through and the stench of blood and offal - already baking in the morning sun - was hideous. The entire place was in absolute ruin. What was worse, perhaps, was that Corrina had gone missing. In fact, she was never seen again, and her story ends in obscurity here.

Dalak, however, was quickly found - still alive, thankfully - and Maturin spent the day at the battlerager's side, tending the injuries he had unknowingly inflicted. Dorian and Boregar spent the day in vain, searching for Corrina, but at length they grimly admitted that they had no way to find her, and had to move on. The road continued, and so must their quest. Silently, solemnly, and somewhat numbly, the survivors built a pyre to cremate the remains, sending a great column of black smoke into the desert sky. They then assembled piles of weapons and supplies that could be salvaged from the ruined outpost. Most of the items that the soldiers had stripped them of were recovered, as well as sufficient survival gear and supplies for the journey to come. Another night and day were spent among the ruins while they cached what goods and wealth they could and waited for Corrina. They finally set out as the sun sank low on the evening of the 20th. They had lost much - a companion, as well as many of the items they treasured and needed for the journey. Maturin, however, left everything behind him that day. When Warburden - the only horse that survived the hellish night - was untethered, the steed shied away from his master's touch, broke free and galloped into the desert at great speed. Maturin knew only too well what this meant - Ilmater had turned His back on the paladin, and so began the Damaran's long, lonesome and godless journey in search of atonement.

== 26 Kythorn to 4 Flamerule - Walkers of the Sands ==
thumb
The remaining companions may well have perished had they not fortuitously met a benign tribe of Bedine, who called themselves the Neshaba, towards daybreak on the 26th. The desert nomads, led by a weather-worn elder named Kabina were quick to offer their assistance to the travelers, despite their ferocious, war-like reputation. Kabina wore a jerkin bearing a large green orb. As the companions told their story, leaving out certain details which they did not care to spread knowledge of, it was learned that the lion they had faced, which Kabina called \"Grandfather\", was in fact a champion of the god worshipped by Neshaba - a deity whose name, it was later learned, was Muir’Quaggoth. Though it was never mentioned, the companions were sure that this band of nomads must have known about the infection that coursed through their veins.

The Neshaba taught the companions how to find sustenance from certain lichens that could be found in the desert, passed along instructions for accurate navigation and landmarking, and provided desert robes that were much more comfortable and suitable than the ragged clothes the travelers had scavenged from Vuerthyl. The companions took their leave of the band, which was traveling in an opposite direction, and set off again into the desert along the path the nomads indicated.

The following day was remarkable in that the unending vista of sand was briefly interrupted by a circle of lifelike statues outside a rocky cavern opening not far off the path. Upon closer inspection, it turned out that these were actually petrified adventurers, which meant the cave was most likely the lair of a basilisk - and the companions wisely (and quickly) hurried on. For the most part, however, the days ahead were largely indistinguishable from one another - dunes, rocks, and the haze of distant windstorms. After several days, the companions realized that what they thought had been the road was in fact a naturally bare rocky shield, and on 2 Flamerule, after attempting to retrace their steps, they were forced to admit that they were once again lost.

== 5-6 Flamerule - Treachery at Olomaa ==
thumb|400px|A map of the Cyricist enclave at Olomaa
After days of wandering in search of the road, the companions spied a plume of smoke rising from the desert on the afternoon of the 5th. This turned out to be the second Zhentarim outpost, the oasis of Olomaa. Knowing that word of their approach - and the disaster at Vuerthyl - may well have preceded them, they decided that it would be best to avoid the oasis - though water was beginning to be a problem. However, in their attempt to circumvent the place, they were intercepted by soldiers wearing similar insignia to those whom they had first encountered at Vuerthyl (later identified as the 14th Company of  mercenaries). The soldiers attacked immediately, but the heroes managed to scatter the company, killing them nearly to the man, but it was clear that these mercenaries were intelligent fighters, and had had elite training.

It was bad luck that the companions' water supplies had been sabotaged during the battle, and they now faced a difficult situation. They decided to take the survivors hostage as a means of securing honest dealings with the other soldiers garrisoned at the oasis. They brought the survivors to the gates of Olomaa after dusk, and negotiated a parley. When he opened the gate to hear out the companions, the Zhent factor they dealt with seemed almost amused at their offer, but agreed to sell them water - at an outrageous price - in exchange for the safe return of the hostages. He also promised that they could set off in peace, and that they would not be followed - though they would never leave the desert alive, he predicted.

It was the next day before the companions realized that the water they had purchased was, in fact, laced with a poison which very nearly killed the three non-dwarves. Fortunately, Dalak remained lucid enough to care for his companions through a day fever and cramps as their bodies purged the toxins. True to their word, the Zhents did not follow - likely expecting that the desert jackals would finish their work for them.

== 11 Flamerule - Broken by Wind and Bound by Fire ==

Days later, as they trekked through the sands, the party encountered another 'Golden Death' storm. This storm's ferocity dwarfed the one that they had weathered previously, and was indeed the worst they would endure. The storm blew hot sand at the companions for a full day and night, and though they did their best to take precautions, the ropes they used to tie themselves to each other were worn through. Dorian was physically blown away, and Sparrow became separated from his companions and was forced to take shelter alone. The rest lost almost all of the supplies they had managed to salvage from Vuerthyl. When the storm finally subsided, both Dorian and Sparrow were nowhere to be found, and no tracks or trails remained to be followed. The desert had seemingly just swallowed the men up. They were barely more than a memory.

===Ilmater's Messenger===
thumb|250px|Shildevan the Wanderer
Three days after the storm had broken, and two nights before the full moon would rise again, a boon came from the desert, bringing new hope to the grief-haunted expedition, particularly into the hearts of the Forsaken Paladin. Dalak, Maturin and Boregar - fearing themselves the sole remaining survivors of the Daggerdale Expedition, had taken refuge at a strange circle of stones in the desert when in from out of the dunes walked a stick-limbed, trampish vagabond supporting a sunburned, delirious and half-conscious Dorian. The man said he had found Dorian the previous day by following birds of carrion to the patch of sand where Dorian had lain, staring at the sun and hallucinating, and drawing what he had thought would be his final breaths.

As he talked, the companions began to realize that there was more to this man than they had thought. His words carried a strength that his frail figure at first belied, and when they looked more carefully at his figure, they saw that beneath the man's hair shirt, his flesh was covered with scars and wounds that marked him as a flagellant. Indeed, this man, Shildevan the Wanderer, was a holy man of Ilmater - Maturin's former patron deity. He said that he was on a holy pilgrimage to harsh lands everywhere, and that the Crying God had guided him to the straying expedition.

=== Atonement ===

Shildevan's eyes burned with a holy fire as he told them of his reasons for being there, and they were taken a back when he named their horrible affliction - lycanthropy - and told them that he, or rather, through him, Ilmater, could cure them. Maturin knew this meant that Shildevan was a powerful servant indeed, to have such favour with the Deity. There was a caveat, however - all had to agree to submit to a holy quest in exchange for such a blessing. When the companions agreed - eager to be cured, no matter the cost - Shildevan intoned a prayer that seemed to bend their wills to his.

To Dorian was given a quest to \"eliminate the threat posed by the Beholders\", during the fulfillment of which, he was not to accept any payment of any kind. Though the clever Sembian agreed to not accept a single \"copper piece\" for the entire quest (\"I won't accept another copper for payment until this whole business is done\", were his precise words), he did not realize the scope of his challenge - he could not know that the scope of the quest stretch over years and leagues, or that it would bring him back to the very streets of Waterdeep he was fleeing.

To Boregar was given the charge of remaining true to his friends, and to return and set aright \"all the wrongs he had left behind\". This, too, seemed an overwhelming task given all the foes they had avoided and left behind during the journey from Waterdeep.

Finally, and with the most solemnity, Shildevan turned his eyes to Maturin, and the feeling of censure and anger that was directed at the former paladin was palpable. More than it had with the others, Shildevan's voice took on a quality that made it seem that the priest was nothing more than a medium for a greater power. Maturin was commanded to free the slaves of Daggerdale, and accomplishing that, he was to return to the desert and face Grandfather - the chosen champion of Muir’Quaggoth - in single combat. Only then would the paladin's sins be expiated. Should he fail in either task, or perish in the attempt, he would never again feel the peace of Ilmater's grace - in this world or the next.

As the companions slept that night, Shildevan whispered prayers over their sleeping figures and tended the a warm fire that brought more comfort than the party had felt since leaving Waterdeep. He made his egress the next morning, leaving Dalak on watch.

==18 Flamerule - Assault on Bhaerlith ==
thumb|400px|A map of the Zhentarim trading and military outpost at Bhaerlith.
thumb|The stuff of nightmare
Recharged both physically and spiritually after their fateful encounter with the holy wanderer, the members of the expedition began to make solid headway along the Zhent trade route. They encountered no other traffic before the evening of 18 Flamerule, when they came to the great Zhent fortress-oasis of Bhaerlith - the \"Hanging Rock\". Despite their sacred charge to eliminate the Zhent threat, the companions still felt that an outright attack was unwinnable. Their best bet, it was thought, would be to pass quietly into the Dales, and strike directly at the heart of the slave drivers' organization. Still, they needed supplies, and Boregar disguised himself as a Neshaba nomad, in order to trade secretly with some slaves for food and water - offering nearly all that remained of their wealth in exchange for their silence.

Unfortunately, the ruse - if it had even worked at all - was quickly discovered, and living shadows slunk out of the desert recesses around the fortress, attacking the companions without warning. The creatures were quickly joined by Fist soldiers and the wizard Farim, who had escaped the terrible fate of Vuerthyl. During the fight, the mage managed to overpower Boregar, and the rotund warrior was dragged struggling into the great fortress. From a safe vantage point above the fray, the wizard then direct a flaming sphere after Dalak. Though he survived the attack, the dwarf's attempt to flee the sphere through the shifting sands as it gained inexorably on him would plague his nightmares for years to come. \"It was all bad in the 'Roch\", he would say in later years. And he was right.

Eventually, however, the companions managed to fend off the remaining enemies and take shelter in the rocks a little ways from the fortress. They were in a tight spot - while they would never have dreamed of abandoning their rotund companion in any case, the fact that he was the only one among them who could help them survive in the desert without a guide or adequate supplies made his rescue imperative. A quick plan was hatched to free Boregar and the slaves - who, it was hoped, would create a distraction large enough to help the companions escape. It was a cruel, calculated and ultimately, Maturin insisted, soulless plan, but there seemed no choice.

=== The Fat Man's Rescue, or Boregar Finds a Hobby ===

At midnight the heroes worked their way into the slave quarters near the great bridge that led to the fortress, and began to open the pens. Though Boregar was quickly found in one of the inner pens, the incursion was detected almost immediately, and as magical floodlights soon illuminated the increasingly chaotic scene, soldiers began rounding up the slaves. The wizard Farim was seen atop the bridge, yelling orders to the soldiers below, and Boregar - uninjured - knew exactly what to do. During his brief imprisonment, he had seen that the construction materials that the Zhents had been using to fortify the oasis were kept in a shed near the base of one of the bridge's support pillars - and that this shed contained the explosive flashpowder that was used in nearby quarries. Bringing his mighty fist down on the head of a guard, he snatched up the man's torch, ran surprisingly quickly to the shed, threw the flaming brand in through an opening in the wooden slats, turned away, and bellowed \"RUN!\".

The results were nothing if not spectacular. As the Wanderer ran from the unassuming little shack, there was a deafening crack, and the shed vanished instantly, replaced by an enormous and expanding fireball that engulfed the support and the bridge above. The wizard Farim was heard to screech as the flames engulfed him and the portion of the bridge on which he was standing collapsed, sending him to a fiery doom below. This proved distraction enough to allow the companions to evade the guards and soliders, who were suddenly facing a slave-mob whose morale had suddenly exploded in an equally dramatic fashion.

Though the rescue had been successful, the men and the dwarf were grim as they hurried to leave the confusion far behind them. Not only had they once more left injustice, slavery and enemies behind them, they had also lost all of the supplies and wealth for which Boregar had been negotiating. What's more, most of the Wanderers possessions - including the enchanted starwheel pistol he had taken from Grimwald in the West - had been confiscated, and there seemed little hope of getting any of them back.

== 21 Flamerule - Nomads ==

On the evening of the 21st, as they struck camp and prepared to continue, the companions once again met Kabina's band of Neshaba, who, it seemed, had made their own way east along secret Bedine routes, avoiding the Zhent-controlled roads entirely. The nomads once again offered food, water and assistance to the travellers, who welcomed it with great relief. Maturin told Kabina of their encounter with Shildevan and the Zhentarim, but the elder simply nodded and tugged his beard before spitting nonchalantly into the sand at his feet. The fact that the men had been cured of their lycanthropy seemed to be without import for the desert nomad.

The next day, Kabina gifted the party with four camels and assigned a man named Didaji to act as a guide for the travellers. With a brief word of farewell, the bearded nomad and his people then disappeared into the southern sands, following no road that the companions could see. Didaji was soft-spoken (when he spoke at all), and indicated that he was to accompany the party as far as the next oasis, which they might reach in perhaps two days travel. Didaji warned them, however, that another great sandstorm was approaching - which proved true over the next two days, as the Golden Death set in once more, this time separating Dalak from his companions for the better part of a day. Didaji found the sand-blasted dwarf in much the same way that Dorian had been found by Shildevan - through the dependable desert technique of waiting for, then following, the carrion birds that such wanderers infallibly attracted.

Despite the storm, the party reached the oasis - known as the Well of the Cloven Rock - just over two days later on the evening of the 24th. The spring - gushing out of a large tor, which had been riven in two by some natural catastrophe - was a welcome sight for the parched heroes, and they spent a night and day there before setting out on the evening of the 25th, as refreshed as they could have had any reasonable expectation to be. Didaji bid them farewell there. After pointing out the direction in which they had to travel to return to the Zhent trade route, he took off alone heading south, quickly disappearing, ghostlike, into the golden dunes.

== Midsummer to 10 Elasias - At'ar's Looking Glass ==
thumb|At'ar's Looking Glass

By Dorian's reckoning, it was dawn on Midsummer's Day when the companions' camels suddenly walked out of the dunes onto a path marked by the great stone cairns that marked the Zhent trade route, and it was with immense relief that the companions set up camp. They decided that they would rest here for a time - for the next day was Shieldmeet, the quadrennial festival established in the calendar of Harptos - and they were finally able to devote some time towards mending and maintaining their remaining, well-worn equipment. The holiday brought out a festive spirit in some of the companions. Dorian, his damaged throat now completely recovered thanks to Maturin's ministrations and the last of their healing potions, surprised all by pulling a whistle from his back and holding an impromptu concert, joined by Dalak beating a shield and Boregar's accompaniment on a drinking gourd-turned-jug. Maturin did not join in the merry-making, but a smile cracked his face for what may have been the first time since Vuerthyl. For a day at least, the companions felt able to relax. None knew what lay ahead, but they had made it this far, and all were thankful.

The companions struck camp that evening, and traveled east along the road, illuminated by the stars, bright and beautiful in the darkness of the new moon. Towards daybreak, they met a caravan that belonged to the Shield Trading Company, which was headed west towards Dekanter. The Shields were incredulous to meet such an ill-equipped party so deep into the desert, and the companions were able to barter for the few scant supplies the caravan could spare. They were warned that a great trial lay ahead of them, a few days travel to the east - the giant furnace of At'ar's Looking Glass.

On the 6th, the companions passed into this final challenge - a perfectly flat, forty-league-long mosaic of coin-sized stones, devoid of sand or water, and named for the Bedine sun goddess - the merciless, spiteful, faithless At'ar. The open flats were covered with ruddy brown pebbles, polished glass-smooth, and which gave the plain a fiery, pebbled appearance. The area captured the sun's heat, and even the party's camels began to limp in as their feet trod the harsh terrain. It took the heroes five uncomfortable days to cross this barrier, and they were careful to avoid At'ar's baleful glare as much as they could during the simmering heat of the day.

== 11-24 Eleasias - Into the Bad Lands ==

===Yin-Xu===
thumb
As the weary companions finally emerged from the great crucible of At'ar's Looking Glass, the glossy pebbles gave way to cracked earth dotted here and there with brittle scrub that, if not bursting with life, reminded the blistered heroes of the possibility of greenery. Clouds returned to the sky, which darkened in the distance. Any relief these clouds might have brought, however, vanished on the afternoon of the eleventh, when the skies suddenly darkened unnaturally, and a voice boomed out of the east that the companions must \"turn back or perish\". The companions were immediately on their guard, but the source of the ultimatum remained elusive. Again the warning came - this time from the north - and when Boregar roared an aggressive refusal, shaking his fist at the very skies themselves, the clouds erupted with lightning and a giant green-blue figure burst forward, attacking the heroes. This, they later learned, was the fearsome ogre-mage, Yin-Xu, engaged by the Zhentarim to thwart the advance of the Daggerdale Expedition which. Yin-Xu's onslaught was fierce, but ultimately ineffective, and he vanished into the east as suddenly and mysteriously as he had arrived once his battery of magics had been exhausted.

The attack of the inhuman wizard reminded the companions that they had as many enemies ahead of them as they had left behind, perhaps more, and their sense of achievement upon entering the eastern badlands was dampened by the thought of how to proceed. According to Garrik's map, there remained one more Zhent outpost before they would properly leave the desert, and in their sand-, sun- and now lighting-blasted state, they had little hope of success. That night, they slaughtered the camels that had borne them across the Looking Glass for nourishment - they had exhausted their food supplies two days earlier - and continued on foot until long after the sun had set and the stars wheeled in the night sky.

===A Path across the Peaks===
Early on the fourteenth, they came to the volcanic hot springs of Oasis of the Coloured Waters, where the road forked south to Tel Badir, and east to Addas Babar (the Zhent fort). The southern fork would take them long out of their way, and take them into a different kind of danger than that posed by the Zhents. It was decided that the party would continue east, but circumvent Addis Babar, trusting Boregar's guidance to lead them through the Desertsmouth Mountains. Plant life in the badlands was becoming more abundant as they pressed on, and the Wanderer felt confident that he could his companions into the Dalelands without the need to risk resupplying at Addas Babar. Even trees had become a common sight, and a day out from the oasis, they carefully skirted a copse infested with a swarm of stirges, the buzzing hive audible for kilometres in the distance. They passed well clear of the site where the map indicated Addas Babar was located, heading north into the foothills of the Desertsmouth Mountains.

The final spectacle offered by Anauroch before the heroes were to leave that sandy domain was the awesome display of fire and strength of a draconic duel between a great emerald wyrm and a younger red male dragon, which dominated the sky south and east of the companions on the morning of the 21st. The two massive creatures jousted for over an hour above the foothills, now only half a day's journey to the east, before the two flew off into the east, the red seemingly pursuing the green, obviously wounded as it sped away. The sight of the dragons brought both fear and wonder to the companions, who could only guess at the perils that lay in wait across the Desertsmouth Range. It took Boregar the better part of two days to navigate the companions over the range, and on the evening of the 24th, the heroes crested an unnamed pass that looked down over the great forests and vales of the Dalelands, where their destinies awaited them.
