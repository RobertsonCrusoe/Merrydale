Another few cool things to note. 1) The Aurelian walls were built by regular citizens which will make for a good comparison. We should be able to replace some labour with unseen servants for example. 2) We will need to survey the wreckage, but the old wall foundations should at least be there which will also cut down on our time. 3) City walls post cannons ended up being shorter because taller walls were not as strong as a whole. I would imagine giants throwing boulders are quite cannon like. So our 15' walls will make sense.


STEPHEN (20 Jan 2024, 13:38):

You might be interested in this Master's thesis, which is way more exhaustive than we need to think about, but it gives about as good an idea of the construction of the Aurelian wall as you're likely to find. The author calculated that building the wall took 12.8 million person-hours in total (which includes excavating, producing the mortar and bricks, recycling materials, etc.)

If the entire guild of 440 work crews of 3 men each were dedicated to the project to the exclusion of all other projects, he figures the wall could have been built in 194 days = or one year in wall-building season.
