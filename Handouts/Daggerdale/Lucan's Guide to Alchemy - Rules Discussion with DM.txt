Lucan's Guide to Alchemy — Rules Discussion with DM
Stephen Robertson (as Lucan) and Les Blackwell (DM)
Email thread: "Lucan's Guide to Alchemy", 28-31 Jan 2025
Thread ID: 194a935308885e74

A 3-message rules discussion in which Stephen presents a comprehensive breakdown of Lucan's alchemist archetype build (taken at level 3) and Les pushes back on the Quick Alchemy mechanic. Stephen argues that Quick Alchemy should be thought of as spell slots rather than free crafting. The thread ends with Stephen's counterargument; Les's final ruling is not recorded here.

---

STEPHEN (28 Jan 2025):

[Detailed breakdown of Lucan's alchemist archetype:]

Lucan has 8 alchemical formulae total:

From Alchemical Crafting (Level 1): Dread Ampoule (lesser), Tanglefoot Bag (lesser), Cognitive Mutagen (lesser), Ghost Charge (lesser)

From Alchemist Dedication (Level 1-3): Acid Flask (moderate), Bravo's Brew (lesser), Alchemist's Fire (moderate), Numbing Tonic (lesser)

Daily resources: 4 versatile vials, usable as acid bombs or for Quick Alchemy.

Quick Alchemy: 2 actions (1 to mix, 1 to use). Quick Bomber feat reduces to 1 action.

At level 4: Advanced Alchemy adds 4 infused items (24-hour duration) in addition to versatile vials.

Questions for DM:
1) Are you okay with an Archetype build?
2) Action economy seems balanced?
3) Did Ribalest's laboratory contain alchemical formulae books?


LES (30 Jan 2025):

I sort of hate the quick alchemy stuff. I'm having a hard time wrapping my head around a character that gets to make a bunch of stuff for free that mimics the effects of magic items. Let me give it a think. Overall, I like the alchemical aspect to the game and the campaign, but as an old Grognard, free supplies don't sit proper with me.


STEPHEN (31 Jan 2025):

[Stephen argues Quick Alchemy should be understood as spell-like ability, not free crafting:]

Think of the vials as daily spell slots, usable for any known formula, but limited in number and duration (until next turn). PF2e doesn't use material components for wizard spells — this is a similar conceit.

Without Quick Alchemy, Lucan would be "constantly stuck in the lab" and could never actually use alchemy in play, since regular crafting takes 4 days per batch. Multiple sessions occurring over a few days of game time leave no downtime for resupply, whereas "Jasper and Mari's spells respawn each day."

The archetype build is much weaker than a full alchemist (who regains 2 vials every 10 minutes during exploration). "If Lucan is going to spend feats on a sideline in alchemy, I want it to be more than what, in effect, would be having a day job so that he could pay half price for alchemical items."
