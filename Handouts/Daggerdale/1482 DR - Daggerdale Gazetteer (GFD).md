DC – 1482 – 3: Daggerdale Gazetteer 
Being the only such document about the dale from Our Century. 
GFD Notes: 
We come now to a primary source completely of my own making.  Compiled in the style of Volothamp Geddarm's famous encyclopedic accounts of other remote places, I was inspired to put together as tidy a summary as I could summon.  A great deal of the information below is from the Yellow Abbey Booke of Surveys and Measures, 1481 edition (the 1482 edition was not published before I left), but the rest is all from my first-hand experience in the dale. 

I have done my best to remain unbiased in my accounting below, but before you furl your brow and proceed to read askance on account of the very mention of bias, let me say that the people, the forest, the mountains, the farms, the rivers, lakes and the very air of the place were such a delight to the senses, that I think I may be guilty only of a pastoral bias.  The numbers, names and themes from the dale have had a hard accounting, and I am sure you'll profit by them. 

I will begin, if you don't mind, by sharing a ballad I heard about the dales while I was a young man.  It stuck with me, and now I wonder if there isn't a bit of old Jonstan the Rover in me.

Geography 
As Volothamp wrote in 1367: 
"If the gods were more disposed to smile on the Dales, this pretty wooded Dale (once known as Merrydale before a tragic infestation of vampires changed its people's character) could well be the most popular destination for hunters in all the Dragon Reach.  Its rolling hills are only lightly settled and teem with game of all sorts.  Streams and unspoiled ponds are everywhere , and one can see in this pleasant land many ferny bowers, caverns, and picturesque ruined cottages." 

Although much has changed in the last 100 or so years, that description still holds, with now some exceptions for large settlements, and the great forest of Cormanthor doing its best to hedge them in.   

 Daggerdale is nestled deep within Cormanthor, a forest which is now rumoured to be full of Feywild Passages, which unwary travelers may not realize they have traversed.  The forest is mostly composed of Cormanthor Oak, a thick-trunked leggy tree that often grows to over 60' in height and diameter.  In many parts of the forest, the trees grow at about five-times the rate one would expect, which means a very rich supply of lumber for the area, but also a constant struggle to keep fields and roads cleared. 

To the west of the dale are the Desertsmouth Mountains, unchanged in name even though the desert of Anuaroch has now been transformed into the emerald fields of Netheril.  It is the will of those in Daggerdale that Netheril return to the Shadowfell, and perhaps they are keeping the name of the mountains in hope of their victory over a much greater foe.  The mountains could be described as medium-sized, with many high enough to be snow-covered throughout the year, but without the glaciers and other extreme elevation features seen in larger ranges to the north. 

Much of Daggerdale has been settled in the Tesh Valley, a rich-soiled valley that cradles the great river that comes down from the mountains to the west, and eventually finds its way to the Moonsea far to the east. 

To the south and east of Daggerdale are the Dagger Hills; a maze-like network of hills and small limestone cliffs and caves that has been swallowing up explorers for centuries.  In the current era, Cormanthor has swallowed up the hills as well, adding to the confusion and danger of travel there.  The hills are rich in ores, and in bygone eras men and dwarves attempted to mine these riches, but today the hills are home only to goblins (as far as intelligent beings go), and scores of different types of monsters.  Owlbears, large spiders, stirges, wolves and dire wolves are but a small sampling of the dangers (or exciting hunting opportunities, depending on your vocation) to be found there. 
Politics 
In our era, the settlements of Daggerdale are nearly entirely large walled villages the locals call 'farms', though they are more like fortresses, complete with a standing army (more on that below).  Surrounded by large well-kept fields, the 'farms' typically command a hilltop from which they can see anyone approaching for nearly a mile.  Of course, this is made possible only by a constant effort to cut back the encroaching forest, but the prevalence of the church of Chauntea in the region makes this task a bit easier. 

The people of Daggerdale have always been described as dour, but I find that generalization to be either outdated or perhaps unfair.  Although they take their security seriously (getting into Dagger Falls involves answering a lot of questions from 'magistrates' that feel more like inquisitors to me), the people are friendly, if a little aloof.  Travelers to the region can expect friendly nods from the locals, but little in the way of conversation unless pressed.  There are exceptions, of course, and many of the inns would be considered comfortable and well-appointed anywhere I've been, and the company friendly if not lively. 

As of 1482, Daggerdale is governed by a Governors' Assembly, which is composed of members from each farm, the prominent churches, and significant allies (more on those, below).  This Assembly is the closest thing to a regional government in the dales and is largely the result of the strength of the Church of Amaunator in the area, and their desire to organize a common defense and the payment thereof.  Each individual settlement elects a Governor by its own custom, and these governors meet semi-regularly in Dagger Falls to discuss the defense of the dale.  This is a new organization, but it is made up of very successful organizations that preceded it. 

Beneath the Governor's Assembly is the General Assembly, which is made up of many different groups, religious, business, labour, and adventuring companies, and it meets regularly to propose rules and actions for the Governors' Assembly.  Overall, the dale is run very well, and has been for over 50 years. 

The day-to-day governance of Dagger Falls is headed by the office of Mayor.  It is worth noting that this office never had a formal election process, clearly defined duties, compensation or clearly written authority.  Until the Daggerdale Concordat, this position was something of a ceremonial honorarium, as the town's defence and other matters were handled by various other organizations.  However, the Concordat clearly defines all of these things, and formally acknowledged Lilly Cassimar as the current Mayor.  Mayor Cassimar is a friendly woman of means, who enjoys hosting large dinners at her family home in the centre of the town.  She is a dedicated Sunite and is known for her stunning appearance but even more stunning manners, patience and kindness. 
Prominent Religions 
There are two main religions which essentially run the dale; the Church of Amaunator, and the Church of Chauntea. 

The Church of Amaunator is headed by a Zenith Lord, currently Argent Whitegate, currently residing in Castle Radiance in the mountains.  The Zenith Lord is primarily concerned with countering the evil of Netheril, and as such is located as close as safely possible to Netheril.  The church is effectively run by the Bishop of Dagger Falls, currently Blaze Whitegate (grand-nephew to Argent), who resides in the Cathedral.  Beneath the Bishop are the magistrates of the dale, who have a complicated hierarchy and disparate duties.  The term 'magistrate' could mean anything from a country church priest to a 

dangerous inquisitor.  The Church has an impressive institute of learning called the Yellow Abbey, which is adjacent to the Cathedral of Dagger Falls.  This is a seminary for new priests and magistrates, as well as a grammar school for children as old as 18.  The Yellow Abbey has a library called the Historic Wing, which is a repository of secret and semi-secret writings that is difficult to gain entry to. 

The Church of Amaunator had two military branches, until 1482; the Order of the Lambent Shield (an order of Paladins and other holy warriors), and the Sunblades.  After the Daggerdale Concordat (Elesias, 1482 DR), the administration and cost of the Sunblades was passed onto the Governors' Assembly, but the ranks are still filled with many worshipers of the Sun God, including most of the officers.  The Sun Blades can be seen on every settlement and road in the dale, and are usually friendly, though some are accompanied by zealous inquisitors who will ask many uncomfortable questions of new travelers.  The Lambent Shield is usually deployed from Castle Radiance, postured against the military might of Netheril. 

It should be said that most people in the dale speak well of the prosperity that Amaunator has brought, especially as delivered by the Whitegate Family, who grew the religion and the defense of the dale for decades.  Though the family is nearly extinct now, after a terrible battle with a villainous group of druids known as the Dark Garden, some of the highest-ranking positions in the dale are still held by the few remaining old men of the family. 

The Church of Chauntea is something you will find in every dale, and Daggerdale is no exception.  Every farm has a large flock of worshipers, and the Church's position in the dale is enshrined in the new Concordat that ensures it a voting seat in the Governors' Assembly.   

Though not as well organized or hierarchic as the Amaunatori of the dale, the Chaunteans do have a functioning network that allows them to share information and work together with their neighbours.  The head of the church is chosen every year after the Greengrass Festival, which is one of the highlights of the year for all members of the dale (it is quite lively and oftentimes licentious) and carries the title of High Cultivar.  The rest of the clergy are simply called 'Cultivars', which could represent a neophyte priestess, or an accomplished arch-priestess.  The clergy are mostly female, but the body of the church itself is evenly split between males and females. 

The current High Cultivar is Madeleine Oakdale, of Crossed Blades farm.  'Maddy' as she prefers to be called spends only a tenday per month in Daggerfalls (the third tenday is when the Governors' Assembly sits), but is otherwise found on her farm where she remains the head of her faith. 

Many other gods are worshiped publicly or privately in the dale, and an approved list of these gods can be found posted in Dagger Falls, and in many of the border-settlements of the dale.  The Approbia Dei as it is known, is also publicly posted along with outlawed gods on a list known as the Approbium Dei. 
Military 
As mentioned in the section on Religion, the Sunblades can be found just about everywhere in the dale.  This organization is made up of platoons of 24 soldiers lead by a sergeant.  Three platoons make up a Company, lead by a lieutenant.  Divisions are composed of 1 to 4 companies and are lead by captains.  

The captains in turn are lead by General Dauntless Thunderstorm, who is the head of all 18 Companies of Sunblades, or roughly 1200 soldiers plus officers. 

At every farm and other settlement you will find a platoon or more of Sunblades.  They are typically dressed in chain shirts, trained to fight well with swords and axes, and every other man bears a heavy shield and is trained in the defense of the men adjacent to him.  These platoons are the elite core of the defense of the dale and are actively involved in the training of the Militia, which is many times the size of the Sunblades. 

In every settlement, you will find that a high proportion of men and women of able body are trained in their defense, with arms nearby to address sudden threats.  Though not considered a 'regular standing army', many an invader to the dale has learned the hard way that these men and women are far from green conscripts.  The young women of the dale are infamously dangerous with short bows, which they use for sport and entertainment as well as defense, and the men are all deadly with axes, both swung and thrown. 

The Amaunatori Knights known as the Order of the Lambent Shield, lead by High Commander Luminous Whitegate, are rarely seen in the heartland of the dale.  They are stationed in Castle Radiance and are typically deployed on the front with Netheril.  This is one of the most battle-hardened fighting forces in Faerun in Our Time, by many estimations, with even the lowest ranking men and women being veterans of scores of major battles.  Typically found dressed in half plate armour and wielding magical longswords, these men and women also display a high degree of individualism in their gear and tactics. 
Allies & Enemies 
Daggerdale's first and most reliable allies are its neighbours.  Shadowdale is the only dale with what could be considered a shared border with Daggerdale, and although in bygone centuries relations were tepid at best between them, these two dales conduct a great deal of trade between themselves and will quickly jump to defend each other when needed. 

The Kingdom of Tethyamar, which is undergoing rediscovery and reorganization is a very strong ally and trading partner, and indeed holds a seat on the Governors' Assembly.  At the time of writing this, a little over 1,000 dwarves have come to the area to reclaim the halls of their ancestral home, but more than half have chosen to stay living above ground, in some of the settlements of the Dalesmen.   

The elves of Myth Drannor are a true ally, although their operating timeframe makes them seem aloof to the common folk of the dale.  Holding a seat on the Governors' Assembly as well, they are rarely in attendance, as they are busy with their own struggle against their enemies both ancient and new.  They have not made a notable military contribution to Daggerdale in the last 10 years or so, their absence from the escalating war with Netheril and the Dark Garden noticed and commented upon by many who bled at those battles.  What is not fully understood by any but the most well-informed diplomats of the dale is that the efforts of the elves of Myth Drannor make a tangible contribution to the war with Netheril, and are likely responsible for that great enemy not having the strength to overrun Daggerdale on a whim. 

The Kingdom of Cormyr has long been involved in the politics of Daggerdale.  In the 14th century, the Cormaeril family had a claim to the rulership of the dale, though that was short lived (dying with William Cormaeril in the late 1390's).  Today, Cormyr is an ally of necessity, as they both share a devious and determined enemy in Netheril.  Cormyr essentially provides a bulwark against Netheril for the south-western portion of the dales, and similar to Myth Drannor, siphons off enough of Netheril's might to keep them from overrunning smaller enemies.  An embassy from Cormyr Drannor used to be located at Castle Radiance until the Concordat of '82, at which time it was moved to Dagger Falls.  A small garrison of Purple Dragons can be found at the Embassy in town, and a larger force of 12 Knights and 120 regulars are stationed at the Castle, sharing the defense against Netheril. 

The primary foe of the dale is Netheril, and specifically one of the Twelve Princes of Shade, Melagaunt.  Melagaunt has been tasked with taking command of Daggerdale but has been unable to do so.  While he has many agents, chief among them is his 'Curator', Loki Mouvranesh.  Mouvranesh is an accomplished spy and sorcerer and has been the author of many devious plots against the Dalesmen.  In recent years, she supported the Dark Garden coven in their plot to overthrow the dale, as well as the brutal cult known as the Sons of Bel.  While the Dark Garden was defeated utterly in 1480, many of the instigators from the Sons of Bel escaped justice and remain at-large, whether within the dale or without, none I've met can say. 

Like many nearby polities, Daggerdale has constant low-intensity conflict with organized and semi-organized groups of demihumans, such as the orcs that issue from the Desertsmouth Mountains, tribes of goblins from the Daggerhills, and Hobgoblins from the northern reaches of Cormanthor. 

Of old, the chief enemy of the dale were the Zhentarim.  The events of the late 14th century put an end to the Black Network's hold over the dale, and especially over Dagger Falls (which it held fast for many years), but we again hear of trading costers coming out of places like Voonlar, Hillsfar, Yulash and Teshwave.  Although merchants from these costers claim that they are a peaceful lot that have simply been identified as 'Zhentish' due to their "Moonsea-ish looks and bearing", some names from the past have re-emerged; one such coster is Company Drurginz, which recently annexed the ancient and storied Shield Trading Company in Voonlar.  Now operating as Company Drurginz, it has reconnected routes from Sembia to Waterdeep for the first time more than a century. 

Many local adventuring companies could also be considered 'allies', though some more appropriately could be labelled 'mercenaries'.  The Free Defense League, lead by Captain Bargle Battlemaster, is headquartered at Castle Radiance, and has nearly 40 members who take 'bounties' posted at the castle (these are often related to orcs and Netherese scouting parties that the Lambent Shield doesn't have the time to deal with). 

Another, the Redjacks, is lead by the prickly Hastle Redwater, a veteran of dozens of campaigns.  His Redjacks number more than 60, and are very capable, if somewhat self-interested, soldiers and adventurers.  The Redjacks have a tower at Castle Radiance where they also take bounties, and now have a new lodge in Dagger Falls, from where they hope to grow their footprint in the dale. 

Finally, the famous White Cloaks round out the largest of the private companies.  This is the youngest of the three organizations but is the fastest-growing and has had the most spectacular successes (and failures too, though those tend to not make any songs).  Their victories against the Dark Garden and the Sons of Bel have cemented a place for them in the hearts and lore of the dale.  Ostensibly allied with the Church of Amaunator, they permit members of all faiths on the Approbia Dei.  I give a full accounting of their holdings and roster in the Dagger Dale Chronicle (c.f. DDC – 1481 – 1), but it is worth repeating here that they are lead by Captain Iolus Warburton, a paladin of Amaunator and native of a now-abandoned farm to the north of the Falls.  The White Cloaks hold many properties, including estates in Dagger Falls (Loedegan Close), Charity (Arwencroft), Tethyamar (Jarl Orgen's Hall) and Castle Radiance (White Cloak Tower).

Economy 
Daggerdale has always been a place of tough people eking out a tough existence from the land.  This is still largely true today, thought their security and profitability have both increased. 

Most of the inhabitants of Daggerdale live within one of the major walled 'farms', and work within bowshot of the ramparts.  With a flourishing church of Chauntea in the region, farms are generally productive, producing an abundance of the types of things you'd expect; 
 Wheats and other grasses 

Fishing is generally considered a hobby in the dale that many people participate in, but in Dagger Falls, there is a rich tradition of making a living from the bounty of the Tesh River.  This is now also true of Charity, where fishermen that work the Charnel River make a good and safe living. 

There are many productive mines in the region, especially around Charity, where iron, copper and other metals are mined and refined in abundance.   

The chief exports of the dale are wool, lumber and metals.  Only Charity focuses on the export of refined goods rather than raw resources, and this strategy has served their economy and their landed families well. 

Until about 1481, a gold mine owned by the Whitegate family drew a great deal of interest from people from around Faerun, whether merchants, mercenaries or just people looking to settle someplace new and with a hope of prosperity.  With the mine nearly empty, the chief draw to the area now seems to be the stability that it is enjoying, and the growing amount of trade, especially in refined and luxury goods, that is now starting to go in and out of the dale through its major trade corridors (the Tethyamar Trail to the south, and the Tesh River/Trail which turns into the Moonroad to the east). 

This increase in stability and trade has once again attracted the caravans of the Moonsea region after nearly a century of isolation.  Costers from Yulash, Voonlar, Hillsfar and Teshwave are once again showing up at the gates of Dagger Falls and most of the other settlements of the dale. 

Dagger Falls 
It is worth spending a few pages to go over the changes that have taken place in the Falls over the last several decades.  What was a dangerous, muddy, and dreary camp in Volo's time is now a thriving and impressive fortified town, clean, prosperous and safe. 

My initial impression of Dagger Falls was misleading, as it is for most.  I approached from the Tethyamar Trail, having come from Shadowdale to the south.  The last couple miles of road before the town are broad and well drained, having been ditched on both sides of the road expertly, with many places having stone reinforced embankments.  Along this road, you get many glimpses of the town from miles away, as you wind up, around and down the hilly terrain that slowly descends into the Tesh Valley.  The shimmering roof of the Cathedral is a spectacle, even at several miles distance. 

As you top the last hill before the final bit of road leading down to the river and the town itself, you get a very good look at the fine thick walls, behind and before which is a great bustle.  The town is busy, and it has spilled out from the expanding walls; there are small farms on either side of the roads leading from the town and some fine lodges being built by the newly well-to-do in the area, of whom there are many. 

To enter Dagger Falls from the South, you must first wait in the Watergate Customs Court, a large staging area adjacent to the bridge and customs building from which it derives its name.  I arrived on the 9th of Flamerule, 1482, and was one of more than 100 travelers from several caravans queued to enter.  The court itself is a 100 by 100 yard 3-sided affair, as you'll see from the map below, with new barns left and right, and the customs house, a new inn (The Skiff and Wheel) and the Watergate gate ahead.  While I waited, magistrates interviewed every single person desiring entry to the Falls.  From my personal 'interview' with them, it is clear that they are looking to keep out undesirables, followers of Shar especially.  It is also clear that the settlement is more xenophobic than other similar-sized settlements around the Realms.  They admit almost no other races beyond humans, halflings, dwarves and the occasional dragonborn.  In fact, the Skiff and Wheel seems to cater to those that are 'unfit' for entry into the Falls, which is a large portion of caravan guards with a touch or more of orc in their blood. 

It should also be noted that a full company of Sunblades was present, as well as a pair of Knights of the Lambent Shield.  While the Sunblades were professional and aloof, even friendly, the Knights were silent, vigilant and frightening.  Everyone gave them a wide berth including myself. 

Once you are approved by the Magistrates and issued your 'papers' (which you are likely to be asked to produce many times in the Falls until the locals get to know you), you then cross the Watergate, formerly known as Lordsbridge.  This was built in the time of the Zhent occupation last century, and is a very impressive structure, being essentially a small fort that has been built across the river. 

Inside the first gate you find yourself in the Eastern Bailey, another feature built by the Zhents in the 1360's & 70's.  Caravans that have been approved to enter the town are required to lodge themselves and their mounts here, and this courtyard was abustle when I arrived.  There were animals being lead every direction, and merchants unloading boxes, barrels and crates of every variety.  Most of the trade between the town and merchants occurs in the Eastern Bailey, making it the liveliest part of the town.   
The two most conspicuous structures in the town are the Tower and the Cathedral. 
The Tower, which has had many forms and stories since the 900's, is now a very intimidating redoubt commanding the highest part of the town, perched at the edge of the escarpment which produces the falls from which the town draws its name.  The walls about the Tower have many watchful Sunblades surveying the town about them.  These are mostly eagle-eyed young women with short bows. 

On the second-highest part of the town, the Cathedral Bailey (rising about 50' from the river), the Dagger Falls Cathedral of Amaunator catches the eye, its gilded roof resplendent even in slightly overcast days. 

Most visitors will spend most of their time in the Old Bailey, which contains most of the fixed commerce in the town.  Most buildings in this bailey have shops of some sort on the ground floor, with 2-3 floors above for apartments.  The Old Market Square is a small courtyard about 75 yards a side, and is the only place in this Bailey where open-air market stalls are allowed.  Natives of the Dale can apply for a permit to set up tables and tents here to sell their pre-approved goods.  This is where most of the food for the town is procured, as farmers, fishermen, hunters and trappers sell their goods here.  Many other cottage-goods can be found here as well, such as baskets, blankets and other textiles, and the like.  The Old Market Square is sedate compared to other such places in the Realms, and doesn't come close to what I would call a 'bazaar'.  A small team of older boys keeps the Square pristine. 

The cleanliness of the Eastern Bailey and Cathedral Bailey make them stand apart from any similar settlements I've visited in my travels.  In recent years, the Whitegate family installed a system of sewers beneath the streets here, before cobbling them over expertly.  Littering is a crime immediately punishable by steep fines or jail time, and it seems like everyone here takes this seriously. 

The Cathedral Bailey was built by the Whitegate Family during the height of their influence in the 1450's.    The town was already expanding beyond the walls of the existing Baileys (indeed, the Red Rock Tavern had been here for more than a century), and the family began one of its most impressive building projects, the Dagger Falls Cathedral.  The Bailey itself rises gently up the slope of the valley, giving the Cathedral a commanding view over the town.  The Cathedral, as well as the attached Yellow Abbey, Historic Wing and the surrounding cemetery sit atop a fine terrace that overlooks the rest of the Bailey. 

Many new organizations and buildings call the Cathedral Bailey home, including the General Assembly Hall, the home of the new regional government as well as the older guilds.  This Bailey is the new centre of commerce for the lettered professions that have begun to grow in the town, and the industries that support them.  One can now find fine bookshops and scribing goods wedged between the offices of accountants and legalists. 

There aren't as many taverns as one would expect in a prosperous town like this.  There are some establishments that date back to Volo's time that still do brisk business (The Red Rock and the Broken Dagger), as well as the inns outside of the gates which have the only rowdy taprooms you'll find for 20 miles, but little else besides.  The only 'new' establishment inside the walls is Quick Axes Tavern and Inn, adjacent to the Old Market Square.  This is a large and impressive building, if not a bit foreboding.  It was a temple of Cyric in the 1370's, and was given to the Quick Axe brothers for their part in liberating the Falls from the Zhentarim.  This tavern is more lively, with great beer and live music, and the inn is respectable, clean and staffed by friendly people. 

Security inside the Town is as good as I've experienced.  It is as clean and safe here as the tightest streets in the Castle Ward of Waterdeep, and you are rarely outside the view of group of Sunblades or a wandering magistrate. 

Overall, the people inside the gates are polite, if not gregarious.  Despite the anxiety of the Customs Court and the ordeal of gaining entry, the town itself is calm and produces a feeling of safety and prosperity.  I enjoyed many luxuries in Dagger Falls I would not have expected to find here;  a coffee house with a hot and exhilarating drink that matched the political discussions of its patrons, a public bath with perfectly warm and perfumed waters and company, and a book store with rare and interesting books and patrons. 

If the people of Dagger Falls and their allies from the hinterland and beyond are able to keep Netheril (and other enemies) at bay, I don't doubt that this town will soon be a destination, rather than an interesting bywater, for most of the great mercantile and other interests of the Realms.  Like the rest of my visit to the Dale, I was enchanted by the place, and as I finish this final narrative, seated comfortably in my den in my modest Delzorin Street apartment, in I find myself, like old Jonstan the Rover, longing to return.
