== The Trouble with Tremalkin ==

It was an example of Maturin Horgath putting aside his own agenda once more and making himself available to the needs of others. The summer was ending and in the Graypeaks, winter arrives quickly and like a hammer striking an anvil. Two men and one dwarf left the outpost that would eventually be known as Falcon's Roost in the late summer of 1374 DR. Battle-worn Ilmatari, Maturin Horgath, the dwarven priest, Korugar Helgorod; wavering in his duties to Berronar Truesilver and an increasingly despondent Gaebril Tremalkin, First of Shar. The three left upon their mounts, with Tremalkin uncharacteristically unarmoured and unarmed. His ferocious looking black plate, bundled away with his menacing black sword upon the hindquarters of his ebony destrier. The first night that the three made camp as they ventured in the direction of the Monastery of the Adamant Hearth, Korugar was sleeping deeply and snoring powerfully, leaving Tremalkin and Horgath at the waning embers of the fire. There had never been much time to speak since the battle at Murtaw's Watch, or to discuss what transpired during that battle between Gaebril and the goddess he once seemed so devoted to.

\"What happened, Gaebril?” inquired Horgath. Tremalkin stayed silent for a while, simply looking into what was left of the fire. Horgath, a paragon of patience allowed his friend to take all the time he needed in his response until eventually, the pale-skinned paladin of Shar looked up, his jaw, tensed with emotion and the dying light of the fire dancing and shimmering upon the tears that slowly slid down his face. He spoke in a voice that was fraught with weariness and thick with ragged emotion.

\"I never should have tried to forget Kyria. I should have endured her loss...like you would have done, Maturin.\" Horgath said nothing, he simply gave his friend a grim smile and simple, curt nod of understanding and acknowledgement before Tremalkin began to speak further. \"The Lady has...she...everything has changed. She used to bury me in darkness, so perfect, so absolute, so cold that nothing could get in. It scoured my soul clean. She's no longer content with that. Something is changing Maturin. Something is coming. I can feel her more and where that should feel...it should feel good, it's the furthest thing from it. There's a desperation to her now. A hunger. She wants blood...and power. She's not content with providing solace any longer...and the sword...\"

Tremalkin's voice dropped to a whisper, his dark blue eyes looking to his packs resting upon the ground outside of the amber circle of light provided by the fire. \"The blade never stops. It wants souls, Maturin. Gaucon'Suhl Nerhuar is full of hunger and hate. I believe that were I to plunge it into the heart of the Morninglord himself it would draw all of the light from the world and still its hunger would not be sated.\" Gaebril let out a shuddering breath as his voice grew bolder once more and less a desperate whisper. \"I am afraid, Maturin. This is the second god I have turned from now. I am inconstant and weak of will. I am embarrassed and ashamed of all the people I have failed and the oaths I have broken.\" Horgath simply listened and at the end, assured Tremalkin that upon arriving at the monastery, there might be a course of action they can set upon to help him.

== The Monastery of the Adamant Hearth ==

Speaking of his fears seemed to help Tremalkin some although he certainly could not have been described as jovial or gregarious, the journey to the Monastery of the Adamant Hearth was decidedly less grim than when it started. The monastery and the dwarves that worshipped there welcomed and accepted the presence of the two paladins where some other religions or places of worship might not have done. Horgath learned more of dwarven culture and Berronar Truesilver, and the dwarves in turn learned more of the teachings of Ilmater and never failed to be impressed by the fortitude and constitution of the Chosen of Ilmater. It was an austere existence for all that called the monastery their home and what was meant to be only a visit, turned into a year, followed by another. Horgath was kept busy with converts from the original faith at the monastery and he became one of two spiritual leaders there for the two faiths that worshipped and studied there. He was also kept quite busy as well as others who assisted him in the \"saving\" of Gaebril Tremalkin, once First of Shar and two times oathbreaker. It seemed that the prolonged lack of exposure to the blade was having an adverse affect upon the once-paladin and after the first year, he had been forced to be held in locked stone room, only to be tended by Horgath as his actions, thoughts and words were becoming more and more erratic and dangerous.

== A Discourteous Visitor ==

Treating his friend was beyond Horgath's expertise as well as being beyond what any of the other priests were capable of. Falcon had visited twice since the paladins had arrived at the monastery, the ever-present confident smile upon his face only wavering when he had seen Tremalkin's fate. He had been busy with the rebuilding of Falcon's Roost, but he vowed to find help for the man that he shed orc blood with and true to his word, three months later, with snow well above the heads of the dwarves that stayed at the monastery, a slender, cloaked and hooded visitor arrived. Maturin Horgath was widely regarded by the dwarves of the monastery as being unflappable which was why they were surprised by Maturin's clearly displayed discomfort around the visitor that had arrived. When the hood was pushed back, an elf was revealed. Bald, with a face ravaged by scars, and wearing a pair of odd-looking insectoid-like goggles, the elf denied all offers of comfort, heated drink or opportunity to rest even a moment after such a long, cold journey.

\"Take me to him,\" was all he said to Horgath for the moment. Despite the lack of warmth shown between the elf and Horgath, the paladin complied and led the elf to what was now little better than a cell for the former paladin of Shar. The elf titled his head slightly from one side to the other as he stood in front of the heavy stone door, almost as if he was looking through it to see what was within before he let out an almost imperceptible sigh and reached out for the door. A heavy, calloused hand upon his shoulder stopped the progress of the elf; the hand belonging to Horgath, accompanied by a warning from the paladin.

\"Be wary. He is not who he once was,\" the elf turned his head, his brow furrowed in clearly displayed irritation as he looked down at the hand upon his shoulder before looking up at the much taller paladin. Horgath smoothly slid his hand away from the elf's shoulder even as the elf gave the man a look much the same as a parent would give the child before speaking in a low, calm voice, edged with just a hint of sorrow.

\"It would seem that none of us are. We have history, he and I. Leave us to it.\" The elf's attention left Horgath and returned to the door, unbarring it and opening the heavy stone door with a display of strength that was surprising for so whip-thin and then proceeded to close it without looking back.

== The Nerve of Steele ==

The elf was in the room alone with the spiritually ravaged paladin for just over a day, all while Horgath patiently waited outside the room. When the elf left the room, he looked to Horgath and spoke in the manner he had since he arrived. Calm, plain...

“Where is it?\"

Horgath spoke in return. \"It is put away.\"

\"I sincerely hope it is more than simply a matter of being placed aside, paladin,\" was the elf’s slightly contemptuous response.

\"We are well aware of the dangers the blade presents to those that wield it. Do you need to see it?\"

\"No...no, you are not, paladin. You are not aware at all,\" the elf snarled quietly. \"And no, I do not wish to see it. The answers needed to assist the archer are beyond anything I can provide. I know of a man that can pull answers out of the air as you and I pull our next breath. I will try to find him and send him here.\" The elf turned back to the closed door and reached out to touch it with a black-gauntleted hand. His fingertips brushed against the stone door and, shaking his head slightly – almost in lament – and speaking in a low, intimate tone, he asked, \"If not mages, then gods ha? Be well, archer.\" The elf then turned away from the door and walked swiftly past Horgath, with the paladin looking surprised as well as relieved that the elf appeared to be leaving.

\"You will not take rest or break bread with us?\" asked Horgath. Without looking back, the elf's dark cloak swirling behind him, the elf responded as he pulled up his hood.

\"Unlike some in this realm, my time as well as Tremalkin's is finite. I do not plan on squandering it when it is better spent seeking answers. If I can find the Oghman, I will send him. See to it the archer survives until then.\" A cold blast of air washed past them all as the large doors to the monastery were opened and the elf was enveloped in the swirling snow that awaited outside.
