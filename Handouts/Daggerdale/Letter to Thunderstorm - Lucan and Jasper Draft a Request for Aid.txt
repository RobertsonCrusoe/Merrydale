GREENWOOD FARM - EVENING
A sturdy wooden table in the corner of the main hall serves as an impromptu writing desk. JASPER sits hunched over a piece of parchment, quill in hand, while LUCAN paces energetically behind him. JESKA's notes rest at the edge of the table.
LUCAN (grandly) “From Company Chrysalis, Masters of the Eastern Front, to General Thunderstorm, who hides behind the walls of Fort Lastsun—”
JASPER (quickly interrupting) Perhaps just “From Company Chrysalis, Stewards of Greenwood Farm and Thistle Ridge, to General Dauntless Thunderstorm, Commander of the Sunblades”?
LUCAN (waves dismissively) Bane’s brass bollocks, Jasper, you're as dull as an Oghman’s quill. Fine, have it your way. Though I bet the man strokes his family tree more often than he strokes his sword these days.
JASPER (blushing) I've got the salutation. Please, what's the next part?
LUCAN (clears throat dramatically) “While your men polish your armor, among other things (winks), we face imminent slaughter by goblin hordes. Two hundred of the beasts march on Thistle Ridge Tower with their blades out and ready to thrust into our defenses.”
JASPER (wincing) Must you make everything sound so... indecent? Maybe we could state the facts without antagonizing the very person we're asking for help?
LUCAN (sighs) “Intelligence gathered through my careful and inspired questioning of Sanduskin Vale and his owl familiar confirms that a massive force of approximately 200 Torn Cheek goblins, accompanied by siege engines, is on the march and will attack tomorrow.”
JASPER (writing, muttering) “I write with grave urgency regarding an imminent threat...”
LUCAN (noticing, annoyed) That's not what I said! Hoar’s whores, man! You’re writing is as stiff as a troll in a brothel. Speaking of which, make sure you mention those ugly brutes. Huge, veiny chaps with their throbbing muscles and arms almost as thick as my—
JASPER (interrupting quickly) Lucan!
LUCAN (grins) Fine, fine. Just making sure you're paying attention. Write what you will, but make sure to mention the trolls. And don't forget to mention the alchemicals we need.
JASPER (resolutely) I'll mention the trolls and the need for fire and acid to counter their regeneration. In appropriate terms. And I'll detail the alchemical weapons we've seen the goblins use, and what countermeasures would help.
LUCAN (looking over Jasper's shoulder) Don't forget those goblin bombs. Nasty things, exploding all over everyone. Makes a terrible mess. Gets in your eyes, your mouth...
JASPER (cutting him off) Yes, I'll detail the alchemical weapons and what countermeasures would help. “In our previous encounter, they used caustic compounds that burned through armor...”
LUCAN (satisfied, for the moment) And the payment? One hour, ten questions with Myth Drannor. Tell him it's a better deal than he’d get at any brothel in Sembia.
JASPER (firmly) I will do no such thing! I'll say it's invaluable knowledge against Netheril, which it is. “As payment for this extraordinary aid, we offer access to Myth Drannor's ancient knowledge: one hour per month during which you may ask ten questions to assist in your western campaign.”
LUCAN Make sure you emphasize the strategic advantage we’re offering. A thousand years of intelligence versus his...what? Fifty years of fighting the same battles? Even a desk-bound commander should understand that if Thistle Ridge falls, the eastern approach to Dagger Falls is wide open.
JASPER “Should the eastern territories fall, it would create a strategic vulnerability...”
LUCAN And don't forget the settlers. There are families here now because they trusted us to keep them safe.
JASPER I'll mention them.
LUCAN (grinning mischievously) “So what’s it to be, Thunderstorm? Are you a Sun-blade? Or a Sun-blunt?”
JASPER (puts down his quill in horror) We absolutely cannot write that!
LUCAN (laughs) Come now, Jas! We need to light a fire under him. Men like Thunderstorm need their pride pricked occasionally. Gets the blood flowing. The good general sits comfortably in his fort while we risk everything. How about: “Will you live up to your name Dauntless Thunderstorm? Or shall we call you Gutless Goldenshower?”
JASPER (firmly) No, Lucan. The General is fighting his own war against Netheril. Jeska specifically instructed that we need his help, not his indignation.
LUCAN (gives an explosive sigh) Fine. Now end it with something about how history will judge his response. Nothing puts the fear of the gods in a Tormite like the threat of looking cowardly to posterity.
JASPER (writing, tactfully editing) “Time is of the essence, General. The fate of the eastern territories rests in your hands.”
LUCAN (begrudgingly approving) Not bad. Subtle, yet pointed. Though my ending would have gotten a faster response.
JASPER (signing the letter) “With respect and urgency, Ambassador Jeska Monarch, by the hand of Jasper Hawkford and Lucan Alavandor.”
LUCAN (stage whispering) Let’s add a postscript? “P.S. Your wife sends her best. She mentioned you haven't wielded your sword in quite some time. Perhaps this is your chance to remember how?”
JASPER (horrified) Absolutely not! This letter represents all of Chrysalis!
LUCAN (laughing) Just testing your resolve, my uptight friend. Though I do wonder if the good General ever dreams of real battle anymore, or if he's content pushing little figurines around a map table.
Jasper shakes his head, carefully folds the letter, and reaches for the sealing wax, visibly relieved that the diplomatic integrity of the remains intact.
