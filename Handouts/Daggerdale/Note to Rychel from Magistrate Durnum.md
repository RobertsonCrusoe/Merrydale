Mr. Voordan;

I have need of your professional and renowned services. Rather, my Office has need of them. I am not in a postiion to be seen going into the office of a man such as yourself (no insult intended, you can be sure), and so I thank you for indulging me this manner of correspondence.

If you would be so kind as to meet with me at 7 bells tomorrow evening outside of the West Gate, I should be in your debt. Please forgive me the brevity of ntoice, the unusual venue and the dark-lantern manner of this business. I regret that I can say nothing of this business in this letter, as I am sure a man such as yourself can understand.

Yours in Justice,

Court Magistrate Durnum

PS; Please bring this letter with you to our meeting. I shall require it as proof of your identity.}}
