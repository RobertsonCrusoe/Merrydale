This is the second in a series of missives smuggled out of Dagger Falls by an unnamed Harper agent during the Zhent Occupation of 1372. The letter is dated 25 Eleint, 1372 DR.

Things are changing here very quickly now, and it is difficult to keep track of it all.

I was personally looking on yesterday when you and your friends marched those hundreds of slaves out of the Eastern Bailey and south to freedom. My heart leapt for the first time since I started this assignment, and later I wept, that our hard work has finally brought about some good.

Well, there is more, though good or bad I cannot say. It is difficult these days, and with this type of enemy…

First of all, early this morning, William Cormaeril addressed the town, declaring the rule of the tyrant Yarkul to be at an end, and declaring himself the rightful ruler, by birth, of this town. He announced that Yarkul had been killed by a Harper assassin. I suppose this isn’t true, but whoever did that job has my nomination for certain.

thumb|Tabards of the Garrison of Cormaeril's Dagger Falls
At any rate, the garrison all donned new tabards of yellow, with a coat of arms of four fields: one a purple dragon, one the Daggerdale coat, one with the Cormaeril sigil, and the fourth quarter a tacky rising sun. The twenty or so men that surround Cormaeril wear purple tabards of the same style.

Someone had to be expecting this to happen, as even the cleverest invoker would have had a difficult time conjuring up a couple hundred bright yellow tabards in one day.

I heard a commoner in the tavern this evening remark that “at least the Zhents didn’t make such a show of lording it up over the people.”

I have a source that sits on the council in the Tower who filled me in on some of the political changes that William is going to instigate:

#No religious oppression and no tolerance of religious quarrels. The town will be paying to build a temple to Lathander as soon as a capable priest is found.
#Absolutely zero slavery. Slave ownership and trade is punishable by hanging, or at the end of a sword…
#Establish a trade route to Cormyr. What they plan to do about Tilverton, I do not know.
#An alliance between the Dales is also desired by William, as WELL as with the town of Yûlash. I don’t know how that can be done right now.

That is all I know about the political changes that Cormaeril wants to make.

A couple of other events here are worth mentioning as well.

Today at about noon, a bunch of the Garrison dressed in yellow tabards marched into the temple of Bane and slaughtered the clergy to the last.

This came about after a man in the town council accused the Banite church of retaining its slaves. I don’t know who it was that made this accusation, but it is an interesting play; certainly all the Churches in town and likely the Tower itself still have slaves in them. Nothing would be done about this if not for the publicizing of this fact by a power-elite member.

At any rate, 24 more slaves became free men and women today, and the Church of Bane in Dagger Falls is no more.

Related to this, my informant on the Council tells me that he was told in confidence by William himself that he (William) is worried about the three demons living in Brotherhood Tower (the Xvimite knights’ building). He knows he lacks the strength to overcome the Baron and his remaining men, and this keeps the voice of Hatemaster Dragkul a very powerful one on the council. Certainly, we shall not see the people who are enslaved in that horrible place freed anytime soon and no accusations have been made against the Xvimites.

I feel this is something we shall have to deal with ourselves.

The Fist may not be out of the picture yet. Riders came in this morning from the north road, reporting that they had been ambushed by a group of ogres and hill giants while trying to reach Addas Babar. From the report, ten men were cut down in seconds, and only two managed to escape. From what I have been told of the tactics used in this encounter, it sounds like elements of the 5th and 12th Companies are watching the road to ensure that no one will be able to either follow the ‘Beholder Stones’, trade west of Dagger Falls, or both!

Things appear to be getting better, but our war is far from over.

That is all for now.
