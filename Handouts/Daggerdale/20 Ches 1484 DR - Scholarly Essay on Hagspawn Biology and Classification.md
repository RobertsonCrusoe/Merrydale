Once known as "Changelings" or "Quicklings" by the ancient Netherese (and earlier peoples—what ho?!), we have called them Hagspawn in these parts of Faerûn for as long as I can remember—and that's long enough, even by elvish measures, now. 

I should start with the boys, for this is where the greatest misconceptions arose so long ago. But no—let me rather begin with the habits of progeny among these remarkable creatures. 

To begin, I must say: though they appear terrible to most Faerûnians, Hags are still Fey, and this means they are capable of great and wondrous things—though they do tend toward vice, and more than a little mischief. But couldn't the same be said of just about anyone worth the ink for a remark? 

Like all races, they have a drive to reproduce, which is why people of all walks of life have been discussing them for over ten millennia. As a rule, they do not take a single mate (would that others followed their example!), and unlike most species, they appear able to breed across a wide range of others. In fact, it has been surmised by scholars of various degrees of competence that their "survival strategy" (belonging to their race, if not each individual) has been to conduct a sort of experiment with their wombs—to see what issue may result from this mate or the next. 

It is fairly certain that in the dimmest morning hours of civilization, trolls, ogres, and other foul creatures were early successes of these experiments. In the Western Heartlands, there is—such as we can build it—a round consensus among our order that the Bugbear, though no longer imbued with many vestigial fey qualities (beyond a preternatural stealth far greater than their size should allow), is the product of copulation between certain Hags and the hobgoblin race. Hags remain fond of both, though only bugbears seem to appreciate that fondness—if only in their vice-ridden fashion. 

I have thus established that Hags will mate with a variety of prospects (lucky girls!), and it is well known among the learned that they give birth to both males and females—whom we may call "changelings," if you like. 
Let us begin with the boys. 
For the most part, they kill the boys. Why this behavior should persist over the millennia, we do not know—but it is well attested that such is their practice. And yet there has, through all this time, remained a confusing notion that all children of Hags are shapeshifters. This is true only of the males. The term "Changeling," as used by the Netherese and translated into our vulgar tongue, referred specifically to male offspring. 

True shapeshifters, possessing the highest degree of control over their abilities, appear in tales that echo through all ages—terrible and wonderful stories, for the most part. But I have never met a true changeling. I suspect very few ever do, as the infanticide rate for such creatures is nearly perfect. Only rare escapes from the mother's deadly wishes occur, usually made possible by rival Fey. 

Now to the girls—numerous and interesting, though meaner than their mothers and possessing fewer of the Fey qualities we might admire in Hags. 

Let me return to the title of this chapter: female Hagspawn always have two differently colored eyes—one representing the nature of their mother, the other reflecting that of the father. 

Thus we may find, in regions such as Saerloon, where Sea Hags have dwelt for generations, disparate sisters walking the countryside or the shore—usually causing mischief or worse, or (once in a long and unusual while) something wondrous. These sisters share both similarities and distinctions in appearance. This one might be tall and willowy, with fair, nearly imperceptibly blue skin, a sea-green eye, and a blue eye—marking a Sea Hag mother and a human or half-elven father, while another might be stout and mean of aspect, green-skinned, with a sea-green eye and a grey eye—born of a Sea Hag and some other monstrous sire. 

Much can be learned from studying the eyes of these creatures; the vice and narcissism of their mothers are oft betrayed in the daughters' gaze. 

Here are some of the known eye colors of Hags in the Heartlands East of the Stormhorns: 

 Night Hags, found south of the Dragon Coast, bear always an eye of jet. In this century, they favour the vile devil-folk who now walk with impunity in gentle places, and produce in a changeling a reddish eye. 

 Moon Hags, often found clinging to the crumbling tombs of centuries past, bear an eye of putrid milk. They favour, when they can pin them to an old sarcophagus, mates from the Fey Realm (now over-abundant by any reasonable estimation), and produce changelings with eyes of glorious amber or shimmering saffron. 

 Swamp Hags, who have plagued the Dales since before the erection of the Standing Stone, have eyes of crystal grey. They favour any stiff mate they can trick or capture, often breeding with the "nephews" of their race (in true Dales fashion): goblins, hobgoblins, and bugbears—producing myriad distasteful hues in mean-eyed villainesses. 

 Annis Hags, whom the Purple Dragons have never succeeded in extirpating from their Royal Forests, bear purple eyes to match their natural hue. As everyone knows, they prefer the very Men that the Purple Dragons vainly seek to protect. From such men they often produce a clear blue eye, though occasionally brown—where the Cormyrean line has been muddied long before the unholy copulation in question.
