A letter from Jasper Hawkford to the Lady Khara Sulwood​
​

My dearest Khara,

I am alive, well, and progressing with my appointed task within Daggerdale. The past few days have been

eventful, and I have found myself now entrenched deep within the Question itself.

We’ve left Dagger Falls behind as victors of the competition to retake Thistle Ridge, and have now begun in
earnest to carry out the terms of the contract.

Our first task was the re-establishment of a decrepit farm which had previously held the name Greenwood. This

ruin lies about a half-day’s walk from Dagger Falls, though the location has been overgrown, threatened with
being consumed by the expanding Cormanthor.

The military units which now serve as our retainers established their first camp at Greenwood, but our small

company carried on to clear out the ruined Tower that sits within a mile or so of Thistle Ridge itself.

I will save the details of our battle and victory in taking this tower until we are once again together at the Old

Skull. While the defeat of the goblins that held the place was decisive, and we managed to take out a bugbear of

some infamy, the grisly scene that greeted us when we delved into the inner areas of the tower is not yet
something I want to revisit in my mind in order to set it down in writing.

I will mention that we were able to liberate one prisoner, a Hillsfarian merchant by the name of Jendwendle who

had been a part of the vanished Durginz Coster Caravan. The man had undergone a horrendous amount of

torture, and I dare say I am not sure if he is lucky to have survived. We were able to get a small amount of

information from him, but I fear the man’s mind is almost entirely gone, and it will take some great effort and
considerable time to bring him back.

After our liberation of the Tower, we returned to Greenwood - except that along the way we came across a most
peculiar shrine hidden deep in the wilderness.

The goblins set up an ambush for us at this shrine, which we were able to dispatch thanks to the usual heroic
efforts of Ser Rowan and the Lady Jeska.

The shrine contained similar elements to those found at the House of Plenty. While not exact, some of the

iconography had echoes and shades of the Chauntean; and yet it is hard for me to say for certain that the shrine

served as a place of worship to the Earthmother. In fact, there was a sense of some unease there that I could not
quite place.

I have learned of the existence of a Netherese deity referred to as ‘Jannath’ which has similarities to Chauntea,

but it is difficult to say whether they are one and the same, or if the Chauntean faith simply adapted and absorbed

                                                         some of the symbology and ritual of this older faith when
                                                         Netheril fell.

                                                     In any case, the existence of the shrine is very strange, and

                                                     has been nagging Lucan’s mind as well as mine. Lucan

                                                     has drawn a rough sketch of the shrine and some of its

                                                     statuary, which I have included along with this letter in the

                                                     hopes that you might be able to discern some clues about it

                                                     from the Harvestmistress at the House of Plenty.​
                                                     ​
                                                     I will return to the matter of this shrine further in this letter.

                                                     But for now I must turn to the heart of my report.​

                                                     Upon our return to Greenwood, we discovered that one of

                                                     the workers that had been hired by Chrysalis to reclaim the

                                                     farm had had his tools stolen, and he himself had gone

missing. This, in addition to several chickens having been taken as well. We were informed by Sir Reginald

Boxfray - a good and proper Dalesman from Battledale; I think you’d like him - that faeries had entered the camp
and had begun to cause mischief.

We have encountered the influence of the Feywild before within these woods, but this theft inside of our

protected farm had the promise of spiralling into a chaotic nightmare that would hinder our efforts to the east.

Or so we thought.

As we followed this new thread, we’ve discovered that the presence of the Fey within Cormathor is not only far

more pronounced than we had previously thought, but that their presence here is both nefarious and intentional,

posing a far larger threat to both Daggerdale and Shadowdale than anything we had previously considered,
except for the Netherese.

In fact, while we had previously assumed that the Fey and the Shades were in opposition to each other, the

information we have uncovered suggests that, at least in part, there seems to be some semblance of cooperation
between both factions here in Daggerdale.

But I am getting ahead of myself.

Our first search proved fruitless as the fairies had obviously created a false trail to confuse anyone attempting to

discover them. Fortunately, both Jeska and Mari were quick to see through the ruse and set us in the proper
direction.

We eventually discovered our missing mason, enthralled by a creature we eventually discovered was named

Vainya. The exact nature of faerie that this Vainya was I would learn later,, but suffice to say that she was quite
formidable and dangerous. We were able to defeat the creature with
some difficulty, though she escaped into the depths of the forest at the
moment of our triumph.

I have asked Lucan to draw up a sketch of this creature for me as, I
must admit, I never actually saw her myself. During most of this
skirmish I was beset by a horde of monsters I would later learn are
called Grimples, and by the time I was able to join my comrades,
Vainya had gone.

During this rescue, I was able to befriend an amazing sprite by the
name of Masra. With her aid we were able to put an end to the battle
once Vainya had retreated and before more innocent fairy lives were
lost.

I should mention now that through Masra we were able to learn that
this group of Grimples and Sprites had been bound in service to a creature named Dol’Rullian. This creature had

taken their ‘Contessa’ - a powerful fairy named Calix of the Limestone Court - hostage, and bound her servants

and followers into his service by threatening the Contessa’s life. By agreeing to free the Contessa from
Dol’Rullian, we gained some semblance of trust from the sprites.

                                         Masra herself proved a fountain of information about the presence of the

                                         Fey in this area. (I have asked Lucan to sketch a portrait of Masra for

                                         you, though I do not think he captured her likeness very well. I should

                                         explain that Masra dislikes Lucan quite intensely, and judging by the

                                         sketch he has handed me, I believe the feeling may be mutual.)

                                         For starters, we learned that the presence of these creatures in the forest

                                         goes back both much further than we had previously thought, but also

                                         much more recent as well. I know this seems contradictory, but I shall
                                         explain.
We know that Cormathor accelerated its expansion as a result of the Spellplague, and in some areas we have

fought a losing battle against the forest as it grows and consumes much faster than we’ve been able to hold it

back. For a long time we have assumed that this growth was centred on the old Elven Court and around the area

of Myth Drannor. Indeed, that has seemed to be the case for us in Shadowdale as our eastern frontier continues
to recede year after year.​

What has become clear, however, is that there are other areas, perhaps scattered throughout the Dales, where

the Feywild has broken through into the Realms and created these expanding islands of extra-planar influence.

I will add that it is reasonable to assume that what is true for the Feywild, is also true for the Shadowfell. Like

these islands of Fey, it is quite likely there are similar islands of Shade beyond the confines of Netheril, and these

are scattered and well hidden throughout the Dales. In fact, it is becoming more and more apparent that such an

‘island’ exists within Thistle Ridge itself, and even in the Limestone Court (though again, I am getting ahead of
myself.)​
​
What concerns me is the likelihood of something similar lurking within the borders of our own home. If this is the

case, then the looming danger you have sent me to investigate is probably already entrenched within our own
Dale.​

I will leave that grim thought to the side for now, as I return to the subject of Daggerdale.​

The first ‘Contessa’ that we learned about is a Dryad that goes by the name of Quercina. It is said that she has

been in our world for over a century, and had set up her Court within the confines of Thistle Ridge itself, and had

bonded with the great Oak Tree known as Harngorn. Harngorn was cut down and burned during the Tragedy of

Thistle Ridge, a traumatic event that caused Contessa Quercina to collapse into a deep depression and

eventually disappear from sight. It is unknown what became of her. I am certain that finding her and restoring her

to her ‘Oaken Court’ will be key if we are to successfully reclaim Thistle Ridge.

It is of course natural to ask why we should ally with the Fey in an attempt to stop the expansion of Cormathor

and establish deeper roots of human civilisation here. On first glance, the two seem to be opposed to each other,

as the forest actively fights us off, and Man actively pillages the forest. But it is precisely why ingratiating

ourselves with the Fey will allow us to pursue our goals against the Shadows without having to also fight them
.
I remember when we were children and our parents took all of us down to see The Standing Stone after it was

restored and moved to Ashabenford. We stood in front of it, and I said that I didn’t understand why everyone as

making such a big deal about it, since it was just a dumb rock. I remember you very patiently explained to me that

it was a symbol of the covenant that the Elves and Chondathan settlers had made. We were allowed to settle

these lands as long as we respected the rules. That lesson has remained with me all these years, and it is a

valuable one today. If we hope to permanently reclaim Thistle Ridge, we will need to establish our own compact
with the Fey, or we will be fighting an endless war we have no hope of winning.

Quercina’s aid becomes even more relevant, when we learn about the next Contessa: a truly despicable hag that
is known by the name of Palus.

Contessa Palus rules something called “The Sorrow Court”, and according to Masra, is both sister and enemy of

Quercina. Where Quercina established her bond with the Harngorn and seemingly lived peacefully with the

original residents of Thistle Ridge, Palus has allied herself with the Shadowkind and is directly responsible for

The Tragedy and the murder of the three girls. She has control over the Yellow Tooth Clan, and likely dwells in
The Sorrow Bog south of the River Tesh.

Contessa Palus has emerged as one of our primary antagonists, and yet we had never heard the name before

today. It is with her that the forces of Shadow and Fey have converged, and have stoked the flames unleashing
the goblins and other dangers unto this Dale.

As with other topics I have raised in this letter thus far, I will return to Palus momentarily.

The next Contessa is a dryad that is known as Fluvia, and she rules over something called The Flowing Court.

The only thing we know about this faerie is that she arrived about three decades ago, and has kept herself

entirely secluded. Why she chose to come here, and whether or not she remains here, is unknown. Not ever

Masra seems to know more. Finding her will prove difficult, but necessary, as we build alliances and continue our
investigations.

And our final Contessa, is Calix, whom we have rescued.

Calix seems to have arrived at the same time as Fluvia, and created the Limestone Court in an abandoned mine.

Over the decades she cultivated ties with the farmers at Greenwood, and was downright friendly with them.

However, I am unclear as to why her existence was not made known to us prior to now. Of course, I suspect

Amaunatori censorship from the likes of Invictus. I am also more curious now about the downfall of Greenwood

Farm. Greenwood was destroyed by Dol’Rullian some time ago, and the Limestone Court offered little

protection. Calix only became imprisoned recently, when Dol’Rulian returned, so there seems to be an empty
space in this story that needs some elaboration.

As mentioned, Dol’Rullian came to the Realms previously with a deep hatred of humanity and civilization, and

destroyed Greenwood Farm. He seems to have left, but recently returned to cause havoc again once we began
the reclamation process.

With his capture of Calix, and Masra’s information, it became apparent that we needed to act immediately.

With some luck we were able to obtain a map from the Tower which had some rough details of the enemy’s

locations in the area. One of these was the location of the Limestone Court, though Masra and her friends were
able to provide us with guidance that proved invaluable in ensuring our success.

It is here that I should mention that I have become “bonded” with Masra. It is a strange ritual of fey magic that will

take much more paper than I have at hand to explain, but suffice to say that the strangeness of this bargain is

unlike anything I have experienced before. It is both euphoric and terrifying. Imagine approaching someone and

smiling at them. The other person feels happy, and smiles back. This in turn makes you happy, but you can also

feel the other person’s happiness. And again, in turn, the other person feels your happiness, their happiness, and

the magnified happiness that both of you have created. And so, this never ending loop of happiness continues,

reverberating, and growing back and forth, letting that small initial emotion grow and grow and evolve into

something altogether different. I use the word ‘Terror’, but I do not think that does justice to what this is. But as I

say, to explain further would require far more paper, ink and time, and truthfully I do not understand but a single
leaf on a single tree in this expansive forest that I have entered.

It is enough to say that I have entered into some form of bargain with a fey creature, which is precisely what all
those bedtime stories we were told as children warned us not to do.

However, it is this bargain that afforded me some semblance of protection from Dol’Rullian’s snares as we
entered into the hijacked Limestone Court in an effort to free Calix.
.
The battle resolved itself mostly as you’ve grown to expect by now. Lucan immediately set fire to the woods,

Jeska was a whirlwind of death, Ser Rowan bravely absorbed blow upon blow, Mari’s healing grace kept
everyone alive, and I struggled to find a moment to use my magic.

What was somewhat unexpected was Lucan braving the worst of the dangers in order to try to rescue the

Contessa himself before Dol’Rullian’s minions could execute her. I usually do not give the man sufficient credit for

his bravery, but I am told he selflessly took on the challenge and saved not just Calix, but likely all of us as well.

I should say that I did spend the majority of this battle quite far away from the action as I was once again beset by
Grimples who prevented me from casting what magic I could.​

One of the surprises was the appearance of Vainya’s sister, another creature which is called Vallia. Since I did not

see her myself, I have asked Lucan to provide me with a sketch, but he essentially drew exactly the same picture

as he did for Vainya such that I cannot tell which is which. I have not bothered to include the second drawing, as I

am told that they are sufficient enough alike that should you encounter them you will know.

In the fight to liberate Calix, Dol’Rullian was defeated, though I fear that by his very nature as a Fey creature he

remains alive, nursing his wounds and plotting revenge in the Feywild. We will have to deal with him on a more

permanent basis now, though none of the options open to us is appealing. He will return to the Realms, in which

case he will do so in a more powerful guise and likely with stronger allies. By his very nature, he will strike when

we least suspect it, or when we are at a disadvantage. Even if we do defeat him again, he will return to the

Feywild and rebuild his strength. He is, after all, an immortal creature if I understand the nature of the Fey from

what Masra has told me correctly. He can only truly be defeated in his home reality, which is the Feywild. This

would mean having to travel to that plane and hunt him down in his own territory. I do not believe we are capable

of such a task. The third option is to secure the allegiance of powerful Fey so that they may trap Dol’Rullian in the

Feywild and prevent him from returning to the Realms. This, of course, would require a bargain, which might
prove to be far more dangerous than fighting Dol’Rullian himself.

We can do no more right now but speculate and prepare. Hopefully, we have sufficient time.

I should mention as well that both Vainya and Vallia escaped. Unlike Dol’Rullian who was physically defeated, the
sisters fled into the forest and are likely also plotting imminent revenge.

It is a dark thought I should keep with me at all times.

On the brighter side, the defeat of Dol’Rulian and liberation of Calix brought about a wondrous change to the

Court that truly took my breath away. While it is difficult to describe the wonders and mystery of a landscape

touched by Fey (I suppose the only acceptable word is simply: “more.” Whatever you think it is, it is more) the

transformation back into its happy form, free of the corrupting influence of a creature like Dol’Rullian was truly

something to behold. The entire grotto changed from emanating menace, to emanating joy. The peace that came

over all those of us in the Court was infectious and truly something not of this world.

I remarked earlier regarding the bond that I had created with Masra. It will be many years before I think I can

properly convey what happened next, but such feelings of joy, relief, happiness and excitement all being felt at

once, and magnified beyond comprehension… I’ve heard the word “stupefied” used before without properly

understanding its meaning. I think I understand now. Ser Rowan tells me that I was “stupefied” for quite some

time, unable to speak or move. I was overwhelmed with such emotion that my body completely closed down.

Khara, we have known each other since we were born. We’ve grown up together, and shared everything in each

other’s lives. You were once betrothed to my brother, and had he lived, we would’ve been bonded together as

family. We have no secrets between us. So you must trust me when I say that what transpired next is something
I can never tell you.

I will only say that we were treated to the finest of Fey hospitality and I shall leave it at that.

Later, we learned from Jeska of the events that had transpired that led us here.

Calix had indeed arrived around 30 years ago, and settled her Court in this forgotten limestone mine. A location

well hidden and protected. Unfortunately, as I have said, our efforts to reclaim the nearby Greenwood farm

attracted the attention of Dol’Rullian, who had previously destroyed the settlement. He arrived with the sisters

Vainya and Vallia - whom I learned are creatures called “Nuglubs” - as well as with his Grimples and other

creatures referred to as “Twigjacks”. Dol’Rullian himself is something called a “Grimstalker”. All these terms for

such dangerous creatures are somewhat difficult to keep straight, but I am trying to learn the intricate differences
of the fey heirarchy.

Destruction is Dol’Rullian’s purpose, and though I initially thought that those that follow him must also share in his

appetite for desolation, as I learn about the complicated politics of the fey I wonder if these other creatures aren’t

following him out of some semblance of bondage or bargain. If this is the case, it might be possible to set them
free, or alter their allegiance.

Indeed that seems to be one of the primary currencies of the fey. I am bonded to Masra, and in turn Masra seems

to be bonded to Calix. When first I encountered Masra she insisted that I promise to free Calix. Looking back on

it, I am beginning to suspect that when I made that promise, perhaps some form of fey bargain was struck that I
was unaware of.
.
It brings to mind the promise I made to Lady Coventina Cassimar.. When I remarked that I would see Loki

Mouvranesh dead, she became almost possessed of a maniacal desperation as she insisted, and in fact

demanded, that I swear I would kill the Curator. The similarity in the way that both Masra and Coventina made
me make those promises is curious.

Calix revealed to Jeska the existence of a blocked passage within the Limestone Court, or rather, I should say, in

the mine where the Court resides. Dol’Rullian showed great interest in gaining access to this passage, but was

unable to do so. As Jeska and Calix relay to us, “He was unable to damage even a single stone.” The news that

there is such power that can prevent access for a fey creature such as him is heartening. Perhaps we can learn
the nature of this so that we may trap him when he returns.

The nature of this passage is unknown, but it is somewhat safe to assume that there is a link to the Shadowfell

beyond. One of these islands of Shadow I spoke about earlier. Calix says that Dol’Rulian’s interest in it revolved

around gaining allies and power from the Shadow, just as Contessa Palus had done before.

We now return to the hag.

As Calix tells us, Palus has perhaps been in the Realms far longer than Masra had surmised, and indeed has

been here since long before the Spellplague. We know that she made a bargain of sorts with the creatures of the

Shadowfell, gaining great power, which allowed her to precipitate the destruction of Thistle Ridge.

I mentioned previously that Palus had emerged as a primary antagonist, and catalyst for all the death and

destruction that has visited this land. What follows here is a transcription as best as I can remember of the words
that Calix spoke regarding her fellow Contessas:

“When I came here, I knew about Palus, and her sister. They were the Contessa of the Fen and the Contessa of

the Grove, she and Quercina. They coexisted peacefully for more than 100 years. Then the world changed, and

the Shadow came. Palus allied with the Shadow; she gained great powers, but lost so much to gain them;

indeed, she had a daughter and a grand daughter, and these she sacrificed to the Shadow, and other daughters

besides. She used her powers to gnaw at Thistle Ridge from the inside, and her treachery was such that she

tricked a girl inside that farm to play a cruel joke on her friends. All sacrificed, so terrible. Palus’ daughter, grand

daughter, the girl from Thistle Ridge and her three friends. So cruel and unnatural.”​
​
I will interject here that this revelation sent a terrible chill down my spine. The cruelty and horrid evil of sacrificing

one’s own children not just for the lust of power, but for wanton destruction and murder is beyond me.

This entire journey I have been following the trail of these three girls. To learn that they were victims not just of
murder, but of an intentional malicious game makes me boil with an awful rage.​
​
I came to Daggerdale to find out the nature of the threat and report back to you so that our own Dale could take

steps to protect itself and our people, should the Shadow spread. As I learned more of what has transpired here,

I must say that despite everything, my allegiance now is to those girls. I will not stop until I have found justice,
peace and eternal rest for Lilac, Lily, Poppy and Alaventa.​
​
  I will continue with Calix’s words:

“But the men of Thistle Ridge are also to blame. They profaned the Waters and the Grove. Not the original men

and little Sisters, mind you. The new men. They closed up the Grove, and they drove the Waters to the depths.

Palus cackled about it when I met her. I sought her out to see if she knew where to find Contessa Fluvia and

Contessa Quercina, and she said “the foolish men came, looking for the place where the water would meet the

roots, and they found it, or made it, or both. They delighted in their profanity, for it served their purpose. They

came to fix a curse of generation on their lines, placed on them by the Shadow. But they were undone by the
Shadow in the end. The Shadow through me!”​
​
These words shed some light on the Alavandor mystery, though as always, it raised more questions than they

answered. I’ve yet to speak with Lucan in detail about these revelations so I have not much to add regarding his
family.
Calix continued:​
​
‘“The new men drove them off, even if they didn’t know it, and when the Shadow returned to the small lives of the

new men, their forces rent Harngorn and poisoned the water, and Fluvia and Quercina have been powerless

since. I pity them, especially because of my role in their diminishment, but I am powerless to leave and help
them, and you would be a fool to undo what the Shadow wishes done.”​
  What is of interest to me here is the small revelation that while Fluvia and Quercina are diminished, they are not

dead. Indeed, as we have learned with the fate of Dol’Rullian, these creatures are somewhat immortal, so it is

unlikely that Palus would be able to defeat them outright. At least not here in the Realms.​
​
So they remain here, perhaps trapped or imprisoned, or even living in disguise. But if they are alive they can be
found.

Calix again:​
​
“This is a complicated story, and I’ve never put it together exactly. Some of the men of Greenwood remembered

Thistle Ridge, and I knew their daughters and their friends. They were all my friends. From them I learned that

there was some trade and kinship between Greenwood and Thistle Ridge before separate events destroyed both

at different times. The connection between the new men, the hag, the girls, the dead daughters and grand

daughters, the profane temple…it is too confusing for me, and I can’t keep it lined up in my mind very well. I just
don’t understand how shadows and men and hags can get so tangled.”​
​
It is, of course, a simple question to answer for us, though I can see how a fey creature might not understand,

Just as their very nature is alien to us, so must ours be alien to them. The same vices that have plagued us since

the beginning, plague us still. Lust, greed, wrath… there is no end to what a man will do when gripped with these

fevers. And no bargain he will not strike if these creatures can provide him with his desires.​
​
Again, it is not difficult to see just from what you have seen of me. I am bound to you, and yet also bound to

Coventina, and also to Masra, and to Jeska, and to the dead girls. Bargains all. My motivations might seem

righteous, but it is not difficult to see the path of corruption that lies ahead of me should I stray for just a step or

two. Should I succumb to my rage, might I not become no different than the hag herself?​
​
I am reminded again of your lesson at The Standing Stone.​
​
  Calix continued, offering fresh insight into the small shrine we had previously visited:​
​
“You know the place. The small profane temple in the morning shadow of the small hills sunrise side of us. I say

profane, because I think that also means ‘secret’, and the Greenwood men never knew about the temple. But

Palus was always trying to lure the girls of Greenwood, and I think probably Thistle Ridge too, but that was done

before I arrived, to the temple. I was always scared of the place. It has the smell of Shadow on it, and I don’t like

the stone ladies. I think one looks like Palus, or like Palus did. I don’t know who built it, but it was here before

me, and it was built in that spot to hide it. Men don’t naturally wander there.”​
​
This, of course, answered no questions that we held, and only asked plenty more. It served to reinforce the need

for us to return to the place and explore it in a more thorough fashion. I do hope that your queries with the House

of Plenty prove fruitful as this shrine might be of great importance in our struggles ahead.​
​
Calix finished with a summary of where Palus’ goals and status might be today:​
​
“Palus does not realize it, but she too was sacrificed, along with her daughter and grand daughter. Crone, and

girl and girl-of-girl, all destroyed utterly through dark covenant with the Shadow. Little is left of Palus herself now,

except hate and cunning. She works mostly through her Court of thralls; Fey and Shadow unhallowed and

intertwined. Like you and your court and mine, but degraded and cruel. She watches all the land, as you know,
with a magic not entirely different from mine.”​
​
Throughout the last few days we have been encountering small fetishes that have given away our location, and

have dogged our attempts to move through the forest in secret. I had assumed this entire time that they were of

Goblin make and held some form of shamanistic magic that was too primitive for me to understand. Now I see
that it was of a more alien nature, since it is Fey magic.​
​
I hope that now that I know what I am looking for I may be able to spot it and perhaps counter it as we move
forward.​
​
Calix finished:

“Palus’ chief if Charrah, or Charrah is her chief. I am not sure. But they hold Thistle Ridge together. More

importantly, Palus it is that holds the spirits of 3 girls in Thistle Ridge, and if you are to free those girls, you will
one day have to face Palus to end her magic.”

And so Calix summarised the road ahead and the task at hand.

It has been an eventful few days. I can hardly believe that we walked out of Dagger Falls less than a tenday ago,

and yet we have already settled Greenwood Farm, cleared the Tower, and liberated the Limestone Court!

Jeska has decreed that we rest and regroup for some days before we move forward with our reclamation. I intend

to stay behind here with the Fey and discover as much as I can about Palus. I fear that because of her

association with the Shadow, Calix may be unable to fully comprehend the nature of the corruption that has taken
root in this adversary.​
​
It is possible that I may have to return to Dagger Falls and visit the Abbeye, though I do not believe Invictus will

grant me access to the necessary information. And beyond that, I do not trust any Amaunatori with the

information that we have learned. They’ve already begun to sink their greedy teeth into the meat of our
expedition, and I expect their intention is fully to rip us apart.​
​
I’ve not been much of a religious man. Remember how we used to sneak out of the House of Plenty during the

harvest service to feed the geese along the riverbank? It was bad enough we were dragged to the Chauntean

temple by my mother every week, but as we threw bread into the river and we saw the ridiculous Morningdawn

Hall behind us, I remember thinking, “at least we’re not those poor bastards going around scared of the dark!”

Whatever the outcome will be of our adventure in settling Thistle Ridge, no good will come from the Amaunatori

This is my last sheet of paper, so I must end my letter here. There is more that I need to report, but it will have to

I remain well and faithfully yours. I trust that all continues as it should be back home. Please send my regards to

your mother, tell my brother Abner to stop complaining, and tell Glaun to do us all a favour and go drown himself
in the Asha.

I remain faithfully yours,
Jasper Hawkford

Limestone Court
20th day of Tarsakh, 1483 DR
