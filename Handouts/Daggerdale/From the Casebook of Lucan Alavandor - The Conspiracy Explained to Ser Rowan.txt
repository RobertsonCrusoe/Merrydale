Tl;dr: Lucan Explains the Conspiracy to Ser Rowan

Setting: A quiet corner of the Greenwood Farm camp, near a low fire after the evening meal.

(Lucan swirls his wine cup, watching Ser Rowan wearily check Geldhammer's shaft)

Lucan: Rowan, my stalwart friend, give that hammer a rest for a moment. Admirable weapon, truly, I've seen what you can do with it—but let me tell you, the real knock-out blow in this fight comes from up here. (Taps his temple) Let me lay out the mess I've untangled. I think I we should have the lay of the land before we go knocking on the doors in Thistle Ridge, eh?

Ser Rowan: (Looks up, rubbing his eyes, clearly tired) Gods, Lucan, me head's already poundin' from thinkin' about trolls and that goblin column. Just... give it to me straight. No fancy twists. What's yer latest grand theory? Make it quick, I need to think about watch rotations.

Lucan: (Leans forward, grinning, eyes alight with self-satisfaction) Quick? But the beauty is in the details! Still, I've never been one to refuse a friend. First: Netheril. Needed stooges. Found some poor bastards cursed sterile back in Daerlun – probably Netheril's own dirty work, mind you – arranged for the real Alavandors to conveniently bugger off, and shipped these impostors here under fancy stolen names. Dangled Thistle Ridge's magic baby-making reputation like a carrot in front of a horny Sunite. Classic setup!

Ser Rowan: Impostors... the curse... Aye, the Governess mentioned something. Get on with it.

Lucan: Second: Palus! The hag wasn't just lurking. She was in on it, cosy with the Shadow before our fakers even set foot here. How do I know? Obvious! The hidden temple they built? Had a statue looking like her back when she was less... haggy! And who did Abrecia's ghost finger for the '39 sacrifice? 'The Ancient Mother'! Who's likely hiding behind that Jannath goddess nonsense? And who fessed up to the '41 sacrifice? Palus! It's her, Rowan, playing dress-up! Been pulling the strings, slick as greased lightning!

Ser Rowan: (Sighs heavily) A hag playing dress-up... Right. Sounds about right for this accursed Dale. Next?

Lucan: Third: Palus played them like a cheap fiddle at a dockside tavern! Got them doing her dirty work – 'profaning' the land, she called it. Fixed their little plumbing problem, sure, but it also shafted her sister Quercina and buggered up the grove. Two birds, one nasty stone! Then, 1441, the big climax! Sacrifice the girls – bang! – power up Netheril's shadow-boxes, Palus gets a big gulp of ghost-juice. Curtains for the pawns.

Ser Rowan: So, Netheril and Palus both got their rocks off on that one...

Lucan: Precisely! Fourth: But here's the kicker, the part that takes real brains! The betrayal! Why no Shadovar flags over Thistle Ridge for 40 years? Because Palus, high on power and probably bored, double-crossed them! She kept the ruin, the ghosts, the whole damned playground for herself. Went from being Netheril's doxy to queen bitch of the bog!

Ser Rowan: (Nods slowly, looking even more tired) Right. Rogue hag. Netheril wants her gone too. Fair enough.

Lucan: Fifth: Which means we, my dear, hammer-swinging knight, are likely the punchline to Netheril's joke! They sit back, watch Daggerdale send us – brave, handsome, expendable us – to deal with their rogue hag problem. We take the hits, bleed buckets taking her down, and then Netheril waltzes in to claim the spoils from the knackered survivors. Those recent attacks? Just tenderizing the meat before the feast!

Ser Rowan: (Lets out a long breath, his moustaches flapping) I don't know about all that, Lucan. Above my pay grade, I reckon. But if I follow you, Netheril used those cursed fakers. Palus, pretendin' to be a goddess, used them too, then probably betrayed Netheril. And now Netheril might be using us to get rid of Palus so they can take the Ridge? Is that the damned story? Helm's teeth, my head aches. All I know is my hammer feels heavy, and there are goblins needing smashing. Orders are orders. Can we just focus on the hitting part?

Lucan: (Smirks, takes a sip) A functional summary, Rowan! See? Even a mind focused on the satisfying crunch of bone can grasp the essentials when presented so clearly. But keep this 'gist' tucked away. Knowing why the hammer needs to fall makes the swing so much more meaningful. Now, be a good chap and pass that wineskin, hmm? Unraveling conspiracies is thirsty work.
