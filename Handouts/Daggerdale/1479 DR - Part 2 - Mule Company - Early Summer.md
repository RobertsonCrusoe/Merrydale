The story of the men that would come to be known as Mule Company begins in the spring of 1479 DR.  Travelling towards Dagger Falls and Castle Radiance in various caravans, a group of men (and one drow) met at the taproom of the Castle Gate Inn in Crossed Blades Farm, which included Dane Grimwald, Erevan Ilythiirian (aka Grym), and Banitt Harbaugh.  They got off to a shaky start, with Dane clearly distrustful of the smooth-talking drow, and the Pilgrim (Banitt) being uneasy with all of them.  Each of these men was going to the Castle for his own reasons; Grym on some ancient and unspoken family business, Banitt as a pilgrim from a far-and-frozen land to see the tomb of Ardelusk Whitegate, and Dane to confront some old demons, or possibly just returning to, or running from the scene of some old crime.

By the time they reached the Castle, they had resolved to stick to together; the world was a dangerous place as it was, and the Desert’s Mountains even more-so.  In Dagger Falls they added the mercurial creature Zephyr to their ranks, making them easily the motliest group of souls in the region.  They had a difficult time even gaining entry to the Castle, and only by the will of the Pilgrim, and some pass given the Drow by the Myth Drannor Embassy did they make the gates.

Inside, they sought out a man named Bargle, who is the head of the Free Defense League; a group of mercenary adventurers who seek out the well-paying bounties that are posted in the Castle.  They were joined in negotiations with Bargle by two other men who would later join their ranks; Corran Altair, a paladin of Torm with a sordid past in this very Castle, and Mr.Bollinger, a nuanced artificer and associate of the Wonder Bringer Warsmiths.  These men all agreed to Bargle’s terms, and joined his League as his third team.  His two existing teams, Gryphon Company and Dragon Company were away for most of this part of the story on distant and protracted assignments.

By Greengrass of 1374 DR, the company was assembled, had its first bounty to claim, and was ready to set off.

==The Old Man’s Tooth==
450px|right

Bargle had managed to procure a lucrative bounty for his new team.  The Purple Feather Bounty seemed like a good start for his new team; it was close by, the enemies seemingly few, and the rewards decent.  It would also overlap with the Whitegate Census Bounty, for added income.  What could go wrong?

===The Shepherd’s Crook===
The heroes did some investigating before they set out, getting an account from Lt. Northman, as well as a first hand story from a dwarven mason that was at the site, and finally some high level intelligence from Captain Rainyerd.

The company first investigated the building site for this new tower on a strategic point on Paladin’s Road, which lead through the south-west of the Desertsmouth Mountains, down from the Castle.  A group of cunning orcs had used sophisticated ambush and guerrilla strategy to best the garrison there, and had made off with a number of skilled masons working on the project.  A detachment of Sun Blades (2nd Mountaineers, Red Platoon) under Lt. Northman was dispatched to reinforce the location, but even they suffered significant casualties in a series of ambushes, and he was ordered to leave the site.

The company investigated the site, and found that it was in fact being held by a very small group of orcs who shot the purple arrows which they came to be known by, and again they proved to be more organized and dangerous than usual orcs.  They were well armed with high quality longbows, excellent arrows, and dangerous poisons.  In the end, the heroes took the location, but they realized they were up against more than just barbaric humanoids.  The orcs here had been selectively plundering the site, favouring the masonry tools and other construction supplies.

The heroes managed to capture one orc alive, and interrogated him harshly in order to find the chief of this new and dangerous tribe.  They learned that this new tribe calls itself the Purple Eye, and that they are lead by an ork named Tuumig, who was a recent convert to the faith of Shar.  Further, they learned that he had a very capable scout named Dohgohg who they thought they might have seen observing them from afar, and an inspired bowyer named Rohohg.  Theirs is a break-away tribe from the large and powerful Ak’ron orcs on the nearby mountain named the Old Man's Tooth.  Finally, the captured ork suggested that the Purple Feathers were using a secret natural stair to come down off the mountain rather than the Chute, and he gave a rough indication on the company's map of where it might be.

Returning the half-day’s ride back to the Castle, the heroes did a bit more intelligence gathering, and learned a bit about the Chute from a local guide named Seth Forester, and the conflict between the two large tribes of orks on the mountain.  They consulted briefly with Bargle, who decided it would be best if they went up the mountain to find Tuumig and his band in order to claim the bounty, and for the general good of the men and soldiers of the Castle.  Based on the intelligence of Forester and the interrogated ork, they decided to look for one of the secret trails on the back-side of the Old Man's Tooth, rather than risk The Chute.

===Man versus Nature===
650px|right|thumb|The unforgiving terrain of The Old Man's Tooth
The company found themselves ill-suited to path-finding in the rugged mountains.  They spent several days longer than anticipated trying to find the trail; they spent several days lost, and had to fend off many brutal attacks from the orks that inhabit the region.  They managed to win every battle, and were better as a team for the experience, but by the time they fought their way to the top of the Old Man's Tooth, they were exhausted, and were forced to press on in the most hostile country they had experienced.

Surveying their surroundings, the heroes decided it was best to move off the trail they had found, and slog their way across the rugged plateau.  They knew roughly where the Purple Eye camp was, based on their interrogation, but their efforts to find it quickly and quietly failed; they were unable to navigate a decent route parallel with the road, and at one point had to walk in the open along the main road, and ran into a large patrol of what they took for Ak'ron orks.  They managed to desperately cut them down and successfully chased down the runners that had fled the battle to warn their tribe.  They hauled the bodies off the main road, but knew that they now were working against the clock; it would not be long before that patrol was believed missing, and the inhospitable mountain top would become aswarm with angry orks.

===The Purple Eye Camp===

After a full day and night sneaking around the Old Man's Tooth trying to remain unseen, the company finally came to the opening in a cliff wall they had been looking for.

The battle at the camp went well for the heroes from the very beginning; the well-built palisade was little obstacle to Dane, who managed to jump half way up and grab hold of a banner, which he then used to scramble up to the top quickly.  He managed to clear the battlements, throw open the doors, and the heroes were then amongst their quarry.

They were able to fight off wave after wave of orcs, and eventually confronted Tuumig and killed him.  Dozens of orcs managed to flee through the gates and into various tunnels in the mountain which surrounded the village.  The heroes found the dwarves they were looking for, and a few other human slaves besides.

The most significant discovery, and one which would cause trouble later on, was that of Tuumig's sex slave, Josalle, who they freed from a cage in his lair.

As the heroes freed the slaves, and plundered what there was of value, they realized they would have to hurry their retreat off the mountain.  They left as quickly as possible, and fled directly along the road with a dozen slaves in tow.

Before they had gotten half-way to the back-stair off the mountain, they heard drums of war beating from the Ak'ron village in the distance, and their hearts sank.  The village was closer to the stair than they were, and with the slaves, there was no way they would make it there before a war-party found them on the road.

However, as Banitt tells it, Amaunator himself intervened to save them.  As the first rays of the sun broke high atop the mountain, fires began to light up the distant village, and the distant sound of battle could be heard.  The heroes managed to make the stair unmolested, and fled the Old Man's Tooth in the safety provided by some unseen conflict behind them.

===A Tense Arrival===

Three days later, the heroes arrived back at the castle with their liberated masons and other slaves, including the sex-slave, Josalle.  She had been very quiet for much of the trip, but warmed up to Corran, who found her old clothing in one of Tuumig's trunks, and had been the first to cover and comfort her.  She explained that she had been a Cormyrean merchant who was captured in a raid over a year ago, and that she passed through many hands to finally end up as Tuumig's play-thing.  She explained that she had been up at the camp for about 6 months, and saw Tuumig go from being a shaman of Gruumsh to a powerful shaman of Shar in that short time.

When the heroes arrived at the South Gate of the Castle, they were met by the usual host of scribes and security, and the interrogation of the slaves was just as harsh as any other person asking for admission to the Castle.  In fact, the interrogation of Josalle was so harsh that Corran stepped in, claiming that he did not travel through an 'orcish crucible' to rescue these people to have them treated worse than the scum that took them.

A magistrate was eventually called to explain things to Corran.  Magistrate Wlodimir had this to say to Corran and his friends;

\"This woman has come through hell.  She has been the slave, and a harshly-used slave by her appearance, of foul and wicked orcs.  She has lost everything and somehow endured over a year, by her own confession, of barbaric captivity.  This is the very vessel in which the Black Bitch plants her seed.  We have not achieved the degree of safety in these mountains, and bought the freedom and safety of our homeland, by making decisions passionately; this woman's case is one that would stir emotions in most, but I do not have that luxury.  I must decide her fate based on logic and the laws we have established here.\"

Corran had his hands on his blade by this time, and a squad of Sun Blades closed in, with stony determination on their faces.  It was Banitt who disarmed the situation.

\"Corran\" he said, putting a hand on his companion’s sword-arm.  \"The Magistrate is right.  She is a likely candidate for a convert to the...Black Bitch...and their rules have gotten them this far.\"  Everyone, including Corran and the Magistrate seemed to relax, though Corran wore a look of confusion and upset on his face.

\"However,\" Banitt continued \"I will vouch for this woman.  Finish your interrogations and your paperwork.  Do your duty.  You will find that she is suitable for admission.  I will see to it that she is taken care of, and given proper instruction in worship\" he said, pointing skyward with one finger.  \"And let any man who treats her wrongly or speaks ill of her answer to me!\"

By this time, Argent Whitegate had arrived to see what the commotion was, and having caught the end of Banitt's speech, did grant her admission.  The clergy and the Blades seemed surprised by the decision, and the Wlodimir was visibly angry, though only for a moment.  Argent gave a nod to Banitt from across the crowd, and many turned to take in the Pilgrim who had just challenged a magistrate and won his case.

==Last Sands of Anauroch==

450px|right
A week or so after the successful Purple Feather Bounty expedition, Bargle's new heroes came up against a task they could not complete.

Bargle managed to get a claim on Last Sands of Anauroch Bounty, which was difficult to obtain, given the competition for bounties related to Netherese ruins.  The company immediately set about gathering information about this bounty.

===Intelligence===

They first found the scout that had given the vague intelligence which the bounty was based on.  Silas Fletcher had this to say about his encounter with the Netherese ruin;

:I’m not 100% sure where I saw that ruin poking out.  The hills and the sands changed in between the time I first passed through there, a month earlier, and the other day.  I was making as fast a haul as I could; I saw a herd of them Nether Sables off in the distance, and they are not to be trifled with.  Anyway, what I saw looked like the top of a tower; it was a pointy roof on a round building, and I could just barely make out some arrow slits, or something of the sort, and nearly decided to hide out there, but thought better of it; I know I can outfox Nether Sables when I have to.  Who knows what’s inside some old ruins in the desert?

Next, Grim spoke to his friend, Ambassador Miritar who provided this cryptic but knowing advice;

:Ahh…that is an interesting bit of desert, that.  Well, I guess it isn’t desert anymore, and if what I hear about the ruin is true; a small tower with red clay tiles, we know this is from the Late Empire, and likely holds something sinister and potent in the basement. It may have the bearing of a simple keep, but the Late Empire outposts all had powerful devices in the deepest reaches.  There might be something for controlling the very weather itself down there.  Who knows?

:I suppose you won’t be the only ones headed there, so make haste.  By the way, I would be happy to have a look at any valuables, especially books, you might be able to take out of there.  We might be able to work something out…

Finally, Mr. Bollinger spoke to Curator Gambledin (Mid-ranking Tethyamar League) who provided little in the way of positive intel, but did provide some portent;

:I doubt very much there’s anything Dwarven that far out.  That’ll be Netherese, and that means that you won’t be the only ones looking for it.  In fact, how long did it take for that scout to get back?  I’d count on showing up late to the ball, friends.  If you find some Netherese outriders before you get there, you know you’re too late.

===Man Versus Nature: Round 2===
right|450px|thumb|An Asabi Hunter erupts from the last sands of Anauroch
The company set out hastily the next day, and made good time getting through to the western side of the mountains.  Once they were about 20 miles from the Castle, they had to fend off a couple skirmishes of orcs with the following description;

The orcs that have assaulted you look nothing like the orcs of the Old Man’s Tooth.  These are larger and more feral looking, and are markedly paler.  They all wear black bear skins and have their faces painted with black mud in rough skull shapes.

These orcs proved to be much less of a challenge than the Purple Feather orcs they had spent much of the previous month battling.  However, by the time they came to the foothills on the Netherese side of the mountains, they began to encounter White-Face Clan ogres and goblins.  Their battles with these monsters and the cave bears they used as hounds delayed them by several days from arriving at the excavation site.

After several run-ins with the White-faces, the company eventually found their way out of the foothills and onto the edge of Netheril.  From high in the hills, the heroes could see that the emerald grasslands of Netheril had come nearly to the edge of the foothills, leaving only a small ribbon of sand, a few miles across in some places, perhaps as many as 10 miles in others.  They pressed on, map in hand, and with the directions that Silas had provided.  However, their wilderness skills continued to prove abysmal, and they spent a couple more days drudging through the sands looking for the ruins.

In their search of the sands, they began to encounter agents of Netheril.  The first Netherese agents they encountered were the lizard-like slave race known as the Asabi.  The Asabi encountered in the region around the ruins seemed to be patrolling apurpose, but they were cunning and stealthy, and always attacked from hiding positions or from the very sand itself to catch the directionless adventurers off guard.

After several encounters with the Asabi scouts, the adventurers managed to stumble their way to the excavation site.

===The Excavation Site===

At length, the adventurers came to the excavation site of the Netherese ruins.  Their encounter began with the following description;

Cresting a rise in what was a hilly region covered in light bush and scrub, with small trees and green grass, you look out onto a different scene.  Immediately on the other side of the hill is a vast hill of sand, a couple of miles long, and very very high.  Beyond this huge sand dune are the beautiful yellow grasslands of Netheril, which some of you are seeing for the first time.  Having looked upon one of the last remnants of mighty Anauroch, and recalling all the tales of the desert from your youth, you find yourself at odds with the image before you;  you can see the end of something that was unforgiving and barren, and the beginning of something vibrant, though with a sinister motivation.  The grasslands of Eastern Netheril are truly breathtaking, especially for people who have never seen such geography, but they mark the boundaries of the ever-expanding Empire of Netheril.  While you stand there to take it all in, you can see a powerful wind attacking the vast sandy hill, and the sands are blowing off slowly but surely, never to return.

About a mile away from where you are now, you can see, like looking at ants, some activity on this side of the sand dune; there are many creatures crawling around what appears to be a number of buildings rising out of the sand.

Approaching the site closer, you can see that the sands have been blown away to reveal a number of small buildings, all round, and two lengths of long wall.  There is quite a bit of excavation going on in the centre of the area, by the inner-most buildings.

This area is crawling with men and lizard-looking creatures.  Some large lizards are digging, while some men seem to be overseeing the efforts of the lizards.  There are many guards in the area, and a couple distinct camp areas.  Supplies in barrels and crates are piled by the main excavation.  There are many pack animals, horses and carts, likewise loaded with boxes and barrels.  A large canopy with a desk and yet more campaign supplies nearby seems to be the focal point of the excavation, and a pale-faced woman in fine-looking clothes seems to be watching over everything that is happening.

The heroes, hidden behind a dune hundreds of feet away, decided they would try to sneak up to the camp as closely as possible.  They even concocted a complicated two-pronged attack plan, in order to hit two targets simultaneously; the tent/epicentre of the excavation, and the peak of the huge dune upon which the highest ground in the area was found, as well as a large group of sentries.

The battle that followed was hard-fought and gruelling; Asabi and Bedine slaves seemed to come swarming out of tents, the ruins and the sand itself, and by the end of it, there were nearly 100 dead enemies in the sand, and the adventurers were exhausted but victorious.

The 'pale-faced woman' turned out to be a low-ranking Shadar-kai, and she was more than a little slippery.  However, Dane managed to stick two blades through her chest under the canopy she was defending, while Grim scrambled desperately for a sheaf of papers held down by some rocks on a nearby desk.  One of the papers was actually a large map which tried to fly away on its own accord once freed of its weights.  Grim managed to catch this vital map in midair.

With the surface of the excavation site under control by the company, they drove off the remaining Bedine non-combatants (nearly 100 more workers), and made a quick appraisal of the goods in the camp.  It was plain to see that there were supplies for a huge excavation project on-site, and obvious tracks of many heavily-laden wagons which had gone both to and from the camp, heading into the heart of Netheril.

They also found crates and barrels and boxes of Netherese artefacts which had already been dug out of the ruins, and were awaiting shipment to the Sharspire.

The ruins themselves consisted of an 8-tower fortress, which the sands had blown away to reveal.  The dune had blown away in the south-east the most, and had revealed the top 30' of a guard tower, while the main entrance seemed to be in an inner-ring tower of the fortress more towards the middle, which was showing only the top 10'.  The excavation team had made an entrance by breaking open a huge hole in the wall.  Listening at the entrance to this tower, a deep low-pitched hum could be heard from somewhere very deep down in the ruins, as well as the noises of people or creatures scrambling about, several floors down.

===Descent and the Death of Dane Grimwald===

There was a fierce debate among the company about how long they should wait to descend into the ruins.  They looked at the terms of the bounty, and decided they had not filled it yet, as there was clearly something important in the depths of the ruin; something that was commanding the wind itself to peel away the layers of sand covering the site.  They were exhausted from the battle on the surface, however, and some wanted to rest for a day to recover, while Corran and Dane argued for a prompt assault on whatever was lurking below.  In the end, they decided time was not on their side, and made preparations for their delve.

However, just as they began to descend into the ruin, Grim noticed another flying note, this time coming out of the smaller tower that had been uncovered more by the winds.  They managed to shoot the note down and intercept it, though they were not able to decipher it until much later.

====The Upper Reaches====

There has been a rather direct excavation leading straight down from the small tower for several levels.  Most of the entries to other floors  from the winding stairwell are boarded over to keep back the sand, but a few have had a  horizontal excavation started, reaching into the black insides of this fortress that have not been seen for over a thousand years.

After going down 6 flights of steps, and perhaps as much as 100’, the excavation opens up completely.

====The Main Hall====

450px|thumb|right|Loki Mouvranesh, Shadar-Kai Controller and head of the excavation

The main hall is a large, vaulted chamber with a balcony-level running all the way around.  The main floor has a set of huge bronze double doors that have been barricaded to hold back the tremendous weight of sand behind them.  This hall is full of debris; there are fragments of statues that appear to have been recently smashed apart, and several pools of dried blood marking recent conflict.  Despite that, this area has been thoroughly excavated, some parts of it looking like they have actually been swept clean of the millennial sands.

The hall is presently guarded by a host of Netherese agents, who are clearly expecting you.

The creatures before you look like some sort of cross between humans and goblins and shadow.  They have misshapen heads that look like caricatures, with elongated jaws, pronounced brow ridges and impossibly big noses.  Some have small horns or protruding canines.  Their skin ranges in colour from ash-grey to a sickly grey-green, and their hair, which grows in no consistent fashion, is mostly stark white.  These creatures appear to be directed by a pair of women, both seemingly wizards.  One is clearly a shadar-kai, and the other looks to be about half way between shadar-kai and human, though she is dusky and Bedine-looking in hue.

The shadar-kai mage is tall and lean for a woman, and her skin is the colour of ash.  She has stark-white hair which is cropped short, falling to her pronounced cheek bone.  She is pretty, but has a number of bits of metal sticking out of her face, 2 large silver canine-looking piercings coming through her lower lip.  She wears beautiful robes of jet black, and her graceful movements and light footfalls add to her already shadowy-appearance, giving on the impression that this woman is, indeed, half made of shadow stuff.

This Shadar-Kai, they would later learn, was indeed the head of this expedition, Loki Mouvranesh.  She was a devious controller who sculpted the battle field to her advantage; she used a 'hypnotic pattern' to lure half the party over a ledge and into a pit where a pitched battle was fought, and mired the rest of the party with 'Grasp of the Grave'.  She was relentlessly pursued by Grim and Banitt, but she was able to slip past their best efforts to catch or kill her.

During the battle, Dane was mercilessly dragged into the 'Grasp of the Grave', where he succumbed to the necromantic tendrils put there by the Shadar-Kai mage.

350px|left|thumb|A Krinth warrior

In the end, most of the soldiers, Krinth and Asabi alike were destroyed or routed, but the heroes themselves retreated to the surface, disheartened at the loss of their friend, and by the seemingly overwhelming prospect of laying siege to the dungeon below.  To further dishearten them, the laugher of Loki from deep within the ruin could be heard just before the thunderous sound of stone hammering on stone deep under the earth.

Mule Company packed up what they could, taking three huge wagons laden with Netherese artefacts and expedition gear with them, and headed due north to Fort Lastsun.  Corran was loath to go there again, but he knew he could find the place, and they needed to get to safety sooner rather than later; the Netherese would soon reinforce the excavation team.

===A Desperate Flight and an Unfriendly Welcome===

The adventurers made haste across the sands as best they could, this time finding their way under the guidance of Corran, who had spent years in this region, while stationed at the Fort.  They were harassed by White Faces throughout the journey, and managed to kill a pair of hill giants who tracked them relentlessly for two days, just outside the patrolled limits of the Fort.

Racing to the Fort after the desperate battle with the hill giants, the company was held at the gates and underwent a demeaning interrogation.  The Fort falls directly under the control of Master Knight Grendel, and most of his brotherhood (about 30 paladins and many other soldiers from the Order of the Lambent Shield) and 2 platoons of Sun Blades are permanently garrisoned here.  Grendel knew Corran from his military past, including the chequered part, and did not make his brief visit to the fort welcoming or comfortable.

The adventurers were roundly mocked for their failure to complete the bounty, and for letting so valuable an asset (Loki) slip away from them.  Most of the soldiers in the Fort seemed to look down their noses and Corran, and none of his friends understood why this was, or why their companion seemed so changed while he was here; he seemed to withdraw into himself a little, and though he tried to be proud and defiant, he was unable to meet the gaze of any Sun Blades or Paladins inside the fort.

It was here in Fort Last Sun that the adventurers were given the unfortunate name of 'mule company', for the stolen Netherese mounts that had dragged back three loads of mostly-worthless artefacts from the excavation.  In the morning after their arrival, the heroes, who were forced to sleep outside in the rain, were investigating their plunder when a group of Sun Blades walked past them.  They were heard to say;

Ain't those Bargle's new boys?

Yeah; managed to capture six Netherese mules and some clay pots, and only lost one of their own in the effort!

What's the name Bargle's other boys go by?  The ones that were here last month?

Gryphon Company

Ahhh....this must be Mule Company!
