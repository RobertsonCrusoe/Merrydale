DDC - Forword from an Itinerant Scholar 
An account of the major political and religious happenings in and around Daggerdale from 1367 – 1482 DR, compiled by Grafton Fitzdecker, Independent Scholar. 

Well Met, Reader: 
This is the forward to the first edition of this book. I say 'first edition', because as I write this, I've heard that the Black Network is once again operating at full capacity in the Moonsea region, and just yesterday I spoke to a man that claimed to work for the Drurginz Trading company, here in Waterdeep. As you read on, you'll learn that I am right to be surprised to hear this name, and you'll guess, as I have, that this story is not yet concluded. 

The edition that you are reading was compiled by commission from Candlekeep, last winter. There were much greater works of writing that I desired to access in the Keep, and their price for this access was a compilation of primary sources regarding beholder activity on the Sword Coast in the late 14th century DR. My handler at the Keep knew that I sometimes dealt with the deranged Waterdhavian elf, Steele, around whom many parts of this story are woven. Indeed, this very same character will appear in many of the primary sources you are about to enjoy. 

Getting anything from Steele is usually a fuss, but on the particular occasion that I chose to darken his door, he was already in one of his legendary black moods. I was cautioned to give him space and time, but I pressed, and was rewarded. When I asked him what he knew about beholders, he told me "Apparently not enough", and he veritably threw a box of papers, books and scrolls at me. Looking quickly at the mess about me, I could see that there were journals, maps, notes and other collateral relating to beholders, Zhents, Harpers, and a town by the name of Daggerdale, that I hadn't heard of before that moment. 

I motioned towards the mess inquisitively, as if to say, "may I?", and he threw a dagger by my head, which stuck in the door behind me. "I'll take that as an "of course, that's why I threw it at you, now sod off" and thank you presently. I'll bring it all back as soon as I've made copies." At this point, Steele was brimming over. 
"Don't you dare, you little pig! Take it and get from my site forever!" 
I gathered it hastily, and followed his instructions, though I have some burning questions that only he can answer. Those will have to wait, maybe for the next edition, eh? 

The box was full of everything I needed to know about the topic at hand, and I wasn't long in guessing why the Keep was so interested in it, and why Steele had it all together and ready to be flung at my person. The most recent notes were from 1481, and while centred mostly on distant Daggerdale, some 

names and locations were circled in red with angry annotations in Silver Elven, no doubt Steele's own hand. Linkages between the beholder and drow schemes in the Sword Coast in the 14th century were now being seen in Daggerdale in the 15th. I envy the Long-Lived races their ability to plan over decades and centuries, and admit that I find their machinations mind-boggling in their scope. 

I have learned that events that seemed to start in the 1360's are playing out new chapters currently on the other side of the continent, and that their persistence is the source of Steele's rage. He has waged war on these forces for over 100 years, and he is no closer to victory. 

How my research into these events turned from a commission to a passion is explained by one simple fact; that I have been in love with a woman named Sarah Estelmer for some 35 years. We studied together in Waterdeep as children, and I have loved her since grammar school. She never knew this, as for some reason she is the only soul on Toril for whom my loquaciousness falters, and our paths kept us apart for long tracts of time, with only brief intermissions. Sarah, always my academic superior, found herself in the Order of the Tome, while I have been a rogue scholar all my life. 

Toward the bottom of the box of documents gracefully provided by Steele, is Sarah's Report from 1481, entitled 'The Shadow Lifted', which I have reproduced here in its entirety. It was heartwarming to see that she too was a rogue in her own Order, and that she lived a full life with meaningful friendships, and even love. I in fact read her report before anything else, and this changed the nature of my investigation from beholders, drow, and Zhents on the Sword Coast in the 14th century, to the same and other matters in Daggerdale in the 15th. The latter is more interesting given our proximity to it, the value of the source documents from that era, and through my declared bias for the writings of an amazing woman. 

While I expect that Candlekeep will be a little tilted at my deviation from their specification, I will boldly and presently claim that this is the best work I've ever put together. As you'll read, the documents that I present below are not all from one box of papers from an insane elf. I have spent every waking moment of the last twelve months working on this publication, including a trip to Daggerdale to gather more information. 

As far as I know, this is the most detailed and comprehensive account of the affairs mentioned in the title, and I suspect this publication will be a useful source of information and intelligence for those that need to get involved in the current resurgence of the Zhents and the Netherese on either side of Anuaroch (or Netheril) 

Finally, just a note on format; Below are dozens of primary sources that I have transcribed and put into a format suitable for a Gondsman's Press. The original documents are stored at the Keep with my first printing, and it is my hope that the Keep will produce copies and circulate it far and wide. For now I shall confess, probably to the dismay of the Keep, that I indeed have made three copies of this work; one for Candlekeep, one for myself, and one for the amiable and helpful Administrator of the Whitecloaks in Daggerdale, without whom many holes would remain in the chronicle. 

For most sources, I have given some notes on context (where I got it from, how reliable the source might be, and so on). I have given some commentary on the more interesting parts of the chronicle, even though I was advised not to. 

If you are reading this out of pure academic interest, I hope you'll enjoy every word at a leisurely pace. If, on the other hand, you are reading this to prepare for conflict in Daggerdale, know you this; I haven't left anything in this account that might not save your life in some cave, castle, or forest in the Dales. 

Forewarned is forearmed. At your service, 
Grafton Fitzdecker 1st Alturiak, 1483 DR
