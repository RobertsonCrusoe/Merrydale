Dearest Father and Aunt Haelra,

I trust this letter finds you both in good health and that the concerns of House Alavandor continue to prosper in the civilized streets of Saerloon. The roads to Daggerdale (how fitting a moniker) proved more treacherous than anticipated, but I arrived safely and have much to report about my new associates.

As you suggested before my departure, I sought out Valerian's recommendations regarding reliable caravans. Information was scant, but I ended up finding passage with the Shield and Anvil Trading Coster. I needed to leave Saerloon quickly, as you will recall. It was about forty days, all told, from Saerloon to Dagger Falls. There was a shadow over my traveling companions, it seemed, until we reached the Dales proper after crossing Blackfeather Bridge. After that, some troubling incursions into our encampments on our trip through Cormanthor (wood elves, apparently - Madam Undercliff and the other dwarves taught me a few choice euphemisms for elves that I likely shouldn't commit to paper). The first caravan out of Daerlun this year, was Madam Umberta's caravan, and although they are a hardy bunch, those dwarves are as dour as a Vaasan funeral. From Essembra to Hillsfar we traveled in haste, making the Moonsea in what must have been record time.

Early in the morning after a day of resupplying we set out for Yulash, where I lost a fair bit of coin gambling. The fifteenth of the month is never a lucky day for me. Nearly a century and a half of discord between Hillsfar and Zhentil Keep has left the town in a state of near-permanent ruin. I asked around regarding the latest rumours, but couldn't get a straight answer. All of the money a man can lose at cards can't tell you which way the wind is blowing in that town. It reminds me of how Mother Trelawney can talk all day without saying anything at all. After Yulash we hired two guides who got us over the Tesh to Teshwave, before the final leg of our journey upstream to Dagger Falls.

Sold a few trinkets that fell off the back of another caravan to renew my purse. The children who roam the streets of this so-called city aren't above running an errand or two for a generous paymaster. Folks are into some strange customs in this backwater... frontier town. Although slavery and bondage are notably absent, the place is governed by a disciplinarian church, surprisingly similar to what their Western counterparts do with their own Inquisition. This is likely an artifact of their respective monotheistic approaches to a pantheistic cosmos. The locals come across as guarded, hesitant to speak freely or share their true thoughts, for fear of getting on the bad side of the church. It was a surprise to me, by Waukeen.

Silver speaks as clearly and purely as a Lliran orison, however, and your prodigal son has entered into commerce. The ravens or doves or whatever that deliver this message should have it to you in good time. There are important things that I need to discuss with you about Grandmother Lyralei's lost brother and his family, but I find I am being called away, and must leave that for another time.

With filial devotion, I remain,

Your most handsome son,

Lucan
