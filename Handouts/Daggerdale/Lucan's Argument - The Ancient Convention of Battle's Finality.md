Lucan's Argument — The Ancient Convention of Battle's Finality
Stephen Robertson (as Lucan) and Todd Burry (as Ser Rowan/Mari)
Email thread: "Lucan's argument", 25-26 Apr 2024
Thread ID: 18f1864a530563f7

Stephen drafts two versions of a legal argument Lucan intends to present to the competition's adjudicators, invoking the "Ancient Convention of Battle's Finality" to argue that Chrysalis should not be penalized for an accidental killing of a Redjack competitor during a match. The argument contends that once a combatant is slain, their entire company forfeits the round. Todd approves and suggests working in "god's will" so Mari can lend support.

---

STEPHEN (25 Apr 2024, 23:18) — First Draft:

Most learned adjudicators, I invoke the Ancient Convention of Battle's Finality to contend that our Captain's unforeseen action upon yon errant Redjack cannot be judged forfeit nor breach. For by the self-same precedent permitting Accidental Slaying in the heat of fray, it must be acknowledged that one whose life is forfeit holds no claim to victory's laurels.

Just as in the solemn rites of Personal Combat by Code Duello, where a mortal wound etches the instant of decisive defeat - so too in this grander theatre of martial conflict between assembled companies, must the fall of one contestant be viewed as concluding forfeit for their whole.

A broken blade cannot continue its course nor claim the prize. Just so, a party whose ranks are cloven holds no further entitlement to that round's accolade and must recuse themselves in favour of their rightful opponents.

We merely administered Finality's rule, unlooked for though it was, and therefore cannot be judged wanting. Whereas our doomed adversaries are now wanting, and so must want for spoils fairly lost when first one of their number was carried from the field. By Ancient Convention, the round is ours!"


STEPHEN (25 Apr 2024, 23:22) — Second Draft (counter to Jeska's wish to forfeit):

I must regretfully counter our esteemed captain's desire to forfeit the match with a heavy heart. For while her intent is unimpeachably noble, the Ancient Convention of Battle's Finality decrees that the matter was settled upon the very moment our adversary's life fled their breast. We take no joy in this ruling, but the timeless precedents are clear - when an opponent is slain in the fray, striking that fatal blow immediately decides defeat for their full company. The combat's finality is writ in the spilling of blood that can never be refunded. Just as a duelist in single combat concedes at the fatal wound, so too do the ranks arrayed behind a fallen champion. To permit otherwise would be to untie finality's knot, unraveling the very foundation of these revered games. Our hands were not stayed by treachery, but drove true in the passion of battle's crucible as all present embraced. Unfortunate though the outcome be, it stands as finality's child, quickened by the same maternal conventions that lent this contest breath. I implore our captain to lay down her admirable burden. The deeds are done, the price of bravery's gambit exacted according to rules as immutable as the headsman's axe. We cannot forfeit what finality has already bestowed - only humbly accept victory's mantle where rules anointed bind it to rest.
