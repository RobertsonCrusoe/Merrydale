FROM THE CASEBOOKS OF LUCAN ALAVANDOR - THE INVESTIGATION BEGINS (Stephen, 15 Aug):

So, I unearthed a letter from among my great-grandmother's belongings in Saerloon, a relic dated 3540 MC—what Brother Vessuvian confirms is the Mulhorand Calendar equivalent of 1406 DR. The letter, signed with the initials "AA," seems to be the work of Alvund Alavandor, my great-grandmother's elder brother. My aunt corroborated this, recalling that Alvund, once a struggling artist, severed ties with the family after leaving Sembia—or perhaps it was the other way around.

The letter hints at a deal struck between Alvund and a shadowy figure known as the Old Patriarch. As part of this arrangement, Alvund's family and another—presumably the Clarandals—agreed to "take what we were given and vanish forever." This implies some significant transaction or event that necessitated complete secrecy and relocation.

The involvement of this Old Patriarch raises several questions. His influence seems considerable, as he ensured their journey was safe and comfortable, which suggests either substantial resources or power. Or both, I suppose. The requirement for secrecy is evident since Alvund writes that he was breaching just such a condition by writing the letter I discovered.

The Old Patriarch's influence extended to a factory in Luthcheq, Chessenta—apparently marking the boundaries of his reach. This connection, though distant, may provide a clue as to his identity or the nature of his dealings. We know, for example, that the Alavaxi families that "came out of the Iron Hole" reportedly came from Chessenta.

Notably, the name "Alvund" is also associated with the first Alavandor settler in Thistle Ridge. A fundamental assumption in this case will be that Alvund the correspondent was also Alvund the settler. Brother Vessuvian's records mention that Alvund was married three times, each wife sharing the same surname: Maeliticus. Presumably, Senthia, Abrecia, and Aisling Maeliticus were part of the same influential family, suggesting a pattern or an alliance that warrants further investigation.

At this point, I should report a curious remark Vessuvian mentioned when I was discussing his summary with him.

"This name… Maeliticus, is indeed curious... Now, as a Sembian, I suppose I needn't remind you that in Netherese—to imagine we once thought of it as a dead language used only by scholars!—the suffix "-iticus" does suggest a sort of... oh, how shall we say? A character or essence of something. Yes, quite characteristic of those ancient scholars, ever so precise in their linguistic structures. But in Netherese "Mael" would likely derive from "malus" – meaning evil. Not a very auspicious name, not at all. The name takes a much more shadowy hue, hinting at "mal"-evolence, you see? Perhaps due to Netheril's storied affinity for the more... ah… robust aspects of the arcane.

And yet, we mustn't leap too quickly, for we find a rather more... uh, pastoral nuance? Yes, pastoral. In Thorass—the Old Common, that is—"Mael" speaks to marks, significant ones, not merely shadows but... milestones, yes, milestones of history and legacy. Fascinating, truly, how a name tumbles through language and time like a... well, like a leaf in the wind, isn't it?"

Alvund Alavandor's marriages present a curious pattern, particularly given the lack of known offspring from his first two unions. The absence of records on the Maeliticus family only deepens the mystery. Whether Alvund left Sembia with a wife and two sisters, a wife and one sister, or just one sister, Aisling Maeliticus is the only one we can confirm was with him in Thistle Ridge. Indeed, Brother Vessuvian's notes indicate that the first two Maeliticus sisters perished before the advent of the Sembians in Thistle Ridge.

We know that Aisling Alavandor established a school and an apothecary, the Phylactery, soon after their arrival in Thistle Ridge. According to the 1440 DR edition of the Yellow Abbey's Booke of Surveys and Measures, this Phylactery was notably successful, particularly in producing fertility medicines. On this point, I should mention another comment of Brother Vessuvian's:

"Master Alavandor… I say, this "Phylactery"... quite the peculiar name, don't you think? It has roots in Old Chondathan, a language of ancient wisdom and deep mysteries. Originally, the word was "phylaktērion," meaning "safeguard" or "protection." As you are no doubt aware, this term is derived from "phylax," meaning "guardian" or "protector," if you will. You're no doubt aware of the term prophylaxis. My goodness, I hope so. But over time, the word took on darker connotations, especially among those who delved too deeply into the shadowed arts. It became associated with practices that sought to preserve things that should have passed on, to hold onto power in ways... not quite natural. One wonders why such a word was chosen... unsettling, really."

Why Aisling chose such a name is indeed curious. Indeed, it may suggest that her practices were more than just herbal remedies.

The births of Andarl, Senthia, and Abrecia in rapid succession after their arrival further suggest that Aisling's expertise, combined perhaps with the unique properties of Thistle Ridge, played a significant role. Here, I am alluding to the exceptional fertility noted by Blaze Whitegate, a young magistrate at the time, and the Chaunteans' belief that the settlement was located on sacred ground. The productivity of the gardens, the quality of the fruit, the effectiveness of their medicines, and the remarkable health of their children were all attributed to this sacred influence. The Yellow Abbey's Booke even acknowledges that the success of the settlement's fertility products bordered on heresy, though formal inquiries found nothing overtly occult.

I note that the Tragedy struck as the third generation was being born. Claudius had three wives, the third of which gave him only two live children?  This is an imbalance that stands out. We must travel to Thistle Ridge as soon as possible.  Every day we delay here in Dagger Falls is a day the goblin horde can destroy more of the information that we seek.

J.H.

FROM THE CASEBOOKS OF LUCAN ALAVANDOR - THE GENEALOGICAL PUZZLE (Stephen, 17 Aug):

I have been a fool. I've been so focused on the absence of a blood relationship between myself and Lilac that I forgot that Lily Clarandal should also have been an Alavandor through her mother. Nemia seemed preternaturally certain that none of the ghosts were Alavandors. Let me explore the ramifications here.

Tenet 1: Alvund and his sister were true siblings, and both bore the name Alavandor.

Tenet 2: I, Lucan Alavandor of Saerloon, am indisputably of the Alavandor line through Alvund's sister.

Tenet 3: I do not share a blood (or whatever ghosts have) connection with *either* Lilac Alavandor or Lily Clarandal.

Implication 1: Lily's mother Senthia Alavandor was not the true child of Alvund Alavandor

Implication 2: Either a) Lilac was not the true child of Andarl Alavandor or b) Andarl was not the true child of Alvund Alavandor

Applying the principle of parsimony to the above, the most likely secondary implication is that neither Senthia nor Andarl were Alvund's children. The most likely candidate for their paternity (if we do not look elsewhere) is therefore Claudius Clarandal.

Implication 3: The marriages between Andarl and Susserah for one, and Tergus and Senthia, for another, were incestuous, as both husbands were half-siblings of their wives.

Open questions: Did old Alvund know? Was Abrecia also Claudius's daughter?

Let me see how this fits into my earlier suppositions.

Tenet 4: Saralla Sakurth and Aisling Maeliticus were each twins of their own husbands' previous wives.

Implication 4: Dorian and Lobelia were the children of a twin (Susserah) and her half-sibling (Andarl), each of whom was the child of the same father (Claudius) by two different twins (Saralla Sakurth, mother of Susserah, and Aisling Maeliticus, mother of Andarl)

Very odd.

I am not sure how any of this is significant, but if I'm to believe Alvund was my great-grandmother's true brother, there does not seem to be any other explanation.

Finally, another thing I now realize is that Alvund and Claudius were both old enough to plausibly be their wives' grandfathers:

Historical Fact: Alvund (born 1349) would have been 41 when Aisling (born 1390) was born.

Historical Fact: Claudius (born 1345) would have been 46 when Saralla (born 1391) was born.

Not necessarily out of the question for widowers, but I suppose, but it's another oddity that bears keeping in mind.

Must get out to work on my alchemical laboratory...

THE BEDSHEET - JASPER'S BLANK EYES (Gus, 17 Aug):

Jasper looks at Lucan with blank eyes, as for the third time the Sembian tries to walk him through the genealogical diagram he has painted on the back of a bedsheet he has hung up along the wall on one side of the room.

"How can you not get this?! I thought if anyone understood inbreeding it would've been you, Jas!"

The wizard blushes, looks down to the table top at the pungent carcass of a crow he is in the process of experimenting on, and then begins to shuffle papers around, unable to come up with an adequate riposte.

After a moment, he looks back up at Lucan and says: "Well that might be motive enough to wipe them all out, no?   I mean…. Uh…. Well, suppose they were sent here to create some kind of special offspring, but instead of, you know… (he waggles his eyebrows knowingly) … instead they, um… you know… (again, an attempt to convey incestuous adultery via eyebrow communication) … then perhaps the whole project was ruined. Perhaps their patron was angry and wiped them all out when this, err, incest, was discovered."

"That's it!" the annoyed voice of one Meriadoc Burgundy, comes from the threshold of the door.  He surveys the chaos of the room: a bedsheet along a wall containing detailed genealogical pictures and notes, books and papers strewn throughout the room, empty bottles everywhere. "The both of you are doing the laundry this time."

"Grawwk!!!" cries out the undead crow, startling both men, as it leaps up and begins to wildly peck at Jasper, feathers flying every which way, until Lucan smashes an empty wine bottle clear across its beak, bringing the abomination down.

LUCAN'S RANT - THE BREEDING CONSPIRACY (Stephen, 17 Aug):

Looking at the spray of beak shards, feathers, and entrails covering his friend, Lucan pulls a face.

"Bane's bowels, Jas! I expected it would be more... well, I mean, less... Anyhow. Strong bottle I guess..."

Looking away, Lucan's eyes fall on the bedsheet again and then blaze.

"No, that's not it, Jas!" He puts down the empty bottle and begins pacing around the room, peering into each bottle he comes to.

"Don't you see? This was the plan all along! I tell you, the Old Patriarch - I'll bet he's one of the Princes of Shade! He wanted to breed some kind of seventh son of a seventh son or something, a twin from a line of twins! Then bring them into the Shadowfell and ... do unspeakable things. Old Alvund got caught up in it because... his wife was a twin, and ... no, she was in on it! From the start! Her sisters, too! They got Alvund the artist in their clutches - trying to use him as a stud! He sold himself to them! But it wouldn't work! These families with lots of twins, they're weird you know? Sometimes a woman is barren, and sometimes she pumps out nothing but twins and triplets..."

Jasper nods knowledgeably, then watches Lucan walk over to a desk. Opening a small drawer, he retrieves a small stoppered bottle. He yanks the cork, tips it into his mouth, and then gasps. When he turns back to Jasper, his pupils are hugely dilated. He continues pacing, gesticulating assertively.

"So no luck. But they were too far along to stop! Maybe already generations in! There was a plan ... Claudius was brought in as muscle. He was on the Shadow's payroll in Daerlun. Then, they killed his first wife - no! He did! He took Alvund and Aisling east, then he married a pair of Chessentan twins.... Where else would a name like Sakurth be from? They were the reason they all went to the Orient! To pick up the other bloodline! But there was still a problem. Still no babies! Try one wife, try another, try another. Still no babies! But Aisling had heard something about a place in Daggerdale...

"So they came to Thistle Ridge. Got some of those Old Religion potions from the Thistles. But something was still wrong... Alvund still couldn't perform. But Claudius could, so...  Aisling kept him hooked on fertility drugs and cuckolded her husband. Six kids, two mothers, one father, and then the kids all marry each other! They wanted to trap some essence of the blood.... That's why she called it the Phylactery! Brother Vessuvian even told me so! Then in the next generation, a whole passel of children. And one pair of twins... Lobelia and Dorian! They're the ones, Jasper! Those twins! The key to it all! I know it!"

Jasper looks blearily at Lucan. "But you know that one of them died in a hunting accident, right? And also, what in Mystra's name does any of this have to do with anything?"

Lucan stops ranting and looks at Jasper with confusion, then disappointment, then elation. "Yes! Everything! It ruined the whole scheme! Dorian died and it all went up in smoke! The whole seven-generation plan! That's why Aisling killed herself. Her life's work! Just... hit his head on a branch and died, or whatever."

Jasper frowns skeptically. "I still don't-- So, then, why destroy Thistle Ridge?"

Lucan stands stock still, then holds a finger up to the side of his nose.

"Not Thistle Ridge... The Evidence!"

At that, Lucan's stomach gurgles audibly, and with a desperate look, he lurches for the door.

As his friend runs out of the room, Jasper yells out exasperatedly, "Evidence of WHAT!?"

FROM THE CASEBOOKS OF LUCAN ALAVANDOR - THE REVELATION (Stephen, 23 Aug):

I awoke from a dream early this morning, and am writing it down before I lose the memory.

I was in Father's rooms back in Saerloon. On the wall of the study hung a portrait of my great-grandmother, appearing quite different from what I remember. She was no longer dressed in the Alavandor colours (though I doubt she ever did in life, as that painting was commissioned in Father's lifetime). Instead, she was draped in silks of white and gold, wearing all manner of jewelry in an exotic fashion.

As I stared at her likeness, it seemed like a golden mist began to permeate the room. Something large and metal dropped to the floor and began rolling.  It was a golden coin, rolling toward me on its edge. The sound grew louder and louder, but the coin never seemed to get any closer. I watched the coin approach-but-not as the sound began to thunder in my ears. Finally, as the sound began to overwhelm me, the coin slowed and fell on its side to reveal the face of a sharp-eyed queen. The coin sparkled, and then there was a sudden flash.

I was suddenly elsewhere. I saw a man who looked like my father but wasn't. He was dressed in extravagant and seemingly expensive foreign garb, standing aboard a strange ship holding a spyglass and some papers. The wind smelled of salt and the sea. He looked into my eyes and smiled wryly. "The agreement with the new Alavandors," he said carefully, "was that my family and the other would take what we were given and vanish forever."

The square sail of the ship suddenly boomed taut with the wind. Looking up, I saw that the sail featured a device that resembled a pair of horns cradling the disc of the moon. When I looked back the man was gone. In his place was a winged lion with the head of a ram. Yet it spoke to me in the same voice, echoing the man. "Vanish forever...."

Then I awoke. And I knew I'd been wrong.

Everything I have thought up to this point falls too far across Deneir's Line of Simplicity. There was no conspiracy of bloodlines in Thistle Ridge. Alvund Alavandor was not my great-grandmother's brother. Not this Alvund Alavandor, at any rate. My great-great uncle sailed east and never returned. The Sembians who came to Thistle Ridge in 1407 were impostors. They came from Daerlun in the same year that the Shadow took control. And their arrival was planned in advance. So were they fleeing Netheril, or were they its agents?

Jeska has asked for a report on our investigation. Luckily Jasper shows an excellent mastery of the quill. I'll leave it to him.
