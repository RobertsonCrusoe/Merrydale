The incredible tale of the Daggerdale expedition becomes it's own tale begins on 6 Mirtul, when the heroes of this story depart the Roarke's tavern and the NC.  Maturin, Raven, Sparrow, Hannibal and Corrina take the Long Road north and west towards Rassalantar, and ultimately towards the road through Anauroch to Daggerdale. 

The first village along the route towards the desert is Rassalantar.  Here, the Daggerdale Expedition (DE) found that they were being followed by 4 members of the Rangers. 

Sheriff Grimwald, former corrupt Sheriff in the Dalelands, skilled swordsman, mage and armed with the Repeating Gond Pistola. 

Agilos Vourn, skilled tracker from the Dales, armed with a pair of powerfully enchanted magical long knives. 

Archibald Suresmith, former Purple Knight of Cormyr, still bearing the arms and armour of a knight. 

Stoneman, youngest, largest and newest member of the Rangers, armed with a large axe.  

In the village, the DE learns that a large caravan had disappeared outside of town.  Thinking that Zhents might be responsible, they go to investigate.  Boregar and Raven find evidence that 30-40 humanoids with heavy boots appeared out of nowhere and attacked the caravan.  Further, a large spider dragged many of the bodies off to the west.  It is late at this point, and the heroes return to town. 

That night, Maturin gets into a fight with Stoneman, and looses to the young Ranger, though he is not killed. 

The next day, the DE follows the spider tracks found the day before.  They lead to a small ruined keep in the woods.  Bodies are found scattered everywhere, and soon a large, cunning spider attacks the party.  They manage to repel the thing, but then find that Grimwald's Rangers have followed them.  In the brief melee that followed, the DE manages to drive them off. 

Leaving the ruined keep, the DE splits up for a time.  Maturin decides that he must return to Rassalantar and inform the authorities about the keep and the spider, and the rest continue north.  Maturin's great steed, Warburden should be fast enough to catch the rest of the DE. 

Things go poorly, however, when Maturin is confronted on the road, alone, by the Rangers.  He fights a knightly-duel with the Sheriff, the two charging at each other on horseback, making a quick exchange, and then wheeling about for another pass.  Pass after pass they made, and Maturin seemed to be getting the best of the Sheriff.  In the final pass however, the Sheriff ducked behind the neck of his great horse, and fired from this low, covered position.  Three shots from the artifact pistol made the Paladin go limp in his seat. 

Later that night, Warburden charged into Amphail, the Paladin of Ilmater lying prostrate in the saddle.  His companions manage to bring him back to health through magical means, but all are somewhat worried about the Rangers.  Boregar especially has some concern, though he tries not to let it show.  He has had run-ins with the Rangers years ago in the Dalelands. 

Leaving Amphail for Bargewright Inn the next day, the DE sees a farm burning to the north of the trail.  They rescue some farmers from some well-organized ettins and Orogs.  This is the farm of Ned Griffin, and despite the help that the DE offered here, they will find soon that this encounter would taint their name.  

Arriving in Uluvin the next day, the DE meets Razor, a half-orc head hunter who is hunting after an orc slaver named Curlclaw.    The party learns that Curlclaw is in the village meeting with a caravan that has arrived. 

Then next morning, the DE and Razor attack Curlclaw and the caravan masters who are staying at the inn.  The Caravan turns out to be a Zhent caravan that was buying slaves from Curlclaw.  The DE and Razor manage to kill the orc slaver and the caravan masters, and free about 20 slaves. 

The DE presses on into the town of Secomber.  Here, they have their reckoning with the Rangers.  The quick and chaotic battle went the DE's way for the most part, but the Rangers proved to be deadly adversaries.  Agilos, Archibald and Stoneman were all killed by Razor, and Grimwald was eventually bested by Maturin in another duel, but not before he gunned down Raven, who charged bravely at him.  Hannibal's wild magic seems to have consumed him, but none are certain of the fate of this mage who seemingly vanished from the battle. 

The next day, the captive Grimwald was put to death in his cell by Boregar, who shot him with his own pistol.  Boregar assumed possession of the Gond Pistola from that point. 

Heading further east the next day, the DE (who has parted ways with the miscreant assassin, Razor) camps out at some ruins by a river crossing.  Here they meet Dorian of Sembia, in the guise of Tristan of Shadowdale.  The DE was most suspicious of this 'druid' who was wearing chain mail armour and carrying a sword.  They did not yet realize that they were traveling with the Greatest Swordsman in the Realms.  The DE is also joined by the dwarven battlerager, Dalak, who is searching for his brother Balak, who has likely been captured by the same slavers that the DE is pursuing in Daggerfalls.   

The first obstacle to this new group of friends was the Ebon Spur.  This caravan was clearly a Zhent caravan, and was also well seeded with assassins, fighters and priests.  A priest named Cauldrawn and a mage named Duncan were bested by the new company, and the following letter was found upon the body of Cauldrawn; 

There are a pair of men that you should know about heading your way. 

1)    Mourn "Raven" Raventree of Meiliki.  This able ranger is leading a team of Harpers and associates to Daggerdale.  He is a large, clean-shaven man with a patch over his left eye.  He is of noble blood. 

2)    Boregar "The Wanderer".  This fat Harper is a master of the staff, if not personal hygiene or manners. 

You will be rewarded 500 gold for each of them, should their heads be brought to my man in Daggerdale.   

They also travel with a paladin of Ilmater, a mage from the Dalelands, a gnome and a shady character named Sparrow.  Each of them is worth 100 gp to you. 

Use whatever means you can to stop them from reaching Anauroch.  Contact the Ebon Spur in Llorkh, and tell them that Duncan Eldoun will no longer be indebted to me, should this task be done. 

This letter was dated 2 days after Boregar and Raven met, and the means by which this information reached points east so fast is still unknown to this day. 

Along the road to Loudwater, in the town of Zelbross, the DE encountered the Harper Garrick.  Garrick and Boregar were old friends, and were glad to see each other.  The DE and Garrick were put up for a night at the tower of a mutual friend of the two harpers named Durnax, a retired adventurer.  Garrick explained that he did not trust the 'new' manager of the inn in town, the Last Place.  The proprietor, Ordilar appears to have come into possession of the inn under suspicious circumstances.  Corrina went poking around and made an interesting find.  Atop the small inn, she found a shack with some pigeons and empty cages.  Using her magic, she learned from a pigeon that winged snakes were used to fly across the desert and deliver messages to this shack.  From here the pigeons take the notes to points west.  Corrina managed to convince the pigeon to take its notes to Durnax's tower from then on. 

Back in the tower, Garrick had grim news to give to the travelers.  He has many details about the troubles blocking travel and trade from East to West, and all things seem to point to an enormous Zhent plot that seems to be working.  Monsters on the borders of Cormyr, rampant privateering on the Sea of Fallen Stars, The risen cult of Bane, the war in Cormyr with Sembia.  All key trade routes to the East were closed except for the recently re-opened route through Anauroch. 

He explains that the Zhents have found the capital and slaves enough to rebuild Dekanter (an ancient ruin) and that their influence in the mountains and hills to the west of Anauroch is strong.  Orogs and orcs in the area have been well trained and well armed, and are letting only select caravans through. 

Garrick produces a map of Anauroch at this time, which he gives to Boregar.  The map is of Zhentish origin, and marks the road (really, just tracks through the sand) through the desert, and the 4 Zhent outposts along the way. 

Garrick has some bad news to give Boregar that night in the safety of the tower; Harpers all throughout the Realms are turning up dead or going missing.  Their numbers are dangerously low. 

The next day, Garrick departs, indicating that he is going to 'find another way through', and that hopefully he and his old friend will meet on the other side. 

The DE continues east, and arrives in the morose town of Loudwater.  Loudwater was still recovering from the afflictions of a mage named Azmund, who cast some powerful spells upon the town two years previous.  The mage had been captured (Kennyth and Gaebril and Garrick are responsible for this), but the mage was still imprisoned.  Apparently, any man or woman attempting to put him to his death is struck with a fear of their own demise, and find themselves unable to do him in.  Thus, Azmund lives in the dungeons of the Keep in Loudwater to this day. 

Another more interesting discovery in Loudwater was this poster; 

A Group of Criminals heads this way! 

A gang of murderers being led by a brigand named RAVEN and a rapist named BOREGAR have been spotted and chased out of points west (known to have killed 10 people in Amcathra, abducted 2 children from Bargewright Inn and raped 2 women on the road to Secomber at Griffin's Farm. 

RAVEN is described as a large, muscular man with a patch over his left eye, and BOREGAR is described only as a large, fat man who favours a staff. 

These men should be avoided by all but the most skilled of swordsmen. 

A 200 gold piece reward for either of these men can be claimed in Llorkh at the Garrison. 

Not only did they find that poster, but once again ran across the assassin Razor, who by this time was calling himself the 'Greatest Bounty Hunter in the Realms'.  Razor had a copy of the poster in his greasy jerkin, and smiled cruelly at the DE as they passed him by. 

Loudwater was the last civilized place on the East Road at this point in the journey, and the DE paused in this solemn town to re-supply.  All stayed in Loudwater for a time, except Boregar who argued that there was no time to waste.  And so, he lived up to his name and left early, to wander the East Road. 

The rest of the DE left a couple days afterward, and upon their travel along the East Road, they received magical word from Beljuril that the NC had been victorious.  The grim news about  the death of the Harper Initiate Rufus is taken hard by Boregar, who is starting to suspect some greater plot, and fears that someone has infiltrated his order, or sold them out. 

While crossing the mountains that border the great Sand Sea, the DE run into many encounters with humanoids, both unorganized and organized.  In one particular encounter in a wild magic zone, a demon came to the rescue of the DE, killing a pair of hill giants and chasing a third off into the hills. 

The DE comes to Dekanter, and finds that the town is under quite a bit of construction, and that they make no efforts to hide their allegiance; banners of the Zhentarim and of Cyric fly freely here, and there is clearly much slavery here.  The DE was loathe to leave this abomination behind, but a great task had been set before them, and so they pressed on, the desert just a day away. 

Heading into Anauroch, the DE is disheartened to find that they have traveled less than 20 miles after about 4 days, and soon after that they learn that they have traveled in a circle for a long time.  Sand storms called 'Azirla Dahab' plague their passage, and it seems as if though the desert was not going to let them pass. 

On the 16th of Kythorn, the DE, and Maturin especially, had an encounter with fate.  It was on this day that they encountered Grandfather, a great werelion, and his consorts on a rocky heel jutting out of the hot golden sands.  Grandfather demanded tribute in flesh for passage, but none was offered.  Grandfather and his consorts attacked, and in a long battle with the creatures, Maturin, Boregar, Dorian and Sparrow were all wounded by the great werelion.  Maturin, especially, took wounds numerous and grievous enough that there was little doubt that he had been infected.  Dorian was spared his death by Tymora alone, as Grandfather's great maw found his neck, and flung his limp body into the sands before ripping at the guts of the dangerous battlerager.  In the end, they managed to repel the creatures, but their wounds were severe.  Here are the recorded thoughts of Maturin the Grey from the next day; 

It is less than two months since we left Waterdeep, yet it seems that years have been stripped from my life.  Our party has been five days in the desert, and we have covered less than twenty miles. 

The desert wind whips my robes about me, the sand stings my eyes and open wounds.  Sharp pains shoot through my skull.  My vision blurs.  The bite of the Lion-Creature, the harsh and blinding light of the desert sun, and my own internal turmoil are almost more than I can bear. 

We are stopped for a rest on the path leading us towards salvation or damnation.  The first Zhent-tainted oasis lies over a day's travel ahead.  We will find no respite.  What we might have paid for will be denied us- my money is gone.  Our horses are gone. 

Dorian is crouched on his haunches beside me, coughing silently and clutching his ruined throat.  I watch him spit blood onto the sand.  The moisture evaporates immediately, leaving a faint discoloration on the pale sands.
