== 22-24 Eleint - Tipping the Balance of Power ==

The next day at noon, a mage named [[Grimspite]] flew into Shadowdale from Dagger Falls under a banner of truce. The man was acting as an emissary for Yarkul, and brought a message to Lord Amacathra and the people of Shadowdale which outlined the terms of a [[Yarkul's Treaty|treaty]] of non-aggression with Zhent-controlled Dagger Falls. In a reversal of his former policies, Yarkul now proposed to open the borders of Daggerdale to both trade and travel, and to free any and all slaves in Daggerdale, in exchange for a cessation of hostilities between the two governments.

=== A New Day in Dagger Falls ===
[[image:Daggerfalls 1371.jpg|thumb|650px|Occupied Dagger Falls in 1371 and 1372]]
Lord Amcathra tentatively agreed to these terms, but requested that his own emissaries be sent back with Yarkul's messenger to confirm that the terms of the treaty were being upheld. On the morning of the 23rd, the desert companions set forth to fulfill this mission, and to provide escort for any refugees who wished to return with them to Shadowdale. Their passage was unhindered, and the journey was made in less than a day. 

It was only when the party arrived in Dagger Falls that Boregar was able to comprehend true impact of the Zhent presence there - the town barely resembled the Dagger Falls he had seen the year before. A great wall of solid stone had been constructed around the town perimeter, and an impressive fortified bridge now spanned the River Tesh, its architectural beauty marred by the bodies of hanged men that decorated it. One of these, he recognized, was the corpse of [[Randal Morn]], and Sparrow noted that the former Lord of Daggerdale's death-grimace had been preserved by means of a slight necromantic dweomer.

Inside the new Eastern Bailey, its great gates guarded by a pair of bored-looking hill giants, hundreds of slaves had been gathered, looking frightened and malnourished, but nevertheless in apparent safety. After briefly examining the state of the town, which seemed quiet, it was decided that the emissaries would escort the slaves back to Shadowdale immediately under the medical guidance of Maturin, and only then return to parlay with Yarkul. Unsurprisingly, not one of the refugees opted to remain in Dagger Falls.

===The Lord of Daggerdale===
[[image:fish.jpg|thumb|Sparrow avoids an ignominious fate]]
Sparrow, however, stayed behind that night - 24 Eleint - and infiltrated the Tower to carry out his holy mission of assassinating Yarkul. Locating the wizard's study, he lay in wait for the Zhent until late in the evening when, at the stroke of eleven bells, he sprang on the unsuspecting wizard, and the two began a spell duel that tested the ingenuity of both men. A polymorph enchantment by Yarkul nearly resulted in Sparrow's retirement to a goldfish bowl atop the Zhent's mantle, but Sparrow had one last ''dispel magic'' available - a boon from Mystra herself - and managed to reverse Yarkul's spell even as the Zhent was reaching down to pick up his flapping, fishy form. Beljuril immediately struck his surprised adversary with a sudden blow, and Yarkul crumpled to the floor, slain. So was Mystra's mission fulfilled that day, as the Xvimite wizard Yarkul was sent to Kelemvor's justice by the Sparrow - tying off at least one loose end in the Zhentarim web that had been cast far and wide across the Realms. When Sparrow continued his investigations however, he was magically bested by an unknown party. His return to consciousness was but brief, and though his eyes were covered, he heard an unfamiliar voice tell him that he had meddled where he was not wanted, and that though his life would be spared, he was forever exiled from Dagger Falls on pain of death. In a cruel twist, his hand was then struck off by a hot iron - much as Yarkul's own hands had been struck off by Rychel in Waterdeep before they were replaced by the twisted claws he had died with. The intense pain of the blow was too much, and the Sparrow again lost consciousness.

== 25 Eleint - Waking Dreams ==
It took the better part of two days to escort the great host of freed slaves back to Lord Amcathra's domain, and their arrival on the evening of the 25th swelled the ranks of patients in the charge of the already overworked priests and retinue who were all that remained of the Order of the Lambent Rose. Maturin, though weary from his travels and still unwelcome among those who had once been his brethren, nevertheless understood his duty and stayed up all the night to help in the treatment of the refugees. They, at least, welcomed his presence among them, and profited by it. 

[[image:sparrow.jpg|thumb|250px|Sparrow's hand bears the mark of Mystra's favour]]
As he worked, his exhaustion must have overwhelmed him, for the wounded and weak he tended began to be as one. At length he entered a tent where he found a large armoured figure standing over a sleeping man. When the figure turned toward him, Maturin recognized the armoured figure as that of Torm. "You have done well, paladin," intoned the avatar, "But your victory will mean naught if you let the tablets pass beyond your reach. You and your companions must leave now - there is yet time." Even as the avatar spoke, Maturin felt the deity's energy flow into him and his vision briefly went blank. When his sight cleared, the figure was gone, and a young orderly was standing at the door of the tent, calling him to see another patient. Strangely, his exhaustion had been wiped away, and he felt full of restless energy, and resolved to set out in the morning when his comrades had rested.

Upon returning, Boregar also found that [[Dagger Falls Harper Missive (3)|another note]] had arrived from his agent in Dagger Falls, dated five days previously. The note detailed information that the heroes were already aware of, but the Harper was glad that his agent remained safe and observant. 

A final event this day was the brief appearance of strange riders, who galloped into the town late into the night, pausing only to convey the lifeless figure of Sparrow into the care of the guards posted at the entrance to the tent city before riding back into the night without further explanation. The shadow-walker was quickly taken to one of the communal medicine tents, where he passed a fitful night of visions and fever-dreams as his body fought the infections attacking his bloody stump of a wrist. In one of these, he saw himself standing, whole, before his Lady Midnight - the goddess Mystra - who commanded him to establish a branch of Her church in the Dalelands.

Upon waking the next morning, he found his wrist healed over with healthy scar tissue - but that true to his vision, he was now whole in Mystra's eyes: a spell-fire blue spectral hand appeared when he worked the Weave.

== 26 Eleint - Decisions at the Twisted Tower ==

[[image:shadowdale.png|thumb]]
Night blended into day, but dawn brought no end to the rapid developments that were to shift the course of history in the Dalelands. 

As Lathander's Crown broke the eastern horizon, the Shadowdale Council convened to hear the testimony of Amcathra's emissaries and several representatives of the refugees that had returned with the heroes. In addition to the by-now familiar faces of the council members, three new faces now joined, one of which was known to the desert companions as [[Garrik Shining]], whom they had last seen on the other side of the desert. Shining had arrived the previous day by way of the south road, after a long and brutal trek through the Goblin Marches. The other two newcomers were the ranger's companions - [[Eiran]], a druid who served as a gamekeeper for a powerful Waterdeep family with interests in the Dales, and [[Gaebril Tremalkin]], a mysterious knight whose fierce countenance was softened by sad, clear eyes.

At length it was decided that the desert companions - accompanied by Garrik, Gaebril and Eiran - would set out for Dagger Falls that day. The details of what came to pass at this meeting are best recounted through the words of Maturin, who recounted the discussions and later events in a [[Maturin's Letter to Balak|letter to Dalak]] days later. These will be revealed in turn, but it would be best to first report certain events that occurred directly after the council meeting, as the morning drew on.

When Dorian returned to his tent to make preparations for the impending departure, he found Dragon waiting for him. She had stolen surreptitiously into his quarters this day to deliver not a dagger in the back, but a word of warning. She brought news of Sparrow's accomplishment, and reported that the Xvimites had sworn that the shadow-walker must die. She was sure that an attempt was going to be made on his life soon, for Yarkul's former succubus concubine, [[Amaretta]], who lived in the Xvimites' den in Dagger Falls, had sworn to murder the assassin herself. The two exchanged what was to be their first kiss of many in the days and months to come, and she departed as soundlessly and secretly as she had arrived.

Dorian set out immediately to find Sparrow, who he learned had been brought into the camp the night before, but it took some time for him to track down the tent to which his companion had been taken. Eventually, he found the tent, but learned that a child had delivered a message to the priest as soon as he had awoken, and that Sparrow had thereupon met with a beautiful woman outside. According to a medic, the two had departed in the direction of town. 

The Xvimites had made their move. Maturin's letter to Dalak details the portentous events that followed.

== 26-27 Eleint - The Redemption of Dagger Falls ==

Early on the morning of the 28th of Eleint, two young men rode into Shadowdale from Dagger Falls, insisting that they speak to Master [[Balak]]. "We bear missives from [[Maturin|Lord Horgath]]!" they cried. Taking a brief respite from his hearth, Balak received a [[Maturin's Letter to Balak|pair of letters]] and a sack of coins from one of the envoys before they rode into the town with more letters. What follows is the second of these letters, addressed to Maturin's convalescing companion, Dalak.

===Maturin's Letter===
{{script}}

Dear Dalak,

It is nearly over, my friend. I trust that your health has improved under Brother [[Turan]]'s conscientious care and [[Timfast]]'s cookery. I don't need to tell you... On second thought, I'll remind you that the wounds you suffered at Freedom Hill last week were grievous indeed, and your life is still in the balance. This time next year we may be able to share an ale together, but ONLY if you follow Brother Turan's medications and keep your head. I intend to see you hale and healthy when my work is done. 

I wish to tell you that [[Baron Drake]], the devil we fought at Anathar's Dell, is dead and the Church of Iyachtu Xvim in Dagger Falls is broken. If I get ahead of myself, it is to spare you the suspense, which is not beneficial to you in your condition. I will tell all in turn.

====The Shadowdale Council ====

Following our return to Dagger Falls with the refugees from Yarkul's regime, one of the refugees, a man named [[Cornish]] who had been enslaved in the kitchens of the Banite temple, told Lord Mourngrym about the destruction of the Church of Bane in Dagger Falls by the forces of [[William Cormaeril]].

At the same council meeting where the refugee’s story was heard, we learned that the Zhents in Dagger Falls had come into possession of a primer for [[Harper]] Code, and a list of the names and descriptions of known Harpers. The Harper agent [[Garrik Shining]] and a companion had set out only a week after our departure from Loudwater this past spring, coming southeast through the Goblin Marches rather than through Anauroch with their news. He brought the unhappy news that only a handful of Harper agents remained in the Western Realms. Garrik's companion is a large man named [[Gaebril Tremalkin]], a stony-eyed and determined holy warrior of a strange faith.

Boregar, too, brought news. He had received not a half-hour earlier word from his agent in Dagger Falls, int he form of a [[Dagger Falls Harper Missive (4)|missive]] which brought word that Yarkul had been overthrown, and that William Cormaeril had succeeded in seizing control of Dagger Falls - but that the city was still rife with corruption and fear. While Cormaeril pressed negligently on with reforms and grand plans to ally the New Dagger Falls with Cormyr and Yulash - ([[Thagdul]]'s new stomping ground), daemons walked among the citizens, and the murderous cultists of the Baneson brazenly flouted the new laws, kidnapping unwary citizens and keeping them as slaves.

With the mysterious slabs making their way ever deeper into the desert, [[Sparrow]] sought the advice of Lady Mystra, and divined that we would have no hope of catching them before they reached Addas Babar, the largest of the Zhents' Anauroch outposts. [[Dorian]] and I disagreed on the best course of action, but with the counsel brought by Boregar, Gaebril and Garrik, we decided to cleanse Dagger Falls for once and all. We have been joined by one other - a man named [[Eiran]], who seems to be a druid of great ability. and the last surviving member of a sylvan organisation called The [[Circle of Nine]].

==== Sparrow Disappears ====

Following the council, we were to get some rest before heading north at noon. I had barely caught my first wink of sleep in 48 hours when Dorian burst into my tent to tell me that Sparrow was both in grave danger and missing. I know not how the warning came, but Sparrow was nowhere to be found. A hurried search was organized, and with a presence of mind that still surprises me, I tracked Sparrow to the Old Skull Inn, where patrolling guards had seen him head in the company of a beautiful woman.

En masse, our party charged into the inn and up the stairwell only to find ourselves confronting an obviously ensorcelled member of Shadowdale's emergency militia. Our friend Dorian, hardly the most level-headed of men these days, failed immediately and spectacularly in his attempts at negotiation and began to scuffle with the man. I ran downstairs with Boregar to enlist the aid of any spellcasters who considered themselves decent enough to come to our aid and I am surprised and sorry to say that only one soul in the bar saw his duty clearly. By the time we reclimbed the stairs, Gaebril and Dorian had forced their way through the door that Sparrow and the woman were sequestered behind. I came to the doorway just in time to see her figure sprout wings, let out an inhuman screech, and launch upwards, through the very skylight the assassin Dragon had broken in her own escape only days ago.

While Garrik fired an arrow up at the quickly diminishing beast, I bade the spellcaster dispel whatever magicks lay upon Sparrow. But even as his eyes cleared, I could see that his movements were growing sluggish. I suspected that the foul thing had poisoned him in some way. When I asked him, he indicated that it was so. I quickly jammed my finger down his throat to induce vomiting, and, satisfied that he was safe, helped him down to the bar. By now, the inn had emptied to watch whatever succubus or harpy that had been preying on our friend fly quickly north, towards Daggerdale.

Our course was clearer than ever.

==== Dagger Falls ====

At noon we set out for Dagger Falls, arriving the following morning, chased through the night by a cold autumn wind and lightning storm that dogged our heels. Sparrow was afraid that he would be recognized at the gate, but whatever forces had issued the capital interdiction against his return had evidently not passed the order on. (Happily, Sparrow's spellcasting ability has not diminished with the loss of his hand - whenever he attempts to cast a spell, a phantasmal hand of blue light appears to work the somatics - truly a sign of his sanctity, at least in Mystra's eyes). Passing through the gate relatively unmolested, we sought out the to the north where we were to meet a contact of Boregar's at a pub outside the gates. (Why must we always seek out these houses of sin? Would it be too much to ask to meet a contact in a church once in a while?). I must admit that certain among us were not very circumspect about our inquiries, which may have had dire consequences that I will speak of soon.

Investigating the town, we confirmed that though Yarkul had fallen, the evil grip of the Zhent churches still held sway. Boregar, as the official ambassador of Shadowdale, sought an audience with Lord William. Though Cormaeril could not command the sacrifice of his guardsmen, and would not allow any volunteers, he indicated that, should we take up arms with the Xvimites, his forces would not intervene. I will tell you that I do not like this petty little man. Not only did he force his own cowardice on his men, who could not have refused duty's clarion call to rid the Dale of the Baneson's evil taint, he played us against the Xvimites without committing himself to either hand, seeking, no doubt to ingratiate himself with whatever force proved victorious. A Cormyrean by birth, he seems every inch the bastard son of a Sembian and a Zhent.

==== Assault on the Baneson's Lair ====

With Cormaeril's distinctly pusillanimous blessing, we waited while Sparrow did reconnaissance on the Xvimite temple. It was locked solid, without windows, having only metal doors, and being completely encapsulated with both stone and metal, it seemed impregnable. Before long, Xvimite priests appeared as from nowhere and began to work their magic against us. Immediately, Dorian and Boregar charged, while Garrik, Gaebril and I put our backs to one another and began to face off against the companies of armsmen that came at us from either direction. With his battery of spells, Sparrow ran an impressive but conservative guerrilla campaign as support.

For over an hour we fought, with darkness, paralysis and the curse of the Baneson laying on us like a foul haze. But we did not tire, and our courage did not flag. But just when we thought the infidels might break, a gong sounded, the remains of the force let out a hoarse cheer, and the Baron strode from the opening doors of the temple, flanked by ten experienced warriors.

Without hesitation, Sparrow through a fireball into their midst.

Horribly burned, all but the Baron retreated into the dark fastness of the temple, shutting the doors in front of them. Though Garrik, Gaebril and I were still engaged with the warriors, Dorian rose to the challenge of fighting Drake, his confident swagger unchecked by the cambion's foul presence. My memories of the battle become hazy, but I know that Garrik broke from our circle to attack the Baron and was cut down, though whether by the Baron or a craven armsman I know not. Sparrow barely managed to bandage his wounds in time to save his life. Were he not with me now, I should have said he died.

==== Dorian's Dance with the Devil ====

Soon Gaebril too broke from the fight and confronted the cambion with Dorian. I could see nothing from where I stood, all being concealed by the foul thing's darkness, but when the miasma cleared, Gaebril had fallen, and Dorian began a solitary dance with the devil. By now we fought on the strength of our potions alone.

For what seemed an eternity, I was facing off against three trained warriors, Boregar with another two and Dorian seemed at an impasse with Drake. Then, in a critical instant, the Baron lunged at Dorian and overcorrected, and Dorian's returning blow nearly cleaved off the daemon's arm. Seeing our chance, I broke from the circle and took a few short steps towards the prostrate thing to bring Raventooth down on its black and battered head, cleaving it off. (Though I meant no offense, Dorian has been quite curt with me since this decapitation. Apparently I stole 'the glory' of the moment from him at his finest hour. I cannot honestly comprehend how someone's code of honour would see necessary bloodshed as glory, and not as a hideous last resort).

With a look of shock on its face, the daemon's body collapsed. Immediately, its armour and weapon began to bubble and sublimate into foul mist. The remaining Xvimites broke, hard. But I do not think they escaped. While we began to take stock of the situation, Boregar brought Drake's head to Cormaeril, who seemed overawed. People began to float back into the streets to marvel at what we had done, and the guard quickly organized to finish the job and force open the gates of the temple. At length, it was done.

==== A Bitter Victory ====

Never have I been involved in so fierce or lengthy a battle, and never again do I want to see so much bloodshed. I have a new appreciation for the stalwartness of your kind, my friend, and a new sadness in knowing what sort of life you live. May Ilmater let my blessing upon you be heard. We managed to rescue the men who had been enslaved in the bowels of the temple, and through no small degree of argument with that bastard Cormaeril, I was able to commandeer some supplies and care for their upkeep, at Dagger Falls' expense.

Unfortunately, early in the afternoon, we discovered the fresh corpse of Boregar's contact. Evidently he had been murdered while the battle raged. Dagger Falls is still without true peace, as I fear it will ever be.

==== An Uncertain Destiny ====

As for our current situation, I will be as informative as I can. Dorian has left us, and I do not know if he will return. His plans for the future have varied erratically over the last week. I hope to see him again, but will leave it to Tymora to see that our paths cross again. Gaebril and I, and perhaps Boregar, are heading into the desert in pursuit of the slabs, and I intend to hunt Grandfather to fulfill my pledge to Ilmater. Garrik's plans are uncertain, though I expect he will accompany us. As for Sparrow, he has decided to reconsecrate the Xvimite cultists' den and build a temple to Mystra and Lathander in partnership with Cormaeril. I pray that they are successful.

We have accomplished so much in the last months, and yet so much more is before us. Of the four of us who crossed the desert together, I am comforted that you, at least, have achieved peace, though at great cost. I wish you and Balak all the best and bid you take care. Boregar and Dorian have much to deal with, and I cannot escape the feeling that our destinies are still linked with Dagger Falls and Anauroch. For myself, doubt plagues me like a shadow, stronger now that the path seems clear. I had another vision from Torm yestereve, urging me again into the desert, away from Dagger Falls. There is yet to be a reckoning.

Ilmater guide us all,


{{sig|Maturin}}


</div>

The morning of the 27th had indeed dawned overcast and grim. Even today, years later, many locals claim that they woke up that morning to great crashes of thunder and lightning, with the sense that some great cataclysm was imminent. Almost all good citizens report that they felt very anxious as the sky went from deep black to grey, and many claim to this day that it was the Liberators of Dagger Falls, our heroes, who brought the storm with them.

For a generation of Dalesmen to come, this battle was known as the "[[Liberator's Storm]]", and those heroes that defied the Baneson in his own lair became known thereafter as the Order of the {{catlink|Eastern Storm}}.

=== The Count of Castle Daggerdale ===
[[image:Castle Daggerdale.jpg|thumb|Castle Daggerdale]]
The rest of the day was spent in the purification of Brotherhood Tower and the treatment of their injuries. Despite Maturin's visions from Torm, he remained undecided about how to proceed. To pursue the tablets now would leave the Cyricists as the sole religious presence in Dagger Falls - and a powerful one at that. To tarry and face the Cyricists, however, would mean to risk losing the Beholder Tablets for good. The heroes [[Warriors' Debate|hotly debated]] the issue of leaving, to pursue the stones - an option championed unambiguously by Dorian - but many were unhappy with the situation in Dagger Falls still. The death of Boregar's informant indicated that the swiftly metamorphosizing Dagger Falls was even still a nest of treachery, and that there was yet work to do in the Dales.

Dorian became enraged at this lengthy decision-making process over what to do next, and rode off alone to pursue the tablets, damning his companions for their slow-wittedness. Maturin need not have worried about his comrade, however, for he was soon convinced to change his tack. As he rode along the North Road, he encountered the two outlanders the companions had encountered in Shadowdale. The two had just killed a Zhentarim mage-messenger, and were themselves on the trail of the Beholder tablets. They convinced the swordsman that his best chance would be to follow the path that his comrades chose, and as the heat of the day's battle and his bloodlust faded, Dorian's senses soon returned. He rode hard to his new home in the ruins of Castle Daggerdale, only a few miles away, and where he intended to spend the night. He had one more surprise that day - Dragon had been following him the whole time, and the two shared their first night together there in the ruins. The next day, they parted, Dragon heading west by way of the South Road, and Dorian back to Dagger Falls to rejoin his friends.

==28 Eleint - The Final Purge ==
At noon the next day, Dorian returned to his companions, and [[Warriors' Debate|the discussions]] over how to proceed continued. Boregar and Dorian even went so far as to suggest deposing Cormaeril and placing Sparrow in charge of Dagger Falls, in the hopes that Mystra's influence would balance that of Cyric. Such talk was soon mooted, however, as Cormaeril called a meeting of the Daggerdale Council, to which many of the heroes were personally invited. All major players in the Dale were present, and the matters that were up for discussion were quite wide-ranging. The nature of the council was changed to include both Sparrow and [[Rewel Thunderstorm]], and plans were made to turn Brotherhood tower into a joint place of worship for Lathander and Mystra. Further, Dorian had that morning negotiated the purchase of the deed to Castle Daggerdale and the small barony that surrounded it, thus landing himself title and a permanent position on the Council.
=== The Man behind the Curtain ===
[[image:cyric.png|thumb]]
As might be expected, tempers soon ran hot at the council, and Cormaeril refused to accede to any requests that the Cyricist temple be investigated for slavery or other contravention of the policies of the new order. At length, Gaebril and Sparrow had seen enough, and the two began a brief but effective inquisition at that very council table, to astounding effect. As Gaebril drew his sword, Sparrow swiftly moved to dispel any magic in effect in the council chamber, and the visage of [[Morgul]] the Vigilant Talon - the ranking priest of Cyric in the town - was replaced by the withered face of a zombie puppet. Another surprise was the figure of a man who had apparently been clinging to the ceiling. Becoming suddenly visible, he crashed heavily to the floor behind Cormaeril. 

The man recovered from the fall with amazing agility, however, and after muttering a quick incantation he dove out the tower window, taking flight and disappearing in quick succession. Despite his quick escape, however, he had been unable to avoid being struck by one of Garrik's unerring arrows, and through miraculous effort, the Harper was able to track the spy's dripping blood to a set of chambers in another part of the tower, where the man was discovered and quickly slain. This man turned out to be a Zhentarim spy named [[Venosar]], and Boregar was troubled to find a satchel containing a number of bloodstained Harper pins in the man's possession. Clearly Venosar had been responsible for his informants' capture and assassination. With a sickening sense of responsibility, the heroes realized that the number of demons reported to be in Brotherhood Tower had been whispered in confidence to all the members of the council, with each member being told a different number. This had been how Boregar’s man was discovered and subsequently executed.

Another important find in the bed chamber of Venosar was '[[Ratbiter]]' - the fated longsword of [[Morgunn Lathkule]]. Yarkul had apparently carried the blade with him all the way from Waterdeep, as a souvenir of his defeat and humiliation of [[Raven]], the heroes' departed companion. The blade was taken up by Dorian, but the stewardship of this legendary weapon was not to end there.

=== Defying the Dark Sun  ===
[[image:Dark Sun Temple of Daggerfalls 1371.jpg|left|thumb|200px|The final bastion of evil in Dagger Falls]]
Returning to the Council Chamber, which was now in an uproar as the various members that represented the interests of the Dalesmen squabbled over what had happened, the heroes found William Cormaeril slumped in his seat, clearly shaken by the encounter. He was soon able to rouse himself however, and the crowd quieted as he spoke, saying simply, "There is a temple to Cyric in my town. We have work to do."

All the resources of the Tower were mustered to heal the wounds the heroes had suffered the day before, and as the priests rested in meditation to prepare for the siege to come, Cormaeril's men stockpiled weapons and enchanted scrolls which would be used in the battle. At length, the priests were ready, and the united forces of the Order of the Eastern Storm, the Thunderstorm Faction of the Lathanderite congregation from Shadowdale, and a clear-minded William Cormaeril and his hearth guard led a three-pronged attack on the monstrous bastion of evil that was the Church of Cyric.
[[image:daggerfallsundead.jpg|thumb|Morgul's undead army pours forth from the temple crypt|450px]]

Another battle of epic proportions followed, as hordes of undead and spectral creatures boiled forth from the crypts beneath the heathen temple. Wave after wave of innumerable undead of various forms bolstered the ranks of priests and warriors of Cyricist brethren, and the battle was a long and difficult one indeed. The presence of Rewel Thunderstorm and his aides, however, lent a key strength to the heroes, and by dusk, the Church of Cyric - last of the villainous faiths whose poisonous presence had been encouraged by the defeated Zhent government - was completely eradicated, and [[Morgul]] the Vigilant Talon was slain.

=== What the Corpse Knew ===
Morgul's death did not necessarily mean that he could no longer be of use to the heroes, and in a closely-guarded ceremony later that night, Sparrow employed fell magics to wrench Morgul's spirit back from its torment beyond, to be interrogated by the heroes, and the account the spectral villain gave of the events that had led to the enslavement of the minds and hearts of the men of Dagger Falls was a terrible one indeed. Some of his words corroborated the suspicions of the heroes - as Lord of Daggerdale, Yarkul had been but a puppet, though one with many masters. One of these was the Moonsea man, [[Thagdul]], an ally of the Cyricists who had been in Yûlash for at least the past three months. Yarkul had been Thagdul's vassal, ruling only at Thagdul's behest. Yarkul's relationship with the Xvimites had been a factor in their power in Dagger Falls, but they had gained a foothold much earlier. The succubus Amaretta had been conjured by powers that pre-dated Yarkul's return to the Heartlands. Chuckling, Morgul revealed the Cyric had indeed been influencing the Shadowdale Lathanderites through [[Cassimar]]. The Zhentarim assassin, Venosar, had been the Cyricist's "man on the council" - he had accompanied Cormaeril's party in secret, as part of a plan to replace Yarkul, whose untimely death had simply pushed the Cyricists' schedule forward. The villain had little to say about the beholders however - he had been in Dagger Falls for six months, but had seen nothing. He did know, however, that Cyric opposed the idea of the tablets heading west. At this revelation, the Vigilant Talon's body fell limp, the corpses dead eyes now closed again forever.

== 29 Eleint - Liberation Day ==

The next day dawned bright and warm, as if summer had returned - if only briefly - to the embattled Dale. The sun shone brightly down on the fortified tower that had housed the Cyricists, and in the bright morning light the building seemed nothing more than a shabby construction of bricks and metal. The aura of gloom and fear that had surrounded it had dissipated with the evil that was now gone forever. The corporeal remains of the undead army were sanctified and blessed, and a great pyre was made to send the poor souls that the Dark Sun had enslaved to a final, peaceful rest.

Lord Cormaeril declared that a great feast be prepared to celebrate the accomplishments of the heroes, and to reassure the citizens that they were finally free of the Zhent yoke. Most of the Eastern Storm remained in Dagger Falls for this victory celebration. Maturin knew that they would be heading now to Anauroch, and engaged a local blacksmith to customize his armour so that he might be prepared should he survive to take on [[Grandfather]]. Dorian began recruiting teams of labourers and hiring foremen to begin the rebuilding of Castle Daggerdale. Sparrow did not wait to begin his modifications of Brotherhood Tower, where he planned to start his own church of Mystra, and Gaebril and Garrik - resolved to accompany the desert companions in their quest - began to secure supplies that the heroes would need on the long road West.

Boregar, however, took leave of his companions briefly, to return to Shadowdale with Cormaeril's emissary, and deliver the recovered Harper pins into the hands of [[Shilve]] and the few Harper agents still active in Shadowdale. His organization had dealt a fearsome blow by Venosar, and even the assassin's death did little to assuage his sense of loss. Though Dagger Falls was free for a time from the influence of the black faiths and Zhentarim schemes, he knew that it was only a matter of time before the Black Network began to send out feelers and start to rebuild its web. He only hoped that it would take long enough that when the time came, his allies would again be ready.

== Harvestide - Legacies of the Eastern Storm ==
[[image:harvestide in dagger falls.jpg|thumb|400px|Harvestide celebrations in Free Dagger Falls]]
By the time things were cooled off in Dagger Falls, Harvestide was upon the Dales. The first Harvestide Festival of Free Dagger Falls began early on Liberation Day and continued into Marpenoth as Dalesmen and women celebrated the heroes and their freedom. Rewel Thunderstorm held the Lathanderite ceremony known as the First Feast, giving Lathander's blessing, and the days were filled with merriment and laughter. For the Eastern Storm, however, these were days of preparation. Winter was coming, and they were past due to leave. News was filtering back from the west that orcs had taken the mines along the Tethyamar trail, and that giants were blocking the road west. All signs indicated that the Tablets were weeks ahead of the heroes, but they felt that they had no choice but to move quickly, and hopefully catch up to them in the desert. When Boregar returned on 1 Marpenoth, the heroes bid Sparrow and Thunderstorm farewell, and set out on the road. They were accompanied to the borders of Daggerdale by William Cormaeril and his hearth guard, and the Cormyrean - looking more like a noble than ever, bid the companions godspeed. 

In the days to come, Dagger Falls became again a place of peace. Thunderstorm made his peace with his old friend Cassimar, and stayed on in Dagger Falls to found a new Hall of the Morning there. Sparrow, too, had stayed as his goddess had bid him to do, and brought the worship of Mystra to the liberated Dale, and began the indoctrination of a select number of adepts into the mysteries that might prevent Dagger Falls from ever again falling under outside tyranny. Sparrow and Thunderstorm opened a temple to the two faiths in the former Brotherhood Tower, sanctified and purged of its former taint. The three evil faiths were not heard from or seen in Dagger Falls for many years to come, and quiet came upon the place, which began, in some small ways, to show some of the traits that had once earned it the welcoming name of Merrydale.

An oratory given by Maturin that very day also spawned an intense, but briefly lived following of Ilmater, but it was a sermon given by Gaebril that took root in the fertile soil of the citizens' hearts. In the wake of the dark paladin, a cult of Shar began to blossom after the Eastern Storm passed on. The Sharran's sermon, delivered during the great victory feast on the 29th in the Eastern Bailey, convinced many to turn their hearts to the Mistress of the Night. Many found succour in the philosophy of enjoying their new lives in the present, rather than dwelling on past evils, best forgotten.

To this day, the faiths of Lathander and Shar are the most influential in the Dale and in the town itself. The temple to Mystra is also a very busy place, and it is well known that this attraction brings many practitioners of magic, and their money, to the town every year.
