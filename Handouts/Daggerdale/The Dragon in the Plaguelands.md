== Dalesmen in Distress ==

\"Sometimes you have to decide, Brother Altair\" were the last words that Kesh, his mentor and adopted father, had ever said to him. It was also the first time he ever called him 'brother' rather than son.

The expedition started badly and ended worse. Kesh, his three pupils – one of which was Corran – and a small host of willing and well-paid soldiers headed out of Fort Sansum towards the Plaguelands. It wasn't the first time out for any of the recently indoctrinated Tormites, but the circumstances had never been so pressing. Intelligence had come down that an entire family of farmers from the other side of the mountains had been taken from their homes by some flying devil, and that they were being held prisoner by a band of Plague-spoiled kobolds.

\"Kobolds aren't such a nuisance,\" Kesh was explaining. \"It's the thing that can fly over mountains and carry off a whole family, and then have the cunning and cruelty to forgo eating them to draw us into a hopeless rescue effort that worries me. Still, our duty is clear. We are going to rescue that family!\"

The march into the plaguelands was bitter and tormented like all journeys there. The storms of blue fire and blistering sand never relented, and the Tormites on their mission of duty had to fight every step of the way against its fury. \"It doesn't want us here today\" one of Kesh's pupils muttered. \"If this is a trap, why try so hard to keep us out?\"

== Depths and Deviltry ==

After two days of fighting the storm, they came to the caves at the heart of the plaguelands; an area seldom seen, but which is the epicentre of the unnatural disruption of the land. In the heart of these caves, Kesh revealed, was the source of the plague. However, he continued grimly, they were extensive, reaching even into the Deep Wastes of the Underdark, and none had returned that had ventured there.

His sobering warning aside, Kesh had learned something of the caves and their denizens. The upper warrens crawled with all manner of plague-spoiled, the most common being the kobolds which worshipped the spell-spoiled devil that lived there, the cursed name of Anioch being whispered in the deep. None who have seen Anioch have ever lived to give it a description, Kesh claimed, but he was determined to change that with their mission. Anioch may have laid a trap for them, but Kesh thought that he and his companions had the fortitude and faith to defeat it. He was almost proved right.

Fighting their way through the maze of warrens, through cavern after cavern of the disfigured and insane kobolds, they finally came to the one for which they had come. An enormous cavern, the size of which made it impossible to see the ceiling of the place with any light source they had, housed an entire village of the deranged kobolds, divided in its centre by a great chasm, narrow but unimaginably deep. Suspended above this chasm was a large wooden cage, in which the family from the Dales the Tormites came there to rescue was being held captive.

== Demon in the Deep ==

The battle was soon started, and the soldiers and newly anointed paladins and their mentor fought their way to the centre of the village, and to the edge of the chasm after much hard battling. As they neared the small platform and ropes that led to the cage, however, things took a dire turn. From the blackness of the cavern’s unseen firmament swooped a blue dragon of respectable size. Its sudden strike drove some of the men into the chasm with not only ease, but what seemed a malevolent draconic joy.

It was Corran who was the nearest to the helpless family when this happened. He watched from across the small bridge as his mentor and other students closed into combat with the beast. As his comrades engaged the enormous murderous dragon, he stood alone against a mob of kobolds that had interposed themselves between him and the family. 

Torm smiled as the battle was taken in stride. Corran laid low kobold after kobold, while his comrades landed several telling blows on the thick hide of the dragon. At one point, the dragon shrieked in agony as Kesh straddled the great wyrm’s back, plunging his blade repeatedly into its spine, severing sinew and nerves. The wings of the thing went limp and lifeless with one deep blow, and the dragon reared and screeched again. Kesh was thrown, as the writhing monster stomped the life out of one of his pupils. The battle had suddenly taken a desperate turn and Corran turned from the dragon's minions to come assist his brothers in arms.

The dragon roared an order to the kobolds that stood in Corran's way; \"Cut the ropes! Drop them into the chasm!\" The kobolds immediately disengaged from Corran and began to hack at the heavy ropes that held the cage full of innocents over the chasm.

== Between the Dragon and Damnation ==

The dragon was staggering, bleeding heavily. It seemed nearly oblivious to the one remaining pupil who slashed at its haunches while it gathered its senses. Kesh lay on the ground, struggling to get up, seemingly dazed by a blow to his head from his fall. One of the young paladins shouted at Corran, \"Now's our chance, Cor! Come, help me slay this monster and end this!\"

\"Torm, guide me! What do I do?!\" His internal struggle was only a half-second in real time, but he spent an eternity inside his own head dissecting this problem; the dragon could be slain, but the kobolds would drop the family to its death.

Torm had no guidance for the paladin and he looked over to his mentor for an answer, but Kesh's full attention was on the dragon that was towering over his prone form. The sole standing novice continued to harass the dragon, and the kobolds continued to wildly hack at the ropes that suspended the cage.

\"Torm chooses to test my quality this day...\", growled Corran.

Brandishing his blade, Corran charged the kobolds that were focused on dropping the family into the chasm and cut them down even as the dragon wheeled about to turn its attention to Kesh.

== A Desperate Defense ==

Anioch's enormous maw snapped up the dazed and wounded Kesh. As the dagger-length teeth closed on his body, slicing through armour and flesh alike, the blossom of pain inside the old paladin brought him to his senses. He had no sword in his hand, but he put one gauntleted fist into the creature’s eye, and the creature rumbled terrible and deep. It clenched its jaw muscles as hard as it could, and the upper half of Kesh fell free from the monster to land with a wet thump on the cold stone floor. The old paladin struggled to rise, not realizing that he had been bitten in half, just above the waist.

Anioch spat out the armoured lower half of Corran's mentor, then turned around in an awkward limp, and brought its bloody countenance, one eye now forever shut, down to the face of the last young paladin on its side of the chasm. From across the pit, Corran charged to aid his brother, the family now safe. The creature let out a sudden hiss, and the world when blinding-white for a moment. When Corran's eyes cleared, all he could smell was the terrible scent of burnt flesh, and the strange ozone after-scent of lightning. His former comrade lay limp upon the ground, small arcs of electricity still dancing over his corpse. Corran's immediate instinct was to charge the badly injured dragon, now limping way into the protective shadows of the cavern. Anger drove him forward but a desperate plea for help held his charge. Another wave of kobolds was falling on the rescued family and with a low snarl, Corran turned and charged the kobolds that were howling in rage as they fell upon the helpless family.

== The Duty to Decide ==

When the battle was over, the kobolds laid around Corran like fallen leaves from a tree. Anioch was gone and he was amazed to find that Kesh was still alive. He was breathing in short, gurgling breaths, but the light was beginning to fade from his eyes. There was no help to be given and with a sick and heavy heart Corran did what he could to provide solace for his fallen mentor. Corran brought Kesh his shield and his broadsword and laid his own cloak across the old paladin's bloody midriff. Kesh coughed and spit upon the ground as he spoke thickly. \"The shield and blade are yours. They served me well, as they will now serve you.\"

\"I'm sorry, Sir. I'm sorry I couldn't help you. It was them...it...it was them or you.\"

Kesh gripped Corran's hands with his own strong, calloused hands and gave a small smile.

\"Sometimes you have to decide, Brother Altair\"

== Departure from the Desert ==

Two days later, Corran was leading a forced march out of the plaguelands; the storm had actually gotten worse, and it was nearly fatal for the small children. As he neared the edge of the storm and the corrupted landscape, he heard a sudden great thumping on the ground behind him, faint at first, but growing louder steadily and quickly. Turning to face the approaching threat, and instinctively putting the children and mother safely behind him, he saw through the whipping maelstrom the terrible blue face of Anioch. In an instant, the wyrm was upon them. Vainly he swung his blade at it, but it deftly dodged the blow and took another stride towards him, its body clearing the blinding dust, and rearing up on hind legs to loom over Corran and the people he was dutifully protecting. To the immense surprise of everyone present; it spoke,

“Corran Altair”, it said slowly and deliberately, like someone pronouncing a name from another world. “I have the smell of you now, and I shall not forget it. Leave these tormented lands, and lead no more soldiers back here. If I ever see armoured men in my domain again, I will scour these lands, seeking the stink of your father, the scent of your mother, the perfume of your daughter. This is my oath. My oath, human; mark it!\"  Then it drew in a deep breath, and as a malicious scowl overtook its one-eyed face, let out a great bellow; “NOW LEAVE!!!”

Corran arrived at Fort Sansum a day later, weary from the journey, but with the mother and three children still alive, if a bit wild-eyed. After ensuring that they were to be well cared for, he left the home he had known for the last seven years and headed south for Fort Last Sun.
