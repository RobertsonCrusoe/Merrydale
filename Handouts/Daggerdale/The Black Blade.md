Information collected by Mendel Krioy, eighth of The Monastery of Sundered Faiths in 1441 DR

The first time the name, Gaucon Suhl Ner'Huar was ever heard of or recorded was by Mincham Goringgrimm during the Battle of Sundered Faith where a demon demanded Gaebril Tremalkin and Gaucon Suhl Ner’Huar to be handed over to it, and the Sharrans that seemingly summoned the demon.  It is assumed that the blade that Tremalkin used while he had been the First of Shar is the Gaucon Suhl Ner’Huar that the demon speaks of.

Any further effort to reveal information about the name or the blade itself has failed.  If there are records of the blade or the name somewhere, a number of skilled Loremasters have been unable to find them.

It is pure speculation at this point but it seems that the blade possessed Tremalkin to some degree and although it was able to empower him at times while in battle, the influence of the blade had a decidedly unpleasant affect on Tremalkin physically.

There are close to a dozen tales regarding when and how Tremalkin obtained the blade.  Some as mundane as Tremalkin simply finding it on one of his adventures to as ridiculous as actually being Shar’s lover and her giving him the blade as sign of being her chosen consort.

However obtained, it is clear that the blade is extremely dangerous.  Not only to those that are at the pointed end, but also dangerous to the individual that wields it.

There is speculation over the power of the blade to possess it’s wielder.  It seems that Tremalkin, although grim was not affected as emotionally or physically when he first had the blade as he was later on.  It is possible that the blade might adversely affect a man of lesser will and constitution far more quickly.  It has also been theorized that events leading up to the Spellplague had caused Shar’s demeanor to change.  There were a few times where Tremalkin actually said, “Something has changed.  She’s changing.  She’s not what she once was.”  It is theorized that as the power of Selûne waned, the power of Shar grew and as Shar’s power grew, so did her darker and more malevolent tendencies.

It is possible that none of these are accurate and there could be another reason why the blade affected Tremalkin they way it did.}}
