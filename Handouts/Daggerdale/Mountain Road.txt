This road leads from Dagger Falls to Castle Radiance.  It is a good road, perfectly maintained and regularly patrolled.

Distances on the Mountain Road;

*Dagger Falls to Castle Radiance - 130 miles total
**Dagger Falls to Daggerford Farm - 30 miles
**Daggerford Farm to Rain Hill Mine - 50 miles
**Rain Hill Mine to Fort Luminous - 15 miles
**Fort Luminous to Castle Radiance - 35 miles

The trip between town and Daggerford Farm is mostly along the bank of the River Tesh, with the huge oaks of Cormanthor on the other side.  3 miles from the farm, the forest opens up into large cultivated fields.

Between Daggerford Farm and Rain Hill Mine, the trip is mostly through foot hills, with the vegetation a thick coniferous forest; large straight pines grow all over the hills, with patches of open maple bush.  The road here is still relatively straight and easy to follow, as the foothills are not terribly steep or high.  There is a well-used campsite at the halfway mark on this section of road that is cleared by the Sun Blades regularly, and often guarded by them.  It has been the site of ambushes in the past, however.

From Rain Hill Mine to Fort Luminous, the road begins its steep climb (or descent, if the traveler comes from the other direction).  The road is still in good shape, reinforced with stone banks and retaining walls in many places, and furnished with proper ditches and drainage pipes to keep water from the mountainside off the road.  It is very steep, however, and the going is much slower along this track.  This section of road is called 'The Climb' by many who frequent the roads, including the Sun Blades who patrol it.

Between Fort Luminous and the Castle, the Mountain Road snakes its way through pine-covered valleys, keeping to the low land.  It is a much easier bit of road to travel than The Climb, and the traveler can enjoy the heights of the mountains and the many vistas that open up around every turn.  This length of road is known for the many bridge projects that Lord White gate has begun in recent years.  There are five places where the road crosses deep ravines, and there are sturdy wooden bridges at all of them, as well as guard towers (only infrequently garrisoned).  At most of these sites, great stone bridges are under construction by the most talented stone masons that can be found within 1000 miles.
