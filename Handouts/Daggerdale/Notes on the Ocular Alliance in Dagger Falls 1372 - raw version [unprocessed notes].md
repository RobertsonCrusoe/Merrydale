Notes on the Ocular Alliance in Dagger Falls 1372
Report by Mnestheus Melshimber with files from Jeryth Phaulkon
Submitted 10 Mirtul, 1373 to Twilight Hall in Berdusk
Following the resurrection of Bane in the autumn of 1371, the constellation of powers and allegiances of many churches, governments and organisations underwent a not unexpected period of adjustment. Not least did this affect the Black Network of Zhentil Keep. By 1371, the Zhentarim had become enmeshed in the machinations of a powerful group of beholders, here referred to as the Ocular Alliance. This marriage of convenience has had far-reaching effects on both the Black Network and the inter-regional trade in Western Faerun. While the aims of the Ocular Alliance are as yet unknown, they have been extensively speculated upon elsewhere by this author, and it is not the purpose of this report to do so further. Rather, the following pages will detail the role played by the various organisations involved in the conspiracy, and the schism of 1372 that led to the current enmity between the Church of Cyric and the Ocular Alliance. These notes have been compiled with the assistance of Lady Jeryth Phaulkon, who knew many of the eyewitnesses personally, and without whose knowledge and dedication to our cause we would have remained ignorant of the following facts.
-----------
In the days after Greengrass of last year, a small gathering of our order in the western city of Waterdeep initiated three missions that would prove to lead to the unmasking of the Ocular Alliance and severely hinder its aims. The existence of the Alliance, long suspected by many, had until then been unsubstantiated, owing to the recent difficulties experienced in the North and East (see the accompanying Notes Concerning the Unstrung Harp, also by this author). The Church of Cyric was to play a central role in the expedition undertaken by those who became known as the Company of the Eastern Storm

The Church of Cyric acted as a stabilizing agent for the Ocular Alliance's domination of Dagger Falls., playing a principally defensive role after the consolidation of power in the spring and summer of 1372.

Their primary objective was the paralysis of any organized opposition by the other Daleland communities to the activities of the Alliance. This was played out in Shadowdale through the agency of High Dawnlord Cassamar, a priest of Lathander that unwittingly played into the hands of the Ocular Alliance by advocating non-interference in the affairs of Shadowdale's neighbours to the north.

When the concentration of interests opposing the Alliance reached a critical mass with the arrival of the knights of the Holy Triad, a new approach became necessary. Lord Maturin Horgath, at the time a paladin out of favour with his patron deity, was the ideal candidate for the Dark Sun to use to advance His interests. Through the guise of Torm the True, Cyric arranged an ambush for the forces of good at Anathar's Dell, where they were pitted against the unholy orders of Bane and Iyachtu Xvim.

Cyric's cunning in the arrangement of this ambush, however, would prove to be His undoing. The decision not to support the Knights of the Black Gauntlet and the Brothers of the Black Fist in their clash against the Holy Triad was taken by the other members of the Alliance as an outright betrayal, and Cyric's role was quickly written out of whatever plan was being pursued by the Masters of the Alliance

Ultimately, however, such ruminations are of exclusively historical interest in light of the events that followed the second battle of Anathar's Dell. On the 7th of Eleint, 1372, the forces of good and evil clashed in Cyric's well-orchestrated ambush. The knights of the Triad were sorely diminished before the Brothers of the Black Fist were driven away and the Knights of the Black Gauntlet were destroyed utterly. In retrospect, it seems, this victory was only possible given the absence of the Order of the Ebon Spur.

Martial wisdom advocates keeping one's enemies closer than one's friends. Cyric's seeming position of trust in the Alliance revealed itself to be just such a short leash, keeping the devious deity's agents under the watchful eyes of the Oculars. A short-lived outbreak of violence in Dagger Falls the day after the tablets were excavated revealed that the theological arms of the Alliance were becoming unstable. A squabble between Banites and Cyricists required the intervention of the Fist soldiers at the behest of Yarkul, then in control of the settlement.

Reports from an agent who played a key role in the liberation of Dagger Falls and shattering the Alliance's stranglehold on trade across Anauroch indicate that it was at about this time that Cyric began to move against the Alliance, through his influence over Lord Horgath. It is interesting to note that Yarkul, the Zhentarim mage who played such a key role in the Alliance, was cut from its ranks at almost the same time as the Cyricists. The fact is made more interesting still when one considers that Yarkul was an ardent follower of the Church of Xvim, and consort to a demon of Xvim, a succubus named Amaretta. Yarkul had been instrumental at securing a role for 'The New Darkness' in the ranks of the Alliance in 1371 when he made contact with them in Waterdeep, and had brought them to the east after completing whatever role he had played there.

Yarkul's subsequent efforts at peacemaking only resulted in his (long overdue) assassination, and the establishment of a new government under the hereditary rule claimed by Lord William Cormaeril of Cormyr. The relative strengths of the Churches of Xvim and Cyric can be seen in the rapid elimination of the Church of Bane after Cormaeril took hold of the reins of power. There is, however, a sizable caveat to this statement. Cormaeril, as was later revealed, was acting as an unwitting pawn for yet another Zhentarim mage, one Venosar, allegedly sent by his superiors in the Zhentarim to replace Yarkul (prior to, it must be noted, Yarkul's death). Cormaeril's short-lived policy of establishing trade links with Yulash, another Zhentarim-controlled town, indicates that Venosar was likely an agent of Thagdul, the mage who controlled that polity for most of 1372. Little is known of this man, save for his involvement in criminal activities during the previous year in Waterdeep, and that he was allegedly responsible for the installation of Yarkul in Dagger Falls. (It should be noted that it is the working hypothesis of this author that Thagdul is, or was, the spokesperson for Zhentarim interests within the Ocular Alliance.

Hatemaster Dragkul and Morgul the Vigilant Talon waited each for the opportunity to seize control and oust the other. It is the hope of this author that the intelligence and analysis herein will prove in some way useful towards the elimination of the threat posed by the Ocular Alliance. While the fact remains that our true enemy is, as has always been the case, the Black Network, the Ocular Alliance has proven to be a powerful tool for their efforts against us.

One other note bears mentioning. While our sources indicate that the Eastern Storm have recognized Cyric's treachery and that Lord Horgath has once more achieved a state of incorruptiblity, Cyric's role in the affair of the Ocular Alliance may yet be unfinished. The connection between the mage Thagdul and the Church of Cyric, while poorly documented, may be stronger than is yet known. With Venosar's death and the elimination of his soldiers and Church in Dagger Falls, Cyric may now look to

Thagdul to advance His interests. It is the recommendation of this report that resources be allocated to the investigation of this mysterious person, and while such an investigation may be impractical given the recent strain on personnel, it is nevertheless of utmost importance. Thagdul's role in this affair must be deciphered if our knowledge of Cyric's modus operandi is to be of any constructive use in the fight against the Alliance.

Finally, it must be admitted that an alternative hypothesis to the events of Eleint 1372 exists - that the Xvimites and the Cyricists, both key players in the Ocular Alliance, were betrayed not by their unseen masters, but in fact by an as yet unknown force external to the Alliance itself. However, the evidence on the matter indicates otherwise. Whatever, or whoever, such an external force could be is entirely unknown.
