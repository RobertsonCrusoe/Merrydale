After the merger of Mule Company with the White Cloaks, Alystair Dretch decided he would take on some special projects for the organization, investigating loose ends in the dale.  He was urged by Lumina Whitegate to act upon the dreams he had been having.  In these pursuits, Alystair was often accompanied by Tuugan Swift whose skills as a ranger are a much-needed asset for many of these investigations.  Tuugan's adherence to the faith of Chauntea has also provided both valuable information in the investigations of those in the dale who still live and worship the traditional goddess, as well as providing some perspective outside the rigid faith of Amaunator.

==Trouble at Dwimmerholme Farm - Harvestide 1479 DR==

Alystair's first investigation was based on his dream that troubled him most nights after his delivery from the Shadowfell.  The dream led him to Dagger Falls, where he began to sniff around for leads.  It wasn't long before he found out about a caravanner from the Moonsea region who had been imprisoned.

===A Gift from Shar===

The caravanner's name was Tzunamm, and he was the head of a new coster called the Moonsea Collective.  This caravan had returned a few days previous from a trip to Dwimmerholme Farm.  They offered a very reasonable letter of credit to the farm, at 5% interest.  There were two problems with this, however.  The first is that usury is illegal in Daggerdale, and the second is that suspect business practices will land the merchant in The Tower until a thorough investigation is done.

Alystair went to The Tower to talk to the imprisoned Tzunamm, and was given the following story;

:It's like I told that damned magistrate; when we came to Crossed Blades a couple weeks ago, my security boss, Yarlick Mean, told me he had a plum deal for us; if we were to go to Dwimmerholme and give them what they need, they would pay 5%, AND we'd get paid in Cormyrean trade ingots for 50% of the value once we got back to town.  He showed me two of the ingots, and so I was fooled.

:I did my part; I went up there to Dwimmerholme (that place is as miserable a farm as I've seen in this dale; they've fallen on hard times, they have) and I gave them everything they need to carry on for another year; tools, seeds, cloth, and so on.  I got the letter of credit, and returned.  When I got back, some snoopy clerk thought my business didn't smell right, and he was right, I guess.  He had Invictus come down and talk to me; I was put in jail awaiting my trial, and my letter was confiscated.  I never saw another Cormyrean trade ingot, either.  This is all Mean's fault, I tell you!

Alystair spoke to Invictus about this.  The Magistrate confirmed that this was exactly the story he had been given by the trader, and that he was inclined to believe him.  However, Invictus said that when he spoke to Mean, he was convinced that the guard had nothing to do with this, and that the whole scheme was the creation of the caravanner.

===Yarlick's Story===

Alystair found Mean in the taproom of Quick Axes, surrounded by a bunch of his caravan guard.  When confronted about the trade story and threatened to be brought to the Tower for questioning, weapons were drawn, and before long Alystair and Tuugan had Mean surrendered at swordpoint, and a bunch of guardsmen lying on the floor battered and bruised.  Mean was brought to The Tower for questioning; Alystair interrogated the man while Invictus watched.  Mean claimed the following;

:I was approached by a fine-looking woman named Lotus in the taproom at Crossed Blades Farm.  I had met her a few times in the past; I've been coming here for 10 years, you know?  She offered me these two trade bars to convince Tzunamm to do what you heard him say he done.  We all did our part.  But when we got sniffed out by the gate-clerks, they hauled off the foolish merchant, and he never got paid.

:Later, in the very taproom you boxed my ears in, Lotus came to me and told me she was going to take care of things.  She gave me a story to give to anyone who asked, and sure enough the zealots left me alone.  I wasn't ever interrogated by the magistrate, though...

Invictus claimed that he believed both men now, but that there was a troubling development: the Yarlick Mean in front of him was not the same Yarlick Mean he had spoken to a couple of days ago.  It was uncertain at that point if he had been or was possessed, or if there was a look-alike or doppelganger of some sort in the town.

A forceful investigation at Quick Axes turned up a lead; one of the waitresses recognized the description of Lotus that was given by Mean, and further recalled that she had rented a room on the third floor for about 10 days the month previous, and that she had 2 other visitors with her, both women.

After these revelations, Magistrate Invictus suggested that it might be a good idea to head up to Dwimmerholme to see what is going on up there.

==The Woes that Came to Dwimmerholme==

Alystair came to Dwimmerholme a day later, to find a curious site; the fields were half burnt, and there was evidence that the place was surrounded by packs of wolves, but when they arrived in the compound, they found that the Harvestide Festival was in full swing, much to the delight of Tuugan who was quick to find a jug of ale for one hand and a young lady for the other.

The duo investigated the place, and turned up the following facts;

===Troubles===
*A year ago, an orc attack left several entire families killed, including the fiancée of Roger Dunland, the 30 year-old son of the mayor
*The orcs were hunted down and killed by the Sun Blades, and one survivor was interrogated, and indicated that the small band of orcs was told that the farmers would be easy pickings and unable to retaliate; they had been fooled by some unknown orc who remains nameless and unfound
*Several fields have been mysteriously burned over the course of the entire summer
*The fields and the forest nearby are plagued by dozens of large wolves which have been attacking anything that comes close; no one will leave the compound in anything but the largest armed groups

===Facts===
*Roger Dunland went to town three months previous, and has been telling his closest friends and family that he has found a way out of the trouble the farm is in
*Roger Dunland promised his friends and family that relief would come, and was instrumental in convincing the farm elders to take the deal that Tzunamm offered
*Magistrate Hoetmer, the aging and Chauntea-tolerant head of the Church of Amaunator in the farm has been having dreams about the Dark Garden as well

Everything seemed perfectly set up for a Cult of Shar; a farm in dire straits with a charismatic and influential prodigal son to help bring the Oblivion to the community.  Tuugan and Alystair just had to figure out why and how this would happen.

The following day after their arrival was Harvestide, and they took part in the ultra-sacred rite of The Harvest with the Chaunteans from the farm.  These people, numbering more than half of the population, took to the fields in three groups; the group with the Dunlands was followed by Alystair and Tuugan, and though the group was constantly ringed about by wolves, the wolves always recoiled at the approach of the harvesters.  It turns out that the other two groups were involved in pitched battles with the wolves all day, leaving many seriously wounded.

The inhabitants of the farm were in a bit of turmoil after the morning's events; many flocked around the son of the Mayor to hear about their wolf-chasing harvest, while others retired in defeat, fearing the worst.  Tuugan and Alystair went and spoke frankly with the Magistrate, both revealing what they knew about the situation.  The following facts came to light:

*It was rumoured that the Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin was brought to Daggerdale by a mysterious stranger a few months ago.
*Legend on the Dwimmerholme farm has it that Gaebril Tremalkin sired a child with a freed slave woman over 100 years ago
*That woman took the name 'Liberty' and the bastard son had a prolific line
*Roger Dunland's mother was a Liberty
*Roger came back from his trip to town a few months ago with much livestock, which included a white goat and a white cow
*There is no longer a white goat to be found at the farm
