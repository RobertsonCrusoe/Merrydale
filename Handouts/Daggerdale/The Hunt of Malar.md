==Pawn of the Beastlord==
<i>
Sitting upon his throne the Beastlord cackled aloud.

Once again, his little game was proving to be one of his most amusing endeavors.

And once again, the little half-elven pawn had taken to his placement of pieces upon the board. The rumor of a group of wood elves in Cormanthor hunting humans at HIS bidding would be all was required to send her to them straight as an arrow.

How long had he been using her in his little game? How long had she been cursing his name, hunting his followers?

Yes, the destruction of his lesser minions was to be rued, but the hatred that this woman felt… her undying belief that he, Malar, was alone in the Pantheon. That he alone held sway over the lives of mortals.. how her hatred and belief gave him strength.

He knew her mind. Ever since the destruction of her little village on the border of Amtar Forest, some 18 years ago, he knew her. She believed she was drawing him closer to her by destroying his followers. She believed he was being weakened, until one day he would be forced to leave his otherworldly sanctuary. She believed she was bringing him down.

HA! He cackled again. She was indeed getting closer to him, but she was rising up. Her unyielding belief, her Hunts, the bloodshed.. it made him STRONGER. The more she sought to harm him, the stronger her faith in him grew, all the more she would intone his name, he could hear her prayers.

“Malar, I have dealt you a blow, my hunt was a success, the blood of your followers is upon my hands, I am coming for you Beastlord!”

And once again the Beastlord cackled...

==The Dragon's Gate Inn==

Carpathia Twist sat with the two men she had just met, as usual, disinterested in the conversation. Her thoughts were inward. She felt the need to mock herself and all she had done. It had been 18 years, she was a young woman no more. And she yet had so much to do. Nothing at all remained of the young spry half-elven maiden from Amtar Forest.

Not since the day the Dwarves of the East Rift denied her people sanctuary.

==From Prey into Hunter==
===Quarry===
thumb|350px|The Hunt
The Hunts of the Beastlord had been coming for years, and yet, each time the entire village would have warning and flee to the safety of the Rift, spending weeks or sometimes months under the protection of the Dwarves. Until that one day.

She turned 24 the year they were turned away. The dwarves claiming that sanctuary could no longer be offered.  She supposed it could be understood, times were hard for everyone, and in hard times, kin comes before friendship. She remembered living on the move for 2 months that year, until the Hunt of Malars minions finally subsided. After that fortifications were made for the first time around her home. And these would prove to be her peoples undoing.

The Hunt came more frequently, and from different places. The servants of Malar were everywhere. Up from the Underchasm, down from the Gnollwatch Mountains, even from as Far as Wyrmbones and the Dead Vale. The attacks never ceased. Her village of 250 brave half-elves was utterly destroyed. A mere handful survived in the end by fleeing their home.

===Flight from Hearthstone===

And so, Carpathia Hearthstone found herself alone. With only what belongings she could load into a cart that would not overburden the small fetlocked mare she named “Freedom”. For six weeks she traveled at what speed she could, afraid, cold and alone but for the mare. It was the foothills of the Smoking Mountains after a grueling crossing of the Black Ash Plain where her life forever changed. Seated in the shelter of an outcropping of rock Carpathia heard a soft coughing sound. Weary, but too tired to think better of it, she arose to investigate. Following the sound she was led to a natural rock funnel leading down from the mountains. The coughing came form 100 feet above. She ascended the funnel, grateful for its gently curving slope and smooth bottom. The source of the cough took her breath away.

===Twist of Fate===

Upon a poorly kept nest of straw and twigs lay an ancient Gryphon. It was thin, it’s hair matted and it’s feathers had mostly fallen out. Carpathia could still see the majesty and power the creature must have once possessed. Truly, to her, this ancient marvel was a reflection of how she herself felt. She took a step toward it.

The Gryphon looked up at her, barely able to raise it’s head, and nodded. For a mere instant the creature met her gaze, and in that instant the memories of lifetimes passed between them.  With no fear Carpathia strode forward and knelt beside the dying Gryphon. She held its head in her lap and stroked its long sinewy neck.
To her amazement, the creature spoke.

“You must hunt Carpathia, you must take vengeance for your village.”

Carpathia’s shock quickly waned, and with a tear in her eyes, she replied.

“I know, but I am afraid. In my heart I wish to kill the Beastlord himself for all he has done. But my fear is too great, and I am alone”.

With the last of it’s strength the Gryphon turned its head once more, to look her in the eye and gaze upon her soul.

“I am a creature of life, and magic. When this body dies, my spirit lives on. I shall accompany you on your quest, that you need not be afraid. Find others to aid you in your quest, and I will do what I can to assist them as well.”

With these words he pulled the last of his healthy feathers from his wing and gave it to her. “I am named Torestriwansolinath, in your tongue - Twist. Take my name, and this feather, carry them, and my spirit shall accompany you”. With that the last light slowly fled from its eyes.

Carpathia to this day doesn’t know how long she sat upon that mountain side.

But when she decended to her cart, and the mare Freedom, she was Carpathia Hearthstone no more. She was Carpathia Twist, and there was a light in her eyes.

She had hunting to do. She had a God to kill.
