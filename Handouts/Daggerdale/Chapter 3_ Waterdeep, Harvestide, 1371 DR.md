In the days before the Waterdeep Harvestide celebrations of 1371, DR, those brave souls who had undertaken a pledge to the murdered Harper, [[Zaltim]], known as the Road Mage, joined forces with the [[Company of the Red Herring]]. The mystery that brought these two bands of heroes together centred on the mysterious Moonsea man, [[Thagdul]], and the machinations of the Zhentish trading companies of Ersgul and Drurginz. In the days and weeks that led up to Highharvestide, these same machinations erupted in fire and blood throughout Waterdeep's wards, and it became clear that our heroes were in the swirling and unstable midst of a conspiracy that spread not only across the Realms, but across the very fabric of the planes themselves.

== 25 Eleint - Ersgul Makes Its Move ==

===Yarkul's Olive Branch ===

This portion of the tale actually begins on in the last days of the month of Eleint, as the City prepared itself for the grand weeklong celebrations that mark the culmination of the trading season for the thousands of souls that live in the market center of Waterdeep. On 25 Eleint, as [[Garth]] and his companions were sitting down to break their fast in the building that had come to be the Red Herring's headquarters, a crisp knock was heard at the door. As had happened so many times before, the company members tensed and readied themselves, expecting another unwelcome visit from their enigmatic patron, [[Thagdul]] of [[Company Drurginz]]. Surprisingly, the visitor turned out, in fact, to be the half-orc [[Gul'dan]], the leader of the {{catlink|Reprobates}}. The villain had come calling dressed in his most garish finery, acting as a representative of Thagdul's nemesis - [[Yarkul]] of [[Company Ersgul]]. At the sight of the scoundrel, [[Raven]] very nearly drew his blade on the spot, but paused as Garth spoke up - bidding the messenger remain outside, and speak his piece at once. In a display of politic most incongruous with what the companions knew to be his churlish nature, Gul'dan invited the Company to lunch with Yarkul at [[Gounar's Tavern]] that very afternoon, where they were to hear a proposition that Gul'dan assured them would be 'to their conspicuous advantage'. Narrowing his eyes at the dubious, even comical display, Garth accepted on behalf of the company, then nodded to [[Raven]], who slammed the door in the creature's brutish face.

A few hours later, the Company of the Red Herring - [[Mourn Raventree]], [[Jafar]], [[Tomas Quoyle]] and [[Garth Shanks]] - set out to meet with the Zhent mage at the appointed locale, where they found Yarkul expecting them, and apparently, alone. Yarkul bade them to order their fill, and once the food had arrived, he put forth his offer: in exchange for the Company of the Red Herring switching  their allegiance from Drurginz to Ersgul, Yarkul would waive any debts or liabilities they may have incurred with his organization. To a man, his audience refused him offer bluntly. Livid, Yarkul warned that they had chosen foolishly, and intoned portentously that “Harvestide will be wartide!" At this, Tomas, Raven and Jafar pushed their plates aside and leave the Tavern, while the ever-pragmatic Mr. Shanks continued eating his meal, eyeing Yarkul's petulant face across the table and shrugging at his companions' impetuosity. Fuming, Yarkul threw his hands into the air and withdrew to the streets whence he had come. Garth finished his meal alone.

=== City on the Razor's Edge ===
[[image:anteos.png|thumb|Crest of the Waterdhavian Noble House of Anteos]]
It was not until later that afternoon that Thagdul finally showed himself, meeting with the Company of the Red Herring and the Companions from Neverwinter, who he had asked Jafar to bring together for a gathering. [[Thagdul]] was visibly heartened at the decision by the Red Herrings to turn down Yarkul's offer, and even indicated that he had an inkling as to what the Zhent's motivations may have been in making it. Yarkul was worried, he explained, about the allegiances of the Waterdhavian [[Anteos]] Family, one of the noble houses of the City. Yarkul was well aware that the patriarch of the family, [[Dulbrawan]], carries an open hatred for him on account of his Company's dealings with [[Company Dalael]]. Not only had [[Quevin Dalael]], who oversaw his family's mercantile dealings, fathered a bastard on Dulbrawan's consort, [[Ranaya]], but the ambitious family heir, Dulbrawan's younger brother [[Korass]] had pledged his services to both Ersgul and the Dalael, splitting the family's interests in twain. [[House Anteos]] is old and powerful, and as Dulbrawan happened to be a friend and ally of Thagdul, this battleground was quite important to both sides in the Ersgul-Drurginz conflict. Should Yarkul have succeeded in his bid to bring the Company of the Red Herrings into the Ersgul fold, Drurginz and its allies would have found themselves in a dangerously weakened position.

After this explanation, Thagdul motioned for Jafar to bring forward his new acquaintances - the party of adventurers that had come to Neverwinter in pursuit of the Zhentarim plot that had resulted in the deaths of the Northern [[Harpers]], notably [[Zaltim]], [[Laurel Dawn]] and [[Ansal Bloodshoulder]]. To these heroes - the dwarves [[Alt Kor]] and [[Wilk Shtub]], the priests [[Rufus]] of Selune and [[Beljuril]] of Azuth, and [[Hob]] the halfling - Thagdul explained that he is in truth, a [[Harper]] agent. He has gone deep under cover, in the guise of a known Zhentarim agent named Thagdul, whom he had killed years ago in Zhentil Keep. Jafar seems convinced, but many other of the Company members, and even the companions - who had intercepted a [[A note to 'Belduran'|note]] saying as much - continued to harbour their own private doubts about the truth of Thagdul's revelation.

The delicate balance of power between Drurginz and Ersgul notwithstanding, it was at this fateful joining of the parties that Thagdul proposed that the Red Herrings be 'snipped from his apron strings' and set to their own devices. To Beljuril and Rufus and their companions, the mysterious man suggests that while he has much to tell them, they should first learn all they can from the Company of the Red Herring.

==="Five Days to Live"===

For the time being, at least, it seemed that the Red Herrings were finally free of Thagdul's influence. Nevertheless, there remained from their exploits that summer many loose ends that remained unsolved. Chief among these, to the cupiditous turn of mind that had begun to characterize the Company as a whole, was the matter of the missing shavings of the [[Heart of the Earth]]. Garth's investigations [[months earlier]] had pointed to [[Haskar Hunabar]] as the most likely suspect, and thus it was that the Company of the Red Herring decided to address itself to learning all they could about the inhabitants of the [[Hunabar]] villa. 

Garth, Ellyth and Jafar set about these investigations, but Raven and Tomas had other fish - or, to be precise, filthy rascals - to fry. While their comrades made inquiries about the Hunabar family, these two men took it upon themselves to pay a visit to the safehouse of the Company of [[Reprobates]] to return Gul'dan's courtesy call of that morning. At the safehouse, the door was answered by the imposing figure of [[Sergeant Krag]], the Reprobatse' half-ogre enforcer, who grunted that he was the only one there. Tomas and Raven informed him in no uncertain terms that it was time for Gul'dan's crew to dissolve their relationship with Ersgul. Should the Red Herrings find that the Reprobates continue to work for Yarkul's organization against Drurginz, there would be hell to pay. Krag glowered at the two, but agreed to deliver the message. As Raven and Tomas turned to leave, however, Rubikawn emerges from the shadows opposite the doorway and points a spidery finger at Tomas, leveling a portentous threat: "You have five days left to live."

===Enter the Dragon===

After this encounter, Tomas and Raven decided to retire for a drink at the Dripping Dagger, where they then parted ways - Tomas returning to his home in Dock Ward, Raven to his family's villa in Sea Ward. Unfortunately, in his misplaced eagerness to match the young fisherman's prodigious intake, Raven found himself slightly the worse for drink, and it was in this inebriated state that the young ranger experienced his finest (and bleakest) hour. Pausing to relieve himself in a darkened alley, came face to face with a young half-elf woman flanked by a half-dozen drow. This, it would later be learned, was none other than [[Dragon]], Yarkul's half-elf daughter and a powerful lieutenant in the Ersgul hierarchy. True to form, however, Raven wasted no time with pleasantries. No sooner had Dragon begun to speak than Raven brought his fist up into the willowy villain's face, not only knocking her out cold, but marring her beauty forever with a broken nose that she bears to this day. Alas, Raven's lucky drunken punch was a one-time affair, and he was soon bested by the half-elf's drow minions, who riddled him with sleeping darts almost before he could catch his balance. As he lost consciousness, Raven later recalled that he heard the name [[Wavesilver]] briefly whispered, then darkness.

== 26 Eleint - Hunting the House of Hunabar ==

=== Lockdown at the Villa ===

Raven's ill luck was still unknown to the heroes when they met the next day to pool their findings on the Hunabars. Garth had learned that in the months since he learned of Hunabar's alleged dealings with the fence [[Alek Lenter]], the family had been receiving death threats of some sort, and that the patriarch had been leading a life of veritable house arrest, under the watchful eye of a security detail of sellswords hired through House [[Silmerhelve]]. This security, Jafar added, also included the services of one [[Kalindos]], said to be a powerful invoker in the circles at the [[Watchful Order]]. 

Ellyth, too, had learned of the Silmerhelve hire, and had trailed a captain of the mercenaries, a junior scion named [[Tristan Silmerhelve]], to the [[Dripping Dagger]] after his inspections the previous day. To learn more of Silmerhelve's qualities, she had been able to quickly enthral a local patron by the name of [[Edgar Grimbold]] (who had played a not inconsequential role in this saga in years past, and was to do again, as the canny reader will note) and convinced the poor sap to begin a bar brawl - allowing her to witness Silmerhelve's talents firsthand. Grimbold had been quickly and unceremoniously knocked out by Tristan, and Ellyth confirmed that the Hunabars had hired some well-trained men indeed.

=== A Moonlight Heist ===
[[image:hunabars.jpg|thumb|400px|A robbery by moonlight goes horribly, horribly wrong.]]

That night was the night of the full moon, and the heroes felt that it would be an ideal time to undertake a raid the Hunabar villa and retake the Heart of the Earth. They spent the better part of the day planning the assault in careful detail, and that evening, along with Rufus, Beljuril, Wilk and Hob and two others - the aforementioned Edgar - who asked to be called by his nom-d'aventure, [[Stuart Bold]], and a companion whom he introduced as [[Rychel Voordan]], a skilled second-story man -- the Red Herrings easily penetrated the villa's defenses and gained entry to the main building. Despite the heavy guard, the villa itself seemed eerily empty, though the faint sound of what sounded like chanting could be heard from several stories above. As the companions began to spread out to investigate the building, however, the silence was broken by the sudden arrival of two women who rushed into the main hall dressed in strange, ritualistic robes. The women stopped short at the sight of the intruders, and after a brief stunned silence in which the two parties eyed one another, unsure of what to do, the two began to scream warnings to the upper stories. As the two screamed however, their voices - and bodies - underwent a startling metamorphosis before the very eyes of the companions. Where two women had been standing, the heroes were suddenly faced with two snarling lupine lycanthropes - werewolves. The creatures sprang the attack, and were soon joined by several other ravening werewolves that descended from the upper stories.

During the confusion, Jafar - protected by an ''invisibility'' enchantment - had managed to make his way upstairs, where he began methodically exploring the rooms in search of [[Beholder Staff|the prize that he had been told to expect]]. As he came to what he had thought to be an empty bedroom, he was surprised to discover a figure rifling through a wardrobe. It was Rychel, who had entered the villa via a bedroom window while the rest of the party came in from below. When he saw what Rychel had pulled from the wardrobe, Jafar made his presence known to the stalker. In Rychel's hands was a [[Beholder Staff|staff capped with a small beholder]], much like those carried by Yarkul and Thagdul. Rychel had also discovered a box that seemed to be the same one recovered that summer from the {{catlink|Southern Star}}. Their objective accomplished, Rychel bade Jafar make good his escape with the take, while he turned to observe the events that were unfolding below.

=== The Wolfman's Wrath ===

In the main hall, the battle had escalated to a fever pitch. Two of the smaller werewolves had fallen to Wilk's axe, but the rest were holding their own against the companions, who were fighting as defensively as possible, for fear of infection by the plague that coursed through their enemies' veins. The standoff was broken, however, when the largest, and most grizzled, of the lycanthropes leaped to a landing above the main hall and reverted to human form, bellowing for the chaos to cease. This was [[Haskar Hunabar]], the patriarch of the family - as grizzled a human as he had been as a wolf-thing, and dressed in robes similar to (though slightly more ornate than) those worn by the two women that had discovered the intruders. As the combatants warily lowered their weapons and the wolves backed down, pulling the fallen bodies - which had reverted to the form of human children - away from the intruders, Haskar lowered a finger at the intruders and, visibly incensed, began to speak.

"What do you mean by invading MY home and attacking MY family during our religious observances? You have murdered my children!"

To raid the Hunabar villa. With a good plan, well executed, the adventurers gain entry to the villa, and find the Hunabars involved in some strange ceremony (later revealed as a ceremony to the new god [[Muir’Quaggoth]]). The Hunabars are revealed to be werewolves, and a bloody battle ensues. When [[Wilk]] kills one of the Hunabar werewolves, the patriarch [[Haskar]] sues for peace. The adventurers withdraw. Meanwhile, [[Jafar]] and [[Rychel]] have been sneaking around on the upper levels of the villa, where they find not only the box that contained the Heart of the Earth, but also a [[beholder staff]]! This staff is 6’ and has 6 stalks upon it.

==27 Eleint - The Mysterious Istolil Dalael ==

=== Following the Trail ===

The box that had contained the Heart of the Earth shavings is found to be empty of them at this point. Instead, a locket is found inside, with this inscription upon it;

{{script}}
Dear [[Joldara]],

This symbol of my eternal love,

{{sig|[[Istolil Dalael]]}}

</div>


At the time, it was unknown how the box got to be here, or where the Heart of the Earth shavings had gotten too. However, the box had been lined with an exotic textile, and this would prove to be a lead that would be followed up later.

The next day, Rufus went to the Hunabar villa to speak with Haskar. Haskar indicates that he would like the staff back, and Rufus gives him a note that was penned by Rychel. The note, unread by Rufus, made Haskar furious, and he attacked Rufus. Rufus managed to escape by changing into his rodent form and abandoning the armour that he had gotten from the [[Road Mage]].

=== The Vortex ===

Later in the day, a few of the adventurers went about the city seeking out the exotic material that the box had been lined with. Magical divinations provided by [[Beljuril]] indicated that there was a bolt of the material in an area of the Sewers. Rychel went down to investigate, and there he finds a safe house guarded by a drow claiming to be Istolil Dalael. However, while he didn’t seem to know much about the box, he did offer to help Rychel by telling him where Raven was being kept.

The adventurers then went to the warehouse that Istolil had indicated, where they fell into a trap. The warehouse was full of panes of glass that were covered in drow poison. A hidden mage shattered all the glass magically, and summoned a whirlwind which whipped up the shards of poisoned glass, engulfing the adventurers. Tomas and Rufus were cut down pretty quick, especially once some drow engage them, but Rychel manages to save the two.

After this raid, [[Magistrate Thuman]] shows up, with Watch Wizard [[Sabrel Lightstar]] and Captain of the Watch [[Griswold Horn]]. The trio arrest adventurers, and Rufus is taken to a separate cell where he is interrogated and tortured by Sabrel. The wizard demands to know Rufus' ‘true name’. Fortunately, Rychel has [[Harkas Kormallis]] show up to bail them out, and is surprised when [[Magistrate Vader]] simultaneously shows up to do the same. Kormallis is unimpressed by the company that the CRH and the Neverwinter crew are keeping (Vader, like Thuman, is notoriously corrupt), and expresses this to Rychel. Kormallis seems to know something about Vader that he does not like. Vader does not seem to favour Kormallis, either.

==28 Eleint - Showdown at the Dripping Dagger==

=== Five Kinds of Carnage ===

The next day, a [[Yarkul's Note|note from Yarkul]] is found stuck to the door of the CRH HQ, demanding an answer from the heroes.

This is how one of the messiest battles in Waterdeep started. Of course, the adventurers had no intention of showing up at the [[Dripping Dagger]] to switch sides - Thagdul had been good to them so far, even if they did not quite trust him - but they did intend to show up.

As it turns out, Yarkul did not trust the adventurers, and he had the place filled with his men. Not only where there numerous wererats and Zhents and the place, but [[Quevin]] was there with two other drow and a pair of Ghaunadan. On top of that, Yarkul himself was upstairs.

When the inevitable fight broke out, things got really messy. The dwarven heroes of Neverwinter, [[Wilk Shtub]] and [[Alt Kor]] were slain. Rychel went toe-to-toe with Quevin for quite some time, before Quevin decided to make his escape. It was at that time that Rychel turned his attention upon the recently discovered invisible Yarkul. Yarkul stood not a chance against Rychel, and was quickly dispatched. Rychel risked nothing; he put Yarkul over his shoulder, grabbed the [[Beholder Staves|staff]] and left before anyone could say a word.

In the end, the Dripping Dagger was destroyed. A combination of fires, acid and physical violence turned the place into a nightmare hazard, and the place was closed.

Rychel took Yarkul to a safe place and interrogated him at this point. Rychel manages to learn a good deal from the mage about the Company Ersgul. Rychel cuts off Yarkul’s thumbs to make sure he can’t cast spells anymore (a measure that proved to be inadequate). 

The CRH returns a little while later to their HQ to find that the Hunabar staff has been stolen, despite the many layers of magical and mundane security that Beljuril had ensconced it in. The CRH is absolutely livid at this point. Soon after, Rychel brings Yarkul to HQ, and further interrogation takes place. A list of Ersgul resources and contacts is made, and it is learned that Teskul has a romantic involvement with Yarkul’s daughter, Dragon.

Ersgul, it would seem, had been broken.

=== A Trade ===

Later in the day, a message arrives from a man named [[Elbing Hightimber]], a priest of Cyric who is living at the [[Wavesilver Villa]]. Hightimber agrees to trade Raven for Yarkul, and the adventurers agree that it would be prudent to do so at this point. Raven is returned, and Yarkul is handed over to Hightimber. Raven appears to have been drugged. He is bruised and covered in lacerations, and does not seem like himself at first.

Later that night, Raven seems to be recovering, and with the magical help of Beljuril, he manages to remember some of what happened. He remembers being in a pit full of slime and broken glass and other broken metal items. He was wearing only a loin cloth, and killed many people in the pit. Many eyes of different colours watched the gladiatorial carnage unfold, and one of the spectators in particular was a man built of deep darkness and wreathed in shadow and fear. This man, it would turn out, is [[Baron Drake]], a Cambion and champion of Iyachtu Xvim.

As Beljuril slept that night, he had a portentous [[Vision from Azuth|dream]], a boon from Azuth. While the others slept, Jafar took the staff of Yarkul (6.5’, 7 stalks) to lock it up for safekeeping at the Tower of Order.

== 30 Eleint - Eve of Destruction ==

The next day (the 30th, the day before Harvestide), Esperel gathers information about the [[Wavesilver]] Family. As it turns out, they began to loose their fortunes years ago when they began trading with the [[Company Dalael]]. Ruin found the family even more completely in recent months, as most of the family holdings were purchased by elements of Company Dalael and Company Ersgul. At length, the Family lost almost everything. The shamed patriarch of the Family, [[Bleskos]], retired to the one failing vessel he had left in the harbour, the [[Silver Spear]], and left the rest of the Family to whatever fate would find it.

It is learned that almost all the remaining family was living in the guesthouse at that point, and elements of Company Ersgul had taken up residence there. The only Wavesilver left in the house was supposed to be the consort to Bleskos, [[April]]. Esperel further found out that the villa was built on an ancient site, and that a powerful Mythal was in place there. As well, the compound is well guarded by watchspiders, which live in the trees.

Later in the day, the CRH and remaining members of the Neverwinter crew (NC) met with Thagdul in the [[Thirsty Throat Tavern]], where a large Drurginz staff party appears to be held. They are celebrating the ruin of Yarkul, and toast the CRH, NC and Rychel in particular. Rychel leaves, as he still does not trust Thagdul one bit. Thagdul rewards the CRH and NC members for their service. All are given cloaks and some money for their service.

== Harvestide - Heist of the Phaulkon Cargo ==

The day between Elient and Marpenoth is known as Harvestide. On this day, great feasts are held throughout the Realms, but in no other place are the festivities as lush and debaucherous as in the City of Splendours. The Harvestide Festival lasts until the 10th day of Marpenoth in the City.

However, on the day of Harvestide 1371, a daring and bloody heist mars the festivities of the day. The merchant house Phaulkon assembled its last caravan for the season. It was to depart for Cormyr the next day, but it was attacked in the court of the White Bull. The New Knights of the Sword Coast, who had been hired to guard it, were all killed, as were all the usual caravan guards and the caravan master. A dozen citizens were killed by various fire-spells that were used in the heist. The entire caravan disappeared around a corner and into the sewers in a matter of minutes. The guard didn’t seem to get there on time...

This heist ruined Phaulkon house, and the old master of the house, [[Lord Phaulkon]] himself, hung himself. His villa, Phaulkonmere, would later be purchased by the CRH as a new base of operations.

== Assault on the Wavesilver Villa ==

The first mission that the CRH and the NC decide to do is to raid the Wavesilver Villa. It is known that the cult of Iyachtu Xvim is operating out of the lower levels of this place, and that many of the higher-ranking members of Ersgul that are left reside here. A protracted, chaotic battle ensues. On the surface, the fighting isn’t too fierce, but it is discovered that most of the Wavesilver Family members are dead in the guest house, and not a soul can be found otherwise.

Below, in the basement levels, the stolen Phaulkon Cargo is found. It has been stowed away only recently. A very small portion of it is stolen by the CRH and NC members. The fight continues deeper and deeper into the ground, however. The adventurers find an old sewer system deep beneath the basement levels that has been cut off from the City’s sewer grid. Following one channel, they hear voices up ahead. Despite have had a dream to warn him of such things, Tomas charges ahead through an illusionary wall, and falls right into the same pit that Raven had been contained to previously. The adventurers charge in to help the brave Tomas, and find a veritable Host of the Damned. In the chamber beyond the illusory wall, Elbing Hightimber is conducting some sort of ceremony. The last of the Wavesilver family is dead and dying in the pit. The half-demon Baron Drake is there, as are a number of Drow including Rubikawn and Quevin. Dragon is also present, as are some Ghaunadan. 

Immediately, Tomas is held by Hightimber, and Rubikawn jumps down to cut his throat, thus making good his threat to the wayward Dockward boy. A chaotic battle ensues in which several drow, priests of Xvim and Rubikawn himself are slain. At the peak of the bloodshed, the Baneson Xvim is manifested as a huge green snake that begins to consume the souls of the fallen, including Tomas. At this point, all agree that it is time to bug out, including Quevin, who helps to carry out Dragon. It seems that Quevin had changed his mind about his allies at that point.

== 1 Marpenoth - The Storm Passes ==

The next day (1 Marpenoth) Thagdul sends his man [[Gotlieb Erlgund]] to help the CRH and the NC to liquidate some of the swag they took out of the Wavesilver basement. After Erlgund leaves, [[Esperel]], Tomas and Rychel follow him, only to find that Teskul is following him too. The three manage to catch him, but he only explains that he is watching Erlgund to protect him, and the CRH’s interests. They let him go, but all are now suspicious of Teskul. Teskul does warn that Ersgul is going to collect itself to make one last attack against the CRH and Drurginz.

Later in the day, Rufus and Esperel encounter Dragon on the streets. She was following Rufus, and was found by Esperel. She manages to fight off both of them, making them flee. 

On 2 Marpenoth, Thagdul comes by HQ to deliver a 1500 gp reward in the form of [[Lantanese trade bars]]. He is happy about the operation that the CRH and NC ran against the Wavesilver Villa. He also delivers money for the mithral that was taken out of the Villa. They discuss what happened to the [[Hunabar Staff]] at that time. Thagdul suggests that such a professional job must have been done by someone like the Shadow Thieves, or perhaps the [[Dalael]]. Some amongst the CRH suspect the Hunabars, but most suspect Thagdul.

On 3 Marpenoth, Thagdul comes to the HQ to deliver a message. He has learned that Ersgul is going to be shipping the Phaulkon Cargo, by sewer, to a cog on the 10th. This cog, the [[Silver Pride]], is captained by [[Semuldan Anteos]]. Semuldan, despite being the son of [[Korass]], seems sympathetic to the patriarch of the Family, Dulbrawan. Thus, he sold this information out, and so it came that a rogue named [[Gauntlet]] delivered this message to Thagdul, who in turn delivered it to the adventurers.

== Massacre at the Wavesilver Villa ==

The CRH and the NC decide that they should seize the initiative here, and attack the Wavesilver Villa again. This proves fruitless however. During the raid, Rychel fought the Baron for what seemed an eternity, while the Baron calmly deflected attack after magically-enhanced attack from Rychel. To further add to their failure of this mission, the Beholder [[Xulla]] confronted Esperel, who was sneaking around, and commanded him to ‘seize Jafar’s staff’. Of course, he meant Yarkul’s staff, which he was using to immolate whomever he could find. The quick-thinking Esperel gave Jafar his staff, and then took it, and brought it back to the Beholder. How he escaped after this event is unknown, and indeed many doubt that crazy elf’s claim that he ran into Xulla *again*. 

The culmination of this raid came in the form of a fireball from Jafar, which killed a score of City watchmen, as well as immolating [[Magistrate Thuman]]. The adventurers then made their escape. The Wavesilver Villa was put under the watchful eye of [[Magistrate Richter]] at this point, and over 100 watchmen were put to the task of guarding it.

Back at HQ, the CRH and NC get into an argument with Jafar. Most, and Beljuril especially, are angered that he killed so many civil servants with the fireball, and are worried about their place in the legal structure of the City (seemingly, for the first time). Jafar begins to fade into obscurity at this point.

== 10 Marpenoth - The Phaulkon Cargo ==

On the 10th, the final day of Harvestide, the CRH and NC planned their ambush of the Ersgul/Phaulkon Cargo. The rogue Gauntlet was going to be leading the Cargo, and he had told the adventurers where to set up their ambush.

When the Cargo was seen coming down the channel, the ambush started. The fighting did not last long, however, before the Dalael showed their hand, and trumped all. The very floor of the sewers gave way and collapsed. It fell some 50’ into a great chamber from which silver-sash-wearing drow issued from every dark hole. There were at least 20 of them, and the adventurers knew they were beat.

The power of Ersgul vanished in that pit, as the Dalael butchered the few that survived the fall. Hightimber was cut down by Rufus.

The beholder Xulla made another appearance at this point. It and Quevin exchanged some harsh words before both sides made their egress. Rychel confronted Xulla, only to be turned to stone without any effort from the creature.

== Denouement ==

Company Ersgul put it’s faith in the wrong allies, it would seem. 

Yarkul’s Reprobates were no match for the CRH, and he continually lost ground all summer to because of this. Further, he thought he had annexed a failing company in the Dalael, but it turned out that he had the picture all wrong. Dalael managed to use Ersgul to bring them back into the game, and the Phaulkon Cargo (which turned out to be a major pot that everyone was playing for) was easy pickings after the losses that Ersgul had suffered throughout the summer, and also through the treachery and complications of the Anteos Family, upon which everything seemed to hinge.
