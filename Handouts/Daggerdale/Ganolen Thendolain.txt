It had been over two weeks since Midsummer, the night Adjutant Ganolen Thendolain (Paladin 2nd-Order) had left the bustling streets of Uthmere on the long road to Castle Radiance. His orders: to be a notable and stalwart presence in the conflict his brothers and sisters in Torm were facing with the Sharans and Netherese in the west.

<nowiki> * * * </nowiki>

By now the driving rain had soaked through his clothes and the wind coming down off the mountains bade him face away from the lands of his forefathers.

\"The winds comin' off'a them mountains would kill a lesser man, come on below deck and get some mead in you!\" Shouted one of the deckhands of The Wallflower, a merchant ship Ganolen had chartered in Uthmere on its way back to Yhaunn in the Featherdale.

Ganolen waved the man off and looking out over the blackened coast and tried to imagine a time before Impiltur fell into contempt. \"Corruption and weak men.\" He said to himself and descended the stairs into the lower deck as the Wallflower sailed on through the cold shadow of the Earthspur Mountains.

His mission, he knew, was more than just being a representative for the Hand of Torm and Halls of Brilliant Justice. Justice Majoris Larson Thesk (Paladin 1st-Order) emphasized the Tormite's necessary completion of the Penance of Duty. Ganolen was being sent to repay one of the Three Debts. To atone for the persecution the other good faiths suffered at the hands of the Church of Torm.

Justice Majoris believed Ganolen to be worthy of aiding the Amaunatori against the darkness in the west. And in doing so, would display his piety and secure possible allies to shed the light of Amaunator into the depths of the demon-cursed Dunwood Forests.

Though, for Ganolen, providing a strong sword arm and sturdy shield for the Amaunatori was reason enough to make the journey.

<nowiki> * * * </nowiki>

Since leaving Uthmere, it took Ganolen nearly two months before he arrived at the gates of Castle Radiance. \"It will be good to be among the faithful once again,\" he thought, \"it has been a long journey.\"
