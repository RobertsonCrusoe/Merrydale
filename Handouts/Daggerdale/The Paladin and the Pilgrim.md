==Corran Seeks Solace==

Corran sat in the tavern of the Tethyamar Hearth Inn. Here, he had first met Banitt, Bollinger, Erevan and Dane. Here, they had joined Bargle’s Free Defense League. He had been nursing a warm tankard of Eldridge Amber, one of the few indulgences he allowed himself these days. It was simply good to be free of the influence of the White Cloaks and the Sun Blades at times. As far as he had fallen from grace, it seems like he had been pulled back into the fold over the last year. The rest of the dale still had no idea they were at war. Even when he had been in Dagger Falls recently, his parents and others there had no idea what was happening in the west. He had been worried about their welfare, especially when the guards, incited by Sergeant Warburton’s revealing of Sharran worshippers in the very heart of Dagger Falls were kicking down doors and putting people to question. He had been torn between his duty to the White Cloaks and duty to his family, but Sergeant Warburton intervened by relinquishing his badge of authority to his parents and told to use it as a means of negotiation should the guards of Dagger Falls grow...exuberant with their inquisition.

He was pulled out of his thoughts by two gruff looking member of Bargle’s league sitting down at the bar beside him. He had seen both of them around before – both at the Inn, as well as around the Castle. He knew little of them other than that they appeared to be the typical sort of mercenary that was drawn to Bargle’s deals.

“Altair! You need to help us solve an argument I’m having with Mortun here,” the man gave Corran a smile that was more drunken than friendly.

“Aye!” grinned the man that Corran assumed to be Mortun.

Corran simply took a slow sip from his tankard and lifted a single eyebrow to let the two mercenaries know that they had his attention.

Mortun got comfortable and spoke. “Jahdah here is wondering why the White Cloaks are bragging about slaying a black dragon when there are reports coming from the farmlands that a black dragon was seen flying about not four days ago? I let him know that the high and mighty White Cloaks would never lie about something like that. Clearly the White Cloaks killed a black dragon and then a second magically turned up!” Mortun gave Corran a toothy grin and then looked past Corran to give a wink to his friend, Jahdah. Jahdah placed a large hand on Corran’s shoulder, earning him a simple and seemingly calm glance from Corran before speaking and smiling.

“And I say that Mortun is speaking out his ass just like that good for nothing Warburton that’s claiming he’s a mighty dragonslayer.”

Corran ignored the hand that still was on his shoulder and quietly placed the tankard atop the well-worn surface of the bar he stood beside. He found himself torn between the truth and backing up his sergeant. A man that had gone out of his way to protect Corran’s family when there was no reason or need to do so. He turned to Jahdah and spoke,

“There is something to be said about a man that finds himself in a position where he must fight and be killed by a dragon, only to survive that encounter and ensure that the dragon is no longer a threat to himself or his homeland.” Corran was not even sure if he believed what he was saying himself. He would definitely have done things differently than Sergeant Warburton but he could not doubt the man’s courage or conviction. The man stood down a dragon, and to prove his honesty, unarmed, he approached trolls three times the size of a man and was nearly killed by the heavy stones they had thrown at him. Jahdah snorted and removed his hand from Corran’s shoulder only backhand his tankard off the bar, earning a disapproving and cautionary look from the bartender. 

“You sound like yer full of as much shit as Warburton.”

“Warburton can’t help it Jahdah”, sneered Mortun. “Being a coward’s in his blood. Seems like he’s smart enough to surround hisself with other cowards though. That way they can spread their bullshit around all even-like.”

Corran pressed his lips together in a thin line, his nostrils flaring in irritation for a single heartbeat before letting out a deep breath.

“...I know what Dane would do with this two,” he thought. They would already be on the ground bleeding, and Dane would be standing over both of them, drinking and talking about what he was planning to do with their mothers. I’m not Dane though, am I? His attention was drawn away from the two men for a moment as the bartender placed another tankard of ale in front of him to replace the one he lost. He let out a second deep breath before turning his back to Jahdah and speaking to Mortun.

“Just so that I am clear about this. Are you openly questioning the merit and honesty of a ranking officer of the White Cloaks? Before we continue our discussion, I just wanted to make sure that we are all speaking about the same thing.” Corran arched an eyebrow imperiously and spoke in a firmer tone. “So is that it? Is that what we are discussing?”

Mortun narrowed his eyes as he looked at Corran and Corran could hear another indignant snort coming from Jahdah behind him. Corran continued speaking, “I thought so. “ Corran made a motion with his head towards the entrance to the tavern. “You can leave...now.”

== Pilgrim in the Public House ==

Even as the two mercenaries glowered at Corran, they turned and headed for the door. Corran’s attention stayed on them right up until when he saw Banitt enter and he gave his friend a silent nod of acknowledgement before motioning him to come over.

Banitt’s mind was racing, still fuming from the ire he had put into his missive to their captain. Upon seeing Corran wave him over he stopped in his tracks.

He was not sure he knew what to say to his friend.

In his heart Banitt knew he did not believe Iolus to be a worshipper of Shar. But their sergeant HAD used his position irresponsibly with respect to his own mother’s business and the family of Corran. His head told him this, but in truth, Banitt cared little for ranks and military dealings. In truth the actions of Iolus had not fazed him at all.

He WAS ready to quit the White Cloaks though. In his heart the reason being he had no intention of being a part of a mercenary company; he had enough of that with Bargle’s troupe of ingrates. 

He also knew that no matter what happened he could not just leave Castle Radiance. The sword on his back weighed heavily, a reminder of the vow he had made, the words echoed through his head.

“I anoint you as Guardian of the Tomb, Banitt Harbaugh, First Pilgrim, and I charge you with the ferreting out of the Black Saint, and the destruction of his followers and allies. If you will take this title and this charge, you may likewise take this blade to help you in your duties.”

He knew he must follow through on his vow, and he knew he could not do that alone.

With that he straightens his back, lets out a deep breath and moves to sit beside his friend at the bar.

Corran gave Banitt a speculative look and concealed the lower half of his face with his tankard of ale as he spoke.

“We have put our foot right in it this time, my friend. It seems that people are already talking about the dragon.”

He motioned to the bartender, holding up one finger before motioning with his head towards Banitt and lowering his tankard to the bar.

Banitt looks to the man he would call friend – perhaps the only person he would call such in his present state. It is a long look, not searching, not accusing; truly just a blank stare. He fully expected Corran to tear a strip off him, half hoped he would. Ah, the priests of Ilmater sometimes make sense.

The bartender delivers Banitt a pint. Without waiting he lifts it to his mouth and unceremoniously drains it. Very unlike himself he muses, but, desperate times...

He looks to the Paladin winks and says..

“What Dragon?”

==Corran==

Corran gave Banitt a dubious look before motioning to the bartender to fill Banitt's tankard.

“What dragon indeed...” he murmured quietly before looking to Banitt once more. “You and Warburton need bury whatever problems you have with one another. I will not say I disagree with your assessment of the White Cloaks, and I certainly will not hold you to the level of duty that is expected from the Tormtar. I have to tell you that as a man, and as companion of mine, I expect you to not openly question and cast doubt upon those of higher rank. We are at war Banitt. If we are victorious against the Netherese then it is possible that the White Cloaks will no longer exist afterwards and if we are not victorious...well...”

Corran took another drink from his tankard as he looked away from his friend.

== Banitt  Makes His Case==

Banitt takes in what Corran says. 

“Corran, I think you have me wronged here. I respect Iolus profoundly.. as a man. But not for any rank.”

He pauses takes a sip of ale to wet his tongue then turns on his stool to face Corran.

“Of first note is that said rank, that of Iolus and Lumina were granted as being the founders of the White Cloaks. However, at the time the White Cloaks were founded there were no ranks. It was not an army following orders, it was a simple matter of doing the right thing. When I gave the townspeople half of our 'loot' I was following the will of Amaunator. That will always be the case for me.”

He lowers his eyes and continues “Iolus accused me of not respecting our fellow White Cloaks because I refused them their greed. Perhaps I did disrespect them. I am unsure. I do know however that the same members of the party I was accused of disrespecting were forced to sleep in the stables. And our Sergeant allowed that to happen, he did not honour them, did not even put up a fight. But when it came time to salvage his mother’s business he was ready to do battle. When it came time to protect YOUR parents, he gave up his sigil – a badge of honour, and a token of rank.”

A very brief pause.

“Both the protection of your parents, and the willingness to fight for his mother are honourable traits... but neither was White Cloak business. Neither were the actions of a good sergeant, just a good man. In truth it was this that has led me to resign my Cloak. I was looking at a good man, doing the right thing, and finding fault in him for not being a good soldier. No Corran, I did not openly question or cast doubt on one of higher rank. Just another man. I have no rank, nor will I. If I wanted rank I would join the Order. It is not only the White Cloaks at war. The whole region is at war. I do not need the title I was bestowed to have the will to fight the Netherese, and I certainly do not need a rank in some spoiled princesses little army.”

Without waiting for a reply Banitt steps off his stool to make for the door. 

==Corran==

He frowned into his tankard and shook his head a moment. Clearly Banitt was being too headstrong for subtlety. He'll speak of respecting Warburton but then recite a list of every misstep. He let out a tired sigh and threw aside caution before turning from where he stood and speaking clearly.

“I did not mean Warburton. I meant Lumina. Warburton is a man of rank as much as you are the servant of the White Saint. Both titles were foisted upon both of you. Neither one of you really desire to have the title bestowed upon you, but both of you are of too high quality to stand up and speak what is in your heart. Instead, you both bear it and hope for the best. Lumina, Banitt. I meant that you should not have openly questioned Lumina amongst other members of the White Cloaks and now you do so once again, here of all places.”

He moved away from the bar and the simple white clothing he was wearing shimmered for a moment, only to be replaced by his well-tended heirloom armour. He moved up alongside Banitt and met his gaze.

“We are at war Banitt. War is the domain of men, and currently you and Warburton are behaving like boys. I expect more.”

==Banitt Stands Firm==

Banitt stops mid-stride and sighs.

“Yes.. you are right.. Toward one another we are being childish. But we both have enough faith in Amaunator to easily forgive childish words. We both trust that if we each remain connected to HIM, we are not far off our paths.”

“In fact, that is what disturbs me most with Lumina. She is of the clergy as well as being a Whitegate. But I think she has confounded the two. I have no faith in her as a captain of men, or truly as a captain of faith. What she asked for, 'to be beholden first and foremost to one another', and with that loyalty to her as our leader, that I cannot give. ‘Twas she that made the proclamation that this vow was required to be a White Cloak. I have already made all the vows I can. Because of that I cannot agree to blindly follow the orders of others.”

“As for respect in public... my belief is that her 'army' is wrong. I disrespected Lumina the military leader, not Lumina the Priestess. Were she to drop her swelling pride and act on faith, she would see that the White Cloaks would be a much more fierce force against the Netherese. We SHOULD be fighting as you say, for humanity, for the Dales. All of its people, all of its faiths, all of its beauty. We SHOLD be fighting because we believe in that cause. Her military structure, and assurances of 'bounty and pay' draw to the White Cloaks those seeking only their own fortune, or those who simply want to follow orders. I gave her every opportunity to do away with the military style command, but she refused. So I will not wear the Cloak.” 

== Enter the Knight ==

As Banitt is speaking these last words, the doors of the inn open up, and the tall, armoured figure of a knight with the gleaming yellow tabard of the Order of the Lambent Shield enters the room, and looks around. Seeing Banitt and Corran at the table, he nods and approaches the table.

“I hope I'm not interrupting you men” the knight begins. There is no mistaking this man; his tabard veritably shouts his affiliation, and as he gets closer, the twin-stacked chevrons of white across his chest indicate that he is a Master Knight. The long silver hair worn in a thick braid down his back and his bushy silver beard identify him as Master Knight Derfel, one of the three Master Knights of the Chapter. He really needs no introduction, but still politely says his name, and continues. “I am hoping to have a word with you, Pilgrim. That is, if you and sir Corran are quite through.” He gives a slight nod to the Tormite.

“I've been sent by the Grand Master, to make you an offer...”

Banitt looks from the Knight to the Paladin and chuckles.

“If I know my friend here I have only just begun to hear what he has to say, but his words will likely continue for years, not minutes. He is no fan of petty tomfoolery.”

He nods respectfully to Corran.

“I am sure he will not mind giving us a minute, perhaps we can take a table?”

Banitt motions to a table in the corner and proceeds to it with the knight.

 

== Aumanator’s Office==

“There is nothing so poisonous as secrets and rumours, Pilgrim. We can stay here. Brother Corran can hear what I have to say.”

The Master Knight slides a chair back, and settles down into it, his plate armour protesting a little and causing him to sit somewhat awkwardly. 

“I am short a chaplain, Banitt. The Grand Master has suggested that you might be a good fit for the Order, and by all accounts, he is right. If you are interested in serving Him in a more structured fashion, I have a spot for you in the Order. Take the day to reflect on the matter, and meet me before Vespers at the Fortress Cathedral tonight.”

He rises, though much faster than he did settling down. “The sun shine upon you both” he says with a slight bow, then turns on a heel and marches out.

Banitt blinks a little in surprise as he watches the Master Knight walk away.

He speaks softly to Corran “It seems Amaunator works in mysterious ways, I could not make the vow to loyalty Lumina demanded, much as it pained me. And yet, I could not fulfil my vow to hunt out the Black saint on my own.”

He looks into his empty glass for a long moment. “My friend, I believe I must take up this offer. I will join the Order of the Lambent Shield, a structure of faith un-moving is one I can live with. Also, the timing of this offer is simply too fortuitous for me to deny.”

He pauses again then looks his friend in the eye.

“I truly hope this will not mean I am to be parted from your company. You have been a stalwart friend, and I will still give my life for any who bear the White Cloak! To battle we will go, two arms of the same warrior, but both wielding one blade to strike down the agents of Shar! Yes. I can feel the strength returning to my limbs, this is as it must be.”

You see him smile for the first time in days, his shoulders settle into their broad, high bearing of confidence you are so used to in this young man.
