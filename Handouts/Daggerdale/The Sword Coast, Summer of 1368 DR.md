== The Warders of Waterdeep ==

The  first encountered the Company Dalael this summer. Both parties were searching for an island known as the Island of Three Curses, somewhere southwest of Neverwinter in the Sea of Swords. On this island, Company Dalael was seeking a location known as Kulgen Sussun in the drow tongue (meaning 'Fortress of Light'), while the Warders sought a keep known as Blethmark. It turned out that Blethmark was one of many layers built upon the millennia-old drow fortress.


In the sub-levels of the fortress, the Warders encountered the Dragon Bliz'Anioch.  The dragon nearly killed the lot of them, but had it's tail cut off by Tallinvor.  In the end, the Warders and the dragon faught to a stand-off.  They negotiated an escape for the dragon, which was last seen flying eastwards.

During the race to find this location, the Warders and Dalael crossed paths many times, both in the City of Splendours during the research phase, and on the seas and in the archipelago during the exploration phase.

The Dalael were known at that time to have infiltrated many noble houses of the City, including the Anteos, Dezlentyr, Eltorchul, Margaster, Umbrusk and Wavesilver families. The heads of the company, the Brothers Dalael, had married or had children with women of the Anteos, Dezlentyr and Eltorchul families. They also had some influence over the Durinbold family, through manipulation of Breton Durinbold’s hatred for his brother, Tallanvor, a member of the Warders. The Warders were able to foil many Dalael operations and managed to raid their stronghold in North Ward.

In the end, the Dalael left the city in defeat. The only Dalael to stay in the City thereafter was Y'morth Dalael, known in his surface guise as the elf 'Dally'. Dally was not active within his relations' Company, and was accepted by the Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors as a contributing member of that guild.

Though some have perished, several members of the Warder survive, and plague the Dalael to this day:

* Steele, an elf of questionable morals and jack of all trades
* Kennyth Thames, a priest of Oghma, now Loremaster at the Font of Knowledge
* Rychel Voordan, an urban stalker, now retired from adventuring
* Gable, mage, currently retired, whereabouts unknown
* Edgar Grimbold, (aka Stuart Bold), swashbuckler and monk, recently returned to Waterdeep from a sojourn in the Eastern Realms
* Phineous Reoul, a wild mage and worshipper of Mystra with a penchant for lighting things on fire...when he's not accidentally turning his companions into stone or turning himself into a winged cat.  Current wherabouts unknown.
* Gaebril Tremalkin, a well-meaning warrior with a strong affinity for the bow and an inclination to become a paladin of Selûne.  Currently travelling with the Company of the Eastern Storm

The Warders formally dissolved their association following the assassination of Tallanvor Durinbold, his brother, and Kyahs during an assault upon the Durinbold Family Villa. Further harassed by Magistrate Richter, they were only saved from prosecution through the good offices of the paladin Harkas Kormallis.
