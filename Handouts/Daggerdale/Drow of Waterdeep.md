These notes were composed by the Harper wizard, Agamemnon, and left with a letter addressed to Beljuril of Azuth shortly before the wizard's disappearance. This intelligence has been cross-referenced with other files in possession of the Rooken Network.

==What I Have Learned about the Drow of the City==

There has likely always been a Drow or two, skulking about the City of Splendours. But there is a problem in this City, today, that is much worse. The Drow needn’t hide as they used to, as they have supplanted themselves within the Noble Community here in Waterdeep. Here, briefly, are many of the details I have learned;

== Three Brothers ==
The Brothers Dalael, run a trading business in this City, importing goods from the Underdark, and exporting surface goods to the Depths. The Oldest is referred to as ‘Firstboy’, this being Quevin Dalael. The next oldest, ‘Secondboy’, is Quellar, and the youngest (that I know of) is Issilith, or ‘Thirdboy’. The Dalael family is a rarity among the Drow, being Patriarchal. I assume, therefore, that they do not worship the Spider Queen, and I have heard whispers of a Cult of Vhaeraun in this ‘City of Splendours’.

===Quevin Dalael===
Firstboy Dalael has supplanted himself in the Anteos Family, through no small treachery. He has sired a child with Ranaya, the wife of the Family’s Patriarch, Dulbrawan. The child is now 5 years old, and is named Millathi. This Winter, Dulbrawan was found to have hanged himself, after being found cavorting with Sildreen, the wife of his younger brother, Korass. This leaves the Anteos estate to the boy, Millathi, who no one in the noble community will challenge as rightful heir to the Family fortune. In the present, the family is managed by Ranaya, and two of Dulbrawan’s brothers, though certainly Quevin has some degree of control within the family. He masquerades as a normal elf, a close friend and business partner of the family, and is known to live at the villa. He and his brothers all go by their given names at Noble functions, masquerading as elves of surface-origin. They are quite well known among many families, and accepted as essential parts of the Family Retinues, or more. I suspect Quevin is a servant of the obscure god Vhaeraun; I have met him, and he has the calm and confidence that I only find in the Truly Faithful of many races. He bears no arms, but rarely travels from the Villa these days, and only then, with large escort.

===Quellar Dalael===
Secondboy is the dangerous one, mark this. This Drow is rumoured to run the Company’s more risky business endeavours, and is also rumoured to have a short fuse, for such a long-lived race. Quellar had the good sense and fortune to marry into the Dezlentyr Family. This family has always been the seat of discord in the Noble Community for a couple reasons; it was the first Family to dilute the human bloodlines of the Nobility, when Arlos married and elven maid 40 years ago. Although she is now dead (many rumours of her death abound), Lyrethian sired twins, Corin and Corinna, who live on. Corinna is openly married to Quellar Dalael, who was first a business associate of the family. This marriage is publicly known, as are their two children, 3 year-old Lyrethian, and 2 year-old Istorvir (ages as of 1372). This family has the most enviable of Fleets on the Sword Coast, and has conducted a voluminous and reliable business as a result. They were known, 6 years ago, to be shipping goods from the Underdark, all over the Sword Coast, and seemed to have a monopoly on the source. They are a wealthy Family, and Quellar is deeply entrenched with them. He is loved by the Family, especially Arlos and Corin, whom he is rumoured to have taken on ‘exotic trips’ which last 5 months and don’t go by boat or coach. He is much like his wife, Corinna; he craves adventure and danger, and is seemingly fearless.

===Issilith Dalael===
Thirdboy has had the longest-running open relationship with a Noble Family of any of his brothers, despite his young age. He married Fea Eltorchul 12 years ago, and today has two children: 11 year-old Julia and 10 year-old Quevin. Issilith is known to be the business man of the Dalael. It was he who introduced Quellar to the Dezlentyrs many years ago, long after he had already initiated trade dealings with that Family. The Eltorchuls have likewise long been the only providers of some of the rarer Underdark curios and substances that are sought out by the City’s Mages. There is no guessing how these reagents come into their possession. The Eltorchul Family is better at hiding it’s secrets, and I am hard pressed to find out about Issilith’s relationship with the rest of the family, but I do know that the Mage Dally (Y’Morth Dalael, cousin the Dalael boys) is a very good friend of Thesp’s and also an instructor at their college. This leads me to believe that the Family has no reservations about dealing with the Drow, and having them as a part of their lives. The Family is a private one, besides. Issilith is the easiest of the Dalael to find, as he is always about on business. He goes from one Noble Villa to the next, and from many businesses in the City, conducting the bulk of the Company’s business. When I used a trusted Divine Consultant, I was told that Issilith is the most law-abiding of the three brothers, and that he may actually crave having a legitimate business on the surface. Perhaps becoming a father will do that to the rest of them.

===Other Dalaels===
As I have noted, there are cousins to Quevin’s kin, here in the City. These include the mage, Dally, who is well known in many circles, and indeed by myself. He has been in the City for some 18 years, and has established a reputation as an excellent instructor at the Eltorchul Academy. There is also an elf named Nym Dalael, who operates a place called the Dark Lantern. I have learned that he is often at odds with his cousin, Issilith.

===Other Noble Families===
The Dalael boys seem to crave acceptance in the Noble Community, and as such, are often found in places that these people frequent. I have seen Nym at Gounar’s Tavern, more than once, with the Lady Fairworth Cragsmere. But I have seen no other evidence of their infiltration of other Families. As a matter of fact, I have seen the Dalael boys completely and deliberately ignored by the frequently-seen Umbrusk youths. There is certainly tension between this family and the Dalael.

I remember many years ago, being summoned to the City on my first assignment. I was still being trained, and was supposed to go to Waterdeep (a city I had not been to, at the time) and locate my next mentor; which was common practice in those days. When we met, she told me that we were going to investigate a possible slave-trade organization that was likely operated by Drow. This was when I met Dally, who openly confessed to me that he was a Drow, and that he had family in the City who ran a trading business, but that they had nothing to do with slaves. I could not find any evidence of this practice by the Dalael at the time, and indeed, to this date I have not found a single item to indict the Dalael on. I continue to study them, though, as others think that they are involved in some illicit commerce. However, it does not seem likely at this time, that they do any more illicit commerce than the average Trading Family or Merchant House.

===The Phaulkon Cargo===

The one single item that I have pursued is the disappearance of the Phaulkon Cargo, last Harvestide (1371). Reliable accounts of the situation places silver-sashed Drow at the root of the heist, one of which may have been Quevin, who exchanged harsh-sounding words with a beholder. This beholder is thought to be Xulla, a beholder which supported the Co. Ersgul, with which Dalael may have been involved. This incident piqued my interest in the activities of the Drow and the Dalael particularly.

The result of this heist, it would seem, is that the Dalael have come back this Season with greater numbers. It is well known that the trip to Menzoberranzan and back takes 5 months, by the way used by the Dalael. Five months after the Phaulkon Cargo vanished to the Depths, the Anteos Villa began to crawl with ‘unremarkable-looking elves’, and many of the Family interests were suddenly encircled by Jardeth Family mercenaries. As well, an unprecedented supply of Underdark goods found their way onto our markets, through many legitimate channels. It is my opinion, lacking sufficient evidence as it may, that the Phaulkon Cargo was used to purchase these goods, and bring more Drow to the surface. This bears closer investigation.

Though I lack the political clout to attack the Noble Families that have dealings with the Co. Dalael, I do intend to nip at their heels; those businesses and connections they have that are found outside the protection of Noble walls and Noble rules. Indeed, if the Dalael are responsible for the Phaulkon Cargo heist, they likely did not use Noble resources to manage this task. And if they are responsible for this event, they are likewise responsible for the deaths the men who transported it, and also for the death of Master Phaulkon, the father of my closest friend.

== Other Drow ==
I have recently found out about a second faction of Drow in the City, which at first seemed to complicate matters. My Divine Source informed me that a powerful female Drow, the only survivor of a Family from Menzoberranzan, had come to the surface with a small army of mercenaries. They did not come to the surface by way of Undermountain, and indeed had to travel by boat for some distance, to get to the City. They actively sought out the Dalael, whom they apparently learned about in Baldur’s Gate. I happened to run into a fellow who was doing some information gathering for these displaced mercs, but managed to learn only that they knew of the Dalael, and wanted to meet with the Firstboy of the Family.

I have been doing my best to look for men looking for the Dalael, and to track these others to their hiding place and expose them. If I can expose these other Drow before they can merge with Dalael, I will certainly have shut the barn door before the horses get out. I have learned the identity of a man who is working for these new Drow, and have even arranged a meeting for the morning of the 10th with this fellow (always work during the day, when it comes to Drow; it won’t help with Drow like the Dalael boys, but this other faction are new to the surface, and still incapacitated by the Sun, I would suspect). The man’s name is Gettard. The Lady Drow, as I have learned is named Briza Uloavae.
