Selected Entries from the Journal of Aisling Maeliticus

A slightly damaged small & thin diary, discovered in Charrah's Chambre, Greengrass 1483 DR.

Greengrass, 1439 DR – I Begin a New Truth

I begin this fresh book by committing to myself to lie no further. We came here to create new lives, and in these pages, I will wrestle with my own conflicted thoughts about what we've done, and why. A record needs to be kept. The Amaunatori bastard is, I'm afraid, right about that. Still, I won't share this with him or his kind until I understand some truths; looking into our past (this all started when I was just a girl, foolish, frightened and enamoured with a powerful man), judging what we've done in Thistle Ridge, and figuring out why we were sent here in the first place and whom we can trust and whom we cannot. For the moment, I trust none but the Sweetberry's, Thistles and their people, whom I'm afraid we've done a terrible disservice to.

Today is an auspicious day to start down this path. It is before sunrise as I write this, and the day will be full of joy and our faith in renewal; the town is adorned for the very purpose, and I can smell many ovens which fill the streets this fine spring morning with the promise of a delicious bounty. We will gather and sing, we'll play our games and worship in our way; The Consummation will surely deliver a wonderful Midwinter Bounty, and Yondalla's Children will fill us all with joy and satisfaction. But a terrible secret lays beneath it all. Always beneath it all.

The double-masquerade nearly has me pulling out my hair. I know that Jannath isn't what she seems, and she has made me not what I seem as well. I can invoke all that a Chauntean priestess should be capable of and more; my tinctures and potions have helped bring a thousand children to the women of the Dale, and a great deal of the art of the Sisters of Charnel Priory relies upon my garden, and the powerful herbs therein. But it seems a lie, given that I pray to a goddess outlawed both Here and There, and though I have power, I know not exactly whom I owe for it.

I will play my role today, but tomorrow I will ask Her. She will be at the temple leading the prayers of us whom the secrets hold. She will ask for sacrifices again, and other obscene things. And I will challenge her. Enough is enough.

20 Kythorn (Solstice), 1439 DR – Truth, Lies and Freedom

I have been her prisoner for a month and a half, and have just come back to town. The lie that was told to explain my absence was that I was collecting rare mushrooms in the Sorrowbog. But in truth, when I confronted her after Greengrass, she did not take it well, and apprehended me.

"You are not, Jannath, not the Crone. You are a fraud and a trickster" I accused her, right in the temple in front of everyone; my son and daughters and their spouses. I wish that Alvund had not passed in the dead of winter; he would have had the courage to stand up to her. I now wonder at the illness that took him, that I had no art to relieve.

"You must be tired from the festivities yesterday, and hopefully last night, my little plum" the accursed thing replied. I have seen her for what she is now, and what she may become. But in that moment, she still appeared to be the Crone as she had been enshrined, and I the daughter, and my daughters the children. She was imperious and beautiful, but that is all a disguise. She fooled us long ago when we first arrived here, and I realize now that we were set up from the beginning, from the time we made the pact with the coven in Daerlun, and forsook truth for safety, to come here and hide from the Shadow.

"I will do no more of the disgusting things you command, and it is time everyone knew the truth. Time for you to disclose it. A shadow draws near, we can all feel it. We see it in the Alavaxi girl, we feel it in the absence of her family, the Thistles and the Sweetberry's will discover it all soon. You have fooled us as we were fooled decades ago by three just like you. They served us up, and you have eaten of us ever since. But today you will confess your true nature; you are not Jannath, not the Crone, nothing but a witch…"

"Mother," Andarl began, and his lovely wife clutched his arm earnestly, sensing that something wicked was unfolding. "We are Alavandors and Clarandals, and our families have prevailed through decades of exile. I am not blind; I can see that you all came here escaping something, and you have. We have…"

I interrupted him. "We are neither of those things, and we have not prevailed. Your father's name was not Alavandor…" and with this, she struck me. The hag that I would learn the name of shortly struck my face and left me dumb and helpless. There was shock and horror in the temple, but the cunning of the hag was great, and she quickly convinced everyone that I needed a rest, and that I would accompany her on a holy pilgrimage to the swamp, and before I knew it, I was lead away from the temple and my home, and into the swamp.

There in the heart of the putrid bog she showed herself to me. A hag of great legend, the sister of the dryad we drove off years ago. Yes, she had deceived us, she plainly said. And yes, she had asked for sacrifices which we willingly offered, and yes, we had performed vile acts and beget revolting things with her, but what harm? That was her argument. She brought me to her home, to take off her disguise, and show me the plain truth of things, and to convince me of the greatest lie of all; that the ends justify the means, and that any deception, any small sacrifice is acceptable if our children come into this world and beget children, and that they live in peace and happiness. And hadn't she helped us to accomplish that?

This was compelling, and over dozens of nights in her captivity, with her constantly testing my resolve, there were nights that it flagged. There were nights when I agreed that she had made good on a promise to help us have children again. When I would say "yes, you freed us of the curse of the coven, and helped great families renew their lines", but I would remember some simple truths.

My name is not Aisling Alavandor. Neither of them. But my sisters were Senthia and Abrecia Maeliticus, and I have preserved their names. I have daughters that bear the names of my sisters, who paid the ultimate price for us, their blood buying favor with the coven that 'freed' us. And I have told the Amaunatori bastard our last name, and that is the only truth in their books, though Alvund was enraged at my disclosure at the time. A 'flicker of light that will lead the shadow to our door' he said. He might have been right.

I stayed focused on the names of my sisters and daughters those long nights in her captivity, until I mastered the very art she used against me; I convinced her of my conversion to her truth, and she let me go.

I have been reunited with my family, to the great joy of all. I have checked on the girls running the Phylactery, and they have kept things running well, and have seen to the needs of the many girls who already show signs of the fruit of The Consummation. I have set my affairs in order; made good with my son and his wife, assured them that I will do my duty, and continue to be a conduit for the blessings of Chauntea or Jannath, whichever they prefer, and will forget forget forget.

But I will not.

My sisters names were Abrecia and Senthia, and my daughters names are Abrecia and Senthia. Maeliticus, and that name means Legacy. I am free.

Middle Entries

The diary is heavily damaged in the middle from moisture and mould. Only the final record remains legible.

26 Uktar, 1439

It is exactly a month since we buried two of my children and my sweet grandson, Dorian. I cannot stand to see their names on their stones in the yard, a lie chiseled on a lie. It is obscene, unacceptable. But I need to know the entire truth before I take action and risk everything. The hag has taken my children, and she has made it so I can't take hers without wounding my own heart. But I will end her, even if I must make uncomfortable allies to do so.

The key to finding those allies will be to find out what is behind the darkness in my granddaughter. It is not the passing of her mother; her darkness was there before. And there are many in town that say hers is a cursed line (little do they know…), and that her darkness issues from the seed of her father, and the rest of his family. What of the rest of his family? Xerces won't say, because he was young when they came, now more than 20 years ago, and he doesn't remember much about the journey. He has been assured by the Amaunatori bastard that his family is recovering at the war hospital, but how long do they need to 'recover' before they can return a letter?

My mind is racing, speeding toward the truth, I hope. Here is what I will do, and then I depart;

I will tell Senthia EVERYTHING. She has a right to know, and Tergus already suspects something. He is a good man and will support her. He sees something is wrong with my granddaughter, as she spends much time with our Lilly. I have been blessed to have so many granddaughters, but I fear, I fear, I fear…

I will also tell my darling Cole what I am doing and where I am going, and a little of the truth. She has a right to know, as she is one made most complicit, without knowing. I hope she takes the news well, as she is bearing children (we suspect twins), and Chessentans struggle more in labour than Condathans.

Then, I am off to the Falls, where I will take one of two actions (I know not which, yet); I will either confront the Amaunatori bastard full on, with the truth in hand, and see if he can't send his most hateful priests or knights into that swamp and kill the hag, or I will venture straight to the Castle, and see the older Alavaxi. It would bring me great pleasure to see them well, or recovering, and to tell them the names of their grand daughters. And to hear the truth of their journey.

This book I leave behind for safe keeping, and hope the gods, the real gods, will give me the chance to bring truth and happiness to my people.
