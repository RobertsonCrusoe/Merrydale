Late Tarsakh, 1373, Rassalantar.

Sure enough, on the 29th, a storm blew all night that kept half the citizens of the village awake all night. It was known that the Eastern Storm was coming, and it was rumoured that the two men staying at the Inn were waiting for them.

Early in the morning of the 30th, the storm peaked as 3 riders and one runner came into town at full pace. They dismounted and entered a small tavern that a couple of them had been at just a year before, when the world was a much different place. The numerous guests (caravaners and other travelers) eating breakfast in the tavern that morning recall that the doors seemed to be blasted open, and everyone was startled and frightened. They recall that a great figure stood in the door, the wind blowing a great golden cloak of fur all about him. In he marched, and behind him three others came, all just as terrible as the first. None could speak or avert their gaze while these four men, Heroes of recent Legend, sat down to breakfast in that very tavern.

It was here that the Heroes were rejoined with Garth and Beljuril, and it took hours for them to exchange tales. The news that Garth brought from the City was most disturbing to the Eastern Storm, but vows were quickly said, and plans began to unfold. 

Before noon, the band of six Heroes was ready to depart, and shortly after nightfall, they arrived just north of Waterdeep, at the crossroads where the High Road and the Long Road fork, where Roarke’s Tavern marks the last bit of civilization for a good distance.

Roarke’s is an impressive place for a tavern. It was an old cathedral to some forgotten god, and the place still has the feel of a large and ominous canonized building. The vaulted ceilings and rows of thick buttresses reveal the original reason for this most venerable building. But, whoever it was built to venerate is forgotten, and the vaulted ceilings are now choked with smoke from scores of pipes. The pews are all gone, and in their stead are dozens of tables, which, this time of year, are all packed full of the myriad forms of those that use the road. It is a noisy place, and lively. It is not, however, rowdy or bawdy, like the festhalls of the Dock or Trades Ward, nor is it prim and snooty like the gilded gentlemen’s clubs of North Ward. This is an honest place, filled by the earthy many that have the wisdom of the road.

The Heroes were immediately shown to a private room in one corner of the cyclopean structure; a fortified tower that had been turned into a private dining room. There, they met with none other than Khelben Arunsun the Blackstaff. Khelben explained that things in the City were as bad as he has ever seen them, and requested the help of the six men at the table. He adorned them all with badges, which would provide them with some freedom in the City. While not completely above the law, the badges would allow the Heroes some freedom from corrupt Watchmen and Magistrates, and provide a legal means (for those who need them) to kick down the appropriate doors and kick the appropriate behinds. Further, he pursed the Heroes with 20,000 to help pay for what would certainly become an expensive endeavour. 

He made specific references to the Company Aldebarran, and insisted that it be brought to heel. He also mentioned the Company Dalael, which though he had no solid evidence to base his opinion of them on, though them villainous all the same.

The Blackstaff took his leave early, explaining that he had matters to attend to. The Heroes stayed on, eating the sumptuous meal paid for by Khelben, and discussed goals and plans.

Greengrass, 1373, Roarke’s Tavern.

Shortly after midnight, the Heroes decided it was time to get to the City. It was only a couple hours south of Roarke’s, and the road is not very dangerous. But, they did not get far before trouble started again.

Leaving the private dining room in the corner of the great hall at Roarke’s, a shot rang out, and a bullet struck Beljuril in the shoulder. The last two members of Grimwalds Rangers had surfaced again to hunt for the heads of the Good for their own selfish ends. They had also been sent with a score of second-rate assassins who were intermingled in the crowd, who attempted to slow the good guys, while the elite assassin, Shellwynd, fired his rifle time and again.

The efforts of the assassins seemed especially focused on Beljuril, and they seemed to know a few of his tricks and were prepared for them. Thus, it was no surprise when one of the second rate assassins was captured and interrogated, that he gave up the man known as Clavius, as his employer (who later turned out to be Jafar). Shellwynd and Bartelos both managed to escape mostly intact, and days later, as Beljuril scried upon them, they were seen riding east along the East Road, away from the City, hopefully for good.

Waterdeep

Returning to the City of Splendours, the Company of the Eastern Storm was surprised to find how bad the City had actually become. The streets were littered with violent-looking gangs, many of which openly (albeit foolishly) challenged the six heroes. The pity of Maturin the Grey stayed his companion’s desire to educate the seditious youngsters, even after he was spit upon.

The company put together a list of equipment that they wished for Nero to outfit them with. Much of this gear was delivered, but a few high-end items remain to be delivered.

A trip to one of the poorer, and more dangerous neighborhoods of Dockward lead to an interesting encounter. In search of the Chessentan Entropists, Amon and Szuth, elements of the company visited a Waterdeep neighborhood populated by the dusky people of this part of Faerun. They were not difficult to find. Little Chessenta, like many neighborhoods of the present-day City, is much like a little nation unto itself, complete with a standing army of dark and threatening soldiers.
