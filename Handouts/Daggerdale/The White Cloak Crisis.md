==Meeting of the White Cloaks==

A few days after the Deadnettle task force returns to the Castle, Lumina is forced to call a meeting with all the White Cloaks that are in the Castle.

There has been much grumbling and whispering about many things, and the Captain has assembled all of you (minus Tuugan and Alystair) to the second-floor training room for a meeting.

With the swelling of the ranks recently, there is no longer enough room in the conference room on the third floor to accommodate the entire company, so you are all sitting on the floor of the sparing area, with Lumina standing before you.

Lumina has not been the same ever since her trip to the Shadowfell. Many have whispered that something cracked or died inside her on that ill-fated journey, and to be fair, she has been aloof at the best of times ever since, and completely distant other times.

Today, however, the light that used to shine behind her eyes before last summer is back, and as you are all assembling, she is standing with her arms folded across her chest, and a look of resolve is upon her face. She holds a number of rolled up papers in one hand, and as the last of you take a spot on the floor, looking up at her, she puts her hands at her sides, tapping the papers against one hip, and addresses the assembly.

==The Captain’s Words ==

\"White Cloaks! I have called this meeting to share some information with the lot of you, and to bring some reassurances to those of you who are in doubt.

\"First, I must make a guarantee to all of you. I am the Captain of this Company. I am YOUR Captain, and I make a covenant with you that I break for no other duties or obligations. What we share within these walls is our business alone, and I do not share information given to me in confidence with anyone outside of these walls. I have other obligations, and they are weighty. But I do not make any arrangement or promise that would have me compromise my integrity or covenant with another.

\"I expect all of you to make the same covenant with me now, because I am going to share information with you that is privileged, and which you may not share with those outside our Company. Know this; I will never ask any of you to say or do, or not say or not do, anything which would be an affront to your obligations outside of our Company; I know many of you have such obligations, and I am glad of it. I am glad to have the Tormites here, and others of many Faiths. But we must also trust in each other, and protect each other. If any cannot make this covenant with us, he can now turn in his cloak, and part company.\"

=== Successes and Failures===

She continues..

\"The mission that Sgt. Warburton led was a mixture of success and failure. The team was able to achieve all the primary objectives; they destroyed a nest of Trollspawn, they uncovered a Sharran temple in Daggerdale, they uncovered and chased off a member of the Dark Garden. They brought relief to a struggling and tragedy-struck farm, and tamed a dragon. They discovered a gate to Tethyamar, and have changed the politics and strategic nature of the region for good.

\"These are all successes, but they are tempered by some failures, and some conflicts which have arisen recently. 

\"The first failure I want to address is the failure of people to follow orders. There was some sedition in this last mission, and this will not EVER be tolerated again. We have a military structure of chain-of-command for a reason, and it will be respected at all times.

\"The next failure was that of the quartermaster to respect the troops he was charged with provisioning and rewarding. Banitt made a decision based on his peerless generosity, and even though some of you failed to live up to his standard of philanthropy, I do not expect every man here to adhere to any other man's specific ideals, so long as he lives within the rules we have set for ourselves. You are entitled to a share of the loot for any mission, and you will be paid it in the future. How you choose to spend it is your decision, though there may be repercussions later.

\"Next, is the Dragon, Kruhwahll. Sgt. Warburton decided to make a deal with a wicked dragon. This was his decision to make, and it was not an easy one. He was forced to weigh the possibility of having some or all of his men, including himself, destroyed by this menace, against the possible good of making an enemy for our enemies. This was a hard decision, and he has been criticized for it, though not always openly. I have read the reports, and know all the circumstances of the deal he made with the dragon. I do not approve of this decision, but we will all live with it. It could be that the dragon has some part yet to play in our struggles against the Netherese, and for the time being, Kruhwahll is one more enemy for the Dark Garden.

\"I was displeased that some of our new recruits would not willingly share out some of the loot they were rightfully entitled to. The people of Tesh Valley Farm were hit very hard by the Dark Garden, and indeed, our actions there seem to have drawn the dragon to that town, where it killed dozens of people and destroyed important defenses. We did give them a generous gift, but to have you argue about it in front of them was as good as taking it back; the people of that settlement have a mixed opinion of this outfit. If the payout of the quartermaster would have been humbly respected, and not openly and seditiously contested, we would count the entire population of Tesh Valley Farm as our friends.

\"I am dismayed that some of you are using this company for conspicuous religious agendas. The White Cloaks are a reflexive instrument of warfare in Daggerdale; we are a force of many and various talents, and our mix of faiths strengthens, rather than weakens us. This Company is not now, nor will it ever be under my leadership, an extension of the Church of Amaunator. I will always be faithful, and my duties here will never conflict with my duties to Him, who has brought prosperity to this region. However, where the need to respect others arises in our Company, I will use the covenant I have with you as my guide. Amaunator has always been the Celestial Notary; he respects and endorses good and lawful contracts, and that is what we share.

===Rumblings of War===

\"I will now share one more thing with all of you. There have been many reports of escalation on the western front for nearly a year, and the word 'war' has been bandied about so much for so long that some are starting to doubt it. I can tell you that war has come to Daggerdale. Before the snows come this year, the western front will become a battlefield the likes of which none of us has seen. 

\"However, we will not fight in the west. We are assailed from many sides, and from within. The Dark Garden has planted a seed that has grown, and a harvest of blood within the Dale will soon be reaped. This will be our war, and it is upon us. We will continue to hunt down the Dark Garden, and undo their wickedness, and there isn't one among you that can be spared.

\"The White Cloaks will go forward as a company of men and women beholden first and foremost to each other. We go to face our own war, and we will need order and discipline as well as our bond to each other to see our job done.

\"Let all those now that will make this covenant with me and this company of White Cloaks now come forward and stand with me.\"

==The Pilgrim Speaks==

Banitt is the first to march forward past the assembled White Cloaks. He crosses from the back of the room where he stood beside Corran.

All are a little shocked to see that he carries his cloak in his hands rather than wear it.

He speaks in a broken voice.

\"I am afraid I cannot accept your terms, I am and always will be beholden first and foremost to my god. When he speaks to me, I follow his command, and no military chain of command can supersede that, especially one that does not always act with the ideals of the faith at the forefront. You should know better than to ask this, Lumina, being of the faith yourself. Why is it do you think that the castle army is divided into the Sun Blades and the Order of the Lambent Shield? Pious men care not for military orders that countermand their faith. They MUST believe they are following the will of their Gods. They accept commands only from those whose faith is more inspired than their own.\"

He turns to face the assembled men and women.

\"I know that my beliefs have been hard to accommodate, but even though it pains me to see the disapproval in your eyes, I cannot stray from my faith. I have made a vow, a vow to all the faithful in this land. I will do what I can to hunt down the Black Saint. I will do what I can to eliminate the threat of the dark garden. I will strive on my own if needs be to bring to justice those who would persecute this land. And I would truly love to have the aid of the White Cloaks, and I will give my life for any of you that aid in my reaching that goal. But we do it as individuals – each with their own devices and methods, each with their own path to follow, but each with this common goal. Each with an equal say, and yes, an equal reward to utilize as they see fit\"

He turns back to Lumina

\"If you are set on your little army then I respectfully resign my cloak\"

He holds the cloak out to Lumina, pain written on his face, and tears welling in his eyes.

==Altair Aghast==

A look of incredulity appeared on Corran's face. None of what Lumina had said had surprised him. He'd been around leaders in the past and it was not the first speech he had heard. What had surprised him was Banitt's reaction. He had heard Sergeant Warburton's' thoughts on what was going on with Banitt and although he didn't agree with it, what Banitt had just said did lend weight to what Warburton had said. Banitt appeared to be conflicted. He had no reason to doubt himself when it came to his piety but the damned blade that was on his back might have nothing at all to do with god, and more to do with political manoeuvring and ought else. 

He was not sitting like of some those present. One did not worship The Loyal Fury and oft find themselves resting on their hind-quarters. His voice was quiet, but firm as he pushed his way to the front and gently put his hand on Banitt’s wrist. \"Hold...\"

Corran had never been one for subtlety and he looked back over his shoulder to see the frown on Sergeant Warburton's face. He was not sure what the source of that frown was but for the moment, Warburton's discomfort was of little concern to him.

He spoke simply and earnestly, speaking like a man that cared little if he was in a room full of hard-bitten warriors, passionate zealots, or eager mercenaries. He spoke like it was simply him and his friend were alone in a room.

\"Banitt...this is folly. This is pride that drives you and I can understand that pride, but I need you to put that aside. We both know that you will not find The Black Saint on your own. In fact, it is entirely possible that the sergeant was accurate in his assessment. The Dark Garden has not the strength or the courage to enter a battle. It is quite likely that your Black Saint will be leading the charge. I cannot hold the line without you Banitt. You are needed. If your Black Saint is not at this upcoming battle then I will leave with you and we can find this person and put purpose to that blade upon your back.\" 

He stopped to look at the rest of the White Cloaks in the room before looking back to Banitt.

\"We are all different Banitt and we will not always agree with one another but we are all on the same side. We all want what's best for the people of Daggerdale. If you turn away now...if you turn your back on this when you are needed most, it is only moving all of us one step closer to being defeated by the Shadovar. Stay. Stay Banitt. Stay and fight.\" 

==The Pilgrim’s Passion==

Banitt turns to face Corran, the pain clear in his face.

\"You Corran, I would expect to understand more than any my conflict. I WILL STAY!\" He bellows this last sentiment and you are sure all those of the faith in the Dales heard his words, almost as if some power drove them. 

Banitt lowers his head struggles to calm himself... he shudders then lifts his head once more, the tears roll down his cheeks, but it is passion Corran sees, not woe.

\"I will fight at your side Corran, till the end of my days, short as they may be.\"

He turns to the assembled White Cloaks.. \"But if this is to be a military organization, with military rule, I will NOT wear the White Cloak\". I will stand at your sides, I will, as I have said, give my life for you. But I cannot accept the rule of any but my God. I will stand at your side as the Guardian of the Tomb, First Pilgrim... and friend if you will have me.\"

He turns back to Corran and whispers \"I am doing what you should have done all those years ago. A man of faith cannot accept military rule, the Sun Blades were your awakening. The White Cloaks are mine. Too many count on me, I cannot endure what you have.\"

He then turns to Lumina, and speaks in an almost stern voice... almost.

\"You try to make a name for yourself. You are 'Captain' by birthright in the dales, not by merit... Lumina. Have you forgotten your history? The White Saint EARNED his stature. He did not command it.\"

He barely pauses to take breath.

\"We do not need a military order. We all are here for the same purpose:  to eliminate the threat of Shar upon the realms. The White Cloaks should be seen to take action not due to a command, but due to a calling. Each of us has his or her own reason, but the action we take is directly in line with one another. If we wanted the structure of military command, well, I would be of the Order, and many of you would be Sun Blades. I believe that to silence any tongue is FOLLY! Let us be free to choose, and show that we will choose for the good of the Dales!\"

To Lumina he then whispers \"The trouble with military command is that the agents of Shar need only infiltrate the top level. Give each member, green or praised an equal say… and we need not fear that darkness can swell in our midst.\"

==Warburton Worries ==

Iolus was frowning for a number of reasons. Captain Whitegate did not agree with how he handled the situation involving the dragon. 

Really, he thought to himself. I presume you would have handled it differently? 

Unimpressed with how the Captain was handling this entire thing had him slowly shaking his head slightly from side to side. He was not exactly pleased to hear her undermining his decision while in the presence of people that he might very well need to command over the coming days. 

He was surprised to see Harbaugh openly question her right to leadership. She is as much a leader as Harbaugh is. Lumina is a captain through birthright and name and there is no reason beyond that to suggest she is worthy of the mantle of leadership. I was present for Gang'Dar. I witnessed what her leadership had wrought. I also witnessed her leading us blindly into the Shadowfell like children to the slaughter. I witnessed the aftermath of that and all of us are still dealing with those demons. She is doing the very best she can and it is barely adequate.

He was relieved when Altair stepped forward as he did not want to have to be the one to step forward and beg Harbaugh to stay with the White Cloaks. As often as Harbaugh's and his beliefs have been opposed of late, he agreed with Harbaugh in one respect. He did not know what the White Cloaks were. A clever idea from Lumina Whitegate, playing pretend soldier to impress her family. 

Iolus did not agree about Harbaugh's belief that everyone should have a choice. What everyone had to say mattered? That was clearly wrong but he was definitely beginning to doubt the efficacy of the White Cloaks as a tool of a failing administration.

There was clearly a need to speak to Harbaugh, but that conversation would be in private and no in front of the other members of the White Cloaks.

==The New Guy ==

Drake Sterling sits crossed legged on the floor, a little out of his element.

All this talk has him utterly confused. He IS a soldier. Do they really want his opinion? He hopes not... he just wants to be told \"that’s the line the enemy can't cross,” and woe to he who tries to cross that line. He will drag them down and beat them into submission like he did with so many childhood \"friends\" … and so many \"suitors\" looking for his sister’s charms … and so many \"tax collectors\" ready to take his home... 

He had no love of the blood... but the feeling of rendering someone helpless… priceless. 

Yup. Drake was pretty sure he was happy enough being told where to go... when to sleep... what to carry... his justice would be served up in the end.

He kept his mouth shut. Sure he imagined the \"captain\", the \"paladin\" and the \"pilgrim\" all writhing under his grasp, but he did that with everyone he looked upon. To him.. it was all the same... the line will be drawn... and someone – hopefully Drake Sterling – would be asked to allow none across.
