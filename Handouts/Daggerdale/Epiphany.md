\"I've had an ephiphany, neighbour,\" Eremus said jovially as he seated himself next to the trembling human's head. The elf leaned his imposing, freshly-stained axe against one knee and wiped his hands against his hide leggings. \"You don't mind me calling you neighbour, do you? How about brother? After all, when you think about it we're all one big family, every single one of us.\" He smiled at the thought. His companion didn't reply.

The elf glanced downwards. The foreman's face was very red, with tears or sweat or both leaving a disgusting sheen over his ruddy skin. Eremus wiped his own brow, noting the moisture, coughing slightly afterwards. The air was thick with smoke and heat. The avenger looked around himself at the mess they had made - the lumberyard was gripped in conflagration, irrevocably so. Several of the woodcutters had attempted to form a chain of some sort, passing buckets of water from the well to put out the flames, but it was very much a lost cause. Many of the round-ears had already fled, abandoning their livelihoods without so much as a second thought.

\"Cowards,\" he muttered, a momentary frown crossing his pointed face.

He looked again at the foreman. Hands and feet bound tight, the human had been laid with his head resting upon the stump of a once mighty oak. Eremus ran a finger along the breadth of the stump's edge. A wistful sigh escaped his lips.

\"Oh yes!\" He clapped his hands together, a smile reappearing on his face. \"My ephiphany. I wandered off there for a moment, my apologies.\"

The avenger cleared his throat, then spoke.

\"You see, Silvanus, Lord of All Nature, is a compassionate and temperate deity, understand? And since we are all His children, all creatures of nature born to this world, he cannot bear to inflict punishment unto us, even those who sorely deserve it. That's why we have to do the punishing for Him! It took me so long to figure it out, can you believe that? See, you and your kind, and all the other lesser races, you ravage the land. You take far more than you need and leave the rest to rot - in your wake lies naught but barren land and shattered lives, and His children are always the first to suffer under your gluttony. That's not okay. Things are not right, not right at all my brother.\"

Eremus was standing now, axe gripped tight in both his hands, staring down at the weeping man before him.

\"So that's my revelation. It took me so long to figure out why everything was so twisted in these parts. It is unbalanced, out of control. My people are content to sit back and watch their homes fray at the edges like cheap cloth, whittled down day by day. Silvanus refuses to raise a hand against the worshipers of his allies, and so it falls to us to do the punishing for Him. But I am not so cruel as to leave you without a chance to correct this mess you've made. True repentance is born of introspection, after all.\"

He nudged the foreman's head with the tip of his axe. Only a whimper left the human's rough, hairy face.

\"Turn your mind's eye inward and see the sins you have committed. The lives you have ended, the majestic trees that for so long had stood upon this soil, centuries of beauty cut short to panel your homes and hold your meals off the ground. Repent!\" Eremus shouted.

\"Damn you!\" the foreman cried, his voice harsh from the smoke.

\"Repent!\"

\"DAMN YOUR TREES TOO!\" the human shouted, his entire body shaking with uncontainable sobs.

\"Very well.\"

The foreman's neck parted under Eremus's axe with little resistance, blood spraying the air as the head rolled off the stump and into the grass nearby. Eremus gave the limp body a sharp kick, rolling it out of the way as he reached behind himself and drew the second human up into position. Carefully, the avenger placed the sub-foreman's head on the stump and sat down for a second time, turning his aside only a moment to spit out a gob of the human's blood that had splashed onto his lips. Placing the axe against his knee once more, he looked down at the human beside him and spoke with a calm smile on his face.

\"I've had an epiphany, neighbour.\"
