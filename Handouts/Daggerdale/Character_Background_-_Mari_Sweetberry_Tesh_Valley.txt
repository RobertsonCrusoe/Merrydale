Mari Sweetberry 
Here is some information on some halfling families in a fortified farm town called Tesh Valley. 
According to the 1482 census, Tesh Valley Farm has the 4th largest concentration of halflings in the dale, behind Dagger Falls, Tethyamar and Crossed Blades Farm. There are 4 families of halflings in Tesh Valley representing 68 (and more on the way) halflings. 
Tesh Valley Farm is one of the more remote farms in the dale, or at least it used to be; after the discovery of the East Gate of Tethyamar a few years ago, and the influx of dwarves to the area to reclaim their lost home, there is now quite a bit of traffic between the Falls and Tesh Valley. 
Of the 847 people on the census there for 1482, there are 608 humans, 144 dwarves, 68 halflings and 27 others (half elves, others). This is the third largest concentration of dwarves outside of Tethyamar and the Falls, which along with the halflings and others makes it one of the most diverse communities in the dale. 
This is the ancestral home of the Blackmane family, who are related to the Whitecloaks, and who still hold important positions in the farm and in the dale. 
There are 4 families of halflings at Tesh Valley, including Sweetberry's. These Sweetberry's are related to those at Thistle Ridge by way of the marriage of Otho Sweetberry (who moved from Thistle Ridge in 1398 to Tesh Valley) to Gilly Weatherbee of Tesh Valley (the Weatherbee's being one of the first halfling families in Tesh Valley). 
The current Matriarch of the Tesh Valley Sweetberry's is Lily Sweetberry, granddaughter of Otho and Gilly. She is also the Cultivar for the farm, an important religious and political figure. The family business is gardening and beekeeping, and they produce a Sweetberry Yellow, an acacia-honey that is a popular favourite throughout the dale, and a great treat for the dwarves. 
Since the discovery of the East Gate and the migration of Shield Dwarves back to the region, many have settled at Tesh Valley, and have built an impressive fort attached to the farm called 'Stationer's Hall', which serves as a trading post for the dwarves. 
With the announcement of the Reclamation, Lily has decided that she would like to lend her assistance, and find out what happened to her grandmother and other family members at Thistle Ridge. She had a cousin named Esmerelda that she remembers playing with on her one visit to the farm in 1439, when they were both young girls. She often dreams of Esmerelda, though she won't discuss the dreams with anyone. 
Lily is also interested in finding out if the gardens and fields of Thistle Ridge are still as productive as they were. The acacia that grows in your gardens in Tesh Valley was actually brought from Thistle Ridge with Otho years ago. 
I'm happy for you to figure out exactly how you fit into the picture here, and what you hope to achieve. If you want to be a Sweetberry, a Weatherbee or any other family related (or not) to the Thistle Ridge family, all good.
