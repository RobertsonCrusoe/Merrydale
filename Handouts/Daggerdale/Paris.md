A character that didn't get as much play as he should have. This was probably the character involved in the [[Conversation at the Thirsty Throat]] during Harvestide of 1371.

== Character Concept ==

Which in turn, brings me to my old watchman. Blacky, hit it right on the head. This is a character I've been wanting to play for years, but have never felt the opportunity was right. This may be it. What does it have to do with potions of healing? Well, I'd always thought that watchmen, especially the Sergeants or Captains, would have a potion of healing or two as part of their standard equipment when on duty for emergencies. And I figured, you know, sometimes the beats are boring, and no one is pulling out their wands of paralysation on anyone else, and there are no virgins on unicorns riding through High Street, and the dragon didn't show up to unleash its terrible wrath, and the Zhents and Drow and other Zhents stayed home today, and well, its damn hot in that armour, and the leather is rotting and starting to get full of bugs and mould, and, well, the water's not fit for a pig to piss in, and they raised the price of a half pint over at the Dripping Dagger cause of the latest rounds of impromptu renovations, and, well, there's this nice, if lukewarm, bottle of perfectly drinkable liquid right there in this pouch, and so what's the damn harm in just sitting down for a bit in the shade to rest the old feet, and having a drink?

Like I said, I've had this watchman in my head for years. The whole concept has changed over time but the latest version is a guy who became a cop when he was 16, served his 20 years on the force, most of it, like with most cops, sitting on his arse. Well, he's done his 20 years, and has been pensioned off. After about 6 years of retirement, he realises he's still a healthy and able man, and, as she has no ties to bind him, decides to set off and see a bit of the world.

I've got pages and pages of backstory for him. Every time I hear someone tell a good old man story ("Back in my day..." or "When I was a young man...") I write it down as part of this guy's past. He's a no-nonsense old copper who knows what the good things in life are (a bit of shade and a cool drink on an afternoon when no one is trying to kill anyone else) and knows how to fight properly (kick him in the nuts, bring him to the ground, and gouge out his eyes while you bite off his ear.) Yup, that supposedly civilised "dungeon fightin'" with your fancy long swords and posh shields ain't for the likes of him. Out on the beat, a sword's gonna do you no good when the alley's only 6 feet wide. And that damn shield's just gonna get in the way when that scumbag's got you on your back and he's going for your throat. Never mind the fancy plate armour that'll keep you from getting up while the bastard knifes some little girl as he makes his escape. No sir, the pretty-boy paladins can keep their swords and shields and armour and can go fight the damn dragons with their honour and privilege in a nice open field with plenty of room to manoeuvre. Meanwhile, the real work has to get done.
