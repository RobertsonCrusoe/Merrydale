== Kythorn 1372 to Alturiak 1373 - The Tarnished Jewel ==

Winter is always cruel in Waterdeep, and for the most part, the City goes into a sort of slumber, relative to the amount of activity it sees in other seasons of the year. This general rule used to apply to the underworld of the City of Splendours, but this winter was different.

Starting in Kythorn of the previous summer, the City had changed drastically, and for the worse. For one reason or another, criminal organizations began to crop up everywhere. Gangs formed to inflict extortion on business and merchants. Thievery hit such a peak that the Shadow Thieves of old are not known to have inflicted so much fear and loss in their hay-day. People started to vanish from the streets, and some turned up dead. It seemed like the City was being overrun completely by the gangs. Bands of wererats and other monsters wandered the streets.

Battles on the streets of the City between these gangs, the Watch, and bystanders became frequent. As gangs expanded to take over new ventures and new territory, they locked horns and the results were often quite devastating.

=== Year of the Unseen Army ===

With all these things happening, Garth thought it would be a great time to start his networks. However, the opposite proved true. With all the violence and suspicion, and disputes over territory, he discovered that his Jack Dashers were almost always injured, and many of the Rooken Network vanished in the line of duty. With the help of many of the friends he had made in the past, he was able to keep his operation afloat, but the year was not profitable. Not in a monetary sense at any rate. The Rooken network did build a tremendous Street Encyclopedia for Waterdeep, but at great cost.

Among the many things that they did turn up, one particular find was most noteworthy. They tracked down a man named Jacob Fink, who was operating a catalogue business for ‘adventuring and specialty tools’. When a meeting was arranged between Munk Applet and this trader, the two men had a very awkward moment of realization. Jacob, in fact, was Nero, an old associate of Garth’s. Garth was quite interested to know what had happened to him down in Undermountain.

=== Nero's Tale ===

Nero's tale was neither long nor pretty. He stated simply that Quevin and Quellar came into the dungeon, and gave the spelunkers a sound beating. When things did seem like they were going to go their way, Steele turned on his companions! He lashed out at the Huntmaster, who managed to get away. The drow hunter, Lotharil, was down bleeding but not dead as well. At that point, Nero ran. He did hear Quevin say to Steele “you made the right choice”. Many days later he emerged from Undermountain after a series of horrifying experiences, and he retired from adventuring.

He began a life as a catalogue merchant with no discernable place of business besides this restaurant or that. Business has been excellent, he stated, but you have to be more careful than ever. Many of the ‘old rules’ don’t apply any more, with the criminal climate being what it is. He also confided in Garth that he had learned that Force Grey had been either disbanded or destroyed, and that this was one of the major reasons for increase in crime in the City. Further, the coffers in the City were not what they used to be, and many members of the Guard were not getting their pay, which lead to corruption in the few men who did stay on as members of the Guard. Things were really bad, but Nero was profiting from it, and keeping himself neutral in the affairs of the underworld of the City.

An important footnote to the delving of the Garth and his network is that he established a very good rapport with Loremaster Kennyth Thames at the Font of Knowledge. Kennyth ‘can see through a brick wall’ it is said, and the portly Oghman did what he could to help Garth in his endeavours, knowing what side he played for. On top of a wealth of information, he also provided him with a tome of great power. This magical book, a Manual of Quickness of Action, proved to hone Garth’s dexterity, making him more survivable in his increasingly dangerous job.

=== The Boy Prodigy ===
thumb|Beljuril Cornerstone, High Protector of Practioner's Tower
The tale of Beljuril’s return to Waterdeep is a short one. When he returned to the City, he found a host of Priests of Azuth at his modest temple. They had been sent from many places, including Halruua, Tethyr and Rashemen, in order to fortify the position of Azuth in what is clearly becoming a city of mages. Beljuril was duly thanked for his efforts in spreading the faith, and he became involved in building a new temple. It was decided that they would build right over the location of the old temple (formerly Reprobate HQ). They purchased a good deal of land and street around the old building from the City (which was happy of the infusion of gold) and constructed a 10 story, pentagonal tower right in the heart of the City. This tower was named Practioner's Tower, and membership in the faith grew rapidly. Beljuril’s magics were heavily relied upon, and his role in the Tale of the Beholder Tablets diminished for some time, while his obligation to the Church took up his time. Further, he began to fabricate the gear he would need for next summer.

He did not drop out of the story altogether, though. He began an intensive divination process, trying to find any information about Jafar and the Beholder Staves. This, of course, turned up very little. However, he began to help out Garth in his exploits, and this turned up valuable information about the drow and the Zhents in the City.

=== High Protector of Azuth ===
100px|left
On Alturiak 15, Practioner’s Tower was completed. In the first ceremony in the completed Tower, the head of the Church announced Beljuril as founder of the faith in Waterdeep, and knighted him High Protector of Practitioner’s Tower. This title is not recognized anywhere else in the City of course, and it does have some ceremonial duties that detract from his pursuit of the Zhents, the Beholder Staves and Jafar, but it does have some benefits.

The first, and most tangible benefit is his badge of office, which he keeps as long as he is faithful in his duties. This badge is a magical Ring of Spell Storing that is reported to have been fabricated by Azuth himself, when he walked the Realms.

Further, the library in the Tower is growing quickly, and will no doubt outstrip that of the Watchful Order at some point. This has caused a little tension between these two organizations. The clergy are not trying to compromise any interests of the Watchful Order, but because of the nature of Azuth, the availability of spells has increased dramatically, while their costs have decreased.

Be that as it may, these two organizations continue to have a peaceful relationship, despite the present turmoil of the City.

=== Tidings from the East ===

One of Beljuril’s most important roles in the Story, as it picks up again, is communication. He has been able to communicate directly with Sparrow in Dagger Falls, and May in Neverwinter. He has learned that things are good in those two places, and that despite the clear and utter failure of the Waterdeep Team, the others did what they were supposed to. Further, he has been watching the progress of the Company of the Eastern Storm, and has arranged for Garth and himself to meet them a day outside of the City, in the small town of Rassalantar.

== 30 Tarsakh, 1373 - Return to Rassalantar ==

Sure enough, on the 29th, a storm blew all night that kept half the citizens of the village awake all night. It was known that the Eastern Storm was coming, and it was rumoured that the two men staying at the Inn were waiting for them.

Early in the morning of the 30th, the storm peaked as three riders and one runner came into town at full pace. They dismounted and entered a small tavern that a couple of them had been at just a year before, when the world was a much different place. The numerous guests (caravanners and other travelers) eating breakfast in the tavern that morning recall that the doors seemed to be blasted open, and everyone was startled and frightened. They recall that a great figure stood in the door, the wind blowing a great golden cloak of fur all about him. In he marched, and behind him three others came, all just as terrible as the first. None could speak or avert their gaze while these four men, Heroes of recent Legend, sat down to breakfast in that very tavern.

It was here that the Heroes were rejoined with Garth and Beljuril, and it took hours for them to exchange tales. The news that Garth brought from the City was most disturbing to the Eastern Storm, but vows were quickly said, and plans began to unfold.

=== An Anniversary at Roarke's ===

Before noon, the band of six Heroes was ready to depart, and shortly after nightfall, they arrived just north of Waterdeep, at the crossroads where the High Road and the Long Road fork, where Roarke’s Tavern marks the last bit of civilization for a good distance.

Roarke’s is an impressive place for a tavern. It was an old cathedral to some forgotten god, and the place still has the feel of a large and ominous canonized building. The vaulted ceilings and rows of thick buttresses reveal the original reason for this most venerable building. But, whoever it was built to venerate is forgotten, and the vaulted ceilings are now choked with smoke from scores of pipes. The pews are all gone, and in their stead are dozens of tables, which, this time of year, are all packed full of the myriad forms of those that use the road. It is a noisy place, and lively. It is not, however, rowdy or bawdy, like the festhalls of the Dock or Trades' Ward, nor is it prim and snooty like the gilded gentlemen’s clubs of North Ward. This is an honest place, filled by the earthy many that have the wisdom of the road.

=== Deputies of the Black Staff ===

frame|Khelben Arunsun expresses his concern over the rot that has set into the City of Splendours.

The Heroes were immediately shown to a private room in one corner of the cyclopean structure: a fortified tower that had been turned into a private dining room. There, they met with none other than Khelben Arunsun the Blackstaff, accompanied by Kennyth Thames, Rychel Voordan and Rychel's former protegé, Nero Agundar. Khelben explained that things in the City were as bad as he has ever seen them, and requested the help of the six men at the table. He adorned them all with badges, which would provide them with some freedom in the City. While not completely above the law, the badges would allow the Heroes some freedom from corrupt Watchmen and Magistrates, and provide a legal means (for those who need them) to kick down the appropriate doors and kick the appropriate behinds. Further, he pursed the Heroes with 20,000 to help pay for what would certainly become an expensive endeavour.

He made specific references to the Company Aldebarran, and insisted that it be brought to heel. He also mentioned the Company Dalael, now fully infiltrated into the Anteos family, and though he had no solid evidence against, thought them villainous all the same.

The Blackstaff took his leave early, explaining that he had matters to attend to. The Heroes stayed on, eating the sumptuous meal paid for by Khelben, and discussed goals and plans.
