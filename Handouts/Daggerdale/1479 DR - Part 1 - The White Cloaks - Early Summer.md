A summary of the significant events of the Summer of 1479 DR

In the early summer of 1479, a company of capable men and women came together that would eventually be known as The White Cloaks. The company was founded by Lumina Whitegate (granddaughter to Zenith Sunlord Argent Whitegate), Dorian Dale, Tuugan Swift and Alystair Dretch.

The group travelled to Castle Radiance together, and decided to band together to start taking bounties.  Their first assignment bonded them forever, and added to their numbers.  They were to make for a bridge a day away from the Castle that had been raided by orcs.

Arriving at the bridge the next day, they found that the place had been hit hard and fast by orcs.  There were actually two bridges here; an old wooden one used to cross a small canyon and river bed, and the new stone one being built by a group of masons under the leadership of a hired master builder named Endle.  The site had been attacked two days ago, and though the guards were found dead, none of the masons were to be found.  Most of the supplies had been taken away as well.  However, a small band of rear-guard orcs were encountered, and easily scattered by the heroes.

The group tracked the bandits back to a temporary camp where Endle and his team were staying, down in the canyon a mile upstream.  There, they found a scene of nightmare and ruin; the orcs had come and had done terrible things to those that resisted them, and hauled away more prisoners and supplies.  Here again, another small band of rear-guard was encountered, and with some little effort they were dispatched and hunted down mercilessly to answer for their grisly crimes here.

It was not difficult to track the orcs host and the driven slaves;  they were many, heavy-laden, and being pursued by Tuugan Swift, who misses no track.  The track led out of the mountains and into the foothills to the east.  They lead to a new fortress called Gang’Dar which was mostly a wooden palisade and wooden towers, but it was found to be covered in scaffolding and labouring mason-slaves who were reinforcing the walls.  And it was full of orcs; perhaps a couple hundred.

==Assault on Gang’Dar/New Friends==
The heroes stormed the palisade with little hesitation (or planning) and found themselves in a very desperate fight for their lives, trapped in the bailey of the palisade, surrounded by orcs.  Perhaps by chance, perhaps by fate, the heroes retreated into one of the wooden towers where they found some more slaves bound up, including Iolus Warburton, whom Dorian and Tuugan recognized almost immediately; he had been stationed with the bridge defenders, and they shared a few words when they passed by on the way to the castle over a week previously.  Dorian looked about for a face he had met only briefly, but which had haunted him ever since the meeting on the bridge; Troia Trustar, the priestess of Sune, who had looked at him warmly while she hummed the loveliest tune.  She said very little to the stout-hearted warrior, but the memory of her burned in his heart like a flaming brand.  But she was not here.

When Dorian desperately asked about her of Iolus as he cut his bonds, Iolus only shook his head, and said “she has been taken to the keep , to...entertain...the orc chieftain here...”

Little did anyone in the wooden tower realize that Troia was already fighting her way free at the top of the keep, taking advantage of the chaos and confusion that had been caused by the storming of the palisade by the heroes.  She managed to free herself from her bonds, and kill the few orcs barring her way out; however, she had only two ways to get out; down through many infested layers of the tower and a complicated underground warren, or straight up, and onto the ramparts of the keep.  This she did, and at the very moment that she mounted the ramparts, the heroes emerged from their tower to redouble their assault, this time with Iolus and some freed masons joining the fray.

Troia found the ramparts covered in orcs, but she had the surprise; there were too many to fight, but she decided she would risk a jump, and ran and leapt from the keep, across midair through the bailey, and to the roof of the wooden tower in which the heroes were now desperately fighting their way out of.  Stark naked, bleeding from a score of cruel depredations and torments, she flew through the air with a grace and dignity that is unexplainable if not witnessed.  She landed in the midst of the heroes, Dorian helping her to her feet after her crash-landing on the roof.  He gave her his cloak, and the lot of them continued the assault on the fortress.

By the end of nearly an hour of fighting, most of the orcs were routed, but many had fled to the safety of the keep, where they were mercilessly hunted down by the heroes.  However, insane with vengeance for the misdeeds visited upon Troia, Dorian continued to lead the charge up and up each new level, taking no time to pause.  At the very top level, his energy waned and his desperate foes knew they must fight for their lives, and so it was that Dorian swung his blade for the last time.  His allies finished the assault, but he lay dead on the ramparts of the keep as the first rays of morning crept over the hills.

The group returned to the Castle with scores of freed slaves after burning out the keep.  They were rewarded and praised for their courage, and took the name ‘White Cloaks’, formally registering their company which now included Troia and Iolus.

==The Bloody Eye==
The White Cloaks mourned the loss of Dorian Dale but briefly before they took up their next bounty.  This time, they were bound for the interior of the Dale, to Crossed Blades Farm where a problem with the denizens of the Dagger Hills were causing trouble for the first time in decades.

On the Mountain Road just a few miles before Foothills Farm, the White Cloaks were ambushed and nearly killed by a very skilled group of bandits known as The Bloody Eye.  A deadly rogue nearly ended Alystair's life almost immediately when the ambush was struck, but the group managed to fight off the bandits before any more of their company hit the ground.  They were then found by a Warden from Deep Cormanthor named Malvau.  This wood elf agreed to help the White Cloaks if they would help him; he was hunting The Bloody Eye; a mission he had been on for months, and which only recently turned sour when his companions were all ambushed when they finally closed in on their quarry, leaving only him alive, and with little hope of finishing his mission.

The Bloody Eye would have to wait, though. A couple of days later, the White Cloaks and Malvau found themselves at Crossed Blades Farm, and uncovered a very disturbing alliance.

==Here, in Daggerdale?  Does the Zenith Sunlord Know?==
The bounty which brought the White Cloaks to this farm involved an investigation into the sighting of a werewolf inside the walls of the farm.  A patrol of Sun Blades spotted the enormous creature as it leaped easily over the 15' walls, snatched up several sheep, and made an easy escape.  The soldiers had told the local magistrate, Magistrate Madrick, who dispatched a message via carrier pigeon to the Castle.

When the White Cloaks arrived in town, they didn't find much out of the ordinary.  The people didn't seem too concerned, though the garrison and magistrate Madrick were visibly troubled by the recent sighting of the werewolf.  Before long, the White Cloaks found themselves lodged in an abandoned but cosy cottage at the edge of the compound, where they were given some information by an man named Otis Fairfield.  This town elder provided them with the accommodations and provided his nephew, Davis, as a guide and servant for their stay at the farm.

When asked about the werewolf, Otis didn't seem too concerned.  He said little at first, but warmed to Troia and he gave a weak laugh and said \"Oh, one that big must have been Braxus his'elf.  I ain't seen ol'Braxus in, well, I dunno...decades now anyhow.  But I don't tend to the old compact anymore.  My nephew now takes care of that, and I take care of my garden, which presently has a terrible beetle infestation that worries more than werewolves, and so I beg your leave.\"

Davis had to be pressed hard about it, but eventually explained the 'old compact'.  This was a nearly 100-year old agreement between the Silver Tail Clan of werewolves from the Dagger Hills; they ensured that the more malevolent elements of the hills steered clear of the farm, in exchange for 2 sheep every full moon.  For decades the Fairfield family had maintained this compact, keeping it secret from the Church of Amaunator.  Until the sighting of Braxus himself, the current leader of the Silver Tails, the local garrison and Church (of Amaunator; the Chaunteans themselves established this pact) had never seen a lycanthrope either within or without the walls.  Two things changed recently, however; Braxus himself came instead of one of his younger clan members, and he was 2 weeks early.  Something was amiss.

With this information, the White Cloaks headed into the hills.  There, they encountered Braxus and what remained of his clan; just a couple of females and some timber wolves.  The White Cloaks were able to subdue Braxus through some force and some diplomacy, though his spirit seemed broken before the battle even began.  He explained that a few nights ago his cave was somehow attacked by mysterious men who caught the whole clan off guard.  They slaughtered most of the women, young men and children, and by the time Braxus came back to the cave to defend his people, the attackers vanished.

With his clan nearly annihilated, he knew he would not be able to defend himself from rival clans, so he approached something called The Old Man in the Hill that has some sort of overarching authority over the lycanthropes of the Dagger Hills.  He was going to ask for help with his situation, but the Old Man made it worse; he demanded his monthly tribute 2 weeks early for the disruption, threatening that if he didn't get it immediately, he would notify the closest rivals, the Hollow Hills Clan of his weakness.

The White Cloaks seemed interested most in the mysterious attackers of the Silver Tail den, Alystair believing that they were Shadovar, based on the description of the attack.  They vowed to check into the cave and see if they could help Braxus, making him promise to upkeep the Old Compact with the farm.  Braxus agreed, and brought them to the cave.

==Heart of Darkness==
thumb|450px|The Shadow inside the Shadow
Inside the cave was a scene of wanton carnage and torment.  Werewolves of every age were strewn about the place, and even though they were creatures of terrible legend, the civilized discussion the Cloaks had with Braxus, and the horrible sight of mothers laying dead next to their children was enough to invoke the rage of the heroes, Alystair not the least; he dipped his leather armour into the blood of the infants, and vowed vengeance.

Searching the deepest extent of the cave, the heroes finally came to an anomaly.  One passage of the cave seemed to swallow the very darkness itself, and the White Cloaks were reluctant, some even terrified, to enter deeper.  However, when Lumina brought the radiance of the sun into the cave, they took heart and pressed on into the gloom.

They had entered the Shadowfell.  The world changed to a shadow of itself; dark and dreary, void of colour or hope; it became a depressing and gloaming negative image of itself, as the White Cloaks rounded a section of cave that lead back into the very den they were just in; this den was the Shadowfell copy, though; there were no dead pups or mothers, no other signs of werewolves.  Just the cave, empty save for the omnipresent dread and hopelessness.  It was in this shadow that shadow itself struck out; three Netherese Shadovar assassins attacked the White Cloaks in a confusing melee of swirling shadows and vanishing assassins.  However, the strength of the White Cloaks prevailed, and the light of Amaunator burned like a beacon in that Shadowfell cave, and the three assassins were all killed.

The party searched the cave, and found light expedition gear for four people, including a Netherese map of the Shadowfell of the immediate area.  They stepped outside the cave briefly, to take in this corrupted image of their world, but the dread took hold again, and they could not press on much past the mouth of the cave.  They turned back, retracing their steps and arrived back in the cave in their own world.  They went outside to search for Braxus and tell him their findings.

==Hollow Hills Clan Smells Blood==
In the twilight of evening, in the New Cormanthor of the Dagger Hills, the party met the Hollow Hills Clan.  They were expecting Braxus to find them again; an easy task for the former master of this part of the hills, so they were not wary enough when a pack of large wolves came through the trees towards them.  They did not notice that the largest wolf had black fur instead of grey and silver, and that he was not quite as big as Braxus, but the fatigue of the battle with the Shadovar and the dim light was against them.

The Hollow Hills Clan had actually encircled them, and by the time they realized it wasn't Braxus, the battle was begun, and wolves and werewolves came at them from every direction.  The numbers and power of the monsters was more than a match for the weary heroes, and they would have been a meal for this pack if Braxus and his remaining mates had not come to their aid.  The Hollow Hills Clan broke off the attack as soon as the Silver Tails arrived, and a parley between the two pack leaders ensued.  None of the White Cloaks could understand the strange language of the preternatural monsters, but the message was clear; Braxus was defeated.  The rest of his mates left him on the spot to join the Hollow Hills Clan, and Braxus and the White Cloaks had to leave immediately.

As they made their way quickly out of the hills, back towards the farm, Braxus said \"they will not honour the Old Compact, but I think the dalesmen have bigger problems now anyway.  I smell one of those...things...up ahead.  I have been following it's stench for a mile now, and you will soon overtake it if you head directly towards the farm.  Kill it or die trying, I care not.\"  He was about to take off, but Troia stopped him with her musical words, and soothed his bitter rage, if only for a second.  She told him of a place he could make his own, and rebuild his clan.  She told him of the ruin of Gang'dar, and said she'd be happy if he stayed there to keep orcs out, if nothing else, and promised to visit with him once in a while.  The dour old wolf grumbled some sort of agreement, and bolted off.

==Hunters and Hunted==
At the very edge of the cultivated fields of the farm, about a mile or so away from the walled compound, the White Cloaks were discovered by the fourth of the Shadovar assassins, like Braxus had predicted.  This one, however, had a number of strange shadow-hounds with it, and in the dark night of this encounter, it was a difficult battle. However, the heroes had the benefit of foreknowledge of the attack thanks to Braxus, and were prepared.  They managed to kill the beast-master assassin and his hounds, accounting for all four of the assassins from the Shadowfell.

With the proximity and direct route to the farm that this Shadovar had taken, and with the only-partially complete map of the local Shadowfell (one of the packs contained alien implements of cartography), it became clear that this was some sort of scouting mission, and that the Netherese had found something that they needed; a back door into Daggerdale.

==Indecision at the Monastery==
Returning immediately to the magistrate at the monastery, the White Cloaks disclosed their terrible findings about the Shadovar and the cave.  They glossed over the bit about the wolves and the Old Compact (though Troia informed Davis and Otis so that they could figure out how to proceed).  Iolus and Tuugan became impatient with the magistrate who, true to form, sent off messages via carrier pigeon to his superiors to await his orders.  The guard was increased, the compound put on watch, and so forth.  However, the White Cloaks knew that was not going to be a problem from Shadovar for a while; the party they had wiped out seemed provisioned to stay in the field for quite a while, and it was unlikely that the Netherese could exploit this situation for a little while at least.  However, they insisted that they seize the initiative and set up defenses at the cave, or at least collapse the cave to block it.

Magistrate Madrick was incapable of making a decision to commit resources to the problem, however, without the proper orders.  Such is the rigid nature of the armed forces of Daggerdale.  There was some bickering about this in a meeting with the town elders, the officers of the garrison and the magistrate, but finally, Otis himself, long known as a devoted follower of Chauntea and cold towards the bureaucracy of Amaunator, admitted \"Madrick is right to take his course; Amaunator has brought some peace to this dale by showing up at the expected time every day, and not diverging in his intractable course.  We will abide the command of the agents of the Sun God in this crisis, though my heart too would have me marching into the hills.  We'll wait and see...\"  With the words of this elder, the discourse was closed.

The White Cloaks returned to their cottage to dry out their gear and recover from the wounds they had suffered in the hills.  They had an obligation to help out the Warden, Malvau, which they would see to next.
