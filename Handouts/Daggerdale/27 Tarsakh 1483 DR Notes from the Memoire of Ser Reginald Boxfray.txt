27 Tarsakh, 1483 DR – Notes from the Memoire of Ser Reginald Boxfray 
Then came the morning of the 27th of Tarsakh; a very light but very late snowfall gave a nice contrast to the budding trees and forest floor, but most of all to the smell of fresh bread.  Yes, indeed!  The bread oven in what is now Ambershields Monastery was fired up for the first time this morning, and the warm smell filled our lungs and hearts with joy and optimism.  For here we are, at the wild edge of civilization, trying to carve an existence out of a forest that seems determined to repel us.  And this morning we ate fresh bread! 
I no longer use the term 'camp' for our settlement.  The carpenters and masons stood up the walls of the central farmhouse quickly, and there is already a shingled roof over it all.  With 2 floors, we have more than enough room to move in all the soldiers and supplies, and are grateful to be out of the elements, fine though this spring has been.  We are now calling this 'New Town Hall'. 

The settlers that came last week have proven incredibly industrious, especially compared to my soldiers.  Without the need to keep one eye on the treeline for trouble, trusting that the Free Defense League has our affairs in order, the five families that have settled here have made astonishing progress; they all banded together to clear the rubble out of the old foundations of a collection of buildings, and with direction from Billy Mason and John Carpenter, they have stood up stone walls on the first floor, wooden walls complete with exterior plaster on the second, and a finished roof of slate shingles.  They have, as I have already bragged, lit the old bread oven, and are now clearing the old foundations of several other buildings around the farm. 
It is worth noting our numbers on this late Tarsakh morning.  We are the following; 
 Free Defense League 14th Company 1st Platoon Dawnshields (20 men and myself) 

We are 58 souls and counting. 
Mayor Winters and the elders from Gryphons Farm allocated a squad of Sunblades and a crew of men to clear the old road as far as Greenwood, and we now have regular visits daily from that settlement.  Mostly soldiers, but just yesterday a group of over 20 women, young and old, Chaunteans all, came to bless our farm.  They brought a bounty with them, including the flour needed to make the bread which I have just watched my men enjoy this morning.  It has taken less than a tenday to establish this route, and the small buildings we have here.  I am eager to see what a month or a season brings! 
Of the enemy we have seen very little.  We scout only a half-mile from the farm, and there are some thickets that I know the men avoid.  We have chased off some very small scouting parties of goblins, but haven't seen a single one in a few days.  We have, however, run into our Fey allies who seem to be watching our border to the north.  We suspect they have been playing mischief with the goblins, confounding and harrying them, if not outright attacking them, in their way.  We are grateful for the ties that the Captain made with the Limestone Court.  The cost of a quiet northern border has been a few jars of honey and jam up until today.  As I write this, a few of the sprites have turned up, attracted by the smell of the bakery, and just now they are chasing a few of the children (yes, children!) around the monastery, who wave buttered rolls teasingly at the small fey.  It is a distraction from our work, but it is also the experience of living that we have all come here to build.  Even those of us that have been paid gold to fight and deliver it. 
28 Tarsakh, 1483 – Post Shadowvar Battle at the Temple of Jannath 
As Jeska and RumTum return to the temple after their brief, ruthless and inescapable hunt for the three escaped goblins, Abbot Ambershields seems to be recovering from the ordeal. 
"Though His strength courses through me, I am no warrior, noble though my heart may strive to be," the Abbot began, making a humble attempt to straighten his tattered habit.  His voice, normally deep and calm, his thick Cormyrean accent lending dignity and gravitas, is somewhat quickened and heavy from the exhilaration and terror of battle.  "In truth, I was sore afraid, and sorely tested—for the Shadow crept close, and I did feel it weigh heavy upon my spirit, just as its blades rent my habit and flesh. I know not how such horrors are met by your company with such steadfast resolve. Yet I am grateful beyond measure. Grateful for your trust, for your sword-arms, and for the light you carried with me into this accursed place. It was my honour to bathe this ground, and our own rent flesh, in His radiant grace." 
Ambershields' hands and legs tremble a bit with the post-battle energy that most of you have forgotten about.  RumTum nuzzles up against the man, who strokes the shaggy back of the beast with a gentle hand. 
"A most noble hound," he says with a soft laugh, "the truest champion of our deliverance from the Shadow." He kneels, his arms encircling RumTum with a warmth born of deep affection, the dog replying with a joyous tail that wags the whole of his stout frame. "I knew not that such dogs walked this world. Surely he is gods-sent. Guard him well, and treasure him—for he is a blessing upon your road." 
With this, he stands and returns to preparing for the consecration, now with a bit more haste.  He is clearly still in an elevated state from the fight, slightly clumsy and jarred.  When Mari offers a steady hand to help him set up, he returns a very warm smile and a hand on her shoulder.   
"Lady, my heart is lifted by thy courage. I am in thy debt—not only for thy aid, but for thy example. I had not thought the smallfolk capable of such towering spirit. When the Shadow fell upon us, thou didst not flinch, nor falter. Thine heart is unblemished, thy courage like unto the greatest knights and paladins of old. To serve beside thee is an honour I shall not forget." 
The ritual takes nearly an hour to complete, and it is a time of great tension.  Though you have hunted the last of the Shadowvar and their tainted-goblin minions, you are not sure that you are safe here.  There is a lingering sense of dread, and only RumTum seems to be at peace, as he lays contentedly by the kneeling priest during the protracted incantation.  The little chapel is filled with candles and smoking pots with exotic incense that fills it with a sweet and slightly acrid smoke.  In advance of the ritual, Ambershields casts a powerful light spell to illuminate the entire place, which affords you the light and time to make a thorough investigation. 

Your investigation turns up very little, though you now see the connections that others made on your behalf; the Sembian details in the architecture are now clear, and the statues, which you examine as close as possible for nearly an hour, are unremarkable aside from their depiction of the Crone, Mother and Child that you all now perceive in a different light. 
Lucan is visibly frustrated with the investigation when the ritual concludes.  He has turned over every stone, tried to move the statues, tapped at every flagstone on the floor.  "There's not a damned thing here" he concludes quietly to himself, then looking to Jasper.  Jasper shrugs in abdication, making Lucan even more angry.  "Nothing, Jas?!" he now says louder and with some anger in his voice.  "We have been ambushed here twice, today by Netherese assassins, and there is nothing here?!  That makes no sense!" 
Ambershields then closes up the book he has been reading the long prayer from, and stands.  "It is done" he declares to the group.  A moment of breathless silence by everyone is broken by Lucan. "Really?  Really?!  What is done?!  It doesn't look or feel any different to ME, aside from the smoke.  Tell me what we're doing here" 
"We defeated the Shadow, my son," the Abbot replies, gently but firmly, "by standing where it seeks to claim dominion and saying with full hearts: 'Thou shalt not take root here.' I sorrow that no hidden truth lies beneath these stones to ease thy burden. Yet the victory is no less for being unseen. This place was tainted; it is now sanctified. The evil fled before the light. That is enough. We have not failed." 
"Come now. Let us quit this cold sanctuary. Greenwood Farm awaits—with bread still warm from the oven, with milk and honey, and hearts unburdened. The temple stands consecrated... but the hearth is where we heal."
