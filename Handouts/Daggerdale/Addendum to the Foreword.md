DDC - Addendum to the Forward 
Wherein your humble author shares a conversation at the Old Skull Inn, in Shadowdale with three venerable gentlemen. 
GFD Notes: 
I'll grant you that this is an unusual sort of entry for a book like this, but I thought it captured perfectly the essence of the dales. This is a conversation I had directed at me in the taproom of the Old Skull Inn, which is on the main road in Shadowdale. The caravan I had hitched a ride with from Cormyr was stopping here for the night, before keeping on to Liberty Hill, and I had a chance to sit and write with a full mug of beer. 

I couldn't help but sit and listen to the locals. Their regional accent was so strong I found it hard to understand them, but when they started talking about Daggerdale, I was obliged to listen; these were local men with views I'd never heard before and were my first real impressions of what Daggerdale and the dales are really about. And they were happy to share a table, a mug, and a story with me. 
Please enjoy, fair reader! 
-GFD 
"Just plain un-dales-like, I says" is what I must have heard a dozen times in the tap room at the Old Skull Inn after my caravan-master announced that we were on our way to Daggerdale. Sitting at a table with three local septuagenarians, I was given a colourful accounting of the history and character of Daggerdale. 

"Those folk up there just aren't right," one old codger named Harald was remarking, when he was interrupted by one of his two cronies, Zeb. 

"Never damned have been, what's more! It's all 'Sun God keep you' and 'Sun God damn you' just nows, but it weren't long ago but half of 'em worshipped the dog-humping shadow bitch herself! They's just damneddamneddamnedI dunno" 

The third old man, Diff, chimed in, filling in for his stammering comrade "extreme" he said slowly and with a pointing finger and squinting eyes. 

Zeb nodded assent and continued his line of thought. "Extreme. That's what I was saying. Don't know how to takes just a bits of this, and a bits of that. Always haves to jump in head damned first, whethers it's damned good or damned evil, damned right or damned wrong. Where's else could you finds the sodding Zhents dugs in for so many decades" 

"Or vampires! That's what started it all, see" Harald exclaimed. "In my day, they called it Sorrow Dale, for all the misery that was clutching the place" Harald was saying when Zeb cut him off. 

"Oh dammit, Harald! They's never damned called it damned Sorrow Dale or damned Misery Dale or goddamned Damned Dale, and nuthin' else you says they dids, not in your damned day! She's been Dagger damned dale the whole damned time. But yers right about the vampires. That was in my great, great, great grand daddy's day, and he used to say the damned blood suckers sucked the, sucked thesucked thesucked theI dunno" 

Diff squinted his eyes and wagged an accusing finger at Zeb, and slowly said "soulsucked the soul outa the valley herself." He nodded slowly with a look of conviction as though he had announced something as indisputable as the time of day. 

Harald nodded at his friend, and then turned to me. "When Diff's right, Diff's right. Never been the same since, and that was hundreds of years ago. They lived in uncertain times up there beforeUncertain Times. That's why they called it 'Dagger Dale'; because you used to have to keep a dagger in each boot, because you never knew which side you was going to get it from." 

Zeb interrupted Harald again, saying "They called it Dagger damned dale because of the magic pig- sticker theys used to kill the damned vampires, professor Harald. Anyhows, that ain't neithers here nor goddamned theres now any goddamned way. They mays as well calls it 'Sunny damned dale' or 'Amaunator's Arse Farts Sunshine Dale' for all the rantings and preachings they does up theres. They goes from vampires to damned Sun Gods, from Zhents to damned Harpersthey just seems to goesfrom, wellwellwell" 
"Extremes" Diff drawled out slowly. 
"Diff's right again, friend. You need a fill-up on that tankard?" Harald called for more beer for the table, but I could see that he and his two cronies were just pretending to sip at theirs. "Where was we? Oh yeah; here's the thing about Daggerdale you have to get your head around. They never knew peace up there. Probably never will. When the rest of us was ploughing our fields and our wives in peace and comfort, the folk up in the Tesh Valley were still fighting with goblins and ogres and giants and that sort of thing, and when that wasn't enough, they had evil wizards, evil Zhents, vampires, beholders, evil paladins, and anything else evil you can think of to chew on'em, from the inside-out half the time, and that hardened the lot of 'em." Diff and Zeb just looked down into the tankards they were clutching on the table, and kept listening while nodding in either assent or oncoming slumber. 

"Yup. It hardened 'em, I says" Harlad continued. "When the real Hard Times came, well, they should'a just moved on. No one would have said nuthin', or talked 'em down. But they stuck it out; through dead gods, armies of orcs and goblins, plagues, the Netherese, all of it. They stuck it out. Now they got 

Amaunator up there, and things run like a gnomish clock, or so I'm told, but you'll hear a lot of us say they aren't like regular dales folk. But the truth is; they are the most dales-like among us. There's nothin' more makes a man of the dales than stickin' it out."
