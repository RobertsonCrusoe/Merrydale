26 Ches – Into Cormanthor! 
Opening Narrative 
By 10 AM you set out from the village of Gribbons' Farm, escorted by Lt. Maddy Riverstone, and 12 of her Sun Blades. 
Being in the closing days of Ches, and enjoying an early spring, the cold weather is behind you, and everything is starting to bud.  The air is cool with a pleasing gentle breeze that delivers the natural fragrances of the season.  "Oh!  We'll see our first Hyacinths on the other side of this hill!" Mari exclaims as one particular scent drifts by, and as you top the small hill, you see a field of blue & purple flowers abuzz with the earliest bees. 
It only takes about half an hour to reach the base of the escarpment from the village, and the switch-back road that has been cut out of it.  At the base of the escarpment is a little stone cottage, outside of which a family is tending their gardens.  They exchange friendly salutations with the Sun Blades, and the father of the family escorts the lot of you up the road, talking the whole time with Maddy.  It is clear that he maintains the road and points out the work he's done this spring to keep it in good order. 
The road goes up over 100', and at the top the view is spectacular.  From here you can admire the expanse of the Tesh Valley; the river stretches out before you, winding lazily through the valley, following the limestone escarpment it has been carving for millennia.  To the west, you can see the imposing Desertsmouth Mountains, and the foothills before them.  You can clearly see the walls of Dagger Falls, and the drifting smoke from dozens of chimneys.  Laid out below you are the many hamlets and fortifications collectively known as Gribbons' Farm, and you can make out groups of farmers with draft animals working a dozen fields.  Across the river and the valley you can see the menacing Dagger Hills, where many outcroppings of jagged limestone mix with the forest to make what should be a beautiful vista, if not for the knowledge of the danger of the place.  You can clearly see the large round wall and grove of Mystral Farm at the base of those hills and realize just how close that farm is to danger. 
Behind you, atop the escarpment, is Cormanthor.  This forest is dominated by the Cormanthor Oak, a fast-growing tree with some connection to the First World.  The trees grow to over 100' in height, with great thick trunks and broad canopies made by strong limbs.  The trees are just starting to produce leaves, so there is ample sunlight today, but you can see that in a couple of weeks from now, the forest floor will be deprived of light.  For the most part, the forest is pleasant to navigate; huge glades of mature oaks have choked out most life on the forest floor, save for the mosses and lichens that favour the limestone-based soils here.  A horse can easily navigate the open spaces between the large trees, and one can often see a long distance in these glades. 
The glades give way every few hundred yards to thicker copses of trees and bushes that provide more ground cover.  Here, the aspen, poplar and dogwood trees provide cover for juniper, buckthorn and honeysuckle bushes, with occasional patches of wild strawberries, blueberries and blackberries (not yet in season).  The forest has a thick musk of rotting leaves, with the gentle breeze often delivering the refreshing scent of the bloom.  The air today feels more like drinking than breathing, the quality of it appreciated by the most urbane to the most rural of you. 
Many small animals mark your passing; large black squirrels are common, and their smaller cousin, the brown chipmunk, can be found on nearly every tree, scurrying thither and hither with acorns.  Indeed, Mortimer's time is full of meeting so many new friends as to require reminding by Jasper to keep up and stay close.  Birds are likewise plentiful in this part of Cormanthor.  Red-headed woodpeckers favour the older oaks and crack open acorns on the forest floor, while flocks of small-bodied thrushes race through the branches overhead, often following your party.  A couple of the Sun Blades open crumb-filled palms for the birds, which confidently perch on a finger to enjoy the bounty. 
Of larger game, you see much sign; droppings from large deer are found throughout the day, and you realize that Maddy is following an old road that has maintained itself as a game trail to Greenwood Farm.  You also see signs of black bears, foxes and coyotes as the day goes on. 
At one point shortly before noon, a great stag is seen off to your left.  It is large and mature and appears to have a scar over the left side of its face which has taken an eye.  It doesn't seem troubled by your passing, taking you in from about 50 yards, casually nipping at honeysuckle the while.  Lucan begins to pull out his bow but finds a hand from a nearby rider checking him.  The hand belongs to Sephany, a forester about 20 years old.  "We're not hunting, Sembian" she says quietly, watching him closely as he stows his bow.

Sephany's kind round face gives Lucan an approving smile as he follows her orders, followed by a brief nod which causes her curly brown hair to dance just a little on her shoulders. "This will make smoke, Sembian.  Let me show you what to burn and how."  With Sephany's words, a quip comes quickly to Lucan's mind, but before he can give it voice, she puts a finger on his lips. 
"There is no need for you to wag your tongue and say charming but inappropriate things to me.  You're as cute as I've heard, and your helplessness out here is somehow charming.  But I've got my orders to give you enough knowledge to live at least for an afternoon on your own in Cormanthor, and that leaves us little time for diversions.  Save your fancy words and overtures for town-girls, Sembian.  If you listen to me, maybe you'll live long enough to bed another dozen of them." 
And over the next couple of hours, Sephany checks in regularly with Lucan, showing him how to adjust his pack so it won't get caught on trees, how to properly oil his weapons and gear so everything is protected and supple without the tell-tale odour of oil to give away his position, how to pack things to make the least amount of noise during travel, and dozens of other tips that would be impossible to bear from anyone without her curly brown hair, rugged-but-womanly figure, and compassionate demeanour.   
The night is cool, getting down to just a little bit above freezing, but the fire is warm and the company is good.  The foresters share a bag of what they call 'Trail Treasure' with the rest of the host.  This is a bag full of mixed salted nuts, oily seeds, and dried bits of fruit that the men pass around with great thanks and broad smiles.  "We march on little more than that when we're up here" explains Sephany.  These rations pack the most fortification and taste in the smallest possible package.  The only thing you have to look out for is" she is cut off by Jasper loudly whispering "Mortimer!  Out!" as his familiar jumps into the bag is it's handed from one man to another. 
Between the 8 men, 2 flasks of mead are shared, which they readily share with your group.  "You'll recognize the flavour, Mum" Maddy says to Mari.  "This is made with your honey.  It warms the cheeks just enough, and lets the men sleep well." 

As the camp comes together, bellies are filled, gear is squared away for the morrow, and everything is in order, the watches begin, while the rest of the camp gathers around the fire.  A couple of the men are already sleeping, but most of you gather in close to share quiet stories, jokes, recent news from town (the soldiers are rapt when you discuss recent affairs), news from afar (they even more rapt by the words of the Sembian and the Elf, who are both exotic to these men).
