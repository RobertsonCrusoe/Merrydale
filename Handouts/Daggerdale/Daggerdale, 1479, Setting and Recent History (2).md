# Daggerdale, 1479, Setting and Recent History
# Source: The Sieges Perilous wiki (backup dated 2010-02-21)

1372-1400: Peace in the Dale

Dagger Falls and the Dale as a whole experienced the most peaceful years it ever knew following 1372. The cults of the evil gods had been eliminated, and Daggerdale was ruled by a tripartite council composed of the High Priest of Lathander, Rewel Thunderstorm, the High Priest of Mystra, Yarron Forrestar, better known as Sparrow, and Lord William Cormaeril. Cormaeril was always a weak ruler, however, and the two churches held great power in the Dale, and for the better.

Many of the great works that had been begun by the Zhents during their wicked rule were completed, including the new walls of the town of Dagger Falls, as well as the many sturdy and beautiful towers that lined it. The former temple to Iyachtu Xvim, Brotherhood Tower, was co-opted by the Lathanderites and Mystrans, and extensively renovated and consecrated as the bi-faith temple that came to dominate the politics and life of most of the people of the Dale. This temple, renamed Morning Star Cathedral, stands to this day, though it has changed a little.

The biggest change to the dale came, as it did for the rest of Faerûn, in 1385 DR, with the death of Mystra at the hands of Cyric, and the beginning of the catastrophe known as the Spellplague. The effects of this are extensive and detailed in other sources. The most relevant changes for Daggerdale were the death of Mystra, and the deimorphosis of Lathander into his ancient and historic form of Amaunator.

The death of Mystra left many of her followers powerless and worse. The High Priest Sparrow is said to have gone mad in 1386 and ventured out into Anauroch, alone and nearly naked. His disappearance was the last blow for the flagging church of Mystra, and the Lady of Mysteries has not been openly worshiped in the Dale since Sparrow left, though it is whispered that some small, private and secret shrines to Her still exist.

The faith of Lathander was likewise thrown into turmoil. The faithful of Lathander had experienced a crisis of leadership in 1372, when the church underwent a schism. Rewel Thunderstorm, formerly the penultimate clergyman of Lathander in Shadowdale, became the High Priest of Lathander in Daggerdale, after leaving with a portion of the flock. However, a worse crisis for the church occured in the 1480's, during the short period in which Lathander was changing into Amaunator.  No priests of Lathander had communion with their god, which in Daggerdale meant that no major deity was guiding the people. 

This could have gone poorly for the community, but a leader stepped forward. In 1389, A now-famous man named Ardelusk Whitegate stood up in Morning Star Cathedral during a first-light service in which many of the parishioners and even some clergy were questioning Rewel and their god. Ardelusk, a former slave of the Zhent mine called West Hill Mine, took the podium and testified that he had a vision of Lathander growing into something great while he was working in the mines, and it was this vision that kept him in Dagger Falls after his freedom was gained, though his family was from some distant place. He said that a great sign would be revealed to the people of Daggerdale if they only had faith and patience.

The following morning, Ardelusk led a small pilgrimage to the West Hill Mine. It took them several days to arrive there on foot, but when they did, they were met with a small miracle. The noon-day sun was beating down hard when they arrived, and from a deep crack in one of the open-face mine's walls, the sun was reflecting off of something brilliant. It was soon revealed to be a significant vein of gold! Ardelusk then revealed that not only had he purchased the deed to the mine a week earlier, but that he had made a promise to Thunderstorm that he would donate 90% of the income from the mine to the church. The story of the Whitegate Epiphany is a lengthy one, but it culminates in the emergence of Amaunator in the region, and the establishment of communion between the former Lathanderites and Amaunator. 

Whitegate kept good his promise, and he did indeed donate 90% of the mines income to the new church of Amaunator, and the flock grew rapidly. It absorbed all the now-godless parishioners of Mystra and a few other less significant gods. In the end, Daggerdale became a nearly homogeneous religious landscape, with few humans worshiping anyone other than Amaunator.

From 1387-1400, Daggerdale knew mostly peace and prosperity, despite what was going on in the rest of the world. The vast wealth that the dale enjoyed from the mine actually centred the population more towards the town of Dagger Falls itself, and some farms began to shrink or vanish. During this time, the Whitegate Family grew and prospered as well, as it continues to do today. After Rewel died of natural causes in 1399, the leadership in the church passed to the Whitegates, and it has remained so ever since. The Whitegates continue to honour the agreement that Ardelusk made with the church so many years ago, and still give most of the profit from the mine to the service of Amaunator.

1400-1479: New Troubles

The age of peace for Daggerdale ended in 1400, with the siege of the town by an enormous orc army led by Warlord Zguumush. He surrounded the town with an army of orcs, goblins, trolls, ogres and other nightmares, and though the siege was largely unsuccessful, it did awaken the prosperous people of Daggerdale to the new reality of Faerûn. In only one or two short generations, the monstrous population in the area surrounding the dale had swelled to historic levels, and hardly anyone had noticed. Worse, divine guidance from Amaunator suggested that the organizing force behind the siege came from the west. 

A small group of adventurers led by Ardelusk and his two sons headed west to investigate this divine lead, and their story, which is often recounted in the dale and further abroad in "The Song of the Searing Sun", is long. It does however end in the discovery of the imperialistic aspirations of the growing Empire of Netheril, and their strong allegiance with Shar. The story also culminates in the founding of Castle Radiance, about which much history and fate now centres.

Castle Radiance became the new focus for the efforts of the dale, the Whitegates, and the church. It was discovered by Ardelusk and his small company as they were fleeing from some Netherese agents, and Ardelusk had been mortally wounded. The Song describes how the dying Ardelusk was shown the site of the castle by a single stabbing ray of sun through an otherwise dark and cloudy day, and how the heroes made a stand there against overwhelming odds. In the end, Ardelusk lay dead, but the remaining heroes, including his sons triumphed, and decided that the location, which was seemingly a dwarven ruin, perhaps from the Tethyamar Empire, was a divinely appointed location for the staging of a new defence against a new enemy. That very day, Summer Solstice as the story goes, the first blocks were laid in the form of the tomb of Ardelusk, and construction there has not halted since. The wealth from the mine has been used to build and defend the Castle ever since, and it has remained inviolable to this day. 

Since 1400, Netheril has always had her vision fixed upon her eastern borders, where the people of Daggerdale are trying desperately to entrench themselves. In the years since, the great Desert, Anauroch has given up its blistering sands for rolling grasslands, and many ancient Netherese ruins and artifacts have as well. With the changing of the Desert and the new surge for the discovery of old treasures and lore, the forces of Castle Radiance have been in a constant state of conflict with Netheril and other powers besides. Though Netheril has never mounted a true army of her own soldiers against the dalesfolk, it is known that she motivates and directs many of the other enemies of the dale in the area.

While the sands of Anauroch were being replaced with grasslands, the farms and other clear lands of the dale were beginning to be replaced by a growing Cormanthor. This is partially because the dale moved from a predominantly agricultural economy to a mineral economy, and partially because of the invigorated growth of the ancient forest. Cormanthor has been expanding in many areas of the Dalelands, but in none other like Daggerdale. The great oaks of the ancient wood now reach the very foothills of the Desertsmouth Mountains, and only in areas that are actively farmed by the dalesfolk is the expansion of the forest checked. Of course, the forest-regrown hides many new populations of monsters and enemies, though some peaceful fey and elves as well. In fact, Daggerdale has a peaceful allegiance with the Elven Court, and Emissaries from Myth Drannor are always stationed in the Castle.

Daggerdale Today

Daggerdale in 1479 is a place that looks nothing like it did 100 years ago. All aspects of the landscape have changed; geographical, political and religious. The forest has closed in the few small farms and settlements of the dale. The entire dale is now run by the church of Amaunator exclusively; the Cormaeril family was thrown out years ago for failing to acknowledge the primacy of Amaunator in the region. Where most areas of the Realms enjoy a healthy polytheism, Daggerdale is very nearly monotheistic, with over 90% of the people worshiping Amaunator exclusively. 

The dale has had a dwindling population for the last 70 or 80 years. The population peaked in the early 1400's, but is now around 12,000, with 8,000 of those living in the fortified town of Dagger Falls.

Daggerdale could be labelled a theocracy today. High Sunlord Argent Whitegate is the head of state in Daggerdale, and the Sunlords beneath him are the magistrates that administrate all aspects of life in the dale. The administration of the dale is relatively efficient and productive, compared to many other theocracies, but this is only because of the vast amount of wealth it has. The church has enough money to have a surplus of paid bureaucrats, which the rigid dogma of the faith of Amaunator seems to relish. The bureaucracy of the dale is not a lumbering beast, however. Officials are excellent at their jobs, and are all motivated and enthusiastic, but they are just unswerving their duties, and are unwilling, as a whole, to cut any corners or disregard any small law or protocol. 

There are still many farms in the dale, but they have all congregated into fortified communities in the centre of the farmlands, through a necessity for common defence. The forests are just too full of dangers of every kind, and so the traditional family-farm has vanished, to be replaced by small communities of perhaps 10-30 families, with standing guards and a permanent temple to Amaunator.

Politics in the Dale

Daggerdale enjoys a good rapport with the rest of the surrounding dales. Though the Dalelands Council hasn't sat for decades, Daggerdale does send out emissaries to her bordering dales at least once a year to share news, and sometimes to offer aid. The other dales give Daggerdale all the respect it deserves for doing so well in so difficult a time, though they treat her with some aloofness, as her theocracy is the antithesis of most dalesfolk idea of good government.

Daggerdale has remained on the side of her neighbours during the conflict with Sembia. She does not trade with Sembia, or otherwise have any friendly dealings, though the two polities aren't in open war yet.

Cormyr has become an ally of the dales again, after many years of conflict. Cormyr shares a common enemy with Daggerdale in the Netheril Empire, and so they have offered each other common defence from time to time. The kingdom of Cormyr in fact leases a tower in Castle Radiance, where they have a small number of soldiers led by one veteran Purple Dragon, and a permanent diplomat.

Myth Drannor likewise holds a tower in Castle Radiance, and keeps a small, permanent garrison. They have a small embassy inside the walls of Dagger Falls, and maintain a very good relationship with the dale, despite problems they have had with others. Again, the two share a common enemy in Netheril, and the Elven Court is happy to have this conflict play out at the edge of Daggerdale, though they offer much help in arms and counsel.

Dalelands Security

Travel inside the dale is not safe, for the most part. Caravans of less than 40 men and animals can expect to be harassed by any variety of monster or bandit on the roads, and travel off the roads is just plain suicidal for those who are unprepared for it. The roads are patrolled with some frequency by the Order of the Lambent Shield, a small, regional knightly order dedicated to Amaunator. They number about 120 knights of varying skill and renown, and are lead by the patriarch of the Whitegate Family, and founding father of the Order, Luminous Whitegate. 

The dale keeps a sizable standing army besides the Order, called the Sun Blades, which numbers about 400 at any one time. All men in the dale are required to serve for 2 years, though many stay on longer. In an emergency, the dale can also summon a militia of 2000-3000 men in pretty short order, many of whom have already given military service in the Blades.

With all the money and conflict in the region, it is not to be wondered at that adventurers are drawn to the place. Adventurers are mostly welcome in the dale, though they are directed first to the Bureau of Visitors in Dagger Falls. There, they are interviewed by a Sunlord in order to determine if they are a moral fit for the community. They are all asked about what god they worship, their history, skills, plans, goals and so on. Suitable adventurers are then given directions to Castle Radiance, and unsuitable people are politely escorted out of the town, sometimes to the edge of the dale.

Current Threats

There are many threats to Daggerdale in 1479, many of which are commonly known, and others not so much. Below is a list of the most commonly known threats to the dale.

Orcs and other Humanoids

Daggerdale is not unlike any other part of Faerûn in that it suffers from the demographic boom of these creatures. Daggerdale has the unfortunate confluence of circumstances which includes not only the unexplained population explosion of humanoid monsters, but also the rapid expansion of Cormanthor to hide them in. There are populations of orcs in every part of the dale. They often include other humanoids such as goblins, bugbears, ogres and others. Orcs in the region seem to be organized into orcish war bands, which are territorial groups of 100 or more adults, often lead by powerful, intelligent and capable leaders called the warlord. Warlords are usually orcs themselves, but some powerful and successful bands have been lead by half-orcs, tieflings, and even miscreant humans.

There are some tribes of goblins in the foot hills to the east of the Desertsmouth Mountains, and a couple larger tribes in Cormanthor that are semi-nomadic. They are often displaced by orc war bands, though, and try to avoid conflict with them.

The Desertsmouth Mountains accommodates a few groups of larger humanoids as well. There are war bands of bugbears in the mountains north of the Castle, and war bands of ogres in the south, that sometimes share, sometimes fight over, territory.

Lycanthropes

The Dagger Hills in the south east of the dale have been home to were-creatures of many types for as long as anyone can remember. With the growth of expanding Cormanthor right over these previously bare hills, the populations there have boomed as well. It is well known that the hills used to accommodate large families of wererats, but these days it is rumoured that they have mostly been displaced by werewolves and werebears. These creatures tend to leave the farms alone, knowing better than to draw the wrath of the soldiers of Amaunator, though they are famous for hunting the Tethyamar trail for new travelers in the region.

Netheril

Netheril is Daggerdale's chief enemy. Netheril has been stirring up and directing many of the monstrous enemies of the dale for decades now; the warlike and disorganized forces of orcs and other creatures of the region aren't a big problem until they are organized by Netherese agents. Netheril does not march her own troops into the mountains at all, instead preferring to use the humanoids of the region as her pawns. This has kept the forces of Daggerdale busy for nearly 80 years, though they have been able to overcome all challenges that Netheril has thrown at it.

The Eastern region of Netheril is a rolling grassland that is growing ruins every month. In the last few years, three significant ruins have been found east of the The Sharspire, and these have been turned into permanent garrisons for the Netherese Army. With the renewed interest in the artifacts and ruins of Netheril that are now surfacing, Daggerdale has had difficulty keeping the Netherese (and indeed, some other interested parties) out of her western border lands. The foothills on the western side of the mountains are turning up new artifacts and ruins almost monthly now, and the Netherese scouts and raiders are becoming more frequent and bolder.

It is rumoured that one of the 12 Princes of Shade has taken a special interest in (or been given the job of overseeing) the doings of the dalesfolk and the actions of the church of Amaunator and Castle Radiance in particular. This Prince is likely working out of The Sharspire, and even Lord-Brother Luminous has expressed some dread at having to deal with such an adversary. 

Plaguelands

In the last 20 years, a small bit of Plagueland has been slowly growing in Eastern Netheril, north of the Castle. The area is perhaps 50 miles across, and though it had been expanding and contracting over the years, it seems to have reached a stable size; it hasn't grown or shrank in the last few years. Nightmares and abominations come out of the Plagueland on a regular basis, and the Order of the Lambent Shield has a particular dislike of these monsters. There almost always a detachment of knights of the Order patrolling the south and east border of the plagueland. The plaguespawn that come out of it are a particular affront to Amaunator, and thus draw undue attention.
