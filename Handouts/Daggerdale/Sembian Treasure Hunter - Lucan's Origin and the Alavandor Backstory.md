This thread establishes Lucan as the 4th son of a Saerloon branch of the Alavandor family, provides his starting equipment, and includes Stephen's detailed timeline reconstruction of the Alavandor-Clarandal emigration — which Les confirms as substantially accurate. You are indeed the 4th son in a branch of a Sembian family from Saerloon. Your great grandmother was the sister of Alvund Alavandor.

STEPHEN (11 Jan 2024):

Hey Les,

I'm rereading your Thistle Ridge PHB in detail to prep for giving you Lucan's actions for the day. Once again, great work on this. One small point - in Blaze Whitegate's 1440 report, under 2) Tax Revenue / Taxable Revenues, am I correct in assuming that 1479 and 1480 should be 1439 and 1440, respectively?

Also, having reread Lucan's handout and the Thistle Ridge before and after documents, here's some timeline building I did for Lucan's family:

PRE-1403: LIFE IN SEMBIA
- The Alavandor family, including Alvund, were part of the working poor in Saerloon, Sembia.
- Alvund's transformation from a struggling artist to a successful merchant marks a significant change in his family's fortune.

THE AGREEMENT AND EMIGRATION
- By 1403, the Alavandor family had already entered into a secretive agreement with "the new Alavandors" (newly rich? newly invested? newly recruited? rebranded?) necessitating their disappearance and relocation together with the Clarandal family.
- This agreement was likely brokered with the influential figure known as the old patriarch, who was capable of facilitating their safe journey and resettlement.
- The two families undertook a long journey, crossing the Sea of Fallen Stars and passing through regions such as Turmish and Chessenta, eventually reaching Unther, where they may have served as field agents for the patriarch. In any case, they went there as part of the agreement.
- The specifics of the agreement remain unclear. However, it involved them vanishing, indicating a need for secrecy or a covert operation.
- Alvund's 1403 letter to his sister, sent after the family had left Sembia, indicates they were already in the process of fulfilling the terms of this agreement.

ALVUND'S CHARACTER
- Alvund Alavandor, as portrayed in his letter, is an observant and pragmatic individual with a strong sense of responsibility towards his family. He shows adaptability and a practical mindset in embracing new roles and environments. However, his acceptance of controversial practices, like the keeping of slaves, suggests a complex sense of morality that aligns with the cultural norms of the societies he encounters, indicating a certain moral flexibility. His willingness to break the terms of a secretive agreement for the sake of familial connection reveals a blend of defiance and loyalty.
- In the letter, he evinces admiration of and a sense of ethnic kinship with the Chessentans, not least for being able to subjugate elves as slaves.
- In Blaze Whitegate's 1440 report, he is reported to admire the settlement of Charity, another slave (or slavery-adjacent) culture.

SETTLEMENT IN THISTLE RIDGE
- The Alavandor and Clarandal families, following the terms of the agreement (or, equally possibly, once the agreement had been concluded or broken), arrived in Thistle Ridge in 1407 and had settled there by 1408.
- The Alavandors leveraged their mercantile skills to build successful agricultural and trade operations.

KEY LORE POINTS:

- Lucan is the 4th son of a Saerloon branch of the Alavandor family
- His great grandmother was Alvund Alavandor's sister
- Alvund went from starving artist to successful merchant "nearly overnight" in the early 1400s
- The family left Saerloon under mysterious circumstances and none of the Saerloon relatives would have anything to do with them
- Alvund's letter (attached, not extracted) contains clues that merit "a 3rd reading"
- The Alavandors and Clarandals entered a secretive agreement, traveled through Turmish, Chessenta, and Unther before settling in Thistle Ridge by 1407-08
- Alvund admired the Chessentans and the slave culture of Charity
- Stephen's character inspirations: Edgar Allan Poe's "The Gold-Bug" and "The House of Usher"
- Campaign setting: 1483 DR, no Second Sundering — Les "hates all that apocalyptic stuff"
