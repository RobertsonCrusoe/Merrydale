== 3 Eleint - "Three men and a dwarf!" ==

The surviving companions made their way towards Shadowdale, where Boregar had allies that he hoped would aid them and give them some much needed information about the current state of affairs in the Dalelands. It wasn't long, however, before they encountered a group of riders. Hearing the pounding of hoofs approaching, the companions ducked off the road into the murky shade of the mountain forest, but their movement was noticed by the lead rider, and a group of what looked to be Moonsea Zhents pulled up their mounts as they neared the party. Narrowing his eyes to peer into the wooded shadows, the man who seemed to be the commander of the riders demanded to know who they were. There was no answer for a moment as the companions eyed one another, but then Dalak's gruff voice boomed out: "Thee men and a dwarf!". 

As if this had been the signal they were waiting for, the men immediately drew their weapons, making ready to fight. They had barely begun to dismount, however, when dozens of arrows whistled out of the forest into the group, and their horses wheeled about in confusion. The leader, regaining control of his mount, shouted a retreat, and the group broke and ran, galloping back towards the east. While Dorian, Maturin and Dalak held their breath to see what new threat had come, Boregar heaved a sigh of relief. Slapping Dorian reassuringly across the back, he stepped back onto the road to greet a party of elven rangers who had soundlessly emerged from the forest.

== 4 Eleint - Shadowdale ==

The elves of Cormanthor escorted the companions as far as the settlement of Shadowdale, whose frontiers they crossed early the next day. The sun was sinking low when they reached the Inn of the Old Skull - the first piece of true civilization they had seen in months. The Wanderer was noticeably lean after the ordeal in the desert, while the others had an emaciated, almost skeletal appearance. As a group, they were an altogether haggard, filthy and wretched lot. After they were cleaned up (which took some time), and donned some new clothing, it wasn't long before they were well settled in the inn's taproom. Crowds flocked around them (despite their want for some peace) and  many questions were asked of these travellers who had come from the desert to liberate the slaves of Daggerdale.

One of the guests at the inn that night was the noble fop, [[William Cormaeril]]. Claiming the right of succession to the Lordship of Daggerdale and of Dagger Falls after the death of [[Randal Morn]], Cormaeril had travelled from war-ravaged Cormyr with a small retinue that included a half dozen Purple Dragons. He scoffed at the travellers from Aunaroch, and boasted that '''he''' would be the one liberate Daggerdale from the treachery of the Zhents. Few took him seriously.

For the first time since they left the tower of [[Durnax Vorn]] back in [[Zelbross]], the companions had proper beds to sleep in (save Dalak, who preferred the stone floor of the common room). Alas, peace was not to come so easily. [[Yarkul]]'s daughter [[Dragon]] (whom Maturin and Boregar knew of only from Raven's tales) stole into the inn, where she made an unsuccessful attempt on Dorian’s life. When it became apparent that the swordsman was not such an easy mark, she escaped before any harm could befall her. Though she had both scorched and skewered the Sembian swordsman, her most grievous wound had been to his heart. The fool was beginning to fall for her.

== 5 Eleint - The First Battle of Anathar's Dell ==
===The Order of the Broken Blade===
In the hours before dawn the next morning, it began to be apparent that the Dalelands were to become the theatre for a clash of truly epic proportions. The {{catlink|Order of the Broken Blade}}, including eight knights of Tempus and their retinues, rode into Shadowdale with full pomp and ceremony. These men, too, announced that they would be heading to Dagger Falls to cast down the tyranny of Yarkul. Nor would they wait a day to do so - they had ridden all night, and were stopping in Shadowdale simply to water their horses and gather those true men who would meet the call. Their leader, [[Sir Dunbold]], implored Cormaeril to ride with them and take his rightful seat after a valiant battle. Cormaeril equivocated, however, and disgusted at his cowardice, Sir Dunbold led the Broken Blade out within the hour.

===Omens===
[[image:lathander.jpg|thumb|Holy Symbol of the Morninglord]]
As the Paladins of Tempus rode west out of town as dawn broke, all took it as a good sign. But later that morning, the heavens put the lie to the portentous departure, and a green and black vortex was seen to appear in the skies above Shadowdale. This vortex was taken as a omen of evil tidings by many, and it inspired panic throughout the village - ultimately provoking a schism within the town's Lathanderite congregation. [[Rewel Thunderstorm]], a ranking Dawn Priest under High Dawnlord [[Cassimar]], interrupted the Dawnlord’s sermon, and declared that the vortex was a sign that the church should have sent all available Lathanderites to assist the knights. Furious at the impudence of his subordinate, Cassimar insisted that the wisest course is that of caution, and the result was the split of the church into factions led by the two charismatic priests. 

The vortex had a strange effect on Maturin, as well. The moment he looked into the swirling clouds, he entered a catatonic state, and his consciousness seemed to soar into the skies, north and west along the Tethyamar Trail to small exposed vale where he saw the Knights of Tempus charging a host of innumerable black-armoured figures. Powerless to intervene, he watched as the heroes were overcome one by one, until none were left standing before that demonic host. In a sweat, he suddenly awoke back in Shadowdale, fearing that this had been more than a dream.

(By a reckoning put together by [[Kennyth]] much later, this was the morning that the slaves in the Dagger Hills mine uncovered the [[Beholder Tablets]], and it is possible that the vortex may have been a manifestation of some god associated with the beholders.)

===A Return===
The night brought tidings both good and ill to the desert companions - and to the town at large. Their lost comrade Sparrow had miraculously emerged from the desert - alone. His face was gaunt and bronzed even darker than those of his companions, and he was dressed in the robes characteristic of the desert nomads among whom he had spent the interceding months. He said that he left them west of the mountains that bordered the desert, able to rely on his magics to sustain him. Indeed, the Sparrow exuded an aura that could be felt almost physically by those who greeted him.

The news he brought with him, however, was as unwelcome as his return had been welcome. He had passed that day by the site of a battle, not twenty miles from Shadowdale, at a location identified by the locals as Anathar's Dell, and which Maturin recognized as the vale from his vision. As this news spread, so did the bifurcation of the Lathanderites. In the confusion, no one thought to ask how he had overcome the lycanthropic curse that had been [[Grandfather]]'s legacy.

== 6 Eleint - The Trail Least Cold ==

The next morning, the reunited companions awoke hopeful and rejuvenated - bed rest in Shadowdale had given them a chance to recuperate, but even more meaningfully, Sparrow's return had renewed their hope and sense of purpose. The reappearance of Mystra's servant hinted at a greater destiny for the heroes, and for the first time since Garrik and Durnax Vorn had outlined the threat in the Heartlands, the daunting task they had undertaken at the behest of the Harpers began to seem possible. 
=== The Triad ===
After discussing the best plan of attack, the heroes planned to spent the day re-supplying, and to set out on the morrow to begin their toil. At mid-morning, however, their preparations were interrupted by the blaring of trumpets as Shadowdale was once more visited by agents of the Gods - the holy orders of the Holy Trinity of Tyr, Torm and Ilmater. The townsfolk of Shadowdale paused in their toil to watch that day, as the three Knightly Orders marched their brightest lances and blades into the town - the Tormite {{catlink|Order of the Golden Lion}}, led by [[Sir Gethard Soulbright]]; the Ilmaterinte {{catlink|Order of the Lambent Rose}}, led by [[Sir Lexinauth]] and Father [[Eisley]] of St.Collin’s Abbey, and the {{catlink|Knights of Justice}}, a brotherhood of knights devoted to Tyr, and led by [[Sir Wildermot Silverlance]]. Though they cut an impressive figure, the knights were exhausted. They had been riding day and night, desperately trying to catch up with their fanatical comrades in the Order of the Broken Blade.

One of the men in the tent-train that followed the Order of the Lambent Rose was the Revered Brother [[Turan]]. Turan was a friend of Maturin from his homeland in Damara, and the two had served together in the Bloodstone Wars against the Lich King of Vaasa. While his superiors - Father Eisley and Sir Lexinauth - refused to speak with the fallen paladin, Turan warmly embraced his old comrade. Through Turan, Maturin communicated the events he had witnessed in his vision the day before to the knights, insistent that they leave at once to discover the fate of the Knights of Tempus. While the paladins of the three orders were loath to deal with their disgraced compatriot, Sparrow's corroboration of the events at Anathar's Dell convinced them. However even these zealous men knew that they and their troops would need rest before any confrontation, and they declared they would set out at dawn the next day.

=== Intelligence and Augury===
While the bulk of the townsfolk's attention was occupied with the knights and their retinue, few noticed the arrival of another contingent from the north - the wood elves of Cormanthor. Still, [[Mourngrym Amcathra]] and many of the Dalesman foresters (including Boregar), were much more interested in the news brought by the elves than in the military trumpets of the Trinity. Yarkul had been spotted at a mine in the Dagger Hills the day before, accompanied (perhaps - the elves seemed unsure, as none had been themselves eyewitnesses) by a [[Unknown Tyrant|beholder]]. Two large and heavy objects had been brought out of the mine, loaded onto wagons and marched away, with Yarkul leading the caravan.

Of the desert companions, only Sparrow favoured investigating the mine in the hills over joining the paladins in their ride to Anathar's Dell. Through his divinations, he had learned that on the same morning that the green vortex had appeared in the sky over Shadowdale, there had been a calamitous event at the mine in the Dagger Hills - likely the event reported by the elves. Mystra's messenger revealed that "the trail least cold lies there". But Maturin's vision worried many, and presence of the knightly orders - and their insistence on riding for Anathar's Dell, further politicized the situation.
===The Shadowdale Council===
That evening, Lord Amcathra hosted a great conference was held in the Twisted Tower, attended by the leaders of the Triad, the desert companions, and the feuding leaders of the Lathanderite Church. Discussions over best course of action gave way to debate, and the atmosphere grew heated. Finally, it was decided that the desert companions would accompany the knights in their mission. Sir Soulbright sued for Amcathra to dispatch Shadowdale's own forces to fill out the army, but none could be spared from the Dales' defenses. However, Amcathra did give his permission to Thunderstorm to dispatch a select few Dawn Priests to aid the war party.

== 7 Eleint - The Second Battle of Anathar's Dell ==
[[image:tempus.jpg|right|150px]]
At dawn, a war host whose equal had not been seen in the Dales since the Time of Troubles rode forth from Shadowdale, comprised of the desert companions and the three orders of the Holy Triad, accompanied by a retinue of priests of the Crying God and the Morninglord, as well as a few Shadowdale men who had decided to follow with what arms they could muster (Lord Amcathra was not pleased that these men had disobeyed his wishes, but he did not move to stop them). [[Cassimar]] and [[Lord Amcathra]] accompanied the host as far as the western edge of the settlement, then, wished them luck, apologizing that they could offer no further aid.
===The Fallen Sons of Tempus===
Two hours later, the party reached the ruins of Anathar’s Dell, twenty miles west as the crow flies from the Tower of Ashaba. There, they found the small farming village that surrounded the inn that gave the area its name razed to the ground, the fields around churned up by hooves and strewn about with the bodies of the slain Knights of the Broken Blade, defiled and rotting in the late summer sun. The knights of the Triad solemnly dismounted and began the gruesome task of gathering their fallen comrades. Holy rites were performed and a pyre built by these holy men, even as they cursed the impetuousness of the Tempurians. The Order of the Broken Blade had marched with the others for many days, but had broken off after it became clear that the Ilmatari tent-train could not maintain their fervent pace. They had thus found their enemies - and their doom - two days too soon.

===The Devil's Host===
[[image:Anathar's Dell.jpg|thumb|450px|Martyr's Grave]]
As the smoke of the funereal pyre billowed into the sky, lookouts sounded the alarm, and indicated an host of soldiers gathering on the higher ground at either side of the dell, as black in their armour as the knights of the Triad were bright. To the north, the banner of the recently reinstated {{catlink|Knights of the Black Gauntlet}}, servants of the resurrected god Bane. To the south, the more formidable {{catlink|Brothers of the Black Fist}}, led by the demonic cambion [[Baron Drake]] had assembled. The militant arm of the church of Iyachtu Xvim had come into its own with the establishment of a main headquarters and training ground at Brotherhood Tower, formerly Constable's Tower, in Dagger Falls.

The host that had marched from Shadowdale barely had time to ready themselves for battle before the Baron let out an unholy shriek and the twin forces descended in force. A brutal battle was joined, and the forces of all that was good in the Realms met the blades of their antitheses. The consecrated champions of many deities struggled towards victory that day, but in the end, it was not found by either side. The Black Gauntlet were utterly destroyed, and the Black Fist repelled, but at awesome cost. Baron Drake himself slew paladins of each of the three orders that opposed him, even as the forces of light vanquished the Black Gauntlet. In the end, the cambion marshalled his forces - only lightly wounded - and led them in an orderly retreat from the field, laughing as his demonic steed bore him off. 

The desert companions, and the surviving members of the Triad had held the field, but had been sorely tested. Their devilish foe had dealt a blow to Dalak that had nearly eviscerated the battlerager, and only Maturin's emergency attention saved the dwarf's life. Many good men had not been so lucky, and had perished on the field. Of the Order of the Golden Lion, only [[Daniel Overcourt]], a squire, had been spared. The Tyrran [[Sir Durin Saddlemoor]] had lost an arm, but not his squire, [[Quiggan]], and though Sir Lexinauth of the Lambent Rose had perished, ten Ilmatari priests - including Turin of Damara and Father Eiseley - had survived to tend the wounded.

Since this battle, Anathar’s Dell has often been referred to as Martyr's Grave, in honour of the twenty-one Paladins buried there.

It was late into the night before the war host marched again. Though they had planned to move towards Dagger Falls that night, their ordeal at Anathar's dell forced them back to Shadowdale, a safe haven where they might recover from the setback.

== 8 Eleint - Houses Divided ==
[[image:cyrlath.jpg|thumb|Discord deepens.]]
The next day was filled with activity. To be sure, there was much to do, but for many, there was also the need to stay occupied, so as to not dwell overmuch on the previous day's tragedy. The Shadowdale Council was reconvened, during which the events of Anathar's Dell were related to Lord Amcathra. Amcathra was troubled by the absence of the militant order of the Church of Cyric - also a strong presence in Dagger Falls. Why had not the {{catlink|Order of the Ebon Spur}} taken part in the attack - especially when their presence must surely have brought victory to the forces of darkness? The Lathanderite schism deepened, as Cassimar and Thunderstorm heatedly argued over the interpretation of Lathander's augury. In a high fury, Thunderstorm went so far as to declare that the Dark Sun had not been at the battle because he was at work in the heart of Cassimar. 

=== Our Man in Dagger Falls ===
After the Council adjourned, Boregar set out pay a visit to his life-partner, [[Silve Silverhand]], from whom his wanderings had long separated him. Like Boregar and her mother, [[Storm Silverhand|Storm]], Silve was also a Harper, but the couple lived harsh lives that kept them both on the road and apart, carrying out missions for which they received little thanks. Fate had once again united their purposes, and they met as comrades and lovers on the Silverhand farm. Silve revealed that she had long ago planted a man in Dagger Falls, and that he had been passing her valuable intelligence on the activities of the Zhents for some time. She gave Boregar a [[Dagger Falls Harper Missive (1)|bloodstained letter]], addressed to Lord Amcathra, and a [[Dagger Falls Harper Missive (1)|second letter]] in the same handwriting, and urged Boregar to take care. The first letter detailed the situation in Dagger Falls, and the second, troublingly, told of slave massacre perpetrated by elves at the mine in the Dagger Hills.

Boregar took the note, and, after a time, his leave, and went about contacting the Cormanthyrian elves in the surrounding woods to find out the truth of the events in the Dagger Hills. The elves pled their innocence, and passed some intelligence about [[Yin-Xu]], the ogre-mage that had stalked the companions in the Desertsmouth mountains, and gave Boregar the gift of a magical elven cloak. Yin-Xu, along with the 12th Company of [[Fist]] mercenaries (a company of ogres) had since been stationed on the Daggerdale-Shadowdale border. Boregar was certain that this posting had the specific goal of eliminating the Harper threat. Thanking the elves, Boregar rejoined Silve and gave her a message to pass to her informant. To evade Yin-Xu's scouts, Boregar returns to Shadowdale via the Dagger River, where he spends a wet summer afternoon floating downriver attached to a log floe.

=== "The Enemy of Your Enemy" ===

By the time Boregar returned, William Cormaeril and his Twelve Companions (former Purple Dragon knighs) had already set out on their expedition to purge Daggerdale of the Zhent poison. Cormaeril brashly declared that he'd have Dagger Falls liberated in but a few days. When the Council reconvened, Amcathra expressed his frustration at the rapid fragmentation of what might have been a powerful fellowship. He warned that should the forces of good continue to attack their foes in bits and pieces, then they would surely end up that way. Again, he implored that the two factions of the church of Lathander reunite, but such efforts were in vain. Thunderstorm coldly announced a vision he had had from Lathander, which had conveyed to him that "the Enemy of your Enemy is not your friend". At length, the council decided that more manageable goals needed to be pursued if the forces of good was to fight by inches. Boregar brought forward the contents of the Harper informants' letters, and when Sparrow revealed the contents of his vision to the Council, the mine in the Dagger Hills was set as the next objective.

The same day saw the arrival in Shadowdale of yet another pawn of Power. Two dark-skinned and sinister-looking travelers from a foreign land had come seeking [[starmetal]] (later explained by [[Kennyth]] to be metal forged from a rock that fell from the sky), as well as the {{catlink|Beholders}}, which they were sworn to destory. They said they would be heading for Dagger Falls soon, to set things to rights there.

== 9 to 11 Eleint - The Dagger Hills ==

=== Strangers from a Strange Land===
The next morning, the two outlanders, wearing black bracers with scimitars sheathed on their backs, took part in the service for Lathander. Following Cassimar's sermon, they rose and proclaimed their mission to seek out the beholders - the true threat to the Dalelands, they said - and called for the assistance only true cowards would deny them. The crowd was clearly uncomfortable, and many looked to Cassimar. Outraged by the infidels' insolence, the high priest then attempted to command the interlopers with holy invocations, but they seem impervious to his powers, merely shaking their heads in apparent disgust before exiting the church, and soon after, the town.

Later that morning, the desert companions - including still recovering Dalak - were deputized to act for the Shadowdale Council and made preparations and set out for the Dagger Hills along the Tethyamar Trail, then forking towards the small independent hamlet of Shallain Freehold, located atop a large rise southeast of the Hills. Though the distance to the mine is not far, the terrain was going to be difficult, and the companions bedded down in the walled settlement.  

That night, however, Sparrow begged leave of his comrades, to spend the evening "communing with Mystra" in hopes of learning something of what awaited them at the mine. This was, however, a ruse, and Sparrow spent the night in a sealed cave underground, far from companionship, and from the full moonlight.

=== The Pit, or The Fool's Errand ===
[[image:HS_Torm.jpg|right|200px]]
The companions set out early the next morning, but the terrain was treacherous. In the years of the Zhent occupation, the little farmland that had been cleared in the Dagger Hills had been reclaimed by nature – not by the forest to the south, but by the dense tickets and thorn fields of the northern hills. As a result, the companions did not reach the mining site until evening, when they found it abandoned, as they suspected it might be. In one of the pits, Boregar uncovered a pile of bones and bodies - some recently dead, but others months, even years since gone. From the properties of some of the mine leavings, and their similarity to the materials borne by the foreign travelers that had come to Shadowdale, Sparrow concluded that the ore being mined here must have been starmetal.

The companions spent the night warily encamped in some of the mine outbuildings, but there were no interruptions - save one. In a dream, Maturin was visited by 'Torm', the noble god's countenance almost blinding Maturin to the figure of Ilmater, his patron's lacerated and bleeding back turned away in the distance. In the vision, Torm gestured at the pit that the companions had excavated earlier: a great pillar of skulls towered out of it, rising into the sky. Torm intoned that this tide of death would only stop if Maturin delved into the pit to find his answers. "That which was taken from the pit must be followed at all costs." In a flash of gold, Torm then disappeared. Maturin awoke, troubled. To follow Torm's mission would put him further from that task set for him by Shildevan, for it would prolong the occupation of Dagger Falls.

=== The Beholder Doors ===
In the early morning of the 11th, the companions set about exploring the great pit, sifting through bones and rubble. Boregar was able to rig up a hoist "Fer fishin' through the rubble" from the remains of a scaffolding apparatus that seemed to have been purposefully destroyed, and after moving the masses of bones and a great deal of rubble, a pair of great doors carved with what might have been Ocular (beholder) script were found. Summoning Mystra's insight, Sparrow was granted a vision of two huge tablets being hoisted by pulley from the mine. Suddenly the vision jumped, and he saw twenty men (perhaps Cyricists) being led into the pit, where they fought with four beholder-like creatures until the great doors were closed, the survivors buried alive. When Sparrow attempted to direct his point of view beyond the doors, the vision ended abruptly - the doors seemed completely impervious to magic (and it was therefore suspected that they were likely constructed of the starmetal being sought by the foreign travelers, Amon and Szuth). Boregar's hoist provided no help in opening the great doors, and with no other options at hand, the companions and quickly decided that their time would be better spent freeing slaves at the remaining Zhent mines, Chillholm and Rainhill.

== 13-15 Eleint - Liberation of the Mines ==
===Chillholm===
[[image:The West Mine.jpg|thumb|The West Mine at Chillholm Mountain]]
[[image:Rainhill Mine 1371.jpg|thumb|The Rainhill Mine, before Boregar's Blast]]
By the night of the 13th, the companions were surreptitiously advancing on the mine known as Chillholm. Making himself invisible to avoid detection, Sparrow stole into the mine to gather intelligence on the layout of the operation. From a hiding location, he espied the presence of what appeared to be the 11th Company of Fist. Apparently, they had been trying to track down some fugitives, but had been unsuccessful and were mustering to return to Dagger Falls in the morning. In particular, Sparrow was able to make out a tense exchange between the Fist Captain and Captain [[Aegis]] (of the 3rd Dagger Falls Garrison). 

"In the meantime, no one leaves. If any of these men try to leave, kill them."

Aegis and his two lieutenants seemed unhappy with the order, given recent attacks by local orc bands, but they acquiesced without further complaint - perhaps indicating the relative positions of authority of the Fist and the Garrison.

After witnessing this exchange, Sparrow entered the mining compound, where he discovered what appeared to be a director's office. It wasn't long before a scholarly looking man wearing red-tinted spectacles and a cloak bearing the symbol of Kossuth entered the room. The man barely had time to register surprise before he was struck down by the Sparrow's fist of stone - a perfect uppercut - killing him instantly. The man turned out to have been a wizard by the name of [[Singe]] (or possibly [[Dragonwing]]). Securing the man's weapons - two long, lightly enchanted knives - Sparrow then quietly made his way out of the compound.

After Sparrow's report, the heroes decided to wait until morning to observe the reaction of the Zhent troops to the assassination. Just after dawn, forty-five men and one riderless horse rode out of the mining compound towards Dagger Falls, leaving a skeleton crew of guards and slave-drivers, seemingly confused by this turn of events. After making sure that the riders were truly gone, the companions moved in on the mine, cutting through the remaining defenses and slaying them almost to a man. Over six score slaves were rescued from Chillholm, among them the long-lost [[Hob Farwood]] - recognizable from Raven's description by Maturin and Boregar, but only just - and the missing dwarf Balak, whose brother emotionally welcomed with a vigorous but tender head-butt. After a day of preparation, the group began the long exodus back to Shadowdale.

=== Rainhill ===

Maturin did his best to direct the refugee caravan, providing healing where necessary and encouragement to those poor souls who lagged behind, but it quickly became obvious that the companions would have to split up to make any move on the Rainhill mine. It was decided that Dalak and Sparrow would remain with Maturin to defend the refugees while Dorian and Boregar investigated Rainhill. This turned out to be a fortuitous decision, for when it came to powder kegs and crowded barracks, the instincts of the Sembian and the Wanderer were much keener than those of either a paladin or a battle-rager. The two singlehandedly destroyed the entire [[Fist|10th Fist]] before making their escape.

== 16-20 Eleint - Exodus ==
[[image:ankheg.jpg|right|400px]]
The exodus that the heroes led back to Shadowdale was far from easy, and many trials lay yet before them. Maturin had his hands full tending to the failing health of the liberated slaves, and despite his best care, many were simply unable to survive the journey. In addition to the rough terrain, the wilderness was infested with dangerous beasts, and their accidental discovery of an ankheg nest on the afternoon of the 16th claimed more than a few lives. Furthermore, though the Daggerdale Zhents had yet to turn their full attention to the refugees, they were not ignored. Near midday on the 18th, the train of refugees was caught by the [[Fist|3rd Fist]], and still further losses were suffered before the desert companions were able to repel the mounted mercenaries.

=== 19 Eleint - The Battle of Freedom Hill===

The Zhents regrouped that night, and early on the morning, the combined forces of the [[Fist|2nd, 3rd and 4th Fist]] attacked the hill where the refugees had encamped. Fearing just such a move, Dorian and Maturin had outfitted the healthiest of the slaves with what weapons they could spare, and had spent the evening constructing makeshift defenses in fortified ring atop the hill. The Fist companies struck as the pre-dawn light was creeping into the eastern sky, and the battle was long and bloody. Though they were vastly outnumbered by the Fist mercenaries, Tempus and Tymora smiled on the defenders that day, for the slaves, even at the very brink of exhaustion, fought with valour (and not a little desperation). The fortifications held, and when the companions took out the leaders of the charge, the attackers broke ranks, and were soon routed - but not without cost, for the fighting career of Dalak the battle-rager came to an end that day. Still suffering from the grievous wound dealt him by Baron Drake only days earlier, the dwarf had fought valiantly, dispatching several half-orc mercenaries. In the end though, he finally succumbed to the blade of Fist Captain [[Garlunk]], who delivered a blow that reopened the Baron's wound, and sent him to ground. Through a miracle, however, his life was spared, but his days of battle were ended. In the months and years that followed, the site of this battle was referred to as Freedom Hill by many Dalesmen, to celebrate the men who would bow no longer to Zhent tyranny.

=== Safety at Last ===
Depleted and very near broken, the refugees continued their journey towards Shadowdale for another day, until they were finally met by a large retinue of elves on the morning of the 20th. The elves offered a secure escort as far as Shadowdale, and brought news to Boregar that the Rainhill Mine had been abandoned by order of Yarkul - the 2nd Garrison had returned to Dagger Falls.

Riders were sent ahead to announce their arrival, and to begin preparing quarters for the inflow of refugees. The group made camp a few hours from Shadowdale that afternoon to rest for the final push, and on the morning of the 21st, the elves, desert companions and over a hundred survivors of the Zhent mines arrived in Shadowdale amidst great celebration. Lord Amcathra himself came out to welcome the great throng. The remainder of the Ilmatari Order of the Lambent Rose had set up a series of hospital and medicine tents, and began the weeks-long task of working around the clock to tend the refugees. The day was also marked by the identification, sentencing and hanging of a number of Zhent spies in the village.

Following the events that followed the desert companions' arrival, the balance of power in the Dalelands had shifted significantly. Dagger Falls had lost many fighting men, as well as an important resource base in the mines. The recall of the Fist to Dagger Falls indicated that Yarkul was in the midst of recalculating his policies for the rule of Daggerdale. There was more to this realignment than was yet known, but the heroes were soon to discover that many more changes were afoot in the politics of Dagger Falls.

At the time, however, only Dorian took an active role in the political manoeuverings that were beginning to play out. His infatuation with Dragon prompted him to send [[Dorian's letter to Dragon|warning]] to her that [[Thagdul]] himself was making a power play in Dagger Falls, and that she and her father had best be warned. Though none of his companions new of this, the letter would later resurface, along with questions about Dorian's true loyalties...
