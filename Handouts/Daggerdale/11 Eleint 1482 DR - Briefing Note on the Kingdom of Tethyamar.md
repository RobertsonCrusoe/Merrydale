Report Penned by Br. Darred 
Committed to the Archive this 11 Eleint, 1482 DR. 
The rediscovery of Tethyamar by the White Cloaks and the impact this had on the politics of the region can be found in S. Estelmer's report, The Shadow Lifted, Part 4. I will herein add to her observations and continue the accounting. 

With the migration of dwarves into the region, including claimants to the throne of Tethyamar, the Tethyamar League was renamed the Emissaries of the Kingdome of Tethyamar, and Gambledin was appointed Ambassador to the Dales by the Council of Silver. With this, Tethyamar became an official political entity in the region again, albeit one that was fighting for its existence with its enemies, and among its own ranks. 

As of early spring, 1481 DR, Tethyamar itself is a battleground. Only the Upper East Halls have been recaptured, and depending on what maps one goes by, this is only 2-5% of the entire city. 
Political Relationship with Daggerdale 
There can be little doubt that the people and leaders of Daggerdale are grateful for the presence of, and military help provided by the dwarves of Tethyamar. The Embassy is on excellent terms with the administration of the Castle and the Dale at large, and has a permanent seat in Loedegan's Hall, where they report daily on the happenings in the deep. 

Officially, the government of Daggerdale and the Church recognizes the authority of the Council of Silver, and their appointed ambassador, Gambledin. 

Addendum by GFD: After the Governors' Assembly in 1482, The Council of Silver was recognized as a voting member of the High Assembly, with Gambledin currently filling that seat at Governors' Hall in Daggerfalls. 
Tethyamar in the Dale 
It is important to note that Tethyamar has a significant presence in the dale, and that this presence is expanding every day. Since Discovery in 1480, the population of dwarves in the region (in the Castle, the Town, the mines and the City of Tethyamar itself) went from about 30 to nearly 1000. 

About 200 of all dwarves in the Dale live outside of Tesh Valley Farm, in the new settlement called Provisioners' Hall. This camp and building project is a significant trading post (for goods and information) between the men and the dwarves of the Dale. The population of dwarves and humans here are exceptionally cooperative with each other and have a mutually prosperous arrangement. 

About 700 of the dwarves in the Dale are in the deep, involved in the recovery of the city of Tethyamar. 

The remaining dwarves, about 100 or so, are found mostly in Town, with an additional 5 at the Castle. They are traders and artisans who support the efforts of their struggling kingdom through their 

professions. This group of skilled dwarves is helping to forge a solid financial relationship between Tethyamar and the Dales, as well as offering the most significant cultural exchange in the Dale since the Zhent occupations of the 14th century. 
Brief and Recent History of Tethyamar 
For the origins of the mines and history before 1364 DR, much greater works exist that I will not reproduce here. My account will begin with the Zhent Occupation of the dale in the 14th century. 

During the Zhent occupation of Daggerfalls the enigmatic man known as Thagdul found the East Gate of Tethyamar. His motivations for this search have been hardly guessed at, much less discovered, but it is known that his operation had at least three slave-worked mining operations in the region. 

A trunk belonging to the long-dead wizard was discovered by an amateur historian in Tesh Valley Farm, and this trunk was turned over to the White Cloaks in their mission to stop the Dark Garden, in 1480. 

This trunk, among other things, contained a map of Gorimdun Mine, which although covered in secret runes and signs, was clearly a map to the East Gate. 

After Discovery, and the arrival of the Host of Tethyamar and the pivotal point of the war with Netheril during the Siege of Fort Lastsun, migration of dwarves into the dale began, and the current era of Tethyamar was begun. 

The Two Princes of Tethyamar 
The throne of Tethyamar has been controlled by Clan Iron House since its founding nearly 1600 years ago. The head of this Clan has always been the King of Tethyamar. This lineage has been monitored, and the title passed down even during centuries of exile from the city itself, within the Clan. The last uncontested owner of this distinction was Ghellin Thordbardson. He died in 1469 DR, and left behind twin sons, Tasster Ghellinson and Tezster Ghellinson. These two dwarves are unfortunately not on good terms and have split their Clan and the dwarves who have migrated back to the Kingdom into two factions. 

Tasster is the more militant of the two brothers, and a dedicated follower of Clangeddin Silverbeard. His following is the larger of the two and is also more aggressive and enthusiastic. His faction attracts most of the militant dwarves that have come to the region, for obvious reasons. 

Tezster is a follower of Dugmaren Brightmantle, the exarch of scholarship. Tezster has long used libraries and other sources of lore to look for his ancestral city. It was in fact Tezster who helped establish the Tethyamar League several years ago; he funded the League and furnished it with all the resources he had to help find key locations. 

The two brothers both have a different approach to unearthing their kingdom; while Tasster wants to rush in with as many hammers and shields possible, Tezster prefers to do cautious probes and research. His is a small but disciplined following. 

The two cannot agree who should rule as King, nor even how to proceed with the retaking of their kingdom. As such, there are two distinct camps in the Upper East Halls of Tethyamar, and each is conducting its own campaign of recovery. 

In order to provide a unified polity to the dalesmen and to their enemies, the dwarves assembled the Council of Silver to make decisions. This Council is made up of 12 dwarves, one from each of the 13 major clans (excluding Clan Iron House) that has come to the region to retake Tethyamar. The Council of Silver is represented in Daggerfalls by Gambledin, who holds a seat on the Governors' Assembly. 

Dwarves of Tethyamar 
These are almost exclusively shield-dwarves. They come from all around the Sea of Fallen Stars (one clan is from Chessenta) and the North. The males are mostly large, at about 4 ½ feet tall. They speak a common dialect. They are mostly fair-skinned, with a variety of hair colours ranging from white, to bright red, to black. The dwarves that have come to the region are optimistic and outgoing. They generally get along with the humans of the region and are working towards a mutually beneficial relationship with them, as Tethyamar of Old was known for.
