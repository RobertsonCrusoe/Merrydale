The next day (Mirtul 25) you guys make your way the short distance to the Yellow Abbey.

Tuugan says he has other business to attend to, and that he will meet you back at the Close in 2 hours.

==The Yellow Abbey==

Most of you have never been inside the Yellow Abbey, though you have all heard of this 'special' magistrate that is the 'curator' of the curious wing in which he is prisoner. You have also heard how difficult it is to actually get at Hoetmer; he is hidden behind a wall of bureaucracy; endless paperwork, questions, more paper work, more questions, and all of it given serious force of arms with many Sun Blades and stone-faced clerics. However, the usual machinations of bureaucracy which keep all but the most determined visitors from the 'Historical Wing' of the library, in which Magistrate Hoetmer eats, sleeps, and studies, quickly wither when challenged by Alystair. You can appreciate that most of you would not get close to this man.

You are led by a cleric, the same cleric as always, a middle-aged man named Grafton, up to the study of the Magistrate, where he leaves once the door is opened and Alystair waves him off.

==A Singular Woman==

Inside the study, the lot of you surprise and are surprised by the TWO people in the room. Hoetmer himself sleeps in his deep leather chair, but you see that a second chair now sits next to his, and his hand rests on the knee of the person in that chair.

The woman sitting in the wooden chair is of an indeterminate age; her hair is black but with some grey streaking through it; it is worn in a conservative bun on top of her head. Her face is what makes it hard to guess her age; she remains a beautiful woman, and there is no doubt that in the spring of life she must have turned every head. There are just a few wrinkles at the corner of her eyes, and just beneath her chin her skin is a little bit loose, but the cheeks and forehead have the bearing of someone in her early 30's. She has a pair of wire-framed spectacles on her face, and wears simple clothes; a grey cloth dress which one might wear under a more opulent garment. In fact, the more observant among you notice the habit of a cleric of Oghma hanging on a clothes peg in a corner.

The woman is reading a book, which she holds gracefully aloft with one hand, about 1/2 an arm's length from her face, while her other hand rests on the hand of Hoetmer, which in turn rests on her knee. She doesn't seem to notice the lot of you filing in, and when she does start from her book with the noise of you entering the room, she looks quite surprised.

She squeezes the hand on her lap, and gives it a little shake. \"Phin\" she whispers to the sleeping Magistrate. \"Wake up Phin, we have visitors.\"

==Hoetmer Awakes==

The slumbering magistrate slowly starts to come around, and the woman just looks at the lot of you with a warm smile on her face, but you notice that her cheeks are red, as though she's embarrassed. \"Alystair...\" the Magistrate says, long before his eyes are open. He is only half awake, and mumbling, and this makes the lady next to him anxious. She squeezes his hand again, and in a slightly louder voice says \"Phin! You must awaken...\"

\"Alystair is here...I know...\" he takes his hand from her lap and rights himself in his chair. \"Terribly rude of me, isn't it? I do doze off now and then, but I assure you I get just as much work done dreaming these days as I do when awake...well...that didn't come out right, but you know what I mean...\"

\"Phin...\" the woman interjects with a slightly anxious voice.

\"Right, pardon me...This is Ms. Estelmer, the Oghman that I was telling...who did I tell? Warburton I think, or the Pilgrim, I don't recall. Anyway, Ms. Estelmer has come from Waterdeep to help us.\"

She stands at this point and gives a gracious curtsey in her plain grey dress as though she was dressed like a princess. 

\"Pleased to meet you. Alystair I recognize, and Ms. Trustar anyone would know. I'm afraid the rest of you have me at a disadvantage...\"

==Alystair==

Alystair waits patiently for the two newcomers to answer... for precisely three seconds, at which point Alen and Ganolen each feel a large hand slap against the back of their heads. Their heads rock forward as Alystair steps between them, his expression blank.

'M'lady\" he says with a nod. \"Please forgive, the 'dumbness' of my allies. This is Alen \"the sneak\" and Ganolen.. um... well... \"the Tormite\". Alystair smiles. It is suddenly cold in the room. \"I am sure that they will find their voices soon, lest they should find them in their heads out in the alley.\"

\"I would gladly 'scold' them for their rudeness here and now, but I have need of the Magistrates thoughts, if I may.” 

He moves to the side of the bed and places his hand upon the frame, you note he is careful not to touch the old man.

\"Hoetmer, it has been too long, what news of the world can you share, what news of the fate of Liberty Hill?\"

==The Oghman's Analysis==

\"Actually,\" the magistrate begins, and looks sidelong at the woman beside him. \"I’ll let Ms. Estelmer explain a bit of this to you. Madame?\"

\"Yes\" she says, as she shuffles through some papers on the very busy desk. \"Well, when Iolus and Banitt were here talking to Phin...Magistrate Hoetmer...a few weeks ago, they mentioned something quite clever. You see, we get reports daily here at the Abbey, the Cathedral, and the Tower. They come in on pigeons mostly, but also by letter-carrier. The reports from our magistrates had to be closely dissected, and we spent much time pouring through the information.\" She shuffles a few more papers, and you can see that she is organizing them into a dozen piles. \"Just one moment\" she says, as she straightens the last couple of piles up neatly.

\"Now, as you can see\" she begins with a new voice, this time sounding like some sort of school-mistress giving a lecture. \"Here I have 14 piles of papers; 1 for each farm in the dale, and 1 for each permanently garrisoned fort. 13 of these piles are all about the same size, would you agree?\"

She looks up, and you can see that she is waiting for some sort of reply. There are indeed 13 piles about 50-60 sheets high, and 1 that is only a few sheets high. A number of you nod and agree.

\"Agreed. This pile here contains only 3 reports. They are monthly reports from the Magistrate there for Hammer, Alturiak and Ches. There is no report from Tarsakh, which should have been here weeks ago. This is the hole in the intelligence we were looking for.\"

Hoetmer slowly climbs out of his chair, and drops one heavy finger tip-first onto the three reports. \"This is what Iolus and Banitt told us to look for. They were right. Something is going on down there, but I guess I don't have to tell Alystair that, do I, friend?\"

== Alen Reks ==

Whoosh. Something passed his head. And then Alystair was talking again. Always talking. Talk, talk, talk.

\"...Alen \"the sneak\" and Ganolen...\"

The changeling was crouching by a bookshelf, one of many lining the walls of the study, grasping a large leather-bound tome. Alen had been in the process of sniffing the cover of the old book, marvelling at the wonderful scent of what he could only assume was calf-skin. It was heavy in his delicate hands, but not uncomfortably so. 

Mention of his name swiftly diverted his attention.

In a smooth motion he replaced the book and rose to his feet, turning in time just to finish hearing whatever it was that the bespectacled woman had finished saying. Ches, Hammers, reports from Tarsakh. Not enough of them, the pile wasn't high enough. Or something. Alen delicately swerved past Alystair, and stopped beside the desk, bent over and lifted one of the sheets from the smaller pile, the one he assumed was the one he should be looking at.

Passing a quick smile to Ethel or Estelle or whatever her name was... oh, she was quite fair, hmmmm... Alen looked away and blinked, then stood up, hand stroking his chin, forehead furrowed in dubious attempt at concentration.

\"Alen Reks, pleasure. I fix problems, you may have heard of me. Probably not. So these are the reports from Tarsakh, you say?\" Had he a pipe, Alen would have looked every bit the professional inspector. The changeling made a mental note to acquire one the next time he had the opportunity. Granted, he was holding the report upside down, a fact only apparent to Ms. Estelmer and himself, although he himself was quite capable of reading upside-down regardless.

\"We have no time for scoldings, comrade Alystair, as much as I enjoy your 'punishments',\" Alen put hard, hard emphasis on the word, a sultry grin riding his face,\"Investigating the going-ons at this... Tarsakh is top-priority.\"

==Alystair==

thumb|Alystair educates Reks

\"Interesting deduction... Dolt.\" says Alystair as he walks over to the table... he pulls a sheaf of paper from within his cloak and slides it across to Reks.

Alystair smiles at Reks, and continues;

\"While you are investigating the Month just past, the rest of us will be going to... at my best guess… Liberty Hill Farm?\" He raises a questioning eyebrow to Ms. Estelmer.

Anyone close can hear him murmur under his breath \"Where does Lumina find them.. and why does she keep sending them to me...\"

==Alen Reks==

Alen closed his eyes and spread his hands with a dramatic flourish, releasing the report in hand with a dismissive flick of his wrist. It landed atop the smallest pile, adjacent to the sheaf Alystair had just placed on the desk. Alen pondered a moment why the man would have a calendar on hand, stashed amongst his belongings. Perhaps he had a poor head for dates. The rogue could understand -- all those religious holidays and times of worship no doubt came long and often.

He glanced once more at Ms. Estelmer, and then back to Alystair, nodding and murmuring agreement at his correction. \"I see, Liberty Hill Farm. If you're certain, and I imagine you are, since you are the expert here. Alas,\" Alen said with much theatrics, standing up from the desk and shaking his head,\"I am not one for logistics, embarrassing as it is to admit. Perhaps I should stick to where my talents suit me?\"

The smile he directed at Alystair was... discomforting.

==Alystair==

In his head Alystair decides to like this creepy little man, and also decides to keep an eye on him.

\"I am sure your talents will be of use in a short time.\"

He looks to Ms. Estelmer \"Tuugan and I recently made an attempt to investigate Liberty Hill Farm...\" he pauses and looks away \"We were turned back before we came even close... dark things walk the nights in that land.\"
