Shortly before the culmination of the bloody events of the Harvestide festivities in Waterdeep, Hob headed north to Neverwinter in search of the man known as Belduran.

== Back to Neverwinter ==

After a couple of days of looking for passage by ship, you realize that you have missed the last ships out of the city, and have to head by land up the High Road, with one of the last straggling caravans that you found leaving the city. The company that you set out with is known as the Red Shields, and they have been unable to safely travel in the north all summer, but have just recently got the word that the woes of the High Road north of Waterdeep have been destroyed. Travelling with them, you find out that the only caravans that made the run all summer were owned by a Neverwinter-based company called Malthoon’s High Road Shippers. One man in the forty-man caravan also knew that this company managed to diversify, and purchased a good number of ships for sea-faring trade down the Sword Coast.

This information made finding Malkor easy; it was too clear that he had something to do with the success of Malthoon’s, and so you first went to check out this establishment. After paying a tour-boy his copper piece, you found yourself outside the shipping house of Malthoon’s next to the harbour board-walk. Upon inquiring in the small front office about the affairs of the company, the clerk responds, “I don’t know much about what you are talking about, but my boss is just in the back if you would like to talk to him. He is not too busy right now.” He leads you into the shipping area, where there are but a few stacks of crates remaining, and a single man sitting upon one, poring over some invoice or other such thing on a clip-board.

== Malkor Githeas ==

The man has long curly red hair, blue eyes and a face only a mother could love. His deep-set eyes are rimmed by dark bags, and are themselves somewhat reddish. He seems sturdy of build, and is dressed in red breeches and chemise, with a grey cloak, broad hat and gloves.

“Nice of you to pay me a visit, Mr. Farwood,” he nods, touching the brim of his hat, and dismisses his help with a short gesture. He puts down the clipboard, and hops off the crate. A smile crosses his ugly face, and the crates in the dark warehouse explode in a wail of screams from at least two dozen darkenbeasts. After a valiant but quick fight with the overwhelming forces, you find yourself at the mercy of this man, and hits gibbering minions.

Bleeding from a dozen wounds, disarmed, and being held down by the beaks of these foul creatures, you fear for your life. It is dark, and the blood in your eyes isn’t helping you to visually assess the situation. You do hear the steps of the man in red and grey slowly approaching, though, and then find him perched above you, dagger at your vulnerable throat.

“You and your friends have caused me a great deal of loss, halfling”, he explains, as he searches inside your shirt for something. Coming up empty handed, he smiles, “Well, my diminutive friend, I suppose you are expecting to die right about now, aren’t you. Like all your Harper friends and your friends from Thundertree.” He twists his neck awkwardly, producing a horrible cracking noise and shoots you another ugly, crooked-toothed smile. “Alas, I have a fate worse than death for you. A caravan leaves here tomorrow for the Dales. Upon it are valuable tools which are needed to restore some important architecture in Daggerdale. You shall be one of those tools. Perhaps a short life as a slave in a rock quarry will teach you to meddle in the affairs of Malkor Githeas!”

== Hobbit in a Barrel ==

You awake some time later, quite disoriented. You are in a very tight space, it is completely dark and you are being jostled about continuously. You reek of your own urine, and are starving, cramped, and ache from a dozen wounds. Things are bad. You manage to buck up however, the halfling spirit in you taking over. Concentrating on the only sense that can tell you anything, you listen, and listen, and listen. For days and days, you listen, but grow more sore, hungry and despondent. You hear muffled voices, and have figured out that you are in a barrel, which is on a wagon.

Finally, after an eternity, you hear a noise at the lid of the barrel, and blinding light follows as the lid is pried off. In desperation, with what little energy is left you lash out, but you find your face to be on the receiving end of yet another weapon-hilt. This one doesn’t knock you out completely, though and you vaguely hear a voice say “Those little bastards got more fight in ‘em than most things thrice their size.” Another voice laughs and responds, ”Yep. Mosta’ the others can barely move, and a couple died.” It takes a little while before you orient yourself from the smash to the face, and by the time your head is back on its shoulders, the lid is back on the barrel, and you hear the awful noise of nails being driven back into the lid.

It is here that you nearly wept for pity of your situation, but you noticed that a water skin had been left in the barrel with you. What little strength you had, you mustered to drink, and the cool water which kept your physical body, clinging to life gave your spirit renewed vigour. Days turned into tendays (by your best estimate) and every so often a pittance is water would be dropped into the barrel, with crusts of bread coming in one time in five. The pain in your stomach reached such a point that you could not remember the feeling of a meal, and during periods of lucidity (which were few and far between) you noticed that your bones were now highly palpable beneath your sickly flesh.

== Decanted ==

But your body and spirit held, and one day (after a time completely inestimable) the blinding light of the barrel lid being removed was accompanied by a strange sensation of falling. Then a splash, then total shock. Your senses screamed with the input; loud laughing and shouting, light blinding your eyes, and the horrible shock of landing in near-ice-cold water. Panic enveloped your head, but your body could not respond from days (probably months) of cramps, dehydration, malnutrition and infected wounds. Slowly though, you realized that you would not drown. The water is indeed freezing, but only about a foot deep. You drank deeply of it, while your eyes adjusted, and you listened to the sounds of men yelling and laughing.

At length, you were able to assess the situation. You and many others were crawling about in a small pool on the edge of a settlement. The edge of the pond was lined with armed men, and other men pouring people (halflings, humans and half-elves) out of barrels similar to your own. The men were yelling at all of you to clean yourselves, and after a while you noticed that they were recoiling from a stench you had long since forgotten. A couple of them were skin-deep in the water, cutting the putrid clothing off of the skeletal-looking slaves, and throwing it on a nearby fire. You noticed that the fire itself was fuelled by the barrels that people were being poured out of. The sight of the sickly people made your heart sink; all of them looked so horrible; so thin, and weak, bruised and battered, and without expression. Then you noticed that the hands that brought the cold water to your moth were just as horrible looking, like bones with a layer of blue-white flesh and nothing else covering them. These were your hands.

After about 30 minutes, the men started hauling your lifeless forms from the water, satisfied that the stench was adequately reduced. AS you were prodded along by clubs and mailed-fists, out of the water, you noticed that at least four or five bodies were being left face-down, motionless in the waste. Your weary legs barely carried your much-reduced weight up the trail towards the town, but you managed to keep your head up, while all of the others were unable to do so. You took in the mountain town; the little establishment was built on the side of a steep foothill, and composed of several large stone houses. Beyond the foothill, a great mountain rose up sharply, the sun setting behind it. Taking this in, you cautiously turned your head, but got a rap above the eye for it. But something made you defiantly turn back; the horizon at your back was gold. An ocean of gold beyond a nearby moor. An ocean of gold stretched for as far as he eye can see.

== Penned In ==

It all seemed surreal. Your senses were just barely returning when you found yourself thrown into a pen with the others. A stone wall some 8 feet high, and 20 feet to a side contained the 20 or so captives that you were part of. All of them seemed lifeless. Many crumpled to the rocky ground of the pen, and you yourself sat against a wall, naked and cold, and watched the sun disappear behind the looming mountain.

Disbelief. Snow was falling. Not a sound from 20 captives. You were scared, but your senses had recovered, and you noticed that there were two men atop the wall who kept an eye on everyone, and a steel gate keeping you in. The gate opened soon after sundown, as the stars began to twinkle through. Bundles of blankets and clothes were thrown in. You found yourself springing atop them and covering yourself, but no one else moved. You looked around to find the others just blankly staring, the sparse flakes falling from the cold night sky. Your heart sank for a moment, but then jumped, and you found yourself moved to action. Naked, you went from captive to captive, covering them in the blankets, and leaving clothing at their sides. You could hear the men talking about you on top of the fieldstone wall. “That one’ll have been worth the effort.” “Never seen one able to move like that by Dekanter.” “Nope.”

With this, your reserves were spent, and you prepared to bunk down amidst some blankets against the cold stone wall. Shortly though, a huge pot of some steaming hot substance was brought into the middle of the pen. You started to rise to your feet, but found a boot holding you against the wall. Looking up past the long leg of the man, you could see a serious face staring down at you. The man was holding out a wooden ladle, aiming it at your head, and you expected a throttling, but the thing simply landed in your lap, and the man walked away, saying “Feed your comrades, halfling.”

The efforts of the night were almost too much to endure; the simple act of going captive to captive to administer a ladle-full of the foul grey soup was nearly beyond your means, but your spirit held, and the 21 other captives were fed.

== Elgan Halfelven ==

Three nights passed like this; you had to feed everyone again the next day, but the second day, people began to dress and fee themselves. And it was on the third day that you would befriend Elgan Halfelven. Elgan helped you administer food to those who were too far gone to ever walk out of the pen. “I have heard,” he explains to you in the first words that any captive ever spoke, “that we are leavin' tomorrow, and that we shall be marching.” The two of you managed to fit in bits of conversation around the protests and threats of the guards, and you learned from him that you were on the western edge of Anauroch, and would be marching across tomorrow. He told you bits about himself. Born in Cormanthor, he moved to Ten-Towns, life as a fisherman, orc raids, and then a barrel of his own. You told him little bits about yourself. But conversations are stopped by the guards, and it is hard to think of what to say after months of silence.

Sure enough, the next day the lot of you were tied in concession, and marched out of the mountain-town, down into a moor, and out of the light snowfall. And by the end of the first day, you were walking on the cracked, dying lands o the edge of Anauroch.

What followed was another immeasurable length of time; marching, marching, marching…

One day, Elgan mentioned, quite obliquely, that it was a futile attempt to sneak up on a diviner, and looks at you. “Malkor, eh friend”?” and nodded. And you nodded.

== Across the Sands ==

The cracked lands turned to scrub, then sand. Dusty town followed dusty town along the Zhent road you followed. Soon, it became hot, and you were cut loose, to walk along with a bucket of water, and administer sips to the marching captives. Elgan Halfelven was made to pull a cart through the desert for a time. When you had gander enough to protest, you were given his spot and he yours. And so days, and days passed. Your frame remained skinny, but your muscles were thickening slowly. Your sickly skin became healthy-looking again, by the many scars from Malkor’s darkenbeasts remained. The days turned once-again into tendays, and the column of captives marched on. The forty or so Zhents were not far away, all the while, with their dozen wagons that they pulled through this unforgiving desert.

One day, hope (being the absence of desert) appeared on the horizon: little mountains. But you passed north of them.  Then a town, then more mountains to the south. And days later, mountains that got bigger and closer, and finally the desert-trail ended at another Zhent town at their base. Two days travel through the small mountains brought you to a high pass, where beyond, you saw the familiar lands of the Dales. But you had your bearings now, and you knew that Daggerdale was first. And that was your final destination.

Your transition from a marching slave to a quarry-miner was punctuated by a sound beating that left you dazed for hours, with a permanent boot-mark over your left eye. You have become the slave of the Zhent force occupying Dagger Falls.
