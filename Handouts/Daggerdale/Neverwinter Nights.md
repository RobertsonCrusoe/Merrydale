== 25 Mirtul - Arrival in Neverwinter ==

On 25 Mirtul, the companions finally arrive in Neverwinter, which, despite their familiarity with the city (Beljuril and Rufus met in this city a year earlier), they find difficult to navigate through. The streets are all deliberately winding, and the place does not seem to make sense. They have to hire guides (young boys, who are paid one cp) to take them through the city. One guide explains that years of warring with Luskan and goblinoid hordes in the North has necessitated the confounding of the city planning. Maps, in fact, are forbidden by decree of Lord Nasher.

After the events of the past twenty days, a hearty meal at the Board Laid Bare restaurant is enjoyed by all.

== 26 Mirtul - Malthoon's High Road Shippers ==

The next morning, after an uninterrupted night of welcome at an inn by the name of Gideon's Rest, Beljuril scries upon the last two Rangers. The two are seen are heading south through the mountains. Seemingly they have been routed.

The first lead that the companions follow is the trading company, Malthoon’s High Road Shippers. However, this proved to be a bit of a cold trail. When the warehouses of the company are visited, a grizzled merchant is directing his underlings in the cleaning and repair of the rundown buildings, which seem otherwise all but abandoned. When accosted, the man introduces himself as Master Elboreth, and relates how he bought the Company from \"Githeas\", \"an ugly chap with long curly red hair, who went about in a red chemise and vest, and had a staff with a dark globe at its tip\". Elboreth know that Githeas once had an office in a house across the street from the temple of Oghma, but that’s all he appears to know. It seems that Malkor had sold the company's assets to Elboreth at an exorbitant price, perhaps his last profit from the villainy the year previous.

Unsurprisingly, the house opposite the temple is empty, though one of his neighbours - an well-to-do priest of Oghma - tells the Companions that Githeas left town only seven days previously - for \"more fertile waters\". The priest remembers that Githeas had always carried a large oak staff with a black sphere at the top, but at the end of the summer previous, this staff had been replaced with a staff of darker wood with a globe of intangible darkness atop it. He also mentions, somewhat curiously, that while the house was occupied, that the servants had to take loads of dung out of the place every day. While the others approach the Oghmans, Rufus and Oberon search Malkor's house, but it appears the mage left nothing behind. The Oghmans are sympathetic to the companions' cause, but find that they are unable to provide any further information, and further, their attempts to divine Malkor's location by mystical means reveal nothing. The Oghmans suggest that the militia may be able to help, for they have nothing further.

That afternoon, after approaching the Neverwinter Militia and disclosing that they here to rid the town of Zhent spies, the companions are quickly given audience with Lord Nasher, the ruler of the City. Lord Nasher is quick to give thanks for their efforts, and assures them that they will not encounter the same corruption found in other cities of the North. He offers his assistance, and bids the heroes to discover what they can.

== 27 Mirtul - The Moonwheel Trading Coster ==

The next morning, Beljuril acts on a suspicion he has been nursing since hearing the Oghmans' story. Attempts to scry upon Malkor himself, he finds his magics mysteriously - but not unfamiliarly - blocked. The companions suspect that this is because of the 'new' staff Malkor bears, and its possible connection to Jafar's perfidy and the events in Waterdeep the previous autumn.

A visit to the Neverwinter Mage's Guild reveals that Githeas had been a fee-paying member until the previous year, but that he has recently withdrawn. Money owing for services he had rendered was to be forwarded to a man named Rothagan at the Moonwheel Trading Coster, whose offices of the Coster were at the northwest corner of the Harbour, adjacent to the boardwalk in what turns out to be a well-turned out building with a large warehouse behind. The door to the office is quite sturdy, crafted from rose-quartz and glass, and fitted into a brass frame.

The companions surprise the lone occupant of the office, a short fellow with close-cropped black hair, and beady eyes who turns out to be Rothagan himself. Through his magical abilities, Beljuril is able to discern from the man's thoughts that Malkor has not left town - that in fact, Rothagan expects to meet with the man himself and a business associate that afternoon at the the Gilded Serpent.

The companions show up at the Serpent and case it out, waiting for either Malkor or his ‘associate’ to show up. Presently, a familiar face is spotted by Rufus - Atemnon stealthily enters the building through the kitchen entrance in the back. Inside, the rest of the companions are dining, and Oberon finds that his food has been tampered with (at the very least poorly prepared - no Netherese cuisine this northern fare). After a brief discussion, the hatch a ridiculous plan to have Atemnon arrested for the ‘poisoning’ of Oberon, but the whole thing fails miserably, and as Fazil investigates the kitchen, he sees Atemnon slipping out through the rear.

Investigation that afternoon, however, turns up more information on the Moonwheel Coster. The 'security' staff (thugs and goons the lot) that worked the previous year for Malthoon’s are now on the Moonwheel payroll, and Githeas himself is listed in the city registry as the patron of the Moonwheel Trading Coster. Further, the Coster has an office in Waterdeep, and, it is whispered, that it must maintain some manner (magical?) of inter-city communiqué between the offices. It is learned that barrel-loads of dung are carted out of this warehouse daily, just as with Malkor's home the previous year, and flying creatures have been rumoured to be seen haunting the night skies around the harbour. Witnesses say that they disappear under the boardwalk somewhere near the Moonwheel offices, but none have investigated too closely.

It is also learned that a man named Onyx has many dealings with this company. Onyx is described as a moustachioed Luskanite with blonde hair, and a frequent patron of the Drunk Mermaid, where he can be found every other night.

Further investigation about ships reveals that only a few ships in the entire merchant fleet have been able to navigate on the Sea of Swords. These are the the Helmfast sloop Myrmidon (registered to the Moonwheel Coster and moored opposite its offices), the Dolphin Star (registered to a trading company called Emuldan’s Carters) and an independent, Waukeen’s Wyvern. Githeas is said to be living on the Myrmidon, which is moored in the harbour beside The Moonwheel office. A well-executed infiltration of the boat, however, turns up nothing.

That night, Oberon and Oliver visit the Moonwheel Office 'after hours', and turn up a note that gives the companions much to think about...

==28 Mirtul - Emuldan's Carters, Rufus' Demise==

Late the next morning, the companions follow up the information on Emuldan's Carters. At eleven bells, Beljuril raps on the door to the offices, but there is no answer, and the door appears to be locked. Oberon eyes the lock and notes that it has been trapped. Unnerving shrieks can be faintly heard from inside the building, and Rufus recognizes the blasphemous cries of darkenbeasts - indeed, there are no windows to the building, and the inside must be completely dark. Oliver clambers up onto the roof to remove slate tiles, but as he does so, a pair of crossbow bolts shoot out at him, and a flying figure follows soon after. The companions are quick to react however, and soon bring the cagey assassin to heel, and Fazil is sent to bring the guard.

Beljuril's mystical interrogation techniques reveal that the man's name is Ryerson, and that he is an agent of Malkor's, who is living on the Waukeen's Wyvern. He is carrying little, but one document on his person draws another link between the Moonwheel Coster and Emuldan's Carters. Further investigation of the offices turns up two more notable pieces of correspondence:

*Ryerson's Note to Ezmail
*Ryerson's Note to Gaskar

It is later discovered that the men to whom these letters were addressed, Gaskar and Ezmail, were caravan masters for Emuldan’s Carters. Neither is found, and it is assumed that they have left the city.

Several paladins of the watch arrive, and take brief custody of Ryerson before executing him as a slaver and spy as the militia wizards destroy the warehouse containing the darkenbeasts.

Events are moving quickly now, and the Neverwinter Companions quickly acquire a boat, which they use to take them out to the harbour. As their skiff nears the Myrmidon, however, an arrow is shot into the bow, wrapped in a stiff vellum scroll. It is a warning from Malkor - \"Whosoever comes near me will perish. So says Malkor\".

Waukeen’s Wyvern is quickly found, moored at the docks, and a battle is quickly joined by the NC, Malkor and the few men he had around him. Were the fight at night, there may have been problems from his numerous darken beasts, the battle is in the middle of the day, and the Neverwinter Companions have the advantage.

The battle soon goes sour, however. Malkor, recklessly wielding the powerful Hunabar Beholder Staff, wantonly looses numerous magical fireballs into the midst of both the companions and also civilians on the docks. Rufus bears the brunt of several of these spells. To his credit, he absorbed more damage than a dozen men, but in the end it was too much, and he succumbed to the powerful destructive magics of the staff and the cruel will of the madman wielding it.

== 29 Mirtul - The Wyverns Flight, Oliver's Triumph ==

Malkor managed to make good his escape at that point. His ship put to sea, taking refuge amidst a cluster of Luskanite rakers which appeared as if from nowhere. It appeared that the mage had been planning his flight for some time. Nasher ordered the immediate dispatch of a fleet of Neverwinter military vessels, and only hours later as the sun sets and the tides recede, the fleet puts out to give chase. The Neverwinter Companions take passage aboard one of these vessels, the captain only too happy to have them along. He introduces them to another adventurer who has lent his heavily tattooed arms to assist the pursuit - a wild, strange man from the far west, who was known as Wyrmtongue.

The chase lasts long into the night, during which the pursuing Neverwinter fleet is plagued unceasingly by darkenbeasts, and it isn’t until the pre-dawn hours of the 29th that the slower, if larger, Luskanite rakers are overtaken, and the naval engagement begins.

Several ships were sunk in the engagement, and, regrettably, many men on both sides died. Wyrmtongue nearly perished when he and Fazil stormed a sinking raker to recover a chest of treasure. Fazil may have earned himself a permanent enemy when he allowed Wyrmtongue to go down with the ship, choosing to secure the recovered wealth rather than his newfound companion. But of the perils experienced by Neverwinter Companions, those encountered through the unsung heroism of Oliver Windemchester are without equal.

Despite the seemingly incessant fireballs being loosed from Malkor's staff, of which he personally bore the brunt of two, Oliver took the desperate gamble of attempting to disarm Malkor of the staff with a well-placed arrow, which - against all odds - succeeded. However, this was only the first of decisive manoeuvres by the Master of Common Sense against the insane mage. Though he had temporarily knocked the staff from the mage's grasp, Oliver knew that Malkor had to be prevented from taking it up again, and he took another huge risk, diving aboard the flaming Wyvern to recover the staff himself. In this he succeeded, and Oliver deftly nabbed the staff before diving into the icy waters of the Trackless Sea, thwarting Malkor and securing the staff for the heroes.

After he was recovered from his icy swim by the men of Neverwinter, Oliver had one last, decisive contribution to make to the battle. As Waukeen's Wyvern pulled away from the Neverwinter fleet, still engaged with the Luskanite rakers, Oliver loosed a magical taunt, ridiculing Malkor and his cowardice. The result was dramatic. In high fury, Githeas ran to the back of the ship to shake his fist at the goading rhymester, thus exposing himself to the Neverwinter fleet's archers. He was still raging impotently at the heroes when an arrow suddenly bloomed out of his chest, and he fell forward into the churning sea, floating lifeless for a brief instant before sinking into the depths of Umberlee's briny bosom.

The Wyvern itself, however, managed to make its escape, and the expressionless figure of a leather-clad northerner was seen on deck, staring coolly back at the Neverwinter Companions and their fleet before the ship moved out of range, still bound for Luskan, with the remaining rakers. With their flight, the Zhent threat to the North faded, and the pledge made the year before to Zaltim the Road Mage was fulfilled.

==Early Kythorn - Funerals and Farewells ==

On the evening of 1 Kythorn, after a day of recuperation, reflection, relief and regret, Lord Nasher throws a grand feast to honour the victory of the Neverwinter Companions and the sacrifices of Rufus and the many soldiers and sailors lost in the encounter. Beljurils magics are able to send word of Malkor's destruction, and Rufus' loss, to the Harpers in the Daggerdale Expedition and in Waterdeep. Information is also passed along regarding Emuldan's Carters and the caravan headed by Gaskar towards Dekanter.

The Zhent threat to Neverwinter had been eliminated, and a statue of the heroes was begun. To this day, high atop the Southern High Road Gate, by which the heroes first entered the city, a 25’ high statue sits atop the 40’ keystone of the gate. The statue depicts the heroes, including Rufus and the archer that killed Malkor. All are standing in a circle atop a mound of dead darkenbeasts, holding the captured staff up high in the air.

The house that Malkor had owned was given as a reward to the heroes. Oberon and May stayed on at the house, and dropped out of this story. Their story does not end here, but their part in this tale does. They bid their farewells to their friends, especially to Oliver, with whom the two had traveled so farh, and to whom Oberon still owes the debt of his freedom.

On 8 Kythorn, Beljuril and Oliver departed by sea for Waterdeep with the staff, and arrived in good time only two days later. Beljuril’s tale continues in a later chapter, but for Oliver, his part in this story, too, had come to an end. Oliver found his old friend Falcon, who was putting together an expedition to the south, which he was happy to join. Oliver bid farewell to Beljuril, and here he leaves this story. Like May and Oberon, his tale is not here told to it’s end, but his pivotal involvement in this tale here is concluded.

What became of Beljuril in the City of Splendours is told in a later chapter.
