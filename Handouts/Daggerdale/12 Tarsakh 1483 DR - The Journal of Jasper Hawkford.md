The Journal of Jasper Hawkford

Dalesman of Shadowdale
Companion of the Chrysalis Company of Daggerdale


Kept and Written
as a
Record of the Reclamation of
The Thistle Ridge Farm of Daggerdale
as
Commissioned by
Brother Vessuvian of Candlekeep,
Attache to the Yellow-Abbeye at Daggerfalls



8th Day of the 1st Tenday of Tarsakh in The Year of the Tasked Weasel, 1483 Dalereckoning

My friend at the Yellow-Abbey, Brother Vessuvian has approached me with an idea to record the experiences and events of the months ahead, as the Chrysalis Company attempts its reclamation of the cursed Thistle Ridge Farm as bound by charter.

I have never been much of a writer or a journal keeper, but I will try my best to provide as detailed and impartial an account of the events of the forthcoming months and years as they transpire, in order to provide as official a first-person record of the re-taking of Thistle Ridge.

I will not include within these pages information that can otherwise be found in what I trust is the accompanying volume to this work: The Daggerdale Chronicles, as compiled by Grafton Fitzdecker.  That is a far more comprehensive explanation of the politics and historical context necessary to understand why Chrysalis Company has won the right to reclaim Thistle Ridge.  I am likely to make mention to that work frequently, though my commission from Vessuvian is to strictly provide a daily account of our progress.


10th /Tarsakh, 1483DR at Dagger Falls

All the proper preparations for the expedition have been made, and our small company is prepared to leave the comfort and security of The Embassy here in Dagger Falls.  I have attached as Appendix A to this journal a detailed breakdown of the forces provided to us by the Dale, as well as the hirelings we have employed to support the reclamation.  In addition I have copied the Comapny's ledger detailing the investment in men and materials that has been made in advance of our march.

11th Tarsakh 1483 DR at Dagger Falls

I have named my horse Elminster.  Some might believe it is out of nostalgia for the deceased protector of my home.  Truthfully, he is a slow-witted animal with a long shaggy mane and a dopey look in his eyes, so I thought the name appropriate.

The company rides out this morning towards Gribbons Farm, where we will connect with those elements of our column that have marched on ahead.  From there we expect to make additional progress to Greenwood Farm, where our initial base of operations will be established.

-addendum-

We have arrived at Gribbons Farm, and have successfully reunited with our entourage:  Lieutenant Forge Farmountain and his Sunblade company, Sir Reginald Boxfrey and his Free-Defense League Mercenaries; and Swelter the Engineer and his builders and workmen.

After some refreshment and a short conference, we decided that we would move ahead on foot escorted by the Sunblades' Foresters, led by the young Sephany.  Farmountain and his Sunblades would follow at a regular pace, with Swelter and his engineers beginning to lay down the survey of the supply road, and the Ser Reginald's FDL providing protection to the entire enterprise.

I left Elminster in the care of Ivy Thickickle, Quartermaster of the Sunblade company and daughter of our own Mari.  She likewise took the others' mounts and promised to see them safely to Greenwood Farms.

-addendum 2-

We pushed on at a decent speed, freed from the encumbrance of the military column, and aided by Mari's guidance through the woods, and the Foresters' experience of the area, arriving at Greenwood Farm several hours ahead of the others.

At first glance, we found the place much as we had left it only a pair of tendays ago.  However, knowing that the Curator's spies are everywhere, we proceeded to do a more thorough investigation of the ruins, and sure enough, we found evidence of recent goblin activity, as well as a wicker token in the shape of a doll, quite obviously placed as an alarm to warn someone that we had arrived at Greenwood.

We expected this, which is what we have marched in force, and intend to be ready when the assault comes.

After inspecting the doll as closely as I could, I incinerated it until it was nothing but ash in the wind.

With means both magical and mundane we searched the remainder of the ruins and surroundings, but turned up nothing else of great concern.  This location is to be our home for the foreseeable future, so we began clearing and preparing it for the encampment.

-addendum 3-

The remainder of the column has arrived at Greenwood and the work of settlement has immediately begun.  Barracks, kitchens, latrines, fortifications, stables, and so much more, all need to be built and built fast.  It is impressive what military organisation can do, for these men and women have spent most of the day marching, and without a moment's rest, have begun their labours of turning this location into our fortress.

We convened our first Council as we ate our evening meal.  Farmountain, Ser Reginald and Swelter joined the rest of the Company.

I should interject and clarify that although Ser Rowan Grumbold is not a shareholder of Chrysalis, and thus would normally be omitted from such Councils, he is an invaluable member of our company, and it would be inconceivable to hold any such conference without him present.

Others have attempted to enter into our Council, but I have taken responsibility to make it clear that at this moment, so early into this campaign, we need only the ears of these three men.

In truth, I am quite certain that the Curator's spies are within our camp already, and it would be of great use to her to be within our Council.  My brother has served under Farmountain, and it is on his word that I am willing to accept the Lieutenant at face value.  Both Swelter and Ser Reginald are men that fought us in the arena, and they did so honourably.  Swelter, as a dwarf of Tethyamar, would truly be betraying his homeland and his heritage should he take sides with Netheril.  And Ser Reginald is a Dalesman, and not from this strange  Daggerdale.  That may not mean much to folk from the outside, but the fact is that Ser Reginald is from Battledale, and I know that his ancestors shed blood and broke bread with my ancestors many times throughout centuries past, and to those of us who are Dalesmen, that means something.  Furthermore, Nemia has vouched for both men, and if there is one thing I know now about Nemia Ul-Nishaadrh is that these men would be dead already if their loyalty was in question.

And with the exception of Ivy Thickwickle,I cannot speak to the trustworthiness of the rest of our cohort.  Some are here because they believe in this war against Shadow.  Some are here because they were told to be here.  And some are here because the gold is enough.  All those can be turned.

It is not my intention to create a witch-hunt for the Curator's spies.  The Lady Jeska is capable enough of flushing them out.  And Lucan, should he put his mind to it, should be able to greatly assist in that endeavour.

For now, the smaller we keep our council, the better.

And thus, with these three men around our fire, we laid out the expectations and chores to be undertaken for the next few days.

Afterwards, I took each aside individually and informed them of the token we found when we first arrived here, and have asked them to act accordingly without alerting their own men.  Farmountain, as a military man, went off to make sure the guard postings were properly positioned and staffed.  Swelter, having fought alongside Nemia simply swore, spat on the ground, and carried on.  Ser Reginald, as a Knight of Tempus, argued that we should ride out in force and burn them all out of the forest.  And while there is much to like about that proposition, we have learned our lesson from our previous visit here, and need him and the FDL to defend when the inevitable comes.

Chrysalis would march out the next morning to do reconnaissance of our first prize: the Thistle Ridge Tower.

Swelter and his builders and engineers would continue preparing and clearing the road back to Gribbons Farm, and establishing the basics for fortifying Greenwood.

Farmoutain and his Sunblades would begin the labour of building that fort, while the Foresters provided a guard perimeter.

Ser Reginald and his FDL would set up defensive positions along the road back to Gribbons to keep our supply lines protected and open, and provide protection and support to the Sunblades.

With that, the day came to a close, and the real work of the reclamation will begin tomorrow.


12th Tarsakh 1483DR

It is early; about an hour after dawn.  I have just finished my morning preparations, and as always, I second guess my spell choices for the day.  It is difficult to know what will be required of me.  I know that a fight is ahead, yet I do not relish the thought of engaging in one.

Greenwood is already awake with activity.  Lumber is being chopped everywhere and by everyone it seems.  Not only do we need to clear the area, but we will need wood for fuel, for barricades and palisades and buildings.  A full third of the Sunblades are up, with axes in hand, chopping wood.  Another third stand their guard posts, while the final third, somehow, is trying to sleep through the commotion.  In a few hours they will rotate: the woodchoppers will sleep, the sleepers will guard, and the guardsmen will chop wood.

Swelter and Ser Reginald are similarly busy sending out detachments of workers escorted by FDL mercenaries to start clearing the road.  A few  FDLers have been seconded to Ivy Thickwickle's command, who is directing the establishment of the camp's stores and supplies.

Yesterday this place was an abandoned ruin, and today there are nearly 100 men and women, and a score of animals, rapidly transforming it.

It is about 3 miles to Thistle Ridge Tower.  We could expect to reach it in just over an hour's time.  However, due to the heaviness of the forest, and the dangers we face, I do not expect to reach it before midday.

It is about 7 bells now, and Ser Rowan is indicating to me that we are moving out.

We leave the camp and its hive of activity. The five of us (Lucan Alavandor, Mari Thickwickle, Ser Rowan Grumbold, Jeska Monarch, and myself) will venture forth alone to scout the Tower and perhaps catch our first glimpse of Thistle Ridge itself.

-addendum-

We have survived our first battle of the Reclamation.

We are only about a mile east of Greenwood; still close enough that if we strain we can smell the scent of the burning fires and faintly hear the sounds of axes on logs.

I will describe the battle momentarily.  Though it has been an hour, I find myself still shaken by the experience.

There is no secret in that I hold an interest in anatomy and its relation to the necromantic arts.  I am one who understands that in order to combat and defeat the undead, one must learn all we can about them.  I just never thought that the skills I learned from poking around cadavers would be employed to sew up gash wounds on Ser Rowan and Lucan.

I am no medic, and certainly no cleric, and have always been taken queasy at the sight of blood.  Yet I look at my hands now, and they are stained, not only with my own blood, but also that of my companions.

About an hour ago we entered a section of the forest where we spotted a small abandoned campsite located quite close to a fetid pool of stagnant water surrounded by dead brambles and trees.  Here in the heart of Comanthor, where life is so prevalent and indomitable, we should have suspected a greater evil when first we spotted so much death.

But in truth, the first signs we saw of the trap were of life.  A young woman tied to the trunk of the dead tree, pleading for help.

I know Jeska's first instinct was to destroy her and the entire scene, and of course, that should've been the course of action we should have taken.  But she is an elf of Cormanthor, with experiences and knowledge that none of us can even begin to understand.  Simple Dalesfolk, like Mari and myself see a person in distress and we know that we need to help them.

But the trap was laid, and sprung.

We fanned out and approached the site from three directions.  Lucan took to the north, Jeska to the south, and Mari proceeded down the centre.  I decided to investigate the small campsite first, which showed every indication of having belonged to the Red Jacks company, and indeed yielded the pack and belongings of the dwarf Buddy Gorehammer.  The valiant Ser Rowan, kept watch over me.

It wasn't long before Lucan had taken position to the north, spotting the corpses of many goblins hidden in the bushes, but also the body of Gorehammer himself within the dead brambles near the tied up woman.

For her part, Jeska took position in the south, spotting similar goblin corpses in the bushes near her location.

Not wanting to approach the woman, Lucan fired an arrow from his bow, attempting to break the bonds that held her to the tree, but his aim was not true.  Meanwhile Jeska lit a torch and threw it at the dead bushes hoping to cleanse by fire what Cormanthor seemingly could not.

It was Mari who valiantly took it upon herself to approach the bound woman, and thus set of the trap, as she revealed herself to be some form of zombie.  The woman took hold of our halfling cleric, and dug her claws deep into her.  At this, both Ser Rowan and I ran forward to engage the zombie and save Mari.

This initial combat was short-lived, as the woman succumbed to ser Rowan's hammer quite quickly. However, it was only the beginning of the battle as Gorehammer and the other goblin corpses rose up and began their attack on us.

It was a battle that could easily have seen us all dead, were it not for the incredible faith of Mari Thickwickle!  Her faith in Yondalla so complete that she simultaneously healed those of us who had suffered grievous wounds, and destroyed those foul necrotic creatures which beset us on all sides.

I will not lie on these pages.  I was gripped in true terror that we would not live out the next minute, and then I felt the warm embrace of Yondalla's care, and well, here I sit alive and writing these words.

As a child I grew up praising Chauntea for our farm and our bountiful family.  She has been an ever-present aspect in the background of my life, though I have never paid her much mind.  And here we are, heading towards a mystery where we find both Chauntea and Jannath, and where we are saved by Yondalla.

I feel compelled to write home to my own mother; alas she has been dead for over 5 years now.

Mari's blast of positive healing energy was enough to change what had been our imminent deaths to a total rout of the enemy.

Once Gorehammer and the goblins had been brought down, we set about quickly investigating the area, which we discovered was a small circle of negative energy which gave power to these undead.  We have been able to deal as best we could with this circle.

Within it, we have found three wicker dolls - totems - similar to the one we have found at Greenwood Farm the day before.  Three of them, dolls made to look like small girls with different coloured ribbons tied around each one.

I intended to keep them this time, as I believe I can use them to locate the one who is making them and placing them in our path, but Jeska has insisted that they be destroyed.

She is usually right, and of course there will be regret over what I am going to do.  But I am compelled to know who is behind these dolls, and what they are, so I have surreptitiously kept one and hidden it away in my bag for further investigation when we get a chance.

Now that everyone has healed and rested a bit, we will investigate the zombie woman and Gorehammer, as well as the contents of his pack and the surrounding area, and then hopefully we will carry on eastward, ever closer to Thistle Ridge Tower.
