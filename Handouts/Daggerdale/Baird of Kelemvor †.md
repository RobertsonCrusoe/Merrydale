== Manner of Death ==

Baird of Kelemvor was killed on the twenty-third of Mirtul, 1479.  In a vicious fight with the Mercykillers in the ruins of Spellgard; surrounded by foes, Baird was attacked from behind by the goliath, Bodr.  Baird stumbled to the ground and while struggling to stand, the man known as Stringer took aim upon the struggling paladin and put a crossbow quarrel through the paladin's skull.

== Orphaned by Undeath ==

Baird was the son of two of the soldiers stationed in Dantalien Citadel; Dorn Ahonen and his lover Keela Creyarch. The atmosphere in the citadel was so grim at the time, that many of the soldiers rallied around keeping Baird there despite the fact that it was \"against the rules\". Baird became a spark of hope in desperate times. 

Shortly into Baird's third year, Dantalien was assaulted by a large force out of Undumor. A significant number of the citadel's forces were killed, and Dorn and Keela were counted amongst those that had fallen defending the citadel from the countless waves of undead.

A lone rider had been sent out with Baird as things grew more and more grim and after a harrowing journey south, Baird was left in the care of a small cluster of priests of Kelemvor. These priests were stationed at the Watchwall in the south to help the defenders of the wall deal with the abominations that were then crawling from the Umber Marshes with greater and greater frequency.

== Brothers in a Desperate Land ==

The young Baird was given into the guardianship of a young priest, Mattrick Lychaeus, and the two became like brothers as they grew up together, growing in knowledge not only of the ways of Kelemvor, but all too well of the despair that hung like morning fog over the eastern edges of Aglarond.

The despair had been growing as the years passed. Thayan strikes were becoming more and more common and more and more bold. Patrols into the Umber Marshes had all but stopped and what few men were left to guard the Watchwall did that and only that. The Thayan-occupied fortress-city of Emmech (now called Undumor) seemed more and more resilient to assaults by the forces of the Dantalien Citadel - who had become so desperate that they began hiring mercenary adventurers to brave the terrors of Undumor and do as much damage as possible to the undead forces contained within.

Far to the south of the Watchwall in Chessenta, the sight of Mount Thulbane was also proving to be an ill omen. Seemingly, an ancient volcano had awoken in the mountain and a plume of oily black smoke rose steadily from its shattered peak. On days when the winds were against them, which were many, the troops of the Watchwall would be bathed in flakes of oily, black ash.

== The Simbarch's Solution ==

Hope was fading, and there were rumours that the Simbarchs were growing desperate for any manner of solution to assist with defending Aglarond from Thay. It was on a day where Mattrick and Baird were watching the grey fog of the Umber Marshes, wondering what manner of monstrosity would next emerge, when they were approached by a Simbarch and her retinue. An elf by the name of Seriadne, one of the fifteen Simbarchs that ruled the country had traveled to the Watchwall to speak to...them?

Of what use were an embittered and stress-worn priest of Kelemvor and his ward, a paladin of Kelemvor who most likely didn't know the business end of his own long sword? Despite their incredulity, Seriadne spoke compellingly and eloquently, weaving a tale of an ancient ruins in the far west that might very well be the solution to all of Aglarond's problems.

The Simbarchs had learned of an ancient spirit - or some other entity - that was said to answer any question asked of it. The only challenge being finding the entity in the ruins that it was rumoured to haunt. Seriadne passed Baird a note which she besought him to commit to memory. If he and his guardian were able to find this entity, they must ask this question on behalf of all Aglarond: \"Where are the Horns of Eltab?\"

She instructed both Baird and Mattrick to tell no one of that which they sought. She gave them a map, which detailed the area surrounding a declining trading town on the western edge of Anauroch, called Loudwater. East of Loudwater, the Simbarchs hoped, the ruins of Saharelgard could be found in the Fallen Lands. They two were also given two well worn pieces of parchment covered in whisper-thin text written in an unsteady hand - fragments of the Analects of Kuryon.

== To Destiny in the West ==

The two men left the following day. The captain had approved them leaving, much to everyone's surprise and there was much talk of the two of them being sent on a \"fool's errand\" but if anyone had to be lost, perhaps those two were the two they could afford to lose.

It was a long and unpleasant journey with both men having their horses killed by slavers when they were traveling through the Dales. The entire journey took three months to accomplish but the two of them did arrive in Loudwater, road-weary and nearly destitute but definitely with a great deal of purpose.
