15 Tarsakh, 1483 DR – Jasper Hawkford's Reflections 
It is early evening, and Lucan Alavandor is swinging lazily on a makeshift hammock he had a few of the FDL lads string up for him near the shore of Greenwood Lake. His hat is low, covering his eyes, and only occasionally does he swat violently at some of the season's first mosquitos that are now beginning to emerge from their slumber. 

Nearby, a few planks of wood have been lashed together and erected vertically, with a crude, yet passable, likeness of a goblin painted on. Jasper is standing about ten paces from the boards, dagger in hand, concentrating, and then... throwing! 
<whoosh, whoosh, whoosh, thunk> 
The dagger sticks into the grass about 20 feet beyond the target, and the wizard sends his telekinetic hand to retrieve it. 
Jasper once again squares himself up, takes aim and throws. 
<whoosh, whoosh, whoosh, splash!> 
Jasper, already standing barefoot and with his trousers rolled up from at least a dozen previous visits to retrieve the blade from the water, steels himself up before once again wading into the ice cold water to look for his dagger in the murky reeds. 

Lucan rolls his eyes, and pulls his hat even lower down.  "The dolt should stick to his spells," he thinks to himself, "Why did Jeska have to go and encourage him to learn to defend himself with a knife?  

"Keep at it, Jas!  You'll hit the target eventually.  Maybe try standing only five paces away," calls out Lucan. 

A few moments later, Jasper slumps down at the side of the hammock and uses his Ignition spell to rekindle a small campfire to warm himself up.  From his satchel he pulls out a small bag full of honeysuckle berries Mari had given him earlier that day.  She said it would help with the wasp bites, and might help keep any latent poison or infection at bay. 

He takes out a small kettle, and after filling it with clean water from his canteen sets to preparing a tea from the berries. 

After a few minutes of silence, poking at the fire and listening to the sounds of the camp at work intermingled with the small waves on the lake, and the wind in the trees, Jasper says to Lucan, "You know, what I can't figure out is why that temple is where it is." 
Lucan shifts in the hammock, fishing his pockets for a cheroot. 
"I mean, we know that it has something to do with Jannath, sure.  And we also meet our favourite number, three, yet again.  Ok. But why build this shrine way out there in the middle of nowhere in the woods? And you're right, why didn't Malveau or anyone else ever mention it?  Was it that well hidden?  On purpose or did the woods simply overtake it?  I do hope your letter to Vessuvian turns up some answers. And hopefully my letter to Malveau brings her here, so we can ask her ourselves.  I think we might need to do a more thorough investigation of that shrine." 

As he waits for the water to boil, Jasper gets back on his feet, grabs his dagger and squares himself up to the target again.  Ten paces. 
<Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh, splash!>
