Into Dagger Falls — Beliard, Tyrian and Carpathia Arrive
Source: Into DF.docx, from DND/Daggerdale Forum/ folder
A narrative opening scene depicting the three companions entering Dagger Falls through the Bridgegate and encountering the Sun Blades registry.

---

The sun was dipping behind the Tower as Beliard, Tyrian and Carpathia finally crossed over the Tesh into the Bridgegate. The scent of the city had grown stronger by the mile, and a wind from the Desertsmouth Mountains was bringing the full brunt of the city's miasma on the caravanners seeking to enter its walls.

"Beshaba's Breath! That's a foul perfume," remarked Beliard, wrinkling his nose atop his palfrey, Anadil, a spirited mount of Murghomi stock. As if in agreement, the horse whinnied and shook its head in apparent disapproval. "One would think the entire city has upended their chamberpots in the street to welcome us."

Carpathia looked over at him from her position atop her small wagon. "Tannery. They soak the leather in urine and dung to make it supple. That's what you smell, that and putrefied animal skins."

Beliard frowned again, blowing his nose into a handkerchief, then brightened. "Well, better them than us."

Carpathia continued. "Could be dung, anyway. Could be brains too. That's what my people used."

Tyrian looked at her from atop his destrier, incredulous, and looked about to speak when a sudden command was barked by a trio of Sun Blades that had approached from further up the line.

"DisMOUNT! Prepare yourselves for the Registry!" The gruff-looking soldier spoke the words mechanically, barely registering the three travellers before continuing past and repeating the words to the next in line. Beliard noted, however, that the two soldiers that followed were much more attentive, their eyes lingering suspiciously on Tyrian's armour and Carpathia's wagon. Beliard met their gaze as they walked past him. They stared back, unperturbed, then proceeded down the line, following their commanding officer.

"What is your name, and from whence do you hail?" Two bespectacled clerks in yellow robes approached the travellers, having questioned and inventoried the wagon team immediately ahead of them. They looked to Carpathia like two hawks — predatory, alert.
