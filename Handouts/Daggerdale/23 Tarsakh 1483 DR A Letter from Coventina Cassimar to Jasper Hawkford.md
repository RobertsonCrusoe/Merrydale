23 Tarsakh, 1483 DR – A Letter from Coventina Cassimar to Jasper Hawkford 
Dearest Jasper; 
I worry about you every day.  Though we got to spend very little time together you might think, you are the only young man I've had a real talk with in a couple of years.  It is lonely in Cassimar Tower, and Aunt Lilly's obligations weigh me down, though she doesn't realize it.  I don't begrudge her this weight, as we all have a role to play. 

I often wonder what terrible adventures you've fallen into.  I hear things from time-to-time.  I probably hear more than I am supposed to, which is exactly why I write you today, and hopefully no one else has read this. 

Just yesterday there was a heated discussion among the Adjudicating Committee.  I didn't realize they had any work left to do, now that their silly and reckless contest is over, and you're away in the forest risking your life for them.  I hope that stupid pile of old stones on Thistle Ridge is worth the risk.  I can't imagine how it might be. 

At any rate, I overhead Aunt Lilly and the others in one of our drawing rooms.  They had assembled without Bishop Whitegate, which isn't unusual (he's a bit of a snob) and of course the General is up at the Castle with all the other 'heroes' fighting the 'enemy' or something.  It was just Aunt Lilly, the Ambassador from Charity (he's nice, I like him) and that earthy-smelling old Chauntean matron, Maddy Oakdale.  I know I shouldn't be snooping, but this is what I heard them say; 

Lilly Cassimar: "I don't like it one bit.  We shouldn't be meeting in secret about something so important" 

Arthur Whitmore: "There is no harm in a little discretion, Madame Mayor.  No harm whatsoever in getting our ducks in a row, and figuring out how best to address this with the Bishop.  If we don't articulate our position clearly, the Bishop's hound will bite all of our hands, and that will be that.  It will be over before it's begun." 

Maddy Oakdale: "He's right, Lilly.  His perspective and professionalism here are an asset.  We've known the Bishop and Invictus too long, and have too much baggage to make this decision clearly.  Let's listen to what he has to say"  A pause 

AW: "Thank you, Maddy.  You don't mind if I'm familiar in these cozy rooms, do you?  Much obliged, I'm sure!  Invictus has the Bishop convinced that the place must be destroyed utterly, right away.  If half of what we've heard is true, then perhaps there is a case to be made for haste, and for blind faith.  But we have heard everything third-hand, and don't really know the truth of it, do we?" 

MO: "I require only that a quarter of it be true, to side with the Amaunatori in this instance.  What was found is evidence of the Netherese pantheon infiltrating our society, even if it was with a people that are now gone, and in a place that remained concealed from us for so long.  I can't imagine what might be there that merits further investigation.  Or at the very least, what could there be there that warrants the expenditure of time and resources, our own, or those of Chrysalis?  Why not wipe it from the game board and be done with it?  One less variable to consider?" 

LC: "I'm inclined to side with you, Maddy, except for the fact that I've never heard you endorse a strategy from the mind of Invictus" 
MO: "I hope you're not implying that he's gotten to me.  I assure you" 
AW: "No one is suggesting that, Maddy.  We know you are above reproach here, and further that I doubt Invictus would try any of his tricks with you.  Your sanctity is beyond dispute, and I doubt he wants to challenge you.  But I share Lilly's curiosity.  As I understand it, the ritual that Invictus is suggesting is something of a salted-field approach.  No turning back and all that.  If there is one useful bit of information, or even a way to leverage the temple in the future, that option will be taken off the table." 

MO: "I understand that.  I just can't imagine what we could hope to glean from the place, or how it might be a lever down the road.  To me, it's just a gigantic liability.  What's more, it's a bit of a thumb in the eye of the people that settled up there.  Everyone has enough reason to hate Sembians, without suspecting them of worshiping the Ancient Faith.  Which by the way they do, in some parts of Sembia.  It's bad enough that this might actually be true.  Let's wipe it all clean." 

LC: "Your zeal here could backfire on you, Maddy, be careful.  'wiping clean' history is something that the Bishop preaches against, albeit with a great deal of hypocrisy.  All the same, Invictus always has his ears listening for this type of talk.  Who knows who might hear this sort of talk and get the wrong idea" 

MO: "Now I'm convinced.  If you think it's a bad idea because you don't trust Invictus, and you also think I am wrong because I want to start with a fresh garden bed up on Thistle Ridge and bury the past like a Sharran" a pause and a bit of uncomfortable shuffling of chairs "yes, a Sharran, that Invictus would put to the rack, then I expect I am exactly right here. I'm going to let Monarch know my mind, on my own terms.  Ultimately, I'll support whatever she decides.  She was there, we weren't.  She's risking her life in this campaign, we aren't" 

AW: "Are we not, Maddy?  No indeed?" 
LC: "All leadership comes with risks, we all know this.  And most of these jobs don't come with a retirement plan, like some journeyman in a guild.  I've made my peace with that.  For my part, I will also inform the Captain of my decision, and will likewise abide by her own counsel." 

AW: "Ah yes, my turn, I suppose?  What is the counsel of one Charity-born man next to that of such powerful women?  I doubt Monarch cares a fig for my opinion, but I will caution her against taking brash actions, and like your learned and wizened selves, leave her to make up her own mind.  We shall see if Invictus and the Bishop are so circumspect" 

That is all I heard of this troubling discourse, but it is enough.  Aunt Lilly and the Ambassador are wrong, and for once, Invictus is right.  Your team should tear down that abominable temple, and do whatever it is he asks of you. 

I earnestly hope you'll come by the Tower when next you're in town.  I won't submit you to the labyrinth next time, but will be a proper hostess, and see you to a comfortable drawing room straight away and see to your every comfort. 
Until then I am, 
Your dearest friend, 
Coventina Cassimar
