This is the largest building in the Outer Bailey.  It is built against the outer wall, and is a solid stone structure, 3 stories tall, with a 100' long by 50' thick footprint.  While the rest of the buildings in the Outer Bailey have doors opening at ground level, this structure is set a ½ story higher than its neighbours, and a broad double-stair leads from the ground to the stone balcony in front of the great double doors that grant entry.  

The windows of the ground floor are all small and set high, so that there is no easy entrance into this building below 12’ from the ground level.

A huge set of iron-bound solid oak double doors is set in the middle of the long face of the first floor, and they are carved and painted to resemble a pair of dwarves sitting in front of an enormous, roaring hearth.

Inside, an enormous tap room dominates most of the space of the first floor, and a soaring ceiling with two levels of balconies beneath gives the place a very spacious feel.  There are 4 rows of tables with benches built into them that fill most of the tap room.  A huge bar lines the west wall of the taproom, and the wall behind it climbs up the entire height of the building, uninterrupted by the balconies which line the other three walls.  Towards the top of the wall, a great round stained glass window admits light through a colourful depiction of the same two dwarves seen on the doors of the place, this time hiking in the mountains beneath a bright sun.

During meal times, this place is absolutely packed and bustling.  There might be as many as 120 customers in here eating and sharing stories and songs, and a dozen frenzied boys going from table to table with trays of food which they sell on the spot.

The bar is also busy, but a sign above it reads “Two drinks per meal, maximum” tends to keep men from loitering there like they might in any other taproom.

A typical meal here consists of skewers of potato, onions and game, and a bowl of rich broth, which costs 2 sp.  There is only one type of beer served here, which is the house brand; it is a mealy, dark drink that costs 1 sp/tankard.

==Rooms==

Rooms can be rented here on a nightly or tenday basis.  There are two types of rooms; 
*Standard; 10’x10’ room that sleep 2 on a bunk bed.  There is a small desk and 2 foot lockers each with a lock and key.  These rooms go for 5sp/night.
*Large; 10’x15’ room that sleeps 4 on 2 bunk beds.  This room has 4 footlockers, each with a lock and key, and a small table and 4 chairs.  These rooms go for 1 gp/night.

==Staff==

The proprietor of this establishment is Turig Forester.  He is a native of Daggerdale from a successful farming family.  He is a plain looking man with plain mannerisms.  He has a memory like an iron trap, though, and never forgets a face or the bottom line on the bill.

Turig has 3 sons that help him manage the place, and has a staff of nearly 20 young men and boys as well.  Turig insists that a fortress is no place for women, and hasn’t hired any to date.
