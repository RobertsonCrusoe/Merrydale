Jasper's Dream - The Clarandal Nightmare and the Return of Mortimer
by Gus Miranda (as Jasper Hawkford)
Email thread: "Jasper's Dream", 29-30 Sep 2024

A dream sequence written by Gus Miranda, originally sent privately to Les and then shared with the whole group. Set a few nights after the party's return from Clarandal Farm, it depicts Jasper's nightmare of the burning farm, the whispering voice from the room where he found the dolls, and the horrific deaths of his companions. The dream culminates in Mortimer's transformation into a shadow-touched familiar — black fur, empty eye sockets — and the revelation: "She waits for you." Gus used this narrative to justify taking the Multiclass Witch Dedication for Jasper's character build.

Gus's note: I sent this to Les before, but I'm sharing it with the whole class now for a little extra context. This occurred a few nights after returning from Clarandal Farm:


Jasper tosses and turns in his bed.

I am running through the forest now. It is night time... or at least, it is dark... I really can't tell. All the light seems to have been drained from the forest, and all I can see is shades of grey and shadow. Where am I? I can hear the shrieks of the goblins approaching from behind. I am running again. Yes, I remember now... or do I? The smell of the burning Clarandal Farm is in the air. The goblins are chasing us, as we run, run, run back to Greenwood Farm.

But I don't see the others. I don't feel them nearby anymore.

I must run.

Run.

Run!


There is a clearing ahead. The trees part, and... this is no clearing. This is the ruined husk of the Clarandal Farm. But the fire has died down. The fire is gone. This is Clarandal, but there is no sign of goblins, or of the battle that just took place, or of the fire. It is an empty, abandoned ruin in the forest. It is silent.

It is just an empty ruin. Sitting in shadow and silen-!

"Jasp..."

A voice! All the hairs on my body stand on end, and I freeze on the spot. I dare not even breathe.

"... per... Jasp..."

It is but a whisper, but the voice is there. Calling my name. It comes from the house. From the second floor. From the room where I found the dolls.

I try to walk away, but instead I move closer towards the house.

Before I can even think, I am at the door. But this is no ruin. The house is standing as new, the gardens are trimmed and maintained, the forest held back, the door is in place. I reach out and touch the polished oak wood and the door swings open with no effort. Everything is where it should be: carpets, tapestries, furniture, chandeliers. The whole house is as it was in its prime, and yet there is no colour here: just shadows.

And there is no life. There is no one here. The candles burn in the candelabras, but there is no light. There is no life.

"Jasper..." again the voice, just a faint whisper in the wind. It slithers down the stairs, like a serpent coming to coil itself around my legs.

I try to turn and run out of this infernal building.

But now I am at the top of the stairs, I am looking down the corridor to where the open door awaits.

The building is a ruin once again. This is the Clarandal that we found. The Clarandal Farm I know. I can hear the sounds of battle below, and beside me. The others are fighting off the goblins. I can hear myself casting the spell that will mimic the voice of the dead girl.

That voice...

Is it the same?

I must find out. Beside me, my friends begin to die. I turn to see Lucan disembowelled; Mari lies on the floor a short distance away, her head bent on the neck at an impossible angle; Ser Rowan is hunched at the top of the stairs with a warpick stuck in his forehead. Only Jeska fights on, but I can hear the mass of goblins grabbing, tearing and pulling her down - hers will be a fate worse than death.

I turn away from them all and walk towards the open door.

"...Jasper..."

I reach the room and look at the darkness inside...


Jasper awakens with a start, leaping out of his bed, tripping and falling on the floor hard. He stands, gritting his teeth through the pain, and hurries towards the window. He flings open the shutters and lets in the cool morning air, but more importantly, the rays of early sunlight. Of colour! A world of colour! The darkness and the shadows retreat to the edges of the room.

He slumps down to the floor, breathing heavily.

He sees movement on his bed. Jasper leaps up to his feet, a spell at the ready.

From the tangle of sheets, a squirrel emerges, and sits on the edge staring intensely at the young wizard.

"Mortimer?" Jasper is baffled for a second. But then a chill runs through Jasper's spine, and the memory of the dream returns.

This is Mortimer, of that there is no doubt. The bond between wizard and familiar is unbreakable. But this Mortimer is different. His fur is no longer the grey so common amongst the Dales squirrels, but rather a shadowy mass of deep black. His eyes are gone, leaving only empty sockets behind.

The squirrel speaks with a voice that echoes from the depths of a long forgotten grave: "She waits for you."
