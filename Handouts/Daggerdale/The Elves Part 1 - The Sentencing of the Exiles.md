The Elves, Part 1 — The Sentencing of the Exiles

Source: Email from Les Blackwell to the group, 12 January 2011. Subject: 'The Elves, Part 1'

Summary: Les's narrative covering the sentencing of three wild elves — Ineluki Elderbough, Eremus Elderbough, and Cirdan Vardamir — by Marshall Medeila in a Cormanthor courtroom. They are found guilty of murdering three Dalesmen and violating their Sacred Oath to maintain treaties. Sentenced to an unprecedented 160 years of exile (60 for the murders plus 100 for the oath-breaking), to be served entirely in Daggerdale under Ambassador Miritar's command. The sentence can be shortened if Miritar deems them redeemed. Also references Ultho Elderbough, previously exiled for 20 years in a related case.

---

*Sentencing*

Marshall Medeila has the three renegades, Ineluki and Eremus Elderbough, and Cirdan Vardamir arrayed before her in the 'courtroom'. This room has walls made up of alternating sections of delicately carved white stone and great oaks; the boughs of the oaks provide a mushroom-like canopy around the walls, but leave a 30' opening in the middle, down which sunlight falls, illuminating the three on the white stone dais with the light of day. In the semi-shadowy perimeter of the room, scores of elves, both wood and Eladrin, stand silently, waiting for the Marshall to deliver her sentence.

The Marshall steps out of the shadow, and onto a white stone dais, this one smaller than but elevated above that of the three wild elves. She wears a solemn expression on her face, and seems to be stuck in contemplation of something for a long time before she speaks.

"It has been 33 years since I stood here and was asked to pass judgement on one of our kind" she begins, addressing the three below her, but her voice filling the entire courtroom as though magically amplified. "The unenviable duty of judgement here is most often a case of us against those that encroach upon our land. However, in this case, and that last which I have mentioned, I am forced to pass judgement on our own kind." The fact that she has declared wood and Eladrin together as 'our kind' is not lost on the indicted or the crowd watching. There is something of a collective murmur or shuffling, something not quite whispers or gasps, but the subtle fey variety of these expressions washes over the courtroom. Marshall Medeila is clearly playing some politics here, as well as acting as judge.

"The last time I passed judgement like this was on Ultho Elderbough. I don't think I need to bring up the details of that case. Everyone now in attendance was present for that sentencing as well." Memories flood back to the younger elves that stand to be sentenced. The memory of Ultho being cast out of Cormanthor for 20 years, a small amount of time for a long-lived race, but a painful exile all the same, comes back to the three accused with a mixture of bitterness and pride. Ultho had walked out of that courtroom that day without turning back to say a word. He never said goodbye to his weeping family or anyone else, and was not seen for 20 years to the day. The thought of such an exile twists in the guts of both those that stand to be sentenced, and those that watch the proceedings.

"Today, I must pass judgement and proclaim sentencing on three of our kin, rather than one. This is troubling for all involved. It is even more troubling because of the clan-ties to the last of our kind to be sentenced in such a matter. I will now proceed with Judgement.

"Ineluki Elderbough, Eremus Elderbough, and Cirdan Vardamir, you have all been found guilty of violating your Sacred Oath to maintain our Treaties with the Dalesmen to the west. You are all found guilty of inflicting an undue measure of violence against three logging camps in Daggerdale. You are all found guilty of murder..." with this word, there is again that sensation of hushed whispers and gasps in the courtroom, though only a fey would perceive it. "You have killed three humans, and I hold each of your guilty for each death."

The Marshall pauses for a long time after that, and the whisperings and anxiety of the fey crowd grows to such a point that she is forced to hold out both her hands in a gesture asking for silence, which is followed immediately. No one wishes to attract her attention on a day such as this.

"I will now proceed with sentencing" she declares, her words coming coldly and matter-of-factly, all the warmth of her regrets and other emotions having vanished after the pause.

"We are well aware of what you three would be sentenced to, under the laws of the Dalesmen. You would be hanged, and rightly so. If our laws allowed for the destruction of our own kin, I would pass this sentence without a thought; your behaviour has put a century of diplomacy in peril. In fact, I'd let the Dalesmen hang you themselves, and leave your bodies hanging from the gallows to decompose in whatever crude and base fashion the carrion creatures of the wood would allow" and with this, the anxiety and surprise in the audience is palpable to any watching, and the worried looks on the faces of the three elves being sentenced are too much for many of their kin to bear; many relatives turn away, while others are working up the nerve to make an open protest. Before the crowd's anxiety can escalate, however, she brings her hands up again to silence everyone.

"However, our laws do not permit such barbaric punishment, even though you yourselves have meted out such wickedness to our allies. Our laws are optimistic, and reflect our belief that time and introspection can correct such flaws in one's character." There is now a palpable relief passing through the crowd and the accused. This is short lived, though. "I am not as optimistic. I think you three stand little hope for redemption. I think you three will continue to be a threat to our community and our allegiances. But, *some* have hope for you, and because you have inspired *some* by the strength of your convictions, erroneous though they are, I have arrived at the following sentence..." She pauses, and there isn't so much as a leaf rustling in the forest canopy above to break the deafening silence.

"For the murder of three Dalesmen, you are each sentenced to 20 years of exile for each of the slain men." There is a tumultuous wave of gasps and whispered protest in the courtroom, especially from the clansmen of the sentenced. 60 years! That is triple the punishment handed down to Ultho!

"For the breaking of your Oath to maintain our Treaties, you are each sentenced to a further 100 years of exile..." she has hardly spoken the word 'exile' before there are near-riotous protests from the crowd. However, the audience is divided into two groups; surprised Eladrin and outraged wood elves. "Silence!" she shouts, and the room falls obediently and instantly silent.

"That is 160 years of exile for each of you, from Cormanthor. You know that our laws allow for execution in only one instance, and that is the breach of exile. I will make this clear; only on pain of death will you again set foot in our Kingdom for the next 160 years." The Marshall has to wave her hands with a bit more enthusiasm to break the protests in the courtroom at this declaration.

"However, as I have said, there are some parties that feel that you are redeemable. And so, I attach one caveat to this sentence; you are to spend the entire duration of your exile in Daggerdale, under the command of Ambassador Miritar. The Ambassador will direct you as he sees fit, and in such a fashion as to foster good will between our people and the Dalesmen. When the Ambassador feels you are fit to return home, you will be allowed to return."

The emotions in the courtroom are varied and obvious; there are quietly weeping clansmen of the three accused; little sisters, mothers, cousins. There are stern-faced males that glare defiance at the Marshall in one instance, and disappointment to their three kin the next. There are Eladrin from a half-dozen households whispering about law and precedent, and discussing the politics and nature of the Ambassador. All the while, the three stand motionless, facing the Marshall, all desperately wanting to give sidelong glances to their kin, but all held to the standard of order for this courtroom.

After a few moments of the three being locked into the cold face of the Eladrin judge, and the tumult going on around them, Marshall Medeila raises her hands for silence, which is again instantly and dutifully obeyed. She then raises one arm, and points out the western archway of the canopy.

"What are you waiting for?"
