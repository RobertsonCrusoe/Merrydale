STEPHEN'S SETUP:

Lucan is frustrated by coming up empty on his assessment of that shrine's potential significance to the Alavandor/Clarandal settlers. The fact that it is around a century old certainly seems to date it to around the time of the Sembians' arrival. It seems like it could be a clue to their involvement in Thistle Ridge, but if so a fairly opaque one.

A connection to the Ancient Mother, Jannath, seems highly likely.

He's also curious about the fact that the shrine doesn't seem to have been part of any reconnaissance reports. Surely there must be a record of it somewhere. We tasked Brother Vessuvian with scouring the Yellow Abbey for information on the Sembian Families, but it's possible that the shrine was not primarily identified with them, and so wouldn't have come up in his research. Lucan is going to send a letter to BV detailing the statues, the stonework, any architectural features of note, and the location. He'd like BV to look for any records mentioning the shrine at the Yellow Abbeye, suggesting that he also speak to rangers like Malvau Greenleaf and older woodsmen and foresters. A place like that would have made a good shelter against the elements or way station for countrymen and scouts.

---

Dear Brother Vessuvian,

I trust this letter finds you in good health and high spirits (at least as high as one can manage while surrounded by the weight of ancient tomes and the ceaseless demands of scholarly inquiry). I write to you with a matter of some intrigue—one that I believe will benefit greatly from your particular talents, which, while perhaps not as obvious as my own, are nonetheless well-suited to the task at hand.

Recently, while exploring the woods west of Dagger Falls with Company Chrysalis, we stumbled upon a peculiar shrine. Its construction is unremarkable—limestone with a slate roof, roughly a century old—but its isolation struck me as unusual. The shrine houses the remnants of three statues that appear to have portrayed a young woman holding a lamb and a sheaf of wheat, a pregnant matron holding a ewe, and a hale older woman holding a sheep. The pastoral imagery is striking, though its significance eludes me.

What I find most intriguing is that the shrine does not appear in any reconnaissance reports or local records we have encountered. Surely a place like this would have been noted by rangers, woodsmen, or even the occasional traveler seeking shelter. Its absence from the historical record suggests it was either deliberately concealed or simply forgotten—neither of which sits well with me.

I am commissioning you to investigate this shrine and uncover its origins. Here's what I propose:

Scour the Abbeye's Records: Look for any mention of a shrine matching this description. Its age places it around a century old, so it may appear in records from that time.

Interview the Locals: Speak to rangers like Malvau Greenleaf and any older woodsmen or foresters who might recall tales of such a place. A shrine like this would have made an excellent shelter or waystation, and someone must remember it.

Examine Architectural Styles: While the shrine's construction is unremarkable, it struck me as slightly familiar. Perhaps it aligns with a local or regional tradition that has since fallen out of use.

I've included a detailed description of the shrine's location, along with sketches of the statues and notes on the stonework. (I trust you'll appreciate my thoroughness, though I understand if you find it daunting.) Do let me know if you require further clarification—I'm always happy to lend my expertise to those less accustomed to such investigations.

I have every confidence that you'll rise to the occasion, Brother Vessuvian. After all, even the most unassuming of scholars can produce remarkable results when properly motivated. Do keep me apprised of your progress, and don't hesitate to reach out if you find yourself in need of my considerable insight.

Yours in scholarly pursuit (and with the utmost patience),
Lucan Alavandor

GUS'S RESPONSE - JASPER AT GREENWOOD LAKE:

It is early evening, and Lucan Alaandor is swinging lazily on a makeshift hammock he had a few of the FDL lads string up for him near the shore of Greenwood Lake. His hat is low, covering his eyes, and only occasionally does he swat violently at some of the season's first mosquitos that are now beginning to emerge from their slumber.

Nearby, a few planks of wood have been lashed together and erected vertically, with a crude, yet passable, likeness of a goblin painted on. Jasper is standing about ten paces from the boards, dagger in hand, concentrating, and then... throwing!

<whoosh, whoosh, whoosh, thunk>

The dagger sticks into the grass about 20 feet beyond the target, and the wizard sends his telekinetic hand to retrieve it.

Jasper once again squares himself up, takes aim and throws.

<whoosh, whoosh, whoosh, splash!>

Jasper, already standing barefoot and with his trousers rolled up from at least a dozen previous visits to retrieve the blade from the water, steels himself up before once again wading into the ice cold water to look for his dagger in the murky reeds.

Lucan rolls his eyes, and pulls his hat even lower down.  "The dolt should stick to his spells," he thinks to himself, "why did Jeska have to go and encourage him to learn to defend himself with a knife?"

"Keep at it Jas!  You'll hit the target eventually.  Maybe try standing only five paces away," calls out Lucan.

A few moments later, Jasper slumps down at the side of the hammock and uses his Ignition spell to rekindle a small campfire to warm himself up.  From his satchel he pulls out a small bag full of honeysuckle berries Mari had given him earlier that day.  She said it would help with the wasp bites, and might help keep any latent poison or infection at bay.

He takes out a small kettle, and after filling it with clean water from his canteen sets to preparing a tea from the berries.

After a few minutes of silence, poking at the fire and listening to the sounds of the camp at work intermingled with the small waves on the lake, and the wind in the trees, Jasper says to Lucan, "You know, what I can't figure out is why that temple is where it is."

Lucan shifts in the hammock, fishing his pockets for a cheroot.

"I mean, we know that it has something to do with Jannath, sure.  And we also meet our favourite number, three, yet again.  Ok. But why build this shrine way out there in the middle of nowhere in the woods? And you're right, why didn't Malveau or anyone else ever mention it?  Was it that well hidden?  On purpose or did the woods simply overtake it?  I do hope your letter to Vessuvian turns up some answers. And hopefully my letter to Malveau brings her here, so we can ask her ourselves.  I think we might need to do a more thorough investigation of that shrine."

As he waits for the water to boil, Jasper gets back on his feet, grabs his dagger and squares himself up to the target again.  Ten paces.

<Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh, splash!>
