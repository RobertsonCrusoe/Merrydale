On Beholders
Here is the information that has been gathered on the beholders that have played a role in this tale.

Zantiid. This beholder is thought to be the motivating force behind Company Drurginz in the Summer of

71. It is thought that this purple eyed beholder (first seen by Ellyth in CRH HQ in early summer) is an ally of Thagdul, possibly his boss. This same purple-eyed beholder was spotted in Undermountain at Dark Market by Esperel. Additionally, the mark of this beholder, discovered somehow by Quellar Dalael, was found on a note to Malkor in Neverwinter.

Xulla. This is a green eyed beholder that was suspected to have been the force behind Company Ersgul. This beholder was known to Quevin Dalael. Quevin observed that Xulla often had Company Dalael run missions counter to Company Ersgul, however. Xulla was spotted in the Wavesilver Villa by Esperel, and tried to recover the beholder staff stolen from Yarkul. Xulla was also present at the Phaulkon Cargo heist by the Drow, in the sewers. At that time, Xulla and Quevin exchanged harsh words before parting. It was Xulla who turned Rychel to stone.

Vuldan. This beholder is smaller and has only 4 eye stalks. It was known to guard the laboratory of Dally, and has no record of taking violent action against anyone who has intruded into his space.

Directors. There were four of these monsters encountered on the road from Daggerfalls to Anauroch. They had beholder heads attached to large centipede bodies, and were capable of much physical harm, as well as magical harming spells. They were difficult to beat, and were only bested when the servants of Entropy arrived.

A beholder-kin similar to Vuldan inhabited the Irlingstar observatory that Jafar had commandeered for his research. This creature was slain by the drow.

Amon and Zuth, the servants of Entropy that we met in Dagger Falls, told us that Zantiid and Xulla were second-rank beholders, serving a more powerful master that they were tracking.

A note intercepted in Neverwinter discussed the disposal of 'cargo' that had been collected by Malkor's operation. It was to be transferred to a place known as Castle Haerytar, where it was to be met by someone/thing known as "The Eye". This was never investigated. Beljuril has the map.
