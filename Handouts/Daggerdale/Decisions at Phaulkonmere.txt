On Greengrass Eve of 1372, a group of adventurers and heroes comes together at Phaulkonmere, the villa purchased by the Company of the Red Herring, to discuss the Zhent and Drow threats to the City and points beyond. Many present were there because they had been involved in this mess for so long. Others were there through a sense of duty to their fellow man. Some were there to seek as adventure, fame and fortune, and others were there for more selfish reasons. And some were there for the simple reason of pursuing vengeance.

Agamemnon, back from the Eastern Heartlands, told the council that, whatever the truth of the man by the same name who claimed to be a Harper in disguise, the “original” Thagdul had been a servant of Manshoon in Waterdeep. Indeed, Thagdul was conspicuously absent from this meeting, though Jafar insisted that he acted as Thagdul's 'representative'. This did little to improve the CRH members’ opinions of this wayward mage.

== Ill Tidings ==

Events current and past were discussed. From the East, Boregar brought a report most grim. The Zhents had taken over Dagger Falls once again, and Randal Morn himself had been hanged from the new bridge over the Tesh River. Yarkul had been installed as ruler of Daggerdale, and his rule was enforced by an elite mercenary company known as The Fist. Further, churches to Bane and Iyachtu Xvim had been built, and the town appeared to be taken for good.

Further, the Zhents had hundreds of slaves toiling in a number of mines. It was suspected that Hob was imprisoned in one of these slave camps. Outposts had been built in Anauroch, and Zhent caravans were coming and going through the Great Sand Sea with little difficulty, and the town of Dagger Falls was growing rich off the trade through its gates, as it is the gateway to Anauroch from the East.

All trade through the more traditional routes (such as through the gap at Tilverton and then through Cormyr) had ceased due to a couple of reasons. Tilverton had been overrun by goblinoid hordes again, and a Zhent banner flew from the city’s walls. Cormyr was involved in a costly war with the Sembians over a border-mountain wherein Mithril had been discovered. The resurrection of Bane had much to do with the problems in the East, but the Zhents had seemed also to have played the hand that was dealt them in a most cunning manner there.

== Lingering Threats ==

In summary, almost all trade between the Western Heartlands, The North, Amn, Tethyr and Calimshan was coming through the desert upon Zhent cargo trains, or paying huge fees to use their roads and buy safe passage. The Zhents were growing wealthy, and most of this wealth seemed to be pouring into Dagger Falls, where extensive fortifications were being constructed, notably at the Citadel of the Raven. Clearly, an expedition would have to be sent east to set things straight. Yarkul, at least, would die - Lady Midnight herself had accepted Sparrow's pledge to depose the Red Herrings' resuscitated nemesis.

In the North, a second objective was set. Malkor would have to be brought down and the roads made safe once again. The work of the Road Mage was not complete, and Beljuril and Rufus had made a pledge that they would honour.

Finally, the City of Splendours itself became the last objective. It was clear that the Zhents still retained too much influence in this city, and indeed, much of their wealth and power structure was now located here. With the Company Dalael as another huge problem to be fixed, perhaps the group should have assigned a more experienced task force to this objective. Nevertheless, Agamemnon was to remain in the City to provide guidance.

==Investiture==

The following day was Greengrass, in name if not form. In the hours before dawn during a raging snowstorm, Beljuril, Jeryth, Raven and Boregar descended to the basement of the Phaulkonmere residence, where they soon met Agamemnon, who brought with him a man who was a stranger to the heroes, yet known to all of them. This man - Khelben Arunsun - was a senior member of the Harper organization in the Western Realms, and authorized the investiture of Raven, Rufus and Beljuril as Harper initiates. By this measure, the Harpers conveyed their support of the three missions.

Later that day, on Phaulkonmere's main floor, another investiture took place, as Garth officially registered Zeke, Brandil and Raven as members of the Company of the Red Herring, which at the time commanded a share of 1455 gp each.
