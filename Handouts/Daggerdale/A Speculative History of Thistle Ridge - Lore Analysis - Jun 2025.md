PART 1: STEPHEN'S SPECULATIVE HISTORY — STEPHEN ROBERTSON (18 Jun 2025)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message 197845c0956a53e2

"I've been revisiting the handouts, and here's my current theory of what
happened in Thistle Ridge:

PRE-1403 DR: THE CURSE AND THE DECEPTIVE SOLUTION

The story begins in the Sembian city of Daerlun during the ongoing war
against the Shadovar of Netheril. Two families, who would later take the
names Alavandor and Clarandal, were deliberately targeted by the Shadow. As
a strategic act of magical warfare, the Shadovar placed a 'curse of
generation' upon their bloodlines, an affliction designed to cause
infertility and ensure the families would have no future. This curse was
the specific, calculated impetus that would force the families onto a
predetermined path.

Facing the extinction of their lines, the desperate families sought a
solution and were guided to a local coven. This coven, acting as agents for
the Shadow, offered a comprehensive, multi-stage plan for their survival
and escape. The first condition was that the families must shed their
cursed identities to hide from the Netherese who knew them by name.
Following these supernatural instructions, the patriarch orchestrated the
complete buyout and permanent exile of the original, poorer Alavandor
family. This ruthless task was completed by 1403 DR, securing a new
identity under which they could flee.

c. 1407 DR: A PATH BOUGHT WITH BLOOD

With their new identities established, the families fulfilled the second
and final condition of the coven's pact. The price for the knowledge of a
sanctuary and the magical means of safe passage was a blood sacrifice.
Around 1407 DR, as Aisling Maeliticus would later confess, her two
sisters, Abrecia and Senthia Maeliticus, 'paid the ultimate price for us,
their blood buying favor with the coven that freed us'. This horrific act
sealed the bargain that would lead them, in Aisling's words, 'to come
here,' to a specific sanctuary in the Dales — Thistle Ridge — whose innate
sacred energies were potent enough to counteract the Netherese curse, but
also the very place where the Shadow's other agent, Palus, lay in wait.

THE NATURAL CURE AND THE ORCHESTRATED DECEPTION (1408 DR)

When the Alavandor and Clarandal families arrived in the valley of Thistle
Ridge in 1408, the guidance of the Daerlun coven proved true. The potent,
life-giving nature of the sacred land began to immediately counteract the
Netherese 'curse of generation' that had plagued their bloodlines. For the
first time in years, the possibility of bearing children became real, a
miracle occurring simply by virtue of their presence in this new sanctuary.

But they were not alone. This natural miracle was the trigger for the next
phase of the Shadow's plan. Contessa Palus, the ancient hag of the
Sorrowbog and the Shadow's chief regional asset, had been waiting for them.
Informed by her dark master of the pawns' impending arrival, she was ready
to act.

Almost immediately upon or soon after their arrival, Palus revealed herself
to the Sembian leaders, likely Aisling and Alvund Alavandor. She did not
appear as a hag, but in a powerful form befitting a goddess, presenting
herself as Jannath, an ancient deity of life and fertility. She took
direct and immediate credit for the lifting of their curse, framing the
land's natural miracle as her divine and personal intervention — a blessing
bestowed upon them for faithfully following the path set for them by
Netherese agents in Daerlun.

THE NEW PACT AND THE 'DOUBLE MASQUERADE'

The desperate and grateful families, witnessing the 'proof' of their curse
lifting, accepted her divinity without question. They entered into a new
pact, not with a distant coven, but with the powerful 'goddess' who stood
before them. This marked the true beginning of the 'double masquerade' that
would define Thistle Ridge for the next three decades.

Publicly, the Sembians became industrious citizens, and Aisling used her
knowledge to act as a benevolent, Chauntean-like healer. Secretly, however,
they were now the core of a new cult dedicated to Jannath. Aisling, as her
high priestess, was forced to lead the community in the 'obscene' rituals
Palus demanded, such as 'The Consummation'. The purpose of these rituals
was to continuously reinforce the lie: that the families' continued
fertility was a gift dispensed by Jannath, not a natural property of the
land they inhabited. This deception cemented Palus's power, allowing her to
slowly corrupt the very sanctuary the families believed they had found.

With her deception successful and her authority as the goddess 'Jannath'
firmly established, Contessa Palus began the next phase of her plan: the
systematic elimination of the region's benevolent fey spirits. Her first
target was her own sister, the dryad Quercina, who was bound to the great
oak Harngorn.

THE MANDATE OF THE GODDESS

Palus could not risk a direct confrontation. Instead, she used her
influence over her new followers. In her guise as Jannath, she would have
inspired (if not commanded) the Sembian leaders to undertake a grand public
works project: a modern sewer network. To her followers, this was presented
as a divine blessing — a project that would bring clean water, improve
sanitation, and further establish their dominion over the landscape,
proving the superiority of their new faith and their goddess's favor. The
Sembians, eager to solidify their new home and please their patron deity,
would have embraced the task with vigor.

THE PROFANE PROJECT

The construction was a massive undertaking. A 'complicated network of
sewers and tunnels' was excavated from the limestone bedrock beneath the
settlement. Guided by Palus's secret intent, the network was designed with
a specific bias: it serviced the 'newer parts of Thistle Ridge, where our
people reside,' while conspicuously bypassing the older section of town
inhabited by the Thistle family and the Shrine to Silvanus.

The core of the project was the harnessing of the 'great underground river
that flowed through' the caves beneath the ridge. This life-giving artery
was captured and 'redirected to some mysterious bottomless cavern deep
below.' This was the specific act that, as Palus later boasted, 'profaned
the Waters' and 'drove the Waters to the depths.'

THE OUTCOME: A RIVAL WEAKENED

While the Sembians saw a triumph of engineering, Palus achieved her true,
sinister objective. By redirecting the vast underground river, she
deliberately starved the deep roots of Harngorn, the great oak that was her
sister Quercina's anchor to the world.

This act did not destroy Quercina outright, but it crippled her. Her power
diminished and her connection to the land severely weakened, she was forced
to retreat into seclusion. From the perspective of Aisling and the other
Sembians, the once-present nature spirit simply vanished. They believed
their industry had 'driven [her] off years ago,' viewing it as a natural
consequence of progress. They were unaware that their greatest achievement
was, in fact, the successful execution of the first stage of the hag's
personal vendetta, leaving her rival weakened and isolated for decades to
come.

THE TWIN DECEPTION AND THE HIDDEN PRODIGY (1427–1438 DR)

Around 1427 DR, during one of the 'Consummation' rituals she commanded,
Palus secretly copulated with Andarl Alavandor, the son of her high
priestess, Aisling. As his wife, Susserah, had conceived naturally with
twins around the same time, this provided the perfect opportunity for the
hag's most insidious plot. In 1428 DR, when Susserah gave birth, one twin,
Dorian, was born healthy, but the second was stillborn. In the chaos of the
moment, Palus orchestrated a swap, likely arranged through Aisling, by
substituting her own newborn hagspawn daughter, Lobelia, for the deceased
Alavandor infant. Andarl and Susserah accepted the girl as their own, her
differently colored eyes — one grey like a Swamp Hag's, one blue like an
Alavandor's — seen as nothing more than a curious quirk.

The family patriarch, Alvund Alavandor, died in 1430 DR, an event his
wife Aisling would later suspect was murder orchestrated by Palus. As the
years passed, Lobelia's supernatural heritage manifested as extraordinary
talent. In 1435, a visiting Tergus Clarandal marveled at his 8-year-old
niece's 'unparalleled' martial prowess, mistaking her sinister nature for a
sign that she was 'touched for greatness'. The hag's infiltration was a
complete success; her offspring was celebrated as a prodigy at the heart of
the very family she sought to control.

THE UNRAVELING (1439 DR)

The decades of lies began to fray on the festival of Greengrass, 1439 DR.
Tormented by her role, Aisling Maeliticus publicly confronted 'Jannath' in
the temple, denouncing her as a fraud and a witch. This act of open
rebellion occurred before her children and their spouses, including Andarl,
Abrecia, Senthia, Susserah, and Xerces. The confrontation instantly created
a schism within the family's leadership. Palus, seeing that the loyalty of
her high priestess's children was now in question, acted immediately. She
magically silenced Aisling and had her exiled to the Sorrowbog under the
guise of a pilgrimage, containing the rebellion before it could spread.

After feigning conversion to secure her release, Aisling returned on
the Summer Solstice. But Palus had already moved to resolve the schism
permanently. That autumn, she orchestrated a purge. Knowing she could not
simply murder her dissenters, she used her authority as 'the Mother of the
Ancient Faith' to 'trick' the potential rebels — Andarl and Abrecia — into
participating in a fatal ritual. It was likely framed as a secret rite of
appeasement to atone for their mother's blasphemy, a test of their faith.
Believing they were proving their loyalty, they walked into a trap where
they, along with Andarl's 11-year-old son Dorian, became the sacrifice.

This act, disguised as a 'hunting accident', was a tactical masterstroke
for Palus. It eliminated the key adults who might have sided with Aisling
and, by killing the trueborn twin Dorian, secured the position of her own
hagspawn daughter, Lobelia. This event galvanized the grieving Aisling. Her
final journal entry on 26 Uktar reveals her new, desperate plan: to
investigate the 'darkness' she perceived in her other granddaughter,
Alaventa Alavaxi, believing it was a key to the hag's power, before
leaving to find allies and destroy the hag.

Her quest, however, ended in failure before it could truly begin. According
to the official family records, Aisling Maeliticus died later that same
year, 1439 DR, at the Alavandor Lodge — the family's autumn hunting estate.
The cause of her death was recorded as 'self-murder'. Given her resolute
state of mind and Palus's recent purge of her family, it is far more likely
that Aisling was intercepted and murdered by the hag's agents, her death
disguised as a suicide to discredit her memory and neatly close the case on
her rebellion. With her death, the last internal opposition to Palus was
eliminated, leaving the settlement entirely at the hag's mercy for the next
two years.

THE FINAL TRAGEDY AND AFTERMATH (1441 DR)

Two years later, on the Autumnal Equinox of 1441 DR, Palus enacted her
endgame. Her primary objective was to open permanent gates to the
Shadowfell using powerful Tenebrius Ostium artifacts, which required the
sacrifice of a 'pure soul that has done an impure deed'. The operation
began with a masterful deception reported in Daphne Dale's final message:
the arrival of a 'large caravan from Hillsfar'. This was no simple group of
merchants. It is highly probable that this caravan was a Trojan horse,
concealing the command element of the Grentzeldek hobgoblins and their
elite shock troops. By arriving under the guise of traders who were 'not
unpacking to trade,' they were allowed to camp near the settlement without
raising alarm, positioning themselves perfectly for the initial strike.

To accomplish the ritual's activation, Palus used her most insidious and
well-placed asset: her own daughter, Lobelia. As Alaventa's celebrated and
heroic older cousin, Lobelia possessed a level of trust no outsider could
replicate. It was likely she who translated her mother's dark command into
the believable language of a children's 'cruel joke,' manipulating Alaventa
into performing the impure deed that would lead to the sacrifice of her and
her friends. This final betrayal fulfilled the ritual's terrible
requirement.

The sacrifice was masked by the military assault that followed. Having used
the night to get into position, the Grentzeldek forces first destroyed the
tower of the main building and the aviary with it, severing all
communication. They then commenced a 'lightning-fast assault' from the
north while their allied goblin tribes, who had already crossed the river,
blocked any escape to the south, ensuring a 'comprehensive slaughter'.
During this chaos, Palus's forces also 'rent Harngorn,' the great oak,
delivering the final, killing blow to the weakened Quercina. With the
sacrifices complete, the Tenebrius Ostium artifacts activated, and
permanent 'rivulets from the Shadowfell' infested the Alavandor Academy,
the Oak and Shield Inn, and likely the Merchants' Hall.

Magistrate Invictus later noted with deep suspicion that there was no
evidence the caravan was attacked and plundered like the rest of the Ridge;
it simply vanished. This is because they were not victims; they were the
perpetrators. Having fulfilled their contract and been paid with the
settlement's entire wealth, the Grentzeldek simply packed up their
'caravan' and withdrew north, leaving behind only ghosts and a haunted
ruin. Days later, the first scouts from Dagger Falls entered the ruin only
to be attacked by a Shadow creature already dwelling in the Academy,
confirming the Shadow's plan had succeeded."

"Some interesting lore! You've been part of the Daggerdale campaigns since

On Point 1 (Prince Malagaunt and Loki):
  - Prince Melegaunt Tanthul is one of the Twelve Princes of Shade, son of

    to Shar other than being a Netherese bigwig? (In the official Realms

  - The Spellplague ended in 1385 DR, almost 100 years before our current

  - Notably, the return of the Netherese in 1372 coincided with the change

    peacefully up to that point.

On Point 2 (Netherese infertility):
  - Do we know that the Netherese were infertile? I can't find any canon
    on that, and it hasn't been mentioned in this campaign that I recall.
  - We do know that the Alavandors and Clarandals were infertile, which is
    the 'curse' that got them to come to Thistle Ridge. Conceivably, the
    Shadovar could take advantage of the same solution (whether it was
    Palus's profane fertility magic or the natural fertility of Thistle
    Ridge, if it ever existed)

On Point 3 (40-year timing):
  - The 40-year gap appears to coincide with the period between the fall
    of Thistle Ridge and the discovery of the Imperiosus Orbis. Could that
    be relevant? Note that the 40 years before that coincides generally
    with the beginning of the plot to switch out the Alvandors and the
    eventual fall of Thistle Ridge. So we've got an 80-year gap between
    the departure of the actual Alavandors in 1403 (sent off to Mulhorand
    to get out of someone's way) and the present day, with the Tragedy
    pretty much halfway between in 1441.

On Point 4 (Break in the Veil):
  - This is the Feywild curtain, right? This is a hole in our intel. Calix
    said she came through 'around 30 years ago' (so, 1453 DR, after the
    Tragedy). Both she and Dol'Rullian, who originally laid waste to
    Greenwood Farm, were drawn through the Veil by the settlers in the area.
  - One contradiction: the timing of the arrival of Fluvia. I thought we'd
    heard from Calix that Fluvia arrived around the same time she did. This
    didn't make sense if she was also part of the original makeup of Thistle
    Ridge. The only statement I can find about that is in Jasper's letter
    to Khara Sulwood. It may be that Jasper was wrong about that.

On Point 5 (Original religion and arrivals):
  - We only know that the Alavaxi came by 'portal' (from Chessenta via the
    Shadowfell). To the best of my knowledge, we don't have any information
    on how the settler Alavandor/Clarandal lines arrived.
  - The original Thistle family practiced the 'Old Religion' - i.e.,
    Silvanus, not Chauntea.
  - Information on Jannath is scarce. Even Maddy Oakdale, the high
    priestess of Chauntea, said she wasn't sure about the connection. In
    any case, it seems likely that Jannath is essentially a dead god, since
    the remaining Netherese are more or less devoted to Shar. My suspicion
    was that the identity of Jannath was supplied to Palus as a way to trick
    the Sembians.

Can you elaborate, Scott?"

KEY LORE REFERENCE

This thread establishes the following as "agreed" between the players:

CONFIRMED FACTS:
- Melegaunt Tanthul and Loki are agents of Shar
- The original Thistle clan worshipped Silvanus (not Chauntea)
- Chauntea is the modern successor to Jannath's portfolio
- Loki first appeared as Melegaunt's Curator c. 1480 DR
- The Alavandor/Clarandal families came from Daerlun (Sembia)
- The families were under a "curse of generation" (infertility)
- The Daerlun coven guided them to Thistle Ridge
- Palus posed as the goddess Jannath
- Lobelia is Palus's hagspawn daughter, swapped for a stillborn twin
- The sewer project was designed to weaken Quercina/Harngorn
- The Hillsfar "caravan" was a Grentzeldek Trojan horse

UNRESOLVED QUESTIONS:
- How many Tenebrius Ostia exist (one or three)?
- Why are three girls ghosts while Alaventa is "at rest"?
- The significance of Alaventa being daughter of Xerces Alavaxi and
  Abrecia Alavandor
- Whether Alaventa is a red herring
- The exact nature of Netherese infertility (Scott's claim vs. canon)
- The timing and mechanism of the break in the Feywild Veil
- When exactly Fluvia arrived in the area
