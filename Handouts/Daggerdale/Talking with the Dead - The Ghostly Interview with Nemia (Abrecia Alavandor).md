Talking with the Dead - The Ghostly Interview with Nemia (Abrecia Alavandor)
by Les Blackwell (DM), Gus Miranda (Jasper), Stephen Robertson (Lucan)
Email thread: "Talking with the dead", 26 Sep 2024

A short but important thread in which Les asks the players to remind him of the results of Nemia's last Speak with Dead spell. The party had spoken with the ghost of Abrecia Alavandor (an ancestor of Lucan's), and the revelations are key to the Thistle Ridge mystery.

---

Les: Hey! I've got a couple of days off, and I'm getting ready for our session on Sunday. I forget if you guys actually used the last 'speak with dead' that Nemia had, and my notes are a bit scribbly. Can you remind me?

Gus: We did indeed use it. It is a while ago and I have forgotten the details! It is Lucan who took the lead there, and we spoke to someone who revealed to us that there was no accident, and in fact it had been some sort of sacrifice to Chauntea (or Netherese version of her) but "she had lied!" Best I can recall myself as I'm not at home right now to look at my notes. I think Steve wrote it all down as it was more pertinent to his character.

Les: Can you chime in, Ol Robertson? I'm getting ready for the weekend and I'm hoping you made some notes about the ghostly interview with Nemia.

Stephen: Yes, we used it. We spoke with Abrecia Alavandor, who revealed that:
1) There was no hunting accident
2) They were promised that three deaths would be enough to save them (not sure of the exact wording here)

3) The promise was made by the "Mother of the Ancient Faith" (i.e., Jannath from the Netherese Pantheon, we presume).

---

Key lore points:
- The "hunting accident" at Thistle Ridge was actually a deliberate act / sacrifice
- Three deaths were supposed to be sufficient to "save them"
- The promise came from the "Mother of the Ancient Faith" — identified as Jannath (Netherese Pantheon equivalent of Chauntea)
- The implication is that "she had lied" — the promise was false or the sacrifice was manipulated
