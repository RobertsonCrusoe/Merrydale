A Submission to the Abbeye Library - Daggerdale History and Discussion
by Stephen Robertson (as Acolyte Brightbeam / Archpriest Dawncrest), with Les Blackwell and Gus Miranda
Email thread: "A submission to the Abbeye library...", 16-19 Aug 2024

Stephen shared an AI-assisted chronicle of pre-1360 Daggerdale history, written in the voice of a somewhat racist Aumanatori acolyte named Brightbeam, intended as a lore document for the Abbeye library. The full document is on Google Drive. Stephen then wrote an in-character rejection letter from a senior Aumanatori cleric. The thread became a discussion about AI writing, sourcebook research, and Gus's old characters from earlier campaigns.

Google Doc: https://docs.google.com/document/d/1mzuV4D2wmfsrGGmiXuJMWc-IGPemKU1P1f619E2XmVk/edit?usp=sharing

ARCHPRIEST DAWNCREST'S REJECTION LETTER (Stephen, 17 Aug 2024)

Stephen wrote this in-character rejection of Brightbeam's chronicle:

Acolyte Brightbeam,

Your attempt to chronicle the history of Daggerdale, though ambitious, falls woefully short of the standards expected by the Church of Aumanator. Your work is riddled with insufferable repetition, with each page drowning in the same tired praises of human ingenuity. This relentless redundancy not only insults the intelligence of your readers but also betrays a lack of discipline in your writing — an unforgivable flaw in a document that aspires to be housed in the sacred library of Aumanator.

Furthermore, your disrespectful tone toward our dwarven allies is unbecoming of a servant of the Sun God. Your snide remarks and condescending attitude reveal a dangerous arrogance, one that undermines the very principles of unity and respect that our faith is built upon. To belittle those who have fought and died alongside us is not just ungracious, it is disgraceful.

Worse still is your audacity in presuming to know the will of Aumanator so intimately that you attribute every minor event to His divine intervention. Such arrogance borders on heresy. Who are you, Acolyte, to claim such insight into the mind of the Sun God? Humility and reverence are the hallmarks of true faith, not the reckless, unfounded assertions you scatter throughout this text.

In conclusion, your work is a poorly conceived, poorly executed exercise in vanity. It lacks the gravitas, the discipline, and the reverence necessary for a document meant to serve the Church. I suggest you reconsider your approach entirely, or refrain from such undertakings until you have truly grasped what it means to serve Aumanator with the humility and respect He deserves.

In the Light of Aumanator,
Archpriest Lysander Dawncrest
Senior Keeper of the Faithful Histories

DISCUSSION (18-19 Aug 2024)

Stephen: Did you get a chance to read this? I did use an AI to help me write it (it would have taken me days otherwise), so I know it's a bit one-note, but I consolidated as much of the pre-1360 Daggerdale lore that I could find online and in the sourcebooks as I could in order to flesh it out. That includes the description of the vampire attack, which as far as I can tell was only described in any detail in the first Ravenloft novel. I did put the whole enterprise into the words of a somewhat racist Aumanatori, but I was pretty happy with how it turned out. I didn't work in Gus's old character Logan Morn, but I think he's post-Randal....

Gus: I only really glanced at it as I was trying to catch back up in reading the Daggerdale Chronicles. I had forgotten all about Logan Morn, who died not too far from here on the road to Shadowdale I think. He was made at the time as the "DM's voice in the party" as it was during a time when I (wrongly!) believed the DM should have more influence in the party's direction. I did this a couple times, but in the end it was a wrong thing to do as a DM, and I grew out of it. PCs need to PC, and as Blacky is showing so masterfully in Daggerdale, just let us run with the plot without an NPC railroading us along.

He was also supposed to be my 2nd character, but since he died, I made Raven who shared a lot of similarities with him. And after Raven's death, came Dorian, who also shared a lot of similarities with Logan. Single-class fighter with a sword. I blame it entirely on Val Kilmer in Willow. Madmartigan is still my favourite character in all fantasy film.

Les: I read this entirely. I figured it was AI. The tone is too consistent for most human writing, but it did capture exactly what you were looking for, I think. It was nice to see it all collected in one spot.

Stephen: Madmartigan. Such a great name, too. The AI did a pretty good job of organizing all of the various info I fed it, but I came up with the sun-themed chapter titles on my own. The information on the Morn family between Colderan and Randal comes from some comments that Ed Greenwood submitted to the Candlekeep forum for some other people running Daggerdale campaigns (there seem to be quite a few!), while a lot of the ancient material comes from the Lost Empires sourcebook.

Anyway, I wish I'd had time to write it out myself - I don't know how you generate so much material, Les - but I was doing a deep dive into the campaign and Daggerdale last week, and felt that Daggerdale's deep history was worth adding to the materials on hand.

PS - What do you mean, second, Gus? As I recall, your bladesinger Gilthanas was your original character, followed by Jafar and then Raven. I thought Logan was first introduced when Maturin set out for Waterdeep in the company of the celebrated adventurer, brawler, and gentleman entrepreneur, Lord Arkos "Falcon" Thunderstaff...
