The Saga of Maturin the Grey, by Dorian of Sembia

== Prologue ==
:\"Well, where have you been, Maturin, my son?
:What were your deeds? What have you done?
:Did you cross the seas of blue?
:Did you fight the battles true?
:Did you see the righteous through?
:Come now, Maturin, tell me of you!

== Introduction ==

:\"Well, brother, I'll tell you where I have been,
:I'll tell you of heroes and horrors I've seen.
:This is the story of many a man,
:Of Nobles, and rascals, and a failed Zhent plan.
:This is a story of monsters and war.
:This is the story of my long journey far.

:A Council we held in the Jewel of the North,
:Heroes, three groups, set out and went forth.
:Ours was the longest, a trip to the Dales,
:Not destined to see the waves' happy sails.
:For us was the desert, the great Anauroch,
:No pleasantly swaying ships on the dock.

:Eastward we strode, with our armour a' gleaming,
:With courage and honour, our hearts were yet beaming
:There was Raven the Ranger, leading the way,
:(Not long it would be 'ere the close of his day).
:He was a Raventree noble, with a hunger for fight,
:Yet doomed nonetheless, but not by Ravensblight.

:There was Boregar the Wanderer, accompanying too,
:The roads were unsafe - what else could he do?
:A fat man was he, but fattest his heart,
:A wounded companion for miles he'd cart.
:His staff was a whirlwind of parries and blows,
:Villains he vanquished in rows upon rows.

:Sparrow the mageling, priest of the Lady.
:He’d always a spell or a trick at the ready.
:But true was his quest, and truer his aim,
:He didn’t seek gold, nor glory nor fame.
:He ventured with us, pursuing his goal,
:With spells and his wits, he'd vanquish Yarkul.

:Also with us was a man who was bold,
:He could turn himself purple, he could turn into mould.
:Hanibal 'the Wild' was the name of this man,
:Of reason and safety, he was not the greatest fan.
:Nevertheless, he was one of our mages,
:Wild or not, he was one for the ages.

:Corrina the gnome, small and brokenhearted,
:Shed left her true love, and sadly departed.
:She managed the horses, and spoke to the beasts,
:And could turn our poor rations into sumptuous feasts.
:Of illusions she weaved, and knew more then most,
:Of Dragons, and demons, and Riders, and ghosts.

:So this was the troupe, that took up hhe quest.
:For hundreds of miles we would walk from the West.
:We would venture to mountain and desert and town,
:To take from the Zhents Randal’s lost crown.
:Remember their names, O Brother, my dear,
:For this is the story, of which you will hear.

== I ==

:\"Well, brother, I'll tell you where I have been,
:I'll tell you of heroes and horrors I've seen.
:This is the story of many a man,
:Of Nobles, and rascals, and a failed Zhent plan.
:This is a story of monsters and war.
:This is the story of my long journey far.

:We started our quest, with a price on our heads,
:The blood that was spilled turned the trail to red.
:Grimwald the Sheriff longed for our death;
:He would draw from our Ranger his life's final breath.
:But we battled, and jousted, and fought tooth and nail,
:And in Secomber-town, his hunters would fail.

:The sheriff was brave, and quick with his gun,
:But his days as a villain were soon to be done.
:we vanquished his forces, brought him to his knees,
:we thwarted his effort to collect on his fees.
:Grimwald was judged, and brought to his end,
:But the loss of our Ranger, his death could not mend.

:For Raven had died, from a shot to his chest,
:I hope that in slumber, he's found final rest.
:And Hanibal, my friend, vanished from sight,
:I know that he died, turning wrong into right.
:To be claimed by our foes, these were the first,
:But I thank Great Ilmater that they suffered no thirst.

:For the desert awaited, and soon we'd not drink,
:Its hotter, and harsher, than you'd ever think,
:Anauroch, 0 Anauroch, where I met my fate,
:It was there that He stalked and He hunted in wait...
:But Raven and Hanibal, great men that had died,
:For their souls and their families, I cried and I cried.

== II ==

:So, Brother I’m telling you, of where I have been.
:I’m telling of heroes, and horrors I’ve seen.
:This is the story, of many a man,
:Nobles and rascals, and a failed Zhent plan.
:This is the story of monsters and war,
:This is the story of my journey so far.

:In Secomber-town, we bolstered our ranks,
:And cleaned off the blood on the river’s wide banks.
:Help we would need, and so help we sought,
:From a Chauntean Druid (or so we first thought).
:But, he was no Druid, we could see his plate mail,
:But rather a bandit, expelled from his Dale.

:Dorian the warrior, joined in our fight,
:His honour I hoped would soon come to light.
:But alone he was not, for he came with our Dalak,
:A Dwarf with an axe, who missed his dear Balak.
:Dalak was proud, with a look that was savage,
:He'd rant and he’d drink and soon he would ravage.

:O Dalak the Dwarf, the greatest of cobblers,
:Many a Zhent with his axe he would clobber.
:So these were our allies, and help for the quest,
:And so we departed again from the west.
:Dalak, and Sparrow and Dorian too,
:With Boregar, Corrina and myself we were true.

:Eastward we went, onwards our path;
:We'd show those foul villains the weight of our wrath!
:But trouble awaited, from foes we'd not seen,
:In the mountains we stumbled on Hill Giants mean.
:To defeat we came (almost), as spells started flying,
:And a Demon appeared, while the heroes fell dying!

:But our end was not there, for it was not written,
:Nay, to the desert we'd go, where we'd get bitten.
:0 Anauroch, Anauroch, where I met my fate,
:'Twas there where He hunted and prowled in wait.
:So, Father pray, listen up and hear this brave tale,
:For soon we shall hear of Ilmater's great wail.

==III==

:So, Brother I’m telling you, of where I have been.
:I’m telling of heroes, and horrors I’ve seen.
:This is the story, of many a man,
:Nobles and rascals, and a failed Zhent plan.
:This is the story of monsters and war,
:This is the story of my journey so far.

:The desert sands welcomed us with hatred and fear,
:But not yet was the time when I’d get this grey tear.
:But all our great endings must have a foul start,
:And soon was to be the first test of my heart.
:0, Anauroch, cursed Anauroch, damned be your sands,
:You gave us the plague that l spread with my hands.

:We went out of the heat to a canyon so right,
:But we heard a great roar that gave us a fright!
:Grandfather Lion, Hunter and King,
:I knew he was evil, so I took the first swing.
:The battle was bloody, the most horrible so far,
:His infectious claws; my soul they would mar!

:The day was a loss, and we were all broken,
:The beast, all he'd wanted, was a horse as a token.
:All of us were bloody and battered and dying.
:But all I could hear was the Gods' mournful crying.
:Our task was not finished, rather only just begun,
:I’d meet him once more before our quest was done...
