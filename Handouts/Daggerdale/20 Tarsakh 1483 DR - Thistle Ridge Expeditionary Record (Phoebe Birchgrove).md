20 Tarsakh, 1483 DR - Thistle Ridge Expeditionary Record – Phoebe Birchgrove, Sgt. III, 7th Co. 4th Plt, Bright Spears, Aid de Camp for Lt. I Forge Farmountain 
I write tonight with mixed news.  I will start with that which troubles my Lt. 
Though we hold the Tower, we are constantly tested by the Yellow Tooth, and the nerves of some of the men are frayed from the labour of cleansing the basements. 

Probing attacks of Yellow Tooth come regularly as soon as the sun is behind the mountains, and they don't stop until Magistrate Dale is delivering Prime.  In fact, I think she has challenged the faith of some of the non-Amaunatori with her prayers; as she takes the eastern balcony and begins her prayer, the arrows stop, and the enemy vanishes into the shadows of the wood. 

We have had none killed, but several wounded by unlucky arrows and other misadventure.  There have been some minor injuries from our efforts in the basements.  Remnants of the foul traps and unnatural creatures had to be eradicated, with some men suffering burns from a slime, and another nearly losing a foot to a trap he discovered.   

Our worst injuries have been to the souls of the brave men who have agreed to cleanse the basements.  I shamefully confess that after the first reports of the gruesome charnel discovered in the completely degenerate lair of the Beast of the Tower, that I have not volunteered to venture down.  First and Second Squads tried their best, but did not have the stomach for the work.  The discovery of the troubling remains of kin of Sgt. Harrowbridge in the basement was enough for him to walk out against orders.  Lt. Farmountain has been fair with the men on this account.  Fourth Squad has agreed to do the work, and they have spent the most time and made the most progress on the labour; the Vaasan Sgt and the Newharvest girls have set a fine example of courage, though we could use their bows on the walls. 

It must be said that our findings below, which I won't inventory here, paint a picture of cruelty and degenerate vice none have heard tell of.  The Beast perpetrated violations and terrors upon so many victims that it can scarcely be believed that it wasn't a demon in a pit.  We shall be a long time getting rid if his foul memory. 

We currently have one soldier in the infirmary with wounds we are unable to fix here; Fletcher Farmountain, whose brother commands us, and whose ankle discovered the trap I mentioned, has been infected with a poison from the trap that is beyond Magistrate Dale's skill to cure, though his life is not in danger we are assured. 

We have six men on duty with minor wounds from which they will make a full recovery. 
Now for the good news.   
Third Squad while on patrol repelled a small unit of Yellow Tooth skirmishers, and Pt. Sanza Swift managed to capture one of the little beasts, which we have interrogated.  From this creature, we learned that the death of the Beast of the Tower (they call him 'Karn') has swept through the goblin camps, providing a mix of terror, disbelief and outrage.  This Beast was something of a hero or a terror for the Yellow Tooth (I'm sure it was both), and they believe that the Captain herself killed it.  They are mistaken of course, but they blame every misfortune on Cpt. Monarch as a matter of general superstition.  In their tongue, she is called The Crimson Shadow, for her fiery hair and ruthlessness in pursuit. 

Charrah has declared that we won't spend a peaceful night at the Tower until it is retaken, and there are plans to bring up reserves from the Sorrowbog to attack us in force in the near future.  We think reinforcements and logistics will require at least a tenday to organize, and we continue to shore up our defenses here in preparation for an assault. 

Finally, I happily report that despite the injuries and the terrible scene we have been forced to deal with below, the men are in good spirits, and are well-fed.  Before we began felling and burning all the trees and cover without the walls, we took a few large deer which Sgt. Thickwickle has turned into good morale by way of a spiced stew which is nightly enjoyed with great anticipation. 

We are in need of nothing at this point.  Our supplies will last more than 20 days on full ration, and we receive more arrows than we send.
