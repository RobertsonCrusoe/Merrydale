He contacted a devil known as Adramalech, one of the Dark Eight of Avernus, who answers directly to Lord Bel himself. 
Andramalech has an organization in Marsember called the Sons of Bel. These are devil worshipers who steal the souls of victims to buy the services of more devils for their cause. The Sons have been a thorn in the side for Cormyr, and as such have been a boon to Prince Lamorak, whose job it is to bring Cormyr under heel. Lamorak has been more successful than Melegaunt, and threatens his younger brother's position in the council. As such, Melegaunt was happy to draw off these resources that are unwittingly helping his brother, while employing them for his own means.

This is an unsteady arrangement, because the rest of the Lords of Shade would not support making deals with devils, especially those that are connected to what remained of the Zhentarim (or so we thought; the Zhents appear to be thriving again).

Currency of Souls 
The goal for and means of carrying out operations for the Sons of Bel was (is?) the procurement of souls. They do this through a variety of means but the main is an elaborate ritual involving torture, in which the victim usually gives up their soul. These souls are then transferred to a Soul Key or Soul Coin, which can be used as currency for the indentured service of lesser devils, or for limited services of greater ones. The plan in Daggerdale was to use the keys to amass an army of devils to besiege the town of Dagger Falls. This was a long-reaching plan to be sure, and never came to fruition, though we haven't heard the last of Andramalech or the Sons of Bel. 
The ritual that is used is called Bel's Bargain. Specifically, the Sons in the dale had been using it against families, taking the souls of parents and siblings in return for the release of children and other family members. In most cases, these released prisoners were hunted down and killed at any rate. 
Those that forfeit their souls become Thralls of Bel (aka Derelicts); they became zombie like creatures with no soul that follow the commands of one of the lieutenants. These creatures were dangerous and likely lost and damned. However, there is some small hope for them, if they can be captured, as well as their soul keys, and then saved. An elaborate ritual by the Amaunatori ultimately was able to restore the souls of a few very fortunate residents of Dwimmerholme, and later, Charity. 
By the time the White Cloaks Bravo Team got involved, Seir's coven had collected a half-dozen souls and was well on his way to building a small army. 
The Thinly Veiled Guise 
In order to throw people off of his trail, Melegaunt had the Sons of Bel pretending to be Zhentarim slavers. It is well known that the Netherese destroyed Zhentil Keep, and these two factions have been at odds ever since (over 100 years). Furthermore, it is commonly known that the Zhents are active in many places around Daggerdale. As such, the Sons began their campaign of terror disguised as Zhents interested in slaves. This explained the abductions, without people looking for the devils hiding in the country nearby. 
To bolster the disguise, Azbael, operating in the south-west, had set up a small group of real Zhent slavers to deliver a few prisoners to Mulmaster. These were intercepted on the road in Eleint, and ever since then, people had been whispering about the Zhent slavers in the Dale.
