DDC – Chapter Four – Forword and Summary 
Wherein the prosperous dale is set upon by a formidable foe (Netheril), with many heroes answering the call to arms. 

Although the primary enemy of Daggerdale has been the Netherese since their return to Faerun at the start of the Spellplague, and especially in the decades that followed as they secured their previous territories (the desert Anauroch, which has been transforming into the green plains of Netheril for some time), this chapter is about the villains they worked with to attack the dale from within. 

This story is mostly about the religious struggle between competing faiths in the area. Amaunator has been the dominant faith, since being adopted by the Whitegate Family decades ago and has the most administrative and military control over the dale. 

Chauntea is the other major faith of the region, often called 'The Old Religion', as Chauntea predates the Spellplague by more than a thousand years (and as you will learn in the unwoven threads of Chapter 7, may be an even older Netherese god). As a mainly agricultural settlement, it is not surprising to find such a large following of Chauntea. 

Shar is another matter altogether, and if you, faithful reader, have read Chapter Three, you will know that 
the seeds of this faith were sewn in the 14th century, when one of

the Eastern Storm (Gaebril Tremalkin) openly proselytized on behalf of Shar, and created many followers, and indeed, a blood line that becomes the Dark Garden in this story. 

This chapter begins with the rise to power of the Whitegates, who came by all they have honestly. They rebuilt the dale with faith and sword and asked nothing in return. However, this story is dominated by the decimation of their family and holdings by the Dark Garden, and we find their story in it's denouement by the close of this chapter, though the threat posed by the Dark Garden is ended. 
Here are the players in this act; 
The Whitegate Family – Started by Ardelusk Whitegate, who was at the Battle of Liberty Hill (c.f. DDC – 1373 – 2: The Daggerdale Expedition), this family were staunch worshipers of Amaunator, and spread this faith throughout the dale. 

The White Cloaks – a company of adventurers with letters patent to carry out operations in the dale. Started by a group which also included Lumina Whitegate. These men and women are heroes in the dale and beyond. 

The Dark Garden – a circle of black druids who follow Shar, the head of their circle a woman named Belladonna, who is a grand-child (or possibly child) of the Black Saint, Gaebril Tremalkin 

The Yellow Abbey – this is the seminary in Daggerfalls for the clerics of Amuanator. The Historic Wing is a part of the Abbey that contains the more secret tomes and forbidden knowledge that 

has accumulated throughout the centuries in the dale, and was the home (prison?) of Brother Phineas Hoetmer, whose connection to the Dark Garden affair was as inextricable as it was inexplicable. 

Sunblades – This is the militant order of the dale, and is largely considered a branch of the church of Amaunator, though that was formally changed at the Governors' Assembly in '82. There are Sunblades in every settlement in Daggerdale, and many heroes come from the ranks of this disciplined order. 

There are others that played a role, and you'll read of them in the pages to come, and I hope, enjoy this story and the bitter-sweet conclusion of the chapter. But I'm in peril of spoiling the ending and will leave it at that.
