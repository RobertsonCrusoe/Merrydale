== Early Kythorn, 1371 ==

The turn of the month passed uneventfully, as the various members of the Company went about amassing information, contacts, and supplies. Rumours linking the Red Herrings to Drurginz, the Zhents, and the Company of the Southern Star continued to filter in by way of rumour and warning, but few among the Red Herrings paid any heed. Indeed, some among them seem to revel in the notoriety, limited as it was. Even Raven, the most noble of the bunch - by way of both birth and spirit - secretly enjoyed the thrill that could be found in the company he now kept.

Jafar continued his efforts to build the Company laboratory, realizing that there were certain benefits to a laboratory well removed from the offices of the Watchful Order. He was also able to refit and improve a \"casualty wagon\" for the Company. Tomas continued his work at the Drurginz warehouses, and Esperel and Tomas honed their housebreaking skills in Castle Ward - though their efforts netted them little profit, the most significant heist being a box of ceramic plates from Shou Lung. Meanwhile, Ellyth continued to devote herself wholeheartedly to her studies at the Eltorchul Academy under (cough) her tutor Janados.

== 4 Kythorn - Brawling at the Bloody Fist ==
thumb|400px|Sgt. Krag, the half-ogre from the Spine of the World
The reprieve from the realities of the adventuring life finally broke on 4 Kythorn when Tomas found Thagdul in an ill temper at the offices of Company Drurginz. As he arrived, Thagdul had just finished severely reprimanding a crowd of employees, and when the Moonsea trader spotted the young fisherman, he turned his attentions to him. As Welland slunk away, Thagdul snarled to Tomas that he would pay a generous bounty to any man who could find the Reprobates and \"cut the head off their troglodyte half-orc leader Gul'dan\". The Reprobates, it seemed, had been causing headaches for the master of Company Drurginz, and he was out for blood.

=== \"Let’s drain a few at the War God\" ===
Eager for action, Tomas went immediately to take the news to his comrades, but found only Raven at the Company HQ. Discussing the situation, they quickly decided that, between the two of them, they had more than enough muscle to get started on the mission, and left to pay a reconnaissance visit. It was a grim day, and the two hurried through the misty streets to the Bloody Fist, the tavern that Raven's Uncle Morgunn had said was the Reprobates' hangout. The 'Fist's notorious atmosphere did not disappoint its gruesome reputation - the place was thronging with half-orcs, drunks, and all manner of villains. Without even trying, Tomas got into a brawl with a couple of Dock Ward toughs (relations of the young fisherman, it seemed) and while neither of the two proved a match for the young Docksman, the brawl soon attracted attention. After dispatching the fellows, Tomas had not even the the chance to order a drink, before he had been challenged again, this time by the largest fellow in the bar - a half-ogre. This, someone whispered to Raven, was \"Sergeant\" Krag. Krag was an imposing specimen of half-breed, and his arms were marked with tattoos which Tomas recognized as slave-markings from the Spine of the World. Krag, it seemed, was up for a wrestling match, and so was the crowd - a space quickly clearing for them in the centre of the bar. With a bestial roar, Krag launched into Tomas and the two grappled for a good five minutes before Tomas finally brought the great demihuman down to the sound of cheering (from the humans in the crowd, at least).

As Tomas drank in the adulation of the onlookers, however, Krag's great arm reached up from the sticky floor of the tavern and Round Two began. This time, the fisherman did not fare as well, and Raven had to pull his young companion out from under the half-ogre's knees to rescue him from certain asphyxiation. A born manager, the young noble hauled his friend upstairs to the Tempuran surgery above the bar (the Bloody Fist was known as \"The War God\" by the locals, on account of its feisty clientele, and the surgery was a popular posting among Tempuran novitiates). Raven paid for a quick round of healing spells, then pushed a bleary Tomas back down the stairs for Round Three.

By that point, the pair's comrades had arrived, thanks to a note that Raven had wisely left behind. Quickly appraising the situation, the Red Herrings made the most of it, each in their own way: Ellyth started picking pockets, Esperel started a fight, and Garth bet everything he owned against Tomas. Of the three, only Garth was successful, as Tomas was knocked senseless once again, and fell crashing into the ale-stained mud of the tavern floor.

Elfkin at the Bloody Fist were neither frequent nor welcome visitors, and in short order Esperel's antics had both him and his shapely cousin forcibly removed. As a none-too-handsome pair of half-orc bouncers heaved them into the street, the two elves spotted a familiar figure looking on with a sneer: the hunched spellcaster that had been present at the ambush of the Southern Star.

=== A More 'Civilized' Duel ===

As the excitement in the tavern faded, Garth struggled to help his friend to his feet, while Raven watched as Krag returned to the corner table where he joined the hunched figure and a half-orc who Krag addressed as Gul’dan. The big ranger immediately strode over to the table and pointed his finger at the half-orc, challenging him to a \"more civilized duel\" in the alley outside, with swords, in one hour. With a smirk, the richly dressed half-orc accepted the challenge, and exaggeratedly tipped his hat to the noble ranger.

Tomas was in rough shape, and there was no more money for healing from the priests of Tempus (despite Garth's winnings, oddly). It fell to Ellyth, then, to help him seek the ministrations of a Dock Ward wisewoman for some patching up, while their companions hung back to wait for Gul'dan and his cronies -  who they were now sure were the Reprobates - to leave the bar. Sure enough, Gul'dan and his crew soon emerged. The preposterously dressed half-orc was followed by Sgt. Krag, another half-orc, Balkine Adard, the hunchback were-rat mage, and a slight hooded figure that seemed ... out of place, somehow. As the gang made its way through the streets towards the alley where Raven waited, Garth and Esperel followed at a safe distance, endeavouring to keep out of sight. When the hooded figure broke off from the main group, Esperel did likewise, in pursuit. The figure moved quickly, however, circling round to the alley from a different direction, and Esperel soon lost his quarry - until, stepping into the alley, he came under fire from crossbow quarrels, and thereby found it again.

Rushing forward at speed to draw Raven's attention, Esperel cried out \"It’s an ambush!\", just as his body was wreathed with a flickering cold flame. Esperel leapt up towards the roof to take on his mysterious assailant, while Raven turned to face the oncoming threat of Gul'dan, seconded by the were-rat spellcaster. Without warning, Gul'dan threw himself into the exchange, loosing a mighty swing that cut deeply into Raven's side. As Raven drew back from his opponent, however, the swearing figure of Tomas hurtled out of the mist, distracting Gul'dan from his opponent.

===The Impossible Made Possible===

As chaos erupted in the mists below, Esperel had made it to the rooftop, only to find himself enveloped in what he took to be a magical darkness, facing off against his mysterious opponent. Esperel heard the figure chuckle grimly and heard the draw of a blade. Fumbling in the darkness, Esperel did his best to dodge ... an attack which didn't come. When his vision cleared a minute later, he espied his quarry making off across another rooftop across the street, impossibly far to jump.

(Note: This was the first encounter with the ill-starred drow Rubikawn. Eventually, though not without much self-recrimination, Esperel concluded that the drow had clouded his vision with a globe of darkness, not much larger than his head, leaving the drow free to act while Esperel stumbled, then jumped across the street using his Boots of Striding and Leaping.)

Below, Tomas had not fully recovered from his encounter with Krag, however, and went down quickly under Gul'dan's lightning-quick sabre cuts. Luckily, Ellyth marked the spot where he fell, and he was soon dragged to safety and succour by the elven enchantress - but only just. The hunched spellcaster had been reciting an incantation as the courtesan worked, but Raven managed to recover himself enough to disrupt the incantation with a sword-butt to the head. As the mists swirled, both parties cautiously backed off, with no apparent fatalities.

The so-called duel ended, Raven and Tomas returned to the priests of Tempus, and Raven wrote a letter of promise to cover healing prayers for the two. Meanwhile, Esperel doubled back to the Reprobate headquarters in hopes of overhearing some intelligence, but this proved fruitless. When he returned to the Red Herring Safehouse, however, he found his companions standing silent in the main room, staring at a circle of what seemed to be chicken tracks, scattered flour and earth, and scorch marks - surrounding an empty chest.

Impossibly, the Heart of the Earth had been stolen.

== 5 Kythorn - Reconnaissance ==

The night and next day passed slowly. There was little that the Companeers could do - Jafar and Ellyth were convinced that only someone - or something - very powerful could have gotten through their arcane defenses, and there seemed to be no options to pursue by themselves other than to wait, and to keep their eyes and ears open. Such a rare material could not be moved quietly, they were certain, in the City of Splendours.

The day was spent regrouping after the encounter with the Reprobates the day before, with Tomas spending most of the day convalescing. They also began to prepare for future encounters with darkness. Garth provided each member of the party with five (company-purchased) magical light pellets (\"made by my fellow\"), while Raven contacted the  in pursuit of a map of the City Sewer System. Ellyth purchased other equipment.

The others continued their research into the roll of enemies that the Company was quickly amassing. Esperel staked out the Reprobate safehouse, but witnessed no traffic other than the departure of two small childlike figures with long arms, their faces concealed in hoods from which jutted bulbous noses. Goblins. Garth went to talk to Loremaster Kennyth Thames at the Font of Knowledge, researching the drow and (though it was a longshot) the Reprobates. For his part, Jafar researched Beholders.
