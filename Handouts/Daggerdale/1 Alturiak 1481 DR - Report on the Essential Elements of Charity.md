DDC – 1481 – 7: Report on the Essential Elements of Charity 
A report about the obscure town known to the Dalesmen as Charity Hills Farm, 1st Alturiak, 1481 DR 
GFD Notes: 
I was delighted to find another report from Sarah Estelmer, who's work drew me to this project in the first place. Herein, she is simply covering off a check-list of places to go and people to see shortly before she departs the Dale for good, on her journey back to Waterdeep. 

The Yellow Abbey does not extend its book of surveys and measures to the settlement of Charity, and as far as I know, this is the most detailed account of the workings of this curious town this side of Westgate. 

It should also be noted that she seemed to trust Basil Muloch, who would become one of the most vital correspondents in the struggle with the Sons of Bel in 1481. He came to a bad end, unfortunately

Preface 
I should begin by saying straight away that my time in Charity was very brief, and not as productive as I would have liked. Our contact, Basil Muloch, was actually away from town; I had taken for granted that most people of this town do not travel and was disappointed to learn that he was away in Cormyr and was not due to return until spring had loosed the grip of winter on the roads. I could not gain access to Earl Muloch's chambres, and could not, therefore, verify the facts which I was sent to investigate. 

However, knowing that so few documents existed about Charity, I decided to stay on for as long as they would tolerate me in that town. That was exactly a tenday in the coldest weather (both of nature and of company) of Hammer that I have ever experienced. 

The information I provide below has been committed to our archives only; there has been no copy left in-situ, as I regard this work as something of an unverified document, awaiting those better than I to complete. Ideas half formed are more dangerous than those left in the void altogether. 

A brief work demands a brief preface, and so I commit this work to our archives presently. 

Sr. Sp. S. Estelmer 
1 Alturiak, 1481 DR. 

Charity 
On all the maps of Daggerdale in the current century, the town of Charity is noted as 'Charity Hills Farm', in the cartographical taxonomical system of the culture of that dale in this age. However, those that live there simply call it 'Charity'. 

Charity is the oddest of the farming villages of the dale. Like the others, it is a walled settlement that is largely self-sufficient. It is steeped in agricultural and resource-gathering endeavours and has a strong militaristic background. However, the place has an air all its own. 

Charity was founded in 1388 by a group of families from the Dragon-Coast city of Westgate. These settlers brought their own culture with them, and it has endured through several generations; the names, buildings, religions, speech, and every aspect of life in Charity is profoundly different from that in the rest of the dales. 

The walled settlement itself is home to over 3000 permanent inhabitants, with several hundred working in the rich mines and other resource operations of the area. This makes it the second largest settlement in the dale, behind Dagger Falls . The town straddles the Charnel River and is enclosed by a network of walls from the various stages of the town's growth. There are over one hundred buildings within the walls, and almost all are stone structures built in the tall row-house fashion of Westgate. The streets are expertly cobbled, and wrought iron lamps burn oil at every street corner. 

The people of the town stratify themselves into three different castes; landmen, middlemen, and freemen. Lower castes give complete deference to their betters, though even the dirtiest coal-mining freeman can be heard to say 'better to be free of Charity, than lord o' the Falls'. All Charity folk are xenophobic in the extreme, and they are not given to travel; hardly any have friends or relations even in the nearby settlement of Liberty Hill. These people have a very strong accent which quickly betrays them in the rare occasions they are encountered outside of their settlement. They are proud to a fault, and it is not uncommon for this pride, combined with a clashing of ideas and alcohol in the taverns of the dale, to result in brawls between the Charity men and others on the rare occurrence of their meeting. 

Charity is wealthy. Everyone in Daggerdale knows it, because just about everyone in the dale burns coal from their mines, has steel and brass from their forges, and oil from their retorts. Charity is also known to produce the best quality wool as a result of their other big industry; innovation. 

The original settlers of the region brought the religions of knowledge with them, including Gond. As such, this small settlement has always been open to learning, invention and change. They have produced complicated processes for refining 'candle coal' into the oil that is now common in the dale. They produce steel in a process they call 'cementing', and brass in a similar process. They produce gunpowder in such a quantity that they have a militia almost entirely armed with firearms. The result of these innovations, combined with the vast resources of the area has been economic prosperity that is utterly unknown in this part of the world. The homes of all landmen and middlemen are appointed in a fashion that exceeds that of the finest homes in the rest of the dale, and even the homes of most freemen are comfortable and well lit. 

Brief History 
In 1388, the families of four retired sea captains from the navy of the city of Westgate migrated to the area at the end of a very successful naval campaign. The original caravan of settlers numbered over 100, with all the attendants and staff belonging to the families. Though it has never been proven, it is suspected that the navy captains knew of the deposits of minerals in the area 

that are needed to make the precious gun powder that they had grown fond of during their campaign. 

Right away, construction of a stout fortress was begun on a strategic hill overlooking a fertile valley, with line of sight to most of the key strategic resources and militarily important sites in the region. This keep would later become known as Silverstar Keep. 

The settlement established good trade relations with the towns of Dagger 
Falls and Shadowdale immediately, beginning the prosperity that it has enjoyed

for nearly 100 years. At least one of the original four families were devoted followers of Waukeen, and that religion is openly accepted and encouraged to this day. 

The region as a whole was relatively peaceful until the 1400's. Even the catastrophic events involving Mystra and Lathander which had some impact on the rest of the dale, had essentially no impact on Charity, as these gods did not factor into the religious makeup of the settlement. 

The 15th Century DR in the dale began with the siege of Dagger Falls by the orc warlord, Zguumush. 

This siege was masterminded by the ambitious Netherese prince, Melegaunt, and this conflict is ongoing to this day. At the time, the Whitegate Family was firmly entrenched as the leaders of the Dale, though they had aloof relations with the Westgate families of Charity. However, Ardelusk sent an appeal to Lord Falconer, then Chancellor of Charity, to come to his aid in lifting the siege. Falconer agreed, under certain terms; specifically that Charity would always remain free of the governmental and religious structures of Daggerdale. The men of Charity brought their guns and lifted the siege, and the settlement has maintained its independence ever since. 

In the 1420's, the population of orcs and other creatures in the nearby mountains had grown to such a degree that they flowed forth like a plague. For nearly 10 years, the old captains and their sons held Silverstar Keep against the onslaught of orcs and other monsters, their guns and firearms proving more than a match for the vast hordes set against them. During this time, the military structure of the 

settlement (more on that below) was forged and tempered, and remains the same to this day. Charity has prided itself on its military accomplishments ever since. 

Between the intense fighting of the '20's and the current day, there has been sporadic light military conflict in the area, mostly echoing the demographics and politics of the nearby bands of orcs. 

However, there has been no significant threat to the settlement in this time, and it is believed that prince Melegaunt sees Charity as a waste of resources. Some believe that the relative peace around Charity is the result of secret treaties that the Lords of the town have with Melegaunt, rather than the deterrent of the military prowess of the men of Charity. The heterogeneous and xenophobic nature of Charity does little to dispel such speculations. 

Religion 
Selune is the main religion among the landmen and middlemen of Charity. All the settling families worshiped Selune as the prime religion of their native city. Selune is also the second-most important deity for sailors and was a natural fit for the old captains. The local temple to Selune is a beautiful structure that has a small, elite order of soldiers called 'Silver Stars' that have defended it and the rest of the settlement for decades. 

Gond the Wonderbringer figures heavily into this settlement; Charity has been a home to invention since its founding and remains so today. Though there is only a very small clergy, all in the town praise Gond for the innovations which make their lives safe and comfortable. 

Denier, the Scribe of Oghma, is an important figure in Charity. Children of all castes are required to attend school until they are 13, and there has always been a culture of learning and intellect among the landmen of Charity. 

Waukeen is one of the driving forces behind the success of Charity. While Gond has provided miraculous inventions, it is Waukeen that has allowed the

town to exploit them in a mercantile fashion. Waukeen is largely worshiped by the growing caste of middlemen, and the success of these men, visible to the rest of the dale when they travel with their wares, is beginning to take root in Dagger Falls and some other places. 

The Red Knight is the primary military deity of Charity. The armed forces of the town have always been ordered into the traditional structures of armed lords and their men-at-arms. Discipline, superior firepower and the tactical prowess granted by the Red Knight have always kept Charity safe. Even the lowest-ranking soldiers pray to the Red Knight to inspire their captains and lords to give good orders in battles. Other military traditions and religions are mostly kept out of the fighting ranks, for fear of challenges to the chain of command and the primacy of armed lords. 

Originally accepted by the landmen of the town because of her resolute opposition to Shar, Sharess has grown to become the primary deity of the large caste of freemen. Most freemen and their families are hard working people who attempt to enjoy their free time as much as possible. This has made the Old Fort Festhall (the temple to Sharess) a lively and prosperous place, and a key political as well as religious site in the town. 

Kelemvor is venerated by all in the town, in the Westgate fashion. All burials are proceeded over by the local clergy of the Grey Lord, and all dead are buried in the Potter's Field. 

Ilmater has a strange hold on the town; a sizeable monastery, once located at the edge of town, but now within the most recent ring of walls, contains a moderately sized clergy for such a small settlement. The place has the unfortunate name 'Charnel Priory' after the river it sits upon, and it is home to the sick, elderly, orphans, the mad and even many lepers. It has long been a place of pilgrimage for many sick or desperate in the dale who know the reputation of this beneficent organization. The Priory is supported by the other faiths in the town, and by the charity of the Lords and even some middlemen. It should be noted that this Priory is gaining notice among greater theological institutions as a place of exemplary pastoral care, and its leaders, being by nature of the town itself, well educated, are known as peers among even local Loremasters. 

GFD Notes: Charnel Priory has a relic from St. Maturin, specifically his lion-pelt cloak, which is used to treat the sickest patients. 

Siamorphe is worshiped by the landmen of Charity. A marriage between the Falconer Family and the Maernos family of Waterdeep before the migration to Charity brought this Sword Coast religion to Westgate. This religion took hold in Westgate during the 1380's political conflicts of that city of the time. The families that left Westgate for Charity were all royalists who supported King Verovan, and their descendants in Charity are still known to honour his name, and venerate Siamorphe, who eternally supports the rights of Noble Rule. 

Amaunator has the smallest presence here of any settlement in the dales. There is a beautiful church that was built in the 1450's, but the locals all call it 'the embassy', which underlines both the outside nature of this religion in the town, and the local xenophobia of such. Still, this church enjoys good relations with the other faiths in Charity. It does not have any official political power, and the Magistrate is likewise called 'ambassador' by the locals, and functions more like a dignitary than a spiritual leader. There are almost no locals in the body of this church, and none at all in the clergy. 

Beshaba has an interesting relationship in Charity. The people of Westgate, mariners especially, often invoke Beshaba's name in both curses and oaths. While there is no formal church or clergy in Charity, the name crosses the lips of many, especially the lower castes. 

Resources and Industry 
The resources that brought the Westgate families to the region in the 14th century continue to make Charity the most prosperous town in the dale. The hills of the area are rich in many minerals and coal. The iron mines of the area and the coal mines are the largest sources of revenue. Copper and zinc are also both mined in great quantities. 

The hills of the region provide excellent conditions for sheep and cattle. While they do not export livestock or meat, the men of Charity do export wool, though exclusively in refined form as finished yarn which they produce on sophisticated looms. 

Agriculturally, Charity is known best for its animal husbandry, and is not known to export any crops at all. However, they do grow a hearty breed of hops that they use in their copious brewing of beer. They 

produce barely enough grains to feed the town, and not enough of anything else. They import food stuffs from nearby Liberty Hill, which they buy with coal and oil. The only foods they export are beers and cheese. 

Charity is best known for its metals. It produces vast quantities of wrought iron, brass and steel. Great foundries are found at all mine sites, having been moved out of the town proper over 20 years ago because of the poisonous fogs that were produced. The town exports only refined ores and finished products. They trade ingots of iron and brass to both Shadowdale and Dagger Falls, but only trade steel as refined goods such as knifes, swords, spear tips, and so on. 

Glass is also a well-known commodity from Charity. The sands on the banks of the Charnel are ideal for the blowing of glass, and the town has provided glass and glass products to the dale from the very first years of its settlement. In the town itself, most window openings are filled with the characteristic diamond-leaded glass patterns that typify the common architecture. 

Politics 
The citizens of this settlement, from the lowest freeman to the Chancellor, all declare loyalty to King Verovan II of Westgate. They hold several letters patent from King Verovan 

I, declaring the region part of the Verovan Dukedom. As such, Verovan is both their king and their duke, and all titles flow from his grace. Four families (Falconer, Ferguson, Muloch & Whitmore) hold lands in the region that they call 'Earldoms', and each family has an Earl at its head (always a male, not necessarily the eldest; the system of primogeniture is unclear in this curious place). 

It should be noted that these Earls control tracts of land that would be considered small in their native Westgate. The titles themselves were likely honorary titles given because of the military accomplishments of the men that settled this region. 

Falconer Coat of Arms 
A Chancellor is appointed by the King every Shield Meet, usually by remote correspondence. The Chancellor acts as the head of the polity, but in reality, is more of a chairman in the highest court, called the Royal Assembly. Here, only men with titles are permitted, and the highest-level decisions about Charity are made. 

Below the Royal Assembly is the Common Assembly, in which 33 middlemen, some with titles such as baron or knight, hold office. The day-to-day decisions for Charity are made here. This Assembly elects a Speaker every year. The Speaker is the only real full-time politician in the town, and could be thought of as something akin to a mayor. 

Below the Common Assembly is the Labour Assembly, which has a representative from each of the trade guilds in town. There are 15 guilds, and they are all powerful organizations; citizens earn the title 'free' from membership in a guild. While middlemen and landmen inherit their titles, freemen must earn their title by membership in a guild. The guilds have been a powerful force in both politics and finance in the town; they underwrite many developments for their members, and ensure the quality of

Military 
All armed men in this settlement belong to the personal army of one of the four Earls. The size of these forces varies tremendously; the largest and most prosperous house, Falconer, has 80 full time soldiers, and perhaps as many as 200 militia (summoned from the freemen living on the properties of the Earl). 

The head of each military force is the Earl himself, and to be sure, every Earl is a veteran of many battles, and is both physically and tactically fit to lead. 

Ferguson Coat of Arms 
Beneath the Earls are the knights of his household, which may or may not be blood. These are mounted soldiers that all have a small group of mounted, professional soldiers attached to them, as well as a larger number of footmen (both professional and militia). 

All houses have at least one company of artillerists that operate the cannons and other war machines that each house is responsible for maintaining. 

All soldiers, both foot and mounted, train with the ubiquitous firearms which the town produces. Mounted soldiers tend to carry one or more pistols, while ranks of foot set up columns of rifles. 

The land around the town itself is divided into four Earldoms, in which you can routinely find patrols from the livery of their commander. In the town itself, these liveried soldiers are usually only seen in the inner bailey of the old town, close to the homes of the Earls. It is customary for the Chancellor to provide the policing of the rest of the town through his own expense, and his men are 'dressed down', as they put it, in the livery of the town itself (field and emblem inset). 

The town does have a small and professional police force that is organized 
by the Common Assembly. The Constable is the head of this small force of a half dozen men armed with clubs, and although these are all freemen, they are professional, intelligent men who keep the town orderly. 
Personal Economies 

 Freemen (i.e. miners and other labourers, soldiers, etc.): Make 3-10 SP/day. Wives of Freemen can make about the same working from home, or working in the homes of wealthier citizens. 

 Middlemen (i.e. shop owners, skilled artisans, priests, etc.): Make 2-5 GP/day. Some very successful ones might make 5 times that. Wives of Middlemen don't do as much work as the wives of Freemen, as a general rule. Some might have a cottage industry or do some work for wealthier citizens, and might make 5-10 SP/day. 

 Landmen: Are exceptionally wealthy, gaining vast incomes from rent on their properties, licenses to conduct business and harvest resources. These families bring in 50-100 GP/day from various activities. 

Expenses 
 Rent - Freeman Cottage or Rowhouse: 1-5 SP/day 

 Rent - Middleman's Rowhouse (2 levels; ground shop and second floor residence): 1-5 GP/day 

 Food - 1 day/person/simple fare (healthy, respectable portions, good beer): 1 SP (about 3cp/meal) 

 Food - luxury meal for 5-10 people: 1 GP 

 Taxes - Earldoms: All citizens pay a head-tax to their Earl. This is 1 GP/person/year 

 Taxes - Town: All citizens living in Charity proper must pay 1 GP/person/year in order to be 'Free of the Town' (A 'Freeman' with all the rights and obligations that go along with it) 

 Taxes - Church: All citizens are expected, but not required, to tithe one major church, and quarter-tithe both a secondary church and Charnel Priory. About 90% of citizens tithe, while about 50% do both quarter-tithes. 

Example of a Freeman's Family Finances - The Hammersmith Family 

John Hammersmith (35 years old) is a journeyman blacksmith and is Free of the Town. 

He is married to Elizabeth Hammersmith (30), who works as a maid in the Ferguson Manor. 

They have 4 children, aged 15, 13, 11, 6. Their two youngest attend the school during the day, the two oldest both work as common labourers in the town (they are not yet 'Freemen' or 'Free of the Town') 

Income: 13 SP/day 
John - 7 SP/day 
Elizabeth - 4 SP/day (plus scraps of food from the Fergusons, old clothes and other discarded things) Oldest boys - 1 SP/day each 

Expenses: 10s1c/day 
Rent: 3 SP (they live in a respectable rowhouse, with 3 bedrooms, a water closet, and a small garden in the back) 

Food: 4 SP (they eat well, with meals supplemented by the wife's benefits from her job at Ferguson Manor; without this extra bit of help, it could cost 6 SP/day to feed them) 
Schooling: 4 CP (2CP/day for each of their children still in school) 
Tithe: 1s7c (They tithe the Old Fort 1s1c, they quarter tithe the Priory for 3 cp and the church of Denier 3cp) 

This leaves the family with nearly 3 SP/day for extras. John is likely to spend 1sp every few nights at the Old Fort. The kids and wife have good clothes. The house is brightly lit and has good (not fancy) 

furnishings. They have been able to save 1 SP/day for several years, and will be able to retire when they are too old to work. John will have a pension from the Black Smith's Guild, so they will have a comfortable retirement. They are in 'good standing' with the Priory, so they will also have good access to medical treatment for the whole family.
