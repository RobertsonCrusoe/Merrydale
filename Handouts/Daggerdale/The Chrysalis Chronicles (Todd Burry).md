## Prologue

# **A Call for Bravery, Endurance, and Honour\!** {#a-call-for-bravery,-endurance,-and-honour!}

Alturiak 17, 1483 DR.

By Order of the Governors’ Assembly, at its Seventh Sitting, Alturiak 21-29, 1483 DR, the establishment of the Thistle Ridge Reclamation and the creation of an Adjudicating Committee with authorization to oversee the Reclamation is hereby announced to all citizens of Daggerdale, and all her allies notified forthwith.

It is the will of the Assembly that a Company of suitable capability and virtue be Chartered to Reclaim the settlement known as Thistle Ridge Farm, to secure its walls and other properties, to hold and defend those properties from enemies current and future, and to see to the growth and prosperity of that settlement.

The Adjudicating Committee, which is composed of i) Bishop Whitegate, ii) High Cultivar Oakdale, iii) Mayor Cassimar, iv) General Thunderstorm, and v) Ambassador Whitmore (Charity), will receive applications from organizations with a Charter Militant, and those seeking a Temporary Charter Militant for the purposes of this endeavor. They will select the five most suitable Companies and pit them in competition to test all the skills needed to carry out the Reclamation.

The Competition will be divided into three Measures: The Military Prowess Measure, The Mercantile Measure, and The Intelligence Measure. There will also be points from the endorsement of the contest’s adjudicating committee.

The successful team will be given a Temporary Settlement Charter, which will give them authority for the following:

* To clear all hostile creatures from Thistle Ridge Farm and its surroundings.  
* To hold it against further incursions.  
* To dispose of property and assets within the Farm as they see fit.

If the holders of the Temporary Settlement Charter can establish a new farm at Thistle Ridge by the Feast of the Moon, 1485 DR, they will be granted a Permanent Settlement Charter, which will enshrine Thistle Ridge Farm as a member of the Daggerdale Concordat, complete with a seat on the Governors’ Assembly.

## The Dragon’s Last Watch {#the-dragon’s-last-watch}

Sunrise. 1st day of the month of Ches in the Year of the Tasked Weasel, 1483 Dale Reckoning.

Zephrin had opened the gate a few hours earlier, but only now did the first signs of the rising sun begin to stretch on the eastern horizon. He had been waiting for this moment all night.

“So that’s that, eh?” came the tired voice from just inside the gate, where some semblance of warmth and shelter could be found. Sergeant Templeton and the other two sentries had been sitting around a brazier on the other side of the open portcullis, trying to keep warm as best they could. Coutts and Decker stood and stretched, their minds already on the ration of oatmeal and tea waiting for them at the barracks, not to mention their bunks.

“Be seeing ya, then,” said Coutts, with a wave of his hand towards Zephrin. Decker had never quite warmed to the Dragonborn, and simply gave a small nod before following his fellow Sunblade up into the town.

The morning squad hadn’t arrived yet, a fact that caused Templeton to curse them to freeze at Umberlee’s tits \- though he said it beneath his breath lest the Frost Queen be actually listening on a morning such as this.

Zephrin’s gaze was still on the horizon. Those first rays of sun meant he was now a free man. He couldn’t remember the last time he'd been free. Had he ever been?

He’d belonged to the Sunblades for the last 4 years. The True Zhents before that. The Last Spike and The First Spike both, before that. The Church of Oghma even before that...

Running fast into the woods with the twins, Robbie and Ricky Hounslow, when the three of them had each stolen one of Auntie Farah’s “award-winning” apple pies\! She’d been baking those all day for the Midsummer Fair…

Zephrin cracked a small smile at this one fleeting memory of his childhood amongst the halflings of Deepingdale.

Cousins Patty and Fat Vic had found them, and Robbie couldn’t sit down for a tenday after that\! He wondered if Auntie Farah still lived. He wondered if he showed up in Deepingdale this Midsummer if she’d bake him an apple pie.

Zephrin’s smile faded.

It wasn’t long after that day that everyone decided that Zephrin should spend more time at the Leaves of Learning and less time out on the Hounslow farm.

The last time he saw Ricky, she was on the ground, crying. She’d tried to stop him from coming north, and Zephrin had shoved her to the ground, pushing past her. How many years has it been? How many decades?

If he went home, would Ricky forgive him? Would Robbie or anyone else? Was it even home anymore? Or was Rockland right and all that waited for him at home in Deepingdale was a jail cell.

Zephrin took out the paper Captain Rockland had handed him last night: “A Call for Bravery, Endurance and Honour\!”

Sergeant Templeton cleared his throat, bringing Zephrin back from his reverie.

“Well, you big ol’ Lizard: this is the end of it, is it?” He said with a glint of affection in his eye. Like most of the Sunblades in town, Templeton had grown to like and respect the Dragonborn. He was a good soldier, sure. More importantly, as far as Templeton was concerned, he was a good thief and he always shared his spoils with his squadmates.

Almost as if reading the Sergeant’s mind, Zephrin reached into his satchel and pulled out a bottle of Cormyrean brandy, handing it over, and giving the speechless Templeton a pat on the shoulder as he walked away.

Later that morning, having collected his belongings from the barracks and gone through the final bureaucratic trials of officially leaving the Sunblades, Zephrin found himself approaching The Tomb.

Captain Rockland had told him to seek out an elf woman named Jeska here.

The two Redjacks standing guard at the door of the Myth Drannor Embassy had eyed him with curiosity, more than anything, as he approached. Most of the Daggerdalesmen had heard of Zephrin: it was hard to miss the only Dragonborn in town; much less one that had worn the Sunblade uniform and often stood guard at the gates of Dagger Falls.

But Zephrin mostly kept to himself, and neither Redjack sentry could think of any reason why the Dragonborn would be approaching them now. They tightened the grip on their weapons as he approached.

“What’s your business with…um, uh, Myth Drannor… friend?” Said the braver of the two.

Zephrin could see the knuckles whiten on the man’s grip on his halberd. He could see the sweat on their brow, despite the deep winter cold.

Deep inside him, unbidden, his blood rumbled, roiled and churned. Zephrin's throat filled with a metallic tingle, his teeth crackled with electricity.

Lightning. Claws. Thunder. Wings. Wind. Teeth. Waves. Death.

It would be a trifle to end these insignificant mammalian lives. These barely elevated apes. How dare they stand in his way\! How dare they question him?\! You are a Dragon\! Teach them their insignificance\! Let the storm loose and show them their place\!

His grip tightened around the handle of the axe at his waist.

Mammals...

A small tug on his sleeve.

It was almost imperceptible.

Another small tug, somehow enough to bring Zephrin back from the ledge he found himself on.

With a blink, he was back in Dagger Falls. He swallowed hard, the lightning doused and dissipated. The grip on his weapon relaxed.

*What was that?*

He was aware of the two sentries before him, stinking of fear and confusion.

Again, a small tug on his sleeve.

Zephrin looked down at a pile of rags pulling on his sleeve, a child’s eyes suddenly appearing from within. A small hand, steady with confidence and determination emerged from the rags and offered a small, folded piece of paper.

As soon as the paper had left its hand, the pile ran, disappearing deep into the town.

Zephrin looked to the sentries, still quavering, and down to the piece of paper and read: “Tonight. 8pm. Quick Axes. Don’t be late.”

He gave a look back at The Tomb, his gaze no longer on the guards but rather on the window above where a halfling was looking down at the scene. The halfling met Zephrin’s gaze, and gave him a small nod then drew the curtains shut.

Tonight, then.

## Three No Good Days In Tesh Valley {#three-no-good-days-in-tesh-valley}

19th of Alturiak, 1483

![][image1]

Within the walls of the Tesh Valley Farm, a pair of halflings wrestled with unloading a weighty trunk from a wagon.

"Your trunk is a right heavy one, Mum. What've you stashed in there?" inquired the stout halfling man.

Standing beside the wagon, a halfling woman with a mop of greying hair adorned with hints of orange streaks graced her head. Her once youthfully pretty visage now bore the gentle creases of age, etched beneath her eyes and cheeks. Marigold Thickwickle, known as Mari, smiled warmly as she replied.

"If you're sendin' me packin' from my own home, dear, then I'll be takin' my dishes with me," Mari replied.

In that moment, Mari's son, Pim, grunted as his grip faltered on the trunk's corner. He retorted, "Come on, Mum. You know it ain't safe for you here no more. It's gettin' worse with each passing day."

"As you wish, my sweetie. You and your brothers are old enough to make such decisions," Mari replied in her gentle, loving tone.

Just then the door swung open anda cascade of light spilled onto the street from inside the apartment. A youthful halfling lady, bearing a striking resemblance to a younger Mari, her hair auburn and figure untouched by motherhood, stood at the threshold.

"Hurry up and bring that inside. It's too dark to be dilly-dallyin' out here," she admonished, hands placed firmly on her hips.

"Won't take long if we each lend a hand and take a side, Bella," Mari suggested.

Together, the quartet efficiently finished unloading, leaving the wagon sheltered beneath the archway. The two men bid their farewells with brisk hugs and slipped away into the shadows of the night on barefoot.

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

19th of Alturiak, 1483

A distant dog's bark pierced the stillness, rousing Bella and Mari from their shared bed. Swiftly, they left their cozy haven and moved into the adjacent room. Bella cautiously cracked open a window, surveyed the surroundings, and then withdrew her head, firmly closing the window behind her.

"It's them," Bella reported.

"All right, daughter. This time, you'll handle it on your own, just as I know you can. Yondalla[^1] blesses all halfling women who keep a good home," Mari encouraged.

Bella briefly glanced at the black scarf gracing her neck, then sighed, nodding in acknowledgment. Mari retreated to the bedroom, leaving the door impercebtibly ajar.

Before long, a pounding echoed at the door, accompanied by a barking dog. Bella promptly swung the door open and whispered, "Quick, come inside. We don't want to wake Mother."

A dog promptly darted inside, making a beeline for the bedroom door, sniffing and scratching. Unseen by all, a treat slipped through the door's crack, which Mari deftly closed.

Standing at the threshold were Mari's sons from the previous day, supporting a third halfling between them. He appeared roughly bandaged and exhibited signs of severe bleeding underneath. His face and shins bore scrapes and wounds.

To a human observer, the lad might seem like a mere child. However, to halflings, it was evident that he was in his twenties—still youthful, on the cusp of adulthood.

"Argh, it hurts\!" the young man exclaimed.

"Hush, Doddy, or you'll wake Mum," Pim snapped.

“Not to worry, “ said Bella, “Mother has taken the pipe tonight. She won’t be waking up till the morrow.”

"I want Mummy\!" Doddy wailed through the pain.

"Well, you'll have to settle for your sister\! Now, get him up on the table," Bella instructed.

Bella went to work, tending to Doddy's wounds in a proficient but somewhat brusque manner.

"Argh, Bella\! Not so hard," Doddy protested.

"Quit your bellyachin', you big baby. I won't be a moment longer," Bella retorted.

With the wounds dressed, Bella placed her hand on a small brooch she wore, quietly invoking, "Yondalla, heal my family." She kissed her hand and tenderly touched it to her brother's injuries.

Suddenly, Doddy's body tensed, arching before swiftly relaxing.

"Thank you, my dear sister," Doddy expressed, his voice steadier and more composed. "Could you rustle us up something to eat?"

Bella offered a warm smile to her brothers and replied, "Of course. Have a seat, and I'll put the kettle on."

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

20th of Alturiak, 1483

The following morning, Bella, having stayed up late the previous night to see her brothers off, awoke later than usual. After offering her morning prayers, she ventured into the kitchen. The table was set for her breakfast, and Mari appeared to be packing her belongings.

"What are you up to, Mother? You can't just leave right after you've just arrived," Bella protested.

Mari straightened up and approached her daughter. "I'm afraid I must, sweetie."

"But why? We need you here."

"Oh, Bella, my dear. You're a Thickwickle through and through—kind-hearted and caring. You'll do just fine looking after the family. You and your brothers need to forge your own paths for a time."

"But you're a Thickwickle too, for Yondalla's sake\!" Bella exclaimed, her emotions held at bay, but tears glistening in her eyes.

Mari gently but firmly placed her hands on Bella's shoulders. "Aye, I am indeed a Thickwickle through and through, and for most of my days, that's been true\! Yet—I'm also a Sweetberry, and Sweetberrys are cut from a different cloth. We must tend to our kin, but we've a duty to our kind too."

Bella sighed in resignation. "I ought to know by now there's no changing your mind."

"You've tugged at my heartstrings your entire life, my dear. But this is a path I must walk. Come, let me show you something."

Mari led Bella over to the trunk they had moved just two days prior, carefully unwrapping a cloth that concealed a small metal shield beneath. The aged shield bore the marks of time but was meticulously preserved, catching the sunlight with a subtle shimmer of oil. Embossed on its front was the image of a cornucopia, bursting with the harvest of all seasons.[^2]

![][image2]

"A shield of Yondalla..." Bella whispered, her voice trailing off in reverence.

"This belonged to your Thrice Great-Grandmother. She gifted it to me when I was scarcely older than you. I tucked it away to start this family, but now, I am called to bear it."

Beneath the shield rested a humble set of leather armor.

"Is that Father's armor?" Bella inquired.

"Aye, though I believe it will suit me well with a few adjustments to the buckles. Here, help me don it." Mari and her daughter shared a quiet moment, their emotions unspoken yet evident in their eyes, brimming with unshed tears.

"Alright, my dear. The wagon's nearly loaded. Lend me a hand with the last few items from the trunk, and then I must be on my way."

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Outside, Mari climbed into the wagon, taking up the reins of the petite pony that pulled it. Bella stood beside, looking up at her mother.

"You know, Mother, you may have objected to Pim sending you away for the full tenday, but I'm beginning to suspect it was your idea all along," Bella remarked, a faint smile touching her lips.

Mari smiled softly and replied, "Perhaps, my darling. Perhaps." With that, she gently tugged the reins and called out to the pony, "Alright, Peony, let's be on our way\!"

The pony neighed and began its slow journey down the alley toward the south gate. The alleyway lay empty, except for the halfling and her wagon. Unbeknownst to her, the dog from the previous night trailed behind at a safe distance, whimpering softly but never daring to bark.

Mari rode in relative silence, a hopeful smile gracing her lips. She retrieved a piece of parchment sized for humans and unfurled it. Her smile widened as she held aloft the tabloid and whispered to herself, "Perhaps this will bring enduring peace to us hobbits of the Dale."

At the top of the parchment, in bold letters, read:

With a final, defiant scream, Ser Rowan's stance faltered, and he was brought to the ground, his unconscious form a silent statement of the ferocity and sacrifice he had displayed in defense of his companions and the values they stood for.

Simultaneously, Jeska performed a feat of acrobatic prowess. With the grace of a dancer and the boldness of a warrior, she swung across the tavern on a chandelier, landing deftly at Fiona's side, a protective guardian against the ensuing chaos.

Amidst the physical tumult, Jasper, the enigmatic spellcaster of the group, wielded his arcane arts with finesse. With a mere gesture, a tankard flew as if guided by an invisible hand, striking a thug with such force that confusion took hold, causing him to swing wildly at a bewildered bystander. As the skirmish subsided, with the thugs either knocked senseless or cowed by the formidable display of prowess and magic, a newfound respect filled the room. The patrons of "Quick Axes," having witnessed the bravery and skill of Mari and her companions, knew that the tales of this night would be recounted for years to come. Ser Rowan, though battered, had shown the heart of a lion. Jeska's daring and agility were the talk of awe-filled whispers. Jasper's mastery of the arcane was a spectacle of power and precision.

Tucked beneath the chair where Sanduskyn had sat, almost as if by magic, a small chest lay hidden. It was only after the dust had settled and their adversaries lay bested that the chest was discovered. Inside, they found two potions of leaping, alongside a bag heavy with gold. Accompanying these treasures was a note, its message penned with a hand of friendship and generosity.

"I wish no payment in return for these items. Good luck with the competition tomorrow, my new friends."

As they gathered the chest's contents, gratitude filled their hearts, not only for the material assistance but for the bond that had been formed. With spirits lifted and resolve strengthened, they knew that whatever challenges lay ahead, they would not face them alone.

## Chapter 7

# **The Mountain Of Mayhem** {#the-mountain-of-mayhem}

20th of Ches, 1483 DR–morning

![][image14]

The day dawned crisp and cool in Dagger Falls, the late snowflakes pirouetting through the air, a gentle dusting that never quite claimed the ground. The temperature hovered around freezing, yet the day held the promise of warmth and pleasant skies. The Cathedral's bells, faithful heralds of the day, rang out at six in the morning and again as the first sliver of sunlight crested the eastern horizon, signaling the Amaunatori to their prayers. Devotees streamed toward the Cathedral, while others practiced their piety privately, facing the dawn's light from windows or in the streets, basking in the nascent day.

The normally immaculate streets bore the remnants of the previous night's festivities. A smattering of bottles and cups littered the Old Bailey, interspersed with scraps of food, ribbons, and the odd piece of clothing. It was an uncommon sight for Dagger Falls, a place where such disarray was a rarity, soon to be tidied away after the Amaunatori's Prime—their sunrise prayer.

The bakers were the day's early risers, their chimneys already exuding the sweet scent of baked goods as they began their day's toil.

By eight bells, the town was astir. The Old Market buzzed with life, the busiest it had been since the previous autumn. Breakfast vendors did brisk trade among the throng of townsfolk gathered to discuss the day's agenda and the forthcoming tournament. The first round of combat was but an hour away, and a palpable excitement electrified the air—a rarity for the Falls, provoking much speculation and eager wagering on the outcomes.

“I heard they built a mechanical dragon, and that the White Cloaks and those other guys will have to try to beat it to keep going in the tournament\!”

“No no, I saw lots of wood going up there. They built and obstacle course, and the teams have to run across planks while wizards throw fire and lightning at them to try to knock them off\!”

“That’s not it at all. I poked my nose through the barrier they erected, and I seen it with my own eyes; there’s a wooden mountain in the middle of that old corn field, and they have to fight each other to the top. The White Cloaks are likely to win, but I’d take a wager on either side, depending on the odds you’re giving\!”

Outside the Embassy, a small assembly awaited the adventurers' emergence. Some fans, swift to show their support, brandished homemade banners of green with a purple butterfly set against a chrysalis and tree backdrop. Many donned green and purple, a color scheme seemingly chosen by an impromptu committee. Despite the White Cloaks' popularity, the sight of an elf at the Embassy seemed to inspire a faction of the crowd, harkening back to a deep-seated reverence for the First People.

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

En route to The Arena, the full wakefulness of the town was unmistakable. Spectators queued eagerly, while a platoon of Sun Blades, led by General Thunderstorm, escorted the adventurers, wishing them luck. The crowds they passed offered laughter and unsolicited predictions on the day's matches.

The Arena itself was a testament to purpose, surrounded by a temporary fence of posts and beams, with yellow canvases obscuring the view. The battleground was marked by a muddy field, enclosed by a simple fence, and dominated by a wooden mountain in the center—three tiers high, the focal point of the Arena, with smaller structures at opposing corners.

The stands were already packed, the crowd spilling out as more onlookers found vantage points wherever possible. Around the grounds, vendors hawked food and drink, and the atmosphere was one of heightened festivity. Chaunteans, already indulging in ale, added to the din in their holiday best.

Towers at each end of the field housed officials and magistrates, some equipped with Staves of Healing and neophytes clutching scrolls. As the excitement built, the grounds were awhirl with activity: children emulating the day's competitors, minstrels creating a discordant cacophony, jugglers and fire breathers contributing to the spectacle, and wagers being whispered.

Inside the tent provided for the adventurers, a sanctuary of sorts, they could finally pause and prepare. The space, outfitted with cots, a table and chairs, and racks for gear, was illuminated by a magical lamp. "Your Sanctuary, Chrysalis. No one will bother you in here," they were assured, and indeed, the divine magic of the place was palpable.

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

The blare of a horn sliced through the morning air, and a voice, deep and resonant, boomed across the field. The distinctive accent could belong to none other than Ambassador Whitmore, his voice magically magnified, commanding the attention of all present. "We shall begin the First Round of the competition very shortly, everyone\! Please settle in\! We shall summon our heroes momentarily." His words, a clarion call to the eager masses, brought a hush to the crowd, a collective inhalation of breath charged with anticipation.

Ambassador Whitmore's voice, magically amplified, broke the buzz of anticipation, announcing the imminent start of the competition. As his voice fell silent, the cacophony resumed, the charged atmosphere nearly tangible.

Summoned to the field, the adventurers were led to the Mountain by yellow-robed magistrates. As they ascended, they faced the White Cloaks on the summit, greeted by cheers and jeers alike. The Redjacks, standing at the field's edge, didn't hold back their barbs, and the other companies watched with interest.

"Fellow Dalesmen\! Your heroes\!" Whitmore's voice rose above the roar of the crowd. Introductions followed, each name eliciting reactions from the gathered crowd—support, disdain, and everything in between.

“Let me introduce our heroes starting with the unexpected Chrysalis\! Lead by the Elven Diplomat, Jeska, everyone in town has heard her name, and knows she represents a rekindling of our most ancient alliance, for which our very calendar marks its beginning\!” While there are clearly some supporters of the elf in the crowd, and they boisterously cheer Jeska on, a dozen or more people yell out racial slurs against elves, some of the cleverest drawing laughs here and there.

“With her, a Sembian…” with this, a great deal of booing and hissing, which he motions to subdue. “Yes, a Sembian, and the only known living relation of the Alavandor family in the Dales. Lucan Alavandor, or Lucan the Younger as some of you have taken to calling him\!” some laughing and jeering follow, and a little cheering.

“An accomplished priestess of Yondalla, small but mighty, with a heart twice the size of ours, and a family five times the size, Merigold Thickwickle of Tesh Valley Farm\!” A large cohort of halflings in the crowd now stands up, and wave banners and flags they have made to honour Mari. A couple of the older halfling matriarchs wipe tears of pride from their cheeks.

“To provide arcane support for Chrysalis, let me introduce the Mage of Shadowdale…” with this several gasps fall over the crowd, and Whitmore gives an uncomfortable chuckle. “No no, not that ‘Mage of Shadowdale’, no no. This is one of greater youth and perhaps greater destiny\! Jasper Hawkford\!” With this the crowd murmmers its disapproval, over the faint and dying cheers of a few supporters.

“And finally, holding down the Mountain like only one as prodigious as he could, Ser Rowan Grumbold\!” he is interrupted by one loud and nasty Red Jack who yells out “Ser Pumpkin more like it” to the amusement of much of the audience. Whitmore struggles to end the laughing and jeering of the crowd. “Ser Rowan is an accomplished knight of Cormyr who graces the field this morning, and will no doubt surprise you all…”

“Shall we learn the names of your new White Cloaks?\!” he continues once the crowd had settled, but this again sets them off, especially the dozens of children that have come to watch. These younger fans of the White Cloaks are so excited they can’t contain themselves, many of them dancing about in the stands in great excitement, waving white flags all about.

“Leading our new team is the Virtuous Tarra Hilltop, Lieutenant (retired) of the Sun Blades 1st Company, 2nd Platoon, Mountaineers\! A battle chaplain of great experience, Hilltop is sure to lead with skill and integrity\!” and the crowd roils up again. Everyone in the crowd seems to love the idea of this woman, who in the face of all this praise, simply nods respectfully, and salutes the tower, this gesture returned in unison by every Sunblade in the arena, and immediately afterward by many of the boys in attendance. Even the Redjacks are quiet at this, swallowing hard as they see the respect that hundreds of capable soldiers display for their former battle sister.

“Just as impressive are her former shield brothers, Jannus and Tyrell Dale, brothers by birth and by battle, the Dale brothers have lived most of their life at the Castle and served under Lt. Hilltop in the Mountaineers, their spears having found the hearts of orcs, ogres and Shadowvar alike\!” The crowd is nearly as enamoured with these handsome young men as with their leader.

“From the War Wizard Academy of Suzail, the Impressive Alexandus Ward\!” Ward is the only White Cloak with a smirk on his face, but he seems nonplussed that the cheers for him are half as powerful of that and Jannus and Tyrell. Still, he cuts a dashing figure in his crisp white cloak and the jawline that Reks mentioned, and is clearly a favourite of many of the ladies in the crowd.

“Finally, one last recruit from the retired ranks of the Sunblades, this time a Forster of the 16th Company 3rd Platoon, born and raised in the wilds near Lostholme, the dangerous Zelia Oakbough\!”

## Capture The Flag

Atop the crafted wooden peak, Ser Rowan stood, hammer and flag in hand, his presence a testament to the tumult and valor that had preceded this moment. Moments ago, empowered by magic, he had leapt across the battlefield, his landings thunderous, carving the earth beneath him as if to mark the passage of a giant.

With the flag secured, he had vanquished one of the Dale brothers, yet now faced the remaining twin, vengeance burning in the latter's eyes. Retreating was no longer an option; the platform's edge whispered warnings beneath his heels. The flag's position, a beacon of their struggle, left him precariously balanced between victory and the void.

Then, as fate hung in the balance, the Dale brother's advance halted, an arrow finding its mark, sending him tumbling backward into unconsciousness. Ser Rowan, in disbelief, cast his gaze over his shoulder. Jeska, silhouette against the sky, stood balancing on the fence behind him, like a heron on a post.

“Much obliged, Governess,” Rowan exhaled, his breaths heavy, his visage a canvas of exertion painted with the sweat of battle. He eyed the arrow still biting into his flesh, contemplating a moment’s care, yet his concern turned outward to the fallen adversaries at his feet. “T’is just a flesh wound; he’ll be alright,” he murmured, more to the winds than to any ear.

Suddenly, a shield arced over the edge, heralding Mari’s arrival. “Are you all right, Ser Rowan?” her voice, touched with the lilting warmth of concern.

“Aye, Mum. Thanks to ye,” he replied, a smile breaking through the weariness.

Mari, with a halfling's resolve, clambered onto the platform, her words a gentle jest at their shared plight. “Whoever designed these platforms sure doesn’t like halflings. Yondalla, have mercy on me joints\!”

The shared moment of victory, the laughter that followed, spoke of deeper bonds forged in the crucible of competition. “Can you believe it? We won\!” Mari exclaimed, her eyes alight with the joy of their unexpected triumph.

“Aye, Mum. And we won in a way I would have least expected,” Ser Rowan agreed, his voice a reflection of the day's surprises. “Jasper is one clever lad, “ he added.

“A clever lad indeed, “ Mari readily agreed.

Their camaraderie, a beacon as bright as the flag they defended, led them down from the mountain, Ser Rowan carrying Mari with a knight’s care, back to where their companions awaited.
