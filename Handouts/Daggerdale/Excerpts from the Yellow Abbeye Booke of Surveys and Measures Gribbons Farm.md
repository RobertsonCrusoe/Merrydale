Locations of Interest 
Bridgegate: This is a small hamlet at the western end of the settlement, about a mile away from the main village.  There are 41 people here that live in a small fortified farm that controls access to the bridge which crosses the Sukiskyn River, which comes down the escarpment and joins the Tesh here. 

East Watch: This is a small hamlet at the eastern end of the settlement a little over a mile away from the main village.  There are 29 people here plus a platoon of Sun Blades. The village sits on a small knoll in an elbow of the Tesh, and overlooks both the river and the road which passes by, some 300 yards to the north.  There is a stone tower here and a barracks for an entire company of Sun Blades, and the 1st platoon is usually stationed here; the first real defenders on the eastern edge of Daggerdale. 

BansheeВ’s Lament: This is a deep and complicated cave system that has been used as a copper mine from time to time over the centuries. The depths or extent of the caves are unknown to the men of the Dale, and it is supposed that it connects with the vast network of limestone caves in the region, and likely the Underdark. 

Gribbons Perch (Castle & Cathedral): This is an impressive dwarf-laid fortress that was built between 1441-1465. It has been built completely over a small island, and stands 20В’ or more from the surface of the river in most places.  Fortified bridges on the east and west side secure entry and egress, and there is always a significant body of the 2nd platoon on duty here, as well as a number of militia. The only dayВ­to-day activities that take place at the castle for most residents are services for Amaunator, which many residents attend. 

The Village: Surrounding the Perch on the north side of the river is the village of GribbonsВ’ Farm.  There are about 200 people living here in well-made homes, most 2 stories high, with a small number of successful businesses and skilled artisans. 

Autumnhill Terrace Inn В– Built on a small stone terrace on the edge of the river/moat opposite the castle, this inn is a good size and of good quality.  Barns and paddocks at the edge of the village can accommodate any number of animals, and this is a favourite stopping point for caravans who want to do as much business as they can before being taxed at the gates of Dagger Falls.

Fish Market: From Spring until the first snows, a small market operates in the village that sells fresh fish for excellent prices. Local fishermen catch quite a bit bass, sunfish and eels, which can be bought fresh or packed here. 

Hamlets: This farm has 4 other small hamlets besides the Bridgegate and East Watch.  Being in close proximity to Dagger Falls has allowed for the braver settlers to establish small hamlets of a few families one-two miles away from the main village. A few abandoned or ruined ones also dot the landscape here.
