A letter from Kennyth Thames to Steele the Elf.

Steele;

While consulting with Oghma today, I learned a couple things that I suspect could help you, and indeed, may have been intended for your pointy ears and beady eyes. I suspect you are having some dealings with Drow again, and so these facts should interest you.

First; A development occurred last night that has made the Cult of Vhaeraun in our City the most powerful one of it’s kind on the surface. I don’t know what this means to you, and I am certain I can live without knowing, but it troubles me some, and I hope you aren’t in over your head (again).

Second; in an uncharacteristic moment of clarity and unobscurity, Oghma did reveal to me, what I believe is the structural arrangement of the Company Dalael (though not in so many words).  The diagram below indicates levels of power, or a chain of command, with eight active cells at the bottom. These cells represent different types of Dalael resources. The lower cells seem to be business related, while the upper cells seem to be military related.

450px|center

I don’t know if any of this is any help to you at all, but if you are mixed up with these Dalael Boys again, this is worth knowing. This information came to me without my seeking it, so I am sure it is doubly important. Please use it wisely.

After I received all of this, I did some of my own digging, and came up with some interesting knowledge. There is some sort of major conflict going on in Menzoberanzan, between the Families and the Spider Queen Clergy of the city, against a certain faction of Beholders that were former allies. Furthermore, this conflict continues just beneath the surface of our City. Indeed, there are hints that it may have originated here on the surface. Further evidence of the delicate ecological balance that exists here on Faerûn; what happens above influences what happens below. Interesting.

And finally, I have heard a little too much about a book called the Encyclopaedae Ocularis Indomitus, authored by a fellow named Aesclepius of Wreathe. This Nether-Mage is also known by the title of Master of the Eyes. Not only have several people been in the Font of Knowledge seeking information about this Tome and it’s author, but I have learned that the miscreant Jannaxil Serpentil, and the serpentine Elaith have left to track this book down. Divine sources tell me that they have been motivated by Co. Dalael.

I am sure there is an interesting story behind all this, and I hope that you can tell me all about it one day, when it is nothing but a memory, and not a threat to my corpus materia. May Oghma give you insight to your challenges, and my Tymora make them few.

Your Friend,

— Kennyth Thames

Adeptus Librarian, The Font of Knowledge, Waterdeep}}
