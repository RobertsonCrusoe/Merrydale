# Bounties
# Source: The Sieges Perilous wiki (backup dated 2010-02-21)

The Bounty System of Castle Radiance

'Bounties' are dangerous jobs that are posted on the Sun Wheel at Castle Radiance every day. These can be thought of as missions or quests, but with some important qualities.

Firstly, bounties are posted in the form of wooden plaques that have the relevant details of the mission painted or burnt onto the wood. These plaques, which are usually about 1'-2' square, are pegged to the Sun Wheel in the Castle. Those wishing to undertake the bounty must physically remove the associated plaque from the wheel and take it to the Bounty Registrar in Loedegan's Hall, the room adjacent to the Sun Wheel.

After passing through the checkpoints at the entrance of Loedegan's Hall, bounty seekers are escorted to a large office just off the main hall, where they are briefly interviewed by the Bounty Registrar.

Even with new adventurers, it will often be the case that the Registrar will already have a file on the interviewee. Despite this, however, he is sure to ask many of the same questions that have already been asked several times of said adventurer. After meticulously filling out his paperwork, the Registrar will leave the adventurer in the office and seek out Lord Whitegate, who will come to meet the new adventurer in person.

If the adventurer meets with Lord Whitegate's approval, he or she is given official papers by the Registrar, which are signed by the adventurer, the Registrar, and Whitegate himself. The adventurer is then licensed to take bounties out of the Castle.

Registering for Pursuit of a Bounty

The licenced bounty taker need only present his papers and the bounty plaque to the Registrar in order to officially undertake the mission. Only one group may pursue the same bounty at a time, and the Registrar makes records of all who undertake each bounty.  

The office of Bounty Registrar was established to prevent the necessity of arbitrating between multiple parties seeking out (and then claiming) identical bounties. This both averts unnecessary and unproductive conflict between adventuring parties, and likewise ensures efficiency in the pursuit of military and policy objectives by having multiple parties simultaneously pursuing the various bounties set by the governments involved.

Common Bounties

Bounties posted in the middle of the Sun Wheel are called 'Common Bounty'. These can be undertaken by any licensed adventurer, and cashed in at any time. The longest-running Common Bounty is on orc heads (known as the Whitegate Census Bounty). Any licensed adventurer can turn in a pair of orc ears to claim this bounty of 10 gold. There are a few others as well, including;

• Shadow Bounty - A 50 gold bounty for turning in living followers of Shar.
• Intelligence Bounty - A 25 gold bounty for turning in valuable intelligence regarding locations and movements of large groups of monsters or Netherese agents and soldiers.
• Candlekeep Registry Bounty - a 5-25 gold bounty for having an artifact or magic item examined and recorded by the Emissaries from Candle Keep.

Common Bounties must be registered for, but any licensed bounty taker may claim them at the Registrar at any time.

Claiming Bounties

The terms of claiming a bounty can vary, though most of them involving bringing some sort of evidence to the Registrar. Pertinent details are printed on the individual plaques. The Registrar meticulously records all the relevant details (as determined by the Registrar, often to the consternation and delay of restless adventurers) of the events concerning the completion of the bounty mission, and pays out the bounty in coin on the spot.

Posting Bounties

Registered Organizations may post bounties on the Sun Wheel. This is a bit more involved a process than registering for a bounty-taking license, but a few organizations do use the wheel to post bounties. A purse for the remuneration of all posted bounties must be left with the Registrar's Office for surety, and bounty details must be approved by the Registrar (and, at the Registrar's discretion, Lord Whitegate) before they may be legally posted.

Presently, the Tethyamar League posts bounties on a regular basis, as does the Myth Drannor Embassy, and the Emissaries from Candle Keep.
