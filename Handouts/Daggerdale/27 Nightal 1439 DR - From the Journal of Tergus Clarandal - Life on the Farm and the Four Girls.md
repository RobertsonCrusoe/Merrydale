Journal of Tergus Clarandal
Nightal 27, 1439

The chill of winter has settled over the countryside, yet within the walls of our estate, warmth and contentment reign. I have left behind the bustling village of Thistle Ridge, with its ever-growing populace and the shadows that linger beneath its surface. The sense of doubt and dread about the obscure parts of my family's history and the mysterious tunnels beneath the village no longer haunt me as they once did.

Now, I find solace in our country estate, known simply as "the Farm." Here, with my beloved Senthia and our children, I have discovered a new way of life, one that brings me a deep sense of peace. Our library, filled with the books repatriated from our villa in town, has become my sanctuary. Surrounded by the wisdom of ages, I feel more at home than ever.

Tonight, the house is filled with laughter and the joy of family. Cousins, nieces, and nephews run through the halls, their merriment a reminder of what truly matters. I watch with pride as four young girls—Lilac Alavandor, Lilly Clarandal, Poppy Sweetberry, and Alaventa Alavaxi—play together in the snowy garden. Lilly, my precious daughter, is among them, her laughter blending with that of her friends.

The bond between these girls is strong and heartening. They are the future of our families, and their unity symbolizes a hope that transcends our past. My gaze lingers on Alaventa Alavaxi, the pale girl whose grandparents vanished from history. Her presence here is a poignant reminder of the mysteries I once sought to unravel.

Reflecting on my time in the Chapel Hospital, where my inquiries about the Alavaxi were met with resistance, I am now more determined than ever to preserve our history. This has become my new battle—a fight against the shadows trying to cover the past, to bring light and understanding to our family's legacy. My library is my armory, and each book is a weapon against the obscurity that threatens to erase our stories.

I have a garden, a library, healthy children, and a loving wife...what more do I require?

There is one thing I'd like to add to my library, if I must admit my own covetousness even only to myself. In the Academy, Dean Petrark has secured a complete set Libri Perfecti Formae, which I shall inquire after on my next dreaded trip to town.

My duties as the highest-ranking officer of the Sun Blades in Thistle Ridge have not been entirely forsaken, but I now rely on my dependable sergeants to maintain order and drill the men in the village.

Life on the Farm suits me well. The tranquility of the countryside, the love of my family, and the pursuit of knowledge have given me a new purpose. The shadows of the past may be vast and deep, but with each passing day, I am more resolved to illuminate them with the truth.

As I close my journal for the night, I am filled with a sense of fulfillment. The battles of my youth have shaped me, but it is the quest for understanding and the bonds of family that define me now. Whatever the future holds, I am ready to face it with an open heart and a determined spirit.
