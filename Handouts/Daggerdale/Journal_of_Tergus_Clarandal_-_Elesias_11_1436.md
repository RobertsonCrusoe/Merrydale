Journal of Tergus Clarandal
Elesias 11, 1436

After ten long years of service with the Sun Blades, I have finally returned to Thistle Ridge to settle down with my beloved Senthia and our children. The sight of our villa within the protective walls of the village brings me a sense of comfort and familiarity. Yet, beneath this surface of contentment, I find myself ill at ease.

During my absence, a grand project has transformed the village. A sophisticated network of sewers now runs beneath the newer parts of Thistle Ridge, where our people reside. This modern marvel of sanitation has brought fresh water and efficient waste removal, enhancing the quality of life for many. However, this network conspicuously bypasses the older section of town, where the worshippers of the Old Faith dwell, leaving me with a sense of unease.

As a boy, I explored the labyrinthine limestone caves beneath Thistle Ridge, marveling at the great underground river that flowed through them. This river, now harnessed and redirected to some mysterious, bottomless cavern deep below, serves our needs but at what cost? The unknown depths to which our sewage is carried away fill me with apprehension.

Years of battling The Shadow west of Castle Radiance have taught me to be wary of what lurks in darkness. The complex system of sewers, tunnels, and hidden caverns beneath our home seems to invite an element of uncertainty that I cannot ignore. While the surface world of Thistle Ridge thrives in the light of day, there is an underworld beneath us that remains shrouded in mystery.

As I walk through our village, I observe the changes with a soldier's scrutiny. The advancements are remarkable, yet my instincts, honed by years of warfare, tell me that caution is warranted. The shadows cast by these subterranean passages are deep, and I cannot help but feel a sense of foreboding.

I must trust in the wisdom of our leaders and the engineers who have undertaken this project. Yet, I will remain vigilant. The safety and well-being of my family and our village depend on it. I will speak with Father and the elders, perhaps even the worshippers of Sylvanus, to understand more about these developments and the potential risks they carry.

For now, I take solace in the embrace of my family and the familiar rhythms of life in Thistle Ridge. But I will keep my sword close and my senses sharp, for I know all too well that danger often hides in the most unexpected places.
