== Greengrass, 1371 - A Story of Swords ==
''The origins of the Company of the Red Herring are best told by Jalthor Murlyn, Captain of the Night

Watch, Dock Ward in his report on the activities of Company of the Red Herring:''


left|
The only knowledge I have of the Company of the Red Herring concerns the months between

Greengrass and Harvestide of 1371. They did the City a couple of favours, but on the whole we’d have
been better off without them.


From what I understand, the company centred on a Dock Ward orphan that had had more than a few

run-ins with the Watch in my patrol days. Garth Shanks was always something of a character, as I

recall, getting up to mischief with Szarah Mudge’s boy, Tomas Quoyle - that is, when he wasn’t cutting

purses in the markets. He was always one to put on a show whenever anyone the authorities had any

questions. There was one point where he cleaned himself up enough to land a job at a respectable (if

not strictly legitimate) shop as an errand boy. Do you know Serpentil’s Books and Folios? Jannaxil

Serpentil sells maps and books, usually authentic, to a fairly large customer base, but most of his

business is on the shady side, dealing intelligence and worse to the usual suspects. From what I

understand, Serpentil was dealing fairly often in those days with some elf-operation called Company
Dalael.


I imagine they were circumspect enough that Shanks never got beyond the usual suspicions that

anyone of at least moderate intelligence would have when dealing with Serpentil. One of Shanks’ first

jobs was connected to this Dalael business – a ‘delivery’ to a house in Castle Ward. The package

turned out to be an incendiary device of some kind, and I hear the hit really got under the skin of one of

the Zhent traders active in the city at that time. As it happens, the boy was shaken up by the blast, and

he was present at Serpentil's when the trader in question - a tall, dark fellow from the Moonsea, I'm told
- stormed into the shop, hell bent on destruction. That was pretty much it for Garth’s association with
Serpentil, but not with the Moonsea trader.


===Hook...===
The individuals that would form the Company of the Red Herring came together following that business.

Ildar Orsabbas was a fop who owned a Sea Ward trade outlet called '''Orsabbas’ Fine Imports'''. The

idiot fancied himself a bit of a crime boss, calling himself the “Duke of Darkness” or something equally

moronic. We were on to the operation he ran, but he more often than not pointed us in the direction of

more useful quarry. At any rate, he had a pet elf named Ellyth, an ambitious piece I remember, if
misguided in her choice of mentors.


I understand that she was there on Greengrass Eve, when Orsabbas met with a rogue by the name of a
bad end not too long after that.
===Line...===
Tomas told me all this, or most of it, at least, shortly before his own death some months later. Teskul’s

boss had arranged the shipment with Chuldan Helmstar, who manages a warehouse in Dock Ward.

Helmstar's place is usually pretty well looked after, good light, guards on the roof, etc. Won’t keep out

the mages, but it’s good enough to stop hoods like Garth and Esperel. The logs indicate that the cargo

was to be loaded on to a privately-owned cog called The Misty. The five of them decided to ambush the

shipment and make a switch during the commotion of the festival. It wasn’t hard for a couple of Dock

Ward lads to pull together a wagon, some crates and a mule team, and at sundown, when the shipment

moved out, they put the kibosh on the guards and porters, knocking them out to a man. From what

Tomas told me, it worked like clockwork - better than any gnomish timepiece I've ever heard of, in fact.

They made the switch and went back to Garth’s house without any trouble. At least, no immediate
trouble. This is all a prelude, remember, to the real show.


Y'see, what Orsabbas didn’t know, and Ellyth didn’t find out until too late, was that it was Teskul’s boss

who had arranged the shipment. Teskul's boss also happened to be the man whose operation Garth

had unwittingly fire-bombed for Serpentil. They say Beshaba is just Tymora on a bad day.


===... and Sinker===


Once they got back to Garth’s house, that’s when the sloop hit the squall. They were so excited about

the heist, that there wasn’t much more than a token attempt to keep their guard up. They sent Esperel

up to the roof to keep watch, but he'd barely got past the eaves when someone put the drop on him. He

shook off some kind of paralysis spell, but got knocked on the head and that was that. The rest of the

group them didn’t have much to say to their door being beat down by four thugs. Right behind them

were Teskul and his boss, the Moonsea trader I told you about. He was a Zhent, and his name, I later

found out, was Thagdul. He was the factor for the Drurginz Trading Company, a semi-legit operation

with connections to the Moonsea region, but we know that they probably ran contraband through

Skullport more often than not. It turned out that he’d had Teskul build up about as much of a dossier on
this bunch as could be had.


With this bunch, you’d have expected a hopeless fight to break out. But Ellyth saw something in the
doorway that pretty much took the fight out of them that day.


Thagdul knew he had them over a barrel. It didn’t take much convincing to get Ellyth and Garth to agree

to run a few ‘errands’ for Drurginz as a way to mend fences, doing some corporate sabotage on

another Zhent organization in the city, Company Ersgul. Tomas and Esperel followed suit, but

Gilthanas knew the scene and wouldn’t have any of it. No one ever proved that Gilthanas’ death had
anything to do with the Zhents, but I’m a druid if it didn't.


== 10 Mirtul - First Mission: The North Ward Inferno ==


''The reports which follow have been compiled from the memoirs, recollections and stories of the

various parties - some heroic and some not so - which took up arms and common interest with Garth

Shanks during the tumultuous months and years that followed the fateful encounter with Thagdul. The

veracity of this information cannot be attested to with certainty, but barring divine intervention, it is the
best that your humble scribe can offer.''
=== Paying Dues ===
The characters had much to think about during the tenday that followed this encounter. Disillusioned

with his short-lived attempt at making an honest living after his experience at Serpentil’s Folios, Garth

rethought his get-rich-yesterday scheme and took up one of Waterdeep's oldest professions - he

became a Dock Ward landlord. Only a day later, his new home was graced with its first tenant, a

Calishite scholar recently arrived in the City of Splendours. This man, Jafar, was a wizard by trade and

decided to make Waterdeep his home for a time. To that end, he had been sponsored to join the

Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors by a local guild-member, Orilar Thanas, and had been

assigned to the City's Firewatch. In the months that were to follow, Jafar's association with the

Firewatch would prove to be a cruel irony for the good citizens of Waterdeep, but this tale will be told in

good time. Garth's friend Esperel also found sponsorship into one of the city guilds, joining the under

Master Hallthor Duzmund. This association, too, was ill-advised from the beginning, but in Esperel's
case, no one expected any better.


=== Repaying Debts ===


On the afternoon of the tenth of Mirtul, Ellyth, Esperel, Garth and Tomas received invitations from

Thagdul to meet him for dinner at the Thirsty Throat Tavern, where they would discuss their

outstanding 'debt' to him. Unexpectedly for the companions, Jafar also received summons from the

Moonsea man, as it seemed he was already in the trader’s debt for reasons of his own. Oddly, few

remarked on this coincidence, and perhaps even moreso, none investigated it. In any case, Jafar kept

quiet. When questioned about the absence of Gilthanas, Thagdul vaguely indicated that the

bladesinger had made amends independently, and in any case, had recently \"left Waterdeep for
good\".


After a splendid repast, Thagdul laid out his proposal: to clear the debts they owed him, the five were to

burn down a certain house in North Ward. As an added incentive to the would-be thieves, Thagdul also
offered ‘salvage rights’ on the contents of the building.


Despite Ellyth’s odd suspicion that Thagdul may have been a dragon in human guise, the party

reluctantly agreed to the trader's proposal. A ‘diversion’ had been arranged to occupy the City

Firewatch and the City Watch at half past ten bells that very evening. On a map he produced, Thagdul

indicated a building in North Ward, on the block bounded by the High Road and Sulmor, Delzorin and

Vhezoar Streets, and indicated that further instructions were awaiting them in a nearby sewer entrance.


After dinner, the party split up to prepare themselves. While Tomas returned to Dock Ward to retrieve

his armour, Esperel and Garth reconnoitered their supposed target. The building in question was quite

large, and four storeys high. Discreetly casing the property from a safe distance, they were able to

detect movement on the upper storeys, indicating that the house was not abandoned, but an active

concern. While this happened, Ellyth was discreetly making enquiries about Thagdul among her

contacts at a local gambling den, The Gentle Mermaid. Unfortunately, her mark, a noble named Arsten

Thunderstaff II, had no apparent knowledge of the Moonsea trader or his operation, and to soothe her

disappointment at his ignorance, she convinced the fop to cut her in on his winnings.


=== Burning Down the House ===


At ten bells, the party rendezvoused at the sewer entrance, where they found a crate which contained

five detonation devices and what appeared to be a map of the building. Inside the tunnel which opened

onto the city's sewer system, Esperel located a hidden passage that led to a chamber directly below the

building. The room contained the ruins of several statues, upon which Ellyth sensed the residual traces

of some enchantment, but no obvious doors. However, it didn't take Esperel long to uncover a secret

entrance that seemed to lead to the building above. Unfortunately, their arrival had been anticipated -

as the door was opened, three figures (one of whom was a drow) burst in on them, just as two
spider-like creatures attacked from behind.


Thinking quickly, Esperel managed to wedge the door shut on the dark elf, and turned to confront the

two remaining men with Ellyth and Jafar. Garth and Tomas, meanwhile, faced off against the

watchspiders. Although Tomas took a poisonous bite on the forearm from one of the beasts, he was not

severely hurt, and he and Garth made short work of the vicious arachnids. Seeing this, the party's

remaining adversaries surrendered, begging for mercy. The men were knocked unconscious, then
bound and gagged.


When the party turned to open the door again, Esperel found that it had been jammed somehow from

the other side, but Tomas was able to bash it open, and the adjoining chamber was revealed to be a

storeroom of crates and kegs, scattered about with books and scrolls. Ellyth and Jafar set to examining

the room’s contents while the others pursued the fleeing drow. Tomas forced open a second blocked

door, which opened into a room containing the smashed remains of a jade spider statue. As they

entered, their quarry darted out a door opposite, and Esperel and Tomas quickly gave chase up a flight

of stairs, leaving Garth to inspect the room. Upstairs, however, the pursuit came to a sudden halt as the

two encountered a group of surprised-looking men, and hastily retreated back down the steps. Luckily,

only one man pursued – attacking Esperel as Tomas set an explosive charge on the stairwell. The elf

was soon able to force his opponent back upstairs, as Garth emerged from the cellar. Together, the

three made their way up the staircase, splitting up to set the remaining charges – Garth on the second

floor, Esperel on the third, and Tomas pursuing the retreating man to the fourth.


=== A Frightening Encounter ===


Garth found the second storey to have been recently vacated (and possibly ransacked), and after

setting his charge, he made his exit by climbing out a window into a nearby tree. Searching the third

storey, similarly ransacked, Esperel came across two rune-covered porcelain cases, which despite their

apparent value, remained oddly untouched. Sensing danger, he left them be, setting his charge then

retreating downstairs. A floor above, Tomas had managed to close in on his quarry, whom he found

pounding on a door, yelling for “Vuldan!”. Seeing Tomas, the man fled around a corner, but as the

young fisherman made to give chase, the door the man had been pounding on quietly opened and a
strangely calm voice inquired \"What is going on?\"


Turning to face the voice, Tomas was horrified to see what looked like a beholder floating in mid-air

behind him. With unnerving politeness, the creature requested that there be no violence. Tomas,

fear-stricken, broke down and warned the creature of the impending immolation, but 'Vuldan' (whatever

he was) appeared unconcerned, and drifted nonchalantly back into its room. During this encounter, the

man he had been chasing had dashed out onto a balcony, but Tomas was too rattled to continue his

pursuit. After nervously setting his charge right beside Vuldan's door, he returned downstairs to rejoin
his comrades.


In the storeroom below, all were accounted for save Garth. While Tomas argued for going up to find his

friend, Jafar, Ellyth and Esperel argued that such action was foolhardy and much too dangerous: the

explosives would soon go off, and Garth, realizing this, had probably found his own way out. Hoping

this was the case, Tomas joined the rest of the party in their efforts to move what swag they can into

the sewer entrance before the bombs detonated. As the last of the easily moved items was shifted into

the sub-cellar, a great explosion shook the brickworks above and dust rained down on the party. The
mission had been a success.


=== A Significant Find ===


Soon after the explosion, Garth rejoined his comrades from the other side of the sewer entrance,

saying that he had watched from a safe distance and reporting that the building was going up in flames

as he spoke. While he and the others took an inventory of their ‘salvage’, Tomas returned to Dock

Ward to fetch a cart with which they might transport the haul, which consisted of:


* 1 exotic dagger (upon which Jafar identifies a drow enchantment)
* 2 hand crossbows
* 2 stone heads (identified by Jafar as former golem heads)
* 88 lbs. of jade (lightly enchanted – likely a former golem)
* A '''sealed scroll''', written in what Jafar recognizes as the drow tongue, though he can't read it
* 2 journals, also in drow
* 40 books on the subject of Cormyr
* Selections from The Draconomicon concerning \"Kaldunh, the Deeping Dragon\", annotated in drow


== 11 Mirtul - Among Thieves ==


The following day, Jafar offered to take the journals, the excerpt from the ''Draconomicon'' and the
scroll to an acquaintance by the name of home.


Ellyth, meanwhile, made contact with (and charmed) a fence by the name of Azadon, with whom she

discussed the possibility of moving some of the stolen properties. Similarly, under the alias of Farwater,

Esperel contacted the fence Turith Maylar at the Copper Cup. The two quickly came to an agreement,

and Maylar offered 300 gp for the jade recovered from the previous day's efforts.


Meanwhile, however, Tomas had discovered that Thagdul’s ‘diversion’ had included setting fire to

portions of Dock Ward, where many of his friends and acquaintances were living. Guiltily, he spent the

day helping clear the wreckage, then convinced some drunken sailors that they had all gotten pissed
together the night before as an alibi (which no one doubted).


=== The Grey Rogue ===


At the end of the day, the party (minus Garth) met at the Thirsty Throat for dinner. Jafar - suddenly flush

with capital - magnanimously paid the tab for all, and related Azadand's findings and also mentioning

that the books are currently under study. During dinner, Ellyth's eye was caught by Teskul was

watching them from a nearby table. He approached the party and passed on the message that Thagdul

was pleased with their work, then grey rogue took his leave. Teskul's features, it was noted, were quite

similar to those of Thagdul - perhaps both hailed from the same part of the Realms. After the man had

departed, Esperel decided to follow him. Despite his best efforts, however, the elf lost his quarry

somewhere between Samarin’s Way and Golden Serpent St. in North Ward, but as he made to return

to Dock Ward, however, Esperel had the distinct feeling that he had himself begun to be tailed. Through

several canny dodges and redirects, he managed to trick this new pursuer and turn the chase around,

but he soon lost this new quarry without being able to get any clear sight of him.


Back a the Throat, the remaining three party members decided (at Jafar's insistence) to unload the

books at Serpentil’s Books and Folios. Jafar had brought the selections from ''the Draconomicon'' as a

sample, and while Tomas had doubts about selling the books, Jafar and Ellyth paid little attention to the
illiterate dullard.


=== Crooked Fences Make Crooked Neighbours ===


At Serpentil’s, the three interrupted a conversation between Jannaxil and two visitors - a sharp-featured

merchant and a strange-looking elf. The former, it was hard to miss, was dressed almost exactly like

Thagdul, though with longer hair and a beholder staff similar to but slightly shorter than that of Thagdul.

The elf seemed young (perhaps about 120, thought Ellyth - though she would later voice her suspicions

that he was not what he seemed) and was dressed in a purple chemise with a silk cloak and thick

necklace. With the arrival of the three companions, Jannaxil made his excuses to his guests, and bade

Jafar to sit while the merchant and the elf moved off to the corner of the shop. As Jafar began

negotiating with the pinch-faced shopkeeper, Ellyth eyed off the strangers, who in return seemed to be

paying an undue amount of attention to her and her friends. She was disturbed to overhear the elf

whisper softly to his partner, “Will she ever fetch a fine price down below!”. Catching her eye, he smiled
malevolently and winked.


The elf's countenance changed abruptly, however, when he saw Jafar produce the ''Draconomicon'',

and he became visibly irate, but remained silent. Paying no heed to his guest's consternation, Jannaxil

calmly expressed his interest to Jafar, urging him and his companions to bring in their goods. Jafar

somewhat condescendingly ordered Tomas and Ellyth to fetch the books (which had been left behind),

and indicated that he would stay to converse with Jannaxil. As the she and the young Docksider left the

shop, however, Ellyth overheard an exchange between Jafar and the elf that was to provide much food

for thought in the months and years to come. Evidently chafing under the elf's glare, Jafar muttered

\"I'm working on it!\", to which the elf, maintaining a hard gaze on the uncomfortable Calishite, replied,
\"You’d better be.”


Ellyth easily managed to swindle a wagon from some young Dock Ward carters, then she and Tomas

went to meet Garth and collect the cache of books. Together, the three loaded the wagon and returned

to Serpentil’s to meet Jafar. En route, however, the group was accosted by a squad of guards led by a

Court Magistrate named Thuman, accompanied by a watchwizard and the elven stranger from

Serpentil's. \"That's them,\" confirmed the elf, glowering at Ellyth. \"Check the books!\" At Thuman's

nod, the watch-wizard muttered an incantation. Suddenly, each and every book on the wagon began to

glow with a magical sigil - a sigil which happened to be identical to that inscribed on an amulet which

the elf produced from around his neck. Thuman frowned, and as the elf looked on, sneering at the

companions, he charged them with theft and the transportation and attempted sale of stolen property.

Before he could add any further charges, Ellyth began pleading, turning the full force of her charms on

the blustery Magistrate. Thinking quickly, she implicated the fence, Azadon, as the true villain behind

the operation and convinced the magistrate of her innocence and that of her friends, obvious victims in

a criminal conspiracy. Against all odds, the bluff worked. With an appraising leer, Thuman said he

would let the companions off with 'a warning' - provided they stayed in Waterdeep. This lenience

appeared to irritate the elf to no end, but Thuman appeared not to notice. The books and wagon were

confiscated (and a receipt given), whereupon the trio returned to Serpentil’s to inform Jafar and the
shopkeeper of what had happened.


Serpentil expressed concern at the companions' misfortune, but little initiative.


=== Corruption and Lies ===


Soon after their return that evening to Garth's place, the party received a visit from Thagdul, who had

somehow learned of the group’s predicament. Thagdul had some knowledge of the strange elf, he said,

but had a particular familiarity with the elf's companion (Thagdul's near-double). This man's name, he

told the party, was Aleshkun, the director of a trading interest known as Company Ersgul. Furthermore,

he confided, Serpentil himself was an Ersgul agent, and had more than likely set them up - he knew for

a fact that Magistrate Thuman was on the Ersgul payroll. To ease the companions' worries at this

revelation, Thagdul raised a hand. He was was not without his own friends in the Court, he said. With a

cool, calculating look around the room, he offered to save the companions from any \"legal hassle\" in

exchange for their doing him another \"favour\". With reluctance, all accepted the man's terms.


After the Moonsea man left, tempers ran high. Tomas began drinking, and soon started cursing out

Jafar as a sneak. The young fisherman had spent three years on the coasts of Calimshan and Amn,

and he was bloody sure that the while his so-called colleague may well have been some kind of

foreigner, \"he warn't no Calishite\". In fact, didn't Jafar look a lot like the companions \"new friends\"

Aleshkun, Teskul and Thagdul? As the room turned against him, Jafar quickly admitted the

prevarication, but reassured his companions that there was good reason for his disguise. In any case,

he said, it had no bearing on the affairs of the companions, and he would prefer that they not press him

on it for now. All would be revealed in time. This managed to calm the young drunkard down, but in

doing so Jafar had slipped up with another lie, which Ellyth then called him on. Exasperated, Jafar

admitted that yes, the drow writings that they had found were in fact just sitting in his locker at the
Watchful Order, not \"being studied\" as he had said earlier.


=== Burgled, Swindled, Assaulted and Drugged ===


When Ellyth finally returned home to her rooms at the Gentle Mermaid late that night, she learned from

the bouncers that thieves had attempted to break into her room. The attempt had been unsuccessful,

but the perpetrators had escaped. Likewise, Tomas found that his house had been ransacked, and that

his dear mother had been assaulted during the burglary. Except for two harbour moons he had buried

in the dirt floor under his bed, everything of value in the fisherman's house (not that there was much to
begin with) had been stolen, including his grandfather’s spyglass.


During the night, Garth and Esperel had taken it upon themselves to case out Serpentil’s Books and

Folios, but on the way to the shop, the two made a change of plan, and Esperel doubled back to

Garth’s house. Tomas, too, after having seen to his mother’s safety, ran back to Garth’s, where he

awoke Jafar and told the mage of the break in. A burglary on Tomas' household was strange to say the

least, and the only explanation the two could come up with was that someone had been looking for the
items they had taken from the house in North Ward the previous night.


Sensing danger, Jafar returned to the offices of the Watchful Order to make sure his own stash was

secure. Meanwhile, Esperel came back to Garth's, where he found Tomas guarding the jade. The two

decided that it would be best to sell the ill-gotten treasure immediately, rather than risk its being stolen.

They then split up - Tomas carrying a decoy along the main streets, while Esperel took the real swag

through the alleys to the Copper Cup. It was never certain that the ruse worked, for both were attacked

as they made their way through the dark Dock Ward night. Esperel soon found himself running from

two pursuers, whom he avoided by taking to the roofs of nearby houses and littering the alleys below

with caltrops. Esperel soon found himself at the Cup, where he sold the jade to Maylar for 54 pp (an

extra ten percent levied by Maylar for expediting the exchange), then settled in to wait for Tomas - but

the young Dockman never showed up. After an hour, the elf set out into the night to find his friend, but
his attempts proved unsuccessful.


Tomas had fared less well on his journey than his nimble companion. En route to the Cup he had been

accosted by two sword-wielding thugs who had demanded that he turn over his sack. Tomas

responded by throwing the dummy rucksack at them, then drawing his sword. Although he quickly

gained the upper hand - bringing one assailant down with his blade and knocking the other to the

cobblestones - a third party hit him in the neck with a drugged dart. Calling out for the guard, Tomas fell
unconscious onto street, and knew no more.


== 12 Mirtul - Second Mission: Rescue in the Sewers ==


Lathander's rays broke over the city the next morning, streaming in through the eastern window of Dock

Ward guard tower and into the bleary eyes of Tomas, who regained consciousness in what appeared to

be a prison cell. Standing over him as he raised a groggy head were the grizzled faces of Watch

Captain Jalthor Murlyn (whom Tomas knew from childhood as a friend of his father's) and the shifty,

rodentine face of a scruffy halfling by the name of Egan. Without so much as a \"good morning\", Murlyn

introduced Egan as a witness to the attack that had occurred the night before, then proceeded to

interrogate Tomas on what he'd been getting up to - making Tomas feel for all the world like it had been

his own fault that he'd gotten assaulted and drugged. Warning the boy that he'd best keep to the

straight and narrow, Murlyn released Tomas, and the young fisherman made his way back to Garth's to
find out what had happened the night before.


=== Company of the Red Herring ===


Returning to Garth's, Tomas found the rest of his companions there hotly discussing the break-ins of

the previous evening. Garth's house, it turned out, had been the only house to escape invasion -

Tomas, Ellyth and Esperel all reporting either break-ins or attempted burglaries. It was on this fateful

morning - 12 Mirtul, 1371 - that Garth Shanks proposed that the five companions incorporate as the

Company of the Red Herring, using the monies that Esperel had recouped from his fence the night

before as startup capital. A company, Garth proposed, would give the companions an edge of

professionalism that would allow them to deal with such invasions as they had experienced.

Furthermore, it would normalize their relationship with Thagdul and his company, and besides he had

always wanted to form a company. The proposal was taken up by all, and over the course of the

morning a contract and charter was pounded out by the five companions. Upon the finalization of the

document, the five companions - Ellyth, Esperel, Garth, Tomas and Jafar - decided to celebrate by
going to lunch at the Thirsty Throat Tavern.


=== Scuffle at the Slide ===
The companions' merriment, however, was cut short (inevitably, it seemed) when their celebrations

were interrupted by the arrival of Thagdul, who appeared at the table without anyone having noticed

him enter the tavern. It was time to make good on their debt, the dark man intoned, cutting short a joke

Tomas had been telling about a Calishite pirate-girl he had known. Aleshkun's company Ersgul, he

informed them, was going to be making a \"shipment\" into the smuggler's stronghold known as

Skullport - a den of vice that was rumoured to exist in the caves below the city. The \"Company of the

Red Herring\" he said bemusedly, was to stop this shipment by any means they see fit. He then

produced directions to an entrance to the city sewers known as \"The Slide\", and a map to an entrance

to Undermountain which was located in the sewers. Informing the party of the time at which they could

expect the Ersgul shipment to take place, Thagdul bid them good luck and departed without any further

word. As usual, it seemed like the man was holding back information that might have proved relevant,

but the companions were too excited at the prospect of finding an entrance to Undermountain - untold
riches! - to quibble.


The shipment was to take place that afternoon, and the companions quickly armed themselves - Tomas

strapping on armour, Ellyth grabbing her bow - then proceeded to the Slide, quickly drawing up a plan

for an ambush. Before long, the party was in position in the sewers. Taking up positions, they waited -

and did not do so for long. Two men with magical light-tubes were soon seen to be making their way

towards the antechamber where the companions were laying in wait. Almost immediately, however, the

carefully (if quickly) laid plan went awry. As the two men entered the chamber, Tomas and Esperel take

out one, while Ellyth made short work of another. Commotion could be heard from behind them,

however, and the Company then ran out into the sewer proper, where two more men were waiting.

Ellyth and Jafar were the first out the door, and as Ellyth knocked one of the two, Jafar cast some

manner of spell (no one could see any visible effect). The fourth man, brandishing a mean-looking axe,

Raventree. Ravenblight/Doomface has struck again. Nandos cancels the party and gives each member
of the CrH 500 gp


Eseperel muses that the purple mage might have been Ravenblight, but Tomas asks why Ravenblight
would have wanted the Lathkule. The boy Adonai has no suggestions.


After a difficult and misguided journey through Undermountain, the CRH and Gregor arrived at Dark

Market. They managed to contain themselves and keep themselves from ending up in the slave pens

long enough to seek out the Xvimites. Relying heavily upon Gregor’s magic, the CRH managed to
destroy the cultists, only to find that the Raventree children weren’t there.


A mage in purple robes was chased out of the Xvimite lair by Esperel. Esperel managed to shoot many

arrows into the mage before Esperel was intercepted by a beholder that may have been Xulla. This

beholder spared Esperel’s life in return for a ‘favour’. And so it came to be that Esperel became the
pawn of both sides of the Zhent conflict.


The Raventree children were already at the slave pens and ready for auctioning, as it turns out. The

CRH had to spend almost all the money it had to purchase them, but the mage with the purple robe
tried to outbid the CRH for a particular child named Adonai.


At length, the CRH made their way out of Undermountain after a successful mission. Thagdul rewarded
all with magical cloaks and other rewards.


The CRH was not involved in any more conflicts until shortly before Harvestide of that year.
