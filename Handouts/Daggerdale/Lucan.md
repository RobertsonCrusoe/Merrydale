Divine Anatomy and Lewdness

This category includes Lucan’s signature style of invoking the specific and often grotesque body parts of various deities.

-   Bane: “Bane’s balls, but I’m invincible!,” “Bane’s black balls on an anvil!,”and “Bane’s brass bollocks, Jasper.”

-   Gond: “By Gond’s greasy groin!”

-   Jergal: “Dry as Jergal’s dusty sphincter.”

-   Moander: A comparison of green uniforms to “Moander’s festering foreskin....”

-   Oghma: “Oghma’s nethers!”

-   Shar: “by Shar’s Snatch.”

-   Sune: “By Sune’s smallclothes,” “Sune’s slit, Jas – that’s all they need.”

-   Torm: “Torm’s testes, Chrysalites!”

-   Tyr: “Tyr’s tits, you bet!”

-   Tymora: “Tymora’s tits!”

Oaths of Commerce and Wealth

These oaths invoke deities related to trade and wealth, reflecting Lucan’s Sembian merchant background.

-   “I’d wager every crate in Waukeen’s warehouse.”

-   “Waukeen watch me.”

-   “by Waukeen’s Wide Warehouse.”

-   “Waukeen whip me.”

-   “by Waukeen’s wondrous wagons, ol’ Lucan has found an angle once again.”

-   An invocation of his personal wealth and family name: “by my father’s name and the wealth of my house.”

-   His personal oath: “By the silver.”

Curses of Misfortune and Fate

These phrases invoke deities associated with bad luck, thieves, or general misfortune.

-   Beshaba: “Beshaba’s breath,” “Beshaba’s breath blows fair and foul,” “Beshaba’s britches!,”and the sarcastic “Beshaba’s sweet kiss, that’s gratitude for you.”

-   Mask: “Mask’s crooked grin” and the metaphorical “one of Mask’s magpies.”

-   Talos: A dramatic comparison of betrayal to being served up “like brides to Talos.”

General Blasphemies and Oaths

This category includes broader or more common divine invocations.

-   “By all the gods and their mistresses.”

-   “Tyr’s teeth!”

-   “Torm-tossed minute!”

-   “Hoar’s whores, man!”

-   “Aumanator blind them....”

-   “Deneir’s folly, this attempt to save every scrap of meaning.”
