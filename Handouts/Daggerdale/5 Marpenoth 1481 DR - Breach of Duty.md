==A Meeting between Alen Reks and Magistrate Invictus - Marpenoth 5, 1481 DR (Narrative intended for Christian)==

Part way through the final day of the tourney, you notice a bit of a commotion at the gates.  There, you see the infamous Magistrate Invictus coming through the gates, frightened clerks and soldiers parting before him.  Most of the clergy and soldiery in the courtyard notice the determined pace of Invictus as he marches along in front of the stands on the south edge of the courtyard, while a distracting pitched battle between two teams of competitors is waged just a few feet to his right.

Invictus approaches a group of clerks that are off duty, sitting in the stands.  They see him coming and rise and bow as he steps to them.  His crisp yellow gown snaps in the bitter wind that has been blowing all day, and as he raises one arm to point at three of the four clerks as a school master might, the gown flaps about with such violence that now nearly all eyes in the stands around them are taking in the scene.  The three bow quickly and awkwardly, and make for the gates like scolded dogs.  The remaining one keeps his head bowed.  Invictus reaches into his gown and produces a small letter, which he hands to the clerk.

The clerk looks about nervously, taking the paper with reservation.  He makes a brief interrogative of the magistrate, who answers by way of a silent, brooding nod.  The clerk hesitantly opens the letter, which is fighting against him in the wind and drizzle.  However, with great frustration he is able to read something on the page that makes him blanche; he looks up at the Magistrate, who again nods silently.  The clerk then rushes out of the place nearly tripping over his soggy yellow gown.

Invictus then looks about, and takes in the vacancy in the stands that he has just created.  Front row, centre.  He runs his hands down the lap of his gown, and neatly sits down, his posture the picture of rigid discipline.  While the stands are packed, he seems to sit in a pocket, and island of space in the stands that none seem to encroach upon.  His hawk-like face quickly fixes on the combat before him; he can’t seem to take his eyes off the shapely Avenger known as Shea.  He probably sees something that most men don’t, however.  He can see her sanctity issue forth with every artful dash of her blade, and when her team has won the final round of that match, he gives the faintest hint of a smile at the corner of his mouth.

With the close of the match, and the preparations for the final match beginning right away, the courtyard of the Close becomes a chaotic bluster.  Invictus strides through the bustling people headed in every direction, his gait never changing for the crowd, which seems to part before him partly through the deference of some, but through seemingly witless intuition from others who get out of the way without even knowing.  He marches determinedly towards you, and gives what passes for a cordial smile on his normally scowl-ridden face.

“Mr. Reks…” he offers his hand.  “Is there somewhere we can talk…”

Fast forward to whatever room you bring him to.

Invictus lets out a deep sigh before he starts.  “This tournament has been a success, hasn’t it?  I don’t recall a more talked-about event in the Falls since I was a lad.  Well done, Mr. Reks.”  He pauses, and you are not certain if he is sincere or not.  One of the things you have come to learn about Invictus is that he is a cipher; years of interrogating people on behalf of the Zenith Lord have left him with a flat affect; he would say “Sunny day, isn’t it?” in the same tone as he might say “I’m about to start cutting off toes until you tell me who you work for.”  He speaks with what can only be called intense indifference.

“My clerks are certainly enjoying it.  Brother Matthias enjoyed it so much he had a breach of duty.”  He nods his head and makes a wide eyed expression as if to say “yes yes, I’m telling the truth, believe it or not!”  He continues; “Matthias missed a bird that landed in his coup yesterday morning, not one hour after Prime.  Can you imagine such an egregious neglect” these two words rolling long and slow from his tongue, “of one’s duty?”  You are forced to wonder how this is going to go for the clerk.  And how bad can the letter that was attached to the dove be?

“The letter was very bad, Mr. Reks.”  Is he reading your mind?

“I can imagine what you are thinking, Mr. Reks.  I am not known for tolerance, am I?  It is likely that Matthias will be harshly disciplined, but we will salvage the man, you can be sure.”  For Invictus to claim that the disciplinary action will be ‘harsh’ is not a good sign.  The letter must be really bad…

“Indeed. The letter was very bad, Mr. Reks.  At Dwimmerholme, three entire families have been abducted, likely by the same brigands that have been preying on road travelers.  Now they have crossed a terrible threshold, snatching up our farmers, our loved ones, from their very fields.

“What is worse is this; there have been small skirmishes on nearly every road in the dale in the last tenday.  Something is afoot, and we have over-committed to Tethyamar.  I would like to say that there isn’t a spare sword to be swung for love, money or vengeance.  But that’s not true, is it, Mr. Reks?”  You try now to stop thinking too very much.  You have had some short conversations with this man before, and you certainly know him for his reputation; he is the finest interrogator this side of Thay, so they say.  Even Alystair gives a nod to the man’s abilities.  Invictus is a dangerous man, and all secrets are his to gather.

“You seem distracted, Mr. Reks.  Like you’re thinking too hard, or maybe not thinking at all?  If I’ve come at a bad time…” and you make a characteristically genial if nuanced dismissal of his suggestion that his company isn’t wanted just now, though you really want to be out of the man’s mental grasp.

“Very well, Mr. Reks.  You are a proper host.  Where were we?  Ah yes; swinging blades.  Well, every spare hand within 50 leagues is right outside these very walls, aren’t they?  I would hate to suggest a termination of any festivities that may have been planned for the victors of your games, but if you could spare them right away, do you think they could be sent to Dwimmerholme?”

This tournament is costing a fortune (you have yet to see the revenues from the seats).  Feasting these champions won’t be free either.  Maybe they should be turned out right away?  Maybe there would be something in it for the White Cloaks.

“Of course, I think the Church could provide a stipend for their time.  Rather than spending all that gold from the seat sales on celebrations, perhaps they could be dispatched and start earning right away?  I think the Church could pay something.  I will leave the dirty details to one of my clerks.  And as far as information goes, I have said all I know on the matter, I am sure.  So, do we have a deal?”

Let me test this out, you find yourself saying.  You’re a clever bastard!  Of course we have a deal! You think to yourself.  You try not to betray your thoughts with your face, but either he is a mind reader, or he can read faces like nothing you ever imagined.

He gives that same small hint of a smile.  “Very good, Mr. Reks.”  Bastard.

“Sister Shea; she seems quite capable, doesn’t she? It would be nice to see the White Cloaks bolster their ranks with more of the Faithful, would it not?”  You try not to think anything, but it is so hard; the White Cloaks are full of these mad zealots; Alystair is the worst of them, but the Captain isn’t much better.

“Alystair and Iolus would be good company, good role models for such a girl.  We’ll be watching her, won’t we?  We’ll be praying for her, will we not?  I think I will come back tonight and see how this tournament plays out, if you don’t mind…”

He didn’t even pay for his seat, his 4 seats, the first time.

“Oh!  That reminds me…” he reaches into his gown and produces a single gold coin, as bright and shiny as the conspicuous chain of office around his neck.  He snaps it down onto the desk.  “That’s for my seat.  Much obliged, Mr. Reks.”

Alystair has nothing on Magistrate Invictus.  This man is a monster.

You are looking at each other from across the desk, and you are doing your best not to squirm at the intense indifference of the man’s face.  There is a knock at the door, followed quickly by the irreverent intrusion of Ms. Blackmane.  “Master Reks!  You are needed downstairs, sir, right away.  Pardon me, Magistrate!”  She gives the most formal and apologetic bow you have seen her make; this woman could teach Sune about courtly manners.

You are trying not to thank the gods for her intervention, as the Magistrate stands up as though to leave.  “I’ve kept you too long, my good man.  Good evening, Mr. Reks.”

Alystair has nothing on Magistrate Invictus.  This man is a monster.

A few moments later you find yourself looking for Blackmane to see what in the nine hells she wanted.  You find her doing nothing in particular, and are about to inquire when you put it together.  She looks up at you, and says “Sorry; that was just too long to be in the same room with Invictus.”  You and her lock stares only for a moment.  She looks away disinterested and goes about her business.
