== Early Winter, 1373 - Shadowflight ==

The Sun rises in the snow-covered Tesh Valley. Shadows recede slowly but surely to reveal the naked oaks and cherry trees, and the snow flecked pines of the wilds of Daggerdale. Soon, at the heart of the valley, shadow leaves the Tesh River, and the ice-covered river is revealed. High atop the fortified battlements of the Tower of Freedom in Dagger Falls, two fresh-faced guards wearing tabards of purple and rose emerge from the stair. The wind is cold, but the sun is warm on their faces. The sun is the promise of their faith. They smile. Two weary guards in black tabards with silver stars cross the wind-swept battlement to meet their relief.

“How was the night?” asks one Lathanderite of the servants of Mystra.

“Peaceful”.

The sun chases the shadows westward.

Perched atop a rocky heel in the heart of Anauroch, the Abbey of Bhaerlith is the first thing in this barren land to find the sun. The monks and clerics of the Abbey were up long before the sun, but they pause to give thanks as its warmth finds their rosy, wind-swept cheeks.

The sun chases the shadows westward.

At the other end of Anauroch, the sun finds a lonely refuge, ramshackle and salvaged. A thin column of smoke rises from the chimney there. The men inside have been trapped there for long, but two of them know that they will be leaving today.

The sun chases the shadows westward.

It is blinding, on the eastern face of Dekanter Mountain. The brilliant rays sear through the clouds and reflect off the snow covered landscape. A man in dark robes high in a slave-built tower looks out the window. The icicles there are melting. He makes a crooked smile. The roads will be open soon, and business will be booming, certainly. He doesn’t realize that when the snows go, a storm that he will not weather will blow in from the east.

The sun chases the shadows westward.

Through village after village along the East Road, from Loudwater to Rassalantar, people arise with the sun and tilt their heads to face its warmth and radiance. The sun is life in this cold valley. The sun will clear the roads and fields. Things will grow, as they do every year. But the sun today brings a promise, and many are heard to say “Lathander be praised, a new day is nigh”.

The sun chases the shadows westward.

Through the evergreens it shines, as it lights up Neverwinter Wood. Down the river it travels, and then finds the city walls. The sun shines first upon a statue of great heroes on the southwestern gate. From the Keep in the centre of the city, Lord Nasher looks out from his high window, and sees the statue and it’s brilliance in the sun. He can’t see that a group of children are playing inside the southwest gate, underneath that statue. One child wields a gnarly stick, an is chased by all the others. One child runs upon all fours and chases him thusly. The lone child snarls, and points the staff at his quadrupedal pursuer, then looks in shock as ‘Rufus’ jumps on him and drags him to the ground. “Not this time, Zhent!” he cries. They laugh and start over again.

At the same time, the sun is hitting Mount Waterdeep, and the City of Splendours is coming alive. The seasonal flooding of Dock Ward from the meltwaters of the Mountain is a merrily accepted sign of warmer weather and happier days. The people of the City that see the sun rise on this day have hope for the first time in months. The sun brings promise, and Lathander is invoked all over the City, from the worker on the dock to the Noble Patriarch of the gilded villa in North Ward. The sun is going to wash away the troubles of the City soon, it seems to promise this fair and bright morning.

In the newly constructed Tower of Azuth, a young prodigy awakes and begins his morning exercises with the same devotion that he had for the bulk of his young life. But this morning he pauses to look out the window. He has been waiting, and he senses that his waiting will draw to an end soon.

At Phaulkonmere, a very tired man falls back into a sturdy wooden chair, and begins to remove his mission gear. It has been a long night. The nights have all been long this winter, and only the strong have been able to survive this change. As he takes off the thick leather armour that protects his torso, he finds a tear, and finds another wound he doesn’t quite remember getting. Then he notices long shadows growing in his chamber. He does a quick survey of the room, and then pauses to listen. Birds. He hears birds. The sun is shining and the birds are coming back. He musters strength enough to stand and face the window, leaning heavily upon the sill. The sun is coming. He knows it will bring help, and he smiles.

The sun chases the shadows westward.

Out over the Sea of Swords it goes. Amongst the great floes of ice, tremendous booms are heard, followed by deep splashes. The ice is breaking up. The gulls circle and call, and their shrill voices deliver the message to all those that dwell on this great sea that winter is ending. The sun has brought a new day.

== Alturiak, 1373 - Dawn in Anauroch ==

This morning, as Garrik awakes, he finds that only he and Dorian are in their bedrolls. He looks around, fearing the worst and springs to his feet. In the corner, Boregar gives a chuckle. He is fastening his greaves. He adjusts them and readjusts them. His four companions have all learned that he likes the creaky-leathery noise it makes. His boots are on, and his pack is by the door, clearly stuffed and ready to go. Garrik says not a word, but begins to roll up his pack.

Boregar rises, taps his staff twice on the floor that he has spent the last several months pacing on, and crosses the room. “Get up, ya laggard!” he bellows, as he kicks at Dorian’s feet.

“It’s not my watch yet, you bloated troll!” Dorian replies, hiding his head further.

“I ain’t waking you because it’s your watch, you lazy Sembian. It’s time to go. And it’s an hour past your watch at any rate.”

“So...” says Garrik, sleep already far behind him, and a gleam in his eyes.

“I dunno,” replies Boregar. “It was a little spooky. I was watching for princess Dorian over here, and they both got up at the damned same time. Remember them farmers in Scardale, the work-team ones? How they used to swing their scythes in time? It was just like that. Spooky, but impressive. They both sit up, and not a half second later, the sun comes in through that blasted crack which has kept my beard froze all damned winter. They didn’t say one damned word between them. ‘Mornin’’ says I, right and proper and cheerful, and they just starts packing up their rolls. ‘Oh’ says I. ‘A bit early ain’t it, fer all this?’ I says. Gaebril didn’t seem to hear me, and Maturin just looked at me and smiled. That’s right, I said he smiled. And he just shakes his head ‘no’.

“Get up, sweetheart!” Boregar shouts, and kicks Dorian again. “The only momento of this place that Gabe brought with him is a flaming brand from the hearth!”

Boregar shoulders his pack and heads out the door. The wind does not blow it violently open as he does so, and Garrik is relieved. Dorian is starting to sit up in his bedroll by this point, and notices that Garrik is already putting on his boots, and that his pack is now by the door, neatly packed away.

“I will never figure out what spell it is that you damnable chronic travelers cast to get packed up in less than one minute!” Dorian says in his grumpy morning voice. “I’m coming!”

\"Better come faster, friend!” Garrik says in his characteristically short manner, before he too takes off out the door. Dorian shakes his head and crawls out of his bedroll. He begins to pack half heartedly, but then pauses. He sniffs the air. He looks around in a panic. There is fire licking at two of the windows that were boarded up. He hops out the door on one boot, trying to get the other on.

Outside, he is surprised to find three great steeds accompanying Warburden. Maturin is already mounted, and Garrik is tightening the straps on another saddle. “Warburden traveled far last night to bring you lot these mounts,” says Boregar, as he tightens his belt. “You best saddle up!”

“But...” Dorian stammers, looking around, pulling his cloak tight. He sees Gaebril walking around the building, putting the torch to all that will burn. Dorian runs back in, and is soon outside in the snow, packing up all his things. Gaebril is already mounted at this time, and he and Maturin are looking back at the other three, as if to say ‘let’s get going!’

“If it were any other two people in the Realms beside those two,” says Boregar to Garrik, who is mounting his horse “I should think them seriously ensorcelled. This is worse. Divine compulsion, I would say. Or just plain cabin fever. Winter ain’t over for weeks in forgiving lands, and these ain’t such lands.”

Boregar walks up to the two mounted knights, rubbing his hands together to warm them. It is not yet warm outside. The wind has simply let up for a time. The ground is still thick with snow, and the breath of the horses can clearly be seen in this cool morning air. “Well, friends...” he says, patting the shanks of their proud mounts.

“Auril only begrudgingly lets go of Anauroch,” says Maturin. “She ignores it for most of the year, but it gets all of her attention and malice for the remainder. But now, she is letting go, and we will take advantage of this rare gift. We have work to do!”

Dorian is mounted at this point, and behind the heroes, their winter prison has become a full conflagration. Dorian gets a look of panic then. “There are fortunes buried in these sands!” he cries. “The maps!”

“We’ll get it on the way back” quips Gaebril.

“I wish only to forget this place” says Maturin. Upon Gaebril’s face, a smile widens that no one sees.

“Those damned maps ain’t gonna do you any good anyway, you blasted greedy Sembian pack-rat!” says Boregar. “Look at them dunes! They ain’t where we left ‘em!”

Garrik shrugs.

“Well, I better get stretched up...” Says Boregar.

Gaebril and Maturin simultaneously spur their horses. “Stretch in Dekanter!” Gaebril shouts over his shoulder.

With this, the heroes set off west, chasing the sun. The four horses and the boots of Boregar throw up such a dervish of snow and sand that might mark passing of an entire mounted company. After a couple of short days, they blow out of Anauroch like a tempest. Into the moor they charge, the walls of Dekanter visible off in the distance.

In the pit of Boregar’s vast belly, there is turmoil. He knows that he has a great labour ahead of him, as he promised the wandering priest, Shildevan. He wonders if the task might not prove too great. Many citadels they threw down on their journey across the desert. Ghouls and Demons they slew in Dagger Falls. But ahead is a real fortress, and when last they passed by, it was heavily fortified. Their passage is likely no secret, and all are expecting heavy resistance.

=== The Obsidian Blade ===
thumb|Shar's Messenger
That night, however, two boons are visited upon the heroes.

While Gaebril is watching, a visitor enters the camp undetected. The knight of Shar feels a cold breath on his neck, and cringes.

“Behind you” a hoarse voice whispers. Uncharacteristically, he turns slowly, his sword arm limp at his side. Before him is the bent figure of an old woman. She is small and frail, and wears tattered robes of black and purple. “Come with me, if you wish to serve your Mistress” she says, and turns to walk out of camp. Gaebril collects himself, looks to his sleeping comrades, and follows.

Not far beyond the camp, in the blasted moor, he finds her waiting outside the door of a ramshackle hut. She turns, and is waiting for the Knight to follow. He does. As she gets close, he sees by the moonlight that she has only two jet-black orbs for eyes, rimmed with violet. They enter the hut. Inside, it is pitch black, but the Knight of Shar finds himself sitting on an old stool. The haggard woman paces around, whispering to herself, seemingly, for some time, before she speaks directly at Gaebril.

“You have a great work ahead of you, Knight. The lady of Darkness swears it to me! Know you this; She and her Sister have only rarely come together to perform any task. But one great work that they both helped come to pass may be undone. She has chosen you, Knight, to see to it that her work does not come undone. But she does not force this burden upon you. Only willingly will she let you go. Do you wish to serve your Mistress?”

The Knight’s only reply is a nod.

“That is good, Knight. She does not wish for you to go naked and bare into this journey. She offers you this!” and with this, the hag produces a blade of obsidian. In the pitch black hut, it is nearly impossible to see the black blade, but from somewhere impossibly deep within the obsidian blade, a purple glow radiates faintly.

“You may take this blade, Knight, only if you forsake all other weapons. Do you forsake all other weapons?”

Another nod. Gaebril hands over all this blades and weapons, and the hag greedily gathers them up and tucks them in behind some rubbish. Then, slowly, she hands him the blade of obsidian.

“It is powerful, sure! It has a thirst you should be aware of. At your will, it can inflict great harm upon those that it cuts, and their life energy will be stolen and given to you. You may use this power as often as you think is wise. Go now, Knight. Follow your friends. Your paths lay together, and your task shall be revealed to you, if you are a true servant of the Mistress.”

A short time later, Gaebril was found wandering into camp. Boregar was awake, and asked where he had been. When no answer was forthcoming, he was willing to let the matter die. But then he noticed that several blades were missing from the Knight of Shar, and he had a jagged and cruel piece of obsidian in his belt.

“What in the Nine Layers happened to your blades, and where did you get that from?!” inquired Boregar.

“There are no other blades. I have always borne this blade, and this blade only.  It is only recently that I have come to accept it.” says Gaebril, as he swept the long, menacing-looking blade from his belt.

“You damned fool...” Boregar began to protest. Then Maturin's hand is upon his shoulder to quiet him.

“It is a sign of his sanctity, friend” replied the Knight of Ilmater. “Let it rest. I shall.” Maturin then returned to his bedroll, but for the rest of the night, his eyes never left the quiet sentinel standing at the edge of the camp with his hand comfortably resting upon the new blade.

=== A Welcome Reunion ===
thumb|Lady Jeryth Phaulkon, Mielikki's Chosen
Just before sunrise, the Heroes were all awake and getting ready to travel. From between their horses, the form of a woman stalked softly through the shallow snows, and into the camp. Her shapely silhouette was adorned with many blades and a bow, and the Heroes took up arms immediately. But soon, the familiar face of Jeryth Phaulkon came into view in the dim morning light.

“I am glad that you have all got here early.” She says, in her deep, rough voice. “Many signs lead me here, and I rejoice to find old friends and their stalwart allies.” With this, she exchanges embraces with Boregar and Garrik. She clasps wrists with Maturin and Gaebril, and nods to her old friends saying “Truly, the Gods ride with you. These Knights are fell and true, I can see.” And they could see these qualities in her too. The shock of white hair on her head can reveal her nature as Meiliki’s chosen to many people of the Realms with the slightest bit of religious knowledge. But what one Chosen follower sees in the eyes of another, only they know.

“Come. And leave the horses. Today we go to war!”

== Ches, 1373 - The Storm Rages==

That day, the Heroes began a guerilla war against the fortress at Dekanter. While they expected to find a host of Fist mercenaries, none where encountered. Mercs of lesser ability and value were encountered in great numbers, however, and many caravans of Zhent origin were attacked and routed.

After a protracted campaign of about a month, the Heroes, along with Jeryth, made a final frontal attack, and Dekanter was taken by 20 Ches of that year. Jeryth and Garrik stayed behind to manage affairs at this outpost, and to monitor traffic coming from the east, if any. As the remaining four heroes rode away, off in the distance they could see great pyres light up, where Zhent garrison buildings had been. The two Harpers had decided that they would unmake this fortress and had much work ahead of them.

Continuing down the road, the Heroes ambushed caravan after caravan. Zhent caravans stopped coming after a month, but in the time between leaving Dekanter and arriving in Zelbross, they attacked and commandeered a dozen caravans, mostly of the Moonwheel, Tymora’s Road, and Wheels of the Sand companies. Each of these was found to be lead by a mage of Zhent origin, and all were well guarded, though not well enough to protect themselves from the four travelers, who had become adept at road-way ambush tactics.

In Zelbross, the Heroes arrive to find the Last Place burned to the ground, and the shady proprietor, Ordilar, hanging from a scorched archway. It was discovered by Durnax that this man had been perpetuating the slave trade, and that he was a Zhent spy. Many communications intended for the Zhent came to Durnax, due to the enchantment that Corrina had put on the messenger pigeons there long before. One particular note of interest, Durnax kept, and handed over to the Heroes when they stayed the night in his tower.

== Tarsakh, 1373 - An Open Road==

As the four Heroes continued along the East Road to Waterdeep, they found the road getting friendlier and friendlier. In each village they arrive in, they hear that this mysterious character or that has fled town, with news of the pending arrival of the Heroes. They are called by many names. The Four Knights, The Knights of the Sand, Swords of the Compass, and many others. But one seems to stick. Company of the Eastern Storm is the name most often heard from town to town. Many locals note that uncharacteristic easterly winds blow into town on the day before, or the morning of the arrival of the Heroes. Indeed, the tavern at Bargewright Inn is known to have emptied, and troublesome faces disappeared en masse when an easterly wind blew out the candles in the place on the night before the arrival of the Company.

So it came to pass that Boregar neared the completion of his duties. The road had been made safe to wander by any traveler who so desired. He had but two duties left, and that was to rid Waterdeep of the corruption of the Zhents and the Drow.

At length, the company arrived in Rassalantar, and here they met up with some old friends on the 30th of Tarsakh. By now, the snow was nearly gone from all but the largest banks and deepest of woods. A warm breeze has been blowing, and the spring promises to be one of the finest in recent memory.
