==From Westgate by Sea==

Tyrian was ill prepared to make the journey north to Daggerdale.  Almost as soon as Reynard the Boar had issued his challenge, Tyrian was out of the doors of The House of Ilmater and headed to the Bitch Queen's Shrine, where he was sure to find the regular collection of ship captains paying their offerings and uttering their prayers to keep Umberlee's displeasure away.  Aside from the monk's habit he wore, the only other possessions to his name where given to him by Reynard as he left the temple:  a purse with some coins, a sharp knife and a holy symbol of Ilmater.  Most of the captains at the Bitch Queen's shrine laughed him away, but one finally agreed to take him aboard thanks to the holy symbol dangling on the young knight's neck.

The journey out of Westgate was as uneventful as can be expected for seafarers on the Dragonmere: no pirates, no aboleths, no monsters, no storms.  Ilmater and Valkur favoured the voyage, and Umberlee took no notice.  The old cog moved nimbly down the canal that had been dredged to link the old city's harbour with the anchorage at the new shoreline, and then out, across The Neck and into The Dragonmere heading west, hugging the coast to Teziir.  A quick resupply and change of cargo later, the cog turned north with a good, strong sou'easterly wind and in no time at all they were anchoring in Marsember.

==The Forbidden Kingdom==

Cormyr proper.  Tyrian had mixed feelings about this next step.  He had spent his youth admiring this kingdom, and longing to pledge his sword and lance in the service of its king.  Yet, that king had rejected him; banished him from the realm.  It wasn't his intention to remain here, only to cross it as quickly as possible and enter the Dales.  Was he betraying King Floril's order by stepping an uninvited foot in his kingdom?

Heading west out of Cormyr to Daerlun and from there into Sembia and up to the Dales was out of the question.  It would be fall before he reached Castle Radiance if he took that route.  No.  Ilmater had seen to it that the only ship that could carry him across the Dragonmere would bring him here.  Ilmater had not chosen to have him cross from Westgate to Daerlun.  Ilmater wanted him in Cormyr, and now that he was here there was only one way to go.

Leaving Marsember proved to be considerably easier than he had expected.  He did not plan to stay, even for the night, so he headed west out of the city's gate and onto the Dragoneye Way: the road to Suzail.  He remembered that Marsember was a major centre for Amaunator's faithful.  The entire city was dominated by that one faith.  The ship's captain had spoken briefly to him about how for years he had made good money shuttling Amaunator's pilgrims from all over the southern coast of the Inner Sea across the Dragonmere to Marsember.  From there they would group up and head west, then north to Daggerdale in their odd pilgrimage: precisely the route Tyrian was hoping to follow.

His instinct proved true, and no more than a mile outside the gates, as the sun was going down in the distance ahead of him (behind the unseen, yet appropriately named Sunset Mountains... his mountains...) he came across the first group of pilgrims praying to that disappearing disc.  Tyrian waited until the formalities of ritual were over, and then introduced himself and asked to join them in their trek.

==The White Prince==

A few days later, Tyrian found himself in Suzail, the city he had called home for three years, not so long ago.  Leaving his sun-worshipping pilgrims behind, curiosity got the better of him and he made his way to the sprawling palace complex known simply as The Court.  This had been his home.

He had been well loved here, and he in turn had loved them back.  But it was different now.  He was returning to that first love, that love that spurned him, and he found that the passion was gone.  The faces, the buildings, the flags, the emblems: none of it lifted his heart like it once did.  This is just empty pageantry.  Nobility is what Reynard does in the slums of Westgate.

Without turning to look back, Tyrian headed to the north gate and looked out onto Calantar's Way: the north road towards Arabel.  He had patrolled this road countless times in the past.  He knew each and every mile.  He also knew the knights that would be doing that duty now.  If they recognised him, he'd likely be detained and taken back to explain to the king why he had returned to Cormyr in defiance of his banishment.  While Tyrian knew the old king would likely just have him escorted to the northern border once he learned of his appointed mission, he also knew that by this time in summer the king would be anywhere in his kingdom except Suzail.  It could be weeks before they caught up to the old man, and weeks more before he agreed to listen to Tyrian.

With a deep sigh, Tyrian headed back to The Court.  He had one chance, and with Ilmater's grace, it would work.

Entering The Court, Tyrian knew exactly where he was going.  He had made it to the second to last door before he was stopped.  \"My name is Tyrian.  I've come to see Prince Irvel.\"

It was a bittersweet reunion for both men.  The middle aged Prince had been Tyrian's supporter, and had argued in his favour before the King.  After some brief reminiscing, Tyrian got to the heart of the matter: he needed safe passage out of Cormyr.  He knew most of The Purple Dragons, and they knew him.  Though he was well liked by them, he was still a pariah who would need to be detained.  He did not want to put any of them in a position that would compromise their honour.  All he needed was a letter bearing the royal seal allowing him to cross Cormyr to the Dales.

Irvel agreed to do so, not just out of old friendship, but out of respect for Reynard. He issued him his paperwork, though he told Tyrian that he would have to inform his father of this.  He would tell the king that he signed the warrant because Tyrian was acting as Reynard's agent, but the king would likely see to it that the Cormyrean border would in future be closed to Tyrian.

As a final gesture of friendship, Irvel refused to let the young knight leave Suzail wearing nothing but his tattered monk's habit.  He was free to travel in the kingdom now, and should do so in a manner befitting his status.  Before long, Tyrian had been outfitted with plate armour, shield, sword and lance, provisions and a good riding horse.  With a final salute, Tyrian gave the Crown Prince his thanks and farewells, and sped his mount out of the city gate and onto Calantar's Way.

==Calantar’s Way==

It was hard for Tyrian not to smile as he felt the wind against his bare face, galloping north on the road.  He had done this countless times, and for the first time in a long time, he felt like he was truly home.  This was something familiar.  This was something he knew.  Since he was banished, he had languished in his mother's house in Iriaebor driven half-mad with boredom and idleness.  And then, when he had been tricked into the journey to Westgate, he found himself on the other side of that coin: Reynard had stripped him of everything and sent him to tend to lepers and plague victims and lunatics and... worse, in the most vile, dangerous slums in the Realms.  For the better part of a year, he had been under Reynard's care.  No, under Reynard's watch.  A lifetime learning to skewer enemies with a lance had been ill preparation for both the idleness of the nobility of Iriaebor and the horrors of the slums of Westgate.  But this.  Now.  Wearing plate armour and riding at full gallop down Calantar's Way!  This he knew.

And he also knew the knights.  The next morning on the road he encountered his first patrol.  As expected, he knew most of the knights, and some of the squires.  All were surprised to see him, none were in any hurry to arrest him.  Tyrian simply told the truth:  he had decided to pledge his sword to Ilmater and had been sent north to Castle Radiance by Reynard the Boar himself.  He had seen the Crown Prince in Suzail who had allowed him permission to travel through Cormyr on his way to the Dalelands.  Most were just plain happy to see their old friend, and all of them were full of questions about the legendary Boar of Westgate.  Tyrian did not have the heart to tell this cadre of young noblemen that the legendary Reynard, Demon Hunter of the Dragonmere spends most of his days washing the feet of beggars and giving comfort to dying old prostitutes.

After that first encounter, word spread quickly amongst the Purple Dragons and Tyrian soon found himself riding alongside his old mates, trading jibes and daring each other to feats of horsemanship, just as if he was back on patrol.

== Arabel==

Arriving at the The Dragon's Gate Inn in Arabel, the road-house built outside the fortress city as an extension of the old landmark Pride of Arabel housed inside the city walls, Tyrian bade his friends farewell and prepared himself for the next stage in his journey.

The road north from here, the Moonsea Ride, lay ahead.  Tyrian had taken his turn patrolling that stretch a number of times.  He did not know it as well as Calantar's Way, but he knew it well enough to be sure of two things:  first, he should no longer travel with the Purple Dragons, even if they were his friends.  The knights were on duty and the dangers of the road ahead obligated him to let them do their appointed tasks without having to worry about him.  His sword and lance were peace-bound until he left the kingdom.  Second, many of the caravans and pilgrims headed north to Daggerdale would be congregating here, and he would do well to make arrangements to join with one of the groups now.

Opening the doors of the ‘Gate, Tyrian was stunned to discover the tap room nearly empty.  In truth, this had been his first entry into this place despite the many times he had been to Arabel.  In the past he had always been housed in the garrison within the walls and had had no reason to mingle with the merchants, pilgrims and assorted other travellers that congregated here.  He knew this was the place where all voyages north began, and he had expected a rollicking and rowdy tavern.  Even the refectory at the House of Ilmater in Westgate was more boisterous than this room.

After quickly arranging stabling for his horse and securing a private room and a bath, Tyrian retired without a glance back at the tap room, ashamedly longing for his first sleep in a real bed in almost a year.

Very early the next morning, cleansed and refreshed, Tyrian set about exercising himself and his horse, watching carefully as the sun-worshiping patrons of the New Pride went about their morning adorations.  Tyrian had never quite understood Amaunator and his worshipers.  He had been well schooled in theology when he had trained for the priesthood, and knew about all the faiths of Faerûn. He knew enough about Amaunator to know that he did not really trust him or his clergy.  While their devotion to the fight against the world's evil was admirable, and while he had found the pilgrims he had met on the road to be genial and affable on the whole, there was a certain element of trickery and misdirection at play in the faith's history, and its clergy's dealings with the people which had always given him pause.  The heavy-handedness he saw in Marsember and that he had heard existed in Daggerdale added to his skepticism. It may be the same god as Lathander, as they claim, but whatever the case may be, Tyrian was sure that the truth was one thing Amaunator did not shine too brightly on.

==New Companions==

Re-entering the roadhouse, Tyrian made his way to one of the empty tables for breakfast.  Before he could get there, an impeccably dressed, middle-aged man made a discreet attempt to get his attention.  Before long, Beliard Emmarask, newly appointed Cormyrean envoy to Castle Radiance, was sharing pleasantries of tales of life at the Court of the Purple Dragon, and trading names of mutual acquaintances and friends.

Beliard told Tyrian that the best way to get to Castle Radiance would be accompanying a merchant by the name of Denelius Ormath, but it was difficult to know when he would be leaving.  Negotiations were in progress, and it was just as likely that they would depart tomorrow as next week or next year.  A little patience was all that was needed.  With that, Tyrian headed off to the garrison in Arabel, and then to replenish his supplies.

Upon his return in the evening, he found Beliard sharing a table with the most peculiar looking woman he had ever seen.  Tyrian had spent a long time in the slums of Westgate, and had seen people of just about every race and culture there is to be found.  But this woman?  This one was odd.

Tyrian approached the table and was about to open his mouth in greeting to Beliard and his unusual companion, when Beliard turned to the table next to him and spoke:

:\"What's the rush, indeed? Why help the Dalesmen with their little war  when we could wait for them to succumb to the Shadow...\"

Tyrian listened to the orator's words enthralled.

:...He pauses for a second, then his eyes brighten. Turning to gesture at a wide-eyed Tyrian, with whom he has exchanged only some pleasantries so far, he continues: \"In payment, I can offer the sword and strong heart of this fine young knight, for he tells me that he, too, is bound for Castle Radiance.\" He claps Tyrian on the shoulder. \"This young man, whom I know to be a fine and noble man, has sworn an oath of anonymity, and may not name himself until he has found Azoun's Lost Crown!\"
(gasps from the room, now fully enthralled)

Too perplexed from this last statement, Tyrian lets it go, mesmerized by the demagogue before him.

:In a stage whisper, he adds, \"I can, however, tell you, good master, that this man is a favourite of the White Prince. His glorious future is assured, and the favour of your service is sure to pay off in spades!\"

Clapping his confused companion on the back, Beliard smiles brightly at the room - all eyes upon him - stands, and lifts his glass.

:\"Ladies and Gentlemen - the King!\"

Tyrian simply sat, overwhelmed by the spectacle he had just witnessed.

\"Mister Emmarask?\" Tyrian eventually asked in a whisper to his tablemate, \"I was not aware Azoun had lost his crown.\"

Carpathia sat with the two men she had just met, and without provocation, began softly to laugh.
