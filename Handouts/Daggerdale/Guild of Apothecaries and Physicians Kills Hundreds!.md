Excerpted article from the Waterdeep Trumpet, 16 Flamerule, 1372
----

Hundreds of Citizens of our Faire City took their last breaths in the many impromptu sick-tents and warehouse-cum-infirmaries of Dock Ward last month, as the Grey Ague raged through to poorer districts of Towne. No Fewer than 10 large sick tents and 4 warehouses accommodated the count of one thousand victims of this recent sickness. While the numbers of victims grew, and the tents and infirmaries became more and more crowded, fewer and fewer members of the unscrupulous Guild in question were seen.

The Generous Faiths of the City provided all the healing they could, though many Houses preferred, in the stead of doing good, to do brisk business with the moneyed and paranoid elements of the gilded homes of the North End. This left very few of our Sanctimonious Brethren to tend to the real victims of this plague, who dwell in the more cramped regions about the harbour.

The Guild of Apothecaries and Physicians chose to follow the example of the questionable Houses of the City, by selling their services only to those who could afford them and in fact, did not need them. But their abhorrent behaviour is even more gruesome…

The ground and distilled concoction of the rare Ferrow Root, a proven and efficacious treatment of the Grey Ague, was in short supply, as the plague lasted into its third tenday. However, there was supply enough in the supply tent that could have treated twice the count of victims in the Lower End as there were. The supply had been purchased by the City from the Guild of Apothecaries and Physicians for the criminally-inflated rate of 10 gold coins per dose. Though the cost was high, it was not more than the City could bear, and the treatments were purchased...

But on the morning of 13 Flamerule, while over one thousand souls struggled to keep their feet in this world, a gang of sneak-thieves stole into the supply tent, and made off with supplies of medicine for the treatment of the plague. This discovery was made by one Dr. Greggore Kahsic, who was the first into the supply tent that morning. The Doctor immediately reprimanded the Holy Brethren of Helm, who had the charge of guarding the tent. Of course, the Helmites immediately begged forgiveness for what

seemed like gross misconduct on their part. However, the treachery of the Guild would not stand up to a formal investigation, which revealed that, in fact, the only person to enter the tent all night was the Dr. Kahsic.

Though it was shown that he was the only person to enter the tent, it could not be proven that it was he who stole the medicine, as no one had seen him leave with the conspicuously large amounts of medicine that was missing.

On 14 Flamerule, however, the sale of the Ferrow Root Concoction in the North End began anew, and once again, the privileged people of the wealthy end of the City were once again able to buy a cure for a sickness that had not come within a mile of their ivied walls. The cost for the Concoction on 14 Flamerule was 25 gold coins per dosage, it should be noted.

So, the crimes of the Guild seem to include the following;
#The sale of emergency goods to the City at inflated prices
#The robbery of emergency goods from the City, and from the Sick and Dying
#The sale of stolen goods and potential charlatanism
#Wanton neglect for the Sick and Dying

There is a fifth crime though, that they have not answered for, and it is the most heinous of all! It was revealed by Divine Consultation that the origin of the Grey Ague was within the sinister walls of the Waterdeep School of Anatomy and Medicine, where the same Dr. Kahsic is Owner, Provost and Chief Theater Lecturer. Knowing that Divine Consultation has never been enough to strike the gallows of our Faire City, inquiry was made by the Knights of Tyr.

Though they were unable to gain entry into the secreted 'School', they were able to apprehend one student in front of the school, and an interrogation took place in Wendlehorn Tower, in North Ward, under the auspices of Magistrate Richter. The student admitted that he had seen victims of the Grey Ague in the School, both before and during the outbreak of the plague. Though it seemed clear to the paladins that the plague had been started right in the School of Anatomy and Medicine, Richter dismissed the claim, saying that the student was not a credible witness. It seemed absurd to the Knights of Justice, as I am sure it does to you, the informed reader.

If the plague was indeed created by the Guild, and then unleashed upon the citizens of Dock Ward, so that they could sell the cure (twice, in some instances!), this would amount to the most sinister plot ever hatched in our Faire City. However, it seems that the Guild of Apothecaries and Physicians is above Legal Reproach in our City, as are too many other Guilds.

This begs the question: \"Who really runs the City of Waterdeep?\"
