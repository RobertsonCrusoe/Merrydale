Ridge and River Renewal sounds good - I was thinking about whether or not
we should fit the Chrysalis theme, but if we want to keep this venture at
arm's length and run it anonymously, it might be better to downplay any
connection to the actual Chrysalis. Am I right in understanding that your
reasoning for the subterfuge is to save us money - i.e., we pay a little
more for labour to get the best people, but all overhead and profits come
straight back to Chrysalis? Meanwhile, by setting up our own company we
avoid any ire from the various guilds that would otherwise be up in arms
about an adventuring company getting onto their turf?

If that's the case, I think we might want to find someone other than Merry
to be our frontman. We might want to bring in someone like Sanduskin or
Zultim who isn't so closely associated with Chrysalis. Actually, what's Mr.
Bollinger up to these days? Maybe he'd be a good entrepreneurial character.

Here's Lucan's summary of the strategy:

 Setting up "Ridge and River Renewal" as a separate entity has several
advantages:

   1.

   *Anonymity*: By dissociating this venture from Chrysalis, we effectively
   sidestep potential backlash or opposition from local guilds who might see
   an adventuring company's direct involvement in construction as
   overstepping. This anonymity allows for smoother operations without the
   baggage of our primary brand.
   2.

   *Financial Strategy*: Operating this construction firm independently yet
   under the umbrella of Chrysalis allows for a strategic financial flow.
   Investing in quality labor might increase initial costs, but by funneling
   overheads and profits back to Chrysalis, we create a sustainable loop that
   benefits our main operation without the overt association.
   3.

   *Guild Relations*: By creating a standalone company that ostensibly
   competes fairly in the local market, we avoid guild suspicions or
   hostility. This can foster better relations and potentially even
   collaborations, under terms that do not link back to Chrysalis.

In essence, this approach not only secures the operational benefits and
profits but also strategically shields Chrysalis from direct marketplace
friction, positioning both entities to thrive without compromising one
another.
