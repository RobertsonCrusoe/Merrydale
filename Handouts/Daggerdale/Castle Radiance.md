# Castle Radiance
# Source: The Sieges Perilous wiki (backup dated 2010-02-21)

“Don’t let it’s ties to Amaunator fool you.  It’s no church.  It’s a fortress.  It’s more than a fortress really; if Amaunator wears plate boots when he goes to war, Castle Radiance is one of those boots.  It doesn’t…it doesn’t belong.  You’ll see what I mean when you first see it.  It’s impressive, but it sticks out.  I’ve spent my whole life in the Dales and too much time at the western edge of those mountains.  I’ve learned.  If you stick out, you’re the first nail that gets pounded flat.  That place sticks out.”   , Dane Grimwald speaking to soon-to-be companions about Castle Radiance.

Castle Radiance is the strong point of defense against the growing ambitions of Netheril, as well as the multitudinous creatures that are spawning in the restored and vibrant grasslands of Netheril and the mountains. It can be found by following the Mountain Road west out of Dagger Falls, up 60 miles of road, into the Desertsmouth mountains.. It is typically a 3-day walk, or 2 days by horseback, though with fresh mounts at three spots along the way, dispatch riders are able to make the journey in a day by riding hard.

History of the Castle

The site of the Castle was discovered in 1400 DR by Ardelusk Whitegate, and his sons Loedegan and Madigan. The three Whitegates were part of a large expedition that Ardelusk put together to investigate the origins of the horde of Warlord Zguumush that besieged Dagger Falls in the spring of 1400. 

On the Summer Solstice, the expedition was ambushed in the mountains, and had to make a protracted retreat. During the ambush, Ardelusk was mortally wounded, but survived long enough to be shown, as his family later claimed, the location of the ruins where the expedition made a final stand against the many monsters and humanoids chasing them down. As the story goes, Ardelusk saw a single stabbing ray of light come through the clouds on an otherwise rain-soaked and completely cloudy day. No one else in the expedition remembers seeing any sun that day. At any rate, by this time Ardelusk was already known to be divinely inspired at times, and so the bedraggled expedition members, following the guidance of the dying man, climbed to a difficult summit to make a stand against their pursuers. 

At the top of the summit, they found an old ruin of some sort. There, they managed to destroy their pursuers, though Ardelusk finally gave up his life in this world while his sons fought on. The ruins at first appeared to be Dwarven in origin, which were immediately assumed to be a part of the lost empire of Tethyamar. The first block to the new castle was laid that day in the form of the tomb of Ardelusk, which remains unchanged to this day.

The Ardelusk brothers decided to build a fortress on the site of the ruins, as it seemed a good and defensible spot, and was seemingly divinely appointed. The first year of the building of the Castle was the hardest; that year found the builders and those defending them constantly assailed by all varieties of monsters, and it was during this time, 1400-01, that the sons of Ardelusk forged their own heroic legends, leading the defense against uncounted foes and battles. By the end of the first year of construction, it was clear that the site was well chosen; though several builders and defenders had been slain, thousands of monsters and evil humanoids had spent their lives trying to take the site from the Dalesmen. 

Construction of the Castle has never ceased since. The fortress is only now beginning to approach the reasonable constraints that the mountainous site will allow, and works have now begun on bridges and smaller fortresses along the road from Dagger Falls to the Castle. These projects are, of course, subject to the same trials and hardships of the early years of the Castle, but the Dalesmen and the adventurers that come to the Castle are better prepared and more numerous than the Whitegates of the turn of the century.

Description

Castle Radiance is truly a mountain-fortress, which follows the zigzagging contours of the mountainside it is perched upon. It sits on the west face of Bloody Hand Mountain, a mountain of medium size for the Desertsmouth range. The original ruin that is built upon sits on a shelf some 150' below the summit of the mountain, but the Castle has grown up that mountain face so that the highest peak of the construction, the roof of the Fortress-Cathedral of the Sun, is above the mountain peak, and commands a 360 degree view of the horizon.

There are roughly 3 tiers of battlements, with the lowest being the walls reconstructed over the original walls of the ruins. 

• Outer Bailey - A large meandering thoroughfare runs between the outer/lower and middle battlements; at some points it is over 100' wide, at others it chokes to as little as 15'. At each end of the thoroughfare is a great gate; the north gate is called Madigan's Gate, the south gate is called Loedegan's Gate. The outer bailey is the largest open space inside the castle; it runs roughly in a semi circular arch on the plateau that follows the mountain face, and from gate-to-gate, it is over 500'. This is the busiest bailey, with the majority of the businesses and services available to the residents and visitors to the Castle operating therein. During business hours, this is a very busy, but clean and orderly, place.

• Middle Bailey - Between the middle ramparts and the upper ramparts, is this smaller bailey. It is accessed by a pair of towered gates in the middle rampart, which have great stairs and switch-back ramps inside them for moving people, animals and supplies up the 70' incline from the outer to the middle bailey. This bailey has some business inside, including the The Spire Forge, but it is dominated by the massive stone stables at the northern and southern end of the bailey, and the track between them for the exercising of the mounts of the Castle.

• Inner Bailey - Between the upper ramparts and the rocky face of the mountain, which has been mostly chiselled out and built into the Fortress Cathedral of the Sun, is the Inner Bailey. This pristine court yard provides access to all the important Castle buildings; the Cathedral, the Keep, and the Barracks. The Inner Bailey is smaller than the rest, being roughly semicircular in shape, with a radius of 100'. The inner walls of this bailey are all parts of the expansive network of buildings mentioned above. Access to this bailey is through a single towered gate in the outer ramparts, which like the two lower access towers, has a network of stairs and ramps inside to get up the 50' from the grounds of the Middle Bailey to the grounds of the Inner Bailey.

Arrival

On the road to the Castle, the traveler is likely to encounter a number of patrols, and been questioned a number of times about their business. However, when the traveler arrives at the Castle, typically from the South Gate, they are really interrogated. 

The approach to the South Gate is steep and perilous, and during the day there is always a platoon from the 2nd company of the Sun Blades on guard outside the walls, protecting a small array of tents and Castle Officials. A squad of Sun Blades will approach the traveler, stopping them a good distance from the tents to ask a few questions, before sending them off to the tents where they are thoroughly interrogated, explained the rules, and either turned away or admitted. 

The rules of the Castle are explained, in agonizing length and detail, and all the associated fines are laid out, even though a large wooden sign hangs from the walls, immediately behind the last tent where the explanation is given, in plain view of the traveler. 

Travelers are given directions at the tents to their destination in the Castle, whatever that might be, and promising soldiers or adventurers are told to check in at Middlegate Tower.

Features of the Castle

Outer Bailey

• Tethyamar Hearth Inn - This is the only Inn at the Castle, and guests without other arrangements are required to rent a room here. It is a large building which dominates one corner of the Outer Bailey.

• Chronicler's House - A small building that the Emissaries from Candle Keep operate out of. This is where artifacts are taken to be identified and catalogued. 

• Market Hall - This is a long, tall stone building that acts as the general marketplace. Only in here can transient merchants sell their goods, and they must rent one of the 12 large stalls to do so. This is a bustling place during the adventuring season, with most of the stalls often full of merchants and their wares.

• The North Plaza - A row of 5 shoppes along the outer rampart.  These days they are always leased out to permanent businesses on 1 year contracts.  Each shoppe is a large, open 30'x30' room with an appartment above it.  Present businesses include the following;

• *Weatheral's - A leather worker whose business produces a smell that dominates the entire row.

• *The Chordwainers - A rope maker and his two sons who make all types and qualities of ropes.
• *Strongback's Tools - A hardware that imports all if it's goods.
• *Mountaintop Mill and Bakery - Run by Sister Margata of Chauntea, and 4 other sisters.  Sells some blessed breads and food stuffs.

• *Archer's Den - Run by Gimbly, one of the finest bowyer/fletchers in the Dales.

• The South Plaza - A row of three large shoppes, each with 3 stories, along the outer rampart.  These shoppes are also leased out for 1 year at a time, and are always full.  They include the following;

• *Broom and Goldburrow, Cartographers - A very successful firm of cartographers who have done a respectable job of mapping out the Desserts Mouth Mountains.  There are rooms to rent on the third floor.

• *Arcadium's - An alchemy shoppe with 2 floors of labs above it.  This is a busy place, and is well stocked.  

• *The Royal Exchange - This is a sort of pawn shop with no real royal affiliation.  This is a great place to unload used gear, buy some cheap second-hand gear, and find odd bits of magic that desperate adventurers have sold in a pinch.  

• *Mrs. Nettle's - This is a new restaurant that has been established on the second floor of The Royal Exchange.  It is run by a kind widow in her 40's who serves breakfast all day long, and does brisk business.  She rents rooms on the third floor.

• Middlegate Tower - One of the smaller towers on the middle wall, this tower also serves as a gate to the Middle Bailey, as well as an office that adventurers and mercenaries are expected to check it with when they arrive.

Middle Bailey

• The Spire Forge - A very busy private forge run by a man named Grenville, and his 3 assistants.  The prices here are steep, but the work is as good as it gets.

• North Stable - A large stone stable that accommodates up to 200 horses.  This stable is the private stable of the Sun Blades and the Order.

• South Stable - A public stable that can accommodate up to 120 horses.  Stabling and feed for a single mount costs 2 silver/day.

• The Track - an oval horse track of 200 yards that is used for exercising mounts.  During the day there are always stable hands and soldiers walking their horses on the track.  In the middle of the track is a public space for combat drills.  There are always soldiers here during the day practicing.

Inner Bailey

• Loedegan's Hall - A barracks which makes up much of the southwestern wall which can hold as many as 600 men.  There are usually 120 Sun Blades garrisoned here, as well as 60 Knights of the Order of the Lambent Shield.  As well, all of the officials, servants, and other support staff for the Castle, and the Castle leadership including Lord Whitegate himself are housed here.

• Madigan's Hall - A sturdy stone keep that makes up much of the northwestern wall.  This Keep is dominated by a huge festhall, but includes many other important rooms such as an armoury, kitchens, granary, and many smaller meeting rooms.  There is a war room at the back of the Hall in which Lord Whitegate holds his daily strategic meeting.  

• Fortress Cathedral of the Mount - The centrepiece of the Inner Bailey, this impressive stone structure with it's many spires and flying buttresses is a marvel of modern architecture. The five ceremonies of the Church are vigilantly held here every day, and they are marked by the tolling of a great bell in the upper reaches of the many small towers and spires.  The Cathedral backs against the mountain, and a warren of tunnels is found behind it with emergency supplies to outlast a siege.  The Cathedral is meant as the last line of defense in the Castle, and has been designed and built to repel the most brutal sieges.  

• Grounds - A single large courtyard is framed by these three buildings and the towers between them.  This courtyard is usually full of soldiers drilling and practicing.

• *Sun Wheel - This is a large wooden structure upon which bounties may be acquired.

Towers

There are 7 great stone towers that form the strong points of the defense of the Castle.  These towers are contracted out to private organizations who have proven their loyalty and ability.  The contracted organizations are required to provide a standing garrison 24 hours/day, and are also expected to jump to the defense of the Castle in time of need.  The towers lease for 50-100 gold/month, plus the costs associated with garrisoning.  The organizations that lease them appreciate having a permanent base of operations and some degree of privacy.  There are 4 towers on the outer wall, and 3 on the middle wall.  Presently, 5 are leased to the following organizations;

• Outer Towers
• *Tethyamar Leage
• *Free Defense League
• *Cormyrean Embassy

• Middle Towers
• *Myth Drannor Embassy
• *Emissaries from Candle Keep
