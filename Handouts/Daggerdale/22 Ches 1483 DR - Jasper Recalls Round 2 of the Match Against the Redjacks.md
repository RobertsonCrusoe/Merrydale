22 Ches, 1483 DR. - Jasper Recalls Round 2 of the Match Against the Redjacks

"Ha! Take that you fucking bird! Did you see that, Jas?!"

"Huh?"  Jasper had been staring off into the distance, lost in thought.  He was imagining a near future where he and Nemia of the Free Defense League might be prowling though some forgotten graveyard together, hand in hand.  It would be midnight; her silver hair shimmering in the moonlight; the smell of freshly exhumed graves perfuming the night air; He'd look into her eyes, and lean in close...

"Take the fucking key!"

"Err...?"  Jasper blinked and was now staring at an annoyed Lucan on the other side of metal bars.

"Right!"  He now remembered he was locked in a cage.  Lucan was handing a key to him, while he could just see Mortimer bounding off and shouting obscenities back at him.  Down below the platform he could see Ser Rowan facing off against Craster.  Jeska still sat stoic atop her perch.  Where's Mari?

"The other key, you inbred, half-witted Dalesman!"

Lucan was now out of his cage and freeing Jasper out of his prison.

Craster and Ser Rowan were engaged in melee, and the Red Jack looked like nothing short of murder would satisfy him today.  And Mari... Jasper could see the halfling was on her feet now, bloody and leaning on one of the large crates... and yet still standing dangerously close to Craster's swords.

The young wizard leapt out of his opened cage, his legs - asleep and now pins and needles from having been sitting awkwardly he last few minutes - gave way under him.  With as much grace as he could manage, he mostly just fell from the top platform, to the second platform, and down to the ground.

"Right.  Ok.  Err..."  perhaps an attempt to Daze the killer might buy Ser Rowan some time?

Jasper cast his cantrip, and to his immense surprise, Craster immediately collapsed to the ground unconscious.  The wizard looked at his hands in confusion, not quite sure of what had just happened.

The crowd began to roar in approval!  The Red Jack bully who'd chosen to strike at the halfling grandmother rather than face Ser Rowan honourably, had now seemingly fainted for no good reason at all.

Across the river, Zallia conceded the round, and the crowd cheered mightily at yet another Chrysalis victory.

"Um... what... what happened?" asked a confused Jasper, to the universe at large.

"Nevermind that, Jas!" said Lucan, "Help me grab this gold, will ya?"
