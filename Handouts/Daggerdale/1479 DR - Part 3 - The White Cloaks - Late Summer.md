==Hunting the Bloody Eye - Late Flamerule==

When the White Cloaks were rested and ready after their foray into the Dagger Hills, they made good on their promise to help Malvau in his hunt for The Bloody Eye.  This particularly devious and slippery group of bandits had plagued the roads of the Dale for more than a year, and had killed two of his companions before he hooked up with the White Cloaks.

The search began in the town.  It was long suspected that the Eye had spies or some way of gathering information in Dagger Falls, and so the company began to look for ways information might leak.  Before long they realized that it would be terribly easy to find out which caravans were headed in which direction, and the relative value and defensive strengths of each.  Alystair used every unpleasant and often brutal method he had to find out who was leaking information to the Eye, but Lumina finally put an end to the fruitless interrogations; there were just too many possible suspects and too many ways the information could get out.

They decided to return to the roads again, purposefully avoiding linking up with mounted Sun Blades, hoping to draw out the Eye into an open fight.  However, it appeared as though the Bloody Eye got wise to them; perhaps the hard questioning in the town alerted them to this determined foe, or perhaps they just planned on moving on for other reasons.  Whatever the reasons may be, the Bloody Eye seemed to have left Daggerdale.  In fact, by the closing days of Flamerule, reports began to come in about the Bloody Eye in Shadowdale, and then Mistledale.

Malvau graciously excused his friends from their duty, saying that their obligation to help him ended, understandably, at the borders of the dale they had all pledged to protect.  They parted company on the 29th of Flamerule; Malvau picked up the warm trail of his quarry, and parted company.  He has not been heard from since.

The night before they parted company, Alystair informed his friends that he would help Malvau and pursue the Bloody Eye to whatever ends.  However, in the morning the next day, he promptly changed his mind after matins.  He apologized to Malvau, stating only that \"I will have Amaunator's work to do here, and soon.\"

==Return to the Dagger Hills - Eleasias==

After Malvau left, there was some debate about what to do next.  Some of the party members wanted to head back to the Castle to seek a new bounty, but Lumina insisted that they head back to Crossed Blades Farm to see if there was any news from the hills nearby.  Tuugan and Iolus complained that they would be 'donating' their time if they just investigated the matter of their own volition, but Alystair sided with Lumina, stating that 'keeping this Dale and the people here safe is its own reward'.

With that, they set off to the south of the dale.  They were met on the road a few miles north of the farm by a group of mounted Sun Blades, who were also accompanied by a unit of Paladins of the Order of the Lambent Shield.  This is an unusual arrangement for a road patrol, and when Lumina inquired as to the nature of the increased security, one of the knights answered by handing a wooden plaque to her.  Upon it were the terms of a new bounty, the Dagger Hills Reconnaissance bounty.  This bounty seemed to have been purpose-made for the White Cloaks.

The objective of the bounty was to determine if anything else had come through the Shadowfell portal in the former den of Braxus and the Silver Tail Clan werewolves.  The terms of the bounty were vague, and suggested that some backtracking into the Shadowfell would be required.

For two days, the White Cloaks stayed at the Farm, talking to everyone who knows anything about the surrounding countryside and the hills in particular.  However, there was no one who knew of anything beyond the low stone wall that surrounds the farms arrayed about the fortress-town.  The White Cloaks would have to travel into the hills again, blind and unknowing.

There are few trackers in the Dale that can match Tuugan Swift, and he and the company spent several days looking for more tracks around the Silver Tail Cave, but found nothing new.  Finally, they faced up to the fact that they would have to travel into the cave, through the portal and into the Shadowfell to see if there were any Agents of Netheril lurking about.

===Nightmares in the Shadowfell===
right|450px
The White Cloaks stood long at the unassuming entrance to the shadow realm in the back of the cave.  Though they had all been through it before, it seemed more difficult this time to enter, knowing the oppressive nature of the Shadowfell, and the fear it sows in those who enter.  In the end, it was Iolus who gathered the strength to lead them in, though he apologized for this very deed weeks later.

They entered cautiously, and followed their tracks from their previous visit.  Tuugan found that not so much as a blade of grass had grown up in those old tracks, so immutable had they remained.  They tracked the path of their defeated adversaries from their first visit.  The tracks went, not surprisingly, directly west, towards Netheril.

They had so little trouble following the tracks that they should have suspected a trap, and in the prime material realm, they might have.  However, the effect of the Shadowfell on the minds of inhabitants of the prime material is befuddling, and for countless hours they followed the tracks into the murky nightmare-version of the foothills of the mountains.

Deep Night then came to the already gloom-ridden world of the Shadowfell after an inestimable length of time.  Blackness fell that could not be illumed by any means, and the adventurers were all cast into a realm of their own; whether this was some sort of dreamlike state, or something entirely different is impossible to say.  But each hero had his or her own private nightmare to live for an impossibly long time.

As were all who have dreamt in the Shadowfell before them, none were ever the same again.

*Here is an account of the Dreams in the Shadowfell that the White Cloaks endured.

===Ends the Deep Night===
After time immeasurable, the Deep Night ended, and the company began to stir from their mad slumber.  Iolus was the first awake, and he used the party's magic lamp to cast some light in the gloom.  He found himself lying in a pool of blood, and the six or eight feet of the twisted world of the Shadowfell that was revealed to him was a mess of gear, armour, weapons and clothing strewn about haphazardly.  He checked himself for injuries, and found he had a deep gash in his hand, which made him shudder as he recalled his nightmare, but it was not enough to form the pool of blood in which he was sitting.

Iolus got to his knees and began to crawl along the pool, until he saw, first the feet, then legs and body of his friend, Tuugan, lying against a tree pale and seemingly lifeless.  One of Tuugan's swords was sticking out of his abdomen, and with every weak beat of his dying heart, a gout of blood issued from the wound, the first Iolus witnessing hitting him squarely in the face by surprise.

At that very moment, another light a dozen feet away sparked, and he could make out the foggy and muted form of Lumina, who was naked and covered in bruises, looking around confused.  Iolus tried to yell, but the noise sounded like something underwater; the Deep Night was still receding, and the Shadowfell, such as it is, was still returning to its own twisted version of normal.  After a few more shouts to Lumina, each growing louder and louder, she finally heard Iolus, and came crawling towards him, favouring one arm and one leg.

When Lumina saw Iolus trying to wrap their friend's abdomen, she quickened her pace, and fell upon the pair of them.  He had searched for the wound, and though she had all the motor control deficits of an inebriated teenager, her fingertips found the wound, and light grew at her fingers.  Iolus looked at the battered face of the priestess, and she nodded.  With that, he jerked the blade free, and Tuugan let out a moan that sounded like a ghost in a well, and Lumina concentrated and let all the divine strength she had flow into her friend.  The wound closed, but a terrible black network of venous scars issued from the wound which would not take to healing of any kind.

Slowly, Iolus and Lumina began to look for their remaining two companions; they found Alystair first; he was standing and facing off into the distance, his fullblade dangling from a half-closed fist at his side.  They found Troia behind a tree, and although Lumina had found her cloak on the ground and had covered herself with it, she tore it off and dashed to her friend, and waved off Tuugan.  \"I'll take care of her!  Begone!\"

After a few hours of stumbling about in a drunken-like groggy daze, the White Cloaks had their gear together, and were ready to go.  All save Tuugan were free of any debilitating injuries, though none would speak of the dreams that had caused them to do gods-only-know-what in the indeterminable time between gloom-light and gloom-light.  They said little to each other while they assembled a litter from the dust-covered trees to carry Tuugan.  Alystair broke the silence.

\"We're getting out of here.\"

It seemed to take forever to travel the distance back.  Their tracks were easy enough to follow, Alystair this time taking the role of scout.  About halfway through the journey, Tuugan, who had been stirring somewhat on the bumpy ride, his head lolled to one side, held out one weak arm and pointed into the gloom.  \"Tracks\" he said.  \"Three.\"

Alystair followed the direction of his friend's pointing, and found a set of parallel tracks about a dozen feet away, heading east.  \"Were these here before, ranger?\" inquired Alystair.  Tuugan shook his head slowly.

For the remainder of the journey, Alystair would periodically go to the side to look for the tracks, and almost always he was able to find them; tracks of three humanoids; boots of three small men or women, heading towards the Silver Tail Cave.  The tracks merged with their own just before the cave, and lead right into the world of the White Cloaks.

As they entered the Shadowfell mirror of the cave, they found that there had been much activity.  Several sets of tracks lead into the cave, where they were lost in a great confusion of dozens of others, all of which exit the cave into the material world.  They rounded the bend in the cave where the gate itself is located, and could see the shimmering and growing light of their world.

\"There is light on the other side; something is in the cave!\" said Lumina.

\"Nothing sane can come from this realm,\" replied Alystair.  \"I hope those are friends on the other side of the curtain...\"

===Back Through the Curtain===

As the company crossed the threshold back into their own world, all felt a tremendous weight lifted from them.  The venous scars on Tuugan's abdomen went from a dark black colour to an ashen grey, and have not healed since.  They all took in a deep breath of the dank cave air that they knew was the air of their home-plane, and the fog on their minds lifted almost immediately.

The cave was found to have a blinding light coming from the its mouth, and once they had gathered themselves and rounded the bend in the cave that hides the portal, they heard voices commanding them to halt.  Wearily, they simply complied, but right away heard a man's voice yell \"It's them!  The White Cloaks have returned!  Amaunator be praised!\"

A group of armoured men came rushing down into the cave to help them out, grabbing the litter that Tuugan was riding on.  Tuugan was half-struggling to his feet, and waved off help, though one knight insisted on providing his shoulder for a crutch.  The cave was being watched by a unit of Paladins of the Order, and at the cave mouth was a huge concave sheet of steel, polished to a mirror finish, that light up the cave as bright as day.

Being brought out of the cave, the White Cloaks found that a camp had been set up at the mouth of the cave; there were two more units of paladins (3 units = 1 'ride') assigned to this camp, and a unit of Sun Blades.  All were under the military command of Knight Commander Pauldron.  Ecclesiastic command of the camp was under Magistrate Madrick, who was dressed now in chain armour rather than his pristine yellow robes, and wore a different expression on his face than when last the White Cloaks had seen him.

The White Cloaks were greeted warmly by the previously aloof Magistrate, who explained that their expedition into the Shadowfell had disrupted a very important meeting between a variety of agents of Netheril.  A platoon of the 4th Company of Sun Blades under the leadership of Sgt. Leedo Forester managed to intercept and destroy a roaming party of Krinth, and had even managed to take one of the Shadowfell goblinoids prisoner.  Interrogation of this creature revealed a few key pieces of intelligence;

*A significant agent of Netheril knew about the confrontation between the White Cloaks and the Netherese scouts earlier in the summer.
*A copy of the Shadowfell Map had already been returned to the Sharspire by this agent.
*This agent was supposed to meet with the Krinth and several other groups of creatures, but the meeting was ruined when the White Cloaks went into the cave; apparently the agent decided to follow the White Cloaks instead.
*Without the guidance of the agent, the groups of monsters that came to the meeting began to fight each other; they dispersed into the hills, and there has been conflict in the Daggerhills ever since.

When Lumina heard all this, she asked how all this could have happend in a couple days.  She was told by the Magistrate that they had gone into the cave nearly three weeks previous, and were presumed dead.

The White Cloaks rested at the camp that night, and helped defend against a brutal assault of Krinth that left a paladin and several soldiers dead, as well as dozens of the creatures that had attacked them.  During one flash of holy light given off by Lumina, Tuugan swore he could see three ravens sitting in a tree nearby watching the battle.  They flew away when tried to get closer for a better look.

The following day, the White Cloaks returned to the farm to rest.  Not all would be allowed to rest, however.

==Cults and Saints - Eleint==
thumb|450px|right|Sgt. Randall Eversmoore in a battle at dusk with Agents of Netheril attacking the Pilgrims

As the weary White Cloaks approached Crossed Blades, they could see a long column of people lined up at the southern gate, and a buzz of activity in the staging area outside the walls.  Getting closer, they could see that the long column of people numbered a few hundred or more, and that they were a ragged bunch, wearing a motley collection of robes and other light clothing, with not a weapon among them.  This was a column of pilgrims of Amaunator, and they had clearly come a long distance.  In fact, some had come from the other side of the Faerûn, while some were from as nearby as other Dales.  They had all answered some divine call to pilgrimage, and had collected on the roads leading to Daggerdale, and were now together in one impressive host.  They were also travelling with a significant train of traders and their wagons.  Their numbers made it clear that not all could be accommodated within the walls of the farm, and tents were already being pitched in the fields around the south gate staging area.

As the White Cloaks got nearer, there came a sudden and eerie quiet; the birds in the forest line nearby quieted, and a hush fell over the busy staging area and people began to look about.  Just as the sun dropped below the trees, and the long shadow of the last moments of the day fell across the fields and the farm, the forest edge erupted, and a host of dark enemies flooded across in the growing twilight.  The enemies were quite distant, but the small gate would not permit all the pilgrims to get inside safely in time.  Seeing this, Alystair charged out against the enemy.  There was a moment of shock even from his companions; the sight of the avenger in his glimmering cloak flying across the twilight field to face several hundred enemies alone was as dramatic as it was hopeless.  He didn’t get far before his companions were taking off after him, and from the fortified stone walls of the farm, one guard, witnessing the flickering points of light charging out into the field was heard to cry “The White Cloaks charge!  None can stand against us!”

Soon, the Sun Blades were mounted and charging out behind the White Cloaks, and a rolling battle was joined.  The enemy was numerous, and were mostly orcs and goblins, but some krinth were found among them, and a few deranged looking humans as well.  They were accompanied by packs of wolves and some shadowfell versions of other animals and monsters.  The host tried to get at the pilgrims, but the heroes and the soldiers behind them managed to route many companies of the enemy.  Some companies did get to the walls, by they had been delayed long enough that every pilgrim was safe inside, and the defenders managed to easily repel the raiders.

In the end, the majority of the host fled the field and nearly 100 were left dead on the field.  The Sun Blades lost a few men, but all agreed they had died well, in brave service to Amaunator and the protection of pilgrims.  All those who fought that day were later awarded the Badge of the Fearless.

===The Sainthood of Gaebril Tremalkin===

The cult of Shar has always enjoyed some membership in Daggerdale, ever since the vampire infestation that plagued it centuries ago.  When Gaebril arrived with the Company of the Eastern Storm in 1372 DR, his pivotal role in ridding the dale of the cults of Iyactu Xvim, Bane and Cyric, the expulsion of the Zhents and the liberation of thousands of slaves, his role as a figurehead for the cult of Shar was entrenched, and membership grew, even if only in secret.

In 1479 DR, the Cult of Shar is a very secretive organization, and it is mercilessly hunted down by the authorities, especially those of the Church of Amauantor, in Daggerdale.  However, as the state religion of the sworn enemies of the dale, Netheril, and as a cult with a long tradition in the dale, it is impossible to keep it out.

Just as the pilgrimages to see the White Saint at the Castle began, so too did secret Black Pilgrimages at night.  Rumours and whispers of these pilgrimages of secretive cultists began to multiply as the summer went on, and it wasn’t long before there were mutterings about something called The Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin.  The Sharites would risk discovery night after night to make a pilgrimage to secret glades and other places that they were told the Black Hand could be seen and experienced.

Solid intelligence about this relic was uncovered by Alystair Dretch in the Autumn of this year, during his investigation of Dwimmerholme Farm.  It seems that the Dark Garden has this relic in their possession, and that they are using it as a focus for a powerful ritual to create new and powerful cells of the Cult of Shar.

While Alystair and Tuugan were able to intervene in the creation of a large cell at Dwimmerholme, Magistrate Hoetmer, with the help of a mysterious ‘Oghman’, was able to determine that two other cells were in fact created under the power of the Black Hand, somewhere in the Dale.

Saint Gaebril, as he is now known to the faithful of Shar, has become a powerful motivating force for the Cult, and his name is uttered in whispers and threats in every corner of the Dale; sometimes as a promise for deliverance, sometimes as a threat.

===The Avenger and the Pilgrims===

Shortly after the large host of Pilgrims at Crossed Blades Farm was rescued by the heroic charge of the White Cloaks, they were compelled to continue their journey.  There was a great debate in the Cathedral of the Farm the evening before they planned to go.  Many of the pilgrims had similar visions and other signs that it was time for them to go, but Sgt. Randall Eversmoore, the officer in charge of the Sun Blades at the Farm, insisted that they wait for a column of Sun Dragoons to escort them.  The argument seemed like it would be at a stalemate, but eventually Magistrate Madrick intervened, and sided with the pilgrims.

“They have a divine calling, and none can delay them now if they follow that calling.  They will be delivered safely to the tomb they seek, I am assured.”  Those were the final words in the debate.

The next morning, the huge host of Pilgrims set out, despite the knowledge that a determined enemy was lurking not far away.  Their journey would be a slow one; they were all on foot, and many were walking only half as fast as a normal person, their sores and weariness keeping their pace slow.  Eversmoore did send a unit of Sun Blades to assist the White Cloaks in this pilgrimage.  By the end of the first day, just as dusk was beginning to fall, things started to move in the shadows of the depths of Cormanthor, on either side of the road. With a column hundreds of yards long, and so few defenders, the escort was unsure how it could defend all of the pilgrims.

“We cannot defend them” were the words of Alystair Dretch.  “Their would-be-attackers will have to defend themselves.”  With that, he was off.  Before the rest of the White Cloaks could join him, he was a shimmering light vanishing into the depths of the forest.  All that night the screaming and death-cries of his victims rang throughout the forest all around the pilgrims.  There were very few attacks from the maligned host that wished to obliterate the pilgrims that it was denied days before, outside the gates of the Farm.  However, with the Avenger relentlessly assaulting the attackers from their own rear, they were unable to muster a proper assault, and were repelled by the rest of the escort easily.

As the sun came over the forest in the morning, after along and sleepless night for all, Alystair returned from the woods, covered from head to toe in the blood and gore of the countless enemies he had murdered in the night.  Many of the Sun Blades were mortified at the macabre spectre emerging from the black woods, and even his own teammates seemed shocked and surprised to see so grisly a site.  The pilgrims however, all praised his return and offered prayers of thanks to Amaunator for their deliverance, and for the deliverance of the Avenger from his perilous night of continuous battle.  Tuugan Swift was the first to approach the Avenger, and despite the blood-soaked appearance of his friend, he met him with a warm embrace, and the Chauntean was heard to thank Amaunator under his breath.

There was one more night to pass, however.  The pilgrims would never make the gates of Dagger Falls after their first night on the road, going at such a slow pace.  All that second day, they were harried by forms in the woods that would approach to within bow-shot range, and make skirmishing assaults.  These assaults were always met by the retaliation of the Avenger, now with his ranger-friend right behind him.  None could hide from the Avenger once they had attacked the pilgrims he had sworn to protect.  After four or five sorties into the woods, chasing down orcs, deranged cultists and some shadow-monsters, Alystair and Tuugan had made an impression on the harrying force.  The next few attacks were set up as traps for the pair, but they repeatedly fought their way out of every ambush; the keen eyes and peerless woodcraft of Tuugan kept the pair from falling prey to the ambushers, and each time they were able to circumvent the trap and destroy their ambushers completely.

The second night on the Tethyamar Trail was a repeat performance of the first; while the pilgrims gathered in a tight camp, watched over by a group of Sun Blades and White Cloaks, Alystair went hunting in the woods.  Again, Tuugan accompanied him, and the screams of their victims grew more and more distant as the night wore on; they had routed the enemy who had lost too many men, attacking the column of pilgrims in vain, and the gates of Dagger Falls were only a part-day away at this point.  The assault upon the pilgrims was lifted.

The following morning, as Alystair and Tuugan returned, a company of Sun Dragoons linked up with the pilgrims, and lead them safely to the gates of Dagger Falls, where the protracted business of entry into the town began.  Alystair and many other men, Sun Blades and White Cloaks alike, protested the dauntless bureaucratic machinations at the gates, but Blaze Whitegate, the Bishop of Dagger Falls, personally rode out to the gates to thank the escort, and Alystair especially for what they had done.

The Bishop made this entreaty; “You have done more than anyone could expect to bring these faithful pilgrims to our doors.  I thank you.  We all thank you.  But now, let others do their part.  Do not let your good works be undone in a moment of passion.  Let neither pride nor pity govern you.  All things must be done in their own time, and no rule or regulation can be ignore under any circumstance.  This is His will.  This is how we survive.”

The escorting parties were granted a relatively hasty entry to the town, but Alystair remained at the rear of the column of pilgrims, stating he would take his processing after the last pilgrim.  The Bishop himself joined the  ranks of the clerical staff at the gates, and had every spare man summoned to expedite the process.  In the end, a group of four men posing as pilgrims were lead away to the Tower, and from across the crowd of people, tents and desks, Alystair and the Bishop were seen to lock eyes, and both men nodded approvingly at each other, then went about their business.
