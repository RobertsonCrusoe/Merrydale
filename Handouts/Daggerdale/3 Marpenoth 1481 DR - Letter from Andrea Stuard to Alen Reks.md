DDC – 1481 - 1: A Letter from Andrea Stuard to Alen Reks 
3 Marpenoth 1481 
Notes from GFD: 
Graciously provided by Andrea herself on my one-day visit to Dwimmerholme. I am happy to report that the sun shines bright there, and that the White Cloaks were altogether successful in executing this mission. This is important to observe, as Dwimmerholme was also the first victim in the Dark Garden campaign, the year before. Very poor luck 

The letter below really is the start of the White Cloak involvement with the Sons of Bel, and this new plot hatched by Prince Melegaunt and his Curator, Loki Mouvranesh. 
-GFD 
Mr. Reks; 
I am writing to inform you of a terrible thing that has happened here, at Dwimmerholme. I am sure that you have heard about it from the clerks at the Yellow Abbey, but I'll tell you what I know, and what I saw. 

Yesterday, when many of the families were in the fields harvesting the last crops of the season, pumpkins and some winter corn and such, there was a tremendous ruckus on the road, just to the south of the village. There have been rumours of abductions on the roads for a few weeks now (more on that below), and so Lt. Oakdale lead two squads of Sun Blades out to see what the trouble was. 

It turns out this was only a diversion; as the soldiers drew away from town, some of the families in the northern fields were attacked. As I write this letter this morning, the village is still reeling from the terrible outcome of that attack; three entire families were abducted by gods-only-know what. One man, Macalister Dale was killed. I personally helped the Cultivar dress his body; he suffered only one blow to the head from something blunt; there was but a terrible lump there, and no other wounds on his body. 

By the time news of these missing farmers reached the village, it was too late; the villains that took them were long gone, and though Oakdale and many of his men tracked them all through the night, they were unable to catch up. One of his sergeants convinced him that charging into the woods in the middle of the night was the worst of plans, and so they returned. As I write this, Oakdale is pacing in the courtyard outside my door, and wringing his hands; he has been up all night. 

This is the second abduction that has happened in our area; A small caravan company was here last month, and they were attacked on the road. Oakdale's men only found the site of the attack; overturned wagons and such. No bodies. And they didn't take everything. Oakdale has told me that he thinks the men were taken into the forest. There is no trace of them now. There were 10 men in that 

caravan. The owner is, wasa man named Belligereath; he had three men with him that scared the hell out of me every time they came into the 'Hart. I find it hard to believe those three could be abducted so easily. 
The following people of our farm are missing; 
Dale Family; Aelwyn (Macalister's wife, 30), Aella (Macalister's younger sister, 27), Lorella (daughter, 14), Aella (daughter 13), Boregar (son, 11), Suserah (daughter, 6), Jakobus (nephew, Aella's bastard, 7). Macalister (44) is of course, dead of a blow to the head. 

Weaver Family; Dannus (father, 35), Maribella (wife, 25), Dannel (son, 10), Stephiani (daughter, 8), Maxus (son, 8). 

Fletcher Family; William 'Dirty Bill' (father, 40, widower), Michael (son, 19), Darred (son, 17), William, (son, 10). 

The Fletcher boys and their father were also bruisers. That something could haul off so many so quickly is a troubling though. 

We are in desperate need of aid. I only write you because I think Magistrate Renfrew is too honourable and capable a man to ask for help. Also, Mr. Dretch was here only a few days ago, and I know that he was heading to the Shadow Dale to investigate something. He was muttering about Zhents and Cormyreans, and said that there were answers in Shadow Dale. 
Please send help if you can. I'll try to convince the Mayor to reward you. 
Yours in Trust, Andrea Stuard. 
PS; I have been watching Mr. Dunland like Mr. Dretch always asks. Dunland has not changed his habits since Mr. Dretch 'cured' him of heresy 2 years ago. I think we can rule him out of the problem.
