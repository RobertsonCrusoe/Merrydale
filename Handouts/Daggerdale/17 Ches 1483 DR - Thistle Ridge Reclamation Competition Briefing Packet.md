Preamble From Magistrate Blaze Whitegate 
I have been to Thistle Ridge many times in my capacity as a young Magistrate. This settlement is strategically important; it commands both the Tesh River and the Moonsea Road from an unassailable limestone perch within bowshot of both, and it is only about 10 miles as the crow flies from Dagger Falls; the perfect distance to send near-instant communications and for next-day reinforcement if needed.  

However, the origin of the settlement has nothing to do with trade, though this did attract some later migrants, which I'll discuss shortly. The original settlement dates to before the 13th century, though none now living know when exactly. The Thistle Family, who, as I understand it, take their name from the Ridge and not the other way 'round, have lived here for more than a century. They are private people who both give and expect respect at arm's length. The family have been gardeners (I don't think I'd call them 'farmers') and expert herbalists for as long as anyone can remember, and their skill at healing obscure ailments (both real and imagined) is legendary, bordering on heretical. They are a peaceful people, however, and though rarely seen in the Falls, they get into no quarrels when they are. 

The Thistles follow the Ancient Faith, though they do so respectfully with regard to their Chauntean and Amaunitori countrymen, and they do not proselytize. Theirs is a Hereditary Faith, and we shall leave it at that for now. It should be noted that the Chaunteans insist that the settlement is upon 'sacred' ground, and the evidence of that can be seen in the productivity of the small gardens there, the fruit from their trees, the quality of their medicines, and indeed, their numerous healthy children. 

In 1408, migrants from Sembia were settled in Thistle Ridge. These migrants are mostly from two Houses of Sembia and their attendants, namely the Alavandor and Clarandal Families. The former were successful merchants in their homeland, while the latter were of a highly disciplined military order. The previous mayor was from the Alavandor Family, namely Andarl Alavandor. After an unexpected death hunting, his younger sister, Senthia (married to a Clarandal), took over mayorship. She is slated to fill the role until Sheildmeet. 

The Sembians started a completely new era at Thistle Ridge. They brought industry and commerce, as well as a new degree of security and much-needed improvements to agriculture and the overall subjugation of the landscape. They have established trading relationships with neighbours, as well as a reputation for some of the finest products in the dales (the Dale Burgundy Apple, Goldwin Wines, and the finest medicines, just to name a few).  

They also attracted the strange Alavaxi people, Chessentans, or so I'm told. The Alavaxi make the curved blades that some of our military orders now favour, as well as other fine steel products. Obvious foreigners, they have brought some small discord to the Dale, though not in Thistle Ridge Farm, where they have been welcomed by the immigrants before them (the Sembians).  

Thistle Ridge is an experiment in religious plurality and mixed cultural traditions that we are sure to keep an eye on in the future

Locations and People of Interest 
Locations 
Caravaner's Court—a large clearing at the edge of the River Tesh, straddling the Moonsea Road. This is where travellers can first request entry to the town or, more often, will set up tents in the open market to do business with the locals, who will come down from the ridge-top settlement. 'The Court' (as the locals call it, somewhat imprudently) is a busy pace, with permanent barns and paddocks for travellers and a few notable permanent businesses, namely; 

Whistle's House—a public house run by the halfling of the same name. Whistle also owns the large double barn next to the public house, which he rents out for the stabling of mounts and a dry place to sleep for travellers. 

The Broken Spoke—a small wagonwright capable of fixing wheels, barrels, and crates, and providing shoeing for mounts.  

Milly's Mill—a small but very busy flower mill on the river. Milly (the halfling wife of Whistle) grinds most of the flour for the settlement, and her establishment provides much of the traffic that benefits traders set up in the Court. 

The Fish Market—not really named this, but rather just called that, this is a small collection of tents and huts where the wives of a few local fishermen sell the day's catch. Usually, a good supply of fresh pygmy afanc, brownfin, bass, sunfish, trout and eels to be found. 

All Faiths Shrine—found at the edge of Caravaner's Court. This small shrine is built of granite that is not local to the region and has been there since the earliest known records. What faith it was originally built for is unclear, and there are no permanent idols or other embellishments that suggest one god or another. Locals steer clear of it, but some travellers may use it to venerate their odd foreign gods. 

Old Thistle Ridge Farm (Eastern Bailey)—the smaller of two baileys in the fortified portion of the farm, this area is where the Thistle and other older families live. The houses are a little smaller and lower than the newer, Sembian-influenced bailey beside it. This part of the farm is choked by lush gardens of every variety and is quite delightful to walk through. 

Oak and Shield (Inn - Old Thistle Ridge Farm)—this is the oldest public establishment in the area. Run by the Sweetberry family, who have been here for generations, this is a quiet and respectable inn that has clean beds, wonderful food, and excellent stabling for mounts and other animals. The proprietress is Janus Sweetberry, widow and mother of a dozen (I think, or more) very healthy children who run the place with courtesy and attention to detail. 

Shrine to Silvanus (Old Thistle Ridge Farm)—One can't miss the giant Cormanthor Oak that grows outside this small wooden shrine. The oak itself is the real centrepiece for the worship of Silvanus here, with the small building serving to hold some accoutrements specific to their faith, such as it is. 

The Sembian Bailey (Western Bailey)—This is the more substantial of the two baileys, holding most of the commerce and people of the settlement. Although a bit more overgrown than other settlements, the roads are mostly cobbled with well-fitted limestone. There are vibrant gardens beside most buildings, and berries seem veritably to spring from the pavers themselves when not properly cleared. 

Beneath the Sembian Bailey is a complicated network of sewers and tunnels that deliver fresh water to many buildings and take away waste from even more. A map of this network is known to exist in the Historic Wing of the Yellow Abbey, but I have not seen it. 

Town Hall—A Class IV fortified building inside the Bailey itself, this is more a redoubt than a municipal building. While it does hold the offices of the Mayor, her clerks and other dignitaries, it was principally designed as a fortress and would be difficult to assault and easy to defend, with its thick straight walls and cantilevered battlements, atop which command of the streets below is possible.  

The Bastion—A Class IV fortified building that oversees the main entrance to the town. This is a 20' limestone structure that was built in the 1420s and is in the Sembian style of the engineer that the Sembians brought with them a decade before. 

The Garrison—A sprawling network of fortified annexes, this building could be considered a Class III fortified structure, but the Sembian disorganization of the place makes it slightly less than that. However, the main hall is outfitted with all the necessaries for training and lodging troops, and the Bright Spears stationed there seem perfectly comfortable, which is to say, neither too comfortable nor too uncomfortable. 

Shrine to Amaunator—While one of the finest such shrines in our dale, it is certainly the most underutilized, as the size of the Flock here is small relative to similar settlements. The sole clerk here is Daphne Dale, a sensible local unmarried girl who holds an office in the Town Hall next door and can more usually be found there. While the Flock is small here, the Alavandor and Clarandal families undertook the expense of erecting this formidable Shrine themselves, no doubt in appreciation for the land deeded them here. The catacombs of the Shrine are nearly empty and, I'm told, connected to the larger network of tunnels beneath the Sembian Bailey. 

The Phylactery—This is a small school and apothecary under one roof. Started by Aisling Alavandor in the early days of their arrival here, the medicines and students of medicine-making they produce are renowned about the dale and further abroad. Sisters from Charnel Priory often make the journey here for rare medicines and herbs, and the apothecaries here are known to have the most reliable medicines for fertility. As mentioned elsewhere, the quality of the products here and their results have bordered on heresy, but formal inquiries have turned up nothing occult or unsavoury. 

Merchants' Hall—The second largest building in the settlement, the first floor of this 3-story structure is divided into many street-facing shops that can be rented by any merchant licensed by the town. The second floor is made up of apartments for the merchants, and the third is reserved for the Merchants' Guild of the farm, which is a strong political force here. The Hall has a large central stone tower that is a defensible structure in its own right, with the top floor serving as a counsel chambre of sorts for the Merchants Guild. 

It is worth noting that the Merchants' Guild is headed by the Alavandor family and has been instrumental in the economic success of the settlement. 

Artisans' Hall—Another large Sembian building with a cosmopolitan purpose, the Artisans' Guild of the farm owns and operates this facility. The main floor is a workshop used in the training of stone mason's apprentices, but the 2nd floor sees much more traffic. This floor is a large public house, complete with kitchens, a taproom, a stage (with live music a few nights every tenday), and a busy bar. It must be admitted that the best wine in the Dale is vinted at Thistle Ridge, no doubt due to the productive nature of the soil here and the presence of a Sembian family of Vintners that emigrated here in the '20s, the Goldwins. The matriarch of the family, Maura Goldwin, runs the bar and happily sells her wines and other local beverages to a thirsty patronage. The third floor of this large building is for apartments for young unattached masons, and a few are available for rent from the Festhall. 

Brewery, Distillery, Vintner—With no formal name for this establishment, this is the name on Master Cartographer Vost's fine map of the area. This is a collection of businesses, really, with the most prominent being the Goldwins' vintner, which lends the fragrant aroma to the town square that many visitors enjoy. It is well known that the catacombs beneath this establishment have been extensively worked to square them up and provide storage space for hundreds of barrels and casks of product and thousands of bottles. 

A brewer, Darla Dale, buys hops and other grains from local farmers to make a variety of delicious beers and ales, which she both sells locally and exports to the Falls. Her best customer is her close neighbour, the Artisans' Hall, who buys most of her product for sale in the Festhall. 

A distillery here is owned by three brothers, the Kaskills, who produce a variety of creative spirits from their productive gardens nearby. Their most popular distillation is a corn spirit called Thistle Kiss, for which a dozen lewd pronunciations and variations of the name are equally popular. 

The Alavandor Academy—One of the earliest public works taken on by the Alavandors after their settlement here, this establishment could safely be labelled a 'Grammar School,' in the Sembian sense of the word, or so I'm told. It is the largest building in town, has 3 floors and is also in the Sembian style of architecture (busy, convoluted, elaborate), but is a pleasing structure nonetheless, complete with a fine but small courtyard that the instructors make good use of. The Academy is equipped with a small but impressive observatory for the instruction of astronomy, and is the envy of most astronomers in the region. 

The first and second floors of this large building are full of lecture halls, offices, a respectable library, and a well-appointed refectory. The third-floor acts as a dormitory for a few dozen children, some from the nearby outlying region, others from nearby farms (many families at Gribbons Farm choose to send their children here for instruction, rather than the Yellow Abbey, which bears discussion). It also has apartments for the Dean of the Academy, Franko Petrark and his family, as well as the small staff that operate the Academy. 

At the present, almost all children of the farm aged 5-12 spend much of the year attending classes here and are given a traditional education in Grammar. Promising students might be invited to stay on until they are aged 15, and learn Logic, Rhetoric and Astronomy from the Dean himself. To date, all of the teachings of the Academy are in line with the general Moral Guidelines of the Yellow Abbey. 

Alavaxi's (Blacksmith)—Owned by the patriarch of the family, Pharaxes, this establishment represents a consolidation of the various men and women who ply this most noble trade, under something that is akin to a guild structure one might find in Charity or larger cities. Alavaxi himself, although a newcomer (of dubious origin, it is worth mentioning, but seemingly harmless) is the head of this guild, and has operated it with efficiency and focus. With the abundance of Iron and Copper from the two mines outside the farm, there are plenty of raw materials to support the operation, and they are a net-exporter of finished goods in these materials. 

Pharaxes himself is a Master Blacksmith and has forged several magical blades in the curved style of his home country. Pharaxes and his wife, Numestra, as well as a few others that arrived with them in the late 'teens all suffer from a condition of the skin that the best physicians and priests assure me is not contagious. Their children all seem free of the condition. 

Sulphonti's (Herbalist)—Now operated by the daughters of the original Family Medic that accompanied the Alavandor's to the Dales and started this business, Sulphonti's is a peerless herbalist supply, the envy of many colleagues throughout the Dale. The women and girls who operate this business have small but remarkably productive gardens around the property, and under a small glass enclosure that produces even the most delicate herbs in the harshest of winters. 

Sulphonti's stocks all of the herbs and spices expected for the region, but additionally stocks cubeb, nutmeg, saffron and turmeric, which they grow in their glass enclosure. It is well known that the Sisters of Charnel Priory spend a lot of time with the Sulphonti women when they come to town, and leave with many supplies. 

Without the Walls—Hard by the walls of the farm are 60 or more acres under heavy cultivation, being about ¼ each of wheat, barley, fallow and vegetables. The nearby acreage surrounds the walls, neatly cleared out to 800' or so in each of three directions, with the limestone cliff providing the southern boundary of this part of the farm. These fields are exceptionally productive, and the animals that grow upon them fat and healthy. 

Hinterland—The well-trained militia of Thistle Ridge has done an exemplary job of pacifying the 16 square miles (or so) around the walled town. Three additional farms with small fortified homesteads can be found less than 2 miles away, as well as a hunting lodge, a copper mine, a pair of towers that overlook the road and the river, and an iron mine a little further to the north. 

Alavandor's Farm—This is the country home of the Alavandor family, and the main vineyard of the Goldwin family. Although I've never been to it, I am told that it is a well-fortified small holding with a stone, 2-storey castle, and many outbuildings. During the summer, most of the Alavandor family can be found on this estate. A good road leads from this farm to the main town, being perhaps 1 1/3 miles away (northeast of the town). 

Clarandal's Farm—The road to Alavandor's continues to the northeast another 1 1/3 miles, where it finds Clarandal's farm; another Sembian-style summer estate. I am told that this smallholding is a single castle, small but sturdy, that stands on a good hill commanding the 4 square miles around it. While referred to as a 'farm', this is really a forward observation post for the settlement and a specialized training ground for the militia. Here, forest warcraft is taught to the militia, and a small but capable platoon of Foresters is stationed that patrols the northern extent of the hinterland. 

Alavandor Lodge—Also known as the Autumn Estate, this is another small castle with mostly seasonal occupation. From mid-Eleint to mid-Uktar, most well-to-do men can arrange hunts out of the lodge, which are hosted by the Alavandor patriarch himself. Expeditions across the Solmen R. and into the Fog Hills usually produce a bounty of game, including white tailed deer, great harts, black and brown bears, and myriad fowl. The lodge is also a staging point for the Foresters when needed. 

The Apple Glade—a mile and a half north of the town is the source of Thistle Ridge's most voluminous export, apples. Though more than a dozen species are cultivated here, the Dale Burgundy is the most popular eating apple in the region, owing to its sweet and crisp flesh, which preserves better than any other known variety. These can be found in the provision bags of soldiers in every corner of the dale and further abroad. 

The Glade itself is owned in common by the farm, and just about anyone who wants to cultivate a row is permitted to do so. The Thistle family maintains more than a thousand trees, and these produce the bulk of their exports and profits each year.  

There are no permanent structures here besides a couple of large barns for holding the animals that assist in transport of these goods, and their feed. 

The Copper Hole—Copper Mine & Keep—Located 2 ½ miles north of the town, and accessed by roads that come from Clarandal's Farm and the Alavandor Lodge, this is a busy if small mine. I haven't visited it, but I am told it is a neatly ordered affair, exploiting some large veins of copper that have produced more than enough to keep the smiths busy, and exports of ingots healthy. The mine itself is accessed by a small keep that has been built atop it. Those that mine it are typically farmers that mine throughout the winter; apparently the mine is warm and not unpleasant during the coldest months. The limestone hill that contains the mine is riddled with caves, and it is widely rumoured that these caves connect with a broader network that extends beneath the entire region, and indeed, possibly to the Underdark. While no major threats have ever been encountered down here as far as I know, Knights of the Shield do make it a habit to poke around there every other year or so. 

The Iron Hole—Iron Mine & Keep—This is the furthest regularly-used resource in the hinterland. About 6 ½ miles by trail from the town, this is another portion of the limestone cave network that permeates the area. A rich vein of iron has been supplying the retorts and smiths of the area for more than 20 years, since the arrival of the Alavaxi's and their people. This mine is a less-secure setting given its distance from the rest of the settlement, and the depth of the mine itself. The Alalvaxi's lead mining expeditions a few times each year that last for a few weeks; just enough time to get the materials needed to keep the journeymen busy. During this time, there is a heavy military presence here. Skirmishes with deep-things have happened a number of times over the years, and this remains a Location of Interest to the Church and the Military. 
Families and People 
Alavandor Family—Emigrating from Sembia in 1407, they were settled on Thistle Ridge in 1408, along with the Clarandal Family, that arrived with them. In Sembia, the Alavandors were accomplished merchants. The family acumen for the mercantile life is plainly visible in the fast growth of a thriving market and several trade agreements with their neighbours. The Matriarch of the Family is Susserah, widow of Andarl. Susserah runs the Merchant's guild, and by proxy, much of the decision-making in the settlement. This family owns several businesses, a fine home in town, and a sturdy castle estate not far from town. Additionally, they own and operate the Alavandor Academy, which provides free education for local children, and subsidized education for children from out of town. 

The Clarandal Family—Also emigrating from Sembia and settled in Thistle Ridge in 1408, the Clarandal Family can be thought of as the military arm of the Sembian Families that settled here. Bringing with them a right martial tradition and discipline, the sons of this family have distinguished themselves in the pacification of the hinterland about the Farm, as well as professional soldiers and officers of the Sunblades. The current mayor is Senthia Clarandal, herself originally an Alavandor. Additionally, the brothers Claudius and Tergus are now full-time inhabitants of the Farm after long careers as Sunblades, mostly on the Netherese Front. 

The Alavaxi—It's unclear to me if this is their surname, the name of the town they are from, or both, but this is a family of notable origin. Arriving in 1416 from distant Chessenta, this family (or group of families?) became the immediate source of an Investigation by my father and his senior Magistrates. Some of the elders of the family had a skin infection contracted on their long journey from Chessenta, and being so far from home (I have never met another Chessentan, and have only heard of them in books), the investigation was severe. In the end, most of the Alavaxi were permitted stay in Thistle Ridge (where they first arrived in Dagger Dale), but a couple of the eldest were sent to stay under guard in the Chapel Hospital at Castle Radiance, where they dwell today. The Alavaxi are accomplished artisans, especially of worked metals, and their bladesmithery seems to be without peer in the Dale. 

The Thistles—As I mentioned in the preamble, the Thistles have been here for hundreds of years, and are peaceful and insular followers of Silvanus. They have never attempted to cement relationships with other nearby Dalesmen, and their faith might have stood in the way of proper diplomatic relations in any event. However, Nadia Thistle is respected by all who know her well enough as a thoroughly peaceful woman, unmatched in her generosity for strangers despite her adherence to the Ancient Faith. Her many daughters and grandchildren can be found bounding through their half of the settlement, usually barefoot and happy. They operate a few small businesses, but are mostly a self-sufficient people that buy and sell little, and cause no trouble. 

The Sweetberry's—Also natives of Thistle Ridge, and originally followers of Silvanus, the Sweetberry family has embraced their new Sembian neighbours and the changes that have come to Thistle Ridge; the Matriarch of the Family, Janus, operates the Oak and Sheild, and seems to be making a very good living doing so for her and her family. She is a famously fertile woman of countless offspring (with more than one husband), who attend on visitors to Thistle Ridge with warmth and enthusiasm. They are a lovely family. 

The Hillborns—Transplants from Charity Hills Farm, but originally from Hillsfar, this is another family that came to Daggerdale pursuing the prosperity that it enjoyed in the closing years of the Sundering and those chaotic times. While some Hillborns remain in Charity, the majority of that family moved to Thistle Ridge at the request of then-mayor Alvund Alavandor, who was looking for hard working men to help build walls, workshops, and other works needed to grow the settlement. Already doing business with many people in Charity, the Elder Alavandor liked the people of this settlement, and offered them fine properties and trade agreements to help the Sembian settlement grow. The Patriarch of this Family, Harrington Hillborn, is 'Free of the Guild of Joiners' in Charity (a caste-based title of some importance to those people), and is presumable not in exile from Charity. The family operates several of the businesses in Thistle Ridge, as well as being the foremen for the mining operations at the Iron Hole and the Copper Hole. 

The Wheatbushels—One of two Halfling families at the Farm, the Wheatbushels are somewhat new to the area, having emigrated from Ashabenford about 20 years ago. The Matriarch of the family, Milly, runs the mill on the river, and is probably the friendliest and most popular citizen. She deals with many of the wives and handmaids of the settlement on a daily basis, as she grinds their wheat to flour, making pleasant conversation the while. Her two daughters work here alongside her. Her husband, Whistle, runs the Public House with his name nearby, where his three sons help him out. 

My Reconnaissance of the Thistle Ridge Ruin 
By Malveau Greenleaf, Independent Forester 
Alturiak 11, 1483 DR. 
I submit this report to Ambassador Whitmore and would like to thank him and his Office for their consideration for my intelligence. I am told that others outside of his Office are considering a venture to reclaim the settlement known formerly as Thistle Ridge Farm, which herein I will refer to as the Ruin. First, I will give a bit of context in advance of my findings to explain my credentials. 
My Background 
I, Malveau Greenleaf, am of partially-elven descent, my father being a Cormanthor Wood Elf born in the 12th century, my mother, Alexi Greenleaf (I'm not sure this was her true surname), originally a stable maid at Anathar's Dell, in the days before the great battles there in 1372. I have no memory of my father, as he left with his People in the late 1370's in advance of the coming Spellplague. At the time, I was just a child, and my mother as I understand it was not offered the chance to accompany my father. As such, we were left to our own means, and it wasn't long before the harsh wilderness of Daggerdale violently claimed my mother's life and made me a captive of foul orcs. I spent the remainder of my first score of years as the lowliest sort of slave to these vile creatures, being rescued from them by Ardelusk Whitegate and a ride of his Knights in 1398. 

Ardelusk made the most of my experience; having lived among the mountain orcs for more than a decade, I knew more about their language, culture, religion, and other facets of life than anyone else. I was cloistered at the Cathedral for more than a year, where I was brought to full health while reciting all I knew to a team of scribes that have turned my account of the mountain orcs into a volume you can find reprinted and dog-eared on many shelves in the Dale. 

By 1400, I started to meet the brave men and women that came and went from the Castle and became interested in the places they had come from, and the battles they were waging and, usually, winning, against the Netherese. As a small-framed woman, I was taught the shortbow, which I took to quickly. Indeed, I served in the First Company Sun Blades when it was originally commissioned in 1403, and created the archery training curriculum still used to prepare our brave women of the Sun Blades for service today. 

After spending more than ten years at the Castle and ranging the Plains of Netheril, and many excursions into the Wastes, my focus began to wither. I was in love with a Man, for starters. He was a mercenary serving at the Castle, and after a cruel wound to his chest, was forced into retirement. I watched him grow weaker as I hardly aged at all, and it was more than I could bear. I selfishly left the Dale, spending the next several decades ranging the mountains and forests of Faerun, sometimes working with other Foresters, Rangers and Druids, usually in the service of worthy people in need of aid. 

On the hundredth anniversary of my mother's death, I returned Home to stay. No longer young even by half elven standards, and finding it harder to keep up to the demands of the life I had made for myself, I came once again to Anathar's Dell, to find it completely encompassed by Cormanthor. If not for my adherence to the Old Faith and the skills in the forest that I naturally possess and the Blessings I receive, I should not have found my mother's grave. 

I then visited the Cenotaph at Liberty Hill, a settlement I no longer recognized, it now having great walls and a thousand more people than I remember. Unfortunately, many of my old friends there had been dead for decades, and news of the manner of their passing was a pain for me. But I made new friends of their descendants and am happy to say that I am friends still with Lumina Whitegate and Troia Truestar, and consider them my closest confidants. 

I humbly submit that I was a part of the defense of Liberty Hill in 1480, and hope that my involvement was to the benefit of the few that survived that day. 

My time at Liberty Hill with Lumina and Troia as new friends was a mixed blessing. I was heartened that heroes were being forged in the Dale outside of the stamp of Battlemaster and others like him, but I learned that many changes had come to the Dale, and many of the places I loved were now ruins, though others thrived. First among these was Thistle Ridge. 

I knew Zarra Thistle when she was a girl. Zarra and her mother (who's name I forget) would come to Anathar's Dell when I too was a small girl. Her mother had business, and Zarra and I would play together while our mothers worked. Even as a young girl, Zarra was already entrenched in the Old Faith, and I found her fascinating and impressive. I hoped to meet her descendants to talk about her, but found out that her own daughter, Nadia Thistle, who had grown to be an impressive matriarch of Thistle Ridge, was murdered in the Tragedy of 1441. 

I had been to Thistle Ridge twice as a younger woman, toward the end of my time in the Sun Blades at the Castle. By this time Zarra was a young matriarch who led a few families steeped in the Old Faith atop the limestone ridge beside the Tesh River. Zarra and I renewed our friendship quickly and deeply. I would liked to have stayed longer with her and her community; they were Good people and made me welcome. They were also skilled in their own defense in a fashion that does not exist today. They used Cormanthor as an ally, rather than treating it as an opponent, and were sheltered from the types of raiding and violence other farms needed walls to address. The last time I saw Thistle Ridge before my decades abroad was in late Uktar of 1407, just before I left. In fact, I was headed East to answer a call in Damara, where the Witch Queen had resurfaced (I rode off with Maturin and a Host, and watched him go over the walls at Heliogabalus several years later), and Thistle Ridge was to be the last place in the Dales I rested before a perilous journey. 

In 1407, Thistle Ridge was a community of perhaps fifty people from four families, all lead by Zarra. Zarra was now of middle age, a revered time of life for us of the Old Faith, and her daughter, Nadia was an accomplished priestess. The great oak Harngorn was at the centre of the settlement, and was ancient then. The people of Thistle Ridge were friendly with me but had few relations otherwise. They were already shunned by the Amaunatori, and the Chaunteans struggled to square their beliefs with the Old Faith and the forest they despised. It was quiet and peaceful that year, but that changed soon after I left. 
Rumours and Legends of Thistle Ridge 
Before I give a detailed report of my recent reconnaissance, I will take the opportunity to summarize some of the rumours and legends of the place. 

Settlement by the Sembians 
It is well known that two prominent families from Sembia and others besides came to the Dale in 1407 and were settled alongside the Thistles a year later. Any report from that time indicating that the Thistles welcomed them is erroneous. In a letter I got from Zarra while I was in Damara in 1413, she reported that the Sembians were difficult to live with, and had attracted many Chaunteans. The forest about them had been cleared, though Harngorn had been spared. They built walls, and the walls attracted goblins like 'dung draws flies' I recall her writing. Thiers was not an easy coexistence. 
The Chessentan Ague 
I have been to Chessenta and can tell you that the people there do not regularly suffer from the skin and constitutional ailment reported to have been suffered by their elders. What was actually wrong with them is something I would have asked my old Liberator's sons if they were alive.  
The Tragedy—10 Marpenoth 1441 DR 
Having spoken with my friend and colleague, Tuugan Swift, I can verify that the fate of Thistle Ridge was decided by the Grentzeldek hobgoblins, who were in league with numerous tribes of goblins from the other side of the River. They somehow managed to destroy the tower of the main building (something I have seen the ruin of with my own eyes), and the aviary with it, cutting off communication to their neighbours and allies. What followed was a lightning-fast assault, it must be imagined, in which almost everyone in the settlement was killed or taken prisoner by the Grentzeldek. 

It is hard to imagine how this could have been done with so little warning and in such a thorough manner. I've never met a survivor, and don't know anyone who has. I have Prayed for insight into the fates of my fellow Faithful, and was answered when I ventured into the Ruin recently, by way of seeing Harngorn riven and burnt. 
The Grentzeldek 
First, I should include in this report that the name 'Grentzeldek' is misunderstood by the few who even know the name of the hobgoblins living at the northern edge of Cormanthor. The Grentzeldek, Tuugan tells me, are a changed people. They are spit into multiple tribes now and can't be considered one people. The main settlement, called Grentzeldek, is no longer strictly allied with the others, and it has become insular since the late 1440's. I have heard from other sources that they are still a formidable military power, but they never venture south of the Fey Hills now, and as far as anyone knows, since the Tragedy. The current problems at the Ruin are not due to this people, but I don't doubt they could be drawn into a conflict if it grew.  

What they took from Thistle Ridge, and why they haven't returned are complete mysteries. 
The Ghosts of Thistle Ridge 
The Ruin is indeed haunted. For decades, people of the dale have avoided resettling the Ruin because of the presence of multiple ghosts. I am aware of a report in the Yellow Abbey written by a young Magistrate who visited there shortly after the tragedy that confirms this. 

Having crept through the goblin encampment, I can confidently report that the goblins themselves avoid three of the larger structures, and have crude fetishes around them to keep the spirits at bay. 
The Caves Beneath Thistle Ridge 
The entire Northern Escarpment (as I call it, though none now use the term) is a tunnel-ridden limestone formation that connects to the Underdark. An underground arm of the Solemn River passed beneath the settlement and emerged out the side of the cliff face when I was there in '07. At some point, the Sembians blocked up the opening to the river, and diverted it deeper underground. That a river could be diverted entirely indicates that the cave networks beneath Thistle Ridge are extensive. 
Goblin Occupation 
Tuugan tells me that goblins of various tribes have occupied the ruin from time to time over the last 40 years, but that there hasn't been continuous occupation until recently. The goblins are reticent to cross the Tesh, and in the few instances he knew of, outside forces had been the root cause of goblins operating there. The ruin sits empty most years, save the ghosts of the Tragedy that remain. 
Thistle Ridge Today 
I did a broad reconnaissance of Thistle Ridge from 6-9 Alturiak this year, at the request of the Ambassador from Charity. I was able to survey much of the Ruin itself, as well as nearby parts of the original hinterland and other nearby areas of interest.  
The Yellow Tooth Goblin Clan 
The primary residents of the Ruin today are the Yellow Tooth Goblin Clan. This is a new clan, as far as anyone can tell, and likely came out of the Dagger Hills just south of the Sorrow Bog. I confirm that they still operate south of the Tesh in small patrolling bands, and hunt and forage in the bog for delicacies that are delivered to their main force in the Ruin. 

I avoided taking any hostages for the purposes of information gathering. If I'm honest with myself and my contractor, I'm getting a bit too old, and perhaps soft, for that sort of intelligence gathering. Listening as closely as I could to some smaller camps, I learned that their Chief is a priestess of Maglubiyet named Charrah, and that most of the Yellow Tooth venerate her as an embodiment of their god. I understand her to be in the Ruin somewhere, though I never figured out exactly where she operates from. 

The Yellow Tooth are very good warriors, as well as expert beast-tamers. It was the latter which kept me at a respectable distance from them most of the time. They have command of many violent creatures they have bred apurpose, including a species of small but nasty worg, as well as a small version of the mountain lions used by the orcs. Additionally, I saw some even more exotic beats being used as mounts in the Ruin itself, including a giant wasp and a small bear. 

I estimate the clan to be composed of between 400-800 goblins, most of which are military-aged males and females. Half of these are in the Ruin itself, with perhaps 100 south of the Tesh, and a few hundred in the hinterland at various outposts, such as the old estates nearby. 
Bugbears 
I didn't see any bugbears, but they are present in the Ruin for certain. The Ruin has many large patches of Bugberries, which always indicate the presence of the nearby monsters that produce them. By nature cruel and cowardly, I expect there to be at least a small number of them, perhaps between 3-5.  

State of the Ruin 
Blessed Cormanthor has overtaken much of the Ruin, as could be expected. With over 40 years of growth, the fields hard by the walls are gone completely, and inside the walls forest and thickets choke the crumbling ruins. 

The walls themselves have succumbed to the assault of 1441 and the Forest since, and provide no real obstacle at this point. Many buildings have been completely reclaimed by the rapid growth of the area, though most of the largest structures seem to have survived. The major Sembian fortifications, including the tower in the middle of the Ruin, and a great gate-house which was the main entrance from the river or road, were clearly destroyed in the Tragedy, their large blocks being thrown great distances. 

Despite this, the Yellow Tooth are making a home of the Ruin, and many of the remaining buildings that don't contain spirits have been shorn up, in their fashion, and paths have been cleared between them. While the Ruin is much too large for a few hundred goblins to make it feel like a bustle, the place appears somewhat busy with the goblins making this into their home. 
Cause for Concern 
Despite the sporadic appearance of goblins and other monsters at the Ruin over the decades, the Yellow Tooth have been here for a year, and have already caused problems for visitors to the Dale. They were entrenched in the Ruin with overwatch on the river and road no later than Mirtul of last year, when we know they harassed the first caravans arriving in the Dale, and continued to sew mischief all summer long. The culmination of their mischief was the attack on the Drurginz Coster on 29 Marpenoth last year. This was a caravan, I have learned, of at least 20 well-armed guards which included a wizard. The entire caravan was captured by the Yellow Tooth, and no one knows if there were any survivors. I expect that there were not. I have seen the wreckage of the wagons and tents, which have been dragged from the road to conceal the crime, and quite deviously, to not alert suspicion in passers-by. 

The presence of a priestess of Maglubiyet as well as bugbears shows that this tribe is on a war footing and will likely attempt to draw other tribes to their scheme. It is well known in their religion that a proper warhost must include their monstrous trinity of goblins, hobgoblins, and bugbears. They are 2/3 of the way toward this collection, and we must be extra vigilant to ensure that the Grentzeldek to the north don't become involved. 

The fact that this tribe is confident enough to attempt to establish what they must see as a more or less permanent occupation of a human settlement so near to the Falls suggests that they merit more consideration than the usual chaotic bands of these little monsters. I take heart that members of the newly formed Governors' Assembly are concerned about this settlement, and are considering allocating considerable resources to reclaiming it.

The Old Market 
For over 200 years, this location, just inside the original walls, has served as the primary location for the exchange of day-to-day goods for the Dale. Today, it is a well-established market, with more than a dozen permanent and reputable businesses that surround a busy open-air market.  

The market itself is nestled into the Old Bailey, cozily tucked in beneath the rising battlements of the Barracks and the Tower to the North, as well as the thick outer wall of the bailey to the South. Between these thick walls are closely packed 2-story buildings with shop fronts on the ground level, and homes above. While active (see the Market Calendar below), the market is a busy place, with hundreds of people coming and going throughout the day. During the warmer months, vendors from most of the outlying farms and settlements set up tents in the market, as well as authorized merchants from further abroad.  

The market can be a pleasurable experience for the senses, with many sounds, sights and smells. While not as cosmopolitan as other larger markets, it is still a loud place with a hundred voices, hammers from nearby smithies, musicians plying their trade, and the clatter of wagons on the flagstones. Haverstone's  

Bakery provides the principal olfactory tone, but smells of many food vendors, as well as herbs, spices and perfumes add passing interest to the smell of the place. Being constantly cleaned by capable and friendly boys, it lacks some of the fouler smells you might find in larger centres. Horses and other animals are restricted from entering, and livestock trading is mostly conducted in the Eastern Bailey. Finally, with the only approved fish market found in the Eastgate Customs Court, you won't encounter that smell either.

In the centre of the market, a family of halflings known as the Butterbees have a stove, many tables, and a small but popular menu of stuffed pastries and sweet drinks. Around the market one can usually find 3-5 vendors selling skewers of meats, jugs of beer, pastries, cheeses, smoked meats, small lunch bags with a complete meal, and other street foods.

The Vernal Feast – A Shared Holiday of the Two Faiths of Daggerdale 
By Brother Vessuvian, Candlekeep Emissary attaché to The Yellow Abbeye of Daggerfalls, 20 Ches, 1483 DR. 

It is important to observe the religious bicameral nature of Daggerdale, especially as it plays out on the 19th of Ches every year in the town of Dagger Falls. This year, I was fortunate enough to be at liberty for the entirety of the festival which, it should be noted, starts in earnest on the evening of the 18th of Ches for the Chaunteans. This is a conspicuous date, as it is the second-most important Holy Day for both the Amaunatori and the Chaunteans (and, it should be acknowledged, a dozen other faiths of varying sizes and popularity). 

For the Amaunatori, the 19th of Ches is known as the Vernal Epiphany, for the Visions that many soon-to-be worshipers of Amaunator all shared on that date in 1373 DR, in advance of the Spellplague, and the rebirth of Amaunator a year and three months later. Ardelusk Whitegate himself was one such Visionary, though he kept that to himself until many years later, after having met people from all over the Realms that had the same or similar visions. For the Amaunatori, this is a day of solemn observance of the promise Amaunator made to return and rescue the People of Faerun from the ravages of the Spellplague. 

For the Chaunteans, this Equinox is known as The Budding, though there are many other lewd euphemisms to describe the nature of the day. Not only their second-most important Holy Day, it is also their second-most licentious festival as well, and among young mothers and girls it is affectionately known as 'The Courting'. In advance of their most important Holy Day, Greengrass, wherein young couples exchange vows and pleasures of the flesh, The Courting is a day (it's actually two nights and a day in Daggerdale, though I've heard it is shorter in other places) where young men announce their 'interest' in a young woman, the young woman then having the right to accept or refute the young man during Greengrass six tendays later. Of course, young people being young people, many don't wait that long, and many don't limit themselves to one potential lover. And so, it is a debaucherous affair that has Amaunatori mothers keeping their sons indoors if possible, and attempting to shut out the temptation of the games and songs and other inducements that ring out during this time. 

In Dagger Falls, it is a feast for all senses. The streets are adorned with the horticultural and floral decorations of the Chaunteans, and the beautiful candles, lanterns and other lights of the Amaunatori. Every oven is engaged with the making of breads and delicacies, providing a constant variety of mouth-watering smells as one travels the clean cobbles of town. This year was no different, and as I sit here and write this, I still have half a bag of sour-dough pastries at my side and am doing my best to not mix ink with crumbs. 

There is music all over the town. Chauntean revelers well into their cups sing all manner of bawdy tunes, while at the next corner, yellow-garbed Amaunatori sing pleasing, if sombre, songs from their hymn books. This morning, the Choir of the Cathedral began to sound at seven bells, and their voices and bells and harps were a pleasure for any visitor to the Cathedral Bailey all day long. 

The Chaunteans, to their credit, are the sincerely more inclusive party to this two-sided feast. They give a heart-felt "Amaunator Keep Thee" to their countrymen at every meeting, and enthusiastically offer to share the trappings of their feast, this being wine, ale, sweets, dancing and song. Most Amaunatori turn their noses up, occasionally offering an off hand "Chauntea is All", while trying their best not to be noticed noticing the dresses of the Chauntean girls and the bouncing cargo therein. Still, it is a peaceful affair, and one that is most entertaining to those of neither Flock. Again, to their credit, the Chaunteans are quick to invite anyone they find on the street into their revelry, while the only such inclusive offer made by the Amaunatori comes during their Solstice Feast in Nightal, where people of all Faiths merrily partake in the custom of gift-giving and sharing warm drinks by a warmer fire. 

This year, the weather is conducive to a good Feast. There is almost no snow in the town, as we've had some early rains, and the day is bright. An occasional cloud passes over, providing a brief suspension of large floating flakes of snow, which offer a satisfying juxtaposition of bare shoulders and the gentle, ethereal petals of frost, both drifting gracefully through the crisp spring air, creating a dream-like atmosphere.  

Reports of distant roads with this early warm weather have also arrived, as evidenced by the activity in the Eastern Bailey. Or at least, the activity there before the Feast. For the last tenday, wheelwrights, coopers, joiners and all other such tradesmen have been busy packing up the many caravans that are assembling in the Bailey, getting ready to depart for Tilverton Pass, Ashabenford and Hillsfar, and points beyond. Merchants and other businesses are getting ready to receive the season's first caravans from these places, which could arrive any day. But on the Vernal Feast day, there is very little hammering and packing, despite the best attempts by caravan masters to keep their men focused on assembling their trains. Indeed, it appears as though one of the favoured pastimes of the young Chauntean women is to prance by young men who should be working and lure them away as a matter of deliberate mischief. I should note that I experimented with this very thing, sitting conspicuously alone on a bench in the middle of the Old Market Square, with my nose in a book, and was not two minutes into the experiment before being whisked pleasantly away, the details of which I'll reserve as a gentleman ought to. 

Of note on this day in 1483 is the commencement of a great undertaking by the Governors' Assembly, so important that it drew away at seven bells various dignitaries and other mentionable people to an important meeting in Governors' Hall, which has been the talk of the town all day. Five companies have been selected to compete for the honour of receiving a charter to an abandoned farm along the Moonsea road, among them the famous White Cloaks, who are favoured to win. At this meeting were the heads of the two Faiths, who surely had other ecumenical duties to oversee, as well as the mayor, the Ambassador from Charity, and General Thunderstorm. The final figure, it is guessed, chose the date for this opening ceremony, as his duties normally see him at the Castle, but he is always in attendance for the Vernal Feast, which happens to be the last day of the year in the Sunblade contractual calendar. 

The Muster, as it is known in both military and civilian circles, is the tenday following the Vernal Feast, wherein the many companies of Sunblades are reconstituted for the season; soldiers are traded from company to company, and the Recruits (those of the 11th, 12th, 13th, and 14th companies training here in town) are rotated into fully commissioned platoons. With 288 young soldiers going out, and 288 coming in, there are over five hundred additional young people with their appetites to satisfy enjoying the Feast. It is the unenviable task of Captain Jovian Ash (Captain of the Recruits) and his four lieutenants to ensure every soldier finds his or her way back to barracks every night after the liberty they enjoy during the Feast. 

The business with the abandoned farm on the east road has prompted an ad hoc arena or theatre to be assembled apurpose for the competition, and as I sit here and write this in the Yellow Abbeye in the late morning of the 20th, with my head throbbing just a bit more than I should confess, I have just heard the roar of a crowd watching the opening match of the competition not far from my window, and I wish I could see what is driving the crowd to such cheering so early in the morning after so late a night. 

The Feast this year has drawn in a large host of men and women from Charity, the largest anyone can remember since the Zguumish Siege of '04. The Old Market Square is abuzz with these strangely-dressed and stranger-talking cousins of the Dale. One can on any day talk to the clerks at Charity House for an amusing dialectical experience. But today the air of the Old Market and other nearby places is full of their vocal nonsense, and more than a little jeering by their uncouth young men; Chauntea does not figure heavily into the fabric of Charity, and these people misinterpret some of the gestures of the merry makers as invitations to unkind attention. The Sunblades in the Market, who would rather be drinking from half-hidden flasks under their tabards have to do the real work of keeping inebriated factions of young people from having at each other. 

While blissfully wandering the streets of the Old Bailey (where most of the Chauntean festivities are centralized), I ran into Merri Mumfrey, the Town Fool. This man is well known to all in Dagger Falls, though few know much about him, where he came from, or even where he resides in the town. Merri is well known to have liberty to come and go as he pleases, and I have seen him walk unmolested into the Historic Wing itself, mumbling nonsense and pulling at his sleeves maniacally, while the guards did their best to look the other way. He often claims to have 'meetings' with some of the key figures of the history of the dale, and can often be seen laying on the floor of the Cathedral, with his ear pressed against the Burial Stones of the Departed, muttering to himself. 
On this 19th of Ches, 1483, he was singing the following song; 
There are spirits spirits spirits one-two-three; 
Small but great in wrath and sad betrayal, 
Their tragic story is the key to set them all and others free, 
And expose for good an ancient false portrayal.\n 
There are maidens maidens maidens one-two-three; 
Their warm breath in the frosty air a haze, 
They sing and dance and tempt the boys with all they have and little poise, 
To consummate in less than ten and fifty days.\n 
There are shadows shadows shadows one-two-three; 
They are strongest in the west and bend to thee 
These shadows cast a form that stirs a mighty storm 
That darkens river, ridge and road and sea.\n 
There are mothers mothers mothers one-two-three 
Whose husbands toil in the field and the bough. 
They have welcoming rear-ends and sons with lonely friends, 
Who come and go before the husbands' turn to plough.\n 
There are devils devils devils one-two-three; 
Men think that they have got them by the tail. 
We laugh while they are weeping but they dance while we are sleeping, 
And in the cold light of day they may prevail.\n 
There are nanas nanas nanas one-two-three; 
Whose granddaughters have husbands tall and strong. 
These men blow out the candles, more fun than broom handles, 
Before returning to their young wives' legs so lithe and long.\n 
There are monsters monsters monsters one-two-three; 
Who dreamt a man who doesn't know exactly how to seam, 
There's an eye that doesn't close while all the others doze, 
And I think we all might be but a monster's dream. 
I might be thought to be taking advantage of the opportunity to put a lewd song to paper, and this might be half true, but I should say that Brother Darred says that Merri is right about the weather half the time, and that our departed friend Phinneas Hoetmer took him seriously enough for us to give him consideration.  

So there we have it; the setting of the Vernal Feast of 1483. Merri's song sums up at least half of it, and the cleverest among you will have caught his references to the bicameral nature of the Feast which I suggest is important to understand. What the rest is about is beyond me, and we don't have Brother Phinneas to explain the sense of it, if indeed there is any. I doubt there is.

Appendix B: Daggerdale Flora and Fauna 
Dogs 
There are many different breeds of working and family dogs in the dale. Protection dogs are quite common, as are livestock and hunting breeds. Below are some of the breeds that are popular or unique in Daggerdale. A summary of their traits is found at the end of this section. 
Lowsted Wolfhound 
These large-bodied shaggy dogs are the jack-of-all-trades in Daggerdale. Having been bred for over 150 years by the Lowsted family for which Lostead Farm takes its name, these dogs can be found at every farm and settlement in the dale. They are typically 3' at the shoulder, lean with a powerful and springy build, and with a messy shaggy coat ranging from grey, through dark browns to black. 

The Lowsted is good at hunting, guarding property and livestock (though infamously difficult to train to be good with chickens and other fowl) and has the best endurance of any dog in the area, making it favoured by the Foresters and mounted Sunblades). 

The Lowsted has a specialized topline breed called the Lantern. These are always a light dirty grey in colour and have blue eyes (most other Lowsteds have brown eyes). Lanterns are famous for their ability to detect Shadowvar/Shadar-Kai and are highly sought after throughout the dale for this ability. 
Untheric Shepherd 
Originally from the south of Faerun, bred to thrive in blistering hot deserts and freezing cold nights, and with one job; the protection of animals, there is no more fierce livestock dog than the Untheric Shepherd. Bred for decades at Foothills farm, where they keep hundreds of sheep in many far-ranging flocks, this breed can be found on most farms that keep grazing animals without the gates. 

The dog is a very large bodied, light-coloured giant. While it is the best friend of the shepherd and his flock, this dog is notoriously suspicious of all other visitors, and does not thrive in populated environments. 

The kennel master at Foothills has bred these dogs to be merciless hunters of wolves. Their topline specimens are nearly the colour of the sheep they guard, and purposefully hide in the flock to fool wolves into approaching. They will attack wolves as they get close, often singling out the largest and slowest, which they reliably tear apart. 
Cormyrean Shepherd 
These medium-sized herding dogs originate in the cattle fields of Cormyr and have been a fixture in Daggerdale and the rest of Faerun for centuries. They are exceptionally capable at moving herds of animals, and at detecting threats. Being the friendliest of the working dogs in the region, they are also favoured as family pets. 

There are no specialized breeders of this dog, and there are countless variants throughout the dales. However, about 1 in 5 is blessed with exceptional intelligence, and the ability to fully understand language. They will understand the basic concept of any spoken language they hear, and have a complete understanding of the languages they are trained in. Capable of grasping any concept as well as an adolescent human, they are the easiest to train and are coveted as companions and even as extra hands around the farm, shop or home. The topline Cormyrean Shepherds with this language blessing are called Singers, as they are not only gifted at understanding language, but also communicating complicated messages to other dogs of nearly any species, which they do through their refined howling. 
Deepingdale Mastiff 
This is the largest of the specialized working dogs in the dales, this breed originating in the militia of Deepingdale in the 13th century. At over 200 lbs, they are the largest domesticated dogs in the region, and are the best fighters. 

Generally good as a guard dog and a family pet, their only real skill is attacking threats to their family or master. 

The kennel master at Crossed Blades Farm that breeds the Daggerdale variety trains the strongest, fiercest, and smartest of his pups to be Wardogs. These are intelligent and merciless beasts that learn to fight in packs and as part of military units, and it is not uncommon to see one or two of these with most Sunblade platoons or local militia units.  
Some can be bred to serve as mounts for halflings. 

Tesh Valley Wolf (Hybrid) 
People all over Faerun have bred wolves with domesticated dogs for millennia. In Tesh Valley Farm in Daggerdale, the Blackmanes have done this successfully for more than 30 years, creating a hybrid known as the Tesh Valley Wolf. They have a specialized topline known as the Feybane. These dogs look almost entirely like wolves but have a noticeable glow behind their eyes. They are known to hunt down fey creatures with great enthusiasm, indeed often disappearing for days while they track and kill them. They have an innate resistance to some of the magics that the more common fey deploy to defend themselves. 

The horticulturalists at the Yellow Abbey have made inquiries about these 'dogs', and annually request to purchase some for study, but the old kennel master has refused for years to subject them to whatever experiments the Yellow Abbey has planned. This has always caused more tension between the Blackmanes and Whitegates, if any more was needed.

A Call for Bravery, Endurance and Honour! 
Alturiak 30, 1483 DR. 

By Order of the Governors' Assembly, at its Seventh Sitting, Alturiak 21-29, 1483 DR, the establishment of the Thistle Ridge Reclamation and the creation of an Adjudicating Committee with authorization to oversee the Reclamation is hereby announced to all citizens of Daggerdale, and all her allies notified forthwith. 

It is the will of the Assembly that a Company of suitable capability and virtue be Chartered to Reclaim the settlement known as Thistle Ridge Farm, to secure its walls and other properties, to hold and defend those properties from enemies current and future, and to see to the growth and prosperity of that settlement. 

The Adjudicating Committee, which is composed of i) Bishop Whitegate, ii) High Cultivar Oakdale, iii) Mayor Cassimar, iv) General Thunderstorm and v) Ambassador Whitmore (Charity), will receive applications from organizations with a Charter Militant, and those seeking a Temporary Charter Militant for the purposes of this endeavour. They will select the five most suitable Companies and pit them in competition to test all the skills needed to carry out the Reclamation. 

The successful team will be given a Temporary Settlement Charter, which will give them authority for the following; 
 To clear all hostile creatures from Thistle Ridge Farm and its surroundings. 

 Establish the infrastructure and means to host a company of Sunblades in accordance with the Provisions for the Common Defense as laid out in the Daggerdale Concordat. 

 Establish the infrastructure and means to develop and maintain a suitable militia in accordance with the Provisions for the Common Defense as laid out in the Daggerdale Concordat. 

If the holders of the Temporary Settlement Charter accomplish these goals by the Feast of the Moon, 1485 DR, they will be granted a Permanent Settlement Charter, which will enshrine Thistle Ridge Farm as a member of the Daggerdale Concordat, complete with a seat on the Governors' Assembly. 

Application to the Competition can be submitted to Clerk Marlee Willow in Governors' Hall. 

The successful company will be expected to manage nearly all the affairs of the Reclamation and growth of the Farm under their own strength of arms, with their own coin and personnel. The Adjudicating Committee is authorized to lend the following support, which it will fully fund until the Feast of the Moon, 1485 DR; 

 One Platoon of Sunblades, to be formed apurpose, to be Recommissioned as 7th Sun Blade Bright Spears, 4th Platoon, under the command of Lt. Forge Farmountain and such junior officers as he sees fit. 

 One Notary to attend the Farm at such time as security allows and the disposition of assets between the Company and other Parties requires. 

 10 minutes of Standing at the Governors' Assembly for one Representative from the Company every Sitting. 

For clarity, the Temporary Settlement Charter shall also give the following Powers and Jurisdictions to the Company within the Farm and Surroundings, so long as they don't contravene the Daggerdale Concordat in any way: 

 To own the entirety of the Farm and Surroundings and proclaim or document such ownership in a ledger that can be shared with the Yellow Abbey. 

 To enter into trade agreements with neighbours and principled merchants that conduct regular business in the dale in accordance with the Daggerdale Concordat. 

 To establish, if needed or desired, institutions, laws and regulations for governance, education, trade standards, security, and all other matters normally administered by eldermen and other such bodies in Farms in the Dale.
