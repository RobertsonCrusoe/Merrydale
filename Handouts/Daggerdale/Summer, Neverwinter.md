Being the tale of the Road Mage, Zaltim, and his efforts in the North to remove the Zhent influence in the area. During the trading season of 1370, a deadly plague had come to the North - a plague that sought out Those Who Harp and their allies. This plague, however, came not through boils and fever, and brought no wracking coughs nor dementia nor pox. It brought only death, and came as a blade in the back, an ambush in the night. Its contagion was passed by neither vapours nor vermin, but by the whispers of men with dark hearts and the clink of Moonsea coin. Harpers were dying, disappearing, and worse, and the Road Mage, a Harper himself, had sworn by the memory of a fallen friend that he would discover the root of this pestilence, and burn it out. To this end, he took up with a group of strangers who came together by chance on Greengrass of 1371. These heroes were to take up in Zaltim’s struggle and continue his work when he could no longer do so. Here, then, is the story of the Road Mage and his allies.

==Attack on the Docks==
200px|right
Our heroes from this chapter of the story were all brought together instantly during an attempt on the life of Zaltim on the docks of Neverwinter. The Road Mage was attacked by a flock of darkenbeasts, and would have been killed if not for the intervention of our heroes. They were
*Beljuril, the gifted monk and servant of Azuth
*Wilk Shtub, dwarf with an axe and a bad temper
*Hob Farwood, halfling cartographer of Corm Orp
*Rufus Larrigan, the ‘Touched Priest’ of Selûne
*Alt Kor, inscrutable dwarven priest

These heroic men (well, one boy, one halfling, 2 dwarves and one wereweasel) took up with Zaltim immediately, pledging to help him with his labours, each for his own reasons.

== The Zhentish Threat ==
200px|right
The problems plaguing Neverwinter were of a Zhentish origin, and The Road Mage had been tasked by the Harpers to put an end to their strong-arming of the trade routes in the North. He had been tasked to this labor for a couple years before he enlisted the aid of our heroes. His need for aid had been made great by the recent death (two weeks before our Heroes intervened) of his partner and life-long friend Laurel Dawn, at the hands of the Uruk’Dun orcs.

The Uruk’Dun were a very scattered tribe for most of their history, but the Zhent mage Gilth'zan managed to bring them together, and also annexed smaller tribes of Orogs and other orcs under the umbrella of the Uruk’Dun warlord.

Another weapon used by the Zhents where a pair of Deep Spawn, which resided in the ruins of Thurl’Dan. These deep spawn were producing ettercaps and trolls that assisted in plaguing the High Road from Waterdeep to Neverwinter.

At the head of the Zhent organization in Neverwinter was the mage Malkor, who was also known by the name Githeas. Githeas was known as a shrewd businessman in Neverwinter. He managed to buy out  and  at a discount rate. Both of these companies were losing tremendous amounts of money, and face, as their cargoes could not get by the Zhent traps on the High Road.

Githeas also owned two ships, the Dolphin Star and the Myrmidon, which were also inordinately successful in navigating through the Luskan pirate ships that were plaguing the waters that year. Needless to say, business was booming while other trading companies and costers were finding it increasingly difficult.

Malkor employed the help of a mage named Atemnon, a high-level messenger, of sorts. This mage did much of the legwork for Malkor, and was the chief source of trouble for the heroes and Zaltim.

== The Harper Resistance ==
thumb|400px|Thundertree
Zaltim’s only other ally was a Harper named Ansal Bloodshoulder, who lived in a logging village called Thundertree. Zaltim and the heroes traveled to Thundertree shortly after meeting in Neverwinter to meet with Ansal and share news. Along the way they were plagued by more darkenbeasts.

Arriving in Thundertree, Ansal is dismayed to learn of the death of Laurel Dawn, whom he had strong feelings for. Ansal informs Zaltim and the heroes that he is worried about the fate of his people in his village. They intercepted a note from an orc messenger.

Ansal was most distressed to find this note. He did not like the numbers of orcs that he saw on the note. His scouts (few as they were) had been meeting increasing numbers of orc scouts in the last couple of weeks. Further, he has a visitation from a traveling hag that warned of the dangers of ‘Trollwomb’ in Thurl’Dan. These two problems, combined with the death of Laurel Dawn, were all the portent he needed to arrive at the conclusion that ‘hard days are ahead’. The Road Mage and his companions began to harry the Uruk’Dun for some time, finally intercepting another letter from the Zhentarim leadership.

== The Bitter Choice ==
400px|right
At this point, Zaltim and the heroes had to make a decision about what foe they would next face. They could stay in Thundertree and face the Uruk’Dun with Bloodshoulder, or they could strike ‘Trollwomb’ in Thurl’Dan. The decision was not easy, and it was with much grudging by Beljuril, that the company set out to Thurl’Dan to exterminate the monsters dwelling there.

The battle in Thurl’Dan was a protracted affair. Zaltim and the heroes faced a multitude of trolls and ettercaps, and eventually destroyed the pair of vile deep spawn that was producing these monsters. This victory was not without cost, however. As the heroes found themselves worn out and wounded, and surrounded by more enemies than they could face, Zaltim extricated them to a nearby ruined tower through use of magical flight. Unknown to the heroes, however, Zaltim had received a fatal poisoned bite from an ettercap that took his life soon after he brought the heroes to safety. Beljuril and Rufus swore to the dying Road Mage that they would take up his labours and destroy the Zhents, and never did the pair deviate from this pledge.

=== The Wages of Action ===

More tragedy was to be found, however, when the heroes returned from cleansing Thurl’Dan and burying their new friend. As they neared Thundertree, a great column of smoke was espied, and soon the heroes found themselves in the pyre that was Thundertree. Ansal they found slain, his head on a pike. All the villagers had been killed and the entire place put to the torch. Our heroes paused to bury the dead in what fashion time allowed. Doubt was upon them, as they questioned themselves and their decisions. But a pledge they made, and our heroes cast aside doubt and grief for a brief time to take to the road once again.

=== Partial Vengeance ===

Heading back to Neverwinter from Thundertree, our heroes met with two of the mages involved in this conflict. They were ambushed by Atemnon and Gilth'zan on the road, along with a small band of thugs. During the battle the heroes managed to kill Gilth'zan and the host of thugs, but Atemnon made good his escape on horseback. Upon the dead Gilth'zan, the heroes found a scroll tube which contained Ansal’s Harper Pin, and a curious note.

== The Man from Waterdeep ==
thumb|A traveler possessing information of interest.
The heroes returned to Neverwinter for a short time after intercepting this note and attempted to locate the mysterious Malkor. It was at this time that our heroes became acquainted with the man known as Jafar, who had come to Neverwinter in search of a child-prodigy wild-mage named Kionin on a mission for Thagdul, a man whom the companions had recently become very interested in meeting. This boy, the dusky mage related to the heroes, had been captured by slavers - agents of this very same Malkor they sought, he suspected. Jafar had managed to discover the boy's location. A rogue by the name of Shuldan had the boy locked in a safehouse beside the harbour, and the criminal not alone. Jafar needed their help to rescue him. Assistance was gladly offered, and the mage and his new companions set out to rescue the boy - a task soon accomplished. After the horrors of Thurl'dan, common thieves were little match for the heroes. In as much time as it takes to tell the tale, a host of Shuldan's lackeys were dispatched, the man himself captured, and Kionin - a boy with white-blonde hair and piercing blue eyes - was located and set free, safe and sound. Excrutiatingly, however, when they were able to get Shuldan to talk, he revealed that Malkor had left for Waterdeep just days earlier.

With this information in hand, it was decided that there was little left to do in Neverwinter, and that the path of justice lay southwards. Jafar agreed to accompany them, and to introduce the party to his patron, Thagdul. The authorities were called to deal with Shuldan, and the companions left the warehouse to prepare for the journey.

Unbeknownst to his new friends, however, Jafar had been given one final instruction to carry out in the North. Once the heroes had set out, he silently returned to the warehouse on his own, where he ruthlessly and mercilessly killed the bound slaver, Shuldan. When he rejoined the companions on the road to Waterdeep, no one as yet suspected that strings of the Zhentarim plot were even then slowly tightening.
