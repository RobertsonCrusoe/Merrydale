A Jasper Hawkford Tale - Prologue Story
by Gus Miranda (as Jasper Hawkford)
Email thread: "A Jasper Hawksford Tale", 5 Jun 2024

A prologue tale set on the evening of 12 Nightal, 1482 DR at the Old Skull Inn in Shadowdale. Jasper's origin story before arriving in Daggerdale: a caravan master brings rumours of trouble from Daggerdale, Jasper's obliviousness to the barmaid Lesti's advances results in a mug to the forehead, and Khara Sulwood asks Jasper to go to Daggerdale. Gus: "Here is a little prologue tale I wrote instead of doing my homework." Les: "I always enjoy your writing, Gus!"

---

Evening on the 12th day of Nightal, 1482 DR. The taproom at the Old Skull Inn, Shadowdale.

A small caravan coming south from Voonlar has limped into the village at midday. It is late in the season for caravans, but the Caravan Master, an experienced woman named Melka Fairwater, explains they suffered a delay setting off from Voonlar, and the unseasonable warmth made their progress south along the muddy road slow. They should be coming up to Tilver's Gap by now, and well within Cormyr's borders not long after. Instead, they've only made it as far as the village of Shadowdale.

Melka is well known at the Old Skull. Originally from Mistledale, she has been earning a living on the roads for nearly three decades now. From Suzail to Hillsfar and back, every year. Recently she has begun to consider settling down - perhaps after just one more run back from Suzail.

The taproom is quiet this evening. The villagers and farmers have wandered off home before the deep cold of night sets in, and the caravan have set up camp on the common under the protective gaze of the Twisted Tower. Only the ones who can afford the luxury, such as Melka, have taken the warm comfort of a room at the Old Skull Inn.

Melka sits at a table chatting away with Khara Sulwood, great-granddaughter of Old Doust Sulwood, once Lord of this Dale. Sitting with Khara is her childhood friend, a young wizard named Jasper Hawkford.

Lestari Silvermane, the young barmaid at the Old Skull, comes to the table and puts down four mugs of mulled rum, steam wafting up from the fragrant drinks. She sits beside Jasper.

Melka gives a long sigh. She has been on the road longer than any of these three have been alive. Khara Sulwood, aristocrat of the Dale. The young lady is so preoccupied with the well-being and future of Shadowdale that she's never ventured anywhere beyond the neighbouring Dales. Jasper, the mage? She doubts whether he's ever even left sight of the village! And Lesti? She dreams only of a husband and a brood of children, just like her mother before her.

Melka thinks again of a small cottage back in Mistledale, not too far from where she grew up. Perhaps a cottage near the river, with a pair of good dogs. And perhaps a handsome young lumberjack to stay warm with on winter nights. Perhaps this should be the final run.

"So what took you so long to get on the road?" asks Lesti, undoing the braids in her hair, letting it fall loose. She can hear her brothers arguing in the kitchen as they wash the dishes and clean up; the work day has come to an end.

"A group from Dagger Falls. They insisted on coming east up the Moonsea Road and joining us in Voonlar," says Melka, stirring her rum. "They were supposed to arrive by the 20th, and the boss said they had paid a lot of gold for us to take them to Cormyr, so we needed to wait."

Melka's expression seems to harden. "We waited and waited an entire tenday, and when our 'clients' finally arrived, it was just one fever-ridden madman raving about shadows and ghosts and goblins! We had our priest look after him, but he was dead by morning. We waited for nothing."

Both Khara and Lesti whisper a quick prayer to Chauntea.

"Ghosts?" asks the wizard Jasper

"Surely, everyone's heard the tales about the Moonsea Road and Thistle Ridge? Even here?"

Khara snorts protectively, "We're not so isolated as that! We've heard about the Tragedy of Thistle Ridge! But the place was abandoned, no? The goblins scattered back into the woods?"

"Seems not," says Melka. "Something's happening up there in Daggerdale. Something that makes me uneasy. I'm not sure what it is, nor do I really care to find out. My business is south now: in Cormyr. And afterwards... well, I doubt I'll ever be coming back this far north again. I think I'm done."

Melka takes a long sip of her warm rum, and stands up.

"If you care about your Dale, Lady Sulwood, you'd do well to get your fellow villagers to prepare for what's coming."

And with that ominous warning, Melka Fairwater downs her mug, bids her goodnights, and heads upstairs to her private room.

"Well," said Khara, "I suppose that's as evil a thought as any to call it a night!" She stands and grabs her belongings, wrapping up for the cold outside.

"You know, Jasper... it is very cold outside... why don't you just stay here tonight?" asks Lesti, her wide, bright, blue eyes fixed pointedly on his; her loose silver-blonde hair cascading down around her shoulders, framing her ample, pale cleavage, glowing in the light of the hearth.

"Err... well... thanks, but you know, that's why I brought an extra cloak!" says the young wizard as he stands from the table, oblivious.

Moments later, Khara bids farewell and she bounds across the road towards her home in the village, laughing hysterically at the denseness of her wizard friend. Meanwhile a confused Jasper begins the long, lonely walk through the cold night towards his family's farm. Inside the warm Old Skull Inn, an incredulous and angry Lesti is left to clean up and close down for the night.


The next morning, the Fairwater Caravan breaks camp before daybreak. Melka is intent on making up time while the weather holds. The pale light of dawn breaks the horizon as the caravan crosses the bridge and continues its journey south.


Mid-morning finds Khara sitting on the front steps of the House of Plenty. She has just paid a visit to the High Harvestmistress, and has confirmed that a small group of the local Chauntean faithful will travel to Dagger Falls for The Budding festival this year in a show of neighbourly camaraderie. Earlier in the day she had likewise visited Morningdawn Hall and ascertained that the Amaunatori are planning to do likewise for their Vernal Epiphany.

Khara's thoughts are on Melka's story. It is far from the first time that she's heard rumours of trouble to the north. For many months her thoughts have been preoccupied with Daggerdale. It is a puzzle that has been laid out before her, yet she still cannot see how the pieces begin to fit together.

The Lady Adee Ulphor is the current ruler of Shadowdale. She is originally from Harrowdale, and she has led Shadowdale with great ability and grace. Under her stewardship, Shadowdale has retreated back to a simple, quiet, oasis of peace in a dangerous world. Its fame has faded, its heroes forgotten. It has become so inconsequential that many of the newer maps simply omit its existence: just a small patch of farmland and forest lying between Daggerdale and Mistledale.

It has long been assumed by all the Dalesmen that when the time comes, it will be Khara who will succeed Lady Adee as ruler of Shadowdale. Khara Sulwood, great-granddaughter of legends. Blood of the Dale. She was born for it. It is her destiny. Her place in the world.

The time will come.

In the meantime she prepares. She frets and worries about her Dale. She takes counsel from the High Harvestmistress and the High Morninglord alike. She spends most of her days strolling from village to farm to woodcutter camp to hunting lodge, listening to the concerns of her people. She spends her evenings at the Old Skull listening to tales, tall or short, told by travellers passing through.

She listens. She plans. She prepares. The time is coming.

From her seat on the front steps of the House of Plenty, she spots Jasper strolling down the road. A smile comes across her face.

Khara and Jasper grew up together. They were born weeks apart, and their mothers nursed them together, bathed them together, raised them together. As children they were inseparable. They had gone on countless adventures of the imagination together. They had explored almost every inch of Shadowdale together.

She watches across the square as Jasper hops up the steps of the Old Skull Inn, and walks inside.

Then she had fallen in love with his brother Maynard, and that had changed everything. But after Maynard's death...

Even at this distance, Khara can hear the yelling. Jasper crashes out of the door of the Inn, and nearly stumbles down the steps out onto the road. He appears to be holding his cheek.

"YOU! You can go crawl into a dryder cave and die for all I care, Jasper Hawksford!" comes the enraged shout from Lesti, somewhere inside the Old Skull. A wooden mug flies out the door hitting its mark square on the forehead with an audible <thunk!> Jasper wavers, loses his balance and slumps down to the ground, dazed.

Lesti's brothers emerge from the Inn. One tries to calm the young barmaid down, while the other three come and help the young wizard back up on his feet, and look him over to make sure he's not hurt badly. The two older ones, give him a quick slap on the back and head back into the Inn. "C'mon, Lee-Lee! It's Jasper! You know what he's like! Go easy on him!" says one of them as he disappears from view once inside.

The youngest brother, Glaun, helps Jasper over to the steps of the temple and sits him down beside Khara.

"Just give her a day or two. She'll be fine!" says Glaun, handing a small flask of whisky to Khara, then he turns and runs off back to the Old Skull, laughing.

Khara looks over at Jasper with a broad smile. The wizard's cheek is red from where Lesti slapped him, and a bruise is beginning to form on the forehead where the mug struck him.

"What... what happened?" asks Jasper of his friend, true ignorance reflected in his pleading, uncomprehending eyes.

Khara opens the flask, stops, looks back at Jasper, then takes a long drink out of the flask.

"Yaaaaaar!" exclaims the young woman as the whisky hits, looking at the wizard with a smile, "Never change, Jay! Promise me, you'll never change!" She offers the flask to the young man, who takes it but doesn't drink.

The two sit in silence for some time, watching the life of the village go by.

Eventually, Khara speaks: "I think I need you to do something for me, Jasper."

She turns and looks him in the eyes, "I think I need you to go to Daggerdale."
