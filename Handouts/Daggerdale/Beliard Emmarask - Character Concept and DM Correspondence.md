I'd like to play a politico \- someone from the Cormyrean Embassy. My idea is for a very realpolitik, opportunist-type \- a spy-assassin. He is playing the Great Game, and his ultimate goals are not those that he openly declares, which are to foster a greater alliance between Cormyr and Daggerdale against the Shadovar threat. In truth, he is a double agent \- his true allegiance is to a Free Sembia. He has found a place within the Cormyrean Embassy that has allowed him to feed intelligence to a network of citizens whose short-term goals are throwing off the yoke of Netheril, but who are ultimately after what they call the Dream of the Silver Raven, or Sembia Ascendant \- a Sembian hegemony over Cormyr, the Dalelands, Cormanthor and Netheril.

His name Beliard Kathklan Emmarask, special aide to the Cormyrean ambassador to Castle Radiance. He is a knowledgeable expert on the Shadovar and their kind and was born during the Year of the Silent Shadows, when several Cormyrean cities bordering Netheril and Sembia were blanketed by an unnatural miasma. Beliard's father, a minor Cormyrean bureaucrat, took ill during the phenomenon, but his Sembian mother \- and indeed most women who were pregnant at the time \- flourished, giving birth to an almost preternaturally healthy boy. Many of the children born during this time are known for their vigorous health and strong resistance to the Shadoweave, as though they developed an inborn resistance to whatever pollution affected their parents. He grew up despising the Shadovar, whom he thought to have broken his father, and despising his father's weakness even more. In such conditions, he felt more affinity to his mother's family, the Kathklan, who hail from Daerlun. As a child he was known as "The Sembian" among the Cormyrean townschildren, but this epithet he bore in stride, affecting the arrogant pride of his mother's people. In young adulthood, accompanying his widowed mother to her family lands in the now-independent city-state of Daerlun, he made contact with Sembian patriots who were discontent with their homeland's situation. Since this time, he has been methodically working his father's family's connections in Cormyr to position himself in such a way that the cause of a Free Sembia can be realized in his lifetime. He is always on the lookout for information that will be of use to his associates in the Society of the Silver Raven, as the Free Sembia movement is styled. Among the Silver Ravens, his code name is Nycticorax, and his true identity is known only to the inner circle of the organization, among whom the society began in the early 1450s.

He is an ardent Selunite, feeling that, unlike Aumanator, the Silver Lady is prepared to fight the Lady of the Night on her own ground, which Aumanator can never do. Despite this philosophy, he does appreciate the power of the sun god and respects the efforts of the Whitegates in Daggerdale.

In the summer of 1480, amidst reports of war brewing, he was chosen for his unique anti-Shadovar skill set to be dispatched to the embassy at Castle Radiance. He is in possession of one half of a divinatory artifact known as Kordamant's Amanuensis, which appears to be a simple monocle set in a mithril frame. The lens of the monocle, however, is not made of glass but of an unknown crystalline substance, and is unbreakable. It cannot be lost or stolen, only given freely from one individual to another, so long as the current possessor is alive. Anything read through the monocle is translated into the language of the reader, but is also transmitted to the other half of the artifact, a mithril stylus which automatically creates a copy of the document (as written in its original language). The stylus is currently in the possession of a member of the inner circle of the Silver Ravens in Daerlun, where it has been steadily creating an ever-expanding archive of copied Cormyrean state records, as well as whatever other intelligence Nycticorax comes across.

This is essentially the case. Beliard was originally conceived of as a John Le Carre style intelligence agent. (I don't know if any of you have read Le Carre, but his characters get a lot of spying done through paperwork \- think of the Robert Redford character in Spy Game). When I first e-mailed Les, it was to suggest I run an NPC in his ongoing White Cloak campaign, based at the Castle. I decided to ask Blacky to attach him to the Cormyrean embassy because I wanted to be in a comparatively neutral position vis-a-vis the Whitegates and their war with Netheril. Cormyr is of course allied to Daggerdale, but they are not officially at war with the Shadow, so it's an ambiguous position.

When Les decided to put together an online campaign, Beliard was mustered into an actual party situation, which has proved to be unworkable. However, from what Scott has told me, the character concept appears to have interested Les enough that he has become well and truly embedded into the setting. I won't give any spoilers below, but I'll try to clarify exactly what manner of man Beliard is.

From the couple of narratives that I've written, everyone should realize that he is involved in an as-yet-unrevealed secret society (his official situation with the Cormyrean Embassy, but he has ulterior motives). I gave Les the details of the society, and its goals, and he approved of the character concept. (I will tell you that he is on the level, alignment-wise. Not a hint of Cyricism, no sir.) Beliard's political goals are very long term \- he is unlikely to live to see them realized, even if he dies of old age \- but who knows? His short term goals are as follows:

1) To learn as much about Netheril as possible (and especially to seek out Netherese documents)
2) To prevent Netheril from establishing a foothold in the Dales
3) To learn about the balance of power between Daggerdale, Tethyamar, and Cormanthor

Final thought \- gotta go. It's worth remembering that one man's Crusader's Oath is another's Assassin's Creed. There's room for both strength and subtlety in a war setting. Beliard was conceived with the latter in mind.

Beliard will be based at the Cormyrean Embassy, so he won't be entirely outside the Castle's purview, although, as I've said, he's mostly peripheral to the Whitegate power structure. The Embassy exists to promote Cormyr's interests \- and occasionally, these are going to differ from those of Daggerdale. A further level of complexity is that Beliard's true allegiances may not necessarily coincide with those of Cormyr (though, for the present, I believe they mostly do). In that sense, he's a triple agent.

So, my interpretation of the two points above:

A \- You guys work for Beliard as a mercenary company with the authorization of the Cormyrean Embassy, and through them, of the Whitegates. You take bounties and are fully licensed and on the up and up as far as the Castle goes.

B \- You guys work for Beliard "off the books" \- and report to him directly, bypassing the Castle bureaucracy. This will be much trickier to get by Blacky, because his NPCs actively discourage this sort of activity. I think Blacky might like this, however, because it would play into the competition ecology he has set up \- Mule Team vs. the Redjacks, for example. (Or the Saharel Seven vs. the Mercykillers...)

Notes for Beliard:

Notes for Beliard
Between your arrival at Castle Radiance in the spring of 1480 DR, and the current adventure, which kicks off for you on 13 Marpenoth, 1481, you have had an eventful residency. You replaced an aging bureaucrat that had served Ambassador John Tunbridge for several years, basically taking on the role of secretary to the Ambassador. Your daily duties have been many; you receive and send the correspondence of the Ambassador, taking caring of the logistics of the embassy, sitting in on all meetings that the embassy is invited to (and taking notes), and so on. Though this work at first seemed below you, you quickly realize that you have access to some of the most privileged information in the Castle. Further, Tunbridge is old and fat, and he has made every effort to suggest that you will be replacing him when the Grey Lord calls him from this world.

You went on a 3-month assignment to Base Camp Silver in Tethyamar, which was dangerous and frightening. The nearly-constant battle in the deep is paired with a politically complicated scenario that remains bordering on fratricide and civil war. You managed to develop a good rapport with the Silver Council. In fact, due to Tunbridge's health, he was unable to attend this mission, and you are the face of Cormyr to the Silver Council and the various parties involved in this struggle.

You also developed a rapport with Captain Iolus Warburton of the White Cloaks, who had the bulk of his team sequestered in the deep, waging war on the side of Tasster Gellinson. At the time, you were giving orders to General Stuart Angleton and his Purple Dragons, and you managed to provide help in more than one timely instance to the benefit of the moderate Gellinson and the White Cloaks.

Returning to the Castle in the late summer, you made a new friend in the curious figure known as Alen Reks, the man that Warburton left in charge of White Cloak matters in his absence. Reks is certainly an ambitious man; he plays at being a simple servant to the White Cloaks, but you detect a certain cunning in the man, and suspect that he is hiding something. At any rate, the two of you started sharing some information between yourselves, and with a few unimportant morsels you managed to buy yourself something into a very interesting scenario...

13 Marpenoth, 1481 DR.
It is the end of what has been another pleasant day in the front office of the Tower. It has been a fine late summer/early fall up in the mountains, and just about any weather above ground is better than the oppressive humidity and sweltering heat of Tethyamar, which you suffered through for months. You are just about to lock the doors for the day when a runner comes with a small package for you.

The package is an oiled leather folder with another leather folder inside. The inner folder has the initials AR embossed into one corner. Inside there is a collection of documents tied together with a white ribbon, and a note attached to the stack sticking conspicuously out. You recognize the looping hand of Administrator Reks almost instantly. His hand is as flighty and mercurial as the man himself.

PS: The collection is incomplete. You will find the remainder with my erstwhile lackey, Mr. Nathan Gale, including the interesting books around which these plots and schemes seem to revolve. Give Gale my best if you see him. Or don't...

The collection of documents includes the following (I have emailed you this list with the wiki links, for simplicity). Have a read through them, and we'll proceed.

Further to this collection of documents, you know a bit about this cult called the Sons of Bel. It is an organization that has plagued Marsember for decades, dating back to the late 1300's. You have never heard of them operating outside of Cormyr at all, and rarely outside of their home city inside Cormyr.

The cult in Marsember was infiltrated by Royal Agents in 1479, and its activities have been on the decline ever since. However, with the information in the documents above, it looks like they may have been planning on a move at any rate.

There are some geopolitical concerns here; the trouble with the cult in Marsember used up vast resources for the Purple Dragons and other Royal agencies. This, of course, drew resources away from the front with Netheril. As of last year, hundreds of knights, thousands of footmen, and countless subtle resources have been redirected to the Cormyr/Netheril front. The fact that this all happened at the same time as the Dark Garden affair, and the large-scale war that accompanied it on the Daggerdale/Netheril front, is not lost on you.

I'll leave it at that, and hope you respond ASAP. I want to see what you decide to do with this information.
