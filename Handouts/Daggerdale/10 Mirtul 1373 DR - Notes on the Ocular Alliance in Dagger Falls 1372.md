==Introduction==

Following the resurrection of Bane in the autumn of 1371, the constellation of powers and allegiances of many churches, governments and organisations underwent a not unexpected period of adjustment. Not least did this affect the Black Network of Zhentil Keep. By 1371, the Zhentarim had become enmeshed in the machinations of a powerful group of beholders, herein referred to as the Ocular Alliance. This marriage of convenience has had far-reaching effects on both the Black Network and the inter-regional trade in Western Faerûn. While the aims of the Ocular Alliance are as yet unknown, they have been extensively speculated upon elsewhere by this author, and it is not the purpose of this report to do so. Rather, the following pages will detail the role played by the various organisations involved in the conspiracy, and the schism of 1372 that led to the current enmity between the Church of Cyric and the Ocular Alliance. These notes have been compiled with the assistance of Lady Jeryth Phaulkon, who knew many of the eyewitnesses personally, and without whose knowledge and dedication to our cause we would have remained ignorant of the following facts.

==The Harper Missions==

In the days after Greengrass of last year, a small gathering of our order in the western city of Waterdeep initiated three missions that would prove to lead to the unmasking of the Ocular Alliance and severely hinder its aims. The existence of the Alliance, long suspected by many, had until then been unsubstantiated, owing to the recent difficulties experienced in the North and East (see the accompanying Notes Concerning the Unstrung Harp, also by this author). The Church of Cyric was to play a central role in the expedition undertaken by those who became known as the Company of the Eastern Storm.

==The Cyricists==

The Church of Cyric played a key role in the maintenance of the Ocular Alliance's domination of Dagger Falls, playing a primarily defensive role, (at least after the consolidation of power in the spring and summer of 1372). The primary objective was the paralysis of any organized opposition by the other Daleland communities. This was played out in Shadowdale through the agency of High Dawnlord Cassimar, the priest of Lathander that unwittingly played into the hands of the Ocular Alliance by advocating non-interference in the affairs of Shadowdale's neighbours to the north.

When the concentration of interests opposing the Alliance reached a critical mass with the arrival of the knights of the Holy Triad, a new approach became necessary. Lord Maturin Horgath, at the time a paladin out of favour with his patron deity, was the ideal candidate for the Dark Sun to use to advance His interests. Through the guise of Torm the True, Cyric arranged an ambush for the forces of good at Anathar's Dell, where they were pitted against the unholy orders of Bane and Iyachtu Xvim.

Cyric's cunning in the arrangement of this ambush, however, would prove to be his undoing. The decision not to support the Knights of the Black Gauntlet and the Brothers of the Black Fist in their clash against the Holy Triad was taken by the other members of the Alliance as an outright betrayal, and Cyric's role was quickly written out of whatever plan was being pursued by the Masters of the Alliance. That is, if there indeed remained a role to be played.

Prior to the slaughter at Anathar's Dell, it seemed that Cyric's role in the Alliance was one of trust and importance, as key strategic points were exclusively manned by the unholy orders of the Dark Sun. The 4th Garrison of Dagger Falls oversaw the excavation of the mine in the Dagger Hills, where the "Beholder Tablets" were unearthed. Secondly, the desert citadel of Olomaa, along the Zhentarim-controlled trade route across Anauroch, was a strong Cyricist enclave. One of the two Cyricist Fist Companies was stationed there. Indeed, the role of the Cyricists in the fabric of the Fist mercenary company has yet to be understood. Their involvement certainly predated the alliance between the Fist (which we understand to be a military arm of Manshoon) and the interests of the Oculars.

However, such rumination is of exclusively historical interest in light of the events that followed the second battle of Anathar's Dell. On the 7th of Eleint, 1372, the forces of good and evil clashed in Cyric's well-orchestrated ambush. The knights of the Triad were sorely diminished before the Brothers of the Black Fist were driven away. The Knights of the Black Gauntlet were destroyed utterly, but the Ebon Spur were nowhere to be seen. This cowardice of leaving the dangerous work to his allies would prove to be Cyric's downfall, it seems.

==Unearthing Treachery==

The next day, the mysterious Beholder Tablets were unearthed from the mine in the Dagger Hills, and, to the best of our knowledge, the entire Cyricist garrison was marched to its death in the mines, which were sealed thereafter. It is unknown whether these soldiers were a willing sacrifice by Cyric, believing that He still held a position of trust within the Alliance, or whether their usefulness had simply expired, along with Cyric's, and this was the first open move by the Masters of the Alliance against their former ally.

Martial wisdom advocates keeping one's enemies closer than one's friends. Cyric's seeming position of trust in the Alliance revealed itself to be just such a short leash, keeping the devious deity's agents under the watchful eyes of the Oculars. A short-lived outbreak of violence in Dagger Falls the day after the tablets were excavated revealed that the theological arms of the Alliance were becoming unstable. A squabble between Banites and Cyricists required the intervention of the Fist soldiers at the behest of Yarkul.

Reports from an agent who played a key role in the liberation of Dagger Falls and shattering the Alliance's stranglehold on trade across Anauroch indicate that it was at about this time that Cyric began to move against the Alliance, through his influence over Lord Horgath. Appearing once again in the guise of Torm, Cyric urged Maturin to follow the Tablets, and prevent whatever was guiding them west from making its journey. This vision occurred on Eleint 10, a scarce two days after the excavation of the tablets.

The fact that the Church of Cyric remained a powerful force in Dagger Falls at that time indicates that hostility between the former allies was not yet in the open, but it is likely that both the Masters of the Alliance and Cyric Himself understood that they were now at odds. When the Tablets arrived in Dagger Falls and the Fist were recalled from their various patrols, the balance of power rested on the edge of a knife, ready to topple at the slightest breath.

==Struggle for the Dark==

It is interesting that Yarkul, the Zhentarim mage who played such a key role in the Alliance, was cut from its ranks at almost the same time as the Cyricists. More interesting still when one considers that Yarkul was an ardent follower of the Church of Xvim, and consort to a demon of Xvim, a succubus named Amaretta. Yarkul had been instrumental at securing a role for 'The New Darkness' in the ranks of the Alliance in 1371 when he made contact with them in Waterdeep, and had brought them to the east after completing whatever role he had played there.

For all intents and purposes, the Alliance was through with the Dalelands — and the Churches — when the Beholder Tablets were unearthed. It seems that the usefulness of both Xvim and Cyric were limited to that purpose. Perhaps the Masters of the Alliance were punishing their vassals — Yarkul and the Xvimites for their failure in Waterdeep, and the Cyricists for their ambition — or perhaps they were simply tired of having to manage the politics of competition within their ranks. In any event, these internecine worries were left behind in Dagger Falls when the Tablets moved west. Yarkul and the soldiers formerly of the 9th Fist Company were all that remained when the Alliance turned its many eyes to the west.

==Cormaeril==

Yarkul's subsequent efforts at peacemaking only resulted in his (long overdue) assassination, and the establishment of a new government under the hereditary rule claimed by Lord William Cormaeril of Cormyr. The relative strengths of the Churches of Xvim and Cyric can be seen in the rapid elimination of the Church of Bane after Cormaeril took hold of the reins of power. There is, however, a sizable caveat to this statement. Cormaeril, as was later revealed, was acting as an unwitting pawn for yet another Zhentarim mage, one Venosar, allegedly sent by his superiors in the Zhentarim to replace Yarkul (prior, it must be noted, to Yarkul's death). Cormaeril's short-lived policy of establishing trade links with Yûlash — another Zhentarim-controlled town, indicates that Venosar was likely an agent of Thagdul, the mage who controlled that polity for most of 1372. Little is known of this man, save for his involvement the previous year in Waterdeep, and that he was allegedly responsible for the installation of Yarkul in Dagger Falls. (It should be noted that it is the working hypothesis of this author that Thagdul is, or was, the spokesperson for Zhentarim interests within the Ocular Alliance. See my previous reports on Zhentarim trade monopolies on the Sword Coast in 1370-71.)

With the elimination of the Church of Bane, the replacement of Yarkul (friend to Xvim) with Cormaeril (puppet of Venosar, a Cyricist), a delicate balance of power remained in Dagger Falls. Hatemaster Dragkul and Morgul the Vigilant Talon waited each for the opportunity to seize control and oust the other. It was the arrival of the company that would become known as the Eastern Storm that brought things to a head.

Cyric's policy of pitting one enemy against another did not waver, for all that he hoped the heroes would follow the Tablets. If the heroes would not head west, then they could be put to work in Dagger Falls. Venosar revealed his Cyricist sympathies as Cormaeril sanctioned the extermination of the Xvimites. Unfortunately, the heroes unwittingly put Venosar onto the scent of one of our own agents within the Dagger Falls establishment, but his own undoing came shortly and surely thereafter.

Having secured the Church of Cyric as the sole remaining interest in Dagger Falls, the Dark Sun attempted one last time to put Lord Horgath back on the trail of the Tablets. For whatever reason, the knight stayed on for the events that exposed Venosar's dealings and led to the final purge of the Church of Cyric from the town.

==A Desert Crossing==

A footnote to this report concerns the men who had belonged to the 9th Company of Fist. They disappeared from Dagger Falls with the downfall of the temple, and were not seen again until Marpenoth that year, when the Eastern Storm came upon their corpses on the trail of the Tablets. It seems likely that Cyric, having failed to spur his pawn the paladin in time, finally used his own resources and sent his soldiers to stop the Tablets. They must have followed the trail of the rest of the Fist into Anauroch, somehow slipping past the guards that had been left to thwart such pursuit, only to be slain when they finally caught their quarry.

A report from the Eastern Storm indicates that the desert citadel of Olomaa was scourged by the caravan accompanying the Tablets. It seems that there is now no doubt that Cyric and the Ocular Alliance are open enemies.

It is the hope of this author that the intelligence and analysis herein will prove in some way towards the elimination of the threat posed by the Ocular Alliance. While the fact remains that our true enemy is, as has always been the case, the Black Network, the Ocular Alliance has proved a powerful ally for the Zhentarim in their efforts against us. Nowhere is this more evident than in the murderous success of Venosar in the Dales and the insane mage Malkor in Neverwinter and the North. The dissolution of this Alliance can only be to our advantage as an organisation.

==Who is Thagdul?==

One other note bears mentioning. While sources indicate that the Eastern Storm have recognized Cyric's treachery and that Lord Horgath has once more achieved a state of incorruptibility, Cyric's role in the affair of the Ocular Alliance may yet be unfinished. The connection between the mage Thagdul and the Church of Cyric, while poorly documented, may be stronger than is yet known. With Venosar's death and the elimination of his soldiers and Church in Dagger Falls, Cyric may now look to Thagdul to advance His interests. It is the recommendation of this report that resources be allocated to the investigation of this mysterious person, and while such an investigation may be impractical given the strain on personnel recently, it is nevertheless of utmost importance. Thagdul's role in this affair must be deciphered if our knowledge of Cyric's modus operandi is to be of any constructive use in the fight against the Alliance.

Finally, it must be admitted that an alternative hypothesis to the events of Eleint 1372 exists — that the Xvimites and the Cyricists, both key players in the Ocular Alliance, were betrayed not by their unseen masters, but in fact by an as yet unknown force external to the Alliance itself. However, the evidence on the matter indicates otherwise. Whatever, or whoever, such an external force could be is entirely unknown.

Mnestheus Melshimber, Mirtul 1373

==Addendum==

The following notes were added following further conversations by the Circle with Garrik Shining.

The revelation by the interrogation of Morgul the Vigilant Talon that Yarkul had been a vassal of Thagdul, and that Venosar, the wizard assassin, had been his replacement, leads to several questions:

1. To what degree was Venosar involved in the Alliance itself? From the favourable relations between Morgul and Venosar, the instigator of the purges that resulted in the primacy of the Church of Cyric in Dagger Falls, it does not seem that he was following any dictates of the Alliance. Indeed, it rather seems the case that he was employing his wiles in support of the Dark Sun, while also stabilizing and obfuscating the control of the town by the Zhentarim.

2. What does this say of his supposed liege, the wizard Thagdul? It is the opinion of this author that Thagdul is playing a dangerous game of supporting two projects.
   a. Firstly, the continuing role of the Zhentarim power-broker in the presumed service of Manshoon.
   b. Secondly, he remains, as far as this author has evidence, an active member in the Ocular Alliance. It remains to be seen how these perhaps conflicting alliances will be played to their conclusion.

3. Did Cyric's use of Maturin as a vehicle for motivating his designs begin when a Cyricist cleric "healed" Maturin's strange illness in Loudwater?

4. Was the disinformation that "the elves of Cormanthor" had slaughtered the Cyricists, aside from stirring racial tensions in the Dales, meant to fool the Cyricists themselves?

5. What motivated the betrayal of the Cyricists at Anathar's Dell? Resentment of Xvim?

6. If Cyric continues to oppose the Oculars, who will he use as his agent?
