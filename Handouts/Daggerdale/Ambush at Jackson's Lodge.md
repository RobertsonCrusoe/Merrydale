== Discovery ==

It is the 10th of Flamerule.  The lot of you are headed up to Castle Radiance with a rather large wagon train, which set out from Dagger Falls a day ago.  The war in the west with Netheril is just winding down, but there is still a tremendous amount of danger and violence in the mountains, so the trains have been large and well guarded.  The lot of you are travelling with them more for convenience and added security, than for any formal security arrangement.

The train is passing by a minor fork in the road which leads, as a new sign indicates, to something called \"Jackson's Lodge.\"  The side-path is small and in stark contrast with the well maintained, broad road that you are travelling on.  It is a dirt path up through a pine-choked valley between two small mountains.  As your portion of the train is passing by, one of the more alert of you notices a pale hand sticking out from under some low brush.  Heading over to investigate what you think is a corpse, you find that a young woman, perhaps 20 years old, is lying nearly motionless beneath the brush, only her wheezy breaths expanding her small chest to indicate that she is alive.  Her clothing, serviceable leathers which a lumberjack or other frontiersman might wear, is in rough shape; there are cruel gouges in much of it, under which are terrible wounds.  The woman is pale and cold, and on the brink of death.

With the aid of the healers in your midst, she is brought barely to consciousness.  She explains that she was part of a resupply mission heading from the Castle to the Lodge, yesterday.  Her group of 10 men and women was ambushed on the trail at dusk.  She isn't sure what attacked them, only that they weren't men; they were bipedal monsters of some fashion that attacked with claws and teeth.

One of your group runs up to the van of the train, which has continued to march on, unaware of what is going on in the rear.  When you reach the leader of the train, a Sun Blade lieutenant named Marshall, you tell him there was an attack on some mountain men up that last trail.  \"Were there any survivors?\" he asks coldly and matter-of-factly.  When he hears your story, he grimaces and thinks.  \"When we reach the castle, I'll tell my captain.  He can decide what to do about this.  Bring the girl to me, and we'll look after her.  None of my men will be going up that trail to investigate, though.  Orders are orders.  And anyway, that smells like a trap to me.\"

The six of you are the only unattached members of the train that can be spared.  Deciding that you just can't walk away from this and wait for the Sunblades to investigate after the paperwork is done, you head up the path.  About a mile up the steep, winding road, you do indeed find the site of the ambush.  There are 2 overturned wagons, and 2 dead horses on the ground.  The horses have been predated upon by a number of creatures; indeed, as you approach the scene, you drive off a murder of dozens of crows and some circling foxes.  The dead horses have been chewed at by a number of things, but you notice large bite-marks, about 3 times the size of a man's mouth, which seem to have had the first servings.

The wagons are mostly empty.  There are a couple of smashed up chests and barrels, and some unimportant personal items and ruined supplies littering the ground, but you can see that most of what must have been on the wagons is missing.

Further, there are no human bodies around.  You can see signs of a few being dragged off, and several being marched off in a column.  You follow the tracks to the edge of a cliff-face of the mountain, when the wind blows the slightest hint of death down through the pines to your group.  You all pause, and discuss the best way to proceed...

==Reactions==
===Tyrian===
Upon discovering the young woman's body, Tyrian is one of the first to be called over for assistance.  He is there in an instant and provides what aid he can, but always deferring to those in authority or those who clearly have more skill and ability in the healing arts.

He is of the opinion that the residents of Jackson's Lodge are in great peril, and they likely need rescuing immediately. Tyrian voices his concerns to Lieutenant Marshall and the young lieutenant seems to give Tyrian more consideration for his opinion, but ultimately the decision to move on is made.

Tyrian voices his concern to Carpathia, who suggests that with the aid of the elves they could probably handle the situation themselves and catch up to the convoy later.

===Ineluki===
Ineluki stays silent and distant with those that he has been assigned with.  He still bristles at the thought of his punishment and how he will be saddled with the task of associating with Castle Radiance for the next twelve years.  It might not be so bad were he not perpetually in the mountains or the foothills of the aforementioned mountains.

A muscle in his jaw twitched in irritation as he listened to the human female ramble about and try to explain why she could not remember what it was that chewed at half her body.  She dressed like the ones he would see in Cormanthor and invariably drive out of Cormanthor.

The paladin of Ilmater seemed fit to the task of consoling the half-witted female so with a simple nod of his head to his clansmen, he sets out towards the corpses of the horses in order to glean some information regarding what might have been the large beast or beasts that had assaulted the caravan.

===Carpathia===
Tyrian looks to the wagon train, thinking of Carpathia, and hoping she is looking away from this gruesome slaughter. She is nowhere to be seen among the wagons.

Looking around he finally spots her, kneeling to the ground 100 paces off the road in the direction the beasts' tracks led.

She stands, and moves with purpose another ten paces, where she pulls a blood spattered leaf from a low bough. She puts it to her lips and tastes it.

Dropping the leaf she walks directly to Tyrian. She looks demure no more. The pain in her eyes has been replaced with determination, her shoulders are held straight, her head high.

She speaks to Tyrian (and those with him):

\"The survivors cannot be left to this fate.\" Blunt, matter of fact, and seemingly not a question.

===Cirdan===
With a nod from Ineluki, Cirdan begins to walk away from the site, giving it a wide birth as he scouts the surrounding thicket.  He exits the tree-line on the far side of the site, walks further up the path and waits for instruction from the humans.  For now, he keeps an eye on the path into the valley, looking for any other tracks.

He cares not for the plight of that woman, as he has seen several in her state, some by his own hand.  In fact a handful of his arrows still bear the names of women on them, he carves theirs in cursive.

===Beliard===
Beliard Emmarask, the well-bred gentleman with the aquiline nose and rich clothing, sits astride his charcoal palfrey, watching bemusedly as the elves slink in and out of the undergrowth. Turning his head, he watches the last of the wagon train disappear over a rise, then spurs his mount into a slow trot along the path in pursuit of Tyrian and the girl.

Catching them up at the ambush site, he draws a handkerchief from a pocket to cover his face as he dismounts. \"Beshaba's Breath! This is foul business.\" Turning to Tyrian, he commends the younger man. \"You did well to tend that girl, Grey. You are a credit to your faith. The world needs more of you hospitallers. Now, where have those wood sprites gotten off to I wonder...\"

Picking up a stick, he begins poking and prodding at the wounds on the horses and wandering around, with an expression of what almost seems to be exasperation.

When Carpathia makes her solemn announcement, he holds up a hand. \"Let's not bank on their being any survivors, shall we? Best not set ourselves up for a disappointment.\" Seeing the young lady stiffen, he holds up palm. \"No fear, little dove. The spritelings appear to be eager to seek their fate, and foolish though we may be to follow them, we are committed now. But we must arm ourselves with information, and not be hasty. As the Raven said, 'Before all else, be armed.'\"
