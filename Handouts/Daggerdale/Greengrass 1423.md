Journal of Tergus Clarandal
Greengrass, 1423

In this season of renewal, I find myself brimming with anticipation, for in but a year's time, I shall embark on my journey to join the storied Sun Blades. The thought of it fills my heart with both fervor and trepidation. To fight alongside such venerated warriors in the rugged wilderness—this is the dream that has ignited my spirit since I first held a sword.

Yet, even as I prepare for this grand adventure, I cannot quell the unease that has settled within our home. Father's academy, the very cornerstone of our noble lineage, now entertains a most peculiar instructor. This man, with his exotic ways, has introduced a form of combat that relies not on the strength of steel, but on the bare hands and feet of the warrior. Such a practice seems to me to be devoid of reason. How can one defend their honor, their life, without the trusted weight of a blade?

I watch as my kin and fellow students mimic the instructor's fluid motions, striking the air with fists and feet. They call it a discipline, an art. But to my eyes, it appears naught but folly. In the chaos of battle, when the clang of swords and the cries of the wounded fill the air, who will find time for such elaborate gestures?

Still, I hold my tongue out of respect for Father's judgment, though my heart longs for the mountains where the Sun Blades train. There, amid the rocky crags and whispering pines, I shall hone my skills with the sword, and prove that the ancient ways of our forebears are far superior to these newfangled techniques.

As the sun sets on this day, I resolve to stay the course. The path of the warrior is fraught with challenges, but it is one I shall walk with pride. For I am Tergus Clarandal, and my destiny lies with the Sun Blades.
