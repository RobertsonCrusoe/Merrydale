==The Consolation of the Philosopher==
It had been a year since the strange and severe elf had paid his equally strange and severe visit. Gaebril’s condition had deteriorated to the point that he had to be chained to the wall to be prevented from carving strange symbols into his flesh with his fingernails and teeth. He had taken to raving and whispering unintelligibly in a language that sounded ancient and dark and better off forgotten. For all his skills as a healer, Maturin was powerless to affect his friend's slow decline. All he could do was watch the one-time paladin of Shar as he spiralled deeper and deeper into madness. Finally, when the snows had deepened, and the winter grown as fierce as that previous, another visitor arrived – a man as different from the elf of the year before as can be credited. He was well prepared for the weather, which in any case seemed to affect him not in the slightest, and bore a broad smile upon his lips and a golden symbol of Oghma hanging upon a heavy golden chain around his neck. No sooner had he neared the gates of the monastery than he made his presence and business known in a clear and robust voice.

\"I'm Kennyth Thames and I was told that I was needed here.\"

He was the man the elf had spoken of, an dit was soon clear that the elf had not been mistaken. The Oghman was seemingly able to find the answer to any puzzle or question put to him, given only time. But time there was, and Loremaster Thames availed himself of it both greedily and freely. In the end it took two entire years to save the body and mind of Gaebril Tremalkin, and perhaps in so doing, his soul. True, at the conclusion of Thames' efforts, there was not much left of Tremalkin. Though he was alive and aware, his once-powerful muscles and fair skin had wasted away, and what remained was but a shadow of the man he had once been. In addition, although once recovered he was again lucid and capable of conversation, Gaebril increasingly avoided the society of his friends and hosts. When he had recovered sufficiently, he sought solitude and meditation in a subterranean garden beneath the monastery, which had been built by the dwarves under the direction of Thames. Thus was Gaebril Tremalkin – the Silver Arrow, dragonslayer, Warder of Waterdeep, Hero of Loudwater, Paladin of Selûne, First of Shar, and two times oathbreaker – become a quiet gardener and tender of trees and plants.

== The 'Business of the Gods' ==
Even while saving his long-time friend, Kennyth Thames kept busy at the Monastery of the Adamant Hearth. He spoke often of how he enjoyed the isolated location, and how he sensed that in its halls he could hear the wisdom of the aether more clearly than ever before. A number of times, he declared in wonder that the monastery \"was akin to a knothole into the very halls of the gods - more than akin perhaps! And yet as much use as it is for us in learning their designs, the gods know also that we are here; that we listen and watch and wait. All quite fascinating!\"

The priest of Oghma stayed on for another two years in that place, watching over his old friend, and over time, more and more worshipers began arriving to seek him out. By the Year of the Starving, 1381 by the Dale Reckoning, devotees of the following religions had gathered at the monastery: Berronar Truesilver, the dwarven Mother of Safety; Ilmater the Broken God; and Oghma the Binder of What is Known. Pilgrims from far afield sought out the disciples that had settled for a time on the monastery grounds, seeking the to learn from both Maturin and Kennyth, even as the latter grew ever more focused on what he called, in his own words, \"the business of the gods.\"

During the years of his tenure at the Adamant Hearth, Kennyth had devoted no small amount of effort, thought, or toil into what he termed \"redundancy planning\" in the cells and catacombs that lay beneath the dwarven monastery. Indeed, those pilgrims who came to pay homage to Oghma or to seek out his servant's counsel were called upon to lend their efforts to the great work being undertaken in the depths, under the supervision of the dwarven cleric-engineers. Finally, however, after the very worst winter of living memory - either human or dwarven - Kennyth Thames informed Maturin Horgath that the day of his departure was nigh. Something was coming, he sensed. Like a boiling pot, soon to blow its heavy lid, he could feel a pressure building in the aether. Something terrible lay in store for the Realms, and there was urgent need of his knowledge and ability elsewhere - or would be.

Maturin Horgath was most assuredly disappointed to hear such news, for he had come to see Kennyth as a true friend, but the paladin knew enough to trust the Oghman's intution. There could be little doubt that the winds of change were beginning to stir upon Toril, and even as the servant of the Binder began his preparations, the grey knight, too, felt that the time had come to make his own plans to leave the monastery, and continue on at last to Damara, where he might see to the fate of his family's seat in the Duchy of Arcata. The last thing Thames did before he left was to visit Tremalkin in the subterranean garden. He presented his old friend with an oblong package wrapped in silver cloth, holding Gaebril's gaze one last time as he pressed it into the former archer's withered hands. Much passed between the men, though no words were spoken, and at length Thames clapped the once-paladin on the shoulder in a gesture of friendly parting. Despite his demeanour, however, both men sensed that this farewell would be their last for some time to come.

== The Eyes of the Silver Archer ==

Horgath was taking his time to prepare for the long journey and he expected to be leaving the following day when his old friend, Falcon arrived just in time for the snow in the mountains to be passable once again. Falcon was his usual talkative self but he seemed concerned about the fact that a score of of Sharran priests had passed through Loudwater and were headed north into the mountains. He figured he was a fortnight ahead of them if the wardens at Falcon's Roost managed to turn them away and force the priests to take another route into the mountains. Horgath and Falcon were both concerned by this news, and although Tremalkin was definitely on the mend psychologically, even after the separation between himself and the black sword, the physical toll it had taken seemed to have diminished him physically and the man still preferred to spend much of his time in the subterranean gardens. Falcon and Horgath agreed that it was a matter not worth mentioning to Tremalkin and that Horgath might need to delay his departure for the next couple days, just to make sure he wasn't leaving right when the Sharran's might be arriving.

It was the 19th of Kythorn, 1381 DR when a score of Sharran priests accompanied by another score of mounted, black armoured warriors arrived at the Monastery of the Adamant Hearth. They were met at the gate by Falcon, Maturin Horgath, Drondun Darkshanks (Hearth Warden) and Mincham Goringrimm (fledgling Loremaster). The four of them were approached by a single priestess, resplendent in her deeply cut, black silk robes, trimmed with purple. Her hair was long and as black as the silken robe she wore and she regarded them quietly in turn with her violet-hued eyes before her lips curved upwards in a smile and she spoke in a soft, but rich tone.

\"We are here to collect Gaebril Tremalkin as well as the blade he carries. Please return him and it to us before nightfall.\"  The woman was just turning to leave when Falcon spoke.

\"He's not here...and even if he were, we would not be handing him over to the likes of you or your little friends over there.\" Certainly not his best when compared to his work with The Red Hand of Doom but the priestess of Shar did seem amused by his response as she gave him a polite and lovely smile before nodding her head once, returning to the group of priests and warriors before mounting up and riding back from the direction they came. The four of them turned to find Tremalkin walking from the confines of the monastery, a concerned look on his face only to be stopped halfway between the monastery and the gate by the two men and two dwarves.

\"They are here for me.\" , said Tremalkin. His voice was low, and he spoke as if it were a statement and not a question.

\"We sent them packing. Nothing to fret over.\", smiled Falcon, his attention being drawn up into the sky, focusing on something black, circling slowly in the distance above the monastery. \"Nothing to fret about...and...what...is...that?\" Four of the five gathered in the courtyard were looking up into the sky, their brows furrowed as they tried to make out what it was up above their heads. Tremalkin's attention was still on the gate, his blue eyes intent on the directions the Sharrans had headed in. He looked up at the sky briefly before turning and walking past the four others and back into the monastery before speaking calmly.

\"It's a dragon.\"

Eyebrows were raised in surprise and doubt and their attention was drawn away from the speck in the sky to fall upon the back of the once-Selûne blessed archer; once-paladin of Shar as he disappeared inside the monastery.

== The Emissaries of the Nightsinger ==

That night was darker than most. All of the residents of the monastery were preparing for a fight as best they could. Devout worshippers of Berronar, Ilmater and Oghma are not known throughout the realms for their battle prowess but the residents defended their own and despite the fact that Tremalkin did not worship any of the three gods that were worshipped there, he was one of them and they would not let one of their own fall into darkness. The moment the sun had slipped behind the horizon was when the screams started. Tremalkin was assisting Horgath with his armour when he became wracked with pain, screaming in agony, his body rigid from spasms. Tremalkin's cries were unnerving some of the residents of the monastery and he was carried into the subterranean garden to be tended to by those unable or unwilling to fight.

thumb|300px|The Falcon's Stand
What followed was a maelstrom of violence and death. Tremalkin's assessment was true. A large blue dragon landed in the courtyard only to be met by the united front of priests, paladins, worshippers and Falcon. It would have been a dire fight on its own but what was a dire fight turned into a hopeless one as twenty armour-plated warriors rode in on chargers, all crying for blood. There was a moment, with dwarves and men falling atop one another, bodies cleaved by swords and dragon claws, smoking and smelling from the acrid scent of the lightning that the dragon spat from its enormous mouth. Falcon and Horgath were back to back and Falcon had just swept the head off of one of the Sharran warriors with a backswing of his sword when he turned enough to smile at Horgath and said:

\"I should be able to handle the dragon. You take the rest.\"

Horgath had no time to disagree with the confident warrior and simply continued to hack relentlessly at a Sharran warrior's shield arm until the shield and arm the was holding it were lying upon the ground. Falcon's decision could not have been better timed. What remained of the monastery's combatants was mustered by Horgath and led into the fray with the remaining warriors of Shar, leaving Falcon to battle a blue dragon...by himself. There was no way for Horgath to watch anything but the next enemy in front of him, but an Oghman priest that survived the battle can attest to Falcon's prowess and courage. The warrior went blow for blow with the dragon, its sapphire coloured scales nearly black with the blood that leaked from well over twenty different wounds. Falcon somehow managed to close with the dragon time and time again, with the battle turning when he managed to plunge half the length of his sword into the neck of the great beast. The dragon flung back its heavy head, roaring in pain and anger, pulling the sword from Falcon's hands and sending the warrior sprawling to the ground. The wings of the dragon were spread, the force generated by their flapping sent others in the battle tumbling away and kept Falcon pinned to the ground. The dragon's tale lashed violently and the heavy appendage struck Falcon in the chest, hammering the man between it and the ground, only to quickly coil and swing once more, striking Falcon as he lay upon the ground, gasping for air and clutching at his chest, sweeping his body aside and sending Falcon hurtling through the air and into the darkness.  Falcon falling was dire indeed but the dragon, injured badly and furious launched itself into the sky and flew away, heading deeper into the mountains.

== The Ultimatum of the Hellion ==

A few of the remaining survivors cheered as the great beast climbed into the black night sky even as Horgath cut down the remaining Sharran warrior. He had let out a deep, ragged breath, quickly shifting his wrist in order to flick excess blood from the blade he wielded. He was just turning to go look for Falcon when he heard an unnerving howl and looking back to the gate of the courtyard, Horgath felt his heart sink when he saw the demons coming. Not a single man or dwarf fled and they threw themselves into the battle with the abominations that were yowling with rage and hunger. Demons and what was left of the defenders fell alike until the priests that held back in the shadows grew weary of waiting and some suggest that the battle had already gone on too long and the Sharrans were worried that losing the night would also mean losing the battle. Whatever their reasons, the darkness of the night grew darker still and the lesser demons soon fell back to make room for the Sharran's newest terror. The size of a giant, with wings nearly as large as that of the dragon that had been present before and held a sword the size of a man, wreathed in dark purple flame. It strode up to the gate of the courtyard and stopped, looking down upon Maturin Horgath. Speaking around the large fangs that protruded from its mouth, it had a voice like boulders crashing down a mountainside.

thumb|500px|The demon attacks{{hellion|Bring me Gaucon'Suhl Nerhuar and the Oathbreaker. Bring them now and I will make your sufferings, and that of those still here that much shorter, and death will come quickly.|18}}

It was difficult to see with the shadows that seemed to whirl and twist around the face of the demon but for a moment, its fanged mouth seemed to smile.

\"The blade is hidden away and Gaebril Tremalkin and this monastery are under my protection. Go back to your masters before I send you back to them in pieces,\" was the simple but firm response from Maturin Horgath. The demon howled in pleasure and charged the paladin of Ilmater and the battle began once more.

Eventually, it was only Horgath and the twenty foot tall demon that battled in the courtyard of the monastery. They battled with one another all night and as the sun rose where a few thought the Sharrans might flee with the end of the night, the battle continued on throughout the day until nightfall the following day. The will of Ilmater had kept his chosen upon his feet, battling for far longer than any could have imagined. Where the favoured Ilmatari appeared weary but resolutely battling on, the demon also appeared to  be tiring, it's large hands holding its blade lower and its feet moving more slowly. Even with the divine constitution of Ilmater coursing through him, Maturin Horgath was struggling. He had just managed to avoid being skewered by the massive flaming blade of the demon when he stumbled. Weary from the battle, his foot landed in a hole that had been left behind by the heavy blows of the dragon and Horgath tumbled to the ground. He had quickly tried to push himself off of one knee when the demon's sword lashed out, catching Horgath's sword arm and severing it just below the elbow. The paladin's weapon dropped to the ground with the lower half of his arm still holding it and all Maturin Horgath could do was to stare at the burnt, bloody stump just below his elbow.

== The Excruciation of Brother Grey ==

The demon fell upon the paladin with massive kicks and blows from its clawed fists and as the priests of Shar began to file in through the gate, the paladin of Ilmater was prepared. Over time, he had been staked to the ground of the courtyard with weapons from the fallen and the demon towered over his prone and bound form, howling in joy. The priestess of Shar that had originally made the request for the sword and for Tremalkin crouched down beside Horgath and gave him a sympathetic smile.

\"In time, you will tell us where the blade and the oathbreaker are but I believe an example needs to be made. Tell us quickly, and the sooner you will be at the feet of your weeping god.\"

Horgath said nothing, he simply looked up at the night sky, his eyes clear, and the demon went to work on the paladin of Ilmater. The things that were done to the paladin are unfit to even write about but for the entire night, Maturin Horgath was tortured by a demon from the nine hells and not only did he deny them the information they wanted but he did not even favour them with any cries of pain, or screams of agony. It is said that the torture of Maturin Horgath continued for two whole days before the screaming started and when anyone else would have given up; would have perished, the paladin of Ilmater stayed alive. The demon was frothing at the mouth with fury and impatience. Horgath's body laid upon the ground, broken and bloody, flensed and skinned while he still continued to scream in pain and anger right up to when the demon plunged its huge blade through Horgath's chest, impaling him and pinning him to the blood-soaked ground. The screams stopped and everything seemed frighteningly quiet right up until when a voice was heard emerging from the monastery,

\"That will be enough.\"

== The Redemption of the Silver Arrow ==

Three arrows sped out of the darkness within the monastery and three of the Sharran priests died, their bodies slumping to the ground, their black silken robes pooling upon the ground beneath them.  Gaebril Tremalkin strode out of the monastery wearing simple woollen robes, grey in colour and his right hand was a blur as he reached to a leather quiver upon his back, quickly and smoothly notched and released three more arrows in the matter of a heartbeat. Moonlight that had just begun to filter through the heavy clouds over head, glittered off the heads of the arrows as they sped towards their targets and shortly after, three more priests fell dead.

{{hellion|Oathbreaker!|18}}

The demon roared in satisfaction as it left its burning blade where it was, pinning the corpse of Maturin Horgath to the ground. Its long legs devoured the space between itself and Tremalkin while the once-paladin smiled grimly and turned the bow he wielded towards the demon. The moonlight shone down upon the man and the bow he held, the light dancing along the adamantine and pearlescent etching that ran the entire length of the black, great bow. Three more arrows sped through the air and struck the demon in the chest where the heart would be in any other creature. The arrows sunk into its reddish-black flesh, mere inches away from one another and the demon seemed to smile in pleasure and made a sound that might have been laughter.

{{hellion|I have been promised half a day with you, Oathbreaker, should I bring you to the Dark Lady. You are a gift beyond measure. Having you fight me makes it all the more sweet!|18}}

The demon growled and hissed as it continued to stride towards Tremalkin. Tremalkin's jaw tensed and rather than reach over his shoulder for more arrows, he reached to his hip where a simple black quiver hung from his hip. Only one arrow with black fletching appeared to be in the quiver and as he notched the arrow and drew the bow, he grimaced in pain and tendrils of smoke rose from the hand that drew the string taut. His tall, slender form held still, his form perfect as his nostrils flared, exhaled and smoothly released the black arrow. It hurtled through the air towards the demon and landed true. Piercing the flesh of the demon within the cluster of three arrows that already pierced its massive chest. The demon paused a moment, its step faltering as it looked down at the black arrow that was sticking out of it and then the arrow exploded.

The demon roared in pain as a huge hole was left where the arrows had one been. Black, foul-smelling blood poured from the awful wound and after reeling from the explosion, the demon's wings flapping to bring it back to its feet, it began to run towards Tremalkin, roaring obscenities that involved what it would do to Tremalkin's body and soul once it had them. Tremalkin held his ground and without hesitation he reached for the empty black quiver and as his hand drew close, another black fletched arrow appeared. His hand smoking further and blackening as he drew the arrow and released it once more, tears streaming down his face from the pain that clearly filled his right hand and coursed up his arm. The arrow sliced through the air and finding the charging demon, piercing the demon's eye and sending the demon spinning off its intended course and falling to one knee. Its massive claws frantically scraping at its face to try to remove the black arrow that put the demon one eye away from being blind. Its howls of pain rang off the mountain sides that surrounded the monastery all while Tremalkin reached for his hip once more, moonlight bathing him, glowing in the middle of the darkness that seemed to push all around him. A third arrow appeared from nothing and Tremalkin's fingers pulled it from the quiver. If anything else could be heard over the roaring of the demon, the sound of his grunt of pain and grinding of his teeth might have been heard when his blackened hand burst into flame. Tremalkin held solid as he smoothly drew the bow for a third time. The demon looked to Tremalkin and began to entreat the man that stood twenty paces from it with a notched arrow. It promised riches,redemption and even frantically crying that it would give him back the priestess, Kyria all while it struggled to stand from where it rested on one knee.

\"More lies...Tell...the Lady...I am not...interested.\" , growled Tremalkin through clenched teeth as flames danced along his hand and forearm and he released and the arrow flew. The demon's wings beat furiously and it lifted into the air to escape when the third black arrow pierced its muscled neck, the demon continuing to roar in pain, even as its wings beat rapidly, lifting it further into the air. It looked as if the demon was going to manage to escape its destruction when its wings began to slow and its body began to stiffen and over the course of a handful of heartbeats, the demon's body crashed to the ground, having been turned to stone.

== The Beatification of the Martyrs==

Tears were streaming down the face of Tremalkin as he dropped his bow, his right hand blackened and the fire continued to claim the entirety of his right arm, all while he moved towards the impaled form of Maturin Horgath. He was heedless of the Sharran priests that had been watching in horror, fearful to say anything or draw attention to themselves lest they die where they stood like the six dead priests that littered the ground around them. They slipped away into the darkness as Tremalkin groaned and strained against the demon's blade that impaled his friend's body. Using the last of his strength, he managed to pull the awful burning blade free and let it fall to the ground as he collapsed atop Horgath's form, all while he whispered , \"I am so sorry, friend.\"

Arkos \"Falcon\" Thunderstaff perished in the battle. His body was found near the outer wall, his neck having been twisted at a frightful angle after having been swept aside by the tail of the blue dragon he had fought single-handedly and driven off.

Gaebril Tremalkin, after having collapsed atop the body of Maturin Horgath was consumed by the fire that had started at his right hand and eventually coursed along the rest of his body until he was covered in flame, never making a sound as he was burnt alive where he had collapsed. Yet whereas the flame took hold of Tremalkin's body, the fire never seemed to touch the body of Maturin Horgath. His body seemed to be wrapped in a sheath of golden light and even after Tremalkin's body was little more than a smouldering corpse, Horgath's corpse continued to shine with light to the point that it bathed the entire courtyard in it.

The sole survivor of the battle was one Mincham Goringrimm, Loremaster of Oghma. She had been crippled during the battle and was unable to do anything but witness what had transpired that night. Eventually, the few that had not taken part in the battle for the monastery and emerged from the subterranean garden to find the remains of the battle that had taken place. Maturin Horgath's body continued to shine with golden light even during light of the morning, the glow that came off of him was dazzling.

All of the bodies and items of interest left behind by the battle were taken care of by the residents of the monastery and the glowing body of Maturin Horgath was interred within the monastery in a place of honour.

In time, the Monastery of the Adamant Hearth became known as the Monastery of Sundered Faith and the heroic deeds of the three men, Maturin Horgath, Gaebril Tremalkin and Arkos \"Falcon\" Thunderstaff became known as The Grey, the Black and the Bold.
