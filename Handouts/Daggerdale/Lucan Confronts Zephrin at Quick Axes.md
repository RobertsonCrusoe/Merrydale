Lucan's face darkens, anger and wounded pride flashing in his eyes as he listens to Zephrin's blunt assessment. His voice, trembling with indignation, cuts through the tension.

“You’ve got a lot to say for someone with no lips, friend. I admit, your kind is a rare sight this far from the Sea of Fallen Stars. But make no mistake. I’ve met enough of you dragonborn to know your mind. Always the brooding outsiders, longing for sympathy. ‘Boy?’ Mock my youth if you will, but I’ve lived my life. Can you say the same? Oh, did I hurt your feelings? Beshaba’s Breath! Always playing the victim, you dragonkin. And you talk big with that forked tongue of yours, ‘Killer,’ but I heard you from outside the room, and Mistress Thickwistle here nearly had you eating scraps out of her hand like yon pup.”

Pacing the room now with a fiery Sembian swagger, each step punctuating his words.

“Boy? I am a son of the House of Alavandor, sir! For a self-proclaimed scholar, you embarrass yourself by not recognizing the name. There's not a coin clipped or clean goes though Saerloon that doesn't pass through my family's hands. That’s Saerloon in Sembia! And who stands before you but the sharpest scion of the house?”

At that, he whirls to face Jeska, his eyes ablaze with fiery resolve.

“And you, milady... oh, pardon me, ‘Solicitor Jeska.’ Don’t think I haven’t seen through your ruse. Looks to me like the law here is as bent as it is at home. Not that I’m surprised, but I know a shakedown when I see one. Free room and board and oh, by the way, that’ll be 100 nobles to line my embassy’s coffers. Pfah! Myth Drannor crying poverty, is it? No matter.  Rest assured. You'll get more than you bargained for from Lucan the Lucky.”

Lucan keeps pacing, talking half to himself now, bruised Sembian ego giving way to cold, calculating avarice.

“Gold, yes, that’s on the list – gold and what it commands. In fact, I’m pursuing my family’s interests. I’m hardly in the habit of sharing family business with strangers, but am I likely to get my share from the Redjacks or Whitecloaks? No, by Shar’s Snatch, this is my play. So, I’ll tell you straight. I’m after my family’s legacy. A treasure that would beggar anything you could fathom, assuming a brute like you could even see past his own scales. Gold – gold you can count. But this is a treasure beyond counting. And I’m the only one who can find it.”
