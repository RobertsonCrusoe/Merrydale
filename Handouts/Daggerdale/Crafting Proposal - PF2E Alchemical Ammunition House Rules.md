1. # Creating Alchemical Ammunition

## Rules as Written

* No RAW for creating alchemical arrows.

## House Rule Proposal:

**Method:** During **Alchemical Crafting downtime**, Alchemists can create **batches of 4 single-use alchemical arrows/bolts**. These require a formula for a Level 1 alchemical bomb, as higher-level bombs are too unstable for this use. Characters without the **Specialty Crafting (Bowyer/Fletcher)** feat may still attempt to craft alchemical arrows, but take a –2 circumstance penalty on their **Crafting** checks when doing so.

**Bomb Compatibility:** Only Level 1 alchemical bombs only (e.g., alchemist's fire, acid flask, bottled lightning, frost vial) can be converted into alchemical ammunition.

**Effects and Risk Trade-Off:** On a normal hit, alchemical arrows deal normal weapon damage and deliver their primary alchemical effects to the target, but without splash damage. Splash damage occurs only on a miss or critical hit, centered on the target's square, while a critical miss causes self-splash.

**Game Balance:** The increased range is balanced by the loss of splash damage on a normal hit, ensuring bombs remain superior for consistent AoE, while arrows offer a precision alternative

## **Rationale**:

This rule allows archers to use alchemical effects, filling a gap in existing rules. Limiting it to Level 1 bombs keeps it a low-damage, flavor-focused tactical option, preventing high-level bomb arrows from breaking action economy or damage scaling. The trade-off is precision over splash: primary effects hit the target directly without splash damage, while splash occurs on a miss or critical hit, with critical misses risking self-splash. This creates a unique ranged alchemical attack that complements, rather than replaces, thrown bombs, enhancing gameplay for archer-alchemists.

2. # Doctoring Ammunition

## Rule As Written

* Applying a poison to a weapon or single piece of ammunition requires one Interact action, uses one full dose, and remains viable for 1 minute or until it hits a target, with no skill check or risk of self-poisoning.

## House Rule Proposal:

**Method:** Apply alchemical poison or contact coating (i.e., not bombs, elixirs, mutagens, or ingestibles) directly to arrows/bolts.

**Crafting Check:** Requires **10 minutes of downtime** (cannot be performed during combat). Attempt a Crafting check (DC 15 \+ the poison’s item level). On success, roll 1d4 to determine the number of arrows/bolts treated with the dose. On failure, the dose is wasted. On a critical failure, the character must attempt a Fortitude save against the poison’s DC or be exposed to the poison.

**Specialty Crafting Synergy:** Characters with the **Specialty Crafting (Bowyer/Fletcher) feat** gain their usual **\+1 circumstance bonus (+2 at Master or higher)** on these Crafting checks when doctoring arrows.

**Timing:** Doctoring arrows requires **10 minutes of preparation outside of combat** and cannot be performed during initiative. This represents carefully preparing the arrows for safe and effective delivery without rushing.

**Application:** You can treat up to the number of arrows/bolts rolled on the 1d4 in a single session; **the dose cannot be split to treat fewer arrows now and more later**. Each arrow expends its portion of the dose on a hit or miss.

**Viability:** By default, treated ammunition remains viable for **1 hour** after the end of the doctoring session. Characters with the **Specialty Crafting (Bowyer/Fletcher)** feat and advanced **Crafting proficiency** (Expert, Master, or Legendary) know how to fletch and seal arrows with stabilizers and precise techniques that prevent the poison from drying out or sloughing off, extending viability to 1 day (Expert), 1 week (Master), or 1 month (Legendary).

**Game Balance:** This system enables poison use by archers while introducing meaningful trade-offs: a chance to waste expensive doses and a risk of self-poisoning balanced against the efficiency of spreading doses across multiple arrows. It rewards preparation and specialization while preserving PF2e's tension and resource economy. The RAW option for **single-arrow, single-action in-combat poison application** remains available if players wish to act tactically.
