==Banitt's Perspective==

Banitt had his own feelings about the way that the Deadnettle Affair played out, much opposed to those of Sergeant Warburton.

<i>
:The White Cloaks – an arm of the Whitegates, of Saint Ardelusk himself – have been established in this area to fight for the people. To ensure that no deed of the Sharrans will negatively impact their lives or livelihood.

:It was his understanding upon joining that the good of the many is valued above the good of the few. This IS NOT a mercenary group. Those given the Cloak will put the needs of others before their own personal gains. There are many mercenary outfits at the Castle which one may join if monetary gain is their only goal.
</i>

===Accusation===

In response to the accusations of our Sergeant Iolus, Banitt composed an impassioned, if somewhat hasty, letter to the Captain of the White Cloaks, Lumina Whitegate.

Captain;

It was seen in the course of our mission that our sergeant put his personal needs before the good of the Dales. His mother, under suspicion by local clergy was not only protected by the sergeant, but her livelihood was bolstered by his use of rank. He demanded that she be given exclusivity in the making of candles for the town, using his status as a White Cloak to make such demands. Is this why the White Cloaks were established?

Of note on the Sergeant, of whom his family history is sceptical at best.

He was seen to hand over his Badge to another in order to protect them from persecution... a badge he did earn for his heroism... those given the badge for protection did not however earn it. For this I submit that Iolus Warburton be questioned to the extremity of the law for having undisclosed dealings with Shar.

To further this request, it should be known that Sergeants Warburton did make negotiations with a Dragon. A dragon with whom he spoke and which he allowed to remain within the Dales, living upon his family farm under some \"treaty\".

In faith to Amaunator and His holiness the saint I submit my formal request that Iolus Warburton be put to question for dealings with Shar. He is a spy within our ranks and should be dealt with forthwith.

With this missive I hereby return my Cloak and disband myself from any dealings with the White Cloaks. I shall venture another pilgrimage. I should think to Waterdeep. There to spread word of the Saint, and ensure the heresy within the White Cloaks of the Dales is known.

Yours in Faith,

— Banitt Harbaugh}}
