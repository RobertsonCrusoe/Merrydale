=The Cloudy Peak Embassy=

The dead of winter is a difficult time to travel in the mountains for many, but not for one of your tribe; your people are hardly impaired at all by freezing winds and 6’ snow drifts.  Thus, it was decided that your mission would start in just the type of storm that keeps most agents of Netheril out of the mountains.  It was time for you to head to Castle Radiance to pledge your allegiance to the Sun Blades and the Knights with which your people have had a very tenuous peace for nearly a decade.  It was time to give your people hope for survival.

Last year was the hardest time in the living memory of your people.  Yours has always been a small tribe of a dozen families or so, but last year your numbers were cut by a quarter.  The encroachment of the Empire of Netheril into the northern extent of the Desertsmouth Mountains forced a protracted and bloody engagement with many different minions of the Shadow Bitch’s Empire, but a new and terrible strategy was devised by the Netherese that hinted at their resolve to maintain an indefinite conflict.

In the closing weeks of summer, they deployed a new type of agent, the Kir-Lanan; winged beasts of the Shadowfell that had but a single malign purpose; they flew into your village on several occasions and snatched up any child they could.  Though they lost dozens of their number, in the end, the abominations were able to fly off with every single child in the tribe.  The bodies of these children would be found far away, dashed upon the rocks, having been simply dropped unceremoniously from a great height.

The elders met to discuss the consequences of so focused a scheme.  The terror and tragedy of the attacks were self-evident.  But the chief of the tribe, your uncle, pointed out that the real objective was to weaken the otherwise resilient tribe a generation from now, not today.  The realization that the enemy had such far-reaching plans made the elders agree to a formal peace with the Dalesfolk.

“Go to them” you were ordered by your uncle.  “Offer them our unconditional peace and allegiance.  But what’s more, show them our worth.  Fight alongside them, and show them that our people are strong warriors who will make valuable allies.  Learn from them, and let them learn from you.  Stay with them until you are called back.”

Simple orders from a simple man.

Months later, you found yourself at the Castle.  You quickly found a place among a cadre of heroes called the White Cloaks, under the competent leadership of a Daleswoman named Lumina.  You find the strict religious structure of the castle to be almost alien.  Even more alien, but also grimly reminiscent of home, is the fact that there are no children at the castle.  For all the military might and discipline at the castle, you had hoped to hear the voices of children again.

When Lumina asked for volunteers to head into the heartland of the Dale, you put your hand up before anyone else, though you were hardly aware that you had done so before she called your name.  You smiled though.  You would go to their Town, and maybe their farms, and see families and children again.  And you hoped you wouldn’t make a fool of yourself when heard them laughing and saw them running, by joining in their merry making.

And if, by Nicias, something threatened those children too, you would bring all the fury of your people down around them.  You would show the Dalesfolk what it means to make an enemy of your people.
