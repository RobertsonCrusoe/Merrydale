The Judgement of Tyr
DM Handout - Ribalest's Tomb
by Les Blackwell

---

A great statue dominates the eastern wall of this chambre.  It would seem little more, if not for the fact of your having read about it, and for the bit of blue cloak sticking out from the stones that betray this is a door with a puzzle for a lock.

The statue is of a priest sitting cross legged.  His arms rest on his knees, and in each hand is a small bowl.  The priest's eyes are covered by a cloth, and his mouth is gaping open.  The cavity of his mouth appears conspicuously to be the same size as the 9 large round marbles scattered about the chambre.  Though it is difficult to read due to age and filth, an inscription on the robes of the priest reads "Judge Twice, Sentence Once".

Perception DC 20:

The hands holding the bowls appear to move up and down slightly with weight, like a scale.

Society or Religion DC 25:

This is a scale of Tyr.  You will have only one chance to attempt the lock, once you start putting marbles in the bowls.  You must find the one marble that is out of balance.  If you fail to do so, you will never be able to use the lock again.

The Door Opens:

"As the correct marble seats in the mouth of the statue, the jaw closes, and with the hiss of stale air, the statue begins to swing out towards you, revealing a 20' x 20' room beyond.  The room is in good condition, with the exception of a skeleton on the floor, who's blue cloak is caught on the statue.

"The orderly room is a supply room, with 2 large chests, 2 large wardrobes, and a fine mahogany table in the middle sat upon a fine circular rug."
