The Emissaries from Candle Keep hold two auctions every year, selling off many of the magic items that they procure throughout the year from their Candlekeep Registry Bounty.

The first of these is held during the day of Midsummer, which is a feast-day at the Castle, and one of the main attractions for the festivities of the day. The second is held at the end of the adventuring season, during The Feast of the Moon (the day between Uktar and Nightal.)  

These auctions are carried out in Madigan’s Hall, in the Inner Bailey, under intense security and scrutiny. Despite the added security (which the Emissaries pay dearly for), there is an air of festivity about the event. In fact, during the Midsummer auction, there is a tent outside the hall that serves drinks (the one day of the year that excessive drinking is permitted), which has lead to colourful bidding wars and banter between bidders and the auctioneers.       

The Midsummer auction typically sees about 10-30 items on the block, but the Feast of the Moon auction is the one to be present for; with much of the adventuring season behind it, this auction produces nearly 100 items, and it is a well known event that draws travelers and entrepreneurs from distant lands.

 
Admittance to the auction costs 1 gp, and comes with a numbered bidding paddle.

==Ches 29th, 1481 DR - Lord's Auction==

In 1481, by order of Lord Whitegate, the first auction of the season is to be held on the 29th of Ches, rather than Midsummer.
