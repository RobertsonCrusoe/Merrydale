      <text xml:space="preserve">This is a farm found towards the southern edge of the dale, just 15 miles north of the Tethyamar Bridge, at the junction of the Old Tethyamar Trail and the Dagger Hills Road, leading to Castle Daggerdale. There has always been a farming village here since men first inhabited the dale, and today it is one of the more secure farms in the dale. There are over 200 people living at this compound, which has a permanent garrison of Sun Blades, a thick stone wall, a fortified monastery and permanent Magistrate.

The residents of this farm are no strangers to the troubles that have plagued Daggerdale through its long and tragic history. It was from this farm that the vampire infestation that gave the dale its harsh name originated, and the lycanthrope infestations of the Dagger Hills have always been a part of everyday life for the residents here.

This farm is well protected, especially with the added elements of Sun Blades and the presence of the Church, but it has something of a more pastoral and rustic feel than the thick stone walls and the stiff collars of the clergy might suggest. People here have long venerated Chauntea, and continue to do so. They attend most services of Amaunator when they can, but Chauntea still factors heavily into the lives of the people here. One aspect of Her worship is the consumption of alcoholic beverages distilled from the fruits of the land. While the farm isn't exactly a place of wanton revelry, the visitor can expect a good deal more wine and ale to flow than in other places in the dale, and also expect people to be a little more free with their tongues, and generous with food and drink.

Defenses

The farm is secured by a thick stone wall 15' high, which runs around all the main buildings of the compound. It has 4 small towers which are manned by 2 soldiers around the clock. There are two gates into the farm which are fortified buildings with portcullises, and men permanently on watch. The larger of the two gates faces south, where the two roads converge. It overlooks the fork and the activities there. It is here that most of the trading for the community takes place with outsiders, and where most caravans and travelers are allowed to stake tents and tether animals.

Today, the residents are just as hard as they have always been, but also have the benefit of a permanent garrison of elements of 7th Company Sun Blades. This includes a full strength platoon (3 squads of 12 men and a lieutenant) of fairly capable and well disciplined soldiers. The Lieutenant is a man named Randall Eversmoore, whose family are successful farmers and land owners at the Dwimmerholme Farm, north of Dagger Falls. He has a respect and appreciation for the lifestyle of the people here, and has had a spotless record of service here for 2 years.

The militia here consists of nearly 40 men who are retired from service in the Sun Blades, as well as about 40 more young men and women who are capable with axes and long bows. There are drills conducted by Lt. Eversmoore once every tenday to ensure that the militia are sharp and ready for trouble.

The heart of the defense of the farm is the fortified monastery which makes up a large portion of the eastern wall. The monastery accommodates the garrison, and has the highest towers upon which the best archers are always stationed. In the most desperate of times, the citizens of the farm are expected to fall back to the monastery to make a final defense, but to date, this has never been necessary. 

Religious Figures

• Magistrate Madrick - Madrick has been stationed here for 5 years, and has a good rapport with the citizens here. He has always had a bit of a struggle here with the 'new faith' because the locals here have always had so much investment in Chauntea, and continue to do so. Madrick is a typical Magistrate; kind, disciplined, unwavering in his duties.

• Cultivar Marabella - Marabella is the highest ranking cleric of Chauntea at the farm. She is a handsome woman of about 40 years of age, though she looks much younger, whose family has always lived in the area for as many generations as she can name. She is a busy woman, with a large base of parishioners who remain devoted to the Goddess of the Harvest. She has a good relationship with the Dawnlord, but she has many reservations about the growing power of the Church of Amaunator in the dale, and in her community in particular. She has 6 young women in her clergy who are training as clerics. 

• Chaplain Hammerstone - Hammerstone is the chaplain for the Sun Blade platoon garrisoned at Crossed Blades. He is a veteran of many campaigns in the mountains, but is getting a little too old for the hiking and cold air. He is enjoying his assignment in the warmer valley of the dale, where he is well fed and enjoys the rural life.

Economic Activity

The farm seems to do well year after year, and the credit for this horticultural success has long been given to Chauntea and her clerics. The farm harvests many crops from the old fields that surround the compound in a 2-3 mile radius, including all the usual sorts of things for this region; grains, root vegetables, potatoes, etc. There are also 3 small apple orchards, each with a different varieties of apple. There are several grazing fields for livestock, including cattle, sheep and goats, but the people here raise no horses; they maintain that horses are bad luck to husband, and though they stable some, they do not raise any.

The farm has always grown enough food to not only feed the entire population very well, but also to trade in Dagger Falls. A large group of farmers and armed guards heads into town once every tenday, joining up with the Sun Blade Dragoons come through the village, heading back to town.

There is some small cottage industry here including the following; 

• wool spinners and dyers
• leather workers and tanners
• potters
• a furrier
• a lumber mill
• a blacksmith (who makes great tools, but few weapons; he makes decent arrowheads, spear heads, has one design for pot helmets, one design for long swords, and one design for small shields.

• a winery/distillery (this place, called Chauntea's Smiling Brook, is a bit controversial in the village; the Church is always trying to discourage people from giving it patronage, but it seems to do quite well despite that)
