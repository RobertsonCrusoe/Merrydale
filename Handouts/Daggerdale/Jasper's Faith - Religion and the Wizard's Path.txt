Jasper's Faith - Religion and the Wizard's Path
by Gus Miranda (as Jasper) and Todd Burry (as Mari)
Email thread: "Jasper's faith", 11-12 May 2025

A spin-off from the Coventina's death discussion exploring Jasper's relationship with religion and its contrast with Mari's Yondalla-centric halfling faith. Rich character development for both.

TODD (12 May 2025, 10:12 AM):

I didn't know a lot of this! I can certainly relate to Jasper with my Catholic upbringing ;)

Mari would view Jasper's perspective on religion as quite human-centric. Although that also reflects her own halfling biases. I like to think of the human-centric view of fantasy, where humans can be varied, while demihumans are more of a block.

Yondalla has no temples and very little organized worship. She is the halfling mother and only desires that halflings thrive as a people. To Mari, living as a halfling and living a life with family and friendship is honoring Yondalla.

Her efforts to gain more agency for her people are part of her faith, but she is likely viewed as a rare bird. She looked at her daughter leaving home to join the Sunblades as scandalous, yet when her husband died and she got cooped up with her daughter, she ended up doing the same. There is a stronger anti-halfling sentiment in Tesh Valley Farm, and Mari saw that being friendly supplicants of the humans doesn't secure real safety. One lean year can create a wave of resentment towards the halflings, where the humans can see that the halflings always seem to live comfortably, no matter the harvest.

I think Mari would love speaking to Jasper about religion. She would also be more than happy to pray for more spells to learn more of the spirits Jasper wants to lay to rest. She would be sticking by his side to comfort him in his anguish, and will undoubtedly be casting gentle repose on Coventina to help her body return to Dagger Falls with dignity. At the end of the day, Mari thinks of Jasper as a good, kind boy and wishes Lucan could be more like him.

Key lore points:
- Jasper's parents are both dead (he rarely discusses this); his brother Maynard is also dead
- Jasper's mother was a devout Chauntean; he was forced to attend Chauntean church growing up
- Jasper hates the Amaunatori specifically
- Jasper came to Daggerdale originally to study the ghosts rumoured to be in Thistle Ridge
- Core philosophical divide: divine magic requires subjugation to a higher power; wizardry leads to the wizard becoming the higher power
- Mari's faith: Yondalla has no temples, desires only that halflings thrive; living as a halfling IS worship
- Mari's daughter left home to join the Sunblades (scandalous by halfling standards); after her husband Bramble died, Mari did the same
- Anti-halfling sentiment exists in Tesh Valley Farm — humans resent that halflings always seem comfortable regardless of harvest
- Mari will cast Gentle Repose on Coventina's body for the journey back to Dagger Falls
- Mari "thinks of Jasper as a good, kind boy and wishes Lucan could be more like him"
