17 Tarsakh – The Limestone Court Narrative

As Contessa Calix is cut free from the vines entangling her, the nature of the Limestone Court begins to change immediately. The Home Lights slow and pulse dimly for a moment, before returning to life. They seem to flock to Calix, who smiles and waves her hand playfully through them for a moment, before they once again drift away to different parts of the sylvan grotto to cast multi-coloured hues through the now-peaceful place. The motes of light, while still interested in you, seem to do you no harm.

In the moments that followed the rescue of Calix, the changes in the Limestone Court were palpable and amazing. The bodies of the fallen Grimples, twigjacks and even Dol'Rullian himself seem to be absorbed by the place itself, and there is soon no evidence of them left behind. The Home Lights now fill you with a peaceful sense of mild euphoria, and the sound of water dripping and trickling into the cool pool in the centre of the grotto sounds often like the music of tiny bells.

The six small sprites, including your newly bonded sylvan wives, Masra, Jesra and Embra, fly excitedly into the grotto, their joyous and chaotic flight paths through the multi-hued cavernous space bringing them quickly to the small hut you are all standing in with Calix. Their joy, and that reflected in their Contessa is palpable and intoxicating. For Jasper and Lucan, the joy of their bonds fills them with emotions they have truly never experienced before, the unfiltered joy of the Fey very near its zenith. This makes the young men incoherent for a short while. Indeed, Ser Rowan and to a lesser extent, Mari, are all filled with a hazy happiness akin to a very good smoke, and Jeska too is delighted to see the reunion of the Contessa and her court of sprites.

Very quickly, Calix realizes the bond between three of your company and her court. At 4' tall, she is nearly double the size of the sprites, and she gathers the three bonded sisters in a warm hug, releasing them to kneel in front of Jeska.

"You have done my Court a great honour, sister. You connect your Court and mine with bonds that we hold sacred. You have risked the lives of your people to save me, it is plain to see," Calix says as she takes in the state of your company, her eyes staying long on Ser Rowan whose armor and clothing have suffered damage that seem inconsistent with the survival of the flesh beneath.

"We will talk long tonight, Sister, you and I. And Embra; I doubt she will leave your side even if I command it" — and indeed, the red-tressed sprite lighted softly on Jeska's shoulder immediately after hugging her Contessa when she flew in, and has not left since. Though 2' tall, her form is lithe and is no burden to the small elf upon which she is perched. Embra is delighting in weaving their similarly-coloured hair together, while listening to Jeska and Calix.

"You have many questions, as do I, and all will be answered to the fullest. Let us rest now by the waters, and feed and heal our Courts. Come…"

With this both 'Courts' head down to the little pond, where a table and chairs await, and smooth rocks to recline upon. Baskets of berries and mushrooms are soon brought to the table, as well as some very old metal goblets, human-made and worn, but clean and serviceable, are filled with an amber liquid from an old human wine bottle that is likewise worn. The drink is Fairy Meade, you find out later, and it has great healing properties, though it intoxicates the humans (and halfling to a lesser degree) much faster than anticipated.

Indeed, Jasper and Lucan haven't finished their cup before they are being led off by their bonded sprites. The Contessa and the other sprites laugh and clap as their sisters depart, and their joy washes over all your company like the sun coming out from behind the clouds.

Jasper and Lucan are in for an experience that would leave them changed forever. The raw joy and emotion of the sprites at being reunited with their liberated Contessa coursed through the young men like a powerful spell that grants strength, speed and courage, and they soon discovered that returning this energy with any affection at all created a loop of feeling and experience that was impossible to not escalate to greater intensity. In separate private corners of the grotto, the bonded pairs spent quite some time together, each discovering in their own way sensations undreamed of before.

At the side of the pool, reclining on a comfortable stone settee with a basket of berries and a second cup of Fairy Meade, Ser Rowan is being tended to by Calix and her Court, who carefully pluck splinters from the man. He enjoys the bounty of the Fey, the Meade making him oblivious to the painful ministrations of the creatures doing their best to treat his seemingly endless wounds. "I wonder what they're up to," Ser Rowan asks with a slight slur, looking lazily around the grotto for signs of his two companions and their bonds. "How long will they be away, Contessa?"

Calix smiles and the pixies giggle. "They will be as long as they want, brave knight. And what they are up to is between them and them only."

Some time later, the swaggering Sembian and his dark-haired, blue-dressed bond return to the side of the pool. They are flushed and spent, and Lucan declares "welp…that veritably ruins all other female company!" to which his bond, fluttering over his shoulder responds with a mischievous grin and says "tell us all another lie, Stone Tree. I love it when you lie!"

With wounds tended, bellies and hearts full, Calix leads Jeska and Embra back up to the small hut on the escarpment. Therein, they sit together cross-legged for many hours, in deep discussion. They have much news to share, and many things to learn about each other, and the unseen bond between the Fey and the Elves becomes clear for a time. They are in deep communion with each other, and when checked upon by this man or that, it appears that they are sitting in silence, though Jeska comes away much later with a trove of intelligence to share with her company. This is what she learned;

Calix has been here for 30 years. She would be considered 'young' in her realm, and indeed she could be mistaken for a 20-year-old halfling girl, if not for her wings. She is from the First World (the Fey) and was drawn here by the people settling the area, much like her dark counterpart, Dol'Rullian. She is a Pixie, and her Court is composed of six Sprites that are by nature peaceful though sometimes mischievous. They call her 'Contessa' and follow her orders.

The Limestone Court itself was quarried by men long ago. Calix is not sure how long, but at least 100 years ago. She chose the space to settle for two reasons; she likes how the quarry was hidden by small hills and knew she could create a sylvan grotto nearly impossible to find, and she wanted to be nearby the people and protect them. She knew there was something dangerous in the quarry, and indeed shows Jeska a blocked up opening that has been deliberately and completely covered by the Fey-growth of the Court. The opening is about 8' wide and 7' tall, and is filled in with sturdy stonework. Calix knows that some men over the years have come nearby looking for it, and she feared that the kind farmers of Greenwood might accidentally find it. Her Court has kept it hidden and safe the entire time she's been here.

A few days ago, just as the first trees were being hewn at Greenwood Farm, Dol'Rullian came through the Viel from the First World to sew violence and mayhem. He came to the Limestone court with his Troupe of Nuglubs (Vainya and Vallia), Twigjacks and Grimples, and demanded that Calix help him eradicate the humans, as he had done decades before here, as well as many other times in other settlements in distant lands. When she refused to help, Dol'Rullian imprisoned her, took control of the Court, and began his campaign against the people of Greenwood. His Nuglubs were given leave to sew torment as they saw fit, and to use the Grimples as they wished. Vainya, of course, established The Blue Glade, from where she hoped to wage a campaign of trickery and subtle violence, before she was stopped by Chrysalis.

Dol'Rullian knew about the blocked-up passage, and desired to explore it. He told Calix that they would find great allies and great power in the dark. He was unable to damage even a single stone, and thus commanded Vainya to capture the mason and his tools.

Calix isn't sure what is beyond the blocked-up passage, but suspects that it might lead to the Shadowfell. Dol'Rullian was obsessed with the powers that Contessa Palus had gained from her alliance with the Shadow. He spent several hours tormenting and interrogating Calix about where Palus might be found, but she would not reveal anything about the hag of the Sorrowbog.

Calix explains that though Dol'Rullian was destroyed here, he was not completely eradicated. His essence has returned to the First World, where he will be plotting his revenge. He is no fool, and will not use the same form twice against Chrysalis, whom he will no doubt consider his arch-nemesis. His escaped minions (a pair of Nuglubs and a pair of Twigjacks) will likely be reconsolidating and plotting with Dol'Rullian from beyond the Curtain, and are likely to be encountered again once they have grown their strength and courage. Dol'Rullian himself will likewise return when his is strong again. Calix does not know how to destroy Dol'Rullian for good, and explains that Contessa Palus is likely the only one with that knowledge on this side of the veil.

Of Palus, Calix knows quite a bit of great interest. Contessa Palus has been in the area for more than 200 years. She is a hag who has lived in the Sorrowbog for most of that time. She commands all mean things in the swamp, and the goblins, including the Yellow Tooth, revere her and do her bidding. Calix met with Palus once nearly 20 years ago. Of her meeting she says exactly this;

"When I came here, I knew about Palus, and her sister. They were the Contessa of the Fen and the Contessa of the Grove, she and Quercina. They coexisted peacefully for more than 100 years. Then the world changed, and the Shadow came. Palus allied with the Shadow; she gained great powers, but lost so much to gain them; indeed, she had a daughter and a granddaughter, and these she sacrificed to the Shadow, and other daughters besides. She used her powers to gnaw at Thistle Ridge from the inside, and her treachery was such that she tricked a girl inside that farm to play a cruel joke on her friends. All sacrificed, so terrible. Palus' daughter, granddaughter, the girl from Thistle Ridge and her 3 friends. So cruel and unnatural.

"But the men of Thistle Ridge are also to blame. They profaned the Waters and the Grove. Not the original men and little Sisters, mind you. The new men. They closed up the Grove, and they drove the Waters to the depths. Palus cackled about it when I met her. I sought her out to see if she knew where to find Contessa Fluvia and Contessa Quercina, and she said 'the foolish men came, looking for the place where the water would meet the roots, and they found it, or made it, or both. They delighted in their profanity, for it served their purpose. They came to fix a curse of generation on their lines, placed on them by the Shadow. But they were undone by the Shadow in the end. The Shadow through me!' She finished the story by returning to Fluvia and Quercina. 'The new men drove them off, even if they didn't know it, and when the Shadow returned to the small lives of the new men, their forces rent Harngorn and poisoned the water, and Fluvia and Quercina have been powerless since. I pity them, especially because of my role in their diminishment, but I am powerless to leave and help them, and you would be a fool to undo what the Shadow wishes done.'

"This is a complicated story, and I've never put it together exactly. Some of the men of Greenwood remembered Thistle Ridge, and I knew their daughters and their friends. They were all my friends. From them I learned that there was some trade and kinship between Greenwood and Thistle Ridge before separate events destroyed both at different times. The connection between the new men, the hag, the girls, the dead daughters and grand daughters, the profane temple…it is too confusing for me, and I can't keep it lined up in my mind very well. I just don't understand how shadows and men and hags can get so tangled." Noticing Jeska's eyebrows arch at the mention of the profane temple, Calix offers this;

"You know the place. The small profane temple in the morning shadow of the small hills sunrise side of us. I say profane, because I think that also means 'secret', and the Greenwood men never knew about the temple. But Palus was always trying to lure the girls of Greenwood to the temple, and I think probably Thistle Ridge too, but that was done before I arrived. I was always scared of the place. It has the smell of Shadow on it, and I don't like the stone ladies. I think one looks like Palus, or like Palus did. I don't know who built it, but it was here before me, and it was built in that spot to hide it. Men don't naturally wander there."

Of Palus' powers and goals today, Calix offers;

"Palus does not realize it, but she too was sacrificed, along with her daughter and grand daughter. Crone, and girl and girl-of-girl, all destroyed utterly through dark covenant with the Shadow. Little is left of Palus herself now, except hate and cunning. She works mostly through her Court of thralls; Fey and Shadow unhallowed and intertwined. Like you and your court and mine, but degraded and cruel. She watches all the land, as you know, with a magic not entirely different from mine." With this, she fingers the Bond Jeska wears around her neck; a simple natural amulet of elegantly entwined sticks which you now realize the similarities with the other fetishes you've found and destroyed in several places.

"Palus' chief is Charrah, or Charrah is her chief. I am not sure. But they hold Thistle Ridge together. More importantly, Palus it is that holds the spirits of three girls in Thistle Ridge, and if you are to free those girls, you will one day have to face Palus to end her magic."
