<big>The Unjust Rule of the Assassin-Lord Dynasty of Waterdeep</big><br>
A Brief History Lesson about the Rise of Tyranny in Waterdeep

The publication you hold in your hands, educated reader, is a collection of historical facts intended to illustrate how Waterdeep came to be ruled by the Tyranny of Might. Though Waterdeep boasts being the City of Splendours we are ruled by a body that amounts to no more than a secretive nepotistic tyranny. The facts will speak this truth to you, reader, and will open your eyes to such injustices that you may have never suspected. Read on, and open your mind!

== 1256-1273 DR ==

At this time, Waterdeep was ruled by a large body of Guild Masters. The head of each guild sat on a council that made all decisions for the City. They were a large body, with 35 Guild Masters sitting council. This number removed a great deal of fickleness from the Laws of the City, and helped to ensure that many perspectives on all legal matters in the City were addressed. Certainly, there were more opinions on the Council then, than there are now, as we have but one Open Lord – and a dozen Masked Lords. More on this will follow this historical primer.

It was during the time of the Rule of the Guild Masters that Waterdeep experienced its greatest growth. During this brief period, the walls were expanded twice, and the City doubled in population. Commerce was at a peak. Indeed, it has only been recently, some 100 years later, that we are just beginning to exceed the volume of trade that we did in those golden years.

The City streets were safe for the most part. With a population the size of our modern day census, we were able to keep the peace with 1/8 the number of guards! And records indicate that there was less crime! Today, under the rule of the Lords, there are twice as many people and 10 times as much crime.

Towards the end of this Peaceful and Prosperous Age of our City, the Lords began their plotting. They hatched a clever but sinister plot to overthrow the Council of Guild Masters, and regrettably, they were successful. They began by forming an organization known as the Shadow Thieves. This organization practiced all brands of villainy, including theft, rape, murder, extortion, smuggling, arson, and others besides. They masked their crimes as Guild in-fighting, to upset the rule of the Council.

Indeed, by 1262, the Shadow Thieves had done such a smashing job of bringing terror to the streets of the City, that the Guild Council began to argue and fight amongst themselves, as many fell for the deceit of the Shadow Thieves treachery. From this time on, the period of growth and peace that Waterdeep had been experiencing began to dwindle. The Council was plagued with trying to fight the Shadow Thieves, who were adept at turning the Council members against each other with their crimes and deceit. When mistrust became endemic, members of the Guild Council started to disappear. It is clear that the Shadow Thieves began to assassinate the Council members, which only made things as bad as they could possibly be. For a while, the City was in a state of anarchy, as the Council hid, trying to save their necks.

In the end, two Guild Members alone, survived; Ehlem Zoar of the Gem Cutters' Guild, and Lhorar Gildeggh of the Order of Master Shipwrights. It was clear at that time that Ehlem was in league with the Shadow Thieves; the Gem Cutters had suffered the least in the Guild War - despite the fact that they presented the best target. Lhorar Gildeggh, a noble from an Old Family, survived only on his Courage and Bravery alone. He alone came to face Zoar in the Palace when the dust had settled.

Though Gildeggh could not prove the alliance between Zoar and the Shadow Thieves, he did not shirk from duty. None of the other Guilds would elect Masters at that time, for fear of the assassin’s blade, but Gildeggh faced the assassin’s every day, in the bloody Palace.

For years, the two remaining Guild Masters, Zoar and Gildeggh ruled, side by side from the Palace. Though they agreed on nothing, and argued most aggressively each and every day in Court, they endured each other. Many attempts on the life of Gildeggh were made, but he survived, and by his virtues as both a man of achievement and quality, recognized by his election as Master of one of the most significant Guilds of Our City, and by the Qualities of his Noble blood, did he bring Waterdeep back into a time of Peace, in those years. The years 1262-1273 were those ruled by the two diametrically opposed men, who were called the Lords Magister of the City.

It is to Gildeggh’s credit that he was able to bring the City back to Civility, whilst sitting beside a veritable viper who sought to rule on his own, with an army of assassins and rogues in his ubiquitous shadow. Aside from the chaos and turmoil of 1262, the City did again find Peace and Prosperity for the next 9 years.

== 1273 DR: The Last Assassination ==

On Mirtul 9, 1273, two assassins came to court, wearing the robes of Ahghairon’s dead regime; the masked robes of the Lords as we know them today. One of them was a powerful sorceress who unleashed a magical barrage at the Lords Magister, destroying them utterly. The destruction this assassin invoked that day was enough to completely incinerate the bodies of the Lords Magister, and blast their thrones into small bits of rock. This wanton destruction claimed the lives of other innocent Courtesans as well, though this has been completely removed from the history books.

These two assassins claimed to be Masked Lords, previously under Ahghairon’s Authority, and they claimed rulership of the City. One Lord unmasked himself, revealing himself as man known as Baeron. The other, the arcane assassin, remained faceless behind her mask for quite some time. This was vital to the rule of the assassins, you can well imagine. While all could look upon the face of Baeron and know him for their ruler, they would never know the face of the arch-assassin. This fear of the unknown assassin was a vital tool for keeping the Court and the People in the position of slavery that they are still in today.

It is important to note that by the end of the year, the Shadow Thieves were completely defeated. Many had hung, many had fled, and many just vanished. At the time, this achievement was credited to the effective rule of the returning Lords, but it does not take a scholar to realize the convenience of this disappearance. The Shadow Thieves had been put away, much the same as the Smith puts away his tongs and hammer at sundown.

==1273 to the Present Day: Rule of the Assassin-Lord Dynasty==

After the cold-blooded assassinations of the Lords Magister, there were soon again a host of Masked Lords, with Baeron ruling as the Unmasked. It was at this time that the system of  that we have today was implemented. The same system of arbitrary appointment was used to put Capable Magistrates in place. Of course, we have seen how this system brings nothing but corrupt and greedy men into office. Again, there is no system of trial or election for the appointment of these Magistrates, and indeed, they are the most important people in our lives. The Masked Lords may rule at Court, but in our daily lives, it is the influence of the Black and Terrible Magistrates that we feel most.

The system of appointment of Masked Lords continued, as it had in Ahghairon’s days. Baeron appointed 11 more Lords to sit Court with the Assassin-Lord. Baeron wed this mysterious Assassin-Lord in the year 1276, once the system of Fearsome Magistrates was in place, and the terror of the unseen, unknown Assassin-Lord was no longer needed to keep the People terrorized enough to be docile and accept the unjust rule of the Lords.

The coupling of Baeron and his Assassin-wife, now known as Shilarn, produced a girl-child named Lhestyn. Though the People did not know it, Lhestyn was appointed as Masked Lord by her parents at the age of 18, when surely she did not have the experience needed for the position. Such blatant nepotism is acceptable only in archaic lands such as Cormyr, and would not have been permitted in Waterdeep, had the People known.

But the Rule of Waterdeep was about to become a Dynasty that we are still under to this day, as the patient and wise reader shall soon see.

In 1302, Lhestyn, then a Masked Lord, wed the Neverwinter Mage, Zelphar Arunsun, and gave birth to Khelben Arunsun, who is known to this day as The Blackstaff.

In 1308, Baeron died of old age, and the crazed Assassin-Lord, Shilarn, knowing only how to deal with problems through arcane destruction, immolated herself. With two Lords missing, and the Unmasked himself dead, it was time for some new flesh in the Court. At this time, Lhestyn herself stepped forward as the Unmasked, and two new Masked Lords were recruited. You would think that the People of Waterdeep would have balked at the idea of a Royal Family in these, supposedly Civilized, lands. But they did not, and this is why; The Halls of Justice had just been newly constructed in Castle Ward, and the understandably grateful clergy of Tyr, then a much more influential organization than today, spread the word far and fast that Tyr had blessed this ascension of Lhestyn to the paramount position of Power in our Fair City. The propaganda was swallowed by the Citizenry just as the hatchling swallows the proffered worm from its mother’s beak, and the Dynasty of the Assassin-Lords was fully entrenched.

The Masked Lords are a secretive lot to be sure, and there are more than a few rumours that circulate about who may or may not be a Lord. In fact, in any public house in our Fair City right now, I challenge you to find one taproom where this very topic is not discussed this very moment. But some do have their identity exposed, long before they unmask themselves.

One such Lord is Khelben \"Blackstaff\" Arunsun, next of kin in the Assassin-Lord Dynasty. To this day he rules, when he is not off chasing his own private agendas, from behind his mask, though he is known clearly for what he is.

== 1314 DR: The Puppet Lord ==

In the year 1314, Lhestyn is killed by an unknown assassin. Belike an educated and wizened Citizen took the affairs of his City to heart, and decided to end the Dynasty of the Assassin Lords. But he or she was not successful. At the death of Lhestyn Arunsun, the Lord Piergeron comes forth as Open Lord, to the horror of all clever citizens. At this time, the child, Khelben, was too young to rule as Lord. Too young, even, by the forgiving and uncommonly biased standards of the Assassin-Lord Dynasty. But his Road to Rulership was paved with the Crowning of Piergeron, a Knight of Tyr. I am sure that the educated and skeptical minds of the City must have roiled at that point. For here was a chance for the Dynasty to end, but a close friend of the Dynasty, the order of Tyr, was put into power, into the very position to bring Khelben into the fold when he came of age, to be sure.

The genius of the appointment of the Knight was that no one would be willing to say that he was an unjust man. For one matter, he is a Knight of Tyr, a supposedly Honest and Just organization, Faerûn-over. And for another matter, it is well known that the Faithful of Tyr have a habit of forming Justice Mobs that seek out the wicked through divine consultation and deliver justice on-the-spot to those they find wicked enough. And if every once in a while, someone not-so-wicked gets brained and buried in the spectacle, more's the pity. The wise reader will agree that when you see a mob of angry Citizens marching in the Van of a Litany-bellowing madman with the Book in one hand, and the Rope in the other, it is best to stay out of the way, regardless the colour of your soul!

So, Khelben’s Road to Power was cobbled, and no mistake. Certainly, though he does not rule with an exposed face, he does rule this day as a Masked Lord.

== Where’s The Justice, Then? ==

For a City ruled by a Paladin of Tyr, you would think this the most Just Place this side of the House of the Triad. Surely, if you are to be ruled by a Dictator, it is best to be ruled by a Just Dictator, is it not?

Were this so, I should think Waterdeep, the City of Splendours to be a much different place. I would think it a place where Justice cannot be bought, though the luxuries of the world over may be. I should think that the streets would be free of ruffians, to some extent, anyway, and that a man could walk in a fine neighbourhood at High Sun, and not fear for his life.

These things are not so.

Why must the noble houses employ 70 armed men each? Why do they seek to have more? Why are they denied a larger standing guard?

Why are there more deaths every night now, than there were 100 years ago? Are we not more civilized?

Why were unfathomably large taxes placed on the luxuries coming from Maztica? Why are all these ships now sailing to ports in Amn, Calimshan and Baldur’s Gate? Where does Khelben spend his time when not Ruling in Waterdeep?

Why were the racial taxes dropped altogether, when more commercial taxes were introduced?

Why is there no one accountable for the ubiquitous and insidious problems that plague our City today, when every other Civilized State on the Sword Coast has accountable government?

== Bring an End to the Dynasty of the Assassin-Lords! ==

Piergeron is not a young man. He will not sit at Court as the Unmasked Lord of Waterdeep much longer. When he is gone, who do you think will take his place? It is commonly known that The Blackstaff is a Masked Lord, and so it is only logical that he step forth. This makes the intelligent reader wonder just how hard Khelben has tried to keep his identity secret, does it not?

If The Blackstaff is allowed to step forth as the next Unmasked Lord, how will our Civilization be any different from the Secular-Minded Mulhorandis and their God-King – or the astonishingly antiquated Cormyreans and their house of Azoun? What could the alternatives be?

Presently, there are no alternatives. Soon Piergeron will step down or pass away, and the Blackstaff will take his place. Waterdeep will continue to stagnate, while our neighbours to the south continue to grow. Soon, every coin traded in Waterdeep will be clipped for the profit of some Amnian merchant house or Calimshan Pasha, as their gilded trading houses line our crumbling streets. The Lords do not seem intent on steering us from this course.

Waterdeep is in need of more accountable government. The City needs men and women in Power who are known, and are therefore vulnerable to reprisal for any mismanagement they inflict upon the City. The City needs a council that is diverse; it must be made up of people from the many different ways of life that our Fair City offers.

The time has come for a form of the Council of the Guild Masters to be reinstated. The time has come for our nobles to take a more active role in the City than drinking and whoring their ancient fortunes away. We need to form a Ruling Body composed of two houses; The Guild House and the Noble House.

The Guild House should be made up of representatives from each Guild, be they the Guild Master or no. It may well be that a man suitable to Master a Guild might not be fit for City Council, and so, each Guild should elect its Council Man of the Guild House.

The Noble house should be similarly comprised. Politically astute members from each family should be placed in Council to help direct the affairs of the City, alongside the Guild House.

Today, this is just an abstraction. But tomorrow, when you read the death toll in the Trumpet be sure to bend your mind towards what you have read here today, Gentle Reader.
