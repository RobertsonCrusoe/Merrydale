Following the fateful encounter with Quellar Dalael which precipitated the drastic realignment of the Waterdeep underworld that occurred in the summer of 1373, Garth Shanks lays out his suspicions as to the motivations and machinations of the nefarious Dalael.

== Summing up ==

I will now briefly go over my thought process regarding this tricky mess that has just come up.

Of course I felt enraged to begin with as I have already written previously. However, towards the end of the battle I was quite terrified for my life. If one of Quevin’s men can do this to me then what about many of them together? I honestly drafted a mental letter resigning any claim I may have to this city to send to Quevin if he would just leave me alone.

Now that I am more clear-headed I have a more moderate train of thought, but all in all this is of no consequence. Right now I must figure out what is going on and attempt to manage consequences.

=== What happened ===

Now that I think about it I see that someone in the Dalael machine set this whole thing up. Let me lay it out so that I myself have it straight.

* Yesterday morning Thebald is essentially herded towards my influence by a couple of Drow who could have easily caught him.
* Thebald in turn leads myself and Beljuril to the Moonstar warehouse.
* The deal that is going on there is something that I have a personal hatred of, which is known by few, but even so it is a fair subject for my contempt due to any other number of events of last year.
* The whole business at the Moonstar warehouse was of course the stated reason for Quellar’s valiant effort at teaching me a lesson.

But despite my displeasure with the outcome of the event I do not truly believe that Quellar was trying to slay me, but rather to teach me a dangerous and humiliating lesson.

This of course can be answered if Beljuril agrees to study the corpse tomorrow. If not I may have to look to see if Gregor is in town for I do have a few questions. Thank goodness that as I understand it the spell does not reveal any of the personality of the person that was attached to the corpse, for we may not get anything useful otherwise. Man, what a lunatic! He would not even listen to reason before swinging his hanger. However, it is just such behaviour that belies he was defending some prevailing folly, and preserving himself from the ingenuity of owning a mistake.

=== Who benefits? ===

So now let me look at the suspects of which at the moment there are only three.

*1)My first thought would be to go with Quellar, the perpetrator. It is well within his personality as I now look at my file on him. Let me make a little addition under “is bloodthirsty.” How should I put this? Ah yes: “is dead”. Anyway, Quellar could have easily set up a situation to allow him to carry out his bloodthirsty intentions. However, my main bone of contention here is that I should think that Quevin and Isillith could more easily control their hound.

*2) Quevin comes next on the list for the simple reason that I do not think it is him, but rather the coxcomb Isillith. But before I go on I will write out my reasons lest I forget them. Quellar himself, now that I recall, said that it was Quevin that sent him here (and is indeed another question for Beljuril or Gregor). And that has all probability of being true. I can easily imagine Quevin even being as sloppy as to give his degenerate brother free reign over the interpretation of the phrase: “Teach him a lesson.” I can also easily imagine the opposite though. However, why Quevin should set this up is not apparent. He needs no thin veil of fair play; he’s the boss of his family. Furthermore, he could not possibly believe that the Moonstar incident merited really anything, let alone a violent insult. How long has he been in this business anyway? I cannot wholly rule Quevin out though. There might be some reason yet unknown for his doing this. He could also be doing it for pure style points. Another possibility is that he updates his actions as he reads new pages of the encyclopedia, if he is indeed reading it at all.

*3) So the last on my short list, although as I have written there could be others, is Isillith. And Isillith to me is the guilty party so to speak and I will write of my reasons presently.
**a)First, he is the messenger between Quevin and I. He can tell each of us whatever he wants us to hear. He could have, for example, easily told Quevin: “Garth accepts the deal, effective immediately.”
**b)Second, Isillith also delivered the news of Thebald which at least gives him definite knowledge of the ruse. And the fact that Thebald was a tool used to arrange this whole ordeal is unquestionable.
**c)Third, Isillith is not the major power in the Dalael family and would need to employ such guile to achieve an end, whatever his reasons may be.
**d)Finally, and most importantly to me is that I just get the sense that this is exactly the type of thing that the dandy would want to do. Isillith just seems like the type of person who enjoys employing knavery for the sake of pure mischief if for nothing else.

Now, let me justify this a bit, again for myself as much as for someone that may read this when I am dead. I know more than most that in this city, indeed this world of deceit that most times the words we say are just meant to pass the time rather than to convey any actual meaning. It is because of this that one must look at the measure of a man rather than what he says. A lesson I wholeheartedly admit that I learned too late with Thagdul. Now this is my personal theory, and as an admitted bluff I must also admit that my opinion is biased on the matter. But in the end I stick by this theory and that is that.

I will pose a small aside presently. Quevin or Isillith, I am a bit relieved that they were willing to sacrifice their brother for their little game. I was beginning to think that the bad guys (so to speak) were more unified and honourable than the good guys (us).

Thus I have my theory. If I am wrong then I am wrong and I have a bunch of lunatics on the other side. If I am right then we still have a dead Quellar, which, I perceive, when mixed with followers of Vhaeraun, is like as not to produce a result that may be similar to a bunch of lunatics, previous paragraph notwithstanding.

If on the other hand the Dalaels are still open to diplomacy then I may be too. At least to the extent that I may break my word when it is convenient for me, not they thank you very much. However, I cannot be the one to initiate diplomacy for I am the injured party (dead Quellar not withstanding). Moreover, me initiating diplomacy is a sure sign of weakness which I surely cannot afford right now.

Therefore, there is a trick to be played here. I cannot triumph if I must go through Isillith’s treacherous tongue to get to his master’s ear. So I have decided to play a game that I believe will have the desired effect for my purposes and satisfy my current mood of bitter comedy.

== A Paper Gambit ==

I have presently sent the following ad to be printed in tomorrow’s paper. The ad itself is a satire of sorts, but I will explain that after I have given the ad:

::G.,

::Your extraordinary behaviour this morning, and the liberty you were pleased to take with me and my territory, makes me this evening give you this, to tell you, because you are an ill-bred puppy, I will meet you outside the very paper office where this is printed on hour past midnight hence; and because you want both breeding and demi-humanity, I desire you would come with a hanger in your hand, and endeavour to stab me through the heart to teach you your manners. If you fail of doing me this pleasure, I shall say you are a rascal, on every post in town: and so, sir, if you will not injure me more, I shall never forgive what you have done already. Pray, sir, do not fail of getting everything ready; and you will infinitely oblige, sir, your most obedient humble servant, etcetera and so on.

::—Q.

Thank luck that the paper is in fact right beside my home and I have a special influence with them for just that reason. I can hardly contain my smile at the simple trick of it all. Why tell someone that his reason for attacking you was base meaninglessness when you can write him saying the same thing about himself in the tone of a buffoon? And if you can put it in the paper, this modern implement of opinion, then all the better I say.

I also find it quite convenient that both Quellar and Quevin both begin with the same letter so if it was in fact Quevin that ordered the assault then he can take it to be himself rather than the other man.

My main goal is to make Quevin know that I see his assault for what it is. And if the charges were in fact trumped up by Isillith then he will have a hard time explaining my little article won’t he? But what if Isillith manages to keep this paper from Quevin? Well that’s a tricky one Garthy my boy, but is still useful because it can only mean that the seat of power has shifted solely into Isillith’s hands (and that if I do not want Quevin to know a thing then I should endeavour to hide it in the most public place I can find).

Now I must wait for an answer. And if that answer is more violence then I have my own barn full of sociopaths that wish to slaughter Quevin and his darkie brood, indeed they may anyway. On the other hand, if that answer is diplomacy then the bluff will continue for a time.

== Shanks on Bluffmanship ==

There is an art to constructing the bluff. For a bluff, no matter the shape or size, must be entirely plausible. Common sense I know, but the subtlety is this — if there is plausibility then it will sew doubt in any man regardless of who he trusts more on a quality for quality basis. Now taken with a businessman that doubt must grow all the faster because he is unencumbered by such fancies as friendship or family, or at least he only views them as a means to do business. Take that away and the relationship is revealed for the tenuous thing that it is.

And when you look at it everyone are businessmen in their own ways. Acting to get what you want and not what anyone else wants and “thank you very much, don’t let my boot hit your arse on the way out good day.” There is no truth except the game. For truth or fiction: it is all bluff in this wretched city of splendours you know?
