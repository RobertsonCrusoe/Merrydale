“You’ll pay for this, Alavandor!” The wheezing townsman, lying prone under an upturned cartload of produce, shook his fist at the swiftly retreating figure of a lanky young man in a blue cap. The figure stopped, turned, made a lewd gesture, then took of running up a side street.

***

Several hours later, Lucan strolled through a postern gate into the courtyard of Alavandor Place, a villa his father and aunt had recently purchased and converted into a place of business. Doffing his cap to greet the other boys loitering in the courtyard as they waited to run errands for their master, he frowned, wondering at their refusal to meet his eye. At length a husky boy lounging against the trunk of the towering stone tree at the center of the courtyard sighed, looked over at him, and then sniggered as he broke into a smile.

“Your da’s right pissed, Luc” the boy, Jerth, called over. “Had a visit earlier from Councillor Hansible. He looked right pissed, too.”

Lucan grimaced. This was the last thing he needed today. Did the old ferret know? He’d been very careful to keep both of the Councillor’s daughters in the dark about each other, but if their father found out…

“Lucan,” the calm but irresistible female voice of his Aunt Haelra stole insistently into his ears, drawing his attention to the main portico. She stood at the top of the marble steps leading down to the courtyard, her blue merchant’s chemise and trousers blazing cerulean in the westering sun. “Come here, boy.”

Haelra Alavandor, the “Seahawk,” was an imposing woman. She shared the same salt and pepper hair as her brother Alavandros, as well as a quiet and confident authority that all around her sensed. “Your father and I have something we’d like to talk to you about.”
