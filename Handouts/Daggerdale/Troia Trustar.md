==Background==

Troia was born in Scardale under Sembian rule, which in turn meant she was subject to the rule of the Netherese empire.  From a young age, Troia had been identified as an attractive girl and it seemed her lot in life to earn no small amount of attention because of her attractive appearance.  Unfortunately for Troia, much of that attention was of the unwanted kind. During one whole tenday, much of Scardale town had been scoured for young girls to be taken away on slave caravans and Troia, her two younger, human sisters and her step-mother took their leave of Scardale like thieves in the night, vowing to never return.

Troia and her family ended up in the pastoral valleys of Featherdale and although it didn't take long for Troia's step-mother to find a suitor that was more than willing to provide for the older woman and her three daughters, Troia found herself chaffing under yoke of an farmgirl's life.  When Troia was sixteen years of age a group of traveling entertainers came to Troia's settlement during Highharvestide.  Troia was dazzled by what seemed to be their dynamic and carefree lifestyle and the next day when the entertainers left; the simple settlement of farmers in Featherdale found themselves short one lovely half-elven girl.

By the time Troia was seventeen she was well on her way to becoming a skilled singer, dancer and acrobat.  She had taken a lover; a bard by the name of Padishar who instructed her in the ways of entertainment and a year after she had run off from her simpler life, she had become Troia Trustar.  One of the lead attractions of the Crimson Cavaliers who were gaining notoriety in the Dales for their performances.

Life was never boring for Troia.  She was adored and sometimes revered when the Cavaliers returned to settlements they had been to previously.  They were becoming wealthy enough that there were discussions regarding a chance to take all their coin and head south with in into Cormyr where it was believed that they could make far, far more coin and many of them could retire in luxury.

When Troia was twenty-one the Crimson Cavaliers were performing in Shadowdale when they happened to cross paths with a performing troupe of Heartwarders.  Priests and priestesses of Sune who entertained with song, dance and poetry all while doing good and accomplishing things for the good people that they encountered.  It wasn't the first time Troia had crossed paths with organized religion but she was enchanted by the grace, beauty and truth that the Sunites displayed so readily.

She had spent all the time she could speaking to the Sunites, learning of their ways and their perspectives on life.  Two days passed and when the Crimson Cavaliers left Shadowdale, Troia Trustar was still with them but her view of the world and her place in that world was beginning to evolve.


She began to worship Sune and she took beauty to an entirely new level while traveling with the Crimson Cavaliers.  She would pray to Sune for guidance and wisdom but her prayers were unanswered.  She continued to perform and her newly found grace, skill and beauty was allowing for the Cavaliers to make even greater profits than they had before but the more Troia prayed and the more she was denied any signs of the divine, the more she began to change her mindset as well as her motivations.

Her lover Padishar began to become distant, growing weary of her desire to speak of doing good for the Dales and doing what was necessary to make the Dales a place that was worthy of Sune's attention.  When Padishar finally took another lover, Troia was fully absorbed with what Sune wanted of her and during her twenty-third year during Highharvestide Troia's prayers were answered.

She felt Sune.

She knew Sune.

She began giving the audience back their coin or encouraging them to donate it to good causes.  Her performances although still beautiful and compelling became opportunities for her to preach to the people watching them.  Although appreciated by some, it began to chafe those that ran the Crimson Cavaliers.  Repeated requests for Troia to refrain from speaking to the audience during and following her performances were ignored and although members of her band of entertainers were growing irritated they still found it difficult to remove her from the group since The Crimson Cavaliers without \"Troia Trustar\" simply wouldn't be The Crimson Cavaliers.

It was decided that a change was needed and with their coffers filled to the brim with coin, they began to head south from Daggerdale with Cormyr being their destination.

The Crimson Cavaliers became a legend in Tarsakh of 1479.  As they approached the Bridge of Tethyamar they were assaulted by bandits and slain to a man...with one exception.  Troia managed to escape and continued to run south, across the bridge with tears streaming from her face and blood streaming from her wounds.  An escaping woman, although tempting was of little value to the bandits when there were wagons full of coin to be collected.  A day after the destruction of The Crimson Cavaliers, Troia was found by a merchant caravan heading north to Dagger Falls.  She was recognized by a number of the guards that were paid to protect the caravan and she was soon being tended to by the caravan's priest of Tymora.

Troia was devastated to have lost her companions that she had known for nearly a decade.  People that she had grown up with and been moulded by.  Arriving in Dagger Falls was of little solace for her and after the caravan had been dissolved and she had politely and graciously declined offers to travel with other caravans, she found herself wondering what Sune had in store for her.

Daggerdale was a place that had fought back against the corruption and evil that had once held it in it's grip and it's theocratic mindset appealed to her on a number of levels.  She was surrounded by hardened people that believed that their god would lead them to victory even as they were being beset upon by orc and the overwhelming darkness of the Netherese to the West.  Amaunator was driving Daggerdale but Troia felt that these dauntless people would also need inspiration and beauty if they hoped to prevail against the evil arrayed against them.  She needed something else to belong to and she was convinced that Sune wanted her to be in the very center of the fight at Castle Radiance.  She was packed and prepared for the journey the very next day and accompanied the next patrol that was making their way there from Dagger Falls.

*Troia's real mother is Eladrin and has never met her.  She has often wondered about just who her mother might be and why her mother abandoned her and her father but she is never one to dwell long on unpleasant things.  Her father met her step-mother when Troia was four years old, bringing in one younger sister and twelve months later, another sister.  Shortly thereafter, her father, a simple shop owner was killed in a dispute over pricing by an unsavory group of individuals.  This left Troia to be raised by her step-mother.
