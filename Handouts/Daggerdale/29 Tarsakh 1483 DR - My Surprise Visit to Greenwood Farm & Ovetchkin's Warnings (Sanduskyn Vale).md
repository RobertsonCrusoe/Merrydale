29 Tarsakh, 1483 DR – My Surprise Visit to Greenwood
Farm & Ovetchkin’s Warnings
I was reluctant to make the trip to Greenwood farm. It is very busy in my shop before the

Greengrass celebrations, and for the most part, I prefer the sights, sounds and safety of the town,

but it had been a long time since I was out of doors, and the small caravan resupplying the

Sunblades was happy to have a wizard in company, given all the dreadful things that have
happened in the Dale the last couple of months.

But I’m happy I went. The weather was fair, with cool spring air, a rejuvenating sunlight and the

sounds of birds all round, and the company was just as good. Under contract to the Sunblades to

resupply the platoon helping my friends, Chrysalis, The Standing Stone League sent it’s most

reliable caravaner, Captain Hardrada, to deliver the next months’ supplies to Greenwood.

Hardrada is a good man with many rare tales, and makes excellent company on the road. Knowing

my lack of experience in the wilderness, he is a good mentor and aid, pointing out this important

geographical feature and that, and making sure I don’t put a foot anywhere I shouldn’t.

Ovee was happy as you can imagine, even though he prefers to fly about at night. He flew overhead

keeping an eye on the road ahead when he wasn’t distracted by a field mouse or garter snake.

Indeed, his belly was full by the time we made Gribbons, and he sat a while on one of the wagons

while we turned north, heading through the soon-to-be-golden fields of that fine farm.

I think if not for the nature of my familiar, I
would have found the forest daunting.
Even with most of the trees still abud, and
mostly naked, it is dark and unwelcoming.
For the most part. On the other hand, we
passed by a camp of men, a mix of
woodsmen and soldiers, who were busy
clearing and ditching a damp stretch of the
trail. They were friendly and glad to see us,
though not so glad as we were to see them;
we have enjoyed the fruits of their labour
for a couple of miles now, with a good trail
that even the heavy-laden wagons traverse
with ease. For Ovee, Cormanthor is
heavenly. The tall dark oaks allow him to
fly with nearly-perfect cover, and friends
and prey are plentiful.

By 6 bells of the evening, we made
Greenwood Farm. The state of the
settlement surprised me; I understood this
to be a brush-choked ruin just a few short
weeks ago, but today, there is a fine clearing, a tall stone house with a roof, a sawmill, running

water, and the start of a monastery complete with a bread oven. There must be about 100 people
here already, and the place is a hive of activity.

It wasn’t hard for me to find my friends; they had returned only a little while earlier, having been on

some terrible mission that I wouldn’t press them about; Ser Rowan’s new armour had many nasty

gashes, and the garb of nearly all the company showed evidence of terrible wounds restored by

powerful magics. I know that they were going to the newly-discovered Temple of Jannath, but I

wasn’t interested in that, for now. I was simply here to show my support in person, which I think

says a great deal more than a letter (the likes of which Ovetchkin can deliver for me anytime). The

commitment to come in the flesh was also good for me; I got to see first-hand what life is like on the

frontier, and get some appreciation for the lack of security out here; for despite the fact that a stout

                                                        company of FLD soldiers lead by a storied

                                                        leader is here, and Chrysalis themselves,

                                                        there are no walls, or even watch towers.

                                                        The settlement sits on a naked hill that has, I

                                                        have learned, already had its share of
                                                        unwelcome visitors.

                                                       But the view from the small hilltop is

                                                       extraordinary, and no doubt one of the main

                                                       reasons it was chosen for settlement in

                                                       whatever decade that originally happened.

                                                       From here, one can look to the west and see

                                                       the majestic Desertsmouth Mountains, and

                                                       even the smoke from Dagger Falls and some

                                                       of the other farms besides. To the east, the

                                                       valley yawns a promise of adventure and

                                                       opportunity through a pristine landscape.

                                                       But looks can be deceiving, as I’ll get to in a

moment. What can also be seen from here, only a few miles away, is a great plume of black smoke

from Thistle Ridge Tower, which I understand is now in control of Chrysalis by way of their platoon

of Sunblades. And a few miles east of that, small pillars of smoke bely the reason we are all our
there today; the occupation of Thistle Ridge by the Yellow Tooth Clan.

Working my way through the camp to the main building, where I found my friends, and met many

friendly settlers and workers, and was astonished to find so many Cormyrean settlers. It is the

Cormyreans that are building the monastery at the eastern edge of the farm, and they are an

industrious and friendly people, with many tools and no weapons (aside from Faith).

I found my friends in the large and open central room of the new town hall. They were with Ser

Reginald and a few other officers from the FLD. They were going over correspondence from the

Aide de Camp in Thistle Ridge Tower, and scrutinizing their maps carefully. I think they were happy

to see me, though they were distracted at that moment by one of their chief concerns in their Grand
Venture; “What shall we do next?”
I am honoured to say that they shared a little intelligence with me, though Boxfray was guarded. I

think without Jasper there, I would have been taken for nothing more than a meddling wizard, which

might be very near the mark I should confess. But I had one bit of help to offer; they were at pains

to guess when the hammer would fall on Thistle Ridge. Birchgrove had warned the day before

about Yellow Tooth reinforcements coming from the Sorrow Bog, and it would be helpful for them
to know exactly when they might arrive.

“Perhaps I can help” I suggested. I think they were surprised to hear that a book-binder from Town

could augur the arrival of the enemy, until I offered “Well, not me exactly, but Ovee. He’d be

delighted to have a look and report back, and I think he’s just finished his nap, and will be ready to
fly all night.”

They took the help offered, and by morning (after a night of catching up with my friends, and sharing

some professional advice with Jasper), Ovetchkin came back with all the intelligence needed, or so
I hope. He informed me of the following troop movements;

   •   There are 3 hosts of goblins on the move
           o The smallest is about 40 goblins, most riding their nasty dogs, NNE 3 miles of this
               very position
           o The next largest is about 100 goblins with a mix of terrible beasts that is patrolling
               the hinterland north of Thistle Ridge itself
           o The largest is currently moving through the swamp, and is several miles south

               Easthaven Inlet – they are marching apuprose to Thistle Ridge – more on this in a
               moment
   •   Flying over Thistle Ridge itself, he was attacked by other intelligent or domesticated birds of

       prey, and did not get a very good look, though he says some hundreds of goblins are there,
       spread out over many camps, with a
       great concentration around the
       largest standing building in the
       centre of town

Of the host coming out of the swamp, Ovee
knew we were chiefly interested. He risked
getting closer to them, to get as much
intelligence as possible, and learned the
following;

   •   They are the Torn Cheek Warband,
       and number nearly 200
   •   They have been assembled by a
       witch in the swamp to help the
       Thistle Ridge goblins retake the
       Tower
   •   They have several troll-thralls in their
       command that tow a siege engine
    •   They will set siege to Thistle Ridge Tower the evening of Greengrass

The last bit of news was the hardest; trolls and Greengrass. The best celebration-day of the year in
our dale will be spent for our heroes in a desperate battle against evil.

As I write this, it is early in the morning of the 30th, and Chrysalis has a very hard decision to make.

“What do we do next?”
