Life is cheap, but it's hard to say goodbye to beauty.

The gods don’t care. A truth that belies the claims of every priest from Icewind Dale to Unther. They watch, perhaps—amused, bemused, their celestial fingers tapping against the armrests of their divine thrones—but they don’t care. They are beyond caring. That’s the lot of mortals like us.

Jasper cares. Too much, as I’ve told him time and again. He commits his soul to the cause of every doe-eyed waif with a handsome bustline. It’s a shame “All you need is to fire off a few magic missiles to clear your head,” I tell him. “Show the girls your wand and dazzle them with a little fireworks. Sune’s slit, Jas – that’s all they need, too.”

Even so, Coventina’s death has hit him hard. A shameful waste, but… well… there’s plenty of eels in the muck, as old Mudge used to say.

Still, could we have saved her? When I saw that Shadovar bitch - Loki until someone proves otherwise to my satisfaction - across that blasted pit, the only thing I could think to yell out was that "Rumtum is right!" The hound was looking straight at her. Time was short. I had to be concise. I can't blame my companions for their lack of imagination. They didn't grow up with old Boney. The way he’d sniff out contraband in a barrel of salted fish... but I digress. Even if I'd put an arrow in her first, I'd simply have sealed the girl's fate earlier. Saved her a few moments of despair. Or maybe desperate, futile hope. But no, there was no saving her. She might as well have been dead already—no, no foul on Lucan.

But why did she die? Coventina Cassimar. No mage, no priestess, just a soul unlucky enough to be useful. One life, one bright spark. What care the gods for a spark when fires rage across the spheres? See above. We’re the ones trying to count the sparks, to save them before they wink out forever. Deneir's folly, this attempt to save every scrap of meaning. Every half-told tale and unsolved riddle.

Not that we’ll ever stop…

So, now we come to it. That orb I saw Loki grab before she disappeared. What was the prize she claimed we knew nothing of? What manner of device was it? [insert description of what Lucan alone saw]

Three possibilities come to mind, though it’s as sure as Shar’s shit stinks there’s a fourth that brings them all together, given the twisted, arcane excesses of the Shades and their ilk. Let me take them in turn.

I.  The first is that it was a Slave Orb to the Imperiosus Orbis (IO). Let's lay out the facts as we know them:

Origin and Discovery:

-   The IO was excavated from a Netherese ruin in the former desert of Anauroch by Melagaunt’s Curator, Loki Mouvranesh, in Mirtul of 1479 DR. LM had been working the excavation for some time when she was confronted by Mule Company (later part of the White Cloaks).

Composition and Requirements:

-   As reported to us, the primary system consists of two main orbs, each 3 feet in diameter, both being necessary for its function.

-   Each of these two main orbs requires a powerful cleric or mage for its operation or attunement.

-   There are at least three associated Slave Orbs, each 1 foot in diameter.

-   The use of the IO system involving a Slave Orb reportedly requires a sacrifice being made to empower the Slave Orb.

-   The sacrifice must be a spellcaster. (Or perhaps a certain type of deployment calls for such a sacrifice).

Known Deployments:

-   According to the reports we were, Belladonna used Slave Orbs to bolster powerful rituals at Liberty Hill in Mirtul 1480 DR, almost exactly three years ago. These included the Ritual of Transcendence, which turned most of Liberty Hill’s adult population into shadow slaves of Shar. Only children and a few resistant adults (mostly Amaunatori clergy and the Whitegates) were spared.

-   Days later, the Ritual of Corruption summoned a malevolent entity known as the Soul of Corruption, which asserted control over the enslaved populace. This entity (or the Orbs) required continuous blood sacrifice: victims were eviscerated and hung from the Table of the Sun so their dripping blood could susstain the ritual.

-   The IO was also used to create the Ritual of Obfuscation that cloaked Liberty Hill in illusion.

-   The IO was used to darken the skies during the Siege of Fort Lastsun, preventing sunrise to aid the Netherese assault.

-   We heard from the Daggerdale authorities that the IO (through a Slave Orb) directed the Purple Worm and gargoyle attack on the arena in Dagger Falls at the end of Ches, 1483 DR.

Inferred Capabilities:

-   Environmental Manipulation: Control of natural forces, particularly weather and atmospheric conditions (e.g., commanding winds for excavation, darkening skies to block sunlight)

-   Remote Power Projection: Through the Slave Orbs, the IO can channel its tremendous power remotely, allowing the implementation of complex rituals at some distance (reportedly up to 1 km) from the primary artifact

-   Creature Command: The system can summon and bind creatures, allowing the control various monsters and beasts (purple worms, gargoyles), extraplanar entities (Soul of Corruption), enslaved humans (Shadow slaves), and undead to carry out coordinated attacks

-   Ritual Amplification: The IO serves as a force multiplier to enhance dark magical rituals, including those associated with transformation (Ritual of Transcendence), large-scale illusions (Ritual of Obfuscation), and the maintenance of ongoing magical effects (Ritual of Corruption)

Alleged Purpose:

-   The main IO system was originally designed by the Netherese for defensive purposes.

-   The Slave Orbs, however, were intended for offensive actions or mischief.

-   The implication is that the Slave Orbs are a more recent elaboration of a pre-existing system

Status of Components:

-   The Slave Orbs used at Liberty Hill were captured after the Saint's Battle in Kythorn 1480 DR and were taken for examination.

-   The main IO system remains in Netherese hands. Until recently, its last known deployment was in Flamerule 1480 DR at Fort Lastsun

-   It was confirmed to be actively "in play" – along with at least one other Slave Orb – following the calamitous Purple Worm attack in Ches, 1483 DR.

-   The implication is that at least one Slave Orb is or was in the hands of an enemy agent active in Dagger Falls.

So was the orb I saw a Slave Orb?

Arguments in favour:

-   Proximity to a complicated illusion

-   Sacrifice (Coventina's murder was deliberate, not opportunistic)

-   Size (?)

-   Context . (weeks after a Slave Orb was supposedly used to attack a tournament intended to choose a team to reclaim Thistle Ridge, an orb considered important by LM is found in Thistle Ridge. Hard to imagine that there’s no connection)

Arguments against:

-   Previous activations of the Slave Orbs have been large-scale rituals. No such ritual or effect was observed here. The orb could have been charged by proximity to Coventina’s murder, but there was no evidence of such.

II. The next possibility is that the orb was a Tenebrius Ostium as summarized in the report penned by Zoderick Winters (ZW). Here is what we know from his report, which is the only source we have:

Origin

-   Tenebrius Ostia (TO) are Netherese artifacts that function as permanent area denial weapons, creating self-sustaining rifts to the Shadowfell. These devices appear to be designed for long-term territorial control through supernatural occupation.

Activation

-   TO require a uniquely specific sacrifice to activate, namely the sacrifice of a "pure soul that has done an impure deed."

-   The victim cannot simply be murdered—they must first be corrupted through deception or manipulation, then killed while experiencing the trauma of their moral failure. Deployments thus require elaborate psychological operations requiring extended preparation, sometimes over the course of many years.

Effects

-   Once activated, the device tears a permanent rift to the Shadowfell, transforming the surrounding area

-   Divine light fails completely, mundane illumination dims to half-strength, and the air fills with drifting motes of ash and shadow.

-   The environment becomes monochromatic and cold, with sounds muted as if heard underwater.

-   The infestation spawns incorporeal undead such as “haunts” and Civis Tenebrarum (shadow creatures) that appear hostile to all living beings while being nearly immune to conventional weapons.

Known Deployments

-   ZW reports the use of TO in Daerlun during early Netherese-Sembian conflicts (precise timing unknown). Shadovar agents manipulated a Sembian mother into unknowingly selling her daughter into slavery, then sacrificed her during the emotional trauma of discovery. That installation remained active decades later, contained but not eliminated by the Silver Ravens.

-   ZW’s 1445 expedition speculated that the Alavandor Academy, the Oak and Shield Inn, and the Merchant’s Hall in the ruin of Thistle Ridge house active TO, as evidenced by incorporeal undead that drain life force and create undead shadow-doubles of victims and illusions meant to entrap victims (e.g., a locked door that does not exist). These infestations are assumed to date from the Thistle Ridge Tragedy of 1441, when they were activated by the sacrifices of Lily Clarandal, Lilac Alavandor, and Poppy Sweetberry (it is unclear whether these three girls were duped into committing impure deeds, or whether they were the victim of an impure deed by another party, namely Alaventa Alavaxi)

-   My own suspicion is that this was not the first deployment in Thistle Ridge. The “hunting accident” in 1439 DR, two years earlier, was likely another such deployment that had been set up over decades. I believe that the hag Palus used her guise as “the Mother of the Ancient Faith” (i.e., Jannath), to trick Abrecia Alavandor into sacrificing family members, then killed the traumatized perpetrator to activate the device. (When contacted with help from Nemia, Abrecia’s spirit was familiar with the deception, suggesting that she had become aware of the deaths of the others while still alive. Her death around the same time was ery likely the sacrifice of “a pure soul that has committed an impure deed”)

-   That invites the question of where the concomitant gate to the Shadowfell was opened, but that’s outside the scope of this exercise.

So was the orb I saw a Tenebrius Ostium?

Arguments in favour

-   Moral corruption: "a pure soul that has committed an impure deed" – Coventina appears to fit this exact profile: an innocent who was somehow corrupted or tricked into committing an impure act (deceiving and endangering Jasper) before being sacrificed

-   Precedents in Thistle Ridge: 1439 and 1441

-   Individual sacrifice focus: TO require a single, specific victim rather than mass sacrifice, matching the individual nature of Coventina's death

Arguments against

-   Timing - why now? What's been happening for the past 40 years that this is only coming to light now?

-   No apparent immediate effect (but would there be?)

-   No gateway to the Shadowfell seems to have appeared… yet.

III. The third possibility is that the orb was the “terrible, terrible orb” (TTO ) described by the Drurginz Coster survivor, Jendwendle:

Context:

-   The TTO appears to be connected to the recent operations of Charrah and the bugbear matriarch Bolla, operating in conjunction with Karn's band out of the tower.

-   Jendwendle has been held captive since autumn 1482 (so, approximately 8-9 months), placing the orb's active use in that timeframe. This seems mostly coincident with the decision to put together the Thistle Ridge reclamation initiative, presumably triggered by the disappearance of the caravan.

Known Victims:

-   Morganna, a priestess of Tymora and Jendwendle's wife

-   The captain of the Drurginz Coster caravan (likely a Zhentarim mage, given the company's affiliations)

-   Both were "put into the terrible terrible orb" by Charrah

Activation Requirements and Effects

-   The TTO seems to require (or benefit from) the sacrifice of spellcasters specifically

-   Victims appear to have their throats cut as part of the ritual ("they cut her throat, put her into the orb")

-   The orb appears to enable the animation or control of the dead. It seems to trap or bind the victim's essence within the orb rather than simply reanimating them as soulless zombies – the captain is described as "still screaming," and Morganna seemed somewhat sentient.

-   The reanimation of Buddy Gorehammer may be related, even though he seems to have fallen in battle with Karn rather than by sacrifice

-   The TTO seems to be used as a tactical tool for ambush operations, which may include the original ambush of the Drurginz Caravan.

So, was Loki’s orb the terrible, terrible orb?

Arguments in favour of the orb we encountered being the TTO:

-   Association with Charrah

-   Coventina’s death may have been intended as a sacrifice along the lines of the deaths reported by Jendwendle

Arguments against:

-   Coventina (to my knowledge) was not a spellcaster, which doesn’t fit the established pattern

-   Seemingly unique method of Coventina's murder

-   No evidence of undead reanimation.

IV. Now we come to the fourth, perhaps most disturbing possibility, namely that these are all the same instruments, used at three different scales of power. Bane’s balls, but I’d never thought I’d ever again be thinking of Master Fenwick’s lectures on the commercial significance of the Triadic Netherese Mindset. Rule of three again.

If this theory holds, then the differences I’ve noted are not of kind, but of application. The core technology—a Netherese artifact that converts the energy of a sacrificed soul into a potent magical effect—is the same. The variable is the ritual. I suppose it would be a good idea to take this up with Jasper and Nemia, but, by Waukeen’s wondrous wagons, ol’ Lucan has found an angle once again.

It all comes back to Coventina’s death. The deception worked upon her—turning an innocent into an instrument of deceit against Jasper and his friends—perfectly matches the profile for activating a Tenebrius Ostium. Loki and/or Charrabh were preparing the specific, wicked ritual needed to turn another piece of Thistle Ridge into a permanent shadow-haunted dead zone, just as Palus (likely) did in 1439 and 1441. The fact that no rift immediately appeared is neither here nor there; these things may take time to manifest, or our interruption might have stalled the final effect.

If all this is true, then we are not facing an enemy with an arsenal of different weapons: we are facing an enemy with a single, horrifyingly adaptable system. It can be a siege engine, a territorial curse, or an assassin's tool. Aumanator blind them, but those Netherese are elegant sons of baatezu. The question is no longer "Which orb was it?" but "What scale of disaster were they attempting to unleash this time?" With the sacrifice of Coventina, I fear the answer is "a permanent one."
