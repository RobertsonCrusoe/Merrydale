From the Journal of Tergus Clarandal, recovered at Clarandal Farm 1483 by Company Chrysalis

Harvestide, 1437
As the highest-ranking officer of the 7th Sun Blade Company, 4th Platoon, I find a measure of pride and contentment in my role here in Thistle Ridge. Though I have left the prestigious 2nd Company Mountaineers, my heart is glad to continue serving as a commissioned officer while remaining close to home. 

Tonight, however, my thoughts are heavy with concern for my father, Claudius. Born in Sembia in 1345, he has seen over ninety winters, and the toll of a soldiers life has left him bent with chronic pain and near crippling infirmity. Yet, the land here, with its fine medicines and nurturing environment, has sustained him far beyond what I might have expected. 

Earlier this evening, after supper, we sat in the drawing room, and I broached the subject of collecting stories for his memoir. I wished to begin with his homeland, Sembia, a place I have never seen but have always heard about through his tales. As we spoke, he reminisced about standing guard against The Shadow on the walls of Daerlun, mentioning that town several times. Confused, I tried to correct him, reminding him that I had always been told he was born, raised, and served in Saerloon. 

His response was disconcerting. With a dismissive wave, he claimed his memory was failing him due to age. Of course, Saerloon it is, he said, changing the subject with a quickness that only deepened my suspicions. 

Why would he, in his twilight years, misremember something so fundamental as his own origins? What reason could there be for such a discrepancy? These questions gnaw at my mind, and I find myself wondering about the true reasons behind our familys presence here in Thistle Ridge. The Alavandors and Clarandals have always seemed so deeply rooted in this place, yet the past now appears shadowed with uncertainty. 

As I lay down to rest, my thoughts return to the conversation with Father. There is a hidden truth, I am certain of it, buried beneath the layers of time and memory. The land of Thistle Ridge, with its mysteries and fertile soil, has always felt like home. But what if there is more to our story, a past that has been carefully obscured? 

I must tread carefully, for uncovering these secrets might disrupt the fragile peace of our lives. But the pursuit of truth is a warriors duty, just as much as defending the walls of our village. In the days to come, I shall seek out the wisdom of the elders, the histories of both the Clarandals and the Alavandors, and perhaps even delve into the ancient lore of the Old Faith. 

For now, I will stand watch over my father, ensuring his remaining days are filled with comfort and dignity. The answers will come, in time, and I will be ready to face whatever truths they reveal.
