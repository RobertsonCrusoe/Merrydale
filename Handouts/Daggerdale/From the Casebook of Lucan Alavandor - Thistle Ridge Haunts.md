From the Casebooks of Lucan Alavandor
Notes on Paranormal Happenings at Thistle Ridge
Nature of the Haunting
The ruins of Thistle Ridge are confirmed to be haunted by multiple entities, stemming from the Tragedy of 1441.

At least three main locations are identified as haunted: the Alavandor Academy, the Oak and Shield Inn, and possibly the Merchants' Hall.

These hauntings involve "Shadow Infestations," described as localized connections to the Shadowfell, possibly created and sustained by artifacts called Tenebrius Ostium.

In the Academy: An expedition encountered undead shadow creatures (Civis Tenebrarum) native to the Shadowfell. These beings are incorporeal, drain life force, create lesser shadow duplicates from victims, and are difficult to permanently destroy without addressing the source artifact. The environment within was dark, devoid of color, filled with dread, and muted divine light and positive energy.

In the Oak and Shield Inn: The phenomenon described fits the profile of a "Haunt". Witnesses reported a terrifying encounter with thick, ashy air and a maelstrom of debris assaulting them.

In the Merchants' Hall: A blood-curdling scream or howl, possibly sounding like a laughing or crying child, was heard, suggesting a third potential haunting.

The current goblin inhabitants, the Yellow Tooth Clan, avoid these haunted buildings and use fetishes in an attempt to ward off the spirits.
Origin and Identity of the Spirits
The hauntings are directly linked to the Tragedy of 1441 DR, a devastating assault likely orchestrated by the Grentzeldek hobgoblins, which resulted in the slaughter or capture of nearly everyone in the settlement.

Magistrate Zoderick Winters theorized the undead shadows were created through the sacrifice of pure souls who committed impure deeds, tied to the activation of the Tenebrius Ostium artifacts.

The town fool, Merri Mumfrey, sang of "spirits spirits spirits one-two-three" connected to "sad betrayal" and a "tragic story" being key to freeing them.

Nemia Ul-Nishaadrhi, a Ghost Hunter, linked the spirits to three dolls found at the Clarandal Farm ruins, representing Lilly Clarandal, Lilac Alavandor, and Poppy Sweetberry. A fourth doll, representing Alaventa Alavaxi, was said to correspond to a soul at rest.

The fey creature Calix revealed that the hag Contessa Palus holds the spirits of these three girls within Thistle Ridge. Palus, allied with Shadowkind, orchestrated the Tragedy by tricking a girl from the farm into playing a "cruel joke" on her friends (the three spirits), leading to their sacrifice alongside Palus' own daughter and granddaughter.

The "new men" of Thistle Ridge (Sembian settlers like the Clarandals and Alavandors) are also implicated, having "profaned the Waters and the Grove," potentially contributing to the conditions allowing the tragedy and haunting.
Resolving the Haunting
To free the spirits of the three girls, one must confront the hag Palus and end her magic.

Permanently destroying the shadow creatures likely requires neutralizing the Tenebrius Ostium artifacts and possibly resolving the original injustice that led to the sacrifices.

The documents suggest a complex haunting involving undead shadows created through dark rituals connected to the Shadowfell, and the trapped spirits of three young girls victimized during the Tragedy of 1441 DR, orchestrated by the hag Palus.
The Girls of Thistle Ridge
Alaventa Alavaxi
Based on the documents provided, here is what we know about Alaventa Alavaxi:
Family Background: Alaventa was part of the Alavaxi family who emigrated from Chessenta and settled in Thistle Ridge around 1416 DR. Her family's arrival was considered mysterious, with speculation they might have emerged from deep caves near the Iron Hole, possibly connected to the Underdark or even deeper realms. Some elder members of the family suffered from an unusual skin condition and were sent to Castle Radiance, subsequently disappearing from records.

Childhood: Around 1439 DR, Alaventa was a young girl who was close friends with Lilly Clarandal, Lilac Alavandor, and Poppy Sweetberry. They were often seen playing together. She was described as having pale skin.

Tergus Clarandal's Concerns: During the Winter Solstice of 1439, Tergus Clarandal noted a change in Alaventa. He observed her as being unusually subdued, lacking joy, and perceived a "faint shadow dimming her spirit." This caused him significant worry, leading him to wonder if there was "some Shadow inside her," potentially linked to her family's mysterious origins and the disappearance of her grandparents.

Fate: According to the Ghost Hunter, Nemia Ul-Nishaadrhi, Alaventa Alavaxi is deceased. However, unlike her childhood friends whose spirits haunt Thistle Ridge, Alaventa's "soul is at rest".

Significance: Despite her soul being at peace, Nemia suggested Alaventa's story holds significance, describing her role as a "tool, or path" in understanding the broader situation at Thistle Ridge. A doll representing her (pale-skinned, grey dress, silver kukri) was found alongside dolls of the other three girls in Lilly Clarandal's abandoned room.

While Alaventa Alavaxi shared a childhood with the girls whose spirits are now tied to the Thistle Ridge hauntings, her own soul is reportedly at peace. Her family's mysterious background and Tergus's observation of a "shadow" upon her shortly before the Tragedy suggest her life might hold clues related to the events, even though she isn't one of the ghosts herself.

Based on the documents provided, here is what is known about the other three girls associated with Alaventa Alavaxi and the Thistle Ridge haunting: Lilly Clarandal, Lilac Alavandor, and Poppy Sweetberry.

These three girls were friends who played together with Alaventa Alavaxi at the Clarandal family's country estate near Thistle Ridge in 1439 DR. Dolls representing each of them were found years later in Lilly Clarandal's abandoned bedroom at the Clarandal Farm ruins.

Unlike Alaventa, whose soul is reportedly at rest, the spirits of Lilly, Lilac, and Poppy are directly connected to the haunting of Thistle Ridge. They are the "three spirits" whose tragic story is key to understanding the events there.

Their fate is tied to the 1441 Tragedy According to the fey Contessa Calix, the hag Palus orchestrated their demise by tricking one of the girls into playing a "cruel joke" on her friends, leading to their sacrifice along with Palus's own daughter and granddaughter. Their spirits are currently held captive within the Thistle Ridge ruins by Palus's magic.
Here are the specific details available for each girl:
Lilly Clarandal:
Daughter of Tergus Clarandal (a Sun Blade officer) and Senthia (nee Alavandor). Born in 1435 DR.
Her father, Tergus, referred to her as his "precious daughter".
The doll representing her had olive skin and wore a red dress and cape, with a belt, scabbard, and small wooden shield.
Lilac Alavandor:
Daughter of Andarl Alavandor (former mayor of Thistle Ridge) and Susserah (nee Clarandal). Born in 1432 DR.
Her aunt, Senthia Clarandal, was Lilly's mother.
The doll representing her was olive-skinned and wore a green dress with green wrappings on her wrists.
Poppy Sweetberry:
Member of the Sweetberry family, longtime residents of Thistle Ridge originally associated with the Old Faith (Silvanus worship). Her family ran the Oak and Shield Inn.

The doll representing her was a halfling girl with curly blonde hair, wearing a brown dress and an apron embroidered with an oak leaf.

The spirits of these three girls are central to the mystery and affliction of Thistle Ridge, held captive by the hag Palus as a result of dark events during the Tragedy.
