From the Casebook of Lucan Alavandor: Netheril and Melegaunt
Date: 23 April 2025
From: Stephen Robertson (with Gus Miranda)
To: Gus Miranda, Les Blackwell, Scott Mclaren, Todd Burry

[Jasper's response to Lucan's Conspiracy Unmasked dossier:]

"Truffle pig?!" Jasper looks at Lucan who simply continues to stare at the fire.

"I'll have you know a good truffle hunting pig in the Dales is worth more than sir Rowan's shiny new armour. Be that as it may, I can't disagree with any of what you've said..."

Jasper stares into the fire as well and is silent for a few minutes.

"What you're most right about is that we are being used. If we defeat Palus, we will open the door for Mourvanesh's conquest if we aren't strong enough to stop her at that point. It may not be what the Captain wants to hear, but we might need to make an ally of Palus and not an enemy."

A few more silent minutes of staring at the fire.

"Ok, but then answer me this... why? Why any of it? Maybe i am just a simple pig, but the deeper we dig looking for that truffle, the less I seem to understand the 'why' of it. Still makes me feel like we're the ones that's are going to be served up for supper at the Greengrass feast."

[Lucan's reply:]

"Aw, Jas. I didn't mean anything by it. I just mean you're diligent! But you know, you've got a point, kind of. Why did Palus turn on her sister? That's a question for the wives, I suppose. Though I doubt fey know each other's minds any better than we do."

(Lucan laces his fingers together and stretches his arms toward the fire, palms outward.)

"Melegaunt and Netheril now..."

(Lucan swirls his wine, his gaze fixed on the dancing flames. There's a cynical edge to his voice, the sound of someone discussing a predator they know all too well.)

"Right, Melegaunt Tanthul. One of the originals. And his interest in Daggerdale...."

(He takes a slow drink, his expression unreadable for a moment.)

"Look, I saw how they work, growing up in Saerloon. We weren't under their thumb, but... They trade above board and below, and you don't mess with them either way. Netheril isn't impulsive. They're patient. They plan. They reward service. And they come down hard on anyone who thinks they can take advantage. They've been a fact of life for my family - my real family - for over a century now. And from what I'm told about Daggerdale, it's always been a strategic piece on the board – that pass, the proximity to Anauroch, the buffer against the Moonsea. And now Tethyamar as a second route east..."

(He gestures to the west, wincing as he spills his wine.)

"Why? Why now? Maybe internal politics shifted within that Shade Enclave of theirs. Maybe the Zhents got too bold nearby, and the Princes decided to swat them down by taking something that the Keep has always wanted but could never have. Or maybe," (shrugs), "Daggerdale is simply the next on their list. They took Sembia, they've warred elsewhere... they move methodically and inexorably."

(Lucan leans forward slightly, his eyes sharpen in a way that Jasper recognizes from when he's on a case.)

"From their perspective, an independent Daggerdale - it may be small, but it's a vulnerability. A potential staging ground for enemies like the White Cloaks, a disruption to their trade routes to the Moonsea. More importantly, Daggerdale is a symbol that defiance is possible. The Zhents know that well. It's the Lordless Country. But the Netherese... they won't tolerate such things indefinitely. Crushing Dagger Falls would bring the entire area more firmly under their administrative control – and they do like control."

"And if Melegaunt is spearheading it? That just tells you it's a high-level priority. He wouldn't be assigned to trivial matters. Maybe it's a strategic goal for High Prince Telamont, or maybe Daggerdale is just the final piece Melegaunt needs to declare his region fully 'pacified' and integrated into the Empire."

(He leans back, sighing softly.)

"It's not about 'why Daggerdale' in isolation. It's about why Netheril does anything. Control. Resources. Strategic advantage. Eliminating potential threats. It's the same pattern, played out over decades. It could just be that Daggerdale's number finally came up. That's the reality of living under the Shadow."
