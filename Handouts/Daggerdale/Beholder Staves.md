Three of these staves are known to exist.

During the Heroes' Council on Greengrass of 1373, Kennyth Thames revealed that his ongoing research into the history of the Beholder Staves had at last borne fruit. The staves first appeared, to his knowledge, in the ancient state of Chessenta in the seventh century pre-DR. The staves appear again, after a long silence, in the Moonsea region in the Year of Good Tidings (751 DR), when the Westgate wizard Zhentar is said to have used them to secure his position as the ruler of the settlement that would become Zhentil Keep. They surfaced again to be used further for the survival of the Keep in its siege during the Cyrinishad debacle.

The staves have many magic properties, and it is known that they are all very powerful anti-divination devices. From sources he would not disclose, Kennyth had discovered that while the staves functioned as charged magical devices such as those that can be made by wizards, these staves could only be charged by beholders. Furthermore, he indicated that it appeared that the staves may also be conduits for communication with beholders over long distances, a cause for concern.

==Thagdul's staff==

This is a 7’ tall staff with 8 eyestalks. It is assumed that each eyestalk has some magical power associated with it. This staff has been in Thagdul’s possession throughout the entire story. When last seen, through a divination of Bejuril's, Thagdul did not appear to be in possession of the staff, but was riding with a large lead box that seemed big enough to contain it.

==Yarkul's staff==

This staff is 6.5’ long and has 7 stalks. It was in Yarkul’s possession until he was captured by Rychel at the Dripping Dagger during Harvestide of ’71. It was then taken almost immediately by Jafar, and more debate followed between he and Beljuril. Jafar seemed to bend on the issue, and let Beljuril take it to Neverwinter in ’72. However, it turned out that he simply gave the boy-mage a clever reproduction, which was broken during the campaign against Malkor. The current whereabouts of this staff are unknown, but it is supposed that Jafar has it, wherever he may be.

==The Hunabar staff==

This 6’ long and has 6 stalks, and was found by the Company of the Red Herring in the Hunabar Villa when they went seeking the Heart of the Earth. The staff was later stolen from Red Herring HQ, despite serious magical security put in place to prevent such a thing. Much debate over ownership of this staff erupted, especially between Jafar and Beljuril.

Thagdul’s revealed that prior to its recovery by the Red Herrings, it had disappeared during a conflict between Malthoon’s High Road Shippers and Company Dalael.

Following the theft, the staff found its way into the hands of Malkor (by means unknown), and was ultimately retrieved again through the efforts of the  in the summer of ’72. This staff is currently hidden at Phaulkonmere.

Beljuril had several conversations with a beholder or beholders through this staff. The voice(s) from the staff:

*Convinced Beljuril not to destroy the staff when first retrieved from Malkor
*Thought Beljuril was Jafar, initially
*Tried to send us against the Dalael instead of the Zhents
*Tried to convince us to turn the staff over to Kendal Thorne
*Knew about the Istolil Dalael ruse that had baited an Ersgul trap
*Revealed Jafar's relationship with Xulla
*Revealed that Kendal Thorne (aka Altooth Mzoul) may have been unaware of this

==A Missing Staff ?==
Research by Kennyth Thames into ancient records has revealed that a fourth staff, measuring 8' in length with a full ten stalks adorning the head of the staff, was once in existence, but its fate has so far been lost to the Roll of Years.
