Narrative of the trip from Castle Radiance to Dagger Falls, in early Mirtul, 1480 DR;

== 1 Mirtul ==
Spring came quite early this year, and even though it is only the 1st of Mirtul, the day is bright and warm, and the trail down the mountain is edged in bright green undergrowth as the world blossoms to life once more.  The rivers and streams down the sides of the mountain and under the many bridges you cross over are swollen and fast, and the air is already warm, though a fresh breeze carries through the mountains today.

===Fort Luminous===

It is only a few hours on horseback to get to Fort Luminous, and your journey is uninterrupted by any traffic or visitors coming from the opposite direction.  You know that will soon change, as the campaigning season is getting underway, but for now, you enjoy the lonely road.

You stop only briefly at the Fort, and are happy to see your friend Mr. Bollinger, who comes down from the Wonderbringer Warforge when he hears you are present.  Your visit with him is brief, but between his snapping fingers and other conversational nuances, he tells you to be careful, and to watch the skies; flying creatures of some type have been harassing travellers recently, though none have come close to the Fort.

===Rain Hill Mine===

After a meal with your friend, you are off, winding your way down the Mountain Road.  The journey is still a quiet one, with no enemies or travellers in sight.  It isn’t until you come to the inn at the Rain Hill Mine that you see any other traffic.  There is a large caravan in the courtyard, and a buzz of men around it checking packs and straps, while a gaunt and pale merchant haggles with the innkeeper over the price of some kegs of ale.  You overhear some of the guards talking, and learn that the caravan is called the , a name you remember from a report last year from Alystair.  You then remember that the merchant was cleared of his charges, apparently having been an unwitting pawn of the Dark Garden operative named Lotus.  A quick shake-down of the man and some of his guards reveals that they seem to be on the straight and narrow, and carrying on business as usual, despite the tremendous loss at the end of last season.

The large inn at the mine is a strategic location; by the time you get here on horseback, it is very late, and time to stop for the night anyway.  The innkeeper offers your team the best suite for the cost of a simple room; a consideration only in part because of your status as local heroes, but also because there is no one here who can afford that suite anyway, and a little reason not to treat the heroes who may have to help you some day.

The common room is mostly empty this night, with only the gruff and recluse guards of the Moonsea Collective there drinking, and a few miners nursing tankards.

==2 Mirtul==

By morning, the weather has changed.  It is drizzling when you get up, and by the time you have eaten and made ready to hit the road again, there is a heavy and steady spring downpour which doesn’t let up all day.  This makes the track in the mountains slippery and dangerous.

===Daggerford Farm===

“We’ll never make town today if this weather don’t let up some” says Banitt.  He turns out to be right.  With the wash-outs on the road, the slippery rocks and the general low spirits of your animals, it is after dark by the time you make Daggerford Farm, and you are forced to stop for the night.  You are all soaked through and weary.  You find a large group of merchants and what appears to be some mercenaries in the inn here, and find out that they decided to wait out the rain for a day or two.  You realize that if they had gone out, they would be camping somewhere in the foothills or the mountains now.  They probably made the right choice.

The taproom of the River Sticks (the inn at Daggerford) is a busy place.  As mentioned, there are some caravaners there, some mercenaries, and many of the locals, who have been trading with them today, and have a bit of money to spend on alcohol tonight.  It is lively and there are a couple of locals playing string instruments and singing loud and drunkenly, but not altogether terribly.  Some of the local girls have also come to the taproom tonight, and it is only the presence of a few armoured Sun Blades on duty here that keeps the usual sort of problems of mixing booze, strange men and local women from breaking out.  Outside, the rain is a veritable roar, and once in a while the wind gusts so bad that the door bursts open, and people pause from their revelry for a moment.

== 3 Mirtul ==
The following morning, the 3rd of Mirtul, you find that the rain has slowed to a drizzle again.  The courtyard of the inn is bustling with activity as the wagons are packed and checked over, and many men start to mount horses, making ready to head into the mountains.  There are some last-minute transactions between some of the farmers and the traders, but your company is mounted and soon leaves the farm behind.

===Dagger Falls ===
Another half day ride finds you mounting the ridge to the west of town.  From up here, the road begins to take a switch-back course down the steep rocky face down which a roaring river Tesh makes the famous falls for which the town of named after.  This river is normally fast broad by the time it hits the falls, but with the rains from the last couple of days it is a deafening thunder as it rolls over and down the cliff, and crashes into the small reservoir below.  The spray from the violent falls has made the track down the cliff treacherously slippery, and you are forced to help a group of farmers from Foothills Farm as they struggle to get their heavy wagons up.

The mayor of the community is with them, a young man named Helm Thunderstorm.  He is only about 30 years old, but he wears wisdom on his brow that is uncommon for a man his age.  His administration of the farm for the last few years has been beyond criticism, and by all accounts, they are as prosperous as any.  Of course, the Thunderstorm family is old, wealthy and storied, but you can see by the practical goods cramming his wagons full, that the Thunderstorms are a frugal people.

When asked for news, he says “I wish I had something tangible to give you.  There are mutterings of demons on our roads, and beasts in the skies, but I have seen nothing of the sort.  Some of our hunters and trappers have been bringing back wolf pelts the size and quantity of which none of the old timers have heard of, but they haven’t caused us much trouble.  Hell.  I just sold a stack of them for a very reasonable price.  In short, we’re doing just fine, but appreciate the White Cloaks asking after us.”

===The City===

From the base of the falls, town is only a quarter-mile away, down a diving track that follows the turbulent river.  The first thing you notice as you get closer to the walls of town is a group of men on the green outside.  There are dozens of them all lined up, and as you get closer you can see that they are all holding bows, and taking instruction from a few Sun Blade officers.  That’s when you see Tuugan, or the likeness of Tuugan; his younger brother Alaric.  Alaric is giving instructions to the men, and as you get within a hundred feet or so, he gives a command to fire, and you see a stuttering volley of missiles go in a variety of arcs and directions down-range, nearly all of them falling short of the ochre-painted bales of hay.  Alaric begins to shout and curse at the men, and this is when you decide that he may look like his brother, but they are clearly little alike.

It is worth noting that the recruits you see wearing the cadet-tabards are not the usual cocky young men that are normally seen in training.  These men, the first of the trainees for the 9th Company that you heard about, are mostly veterans, and most of them are big.  The bows they are drawing are not the common but respectable long-bows found on every farm in the region.  They are training with great bows, and most of the men are unable to draw the string back at all, let alone to their ear.  You recall many times when Tuugan had a good laugh at someone trying to draw his bow, which is much the same as these; even the strongest of warriors don’t have the years of specific muscle training required to draw them, and you realize that a whole company, or even a platoon of these weapons would be a dreadful force on a field of battle, if Alaric can stop cursing at them and get them trained properly.

===The Westgate===

Trotting past the trainees, you are soon at the Westgate, and for the first time for many of you, you find yourselves being waved in with little interrogation at all.  Your names are recorded in the register, and in you go.  No “twenty questions”, no smug clerks.

The town is busy, despite the drizzling rain.  It is only early afternoon when you arrive, and you can see that the trade-season is getting off to a booming start here.  The streets are crawling with people, townsfolk, visiting farmers and hunters, and traders alike.  In the Eastern Bailey, there are nearly a hundred wagons, twice as many horses, and four times as many men doing business from their wagons-cum-merchant stalls.  There seems to be more of a presence of 1st Company Sun Blades than you remember, and you in fact notice that a couple of squads are wearing markings that denote them as 5th and 6th platoons, which is two more than you remember them having.

Despite the many visitors to the town and the bustling trade, things are as orderly as ever.  Those of you who have been to other towns and cities are amazed to see such clean streets, orderly rows of stalls, a complete lack of beggars, and all the other gritty aspects of busy towns.  This is usual for Dagger Falls.

===“Repent, evil shadow!\"===
What is unusual today is the number of guards on the streets and the ramparts, and the slight feeling of tension in the air.  The Sun Blades seem a little on edge, and some of the more diligent ones are scrutinizing some of the traders uncomfortably closely.  As you are weaving your way through a small crowd of people, a group of children comes scampering past; there are several of them, and they are all wearing a white cloak of varying sizes and degrees of cleanliness, and they are all pursuing one child wearing a dark blue cloak upon which he has drawn a rough skull with chalk.  The boy in the dark cloak runs right into Iolus, who puts a hand on his small shoulder.  The boy looks up and starts to mutter an apology, just as the rest of his friends come barrelling into him and Iolus.  Soon, a bunch of gaping young children are staring up at the lot of you, some turning bright red.  Iolus pats the boy on the head.  “You’ve been caught, little villain,” Iolus says.

“Yeah” one little girl rejoins, “Repent, evil shadow!  You are surrounded by the White Cloaks!”  They all laugh and run along, some still staring up in disbelief as they vanish into the streets.  Rhuun is noticed to watch them intently, his head, being a head above anyone else in the crowd, tracks the children long after they are out of sight of the rest of you.  Long afterwards Rhuun is seen to be watching the skies.
