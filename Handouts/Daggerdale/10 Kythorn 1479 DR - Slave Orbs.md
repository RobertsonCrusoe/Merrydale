The Heart of the Shadow, the Heart of the Dale

The Dark Garden successfully established two covens in the autumn of 1479. The coven at Dwimmerholme Farm, which was to be established by the Dark Garden agent known as Lotus, was foiled by Alystair and Tuugan. However, two were established before that; the lesser of these was at Foot Hills Farm, and was likely established by Deadnettle, who was operating around there at the time. The greater was at Liberty Hill Farm.

Liberty Hill Farm is the hearth of the Whitegate Family. I have included Ardelusk’s account of his founding of this settlement nearly a century ago to give a sense of the central nature of this place to the prominent family of the dale. This farm had always been exceptionally tolerant of other faiths, despite the strong investment that the Whitegate family has in Amaunator, and it was the proliferation and strength of the Chauntean faith here which was exploited by the Dark Garden. In fact, this was the common ‘weakness’ (I am loathe to label it such) for all three farms; they were tolerant of, and had large Chauntean flocks. The ‘Old Religion’ (as they call Chauntea in the Dales) was used as an inroad for the Sharran cults, and where Chauntea was strong, so too was the influence of the Dark Garden.

I will confess that I felt like I had just graduated from rhetoric school when these facts came to light. The term ‘Dark Garden’ carries with it something of a horticultural suggestion, which should have alerted me and others to the divine connection and conflict here in the dale. However, no one made this connection until after these events came to a head.

On the eve of the battle, Liberty Hill Farm had become the Heart of the Shadow. The Dark Garden members had all collected there, and elaborate rituals were used to turn the population of the town into malign and shadowy versions of themselves. There was some resistance to this evil plot as it unfolded, and some of the people of the farm realized what was happening and fought back.

Here is a reconstruction of the events at the Farm, as I interpret them;

  1372: The real history and the events leading up to the battle begin with the Battle of Liberty Hill, in the distant past. Both Ardelusk and the woman known as Arabella Needle fought in this battle, and their descendants ordered the battle that took place there in 1480, though they were now on opposite sides of the same conflict.

  1374: Needle, now going by the name ‘Liberty’, and with a boy-child in tow, visited the farm that Ardelusk had started, and encouraged him to name the farm Liberty Hill. The boy was none other than Gaebril Tremalkin-Liberty, the son of the famous Knight of Shar and the Needle woman. Unfortunately, Needle had already taken the darker path offered by Shar in those days, misinterpreting the message of the Black Knight. Needle’s line was prolific, and many of her descendants, through the Tremalkin boy, still live, including Belladonna herself.

  1395: Ardelusk moves the Cenotaph of Paladin’s Grave, and the Knights interred therein, from Anathar’s Dell to their new resting place on the western side of the town at Liberty Hill. These same Knights would be raised by the wicked Belladonna in the spring of 1480 to assist in the battle.

  1455: This was a pivotal year for a number of reasons. First, the Whitegate family (the most significant family of Amaunatorians) was joined with the Forester family (the most significant family of Chaunteans) in the union of Perspicuus and Lanya. This brought Liberty Hill into the

  Chauntean fold, which increased the health and wealth of the farm tremendously, but also provided an in-road for the Dark Garden. The wedding, on the 1st of Kythorn, turned into a massacre, as Dorian of Sembia returned from the dead, seemingly as some vampire or revenant, to wreak havoc and death at the ceremony. The account given by Decker Blackmane, which we have had for many years, is close, but it misses the mark. Dorian was not in charge of the forces of undead that fought in the fields, taking the lives of Loedegan and Madigan. It was in fact Belladonna who mustered those ranks. She in fact it was that summoned Dorian himself to this massacre.

At the time Belladonna lived at Crossed Blades Farm (though she spent much of her time at Castle Daggerdale), with her daughters. She engineered the massacre as a means of elevating the station of the Chaunteans at the farm, and she was largely successful. She or her agents were able to convince the people of the farm and the administration of the Church that the malevolent powers opposed to the Dale recognized that the marriage of the Whitegates and Foresters, of Amaunator with Chauntea, would be catastrophic for their evil plots. So, an army of undead was arrayed to end the wedding, but the assault failed. Belladonna was at the wedding ceremony, and it was she that incapacitated Dorian, her minion, just as Argent attacked it.

I have not told a soul in the dale this last fact, as it would detract from the authority and esteem of the Bishop, and this fact alone has been kept out of the original manuscript of this report, Oghma forgive me.

But I must continue with 1455; Belladonna installed one of her daughters in the town at Liberty Hill that year; the Chauntean (in public, but Sharran behind closed doors) Remalla Goering (Goering was a man that Belladonna had paired with in Crossed Blades, but who died mysteriously). Remalla was only 15 years old, but she was a good friend of Lanya’s, and so she was raised as a sister to this now well- bred woman. She would grow in prosperity and power at Liberty Hill, ultimately becoming the elected mayor in 1477, when Lumen Whitegate abdicated governance of the farm to an elected official.

  Also in 1455, construction of the Table of the Sun was started and completed. The Table was originally designed to carry out an elaborate ritual, divinely delivered to Argent, to contain the evil of Castle Daggerdale. This ritual has been ongoing since the late autumn of that year, though its location was moved to the Yellow Abbey sub-basement in 1457, mostly to get out of the elements. I have asked Argent about this ritual, but it is the one secret he will not relinquish. He has only stated that “the sacrifice of the clerics that maintain the ritual, giving up entire months of their lives to chant continuously, is nothing compared to the sacrifices made by the men who made the ritual possible” and will state no more.

  1470’s: Sometime late in this decade, Belladonna assembled the Dark Garden, and began a tenuous relationship with Netheril, through the Agent known as Curator Loki Mouvranesh. They began to organize banditry and other small crimes in the dale, slowly escalating to the plots and events of 1479/80. The exact nature of the relationship between Netheril and the Dark Garden is not fully understood, though it seems it was not one of a clear chain of command.

  Mirtul, 1479: The group of adventurers known as Mule Company, some of which would later be rolled into the ranks of the White Cloaks, attempt to claim a bounty (c.f. Last Sands of Anauroch bounty.) They run afoul Mouvranesh, who has beaten them to the Netherese ruin, and had

  already begun an excavation. She uncovered the Imperiosus Orbis, which she used to command the winds to hasten excavation of the site. Mule Company was unable to defeat her, and was driven off after the death of one of their members. Though it was not apparent at the time, this was a turning point in the war with Netheril; the powerful Netherese Orbs were used to bolster a number of malevolent rituals carried out by the Shades, including the rituals that transformed Liberty Hill (see below).

  Kythorn 10, 1479: Summer Solstice and new moon. This was the first successful Ritual of Founding that the Dark Garden achieved. This was the first we missed, though we knew, after the events of Harvestide that year (c.f. Investigations of Alystair Dretch, 1479) that we had missed something. This was the culmination of decades of inculcation of the cult of Shar at Liberty Hill, masterminded by Mayor Goering, with the help of her mother and the Dark Garden. It now seems that as many as 140 people were part of this cult and the ritual.

  Early Spring, 1480: Belladonna begins to assemble an undead horde, unearthing the evil paladins and soldiers interred at Anathar’s Dell, a place that very few could now find.

  Spring, 1480: The conflict on the Western Front escalates to such a degree that no man can be spared; the heartland, despite being under an increased level of threats, most of which have been engineered by the Dark Garden, has no additional forces to aid the struggle against the villainous trio.

  Late Kythorn or Early Mirtul, 1480: Belladonna is given the Slave Orbs for the Imperiosus Orbis, which will facilitate the exceptionally powerful rituals in the days to come. The Slave Orbs allow for the channelling of the tremendous power of the Orbis to a remote location.

  Mirtul 19, 1480: New moon. I believe this is the date that the Ritual of Transcendence was completed, with the aid of the Slave Orbs, turning much of the town into the shadow slaves that were later fought at the Battle. At this point, the entire farm was under complete control of the cult of Shar, and the Dark Garden. Goering, now a shadow-kind and priestess of Shar, was the ruler of the town in its new hellish form, though Belladonna was the head of the cult in the region. All but pre-pubescent children were converted into shadow slaves. The children were kept in a barn, and were slated to be sold to the Bloody Eye, a slaving and bandit outfit that the Dark Garden had employed/manipulated for nearly a decade. This never happened, thankfully.

  The ‘transcendence’ did not go smoothly. There was a resistance mounted against this ritual, and the entire town was thrown into a violent massacre of brother against brother. The terrible stories of this black day continue to be told by its survivors, and each new story brings a river of tears.

  Nearly a hundred adults in the farm seemed to have the same resistance to the powerful transcendence ritual as did the children; this included the entire clergy of Amaunatorians, as well as many in the Whitegate family. Those that resisted but were not killed in the massacre, were kept alive for an even fouler purpose a few days later.

  Mirtul 19-21, 1480: Belladonna unearths the bodies of the Fist mercenaries, interred beneath the three great cairns on the edge of the farm, using the labour of the shadow slaves.

  Mirtul 21, 1480: Ritual of Corruption begins. The summoning of the Soul of Corruption is complete, again with the aid of the Slave Orbs, but control of the thing is tenuous; a host of clerics chant at all times to maintain control of it. The Soul of Corruption is known to the Order, but I believe I am the first to have any involvement so close with the thing. It was likely employed to keep people both in and out of the farm.

The Ritual of Corruption needed not only the bolstering might of the Slave Orbs, but a constant supply of fresh blood, which was grotesquely provided by the prisoners that resisted the

transcendence. These unfortunate souls were eviscerated and then hung, still living but in unthinkable agony, from the walls of the Table of the Sun. Their eviscerations and hangings were expertly carried out so as to leave them living for as long as possible; so long as blood dripped from their still-living bodies, the Ritual of Corruption could be maintained. In this horrifying and gruesome fashion, every member of the Whitegate family that resided at the farm perished, as did Magistrate Zuldrick. Their corpses were found decorating the walls by those that survived the Battle.

  Mirtul 23, 1480: Alystair Dretch and Tuugan Swift attempt to investigate the farm, based on their own research (Hoetmer and I had arrived at the conclusion that this investigation was necessary at the same time, but to their credit, the White Cloaks were a step ahead of us). The pair of able White Cloaks were rebuked by the Soul of Corruption, which met them on the road and drove them off. They had fought through throngs of orcs to get that far, but their determination was no match for this new terror.

  Mirtul 25, 1480: Alystair and Tuugan return to Dagger Falls, and link up with many of their fellow White Cloaks. The group of them met with Hoetmer and myself, and we discussed what we thought was going on at the farm. We were not fully aware of what the situation might be, and unanimously decided that another team had to attempt to get into the farm to see what was going on. Alystair, Tuugan, Alen Reks, Ganolen Thendolain, and Troia Trustar were formed into a team and headed out to investigate immediately.

  Mirtul 26, 1480: The investigative team of Whitecloaks manages to out-manouvre the Soul of Corruption, only to discover the horrible truth about Liberty Hill. The entire farm is cloaked in illusion, under the Ritual of Obfuscation; this ritual was also made possible by the powerful influence of the Imperiosus Orbis. The town and the people appeared to be normal, even though in truth the streets were littered with bodies and debris, and the pale faces and empty black eyes that looked down at them from a hundred windows were likewise masked in a visage of innocence.

  The White Cloaks were being led into a trap by Lt. Hardigan and Priestess/Mayor

  Goering. Unaware of the horrifying truth of the place, they followed the evil Sharrans, and would have been led directly to their deaths, if not for the intervention of Phin. Phin was following the White Cloaks in his Aetherial Dream, and the illusory magic of that terrible ritual did not extend to the Aether. He called out to them, and Alistair and Troia heard him, before they were led into the keep, where a score of undead and shadow-kind assassins waited. Once Alistair and Troia saw through the illusion, a desperate battle and flight took place. The entire town mobilized against the five White Cloaks, and they barely escaped with their lives. Tuugan will not even recount the tale to his friends; the man’s character has been forever changed by this experience.

  Mirtul 28, 1480: The White Cloaks return to Dagger Falls, to find that Lumina and the rest of the White Cloaks have already arrived there. At a conference with every available officer in both military structures, the upper ranks of the clergy of Amaunator, myself and the White Cloaks, it is decided that a siege will be laid to Liberty Hill as soon as a host can be assembled. For a few frantic hours, those capable of inspiring men and women to arms went door-to-door, and in the end, an army was assembled (c.f. Army of the White Saint). They marched out of Dagger Falls that evening at double time.

  Kythorn 1, 1480: The Army of the White Saint arrives at Crossed Blades Farm, where it is joined by Knight Commander Pauldron, Banitt Harbaugh, a ride of Knights, and a company of volunteer militia from the already-beleaguered farm. By late afternoon, they arrive at the fields of Liberty Hill, and the Saint’s Battle is joined.

That is a short version of the events that lead up to the Saint’s Battle, but I think it lays out neatly the salient events that brought the men of Daggerdale to slaughter each other in that terrible conflict.

  As noted, I will not recount the Battle. I was not there. I have collected tales of this historic battle as they have come to me, and have included a few of the more notable ones in this report.

  I shall now go into the aftermath of the Battle, which I will divide into three periods; immediate, rebuilding, and prosperity.

  Liberty Hill Retaken - By Kesh Oakdale

Immediate Aftermath of the Saints’ Battle

The Saint’s Battle came to its conclusion by fits and starts. When the White Cloaks were able to infiltrate the cathedral and interrupt the Ritual of Corruption, it is widely reported that an audible cheer was heard over the entire battlefield, as the Soul of Corruption ceased its merciless assault on the Army of the White Saint, and dissipated harmlessly. The first company over the wall was the 2nd Dalehearth Platoon, and they gained the wall after driving off Lotus. Their banners flying on the walls inspired many other companies to rally and redouble their efforts at the hopeless siege. Finally, when the White Cloaks gained the Table of the Sun, fought with Belladonna and her guardian, and ultimately defeated her through the courageous action of Drake Sterling, the battle was over.

By the time Drake and his captive hit the ground (he actually grabbed Belladonna and plummeted over the side of the tower with her!), there were already 3 platoons of Sun Blades, a small host of Knights, and Bishop Whitegate at the base of the tower; they had fought their way up the street, and the sight of the flashing Blade of the White Saint on the battlements of the Table of the Sun inspired them to victory. So it was that as Drake hit the ground and lost consciousness, there were men on hand to apprehend the villainess that had masterminded this terrible plot. Belladonna was taken into custody by Bishop Whitegate, and Drake was taken to the medical tents.

The Battle was over; Belladonna was defeated, the malign Rituals that had transformed the people and the place were disrupted. Most of the undead had been destroyed, and the human victims of the Ritual of Transcendence who had been turned into shadow-fiend versions of themselves, slowly came to their senses. Those few that were loyal to the cause before that terrible ritual were already dead or captured.

It was discovered that Lotus, seeing that her troops were going to lose the wall once the Soul of Corruption was taken out of play, attempted to flee. However, here a card was played by my Phin. We had scrutinized every report about the Dark Garden we had available to us, a score of times or

  more. We knew that those women could all shape-shift into ravens, and that their apprehension would be nearly impossible. We also knew about the deal that Warburton had made with Kruhwahll. Phin was able to contact the dragon, through means that he would not divulge to me, and made another deal with it. He said he would tell the dragon where the Dark Garden could be found, if it kept out of sight of the men of the battle, and if it left the Warburton farm. The dragon kept to the deal, for the most part; when Lotus tried to fly away, Kruhawahll swept down to consume her. There are rumours of a dragon in the dark skies above the battle, but there is no one who will go on the record to say that they saw such a dragon. Of his agreement to leave Warburton farm, that must be dealt with in another report.

  Deadnettle was also killed in the battle. As the Whitecloaks fought their way up the citadel, trying to get to the Table of the Sun on the battlements, they were attacked by Deadnettle and a host of her Black Trolls. In this encounter, she was slain outright.

  So, the Dark Garden was entirely defeated, as was the Army of the Black Saint. The Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin was in the possession of the Bishop. Liberty Hill Farm was secure.

  However, the pain and suffering were just beginning.

  Before the Dark Garden took over the farm, and enacted its many malign rituals, the population of the farm was over 800. As the sun set on the 1st of Kythorn, 1480, the population of the farm was 225. Of those, most were children; only a handful of adults had survived the Ritual of Transcendence and the bloody siege. The entire organizational structure of the farm had been completely eliminated; there was not a single good priest or priestess left in town, nor were there any elders or military leaders.

  The state of the farm itself was terrible; the slaughter before the siege and the siege itself left hundreds dead on both sides, fires and siege weapons had demolished about a fifth of the buildings, and the fields were black from fires and necromantic infection. It was a disheartening sight for all; bodies were strewn about the streets, including the ghastly sight of the mutilated undead everywhere. From the walls of the central citadel were hung almost 30 citizens, including every Whitegate and elder in the village; they had been left to bleed out slowly to maintain the Ritual of Corruption, and the horror and pain on their dead faces and the gruesome eviscerations that dangled many feet below them was too much to witness, especially for their family members. Even worse; some were yet alive, and though Bannitt insisted that they could be saved, none of them wanted to live after the ordeal. The final details of their lives are best kept off the record.

  Nearly every child in the village had become an orphan. Some of the suckling children had perished for want of their mothers, and there are a few terrible stories about some unfortunate children that my heart can’t bear to report. But, it is a small blessing that most of the children did indeed survive. As it

  happens, the children were spared by Belladonna because she had made arrangements with the Bloody Eye to sell them into slavery. We learned this from Malveau, who showed up a day after the battle, who had followed these bandits back into the dale.

Added to the misery of the raw carnage, hitting the survivors and the troops that liberated the farm shortly after the euphoria of victory was diminished, was the guilt that nearly every soldier and citizen felt at about having to battle their countrymen. There are numerous tales of soldiers having killed their shadow-enslaved relatives, then falling completely apart emotionally only a few moments after Belladonna was in custody. Again, there are countless tales to tear the heart from one’s chest, and they will not be recounted here. Suffice it to say, there was no one glad to have been involved in this endeavour, no matter what side they fought on.

Hardest hit, though, was the Whitegate Family. Lumina, who had been struggling with her own experiences facing off against her enemies, was a shell for a long time after the battle. I am told that she led the 3rd platoon of the Dalehearth Company down the main concourse of the village as the battle came to a close, and that as soon as she came to the great courtyard in front of the citadel, and saw the nightmare hangings thereupon, that she fell to her knees. I can personally testify that she said not a word from that point on, until many weeks later. We all feared for her soul, and though she recovered it, for the most part, it remains to this day a cherished treasure to be closely guarded.

  Immediately after the battle had concluded, there was, of course, a tremendous amount of disorder. Even with Bishop Whitegate there, there was so much madness to be resolved that it was many hours before the wailing, screaming, suffering and general chaos was abated. During these tumultuous hours, a few notable events happened.

  First, and I have mentioned it; Belladonna was taken prisoner, rather than being executed on the spot, as nearly every soldier, citizen and child was screaming for. The Bishop was every inch the lord of the place, and with the support of Knight Commander Pauldron, she was kept alive, and was secured and taken away. I am unsure where they took her right away, but I will tell of her fate below.

  Second; though the battle with the undead and shadow-slaves of the Army of the Black Saint had ended, there were still three companies of orcs engaged with various elements of the Army of the White Saint outside the walls. This part of the battle was not going well for the tired men of the dale, and many might have perished if not for the arrival of Braxus, the werewolf lord from Gang’Dar. He swept through the ranks of orcs and killed and scattered them in little time. The Sun Blades that he had delivered were wary of this awesome creature; none would approach him to give thanks. Braxus simply waited outside the gates, stalking among the dead and dying outside, dispatching orcs here and there. At length, Troia went out to greet him. What transpired between them none then knew. Braxus left and Troia returned.

  Third; some of the Whitecloaks rushed to Lumina when they saw her falter in the courtyard. Though she said nothing to any of them, she did one single act that spoke volumes; she handed the epaulets from her cloak to Iolus. All the Whitecloaks hailed Iolus as their new captain. From that time on, Lumina had no part in the company she helped to create. She has been living a peaceful life at the farm since that day, and Iolus has been an inspiring captain.

  Fourth; Bannitt cut down the body of Magistrate Zuldrick, and was seen to weep at the death of the man who seemed to have died the cruellest death of the lot; he was tortured and mutilated beyond

recognition, save for his blood-soaked vestments. At that moment, Bannitt made an emotionally charged plea to Bishop Whitegate to be instated as the Magistrate of Liberty Hill Farm. For a moment afterwards, all were silent; it is said that White Cloaks and Knights of the Lambent Shield alike stared in disbelief, awaiting the reply from the Bishop. Whitegate took a moment to consider it, drawing the suspense of the moment out. Bannitt made a beautiful speech which I will append to this report; it is well documented. His case was that he had come from a far-off land on pilgrimage to see the White Saint. He had liberated the home of the Saint with his very own blade, and felt as though his journey had come to its end. The bishop agreed, and Bannitt was invested on the spot. He remains the Magistrate at the farm, and a close friend.

  Fifth; the Slave Orbs of the Imperialis Orbis were taken from the Table of the Sun. These were indeed brought by the Bishop right to Phin and I for examination. They are terrible, powerful artefacts that are tied to the Imperialis Orbis, which was plundered from the depths of a Netherese ruin the summer before by Loki. Ironically, it was Bannitt’s team of bounty-seekers that failed that mission, and it was he that carried the blade of the White Saint to victory on the Table of the Sun, thus helping to capture these weapons in the end. I have appended a report about the Slave Orbs.

  In the days that followed, many stayed at Liberty Hill. Many soldiers who had relations there (dead or alive) stayed to help out. Of the White Cloaks, Bannitt, Lumina, and Troia stayed on. Bannitt, of course, had an office to keep, and Troia stayed mostly to look after her friend. For the rest, there was still a war raging, and many loose ends to wrap up. With apologies, the rest of the White Cloaks and most of the army parted the morning after the battle; they were headed off to Foot Hills Farm to put an end to the last of the covens of Shar. I have appended the report from Alystiar Dretch about this encounter, but suffice it to say, the momentum of this victory was enough to settle matters at Foot Hills in a relatively bloodless fashion.

War Raged On

With the Dark Garden defeated, many of the problems in the Heartland sorted themselves out in the weeks to come. Each farm was again able to secure its borders, and before long, there were once again young men marching to the Castle, to join the battle on the Western front.

  The forces of Netheril increased the tempo of the war in the west with the defeat of the Dark Garden. They no-doubt realized that with this threat in the heart-land ended, that additional troops would likely be available to bolster the defences of the Dalesmen on the front. On the 19th of Flamerule, the definitive battle of the war took place; the Siege of Fort Lastsun. I will refer you to the report made by Brother Neddle of this battle. It is by all accounts the best compilation and interpretation of the various reports and stories about the battle.

  I must needs summarize it quickly, however, if only to draw a line between the outcome of that battle and the White Cloaks who unwittingly contributed so much.

  By the 18th of Flamerule, all three divisions of Netherese soldiers involved in this war were massed a mile away from Fort Lastsun. It appeared as though they would make the final surge of the battle; Lastsun would not be able to endure the assault, even though they were likely to inflict unthinkable casualties on the Netherese from their strong defensive position. We now know that the Obris Imperiosus were employed for this battle; they used them to darken the skies above the battle so that the sun never rose on the morning of the 19th; the Netherese General must have known that time was

not on his side. He would have waited until the night of the 9th to launch his attack in the cover of darkness, rather than use the orbs in this capacity and hasten the attack.

So, before breakfast was served, the Netherese divisions began their assault; the valley leading up to the Fort was choked with twenty-thousand soldiers representing a dozen fiendish races. Siege machines launching devilishly-enchanted stones blasted apart the anterior walls of the fort, and a desperate defence was made by the 1500 men inside. However, even though they were slaughtering the attackers at a ratio of 5:1, they would not win the day. But, help unlooked-for arrived.

As the men of Fort Lastsun were down to about 300 defenders, and it seemed as though they would be overrun at any moment, the very earth shook, and the floor of the valley, and the thousands of attackers queued to the slaughter thereupon, vanished. The earth opened up and swallowed them, leaving a deep chasm in front of the ruined Fort. There was panic in the Netherese ranks, and those that remained in the castle, ahead of the army-swallowing chasm, attempted to flee.

To the rear of the chasm, where the Netherese Command headquarters had been smugly watching the slaughter, another great slaughter happened. This time, it was at the hands of the Tehyamar League, which issued out of the chasm proximal to the Netherese Command; 600 dwarven warriors emerged to engage double their number of elite headquarters guard. They exploited the confusion that their trap had caused, and in the end they slaughtered and drove off the Command, though nearly 300 of them were killed in the assault.

The Tethyamar League had rallied dwarves across Northern Faerun in the months leading up to the war, after the announcement of the discovery of the Eastern Gate of Tethyamar. This gate had been discovered by Deadnettle, who was in turn found by Warburton’s team of White Cloaks months

  before. This discovery acted as a lightening rod that attracted hundreds of dwarves to the region, who were convinced by Curator Gambledin of the Tethyamar League to do what they could for the war with Netheril. Their clever trap, the sapping of an entire valley just in the knick of time, and their selfless assault upon overwhelming odds upon the command units, ended the war with Netheril. The League had kept their subterranean arrival in the theatre a secret; Br. Neddle claims that Gambledin did not trust everyone in the command of the Dale forces to reveal his plans, or even announce that he had assembled the host he did.

  So, before Shieldmeet, what Netherese forces remained were in full retreat, and the war was concluded, for the time being. The losses were exceptionally heavy, though; nearly 3000 dalesmen lost their lives in this conflict, and nearly that number again of mercenaries. The cost to Netheril has been conservatively estimated at more than double that figure.

The Fate of Belladonna

As I write this, Belladonna is a captive on the Table of the Sun at Liberty Hill. She was brought there almost immediately after the Saint’s Battle, along with the Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin. She was held there without trial until after Shieldmeet of 1480; Argent and Luminous wanted to be at the trial. These men had visited their ancestral home the day of Shieldmeet (a day I shall never forget as long as I live; I have never witnessed or experienced such peaks and valleys of emotion as that visit inspired), and soon after rushed to Dagger Falls to conduct the trial. I have already forwarded the

proceedings of this trial (c.f. Proceedings of the Trial of Belladonna Liberty, Dagger Falls, 7 Elesias, 1480 DR), which I would recommend to any student of jurisprudence. Despite her unparalleled level of

villainy and evil, she was given a fair trial in which no formality, no matter how trivial, was disregarded. I personally attended the trial, clutching the hand of my friend, Lumina, for the several days that it took to arrive at judgement and sentencing. Belladonna was found guilty of a list of crimes that took thirty minutes to cite. However, she was also acquitted on a number of charges that took twice that long to cite; so clever were her machinations that even as a confessed leader of the worst cult of Shar in history she could not be convicted of all crimes charged her.

When sentencing began, as is customary in the Dalelands, families and friends of the victims were heard from. The Whitegates relinquished their primacy in these depositions; at the outset, the people cried to hear Lumina and Luminous speak, but they allowed all others who had a claim to go ahead of

them. This took another 2 days, and I think everyone who stayed to listen to the extent of sorrow that was spread by this woman lost a year of their life for the hearing of it. I will never be the same after listening to so many weeping mothers and orphans.

Here, I will transcribe a few lines from the Proceedings, as my own words can say them no better;

  11225. Bishop Archer: The court will, at long last, hear the words of the Whitegate family. The court has been advised that Ms. Lumina Whitegate will depose for the family.

  11226. (Loud murmuring and disquietude follows)

  11227. (Lumina stands from her seat, deposing from her bench rather than approach the testimonial box; her hands are held by two women on either side of her. The court falls quiet as she stands silent for about 90 seconds)

  11228. Bishop: Whenever you are ready, Ms. Whitegate…

  11229. Lumina Whitegate: Thank you, I am. The woman convicted in this court has caused more misery than an entire army of Netherese soldiers who waged war with malignant determination died trying to match. She has been sewing unholy discord for decades, by her own confession. She has been the instrument of my family’s near-total annihilation. She has killed most of the people I love. She has done something so terrible to me, that I cannot recount it here, for the sake of my family and friends; the ordeal I endured at her pleasure in the Shadowfell was worse than a nightmare within a nightmare. And yet, I cannot sentence her to death…

  11230. (disquiet in the court, Lumina waits for it to subside)

  11231. Ms. Whitegate: I cannot sentence her to death. To do so would be to let her win. She has earned her place at her master’s side; she will be a captain in Hell, and we will have to deal with her again. I would encourage the court to sentence her thusly; to live in confinement in the Tower of Liberty Hill, where daily she will have to defend her faith from me; I would convert this woman and commit her soul to Amaunator…

  11232. (disquiet in the court had been rising, and a fervour washes over the followers of Amaunator with her last words; the Bishop himself offers his palms, and most of the court follows his lead)

  11233. Ms. Whitegate: I would convert this woman and commit her soul to Amaunator, and I would do it at the very place that bears her name, the very place she tried to destroy. I would take her home, and bring her into the fold!

In the end, after many more hours of proceedings, this was in fact the sentence passed down. To this day, Belladonna lives on the Table of the Sun, where daily, my friend mounts the stairs to face off with her nemesis in a battle for both of their souls.

Belladonna is not kept there by reverence for her sentence alone. She is confined to the Table by the most powerful prayers and rituals of the clergy and magic of the Warsmiths available. Also, Lumina is not her only visitor. Magistrate Invictus has been given a new portfolio; he is personally responsible for the incarceration and interrogation of this woman, and spends much of his time at Liberty Hill, fruitlessly trying to draw the shadow out of Belladonna. To date, Belladonna has not said a word in the company of either of her visitors.

The Whitegates

The massacre at Liberty Hill was an unprecedented blow to the Whitegate family. It is not even rivalled by the massacre of 1 Kythorn, 1455. Both of Lumina’s siblings, Lumen and Luminosus, as well as her cousin Lucidus, were fed to the Ritual of Corruption, dying unthinkable deaths. Her mother, Lanya (Forester) was found in the chapel, and had clearly been the object of the cruellest and vilest entertainments for the monsters that held the place; her body did not endure her torments, and we pray daily that her soul is intact. The

fates of the children of Lucidus and Sarah Forester, who were 2, 3, and 5 years at the time, cannot be

  reported in detail. They are dead, and that is enough. Various other cousins, aunts and uncles were likewise killed, so that as Lumina mounted the walls during the battle, she was the only Whitegate in the town. She was soon joined by her uncle, Bishop Blaze Whitegate, as his unit stormed down the concourse. These are the only two Whitegates to spend so much as one night at Liberty Hill since that dreadful day.

  Her grandfather, Argent, and her great-uncle, Luminous, both survived the war on the western front. Tales of their bravery during the long siege at the edge of Netheril are numerous and

  inspiring. Luminous was every inch a hero, and during those days he moved with such vigour that one could presume he was in the springtime of life again. I personally watched him take stairs two-a-stride in his full-plate armour with great alacrity. However, when the two men came to their ancestral home after the war wrapped up, and saw what had happened, both were again reduced to the old men that they have become.

  Though never diminished in bearing, influence or good judgement, both men now visibly wear their years. Argent remains the Zenith Lord of Daggerdale, and he continues to carry out the duties of his

office without any noticeable deficit, despite his losses. Luminous continues to be Lord of Castle Radiance and Grand Master of the Order of the Lambent Shield, two roles he carries out with the same diligence that he has long been known for. He is a little more aloof, now, however. He is almost never seen below the Inner Bailey of the Castle, and he has not returned to Liberty Hill since the sentencing of Belladonna.

The bulk of the survivors of the Whitegate family are now found at Foothills Farm as Thunderstorms, and at Tesh Valley farm as Blackmanes. There are not presently any children with the name Whitegate, and Lumina is the only child-bearing possibility. But, I get ahead of myself, and betray my own metropolitan proclivities; the people of Daggerdale do not have the same notion of families that we have lived with here in Waterdeep, and I should dispense with any notion that there are hereditary claims to be made for any positions of governance by the Thunderstorms and Blackmanes; those two families do indeed have such roles, but they are well earned and well kept.

The White Cloaks

The White Cloaks were changed dramatically after the Saints Battle. All 14 active members of the company stormed the walls of Liberty Hill during that battle, some as a small and elite unit to infiltrate the fort quickly, others as leaders of platoons of Sun Blades, Militia, and volunteers. All survived bodily, though for some, their story with the White Cloaks ended that day.

Lumina handed over the company to her friend and colleague (in both Faith and Arms), Ioulus Warburton. She has had no active part of the White Cloaks since that time, though I can personally attest to the fact that her cloak remains hanging on the back of her door in her little cottage up at Liberty Hill.

  The tale of Magistrate Harbaugh has already been touched upon. He was granted office immediately after the battle, by Bishop Whitegate, and has maintained that office dutifully and to the betterment of the farm (see ‘Liberty Hill Farm’, below) ever since.

  Tuugan Swift retired from the ranks in the winter of 1480. He was never the same after his narrow escape from the farm on the reconnaissance of Mirtul 26, 1480, though he soldiered on through the Saints Battle and many other smaller engagements through until the winter. He was never able to face Lumina, though, after their excursion into the Shadowfell the year before. I am privy to some of Lumina’s secrets, and though I will keep them as promised, I can say that Lumina and Tuugan shared a terrible experience together in the Darkness; they both carry a great guilt about what happened in there, and are unable to forgive themselves for what they did to each other.

  In early Uktar, Tuugan and Alystair concluded some business at Crossed Blades Farm. Alystair announced that he would be going to see his friends at Liberty Hill, but Tuugan declined to accompany him. His reasons were two-fold; he could not face Lumina, but he had also become involved with a young lady named Milli Oakdale. This devout Chauntean (indeed, daughter of the Cultivar of the farm) is the younger sister of a man named Brag Oakdale, who Tuugan led over the walls of Liberty Hill. Brag had introduced his captain to his sister at the Castle Gate Inn, and things fell into place for the couple. As I write this, I have just seen Tuugan, having been to the Castle Gate, and though he is quiet, he is happy; Milli is due to deliver their first child any day now, and Tuugan is serving drinks at the Inn to make up for his young wife, who he will not allow to work. Tuugan does not keep much company besides his new family these days; Alystair periodically drops in, but Tuugan has not sought out any of his old friends,

and has not since the 1st of Kythorn, 1480, set foot within the walls of Liberty Hill. Alystair tells me, in confidence, that Tuugan has spent many nights patrolling the dark forest surrounding Liberty Hill Farm, unknown to the residents he protects.

The White Cloaks continue to operate effectively under the leadership of Iolus. The current details of the company, including roster and ranks, I will append to this report.

What is important to note is that the company was well rewarded for their efforts in the Saints

Battle. Luminous met with Iolus at the Yellow Abbey after the sentencing of Belladonna in 1480. I was on hand at the time, and was asked to notarize an agreement between these two men. Luminous handed over possession of Loedegan Close (the ancestral home of the Whitegate Family in Town) to the White Cloaks, providing them with a base of operations within the walls of Town. The Close was slated to be sold to the Red Jacks mercenary company, but after the leadership of the White Cloaks in the Saints Battle, Luminous wanted to reward them. Presently, Loedegan Close is acts as a second base of operations for the White Cloaks, and there seems to be much activity there much of the time.

  I would add that I feel as though the gift of the Close was something of a closure for the Whitegates. I think Luminous was happy to be free of this relic from a previous era; that in which his family was growing and prospering. With there being little hope that his name would live another generation, pride gave way to necessity.

  I would also add that the gift of the Close was something of an injury to the Red Jacks. There was already no small amount of bad blood between the Red Jacks and the White Cloaks (indeed, between the Red Jacks and pretty much every professional soldier in the region) before the gift, but afterwards, animosity and acrimony on the part of the Red Jacks was all but enough to have them exiled from Dagger Dale.

  The White Cloaks continue to steward White Cloak Tower up at the Castle.
