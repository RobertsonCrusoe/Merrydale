Notes for Beliard

Between your arrival at Castle Radiance in the spring of 1480 DR, and the current adventure, which kicks off for you on 13 Marpenoth, 1481, you have had an eventful residency. You replaced an aging bureaucrat that had served Ambassador John Tunbridge for several years, basically taking on the role of secretary to the Ambassador. Your daily duties have been many; you receive and send the correspondence of the Ambassador, taking caring of the logistics of the embassy, sitting in on all meetings that the embassy is invited to (and taking notes), and so on. Though this work at first seemed below you, you quickly realize that you have access to some of the most privileged information in the Castle. Further, Tunbridge is old and fat, and he has made every effort to suggest that you will be replacing him when the Grey Lord calls him from this world.

You went on a 3-month assignment to Base Camp Silver in Tethyamar, which was dangerous and frightening. The nearly-constant battle in the deep is paired with a politically complicated scenario that remains bordering on fratricide and civil war. You managed to develop a good rapport with the Silver Council. In fact, due to Tunbridge’s health, he was unable to attend this mission, and you are the face of Cormyr to the Silver Council and the various parties involved in this struggle.

You also developed a rapport with Captain Iolus Warburton of the White Cloaks, who had the bulk of his team sequestered in the deep, waging war on the side of Tasster Gellinson. At the time, you were giving orders to General Stuart Angleton and his Purple Dragons, and you managed to provide help in more than one timely instance to the benefit of the moderate Gellinson and the White Cloaks.

Returning to the Castle in the late summer, you made a new friend in the curious figure known as Alen Reks, the man that Warburton left in charge of White Cloak matters in his absence. Reks is certainly an ambitious man; he plays at being a simple servant to the White Cloaks, but you detect a certain cunning in the man, and suspect that he is hiding something. At any rate, the two of you started sharing some information between yourselves, and with a few unimportant morsels you managed to buy yourself something into a very interesting scenario…

13 Marpenoth, 1481 DR.

It is the end of what has been another pleasant day in the front office of the Tower. It has been a fine late summer/early fall up in the mountains, and just about any weather above ground is better than the oppressive humidity and sweltering heat of Tethyamar which you suffered through for months. You are just about to lock the doors for the day, when a runner comes with a small package for you.

The package is an oiled leather folder with another leather folder inside. The inner folder has the initials AR embossed into one corner. Inside there is a collection of documents tied together with a white ribbon, and a note attached to the stack sticking conspicuously out. You recognize the looping hand of Administrator Reks almost instantly. His hand is as flighty and mercurial as the man himself.

Urgent Correspondence from Administrator Reks to Secretary Emmarask

11 Marpenoth, 1481 DR.

Mr. Secretary;

I suppose it’s time I paid you out quid pro quo, as it were, even if it is not exactly pro quo, as it were. Ha ha! I have a collection of documents that I think you will find interesting. You have no doubt heard about the ugliness at Dwimmerholme farm, and no doubt you have heard of the triumph of my Bravo Team. However, there is something, if I am not mistaken, which will interest you and your masters, unless I miss my mark. I’m sure I don’t haha!

Yes, there is something indeed, but I won’t spoil the surprise for you.

There is more to tell, though. I had the most unpleasant discourse with my colleague, Mr. Dretch, on these affairs. He was adamant that I was not to send the team to Charity Hills, and although he would not tell me the reasons, and although I don’t take orders from Dretch, as you well know, I acquiesced to the man, and sent my team the other direction, as it were haha! At any rate, I don’t doubt you’ll find something relevant to your interests in this collection of documents, clever as you are.

I don’t suppose I have to suggest that we not brag about the help we have offered each other, eh eh?

Your Servant,

A.Reks.

PS: The collection is incomplete. You will find the remainder with my erstwhile lackey, Mr. Nathan Gale, including the interesting books around which these plots and schemes seem to revolve. Give Gale my best if you see him. Or don’t…

The collection of documents includes the following (I have emailed you this list with the wiki links, for simplicity). Have a read through them, and we’ll proceed.

Further to this collection of documents, you know a bit about this cult called the Sons of Bel. It is an organization that has plagued Marsember for decades, dating back to the late 1300’s. You have never heard of them operating outside of Cormyr at all, and rarely outside of their home city inside Cormyr.

The cult in Marsember was infiltrated by Royal Agents in 1479, and its activities have been on the decline ever since. However, with the information in the documents above, it looks like they may have been planning on a move at any rate.

There are some geopolitical concerns here; the trouble with the cult in Marsember used up vast resources for the Purple Dragons and other Royal agencies. This of course drew resources away from the front with Netheril. As of last year, hundreds of knights, thousands of footmen, and countless subtle resources have been redirected to the Cormyr/Netheril front. The fact that this all happened at the same time as the Dark Garden affair, and the large-scale war that accompanied it on the Daggerdale/Netheril front, is not lost on you.

I’ll leave it at that, and hope you respond ASAP. I want to see what you decide to do with this information.
