DDC – 1481 – 12: Reports from Sgt. Paros 
18 – 20 Marpenoth 
GFD Notes: 
Provided by Reks, these are very clean and reliable summaries of what must have been a trying adventure. 
-GFD 
18 Marpenoth 
I've never been to Charity before today, and I have to say that it is quite different than the rest of the dale, as I've always heard. For starters, it looks much wealthier at first glance, but looking a little closer, I wonder if more than half of the people here aren't worse off than everyone else in the dale. But that's not for me to report on now. 

The caravan arrived unmolested, though a day later than we would have liked. The rain made for slow going for the wagons with the mud and all. The road along the Charnel River passes just a bit south of a ruined and abandoned village called Whittenhead, about 5 miles before Charity. We saw movement in the ruins, and it seemed clear to me that we were being observed as we passed them by. 

A little while later, we were happy to see the walls and lights and smoke of Charity as we got near, but our happiness was short-lived. 

In the last 100 yards before the gates, we were able to start taking in some of the details of the place; wrought iron embellishments adorn most buildings, dressing up eaves, the peaks and corners of roofs, and so on. The windows are mostly diamond-patterned leaded-glass, which while prohibitively expensive in the rest of the dale, seem to be common in even the poorer homes here. As we began to pick out detail after detail, our attention was drawn to a flash up high; from a tower perched high in the inner bailey, a fireball blew out the windows on the topmost layer. The flames belched out and subsided, leaving only a flickering flame inside that cast a chaotic, moving light. There was an audible gasp from the dozens of men in the caravan, and some people on the walls of the town. There was one silent instant that was quickly broken by the screaming, flailing, flame-engulfed form of a person coming out of the window and plummeting downward. The flaming spectacle vanished behind the walls in its terminal descent. The spectacle was added to by the forms of what first appear to be large sparks flying from the windows. However, the flight-pattern of these sparks soon revealed them to be burning birds, and before long, their determined flight from the building subsided as the flames took their lives; they fell to the ground leaving contrails of smouldering feathers falling softly behind them. We would find out later who it was that fell burning from that window to his certain death 

With this, the hue and cry went up, and the town came alive, as people came out to either help address the threat of fire, or simply to spectate. It is in this chaos that we were welcomed to the streets of Charity. 

We parted ways with the caravan, and sought out our contact at the Old Fort, Medea Rowan. I'm quite suspicious of this woman; as the Madame of the Old Fort and a self-declared priestess of Sharess, she is at the very least a woman of forbidden tastes who encourages excess and vice. But her hospitality was 

enormous, and she offered more than any of us would indulge in. She was quite busy and insisted that she'd connect with us during the day tomorrow at a place called Arwencroft (from which I write this very note). She then introduced us to a woman named Flora Scarlett, who had been hired by Basil Muloch many days ago to be our steward. Flora is a wonderful young woman, a native of the town whom everyone seems to admire. She had the keys and a legal agreement for Arwencroft, which I am happy to report is ours to use until we're concluded here. 

Tired and wet, we were happy to retire to Arwencroft, which typifies the middle-class comfort of Charity; there were many coal-stoves warming the place up, many lanterns to light it, and fine furnishings the likes of which one does not really find in the rest of the dale. A warm meal awaited, made by Flora's own hand, and we took heart. 

Flora stayed up late with us, to give us all the recent news that she thought relevant to our mission here. 

First, she told us of Dretch's visit, 10 days ago. He came to Charity, knocked some heads around in the Old Fort, and then headed to the inner bailey (this is the part of Charity at the top of the hill, upon which the estates of the 'Landmen' are located). What he did or who he spoke to there is unknown, but he shortly after went into the foothills to the west. This is where his trail goes cold. 

Second, a woman named Sarah Gideon died in an accident on the 15th; walking through the gates from the Inner Bailey to the Middle Bailey, a loose stone fell from the gatehouse, struck her on the head, and killed her instantly. Though there are some structures needing a bit of upkeep in the town, this is a tremendous coincidence, as Sarah Gideon was seen coming in and out of the Muloch Estate a couple of times in the previous days. 

The third bit of news came to us by way of a runner from the Inner Bailey. He sought out Flora, to give her the news that the commotion we saw was an explosion in the library-tower of the Muloch Estate, that Basil was confirmed dead, and that the Silverstars (a quasi-religious part-time fighting force made up of followers of Selune, Waukeen and the Red Knight, mostly Landmen), had locked the estate down, fearing for the safety of any who would wish to enter. 

This is terrible news, as we intended to speak with Basil tomorrow, and indeed, Flora had booked a meeting with him for early morning. We will try to get into Muloch Estate and find out what happened. 
But now it is late, and it has been a long journey 
19 Marpenoth 
Today was a whirlwind of activity, which I will summarize as neatly I can. 
We set out first thing in the morning to investigate the Muloch Estate. This was not easy; the Silverstars, 

Lead by Captain Archibald Ferguson, would not let us on the premises. On top of a few of these capable-looking men, there were a score of Ferguson House Guard around the walls of the Muloch Estate, armed with the rifles for which this town is well know. 

Using an abundance of caution and diplomacy, we utilized the influence of the local Ambassador to Daggerdale, Ridge Oakdale. This is a man I knew as a child, and though he has no real authority here, and no mentionable resources, he is an able diplomat, and knew his way around the Silverstars. He waited for Archibald to vacate the scene, and spoke this his cousin, Simon Whitmore, who saw his duty 

and our place in this investigation clearly. We were permitted entry after many hours of waiting for diplomacy to run its course. 

The Muloch Estate was the hunting-ground for a devil that Artorias later identified as a Watcher. This devil-snake thing hunted us with great cunning through the Estate, and had some minions assisting it in what appeared to be a sacking of the library. In the end we bested the thing, but currently, many of our team have pustulent wounds from the bite of the thing that won't heal. We have sent these team members to Charnel Priory for healing, which I am told will be complete by tomorrow. 

The Estate had been ransacked by the Watcher and its helpers. The top-floor of the library-tower (it is a 3-floor affair, very opulent and well-appointed with learning) was still smouldering from the fireball which consumed Basil and his aviary, and most of the books on that floor. We could find no natural reason for the fireball (it wasn't caused by explosives), and neither the Watcher nor its henchmen were capable of this sort of magic. Someone else caused this destruction and death. 

In the kitchens, we found a few very valuable sources of information, which I have collected and enclosed copies of.

I have just copied a couple of pages from a book of notes from Muloch's hand, which he took in Cormyr this winter during a long visit he made there. Why he went there, no one knows, but he was gone for most of the winter. His notes are full of family histories, one interesting one attached. 

I have given this book and some other valuables to his nephew, Arthur Whitmore, who we met today. Arthur is a stand-up fellow (I'm writing like these people talk), and everyone in the party, our steward included, thinks we can trust this man. 

There were some notes about some of the locals in this pile as well, which I have copied and included. We will be following up tomorrow at the houses of some of these people, who I suspect are tied up in the Sons of Bel business in some fashion. 

So, we missed our meeting with Medea Rowan today, and she has rebooked us for tomorrow, but did have the courtesy to send along nice baskets of food and gifts to Arwencroft for the 'inconvenience', even though it was us providing the offense. 

All of this and a couple of brawls in the street with the locals. The 'freemen' of the town are both distrustful of strangers and fond of fisticuffs. No real blood spilled in either encounter, but this is a difficult place to do good work in 
20 Marpenoth 
We have made some real progress today. The very first thing we (we being half of us, as half were convalescing at Charnel Priory) did was visit the house of Niles Gideon, the husband of the woman who died in an accident several days ago, who also holds the title of Guildmaster of the Scriveners and Editor 

of The Loyalist (the local news publication). As I write this, Gideon is at-large, having escaped us at Whittenhead Ruin 

Investigating his house early in the morning, we found him missing, but clear evidence in his basement of devil-worship. There were two Derelict women in the basement, and two dead men. A nearby pentacle and evidence of torture told us that this was a makeshift site of a Bel's Bargain Ritual. As I write this, the two women have been taken to Charnel Priory, where they are conducting the Prayer of Adduction presently, to recover their souls. We pray for their success. 

One other hideous discovery was made; that Gideon's young wife Sarah had also been made derelict, and that her 'murder' was simply a matter of disposing of the inconvenient husk of a woman who's main value was as soul-currency. Artorias speculates that her soul was likely used to pay for something as powerful as the Watcher we fought yesterday at the Muloch Estate. 

We found notes in Muloch's kitchen yesterday regarding the purchase of Whittenhead by Niles Gideon, and guessed that he may have gone there. So three of us decided to do some reconnaissance to see if we were right. Whittenhead ruin is one of several small ruined settlements in Charity Hills, abandoned for maybe 10 years or so. Our recon turned into a full-fledged battle, however, as we discovered Niles Gideon there with a host of devil-spawn orcs. Though we slew the orcs nearly to the last, Gideon got away (as well as his infernal orc-sergeant), and showed his hand as a capable sorcerer; he indeed threw a fireball at us that has landed all but myself in Charnel Priority recovering. 

Having defeated and driven off Gideon and his foul orcs, we searched the remains of the town and discovered that one of the better-intact homes was being converted into a shrine for the Sons of Bel, and evidence of other rituals was clear. We took what we could to the authorities in Charity, those being; 
o John Constable 

o Simon Whitmore of the Silverstars 

o Chancellor and Earl John Ferguson – Head of the Landmen 

o Milburn Carpenter – Secretary of the Labour Assembly – Head of the Freemen 

Walter Keystone, the Speaker of the Common Assembly and the Guildmaster of Merchants, was summoned but could not be found. Many are looking for him presently, as he remains unaccounted for. We hope that he has not been a victim of Gideon and his schemes. 

We were roundly praised for our discovery of Gideon's plot, and the rescuing of the two common women from his basement (I wish we had gotten there in time to save their husbands). The authorities were quite interested in learning about the orcs at Whittenhead, and we reported exactly this; 

There were three sorts of these foes; regular orcs that you might expect to meet in the mountains, a fiendish orc that was possessed by a powerful devil, and the offspring of the two. Artorias knew quite a bit about this type of enemy, thankfully. The orcs were as difficult as you might expect; fierce and stupid, but they were led by the devilish orc who was clever, and managed to get away. His offspring proved to be the most durable; though young and inexperienced, they were incredibly strong and resilient, requiring magical weapons to harm. 

These authorities had much to order after our report and pledged their assistance to our investigation. 

Some of our team concluded their convalescence at the Priory in the afternoon, and continued to do more legwork in the town. We procured some silver bullets for Nathan's pistol, as these are needed to damage some devilkin, and we finally made our meeting with Medea Rowan. 

Medea proved to be very helpful. In her line of work, she and her subordinates learn many things, and although, she explained, they wouldn't normally divulge the secrets of their clients, she felt no particular need to protect Niles Gideon, who's location she thinks she knows. She's sure that he was seen headed out the Charnel Gate shortly after our encounter in the late morning, and he had a large group of men with him. 

Later in the day we were visited by Earl Ferguson, who we found waiting patiently at Arwencroft, after a runner had been sent for us at the Old Fort. The Earl is not the eldest of the Fergusons, and at about 55 years of age, he is quite fit and sharp. He was accompanied by his wife, Victoria (nee Whitmore) and his sister-in-law, Morgan (nee Muloch, younger sister of Basil). Morgan was very grateful that we uncovered the guilty party to her brother's murder, and the Earl, who is the highest-ranking man in Charity, gave us a letter-patent to Arwencroft (it is now the possession of the White Cloaks ad infinitum), and a chest of 500 gold as reward for our service, and to aid in any further investigation. He further committed a small group of soldiers to accompany us west, when we told him of our plans to track down Gideon and the men that fled with him. I suggested that he could make a map of the other cultists by taking a head-count in town right away, as the men who are missing would likely be those involved, but he countered saying that Freemen, Middlemen and Landmen alike all travel quite a bit between the various camps and mines of the region, as there is a degree of safety (usually) that permits it. 

After many long well-wishes, the Fergusons departed, and our team took to their beds early. We everyone are exhausted from our battle with the Watcher (and the horrible wounds it inflicted), our participation (some of us) in the Prayer of Adduction, which took nearly 16 hours of continuous labour, and now from infected wounds from the orcs at Whittenhead. We will rest a bit before we take to the road to pursue Gideon.
