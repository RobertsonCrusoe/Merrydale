==Immediate Aftermath of the Saints' Battle==
right|450px|thumb|A Knight of the Order of the Lambent Shield fights among the undead at the Saints' Battle

The Saint’s Battle came to its conclusion in fits and starts. When the White Cloaks were able to infiltrate the cathedral and interrupt the Ritual of Corruption, it is widely reported that an audible cheer was heard over the entire battlefield, as the Soul of Corruption ceased its merciless assault on the Army of the White Saint, and dissipated harmlessly. The first company over the wall was the 2nd Dalehearth Platoon, and they gained the wall after driving off Lotus. Their banners flying on the walls inspired many other companies to rally and redouble their efforts at the hopeless siege. Finally, when the White Cloaks gained the Table of the Sun, fought with Belladonna and her guardian, and ultimately defeated her through the courageous action of Drake Sterling, the battle was over.

By the time Drake and his captive hit the ground (he actually grabbed Belladonna and plummeted over the side of the tower with her!), there were already three platoons of Sun Blades, a small host of Knights, and Bishop Whitegate at the base of the tower; they had fought their way up the street, and the sight of the flashing Blade of the White Saint on the battlements of the Table of the Sun inspired them to victory. So it was that as Drake hit the ground and lost consciousness, there were men on hand to apprehend the villainess that had masterminded this terrible plot. Belladonna was taken into custody by Bishop Whitegate, and Drake was taken to the medical tents.

The Battle was over; Belladonna was defeated, the malign Rituals that had transformed the people and the place were disrupted. Most of the undead had been destroyed, and the human victims of the Ritual of Transcendence who had been turned into shadow-fiend versions of themselves, slowly came to their senses. Those few that were loyal to the cause before that terrible ritual were already dead or captured.

It was discovered that Lotus, seeing that her troops were going to lose the wall once the Soul of Corruption was taken out of play, attempted to flee. However, here a card was played by my Phin. We had scrutinized every report about the Dark Garden that we had available to us, a score of times or more. We knew that those women could all shape-shift into ravens, and that their apprehension would be nearly impossible. We also knew about the deal that Warburton had made with Kruhwahll. Phin was able to contact the dragon, through means that he would not divulge to me, and made another deal with it. He said he would tell the dragon where the Dark Garden could be found, if it kept out of sight of the men of the battle, and if it left the Warburton farm. The dragon kept to the deal, for the most part; when Lotus tried to fly away, Kruhwahll swept down to consume her. There are rumours of a dragon in the dark skies above the battle, but there is no one who will go on record to say that they saw such a dragon. Of the agreement to leave Warburton farm, that must be dealt with in another report.

Deadnettle was also killed in the battle. As the Whitecloaks fought their way up the citadel, trying to get to the Table of the Sun on the battlements, they were attacked by Deadnettle and a host of her Black Trolls. In this encounter, she was slain outright.

So, the Dark Garden was entirely defeated, as was the Army of the Black Saint. The Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin was in the possession of the Bishop. Liberty Hill Farm was secure.

However, the pain and suffering were just beginning.

Before the Dark Garden took over the farm, and enacted its many malign rituals, the population of the farm was over 800. As the sun set on the 1st of Kythorn, 1480, the population of the farm was 225. Of those, most were children; only a handful of adults had survived the Ritual of Transcendence and the bloody siege. The entire organizational structure of the farm had been completely eliminated; there was not a single good priest or priestess left in town, nor were there any elders or military leaders.

The state of the farm itself was terrible; the slaughter before the siege and the siege itself left hundreds dead on both sides, fires and siege weapons had demolished about a fifth of the buildings, and the fields were black from fires and necromantic infection. It was a disheartening sight for all; bodies were strewn about the streets, including the ghastly sight of the mutilated undead everywhere. From the walls of the central citadel were hung almost 30 citizens, including every Whitegate and elder in the village; they had been left to bleed out slowly to maintain the Ritual of Corruption, and the horror and pain on their dead faces and the gruesome eviscerations that dangled many feet below them was too much to witness, especially for their family members. Even worse; some were yet alive, and though Banitt insisted that they could be saved, none of them wanted to live after the ordeal. The final details of their lives are best kept off the record.

Nearly every child in the village had become an orphan. Some of the suckling children had perished for want of their mothers, and there are a few terrible stories about some unfortunate children that my heart can’t bear to report. But, it is a small blessing that most of the children did indeed survive. As it happens, the children were spared by Belladonna because she had made arrangements with the Bloody Eye to sell them into slavery. We learned this from Malvau, who showed up a day after the battle, who had followed these bandits back into the dale.

Added to the misery of the raw carnage, hitting the survivors and the troops that liberated the farm shortly after the euphoria of victory was diminished, was the guilt that nearly every soldier and citizen felt at about having to battle their countrymen. There are numerous tales of soldiers having killed their shadow-enslaved relatives, then falling completely apart emotionally only a few moments after Belladonna was in custody. Again, there are countless tales to tear the heart from one’s chest, and they will not be recounted here. Suffice it to say, there was no one glad to have been involved in this endeavour, no matter what side they fought on.

Hardest hit, though, was the Whitegate Family. Lumina, who had been struggling with her own experiences facing off against her enemies, was a shell for a long time after the battle. I am told that she led the 3rd platoon of the Dalehearth Company down the main concourse of the village as the battle came to a close, and that as soon as she came to the great courtyard in front of the citadel, and saw the nightmare hangings thereupon, that she fell to her knees. I can personally testify that she said not a word from that point on, until many weeks later. We all feared for her soul, and though she recovered it, for the most part, it remains to this day a cherished treasure to be closely guarded.

Immediately after the battle had concluded, there was, of course, a tremendous amount of disorder. Even with Bishop Whitegate there, there was so much madness to be resolved that it was many hours before the wailing, screaming, suffering and general chaos was abated. During these tumultuous hours, a few notable events happened.

First, and I have mentioned it; Belladonna was taken prisoner, rather than being executed on the spot, as nearly every soldier, citizen and child was screaming for. The Bishop was every inch the lord of the place, and with the support of Knight Commander Pauldron, she was kept alive, and was secured and taken away. I am unsure where they took her right away, but I will tell of her fate below.

Second; though the battle with the undead and shadow-slaves of the Army of the Black Saint had ended, there were still three companies of orcs engaged with various elements of the Army of the White Saint outside the walls. This part of the battle was not going well for the tired men of the dale, and many might have perished if not for the arrival of Braxus, the werewolf lord from Gang’Dar. He swept through the ranks of orcs and killed and scattered them in little time. The Sun Blades that he had delivered were wary of this awesome creature; none would approach him to give thanks. Braxus simply waited outside the gates, stalking among the dead and dying outside, dispatching orcs here and there. At length, Troia went out to greet him. What transpired between them none then knew. Braxus left and Troia returned.

Third; some of the Whitecloaks rushed to Lumina when they saw her falter in the courtyard. Though she said nothing to any of them, she did one single act that spoke volumes; she handed the epaulets from her cloak to Iolus. All the Whitecloaks hailed Iolus as their new captain. From that time on, Lumina had no part in the company she helped to create. She has been living a peaceful life at the farm since that day, and Iolus has been an inspiring captain.

Fourth; Banitt cut down the body of Magistrate Zuldrick, and was seen to weep at the death of the man who seemed to have died the cruellest death of the lot; he was tortured and mutilated beyond recognition, save for his blood-soaked vestments. At that moment, Banitt made an emotionally charged plea to Bishop Whitegate to be instated as the Magistrate of Liberty Hill Farm. For a moment afterwards, all were silent; it is said that White Cloaks and Knights of the Lambent Shield alike stared in disbelief, awaiting the reply from the Bishop. Whitegate took a moment to consider it, drawing the suspense of the moment out. Banitt made a beautiful speech which I will append to this report; it is well documented. His case was that he had come from a far-off land on pilgrimage to see the White Saint. He had liberated the home of the Saint with his very own blade, and felt as though his journey had come to its end. The bishop agreed, and Banitt was invested on the spot. He remains the Magistrate at the farm, and a close friend.

Fifth; the Slave Orbs of the Imperiosus Orbis were taken from the Table of the Sun. These were indeed brought by the Bishop right to Phin and me for examination. They are terrible, powerful artefacts that are tied to the Imperiosus Orbis, which was plundered from the depths of a Netherese ruin the summer before by Loki. Ironically, it was Banitt’s team of bounty-seekers that failed that mission, and it was he that carried the blade of the White Saint to victory on the Table of the Sun, thus helping to capture these weapons in the end. I have appended a report about the Slave Orbs.

In the days that followed, many stayed at Liberty Hill. Many soldiers who had relations there (dead or alive) stayed to help out. Of the White Cloaks, Banitt, Lumina, and Troia stayed on. Banitt, of course, had an office to keep, and Troia stayed mostly to look after her friend. For the rest, there was still a war raging, and many loose ends to wrap up. With apologies, the rest of the White Cloaks and most of the army parted the morning after the battle; they were headed off to Foothills Farm to put an end to the last of the covens of Shar. I have appended the report from Alystair Dretch about this encounter, but suffice it to say, the momentum of this victory was enough to settle matters at Foot Hills in a relatively bloodless fashion.

==War Raged On==

With the Dark Garden defeated, many of the problems in the Heartland sorted themselves out in the weeks to come. Each farm was again able to secure its borders, and before long, there were once again young men marching to the Castle, to join the battle on the Western front.

The forces of Netheril increased the tempo of the war in the west with the defeat of the Dark Garden. They no-doubt realized that with this threat in the heartland ended, that additional troops would likely be available to bolster the defences of the Dalesmen on the front. On the 19th of Flamerule, the definitive battle of the war took place; the Siege of Fort Lastsun. I will refer you to the report made by Brother Neddle of this battle. It is by all accounts the best compilation and interpretation of the various reports and stories about the battle.

I must needs summarize it quickly, however, if only to draw a line between the outcome of that battle and the White Cloaks who unwittingly contributed so much.

By the 18th of Flamerule, all three divisions of Netherese soldiers involved in this war were massed a mile away from Fort Lastsun. It appeared as though they would make the final surge of the battle; Lastsun would not be able to endure the assault, even though they were likely to inflict unthinkable casualties on the Netherese from their strong defensive position. We now know that the Imperiosus Orbis were employed for this battle; they used them to darken the skies above the battle so that the sun never rose on the morning of the 19th; the Netherese General must have known that time was not on his side. He would have waited until the night of the 9th to launch his attack in the cover of darkness, rather than use the orbs in this capacity and hasten the attack.

So, before breakfast was served, the Netherese divisions began their assault; the valley leading up to the Fort was choked with twenty-thousand soldiers representing a dozen fiendish races. Siege machines launching devilishly-enchanted stones blasted apart the anterior walls of the fort, and a desperate defence was made by the 1500 men in the Fort. However, even though they were slaughtering the attackers at a ratio of 5:1, they would not win the day. But, help unlooked-for arrived.

As the men of Fort Lastsun were down to about 300 defenders, and it seemed as though they would be overrun at any moment, the very earth shook, and the floor of the valley, and the thousands of attackers queued to the slaughter thereupon, vanished. The earth opened up and swallowed them, leaving a deep chasm in front of the ruined Fort. There was panic in the Netherese ranks, and those that remained in the castle, ahead of the army-swallowing chasm, attempted to flee.

To the rear of the chasm, where the Netherese Command headquarters had been smugly watching the slaughter, another great slaughter happened. This time, it was at the hands of the Tethyamar League, which issued out of the chasm proximal to the Netherese Command; 600 dwarven warriors emerged to engage double their number of elite headquarters guard. They exploited the confusion that their trap had caused, and in the end they slaughtered and drove off the Command, though nearly 300 of them were killed in the assault.

The Tethyamar League had rallied dwarves across Northern Faerun in the months leading up to the war, after the announcement of the discovery of the Eastern Gate of Tethyamar. This gate had been discovered by Deadnettle, who was in turn found by Warburton’s team of White Cloaks months before. This discovery acted as a lightning rod that attracted hundreds of dwarves to the region, who were convinced by Curator Gambledin of the Tethyamar League to do what they could for the war with Netheril. Their clever trap, the sapping of an entire valley just in the nick of time, and their selfless assault upon overwhelming odds upon the command units, ended the war with Netheril. The League had kept their subterranean arrival in the theatre a secret; Br. Neddle claims that Gambledin did not trust everyone in the command of the Dale forces to reveal his plans, or even announce that he had assembled the host he did.

So, before Shieldmeet, what Netherese forces remained were in full retreat, and the war was concluded, for the time being. The losses were exceptionally heavy, though; nearly 3000 dalesmen lost their lives in this conflict, and nearly that number again of mercenaries. The cost to Netheril has been conservatively estimated at more than 10 times that figure.

==The Fate of Belladonna==

As I write this, Belladonna is a captive in the Tower of Dagger Falls. She was brought there almost immediately after the Saint’s Battle, along with the Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin. She was held there without trial until after Shieldmeet of 1480; Argent and Luminous wanted to be at the trial. These men had visited their ancestral home the day of Shieldmeet (a day I shall never forget as long as I live; I have never witnessed or experienced such peaks and valleys of emotion as that visit inspired), and soon after rushed to Dagger Falls to conduct the trial. I have already forwarded the proceedings of this trial (cf. Proceedings of the Trial of Belladonna Liberty, Dagger Falls, 7 Elesias, 1480 DR), which I would recommend to any student of jurisprudence. Despite her unparalleled level of villainy and evil, she was given a fair trial in which no formality, no matter how trivial, was disregarded. I personally attended the trial, clutching the hand of my friend, Lumina for the several days that it took to arrive at judgement and sentencing. Belladonna was found guilty of a list of crimes that took thirty minutes to cite. However, she was also acquitted on a number of charges that took twice that long to cite; so clever were machinations that even as a confessed leader of the worst cult of Shar in history she could not be convicted of all crimes charged her.

When sentencing began, as is customary in the Dalelands, families and friends of the victims were heard from. The Whitegates relinquished their primacy in these depositions; at the outset, the people cried to hear Lumina and Luminous speak, but they allowed all others who had a claim to go ahead of them. This took another two days, and I think everyone who stayed to listen to the extent of sorrow that was spread by this woman lost a year of their life for the hearing of it. I will never be the same after listening to so many weeping mothers and orphans.

Here, I will transcribe a few lines from the Proceedings, as my own words can say them no better;

<blockquote>

11225. Bishop Archer: The court will, at long last, hear the words of the Whitegate family. The court has been advised that Ms. Lumina Whitegate will depose for the family.<br><br>

11226. (Loud murmuring and disquietude follows)<br><br>

11227. (Lumina stands from her seat, deposing from her bench rather than approach the testimonial box; her hands are held by two women on either side of her. The court falls quiet as she stands silent for about 90 seconds)<br><br>

11228.  Bishop: Whenever you are ready, Ms. Whitegate…<br><br>

11229. Lumina Whitegate: Thank you, I am. The woman convicted in this court has caused more misery than an entire army of Netherese soldiers who waged war with malignant determination died trying to match. She has been sowing unholy discord for decades, by her own confession. She has been the instrument of my family’s near-total annihilation. She has killed most of the people I love. She has done something so terrible to me, that I cannot recount it here, for the sake of my family and friends; the ordeal I endured at her pleasure in the Shadowfell was worse than a nightmare within a nightmare. And yet, I cannot sentence her to death…<br><br>

11230.  (disquiet in the court, Lumina waits for it to subside)<br><br>

11231. Ms. Whitegate: I cannot sentence her to death. To do so would be to let her win. She has earned her place at her master’s side; she will be a captain in Hell, and we will have to deal with her again. I would encourage the court to sentence her thusly; to live in confinement in the Tower of Liberty Hill, where daily she will have to defend her faith from me; I would convert this woman and commit her soul to Amaunator…<br><br>

11232. (disquiet in the court had been rising, and a fervour washes over the followers of Amaunator with her last words; the Bishop himself offers his palms, and most of the court follows his lead)<br><br>

11233.  Ms. Whitegate: I would convert this woman and commit her soul to Amaunator, and I would do it at the very place that bears her name, the very place she tried to destroy. I would take her home, and bring her into the fold!</blockquote>

In the end, after many more hours of proceedings, this was in fact the sentence passed down. To this day, Belladonna lives on the Table of the Sun, where daily, my friend mounts the stairs to face off with her nemesis in a battle for both of their souls.

Belladonna is not kept there by reverence for her sentence alone. She is confined to the Table by the most powerful prayers and rituals of the clergy and magic of the Warsmiths available. Also, Lumina is not her only visitor. Magistrate Invictus has been given a new portfolio; he is personally responsible for the incarceration and interrogation of this woman, and spends much of his time at Liberty Hill, fruitlessly trying to draw the shadow out of Belladonna. To date, Belladonna has not said a word in the company of either of her visitors.
