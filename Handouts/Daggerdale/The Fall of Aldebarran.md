== Greengrass, 1373, Roarke’s Tavern ==

===Ill Tidings===
Over dinner, Kennyth revealed that his ongoing research into the history of the 'Beholder Staves' had at last borne fruit. The staves first appeared, to his knowledge, in the ancient state of Chessenta in the seventh century pre-DR. The ancient records indicate that a fourth staff, measuring 8' in length with a full ten stalks adorning the head of the staff, was once in existence, but its fate has so far been lost to the Roll of Years. The staves appear again, after a long silence, in the Moonsea region in the Year of Good Tidings (751 DR), when the Westgate wizard Zhentar is said to have used them to secure his position as the ruler of the settlement that would become Zhentil Keep. From sources he would not disclose, Kennyth had discovered that while the staves functioned as charged magical devices such as those that can be made by wizards, these staves could only be charged by beholders. Furthermore, he indicated that it appeared that the staves may also be conduits for communication with beholders over long distances, a cause for concern.
right|75px
Rychel, too, bore tidings of new mysteries. Harkas Kormallis was dead, murdered in his home not a week before, and the Kormalis Villa had been burned to the ground. Rychel also expanded on the drow presence in the city, and noted that he had evidence of Ghaunadan working with the Dalael, and that he suspected that the forces inhabiting the Irlingstar Villa likewise had monstrous allies of some kind.

===Brisk Business===
right|75px
Over the course of the meal, Beljuril got to know Nero and made an arrangement to sell the ring of clairaudience/clairvoyance taken from the mad diviner Malkor after his defeat and death in Neverwinter. Nero in fact did a brisk business with the Eastern Storm and their new allies, arranging to supply them with the necessary implements for the task they had accepted. The company put together a list of equipment they wished for Nero to outfit them with.

Once the meal had finished, Rychel, Nero and Kennyth departed to return to Waterdeep, while the Heroes stayed on to discuss strategy.

Shortly after midnight, the Heroes decided it was time to get to the City. It was only a couple hours south of Roarke’s, and the road is not very dangerous. But, they did not get far before trouble started again.

===The Missing Rangers===
right|75px
Leaving the private dining room in the corner of the great hall at Roarke’s, a shot rang out, and a bullet struck Beljuril in the shoulder. The last two members of Grimwald's Rangers had surfaced again to hunt for the heads of the Good for their own selfish ends. They had also been sent with a score of second-rate assassins who were intermingled in the crowd, who attempted to slow the good guys, while the elite assassin, Shellwynd Thann, fired his rifle time and again.

The efforts of the assassins seemed especially focused on Beljuril, and they seemed to know a few of his tricks and were prepared for them. From the ground floor, Boregar recognized the half-elf Derrick Swift with the marksman. The Rangers had struck hard and fast, and Beljuril soon fell to their onslaught while the rest of the Heroes - blocked into the private room - attempted to make their way past the hired men to reach the Dalesmen. As soon as Beljuril collapsed, the Rangers made their way to a window and disappeared into the night, foolishly believing that Beljuril was finished.
===The Would-be Assassin===
75px|right
Two prisoners were captured from the remaining would-be assassins, while the rest cut and ran. Garth, a seasoned intelligencer, secured two rooms and had Gaebril put the screws to one man while he interrogated another. The stories of both men were roughly similar. The hired men knew little of the Rangers other than their presence and role in the attack. They had been hired by a mage named Clavius to be part of a special operations force, which was comprised of independent cells of 16 men each that would have no contact with each other. Beyond Clavius, they knew little if anything of Company Aldebarran. Each man had been given 2 harbour moons and dressed in ragged clothing as a disguise, though their weapons were of a masterwork quality. The man Garth interrogated answered to the name of Gum and said that he was based in Dockward. Interrogation of the men revealed little else, and while Gaebril sent his ward packing out the door, Garth insisted on turning Gum into the authorities for whatever justice demanded. This turned out to be an unintended mercy, as Gaebril's man was found on the road to Waterdeep, knifed in the back by an unknown assailant.

Shellwynd and Derrick both managed to escape mostly intact, and days later, as Beljuril scried upon them, they were seen riding east along the East Road, away from the City, hopefully for good.

=== Little Chessenta ===
thumb|250px|A little slice of the Old Country
A trip to one of the poorer, and more dangerous neighborhoods of Dockward led to an interesting encounter. In search of the Chessentan Entropists, Amon and Szuth, and following up on the revealed Chessentan connection of the beholder staves, Maturin, Dorian and Garth visited 'Little Chessenta'. Garth followed up on an old lead, asking Stuart about the 'Magebane' phenomenon from two years previous. Stuart led Garth to believe that the Magebane killings were unfortunately (in the eyes of the Entropists) over. Further inquiries about starmetal were met with stony silence. At the end of the meeting, Stuart gave Garth a signal to use with other entropists, should he or the Heroes decide to call upon their assistance. He would talk to Amon and Szuth, who had indeed arrived in the City, and they would be in touch.

News of the Tablets from the Dagger Hills reached the Heroes through the Rooken Network: there was no evidence of the Tablets had passing through the gates of the City. In fact, it was reported that shortly before Secomber of 1372 (just before winter) the caravan of Fist accompanying them had gone north off the East Road. The trail had been lost.

=== Unearthed Arcana ===

That balance of power, it would seem, was now distributed between the Heroes of Waterdeep and Company Dalael. Garth and Beljuril decided to once again visit Anteos Villa, only to discover that Quevin had secured the Encyclopedia Ocularis Indomitus. This book, as it turns out, had been unearthed somewhere on the eastern fringes of Anauroch by Jannaxil Serpentil and Elaith Craulnober, who had been absent from Waterdeep since Tarsakh of the previous year. In fact, in private negotiations with Garth, Quevin indicated that he would be willing to turn over the book, in exchange for a deal that would essentially exclude Garth’s enterprise from involvement with the Noble Houses of Waterdeep.

It would appear there are decisions to be made...
