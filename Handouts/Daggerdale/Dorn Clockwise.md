A dwarf advisor to Garth Shanks, and member of the Rooken Network

==History==

Dorn was born and raised in Neverwinter. His father was a clockmaker of no small ability, and Dorn showed the same promise in the craft as his father. Dorn was more interested in locks, however, and the focus of his trade has always been upon these mechanisms. From an early age he enjoyed getting into places that he should not be. While he is not by nature a greedy dwarf, he has had many great opportunities to accumulate wealth, and he is well off as a result of his locksmith ‘experiments’.

Dorn has been able to get past many magical locks in his day, but it is these same locks that instilled in him a desire to learn something of the Art and apply it to his trade. Though it shamed his father, Dorn took up apprenticeship with a mage in Neverwinter to learn the Art. After many years of study and learning, and after a long period of estrangement from his family, he set out to the City of Splendors to ply his trades.

In Waterdeep, his eyes were opened even wider, and he lusted for more knowledge; knowledge of magical things, knowledge of hidden places, and knowledge of many things he was denied knowledge of. He has always operated as a behind-the-scenes personality, as his ‘field skills’ in the rogue trade are a little bit unrefined. However, he was clever enough to open up a locksmith shoppe in Trades Ward right away, and this exposed him to all sorts of personalities. He met people with locked boxes that they could not remember how to get open (as they stole them from some merchant or noble). He met elements of the upper estate who wished to secure their belongings from his other clients.

Over time, he learned a great many of the secrets of the city, and had strong reputations to his name. He is known as one of the premier locksmiths of the city. He is known as source of all sorts of information. He is known as a source of minor arcane objects, scrolls and potions. It is also known that he can ‘see through a brick wall in time’. Few people are able to deceive this dwarf, even though he claims to be hard of hearing and have failing vision.

Dorn does not favor running ‘ops’. As mentioned, his field skills are weak. However, he is a strong supporting element who knows and loves his role. He is happy to supply information, organize groups of ‘runners’, and provide any other services he has to offer.

==Appearance==

Dorn is of average size for a dwarf. He has a thick black beard that he wears in two braids that go over his shoulders and tuck into the back of his pants. He has a prominent bald spot that has met a receding hair line on the top of his head, though a ring of thick black hair is worn long, and also tucks into his pants with his beard.

He will typically be found wearing high-quality merchant’s clothes, decked out with many large jewels and trinkets. Often times he will have his thick leather apron worn over this finery, and an odd looking contraption over his head with a score of lenses that can be rotated into and out of place, if he has just come out of his shoppe. 

==Disposition==

Most people that meet Dorn think him a true gentleman. Because he is clearly a dwarf, this impression sticks even more. He is polite, pays close attention to those he speaks with, and never says anything untoward. However, those that know him well know that he calls this act his ‘estate face’, which he uses to deal with the appropriate elements of society. He is equally at home in a rough and jocular group of friends, and those that have heard him in his shoppe know that he has all the foul diction available to him that most dwarves commonly use.

==Abilities==

With a Locksmithing skill of +20, this is certainly his premier ability. He has a first rate shoppe for the assembly of all manner of locks and other mundane abjurative devices. 

He is able to scribe scrolls for any spells that he knows (more on this below) and is happy to do so. Further, he can also brew potions for most of his spells, though he complains that it takes too much out of him to do so.

He has tinkered with many magic objects of lesser Art in his day, and is always happy to produce something new, or a better version of an old ‘toy’. A list of the objects that he can make is listed below.

He has recently started tinkering with alchemy as well (why not?!).

He has great personal skills (when he needs them). He has a silver tongue, and a keen eye (diplomacy +9, Sense Motive +16). He learned how to spot deception in his years dealing with all the shady people of the City of Splendors (Cosmopolitan Feat). He is also able to lean on people if he has to (Intimidate +6) and is good at ‘winking and nudging’ (Innuendo +11). Further, he has a keen eye for valuables and treasures (Appraise +13, +15 w/ his ‘spectacles’).

His most practiced field skills are Disable Device (+12, +14 w/ masterwork tools) and Open Lock (+15, +17 w/masterwork tools). He is somewhat stealthy, but not very. He doesn’t mind being an ‘entry man’ but doesn’t go in for hacking and slashing, and roof-top chases. Further, despite having a keen eye for small details and a social wit as sharp as a rapier, he is a little on the ‘slow’ side, and is easy to ambush (Spot and Listen +2).

His skills as a ‘fixer’ are also notable. Gather Information (+9), Knowledge; Waterdeep (+9). Sense motive and Appraise also help him in his role as a fixer.

== Unique Special Items ==

;*Clockwise Lock: This is an enchanted lock that starts out as an Amazing Lock (DC 40), which typically takes him a month to make. Further, there is a high quality clock inside the lock that is set so that the lock may only be opened at a certain time of day/week/year (whatever time interval he or the customer prefers). The clock face cannot be seen from the outside, and until it is at the exact time that the lock can be opened, each hand serves as a smaller lock, preventing the main apparatus from opening (thus, there are 13 locking mechanisms on this lock total). When the appointed time arrives, a key or something similar is used to open the main mechanism, and the lock is opened.

This lock is further enchanted with Arcane Lock and Alarm. The casing is typically made from True Silver (Mithril) and enchanted with Resist Elements (acid, cold, electricity and fire). If the lock is built right into a safe, the safe is typically lined in lead, and also enchanted with Obscure Object just to be safe.

Cost 2500 gold and 200 xp to manufacture.

;*SWAG Bags: These are bags any size from a purse to a large sack. They are lined with a fine Mithril mesh. The bag itself is usually of gnomish material (+2 to conceal it, if needed), and enchanted with Resist Elements (both acid and fire). The contents of the bag are protected by Obscure Object. They are light, durable, concealable, and won’t give away the location of the cargo inside.

Cost 500 gold and 40 xp to manufacture.

;*Shocking Bolts: These are HXB bolts (the only type he can make) that have a shocking grasp (1st level) spell on them. They are masterwork bolts. These must be handled carefully. They are not shock or shocking burst weapons from the DMG. They have a device on them that delivers the shocking grasp spell to whomever they touch. Damage is plus 1d8+1.

Cost 100 gold and 4 xp to manufacture.

Potions and Scrolls

Dorn will make potions and scrolls for any of his spells. He prefers to make scrolls, but realizes the value of many potions (you don’t need the Use Magic Device skill to drink them).

== Belongings ==
Dorn has an extensive shoppe that will take up at least a space 20’ x 20’ at headquarters. This includes his locksmith tools, alchemy equipment, potion lab, scribing desk, and various other tools and equipment for the making of ‘wondrous items’. In a 20’ x 20’ space, this stuff will be packed in and piled floor to ceiling, which is just the way Dorn likes it.

He always wears his chain shirt +2 under his clothes, and his ring of protection +1.

;*Weapons: Dagger +3, Hand X-bow +2, 50 Shocking Bolts, Masterwork punching dagger, several back-up weapons including a +1 shorts word.

;*Magic Objects: Boots of Levitation, Broach of Shielding, Everburning Torch (A small hand lamp), Eversmoking Bottle, Gloves of Arrow Snaring, Goggles of Night, Horn of Fog, Rope of Climbing, Cloak of resistance +2.

While in the shoppe, he wears a strange contraption on his head with many lenses and devices. He calls this the Spider Hat. It can operate with any of the following abilities one at a time. He has to physically change lenses and apparati in order to change abilities;

*Goggles of Night
*Goggles of Minute Seeing
*Detect Magic
*See Invisible
*If used in conjunction with an Identify spell, it reduces the casting time to 2 hours.
