Gussy says:  I liked Nero, my batman-like Fighter/Rogue.  Blacky didn't make his life pointless, genetics did.

Nero Agundar was at one time apprenticed to the legendary Waterdhavian adventurer, Rychel Voordan. His adventuring career was cut short, however, when he emerged from the Undermountain as the sole survivor of what had been an expedition to save a kidnapped girl. He now makes a comfortable living as a procurer of luxury and exotic gear for other adventurers in Waterdeep.

==Significant Events==

Nero was first implicated with the Company of the Red Herring and their associates during the events of Harvestide, 1371, when - disguised as a guardsman named Paris - he had a conversation with that scabrous old pirate, Zechary Mudge.

By 1372, after being recruited to the Harper cause at the Phaulkonmere Council, Nero was angling to pit the lycanthropes of the City of Waterdeep against the  and the Dalael. He spent some time with the members of House Umbrusk and/or House Hunabar to this effect.

:\"That is, hobnobbing it with the nobs, and trying to crack into the Hunabar family by dating one of their girls, and gambling with their boys. I was pretty much spending my time working that angle. The end result was to get them on board as a sort of alternative to the Drow or the Zhents. The thinking being that a Noble family of Waterdeep, albeit werewolves, were a much better alternative than foreigners and deep-dwelling monsters. Waterdeep for Waterdhavians, that's what I say!\"

He later became involved with a loosely organize band of elves (Steele, Lotharil, Llianin and the elusive Innish) that formed an ill-fated alliance of opportunity with the forces of Company Aldebarran against the drow menace.

:I find the situation in Waterdeep hopeless. No matter how hard we try the Drow can just sit and wait until we all die of old age, and that will be that.  ... <strike>Blacky</strike> the drow.... have made Nero's life practically pointless.  ...  in Waterdeep, Nero is a marked man. He lives on an hourly basis because thanks to his botched assassination attempt, the Dalael boys have it out for him. Nero is so fed up with Waterdeep that [he's] taken this trip into Undermountain as a welcomed vacation. Now that's saying something.
