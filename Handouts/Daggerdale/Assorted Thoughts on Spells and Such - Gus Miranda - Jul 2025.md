"Playing spell casters, I'm always reading spells. I've been thinking quite
a bit for weeks on how ineffective the Fireball I cast our last session in
Hudson was, so I put the robot to use to show me a comparison of Fireball
damage across the ages:

System          Fireball Damage    Goblin HP (L5)   Result Summary
------          ---------------    --------------   --------------
B/X             5d6 (17.5 avg)     ~22.5 HP         Likely lethal
AD&D 1e         5d6 (17.5 avg)     ~22-25 HP        Lethal/fatal
AD&D 2e         5d6 (17.5 avg)     ~22-25 HP        Lethal/fatal
D&D 3.5e        5d6 (17.5 avg)     ~30-35 HP        Moderate damage
D&D 4e          3d6+4 (14.5 avg)   ~50 HP           Bloodies most
D&D 5e          8d6 (28 avg)       ~50-60 HP        Often lethal
PF1e            5d6 (17.5 avg)     ~35-45 HP        Heavy damage
PF2e            6d6 (21 avg)       ~60 HP           Moderate; status effects matter

I used a 5th level caster trying to kill a 5th level Goblin. The Fireball
myth is interesting. In the early systems, Fireball very much deserved its
deadly reputation, especially in AD&D. It was a combat nuke for sure. But
it received a big nerf in 3rd edition, and 4th Edition basically made the
spell useless. 5th Edition has made it useful again.

In PF2E it isn't that great at all because enemies have far higher hit
points, and it is easier to make the Reflex save for half damage, or even
critically succeed for no damage.

Basically we believe Fireball is good because it was fucking amazing in
AD&D. But Fireball has actually been nerfed significantly over the last 30
years.

This got me thinking about the other Wizardly staple that is Magic Missile:

System          Missiles   Damage (avg)   Enemy HP (L5)   % HP Lost   Notes
------          --------   ------------   -------------   ---------   -----
B/X             3          13.5           ~22.5           ~60%        Very strong

AD&D 1e         3          10.5           ~25             ~45%        Solid strike
AD&D 2e         3          10.5           ~25             ~45%        Reliable
D&D 3.5e        3          10.5           ~40             ~26%        Weaker vs SR

D&D 4e          --         6              ~50             ~12%        Weak at-will

D&D 5e          3          10.5           ~50             ~21%        Good finisher
PF1e            3          10.5           ~45             ~23%        SR applies
PF2e            3          10.5           ~60             ~18%        Modular use

Magic Missile has gotten progressively shittier with each edition (with 4th
Edition having been complete garbage.)

It is just interesting to me that when the idea of creating specific 'Roles'
in a party and separating the various classes into these roles - Tank,
Striker, Controller, Support, etc - a lot of different things that we grew
up with and still believe to be true, have actually changed significantly.

Anyway, I just find it interesting, and it continues to support my build
with Jasper because post-3rd edition Wizards have no business casting
damage spells."
