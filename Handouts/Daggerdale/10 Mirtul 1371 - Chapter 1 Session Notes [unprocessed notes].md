10 Mirtul 1371 - Repaying Debts
The characters have much to think about during the tenday that follows. Forswearing honest work after his experience at Serpentil’s Folios, Garth decides to invest in Dockward’s real estate market and become a landlord. He purchases a rather rundown house, and soon rents a room to a recently arrived Calishite scholar and wizard named Jafar. Finding the Jewel of the North to be to his liking, Jafar joins the Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors with the sponsorship of a local mage, Orilar Thanas. As a member of the Order, he is assigned to the City Firewatch in order to meet his civic responsibility. Esperel, too, finds sponsorship into the local Finesmith’s Guild, though his reasons are perhaps less noble. Master Halthor Duzmund is either an idiot or a criminal to allow Esperel to join this particular guild, but no questions are asked.

On the afternoon of the tenth, Ellyth, Esperel, Garth and Tomas receive invitations to dine with Thagdul at the Thirsty Throat Tavern, to discuss their outstanding ‘debt’. Curiously, Jafar is also summoned, and it is revealed that he is in the trader’s debt for his own reasons. Gilthanas has not been seen, but Thagdul reveals that the bladesinger has made amends independently, and has left the city. After a splendid repast, Thagdul cuts to the heart of the matter. To clear the debt they owe him, the Company of the Red Herring is to burn down a house in Northward. As an added incentive, he offers them ‘salvage rights’ on the contents of the building.

Despite Ellyth’s odd suspicion that Thagdul may be a dragon in human guise, the party reluctantly agrees, eager to extricate themselves from this sinister man’s devices. Thagdul has arranged a ‘diversion’ to distract and occupy the City Firewatch Guard at half past ten bells the same evening. Thagdul directs them to [[Dalael Safehouse|a large four-story building in Northward]], on the block bounded by Sulmor Street, the High Road, Delzorin Street and Vhezoar Street, and indicates that further instructions await in a nearby sewer entrance.

The party splits up to prepare. While Tomas returns to Dockward to retrieve his armour, Esperel and Garth reconnoitre the target. Movement is detectable on the upper two of the house’s four stories. Meanwhile, Ellyth discreetly makes enquiries about Thagdul among the nobility at one of her local haunts, a gambling house called The Gentle Mermaid. Her mark, Arsten Thunderstaff II, but has no apparent knowledge of the Moonsea trader or his operation. To soothe her disappointment in his ignorance, Ellyth convinces Thunderstaff to oblige her by cutting her in on his winnings.

At ten bells, the party rendezvous at the sewer entrance, where they find a crate containing five detonation devices and a map of the building. Esperel locates a secret entrance that leads into a dank passageway, which leads to a room directly below the building. The room contains the ruins of several statues, and though Ellyth’s analysis suggests the statuary was once enchanted, the magic has long since deteriorated. There are no obvious doors, but Esperel soon uncovers a possible secret entrance to the building above. As it is opened, however, the party is attacked from behind by two watchspiders just as three figures burst in to the room from the secret entrance, at least one of whom is a drow.

Esperel is able to wedge the door shut against the drow, and while he, Ellyth and Jafar face off against the two remaining men, Garth and Tomas dispatch the spiders without much difficulty, though Tomas suffers a poisonous bite in the process. With the odds against them, the two men surrender, and are knocked unconscious by Tomas and Esperel, then bound by Ellyth and Jafar.

Esperel tries the door again, but finds it blocked from the other side, and it takes Tomas’ brawn to bash it open, crashing through to a storeroom of crates, kegs and tuns, scattered about with books and scrolls. Ellyth and Jafar set to examining the contents of the room, while Tomas, Garth and Esperel continue their pursuit of the drow. Tomas’ thick neck proves useful in opening a second blocked door, into a room containing the jade remains of a spider statue. The drow darts out a door opposite as they enter, and Esperel and Tomas follow the retreating figure upstairs, leaving Garth to inspect the room. At the top of the stairs, Tomas and Esperel enter a room filled with surprised-looking men. At these odds, they promptly retreat down the stairwell. For some reason, only one man pursues them, and he confronts Esperel as Tomas sets a charge on the stairwell. Gaining the upper hand, Esperel manages to force his opponent to retreat up the stairwell again, just as Garth emerges from the cellar. Together, the three head up the staircase to set the remaining charges – Garth on the second floor, Esperel on the third, and Tomas pursuing the retreating man to the fourth.

The second floor seems to have been very recently vacated or ransacked, and after Garth sets his charge, he makes good his escape out a window and into a nearby tree. Examining the third floor, Esperel finds two rune-covered porcelain cases, seemingly untouched by whoever has ransacked the building. Sensing danger, he decides to leave them and sets his charge before running down the staircase. On the fourth floor, Tomas finds his quarry pounding on a door yelling for “Vuldan”. As Tomas starts to chase the man around another corner, the door opens and a strangely calm voice asks what is happening. Turning towards the voice, omas is horrified to see what looks like a beholder floating in mid-air. With unnerving politeness, the creature requests that there not be any violence. Tomas, fear-struck, warns of the impending immolations, but the creature is unconcerned, and drifts nonchalantly back into the room. Meanwhile, the man Tomas had been chasing seems to have gone out onto a balcony. Having lost his stomach for the mission, Tomas leaves him, sets his charge and returns to the staircase to rejoin his comrades in the basement.

Hoping that Garth has made his own way out, the remaining party members move what they can into the sewer entrance before as the fire bombs detonate. Garth rejoins the party at the sewer entrance, and while he and the others take inventory of their ‘salvage’, Tomas returns home for a cart to transport the haul. 

Among the items salvaged from the building were the following:
* 1 exotic dagger (upon which Jafar identifies a drow enchantment)
* 2 hand crossbows
* 2 stone heads (identified by Jafar as former golem heads)
* 88 lbs. of jade (lightly enchanted – likely a former golem)
* A sealed scroll, written in the drow tongue
* 2 journals, also in drow
* 40 books, many of which appeared to be about the Kingdom of Cormyr
* Selections from The Draconomicon on a creature named Khaldun, the Deeping Dragon, with annotations in drow
