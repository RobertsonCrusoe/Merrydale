30 Ches 1483 — Journal of Brother Vessuvian, Candlekeep Emissary attaché to The Yellow Abbeye of Daggerfalls

The Contest Concludes — Chrysalis is Named the Victor, and Given Charter

The 30th of Ches would prove to be as busy a day as any that Chrysalis had endured the tenday previous. Thankfully, there were no battles with swords and spells this day, but many of wits and cunning, politics and business.

It all started very early in Governors' Hall, where every imaginable dignitary and snoopy local who could sneak in attended the conclusion of the Contest. Happily, Jas agreed to bring me along, and I was permitted to watch the final ceremony, though many others were turned away.

Little did I know that Jeska and Velemore had come up with a disruptive plan for the conclusion of the Contest. Velemore declared before the formal tallies could be announced that the Standing Stone League would remove themselves from the competition, having given all their intelligence to Chrysalis a day earlier, to ensure that the Chrysalis Intelligence Report would be at least second place, and ensure the victory against the White Cloaks. In fact, if not for this very move, Chrysalis may have scored third, leaving only a single point between them, which could have been made up by any combination of changes to the Endorsements. It should be noted that Chrysalis never did get the endorsement of Bishop Whitegate, and that the White Cloaks could have possibly tied Chrysalis if the SSL stayed in the competition. At any rate, the score in the end saw Chrysalis secure a firm victory. When this was announced, what few Red Jacks there were in attendance spat and left, cursing the whole time and making declarations of favouritism, cheating and worse.

Ambassador Whitmore brought the crowd to order in his own fashion, and proceeded to congratulate Jeska and Chrysalis on their victory, with a long speech I won't reproduce here, but some lines are worth repeating, as they communicated some intelligence from the Netherese Front, and the significance of the undertaking at Thistle Ridge.

"We wish Captain Monarch and her Company great success in the campaign to reclaim Thistle Ridge, and stand here at her service. The Adjudicating Committee has made good on its promise to give Charter to Chrysalis for the endeavour, and to supply a company of Sun Blades to entrench their gains as they are made. We further commit to cooperating to our fullest possible extent in the days and months to come."

The proper-sounding diplomat from Charity brought smiles and nods and huzzahs from the assembled people in the hall, but his bright face darkened, and his next words were drawn out slowly and gravely, a shadow coming over the room.

"But we must be vigilant against an enemy that is already among us. Indeed, is likely in this very hall" he paused, and everyone looked around surprised and suspicious. "The patrons and matrons of this dale and this town have concluded, with the evidence being stacked high, that Netheril has spies amongst us, and is using the Reclamation as an opportunity to sew division, cause chaos, and ultimately continue their work to remove us from the map.

"While we ask for everyone in this room to support Chrysalis and Captain Monarch insomuch as you are able, we demand also that you be wary of what you say and where you say it. For now, while Chrysalis is braving the wilderness and an entrenched enemy in the East, we face an imminent military escalation in the West, and a campaign of subterfuge and evil in the very heart of our dale, our home."

There was more, but I think it most relevant to illuminate the triple-front threat created by Netheril and her agents. I will do my best to support Jeska and my friends in Chrysalis, and will remain vigilant against the Enemy Within.
