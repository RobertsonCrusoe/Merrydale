A Theory about the House of Alavandor in Thistle Ridge
By Lucan Alavandor

What I know:
In Saerloon, among my great-grandmother’s effects, I found a letter dated 3540 MC, which Brother Vesuvian tells me refers to the year of the Mulhorand Calendar that corresponds to 1406 DR. The letter was signed AA, which my aunt informed me most likely refers to Alvund Alavandor, my great-grandmother’s older brother. Alvund was an unsuccessful artist whom my family had cut ties with following his departure from Sembia.

AA had entered into a deal with a figure known as the Old Patriarch. His family and another (presumably the Clarandals) agreed to "take what we were given and vanish forever" as part of the deal.

Safe Passage: The old patriarch ensured the journey was safe and comfortable, showing he had significant influence and/or resources.

Secrecy: The deal required secrecy, with "AA" breaching the terms by writing the letter.

Connection in Chessenta: The old patriarch had a relationship with the factor at a factory in Luthcheq, Chessenta, this was mentioned as the boundary of his influence.
Alvund is the name of the first Alavandor Settler to arrive in Thistle Ridge
Brother Vesuvian noted in his summary of the “Sembial Families of Thistle Ridge” that Alvund was married three times to women of the same surname: Senthia Maeliticus (1st wife), Abrecia Maeliticus (2nd wife), and Aisling Maeliticus (3rd wife).

At this point, I should report a curious remark Vesuvian mentioned when I was discussing his summary with him.

This name… Maeliticus, is indeed curious... Now, as a Sembian, I suppose I needn’t remind you that in Netherese—to imagine we once thought of it as a dead language used only by scholars!—the suffix "-iticus" does suggest a sort of... oh, how shall we say? A character or essence of something. Yes, quite characteristic of those ancient scholars, ever so precise in their linguistic structures. But in Netherese “Mael” would likely derive from “malus” – meaning evil. Not a very auspicious name, not at all. The name takes a much darker hue, hinting at "mal"-evolence, you see? Perhaps due to Netheril’s storied affinity for the more... ah… robust aspects of the arcane.

And yet, we mustn't leap too quickly, for we find a rather more... uh, pastoral nuance? Yes, pastoral. In Thorass—the Old Common, that is— "Mael" speaks to marks, significant ones, not merely shadows but... milestones, yes, milestones of history and legacy. Fascinating, truly, how a name tumbles through language and time like a... well, like a leaf in the wind, isn’t it?

We know of no issue from Alvund’s first two marriages, nor indeed any dates or any history of the Maeliticus family. So, whether he left Sembia with a wife and two sisters, a wife and one sister, or the third sister, we don’t know. In any case, Aisling was with him in Thistle Ridge. As we know from the entry in the 1440 DR edition of the Yellow Abbey’s Booke of Surveys and Measures, Aisling Alavandor founded the Phylactery, a school and an apothecary, that she founded soon after their arrival. Interestingly, Aisling’s Phylactery had a reputation for extremely effective fertility medicines. On this point I’ll mention another comment of Brother V’s:

Hmm, Lucan, this “Phylactery”... quite the peculiar name, don't you think? It has roots in Old Chondathan, a language of ancient wisdom and deep mysteries. Originally, it referred to something meant to protect, a safeguard, if you will. But over time, the word took on darker connotations, especially among those who delved too deeply into the shadowed arts. It became associated with practices that sought to preserve what should have passed on, to hold onto power in ways... not quite natural.. One wonders why such a word was chosen... unsettling, really.
They had three children in quick succession after their arrival:
Andarl, born 1408 DR (later married Susserah Clarandal)
Senthia, born: 1409 DR (later married Tergus Clarandal)
Abrecia, born: 1410 DR (later married Xerces Alavaxi)

We see a similar pattern with the Clarandals, who arrived with the Alvandors. The first father to arrive was Claudius Clarandal, who also had three wives, though only one seems to have been with him in Thistle Ridge. There is no known issue from either of the first two marriages (to Minerva Maerimmon and Sandara Sakurth), but with the arrival in Thistle Ridge, the third wife – Saralla Sakurth – was able to bear the next generation of Clarandals:
Tergus, born 1408 (would marry Senthia Alavandor)
Claudius Jr., born 1410 (would marry Coelathara Alavaxi)
Nothing is known about the Maerimmon or Sakurth families. Again, like Alvund, we do not know which of his wives Claudius Clarandal left Sembia with. In Tergus’s papers, there is a hint that Claudius Clarandal was from Daerlun, not Saerloon.

As a young magistrate, Blaze Whitegate remarked on the notable fertility of the settlement, noting that “the Chaunteans insist that the settlement is located on 'sacred' ground, which is reflected in the exceptional productivity of the small gardens, the quality of the fruit from their trees, the effectiveness of their medicines, and the health of their numerous children.” This is also attested to in entry in the Yellow Abbeye book: “As mentioned elsewhere, the quality of the products here and their results have bordered on heresy, but formal inquiries have turned up nothing occult or unsavoury.”

So perhaps Aisling and Alvund were finally able to have children through some combination of her own professional knowledge and the influence of Thistle Ridge (or maybe the Thistle family).

Now, regardless of the fates of the early marriages, the key figures who arrived in Thistle Ridge to start the Sembian families were:

Claudius Clarandal and Saralla nee Sakurth
Alvund Alavandor and Aisling nee Maeliticus

Is it possible that the Sembian Alavandors cut ties with Alvund because of his involvement with the Maeliticus family?

Notes:
Daerlun was heavily under the thumb of Netheril, whereas Saerloon remained a semi-autonomous seaport. Tergus grew up thinking the families were politically opposed to Netheril, but when he learned that his father might have served in Daerlun, this view was challenged.

Hypothesis: The Old Patriarch wanted these Alavandors and Clarandals out of Sembia. The fact that the two men were likely on their third marriages to a second or third sibling of the same family suggests a desire for family union. This is unlikely to be for political gain, since the families are also exiled at the same time. So there must have been something tied to the bearing of children itself. However it was not only the Clarandals and Alavandors, it was also the Sakurths and Maeliticus. It is possible that the Old Patriarch belonged to one of the wives’ families, and was arranging to get his son-in-law and another family out of Sembia, or away from Daerlun. To protect potential offspring?

Aisling killed herself in 1439. Was that due to the deaths of two of her children and a grandson in a suspicious hunting accident? Or because she found out a sinister truth that she couldn’t live with?

The children who betrayed and were betrayed were Lilac Alavandor, Lily Clarandal, and Poppy Sweetberry. All of whom were friends with Alaventa Alavaxi.
