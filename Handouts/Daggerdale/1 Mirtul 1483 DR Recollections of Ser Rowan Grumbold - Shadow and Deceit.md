Mirtul 1, 1483 DR -- Recollections of Ser Rowan Grumbold -- Shadow and Deceit -- Our Battle in Charrah's Chambre

Transcribed by Phoebe Birchgrove, Sgt. III, 7th Co. 4th Plt, Bright Spears, Aid de Camp for Lt. I Forge Farmountain

The first thing we noticed as we mounted the landing to the third floor of the Town Hall is that the sounds and smells of the building changed dramatically. While we could still hear the battle raging at the doors two floors down, and the hue and cry from the growing crowd of goblins in the court outside, sounds become noticeably muffled as soon as we made the landing. As we drew nearer the main doors to the central room, it became almost entirely silent, until I threw open the doors.

The smell of the place changed, too. Though the first floor, which had become a camp for a large group of goblin guards, was cleaner than most of the settlement, it still stunk of goblin bodies and trash, if not outright excrement. And it was crawling with rats, lice and meaner things besides. The second floor was much cleaner than the first, and indeed, looked as though the floors had been swept, and garbage taken out. The third floor had been cleaned with some diligence, and the smell of goblin bodies transitioned into a very earthy, pungent odour that after a moment we likened to a swamp. It wasn't offensive but it did seem out of place.

Before throwing open the doors, we took the chance to peek out some windows and arrow slits into the court below, and could see that it had veritably filled with goblins of all types; soldiers, females and children alike wee thronging in the open area, brandishing weapons, torches, and improvised tools. They were making a great cacophony, with a mixture of screams and chants, and I could distinctly hear them calling for the death of the captain, with the most loathsome oaths and threats imaginable.

Throwing open the doors to the central room, Charrah's Chambre, refocused our senses in an instant. The smell of the swamp rolled out of the room, and we could now hear various noises from inside that were completely absent a second before; low moans and groans from nearly a dozen men and women in cages much too small for them, the growling and grunting of an ogre-sized goblin on the opposite side of the room, encouraged by the various soldiers standing nearby, and of course, the voice of our enemy, whose presence upon a wooden dais in the middle of the chambre dominated our attention.

"I'm glad you've come, Chrysalis" she began. Her voice was slow and confident and had an alluring mix of mature gravelly earthiness and smoky seduction. She stood with black-gloved hands resting on voluptuous hips that swayed slowly while she addressed us. Her most conspicuous features were her curves, which were ample and accentuated by an old black dress that clung and revealed in all the right places. Her form nearly distracted us from her other dominant features; her slightly off-green hue, stark white hair, very long and pointed ears, and mismatched eyes; one of the clear-grey that we'd seen in a couple of other changelings in the settlement, the other of a smoky blackness. A symbol of a black battle axe was tattooed over hear heart, which Mari later explained was a Symbol of Maglubiyet.

Charrah spoke; "You've proven your strength, over and over again, and I am impressed. We are all impressed, are we not?" and she looked about the room to her various henchmen who cowed under her stare, and nodded silently. "You have won your tournament, you have won alliances and hearts in Dagger Falls, you have triumphed over some of my strongest captains, you have taken the Tower, and you now stand before me in my throne room, though without invite. But I now invite you to a parlay, so that we might find a peaceable solution to this conflict you have created..." she smiled as we furrowed brows at the suggestion that we created the conflict.

"Yes indeed, I lay the blame for this conflict on Dagger Falls, and those that have acted with aggression toward our home. You will know by now that I have eyes in the town, and I heard about the contest that you won; a game that your people made of eradicating mine from a ruin that you ignored for many decades. We have made this our home, and have done so without bloodshed, for the most part. The balance of death and murder surely lays with you. Yes, we have fought, and killed, and taken prisoners, but only in our defense. You can plainly see that our prisoners, though uncomfortable, are unharmed in the main. And further, you can see that I have your wizard's paramour in a cage, and we'll get to her in a moment.

"Before I suggest terms, I desire greatly that you know my mind, and my reach. I desire to control the lands from this 'farm' to my grandmother's hut in the Sorrow Bog. Your road goes through these lands, and we would exact a modest toll for safe passage. This is what I desire.

"My reach, I will give you a glimpse of. I have agents inside your town and many of your farms. Indeed, I have one such agent in that very cage" she said, gesturing toward Coventina, her hips and breasts swaying conspicuously with the motion.

"It is not difficult to find agents and allies in your town; your differing faiths are in conflict, and the Soothing Mistress offers reprieve for the weak-minded humans who think they have suffered. This little scarlet whore is one such; tucked away in a tower in town, mourning the loss of her father by herself, poor poor thing." Her voice was dripping with sarcasm, and she was unable to stifle a sneer in her prisoner's direction. "She's the luckiest little whore in Dagger Falls; a rich aunt, looks that every other girl in the dale would murder for, no real work to toil at. And yet, she is miserable, scared and lonely, and crawled toward the Soothing Mistress willingly, and prostrated her supple young body before her. And what does the Mistress do? She serves the thankless whore up to me, by our unseen network, and allows me to use her as I see fit. But you've likely guessed at half of this, or you should have." Hearing this, Coventina began to scream through her tears in protest.

"Shush shush, little whore. You'll be free soon, I'm sure. I know the young wizard will sue for your safe delivery, and I am willing to see to his pubescent needs and release his little receptacle. Pardon me; his 'little' receptacle is that traitorous slut that moistens his shoulder while it plays with his hair. The three of you will live happily ever after, I'm sure.

"But, let me return to my reach and what I have seen, and continue to see..." and with this, she waved one hand seductively, and a shimmering portal opened to the side of the chambre, allowing all of us to look on a scene of battle. The scene before us was of our two soldiers fighting at the back door, and Jasper recognized immediately that this is from the perspective of the ethereal eye he saw floating on the main floor. We could see that the men were both wounded, and struggling to keep a flood of goblins from overwhelming them. As we looked on, a great green hand of one of the eunuchs reached in and clutched one wounded man, and dragged him outside out of view, and the image then shimmered and changed.

Next, the image was of Boxfray and his men fighting at the front door, again from the inside. We saw a great spear come in through the opening from an unseen enemy and catch Boxfray on an unarmoured portion of his leg. He faltered to one knee, and several spears then thrust at him, most bouncing off his armour, others batted away by his axe. But one spear found the mark, and pierced his throat, a fatal gout of blood following the spear as it is withdrawn from his flesh. The image shifted again.

The portal now showed a perspective of the upper rampart at the Tower, from over the shoulders of the Sun Blades. Before them, the outer walls and buildings were smashed to pebbles and were overrun with goblins that flooded through the ruins. Many were assaulting the upper rampart, while in the background, others could be seen hauling off fallen soldiers, including one screaming and desperate forester being carried to the rear by a merciless mob of goblins that cheered and jeered at her terror. Just then, a deeper shadow fell over the scene, and the men on the walls looked up. At first, the image didn't show what the men saw and were recoiling and ducking from, their faces turned to us showing great fear and hopelessness. A moment later, a dark dragon glided overhead and circled lazily around and out of view, as the image faded completely, and the portal closed.

A brief quiet fills the room as we processed the discouraging images, and I'm ashamed to say that my courage flagged for a moment. All seemed hopeless, pointless, even reckless and irresponsible.

"You can see that you and your friends are quite defeated, and your doom is close. But for all that, I am still willing to make peace. If you will leave now, and bring my terms to Dagger Falls, and have their Amaunitori draw up an agreement that entrenches my ownership of Thistle Ridge, and my right to exact toll, these prisoners, including Coventina can be released. But I will not release them until I have the agreement in hand.

"You may find this unsettling and distasteful. And you are surely crestfallen to learn that you will not own my home. But this is the path to peace, and indeed to your delivery. I know you are fierce, and you have made your way to my throne room, and that you are currently weighing whether you can carve your way through my guards and get to my throat. But I think we all know there is doubt in your hearts just now; you have fought all day and are tired. You stand on the threshold of my domain, and you have seen that I am imbued with great and divine power, and you are unsure of what I am capable. You are likewise unsure who your allies will be when you return to Dagger Falls. This mission of yours, this dream of reclaiming Thistle Ridge has seemed to be a fool's errand the entire time, and you doubt that you were even supposed to succeed, and you wonder if there aren't some in very high places that are actively working against you. Of course there are, and I might even share their names if you can get the peace terms entrenched that I've asked for. What say you...?"

Charrah was quiet then and awaited the captain's response. I noticed then the darkness in the room had deepened, and that only a bit of light from an unseen source illuminated her from behind, and most of her features faded into shadow. Likewise, darkness crept in from the corners of the room, occluding nearly all but Charrah and Jeska, who were then locked in a challenging glare in the last bit of light in Charrah's Chambre.
