The world is a foul and festering oubliette.

Shrieks of the damned and wailing unwanted claw at what last vestiges of daylight and sanity remain.
With effort i push away the noise and stench of a thousand thousand lost souls and try to concentrate once more.
Forget the cages.
Forget the searing hot whips and cold iron shackles.

Be Here Now.

Eyes shut.
Curled up against the world.
I push the red-black pain of the here and now aside and descend to my cool green center.
I hold it to me, precious
And look inside:

-----

I am six years old, and showing off:

My first handstand.

Mother's laughter bubbles forth.  Father nods approvingly, aloof and proud.  when the inevitable happens and i tumble, i know he will cushion my fall with a gentle gust, as always.  Afterwards, mother's song washes away what bruises of pride and body remain.

The memory soon fades, but the song remains...

-----

Maggoty slop sloshes and tin-plate rattles its arrival.  A one-eyed hulk of orc and man intercepts it, glaring violently at any who dare approach.  Lesser creatures slither and crawl in his wake, lapping orc-spittle remains from our sewer grate floor.

It has been weeks since i have eaten.

Again a hunger-fueled desperation gives me strength.
Again i extend an atrophied limb and try once more to will my thin frame to flight.
Again i collapse exhausted.

No matter.

\"Up\" is not \"out\".

This pit of forsaken souls leads only ever downwards.
I must push this place and time from my thoughts.

-----

I am thirteen and darting though the air.

Swift and graceful:  a funeral dance.

A black plague has settled upon my blue green village.

Father has fallen.
Wind and sun are now fire and ash.
Mother sings
A mournful hymn of sandy tears and salty sea:
The ebb and flow of life.

Her small green words ripple and flow through me then

-----

And now:

I feel her song surge

Welling up from my small green core
Filling me up as the Primal Me begins to shift in new ways
For the first time.
 
The sewer-grate floor cannot hold the flowing river-song of the
Here and Now i have become

And like the tide i am

Gone.
