It was a forty-day journey from Saerloon to Dagger Falls with Madam Umbercliff’s Shield and Anvil Trading Coster. The caravan guards weren’t a bad lot, except for that cheating Hillfarian that ended my run of luck with talis. I’d been dealt a Suite of Winds, but somehow, he pulled off Shar’s Silent Swindle for the win. I’d been up considerably by the time we got to Blackfeather Bridge, but it was all downhill after that. The wood elf attack outside Essembra – we never saw them, but there’s no mistaking those arrows, I was told – put an end to our nightly games, and I never had the chance to recoup my loss. I still had the float that Father and Aunt Haelra gave me, thank the gods, but it would have been handy to end on a high note. The only other incidents of note were the bugbear raids that plagued us after Hillsfar, pretty much all the way to Teshwave. The guards earned their money that week, Oghma knows (not that Hilfarian caitiff, though – he conveniently jumped ship in Hillsfar without bothering to settle up with a few of his colleagues). We got to Dagger Falls this morning, and now I’m looking for information on an old farm that’s supposed to be in these parts. Thistle Ridge – have you heard of it?


Barmaid: (wiping a tankard) “New face in town, huh? You look like you've been on the road a while. Where from?”

Lucan: “Just got in from Saerloon. Traveled with Madam Umbercliff’s Shield and Anvil Trading Coster. Forty days on the road.”

Barmaid, nodding knowingly: “Madam Umbercliff’s, eh? Must've been quite the trip. A bit early in the year to be on the road, though, no?”

Lucan: “She had her reasons. Besides, the caravan master is a force of nature herself. The guards were capable, too. For the most part… There was this guard from Hillsfar. Cheated me in a game of talis. Well, I had a Suite of Winds, a sure win. But somehow, he pulled off Shar’s Silent Swindle. My luck turned sour after that. Didn’t win a hand after Blackfeather Bridge. “

Barmaid, with a wry smile: “The roads are full of such characters. What else happened?”

Lucan: “Outside Essembra, we were attacked by wood elves. Never saw them, but those arrows... unmistakable, I was told. Ended our nightly games, and any chance of recouping my winnings.”

Barmaid: “Wood elves, you say? That's rare, but not unheard of around these parts. Anything else?”

Lucan: “After Hillsfar, bugbears hit us several times, pretty every night all the way to Teshwave. Helm’s hounds, but those guards earned their keep.”

Barmaid, shaking her head: “The roads around Yûlash aren’t what they used to be. So, what brings you to Dagger Falls?”

Lucan: “I’m looking for a farm, Thistle Ridge. Ever heard of it?”

Barmaid, pausing: “Thistle Ridge? Yeah, I know it. Nothing but ruins now. Goblins have overrun the place. There’s talk in the city about a recovery expedition to clear them out.”

Lucan, intrigued: “Really? That might be just what I'm looking for.”

Barmaid: “If you're interested, you should talk to the city officials. They're organizing the whole thing. Just be careful; those goblins are a nasty bunch.”

Lucan, smiling: “Thanks for the tip. Seems my journey's just getting started.”

Barmaid, returning to her work: “In Dagger Falls, it's always just the beginning. Good luck.”
