DDC – 1372 – 2: The Daggerdale Expedition 
A collection of notes and narrative by Luminous Whitegate, Submitted to the Yellow Abbey in 1439. 
GFD Notes: 
On both sides of Netheril/Anauraoch, you will find books, hear songs, and meet children named after the heroes that set out from Waterdeep in 1372 to liberate Daggerfalls. This account was put together by Lunimous Whitegate himself, though he likely had help from a number of clerks at the Abbey. Still, at the same time as this was being submitted, he was building Morningstar Cathedral up at Castle Radiance, and fighting off incursions of orcs and Netherese spies. 

Fortunately, as he wrote this, there were still many people alive who remembered what had happened 60 years earlier, and he was better friends with a few dwarves in Town than he would admit to (even when I spoke to him on my visit to the Castle). I 

expect that much of this narrative between primary sources is from Dalak Quickaxes, who still runs a public house over 100 years after these events (which were the inciting incident for him to set up a business and retire from adventuring in the first place). 

Luminous tells the rest better than I do, so I will leave it at that, and bid you happy reading! 
-GFD 
Departure 
The most reliable part of the story as I understand it really starts at the end of Mirtul, 1372, as the Daggerdale Expedition (DE) approaches Loudwater, nearing the mountains that separate the Western Heartlands from what was the vast desert, Anauroch. Much happened between the gates of Waterdeep and the long inland-journey they made, but that part of the story I am not familiar with. 

Though others would join later on, the DE at this point was composed of the following members; 
 Boregar the Wanderer – a ranger or fighter of great ability 

 Maturin Horgath – Paladin of Ilmater from the distant East (GFD Note; 'St. Maturin' in the 15th C.) 

 Mourn "Raven"Raventree – Noble warrior of Waterdeep 

 Sparrow – Arcane assassin? (GFD Note: Ultimately ends the mage Yarkul) 

 Corrina – (GFD Note: Not the same as the Dalael bride, but we'll hear of her later) 

 Dalak Quickaxes – Dwarven battlerage, now Proprietor of the public house in town 

Loudwater was the last civilized place on the East Road at this point in the journey, and the DE paused in this solemn town to re-supply. All stayed in Loudwater for a time, except Boregar who argued that there was no time to waste. And so, he lived up to his name and left early, to wander the East Road. 

It is important to note that the DE had already suffered loss; Mourn Raventree was killed by assassins on the road before Loudwater, but was thankfully replaced by the famous swordsman, Dorian of Sembia. 

Why Dorian fell in with the DE is lost to history, but the impact he had on our community is not. 

The rest of the DE left a couple days afterward, and upon their travel along the East Road, they received magical word from Beljuril that the Neverwinter Company had been victorious. The grim news about the death of the Harper Initiate Rufus is taken hard by Boregar, who was starting to suspect some greater plot, and fears that someone has infiltrated his order, or sold them out. 

The DE comes to Dekanter, and finds that the town is under quite a bit of construction, and that they make no efforts to hide their allegiance; banners of the Zhentarim and of Cyric fly freely, and there is clearly much slavery here. The DE was loathe to leave this abomination behind, but a great task had been set before them, and so they pressed on, the desert just a day away. 

Heading into Anauroch, the DE is disheartened to find that they have traveled less than twenty miles after about four days, and soon after that they learn that they have traveled in a circle for a long time. 

Sand storms called 'Azirla Dahab' plague their passage, and it seems as if though the desert was not going to let them pass. 
Meeting Grandfather 
On the 16th of Kythorn, the DE, and Maturin especially, had an encounter with fate. It was on this day that they encountered Grandfather, a great werelion, and his consorts on a rocky heel jutting out of the hot golden sands. Grandfather demanded tribute in flesh for passage, but none was offered. 

Grandfather and his consorts attacked, and in a long battle with the creatures, Maturin, Boregar, Dorian and Sparrow were all wounded by the great werelion. Maturin, especially, took wounds numerous and grievous enough that there was little doubt that he had been infected. Dorian was spared his death by Tymora alone, as Grandfather's great maw found his neck, and flung his limp body into the sands before ripping at the guts of the dangerous battlerager, effectively ending his career. In the end, they managed to repel the creatures, but their wounds were severe. Here are the recorded thoughts of Maturin the Grey from the next day; 

St. Maturin's Journal, 17 Kythorn 1372 

It is less than two months since we left Waterdeep, yet it seems that years have been stripped from my life. Our party has been five days in the desert, and we have covered less than twenty miles. 

The desert wind whips my robes about me, the sand stings my eyes and open wounds. Sharp pains shoot through my skull. My vision blurs. The bite of the Lion-Creature, the harsh and blinding light of the desert sun, and my own internal turmoil are almost more than I can bear. 
I endure. 
We are stopped for a rest on the path leading us towards salvation or damnation. The first Zhent- tainted oasis lies over a day's travel ahead. We will find no respite. What we might have paid for will be denied us- my money is gone. Our horses are gone. 
I glance around. 
Dorian is crouched on his haunches beside me, coughing silently and clutching his ruined throat. I watch him spit blood onto the sand. The moisture evaporates immediately, leaving a faint discoloration on the pale sands. 
The heat presses down. 
He writes with a shaky hand on a crumpled page of vellum torn from his depleted journal. Precious inks stain his hands. He hands me the note. 
'We must continue'. 
Sparrow helps Corrina into the saddle. She lurches in the saddle. Her face is sullen. She glances at me, and blame, bitterness and anger spew from her eyes. Her cheeks are sunken. Her hair is lank. She fears for her unborn child. 

Boregar pushes ahead. He was withdrawn into himself. Since Raven's death he has been distant and driven. I cannot catch his eye. His boots crunch the sand. 
I follow. 
Warburden marches beside me. Even this most noble creature seems defeated. He tolerates Dalak with no complaint. He carries our supplies. He carries our armour. He carries the folly of my pride. 
The party carries the marks of my sin. 
O Lord Ilmater! It was Your Will that I saw the evil in the noble-looking Lion. It was Your Will that I fight It. But the Creature did not act in evil! Tribute for safe passage. It did not seek war, but peace. It is peace that I long for, and it is peace that You denied me! 

I remember fighting in the Bloodstone armies against the brigand forces of the Lich-King of Damara. I remember the blood, the anger, the hatred, the pain and the sorrow. I vowed to stop the wars. I vowed to heal, to struggle to save lives, not take them. I vowed to ease suffering wherever it existed in the Realms. 

But yesterday, my Lord, You showed me Evil. Yesterday, my Lord, You asked for Blood. Why did it sue for peace, O Lord? Why did it seek to spare us? I feel the blood course through my veins. Lycanthropy. I touch my tongue to my teeth. Are they sharper? My skin itches. Tomorrow night is the full moon. Is this Your Will? 
I feel the eyes of the party on me. They fear me. 
I fear myself. 
A gust of wind. Warburden rears up. Dalak pitches to the ground. I reach out a hand to restraint the mount, then reach down to help my comrade. His wounds have opened. I tend to them carefully, reassuring him. He grunts stoically, but I see the worry in his eyes. I know that my dread writhes equally within him. 

Ignorance and suspicion eat at our bellies. Tomorrow night we'll know. Tomorrow night we'll be tested. For now we continue. 
For now we endure. 
lmater, why have You forsaken me? 

This was the beginning of the great Story of St. Maturin, though he had no way of knowing it at the time. His suffering had only just begun. 
Infection 
The next night, the DE arrived at the first Zhent oasis-outpost, Vaerthyl. Before they would leave this outpost, Dorian, Sparrow, Maturin and Boregar turn into were-lions and ripped the place and everyone 

in it apart. The battle-rager also helped in the destruction, but in the end, he was met by Maturin in his lion-man form, who ripped Dalak apart, nearly killing him. 

The next day dawned with little hope for the heroes. The whirlwind of bloodshed, violence and destruction was but a foggy memory. The four new lycanthropes found themselves each waking up alone and naked in an unfamiliar part of the desert. Most managed to find their way back to the oasis, and there they found the horrific scene of slaughter. Men were ripped to shreds and many had clearly been feasted upon. This caused the four infected members of the party to become sick to their stomachs. Zhents and slaves alike had all been slaughtered. Horses had been butchered, walls clawed through. The entire place was in absolute ruin. Corrina had gone missing, and never again was she heard from. Her story ends in obscurity here. 
Now they were five. 
A great pyre was made to burn all the corpses, and great piles of weapons and such were made. Dalak was found, and tended to by Maturin all day, but things did not look good for the battle-rager. Though the remainder of the company looked for their missing comrade, at length they all solemnly admitted that it was time to move on. The road continued, and so did their quest. They left behind a companion that day, and many of the items they treasured and needed for the journey. But Maturin left behind everything. Ilmater turned His back on Maturin that day, and the Paladin began his long lonesome trial. 

The remaining four members would have perished if not for a fortuitous meeting with a benign tribe of Neshaba. 

(GFD Notes: There are still some of these tribes roaming the planes and the fringe-bits of desert that remain. It might be possible that this group is still out there somewhere, avoiding the Netherese.) 

These desert nomads were quick to help the DE, despite what all had heard about the people of this land. It was not until much later that it became clear why. This particular band bore the symbol of the Green Eye of Muir'Quaggoth, and they must have known about the infection in the veins of the DE. 

After days of wandering the road, they came to the second oasis-outpost, Olomaa. They decided not to tackle this one head on, but in their attempt to circumvent the place, they were intercepted by the 14th company of Fist mercenaries. They managed to destroy this company nearly to the man, but they learned that these twenty men were of elite training, and quick thinking. 
The Wanderer and the Quests 
Days later, as they trekked through the sands, the DE encountered another 'golden death' storm. This was the worst one they would endure, and it was bad enough to sunder the company. Dorian was blown away hopelessly in the storm, and the DE lost almost all of the valuable gear they had managed to retain after the slaughter in Vaerthyl. When the storm finally subsided, Dorian and Sparrow were long gone, and no tracks or trails were there to be followed. The desert had seemingly just swallowed the men up mercilessly. 

Three days later, and two nights before the full moon would rise again, a boon came from the desert, and hope was put into the hearts of all, but especially in the heart of the Forsaken Paladin. A wanderer came from the desert. A man who seemed a beggar came over the dunes, and he helped along the 

weakened Dorian, who he had found the day before. Dorian had been staring at the sun and hallucinating, drawing his final breaths. The wanderer followed the birds of carrion and rescued him. 

This man, Shildevan the Wanderer, was a holy man of Ilmater. He was on a holy pilgrimage to harsh lands everywhere, and the hand of Ilmater guided him to the DE. He managed to bring all but Sparrow back together again. 
Now they were four. 
Shildevan had favour enough with Ilmater that he could cure the horrible lycanthropic sickness in the veins of the infected men, but all had to agree to a quest to receive this blessing. 

Dorian was to eliminate the threat posed by the Beholders, and that he could not accept any pay for this mission. Though the clever Sembian agreed to not accept a single 'copper piece' for the entire quest, he did not realize the scope of his challenge. Indeed, his was the only quest that would bring him back to the streets of Waterdeep over a year later, to equally dire struggles. 

Boregar was commanded to be true to his friends, but to return and set right all the wrong he had left behind. This seemed like a great task given all the evil they had turned their cheeks on during the journey so far. 

Maturin was commanded to free the slaves of Dagger Dale, and then to return to the desert to slay the Chosen of Muir'Quaggoth, Grandfather. 

So it was that these three men were cured of their infection, and were set upon the greatest quests of their lives. 

Delivery into the Dales 
Back on the road, the DE began to make solid headway after their fortuitous encounter with the holy wanderer. They came to Addas Babar, and secretly traded with some slaves for food and water. A small fight ensued, and Boregar was taken down by the mage Farim, the only survivor of the Vaerthyl massacre. Though he was later rescued by his companions, the Gond Pistol artefact that Boregar had wielded was not recovered. 

Days later the DE once again encounter the Nashaba, and this time their headman Kabina assigns a guide to them, so that they may go through the desert quicker. After many more days of travel and many more pounds had come off the frames of the travelers, the Desert's Mouth Mountains came into view in the East, and the hearts of the travelers leapt. 

It should be said that all the obstacles, challenges, misery and atrophy of the desert have not been detailed in this brief summary. The journey was one of constant loss and doubt, and in recorded history it was not known that four lonesome travelers could make such a remarkable journey and live to tell about it, until these four came out alive. 

While traveling in the bad lands between the desert and the mountains, the four encounter the ogre- mage Yin Xu. They find they are unable to hurt the creature before he is able to escape using his gaseous form, but he is also outmatched. 

After crossing the mountains, the four head down into the fertile valley that is the border region between Dagger Dale and Shadow Dale. The hearts of these men were renewed, though their bodies were just beginning to recover from their journey. They traveled off the roads, as they were weakened and had insufficient tools for making war, and knew that the Fist would be close by. 

They encountered a group of Zhent scouts as they neared Shadow Dale, but they were rescued by a group of elves of the Region, who Boregar was familiar with. The elves then escorted the DE to Shadow Dale, where they arrived on the 4th of Eleint. 

Even the Boregar was noticeably thin when the four turned up at the Old Skull Inn. The others had the look of skeletons, and all were haggard, filthy, and wretched. After they were cleaned up, and donned some new clothing, they weren't long fitting in at the taproom. Crowds flocked around them (despite their want for some peace) and asked many questions of the men who had come from the desert to liberate the slaves of Dagger Dale. 

One of the guests at the inn that night was the noble fop, William Cormaeril. This man had come from Cormyr, and claimed that he was the rightful heir to rule Dagger Dale with the death of Randal Mourn. He had a small retinue that included a half dozen Purple Dragons, and he boasted that he would liberate Dagger Dale from the treacherous Zhents, but he was not taken seriously by anyone who heard him. 
Also traveling with him is his wife, Medea Rowan, and their two sons. 
GFD Notes: Medea left the dale in early 1373 with her sons, to return to her ancestral home in Cormyr. A year later, she sent a letter to her husband, William, explaining that their sons both died in a horrible fire. Cormaeril fell into a deep depression and was killed in the field a few months later on a regular patrol. Though I have not seen the reports, he is said to have fought poorly, seeming to welcome death, and reunification with his sons, who were the last of his line. I have been outside his small mausoleum, and can confirm that he died Flamerule 20th, 1374. With that, the Cormaeril line ended, as well as their claims to Daggerdale. This is all fairly common lore in the dale. 

What is uncommon lore, as I discovered in an obscure watch-report in the Abbey, is that his mausoleum was broken into in 1425, but the thieves were stopped by the watch. In the report, they mention that a magistrate gathered up the things the thieves stole and carried them off, rather than re-interring them. I have indeed seen a box in the Historic Wing labelled 'Wm. Cormaeril', but I was not granted access to it

That night peace would not come easily. The half-elf assassin Dragon entered the inn and made an attempt on Dorian's life, but was not successful. She managed to escape before any harm could befall her. Though she scorched and skewered Dorian, her most grievous wound was upon his heart. The fool was beginning to fall for her. 
First Battle of Ananathar's Dell – Eleint 5 
The next morning, the epic scale of the events in the Dales was made greater yet again. The order of the Broken Blade, including 8 knights of Tempus and their retinue, rode into Shadow Dale with full pomp and ceremony. They, too, announce that they will be heading to Dagger Dale to put an end to the 

villainy there, and that they will not wait a day. Their leader Sir Durnbold implores Cormaeril to ride with them and take his rightful seat after a valiant battle, but his cowardice was great, and the Broken Blade rode out that very morning without him. 

As the Paladins of Tempus went west out of town, all took it as a good sign. But then, the heavens gave ill portent. A green and black vortex was seen in the sky, and it inspires panic in the village. As the sun was coming up that morning and the vortex was swirling, the Church of Lathander began its bifurcation. A fragment of the church lead by Rewel Thunderstorm, the second-in-command of the church in Shadow Dale walks out on High Dawnlord Cassimar's sermon. Thunderstorm insists that the vortex is a sign, and that they should have sent all available Lathanderites with Sir Durnbold to help in their noble quest. 

By a reckoning put together by an Oghman in Waterdeep much later, (GFD Note: Kennyth Thames) this was the morning that the slaves in the Dagger Hills Mine uncovered the beholder tablets, and the vortex was likely the manifestation of some god associated with the beholders. 

That night, good news and bad both come to the DE in the inn. Sparrow has emerged from the desert! He explains that he was also helped by the nomads of the desert, and that magic sustained him. He brings ill news though. That day he passed by the site of an ambush, and reports that the Order of the Broken Blade have all been slain in ambush less than 20 miles from Shadow Dale. As this news spreads, so does the bifurcation of the local church of Lathander. 

The next morning, all surviving DE members (now they are five again!) are together and recuperated. They plan to re-supply today, and to begin their toil. That morning, though, more trumpets are heard, and Shadow Dale once again seems to be the stage for a play of the Gods. The holy trinity of Tyr, Torm and Ilmater march their brightest lances and blades into Shadow Dale that morning. The following orders were present; 
 Order of the Golden Lion (Torm), led by Sir Gethard Soulbright 

 Order of the Lambent Rose (Ilmater), led by Sir Lexinauth and Father Eisley of St.Collin's Abbey. Both of these men refused to speak with Maturin. 

 Knights of Justice (Tyr), led by Sir Wildermot Silverlance 

One of the men in the tent-train of the OLR was Revered Brother Turin, who was an old friend of Maturin's. The two had served in the army together in Damara, fighting the Lich King of Vaasa. Turin was glad to see his old friend, despite what politics where at work. 

Word that day comes to Boregar from the local wood elves that Yarkul was spotted at the Dagger Hills mine the day before, perhaps accompanied by a beholder. They took something large and heavy from the mine and marched away. Sparrow insists that the trail least cold lies in that direction, but there were many questions about which task to start first. There were 3 slave mines to free, and the town itself to liberate. The choices were many, and there were many political considerations now as well, with knights of three orders in town. 
The Second Battle at Anathar's Dell – Eleint 7 
The night of Eleint the 6th, a great conference was held in the Twisted Tower. Lord Amcathra hosted a meeting with the DE, the knights of the order, and the two leaders of the bifurcated church of Lathander. There were many discussions as to what was the best course of action. As well, Sir Soulbright sued for some troops from Amcathra, but he could spare none. The only Shadow Dalesmen that would accompany the war party would be a select few priests of Lathander from the Thunderstorm faction of the church of Lathander. 

Early on the morning of the 7th, a war host rode out of Shadowdale the likes of which had not been seen in a score of years. The three orders, accompanied by the DE set out. They had a retinue of priests of Ilmater and Lathander in tow, as well as a few Shadowdale men who decided to follow with what arms they could muster. Cassamar and Amcathra met them on the west edge of town, wished them luck and apologized that they could offer no further aid. 

Twenty miles and most of the day later, the war host came to Anathar's Dell. There, they found the small walled farm village burnt to the ground, and the bodies of the slain Knights of the Broken Blade strewn about, defiled and rotting. They were put upon a pyre and given rites by the many holy men there. Their impetuousness was also cursed at that time. The Broken Blade had marched with the Trinity for many days, but would not abide the pace of the Ilmater tent-train, and thus found their enemies two days too soon. 

While the smoke billowed into the sky, an enemy host gathered on either side of the dell. To the north, the Knights of the Black Gauntlet gathered. This is the militant order of the church of Bane, and had just recently been assembled after the resurrection of Bane. To the south, the more formidable Brothers of the Black Fist, led by Baron Drake assembled. This militant arm of the church of Iyachtu Xvim had been growing for years at this point, and their main headquarters and training ground was located in Dagger Falls at Brotherhood tower. 

A brutal battle was joined, as the forces of good clashed with the forces of evil. Paladins of many gods struggled against each other, and in the end it is difficult to say who won or lost. The Knights of the Black Gauntlet were utterly destroyed, but the Brothers of the Black Fist were merely repelled. Baron Drake slew several paladins of all three orders himself however, before he thought it prudent to lead his forces from the field. In the end, the DE and the surviving members of the war host of the Trinity held the field, but at great cost. And Baron Drake was far from defeated. He laughed as his demon-steed bore him from the field. 

The battle rager Dalak was nearly killed that day as well. The Baron delivered a blow that re-eviscerated the battle rager, and though it did not kill him, it put an end to his fighting days. He spent the rest of this story convalescing in Shadowdale. 

(GFD Notes: I've met Dalak many times, and discussed much. He won't talk to strangers about this time, though. Don't ask.) 

Anathar's Dell is often referred to as Paladin's Grave these days, for the 21 Paladins that were buried there. 

It is important to note that years later, a great cenotaph was erected there. However, as the Spellplague hastened (and still hastens) the growth of Cormanthor, the cenotaph and the knights were moved to my grandfather's farm before I was born.-LW 

It is late into the night before the war host marches again, back to Shadowdale, in acceptance of defeat and setback. 
Preparations for War – Eleint 8-10 
The next day was a day of many activities. A council is assembled again. When the survivors of Anathar's Dell describe what happened to Lord Amcathra, he voices his concern over the absence of the militant order of the Church of Cyric, which is also strong in Dagger Falls. Cassamar and Thunderstorm get into a heated argument over signs from Lathander, and the right and wrong course of action, and Thunderstorm goes so far as to say that Cyric was not at the battle because he was at work in the heart of Cassamar, who should have sent troops to the battle. 

The Boregar set out that day to get word from his allies in the woods and surrounding country side. He visited the significant other in his life, Silve Silverhand. This Harper and daughter of the famed Storm Silverhand was a long-time lover of Boregar. The two had a lasting but intermittent relationship. The two lived harsh lives that kept them both on the road and apart, doing deeds they received little thanks for. This day, they met as lovers and as colleagues. Silve had long ago planted a man in Dagger Falls and had been receiving correspondence from him for some time. She received the following bloodstained letter, which she wished for Amcathra to see;

I have transcribed this note in its entirety, mostly because I'm a military man myself and I admire the detail of this report. But also to illustrate the power that had come to Daggerdale at the behest of the Zhents, and their determination to keep it. 

Another note was also delivered from the spy in Dagger Falls some days later that said the following; 

Eleint 9, 1372 DR Friends, 
The word in Dagger Falls is that the Elves of Cormanthor have marched upon the mine in the Dagger Hills and razed it to the ground. 

It is said that Captain Braehl and the entire 4th Garrison are slain, and that the elves subsequently marched all the slaves into a pit and burnt them alive. 

This seems unlikely to me (the elf-portion of this story, at any rate), but by all accounts the Dagger Hills mine is closed.

Exodus from Chillholm – Eleint 13-21 
A couple of nights later, the DE is close to the Chillholm mine. That night, Sparrow sneaks into the barracks and cleanly assassinates the mage running the operation. Sparrow overhears that the Fist will be leaving in the morning, but that the Garrison Captain there is upset about that, with all the orc sightings of late. 

Early the next morning, 11th Company of Fist ride out of the mine, and shortly after, the DE strikes. In the drawn-out battle, the Garrison there is slain almost to the man, and 6 score of slaves are rescued. That night, the exodus to Shadowdale begins. 

The next day while the exodus marches overland to Shadowdale, Boregar and Dorian make a guerilla attack against the First at Rain Hill. They light off a powder keg in the barracks and destroy almost an entire company of Fist before making their escape. 

The exodus back to Shadowdale was not eventless. Maturin had his hands full tending to the failing health of the liberated men, and despite his best care, many could not survive the dangerous travel home. 

On the night of the 19th, the 2nd, 3rd and 4th companies of Fist caught up with the exodus at Liberty Hill (it wasn't called that yet, of course), and though losses were great for both sides, the Fist did flee the field of battle, and the liberation of those slaves was complete. Of course, my grand father was one of those at the battle, and his account of it can be found elsewhere.
