DDC - 1372 - 1: Decisions at Phaulkonmere 
30 Tarsakh, 1372 DR, Agamemnon's Journal: Summary of a meeting at Phaulkonmere Villa in Waterdeep, wherein it is decided how to address the problem of the Zhents, the drow and the beholders.

30 Tarsakh, 1372 
We met, the lot of us, at Phaulkonmere to discuss the Zhent and Drow threat to the City and to points beyond. Many were there because they had been involved in this mess for so long. Others were there through a sense of duty to their fellow man. Some were there because they were seeking such things as adventure, fame and fortune. Some were there for even more selfish reasons. And some were there for the simple reason of pursuing vengeance, and with good cause. 
Present at Phaulkonmere that day were the following (and I have pre-emptively clumped them into the companies they would form, though they were a chaotic rabble at the start of the meeting); 
Neverwinter Team: 
· Maevri of Rashemen - Witch 

· Oberon – Ancient warrior awoken from stasis (must spend some time with him) 

· Rufus Larrigan – Principled were-creature 

· Oliver Windemchester – 'Master of Human Nature' by his own claims – a bard? 

· Beljuril of Azuth – Priest of Azuth and very capable wizard 

Daggerdale Team: 
· Boregar the Wanderer – a ranger or fighter of great ability 

· Maturin Horgath – Paladin of Ilmater from the distant East (GFD Note; 'St. Maturin' in the 15th C.) 

· Mourn "Raven"Raventree – Noble warrior of Waterdeep 

· Sparrow – Arcane assassin? (GFD Note: Ultimately ends the mage Yarkul) 

· Corrina – (GFD Note: Not the same as the Dalael bride, but we'll hear of her later) 

Waterdeep Team: 
· Rychel Voordan – Master of the rooftops · Zechary Mudge – Dockward tough 

· Garth Shanks – Ringleader of the CRH, principled but undisciplined (or else a master of misdirection) 

· Esperel – Insane elf, very reckless and unreliable 

· Jafar – Unhinged wizard, not sure what side he's on 

· Brandel – Eleven archer of incredible strength 

Also present were myself, and the last Phaulkon (who's family estate we were meeting in, though now owned by some legal trickery by the Company of the Red Herring), Jeryth Phaulkon. 
Thagdul was conspicuously absent from this meeting, but Jafar insisted that he represented Thagdul. This did nothing if not make worse the CRH member's opinion of this wayward mage. 
(GFD Note: Thagdul MUST have been in Daggerdale at the time, given everything his faction of Zhents accomplished there.) 
Events current and past were discussed. From the East, Boregar brought a report most grim. The Zhents had taken over Daggerfalls once again, and Randall Mourn himself had been hung from the new bridge over the Tesh River. Yarkul had been put in place as ruler of Daggerfalls and Daggerdale, and his rule was enforced by an elite mercenary company known as The Fist. Further, churches to Bane and Iyachtu Xvim had been built, and the town appeared to be taken for good. 
Worse yet, the Zhents had hundreds of slaves toiling in a number of mines. It was suspected that Hobb (an ally of the CRH who was kidnapped earlier in the year) was imprisoned in one of these slave 
camps. Outposts had been built in Anauroch, and Zhent caravans were coming and going through the Great Sand Sea with little difficulty, and the town of Daggerfalls was growing rich off the trade through its gates, as it is the gateway to Anauroch from the East. 
All trade through the more traditional routes (such as through the gap at Tilverton and then through Cormyr) had ceased because of a couple reasons. Tilverton had been overrun by goblinoid hordes again, and a Zhent banner was flying from the city's walls. Cormyr was involved in a costly war with the Sembians over a border-mountain that Mithral had been found in. The resurrection of Bane had much to do with the problems in the East, but the Zhents seemed also to have played the hand that was dealt them in a most cunning manner there. 
In summary, almost all trade between the Western Heartlands, The North, Amn, Tethyr and Calimshan was coming through the desert upon Zhent cargo trains, or paying huge fees to use their roads and buy safe passage. The Zhents were growing wealthy, and most of this wealth seemed to be pouring into Daggerfalls, where extensive fortifications were being constructed. Clearly, an expedition would have to be sent east to set things straight. Yarkul, at least, would die. Or so was the pledge of Sparrow. 
In the North, a second objective was set. Malkor, too, would have to be brought down and the roads made safe once again. The work of the Road Mage was not complete, and Beljuril and Rufus had made a pledge that they would honor. Our organization was in great peril, and its survival rested upon the deeds of these heroes. 
Finally, the City of Splendours itself became the last objective. It was clear that the Zhents had too much influence in this city, and indeed, much of their wealth and power structure was now located here. With the Company Dalael as another huge problem to be fixed, perhaps the group should have assigned a more capable task force to this objective.
