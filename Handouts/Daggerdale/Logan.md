Some metagame ruminations from Gustavo Miranda:

...Logan. I miss that bastard. I don't think I've ever put more thought and backstory into a character, only to have him fall ignobly as an NPC. He might have been, or might not have been, Randal Morn's last living descendent. The point is academic, since he ain't living anymore. Although, truth be told, if he hadn't died, I wouldn't have rolled up Dorian, since that was the role Logan was destined for. And of all the characters I've ever played, in any game system, I've had the most fun with Dorian. Jafar was great, yes, but he didn't cross Anauroch twice in one year. Logan was travelling west to Waterdeep, and was to have crossed Anauroch with Maturin, Boregar and the rest to liberate Daggerdale as its rightful king. Instead, he's dead. Buried. Forgotten in a marsh. I learned a good lesson from that one: don't introduce a character you want to play as an NPC when you're in the DM's chair, cause you won't be able to save him without cheating.
