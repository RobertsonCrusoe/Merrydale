# Banitt Harbuagh
# Source: The Sieges Perilous wiki (backup dated 2010-02-21)

Priest of Amaunator on pilgrimage to Castle Radiance to see the tomb of Ardelusk Whitegate

An Auspicious Birth

It had been snowing for days, as was seasonal for Icewind Dale at the time of year this tale begins. The sky had been a dark grey, the clouds seemingly endless right to the horizon. 

Mureil and Josef Harbaugh were in fits at 4am, Josef padding forth and back at the window of their small home. The nursemaid was late. Mureil shrieked in pain as another contraction took her. They had sent for the nursemaid hours ago, Josef had doubts that she would make it through this night’s blizzard in time. 

He cursed himself; the first signs of discomfort had begun at midnight, with only a light snow at the time. Mureil had asked him to send for the nursemaid then, but the ever-practical Josef said they should wait to ensure it was not another false labour.

“Can’t cry wolf a third time,” he had told her. She simply nodded, knowing he would not be persuaded otherwise.

But at 1 o’clock Muriel’s contractions grew more rapid, they sent for the nursemaid then. But by then, as if the storm was mimicking the mother’s pain, the snow had turned to a blizzard.
 
“The baby is coming Josef,” Muriel cried from her position on their bed. “I have to push”

Josef cannot remember the details of the events that took place then, he remembers only hearing his wife roar as she began the delivery on her own. He was frozen in place, staring out his bedroom window. He could not believe his eyes, at his wife’s roar the clouds broke above their house, and their blinding bright in a hole in the clouds was the sun, beaming radiance down upon them. They had not seen the sun for nearly a week, and besides, the sun did not even begin its climb over the horizon until 6 am.

Bannit Harbaugh was delivered, a healthy 9 lb boy at 4:18 on that morning.

None but Josef witnessed the breaking or the clouds and the shining sun for that 17-minute period. But none discredited his tale, for in the morning, when the streets of the town came alive, the roof and walls of the home of Josef and Muriel were bare of snow. There was no puddle, no ice, it were as though something had evaporated the snow where it lay.

And In Youth Lay Mystery

The tale of the night of his birth lost its fascination within a few months. But the mystery that was Bannit lingered on. As he grew into a young lad it was obvious that already he possessed great physical strength and fortitude. Oh, he got sick as often as any other child, but his capacity for physical punishment was more a match to his strength than his constitution. And he when he was hurt, he seemed be able to just sit in the light of the sun and mend.

“I just think about getting better, stare at the sun till my eyes water.. and I am better.” It was the only explanation Bannit could give.

The greatest mystery happened when Bannit was but a child of 8. His father was crushed in an avalanche while guiding a group of miners through a dangerous mountain pass. By the time he was dug free he was unconscious, sure to die from the crushing of his chest and head. Sure to die, it would be told, if not for the presence of his son at the excavation.

His father was dragged out and lay still at Bannit’s feet. 
“Say a prayer for him lad, your father was a brave and good man, some god should welcome his soul”

Bannit fell to his knees and put his hands upon the stiff form of his father, he stared at the sun.

A large hand gripped his hair, shoving his head down,  “You bow your head to pray lad!” said a stern voice.

The man never saw what hit him, but a crushing blow to the chest sent him sprawling through the snow.

Bannit returned his hand to the head of his father, his backhand swing a momentary distraction and returned his gaze to the sun. His eyes began top tear as the sun burned his retina. His tears began to fall upon his father…

All those witness at the event were awed, for before their eyes they could see the form of Josef Harbaugh visibly thaw, steam rose from his body. And suddenly he gasped a breath, ragged, but a breath. As the tears of the boy fell, the mans breathing eased, and he became hale once more. The witnesses saw the boy lean off to the side an slump into the snow, then they saw, as if seeing a miracle, Josef Harbaugh roll over, rise to his knees and collect his son in his arms. He pushed himself to his feet, looked once at the sky, and carried his boy home.

Bannit, to this day has never been able to duplicate this miraculous healing. Small cuts and abrasions, yes, but major wounds seemed out of the reach of his talent.

Coming Of Age In Icewind Dale

Bannit spent most of his young years before manhood sitting quietly on the lakeshore, watching the men carve their scrimshaw. He grew to learn which carvers were in for a dollar, and which loved their work. He became a fair carver himself, but his knife would always seem to dig too deep, or crookedly. Although he could judge the wood, and knew the style, his agility with the blade was just lacking the necessary competence. 

It was expected of every lad, upon his 14th birthday, to serve for 3 years in training for the defence of the Dale. Bannit was no exception. Due to his strength he was trained in the use of the Bastard sword, and was taught to fight in armour of scale.

Bannit did well and learned quickly, but he did not excel as expected. Instead the others in his column during mock combats always seemed to be the ones to excel, but only in the presence of Bannit. On their own they fought, as would ordinary lads. With Bannit by their sides their attacks were harder, and stuck more true.

The instructors all agreed that this was simply due to a need the others felt to shine in the presence of “the miracle boy”.

A Pilgrimage Is Begun

When Bannit was 16 his father gave him a gift, it was a large tome. Inscribed on the cover were these words “Of Ardelusk Whitegate – Chosen of Amaunator – Hero of the Dales”. Bannit never found out how or where his father came into possession of the tome. But his father was clear on one thing. Another sign had been given. Bannit was to memorize the tome, and upon the end of his service to Icewind Dale, was to leave his home. He was to make pilgrimage to the tomb of this hero, it was there he would find his life’s meaning.

So, with much sadness, three days following his 17th birthday, Bannit took a seat on a caravan heading south. His voyage would take him father south than necessary, to Calimport. Then to sea, finally landing in harbour on the Dragon Coast, weary and nearly broke. Two more months of slow, weary travel found him at the door of an inn, The Castle Gate Inn at Crossed Blades Farm.
