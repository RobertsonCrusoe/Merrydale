== History ==

The two reached the end of the ancient tunnel that ran deep into the mountain beneath the monastery before the elderly Monk Azu turned to the young woman and asked, \"How do you feel since I gave you that ring, Initiate Shae?\"

Shae looked down at the plain iron ring adorning her finger.  Azu had given it to her a week prior in preparation for her trial.  All initiates who attempt the trial have been given it since the founding of the Monastery of the Four Winds.  \"I feel fine now, grandfather.  I haven't eaten in five days nor drank in three.  It will sustain me during my trial.\"  She replied and lifted a hand to the rough stone mural that adorned the wall of the tunnel.

Azu placed a withered hand on her shoulder and looked at her calm face for a few moments, \"You're not the little girl I found out in that winter storm anymore.  I am proud of you, my daughter, for coming so far in your training.  Your parents would be proud of you.\"

Shae held her grandfather's hand and smiled at the old man who had raised her since she was a child.  Her family was one of several in a caravan travelling through the mountains.  A sudden winter storm tore through the region before they could reach relative safety and young Shae was separated from the caravan in the ensuing blizzard.  She was lost for some time before a young man dressed in crimson robes found her and brought her back to the Monastery of the Four Winds.  He searched for any signs of her family after the storm had passed but the mountain paths were swept clean of any trace that a caravan had recently walked them.  He and the other monks have cared for her within the monastery for the last twenty years; raising her to be honourable, disciplined, and patient.  The scholarly monks taught Shae in subjects ranging from history to astronomy, philosophy to mathematics.

\"I am ready.\" She said, lowering Azu's hand in her own.

Azu lowered his eyes and nodded, \"You will remain in the chamber until the glass candle is lit.  This can be a . . . harrowing experience, remember your training.  Should you succeed, you will emerge a fully realized disciple of the Four Winds.  Do not take the ring off under any circumstance, not until you again stand here.\"  His hand went to the wall where he pressed a circular stone and shuffled toward a second, a third, and a fourth pressing each in turn before returning to her side.  The harsh grinding of stone on stone could be heard as a segment of the thick wall lowered into the floor.

The wind buffeted Shae as it rushed in to fill the inky blackness that lay before her.  She met Azu's eyes once more before stepping across the threshold.  The grinding sound returned as the door rose slowly out of the ground.  Shae met Azu's eyes and without speaking he made a hand gesture of the Four Winds before the door sealed her in.  She stood alone in the darkness, taking it in for a few moments before cautiously walking toward the centre of the spherical room.  In the centre of the chamber, she was told, would be a raised dais with a single taper made of glass - she reached out to feel for it, her fingers meeting its cold smooth surface.  She sat down on the stone floor in front of it and assumed the lotus position.  She closed her eyes and began to meditate, reaching out with her senses and focusing her attention until she could see the glass candle in her mind's eye.

There she sat, a statue draped in crimson, as the minutes turned into hours and the hours turned into days.  Some initiates had completed their trial in as little as thirty days.  Azu himself emerged after forty-three.  Some should never have stepped foot inside the chamber.  Initiates who gave every sign of being ready to commence the trial but kept a spark of doubt kindled in their hearts; they all died of dehydration and hysteria after three days of removing the ring.

Time ceased to exist for Shae while her spirit travelled through the dreams of gods, the memories of ages, and the imagination of the earth.  When her eyes at last opened she was nearly blinded by the tiniest of flames that danced at the tip of the glass candle.  She had done it, she had completed her journey and returned with her inner flame realized.  She spent the next several hours peering into the small flame before a single bead of molten glass rolled down the side of the candle, solidifying on the stone floor.

The grinding noise echoed off the walls of the chamber as the door lowered beneath the floor signalling her success.  She stood and walked calmly out of the darkness where she had spent the last seventy-seven days.  The glass candle remained lit until her focus wavered at the sight of the blood stains on the walls and floor of the tunnel.

\"Azu!?\" She called out staring at the stained stone, so much blood.  The sound of the chamber door opening in the past brought at least one brother down to greet the emerging Monk of the Four Winds.  \"Azu!?  Renli!?\"  She called out again moving quickly down the hallway now.

The cold mountain air burned at her lungs as she climbed the stairs to the courtyard two at a time.  The monastery was in ruin.  Everything was smashed and covered in mounds of snow: the statues, the pillars, the canvases, the doors and glass windows.

She ran through the broken gate into the courtyard and through the doorway of the grand hall, \"Grandfather!?  Where are you!?\" She cried out into the cold air and whirling snow.  The lanterns looked as if they hadn't been lit in weeks and the wooden floor not covered by snow was stained with blood and soot.  She spent hours running room to room looking for her brothers and her grandfather.  She found nothing, not a single body living or dead to explain what had happened there.  Her home was broken and burnt while she performed her trial.  \"I'm sorry\" she repeated over and over as waves of guilt and loneliness washed over her.  She wept long into the night before waking to the sunrise.

She steadied herself as best she could and resolved to find and save her brothers.  She realized she would need help in her quest - she had only just become a Monk of the Four Winds - chasing down an army would be too much for her accomplish alone.  Shae remembered the people of Daggerdale her grandfather had taught her about, \"Good men, some of them, worshippers of the light.  Should you leave this place, seek first their aid at a fortress known as Castle Radiance, far to the north.  They will help you.\"

The next day she was standing at the end of the bridge into the monastery.  Looking back one last time on her home in the mountains before pulling up her thick red hood over her head and turning north into the howling winds of the mountains.
