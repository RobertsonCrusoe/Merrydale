The scene is the opening event of a tournament to secure the rights to retake and resettle a farming compound called Thistle Ridge. The farm fell almost forty years ago under mysterious circumstances. It was stormed by a united front of goblins, hobgoblins, and orcs. The town of Dagger Falls is hosting the tournament.

The competing teams on the first day are:
The Whitecloaks:
Tarra Hilltop - Team Leader - Priestess of Amaunator, Retired Sunblade
Alexandus Ward - Cormyrean War Wizard
Zelia Oakbough - Ranger, good with a bow, from Lostead
Tyrell Dale - Retired Sunblade, sword, board and spear
Jannus Dale - Retired Sunblade, sword, board and spear

Team Chrysalis:
Jeska P’ijonne – Team Leader – Whisper elf solicitor and swashbuckler
Ser Rowan Grimbold – hired mercenary, a very strong, very fat former knight, has a short temper

Jasper Oakdale – a farmhand-turned-rustic human wizard from Shadowdale. Young and innocent.

Lucan Alavandor – the scion of a major criminal family in Saerloon, Sembia, He is a treasure hunter and duelist seeking his family’s wealth

Marigold “Mari” Thickwickle – a halfling grandmother and cleric of Yondalla, a healer


Opening Moments
Ser Rowan starts off jumping towards the flag, enchanted with a potion of leap. Mari scurries forward on her little halfling legs.

Lucan gets hit by an arrow, perfectly shot by Zelia Oakbough. It is only thanks to the magistrates judging the tournament that he is not killed outright. He is knocked unconscious, however, and what is more, the arrow is a trick arrow with a “tanglefoot bag” attached that binds Lucan’s prone form so that he will be hindered when brought back to consciousness.

Ser Rowan's heels dig into the dirt in hesitation when he sees that. Mari yells, "Just go for the flag! I have Lucan!". Mari runs back and calls on Yondalla to save Lucan, and he is healed. He spends time pulling the tangles off of him so that he can stand up. Starting off the battle on a dire note will make for a great song.

Ser Rowan's Arc
Ser Rowan bouncing is going to be pretty easy. Really, he went for the flag, which was being guarded by Oakbough. He fought with her in a tense battle, getting filled with arrows and spells from Alexandus, the war wizard. At length, he tired of the fight, and simply grabbed the flag and bounced up the “mountain” (the goal of the tournament was to capture the other team’s flag and plant it on “the mountain” – a 15’ high platform in the middle of the arena. He was close to being downed many times but managed to stay up. When he was on the mountain, he swung his hammer at the Dale brothers – twin brothers dressed in scale mail. Feel free to call Ser Rowan anything with the word "Ser" in front of it. He's heard it all. Options include Ser Pumpkin, Ser Goin’, Ser Showin’, Ser Belly, Ser Eatsalot, etc.

Jeska's Arc
Jeska tumbled around to protect the flag valiantly, all the while hurling funny insults at the Dale Brothers, mocking their good looks and career choices. In the end she ended up knocked into the water. One of the Dale brothers bounded across the moat, grabbed the flag and threw it across the moat to his brother who was waiting. I would make her crossbow grappler get the flag and emphasize how hard it was for the Dale brothers to get it to the top. I think it would be nice flavor if Jeska's banter emphasized the Dale brothers being handsome.

Jasper's Antics
This will be one of the good ones. Jasper the wizard tried to cast a grease slick on the ground to slip up the Dale brother who took the flag. Then he uncorked his decanter, sending a gush of water against the fighter, but it proved ineffectual. Just as the Dale lad reached the top of the mountain, Jasper sent a gust of wind that blew the Chrysalis flag from his hand and sent Dale brother flying backward. Jasper then summoned a monkey that appeared out of nowhere, grabbed the flag, and leaped off the mountain with the flag.

Lucan
During this time, Lucan had been shooting arrows at Zelia and Alexandus, trying to knock them out of the fight. Many of his shots hit, but Sara Hilltop kept healing her comrades, bringing them up to full health. Alexandus very quickly lost his temper and tried to fight a spell duel with Jasper, but got tripped up by the monkey, which flung dung at him. Lucan grabbed the flag and ran with it towards the Chrysalis moat, then threw the flag back onto the Chrysalis island to keep it away from the other team. A

t that moment, the Dale brothers were closing on Ser Rowan atop the mountain, but he managed to knock one down. Jeska took aim at the other brother, but Ser Rowan was blocking the shot. She leaned this way and that, trying to get the shot. Maybe she even yells out at Rowan to get out of the way. Finally, she jumps on the fence to get a better shot and says to herself, "Show me your heart, stag," before loosing the perfect shot to knock the second Dale brother out of the fight.

At that point, Tara Hilltop ceded the match, and the Whitecloaks made a gamesmanlike exit, with the exception of Alexandus, who, as a highly trained graduate of the college of War Wizardry, was insulted at having lost a duel to a “hedge wizard” like Jasper, and to have been splattered with monkey dung to boot

Chronology:
Opening Moves:
Ser Rowan uses a potion of leap to jump towards the flag.
Mari scurries forward quickly.
Lucan is hit by Zelia's arrow, knocked unconscious, and entangled in a tanglefoot bag.
Mari heals Lucan's wounds and he begins to free himself from the tangles.
Ser Rowan's Battle for the Flag:
Ser Rowan engages in combat with Zelia, who shoots arrows at him, and Alexandus, who casts spells.
He grabs the flag and leaps up the "mountain."
Jeska's Defense and the Dale Brothers' Action:
Jeska tumbles around to protect the flag, mocking the Dale brothers.
One of the Dale brothers crosses the moat, grabs the flag, and throws it to his brother.

The brother who receives the flag is targeted by Jasper's grease slick but makes it up the mountain with the flag.
Jeska is then knocked into the water by the Dale brother.
Jasper's Counterattack and Monkey's Role:
Jasper sends a gust of wind, dislodging the flag from the Dale brother on the mountain and knocking him backward.
Jasper's summoned monkey grabs the flag and leaps off the mountain.
Lucan's Recovery and Heroic Action:
Lucan, now free, shoots arrows at Zelia and Alexandrus.
He grabs the flag from the monkey and runs towards the Chrysalis moat.
Lucan throws the flag back into Chrysalis territory.
Alexandrus, frustrated, engages in a spell duel with Jasper but is tripped up by the monkey.
Ser Rowan's Continued Defense on the Mountain:
Ser Rowan, atop the mountain, fends off the Dale brothers.
He knocks one of the brothers down.
Jeska's Critical Shot:
Jeska, finding the right angle, aims at the remaining Dale brother, initially blocked by Ser Rowan.
She takes a successful shot, saying, "Show me your heart, stag."
Conclusion of the Match:
Tara Hilltop concedes the match, signaling the Whitecloaks' defeat.
The Whitecloaks exit gracefully, except for Alexandus, who is upset by his loss to Jasper and the monkey incident.


In Dagger Falls, as snowflakes whirled, two teams of valor met,
Chrysalis bold and Whitecloaks proud, in a challenge fiercely set.
The field prepared, the flags were placed, excitement ruled the day,
For boasting rights, and victory, the gods would have their say.

The bell was rung, Ser Rowan took flight and towards the flag did bound,
While Mari, quick and small and brave, ran out to find her ground.
Lucan, laid low by Oakbough's sting, fell tangled, in dismay,
But healed by Mari's fervent prayers, he rose once more to join the fray.

Zelia's arrows flew like hawks, at Ser Rowan aiming true,
Yet he with heart and girth and ire, the flag from her withdrew.
Then up the mountain's lofty peak, with flag in hand, he climbed,
'Midst magic spells and arrows' flight, his valor brightly shined.

Jeska, elf of wit and blade, danced 'round her flag with grace,
Her sharp and stinging verbal barbs led the Dales a spritely chase.
But Tyrell Dale, through melee's din, advanced with purpose strong,
To grasp the flag in eager hands, amidst the battle's song.

He hurled it to his kin in stride, a toss both bold and fleet,
Jannus caught it on the rise, their triumph seeming sweet.
Into the water Jeska plunged, 'twas by Tyrell's hand she fell,
Yet her spirit, bold and undimmed, rose with stories yet to tell.

Meanwhile, Jannus held the flag, and to the mountain path he raced,
Dodging slicks of Jasper's grease, with a skillful, artful pace.
Up he climbed, the peak in sight, the summit his to claim,
But Jasper's wind, with forceful might, soon brought him low again.

Then 'midst clash of steel, spell and brawn, a monkey took the stage,
A creature small, yet bold and brave, a true hero for this age.
The primate, swift and nimble, grabbed the flag with cheeky glee,
Then leapt from the mountain's lofty height, as cunning as could be.

Lucan, with his bow in hand, at Zelia and Alexandus aimed,
But Hilltop's healing thwarted him, their vigor she reclaimed.
So he seized the flag anew, and towards his goal, he ran,
He threw it back to Chrysalis' zone, a shrewd and clever plan.

Alexandus, the Whitecloak mage, strode towards the arena's heart,
To thwart the hedge wizard's simple ploys, to block his arcane art.
Yet Jasper, friend to bird and beast, sought the monkey's sly assist,
To disrupt the mage's eldritch plans, and his pride's fall to enlist.

The monkey, quick with jest and jape, caught the war mage unawares, 
Its arms were strong, its aim was true, it flung dung into his hair. 
Cormyr's son, a wizard proud, was thus by rustic mageling bested, 
His pride was scarred by the monkey's jibe, his temper sorely tested.
Ser Rowan, up atop the peak, with hammer swung in might,
Faced the Brothers Dale in a fearsome fray, a truly gallant sight.
Then Jeska, with her crossbow poised, found just the perfect shot,
"Show me your heart, stag," she whispered, her aim forgot her not. 

At last, the Whitecloaks, waning, their wills by war were worn,
Tara Hilltop hailed the end, her team's defeat was sworn.
Thus, Chrysalis, with heart and might, claimed victory that day,
Their deeds of bravery and skill, in my song I do convey.
