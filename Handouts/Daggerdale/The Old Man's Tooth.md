After several hours of climbing and hiking, you finally find your way to the top of the Old Man’s Tooth.  From your present location, you can see that the ‘plateau’ of this mountain is actually a really rough collection of smaller hills, crags and fissures; it looks like very difficult terrain, but you can see just down the opposite side of the rocky bluff you have just crested, a small road running east-west.  The road climbs up one of the two large promontories at each end, and you can a couple small columns of smoke rising from the more proximal of the two, to the west.  The one in the east is about 10 miles away, and you can make out nothing, except that there doesn’t appear to be much growing up here beyond wispy grass and purple moss.

The road down the hill from you appears well traveled, if not well maintained.  There are what passes for bridges over some small fissures, but the road is pretty much just a relatively flat stony trail in an otherwise very difficult-to-navigate plateau.

Behind you, looming tall and menacing over this mountain is Hyperion, and way off on the distance to the north, you can make out the form of Bloody Hand Mountain, upon which the castle is perched.
