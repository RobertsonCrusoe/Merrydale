2 Mirtul, 1483 DR – Notes from the Memoire of Ser Reginald Boxfray

Time for my axe to rest and be sharpened, and my pen to do some work.

I am writing this morning from the command centre on the third floor of the Town Hall. Less than two days ago, this was ‘Charrah’s Chambre’, but it has been cleaned of the shadow and deceit that fouled it. The evil magics of the Priestess of Maglubiyet and her Shadovar mistress (more on her in a moment) have been eradicated. As far as we can tell, once the eight banners were strung from the turrets of this little castle, the shadows and illusions faded. I’m glad they did, for in the aftermath of the chaotic battle and our victory, I must confess that I was not able to see through the illusions. I think it pained the more magically astute members of Chrysalis to watch my attempt to screw up my brain and see through the tricks; the floor and dais that weren’t there, the cages that didn’t exist, the shadows. It wasn’t until the banners were waving in the morning breeze that the trickery vanished, and we could see the room for what it is.

I’ll jump ahead just for a moment, before returning to the hour-by-hour details of what happened after the captain threatened Charrah, and the mixed outcomes that followed.

We have taken the Town Hall (this name is funny to me; this is a castle, albeit small and with a chunk missing from it’s face that I’m sure we’ll replace) and are dug in securely. We have had encouraging dispatches from all our lieutenants, the Yellow Tooth have mostly fled (though they still infect the bowels of Thistle Ridge, until we see to that too), and most of our command is now stationed in the command centre where I currently record this.

The reclamation of Thistle Ridge is proceeding apace, though there have been casualties…

Though I didn’t see it happen, there was a moment when Charrah was slain, and this sent a ripple through the mob outside. Many of the other priestesses, witches and unholy sisters of the changeling sensed her death, and their dismay infected the mob, which immediately lost morale, and allowed my men and I to shore up our positions for good.

I spoke with Ser Rowan a good long time about what happened next. I think that even though they had triumphed over Charrah, had sent her shadow-mistress packing, and had taken the central strongpoint of Thistle Ridge, the cost was more than they anticipated. As we’ve learned, this is how our enemy operates, making full victories impossible, and sewing doubt even in our most triumphant moments. Below are some notes on his recollections that he gracefully though hesitantly shared with me.

Jeska Continues to Act

Though Charrah had been killed, and the Shadovar skulker whom we now speculate to have been Loki herself was forced to flee, Coventina had died a terrible death, and Chrysalis was despondent, even though we had won a great victory. The mortals among us could not separate victory from the death of a lovely young girl, and the Captain again showed her worth and immortal resolve.

Jeska looked up to see a grim, yet bleary eyed Jasper approach with Charrah's head... she met his gaze with a look of… pride? shame? regret?

"Do with this as you must... I want her eye" Jasper said, handing her the head, and then moving to sit on the edge of the pit.

Jeska saw Lucan move toward his friend and lay his hand on his shoulder.

She ignored them and moved to Mari and Sir Rowan.

Jeska smiled in that grim moment.

"Well fuck me, a Dragon... for real…And that was Loki or I’m a goblin"

She could see confusion and despair in her teammate’s eyes. "Yes, I figured... and she took the Orb… I do not believe Coventina powered it, I saw she was already dead hanging from the chain... but she took another slave... possibly one with magic that can power the Orb... but only Lucan saw anything real...be cautious...she may still be here." she paused, then her eyes alight.

"Nonetheless, we have work to do... the two of you must get to our rear-guard in the basement, heal as you can, we will deal with the cages later... get tapestries to the turrets and unfurl on my signal"... she handed them her last healing potions.

"As for me, it appears I have yet another speech to make." She held up the head of Charrah.

Turning away she looked again to the boys.

"Lads, time to focus! Grief is a game that waits forever! While your vision is still strong with magic search these rooms and find what secrets lay hidden yet! Jasper, you will find your dagger can work equally well on the living… Lucan, draw your sword!" her voice softened "Stay by his side".

With that Jeska made for the turrets overlooking Thistle Ridge, and the routing mob that ensnared us.

Mari and I did as instructed and retrieved the banners from the first floor.

We were joyous to discover that Charrah’s (or Loki’s…when Jasper came to his senses many hours later, he told us that the ‘portals’ that we saw were a mix of real divination and powerful illusions, mixed together convincingly by someone with much greater powers than were displayed by Charrah. Our mutual agreement is that Loki herself was in the Chambre and facilitated most of the illusions, the darkness, and the dragons that we later fought) illusions were just that; Boxfray yet drew breath and held the doors fast with what remained of his FDL soldiers. The two at the rear door were likewise injured but alive, and encouraged by our news.

We delivered the banners to the parapets of the turrets and began to hang them over the sides. We could see the Captain on the northeast corner of the same turret, standing atop the merlons, defiant. Embra flitted about lighting the scene with many lights, while Jeska held aloft the head of the erstwhile lord of the occupiers. This is what I heard her say to the throng as she gracefully and effortlessly bounded from merlon to merlon, turret to turret, illumed by her fey bond;

“Yellow Tooth clan…your occupation of Thistle Ridge is ended. Your matron is dead, her captains slain, your brothers, fathers, sons and cousins continue to die by the blades of Chrysalis, and we revel in your slaughter.” Her voice was high and imperious, and her cruel words sent shivers through the mob. Already, some of the mob was in full route, and others looked up in horror and wailed. I made out distinctly at least three other changeling witches who rent their garments and lashed out at any nearby when they saw the head of their sister.

Jeska, seeing one of these changelings smiled with a cold and indifferent cruelty only her immortal race can produce. She tossed the head to the changeling sister, who caught it, examined it, and screamed in something between defiance and defeat. Defeat won in the end, and the changelings began to depart, and much of the mob with them.

What remained in the court was the hardest of the Yellow Tooth regulars, and some of their larger henchmen. Ogres and eunuchs towered over sneering and defiant goblins, who periodically sent arrows and spears whistling past a nimble and untouchable elf.

“Your clan is defeated, and that defeat will come to you personally if you do not flee now! If you take your families, your children, and head back into the Sorrow Bog, you will be spared. If you remain, your necks will have the same fate as your priestess.” Though the entire camp was now in chaos, and goblins were running in every direction, a core group of regulars, maybe two hundreds, remained to clap shields and defy the elven Captain who danced atop the battlements and threatened them with impunity. Jeska squinted into the distance and smiled, and continued to threaten the soldiers. Of a sudden, she stopped her bounding and haranguing, and stood still and akimbo astride a pair of merlons. A broad smile came across her face.

As many goblins drew bows and readied to fire on the elf, a goblin cry came from the Bastion; “Men on horses in the valley! The road is lost! They are coming!”

With this, a great route began. The hardened veterans in the court hearing the news figured out quickly what had happened hours before; the siege of The Tower had been a failure, the Sunblades had defeated the Torn Cheek, and Captain Garrick Greenfields and the 10^(th) Company had galloped in with the news in the valley, blowing horns and now making ready to gain the Bastion and join our fight.

As the sounds of a skirmish could be heard coming from the Bastion, the last of the banners was hung by Mari and me, and the first hint of the morning sun found the valley. This was the end of the enemy’s resolve for the moment, and they broke.

I would like to say that the enemy fled entirely, but they did not. Though for the next several hours, families of goblins fled Thistle Ridge like so many rats (and many rats and cruel things accompanied them), as instructed (we later learned they headed east, and climbed down the cliff just before the waterfall, though many perished in the desperate attempt to navigate down safely – what a mess they’ve left there…) making for the Sorrow Bog, many of the soldiers fled to the caves and sewers beneath Thistle Ridge, to mount a resistance against us.

And though the banners now rippled in a fair spring breeze, bringing the Chrysalis Coat of Arms to Thistle Ridge for the first time, and ensuring the safety of our little castle, the ruins still lurk with goblins, and other monsters and villains. Indeed, shortly after the chaos of the route of the regulars from the surface, as Captain Greenfields and half of his men (now on foot, having abandoned their steeds to climb to the Bastion) hacked their way down the main street through the chaotic route to find our doors, the villain Bolla revealed herself; Captain Greenfields men relieved Boxfray and the FDL at the main doors. Boxfray was now in the upper castle with us, taking inventory of our weapons and supplies and planning our defense while his men rested on the second level. From out of nowhere, the huge and disgusting form of the bugbear appeared, concealed by preternatural skill unthinkable for something so large and noisome. She set upon the exhausted and unsuspecting FDL soldiers, mutilating two in a humiliating fashion, and making off with gruesome trophies all the while cackling and taunting. In the foul goblin tongue, she said “Come play with me below, and bring larger treats than these puny men have given me…”

Our foulest enemies remain, and they seem undaunted by the protections granted us by the banners, their evil greater than the good given us by the Auxiliary.

Still, as I write this, thirty six hours have passed since Charrah was slain, and we have had only the two casualties described above since. The castle and our protection have kept out the rank and file, and Thistle Ridge, on the surface at least, seems secured.

I will return to the aftermath of Charrah’s death in the main chambre, before giving an inventory of the joyous dispatches we received from the different fronts of our little war, and the situation and decisions we now face.

Lucan’s Reflections

In the aftermath of the encounter, Lucan watches quietly as Jasper moves to recover Coventina’s remains. He instinctively clenches his loins as his mind replays the horrific scene—the girl’s body falling onto the cruciform blades, sliced instantly into quarters before tumbling into the cages below. There is some mercy, he reflects, in the fact that no one else had witnessed what he had seen—especially not Jasper. That Shadovar witch had murdered Coventina with calculated cruelty. Despite Jasper being several years his senior, Lucan feels a surge of protectiveness toward his friend, mingled with a cold, crystallizing rage at the Shadovar for inflicting such a devastating blow.

As he ruminates, a recovered Jesra flits over to his shoulder and throws her tiny arms around his neck. "Oh, Stone Tree! The false-face daughter fell to your sharp-singing arrow!" She twirls in excitement, her glow pulsing erratically. "So brave! Like lightning. Strong-armed mortal mine!" She strokes his cheek with miniature fingers. "Poor flower-lady Coventina, thorns hidden beneath petals she had. Nasty thorns, yes? But maybe she didn’t know they were thorns at all! Not like sister-elf, who wears her sharp edges where all can see and admire!"

She looks over at Jeska, who stands methodically sawing away at Charrah's corpse, severing the changeling’s head to display it from the parapets as a warning to the goblin hordes infesting Thistle Ridge. Jesra's wings hum a discordant tune as she leans into Lucan's ear, her voice shifting between singsong and sultry whisper. "Dragon-slayer she is! Hero of old tales now walking! You've hero-light too, my Stone Tree, but different stars guide your path." The sprite's glow deepens to a sunset hue. "Sister-elf's heart beats like war drums. Like forbidden dances. She makes my wings warm. Such delicious danger!" Lucan feels a surge of lust flow through him, then realizes that it isn't his own.

"Oh! Look now, Stone Tree! The changeling’s tale ends with a tale still to tell! Sister-elf severs the story at its sweetest chapter! Charrah will speak to the yellow-teethed scuttlers without saying a word!"

At that, she launches herself off Lucan’s shoulder and flies over to join Embra and Masra, the trio darting this way and that as they appreciate Jeska’s grisly work.

Moving over to the pit, where Jasper sits with Coventina’s wrapped remains, Lucan looks at his friend. The wizard's body is limp, and he is as still as the package beside him. Lucan isn’t quite sure that he’s even breathing. No – there is some movement. Jasper’s eyes are darting around the room, and Lucan suddenly realizes that the wizard is replaying the events of their encounter in his mind. He sighs and sits down beside his friend, his legs dangling over the edge of the deathtrap. Once settled, he pulls out a silver flask and takes a deep swallow before waving it in Jasper’s direction. Jasper doesn’t even look at him.

"By all the gods and their mistresses," Lucan mutters, his usual bombast deflated as he stares into the pit where Coventina fell. He takes another small sip from the flask, then speaks.

“Jas,” he begins, “We did what we could. You did what you could. Coventina’s gone, but she’ll not be forgotten, eh? And, you know…” he trails off until he sees Jasper turn his head his way. “It falls to us to see how she’s remembered."

“We’ve only got Charrah’s word that Coventina had turned to the Shadow, right? And even if we entertain the notion that that one single detail—that one thing out of the cross-eyed skank’s entire lying monologue, mind you—was actually true, what benefit is there in telling anyone? None. Cassimar won’t thank us for impugning her dead niece’s memory. So why tell her at all? Better she doesn’t know. Just like I don’t tell you every time Jesra and I watch you and Masra through that hole in your door.  Let Coventina remain what she truly was—a victim of forces beyond her control.”
His voice takes on a thoughtful tone, refreshingly free of self-congratulation.
“Did she betray us? Maybe! But what of it? We have circumstantial evidence at best, and only Charrah's word as to what her motivation was. In Saerloon, you’d barely even have to pay off a magistrate to acquit her. For all we know, she acted under magical compulsion. Or maybe she was threatened. Or maybe that witch used some arcane fetish to eavesdrop on her every whispered word. We'll keep her secret, and maybe someday we can profit by it."

"We’ll tell Cassimar that Charrah intended to use Coventina as leverage—to extort influence in Dagger Falls and force the Council to relinquish Thistle Ridge. We’ll tell her that Charrah never intended to honour any agreement, which is why she attempted to sacrifice Coventina to the Orb. We’ll tell her we arrived too late to save her, but we were able to end the witch who captured her and, as seems likely, reclaim Thistle Ridge in the process."
He glances at Jasper's devastated expression and softens his tone further.
"I won't insult you by claiming to understand exactly what she meant to you. But I know you had affection for her. By Sune's smallclothes, what man with eyes wouldn't? A touch needy for my tastes, but there you go. One doesn’t let personality get in the way of soft … right. Sorry." He catches himself. "Look. It may well be that she played you false. But I've known enough lovestruck maidens to recognize adulation. Her feelings for you were true, as sure as Chaunteans shovel shit. It’s a tragedy, I know, but you’re not alone in this. You’ll find Masra can be very comforting. And maybe Jesra can help out, too. I wouldn’t mind at all…”

Lucan trails off, his mind wandering. He shakes his head and speaks again, gripping Jasper's shoulder with one hand.

"Look, my friend—don't let this harden your heart. The Realms have enough cynical bastards lounging about, drowning their sorrows in wine and bedding other men's wives. We rely on you to keep the rest of us honest."  

Lucan glances furtively at Mari, Jeska, and Ser Rowan, then shrugs. His voice drops to a deadly serious tone, all pretense now gone.  

"We got Charrah. That’s an unalloyed good. But this conspiracy started well before her. The Shadovar were as deeply entangled in the plot to dishonor my family name as that hag and her misbegotten whelp. If that shadow-witch was indeed Loki—and I'd wager every crate in Waukeen’s warehouse it was—then this Alavandor's vendetta is far from over. I swear to you, brother, by my father's name and the wealth of my house, we’ll find Loki. And we’ll stab at Shar right through the bitch’s heart.”

Jasper Reflects

Jasper speaks, his voice clear, free of the hesitation and tics that it often carries. It trembles in what you can tell is anger.

“She wasn’t the evil Sharran you think she was. Yes, she was rich, from a fine family and beautiful, but she was also sad, scared and all alone in this world. Loki took advantage of her, abused her and killed her. If you want to blame the dead victims of this war instead of the real evil bastards who are doing the killing, you go right ahead and do that. They have no one to speak up and defend them, so you go right ahead and blame them.

“Poppy, Lily, Lilac and Alaventa are the victims of Palus, and yet the “tragedy” of this ruin is laid at their feet. No one defends them or fights for them. They’re just some evil that haunts this place and stands in the way of Daggerdale’s progress. And we’re here to make things right by purging them with Amaunator’s blessed holy light of righteousness, so we can all grow fat and old as wealthy landlords, right?

“I came to Daggerdale to fight for the victims of this war, not to play house in a ruin. And there, right there in mutilated pieces, lies another victim of this war.

“So yes, she was a scared girl who turned to Shar, and yes, she was a spy for Loki, and you can accuse her of any other crimes you want - she can’t defend herself now, so go ahead - and yet, after what we just saw, you expected her to fight off Loki’s influence and win? You think she ever stood a chance? This is how you judge her?

“I’m not absolving her of her crimes, but there she is: dead. You don’t think she paid a high enough price? What more do you want? Should I animate her corpse for you so you can stick your weapon through her flesh and feel your own justice done?

“You think me just a lovesick yokel.  Go right ahead and think that, I don’t care. But I will see her, like all the other victims, given the proper respect. I will avenge them all. Every last one.

“And if you think that trap that quartered Coventina was gruesome, I guarantee you, Loki Mourvanesh will get no such gentle mercy from me.”

Looking around, Jasper sees that the only audience in the room is Lucan, who stands at his side, a hand on his shoulder, looking down, his gaze going from the floor he’s standing on to the bottom of the pit and back.

“Sorry, Luc…I…”

“It’s all good, brother. You’re right about all of it, most of all going after Loki. For now, let’s do what the Captain asked of us, and figure out this fucking death puzzle of a lair we’ve just taken.”

Charrah’s Chambre Cleansed and Investigated

By the time the Captain had finished castigating the remaining Yellow Tooth, and Greenfields and his men had joined us with their news (which I’ll get to shortly), Lucan and Jasper has fortified themselves and set about the work that Captain Monarch ordered; they conducted a complete investigation of Charrah’s Chambre, and had discovered the following, as Lucan and Jasper relayed to us as we rejoined them.

It should be noted that their investigation became easier once the banners had been hung, and their blessings activated; the shadows and illusions were dispelled, and the vermin in the building fled screeching. Rats and meaner things fled from every opening, the spectacle of which horrified the men who witnessed it at first, but then filled them with a sense of safety. As I have mentioned, the protection of the banners isn’t absolute, and our sense of safety was misplaced, as Bolla seems undeterred by the sanctuary.

The Chambre is divided into five main sections, which I’ll detail below;

The Central Area

This is the cross-shaped central section of the level from which the other areas can be accessed. The dominant feature of the area was a huge opening in the middle of the space that served as a trap; victims could fall into the cages below, or be injured or worse falling on the razor sharp bars that make up the cages below. While the area appeared to have many human-sized cages all round, once the illusion was gone, we found none. The area in fact was quite tidy despite the condition of most of the Ruin.

As I write this a day and a half later, we have closed up the hole in the floor with stout logs and have set up our command here.

The other feature worth noting in the central area are the eight openings that lead to the four other parts of the chambre. Each of these had an evil magic circle in the middle of the opening, which summoned undead looking versions of Charrah, which we think she might have called ‘Forgotten Sisters’. The circles likewise vanished with the magic of the banners, and we can safely navigate the entire chambre now.

Charrah’s body was of course recovered and examined after being relieved of its head, and we found the following on her person;

-   Goblin Qeen Robes – Cloth, Potent +2, Resilient – skimpy old black dress and gloves

-   Accursed Staff

-   Charm of Cold Resistance

In the corner where the Shadovar lurked at the beginning of the battle, Lucan found the trinket that she dropped before she fled to the room in the north west. The trinket is a bronze statue of a dragon. It is only about 4” high and has the name ‘Gelzamael’ carved into the bottom of it. Lucan and Jasper studied the artifact, confirming it to be of Netherese origin, and capable of boosting summons spells. Specifically, if used to summon the dragon Gelzamael, when placed on the ground, the caster need not focus to maintain the summon, need not be present to control the dragon or keep the summon activated, and extends the summon to several minutes. The artifact is cold to the touch, and off-putting for all those in close proximity to it. To be sure, the sprites that accompanied us into this lair won’t go near the thing, and veritably hiss at it when it draws near, and faithful Rum Tum growls at it constantly.

Charrah’s Bed Chambre

In the northeast corner of the floor is one of the 20’ x 20’ areas partitioned by the stout pillars that hold the building up, and the old curtains that acted as dividers. Like all four corners of the building, a serviceable fireplace was in use and currently lends a dry warmth to the cool damp of this building in spring. The room has a good bed and dressing table of recent manufacture, which seem quite out of place (we have speculated that many fine things found in here were from the Drurginz caravan that was raided last year). The dressing table and cushioned stool before it have seen great use, according to Lucan. He insists that there are makeups, perfumes and other implements of vanity that may have come from the caravan, but may have also been brought here by Coventina, a known Sunite. To be sure, examination of Charrah found her to be well looked after, and evidence of vanity were everywhere, at least before the Captain sawed her head off.

The bed chambre also had a stout chest which the boys broke into after a struggle (not their strong-suit). Inside quite a bit of money was discovered (I give a tally of the currencies at the end of this account), as well as personal items of the priestess of Maglubiyet. The most significant finding was a magical kukri of Chessentan design, with the mark of some famous blacksmith from Thistle Ridge from it’s hey-day. While I’m not certain of the significance of the kukri, it made Jasper turn pale when he handled it, and Lucan insists that the handle is of a small diameter, as though fabricated for a small woman or girl. The blade is damasked, and the pattern when studied long enough appears to be an oak bough and leaves.

-   +1 Kukri – Plant Bane

The Laboratory

In the southeast corner of the floor is a small laboratory, again fitted with furnishings finer than we expected, and again likely booty from the Drurginz caravan (of which we continue to find evidence). A small alchemy lab here is bolstered by a witch’s cauldron by the warm fireplace, and a table in the middle of the room with the grisly evidence of many victims. According to Lucan, who was more excited about the discovery than he ought to be, there are roughly 200 gold worth of alchemical supplies here, as well as the following potions and such;

-   4 potions of healing (moderate)

-   4 potions of negative energy resistance (lesser) – we found 10 empty vials of these scattered around the main area, and suspect that the zealots each drank one before the battle began.

-   A large and gruesome book with a dozen nefarious recipes inside – see above about Lucan’s excitement

The Kitchen

This room would be relatively unremarkable, but for some of the wares that Lucan discovered inside, which confirmed our suspicions about the origin of the furnishings in the Chambre. Like all the corner rooms, it has a functioning fireplace that is also used for cooking. There are a couple of well-appointed pantries and crates and barrels of supplies, most of it apparently plundered from the Drurginz caravan. Some fresher foods were found that were clearly purchased in Dagger Falls not long ago; some pastries and cured meats that Lucan recognized as having come for vendors in town.

The Hostage Room

I’m not sure what else to call this room in the northwest corner. The room, the most interesting on this floor, is dominated by a table that had served to confine a prisoner, with grisly evidence of torture and violence on its surface, and manacles at each corner. Next to the table is a comfortable chair and a crate serving as a makeshift side-table, upon which a warm cup of tea was found, as well as a sheaf of notes (which I’ll get to below) and fine writing instruments. Other crates of Drurginz booty were stacked around the edge of the room, and a fine credenza took up most of the northern wall. Most interestingly, upon this credenza was a small stand that held an orb, which I’ll discuss below.

Finally, though all the other magic circles on this floor vanished once the banners’ divinity washed them away, an occult circle remained close by the credenza and the table. Jasper later identified this as a teleportation circle, and estimates that it will transport one or two people 500 feet to one mile away, to a corresponding circle. When asked if he could make it work, he said “likely, but who knows what we’ll find on the other side.”

During the battle with Charrah, Lucan witnessed the Shadovar (that we assume is Loki) dash into this room, grab the orb from the credenza, lay a hand on the prisoner, and activate the teleportation circle, whisking both people and the orb away. Lucan’s keen eyes saw that the orb was an orange/amber glass, encircled by bronze or brass circlets, about one foot in diameter. It did not look overtly magical, but we speculate that this might be one of the ‘slave orbs’ or other Netherese device we’ve heard so much about. We’ll have to see if we can find descriptions of these things and compare.

The Shadovar woman is 5’3”, about 100 lbs, with long straight white hair, and wore black leather clothing, as well as a non-descript grey cloak. She additionally had a loose belt around her shoulder with many potions and other trinkets that appear to have been thrown over hastily. As she moved about the shadows, they seemed to bend to her, and make her harder to see, and but for Lucan’s potion of True Seeing, combined with his keen and quick eyes, it is doubtful that we would have learned any of this.

The man on the table was restrained until he vanished with the Shadovar. He was over 6’ tall, gaunt, wearing only rags, and has clearly been tortured for a long time. Lucan noticed many healed scars all about the man.

Among the boxes of supposed booty from the Drurginz caravan were 3 crates stamped to be delivered to Sanduskin’s Supplies, including several blank spellbooks and materials needed to craft scrolls. Jasper was reluctant to disclose the value of these things, as a heated discussion broke out about how they got here, and why Vale might be doing business with Zhent traders.

-   Spellbooks and scroll making supplies - 1000 gpv

Finally, the sheaf of papers on the crate turned out to be journal entries that had been recovered by Charrah’s minions from various places in the hinterland. A few from Tergus Clarandal got the boys quite excited for some reason, and they sat right away on the floor to read them. Beneath them, however, was a small lady’s diary, still in relatively good shape though the leather bindings are now a bit mouldy. The boys didn’t take note of it at first, as they poured over Mr. Clarandal’s entries. However, Mari took one look at it, and said “I think you better make time to read this, Lucan. Weren’t you just talking about this woman?”

“What is it, Mum?! Can’t you see we have some of the answers we’re looking for right here” Lucan said, somewhat perturbed, and motioning to the scattering he and his friend had made about them on the cold floor of the room. “What woman? Who are you talking about?”

“Aisling Maeliticus” was Mari’s response, and the boys both paled and looked up at the matron. “Look here; it’s her journal, and…” she flipped to the last page “and the last entry is from 26 Uktar, 1439…let me see…” Mari put on a pair of reading glasses, and slowly nosed through the final entry, while the boys, now strung in anticipation, rose to their feet.

“She says she’s sick of the lies, and is worried about Alaventa and her family. She says she’s going to the Chapel Hospital in the Castle to help those people if she can…” Lucan was trembling with anticipation at this point, and held out his hands eagerly.

“Mum…may I…?” he implored, and Mari handed over the book.

The two boys hunched over the thing and fingered through it quickly, muttering quickly and excitedly to each other. Finally, part way through the book, they both straightened up. Jasper looked sidelong at his friend, a look of sadness and sympathy on his face, and put a hand on his shoulder. Lucan shrugged it off, took one angry stride forward and dropped the journal on the blood-stained table.

“I fucking knew it, Jas. I fucking new it…”

All of us gave Jasper and Lucan some space to go over everything again, which they presently comported themselves to do, and we await their report…

The Lower Levels 

Having spent a day and half shoring up our defenses as relief began to arrive, we took the opportunity to carefully and thoroughly investigate the first and second levels of the fort. The first level we will be quite some time cleansing of the filth the Yellow Tooth were happy to live in, but we did find quite a store of treasure, which I’ll inventory with the rest in a moment.

We have cleansed the castle of much of the evil, vermin and stench (indeed, the swampy smell left of its own accord without us finding much less removing the cause), and our position here is secure, and fit for mounting the next stage of the Reclamation. From the turrets, we are able to survey the entire ruin, and put the Captain’s plans and will to execution. We can also survey the lonely and frightening trio of haunted buildings that Nemia and Jasper spend so much time staring at.

I’ll begin the detailing of this brief but busy period by saying that the route of the Yellow Tooth has accounted for about four fifths of the goblins of the ruin; noncombatants all fled, taking the Captain’s advice and heading east to avoid us, even though their route was perilous and fatal for some. The many ‘nests’ (as the boys call them) where large clans or family groups had lived in for a year, were set fire by the goblins as they fled. Whether to deny us the value of the structures or simply as a matter of destructive goblin instinct, we know or care not; the buildings were fouled by these creatures and the thick black smoke which still rises a day later are a comfort for us; we won’t have to tread into those erstwhile vermin-choked warrens.

The lower reaches of the ruin, the sewers and caves, are held against us, for the moment. The bugbear Bolla is lurking about down there, no doubt inspiring or terrorizing the Yellow Tooth regulars that remain, to sell their lives dearly when we venture down to exterminate them.

We have seen no sign of the Shadovar at all, or the man that she teleported away with. They both remain at large, though Jasper says, “they didn’t get that far”, and Rum Tum is uneasy most of the time, and has activated twice this evening, running about and barking at shadows. His senses are a comfort to the men, until they do their duty.

The great flock of crows that infested the canopy of the ruin departed in fits and starts when the Route began. By full sun on the 1^(st), only a ‘regular’ amount of these birds remained, and none of the larger and evil looking ones that the lads call ‘Shadow Crows’.

The Fey Wives Depart 

The emotions of all the men and women here, Chrysalis, FDL and Sunblade alike, are askew. Laughter and cheers and jest are heard as often as tears and shouts of rage. The cost for claiming our foothold in Thistle Ridge has been high, though many doubted it could be done. I will start first with the Fey that helped Chrysalis, for their absence is already obvious and painful, especially to Lucan and Jasper.

I sat dead-tired in a chair on the third floor, and looked on as Masra and Jasper had the following discussion;

Masra (looking tired, unhappy and unkempt): Jazz…I don’t like this place…

Jasper (looking just as tired, and unable to keep his head up, his dirty face streaked with dry tears): There isn’t much to like right now, I’m sorry…

Masra: No, I don’t LIKE this place. I don’t like the straight walls, the cruel steel, the smell…all the smells…meat, death, pain, dark and cold…all of it so terrible, and the worst…

Jasper: The worst of it is me, I know…

Masra: It is not you, it is your pain, which is my pain now. You were fond of the crimson girl, so fair she was, so fair. I was fond of her with you, as I should be. The only thing here is her death, so terrible, so cruel…I can’t stay Jazz…I’m sorry. You have more goblins to kill, and I want to see no more of that. And most of all, you have darkness, shadow and ash to face, and little girls to save. I can’t help in darkness shadow and ash, Jazz. I’m sorry…

The little thing hugged her man so fiercely that his face turned red, and then she wisped away, Jasper sighing and shrinking without looking up to see her off.

I heard that other conversations happened with Embra, Jesra and their bonds. This place is no place for the Fey, or at least not yet. Embra told the Captain that when the time is right, they will return to ‘help’ with their ‘sisters’, but I have learned that ‘help’ and ‘sisters’ are words with many meanings for the Fey. At any rate, they won’t be around to help in the next stage of the Reclamation, and I’m glad of it. While fierce allies in a fight, it would darken my heart to see them hardened like us, and I wish them clean air and blue skies to fly joyfully through while we make this place suitable for their fair kind again.

7^(th) Company 4^(th) Platoon Bright Spears Arrive

It warmed all hearts to hear that members, former and current, of the White Cloaks, the Standing Stone League and the Free Defence League banded together to end the threat of the war machines and the trolls, aiding Chrysalis. All despite their differences in the Arena only some weeks ago.

Farmountain informed us that as the sun rose, Lt. Waterborne’s platoon of the 18^(th) company arrived to relieve them. Having seen only minor skirmishes the night before, the 18^(th) was at full strength and in good order, and knew that Farmountain’s platoon had seen heavy action the entire night before. Rather than resting, Farmountain marched what remained of his tired troops to relieve us, rather than resting himself. We embraced and declared him mad, saying “surely you’ve fought as hard as we have, Farmountain!”

“Perhaps. But we held ground, and had the security of our walls and brothers around us. You and Chrysalis marched into the heart of the enemy’s camp, fighting on all sides against enemies seen and unseen. Yours is the greater effort, and it would be our great honour to watch while you rest.”

And we did just that.

Cleanup Begins, New Plans are Laid

As I write this just as I prepare to sleep for a few hours, late on the evening of the 2^(nd) of Mirtul, three great fires and many small ones are burning away the corrupt occupation of Thistle Ridge. The smouldering nests continue to fill the air with an acrid haze, and the great fires we have lit near the castle serve to reduce bodies and other filth to ash and nothing more. These fires burn hot and high, sending proud pillars of smoke so high that we have heard they are visible from Town, and have stirred a great commotion there. New of our success has travelled quickly, and we have had messages from Gryphon’s Farm already, Mayor Winters requesting a formal visit to roast sausages in the flames.

Reclamation has begun; we hold the castle, most of the Yellow Tooth has routed, and only the regulars remain, holding the bowels of Thistle Ridge as I’ve said. Though we have a great work yet ahead of us, we are more or less free to navigate the streets (though we must be cautious, as myriad openings to the tunnels and sewers have been discovered, and goblins and worse can potentially issue forth just about anywhere, anytime) during daylight, and take stock of what has been won, and what works are ahead of us.

We are also able to survey the largest remaining buildings in town, and the work that must be done there. Three are haunted, as we all expected; the Academy, the Inn and the Merchant’s Hall are intact and require Chrysalis to free of Ghosts and such. This is a matter of some debate and uncertainty yet, and we await the Captain’s instructions on when and how this will proceed. The Garrison is in good condition, and I confess that already I have plans for it that I hope the Captain will approve of. I think I was guided here for a purpose, and I have stood on the parapets and studied it long these last hours. I hope I can make a home and a battle church here. We shall see.

The Artisan’s Hall was in serviceable condition, but the Yellow Tooth put it to the torch as they routed, and though the walls remain, the guts of the building have been burned away completely. It will require a great work to bring back into service.

I will leave it here, as I go to council with the others, to discuss what is next.

Whatever it is, Tempus will have a use for me, and strength to lend us all.
