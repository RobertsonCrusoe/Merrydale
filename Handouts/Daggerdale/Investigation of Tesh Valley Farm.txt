The White Cloaks traveled to Tesh Valley Farm after a couple of days in Dagger Falls.

On the trip to the farm, they were attacked on the road by a new threat; a flock of Kir Lanan, creatures that Rhuun of Thunder was only too familiar with.  These creatures used darkness and confusion to their advantage, splitting up the party and trying to abduct isolated members; they tried to carry off their victims, and would drop them from great heights when they struggled too much.  In the end, they managed to fight the creatures off, but they realized that they had been found.

Approaching the Farm, they were given the following narrative;

:The farm at Tesh Valley was actually consolidated and built at a partially finished Zhent outpost that overlooks the road and the stream along which the fields here are ordered.  It sits atop a small cliff face about 50’ high, and has a commanding look over the valley.  The stout keep that was built under the Zhents still stands, and a large stone wall has been built to enclose a number of homes, barns and shops.

:As you climb the switch-back trail heading up a gently sloping part of the bluff a little to the south of the farm, you notice that there is a ballista on the keep covering this approach.  At the top of the hill, you have to walk along a 100 yard length of road at the edge of the cliff to get to the gates.  The gates are closed when you get there, and it is a bit of a production to be let in.  The guard here is really on edge, and even though your fame often proceeds you, here, they still ask many questions and your entry is much delayed.

:In the end you are admitted.  Inside, you see that the keep sits on the eastern edge of a large courtyard, looking over the valley.  On the west side, walls encircle a bailey that is about 200’ in diameter.  This is actually small for the farming settlements, but it is limited by the size of the bluff it sits upon.  There is a large church built into the west side of this wall, opposite the keep, which is likewise small for the typical church settlement.  The homes here are all row houses like the homes of cities; the inside of the walls are lined with these 2-story buildings, and an inner ring of buildings is situated across a narrow street across from them.  These row-houses are mostly 3 stories, and can be seen above the walls from a long way off.  This inner circle of buildings is actually two circles, with homes and shops on both sides, the inner face opening into a 40’ diameter park in the middle.  There are lamp posts at every corner and several around the courtyard that illuminate most of the settlement.  The whole effect, for those of you that have seen real cities, is that this place appears to be a block of a large city dropped down in Daggerdale.

Their reception at the Farm was mixed.  Tesh Valley is apparently split between two factions; one lead by the local magistrate, who are zealous followers of Amaunator, and the other, lead by the mayor, who are zealous followers of Chauntea.  Of all the farms in Daggerdale, the rivalry between these two groups is fiercest here, and has actually caused some dysfunction in the community.

==Personalities==

Some of the significant local personalities include the following;

*Mayor Decker Blackmane:  This man is 51  years old, and married the daughter of Argent Whitegate (Aestis, died 1478).  The Blackmanes have long been worshipers of Chauntea and Torm, and it was the grandfather of Decker who designed and built this settlement.  He is openly hostile to the Whitegate Family, and his many children have tried to infiltrate the power structure of the Dale, mostly unsuccessful.  Decker was aloof with the White Cloaks, knowing that they take their orders from Lumina.

*Magistrate Weaver:  This man is a distant relation to Aestis, and rigidly adheres to the power structure.  He is basically diametrically opposed to most of what Blackmane does, and since the death of their common ally, Aestis, things have been rocky here.  Recent events here have made Weaver openliy criticize Blackmane’s leadership.  He was a bit more accepting of the White Cloaks, but maintained a professional aloofness.

*Lt.  Ander Millstone (7th Bright Spears):  The leader of the local Sun Blades, Millstone is the 3rd in three years to be stationed here.  Blackmane is always at odds with the Sun Blades, and tends to go through them quickly.  Millstone is patient, though, and has done a good job of ingratiating himself with the locals, who generally side with Blackmane.  Millstone knows that the Militia often acts outside of his chain of command, and blames Swift for the problems that have fallen of late.  He is not accusative, though, and doesn’t want to cause trouble, and will actually appealed to the White Cloaks for help.

*Lt.  Grid Hammerfall(2nd Sun Dragoons):  This is the stoic leader of a mounted unit of Sun Blades who got here two days before the White Cloaks.  He was likewise aloof, but seemed to respect Corran, Iolus and Banitt.  He was here to step up patrols in the area.

*Sgt. Fortune Swift (Militia): This is Tuugan's uncle.  He is the leader of the nuanced militia here.  The militia in this farm has always been the best of any of the farms; there are more of them than any other farm, and they stay on active duty 1 week in 4 throughout the year, adding another 40 men to the active defence of this farm.  These are mostly mounted, and protect the large cattle population.

*Elsa Warburton:  Mother of Iolus.  She has been living here for the last few years, after her farm became too much to handle.  She is a little on the outside here.  She keeps a small home and cottage-industry making candles.  Business is usually not good, and she looked lean and a bit haggard when she saw Iolus again.

*Corporal Wicks: A semi-retired militia man with a crippled leg.  Wicks looks after the livestock bailey, and actually has a small cottage there.  He always smells like animals. Corporal Wicks was gracious enough to accept the demi-humans in the group (Sorashana and Hruun).  However, shortly after he took them in, giving them austere quarters in one of the barns, he was murdered by Deadnettle, who then took on his appearance (she already had his smell), and gave the two White Cloaks some misinformation, in order to tempt them down a dangerous path upon which she would ambush them.

==Recent Activity at the Farm==

During the storm 3 days ago, most of the cattle on the farm got caught in the fields and would not budge.  Several militia went out to help the ranchers to round them up, but by the time they got them moving, they were set upon by orcs.  There were a couple ranchers and several militia killed, and the cattle were all lead off.  There had been much orc activity in the previous weeks, which Lt. Hammerfall thinks were just probing attacks.

==Gathering Information==

Despite the misinformation received by Soroshana and Hruun, Iolus and Banitt were able to get the information they needed in less than three hours of talking to various folks here.  They found the following key bits of information;

*A local villager who runs a tack and harness shop tells you that there is no small amount of animosity between Blackmane and the Whitegates in general.  The local magistrate blames Blackmanes Militia for the loss of the cattle recently, and it is rumoured that the Magistrate even accused him of deliberately arranging the loss of the herd to invite the Black Seed.  The local militia and the Sun Blades are also at odds on the same level; the locals don’t like the outside influence in their home, and don’t generally act in a coordinated fashion.

*You find a rancher who had a chance to look at the site of the battle.  The wounds on the dead men were mostly claw wounds and burns of some sort; not orc weapons.  A couple of the cattle appeared to have been partially eaten at the site, even though the ‘orcs’ didn’t stick around long.  The cattle and the rustlers did indeed head north along the Old Zhent Road, though.

*Lt. Hammerfall tells you that he personally followed the tracks north along the old road until they turned off.  They went into the bush about 8 miles north of the farm (this is a couple if miles further up the road than the old trail which 'Corporal Wicks' suggested).  He actually ordered his men to keep silent about this finding, worried that the local militia would pursue them to their demise.  He marked the path with three stones set 100 yards apart that are hard to miss.

*There is an orc tribe that has been living in an old mine somewhere to the north and west of farm.  They have been trouble for 10 years, but have been impossible to root out.  The mine is well hidden and probably easily defensible.

*Talking to a an older farmer named Gwyllen who has actually switched careers in his autumn years to that of scribe, notary and historian, with a little shop facing the courtyard provides valuable information.  Gwyllen says he knows about the mine, and that it was actually started by the Zhents 100 years ago. He’s not sure what they were looking for, but it has been continuously occupied ever since; some of the dalesfolk continued mining there after the Zhents were removed, but the mine was lost to creatures from the underdark nearly 70 years ago.  Since then it has been inaccessible ever since.  It is known to be inhabited by the orcs that have plagued this area for the last couple decades.  He provides you with a copy of an old map of the area which shows the location of the mine.  It is labelled as Gorimdun Mine, and the settlement here is called Baron's Watch Keep.

==Hasty Decisions, Hasty Results==

It wasn't even supper time when the White Cloaks assembled in the old fort around which this settlement is arrayed, to discuss their findings.  They had a map, two possible routes to take to the mine, and some sunlight left to exploit.  Rather than staying at the Farm overnight, they decided to head out immediately.  Furthermore, Hammerfall agreed to escort the party up the road with a platoon of Sun Dragoons.  The White Cloaks all took their horses as well, which Hammerfall lead back to the farm after the adventurers set off along the path.

This expeditious decision, combined with the use of horses, and the expert navigation of Banitt (who had trained all winter at Fort Lastsun) and Hruun, actually kept the heroes out ahead of the ambushers that Deadnettle had planned.
