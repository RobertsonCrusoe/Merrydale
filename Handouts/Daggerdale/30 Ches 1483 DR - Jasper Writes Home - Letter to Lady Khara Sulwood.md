Jasper Writes Home - Letter to Lady Khara Sulwood, 30 Ches 1483 DR
by Gus Miranda (as Jasper Hawkford)
Email thread: "Jasper writes home", 17-24 Aug 2024

A letter written in character by Gus Miranda as Jasper Hawkford, addressed to Lady Khara Sulwood (Jasper's childhood best friend and Heir-Presumptive to the Lordship of Shadowdale), on the evening of 30 Ches 1483 DR — the day of the Purple Worm attack and the Gargoyle raid.

---

This is a letter written on the evening of 30th Ches 1483 DR, by Jasper Hawkford to Lady Khara Sulwood, his childhood best friend, and Heir-Presumptive to the Lordship of Shadowdale.

My Dearest Khara,

As you no doubt have learned by now, my arrival in Dagger Falls has turned out to be anything but uneventful. I know your wish was for me to have kept a low profile and remained in the periphery of events, but as my first tenday here showed, it would've proved a futile endeavour. Standing as an observer in this town would only have left me returning to Shadowdale empty handed at the end of the Vernal Feast. Unlike our own home, Dagger Falls has a very stratified and rigid social hierarchy, dominated by the Amaunatori. It became obvious rather quickly after I arrived that in order to observe properly I somehow needed to infiltrate this hierarchy.

Despite your friend's assurances that the White Cloaks would take me on, by the time I presented myself for consideration they had already taken a former Cormyrean War Wizard by the name of Alexandus Ward into their service. Alexandus is a talented mage, so I did not begrudge them the choice, but I quickly found all other doors closed to me, even tighter than the White Cloaks had closed theirs. In desperation I took what seemed to be the most unusual and difficult of long shots: I pledged my service to the Ambassador from Myth Drannor.

Now, this was a markedly happy coincidence as she accepted me into her service, though unbeknownst to me at the time, I was not to serve under her directly, and our relationship to Myth Drannor would be essentially nonexistent. I blundered my way into accidental ownership of 1/5th of a new Adventuring Company named Chrysalis. The nature of this Company was to compete in the Tournament for the reclamation of Thistle Ridge Farm. I do not feel it is necessary to go into the details of that tragic place here, nor will I describe Chrysalis, my newfound companions and our success in the Tournament just yet, as you will no doubt berate me for losing focus. No doubt something about not being able to see Cormanthor because of all the trees. I will write separately about those topics.

However, despite the fact that I am writing this on the very evening of the attack, I am sure that by the time you receive this letter you will have heard all about the Purple Worm, as well as the suspiciously timed Gargoyle raid.

So let me move to the heart of the matter. Your suspicions are correct. As always! Once again the forces of Shade attack Daggerdale, and we are all in danger.

First, the Curator is once again at work within Daggerdale, and in Dagger Falls. Second, a large force under Melevaunt's command gathers in the west and already engages the Amaunatori and the Sunblades in the mountains. Third, A large force of goblins, calling themselves the Yellow-Tooth Clan, has taken residence at Thistle Ridge, and threaten the eastern lands. This force already has Bugbears - and worse - under their command, though not yet linked with the Grentzeldek. They are led by a priestess of Maglubiyet named Charrah.

Beyond those three physical threats, there is a larger mystery at the heart of Thistle Ridge. The haunting that attracted me to the place originally is only but a small part in a larger, more nefarious, Shadovar plot that stretches from Netheril to Sembia and on to Chessenta, and perhaps even further. The tragic story of Thistle Ridge is far too complex for me to detail here, but solving the mystery of that tragedy is the key that will unlock the door to the answers we need.

The Shadovar army that marches in the west is likely to be dealt with by the Daggerdale forces under Thunderstorm and the Whitegates, and I do not think that is of imminent threat to us. In fact, I think it is more than likely a diversion to siphon Daggerdale's forces westward, leaving it vulnerable to the Yellow-Tooth Clan in the east. And in turn, I believe the Yellow-Tooth Clan's presence and raids are simply cover for Mouvranesh to do her sinister work.

Loki Mouvranesh concerns me deeply. Not only is her political meddling in Dagger Falls something I have now experienced first hand, but it is her other occupation that troubles me most: she is known as the Curator for a reason, and there is some hidden artefact or treasure that she seeks at the heart of Thistle Ridge - or hidden in the eastern lands of the Dale.

The Shadovar have been hammering away at this Dale for years upon years. And not just Shadovar, but Beholders, Zhentarim, Drow, Devils, and worse! Some great secret lies buried in these lands. Mourvanesh is out to find it, and I aim to find it first.

You always chide me about lacking focus, but this I can say with absolute certainty: Mourvanesh is at the core of this, and I will stop her.

Yours always,
Jasper H.
