12 Marpenoth 
On this cold evening we arrive in Dagger Falls at our new home, Loedagan Close. As we were going through the wrought-iron gates, we see the silhouettes of many people back-lit by the open double doors of the main building across the courtyard. We can see the shapely figure of Lady Blackmane in the door, waving at a man that is making his way from the steps, towards us. This tall, lean, quick figure is followed by a swarm of children of many ages, and I can hear them sending off this man with enthusiasm. 
"Bring hell to their doors, Mr. Dretch!" "Take no prisoners, Avenger!" 
Many other implorations are mixed in the chaos of this send off. Then everyone notices us coming through the gates. Alystair continues his quick pace towards us, but we see the children hesitate and squint into the darkness of the night to figure out who we are. 

Dretch strides up and I can now see that he has already donned his grim leather mask. "I know that Reks wanted to be the first to congratulate you on your mission and to welcome you into our organization" the avenger says from behind the mask. "Congratulations on a job well done, White Cloaks." I could detect the barest hint of a smile somewhere behind that mask. 

He offers no other salutations and doesn't wait for ours. He continues on his previous trajectory forcing us to split and get out of his way. As he passes by Shea, he slows his pace for a few steps, and tilts his head towards her a little. "Invictus is a good man. You can trust him" he says, and then he is off at full speed again, vanishing into the night like a spectre. 

The children of the Close, orphans of the war with the Dark Garden, then close in around us, and they are just as enthusiastic about our return as they were about Dretch going hunting. 
"The heroes of Dwimmerholme return!" 
"Devils fly! The White Cloaks are on your heels!" 
They seem to know only a bit about what transpired at Dwimmerholme, and congratulate us on many mixed up facts as they usher us into the warmth of the Close. At the doors, we are met by Lady 

Blackmane, who welcomes us back, and tasks some of the older boys with helping us with our things, and some of the older girls to get a warm meal and baths ready. 

Within an hour, we are all seated in clean things at a freshly-laid table. There are as many children buzzing about the Close as there were when we left, but there is a conspicuous absence of the adult staffers; there are no adults in the Close besides Blackmane. And Reks, I presume, but I hadn't seen him yet. Blackmane explains that "financial exigencies have dictated a reduction in staff" and that "the children are happy to do the work, and need to learn the value of work at any rate." I now notice that there are fewer candles burning in the halls and rooms than when we left, and that those that burn at our table are tallow, not wax. The fire in the hearth is ½ the size of the one that blazed when we left. 

All of these subtle details amount to the suggestion that money is tight for the White Cloaks. 

We were all well into our meal by the time some of us demanded to see Reks. It seems unthinkable that he would not come down to welcome us home, but then, the man is very strange. Blackmane explains that he has been really withdrawn lately, looking at the books and a large volume of correspondence. 

He is locked away in his study for the night, and will receive no visitors. When pressed, she expertly diverts the inquiries to discussions of our recent mission, and finally, when put hard to it, she says "I would consider it a favour if you just let him stay in his room unmolested for the night", which disarms even the most incensed of us. She's good at her job, Blackmane. 

After the late meal, one of the children, a girl about 8 years old named Karindra Oakdale, says to me that there has been a letter waiting for me all day. She led me to the aviary, where the White Cloak's messenger pigeons arrive, live, and depart from. There, she points out a bird with a note still tied to its ankle. I have transcribed it below; 
Nate; 
I have discovered that the enemy you fought in Dwimmerholme is called the Sons of Bel. I strongly suspect that there is a coven of these villains operating in Charity Hills. I am awaiting direction from Loremaster Solemnius, but you should begin to plan for an expedition to that strange town. I will meet you in-person at Quick Axes on the 14th 
Br. Darred. 
13 Marpenoth 
This morning we are summoned to the dining hall for breakfast, where Reks is found putting plates on the table with the children. He is making a show of being a 'working guy, just like you' to the children, although everything he does requires correcting by an older girl that follows behind him. 

As we file into the hall, he claps his hands together excitedly. In doing so, he absent-mindedly drops a plate that is caught by the same girl that was following him, before it hits the ground. "Brilliant!" he exclaims. "The heroes of Dwimmerholme have returned at last! What kept you? I was expecting you yesterdayanyway, I know that heroing is difficult business to set one's time piece to, so I won't press the matter. Ha ha!" 

What follows can only be described as a 'production'. Reks has gone through some trouble to orchestrate a ceremony welcoming us all as official members of the White Cloaks, no longer with the 'Bravo Team' designation. We are mantled with crisp white cloaks that Reks explains have been 

purpose-made for each of us. The acceptance of each cloak comes with an oath that was doubtlessly penned by Reks himself; it is lengthy and more than just a little odd and inappropriate. 

In the end he explains that the cloaks have been tailored to help "the next generation" of White Cloaks more than the old, standard issue cloaks. These have been enchanted to enhance the specific skill set of each member. 

To his credit, or rather the credit of the tailor who made them, the new Cloaks are incredibly powerful and useful. Mine offers me protection, can shed light, and also makes my senses a little sharper, though I feel a bit jumpy while wearing it, like I've had too much tea. 

After the pomp of the ceremony, the children are dismissed. At this point, some bags of money are brought to the table; there is a bag of 300 gold from Mayor Winters, and another bag of 500 which apparently was delivered by the Yellow Abbey this morning as a reward for our efforts in Dwimmerholme. 

"800 gold, my colleagues!" Reks claps excitedly. Now, let's just share this out, shall we?" The gold is divided into 10 neat stacks of 80 each. 

"Each of you can take a stack" Reks announces as though he is giving us the keys to heaven itself. Each team member takes a share. 

"Sgt. Paros" Reks addresses Hennigan. "You get one discretionary share to payout." He slides one pile to Hennigan, and moves on. 

"One share, for some reason that I was never involved in the discussion of, goes to the Church. Not that I'm complaining too much; they gave us 500 after all, ha-ha! A good arrangement if you ask me!" 

"Finally, 3 shares go back to the company" and he swipes all of the remaining money on the table into a bag with one flourish. "Meat on the table tonight, eh boys?!" He smiles, and then cranes his neck backwards for an awkwardly long period of time. Still with his head back; "now, 3 shares to the company seems dear to some of you, I suspect. But those are our terms. This company needs operating capital forwell; just think of all of it! This building, the fine candles we burn, the warmth of the fire. 

The very cloaks about your shoulders! How much do you think those are worth? A fair exchange, I think you'll agree! 

"Now, if you'll excuse me, I have to see some men that will finally be happy to see me!" and with a jingle of the bag, and a wink, he is out the door. This time, I do not let him off the hook. Before Reks is halfway to the front doors, I grab him by the shoulder to stop him. 
"Charity Hills Farm" is the only thing I say. 
"Dear no" Reks says, his face going pale. He squirms from my grip, puts the bag down and turns to face me. "Wherever did you hear that terrible name?" By this time, the rest of the team are at the archway of the dining hall, watching and listening. 

"There's more Sons of Bel there, Administrator. We need to go there soon. Like yesterday. Start putting it together." 

"You've mistaken me for someone other than your superior, I fear, Mr. Gale" and with this, Reks composes himself, crossing his arms in front of his chest. "I am calling the shots here, you see, and I'll tell you where and when your next assignment is." 

"Is that so? You're sure that Dretch isn't calling the shots? It seems to me that he left here in a hurry last night, and you wouldn't show your face afterwards. We all know that he was in Charity. Could it be that he told you not to send us there? Could it be that you follow Dretch's orders?" And I smile only slightly. 

"I call the shots here" Reks almost whimpers to himself. After a pause, he declares "The White Cloaks are heading to Foothills Farm later this week to follow a lead there. You have a few days to rest. Now, I am off!" Out the door he goes. Reks is at large for the rest of the day, and indeed doesn't show up again until long after dinner. He is again aloof the rest of the night. What is he hiding? 
14 Marpenoth 
I meet with Brother Darred in a private room at Quick Axes tavern in town in the afternoon, as he arranged. Darred is sitting with a man who would become a fast-friend by the end of the day, Artorias. The only person in the room is a mousy serving girl named Hannah that comes and goes with food and drink as ordered. 

Darred slides an ornate wooden box across the table and a leather folio full of papers. 

I open the box and marvel at the pistol found inside. It is more like a work of art than a weapon; the handle is a piece of reddish exotic hardwood, inlaid with brass grips and other hardware. The barrel is damasked steel that presents as a wavy pattern of materials that look like silver and brass, though they feel harder and colder. There are words and symbols carved into the barrel and other metal hardware- bits; the only one that I immediately recognized is a symbol of Gond. 

"This is a most useful tool, Mr. Gale" Darred begins. "Rafter herself crafted this for a client that...didn't surviveno fault of the weapon's I am sure. It is a marvel of both craftsmanship and dwimmercraft; it is magical, though she wouldn't tell me what it does. She said you'd figure it out as you used 
it. Sometimes I think she's daft. 

"Anyway, there are a couple of reasons you are being assigned this weapon. For starters, we need to make sure you are well looked after; you are about to embark on your most dangerous task yet, and while we will pray for your safety, we'll hedge our bets just a bit, hey? Also, you are headed to Charity Hills, where people love firearms. This piece is fine, even for them, and I hope it will help you transition into that town. 

"There is enough ammunition for 200 rounds of fire, and more will be readily available in Charity, I'm sure. There is more..." 

He reaches into the bottom of the box, shuffling around ammunition components, and pulls out a small piece of cloth that makes the hair on the back of my neck stand up. 

Founders Linen. All full members of my order wear a 4" x 4" patch of linen that was taken from a 

garment worn by The Founder. This piece is the requisite size, and the transparent nature of the fine linen is a dead giveaway. This little patch is frayed at the edges and has a number of stains that make the transparency somewhat muddied. 

"Yes, brother" Darred addresses me. "Today you are a full member. I guess you were expecting some sort of ceremony full of pledges, candles, incense, and the like, but it just doesn't work like that. What happens is this" he says, as he leans across the table, and stretching both hands out towards me, opens my cloak, and pins the linen inside. "You are now a full member of the Order of the Tome, brother. This doesn't mean that they are going to reveal the secrets of the universe to you. I don't know much more of that sort of thing than you. But, know that you are part of an Order that is older than either of us ever expected. More will be revealed as you prove yourself; our goals, methods, and so on. For now, you take orders. Can you live with that? Most of the orders are reasonable, and many are fun. 

"I personally spoke for you with Loremaster Solemnius; your accomplishments in Dwimmerholme, painted in the right light, were enough to earn your Linen. 

"But, you only scratched the surface. There is something much more insidious at work in Daggerdale, and there is an evil book at the core of it that we have been seeking for decades. The Diabolus Itineris. I know your ancient Netherese is current, hahaha! There are very particular translators, however, that say that means 'A Devil's Diary', while others say it means 'The Devil's Journal', and still others that argue for 'Devilish Agenda', but it all amounts to the same for guys like us; evil book, must get it. 

"Here's how we know..." and he opens the leather folio, and sorts through the papers. He picks one out and gives it a rap with the back of his hand, and hands it over to me. "This is from our associate, Basil Muloch. Go ahead and read it..."

After I read the letter, he hands over another batch of sheets; 7 pages written in Ancient Netherese which I recognize from the Belial Prayer Book. This is the ritual known as 'Bel's Bargain', and I am horrified to think of it being used against the people of Charity. 

"I can assure you that Earl Muloch translated this expertly. His manner of writing tells me that he is worried to send any message that says he knows too much. As such, I have returned no letters to him. Instead, I am sending you. And not just to bring a letter to an old man. 

"You will notice that your prayer book is full of the infernal text, but it has been written for a Cormyrean to read and translate. This text, this copy of a copy that was sent to us by our ally and associate, this is written in Ancient Netherese, and the details which he has asked me to uncover reveal that it was taken from an original manuscript. We think that it might have been copied from the Diabolus Itineris itself." 

"Your orders are to go and find that book, Mr. Gale. If that book is in Charity, it is most assuredly the centre piece of this Sons of Bel business plaguing the dale. You will need to assemble a discreet team; convince the White Cloaks to go if you can. Convince that crazy administrator to send you and his men. Or find a few trustworthy allies that can assist you and make your way to Charity." 

At this point, Darred introduces Artorias. "This man is from an associate order with great expertise in matters such as this."

Marveling at the boons delivered by the Order, I almost forgot that Artorias was sitting opposite me. He doesn't leave with Darred, so I invite him back to the Close, and introduce him to the rest of the team. He is an experienced devil-hunter and will be a great help in this struggle. 

Just before dinner, I meet with Shea, who seems to be out of sorts. She is wearing a new ring which she can't take her eyes from. I pull her aside and share my news, showing her my Founder's Linen, which she does her best to appreciate. Then she explains to me that she was caught in the street after Matins by Magistrate Invictus, who came back to the Close with her. Alone in our drawing room, he told her the following (as close as I can recall): 

"My darling avenger, you have certainly lived up to your title. I have heard of your heroism in Dwimmerholme. It was I that authorized the funds for your organization as a reward. But I have brought a gift that is for you, my dove. For both of us. For you, this ring is safety, and for me, peace of mind." 

It is a ring of brilliantly polished gold, plain except for a slightly broadened face upon which a symbol of Amaunator is etched. It is warm to touch and gives a slight tingle when worn. A feeling of safety and euphoria washed over Shea for a moment, making her feel almost like she's had too much to drink. The feeling then subsides, but not completely; the feeling of safety in the pit of her stomach lingers. 

"This will guard you from our enemies, avenger. Of course, you still need to do your part; you must pray and be vigilant and uphold our laws and values. And duck when you see a sword coming at your throat. But in your most dire need, it will protect you. It will also help me find you if you get into more trouble than you can handle. Finally, I had our brothers charge it as a very potent prayer conduit. We have charged this ring with the great deeds of many that have come before you; the honour they won for our Lord have all been enshrined in this ring for one purpose; once, and only once, you can use this ring to make a plea to Amaunator himself. He will hear you and he will answer. When you are in such a situation, take the ring off, clutch it in both hands and pray until you are answered. Do not take it off before. Do you understand, Sister Shea?" 

Shea is spellbound by the thing, but I can tell she is also somewhat put off by it. I suspect that Invictus will use it to spy on us, as though he needs more spies. But if it does what it says, the balance of the thing is clearly in her favour. 
15 Marpenoth 
Marpenoth is a cold month here. The trees seem to know it better than most, although this year they seem less than inclined to admit the coming of Winter, stubbornly clinging to yellowed leaves still hanging off their branches. Most of the oaks on the property now stand stark and naked against the stern black walls of the Close, crowding around my new home like the grasping hands of the dead and dying. The entire estate has felt stripped bare, skeletal almost, since the departure of most of the staff. If not for the pitter-patter of young feet we hear so often in distant halls, the Close could pass as a funeral home with little effort. Last night, after I had finished the day's entries, we were summoned to the dining hall to meet with Reks, around eleven. 

The second-floor dining hall we gathered in is quickly growing cold, in both temperature and atmosphere. The clock hanging above the mantle reads quarter past eleven, but we've all been waiting here since well before the hour struck. Administrator Reks is certainly taking his time in addressing the meeting he himself scheduled, although he has been somewhat on-and-off again these last few days. Hastings Blackmane, statuesque as marble and ever in silent attendance, strides carefully to the eastern door of the room. She leans through it, whispering to one of the many young orphans waiting just outside of view. She smiles, motions to pat the child upon his head, and turns to face us with a faint nod of her own. 

"It would seem that Alen has lost track of the time. He will be here shortly." 
Footsteps from the corridor outside the dining hall gradually reach our ears, and after five minutes of quiet waiting, Administrator Reks enters the room with what can only be described as a listless stride. He looks up from the sheaf of documents in his hands, eyes the lot of us suspiciously from behind a pair of delicate spectacles, and furrows his expression into one of annoyed confusion. 

"You're all here quite early." He notes in a low voice, eyes dragging across our collected selves before stopping at the clock on the wall behind him. 

Musing, he taps his chin. "No... that's not right. I appear to be late." 

With a sigh he strides towards the mantle, fetches a fire poker from the basin flanking his leg, and prods the minute hand back up to the twelve, despite the resentful grinding and clicking from within the timepiece's innards. 

With a tiny smile on his face, he places the poker upon the mantle, turns to face us and gestures slightly with his free hand. 

"And now everyone's right on time, ha ha. Perfect. I suppose we should get down to business, then, hmmm?" Alen says, removing the glasses from his sharp nose and tucking them into the pocket of his vest, pausing for but a moment to toss the sheaf of important-looking papers he held into the dying fire of the hearth. 

"Bills," He explains with a grin. He approaches the table, draws his chair slightly aside so as to allow him room to stand, and places a single delicate hand upon the polished oak surface. He stares at himself for a moment, face reflected back in orange and yellow, before looking up once more and drawing a deep breath. 

"There is work to be done, White Cloaks. Dwimmerholme, I fear, was merely the beginning of something greater. Just the tip, so to speak." 

"Demons." A flick of his wrist on either side to punctuate each word as he continued on, the Administrator's voice betraying no emotion on his part. "Devils." A shrug. "Cultists." 

"Nine Hells, I cannot stand before you an honest man and your honest superior, and lie to you by saying I fully understand this debacle that we, brave citizens of this fair Dale, have found ourselves so unwillingly embroiled in." 

Alen throws his hands into the air, shaking his head with blatant false melancholy. "I am no man of the cloth, no delver of damned arcana, no seeker of secrets. I push the pencils, cross the T's, sign upon the dotted lines and all that boring business that keeps the real heroes doing what it is they do so well without being bogged down in the bureaucracy of it all." 

Hand upon his chest, Alen sighs. "I am but a humble elf, doing what best he can." 

And he smiles, turning to pass a warm glance at Blackmane, still standing against the far eastern door. "And so it falls to me to guide spirited individuals such as yourselves, those who are truly fit to settle these dangerous matters by means of thoughtless swordplay and blind faith and all the other crude yet heavenly virtues - "Praise the sun" he mutters off-hand "... dearly within the bosom of our beloved Dale. Under the leadership of Lady Lumina, Amaunator bless her dearly, we were content with charging about with nothing but piety to guide our smiting fists. Were those days these days, however, this devilry plaguing our fair land would have gone unseen until far too late." 

"The time for blind faith is long past, I am afraid. Raw zeal is not enough to ferry us from one day to the next - we must temper our might with wisdom, if we are expected to survive. These cultists, these Sons of Bel..." Alen rolls the word around in his mouth for a moment, judging the weight of it as it rests on his tongue, "these Sons of Bel do not stand in the light. They have no obligation to. It is a knife's edge that we will be expected to walk, I am afraid, while dealing with them. Hard choices have already been made, and more are sure to follow as we wade into this abyss." 

Slowly, the Administrator claps his hands together and paces around the side of the table, eyes focused on our backs. He laughs gently. 

"Aha, but what am I saying? We're not heroes, not at all. We are a business... and like all businesses, we run on silver and gold, not well-wishes and kitten-whispers, delightful as they are. Lions, scratch, coin, what have you. Budgets necessitate tough decisions. To operate at top capacity, I have to be aware of things. Ev-er-y-thing. If it has happened in Daggerdale, the White Cloaks will know. This is my mandate." 

Reks pauses after a full rotation around the table, finger drumming against his slender chin. "Though eyes and ears are expensive in the Dale, and it cost me nearly an arm and a leg just to get my foot in the door. These people keep their noses to the ground for me, and give me a taste of what's what for a small touch of coin. I have heard... a great many things." His eyes fall upon us, lingering overlong on me before passing over the newcomers at the table, Artorias and Abigail. 

Quick as a jackrabbit, the Administrator darts behind me, resting his arms on Shae's shoulders and mine, leaning in close between the two of us, a broad smile spreading across his lips. "Unfortunately, the White Cloaks are not above petty politics. A lot of hungry little lads think they can stick their fat little fingers in my pie without my knowing it." 

An edge of ice, cold as the Marpenoth wind, clings to Alen's tone. "The White Cloaks will not be danced about. If someone wishes to have a say in how things are run, they will have to toss around the moneybags like everyone else, and they will do it plain and clear where the sun doth shine." He pats Shae and I softly on the shoulders before leaning upright once more, hands resting together at his belt line. 

He clearly knows about my meeting with Darred, and Shea's meeting with Invictus. He's said that he has many eyes 

Briefly, the Administrator seems frozen in place, eyes set against the far wall of the dining hall, eyelids 

hung low over a tight, mirthless smile. Then he claps his hands together and chirps a tiny ha-ha, pacing around the table before arriving at his proper place once more. 

"Of course, due to all of this, I have had a change of heart these last few days. The budget is tight, and some leads are less than lucrative when compared to others. I've heard long talk from many different faces that bad business is a brewin' in both Foothills and Charity. Unfortunately, neither you nor I are competent or bothered enough to deal with both simultaneously. You lot," Alen says, idly drumming his fingers against the tabletop, "will be attending to the later. Post-haste." 

The Administrator quickly affects a swift change of tone, his voice piping in concerned falsetto, "But Administrator, what of Foothills! If we are investigating Charity Hills, surely whatever villainy brewing over there will run rampant without the brave White Cloaks to swoop in and save the day!" 

In a dull bass, he replies while leaning in the opposite direction, hands sweeping in a placating fashion. "Worry not, worry not, for Alystair Dretch himself is currently there doing his fair share of the work!" Alen's smile positively drips sarcasm as he leans forward onto the table, hands cupped against his cheek. "Of course, he might be cross with me. He requested you lot hop along behind him like wee ducklings chasing their mother, but I didn't form my Bravo team to serve as a simple clean-up crew for a masked lunatic who can't even be bothered to wash the blood stains out of his knickers. You lot are here to take point, like the well-behaved expendables, pardon, non-expendables you are." 

"And so, Charity Hills it is." Alen nods, arching his back as he casts a weary yawn against the ceiling. "Hennigan Paros will be continuing his role as acting Sergeant, for a job very well done. It warms me deeply to know that there is at least one member of the White Cloaks willing to go... the extra mile, both on and off the battlefield. His reports have been both informative and invaluable to me." His tone becomes less animated as regards me. 

"You," he points with a dagger-like stab of his finger, "will be taking care of the Quartermastering once again as well. Be sure to get an even share out of Charity Hills. Despite the name, they are certainly sitting upon a fat share of wealth. The White Cloaks could make do with some of that, so do what you do best. I'm counting on you. All of you." 

Alen's attention then swoops to the newcomers at the table, a heavy-set man and the woman seated next to him. His brow dips for a moment, a thin frown biting his chin. "Something is off over... you two aren't Bastian. There's not even the slightest resemblance. Where is that lout?" He twists his head, eyeing each chair lining the table. "I said eleven sharpish." 

Blackmane meanders softly towards you, stopping to whisper softly in Alen's ear. A bemused expression embraces his face for but a moment, before he turns to her and nods. "Ah, of course. I remember now. What a shame. However," He says, addressing the two at the far end of the table, "The paperwork has already been dealt with, and you two will be considered associate White Cloaks for the duration of this mission. Bear in mind that your actions will reflect upon us as a whole, so please do nothing to embarrass myself or the faith our organisation has placed in you." 

The elf retrieves a delicate pair of leather gloves from behind his back and gradually slips them over his hands as he speaks. "You all will be expected to act with dignity and decorum. Any fool could tell you that by now the Sons of Bel are well aware of our goals and movements, so I beg you, please endeavor to remain as low-key as you can possibly manage. The White Cloaks are not the battering ram they once were. The time for crass zealotry is over. We must fight fire with fire. Strike without warning, and when you do aim for the vitals. Leave those damned devil worshippers guessing at your every move. But most importantly, and again, I have to stress this..." 

"Keep." And he drags one glove down to his wrist. "Your heads down." And he straightens the other. With a sharp snap of his fingers, the Administrator produces a sealed letter seemingly from nowhere, flicking it deftly towards Paros.

"The letter will inform you of everything you need to know to prepare. Just like last time, I have sent word ahead to Charity Hills. Across the room, you notice Blackmane wrinkle her nose as she turns to stare into doorway beside her.
