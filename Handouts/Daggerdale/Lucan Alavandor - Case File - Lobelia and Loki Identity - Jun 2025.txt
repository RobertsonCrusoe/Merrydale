*CASE FILE:* TR-1441-HAGSPAWN *DATE OF ENTRY:* Mirtul 5, 1483 DR
*INVESTIGATOR:* Lucan Alavandor, Scion of the true Alavandor line of
Saerloon.

*SUBJECT:* LOBELIA ALAVANDOR (Suspected Alias: LOKI MOUVRANESH)

*RELATED INDIVIDUALS:*

   - *Contessa Palus:* Swamp Hag of the Sorrowbog; known agent of the
   Shadow; the subject's biological mother.
   - *Charrah:* Chieftain of the Yellow Tooth goblins; Palus's
   granddaughter; the subject's suspected daughter.
   - *Andarl Alavandor:* Deceased; son of the usurper line; the subject's
   biological father.
   - *Susserah Clarandal:* Deceased; wife of Andarl Alavandor; the
   subject's adoptive mother.
   - *Aisling Maeliticus:* Deceased; mother of Andarl; the subject's
   paternal grandmother and high priestess to Palus's cult.

*SUMMARY OF HYPOTHESIS:* It is my assessment that the Shadovar agent known
as Loki Mouvranesh, who currently directs the forces occupying the Thistle
Ridge ruin, is the same individual as the hagspawn Lobelia Alavandor.
Lobelia, the secret daughter of the hag Palus and Andarl Alavandor, has
successfully infiltrated the Shadovar power structure and now commands her
own daughter, Charrah, as part of a familial conspiracy.

*FOUNDATIONAL EVIDENCE:*

   - My investigation into the family that usurped my own began after
   confirmation from the ghost hunter Nemia that I share no blood with the
   Alavandor ghosts of Thistle Ridge, and from Magistrate Whitegate's
   observation that my brown eyes are inconsistent with the usurper line's
   characteristic blue eyes.
   - The goblin chieftain, Charrah, has described Palus as her
   "grandmother." She is a hagspawn with one *black eye* (from a Shadovar
   father) and one *grey eye* (from Palus's Swamp Hag lineage). This
   indicates her mother must be a daughter of Palus.
   - Official records show a child named Lobelia Alavandor was born in 1428
   to Andarl and Susserah Alavandor. She is documented as having two
   differently colored eyes: one *blue* and one *grey*.
   - The provided text on hagspawn confirms that a Swamp Hag mother imparts
   a grey eye to her offspring. The blue eye corresponds to the known eye
   color of the usurper Alavandor line. This strongly supports the theory that
   Lobelia was the biological daughter of Palus and Andarl.
   - The Shadovar agent Loki Mouvranesh has been observed in a position of
   authority over Charrah.

*ANALYSIS AND SPECULATION:* The primary obstacle to this theory was the
matter of Charrah's parentage. How could Lobelia, who carried the blue-eye
heritage of the usurper Andarl, mother a child whose second eye is black
from a Shadovar? The provided texts on hagspawn lore do not describe
second-generation breeding. However, given the profane and magical nature
of their bloodline, I propose the following principle: *the hag's eye-trait
is passed down, unblemished, through the maternal line.*

This principle resolves the contradiction perfectly:

   1. *Palus*, the full-blooded Swamp Hag, passes her signature *grey eye*
   trait to her daughter, Lobelia.
   2. *Lobelia*, in turn, passes on her own *maternal* inheritance—that
   same *grey eye*—to her own daughter, Charrah.
   3. Charrah's second eye is inherited from her father, the *Shadovar*,
   which accounts for her *black eye*.

The lineage, as told by the eyes, is a perfect match. This theory provides
the most logical explanation for the observed facts and reveals the full
scope of the conspiracy. The command structure we now face is a monstrous
family dynasty: Palus the matriarch, her daughter Loki/Lobelia the Shadovar
commander, and her granddaughter Charrah the goblin general.

*CONCLUSION:* The evidence strongly suggests that Lobelia Alavandor was
Palus's long-term asset, a sleeper agent who has now matured into a
position of significant power as Loki Mouvranesh. This was the true,
monstrous outcome of the "Consummation" rituals practiced by the usurpers
of my family's name.

*INVESTIGATOR'S NOTE:* They took our name to build a house of lies, and it
has collapsed into a dynasty of monsters. The blue eyes of the impostors
have been extinguished, replaced by the grey eye of the hag and the black
eye of the Shadow. My quest began as a search for history; it has now
become a hunt. The legacy of the true Alavandors demands a reckoning.

"It's as plain as day, Jas!"

I was a bit pressed for time yesterday, so I apologize for sending an
unedited dossier. I just set the parameters and let the robot run with it.

Here's the bare bones of the theory:

Premises
1) Charrah is a hagspawn with a grey eye (swamp hag) and black eye
(Shadovar)
2) Charrah implied that Palus is her grandmother ("my grandmother's hut in
the Sorrowbog")
3) Lobelia (b.1428) is likely Palus's daughter by Andarl Alavandor,
switched in at birth (see my other email)
4) Loki Mouvranesh can alter her appearance, much like a "changeling"
hagspawn (although she is female)
5) A Potion of True Seeing only reveals the true form of creatures that
have used a morph, polymorph, or illusion effect. Non-magical, innate, or
permanent physical forms not derived from a polymorph/morph/illusion source
cannot be seen through. This would include inherent fey magic.

Deduction
A) Premises 1) and 2) above imply that the grey eye is passed along the
maternal line
B) Premise 3) strongly suggests that Lobelia survived the tragedy of 1441
C) Premises 4) and 5) leave open the possibility that the "Shadovar" Lucan
saw may have been a changeling able to alter her appearance
D) *LO*-ki, *LO*-belia? Coincidence?

Speculation
Lobelia was a prodigy raised to be a vessel of vengeance or control. What
if, as a part of the bargain between Palus and the Shadow, she was sent to
become an agent of Netheril in the aftermath of 1441? A life remade through
the shadows: Lobelia disappears, Loki emerges. What if, as part of this new
life, she had a child with a Shadovar male —a child whose eyes would be
much like Charrah's? This would explain the ongoing relationship between
Charrah, Loki, Palus, and the Shadow. This would also mean that Charrah's
death at Chrysalis's hands may have been more personal for Loki than simply
the loss of an underling.

Of course, there may be simpler, less conspiratorial explanations
(Charrah's mother was some other unnamed daughter of Palus), but where's
the fun in that? The biggest hitch that I see is the nature of Loki's
shapeshifting. The Hagspawn text specifically said that only the males are
true shapeshifters. I suspect there is some significance to the following
passage that we have yet to figure out:

"True shapeshifters, possessing the highest degree of control over their
abilities, appear in tales that echo through all ages—terrible and
wonderful stories, for the most part. But I have never met a true
changeling. I suspect very few ever do, as the infanticide rate for such
creatures is nearly perfect. Only rare escapes from the mother's deadly
wishes occur, usually made possible by rival Fey."

Lucan will get to the bottom of it, mark my words.

S.
