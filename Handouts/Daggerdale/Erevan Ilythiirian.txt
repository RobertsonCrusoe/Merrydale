==\"Drown him, Boggy!\"==
thumb|right|The River Ashaba on the Doriankin lands

17 Marpenoth 1413 DR

“Boregar Maturin Doriankin!  You throw that thing in the river this instant!  You throw it in and you drown it!”

“Ariana!  He’s just a baby!  I can’t do that!”

“Then you take him back where you found him and you leave him.  His own kind will find him-”

“-and kill him themselves!  Ariana, I killed his father and his sister!  I am responsible for him now.  I will not take him back to be butchered like an animal.”

“You don’t know that!  You don’t know they were his kin.”

“I do, Ari.  I do...  He spoke to me with his dying breaths.  In broken, accented Elven, yes, but I understood.  His children were doomed to die at their hands.  A daughter not fit for the priesthood and sentenced to death, and a boy... a baby boy marked for blood sacrifice before he was even born.”

“Drown him, Boggy!  I beg you, for all our sakes drown him now, before they come looking for him!  Drown him and let’s forget him!”

“No!  He... he was escaping!  He had fled!  He had defeated Ilmater-only-knows how many horrors!  He had made it out of that wretched place with only two of his children left alive!  But he had made it out! Had seen daylight!  Had tasted our fresh, sweet wonderful air!  And I cut him down without mercy!  I shot my first arrow to his daughter’s head, between her eyes.  It went clean through, and she died quick.  With them, it’s always the women you worry about.  That’s why I killed her first.  With the father, I shot him in the shoulder and in the leg.  He was already badly wounded.  Dehydrated and malnourished.  My arrows were enough to bring him down, to wound mortally, but he held on.  I moved in to slit his throat with my knife, Ariana.  I moved in to butcher him like we have always done, and with his dying breaths he told me his tale of escape.  He had risked everything to save his children from their twisted fates.  With his last breath, he signaled to this: this bundle carried on his daughter’s back.  This baby boy.”

“Boggy... he will be the death of us.”

“And if he is, then that is our own fate.  I orphaned him.  It was I who did it, no one else.  And it is I who will take responsibility for him.”

“Boggy...”

“My father was an orphan too.  You know that of course, Ariana.  He was an orphan left for dead on the streets of Selgaunt.  He survived because he was willing to murder, to cheat, to steal, to do anything he had to do to earn his life.  It was only by fighting alongside Saint Maturin that he was redeemed.  I can’t let this boy die.  Nor can I abandon him as an orphan in the wilds of this dale.  Ariana, I killed his father.  I will see him raised properly, and I will teach him the righteous path of kindness and compassion.  I will teach him the ways of Saint Maturin.  He will be our son, and he will be good.”

“He is dead, Master Maturin.  He is at peace now, in his eternal rest.”

“He was a good man, my father.”

“He was a good husband to your mother, your father was.  Never remarried after she died so long ago.  Stayed loyal to her all these years...”

“Hmm...”

“And a finer hunter we’ve not seen in Shadowdale for quite some time!  A spooky wizard with a bow, he was!  Why I saw him take down a full grown stag from two hundred paces when he had tripped and fallen flat on his back! Spooked the stag out, and we watched it take off!  And your father, cool as an eladrin’s tit -pardon my sembian, sir- well, he just eyed him up from where he was flat on his arse, drew back his bow -gods-only-know how!- and took the bastard with one shot, clean behind the left ear straight into the brain!  At two hundred paces, and the stag at full run!  That’s him right there, over the door.  Big tasty bastard, that one!  We ate him for weeks!  An amazing hunter,  Mister Boregar was!”

“Hmm...”

“And he was a good father to you and... and...”

“Hmm...”

“If you do not mind my asking, Master Maturin...”

“I don’t mind, Eltar.  I know what you are going to ask.  You have been this family’s steward for a long time.  You’ve been like a second father to me.  Speak your mind.”

“What will happen with your brother, now?”

“My brother will continue to live with us in this house, as he has always done.”

“But sir... you’ve no obligation to him.  Your father, yes... he felt he owed it to the boy to provide for him, and raise him, sir.  But you are not under the same-”

“I am, Eltar!  I am!  He is my brother, and he belongs here in this house.  My house.  His house.”

“Yes sir.  But, your father’s will left you everything, sir.  He left you the entire estate, and he told you to send him away to Myth Drannor, sir.  But...”

“Come Eltar, speak!”

“But he is a child!  I came into your father’s employ sir, when you were not yet born, and he was but a babe.  It has been close to thirty years sir that I have been employed at this farm, and while I have watched you born and grown into a fine young gentleman well on your way to full adulthood, your brother...”

“Yes, he is still a child. I know.”

“You are not married yet, sir!  You have only seen three and twenty winters!  How many ladies, if you will pardon my frankness, sir, but how many proper ladies have been repulsed at the thought of marrying into the Doriankin family knowing they’d have him for a brother-in-law.”

“Blood is far thicker than any one woman’s delicate prejudices, Eltar.”

“But he is not your blood, sir!  Send him to Myth Drannor as your father asked!”

“He is my brother, and thus he is of my blood, and that is final!  He is a Doriankin, the same as I!  And in the absence of a son, he is my heir!  He is my brother and he will be my son!”

==Miscellaneous Notes==

===Cult of Saint Maturin of Anauroch===

The family Doriankin is one of the handful of participants in the, as-yet, unsanctioned cult of Saint Maturin of Anauroch.  While nominally a subsect of the Church of Ilmater (and thus of the Church of Torm) the cult is more of a philosophical and ethical movement, rather than a religion proper.  Adherents of Saint Maturin endeavour to live their lives in as close an emulation as they can to the morals and ethics displayed by Saint Maturin during his adventuring days.  Followers of the Maturite Rule are few these days, with most of them being descendants of the freed slaves that settled in the Dalelands.  Rumours persist of a monastery-hospital in the heart of Netheril (what was once Anauroch) where the Great Saint Maturin's remains are interred.  With the rise of Netheril it is unknown if these isolated Hospitaller Monks survive.
