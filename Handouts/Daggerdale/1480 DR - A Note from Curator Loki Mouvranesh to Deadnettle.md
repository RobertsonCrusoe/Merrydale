DDC – 1480 – 3: A Note from Curator Loki Mouvranesh to Deadnettle 
Incontrovertible proof of the connection between the Dark Garden and Netheril 
GFD Notes: 
There are some things that Administrator Reks reluctantly shared with me, and this was one of them. He was very coy about showing it to me, tempting me with it, saying it was the key that tied everything together. "Everything" he said with the utmost emphasis. What isn't stated overtly in this letter is that the hideout of Deadnettle, which the Whitecloaks tracked her and her trollspawn to, was also the fabled entrance to Tethyamar. Thagdul's hand at work a century later 

This note was found by the Whitecloaks in the very place it wasn't supposed to be; the old Zhent mine which concealed the ancient and lost front door to Tethyamar. Also, it wasn't destroyed, so it seems that Deadnettle was bad at following orders. 
-GFD 
Deadnettle; 
I don't know what you are cooking up now, but I have been ordered to help you by the Prince. 

The map enclosed with this letter is old and unreliable, but I trust that you will be able to use it to find what you are looking for. If what I hear about your side of the mountains is true, the orcs might have found many things we are looking for ourselves, and you might have an opportunity to help the cause by helping me in return. 

I am sure that the Nefal'im are living in the old Zhent mine at the base of Guzzinhelm, or Gorimdun in the dwarven. It is of the utmost importance that you do nothing to lead anyone to this mine. We have been trying to keep it secret from the Tethyamar League. This is just the sort of thing that will bring them flocking here by the hundreds. Any rumours or suspected Tethyamar sites have been taken care of by my people, but this one is in your court; it is the one gate to Tethyamar on your side of the mountains. 
Don't screw this up. 
Destroy this missive once whatever literate agent you have reads it for you. Curator Loki Mouvranesh 

PS; I know about your plan to use the Troll Spawn. It will never work, but that's your business. I 
sincerely hope you every success possible, friend.
