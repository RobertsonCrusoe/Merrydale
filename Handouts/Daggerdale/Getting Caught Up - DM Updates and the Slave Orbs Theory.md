STEPHEN (18 Feb 2026, Message 5):

Here are Lucan's thoughts on what we've learned from Reks and Invictus. The theory below admittedly benefits from knowing what comes next, but our journey to the Shadowfell merely confirms the first premise.

----From the Casebooks of Lucan Alavandor: The Slave Orbs and the Disappearance of Verinius, 9 Mirtul, 1483 DR

Reks was right, damn him. I've been asking the wrong questions. I think I've put it all together, though I'm not sure where it gets us.

PREMISE I: "VERINIUS ALONE CAN OPEN PORTALS" —REKS

Supporting Evidence:
- Reks suggested that Belladonna used the orbs to open the Shadowfell rift in the Dagger Hills, now guarded by the Shadowdam
- Estelmer's report discusses how the Soul of Corruption was "summoned" to Liberty Hill (implying it came from elsewhere)
- Netherese artifacts have intrinsic shadow-plane associations, and the Shar-Shadowfell connection points to the Soul's origins in the Shadowfell.

Hypothesis: Belladonna used Verinius to summon the Soul of Corruption from the Shadowfell by opening a temporary portal and pulling it through, just as she had used the orb to open the gate in the Dagger Hills. Accordingly, we can safely conclude that Verinius can be used to open a gate to the Shadowfell.

PREMISE II: THE ORBS FEED ON BLOOD SACRIFICE

- Jendwendle's testimony: Morganna (priestess of Tymora) and the caravan captain (likely a mage) had their throats cut and were "put into the terrible, terrible orb" in autumn 1482
- Coventina was a priestess of Sharess (a divine spellcaster)
- My direct observation at Thistle Ridge: The orb Loki grabbed after Coventina was killed was inky black
- Estelmer's report: The orbs were being used to power rituals sustained by constant blood sacrifice up until the moment of capture at Liberty Hill

Analysis: The orbs are glass spheres banded with brass around the circumference; when dark and viewed perpendicular to the band, they resemble a new moon, which is to say the holy symbol of Shar. Invictus confirmed the Sharran Inversion: charged orbs are dark, spent orbs glow. Notably, Vessuvian reported that Vortellius was glowing when he saw it in the Historic Wing (spent), and that Varros was cracked/damaged and glowing erratically (broken, cannot hold charge). Spellcaster blood charges the orbs. Coventina, Morganna, and the caravan captain all fit this pattern. When they were captured at Liberty Hill, the orbs had been powering weeks-long rituals sustained by constant blood sacrifice. Estelmer reports the Whitegates and others being eviscerated and hung from the Table of the Sun, their blood dripping to feed the Ritual of Corruption. Belladonna was thus wielding charged artifacts when Drake Sterling subdued her. Accordingly, we can surmise that Verinius was captured in a charged state.

PREMISE III: THE ORBS ARE "TIED TO" THE IMPERIOSUS ORBIS —ESTELMER'S REPORT

Evidence:
- Estelmer doesn't just say "powered by" but "tied to"
- Hoetmer's cryptic warning: "I would not transport it through any farm or Charity Hills"
- Verinius was held at the Castle for nearly a year before being moved to the Historic Wing

Analysis: Invictus interpreted Hoetmer's warning as concern about theft, but what if Hoetmer feared what the orb itself could do? A "tie" to the Imperiosus Orbis (IO) implies ongoing connection, not just one-way power flow. If the IO can send power through the Slave Orbs, it might also send commands. Remote activation would explain why the transport route mattered: a charged orb passing through populated areas could open a portal at any moment if triggered from afar.

CONCLUSION

If these three premises hold—portal capability, retained charge through blood sacrifice, and bidirectional communication—then Verinius could have been remotely activated to portal itself out of the Historic Wing. Sometime before autumn 1482, someone with access to the Imperiosus Orbis (almost certainly Loki) sent an activation command through the link between the IO and Verinius. The still-charged orb responded by opening a portal to the Shadowfell and passing through it. The orb would have arrived in the Shadowfell at the shadow-correspondence to the Cathedral in Dagger Falls, where Loki (or an agent) would have been waiting to retrieve it. No theft. No forced entry. The three "blessed" magistrates saw nothing because there was nothing to see: just a portal opening inside a locked box, and the orb going through. This explains why the box was empty with no sign of intrusion. And it explains why Hoetmer warned against populated transport routes. He wasn't worried about bandits stealing it. He understood that a charged orb vulnerable to remote activation could open a Shadowfell portal anywhere, anytime, putting farms and villages at risk of either losing the orb or having something come through from the other side. The year-long hold at the Castle suggests that the Aumanatori were waiting for something, maybe for the charge to dissipate naturally. When it didn't, they moved it to the Historic Wing. Still charged, still dangerous.

QUESTIONS REMAINING / QUESTIONS ARISING

1. Was Verinius glowing while under observation in the Historic Wing? This would be a partial confirmation or refutation. If it were glowing (spent), the theory would fail, and we would need an alternative explanation (e.g., could it have been charged while in the box through a nearby bloodletting/sacrifice?).

2. Why wait until autumn 1482 to recall it? Two years after capture. What changed? Invictus suggests that the date of the theft may be a clue, but if we accept that the theft was remote, we should consider the wider context, rather than just who had access to the Historic Wing.

3. Speaking of dates, when was Verinius last confirmed to be in the box? The gap between the last verification and Invictus's discovery defines the window of disappearance.

4. What else did the Estelmer-Hoetmer report contain? Reks claims never to have seen it, yet he somehow knows details about the orbs' natures and capabilities, Belladonna's use of them, and their current status. How? What's in that report that we haven't seen?

5. Who else knows about the orbs' capabilities? If Reks knows without seeing the report, who told him? Invictus? Or does he have another source?

One final consideration: What does this theory tell us about the fate of Phinneas Hoetmer?

Hoetmer died on 29 Flamerule, 1480—two months after capturing three charged Slave Orbs "tied to" the Imperiosus Orbis, and two years, more or less, before Verinius vanished. He was examining the orbs with Estelmer. He understood enough to warn against transporting Verinius through populated areas, suggesting he grasped dangers that others did not. Estelmer says he "went to sleep and never woke up," exhausted from his Aetherial excursions. Hoetmer's death left Invictus without expert guidance. Was his death natural exhaustion, or was he silenced because he knew too much about vulnerabilities someone wanted kept secret?

We need to retrieve that orb.

—L.A.

Glad you are enjoying Lucan's conspiracy spiral. I note that GFD's compilation is curiously silent on several matters that took place in the recent past, notably the Shadowdam. (Point of clarification - is the "Shadowdam" the fort that the Lambent Shield maintains, or is it the rift itself?)

While looking back through my Gmail, I found this nugget from Hicks, fifteen summers past. It seems to be a journal entry by Iolus Warburton.

---

I expected this to be more difficult than it has been. There are still many moving parts to track, but I have surrounded myself with capable people who can keep sight of the many elements I once felt compelled to manage alone.

If one were to ask anyone of real power or influence in Daggerdale about the White Cloaks, the likely response would be a furrowed brow or a dismissive shrug.

Others may concern themselves with acquiring more power. That is of little interest to me. I have precisely enough authority to accomplish what is required while avoiding the bureaucratic paralysis and political posturing that strangle the dale.

Several matters require attention.

First and foremost was the black dragon Kruhwall, who had taken up residence at what was once my family home, Warburton Farm. Kruhwall declared the farm hers until I delivered to her the Sharran druid known as Deadnettle. Deadnettle had deceived the dragon, a task that in retrospect required little effort. She had experimented upon and destroyed several of Kruhwall's eggs, perhaps even hatchlings, and convinced the dragon that I was responsible.

I am not a persuasive man, yet I stood before that vast, reeking creature and convinced her of the truth. I gave my word that once Deadnettle was captured, I would return her to the dragon.

At Liberty Hill Farm, my companions and I confronted Deadnettle. She was dispatched swiftly, too swiftly for me to ensure her survival for delivery to Kruhwall. I took her head and intended to present it to the dragon, hoping this act would satisfy her vengeance and persuade her to leave both my ancestral home and Daggerdale.

I expected treachery. I expected a rampage and renewed claims upon Warburton Farm. Instead, the dragon departed before I could deliver the severed head. That outcome was favorable. Amaunator has not withdrawn his light from my efforts.

I had prepared to face Kruhwall alone if necessary. If matters turned ill, I would have been devoured, and the residents of Daggerdale would soon have slain or driven her off. Alternatively, the dale would have been rid of one dragon. Kruhwall, however, proved more intelligent than prideful. That intelligence warrants caution. A dragon that recognizes an unwinnable position is a dragon capable of patience. I will watch the northern skies.

Less dramatic, but no less concerning, is Lumina Whitegate. Once a companion, though friendship is uncertain. Lumina exemplifies success through repeated failure. From the orcs of Gang'Dar to the fel-bleed now called the Shadowdam, she stumbled from catastrophe to catastrophe, drawing her companions into ruin.

I know what was inflicted upon me under her leadership. I endured. Others did not. Tuugan is no longer the man he was. I do not blame him. He does not carry Amaunator's light as I do, and even I nearly succumbed to despair. I understand he has begun a family. I wish him peace and dreamless nights.

Troia is another matter. I know what she suffered at Gang'Dar. I carry that shame still. I can only speculate about what befell her in the Shadowfell, but I suspect it was worse. My judgment of Lumina is shaped in no small part by what Troia endured. I recognize the bias, yet I cannot extinguish it.

Lumina failed us. More importantly, she failed Troia.

Troia, despite all she suffered, has moved beyond it. An observer might assume she merely buries her pain. That is incorrect. She radiates joy, beauty, and love. The suffering that would cripple another finds no refuge in her heart. She embodies Sune's grace so completely that it sometimes seems Amaunator himself waits upon her awakening to begin the day.

Lumina is not Troia. She lacks that disposition and that certainty of faith. She is fractured within. I saw the fissures before Liberty Hill Farm. Reports from the battle confirm that Lumina and the soldiers under her command were nearly consumed by a roiling, cloudlike demon. Once again, she led others toward despair and likely death.

Her final act as Captain of the White Cloaks was to relinquish command to me. Another misjudgment. I am not a natural leader and have never sought command. Altair or Alystair would have been stronger choices. Yet the cloak and rank are now mine.

Lumina remains a liability. Troia has written to me more than once, assuring me that Lumina's spirit has healed with time. I believe Troia. That belief will not prevent me from placing my own agent at Liberty Hill Farm, one capable of addressing the matter of Lumina Whitegate should her recovery prove illusory.

More troubling still is the indulgence shown in allowing Lumina to attempt the redemption of Belladonna. Amaunator himself would not rescue that woman's soul. He would scour it with unrelenting truth. That is the course I will advocate.

Let her soul flee to her dark mistress. If Belladonna returns as a ghost, I will burn her to motes of ash. If she rises as a vampire, I will reduce her to cinders. If she emerges as demon, ghoul, or any other abomination, I will cast her back again and again, shrieking and smoldering under righteous flame. I will take whatever steps are required to protect this dale.

Beyond Kruhwall and Lumina, other matters demand resolution.

Castle Daggerdale is one. I grow weary of endless caution from the Knights of the Lambent Shield, these aging and fearful men who counsel delay at every turn. I will enter that place with those I trust and ensure that the Knights find other concerns more suited to their temperament.

Alen Reks will be addressed today.

There is also the matter of Chauntea and other faiths within Daggerdale. It is no conjecture that the Dark Garden exploited Chauntean worship as a vessel for corruption. I will not call for the prohibition of all faiths beyond my own. I will recommend instead that wherever any religion other than that of Amaunator gains a foothold, there be five of Amaunator's clergy for every one of the other faith. Some will call this paranoia or oppression. Those voices warrant scrutiny.

Finally, I must determine the future structure of the White Cloaks.

As Captain, I require capable and reliable sergeants, men unafraid to act when action is necessary.

I will speak with Corran Altair, Alystair Dretch, and Vanion Creed regarding leadership roles. They are senior members. I know their strengths and their deficiencies. I trust them.

If possible, I will also ask Vanion Creed to serve as Quartermaster.

As others prove themselves, join our ranks, and fall, I will ensure that the appropriate individuals assume the roles required.
