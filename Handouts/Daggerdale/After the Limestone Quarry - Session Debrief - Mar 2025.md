PART 3: TODD BURRY (02 Mar 2025)
-----------------------------------
Source: Gmail thread 195572e5d78e38a3
From: contact@toddburry.com

That was a great session!

I always dial in for combat and forget to voice my characters during a
fight.

Ser Rowan is feeling like the old cop that is "three days from retirement"
except in his case he's "three days from getting platemail". At this point
his chainmail is starting to look like one of those unraveling sweaters
Kurt Cobain popularized in the grunge era.

So many of those fights have me not wanting Ser Rowan to wade into a tight
spot, but then realizing that Ser Rowan would do that, and I just have to.
It captures the feeling of true bravery to me. Having a round of being
swung at by several foes at 18 AC is terrifying and thrilling—kind of fun
to have a big pile o' HP to absorb damage though. Sticking to my guns and
keeping Ser Rowan low DEX has been such a fun and interesting challenge.

Mari is definitely the worried Mom trying to keep track of everyone. Her
healing power is so mighty, but her ability to be in range of everyone is
not. That round where she did her first healing birst had me whispering to
myself "get the fuck out of the way bad guys". It was hilarious how most of
them did just in time. Mari is getting pretty good at gauging the health of
her teammates. That is, everyone except Jeska. Hint to Scun: get your HP
tracking in Foundry.

Also, hats off to that scene Les. I loved the idea of a dome of trees
growing together. It dovetails nicely with my thoughts on fantasy in that
just having fantastic scenes is enough. You don't have to have a bunch of
big anime CGI special effects. Now I have an urge to go watch Legend again.
It is one of my favorite movies that captures the feel of a fairy realm
like no other.

I agree with Scun's thoughts on logistics. I would agree with anything that
gets Ser Rowan his armor haha.

One thing to add: I've stopped using Mari's continuous recovery feat for
the past few sessions. I think dropping it is suitable for the feel of the
campaign regardless of whether Les changes any other rules. Without the
feat, we are in a place where we can treat wounds roughly once per combat.
I've replaced it with a feat that makes Mari better at treating poison and
disease, and that's what she would like to do right after the combat:
attend to her poor boy Lucan who seems to run afoul of every affliction the
dale has to offer.
