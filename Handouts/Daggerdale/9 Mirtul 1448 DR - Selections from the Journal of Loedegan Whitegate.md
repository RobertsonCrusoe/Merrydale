DDC – 1481 – 14: Selections from the Journal of Loedegan Whitegate, 1448 DR. 
Copied by Magistrate Phineas Hoetmer, Flamerule, 1480 DR. 
GFD Notes: 
This was work that Hoetmer was fascinated by; going through old journals of dead heroes to find links to threats in the present. Here was a relevant find; devils breeding orcs. For some reason of divine inspiration, Hoetmer sent this copy of the journal to his friend, Basil Muloch, a year before anyone knew there were problems with devils in the region. Muloch left this in a package in his kitchen, with other things, for the White Cloaks to find. This letter was ultimately used to attempt to triangulate the origins of this coven, and locate the known cultists that fled town after the White Cloaks starting banging heads together. 

It is one of the rare instances in which the evidence proved unhelpful, which you will unfortunately read about shortly. 
-GFD 
9 Mirtul, 1448 
South East of Nevermore Lake 
We were in the area today following another lead from the confounded dwarves. I hope my brother will soon realize that we will never find Tethyamar, and that we should stop looking. 

What we did find today was a demon. My chaplain corrected me, saying that it was a devil, but they are all the same to me. However, the scene we found was not the same as any other I've witnessed. We happened upon what we knew was a nest of orcs; the smell of their fires and the way that they roast meat, bursting guts and all, gives them away for a half mile. We stormed the camp immediately; pause and planning are the ruin of any chance encounter in the wilderness, as any good campaigner knows. 

Had we stayed our blades only a moment and watched, we may have figured out what was going on, but we did what we thought was best. 

We came upon the small camp like an avalanche, and the slaughter was as fierce as it was joyous. I don't think I'm happier than when I'm killing orcs. However, there was a demon in their midst, and of all things in the world, he was mounting some female orcs. Indeed, one of the men says that there was evidence that he had fornicated with at least a half dozen of these monsters before we showed up and put an end to his damnable rutting. I personally cut both heads from the randy thing, sending it back to the Abyss! 

When the dust settled, we searched the rough camp, and found that they were actually roasting a man. He was rotted and cooked beyond recognition, so I never learned his identity. Or what he was doing in the same neck of the mountains as these devil-breeding orcs. I am forced to hope that he was some sort of summoner who got more than he bargained for and exactly what he deserved, but I am not sure. 

And in the end, the notion of orcs breeding with devils is both repugnant and worrying. 
14 Mirtul, 1448 
Northwest of Bloodpine Lake, not far from the Castle 
Today we found a camp that looked similar to the one we raided a few days ago by Nevermore Lake. Orcish, foul, recently used. 

Someone tried to wipe out an arcane symbol that had been drawn upon the ground, but the very act of doing so only served to highlight the general shape of the symbol; it was what the chaplain calls a pentagram, and he is certain that this is related to the devil (he says) that we killed a few days ago. 

There was evidence that a man, or some other creature of a more gentle nature than orcs, spent some time in the camp. 

We suspect that the man we found roasting lived with these orcs, and summoned this devil for some deranged, depraved purpose. But we can't be sure; we killed them all before we had a chance to ask!
