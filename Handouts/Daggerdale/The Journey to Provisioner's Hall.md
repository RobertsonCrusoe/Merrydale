Part of the opening narrative to \"War in the Deep - The Struggle for Tethyamar - Chapter 1\".

==Castle Radiance to Fort Luminous==

The 30th of Ches is about the worst possible day to hit the road for many people; the preparations being made for the Green Grass Festival by the Chaunteans this year indicate that this year’s festival is shaping up to be the biggest in the recent history of the dale. Relations between the Chaunteans and the Church of Amaunator are, in spite of efforts by the Dark Garden last year, stronger than ever, and there is a certain tolerance that has not existed in the dale for generations.

As you are making the last few adjustments to your packs and bags just inside the South Gate of the Castle, there are young ladies hanging wreaths and strings of bright-coloured berries all over the place. The castle has never experienced this, and there are clearly many young men who are excited about this festival. For your part, you will be marching down the mountain pass and into the dale.

Just before you are about to roll out of the gates, a small host of mounted knights approaches. At the head of this group is the Lord of the Castle himself, decked out in his fighting gear. He wears a broad smile on his leathery face, and his eyes are a-shimmer. They ride up at a trot and stop at your group. Lord Whitegate dismounts with the agility of a much younger man, and strides up to Iolus. Grabbing his wrist, he pulls Iolus close and slaps him on the back.

“May you bring the Light to the Deep, Captain Warburton!” he exclaims. His mood is as jovial as any of you have seen, especially in contrast to his demeanour in recent months, since the Siege of Liberty Hill. “I don’t have any wisdom to give you; I don’t know from tunnels and dwarves and such. Just be yourself, and look after your men. I do have a request, though…” with this he turns around to look up at one of his mounted knights, and he claps his hands together. A small leather bag is tossed down to him, which he snatches from the air with the speed and grace of someone not wearing the 80 lbs of armour that he has on.

“This bag contains a bunch of correspondence for Tethyamar. Would you see to it that it is safely delivered to their Post Master?” He hands over the bag, and his smile widens. “No time for lengthy good byes today, White Cloaks” he shouts to the lot of you. “The sun is shining, and I have skulls to crack on the North Road. Amaunator shine his light on thee!”

Lord Whitegate mounts his enormous war horse, and his men canter off towards the North Gate, saluting you all as they depart.

Though the sun is shining brightly this morning, it isn’t long before the trail leads deep into the shadows of the mountains, and the road is bad; there is ice and snow on parts of it, and where the snow has melted, there is thick mud. Some places have been cobbled over to reduce the mud, but it is widely rumoured that these road-improvements are on hold for the time; the Whitegates have not hired back the crews that did this work in previous years, and the roads are not likely to get any better in the foreseeable future.

The journey down out of the mountains is pleasant but slow; the fresh mountain air and the sun would make for a perfect journey, if not for the trail itself. In fact, it is so slow that your company only makes it as far as Fort Luminous by the time the sun is setting; fully 10 miles from where you might get when the trail is good.

The reputation of your company is well known, and you are admitted into the Fort with no hassle at all. The Fort is a military operation under the command of Captain Northman, who some of you knew as a competent lieutenant of the 2nd Company, Mountaineers. Northman is observant and pragmatic, and not zealous in any regard. He is a Chauntean, and this year for the first time, there will be a small festival for Green Grass in this normally austere and foreboding fortress.

After you are quartered in the barracks and your things are put away, you are welcomed down to the mess hall, where nearly 200 people, including the entirety of the Wonder Bringer Warsmiths and some families of the soldiers, are having a late feast on the Eve of Green Grass. The hall has been decorated somewhat; the simple wreaths and strung berries you saw at the Castle are also here, though to a lesser extent. The hall is festive but controlled, and there is an attractive woman straddling a keg on the stage of the head of the hall who is filling tankards. At first, this looks a little debaucherous, but after watching her for a moment or two, you can see that she actually fills some glasses only half full, and one soldier with a bit of a stagger is turned away politely.

Captain Northman, who is perfectly sobre and decked out in war gear, greets you and offers food and drink. He is eager to share news, as he has not been at the Castle all winter. He has many stories about the activity on the roads, and is free with the compliments he pays his new company.


==Fort Luminous to Rain Hill Mine==

right|450px|thumb|The people of Rain Hill Mine enjoying themselves in a manner that Captain Warburton finds distasteful

The following morning, Greengrass, is a little overcast, but warm. By the time you get going, there are already games being played in the courtyard of the fort. Watching a pair of young blindfolded girls grope around for a dozen young soldiers makes some of you want to stay. However, there is lots of road ahead and likely another day of bad trail to contend with.

It would normally take only a few hours to reach Rain Hill Mine, but again, by the time you get this far, there is not point going further for the day, so you decide to rest the night here. Again, your reputation precedes you, and you are hard-pressed to spend a coin here; your lodgings and food are free, and what few people are here buy you all the drinks you like.

Tonight you are the only travellers at the Inn, but the place has a couple dozen of the miners and their families in the tap room that are wrapping up a day of celebrations. There are a few men passed out in chairs with wreaths resting precariously on their snoring faces, and several boisterous young men and women who are playing a drinking game at the large central table. You are enthusiastically invited to join, and those of you that go to the table are immediately crowned in wreaths by the young women, and given drinks.

==Rain Hill Mine to Foothills Farm==
The next day, Mirtul 1, is bright, clear and hot.  This day finds you descending out of the mountains, and down through the foothills, along a similarly muddy and slow road.

Part way through the day, you take a turn from the main path, heading north towards Foothills Farm; though many of you have been to the farm, none have been down this particular road before, and you are indeed the first travellers upon it this season; this road is the worst yet.

This bit of road, called Blackthorn Trail is narrow and rugged.  It is hedged in close about by tall pines which periodically give way to patches of blackberry bushes and other prickly scrub that is torture for both men and mounts.  Seasonally, this road is cut back by the men of the farm, but being the first along it this year, you have the misfortune of having to cut your way through these patches, slowing your pace and covering you with a multitude of lacerations for your efforts.

As the evening wears on, you realize that you won’t be able to make the farm by nightfall.  For some, this is dispiriting; the ground will be slushy and your meal cold.  To make matters worse, the more keen-eyed of your party have spotted glimpses of movement deep through the pines from time to time as the day closes out.

==The 'Mountain Spiders' Attack!==
Setting up camp is uneasy business tonight.  With every passing moment, the most alert of you feel like you are being watched, and as an hour, then a second, passes, you begin to hear noises off into the woods a little ways; perhaps 100’ or so on one side, then again on the opposite.  This keeps up for a couple of minutes…

The beast you see is certainly a spider of some sort.  But it is bigger than a horse, and it’s long legs probably reach 8’ every direction.  This brute is heavily armoured; it’s chittenous carapace a jagged field of grey and brown, making it blend into many varieties of natural settings.  It slams the ground with 2 mighty, armoured legs, and the strength of the monster is reported by the tremendous ‘thud’ of this threatening gesture.

Similar to the large brute; there are two smaller ones that are grey and brown and with a jagged but not nearly as thick carapace.  These might be younger versions of the tremendous hulk that is causing so much trouble for your party, or perhaps a different, but related species altogether.

==Foothills Farm==

Long known for their considerable Chauntean presence, Foothills farm is not surprisingly decked out from gate to keep and most doors in between with Green Grass wreaths.  Though it is now long past Green Grass, this farm is still in the middle of celebrations. And despite having been the site of the closing encounter of the Dark Garden affair and the second hardest-hit farm in the region with nearly 100 deaths, the place is vibrant and happy as you arrive.

The White Cloaks are well known to the people of this farm, and the peace delivered by Troia Trustar.  You are welcomed in heartily, and are brought to the Thunderstorm Manor home, where you are given what amounts to a royal welcome here.  The Thunderstorms have been fortunate in their farming and other endeavours, and are able to supply you with an outstanding feast and some of the finest rooms in the dale in their very own home.

Thy mayor of this farm is Helm Thunderstorm; a young man of only 31 years.  He seems to have heard quite a bit about you from his uncle, Maddrick, who is the Dead of the Yellow abbey, and a man some of you came to know quite well during the Dark Garden affair.  Helm is vivacious and generous, and once he’s had a couple of drinks, his suggestions to some of the prettier house staff border on inappropriate, though humerous.

Things have been quiet all winter and spring here, a peace that they roundly thank you for.  They are surprised to hear of giant spiders in the woods, and claim that they haven’t encountered anything of the sort themselves.

==Foothills Farm to Tesh Valley Farm==

It is the morning of 2 Mirtul, and you are provisioned with several day’s worth of fresh supplies; fresh vegetables from the large green house on the farm, as well as salted meet from a pig that was butchered in the night a-purpose.

You are happy to find that the road through the hills and pines between Foothills and Tesh Valley farms is in good condition; it is travelled extensively all winter long, as many trading and family relationships between the two farms give cause for it’s year-round use.  In fact, it has been used a great deal more this year compared to others, with all the activity happening at Tesh Valley.

It is a quick and uninterrupted journey to Tesh Valley.

As you are winding down a trail in the hills, just a little under a mile away from your next objective, you catch glimpses through openings in the tall pines of the stout stone walls of the farm that many of you have been to before; this farm is truly a fortress on a bluff, but there are new additions to the battlements that you have never seen before; you can see that to the north of the walls there is a great construction underway, and the place is buzzing with activity.

As you come down and out of the pines, and follow the road across the fields of this farm, you begin to make out the details of this new construction and activity; there are dozens of dwarves and men at work laying the stones of a fortified addition to the town that will nearly double the size of it.  Presently, there are scaffolds that soar up fifty or sixty feet, and the first few courses of massive blocks have been fitted, while the next course is exactingly added on.

Before you are within a half mile of the fortified town, a platoon of Sun Blades lead by Lt. Millstone himself rides out to greet you.  Millstone was a key facilitator for the White Cloaks during the chapter of the Dark Garden affair here last fall, and he is happy to see Iolus and the rest of you.  He is eager for news and has much himself to offer, especially where the dwarves are concerned.

Millstone explains that the farm is booming right now; the work being done on the addition, which the dwarves have named Provisioner’s Hall, has brought about 70 dwarven workers and nearly that many men as well.  Trade in all supplies, materials, and some luxuries has improved dramatically, and the benefits of this have trickled down to just about every citizen of the town.

Millstone brings you through the gates and into the very urban-looking town, and directly to the to keep.  You are brought to the council chambre where you expect to find Decker Blackmane, mayor of the town, engaged in borderline-warfare with the local Magistrate, Weaver.  These two are known to despise each other, and their acrimony was a factor in the limited success of the Dark Garden in the past.  However, in the council chambre today there is a small host of dwarves seated at the large oak table talking to these men and their clerks.  The lot of them are alerted to your arrival by a stout tapping of Millstone’s spear on the stone floor, the percussion of the thing bringing all eyes to bear upon you.  One old dwarf with a long and silver beard seems annoyed at the interruption, until the Lt. announces “Captain Iolus Warburton and his White Cloaks, my lords!”  The eyes of the dwarf widen and he clearly wears an expression of amazement.  He tries to command his emotions, and you see his face contort in a variety of expressions, finally resting on a stoic countenance.  He crosses the room hastily and offers his hand, and when accepted, he pulls you down and in tightly and puts you into a vice-like hug that lasts just a little too long.  The dwarf finally lets you go, and his face is beaming.

“I guess you don’t need introductions to these two old villains” the dwarf says in a thin and gravelly voice, pointing a thumb over his shoulder at the mayor and the magistrate.  “I’m Drago Haylish, chief of the Iron Pillar clan.  I am the Steward and Factor of Provisioner’s Hall, and member of the Silver Council.  And I am your servant, young lords!”

==Elsa Warburton==
Mrs. Warburton can be found in her little candle shop, as usual.  However, she is no longer alone in the shop; a pair of young girls, about 9 or 10 years old, work away making candlesticks and chirping like two happy little birds.  Their names are Maddy and Emma, and their fathr was eaten by Kruhwahll in service as a Sun Blade, when it rampaged through the town last year.  The girls seem happy, though not so happy as Elsa Warburton, to see her son.  She says not a word, but crosses her little shop to embrace you and pull your head onto her shoulder.  The two little girls just watch, but after the gesture goes on for what must seem like a terribly long time for them, they too cross the room, and wrap their little arms around the pair of you.  After one more brief, fond moment, the lot of you break out in laughter for the gesture of the girls.  “Put the kettle on, Maddy” Elsa says.  Before long, the lot of you are sitting in the back room on boxes of packed wax and candles, and sharing a warm cider tea.

Your mother is in good health.  The best that she has been in for years.  The cough that plagued her for the last couple of years seems to have abated, and her colour is much better, though you now see that there are lines at the corners of her face that even good health and fortune can’t keep from the faces of women.  She explains that she has a lucrative contract with the dwarves to make all the lighting needed for Provisioner’s Hall, which has kept her busy enough to keep her new charges gainfully employed as well as herself.  Further, she is getting the services of a dwarven healing woman who now lives in the town, free of charge.

Elsa is as happy as you ever remember her being, and though it is difficult for her to take her eyes off of you, when she does, she stares at the two little girls who seem to never stop moving and talking, and the broadest smile crosses her face.  When talking about the girls, she often refers to them as ‘your sisters’, and it becomes clear that the girls are now living with your mother, upstairs, as they have no family.  “I used to worry about them, Iolus” she explains.  “Being an orphan is difficult, but we never miss matins, and only rarely vespers; they are full of His Light, and daily I make them pray while holding the sword and shield of their dead father so that they will not forget…never forget…”
