STEPHEN (19 Jun 2025, 19:32):

Good questions - and there is a slight contradiction in Blacky's handouts that I'm unsure about (may just be a mistake on his part)

From ZW's report after the fact:

The First Date: 21 Eleint, 1441 DR (The Autumnal Equinox)

This date comes from the firsthand account of Magistrate Invictus, as recorded in Magistrate Winters's report written four years after the event. Invictus's memory is tied to a direct and consecutive series of events: he received the last message from Daphne Dale on the 20th, noted the complete lack of communication on the 21st, and then dispatched his first four foresters to scout the silent settlement on the 22nd. On the 23rd, the Dagger Falls' garrison was reinforced and two platoons were sent to perform a "recon in force" of the ruin.

Invictus's account and Malveau Greenleaf's report both attest to a classic pincer movement, with a primary force attacking from one direction while a second force seals the opposite flank to prevent retreat:

- A "great host of hobgoblins from the north," identified as the Grentzeldek, conducted the main assault on the settlement.
- At the same time, allied goblin tribes came out of the Sorrowbog and crossed the River Tesh to the south "at some point before the 21st" and "blocked any attempt by the people of Thistle Ridge to flee."

My own suspicion is that the caravan was a third force, or maybe the hobgoblins themselves, but evidence is scant.

The odd thing is that in Malveau Greenleaf's initial report, the event is dated in a section heading as "The Tragedy — 10 Marpenoth 1441 DR" — which is 19 days later.

Aisling's journal refers to Quercina as "the dryad we drove off years ago" — my sense is that this happened when the Sembians redirected the underground river away from Harngorn's roots, thereby weakening her. The destruction of Harngorn during the attack was the final blow.

I think that's all the evidence we have regarding those dates.

[Stephen provides a full verse-by-verse annotation of Mumfrey's Song:]

VERSE 1 — THE SPIRITS:
"There are spirits spirits spirits one-two-three..."
Fairly straightforward reference to Lilac Alavandor, Lily Clarandal, and Poppy Sweetberry. The "ancient and false portrayal" was Contessa Palus's adoption of the guise of Jannath, the Mother of the Ancient Faith.

VERSE 2 — THE MAIDENS:
"There are maidens maidens maidens one-two-three..."
The Consummation was celebrated on Greengrass, 42 days after Merry Mumfrey was heard to sing this song on 19 Ches. Maybe he wrote the "ten and fifty" verse earlier in the month? 60 days after 19 Ches is Mirtul 18 — now a little over a tenday away.

VERSE 3 — THE SHADOWS:
"There are shadows shadows shadows one-two-three..."
Netheril's base is in the west, and it is "bending" towards Daggerdale. "Cast a form that stirs a mighty storm" is evocative of the Orbis Imperiosis, which commanded winds in Anauroch and darkened skies during the Saint's Battle in 1480. River (Tesh), Road (Tethyamar and Tesh Trails), Ridge (Thistle Ridge), and Sea (Moonsea?).

VERSE 4 — THE MOTHERS:
"There are mothers mothers mothers one-two-three..."
"Nothing of importance in this verse. Just good fun."

VERSE 5 — THE DEVILS:
"There are devils devils devils one-two-three..."
"No idea."

VERSE 6 — THE NANAS:
"There are nanas nanas nanas one-two-three..."
Maybe Aisling Maeliticus, Saralla Sakurth, and Contessa Palus — all grandmothers. But the granddaughters of the first two did not survive to marry.

VERSE 7 — THE MONSTERS:
"There are monsters monsters monsters one-two-three..."
"An eye that doesn't close while all the others doze" — possibly Lobelia/Louki, the ever-watching spy. "I think we all might be a monster in the old fool's dream" — the fool knows that everyone in Thistle Ridge is complicit.
