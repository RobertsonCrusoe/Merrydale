Last Session - Post-Session Appreciation and the Bugbear
by Scott Mclaren, Todd Burry, Gus Miranda, Les Blackwell
Email thread: "Fwd: Last session", 24-26 Jan 2025

Post-session thread where Scott, Todd, and Gus express appreciation for Les's DMing after a session where he apologised for it not being good enough. The players reassure him it was excellent — a near-TPK involving a creepy bugbear in a 4-level dungeon, with information asymmetry creating genuine fear. Gus's analysis of how one perception roll changed the entire encounter is a highlight.

---

SCOTT (24 Jan 2025, 8:47 PM):

Ol' Blacky I also wanted to note...
Last session you apologized a few times for it not being good enough.

Please re-evaluate, it was great.
You nearly TPK'd us in battle... Jeska.. and everyone else (I am sure) was scared shitless of your creepy bugbear...

We can only play occasionally, so a 2 session accomplishment for Chrysalis is perfect. (You could have made that 4 level dungeon way more complex.. but we would have been 2 years getting through in real time)....

Just want to say thanks for the hours and hours you put in to host this for us.

Much appreciated.

---

TODD (24 Jan 2025, 9:32 PM):

Well said and agreed 100%.

I was afraid of what would happen down there. Ser Rowan charging in felt like a massive risk. He didn't know anything that was around there but knew he had to charge Karn or else we were going to be harried. From his point of view, he was charging into a dark blind hallway.

The information asymmetry is an aspect we've never had at the table, but it was really a fun experience.

---

GUS (24 Jan 2025, 9:35 PM):

I'll double what Scun said! It was a blast and we appreciate all the work you put in!

As a fellow DM. I know how it feels to put a lot of work into an encounter only to watch the PCs completely side-step it, ignore it, or roll really well and just nail it.

If Ser Rowan had bungled his perception roll, we know we would've chased that motherfucking bugbear in the dark stumbling into grey ooze after grey ooze while he picked us off with his crossbow and the rats hampered us continuously. One fucking roll is all it took to turn a clusterfuck almost-TPK scenario into a decisive win for the PCs.

Remember that old Gonzadrel saying: Sometimes you eat the Maggot God. Sometimes the Maggot God eats you.

---

LES (26 Jan 2025, 11:29 AM):

Thanks, fellas.

I was mostly just sad and feeling sorry for myself, with the early demise of my bugbear. Glad you guys enjoyed the sesh!

More mayhem tonight!

---

Key lore points:
- The session involved a 4-level dungeon with a bugbear named Karn
- The dungeon contained grey ooze traps and rats
- Karn used a crossbow and guerrilla tactics in dark corridors
- Ser Rowan's charge into the dark hallway and a key perception roll turned a near-TPK into a win
- Information asymmetry (players not knowing what was ahead) was a new element Les introduced
- "Gonzadrel saying" — a campaign in-joke: "Sometimes you eat the Maggot God. Sometimes the Maggot God eats you."
- Sessions are infrequent ("we can only play occasionally")
