21 Tarsakh, 1483 DR – A Letter from Malveau Greenleaf to Jasper Hawkford 
Master Hawkford: 
I have received your correspondence by way of your dutiful and true friend, Brother Vessuvian.  He graciously spent the time to track me to my current whereabouts and send your correspondence to his colleagues here.   

I am currently at Castle Radiance, having given a commitment to General Thunderstorm to train and retrain as many of his archers as possible, to deal with the threat of gargoyles, which we now know to be a viable and proximal danger for our fine Dale. 

I took heart while reading your letter.  Your accomplishments are as incredible as they are unexpected.  I have learned that there are elements of our polity that did not plan for your success, and I'm happy to see you defying the odds.  News of your exploits reaches the War Room daily.  Every morning Thunderstorm reads the messages that make their way up here, and your deeds are generally known a day or two after they are done.  You will be happy to know that there are great huzzahs raised when the men hear of your burning a farm, taking a tower, and killing a commander. 

Even your former rivals have joined in your praise; Bargle seems pleased with your progress and is quite vocal in his support.  We know his interests are now tied to yours (well done, by the way), but his enthusiasm for your news is genuine.  The knights of the Lambent Sheild touch their brows when your news is received, and their Commander himself has said of Chrysalis that "they place themselves in peril to distract our enemy, and give us every chance we need to prevail.  We are greatly indebted to Chrysalis." 
Of course, the reverse of this is also true, as you well know. 
It is perilous in our West just now.  Divisions of the enemy have formed up in the Plains, and the mountain passes are plagued by gargoyle hunting packs that have made logistics very difficult and costly.  Not all the men setting out from the Falls to join us on the front arrive, for the predations of the winged terrors.  My job is to prepare our archers to face this threat. 

As such, I am unable to accept your offer, though it flatters me.  I think you can take comfort knowing that my skills are already in your ranks.  Your third and fourth squads contain capable foresters that have trained not under me directly, but under the system I put in place decades ago, and which the Sunblades have meticulously maintained all this time.  In the person of Illya Elderbough (who I understand to be a sergeant now), you have a natural forester born not only of the Dale, but of one of the last witnesses of the undead that haunt the ruin.  She is a Ranger First Class, which means I have little left to teach her. 

Of your inquiry about the Fey, I do have some intelligence you could benefit from. 

In my time, which has been long now even by immortal standards, I knew the Dryad of Thistle Ridge, Kersina.  I've known her in two forms, unfortunately.  Before I left for the wars of the East, I spent some time at Thistle Ridge before the Sembians came, and knew the Thistles and Kersina, who at the time lived in peace together.  As a dryad, she was somewhat unknowable, even for my race (or half-race, as I'm reminded by both halves often enough).  But she is something else now. 

I last met Kersina in an old mine north of the Ruin.  This was less than a year ago.  She has become something different since her cherished Oak, Harngorn, was cut and burned decades ago.  I haven't known many dryads, and only one whose tree has been destroyed, so I'm not sure how to fix her.  But she has become dark and dangerous, brooding and pitiable.  Her lair is a dangerous mix of life and despair, in the Fey fashion, and she does not suffer visitors. 

But I made it through her traps and despair, and sat with her.  I wanted to help her, but she sensed my listlessness.  "You left a long time ago, right before we needed you.  You return too late, with too little to offer, and too many other obligations besides.  You cannot help me." 

She was right, as you have already seen.  My obligations lead me far from her, and I don't expect the Fey to interpret my actions here as helping her there, though I earnestly believe they do.  I hope you do as well, and pray you find me excuse.   

You can find Kersina in the old copper mine NNE of the ruins of a Sembian lodge.  The goblins are close, but they don't bother her.  I think they are afraid of her lair, and they should be. 

If you are able to convince her that you truly intend to help, she may have help to offer.  But beware; she is not only Fey, but her mind is clouded in loss, and she will be at the very best, very difficult to interpret and help. 

You may write me here as you see fit, and I will answer when I can, but presently I am headed Fort Last Sun, and don't know when I'll return. 
Tymora and the Rest Bless You, 
MG.
