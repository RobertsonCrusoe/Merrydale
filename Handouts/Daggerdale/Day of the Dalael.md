==9 Mirtul - One Note Forward, Two Notes Back ==

On the afternoon of 9 Mirtul, Steele approached Agamemnon with a proposition. The crazy elf had arranged a meeting with Altooth Mzoul, the leader of Company Aldebarran, for the next day, and suggested that the wisest course of action would be a parlay. Perhaps, reasoned the usually unreasonable elf, further bloodshed could be avoided in this way.

Agamemnon, however, remained suspicious of his mercurial ally, and attempted to leave a trail for more trusted friends to follow, should things go poorly. He left notes for Raven, Beljuril and Rufus detailing his plans. Alas, the City does not always see fit to make things easy for its inhabitants, and Steele and Nero managed to discover these letters and intercept (through no simple means) those to Raven and Beljuril. In addition, Agamemnon's notes concerning the drow threat were also recovered with the letter to Beljuril. These notes confirmed much of what Nero had learned.

The letter to Rufus, however, arrived safely at the House of the Moon, but was never delivered to its intended recipient, for Rufus perished in Neverwinter before he could read it.

Meanwhile, oblivious to the events unfolding elsewhere in the city amongst his supposed allies, Lotharil spent the day at the Font of Knowledge reading a report by Kennyth Thames on the history of the drow influence in Waterdeep. That evening, at the Unicorn's Horn, his evening stakeout finally paid off. On a hunch, he ambushed a \"plain-looking elf\" exiting the establishment into his alley, whcih he managed without raising any alarm. His suspicions were correct - the elf was in fact a drow wearing an enchanted facemask. The drow was dressed in a grey cloak and dark blue silk body suit with a silver sash, and armed with a hand crossbow. It was also carrying a scarab brooch and a potion of some kind. Lotharil made sure the creature was dead, then stashed the body nearby and maintained his vigil until mid-morning the next day. No further drow showed themselves, however - masked or otherwise.

==10 Mirtul - With Friends Like Steele...==

According to the intercepted notes destined for Beljuril, Agamemnon was to meet this day with a man named Gettard, who had information about the drow Briza Uloavae. Whether this meeting ever occurred may never be known, for the fates had other plans.

Whether it was because he trusted the queer elf or simply in his strength to overcome any possible betrayal will never be known, but Agamemnon honoured his appointment with Steele to meet with Altooth Mzoul. As a neutral ground, Steele had chosen the pyramid abode of Dr. Gregor Kahsick. Unbeknownst to both Mzoul and the Harper wizard, however, Steele simply planned on killing both of them at once. When both mages had entered the Pyramid, he had Gregor unsummon the thing, provoking a fierce but brief spell battle between Zhent and Harper as they each tried to escape. Turning to mist, Mzoul was able to move unhindered through the numerous portcullises that blocked the exit, but the pyramid vanished into the ground with nary a trace of Agamemnon the Harper.

It is known that Gregor ceased his association with the crazed elf at that point. (At some point in the days that followed, before Steele disappeared into the Undermountain, he received a package from the good doctor. Inside were a metal box containing a large gem and a note reading \"I want nothing more to do with you. Do not touch flesh to this gem.\" What became of the gem is, like the fate of Steele himself, a mystery.)

Nero spent the rest of the day attempting to unearth more information at the Font of Knowledge, but his mind was clouded with ill premonitions of the events that had been set in motion, and he proved unable to concentrate.

==11 Mirtul - The Margaster Shipping Offices==

The next day, Nero arranged for a member of the Fellowship of Carters and Coachmen to transport the body of the drow recovered by Lotharil two days earlier to Gregor Kahsick, a \"medical man\" who, it was alleged, was able to interrogate creatures even beyond death. Unfortunately, the good doctor regretfully reported that owing to recent over-exertion, he wouldn’t be able to do anything like that for at least a ride (ten days).

Nero and Steele then moved on to the the shipping offices of House Margaster, where they witnessed a meeting between Quellar Dalael and Thentias Margaster. Sabrel Lightstar and Captain Griswold Horn were also in attendance, seemingly as associates of the drow, while Thentias seemed backed by a dangerous-looking (but nevertheless stunningly attractive) half-elven woman. Quellar had been sent to bring House Margaster back into the Dalael fold, but the meeting went foul when Steele and Nero decided to make a move and attack. When the battle was joined, that half-elf came in on the side of Nero and Steele, and with her blades, bow and - apparently poisoned - arrows, she proved herself every bit the equal of the heroes. 

Though Quellar escaped, several drow were slain, as were Horn and Lightstar, depriving the Dalael of valuable contacts within the City Watch.

== 12 Mirtul - Meetings and Farewells ==
=== The Irlingstar Treaty === 

Nero joined the elven contingent of Steele, Lotharil and Llianin for their now regular Highsunfeast at Felzoun’s Folly. During the meal, Nero was approached by a young man named Dulland, who passed on an invitation from his master for the foursome to attend a festive meeting of Company Aldebarran that evening at the Irlingstar Villa. Nero, it seemed, had been expecting this and accepted on behalf of the party.

At the villa, Nero introduced his elven companions to Kendal Thorne, the director of the company. The elves were somewhat surprised to learn that Nero has brokered a deal with Thorne to join forces against the Dalael. Lotharil, for one, had few qualms, but Steele seemed ambivalent. The bug-eyed elf acted strangely (or more strangely than usual, at least) throughout the meal - at times inappropriately fervent, at others haggard and withdrawn. Llianin was ready to refuse, but when she mentioned her quest for the Encyclopaedae Ocularis Indomitus, Thorne surprised her by spinning a tale about the very tome she sought.

The Encyclopedae, he related, was a Netherese codex written by a wizard named Aesclepius of Wreathe. He did not have much information on what the ancient book contained, but did know that Jannaxil Serpentil and Elaith “The Serpent” Craulnober, along with a band of ‘unremarkable-looking elves’, had recently left the city to seek the book at the behest of Company Dalael. One task for the new alliance, Thorne said, must be to secure this book, and prevent the drow from obtaining it. Thorne further indicated that Llianin that he had no opposition to her taking possession of the book for her masters in Halruaa, in the event that she should offer her services to Aldebarran.

In the end, while none were happy with Nero's decision - or his presumptiveness - the alliance was sworn, if only to further the elves' various personal agendas.

Other members of the Aldebarran organization present at this meeting were:
*Welland of Hillsfar, the company ledger-keeper.
*Clavius of Amn, who was introduced as a distant relation of Thorne’s.
*Dulland, Thorne’s personal factotum, who served drinks to those present
*Maryland, a member of the Fellowship of Carters and Coachmen
*Feldon Azadand
*Venom, the attractive and well-armed half-elven woman from the Margaster Offices. She seemed to be Thorne’s bodyguard for the evening.

While this fateful alliance was being struck, a mysterious figure stole into a tower that was an outbuilding within the Irlingstar villa complex, where an observatory had been set up, complete with a large gnomish perspicillum (a telescope). As the figure made its towards a collection of papers, charts and books scattered throughout the room, a calm voice asked it what it was doing there. The figure quickly turned to find a beholder-like creature watching from behind a stairwell. In the same disarmingly peaceful voice, the creature warned that \"a presence\" was approaching that would not take kindly to the figure's being where it didn't belong. The figure quickly leaped up to exit through the window it had entered by. Ad it did so, another, sharper voice called out, \"Go - and never come back\". Strangely, this voice seemed to come as if from nowhere.

=== Exeunt Teskul, Stage Left ===

After the weed and whiskey filled meeting with Aldebarran, Nero and the elves decided to retire to The Copper Cup to discuss the alliance, and to finally seek some answers from Bladetail, who had been avoiding them since the Zulpair fiasco. However, they were careful to take precautions in case they were followed. Llianin and Lotharil headed for the Copper Cup, while Nero, disguised as a drunken, bumbling fop, trailed behind in hopes of catching anyone tailing the others. Steele, meanwhile, took an alternate route alone. 

As it turned out, there was a tail - but Nero wasn't to be the one to discover it. En route to the Copper Cup, the sudden sound of commotion from an alleyway they had passed only moments before brought all three running. They stopped short at the sight of the pale, grey-clad figure of a man, lying sprawled on the cobblestones. Except for this figure, the City streets were eerily deserted, only the distant howl of a hound breaking the silence. Nero recognized the grey-clad figure as that of a man who had been tailing him a few days earlier. Not losing a moment to chance, he frisked the fellow, pocketing several items including a sturdy-looking knife, then he and Lotharil bound and gagged the man and prepared to bring him to Rychel.

As if on cue, however, the party was approached by the whistling figure of Magistrate Vader, who demanded to know what was going on. When briefed on the situation, Vader took one look at the prisoner's face then barked a laugh of surprise. He identified the figure as the rogue Teskul and demanded custody of the man, whom he'd been trying to get his hands on for some time, he revealed. Nero and Lotharil were not going to let this man out of their sight, however, and accompanied Vader to a building on Ship Street (south of DW 15), where the body was handed over to a grizzled old thief. Vader refused to allow the others any further contact until he had talked to Thorne, at which point Nero and Lotharil left to get Steele (intending to return in force).

Llianin, meanwhile, had seemingly vanished. In fact, she had tailed the others to the safehouse in secrecy. She waited until Vader and her allies had left before knocking on the door of the house. Her natural allure magnified by strong enchantments, Llianin managed to convince the old rogue to turn over the body and forget that she had ever been there, then dragged Teskul out into the streets. Before long, however, the frail elf was accosted by a gang of seedy Dockward toughs, and things went quite poorly. The dangerous streets of Waterdeep claimed Teskul's body and another elven purse that night. 

Teskul was not seen again on the streets of Waterdeep by any of the characters in this story, but rumours of the man abound. The most popular of these places him on the streets of Calimshan, doing business as usual.

=== Exeunt Esperel, Stage Right ===

Lotharil and Nero, having found Steele, were frustrated to find Teskul gone. The three began to suspect Bladetail of having put Teskul onto their scent, and Nero and Steele resolved to confront the crazy elf to settle things once and for all. Lotharil told the others to do as they would - he had drow to hunt - and took his leave. In fact, however, the drow-hunter quickly made his way quickly to the Copper Cup, where he found his paranoid relative nursing an elvenquist in a shadowy corner. Lotharil calmly explained his companions' suspicions and indicated that, were he to find out that these suspicions be true, blood-ties would be meaningless and he would kill Esperel himself. He then departed, and made his way to the Unicorn’s horn, where, at four bells, he witnessed the departure of a strange-looking elf, wearing a wide-brimmed black hat - somehow, however, the drow disappeared before Lotharil could act.

Perhaps Lotharil's warning to Bladetail put the old Red Herring on edge more than usual. In any case, Esperel's role in this tale appears to have ended that night. Just as his cousin had warned, Steele and Nero came to the Copper Cup not long after he left. Harsh words led to drawn blades, and Esperel jumped out a window (which he had purposely positioned himself close to) and fled, despite the best efforts of Nero and Steele to follow. Some well placed informants later revealed that, of the elf's known hideouts and safehouses, all had been packed up and deserted. Esperel the elf was gone.

Late that night, fire and violence exploded in areas around the city. It would seem that elements of Company Dalael attacked many Aldebarran interests - perhaps in retaliation for what happened at the Margaster warehouse.

== 13 Mirtul - Kidnapped ==

At dawn, Steele, Nero and Lotharil were summoned to the Irlingstar villa by Kendal Thorne, and they found the villa in a state of high alert, and the wizard himself in an ill temper. Thorne was holding council with the half-elf Venom and a gruesome-looking fellow he introduced as the Huntmaster, a scarred, hairy brute with the mark of Malar branded on his cheek. Upon their arrival, he presented them with letter he had received that morning from a messenger sent from the Anteos villa.

A per the letter's instruction (sarcasm notwithstanding), Thorne sent Steele to the Anteos Villa, where he came face to face with a veritable rogue's gallery. Along with the Board of Directors of Company Dalael - including the brothers of that House - Steele met the drowess whom Agamemnon had been trying to prevent from reaching an agreement with House Dalael, Briza Uloavae. Quevin outlined the terms he was prepared to come to with Aldebarran - namely that the girl would not be hurt if Thorne takes no further action against the Dalael, but that he also intended to keep her as surety until Highharvestide, after which time she would be released. As he dismissed Steele, Quevin invited his old enemy to join his Company. Steele said that he would give the offer his consideration, and took his leave. Returning to the Irlingstar villa, Steele related his story to the still-furious Thorne, who immediately began arranging for a mission against the Dalael.

Thorne, a talented mage, did a great deal of scrying on the Anteos Villa and the Dalael operation. He was unable to learn much, but he did learn that Mhairne was being held captive in a prison beneath the Anteos Villa. This prison was located on the first layer of Undermountain - the passage from the Villa and into Undermountain is apparently the same passage used by the Dalael to reach the Underdark, and head to Menzoberanzan. For many reasons, Thorne wanted this investigated. But most of all, he wanted the girl back.

Why he was so interested in the fate of one little girl, few could speculate.

==14 Mirtul - Short Cuts Make for Long Delays==

On Thorne's isntructions, Lotharil, Steele, and Nero - joined by Huntmaster and Venom (members, it was finally learned, of the Blades of Korombos) convened the next morning at the Yawning Portal, where they met a man with greasy hair and a pockmarked face. Thorne had told them to seek out this man, Kogan, who was a purveyor of rare (and sometimes ill-gotten) charts and maps. They had been told to ask after a map that referred to \"the Ebon Blade\", which, with a sinister smile, Kogan quickly found in a scroll case he pulled from a dirty sleeve. He sold another scroll to the heroes, wished them luck, and turned back to his whiskey, chuckling.

Thus prepared, the heroes paid their gold piece to be lowered into through the torch-lit opening of the large open-topped well in the centre of the bar, into the darkness below - the Entry Chamber of Undermountain. The map, alas, led the ragtag band of heroes and mercenaries on a doomed journey to catastrophe by way of disaster - helmed horrors, wereboars and a mysterious glyphed sarcophagus containing gods know who (or what) were only a few of the encounters along the way that supposedly led to the Anteos villa.

Sometime during the evening of the first day, Venom took fright and fled from the group, seemingly ensorcelled. She was never seen again.

==15 Mirtul - Betrayal ==

At length, the spelunkers found their way to the deepest portions of the Anteos basements, where they could hear the sibilant voices of the drow taunting them as they approached. Though they managed to overcome an impressive number of drow footsoldiers, they were entirely spent when they came to the final room that lay between them and the entrance to the villa itself.

What happened then was related to Garth Shanks months later, when he met with Nero on Rooken business. According to Nero, he had his companions had almost reached the villa basement when Quevin and Quellar came into the dungeon to attend to the spelunkers' incursion personally. Though the brothers were a mighty force, the battle was only decided when Steele suddenly turned on his companions in mid-battle! He lashed out at the Huntmaster, who managed to get away, but as Lotharil tried to adjust to this turn of events, he was struck down - bleeding but not, thought Nero, dead. 

At that point, Nero ran. As he made his way back into the dark halls of the Undermountain, he heard Quevin's voice ring out to Steele \"You made the right choice, friend.\"

==29 Mirtul - Last Man Standing ==

Nero emerged alone at the Yawning Portal half a month later. It known at the Portal that we was a wreck when he emerged, in a state of near-catatonia. He vanished into the City after that, and was not heard from for some time...
