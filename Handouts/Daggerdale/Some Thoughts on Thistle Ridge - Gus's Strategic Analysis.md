Some Thoughts on Thistle Ridge - Gus's Strategic Analysis
by Gus Miranda (as Jasper Hawkford)
Email thread: "Some thoughts on Thistle Ridge", 5 Jun 2024

A substantial analytical email from Gus written while procrastinating from a move, re-reading the campaign handouts and working through the mysteries of Thistle Ridge. Covers the competition's financial logic (or lack thereof), strategic priorities, key NPCs to find, and a detailed line-by-line analysis of Merri Mumfrey's prophetic song. Single message, no replies.

---

I'm sorry you're getting a bunch of emails from me. The movers are coming on Monday, and I fly out a week from today - so I'm procrastinating at full speed right now. And what better way to procrastinate, than by focusing on D&D?

I went back and re-read some of the original handouts from the start of the adventure, and I have some thoughts:


First three thoughts:

1. I think we are being set up to fail. I don't think we can realistically fulfill the terms of the contract within the 20 months given.

2. What happens if we do fail? Does the runner-up get to have a go? Is that why the Red Jacks don't seem to give a fuck? Because they already know they will get to take over as soon as we fail? I know this is conspiratorial, but something isn't quite adding up. There's a lot of money being thrown at us and a lot of talk of what is going to happen when we succeed, but not a single word about what happens to Thistle Ridge should we fail by the deadline imposed.

3. I still don't get why they're not marching the Sunblades out to do this themselves. They've mustered the platoon already, and given the command to Forge Farmountain, who seems capable. What can 5 adventurers do that a full platoon of Sunblades can't? And the dude from Castle Radiance is sending messages down everyday saying the contest should be cancelled and the Sunblades deployed to the east - yet the Adjudicating Committee continues to purposely ignore that advice.

So in terms of the competition as a whole, I think that our resident Investigator should put on his best deerstalker hat and try to find out what's really going on here. There has to be deeper motivations at play than what we are being led to believe. Or maybe I'm just paranoid. But I just can't help but feel like something's not quite right here, especially given the huge sums of money that are being tossed around.

To wit: in the final year of its accounting, 1439 DR, Thistle Ridge contributed 4,000gp to the Dale's coffers in taxes. That was at its height of production. The Adjudicating Committee has spent more than that on this contest alone, and even if we succeed by the deadline set of 20 months from now, Thistle Ridge will not be producing anywhere near enough to pay back the cost of this contest and the future subsidies. Of course the argument is that this is a "long-term" investment, yet we have a 20-month deadline to build fortifications, establish a self-sufficient colony, build infrastructure, dredge a harbour, and establish trade routes? That's a tall order for 5 people to do in 20 months. It's a tall order for 50 people to do.

I understand Ferguson's motivation. His investment makes sense to me. But not the Adjudicating Committee's.


NEXT!

1. I think of primary importance is to find Malveau Greenleaf and speak with her, and better yet, to hire her as our guide to take us to Thistle Ridge. She has been there most recently, and will be the best person to guide us in quietly so we don't just stumble in and stir up the goblin nest. After all, our job here is just to do reconnaissance and gather intelligence, not to fight.

2. The absolute BEST scenario would be to have Malveau Greenleaf help us sneak into Thistle Ridge, and we capture their leader, the priestess Charrah. Going straight for the head - a head that we can interrogate - would be perfect! Of course, that's much easier said than done, and I don't think that can be achieved on our first visit to the site. But it should be the ultimate goal.

3. We need to talk/interrogate Invictus. Not only was he present at the time of the tragedy, but he is also currently the Magistrate in charge of accessing the forbidden wing of the library. He literally knows more than he is letting on. And we need access to that forbidden wing.


NEXT!

I am reminded that much of what we have is epistolary, and filtered through a rather censorious Church of Amaunator. I think we need to find the authors:

- Malveau Greenleaf
- Blaze Whitegate
- Zoderick Winters
- Brindelle Oakbough

I think we need to speak with them and see how much of their "account" is accurate, and what was edited by the Amaunatori to serve their purposes.

I also think we need to gather some allies and think about how to best position them going forward:

The FDL: these guys should definitely come on board of our team. They should come with us when we go do our reconnaissance, and we should leave them behind to establish our forward base of operations. We should get the Dwarf and the Alchemist on the payroll, to help kit us out with gear, and the Paladin and Ghost Hunter for extra protection when the time comes to deal with the undead. Not sure of the Archeologist's utility in the early stages, but we can definitely set him to work once we are at the excavation stage.

The White Cloaks: say what you will about my BFF, but Alexandus would be a good ally to have as well. Jasper's good for a lot of tricks, but we're going to want Alexandus on our team when the time comes to throw fireballs around. We also need Zellia Oakbough and the Dale boys on our team to help bolster the defences of the town once we have it. Tara Hilltop would be great, but I'm not liking the idea of a religious nut - especially from the Church of Amaunator, getting a foothold into our town.


Also:

I still can't get Merri Mumfrey's song out of my thoughts...

"There are spirits spirits spirits one-two-three;"

We believe we know that there are three separate buildings with "spirits" at Thistle Ridge.

"Small but great in wrath and sad betrayal;"

We also now believe that at least one of these is a Sweetberry halfling: "small." And that they are "great in wrath and sad betrayal" - I think this is where we must begin our investigations: with Janus Sweetberry, proprietress of the Oak and Shield Inn. I think this is our first ghost.

I am not a Ritualist mage, but Nemia seems to be, and I am willing to learn. She may prove useful at reaching out and finding out more.

"Their tragic story is the key, to set them all and others free"

Well, that's pretty much what we believe. At least Jasper's plan is to set them free.

"And expose for good an ancient false portrayal."

Not quite there with this line yet, as I'm not sure what the "ancient false portrayal" is. But we will find out once we get there, I'm sure.

"There are shadows shadows shadows one-two-three;"

This can either be about the shadow princes we already know about; or it could be about the three shadows in Thistle Ridge. I am leaning more towards specific shadows in Thistle Ridge, because:

"They are strongest in the west and bend to thee"

This could mean literally that they are strongest in Netheril to the west of here. But I believe this is about the shadows in Thistle Ridge, and it refers to the three locations: Oak and Shield (East), the Town Hall (Centre) and the Academy (West). The most powerful of the shadows is in the Academy - and therefore the one we must avoid until we are strong enough to deal with it.

"These shadows cast a form that stirs a mighty storm, That darkens river, ridge and road and sea."

One way or the other, this is the main antagonist, and I think this is telling us that we will never succeed in Thistle Ridge until we have purged the shadows from the ruins. The mighty storm is likely the goblin horde that is building up strength there. Dealing with those shadows is the key to liberating Thistle Ridge.

The bits about the devils and the monsters I'm not quite getting much yet, but that's because this is early days. Knowledge will come with time, and a deeper understanding of what we are facing once we get there. But there is something there in this song and in the number three, as we have found out that Maglubiyet's faithful won't wage war until they have all three: goblins, hobgoblins and bugbears assembled in a horde.

More thoughts to come, I fear. More procrastination to do and I want to re-read the Ferguson document.
