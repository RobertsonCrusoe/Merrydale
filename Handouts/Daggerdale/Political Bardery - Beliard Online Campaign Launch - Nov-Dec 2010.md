PART 3 — Stephen Robertson to Les Blackwell, 3 November 2010 at 20:33

Get your google on, Blacky.

Rauthauvyr the Raven was the founder of Sembia - and is the sainted hero of the secret network my character is a part of. The coat of arms of Sembia was a raven (symbol of Rauthauvyr) and silver coins (the merchants he put into power), hence the Society of the Silver Ravens, which aims to destabilize Netheril, Cormyr and the Dales enough that Sembia can be free and re-established as a regional hegemon. Unlike the Sembia of old, however, these guys want a new republic, not an oligarchy of merchants.

You can re-read my other e-mail for more background on Beliard, but rest assured I do want to use him. His code name is Nycticorax (which if you've got any Greek means "night raven"), but this is known to few outside Saerloon. I'll have to get your help on the character creation (not sure if you loaded that file I sent you into your DNDmatic), but I figure the cunning bard works well for an adventurer-spy-politico. As for morality, he defines good as "good for Sembia", and evil as "bad for Sembia" and his job is to do what needs to be done in the name of State. He's savvy enough to play the long game, though - I think of him as more of a Machiavellian than a nineteenth century romantic nationalist - and doesn't take unnecessary or foolish risks unless the potential payoff is worth it.

I don't expect that the Sembian angle will be much of your campaign, but I thought it would be fun to play a bit with the wider regional politics. Also, it's up to you to interpret exactly what significance the "year of silent shadows" is - I'd like it to have the effects I mentioned earlier, ie. giving bonuses against shadow magic or whatever - but Beliard is a bonafide shadow hater. It could just be, however, that the miasma was a strategic move by Netheril to taint the children born in that year, for later advantage of the Netherese, or it might be, as most believe it to be, an unintended immunity. Who knows for sure?

Motivations:

Right now I'm happy for his "secret" orders (as a Sembian patriot) to be as follows:

1) Get to Castle Radiance, and gather any intelligence you can on the relative strengths and weaknesses of the interests involved

2) If war is likely to break out, do what you can to make it happen. War among our enemies is good for us and good for business.

As far as his "official" orders (as a Cormyrean diplomatic attache):

1) Get to Castle Radiance and parlay your knowledge of Netheril and the shadow etc. into getting Cormyr a stronger position within Castle Radiance
2) Facilitate communication between Cormyreans and Daggerdale

Let me know if you need any more info to flesh him out.

Stephen

PART 9 — Les Blackwell to Stephen Robertson, 27 December 2010 at 04:46

I have two proposed 'builds' for Beliard, below. They both fit a different role.

1. Controller. This is an unusual controller build, in that it is a melee-build. Lots of your abilities are based off of a long-sword attack. He has skillsy-feats that will make him dominate the skill-end of the campaign.

====== Created Using Wizards of the Coast D&D Character Builder ======
Beliard - Controller, level 1
Human, Bard
Bardic Virtue: Virtue of Cunning
Human Power Selection: Bonus At-Will Power
Background: Occupation - Infiltrator (Learn Netherese)
FINAL ABILITY SCORES
Str 10, Con 14, Dex 10, Int 14, Wis 14, Cha 16.
AC: 17 Fort: 13 Reflex: 15 Will: 15
HP: 26 Surges: 9 Surge Value: 6
TRAINED SKILLS
Perception +7, Arcana +7, Streetwise +8, Insight +7, Bluff +8, Diplomacy +8
FEATS
Bard: Ritual Caster
Human: Friendly Deception
Level 1: Bard of All Trades
POWERS
Bard at-will 1: Vicious Mockery
Bard at-will 1: War Song Strike
Bonus At-Will Power: Guiding Strike
Bard encounter 1: Blunder
Bard daily 1: Slayer's Song
ITEMS
Ritual Book, Chainmail, Light Shield, Longsword

2. Healer. Well, this guy is what we'd call 2/3's of a healer. Bards can only be B+ healers at best, but this guy has an interesting trick; he grants lots of temporary hit points, which helps a party stay on it's feet longer both in single combats and over the course of a day of fighting (he saves them healing surges, which are the ultimate limiting factor for how long a party can fight in a day). His 'daily' power allows people to 'regain' hitpoints during an encounter, which again is better than 'healing' damage, which takes up healing surges. This guy is basically an inspiring leader who can keep the troops on their feet all day long.

There you have it. I have both of these guys fighting with a longsword. You can also make bards that fight with ranged weapons really well, if you want. You just have to fiddle with the powers a bit. The ranged guys tend to offer 'buffs' and 'debuffs' applenty. They make your foes easier to hit, and make your allies hit harder. This makes them B+ controllers and B+ strikers.
