In the eighteen months of his posting to the Cormyrean embassy at Castle Radiance, Beliard Emmarask has made many friends, and few enemies of note. Since his arrival, he has become an invaluable assistant to the corpulent ambassador, John Tunbridge, and acts as secretary and emissary of the ambassador in all but the most important affairs.
When not in the embassy offices or on official assignment (he spent three months at Base Camp Silver as the Cormyrean Envoy to the Silver Council), he is often found in the Tethyamar Hearth Inn, where he entertains of an evening once or twice a week, and keeps an open eye and ear for strangers and news from the world beyond the Dale. He is quite sociable, but should anyone pause to reflect on his character, they realize that the man gives little of himself away. In fact, upon further reflection, Beliard seems to be very good at deflecting the focus of any conversation onto either his interlocutor, or some other topic, such as a mutual acquaintance. Rarely, if ever, can anyone claim to have found out much about the man himself, or his thoughts. Obviously, he is a loyal supporter of the Crown of Cormyr - he is its eyes and ears in this hinterland after all - but beyond what is known of his family (servants of the crown for centuries), Beliad is simply "that bard from Cormyr". The one thing that is unmistakable, however, is his hatred of Netheril and all things Shadow-wrought. Only when such topics come up does an intense anger seem to seethe within him, giving some small window into his private self.
He is a voracious reader, and can often be found perusing tomes and correspondence in many languages through a fine monocle attached by a chain to his person.
In combat, he has acquitted himself several times with a longsword, demonstrating a finesse that comes with having learned fencing as a sport, rather than through the necessity of combat. He is more agile than powerful, and maintains a steady banter of mockery during his encounters, enraging his victims to the extent that they lose control. He is not a trained warrior, however, and violence is always a last resort.
He does not know Gale personally, but will be seeking him out in due course.
