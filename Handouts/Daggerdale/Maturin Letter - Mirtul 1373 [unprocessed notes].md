Mirtul 8, 1373 - Morning
There can be no question of the continuation of this noble mission. I have pledged to see Boregar and Dorian to the end of their appointed tasks and ensure that the Evil that brought the Dales, the Desert and the North to their knees does not succeed in the completion of its foul enterprise. I believe that this Evil, or at least those who have allied themselves with it, are also responsible for the extermination of the Harpers that Beljuril has vowed to end. The assassins Malkor and Venisar each can be linked to this man Thagdul, and it may be that the threat to Those Who Harp is not yet at an end.

I have realized that the answer to the riddle of the Beholder tablets may indeed lie with the drow. Garth, the note you recovered from the drow so long ago indicates that Quellar, through secret means, learned the mark of Zantiid. If we can bring this dark elf to justice, then perhaps we may learn more of the aims of the Beholders. The bookmonger Serpentil, at the behest of the Dalael, has succeeded in what must have been a perilous mission to recover the Encyclopedia Indomitus Ocularis that Kennyth has told us of. Why would the Dalael undertake this if they knew nothing of what the beholders seek?

But the Dalael cannot be trusted to help our cause. It was their machinations that led to the theft of the Phaulkon cargo and the death of Jeryth

s father. With Ersgul, they sent children to the Underdark as slaves. They partnered themselves with the fell cult of the Baneson and participated in its horrid rites. Having knowledge of such injustice, I can not turn a blind eye, but must declare myself their enemy and face what comes.

We must act, but we must not act in ignorance, lest we lose what might be learned of the Beholder threat.
The first mystery I would look into would be of the man known as Teskul.
As Raven, Boregar and I made our way towards Anauroch last year, we clashed with an order known as the Ebon Spur. These men possessed a note, unsigned, that was dated the 2nd of Mirtul - not two days after the Gathering at Phaulkonmere when the three parties were joined. There was a spy in our midst that day, and I believe we need look no farther than Jafar, who has played his hand with the Zhents.
The Rooken have informed you that Sal Worthingham, who you believe to the man
Teskul
, offered the bounty sought by Sherriff Grimwald. I have noted that, Grimwald's men who followed Beljuril and Rufus north worked in concert with Malkor's crony, Atemnon. Further, I have noted that Rychel's interrogation of Yarkul and Dragon revealed that Sal Worthingham purported to be from Luskan, Malkor

s one-time haven, and the origin of the evil blades that Drurginz once thought to ship in boxes of sugar cane. To me, this is more than coincidence.

If Teskul sought to stop our mission, perhaps it was this same villain who sent the message to Cauldrawn and Duncan Eldoun of the Ebon Spur. Perhaps Jafar gave him the information that he used to send these foes against us.
Does Teskul/Sal Worthingham feature in Aldebaran's records? Can he be found? Company Dalael, it can be suspected, would likely have sought out the connections it employed when it was allied with Company Ersgul, and they may have taken Sal Worthingham as an ally. If the man was also on the payroll of Aldebaran, his loyalties are a weakness in the structure of Dalael

s organization. Regardless of this though, he is responsible for the deaths of Mourn Raventree, a noble soul, and my old friend Hanibal Felthian. Justice is due.

The second mystery that concerns me is the doom that befell our colleagues here in Waterdeep last summer. Nero

s version of these events did not make clear to me why he and Steele took the Huntmaster as an ally. Does Nero's company feature in the Aldebaran ledger? Is there something he isn't telling us? I intend to talk to him about these events. Still, he fought the Dalael and escaped. His counsel is needed.

Thirdly, we must learn the weaknesses of the drow. What noble houses are their enemies, and which their friends? Egan tells me that the Umbrusks have no love for Quevin

s kin, and the friends of Phaulkon may yet bring justice to that fallen family. Garth, though I do not make your business my own, you are an expert in the networks that fill this city, and it is to you that this task may fall. Dalael must not fill the void left in Aldebaran's wake.
Finally, some thought should be given to the question of the Beholders. I believe that the beholder that we contacted through the staff was actually Xulla, since it appeared to believe initially that Jafar was trying to contact it. Secondly, it knew the name Istolil Dalael, which was bait that led Heroes into an Ersgul trap at a time when the Dalael were an Ersgul interest. To me this either means that Xulla reports irrelevant information to the other beholder that Beljuril spoke with, or this was Xulla himself. Thirdly, I believe that Jafar is acting as an agent for Thagdul, and thus for Zantiid - not Xulla. Fourthly, Xulla would have more cause than most to threaten the Dalael after their break with it.

This last puzzle is still an enigma, and may prove to be irrelevant to the need at hand. Nonetheless, it bears some thought.
