Falcon's Roost has been around for nearly six centuries although it has only been referred to as Falcon's Roost since 1375 DR, when it was renamed following the third known battle with the orc army known as the Red Hand of Doom.

==Geography and Early History==

It was initially little more than a way-point at which for the dwarves of the Graypeaks to set up camp and take stock of supplies before setting out on their journeys either into the southern lowlands or deeper into the mountains.

There are a number of ways out of the Graypeaks into the southern lowlands, but the easiest and certainly the most easily traveled is Helgorod Pass. A naturally occurring valley between the mountains known as Nevertop and Grimnurr, it is two hundred feet at its widest point at the north western end of the valley, and shrinks down to as narrow as sixty feet across. At one of the most narrow points is Falcon's Roost, originally no more than an oft-used camping area for travelers into and out of the mountains.

The site became more established when a dwarf by the name of Nedard Murtaw set up a crude inn and tavern of sorts. He certainly didn't get rich doing so, but he was the talkative sort, and loved conversing with all kinds of people as much as he loved the Graypeaks. It was said that Nedard Murtaw had been an adventurer in his younger years and ventured east into Anauroch and the ruins of the old Netherese Empire. When he came back, he had no taste for adventure but more coin than he knew what to do with, so throwing it away in a risky business venture was not a worry. It must be admitted that he was an unusual dwarf.

It was not until after the first recorded assault by savage orcs referring to themselves as the Red Hand of Doom, who stormed through the Pass in a raid on the southern lowlands, that the resident ruling family in the dwarven settlement of Andurham beneath the Graypeaks decided that the Pass was too inviting a doorway for the less than pleasant residents of the Graypeaks. In memory of Nedard Murtaw,  rumored to have fought the overwhelming army solely on his own in defense of his self-made settlement, the construction of a crude fortress was initiated, and in time came to be known as Murtaw's Point.

==Murtaw's Point==

Murtaw's Point became infamous for being the end of the line for citizens exiled from nearby settlements, whether dwarven or human. Those found guilty of crimes would be escorted to Murtaw's Point, where they were sentenced to live out their days as defenders of the lowlands and the Graypeaks. There was never a question of whether, or how, these defenders would perish - death at the hands of a ravening orc horde was virtually assured, given time, and there were always bets being placed by the men and dwarves there. Small fortunes changed hands on the rare occasions someone was crushed by a giant's club, as opposed to being stabbed by an orcish spear or blade.

It seemed that, given the choice between facing the headsman's axe or the hangman’s noose, spending one’s days at Murtaw's Point looked quite appealing to some - even if their count be cut the shorter, it was certainly preferable to none. As little as the men and dwarves understood each other's actions or intentions at times, the pairing of the two races has seemed to work effectively, and the settlement has stood in various states of construction for six centuries. It has survived dragons, giants, three known sieges by the Red Hand of Doom and countless other attacks unrecorded by diligent priests or scholars.

==Structure==

Structurally, Falcon's Roost has had a number of different layouts which over time have been destroyed or damaged beyond use by battles. There is only one structure that survives from the original work done by the dwarves in the initial construction, which is the underground barracks.

===The Barracks===

The barracks were carved out of the stone beneath the surface of the valley and have withstood countless assaults. Initially built by dwarves for dwarves, They are laid out simply enough, with a large area where food can be prepared, a large storage area for supplies, a smithy, and sleeping quarters to accommodate two hundred dwarves or men. Over time (close to two centuries) as more and more humans started to show up, the dwarves relented and increased the height of the living quarters where they could as well as carved out an alternative sleeping quarters for humans as well as a hall (dubbed the small hall). The new quarters are indeed taller and less "oppressive" than the dwarven side and roughly half as large, able to accommodate half of the bodies that the original quarters can.

===Main Wall===

Beyond the underground barracks, the only other permanent feature is the wall. The wall is almost always in a constant state of repair and as much as it has been knocked down a number of times, the defenders generally manage to prevail and protect the lowlands from those that wish it harm.

==Falcon's Roost - Modern Structures==

With the ambition and significant coin of Arkos "Falcon" Thunderstaff, Murtaw's Point saw a great deal of renovation and reconstruction. Now dubbed Falcon's Roost, the wall, towers and supporting buildings on the southern side of the wall have never been in better condition. Even a century after the death of the Falcon at the Monastery of Sundered Faith, coin and supplies still come to the 'Roost each month from investments made by Thunderstaff and his estate.

===Main Wall Today===

The main wall of Falcon's Roost is twenty-five feet thick of stone, with the gate consisting of large, iron bars that run vertically and horizontally in a cross hatch pattern. The bars themselves are thicker than a large man's wrist and soldiers at the 'Roost like to claim that they are unbreakable. Others have claimed this before them, but such opinions change when when boulders thrown by furious mountain giants come crashing against them. There are three-foot spikes set along the entire height and width of the gate that point out and defend the northern side of the gate, with the odd one bent where something massive has collided with it, and thus the north side of the gate at Falcon's Roost is an imposing structure to assault.

===Tremalkin Tower===

The western tower situated flush against the cavern wall is called "Tremalkin" and is eighty feet high, square and sturdily build. It houses a crude prison of sorts as well as some spartan living quarters and room for storage as well as the top of the tower is used for storing material and supplies associated with the trebuchets atop the span.

===Thunderstaff Tower===

The eastern tower is a mirror image of the western tower and is dubbed Thunderstaff. It houses more supplies as well as the living quarters for the officers that are staying there. Contrary to custom, where the ranking officer is situated high in the tower, Captain Maeghuen Grimbold insists on having her sleeping quarters on the ground floor, closest to her soldiers and the fight, should it occur. The lower ranked officers are housed up near the top of Thunderstaff tower.

===Horgath-on-the-Wing===

The "span" is the name for the top of the wall and stretches between both towers in an arch. At its lowest, it is sixty-five feet high and eighty at the apex of the arch in the middle. Both the north and south sides of the arch have been elaborately carved in the form of an enormous roc, its spread wingspan stretching from Tremalkin tower to Thunderstaff tower. At the middle of the span is the roc's body and riding atop it with a sword thrust into the air is a carved statue of Maturin Horgath. Just as imposing as the steel spikes on the north gate, the stonework on both sides of the arch is known to stop a charging enemy right in its tracks with its impressive scale and life-like quality.

===The Whores===

Spread out across the top of the span are three well-cared for trebuchets, affectionately known as "the whores" stemming from the lusty way in which they greet all "visitors" coming from the northern side of the wall.
