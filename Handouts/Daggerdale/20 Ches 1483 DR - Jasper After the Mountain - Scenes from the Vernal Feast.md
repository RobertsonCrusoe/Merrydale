by Gus Miranda (as Jasper Hawkford) with Todd Burry (as Mari)
Email threads: "A small scene after the morning's competition" (29 Feb - 1 Mar 2024) and "Continuing on with some scenes from the aftermath of the competition" (2-3 Mar 2024)

Three vignettes depicting the aftermath of the first round of the Thistle Ridge Competition (Capture the Flag on the Mountain, 20 Ches 1483 DR). Jasper is in the Chrysalis tent after their victory over the White Cloaks.

---

SCENE 1: CHESTER AND ABIGAIL VISIT THE TENT

"By Tymora's cunt hairs you're still alive, you blithering arsehole!" At the entrance of the Team Chrysalis tent stood a Sunblade in uniform addressing Jasper who sat on an empty barrel, still in his bloodied shirt, while Mari gently tended to the burns around his face.

And just as Mari was about to reproach the newcomer for his obscene language, three young children burst into the tent past him and straight onto the wizard.

"I knew you could do it!" said one.

"I wanna see the monkey!" said another.

"… and then he was like WOW!  And then she was shootin' arrows at the pretty elf but the pretty elf just kept going!  And then the other wizard went BRAAA!!!  and then there was fire everywhere…". Said yet another.

"I want the monkey!"

"What was your friend doing with the flag, Uncle Jas?  I thought he was just waving it around but Mum put her hand over our eyes and said it wasn't for decent eyes…"

"… and then he was POW! SLASH! and then you were dead!  But not really dead, dead, but still dead!  And…"

"THE MONKEY!!!"

"Chauntea preserve us!  Leave your uncle alone you gaggle of terrible brats!" said the children's mother as she moved past her husband, still at the entrance, and into the tent.  With a quick, approving glance at Mari and her handiwork, she continued, "Well, at least they're looking out for you properly here! Thank you madam for healing our Jasper!  I'm this one's sister-in-law," she said as she put her hand protectively on the top of Jasper's head, "my name's Abigail, and those are my goblins.  The uniformed lout back there, is my beloved husband who seems to have forgotten he's not at the barracks today and needs to choose his words more carefully," she finished with a cold stare at Chester, Jasper's brother.

"Here you go," Abigail slipped a coin to her daughter, "go on out there with your brothers and get some treats from the wagon.  You can also get that nice Ser Rowan a drink; gods he looks like he's melting!"

With the children bundled out of the tent, Abigail turned her attention back to her brother-in-law, eyes burning with fury, "Jasper Algernon Hawkford!  What in the Great Mother's name are you thinking?  You could have been killed!  You were killed as a matter of fact!  Your body should be fertilizing a field now!  All those brains your parents gave you, and not a bit of common sense!  When you said you were coming up here to Dagger Falls to study, we assumed you'd be doing so at the Abbeye! This is not like you, Jasper!"

Abigail's eyes suddenly narrowed.

"Ah!  No, I see! It's that… Sembian!  Isn't it?  He's put you up to this!  I saw that crass display!  The whole of Dagger Falls saw it!  You can't expect much from those people.  They have no morals, no decency! They don't worship proper gods down there! Sweet Jasper, you're a good, gods-fearing Dalesman, you shouldn't be falling in with such… people! Just two days ago you were at my table saying thanksgiving to the Good Mother, and now you're getting in bar fights -yes, I heard about that! - and," she waves her arms in an effort to encapsulate the entire competition, "now this!"

Her fury spent, and lecture given, she bent down and gave him a kiss on the forehead.

"Stop this nonsense, and go home to Shadowdale where you belong.  Come for supper tonight, and we'll get you all sorted out properly, and then on the road home with the next caravan," and with that she was out of the tent, and could be heard chastising her children as she attempted to wrangle them up.

Jasper's brother waved at him from the entrance, and simply said: "Good job, kid!"

With that, he turned away and went out to follow his family.

Jasper looked at Mari, and Mari looked at Jasper.  They both smiled, and she went back to cleaning his burns.

SCENE 2: MERIADOC BURGUNDY'S BARBERSHOP

"Hold still Master Hawkford, you don't want to have gone through all that trouble with this morning's event, only to lose an ear in my kitchen!" Meriadoc Burgundy's tone was patient, but stern.

Jasper was seated cross-legged on the floor of the Myth Drannor Embassy kitchen, bare torsoed (revealing the bruises, cuts and burns that marked the result of the competition's first day) while Mr Burgundy wielded a pair of scissors in one hand, and a razor in the other, attempting his best to even out the remainder of the young wizard's hair after most of it had been burned off by the Cormyrean War Wizard's temper.

Unfortunately for the halfling caretaker, Jasper had been easily enticed to celebrate their victory with plenty of ale, so the mage was finding it somewhat difficult to stay upright.  For his part, the halfling was finding it just as difficult not to cut, stab or slice the wizard with either razor or shears as he attempted to predict the young man's next direction of sway.

In addition to Meriadoc's moving target, the caretaker was further distracted by the commotion coming from upstairs.  A day ago this was his own private domain.  A nice quiet place where he could get his gardening done in peace.

Yesterday the halls echoed in silence, but today the list of new chores he needed to attend to grew longer by the minute.  And here he was playing barber while fires needed to be stoked, meals prepared, linens laundered… he made a mental note to ask the Lady to consider taking on additional staff beyond Fiona if this state of affairs was going to continue after the festival.

"Well... that's the best I can do," Meriadoc had given up.  But, in fairness, he was a gardener, not a barber, and the wizard's hair would grow back. "Now, clean yourself up and get back upstairs!  Don't worry about your shirt, I will see to it myself.  You can wear this for now..."

The halfling handed Jasper one of his own night-shirts to replace the bloodied, burned and torn garment the mage had worn that morning.  It was an awkward fit, but the extra length helped it make do.

"Yes… well… err… thank you, Mr Meri… um…," the young wizard awkwardly found his footing, unsure on whether it was him or the kitchen that continued to wobble and spin.  Jasper placed both his hands on the top of the halfling's head hoping the world would cease its motion and he could find peaceful stability.

"Master Hawkford, if you please!" came the protest from the elderly caretaker.  He took each of Jasper's hands in his own and, gently, but with firm purpose, removed them from atop his head.

There they stood for what seemed like an infinite moment, hands clasped together, two Dalesmen looking directly at each other with eyes wide, and both immediately aware of the tragedy that was about to befall them.

Later that evening, as he was sitting in his chair in front of the fire, wrapped up in his favourite dressing gown, at work with his knitting needles, Meriadoc Burgundy would reflect that it was actually a good thing that the young man hadn't eaten anything since breakfast.  It had been easier to clean up and clean himself.  But gods! He could still smell it! Why had it been so sour? Meriadoc shook with an involuntary chill, then reached over to his side table and grabbed a bit of dried lavender to rub under his nose in a feeble attempt to smell something - anything! - else.

SCENE 3: THE REDJACKS AND BREE

A half hour later, Meriadoc and Jasper sat in silence across from each other at the kitchen table.  The young wizard had bathed and was now drinking an herbal concoction that had soundly settled his stomach and cleared his head.  Meriadoc Burgundy had spent the time cleaning the kitchen, and was still in his soiled clothes.

"I..." began the wizard, but one look from the halfling's face was enough to stop him dead.

Some minutes of silence passed as Jasper tentatively sipped his drink.

"Er... that is, I - " tried Jasper again, only to be interrupted by Mr Burgundy.

"I think, Master Hawkford, it would be best if you returned to the festivities above.  I believe you are scheduled to go see the afternoon's competition."

"Um... yes.  Well, thank -"

"Good bye, Master Hawkford!"

With that, Jasper gathered his belongings and slinked out of the kitchen and up the stairs to the main hall of the Embassy.  He arrived just as Mari was fussing over Ser Rowan and Lucan, making sure they were ready to go and calling out to Fiona to fetch the wizard.

The small party left the building towards the competition grounds, instantly attracting a following.  The throng was in good cheer, singing songs and yelling out Team Chrysalis' praise.  If nothing else, the victory over the White Cloaks had been entertaining, and the people of the Dales ask for little more than that at times like this.

As the procession arrived at the site of The Mountain, Team Chrysalis was met with a small, but noticeable cheer. The morning's underdog champions had arrived, and the citizens of Dagger Falls were happy to settle in for an afternoon where the Standing Stones were now expected to put on a good show against the Redjacks.

The atmosphere was charged with excitement, as food and drink vendors, bookmakers, bards and pickpockets worked the crowds.  Lucan climbed atop the fence and faced the crowd, goading them into cheers and chants, with plenty of crowd pleasing lewd gestures and demeaning taunts towards the Redjacks.  The Daggerdale crowd loved it.

Jasper leaned over to Ser Rowan and was about to say that back in Shadowdale, when Proster Fieldstone kept insulting the parentage of the Harrow brothers at the Old Skull one night, the Harrow boys made damn sure that Proster wo - but he was interrupted by the roar of the crowd as the two teams came onto the field to take their positions for the Flag Capture round.

Before the event even truly began, it was over.

Jasper's head began to spin, and he let loose the vomit that was suddenly desperate to come out.  He was a farm boy after all, and he'd seen plenty of death growing up.  He was even well acquainted with necromancy, and was actively studying it at the moment.  But he had never seen violence like this.

He'd received a few injuries growing up, but that was normal on a farm. Especially when you're growing up with four brothers and two sisters.  The occasional overzealous punch or kick was to be expected. But until last night he'd never really been in a fight.  A true fight. Jasper would've been a good target for bullies or even for bandits or worse, but everyone knew that if you messed with Jasper, soon you'd have to mess with Abner, Chester, Maynard and Rudyard, not to mention his sisters Myrtle and Lizza. That amount of protection afforded Jasper a somewhat idyllic existence in Shadowdale.

But now, in less than 24-hours, he had been in a bar brawl where he had smashed a pint glass on a man's head, and had been stabbed, slashed and burned to near death.  He'd seen Lucan take two arrows to the heart and survive both!

But now this.  That poor woman had died and the dwarf that killed her had done so with glee.

It was the joy in his eyes when the axe found its mark that had most unsettled Jasper.

Death was one thing.  But lustful violence like this was an evil of an entirely different kind.

Jasper felt ill again as he looked back to see bits of flesh, meat and bone still lying on the ground where her body had fallen.  The world began to spin again and his legs wobbled, but Ser Rowan's steadying arm took a hold of him and kept him upright.

"These bastards should be disqualified.  That dwarf should be tried for murder!" Jasper's voice was uncharacteristically strong and confident, marked with a clear burning desire to right this injustice.

He looked up at Ser Rowan, and locked eyes with the genial knight.  He said only three words, but he meant them from the very depths of his being: "That was wrong."

The mood of the crowd had now changed, and the raucous good cheer had been replaced by an awkward, silent shuffle as the drunken crowd made their way out of the stands and towards the town, looking for any excuse to rekindle the party atmosphere that had been lost when Bree had been cut in half.

When the Redjacks filed past on their way out, Jasper made his feelings known that they should be disqualified.  As he'd expected, he was met with derogatory remarks and arrogant bravado from them.  As they filed by, he looked at each of them.

Growing up as the youngest of seven, his brothers and sisters had always looked out for him.  They kept him safe.  He had lived in that protected idyll.  But that protection simply made him more aware that most people did not enjoy the same.  Most people were prey to criminals like these Redjacks. And for Jasper, he felt it was his duty to make sure the bastards didn't get away with it.

He looked at each of them as they walked by.

"Have a drink and you'll feel better," said Ser Rowan, putting a mug of ale back in Jasper's hand.  "They'll get what's coming to them, don't you worry!" he clunked his own mug against Jasper's and the two men took long draughts from their drinks.

Soon after, Jasper was feeling better.  With some liquid encouragement from Ser Rowan, the young wizard had found his nerve and his words and became deeply entwined in conversation with the FDL's Nemia. He had become enchanted, finally finding someone who understood the finer points of necromancy like he did. Their conversation could've easily continued for the rest of the day and into the night, but both teams needed to move on.

Jasper was once again feeling the effects of the ale as he stumbled back to the Embassy with his teammates.  Ser Rowan would occasionally reach out and steady the young man, pointing him in the right direction, while Lucan would swap out the wizard's empty mug for a full one.  As the smells of the festival wafted by, Jasper's stomach gave its familiar growl and the wizard became aware he'd not eaten anything since breakfast.

As they walked into the Embassy, they found Jeska waiting for them...
