After coming back from their trip to the Dagger Hills, the White Cloaks were given a cottage to stay in and recover from their harrowing journey that brought them face to face with a number of terrible werewolves, and a short trip into the Shadowfell where they crossed blades with Agents of Netheril.

They came back exhausted, wounded, and soaked and chilled to the bone, but the stay in the small, cozy cottage put all of them in the spirit of camaraderie, as they warmed themselves with a small fire and some rabbit stew. Malvau sat quietly, listening to the other members of this new group which had tagged along with, learning that they too are just getting to know each other. Eventually, when discussion turned to what they would do next; further explore the Shadowfell, or chase after the Bloody Eye, Malvau begins his tale.

\"I first heard the name 'Bloody Eye' many years ago. I think it was in 1468, by your calendar, and the leaves were already falling from the oaks of Cormanthor, and the usual sort of villains came creeping from their holes with the glut of harvests and the rush of goods along trade routes that would soon be snow-covered. They were operating around Mistledale at the time, but between that time and this, there isn't a road in any Dale, and some lands beyond, that hasn't been the stage for their villainy.

\"That year, they were lead by a big and vicious ogre with some forgettable guttural name. They did brisk business for the season, but they were eventually caught up by my people, and my brother, Ayer, lead an attack against them. It was him in fact that took the eye from the ogre that lead them, and that wound was enough to both brand the bandits, and drive them off. In fact, they have never been seen in our territory since, and I am ashamed to say that there is some diplomacy at work there. I always knew that diplomacy with monsters would come to ill; one cannot reason with a rabid fox, and one cannot expect the ill-bred offal that makes up the ranks of the Bloody Eye to respect borders forever.

\"They have changed leadership many times over the last decade, but have always maintained their chief mode of operating, which is to stay in no place too long. Over the years, the Men of the Dales have banded together more than once to hunt them as a group, rather than leaving each polity to deal with them in turn, but this only had the effect of driving them off to more distant lands, and then, only for a while.

\"Last month they broke crossed into our territory, for which the penalty is death. I was assigned to a small group of hunters given the task of punishing them through the assassination of their leader, Kraken.

\"Kraken has not been leading them for very long, but he is capable and ruthless. However, he would not honour the boundaries that we set before them. They crossed through our lands, and though they attacked none of our people, they desecrated a sacred place with their foul presence, and robbed from us.\"

Malvau goes silent for a while, seemingly searching for words. The fire crackled in the old hearth, while the White Cloaks quietly nibbled at some bread and stew, waiting for him to go on. After a while, he looks up and continues.

\"Three of us set out after them – Rainyalla, Dellastay, and myself. We decided that we were going to do more than assassinate a leader. Anyone can do that. But we didn't believe in this...\" his angular face took on the most sinister and defined look of anger and disgust, and he shook his head side to side slightly, as if in disbelief of what he was about to say. \"...this...diplomacy. It made me sick to think of it, and it still does. The elders said it was the best way to send our message and keep them at bay for...for what?! Another ten years, so that they can prey on others, even though they leave my people alone. We disagreed with the elders, and we said as much. We were given our instructions clearly, and they were restated with clarity when they learned of our disdain for the matter.

\"Well, we should have listened, because Rainyall and Dellastray are dead. We harried them for weeks, and killed a score, but it wasn't enough, and eventually we lost a round in this game of the woods, and it cost me my closest friends, and my honour. I broke my oath to my elders, and now I cannot return.

Malvau goes silent and brooding for a while, and though he is likely a century old, he seems every bit an angry young man at that point, but only for a moment. He again masters his emotions, and finishes his story.

\"I will not return to my people until every one of the Bloody Eye are exterminated. That is my oath to myself, and my oath to you is that I will not flag in my defense of my new friends so long as we work towards eliminating these monsters from not only Daggerdale, not only the Dales, but this living realm. Even then, I am not sure I will be accepted, but such is my oath.\"
