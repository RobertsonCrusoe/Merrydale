The following characters are at the Castle Gate Inn in Crossed Blades Farm;

#Erevan, drow sorcerer
#Dane Grimwald, human fighter
#Brother Banitt, priest of Amaunator from a distant land

==Brother Banitt==

As the drow sits intently focused upon a maiden singing at the bar, his peripheral makes note of a tall, stocky young man. He is dressed in a badly worn robe of grey, but you can see the handle of a sword jutting at his hip. His only adornment is a wooden symbol of Amaunator, which hangs on a leather cord about his neck. His eyes are weary, a pale blue, but you note a great excitement in them. You sense this man is ill at ease in this bar, or just anxious to be on the road once more.

At your look he begins to ramble...speaking very quickly.

\"She ‘as a lovely voice, and comely enough too, fer those‘t find pleasure in such things. I understand thy fascination, like. Oh, I’m sorry if that’s bold of us, just that, well, I’ve never seen one of thy kind... Not that I mean offense by t' statement... *thy kind* like... just well... err....“ 

He takes a deep breath and steadies himself visibly.

\"I sense no evil in thee, good sir, I just needed to tell thee that as... well... thou art ‘eaded fer a place where ‘tis known that dwellers in darkness are looked upon wi’ mistrust... and well err... mayhap it be that... well... pardon me boldness once again, fer I mean no boast... but err... I’m of t'flock o' Amaunator, and I’d be ‘appy to ‘ave thee enter t'Castle at me side... if err... that be where thou art going. Seeing thee wi' one of t'flock may well buy thee t'time thou require’st to show thou bear’st no ill intent... umm... err\"

==Erevan Ilythiirian (Grim)==

\"There is nothing to worry about, kind sir, for I take no offense,\" says the drow gentleman. Dressed in fine grey and black traveling clothes of obviously elven make (as preferred by the Dalelands gentry) the young drow is an incongruous sight. Tall, thin and handsome, his purple eyes and kind smile offer none of the menacing evil reputed of his race. \"I've never seen any of my kind, either.\"

\"Thank you kindly for your offer, good sir. I had as yet no intention to travel anywhere but where the caravan takes me. I just needed a few days in peace to think, so I booked passage on the first caravan I could find that had a seat available on the mail coach. Dagger Falls appears to be my destination, for the moment.\" 

He sticks out his hand... \"Banitt, Banitt Harbaugh... err... oh...\" His speaking slows \"Canst thou understand me?\"

\"Ha! Erevan's the name! Erevan Ilythiirian. And if you know a word of elven, you'll likely know those words, and you'll have yourself a good chuckle. Children, even eladrin... or especially eladrin... will be cruel. It is a pleasure to meet you Mister Harbaugh. Or is it Brother? Or Father? You must forgive me, I am not one for religion of any sort, and know not the terminology. Let me buy you a drink nonetheless, and you can tell me of this castle and why you're headed there. Milli, the barmaid, was just favouring me with the tale of Castle Daggerdale and the legend of the lord Dorian and his lady. Do you know of the Heroes of Daggerdale, friend?\"

\"Well met err.. friend Erevan, as thou see’st I chuckle not, for no word of elvish do I speak. And for the time I would thou simply call us Banitt, for no rank or title do I own in any church.

“I’ve devoted me faith to Amaunator, and ‘tis me hope that t’end of my pilgr’mage will see us in ‘is favour, I feel I’m close to such an enlightening.\" 

He awkwardly sits at the table and shoots a youthful grin. 

\"’Owever, fer the time being, I’m burdened by no strict moral dilemma, and a drink would be welcomed, for I’m parched.\" 

==Dane Grimwald==

Dressed in a road-stained white , hooded cloak as well as a white tabard free of heraldry, a slightly taller than average man stood by the bar with a long sword and short sword sheathed upon his back. Even inside the tavern, he chose to keep his hood up, obscuring his face in shadow while he motioned for the barkeep to draw close.

“Ale...” 

“What kind?” was the affable response of the barkeep.

“The cold and wet kind,” was the quick retort of the man dressed in white. 

The barkeep took the hint that this patron wasn’t the type for empty conversation and simply went about pouring the man a large tankard from one of the kegs behind the bar. Upon returning, there were five silver pieces waiting atop the bar and the men simply nodded his head a moment in silent thanks before taking a long drink from the tankard, his Adam’s apple bobbing a second and third time as he drank deeply before lowering the tankard.

“Top this off and the rest of the coin is yours if you can tell me of a caravan on its way to the Castle.” 

There was only one “castle” in Daggerdale now - at least only one worth inquiring about - and the barkeep spoke to one barmaid and then another before returning with another tankard of ale. 

“At the mo’ the only people plannin’ on headin’ to the Castle are those two over there.” 

The barkeep pointed at the stammering priest and the drow at a table nearby before dropping his voice to a quieter tone. “Although I can’t imagine why you’d wan’ta travel with one o’ them.” 

The man pushed back his hood revealing a man that appeared to be approaching the twilight of being a \"young man.\" His skin was dark from spending a great deal of time out in the sun save a few scars upon his face that were coloured fainter than his sun-touched skin. He turned to regard the man and the drow nearby. Quiet for a moment, watching them over the rim of his tankard as he drank from it before turning back to the barkeep and giving him a small, but confident smile. 

“No need to trouble yourself over the drow that is walking about in the light of the day. It’s the drow that you don’t see…” giving the barkeep an amused look and placing an index finger aside his nose. “…it’s the ones in the shadows that don’t want to be seen. Those are the ones that are trouble.” Giving the barkeep a quick wink before pushing away from the bar and moving to sit at an empty chair beside the drow and the priest. Seemingly unaware that the conversation the two of them were having might be a private one, he placed his tankard on the table with a thump and snorted derisively.

“Aye, I know of these heroes.” he replied with a small sneer on his lips. “From what I’ve been told, they are not quite the heroes that the history books or the church would have you believe.”

==Friendly Company==

At the thump of the mug on the table, Banitt’s eyes meet the stranger’s.

The young Priest’s face visibly pales at the words and he chokes on his ale, loosing half a mouthful down the front of his robes. 

Shaken and obviously afraid of this grisly newcomer... he sits back in his chair, eyes darting to his wet robes, back to the stranger... and finally coming to rest on his new comrade the drow. 

His mouth begins to move.. but no words come forth...

Without so much as a blink of his eyes in surprise, Erevan replies to Dane’s indictment. \"Is that so? Here I had been under the impression that their deeds had been great enough for their names to be remembered by all. Something I don't believe can be said of everyone, isn't that right, Mister...?\" (And he looks expectantly at Dane, he signals to Milli to refill your tankard.)

Dane gives the priest an amused look before turning his attention back on the drow. He listens to what the drow has to say before offering his tankard to the hovering Milli, anxious to acquiesce to the request made by the drow. He lets his eyes linger on Milli’s hindquarters as she scampers away before looking back to the drow once again, his shoulders shrugging slightly as he responds.

“They played a role in lancing the boil that was the Zhentarim in Daggerdale. Zhents are experts at finding defeat long before they any of their plans yield fruit so from what I was told, these Four Warriors did nothing but speed up the Zhents calendar for failure. These heroes…” his tone of voice suggesting that there is no small amount of doubt when he uses such a term as hero. Taking a moment to drink deeply of his tankard, wiping the back of his sleeve across his mouth before continuing. “These heroes simply took one corrupt group and in time, it was replaced by another. I would love to hear from a high member of the church how they feel about one of Daggerdale’s liberators being a paladin of Shar. Is that man considered a hero?”

Dane's eyes dart away from the drow for a moment and gives Milli another appraising look before reaching down to his belt and smoothly drawing out a short-bladed and keen-edged knife. His eyes moving to the table as he begins to carve away at the table-top as he continues to speak.

“…and the name’s Grimwald. Dane Grimwald; save the ‘Misters’ for someone of greater import.”

\"I'll reserve my ’Misters’ for someone of greater import than you, certainly,” replied the drow. “As for a man of Shar... it was a different time, and heroes came from the most unlikely places. Saint Maturin and the Wanderer certainly trusted Tremalkin enough to fight alongside him. As to the church's opinions on such matters, I have little interest. You will forgive me friend Banitt, if I offend, but as I said, I am not one for theological discussions.\"

The well-dressed drow gentleman has remained fairly calm throughout these encounters, with his smile always friendly and his tone always polite (despite the veiled insults).

\"You must tell me of your personal battles with the Zhentarim,” continued the drow. “For they must have been great and perilous indeed if they have led you to believe that the Liberators' battle was piddling in comparison. But for the moment, our friend Banitt here was just about to tell me about this Castle Radiance...\"

Dane’s brow furrows a moment as it seems he’s gotten into a discussion with someone that has a greater affinity with books than with blades. He takes another long drink from his tankard and then defaults to a more aggressive but far more comfortable stance. 

“I don’t need to justify anything to the likes of you.” Dane growled with enough emphasis put on the “you” to ensure Erevan understood that he meant Erevan’s kind as opposed to simply Erevan, himself.

Erevan’s cool and calm demeanour irritated Dane. He wasn’t a smart man, but he wasn’t a stupid one either. He knew when he was being played with and he didn’t like it, not a bit. His grey-green eyes narrowed slightly as he looked at the drow, tucking away the fact that this drow referred to the “Heroes of Daggerdale” by names that he seemed wholly intimate with while most folk normally were not.

He lowered his gaze to look at the wooden table top he was working at with his knife, clearly working on something before looking back to the drow and the priest by the name of Banitt.

“I’m headed back that direction as well. It’s been some time since I’ve been to the Castle.”

He spoke a bit more quietly and not quite as self-assuredly as he had moments earlier all while he continued to carve at the tabletop with his knife. 

\"I meant no offence, friend,\" says Erevan, his voice dripping with friendly charm. Clearly, he has gotten Grimwald's point and quickly moves to ease the tension at the table. With that damned smile and steady voice putting everyone at ease, it is easy to forget one is speaking to a child of Llolth. Even Milli, it seems, keeps coming back and paying extra attention to the table of these three.

\"Perhaps you can enlighten me about this castle” Grim continued. “I had only planned to travel as far as Dagger Falls, but it seems the hub of activity in Daggerdale is Castle Radiance. What awaits you there, friend?\"

His head turning from one man at the table to the next as they speak in turn, Banitt’s mouth never stops moving. He nearly chokes at the implied promise of certain death from the one named Grimwald. However, at the calm, reassuring tone of his drow friend he relaxes his grip on the tables edge for the first time and opens his mouth to speak.

==An Undignified Opening==

The calamity of the crash is one to be spoken of in legend (Banitt had not realized that when he leaned back in his chair at the strangers approach he had actually rocked the chair back on its rear legs, his tenuous grip on the table being the only thing preventing him from falling... when his grip loosened...) 

The young priest’s eyes open as wide as pie plates and his fall seems in slow motion. His arms flail about once, then twice on his backward descent. Luckily for Banitt the man sitting behind him cushions his fall. Not so luckily for the unsuspecting patron. As the priest crashes into his back the man is lurched forward onto his table, sliding it forward into the groin of the man who had been leaning on it. This second man moans a cry of pain and falls, arms splaying to the sides, knocking food ale and coin all over the inn.

Having his descent slowed, yet not stopped Banitt is turned to one side off the man’s back and crashes to the floor, his arms pulled over his head and his knees tucked tight. He lies before you now.

Where a few of the patrons tensed and their hands went for their weapons at the clattering, whirling crash of the priest, Dane stayed focused on his work at the table, waiting for the priest to stop flailing before standing smoothly and then reaching down to hook one hand under the priest's armpit and swiftly lift the priest back up to his unsteady feet.

Dane’s shoulders shook with barely restrained laughter as he spoke. \"I hope you're like this because you've been in the cups for too long. On the journey to the Castle, remind me to buy you a whore. I think watching you with her would cause no end of amusement.\"

\"Err...umm no sir...Thank you... um Dane.. umm.. just fou sips, and one of those as thou may see is on me shirt.\" 

He moves to pull his chair back up to the table, ensuring that Dane is between himself and the rather disgruntled men at the next table. 

He speaks as he moves; \"Good sirs, my apologies, was... well... an accident yes?... ‘Ad I the coin I would refill thy tankard. Alas a humble servant as mesel’ to the Sun Lord,” an almost Machiavellian look in the priest’s eyes as he slightly lifts his symbol of Amaunator “ wanders not with pockets full o’ coin...\" 

Banitt returns to his chair and gestures for Dane to do the same... he whispers \"No-one round here wouldst dare accost a Cleric of Amaunator\" He winks.

==Primer on Politics==

\"As fer  ‘Eroes,\" The cleric continues, \"The only ‘Ero from these parts we’ve heard of in t’Dale is Ardelusk Whitegate. Me pilgrimage ends at ‘is tomb\" 

Dane noted that the priest said \"the Dale\" and not \"the 'Dales\" which he concluded that the priest certainly was far from home and out of his element...but not so far out of his element to miss a chance to ensure his own safety by flashing his holy symbol of Amaunator to the man he had fallen atop of. He gave the priest a sly grin as the priest winked at him, and clumsy or no, this Banitt of Amaunator was agile when it came to thinking, something that might be good to know in the future.

As a true Dalesman from Shadowdale, Erevan completely failed to reach this conclusion. A distinct failure in perception on his part, one might say, and something to worry about for the future.

Dane moved back to his seat and pulled the knife out of the tabletop that he had sunk blade-first when he rose to help Banitt, giving the priest an amused look once more while snapping his calloused fingers and motioning for round-bottom'd Milli to scamper over once more and fill their tankards.

\"Ah...old Whitegate. Mayhap I'll find an abandoned mine full of gold some day in the future and then I can use it to strongarm my religion into the 'Dales.\"

\"Ardelusk Whitegate.” The drow pondered aloud. “The name sounds familiar. Milli had just been telling me of another Whitegate in Castle Radiance, but he sounded very much alive. Perhaps they are related. Ardelusk Whitegate... the name does ring another bell. Was he, by chance, one of the slaves liberated by Saint Maturin?\"

\"Bah...,\" growled Dane as he swept aside more chips of wood on the floor and for the first time, what he had been carving in the tabletop began to bear a resemblance to a face. \"You can't swing a dead cat by the tail in the Castle without hitting someone named Whitegate.\"

\"What did Milli call him?” inquired the drow. “It was a rather... queer name. Oh, yes! Luminous. Luminous Whitegate. Said he was the chieftain of the place, or something along those lines. Never leaves the damn place.\"

\"I hardly think that what our Dales need is more religion.” The drow replied, with some little passion in his voice for the first time. “In fact, one could say that many of our troubles here in the Dales might have come from a surplus of religion. But, I forget myself! We're not here to talk about fickle other-worldly beings. Grimwald, you seem to know Whitegate, too. An interesting man, he must be.\"

Giving Milli a predatory grin before taking another drink from his tankard and lifting it up in a mock-toast, Dane continues; \"Hear, hear. Naught but ill has come from and will come from religion in the 'Dales.\" Going back to his carving and whittling, a small smile forming on his lips as he lets out a short, coarse laugh. \"I'd know a Whitegate when I see one, but they certainly don't let fellows like me get close enough to one to even breathe the same air...although I wouldn't mind breathing some of Lumina Whitegate's air.\" Giving Erevan a sidelong look before continuing. \"She's almost as pretty as you are.\"

==\"Careful there, friend\"==

\"Careful there, friend.\"

And at that moment, you saw it. What you had been expecting all along, but knew was missing. That element which hadn't been there when you met him, but had been nagging at you this entire conversation. With a few quick words, and a narrowing gaze (his smile never leaving his lips) you saw that rage boil to the surface of his eyes. Not just rage, but menace. Sheer, destructive menace; the kind of menace that has caused unending pain and shed oceans of blood between the surface and the Underdark for millennia after millennia. This drow may be playing the part of the foppish elf up here at this tavern, but in his eyes you saw, for just a brief second, a child of Llolth almost begging you to let him slice your throat open.

Dane’s eyes narrowed once more as the drow referred to him as \"friend’\" The area of Mistledale where he’d grown up, the word “friend” was saved for the person you planned on meeting outside the tavern afterwards so that you could club him over the head and relieve him of his coin purse. Those same words coming from a drow, coupled with the momentary look of malevolence certainly didn’t foster any feelings of ease but he kept the majority of his focus on the image he was carving in the table. 

Erevan, having grown up in Shadowdale, had precisely the same definition of \"friend\" as Dane.

Stopping once again to wipe his hand across the surface, Dane shoved wood chips to the floor before continuing to work and taking the drow up on his offer for more conversation.

\"From what I remember and I have no reason to believe anything at the Castle has changed.” Dane said, quickly changing his tone after the warning spoken by the drow. “Luminous is the lord of the Castle. Everything goes past him and he runs that place like it's a secret vault. In fact, you might very well be stopped at the gate and not allowed passage simply because of what you are.\"

\"That is precisely what our pious friend here was telling me before you joined us.” replied the Erevan. “He offered his protection to me as a clergyman, should I desire to enter the castle. I had not planned on venturing further than Dagger Falls, but I must say that this evening's conversation has made me rather curious. I may just take Banitt's offer and go see the castle for myself.\"

Dane continues; “It’s been a little over four years since I’ve been there but I doubt much has changed. Don’t let its ties to Amaunator fool you. It’s no church. It’s a fortress. It’s more than a fortress really, if Amaunator wears plate boots when he goes to war, Castle Radiance is one of those boots. It doesn’t…” frowning a moment as he looks up from his work, taking a long drink from his tankard once more as he looks for the right words before meeting the drow’s gaze and suppressing the urge for violence. “…it doesn’t belong. You’ll see what I mean when you first see it. It’s impressive, but it sticks out. I’ve spent my whole life in the Dales and too much time at the western edge of those mountains. I’ve learned. If you stick out, you’re the first nail that gets pounded flat. That place sticks out.” 

Dane frowns as he takes another drink and empties his tankard before turning his focus back to the carving of the tabletop. He almost seems … disheartened when he speaks. “I’ve seen the Enclave from a distance. I’ve been close to the Scimitar Spires. The Shade Lords are always watching, always … doing. When you get to the Castle, take it all in. Commit it to memory, dark elf, because it’s there only because the Shade Lords allow it to be there. It sticks out, and the Shade Lords will be the ones to pound it flat.”

At the mention of the Shade Lords, Erevan's smile leaves his face for the first time. He sits quietly, letting Dane's final sentence hang in the air between the three of them. He then says \"I've spent most of my life in the Dales as well, and have learned a thing or two about sticking out. The trick, of course, is to hit first, and hit hard. I imagine that's the philosophy taken at Castle Radiance as well.\"

The three new acquaintances notice that the conversations in the taproom began to dwindle and dwindle, as the drow and the Bully intensified their discussion. At the mention of the Shade Lords, there isn't another sound to be heard that doesn't come from your mouths.

Milli had been steering away from the conversation, and by the time the brief-lived theological discussion surfaced, she became scarce altogether. Her and her free Chauntean beer.

As the three men got up to find their own beds, Erevan looks down at the table to see what exactly Dane is carving...

\"Saints\"
