\"I witnessed his arrival, his emergence from the Wastes -- a solitary figure shrouded by coursing snow and bitter chill...

He gripped a blade of darkest iron, riven with green fire... striding without purpose, unburdened by furs or supply, as though he were simply unaware of the biting cold strong enough to drive the breath of life from a man's gasping throat... to think such absence of thought was enough to ward oneself against the wind...

My father had told me stories, stories I myself have told my own child and the children of my kin... of Meloch of Rashemen, the Love-Scorned Lich, and his left hand, all his spite and rage, Durandal of Greenflame... airs of providence rendered me unthinking, and my fool tongue stammered those long-cursed names.

He stopped and regarded me with the greyest of eyes, and asked me simply.

What year, old man?

I told him I knew not. So he left to the west, that fiendish corona dancing across his shoulders as he dwindled once more into the blizzard. I know what I have witnessed. What these eyes have seen.

Demon of the Wastes... one more lost soul, wandering out of time-worn pages like a nightmare given flesh. So I have seen.\"

- Golurauch of the Jaghotai, Nar Soothsayer
