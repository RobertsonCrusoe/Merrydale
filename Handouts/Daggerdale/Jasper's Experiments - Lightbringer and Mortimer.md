Jasper's Experiments - Lightbringer and Mortimer
by Gus Miranda (as Jasper Hawkford)
Email thread: "Jasper's Experiments", 10 Aug 2024

Two linked narrative vignettes describing Jasper's experiments at the Embassy. In the first, Jasper stumbles into the boardroom covered in soot, takes Lucan's drink, and presents a magical pistol called Lightbringer — made of flame-like magical energy in a dark glass casing. The catch: he killed Mortimer testing it. In the second, Jasper's attempt to trap Mortimer's essence in a stone figurine goes wrong, producing a horrifying two-foot rodent that terrorises the halflings before Ser Rowan skewers it with his guisarme. Les: "OMG...wizards..."

---

PART ONE: THE LIGHTBRINGER

One evening, Lucan, Mari and Jeska are working hard in the second floor private dining room, which Chrysalis has adopted as defacto board room. The three are poring over the various details regarding the establishment of a new building company for the settlement of Thistle Ridge.

The door opens, and Jasper walks in. He is wearing his fairly worn breeches, and his much repaired once-white shirt; though that is of no concern, as the wizard is covered almost entirely in soot. He reeks of the awful combination of saltpeter and sulfur, and seems to be both emanating a magical glow and a slight cloud of smoke from his head.

He sits down heavily in one of the chairs, absent-mindedly taking Lucan's glass and downing its contents, before speaking, "71 divided by... hmm... and then the equinox... I mean, of course... hmm...."

His focus drifts off, giving a blink and suddenly becoming aware of the room.

With his free hand, he reaches down and pulls out a pistol that had been tucked into the waistband of his breeches, and he places it gently on the table. The pistol is somewhat translucent, being made almost entirely of magical energy, but when looked at closely it has the look of flames, burning inside a dark glass casing shaped like a pistol. It glows faintly, with small wisps of smoke coming out of its barrel, and it is warm to the touch.

"Well, the good news is it works," says Jasper, "the bad news is I killed Mortimer with it."

PART TWO: THE MORTIMER FIGURINE

Another day. It is mid-morning and The Embassy is quiet, peaceful even. Only a handful of halflings remain, chatting away, lingering in the Great Hall as breakfast merges into second breakfast.

Suddenly, a great commotion is heard downstairs in the kitchen, followed by, first scream of terror from a young lady, then the sound of dishes breaking against stone.

Suddenly, at the top of the stairs appears a... rodent. It is about two feet long, emaciated, with boils and pustules all over its malformed body. At first glance and one might say it was a rat, yet it has a squirrel's tail and ears, its snout unnaturally compressed. The assorted halflings scream in terror and scatter to flee in whatever direction they can find.

"Mortimer!" comes Jasper's voice as the wizard tops the stairs, "Behave yourself!"

The rat-creature, turns to face the wizard, its eyes boiling with hatred. It bears its cragged yellow teeth and gives a horrible hiss:
"PAAAAIIINNNN!!! AAAAAARRSSS!!!"

"Well, look, it was an accident. I didn't know it would hurt you! In any case, this is no way to behave in the mistresses' house!"

Jasper is interrupted as the bulk of Ser Rowan appears behind the monster and skewers the beast with his guisarme. The rodent gives out a hideous wail of pain, and is wracked by bone breaking convulsions and death throes, until after what seems an eternity it simply emits its last gasp of air and dies.

The rodent's body begins to boil and hiss, turning into a small maelstrom of green goo and yellow gasses, swirling around, slowly imploding down and coalescing into a crudely carved stone figurine of what, it is supposed, should be a squirrel, but in reality resembles a giant malformed rat.

The knight looks up at Jasper with horror and confusion in his eyes.

"Well, I can't get him back permanently just yet, so I thought I could maybe trap some essence of him into this figurine. Didn't quite work the way I expected...."

Ser Rowan still continues to look at Jasper in horror.

"Ah, he'll be alright. Much more useful in a fight this way, anyway."

Key lore points:
- Jasper created a magical pistol called Lightbringer — translucent, made of flame-like magical energy in a dark glass shape, warm to touch
- Testing the Lightbringer killed Mortimer (Jasper's familiar/squirrel)
- Jasper attempted to resurrect Mortimer by trapping his essence in a stone figurine — the result was a horrifying two-foot rodent creature
- The Embassy has a Great Hall where halflings gather; Chrysalis uses a second-floor private dining room as a boardroom
- The Embassy has a kitchen downstairs staffed (at least in part) by a young lady
- Ser Rowan dispatched the Mortimer-creature with his guisarme
- Chrysalis was establishing a building company for the settlement of Thistle Ridge
