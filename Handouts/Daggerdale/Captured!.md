The air in the prison cell was thick with the smell of damp stone and old fear. Rufus leaned against the iron bars, his face bruised but his eyes still bright with defiance.

"Atemnon won't keep us here long," he whispered, mostly to himself.

Oliver groaned from the corner where he lay on a pile of moldy straw. "He doesn't need to keep us here long, Rufus. He just needs to keep us here until the ritual is complete. Did you see the way the shadows moved in the hallway? That wasn't just magic. That was Her."

Maevri sat cross-legged in the center of the cell, her eyes closed, her fingers tracing invisible patterns in the air. "The Moon is hidden," she murmured. "But she is not gone. We must wait for the transition. When the fire starts—and it will start—we move."

Outside, the sound of orcish laughter drifted down the corridor, followed by the heavy clanking of armored boots. The Red Shields were coming.
