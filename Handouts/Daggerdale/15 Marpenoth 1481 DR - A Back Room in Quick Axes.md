The only NPC present is Brother Darred.-----Gale is sitting across from Korrick and brother Darred.“Loremaster Solemnius sent word to Korrick a month ago, telling him to come to the dale” Darred says to Gale. “I guess that whatever is going on in Charity Hills is bigger than any of us expected. I understand you two have worked together before? I won't ask about that, but I'm glad of it. You'll need to close ranks in Charity, I don't doubt.” Darred gives you two time to reacquaint yourselves, before continuing.Darred slides an ornate wooden box across the table to Gale, and a leather folio full of papers.Opening the box, Gale marvels at the weapon found inside. It is more like a work of art than a weapon; the handle is a piece of reddish exotic hardwood, inlaid with brass grips and other hardware. The barrel is a damasked steel that presents as a wavy pattern of materials that look like silver and brass, though they feel harder and colder. There are words and symbols carved into the barrel and other metal hardware-bits; the only one that you immediately recognize is a symbol of Gond.“This is a most useful tool, Mr. Gale” Darred begins. “Rafter herself crafted this for a client that...didn't survive…. No fault of the weapon's I am sure. It is a marvel of both craftsmanship and dwimmercraft; it is magical, though she wouldn't tell me what it does. She said you'd figure it out as you used it. Sometimes I think she's daft.“Anyway, there are a couple of reasons you are being assigned this weapon. For starters, we need to make sure you are well looked after; you are about to embark on your most dangerous task yet, and while we will pray for your safety, we'll hedge our bets just a bit, hey? Also, you are headed to Charity, where people love firearms. This piece is fine, even for them, and I hope it will help you transition into that town.“There is enough ammunition for 200 rounds of fire, and more will be readily available in Charity, I'm sure. There is more...”He reaches into the bottom of the box, shuffling around ammunition components, and pulls out a small piece of cloth that makes the hair on the back of your neck stand up.Founder's Linen. All full members of your order wear a 4” x 4” patch of linen that is taken from a garment worn by 'the Founder'. This piece is the requisite size, and the transparent nature of the fine linen is a dead giveaway. This little patch is frayed at the edges, and has a number of stains that make the transparency somewhat muddied. Some of it looks like drops of blood...“Yes, brother. Today you are a full member. I guess you were expecting some sort of ceremony full of pledges, candles, incense and the like, but it just doesn't work like that. What happens is this” he says, as he leans across the table, and stretching both hands out towards you, opens your cloak, and pins the linen inside. “You are now a full member of the Order of the Tome, brother. This doesn't mean that they are going to reveal the secrets of the universe to you. I don't have much more of that sort of thing than you. But, know that you are part of an Order that is older than either of us ever expected. More will be revealed as you prove yourself; our goals, methods, and so on. For now, you take orders. Can you live with that? Most of the orders are reasonable, and many are fun.“I personally spoke for you with Loremaster Solemnius; your accomplishments in Dwimmerholme, painted in the right light, were enough to earn your Linen.“But, you only scratched the surface. There is something much more insidious at work in Daggerdale, and there is an evil book at the core of it that we have been seeking for decades. The Diabolus Itineris. I know your ancient Netherese is current, hahaha! There are very particular translators, however, that say that means 'A Devil's Diary', while others say it means 'The Devil's Journal', and still others that argue for 'Devilish Agenda', but it all amounts to the same for guys like us; evil book, must get it.“Here's how we know...” and he opens the leather folio, and sorts through the papers. He picks one out, and gives it a rap with the back of his hand, and hands it over to Gale. “Go ahead and read it...”After you have read the letter, he hands you another batch of sheets; 7 pages written in Ancient Netherese. If you can't read the language, you recognize some of the symbols almost immediately; you have seen these before in the Belial Prayer Book. This is the ritual known as 'Bel's Bargain', and when you recognize it for what it is, you realize instantly that there is another Cult of Bel in Charity Hills.“I can assure you that Earl Muloch translated this expertly. His manner of writing tells me that he is worried to send any message that says he knows too much. As such, I have returned no letters to him. Instead, I am sending you. And not just to bring a letter to an old man.“You will notice that your prayer book is full of the infernal text, but it has been written for a Cormyrean to read and translate. This text, this copy of a copy that was sent to us by our ally and associate, this is written in Ancient Netherese, and the details which he has asked me to uncover reveal that it was taken from an original manuscript. We think that it might have been copied from the Diabolus Itineris itself.”Darred takes a deep breath, and exhales slowly. “Your orders are to go and find that book, Mr. Gale. If that book is in Charity, it is most assuredly the centrepiece of this Sons of Bel business plaguing the dale. As always, you will operate as covertly as possible. It might be best if you can attach yourself to some other outfit headed to Charity under some bona fide pretense. The Order can fund you a little...” with this he produces a leather bag full of gold; 2500 gp in total. “That is all there is for this mission, and we will trust your discretion in its use.”“I wish I had more to tell you. Try to gain a discrete audience with Earl Muloch. He won't be hard to find, but it will be hard to meet with him without raising suspicions. He is a good man that you can trust; he is an associate of the Order, and a dedicated follower of the Scribe [Deneir]. He will help you, but he has much to lose, and will play his hand cautiously in all matters.”He gets up to leave. “Pour over all the information in that folio. We'll talk again before you set out. I'm in Town for 3 more days, but I have to head over to the Yellow Abbey just now. Start thinking about who you can take with you.”Standing tentatively at the edge of the table, he asks “Is there anything else I can do for you?”________________Scun and Hicks, take it from here. [2012-07-17 23:45:44] Beliard
As the giant cleric Arkadios lumbers behind his diminutive guide into the room shared by Gale and Korrick, a voice from the common hall beyond may be heard to call out in the crisp, canorous accent of the Cormyrean aristocracy - "The Silver Lady lights our way, Zancanelli. I will wager you a sack of steelpence that the men we seek lurk in the shadow of yon colossus. Come, let us follow the wake of those noble thews."

To Balak's chagrin, Beliard and his companion sidle into the back room on the dwaven publican's heels. The bard quickly interposes himself between the  newcomers and the pair already at the table. Replying to Balak's frowning challenge, Beliard laughs.

"What ho, innkeeper. What a pair you make! The titan and the mite - were I a painter your likeness would soon hang in the Royal Gallery in Suzail! As a humble songster, mayhaps I will compose a comic ballad. In any case, you may rest your grizzled mind. My associate and I - " Beliard offers a flourishing bow which somehow incorporates a thumb jerking over his shoulder at Zancanelli, "have every reason to be here, in the storied company of Nathan Gale."

At this mention of the name, Beliard closely watches Gale and Korrick for a reaction. Accepting their suspicious glare as confirmation that he is indeed in the right place, Beliard turns to the dwarf's companion. "By Grumbar's Girdle, moon brother, you are big fellow and true. I am Beliard Kathklan of House Emmarask. How might I address thee?" If he pays attention, Arkadios will be able to see the bard's own Selunite talisman in the form of a silver clasp affixed to an indigo cloak. Although it is obvious he is no priest, the bard shows signs of being a devout follower of the moon goddess.

When introductions have been made, Beliard seats himself and gestures with an air of authority for Zancanelli to do the same. When the dwarf leaves, Beliard's expression changes. His voice lowers, and his characteristic air of blithe affability suddenly vanishes. In a serious and muted tone, he addresses the man he takes to be Nathan Gale. "Now, Master Gale. As you should have received a message not long ago, I believe you are expecting me."

Beliard reaches into the inside pocket of his travelling vest and - slowly, with a wry wink at Korrick - withdraws a somewhat worn piece of parchment. He hands it to Gale, who may unfold it to find that it is a note from Magistrate Hoetmer to Roger Dunland, written before the former's death.

Catching Gale's eye as he glances up, Beliard continues. "I would be very interested in seeing the book that accompanied that note, Mr. Gale. We have a common enemy in these Sons of Bel."

At that moment, the serving girl enters again, and Beliard's attention is distracted from Gale. After she sets down the ale and introduces herself with a curtsy, he grins broadly at her. "Why my young lass, I am astonished. Your father must be the most audacious criminal in the known Realms! Not only to steal the stars from the firmament to brighten your gaze - but to brag about it by naming you! Hah! Well done! I say, well done!" Before the girl has a chance to fluster, he tips back his chair on two legs , puts his feet on the table, whips the lute he carries over his shoulder around into his hands, and starts strumming.

"The king of the thieves, the old rotter,Stole the stars from the skies for his daughter.He set those jewels in her eyes andin a cool advertisementCalled her Stariana - the pride of her father!"

(As the bard's improvised ditty takes effect, the mousy young serving girl's hair seems somehow to become a little less greasy, growing softer and a shade more lustrous. Likewise the sheen of sweat and oil on her cheeks fades, and her pores tighten, clearing her skin. Those with any knowledge of magic who are paying attention will recognize the effects as the result of a prestidigitation on the bard's part).

Letting his chair settle forward, Beliard beams. "Now, pretty one. No doubt in his senescence your master forgot to inform you that I prefer claret to the gutshaker they serve at this establishment. I know that Master Balak has a tun of Saerloon Hills '65 horded away in his cellar. Bring me a goblet and a carafe of that fine vintage, and we may well trade places, for I will be your most obliging servant."

[2012-07-23 00:04:53] Arkadios_Solaris
"Arkadios," says the Selunite, with a pat on the back and a broad smile at Beliard, in response to the question.

The big man makes his way into the room and finds a seat next to Korrick.  When the food arrives, he grabs two plates, scrapes the contents of one onto the other, and tucks in to the mound of food, listening attentively to the talkers do their talking.

He'd been around long enough to know that talkers like these ones, on the whole, like to talk.  Get them in the back room of a bar, tavern, or inn, and well... you're in for a lengthy soliloquy or two.  Maybe even a lecture on some historical anomaly.  And if they're a really good talker, and judging by what he'd seen so far, this man Beliard was probably one of the best, well... we might just be in for some epic poetry.  Maybe even a saga.  This wasn't the first time Arkadios had found himself plotting in a back room, and so he figured he best be getting his fill of hot food and good drink because eventually these talkers would stop talking, and that's when the real work would begin for men like him.

"That's a cute little knife you got there," says Arkadios to Korrick through a mouthful of potatoes, pointing with a spoon dripping with grease to the magical man's sword.
