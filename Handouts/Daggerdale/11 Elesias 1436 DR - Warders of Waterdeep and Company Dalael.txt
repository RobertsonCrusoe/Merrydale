Elesias 11, 1436 

The Warders of Waterdeep encounter the Company Dalael this summer.  Both parties were searching for something called the Island of Three Curses, somewhere south-west of Neverwinter in the Sea of Swords.  Company Dalael was looking for something called Kulgen Sussun on this island, while the Warders were looking for a place called Blethmark.  It turned out that Blethmark was one of many layers of building upon the ancient Kulgen Sussun (Drow for fortress of light), which was built by Drow millennia ago. 

During this race to find this location, the Warders and Dalael crossed paths many times, both in the City of Splendors during the research phase, and on the seas and in the archipelago during the exploration phase.   

The Dalael were known at that time to have infiltrated many noble houses of the City, including the Anteos, Dezlentyr, Eltorchul, Margaster, Umbrusk and Wavesilver families.  The heads of the company, which are the Dalael brothers, were married to and/or had children with women of the Anteos, Dezlentyr and Eltorchul families.  They also had some influence over the Durinbold family, through manipulation of Breton Durinbold's hatred for his brother, Talinvor, a member of the Warders. 

The Warders were able to foil many Dalael operations, and managed to raid their stronghold in North Ward.  In the end, the Dalael left the city in defeat.  The only Dalael that stayed in the City was Y'morth Dalael, also known as Dally.  This Drow was not a participating member of the company, and is known by the Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors as a contributing member of that guild. 

Surviving members of the Warders of Waterdeep include the following; 

-Steele, an elf of questionable morals and jack of all trades 

-Kenneth, priest of Oghma, now Loremaster at the Font of Knowledge 

-Rychel, urban stalker, now retired from adventuring 

-Gable, mage, currently retired 

-Edgar Grimbold, AKA Stuart Bold, swashbuckler and monk, currently retired 

After a debacle with the Durinbold family involving the deaths of two Durinbolds and an assault upon the family villa, the Warders ended their association with that company name.  The surviving men of this company were harassed by Magistrate Richter many times, and in the end were only saved from prosecution by this man by the paladin Harkas Kormalis.
