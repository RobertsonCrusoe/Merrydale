Daggerfalls Gazetteer – Players' Handout 
This portion of the Chronicle has information for the players specifically, including some references to game materials (such as prices in PHB's and that sort of thing). 
Dagger Falls Cathedral 

The Cathedral is made up of 4 distinct portions; 
1. The Main Cathedral 

The Main Cathedral was built between 1411-1414, and at the time was well outside the walls of the town.  This structure is about 250' long, and 150' wide at the broadest transepts.  Inside, the building as impressive soaring space, full of light and air.  Flying buttresses represent the most progressive architecture in the dales, and were designed to convey both greatness and elegance.  Stained glass windows fill the many openings in the stone building, saturating the space in yellow, gold, orange and red light.  There is room for around 1,000 people to attend service, and some services indeed see the place packed to standing room only. 

Entry to the Main Cathedral is possible from sun-up to sun-down every day, and is usually hassle-free.  There are always two Knights of the Order of the Lambent Shield present at the top of the steps leading to the cathedral, but they are mostly polite and don't ask questions.  Inside the open doors visitors will usually be met by a young magistrate who welcomes them, and encourages them to visit the Tithe Confessional.  This is a small room in the first transept where the faithful can talk with a senior magistrate in confidence, and donate to the church at the same time. 

The offices of the senior clergy including the Bishop are in the Main Cathedral, and can be found on the mezzanine level of the building.  Entry to the mezzanine is controlled by magistrate of middling rank who will inquire about the nature of the visit, and make meticulous records about all heading up.  From the mezzanine level, one can also access The Historic Wing (see below). 

Most of the historic figures, heroes and significant clergy of the dale are buried beneath the stone floor of the cathedral, their monuments carved on the stones that people walk on every day. 

The transepts are full of relics from many ages relevant to the Amaunatori.  One of the most significant is The Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin, which is a grotesque severed hand that is on open display on a small pedestal, with a simple plaque engraved into the wall behind it reading "The Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin, Taken from the Field of Battle at Liberty Hill from the Cultists of Shar who were bested that day.  We will not forget.  We will not forgive." 

The other major feature of the Main Cathedral is the Aviary, accessed in the bell tower.  The Aviary is the home for dozens of messenger doves, and is overseen by the Chaplain of the Doves, a very prominent and trusted magistrate who oversees communication between the Cathedral and the rest of the Dale and beyond. 

The Yellow Abbeye was a separate building, constructed in 1413 as a fortified monastery.  By 1425 it was attached to the Main Cathedral, and a second level with residence and refectory added.  Today it is home to 30-35 magistrates in training, as well as most of the remaining clergy of Amaunator that don't have an apartment in the Main Cathedral (another 12 magistrates).  The Yellow Abbeye has 4 main components; 

 The Monastery – most of the east/south part of the building is dedicated to a pair of classrooms, several austere meditation chambres and a couple of office rooms.  As well as the 40+ magistrates, there are another 4 support staff that work in this wing. 

The Historic Wing was built as a separate 2-floor structure in 1419, and attached in 1426 to the Main Cathedral.  It is accessed only by a highly secure door on the mezzanine of the Main Cathedral.  A large stone door with a relief carving of a specific and stylized symbol of Amaunator serves as a magical lock to the building, and is only opened by the holy symbol of the Chaplain of the Athenaeum.  This is currently Magistrate Invictus, who recently inherited the title after the death of his predecessor. 

The Historic Wing is the repository of all books, relics and other information deemed too dangerous by the Chaplain to be in the Athenaeum.  Very few people have been inside the Historic Wing, and none have offered a description of it.  For many years, this also served as the domicile for Brother Phinneas Hoetmer, who was an Oghman, but who worked closely with the Amaunatori clergy for decades on some of the more occult issues plaguing the Dale. 

The Cemetery is the large yard around the Cathedral, and is full of well-maintained paths, small gardens and neat stones.  There are a few small mausoleums for some families in the Dale that have the money but not the sway with the church to be interred beneath the flags of the Cathedral itself.  The Cemetery is a work of art like the Cathedral itself, and is a popular place for outdoor lessons for the children from the Yellow Abbeye, and other townsfolk besides.  On most nice evenings, it is common to see people strolling through this well-kept yard, and there are local games and customs around identifying some of the headstones in an amusing fashion. 
Cassimar Tower 
Known as 'Brotherhood Tower' in the early 1370s, this building was the headquarters of a militant wing of the cult of Iyactu Xvim until 25 Eleint, 1372.  On that day, a group of men stormed the court outside the tower, and waged a battle against the evil knights and prevailed.  Today, the court in which the tower sits is called 'Harpers' Court', and a large round paving stone not far from the entrance of the tower reads "On this spot was the head of the Xvimite Half-Devil, Baron Drake, separated from his shoulders by the Axe of St. Maturin.  Maturin was accompanied that day by Dorian of Sembia, Boregar the Wanderer, and other heroes who desired justice more than fame." 

Today, the tower is owned by what is left of the Cassimar family.  The Cassimars were one of the most prominent families the Dales from the 1310's-1380's, having come to the Dales with Sembian gold and a knack for spreading the faith of Lathander.  In most large settlements, a Dawnlord with their surname could be found until the Spellplague, and the metamorphosis of Lathander into Amaunator. 

The faith of the Cassimar family did not survive the Spellplague, but their wealth did, and what few members of that once great family remain have all retired to Dagger Falls and reside in their now-opulent tower. 

After a fire in 1375 (supposedly an act of arson) gutted the building, it sat empty for decades, until the Cassimars moved to town and bought and renovated it.   

The tower is an imposing stone structure and one of the most conspicuous in town.  The main body of the building is 4 stories, while an additional set of towers built atop the main battlement in the 1460's adds another story and a half, making it the tallest structure in the town.  The Cassimars have been in the habit of painting the dark stones of the main tower in a brilliant lavender, and many lavender & yellow flags and banners with the family crest adorn the building, lending it a bright and pleasing countenance. 

The main body is 75' in diameter, with 14' ceilings on the first floor, 12' ceilings on the second, and 10' ceilings on the third and fourth floors.  A grand winding staircase in the middle of the main building connects the original 4 floors plus the larger of the two new towers.  Each level has a balcony encircling the stairs, surrounded by many rooms and small passageways.  The Cassimars favour a convoluted and detailed architecture, with many fine panelled rooms on every level, many private and intimate nooks filled with tapestries, cushions and carpets with rich colours from around the Realms. 

The largest room in the building is the dining hall on the 2nd floor, which serves the mayor as the focal point for her many parties, but guests are encouraged to wander the tower and explore.  Many of the finer rooms have magical lights with intimate tones, some with twinkling motes that give specific rooms a fey feeling. 

The large battlement at the top of the main building has well-kept gardens of fragrant flowers, and a small arboretum that connects the new tower to the open space. 

The Cassimars employ a small number of private guards, all originally orphan boys whom they have looked after for many years.  There are 8 of them in total, the oldest being Dorian Dale, aged 26, who like his namesake is a capable swordsman, and a former Sunblade.

Local Rumours 
Temple of Bane in the Grotto 
The tower is built atop one of the oldest cave networks in the area, and was originally a smaller building used to conceal the caves and the temple to Bane within.  Randal Mourn himself rooted out the Temple, but was captured inside by the Zhents when he did, leading to his years-long imprisonment and ultimately his execution.  During the Zhent Occupation of the 1370's, the Tower was built to once again serve to control access to the caves.  In more recent times, sewers have been connected with the basements of the tower, and no caves have been discovered. 
Old Money 
It is unclear exactly what the Cassimars do to earn money, if they do at all.  They own no other properties or businesses, but run one of the most expensive households in the Dale.  Most assume that the family is still thriving off the generations of wealth accumulated by the family in the previous century, which has made the tower a periodic target for thieves, especially during the Mayor's frequent parties, where she invites many guests and encourages them to wander the fine labyrinth of her opulent home. 
The Old Market 
For over 200 years, this location just inside the original walls has served as the primary location for the exchange of day-to-day goods for the Dale.  Today, it is a well-established market, with more than a dozen permanent and reputable businesses that surround a busy open-air market.   

The market itself is nestled into the Old Bailey, cozily tucked in beneath the rising battlements of the Barracks and the Tower to the North, as well as the thick outer wall of the bailey to the South.  Between these thick walls are closely packed 2-story buildings with shop fronts on the ground level, and homes above.  While active (see the Market Calendar below), the market is a busy place, with hundreds of people coming and going throughout the day.  During the warmer months, vendors from most of the outlying farms and settlements set up tents in the market, as well as authorized merchants from further abroad.   

The market can be a pleasurable experience for the senses, with many sounds, sights and smells.  While not as cosmopolitan as other larger markets, it is still a loud place with a hundred voices, hammers from nearby smithies, musicians plying their trade, and the clatter of wagons on the flagstones.  Haverstone's  

Bakery provides the principal olfactory tone, but smells of many food vendors, as well as herbs, spices and perfumes add passing interest to the smell of the place.  Being constantly cleaned by capable and friendly boys, it lacks some of the fouler smells you might find in larger centres.  Horses and other animals are restricted from entering, and livestock trading is mostly conducted in the Eastern Bailey.  Finally, with the only approved fish market found in the Eastgate Customs Court, you won't encounter that smell either.

Typical Vendors at Market 
All established farms have a license to set up at least 20'x20' of space in the market, and most do.  These often come in the form of small tents covering a number of tables whereupon wares are displayed.  The most common types of things sold include the following; 
 Produce of all varieties 

There are many local (Dagger Falls) cottage industries run out of people's apartments in town, that use the market as their storefront for 9 months of the year.  There are 4-6 of these vendors at any time, usually just with a small table on the periphery of the market.  The types of things they sell include; 
 Finished leather goods 

There are also a selection of approved foreign merchants that are licensed to sell here from time to time, and they usually have large tents set up.  Two such vendors in the spring of 1483 include; 

 Tamuk's Rugs – a Chessentan who arrived in the middle of winter with a large caravan of rugs.  He has a factory in Westgate, and came here having heard about Charity, but never got that far.  He has set up in the Market, and seems to be doing brisk business there. 

There are many food vendors at the market, and indeed, these are often set up the entire week, rather than just on the 3 or 5 market days.  In the centre of the market, a family of halflings known as the Butterbees have a stove, many tables, and a small but popular menu of stuffed pastries and sweet drinks.  Around the market one can usually find 3-5 vendors selling skewers of meats, jugs of beer, pastries, cheeses, smoked meats, small lunch bags with a complete meal, and other street foods. 
Permanent Establishments in the Old Market 
Bolt & Button & Dutton 
Owner: Dutton Delimbry (Master Tailor) 
Employees: 3 (1 clerk, 2 seamstresses) + owner 
Description: This 2-storey building in the corner of the market has been standing as it is for more than 300 years, and has the characteristically low ceilings of buildings of this vintage.  The first floor is divided into a shop that is filled to the rafters with bolts of cloth of many varieties, colours and patterns, and countless boxes full of buttons, pins, lace and every imaginable thing necessary to making clothes and other items.  After navigating through the packed shop, one finds a busy pair of seamstresses making and fixing clothing in the back.  Their workspace appears chaotic, with the trappings of their trade covering every available space, but they seem very organized and productive.  The proprietor, Dutton Delimbyr, is a talented tailor, and makes customized clothing for the more well-to-do men in the town. 

Goods and Prices: All types of clothing except 'High Fashion' (p. 288 CRB), at standard prices.  Customized clothing available. Dutton is also skilled enough to create enchantable clothing, and has the following available; 
 +1 Robes (4 sets in various styles and sizes) 

 +1 resiliency cloak (grey, hooded) 

Trivia: Dutton is the older brother of Dr. Delimbyr, whose office is across the market.  Despite both being transplants from Waterdeep, and brothers, they don't get along. 
Max & Spenza 
Owners: Maxamillian Maerimmon & Saldvur Spenza 
Employees: 2 clerks + owners 
Description: One of the larger buildings in the market, this is one of only two 3-storey buildings.  The first and second floors are full of orderly tables, racks and shelves of goods imported from Sembia.  This is a wide range of mostly household goods and some perishables, including their most valuable commodities, wine and cheese.  A clerk on each floor will attend patrons with enthusiasm and grace.  The proprietors spend most of their time in Dagger Falls, but do make annual trips to Sembia when the roads are clear and safe. 

Goods and Prices: Semi exotic trade goods, including wines and cheese at 200% price (p.294 CRB).  Stock the finest personal items to be found in Daggerdale, including hair brushes, mirrors, perfumes and such, at 200%-300% the usual prices. 

Trivia: This is the third year that Max & Spenza have been in business at this location.  Their first summer here as traveling merchants, they held a large tent in the open market and did well enough that they decided to settle down here, and establish a reliable trade route between here and Sembia.  They live in what is rumoured to be the most lavish apartments in the town, on the third floor. 
Edge of Reason 
Owner: Grandlund Olifsen (dwarf) 
Employees: 1 clerk, 3 apprentices, 2 journeymen,  + Owner 
Description: This is a very busy smithy, now run by a group of dwarves.  The main business of the smithy is to mass produce weapons for the Sunblades.  Olifsen's apprentices and journeymen take care of that part of the business, while he takes exclusively custom orders for clients. 

Goods and Prices: Standard weapons can be purchased here for standard prices in the CRB.  Olifsen will create magic blades and hammers for customers, and can also work with Cold Iron, Adamantium and Mithril (if you bring it to him).  He charges 80% of the CRM value for these items, but typically has a 1-month backlog.  Has the following on the rack ready for purchase; 
 +1 Dagger – 20 gp 

Trivia: This smithy has been a fixture in Dagger Falls for decades, but the previous owner is childless, and decided a few years ago to sell Olifsen and his company. 
Maisie's 
Owner: Maisie Dale (Sorcerer)  
Employees: None 
Description: While technically adjacent to the market, the main entrance to this interesting occult shop is in the alley behind, though there is a small and little-known doorway that connects it to Meagre's, next door.  This is one of the smaller shops in the market, but one of the most famous around the dale.  The shop is overflowing with occult paraphernalia, most of which Maisie makes herself.  She is a master crafter of Talismans, and her wares can usually be found on the most famous and accomplished adventurers throughout the dale and further abroad.

She kept her 'surname' as part of her business persona.  She has 3 small children that all attend the grammar school at the Yellow Abbey.  Sunblades and Magistrates regularly visit her shop, and while some think they are investigating her, she is outfitting them with the tools they need to do their job, and usually serves them warm sweet teas while they wait. 
Meagre's 
Owner: Luken Meagre ('Meagre') (wizard) 
Employees: 1 clerk, 2 apprentices + Meagre 
Description: The small stone stair leading to this shop is light up at night by a minor illusion of small butterflies that flit about the door, casting a warm yellow glow.  A smaller shop in the market, this place is disorganized and messy.  It is full of books, scrolls, trinkets, garish and somewhat fiendish decorations, and other trappings of a messy wizard.  A polite and mousey girl named Abernell will help patrons navigate the cluttered aisles with apologies. 

Goods and Prices: Arcane Scrolls of first level (80% chance, 75% cost), second level (60% chance, 100% cost) and third level (50% chance, 120% cost). Meagre is a master crafter of staves and wands, and can make just about any of either, for 120% list price, in about 2-4 weeks. Any potion of item level 6 or less (100% cost).  A random assortment of magical items, currently including the following; 
 Bag of Holding Type II – 400 gp 

Trivia: Married to Maisie Dale, Meagre settled down after falling in love with her. He retired from a career as a mercenary wizard, and was a member of the FDL for nearly a decade, where he amassed his wealth and honed his skills in the Plains of Netheril.  He longs to be back in the Plains again, but now has greater responsibilities enrolled in school. 
Tethyamar House 
Owner: Thordhold Hardradda (dwarf) 
Employees: 2 clerks, 1 jeweller, 1 blacksmith (Thordhold) 
Description: This large 2-storey building underwent a face-lift last year, when it was purchased by Thordhold for use as a trading centre and factory for this enterprising dwarf and his company.  Well connected and truly neutral in the Gellinson feud, Thordhold has taken every chance to draw riches out of the struggle in the Deep, in order to help fund the retaking of the Tethyamar, as well as his own pockets.  The building now has a sturdy dwarven stone façade, complete with large burning braziers flanking the new stairs.  Inside, the first floor has been converted to one cavernous space with great pillars, and a small mezzanine, upon which many small doors lead to the private rooms of the dwarves here.  The main floor is tidy and organized, with several tables of every imaginable dwarven ware, including ales, weapons, ingots of precious metals, jewelry and many precious and semi-precious stones.  

Goods and Prices: Dwarven weapons at list price. Semi-precious stones (amethysts, citrines, opals, quartz, tourmalines) can be purchased here at 80% value.  Currently has; 
 "Geldammer" + 1 Striking, Disrupting Warhammer – 195 gp 

Trivia: Thordhold is one of the only dwarves that gets along well with both Gellinson brothers, and has been asked several times to join the Council of Silver and serve as Ambassador to the Dale. He has refused, insisting that the best thing he can do for the cause is turn it into a business. 
Charity House 
Owner: The Common Assembly of Charity.  Operated by Master Myles Mercer (Merchant's Guild) 
Employees: 4 clerks, 2 labourers 
Description: Another relatively large building in the market square, this is a 3-storey affair that has been adorned in the style of the people of Charity; diamond-paned leaded glass windows fill every opening, fine wrought-iron lamps burn 'candle coal' all around the building and inside, and the place is filled with warm details that are a delight to the senses.  The first floor is divided into the front and back.  In front, there are many goods from Charity to be purchased, while the back is a stock room filled to the rafters.  The second floor is full of meeting rooms, offices and drawing rooms for company.  The third is for apartments for the staff living here. 

Goods and Prices: Glassworks, woolen goods, lamp oil, metal housewares (copper, wrought iron, brass) all at 100% list value, steel and brass ingots (80% list), dark beers (including Charnel Stout – 80% list), goat and sheep cheeses, including Nettle Yarg (100% list).  Firearms and ammunition (200% list). 

Trivia: As well as hosting the Master Mercer and his staff, this is also the home of Ambassador Whitmore.  The drawing rooms of the 2nd floor are host to many diplomatic discussions and trade negotiations. 
Haverstone's Bakery 
Owner: Hazel Haverstone and family 
Employees: 4 apprentice bakers, 1 expert baker, 1 master baker (Hazel) 
Description: This building has been a fixture in Dagger Falls for more than 50 years, with 4 generations of Haverstones operating it.  It is a large 2-storey building with public entrances on the west and south faces.  From 06h00-12h00 every day, this is the busiest place in the Falls.  More than 100 people come through here every morning to buy their day's bread, pastries, or a cup of hot tea.  In recent years during the warmer months there are tables set outside for patrons to enjoy their breakfast, mimicking the cosmopolitan cafes of much larger cities. 

Goods and Prices: Breakfast special – Cooked mutton and gravy in a pasty, cup of tea and sugar – 2cp, other bakery items at 100% list price (80% for day-olds), very wide selection  

Trivia: Master Baker Hazel Haverstone was captain of the Sunblades 1st Company over 30 years ago.  She was the first soldier in that organization to achieve a rank of captain without any battlefield experience, being mostly in charge of logistics at the Castle.  Many of her children and grand children have held this position over the decades, and their spears, shields and cloaks adorn the wall behind the main counter of the bakery, a symbol of their pride in their service.

The south half of the building main floor is a shop where many racks and shelves are piled with iron housewares, and the shop specialty, armour.  The second floor is for the Oakhill family, being Owan, his wife, and 3 sons, 2 of which are apprentice smiths.

 +1 Resilient Chain Shirt – 450 gp 

 +1 Adamantine Breastplate  - 1500 gp

There was an incident with Owan's growing magical abilities that forced the family to leave.  In Dagger Falls, the family became ardent followers of Amaunator and supporters of the Cathedral.  They have a good contract to provide shields and chain shirts for the Sunblades, and many Knights of the Lambent Shield are protected by wares from this smithy.  All of their products have etchings of the Morning Lord prominently displayed.

The main floor is divided into a workshop in the back, and retail shop in the front.  The clerk is a young halfling girl named Silvie Rosewater, who is quite gregarious and convincing.  Many unwary shoppers leave with much more product and much less coin than intended.

Trivia: Blossom has contracts with over a dozen foresters to bring her the rare goods she needs.  She's always looking for more.

The Hooded Lantern 
Owner: Cordelia Westember ("Mme Westember") 
Employees: 1 hostess (Cordelia), 3 servers, 3 kitchen staff, 1 expert cook  
Description: This is a single-storey walk-up nestled against the larger Quick Axes.  A stone stair leads up to the fine entrance of this tavern, wherein the stately hostess greets all visitors in her charming Cormyrean accent.  The tavern caters to middle class patrons who want a quiet place to have a good meal.  The decour is warm and formal, with quarter-sawn oak panelling and furniture, warm lamps on each table, and many servers bringing food and drink to their patrons with great courtesy.   

Goods and Prices: Good quality meals (5sp) and wines (1-5 sp each). The lunch and dinner menus each have many options.  Private rooms are available for 1 gp/hour.

She is always dressed in stunning dresses which accentuate her tall and curvy features, and her staff are dressed similarly.  She often hosts events for the clergy and body of the local Sunite church here, and it is well known that Troia Truestar prefers to meet people in a private room here. 
Dr. Delimbry 
Owner: Dr. Drago Delimbyr (Master Physician) 
Employees: 1 clerk, 2 Priory Sisters, 1 apprentice physician 
Description: Nearly hidden between two much larger establishments (Quick Axes and Liberty Hill House), this small doctor's office is a two-story building original to the settlement, complete with low ceilings and small dark windows.  The first floor is made up of exam and treatment rooms for patients, and is spartan and clean.  Dr. Delimbry and his staff treat all manner of injuries and sickness here, and the two Priory Sisters are the real draw.  These two, Lisbeth and Corinna, are both from the Charnel Priory in Charity, and although being novitiates of their order, the reputation of their order brings many patients to the doors.  The second floor is an infirmary, where patients can stay longer term. 

Goods and Prices: Treat Wounds 2d8 (1 sp), Treat Wounds 2d8+10 (3 sp), Treat Wounds 2d8+20 (5 sp), Treat Disease +3 save (5 sp), Treat Disease +5 save (10 sp), Treat Poison +3 save (2 sp), Treat Poison +5 save (5 sp).  Also sell medical supplies at 100% list.  Patients requiring long-term care are charged 1 gp/day for their admission here, on top of their treatments. There is room for 10 such patients here. 

Trivia: Dr. Delimbyr is the brother of the proprietor the tailor shop across the market square, Dutton Delimbry.  Both are from Waterdeep, and don't get along.  Dr. Delimbry is a bit of a strange bachelor who while extremely eligible, tends to avoid the public outside of his duties in his office.

The tents are full of produce, especially the fruits from Liberty Hill (pears, apples, berries) and the grains from Crossed Blades (barley, hops). The House is an informal embassy of Liberty Hill, which doubles as a hostel for Amaunatori pilgrims.  The large building houses all the staff from the farm, visitors and the pilgrims, with room for 50 or more people in total.  The inside of the building is clean, functional and plain.

Quick Axes 
Owner: Dalak Quick Axe 
Employees: 1 bar tender (Daygle Stonebridge, Dwarf), 1 innkeeper (Jewel Stonebridge, female Dwarf, wife to Daygle), 4 waitresses, 1 cook, 2 kitchen staff, 2 chambre maids, 1 master brewer (Dalak), 1 apprentice brewer. 

Description: This is one of the larger buildings in the Falls.  After the defeat of the cult of Bane in the town in 1372, the buildings that housed the cult were given to the Eastern Storm in gratitude for their efforts in freeing the town and the nearby mines.  The Eastern Storm left town shortly after, and deeded those properties to their now-retired companion, Dalak Quickaxe, and his brother, Balak.  Both were expert brewers, and built a successful business quickly.  In the early 1400's, the dwarves demolished the small buildings and replaced them with the large 2-storey stone building that dominates the Old Square today.  This is dwarven construction which is more functional than beautiful, but the place is warm and inviting.  On Market Days, the place is packed with merchants and buyers who have come to take advantage of the market, and there is usually live music these nights.  The main taproom is flanked by two roaring fires, which usually have many locals seated around them drinking the fine beers that the establishment produces.  Upstairs are several good-quality rooms that can be rented by visitors to town, as well as one suite where the operators, Daygle and Jewel Stonebridge live.  In the winter months, the taproom attracts locals who are looking for a warm place with great beer and music, in contrast to the other less-entertaining local establishments. 

Goods and Prices: The drink, food and rooms here are all of good quality, though not fancy. 
 Mug of beer (Sungold Ale, BitterRock Porter, Black Iron Lager) – 1 cp 

Trivia: Dalak was nearly killed twice during the 1372 campaign to liberate Dagger Falls, starting with a run-in with a terrible lion in Anauroch (who's pelt now resides at Charnel Priory). The two opened the establishment together, but Balak died in 1438 of natural causes.  Balak then gave over control of the tavern and inn to his employees and friends, the Stonebridge's, who have run it ever since.  Balak is a recluse who spends most of his time in the basement brewery doing what he loves most; making beer.  He is one of the oldest inhabitants of the town, and one of only a few that remembers first hand events from the 13th century onward. 

The Myth Drannor Embassy 
"The loneliest place in Dagger Falls."  That is what the friendliest of the locals call the Embassy.  Others call it a waste of space, or a blatant offence to the people of the Dale.  This is because there are very few elves left in this part of the Dalelands, as they seem preoccupied with some trouble in their own land, not terribly far away.  Still others call the Embassy "The Tomb", as it was the site of the grisliest battle in the history of Dagger Falls.  In Eleint, 1372 DR, The Eastern Storm, accompanied by William Cormaeril and his hearth guard assaulted what was then "Dark Sun Temple", a large fortified temple dedicated to Cyric.  Inside was a small army of undead, and a protracted battle lasted for many hours, as reinforcements were called in to help clear the temple out. 

For many years afterward, the building lay empty.  Even though it had been thoroughly cleaned out and deconsecrated by many powerful priests.  It wasn't until the late 1390's, when Faerun was just over the most tumultuous years of the Spell Plague, that the elves returned.  The House Miratar, who often supported the Whitegates in the mountains and the growing plains of Netheril, purchased the building, and hired many local craftsmen to put it back into a serviceable condition.  For nearly a decade, there were usually a group of elves at the Embassy, and they worked closely with the local government, helping with local development and defensive measures.  However, by 1415, the elves all left, and for the most part haven't returned.  What is happening in Myth Drannor has them all occupied most of the time.  The windows of this now-fine building are usually dark, and almost no one resides there.  The Redjacks are under contract to provide two guards there at all times, and they have a local custodian, Merriadock Burgundy (a middle-aged halfling male) who keeps the place tidy. 

The building itself is a fortified 3-story affair, integrated into the thick walls of the southern ramparts.  Indeed, the second floor has a rampart encircling its perimeter around which guards can walk.  There are many locked doors into the Embassy on this level, but few dare try the handles. 

Trivia (Known by Solicitor Jeska):  The elves of Myth Drannor are fighting a two-sided battle for their home.  They are battling devils that are deep beneath the surface of their ancient settlement, as well as Prince Brennus Tanthul, one of the rulers of Netheril.  Prince Brennus is obsessed with gaining entry to Myth Drannor, likely to access the archives there and study the now dead mythals.  His agents provide a constant mid-intensity conflict in the area around the elven city. 

They have sent an 'Observer', Jeska, to reside in the Dale and to assist in the reclamation of Thistle Ridge, which they have some interest in.  Jeska answers to Elowen Miratar, one of the leaders of that noble House of Myth Drannor. 
There is a store of emergency supplies in the old catacombs of the Embassy. 
Arrival at the Embassy 
For those characters arriving at the Embassy for the first time; 

Arrival at the Embassy is through a blocky entrance room, about 20' on each side, mostly cold stone.  It is divided by a long desk on the other side of which is a dour-faced man in leather armour and a red cloak and sash; the Redjack mercenary that is one of two that guard the place.  It is his main job to steer people away from the Embassy, but you have been invited, and it isn't long before he has the caretake, Merriadock Burgundy, summoned to bring you in. 

"Merry", as he prefers to be called is a middle-aged halfling with shaggy grey hair and a kind face under some reading glasses perched on his nose.  He wears sensible clothes, and has his sleeves rolled up and some gardening tools hanging out of his pockets as he smiles and greets you.  "Welcome to The Tomb, friends!  I'm so happy to have real company!  Merry at your service, sirs and madams.  Let's get you inside straight away!  I will see you to your rooms, and we'll share some tea at the Symbaern Hearth (Location 3 on the map) once you're settled in!" 

"The Tomb?" you think to yourself.  "This place is the best kept secret garden in the Dales" as you are lead into the main chambre.  The first thing you notice is that it is as bright as day inside, clearly a magical effect.  The sound of trickling water from a fountain in the centre of the large, round, main chambre is pleasing, and a strong scent that is somewhere between fresh pine and lavender is mixed with the mouth-watering smell of baking coming from the kitchens below. 

The main chambre is dominated by great pillars in the middle that soar to the ceiling, between which flowering cherry blossoms arch from pillar to pillar, making a fine canopy.  About the chambre are many potted plants and flowers of many varieties, and you can see that the main work of the caretaker the maintenance of the flora of the Embassy. Five great hearths surround the outer wall, though only one has a fire going.  In front of it, a table has been set with tea, sandwiches, cakes and wine.  The furnishings in front of the other hearths are covered with clean white linens, and their carpets are rolled up next the furniture. 

The second floor is divided into two spaces; a walway around the large opening to the floor below which leads to the main conference room above the blocky entrance you came though, and a very comfortable set of meeting rooms around a grand entry that is also full of flora of many varieties.  Looking through some of the doors, you can see that the furniture of the meeting rooms is stacked neatly and covered, with rugs rolled up and put off to the side.  However, the plants in each room have receive a great deal of care, and are flourishing. 

The third floor is made up of 8 fine bedrooms surrounding a fine common area.  Each room has trees providing canopies for the beds, and most of these rooms are now serviceable, having recently been turned out by Merry in anticipation of your arrival.  One room (#25) is used by the two Redjacks, but the others are all available for you to settle in as Jeska has appointed.
