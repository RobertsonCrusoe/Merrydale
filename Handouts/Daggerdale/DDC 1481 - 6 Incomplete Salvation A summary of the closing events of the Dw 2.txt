DDC – 1481 - 6: Incomplete Salvation – A summary of the closing events of the Dwimmerholme Abductions 
9 Marpenoth, 1481 - Journal of Nathan Gale 
From GFD: 
Another entry from the journal of the missing gunslinger 
-GFD 
The 8th of Marpenoth was the bloodiest day any of us have ever seen. It started with the frontal assault on the cave-network in which Vetis was hiding his command-element of the coven. Bolstered by the Sun Blades assigned to help us, we managed to slaughter dozens of cultists and evil creatures in the cave and woods nearby. 
Using the intelligence from Xaphan, we were able to set up an ambush on Vetis and the rest of his minions as they teleported from Dwimmerholme. A slow, steady effusion of blood followed, as we cut down wave after wave of villains as they attempted their escape from Dwimmerholme. The victory would have been decisive, if not for the liberty of Xaphan and the unnamed female tiefling that managed an escape from the cave. 
(From GFD: The female tiefling was Vetis, and she remains at large as I write this.) 
The 9th was a day of convalescence for the town and ourselves. Some of us had a hard time coming to grips with what we had seen; children turned into soulless husks, families destroyed, souls being taken from a sea of torment on a fiendish world. But there was still work to do. Late in the day, a platoon of Foresters from the 4th Company arrived in Dwimmerholme exactly on schedule. Knowing that there was still one small group of cultists in the woods, and 4 citizens still unaccounted for, we set off in the middle of the night to finish this nasty business. 
By the first rays of light on the morning of the 10th, us and our escort of 24 Foresters surrounded the last encampment of Sons of Bel. Lt. Leedo Forester, the commanding officer of the platoon, had been told by Mayor Winters (who had been his commanding officer in the Foresters only two years previous) to give tactical authority to White Cloak Sgt. Paros. So it was that Paros came up with a plan to send in our team to secure the citizens (husks and intact alike), before the Foresters closed in. In the end, all the citizens were safe and secure, and the platoon of Foresters destroyed the cultists to a man. We found that the remaining cultists here were more men masquerading as Zhents. They had actually been under the command of Doghan, who we had killed early in the campaign. They did have one interesting 'husk' with them; a dryad that they used to control the forest to hinder the travel of the enemies of the 
cult. Without Doghan to command it, however, it had been inert since his death. 
(GFD: 'Doghan is actually Dungorgan, and Gale is mistaken about it being him that controlled the Dryad Husk, but all's well that ends well) 
Our team returned with the Foresters and the citizens by mid-day on the 10th. A package had arrived for me earlier in the day. I opened it to find that it was a copy of a ritual called the 'Prayer of Adduction', from the booked called the 'Codex of Prayer' that I had learned of. I brought the prayer to the local Magistrate, Renfrew, who then assembled as much clergy as he could to begin the 
prayers. The ritual took many hours for each derelict body, and so the ritual went on well into the 12th of Marpenoth, with an exhausted clergy returning the souls of 7 victims. 
Though 7 people had their souls brought back to their bodies, at the end of the day, the biggest challenge was attempting the ritual on the derelict of the dryad. Several attempts were made to free the soul of the creature, but by the witching hour, Renfrew announced that the ritual could not help a fey being. She was taken out of town, and burned in a field. Many stood on the walls and towers of the town to watch the distant pyre; the last bitter flames of this devastating tragedy. 
As the flames from the dryad's pyre began to dwindle, the light of day struck on the 12th. People flocked to the small cathedral of Amaunator in such numbers as Dwimmerholme had never experienced. Renfrew, exhausted from days of praying, seemed completely refreshed when so many parishioners came for morning prayer. As he was greeting the flock gathering on the steps of his small cathedral, Paros and I both decided it was time to leave. There were loose ends to wrap up, and reports to write. "After Matins" was the retort of the Avenger, who was already walking towards the rest of the Amaunatori. 
The rest of us packed up our things in the cool morning of late fall, awaiting Shea's return from what turned out to be the longest sermon of Renfrew's career. By the time it was done, all of us were at the gates, packed and ready to go. She came out of the cathedral, embraced both the Magistrate and the Mayor, and then strode towards us. 
"A new day has fallen on Dwimmerholme. Everything will be just fine" Shea said. The morning light reflecting off her eyes made it impossible to look straight at her. 
With that, we are off for Daggerfalls, to check in with Reks, and follow up on some other leads that we expect are related to other branches of these Sons of Bel.
