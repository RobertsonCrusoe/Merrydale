Between Round 1 and 2 of the Mountain of Mayhem - Vignettes and Jasper's Round 1
by Todd Burry (Ser Rowan/Mari), Scott Mclaren (Jeska), Gus Miranda (Jasper), with Les Blackwell (DM)
Email thread: "Between round 1 and 2 of the Mountain of Mayhem", 20-25 Feb 2024

A collaborative thread set in the gap between the first and second rounds of the Thistle Ridge Competition (Capture the Flag and Three Flags Up, 20 Ches 1483 DR). Contains four player-written vignettes: Todd's mountaintop aftermath and Mari's wagon stall, Scott's Jeska-Fiona exchange, and Gus's extended Jasper narrative recounting Round 1 from Jasper's bewildered perspective. Also includes Les's rules for Round 2 and an AI-generated illustration of Team Chrysalis resting at the wagon.

VIGNETTE 1: ATOP THE MOUNTAIN (Todd Burry, 20 Feb 2024)

Todd's note: Hey gents. It occurred to me that we were standing face to face with each other and the white cloaks, and there would have been an opportunity to say something. I wrote a small narrative if anyone wants to add anything.

* * * * *

Atop the crafted wooden peak, Ser Rowan stood, hammer and flag in hand, his presence a testament to the tumult and valor that had preceded this moment. Moments ago, empowered by magic, he had leapt across the battlefield, his landings thunderous, carving the earth beneath him as if to mark the passage of a giant.

With the flag secured, he had vanquished one of the Dale brothers, yet now faced the remaining twin, vengeance burning in the latter's eyes. Retreating was no longer an option; the platform's edge whispered warnings beneath his heels. The flag's position, a beacon of their struggle, left him precariously balanced between victory and the void.

Then, as fate hung in the balance, the Dale brother's advance halted, an arrow finding its mark, sending him tumbling backward into unconsciousness. Ser Rowan, in disbelief, cast his gaze over his shoulder. Jeska, silhouette against the sky, stood balancing on the fence behind him, like a heron on a post.

"Much obliged, Governess," Rowan exhaled, his breaths heavy, his visage a canvas of exertion painted with the sweat of battle. He eyed the arrow still biting into his flesh, contemplating a moment's care, yet his concern turned outward to the fallen adversaries at his feet. "T'is just a flesh wound; he'll be alright," he murmured, more to the winds than to any ear.

Suddenly, a shield arced over the edge, heralding Mari's arrival. "Are you all right, Ser Rowan?" her voice, touched with the lilting warmth of concern.

"Aye, Mum. Thanks to ye," he replied, a smile breaking through the weariness.

Mari, with a halfling's resolve, clambered onto the platform, her words a gentle jest at their shared plight. "Whoever designed these platforms sure doesn't like halflings. Yondalla, have mercy on me joints!"

The shared moment of victory, the laughter that followed, spoke of deeper bonds forged in the crucible of competition. "Can you believe it? We won!" Mari exclaimed, her eyes alight with the joy of their unexpected triumph.

"Aye, Mum. And we won in a way I would have least expected," Ser Rowan agreed, his voice a reflection of the day's surprises. "Jasper is one clever lad," he added.

"A clever lad indeed," Mari readily agreed.

Their camaraderie, a beacon as bright as the flag they defended, led them down from the mountain, Ser Rowan carrying Mari with a knight's care, back to where their companions awaited.

VIGNETTE 3: MARI'S WAGON (Todd Burry, 21 Feb 2024)

Peony the pony nibbled on a large apple set before her, a well-deserved treat for hauling Mari's wagon from the embassy to the tournament grounds.

Behind her, the cart was arranged into a makeshift stall, displaying various trade goods Mari had transported from the Tesh Valley farm. The assortment included jars of the renowned "Sweetberry Yellow" honey, hefty wheels of cheese from the Weatherbee dairy farm, and a sizable keg of Buckwheat Blonde Ale, generously provided by the dwarves at Stationer's Hall.

And there, Ser Rowan found himself seated on a wooden crate, his body hunched over, face painted with the flush of exertion, as sweat dripped steadily to the earth before him.

Lucan sat across, allowing Mari to attend his wounds.

Mari broke the silence, "That Missus Oakbough knocked ye off yer feet in one shot."

Ser Rowan chimed in, "Aye, and she almost did the same to me. If it weren't for yer prayers I would have been done for. Cheers for that. I haven't been tickled by the gods that much since I was an altar boy."

"Her luck seemed to run out later in the fight," Mari replied, offering a note of reassurance.

"That's because the morning sun was in 'er eyes. We won't be so lucky next round. If it's all the same to you, Mum, I won't be charging Trickshot Zelly a second time," Ser Rowan expressed, his tone a mix of determination and deference.

"Yes, please! My healing was depleted more than I hoped in the first round. Not ter worry though, Yondala has much help left ter give," Mari responded confidently.

"A knight is sworn to lay down his life for his liege," Ser Rowan declared, touching his hand to his heart, though his voice carried a jesting undertone.

Mari simply smiled in response, returning her focus to the meticulous task of healing.

ROUND 2 RULES (Les Blackwell, 23 Feb 2024)

The Rules for Round 2:

2. Three Flags Up
- Your team all starts in your moated area
- Each team has 3 flags that it must bring to the top of the mountain, 1 in each other corner of the field
- You must get your flag into your barrel at the top of the Mountain for it to count
- You may not touch the other team's flags or barrel
- The first team to get all 3 flags into their barrel wins

VIGNETTE 4: JASPER'S ROUND 1 (Gus Miranda, 24 Feb 2024)

"Oh boy!" Jasper said to Mortimer, nestled safely in his favourite spot in the young Mage's satchel, "this is going to be just like old times"  The wizard practically trembled with anticipation as Team Chrysalis was led out from their tent towards their starting position for the first round of today's competition.  "Do you remember when Abner, Chester and I took second place at the Harvestide Pumpkin Games?  Ha!  That... well... that was a good time...?"

Jasper's mood turns from jubilant to doubt in a second.  While it was true, he had taken second place that year, it was more due to the efforts of his two elder brothers more than anything he had done.  They were young men who had seen over twenty winters by then, yet Jasper had barely seen fifteen.  Abner was a farmer and Chester was a soldier.  And Jasper... well... if the book wasn't too heavy, or on too high a shelf, he could probably manage it.

The squirrel familiar nestled deeper into the satchel as the teams entered the arena and the assembled crowds roared with cheers and jeers.

Jasper's excitement returned as the adrenaline began to flow into his veins. "Remember 'The Plan' and you'll be alright lad!" said Ser Rowan as he gave the young wizard a helping hand getting up onto the platform.

"Right.  Er...?  Hmm...  The Plan. Er...?" He had vague recollections of sitting with these people at a table in some weird indoor Elven garden as serious discussions of... something?  All he could really remember of last night was Lady Jeska leaping across to the chandelier at Quick Axes, as graceful as a leaf falling off a tree in autumn, and having the best pork pie of his life.

It had been a good night...  Hadn't it?

By rights he shouldn't even be upright this morning, given how much he drank.  But whatever Mr Burgundy had done to the eggs at that morning's breakfast had certainly done the trick at putting him right in time for the competition.

"The Plan...?"  No.  Try as he might, Jasper could not remember what it was that he was supposed to be doing.  Come to think of it, he now realised that less than a day ago he hadn't even met any of these people.

The signal to start!

"Ok... umm.. good luck Luc-..."

There was an arrow sticking out of Lucan's chest.  The Sembian looked over at Jasper, confusion in his eyes; Jasper looked at Lucan, horror in his.

"Ser Rowan, I think they're shooting at us!" shouted Jasper in disbelief, but Ser Rowan had already leapt off the platform and was now hovering in mid air like a second orange sun shining over the field.

A ray of healing magic struck Lucan just as his body crumpled to the ground.  Jasper reached out to him, but with surprising strength Lucan slapped Jasper's hand away and simply whispered, "Go!"

Jasper scrambled down the platform and off into the field.

"Right.  The Plan! The Plan! The Plan!"  He could only remember Fiona's face as the drunkards had accosted her last night, and...

One of the twins was nearly to Jeska's location, and our flag.  "Use one of the Grease scrolls," said Mortimer the squirrel familiar, peering out from the satchel.

A brief memory from last night of stumbling into a magic shoppe with a bag of coin, and exiting with a pork pie in one hand and a beer mug in the other.  That can't be right.  He reached into his satchel, and there were several new scrolls.

The spell cast, the grease laid, but the twin easily bounced over it.

"You know your Great Aunt Mathilda would've had him flat on his face," said the squirrel.  "Maybe you should try magic one day."

"You're not helping," Jasper took a moment, sheltered by the Mountain to take stock.

"Right.  The Plan.  You wouldn't happen to know what The Plan is, would you?"

"Of course I do! I paid attention," Mortimer's voice dripped with contempt.

"Well, tell me!" said Jasper, as he saw the other twin come around the bend of the structure.

"Well, the first part is easy!" said the squirrel.  "That's where I jump out of this satchel and go hide somewhere safe until it is all over!" and with that, the grey rodent leapt out of the satchel, and into the safety of the Mountain's inner structure.

The Twin was now bearing down on Jasper, a glint of malice in his eye.

"Right!  Err..  stay back!  I really don't want to have to do this! I'll have you know I killed a man at a tavern once with this spell!"

There was no sign of the Twin slowing down, so there was nothing for it but for Jasper to take out his canteen, give it a vigorous shake and cast its Hydraulic Push.  Water exploded out of the canteen with viscous pressure. Jasper looked away, as he expected the poor man to have been thrown back to the wall, and knocked unconscious in a heap of blood and metal.  Poor soul.

Jasper looked back, but the soldier was still standing in front of him, wet and annoyed.  That look of malice had turned to a look of murder as he swung his sword at the wizard.

"Wait!" screamed Jasper, leaping back just fast enough to avoid being cut in half.  "I mean... someone's going to get hurt!"

"That's the idea, boy," the swordsman took another step towards Jasper, who then scrambled back as far as he could go where he found himself in the relative safety of Mari's company.  The Twin looked at the mage, looked back at his brother who had now claimed the flag, and turned away.  It seemed that they were at least following "The Plan."

"He tried to kill me!" said Jasper to Mari, who was desperately trying to keep an eye on everyone, and working hard to keep them standing.

"Of course he did, dear! You did try to kill him first with all that water, though. Get back to it!  Follow The Plan and you'll be alright!" shouted Mari as she dashed off with another spell on Ser Rowan.

As he watched her go, Jasper caught the eye of the Cormyrean wizard who was looking at him from across the field.  Not really knowing what to do, he gave him a small wave and a half-hearted smile.

"He's a creepy one, don't you think?" he said to Mortimer, before he remembered that his familiar had abandoned him and he was now speaking to no one at all.

More trouble from the twins, but a quick Telekinetic Projectile to get that flag might do the-  damn it!  Jasper looked back at the Cormyrean to see him smile broadly in triumph, his cantrip having been counterspelled.

"Right. Ok.  Well, no more of that then," he said to himself as he clambered up onto the lower tier of the Mountain.

A great commotion was happening at the top tier, but Jasper couldn't see much of what was happening.  He could see both the flags were up there, and the occasional flash of steel weapons or orange cloth.

And then the flag was down, on the ground and reachable.  Lucan was close, he could take it.  But not close enough.

"Wait!" Jasper shouted,  "I remember The Plan!"

____

A few moments later, the entire Team Chrysalis was standing at the top of the Mountain, victorious in the first round of the competition.

Shortly after, they were led back into their tent.  Lady Jeska moved off to have a word with Fiona, Mari hurried off towards the Team Chrysalis cart - with a protesting Lucan being dragged along claiming he wasn't that badly hurt - to open up sales and look after the young Sembian.  Ser Rowan, not far behind, planted himself beside them with a well-earned tankard of ale, as gangs of curious children, and groups of assorted halflings came up to shake his hand and pepper him with questions.

Jasper stood for a moment, taking it all in. A broad smile crept across his lips.

Then just as suddenly: the smile faded, all colour drained, and a look of total horror fixed itself upon his face.

"He... I mean,... H- h- h-... umm.. HE TRIED TO KILL ME!"

Jasper's legs buckled, and he slumped down to the ground.
