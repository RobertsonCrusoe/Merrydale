===HARVESTIDE===
By the time things were cooled off in Dagger Falls, Harvestide was upon the Dales. Winter was coming, and it the DE were way past overdue to leave. All signs indicated that the Tablets were weeks ahead of the DE, but they planned on catching them anyway.

Dagger Falls had become a place of peace again in those days. Rewel Thunderstorm, though having made peace with Cassamar, still begat the church of Lathander in Dagger Falls. Sparrow stayed in this town too, to do his goddess’ bidding, and opened his temple in conjunction with the new Morning Lord’s Temple. The three evil faiths were not heard from or seen in Dagger Falls for many years to come, and peace was upon the place.

But Dagger Falls is doomed to have some turmoil. In the wake of the Paladin of Shar, a cult of Shar blossomed in this town. The oratory of Gaebril, given in the Eastern Bailey, convinced many to join this faith, to help them enjoy the new lives they had, and to forget the evils of the past.

An oratory given by Maturin that very day also spawned an intense, but briefly lived following of Ilmater. To this day, the faiths of Lathander and Shar are the most influential in the Dale and in the town itself. The temple to Mystra is also a very busy place, and it is well known that this attraction brings many practitioners of magic, and their money, to the town every year.

==THE ROAD WEST==
Though the first Harvestide celebrations in over 20 years were being held in Dagger Falls, the DE stayed only until after breakfast of the 2nd of Marpenoth. They took to the road, traveling as light and as fast as they could. They intended to catch the Beholder Tablets, but they would not do so this winter.

Less than half a day out of Dagger Falls, the DE was ambushed by the 5th Co. of Fist, Gumbottom’s Guard. This company of 10 hill giants was lead by Yin Xu at this point, and his leadership nearly proved effective enough to turn back the DE. Several were hurt badly, but they still managed to defeat enough of the giants to route them. Clearly, the road was still being watched by the forces of the Beholders, and the journey would not be easy.

A few days after that, the DE was nearly exterminated. They encountered beholder-kin upon the road. A large, tree-like beholder attempted to have the DE turn back by using magical suggestions, but this proved ineffective. A battle broke out, and 4 directors joined the battle to protect the overseer tree-beholder that tried to end the encounter peacefully. The DE was overcome by these foes, and if were not for the intervention of the Entropists, all would have been lost.

These two men, now introduced as Amon and Zuth, of Chessenta, explain that they were pursing the Tablets, but realized they were not suited to travel in the desert. They turned back, hoping to find a way West through the dangerous lands to the south. The two men had a long discussion with the DE. They ex-plained that Zantiid and Xulla were simply ‘second rank’ beholders. They knew of the staves, but little about them. The two seemed most determined to get to the Tablets, but did not state their purpose entirely.

===ADDAS BABAR===

The DE was soon in the desert again. Just as the badlands begin to give way to sands and dunes, one finds the citadel of Addas Babar, perched high upon an island of rock overlooking the first oasis on the long road through the desert.

The DE decided they would not circumnavigate the outposts on this journey. Boregar was on a quest to put to rights all those wrongs he had left behind, and the DE would have been without him if they would not fight his battle with him.

The DE made short work of this fortress, and left it a grave of Zhents and a ruin.

===BHAERLITH===

Bhaerlith was a difficult conquest, given the heavily fortified and geographical nature of the place. At length, however, the DE were victorious, and the fortress was liberated. Of the bounty found in this fortress, the Gond Pistola was recovered and returned to Boregar. Many other powerful tools of war were also recovered.

The many slaves that were there stayed on. Many were devout followers of Ilmater, and though Maturin had clearly been excommunicated, they took it as a sign that they too should isolate themselves and endure the lonesome desert.

To this day, Bhaerlith is an abbey dedicated to Ilmater. The wandering priest Shildevan came to the abbey not long after the DE had departed, and assumed leadership of the flock.

===OLOMAA===

Travel through the desert was made somewhat easier this time around, though Boregar worried that they would not make it out before winter. He explained that even the native nomads of this land brave the underdark during winter, rather than face the harshness of Anauroch’s cold season. They DE was making good time, though, as they were more effectively supplied after each fortress they sacked.

At Olomaa, they found that they did not get there in time to sack the place. The fortress was in ruins, and not a soul could be found upon initial arrival. Boregar investigates further, and finds that a lone priest of Cyric is living there. This man, Ful’Zan, explains that a beholder and the Fist came through and killed everyone. This settlement had been the desert bastion of Cyric, but the temple is now in ruins, and the sole survivor of the place has resorted, not with much loathe, to eating the flesh of the dead flock of Cyricists there. 

When Maturin shows up, Ful’Zan shrieks and hisses, and says “You didn’t leave when you were told! Now you can’t catch it!” Apparently, this crazed priest knew something of Maturin, but the Boregar attempts to subdue the man, and he makes off into the desert. His trail could not be followed.

The DE stays on here for a day to burn bodies and desecrate the remains of the Temple. They ponder the visions from ‘Torm’ that were visited upon Maturin back in Dagger Falls, and conclude that Cyric was posing as Torm again (the ‘Fool Knight’ being his favourite god to impersonate) to motivate the DE to follow the Tablets, and leave the church of Cyric in Dagger Falls alone.

===MATURIN AND THE LION===
Many weeks later, the DE arrives back at Vuerthyl. As Boregar had feared, winter had come and the DE where trapped. They prepare the ruined keep there for their occupation throughout the winter. 

The winter is cruel in Anauroch, to say the least. The remaining five men of the DE train hard all winter, but on New Year’s Eve, the time of reckoning for Maturin and Grandfather is nigh. 

Late that night, as the full moon broke through the relentless clouds and snows, and the eye of Muir’Quaggoth was seen by the Grey Knight in its radiance. A great roar is heard off in the desert somewhere, and the DE do what they can to equip Maturin for the fight. Not long after, Grandfather lets out a roar just outside the doors. Maturin takes the floor, braced for battle, while the other 4 wait at the stairs. They know that this can be a fight between Grandfather and Maturin, alone.

Grandfather crashes open the door and stands on the threshold while Maturin calls out the beast;

Grandfather growled low and strode into the room. The two god-fated combatants stared at each other for a moment, and then the Lion sprung! From the outset, things looked horrible for Maturin. He was knocked over and pinned right away, the giant lycanthrope poised above his prone form. The Lion bit deep into the knight, and tore and tore. All the while, however, Maturin was pulling the beast onto his silver-plated spikes upon his breast plate, and making what attacks were allowed him with the Lathkule blade. The blade was thrust up through the beast’s belly, and it gave a great roar before delivering one final great bite to the knight. It tore deep into his neck, and then both combatants ceased their struggle.

Maturin was hardly perceptible from beneath the giant beast, and neither were moving. When his companions came to his side however, and hefted the heavy monster off of him, they found him still breathing. Though he was covered in blood, both from himself and the Lion, they could make out the shape of a gray tear under the Knights right eye. The knight breathed, and his companions helped him to his knees. Maturin stayed kneeling for what seemed like an eternity. His breathing caused him to heave, and he steadied himself with the bloodied blade that had slain the huge Lion that lay at his side. 

He looked up, and the grey tear sparkled for just a moment, and Maturin nodded wearily. At that moment, a scraping was heard at the wind-assaulted door. Warburden had returned, and stepped in to nuzzle his masters bloodied head. With what strength he had, the weary knight raised one bloodied gauntlet to stroke the beasts great face in silence, while his companions, silent as well, looked on.
Dorian was heard to whisper “Ilmater be praised”.

This final portion of the Daggerdale Expedition is most eloquently told through the words of the Grey Knight, found in his memoirs years after these events;


''Ilmater's boon 'neath the green-eyed moon

Did tame the were-lion's wroth;

The desert trails had their secret tales

For the sons of Muir-Quaggoth;

But a Damaran knight is an outlandish sight,

To rise from Anauroch's haze

And a night of fear in the Rogue Dragon Year

Was Grandfather's end of days.''
