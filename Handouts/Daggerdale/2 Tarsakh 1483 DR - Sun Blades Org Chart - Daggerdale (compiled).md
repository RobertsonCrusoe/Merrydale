# **⚔ THE SUN BLADES OF DAGGERDALE ⚔**

Organizational Chart — Compiled from Wiki & Archive Sources, c. 1479–1483 DR

**Reading This Chart**

This document covers two distinct eras of the Sun Blades: the original Church-administered structure (pre-1482), and the reorganized Concordat structure (post-Elesias 1482). Named personnel are drawn from wiki articles, narratives, handouts, and the Daggerdale Concordat. Where ranks evolved over time, both are noted. Strengths given for the Concordat structure are from the Concordat's Standard Order; earlier numbers are from the 1479 Setting document (\~400 soldiers) and other narrative sources. A separate section lists Storied Veterans from the historical record of Thistle Ridge (early 15th century DR).   
I. Overall Command & Civilian Authority  
The Sun Blades answer to a dual authority. Before 1482, they were a military arm of the Church of Amaunator, answering ultimately to the Whitegate family. After the Daggerdale Concordat (Elesias 1482), administrative and financial control passed to the Governors' Assembly, though the officer corps remained overwhelmingly Amaunatori.   
CIVILIAN AUTHORITY (PRE-CONCORDAT)  
**Lord Luminous Whitegate**  
Lord of Castle Radiance, Grand Master of the Order of the Lambent Shield. Supreme military and political authority in the dale pre-Concordat. Commands both the Sun Blades (through the General) and the Order of the Lambent Shield directly.  
**Zenith Lord Argent Whitegate**  
Head of the Church of Amaunator. Luminous's brother. Opens every Strategic Meeting with prayer. The Sun Blades were originally his church's military arm.  
CIVILIAN AUTHORITY (POST-CONCORDAT, 1482+)  
**Consul Kesh Oakdale** (ret. Cpt., late of 16th Company Sunblades)  
First Consul of the Governors' Assembly, elected unanimously. Calls the Assembly, maintains order, and serves as the civilian head of the Sun Blades. Term: 15 Elesias 1482 – Shieldmeet 1484\. Based in Governor's Hall, Dagger Falls. Formerly Lt. of the 7th Bright Spears, 3rd Platoon (Dwimmerholme/Charity Hills).  
**Bishop Blaze Whitegate**  
Bishop of Dagger Falls. Grand-nephew of Argent. Runs the Church of Amaunator in daily practice. Sits on the Adjudicating Committee (Thistle Ridge). Religious authority over Sun Blade chaplains.  
II. Pre-Concordat Structure (c. 1479–1482)  
During this period the Sun Blades numbered roughly 400 at any one time (1479 Setting document), with all men in the dale required to serve 2 years (raised to 3 years at the Greengrass 1480 Strategic Meeting). The force was organized into numbered companies with traditional names.   
General Command  
GENERAL OF THE SUN BLADES

### **General Dauntless Thunderstorm**

Castle Radiance — Madigan's Hall (War Room)  
Highest-ranking military commander of all Sun Blade companies. Attends every Strategic Meeting. Reports to Lord Whitegate (pre-Concordat) and the Consul (post-Concordat). Member of the Adjudicating Committee for the Thistle Ridge Reclamation (1483). Staff includes Quartermasters, Intelligence officers, and 9 attendants.  
Named Companies (Pre-Concordat)

### **1st Company — “Dalehearth”**

GARRISON OF DAGGER FALLS  
Dagger Falls — Town walls, Tower, gates  
Primary garrison of the town. Provides wall guards, gate security, and customs support. Captain Lucius ordered new lights mounted on towers and lantern-bearer patrols against night-flying threats (1480). The “Dalehearth Companies” (plural, per Alystair Dretch's report) won renown at the Battle of Liberty Hill, suggesting at least two Dalehearth-designated companies existed in the pre-Concordat order.  
**Captain Lucius** — Captain of the 1st Company. Responded to rumours of things flying overhead at night by commissioning new lights and lantern patrols.  
**Sgt. Buck Thistledown** (retired) — Veteran of the Battle at Liberty Hill. Close comrade of Alen Reks. Organized the logistics for the White Cloak Bravo Tourney (Marpenoth 1480).  
Dorian Dale and Lumina Whitegate both served their 2-year conscription in the 1st Company before becoming adventurers.  

Named platoons: The **2nd Dalehearth Platoon** was “first over the wall” at Liberty Hill, gaining the rampart after driving off Lotus. The **3rd Dalehearth Platoon** was led down the main concourse of the village as the battle closed. (Source: *The Shadow Lifted, Part 2*)

### **2nd Company — “Mountaineers”**

MOUNTAIN OPERATIONS  
Castle Radiance and mountain approaches  
Responsible for mountain patrols and the defense of the approaches to Castle Radiance. Captain Rainyerd attended Strategic Meetings and reported intelligence directly. The company had colour-coded platoons (e.g. “Red Platoon”).  
**Captain Rainyerd** — Company Captain. Veteran mountain fighter. Noted that Sun Blades needed to adapt tactics for ranged combat against sophisticated orc threats near Old Man's Tooth.  
**Lt. Northman** (promoted) — Led the Red Platoon. Promoted to Captain of the newly re-mustered 3rd Company “Iron Backs” in winter 1481\.  
**Lt. Tarra Hilltop** (former) — Former Lieutenant of the Mountaineers. Left the Sun Blades to lead the White Cloaks. KIA during the gargoyle attack at the Thistle Ridge tournament (2 Tarsakh 1483). Also a healer associated with the Church of Amaunator.  
**Jannus Dale** (former) — Served under Lt. Hilltop in the Mountaineers. “Lived most of their life at the Castle.” After service, competed for the White Cloaks at the Thistle Ridge tournament (1483).  
**Tyrell Dale** (former) — Brother of Jannus. Served under Lt. Hilltop in the Mountaineers. “Their spears having found the hearts of orcs, ogres and Shadowvar alike.” Competed for the White Cloaks at Thistle Ridge (1483).  
**Lt. Hardigan** — A duplicate officer erroneously created for the Mountaineers alongside Captain Rainyerd. Identified as a continuity error and merged out of the canon, with Rainyerd retained as the sole Mountaineers captain. The Chronicle reference to “the White Cloaks being led into a trap by Lt. Hardigan” is a vestigial reference from before the merge. Hardigan is *not* a canonical Sun Blade.

### **3rd Company — “Iron Backs”**

FORT LUMINOUS OPERATIONS  
Fort Luminous (junction of Mountain Road & Whitegate Mine road)  
Re-mustered in the winter of 1481 DR  
A newly re-mustered company, activated to address the escalation of threats. Assigned to Fort Luminous, a strategic crossroads fort that also hosts the Wonderbringer Warsmiths and their experimental artillery.  
**Captain Northman** — Commissioned from the 2nd Mountaineers (Red Platoon). In charge of operations at Fort Luminous.

### **4th Company — “Foresters”**

CORMANTHOR FOREST OPERATIONS  
Cormanthor — deep forest patrols and counter-insurgency  
Specialized in fighting within Cormanthor forest against orcs, goblins, and other threats. Long-service veterans were common; Alaric Swift had 10 years as a Forester before his promotion.  
**Sgt. Leedo Forester** — Sergeant, 4th Company.  
**Cpt. Darius Winters** (retired) — Served 10 years as a Forester. Retired circa 1480, elected Mayor of Dwimmerholme Farm. Stout, scarred veteran, quiet and direct. Age \~28–29 at retirement.  
**Cpt. Alaric Swift** (transferred) — 10 years as a Forester. Twin brother of Tuugan Swift. Harsh but effective captain and trainer. Promoted out to command the new 9th Company “Deadly Rain.”

### **7th Company — “Bright Spears”**

FARM SETTLEMENT GARRISONS & THISTLE RIDGE RECLAMATION  
Distributed across dale farms: Crossed Blades, Dwimmerholme, Charity Hills, Tesh Valley, and others  

The most widely dispersed company, responsible for garrisoning the fortified farming settlements across the heartland of the dale. Platoons were deployed to individual farms under local lieutenant command. After the Concordat, a 4th Platoon was formed specifically for the Thistle Ridge Reclamation (1483).  
**Lt. Kesh Oakdale** (promoted) — Commander of the 3rd Platoon. Stationed at Dwimmerholme Farm. Later promoted to Captain (16th Company), then retired and elected first Consul of the Governors' Assembly.  
**Lt. Ander Millstone** — Stationed at Tesh Valley Farm. 3rd lieutenant in 3 years at that posting (Mayor Blackmane burns through them). Patient, and has won over locals.  
**Sgt. Randall Eversmoore** — Sergeant at Crossed Blades Farm.  
**Chaplain Hammerstone** — Platoon chaplain garrisoned at Crossed Blades Farm. Veteran of many mountain campaigns. Getting older; enjoying the warmer valley posting.  
**Pt. Sanza Swift** — Named in the sources as a Bright Spears member.  
**Fletcher Farmountain** — Poisoned (circumstances unclear).

### **7th Company, 4th Platoon — Thistle Ridge Reclamation (1483)**

Newly formed platoon raised by order of the Governors' Assembly (Alturiak 1483\) to support the Chrysalis expedition to reclaim Thistle Ridge. Understrength: 3 squads plus command element. Saw heavy action at the siege of The Tower and the Battle of Thistle Ridge (1–2 Mirtul 1483). Suffered 7 KIA during the siege.

**Lt. Forge Farmountain** — Platoon commander. Authorized to choose his own junior officers. Marched his exhausted platoon to relieve Chrysalis at Thistle Ridge rather than resting after the siege of The Tower.  
**Magistrate Madigara Dale** — Listed in the command section. Church magistrate attached to the platoon.  
**Sgt. Phoebe Birchgrove** — Aide-de-camp to Lt. Farmountain. Author of the Thistle Ridge Expeditionary Record.  
**Sgt. Ivy Thickwickle** — Cook and Quartermaster.

### **8th Company — “Sun Dragoons”**

MOUNTED PATROL & RAPID RESPONSE  
Mobile — deployed as needed across the dale  
The Sun Dragoons are the mounted arm of the Sun Blades. Units are deployed to hot spots for rapid reinforcement and aggressive patrolling. A full squad was ambushed by \~200 gnolls on the Moonsea Road near Gribbon's Farm (1480), with half killed.  
**Lt. Grid Hammerfall** — Commander of a mounted unit (2nd Sun Dragoons). Stoic leader. Deployed to Tesh Valley Farm to step up patrols. Personally tracked cattle rustlers 8 miles north along Old Zhent Road and marked the trail with stones.

### **9th Company — “Deadly Rain”**

ARCHER COMPANY (FORMING C. 1480\)  
Dagger Falls (training), then deployment TBD  
Planned: at least 4 platoons (\~96+ soldiers)  
A new all-archer company formed in response to the need for ranged combat capability, as noted by Captain Rainyerd. A deliberate shift in Sun Blade doctrine away from purely steel-on-steel fighting.  
**Captain Alaric Swift** — Older twin brother of Tuugan Swift (White Cloak/adventurer). Formerly a 10-year veteran Forester (4th Company). Not as gentle or patient as his brother. Harsh but effective captain and trainer.

### **10th Company**

CAVALRY / RAPID REINFORCEMENT  
Mobile — deployed to Thistle Ridge campaign (1483)  
Led the cavalry relief at the Battle of Thistle Ridge (1–2 Mirtul 1483), riding into the valley and clearing the flanks of the Torn Cheek goblins besieging The Tower. The 10th Company's arrival was the turning point of the siege.  
**Captain Garrick Greenfields** — Led the 10th Company riders to relieve the Bright Spears at The Tower during the Battle of Thistle Ridge. Joined Captain Monarch and Lt. Farmountain for the post-battle accounting.  
Note: The Shadow Sentinels are also sometimes referred to as Company X, garrisoned at Liberty Hill. It is unclear whether Greenfields' 10th Company and the Shadow Sentinels are the same unit or a numbering overlap between eras.

Greenwood Farm & The Tower (Thistle Ridge area)

**Lt. Williams Waterborne** — Commander of the 2nd Platoon. Garrisoning The Tower until Mirtul 30 or longer.

FOREST ESCORT & PATROL  
Cormanthor Forest — escorting parties to/from Thistle Ridge  
A half-platoon of Sun Blades escorting parties through Cormanthor to the Thistle Ridge area. Includes foresters and scouts. Company affiliation not specified in the sources.  
**Lt. Maddy Riverstone** — Platoon commander.  
**Sgt. Brendle Vale** — Second most senior officer in the escort platoon.

The platoon includes at least 4 female foresters: two ride ahead scouting, one tails the party at distance, and Sephany stays with the main body.

III. Storied Veterans — Historical Record  
The following Sun Blades are drawn from the historical record of Thistle Ridge and other early sources (early 15th century DR). They served generations before the current era but their names endure in the journals and parish records recovered during the 1483 reclamation campaign.   
STORIED VETERAN — BRIGHT SPEARS / MOUNTAINEERS

### **Lt. Tergus Clarandal**

Rose from Sergeant in the 2nd Company (Mountaineers) over five Terms of Service. Commissioned as Lieutenant commanding the 7th Company Bright Spears, 4th Platoon — the original Thistle Ridge garrison (c. 1436 DR). His extensive journals (1423–1439 DR) were recovered by the Chrysalis expedition from the ruins of Clarandal Farm in 1483\. These journals reveal a thoughtful, observant soldier who harboured doubts about his family's claimed Sembian origins and sensed “hidden truths” within the community. Born c. 1408 DR; married Senthia (b. 1409). Fate uncertain after the Thistle Ridge tragedy of 1441\.  
STORIED VETERAN — MOUNTAINEERS

### **Sgt. Claudius Clarandal**

Brother of Tergus. Served as Sergeant in the Mountaineers over five Terms of Service. After retiring from the Sun Blades, became the militia captain of Thistle Ridge, leading one of the largest and best-trained militias in the dale (106 capable men and women). Presumed dead in the Thistle Ridge tragedy of 1441 DR, if not already deceased by that date.  

Note: Sources describe Claudius as “of the 3rd Company, Mountaineers” rather than the 2nd, which is the usual Mountaineers numbering. This may reflect an older numbering convention or a reorganization.

IV. Post-Concordat Structure (Elesias 1482 Onward)  
The Daggerdale Concordat reorganized the Sun Blades under the Governors' Assembly. Costs were shared across all settlements. The force was expanded significantly to 18 companies / 54 platoons / 1,296 soldiers plus officers, organized into 8 Divisions led by Captains answering to the General. Unit structure: each Platoon \= 24 soldiers \+ 1 Sergeant; each Company \= 3 Platoons \+ 1 Lieutenant; each Division \= 1–4 Companies \+ 1 Captain. Costs shared by 20 “shares” apportioned among Castle Radiance (4), Dagger Falls (4), each farm (1 each × 9), Charity (1), Tethyamar (1), and Cormyr (1).   
Chain of Command (Concordat)

| Rank / Position | Incumbent | Authority |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1\. Consul of the Governors' Assembly | Cpt. Kesh Oakdale (ret.) | Civilian head of the Sun Blades. Calls Assembly, maintains order. |
| 2\. General | Gen. Dauntless Thunderstorm | Military commander of all 18 companies. Staff of 9 (QM, Intel, Attendants). |
| 3\. Captains (×8 Divisions) | See below | Division commanders. Can be assigned to answer to local Governors. |
| 4\. Lieutenants | 1 per company (18 total) | Company commanders. |
| 5\. Sergeants | 1 per platoon (54 total) | Platoon leaders. 24 soldiers each. |

Concordat Divisions — Standard Order of the Sun Blades  
DIVISION 1

### **Captain of the Castle**

Castle Radiance  
4 companies · 12 platoons · 288 soldiers  
Garrison of Castle Radiance. The largest and most battle-ready division, positioned at the front line against Netheril. 4 Lieutenants, 12 Sergeants.  
DIVISION 2

### **Captain of the Town**

Dagger Falls  
4 companies · 12 platoons · 288 soldiers  
Garrison and security of Dagger Falls. Wall guards, gate security, Watergate Customs Court, Tower garrison. Eagle-eyed young women with short bows patrol the Tower ramparts. 4 Lieutenants, 12 Sergeants.  
DIVISION 3

### **Captain of the Rides**

Mobile — Dale-wide  
2 companies · 6 platoons · 144 mounted soldiers  
The mounted arm. Successor to the Sun Dragoons tradition. Patrols roads, provides rapid response, escorts caravans. 2 Lieutenants, 6 Sergeants. Every soldier mounted.  
DIVISION 4

### **Captain of the Recruits**

Training facilities (Dagger Falls / Castle Radiance)  
4 companies · 12 platoons · 288 soldiers (all in training)  
All new conscripts and recruits cycle through this division. 4 Lieutenants, 12 Sergeants. After the Greengrass 1480 decree, minimum service was raised from 2 to 3 years.  
DIVISION 5

### **Captain of the South Settlements**

Southern farms (likely includes Crossed Blades, Dagger Hills area)  
1 company · 3 platoons · 72 soldiers  
Garrison for the southern farm settlements. 1 Lieutenant, 3 Sergeants.  
DIVISION 6

### **Captain of the Near Settlements**

Farms nearest to Dagger Falls  
1 company · 3 platoons · 72 soldiers  
Garrison for the farms closest to the town. 1 Lieutenant, 3 Sergeants.  
DIVISION 7

### **Captain of the West Settlements**

Western farms and mountain approaches (Foothills, Tesh Valley area)  
1 company · 3 platoons · 72 soldiers  
Garrison for the western settlements closer to the mountains. 1 Lieutenant, 3 Sergeants.  
DIVISION 8

### **Captain of the Reserve**

Flexible — deployed as needed  
1 company · 3 platoons · 72 soldiers  
Strategic reserve. Available for reinforcement anywhere in the dale. 1 Lieutenant, 3 Sergeants.

Related Military Organizations (Not Sun Blades)  
The Concordat formalized four categories of military institution in Daggerdale: the Standing Army (Sun Blades), the Holy Orders (Charters Militant), the Chartered Companies, and the Militia. The following are the non–Sun Blade organizations drawn from wiki and Chronicle sources.  
**Holy Orders — Charters Militant**  
ORDER OF THE LAMBENT SHIELD (AMAUNATOR)  
\~120 paladins and holy warriors of Amaunator. Led by Grand Master Luminous Whitegate, with Knight Commander Cassius as a senior field officer. Deployed from Castle Radiance against Netheril and the Plaguelands. Funded entirely by the Church. Granted Charter Militant by the Concordat.  

Iolus Warburton (Captain of the White Cloaks) is described in the Chronicle as a “candidate for the Order of the Lambent Shield, which he regularly turns down.”  
ORDER OF THE GOLDEN LION (TORM)  
Holy order dedicated to Torm, the Loyal Fury. Led by Sir Gethard Soulbright (killed at the Second Battle of Anathar's Dell). Known members include Daniel Overcourt (squire to Soulbright; wounded at Anathar's Dell but survived) and Corran Altair (former Sun Blade, now Paladin of Torm; listed as Sergeant of the Order in the Chronicle).  
ORDER OF THE LAMBENT ROSE (ILMATER)  
Holy order dedicated to Ilmater, the Crying God. Led by Sir Lexinauth (killed) and Father Eisley of St. Collin's Abbey. Reverend Brother Turan, a Damaran monk-priest and friend of Lord Maturin Horgath, is also a member (last seen working in the medicine tents in Shadowdale). Involved in refugee care during the 1372 crisis.  
KNIGHTS OF JUSTICE (TYR)  
Holy order dedicated to Tyr, the Even-Handed. Led by Sir Wildermot Silverlance (killed by the Baron at the Second Battle of Anathar's Dell). Sir Durin Saddlemoor survived Anathar's Dell but lost his shield arm. Smallest of the four orders in available sources.  
**Chartered Companies**  
WHITE CLOAKS  
The most prominent chartered adventuring company in Daggerdale. Founded by a group that included Lumina Whitegate. Led by Captain Iolus Warburton, a Soldier of Amaunator. Organized into operational sub-teams (Alpha, Bravo, etc.) with letters patent from the Governors' Assembly. The White Cloaks fielded a large tournament force (the Bravo Tourney, Marpenoth 1480\) and have extensive operations across the dale. Lt. Tarra Hilltop left the Sun Blades to join their leadership. Numerous members including Vanion Creed (Sergeant, Tethyamar Team Leader & Quartermaster), Alystair Dretch (Sergeant), Hennigan Paros, Imogen Mystral, and Sammus Weaver. The Captain of the White Cloaks holds a seat on the Governors' Assembly.  
REDJACKS  
A large chartered company of veteran soldiers and adventurers, numbering more than 60\. Led by Captain Hastle Blackwater (also rendered “Redwater” in one Chronicle passage). Described as “very capable, if somewhat self-interested.” The Captain of the Redjacks holds a seat on the Governors' Assembly.  
FREE DEFENCE LEAGUE (FDL)  
An independent bounty-hunter and volunteer force, headquartered at Castle Radiance. Led by Captain Bargle Battamond (also “Battlemaster”), with nearly 40 members who take bounties posted at the castle. “Ser” Reginald Boxfray commands the 14th Company, 1st Platoon “Dawnshields” (4 squads). The FDL fought alongside the Sun Blades at Thistle Ridge (1483). The Captain of the Free Defense League holds a seat on the Governors' Assembly.  
**Militia & Other**  
LOCAL MILITIA  
Each farm maintains its own militia of fighting-aged people (1/5 enrolled at any time, 4-year terms, minimum 2 hrs/tenday training). Tesh Valley's militia under Sgt. Fortune Swift (Tuugan Swift's uncle) is the largest and best, with \~40 active men, mostly mounted. The historical Thistle Ridge militia under Claudius Clarandal was one of the dale's largest, with 106 capable fighters. Militia captains are paid by their settlements. The Concordat requires common militia-readiness drills 4× per year.  
WONDERBRINGER WARSMITHS  
Engineers and artificers based at Fort Luminous. Operate and develop experimental weapons including the great bronze gun. Include gnomish specialists. Not part of the Sun Blades chain of command.
