Nobles
The ledger indicts the following noble families through various business entries in the book.

It is also well known that after Dalael was defeated by the Warders of Waterdeep that the family allied itself with both Company Drurginz and Company Ersgul.  The Patriarch of the Family, Dulbrawan, was a good business partner of Thagdul

by Yarkul.  After Yarkul left the city 2 summers ago, and Ersgul was defeated, the family stayed their bond with the drow.  In fact, it is well known to you that the Consort to the Patriarch, Ranya, has 2 children with Quevin Dalael.  Last winter, Dulbrawan hanged himself for reasons much guessed at but not confirmed, and the ledger shows a 100% decrease in activity with this family after the old man

Gauntyl:  This family is one of the premier mercenary training families in the city.  The company has 20 Gauntyl mercs on retainer for 1 gold/day, with a further 100 gold/tenday paid to the family.

Irlingstar:  This was a major shipping family.  Last fall, as the last ships were setting out for warmer waters, laden with goods, the Patriarch of the family (Hulraven) himself, lead the family

s fleet to Calimport.  The ledger indicates that the boats were laden almost exclusively with Drurginz cargoes that were seized, legally, by Company Aldebarran.  Hulraven himself signed a letter of insurance for the bulk of the cargo.  When Hulraven was killed and enough of the cargo vanished in Calimport, the family was in default of the letter of insurance, and lost the villa.  Of course, this is the villa that Aldebarran moved right into, after kicking out all of the family, the help, and the guards.  The family was ruined.  The fate of the few surviving members of this family is in your hands now, as your company has cleaned out the villa and thrown down Aldebarran.

Zulpair:  This family is involved in one of the more treacherous and sordid events of the adventure thus far.  In
71 and
72, this family was heavily involved with Drurginz and Teskul.  It was a certain cargo belonging to this family that was destined for the Zulpair
s Pride that opened the adventuring season of
72.  The family was dealing mostly with Teskul, at the time, who was working with the Dalael.  The ledger indicates that Kendal Thorne (AKA Altooth Mshool, the boss) financed some information from one Gerath Zulpair, the heir to the family.  The Blades of Korombos subsequently stole a large Zulpair/Dalael cargo.

However, at some point, the cargo was compromised, likely by a leak in the packer

Information about the cargo being full of drow somehow got around, and the adventurers responded.  Later, Agamemnon would speculate that the Dalael let it be known that they had their assassins in the boxes just to lure out any Harpers or other do-gooders in the area, and it seemingly worked.

Fellowship of Carter
s and Coachmen: This is one of the most important guilds for the type of work the Zhents did in this city.  There are about 30 men on retainer for 2 gp/day, and Jafar is one of them.

Fellowship of Salters, Packers and Joiners: Another important guild in a city of merchant sailors, this guild also has about 20 men and women on retainer for 2 gp/day.  One young lad, Dulhand, is a 13 year old boy that was always found in the Aldebarran office.  Bonuses paid out to these members sometimes totaled over 200 gp.
Master Mariner
s Guild: There were 10 captains on this council who were in the ledger.  Each was paid on a per-job basis, and most of it seems to be on the up-and-up.  The major benefit to having members on your payroll from this guild is that it is much easier to get ships through customs quicker and cheaper.  Two notable names are Helve Moonstar and Jareth Zulpair (each captains of ships and noble heirs).

Order of Master Shipwrights: Kelvar Helmfast was the Aldebarran man on this guild.  He was paid a small percentage of every boat that was purchased by Aldebarran, and certainly kept prices low for the company, and high for the competitors.

Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors: This is a difficult guild to crack, but Drurginz did it years ago with Dolebrund the Green.  Dolebrund, of course, is a mage who fought at the Irlingstar Villa and got away (after wrestling Beljuril in his troll form).  Jafar was also a member of the guild, but is no longer accepted after the fireball inquest.

The Caravan Master, Kelduth Rhork, has vanished.  The office looks empty, but it backs onto one of the larger warehouses in the city.  Certainly, it could not have been completely evacuated in such short order
