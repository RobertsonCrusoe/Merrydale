Jasper & Lucan — Early Character Concepts and Alliance
Gus Miranda (as Jasper) and Stephen Robertson (as Lucan)
Email thread: "Jasper & Lucan", 19-20 Dec 2023
Thread ID: 18c8197084516c5e

A brief pre-campaign exchange in which Gus describes Jasper's personality and proposes teaming up with Lucan for investigation. Stephen, mid-travel from Ottawa to Brisbane, agrees to collaborate. This is one of the earliest player-to-player coordination threads.

---

GUS (19 Dec 2023):

A word on Jasper: he's not really nervous or clumsy; more absent-minded and easily distracted, with a lot of youthful energy, exuberance, and ADHD. I envision something along the same lines as Matt Smith's Doctor and Doc Brown - very scattered until they need to focus, but then when it's time to focus it is total laser focus.

As far as Lucan goes, we do seem to have a common objective: find out about our competition. If Lucan wants to work alone that's fine with me as it probably fits Lucan's MO to investigate on his own. But the offer is there if you want to team up.


STEPHEN (20 Dec 2023):

Just re-booting my brain after flying from Ottawa to Brisbane (heading to Adelaide this afternoon) so apologies for delayed response. I'd be happy to team up and do some investigating with Jasper. Will be in touch if the narrative hasn't already moved on.


GUS (20 Dec 2023):

Wow! Take your time my friend. I remember those 30+ hour trips, so I definitely commiserate! Narrative is whatever you want it to be. I've had the luxury of being the first to arrive at my in-laws, and not suffering any jet lag as France is same time zone as Cameroon. That meant a lot of free alone time for me over the last couple of days to think about D&D, so I've just been amusing myself. No expectations for any contributions anytime before January, and easily edit/retcon to whatever you want to do.

Enjoy your vacation and your family and have a merry Christmas!

(Jealous as I'd love to go to Australia. Not jealous as my own 7 hour flight to Paris was long enough, thank you!)

G.
