The Mountain of Mayhem - Beliard's Songs of the Mountain
Email thread: "The Mountain of Mayhem", 16-23 Feb 2024
Participants: Stephen Robertson, Gus Miranda, Les Blackwell, Scott Mclaren, Todd Burry

This thread began with Todd Burry sharing detailed 3D renders and AI-enhanced images of the Mountain Arena from the Thistle Ridge Competition. The images depicted the arena being built, and a final battle scene showing Chrysalis Company in action during the first round of Capture the Flag against the White Cloaks (Ches 21, 1483 DR). Scott Mclaren requested a triptych of paintings for the Myth Drannor Embassy depicting the transformation of Thistle Ridge from a bleak, monster-ridden wasteland to a flourishing garden under Chrysalis Company's stewardship.

Inspired by the images and the group's enthusiasm, Stephen Robertson composed two pieces of verse in character as Beliard Kathklan Emmarask, a bard of some fame in Dagger Falls.

---

I. THE MONKEY ON THE MOUNTAIN
by Beliard Emmarask
(A ballad retelling the first round of Capture the Flag, presented at Quick Axes tavern)

In Dagger Falls, as snowflakes whirled, two teams of valor met,
Chrysalis bold and Whitecloaks proud, in a challenge fiercely set.
The field prepared, the flags were placed, excitement ruled the day,
For boasting rights and victory, the gods would have their say.

The bell was rung, Ser Rowan leapt  and towards the flag did bound,
While Mari, quick and small and brave, ran out to find her ground.
Lucan, laid low by Oakbough's sting, fell tangled, in dismay,
But healed by Mari's fervent prayers, he rose again to join the fray.

Zelia's arrows flew like hawks, at Ser Rowan aiming true,
Yet he with heart and girth and ire, the Whitecloak flag from her withdrew.
Then up the mountain's lofty peak, with flag in hand, he climbed,
'Midst magic spells and arrows' flight, his valor brightly shined.

Jeska, elf of wit and blade, danced 'round her flag with grace,
Her sharp and stinging verbal barbs led the Dales a spritely chase.
But Tyrell Dale, through melee's din, advanced with purpose strong,
To grasp the flag in eager hands, amidst the battle's song.

He hurled it to his kin in stride, a toss both bold and fleet,
Jannus caught it on the rise, their triumph seeming sweet.
Into the water Jeska plunged, by Tyrell's hand she fell,
Yet her spirit, bold and undimmed, rose with stories yet to tell.

Meanwhile, Jannus held the flag, and to the mountain path he raced,
Dodging slicks of Jasper's grease, with a skillful, artful pace.
Up he climbed, the peak in sight, the summit his to claim,
But Jasper's wind, with forceful might, soon brought him low again.

Then 'midst clash of steel and spell and brawn, a monkey took the stage,
A creature small, yet bold and brave, a true hero for this age.
The primate, swift and nimble, grabbed the flag with cheeky glee,
Then leapt from the mountain's lofty height, as cunning as could be.

Lucan, with his bow in hand, at Zelia and Alexandus aimed,
But Hilltop's healing thwarted him, their vigor she reclaimed.
So he seized the flag anew, and towards his goal, he ran,
He threw it back to Chrysalis' zone, a shrewd and clever plan.

Alexandus, the Whitecloak mage, strode towards the arena's heart,
To thwart the hedge wizard's simple ploys, to block his arcane art.
Yet Jasper, friend to bird and beast, sought the monkey's sly assist,
To disrupt the mage's eldritch plans, and his pride's fall to enlist.

The monkey, quick with jest and jape, caught the war mage unawares,
Its arms were strong, its aim was true, it flung dung into his hair.
Cormyr's son, a wizard proud, was thus by a rustic mageling bested,
His pride was scarred by the monkey's jibe, his temper sorely tested.

Ser Rowan, up atop the peak, with hammer swung in might,
Faced the Brothers Dale in a fearsome fray, a truly gallant sight.
Then Jeska, with her longbow poised, found just the perfect shot,
"Show me your heart, stag," she whispered, her aim forgot her not.

At last, the Whitecloaks, waning, their wills by war were worn.
Tara Hilltop hailed the end, her team's defeat was sworn.
So it was that Team Chryslais claimed their first victory that day,
Their deeds of bravery, luck, and skill, in my song I do convey.

II. MONKEY ON THE MOUNTAIN
(or "The Monkeyshit Mage" as the older, crasser boys were wont to call it)
by Beliard Emmarask
(A children's song that the young folk of Dagger Falls took to singing in the years to come, immortalizing the War Wizard's humiliation)

Stephen's note: "Apologies, I confused the title of the last ballad with a song that the children of Dagger Falls took to singing in the years to come, immortalizing the War Wizard."

I went to the Dagger Falls fair,
The heroes all were there,
Jasper the Sly, under the sky,
Was casting his spells in the air,
You should've seen the monk,
He played in the snowy funk,
He leaped and swung, the flag he won,
And that was the win of the monk,
The monk, the monk, the monk,
The monk, the monk, the monk!

'Xandus, mage so grand,
Strode forth with staff in hand,
He aimed to weave a spell to achieve
A win over Chrysalis band,
But then here came the monk,
With a jest and a leap he dunked,
He flung with glee, his poo you see,
And that was the win of the monk,
The monk, the monk, the monk,
The monk, the monk, the monk!

Oh, I went to the Dagger Falls fair,
The heroes all were there,
The monkey so small, outdid them all,
A feat beyond compare,
You should've seen the monk,
His spirit never once shrunk,
With daring deed, and cunning speed,
Oh, that was the win of the monk,
The monk, the monk, the monk,
The monk, the monk, the monk!
