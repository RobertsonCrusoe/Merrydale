== The Zulpair Cargo ==

In ’71 and ’72, the House of Zulpair was heavily involved with Drurginz and Teskul. The family was dealing mostly with Teskul, at the time, who was working with the Dalael. Near Greengrass of 1372, Altooth Mzul financed some information from one Gerath Zulpair, the heir to the family. The Blades of Korombos subsequently stole a large Zulpair/Dalael cargo. At some point, however, the location of the stolen cargo was compromised (likely by a leak in the packer’s guild). The stolen cargo was then relocated by Teskul, and repackaged without the knowledge of Aldebarran. The Aldebarran cargo was then replaced with waiting drow. Information about the cargo being full of drow somehow got around, and the adventurers responded. Later, Agamemnon would speculate that the Dalael let it be known that they had their assassins in the boxes just to lure out any Harpers or other do-gooders in the area, and it seemingly worked.

== Gideon’s Warehouse==

Approximately Greengrass 1372 – a robbery at Gideon’s warehouse leaves Gideon dead as well as Gotlieb (ex-Drurginz) and Everyone associated with Gideon.

Gideon (ex-Ersgul) was an Honourable Member in the Fellowship of Salters, Packers and Joiners. He had his own shipping house and 8 other Guild Members under him. Like Ersgul Mlshool, this fellow was also found dead just prior to Greengrass of 1372. His death may have something to do with the deaths of the Drow and Mlshool. He was found hung in his own warehouse just before Greengrass, his testicles in his mouth and a symbol branded into his forehead. His 8 men were never found, but many bodies had clearly been dragged from the scene.

Ersgul Mlshool (ex-Ersgul) This fellow is now dead, but worth noting. He was a member of the Stonecutters' Guild, and had the title of Foreman. He had 5-10 loyal men under him, and was loyal to Yarkul. He was recently found dead in his well-to-do Castle Ward apartment, along with a dead Drow. These two deaths likely marked the beginning of the adventure/underground commerce season for The City in 1372.

Gotlieb Erlgund (ex-Drurginz) was a favoured member of the Fellowship of Carters and Coachmen in Thagdul’s employ while Company Drurginz was active.
Status: Deceased. He was found dead and floating in the Harbour with his crew of 7 boys, the same morning Gideon was found dead in his warehouse.
