===The House of Achres===
For centuries the House of Achres has stood guard on the western approach of the Trade Way as it enters into the Sunset Mountains. It is a harsh land, this barony, but it is, or was, the key to all trade between eastern and western Faerûn. Lord Bartol IXX, Baron of Achres, Keeper of the Sunset Gates, Steward of the Ramparts, Marshal of the Free Towns of the Trade Way rules here. His ancestors have stood guard on their fortress, which they simply called “The Gates,” and ensured that the road would remain safe and open for all to use. Although nominally they fall under the authority of the city of Iriaebor, the barony’s existence has meant that neither Cormyr nor Eltugard nor Darkhold nor any other power could lay claim to this most crucial bottleneck. The House of Achres kept the road and surrounding lands free, and the merchants of Sembia and Baldur’s Gate and Amn paid handsomely for that freedom.

To appease his neighbours however, it became tradition for the sons and daughters of Achres to intermarry and reside in Eltugard or Cormyr. These personal relationships became key to their survival since, with time, the Barons became cousins of almost every noble, knight and merchant prince in the kingdoms either side of them.

===Whelp of the Litter===

Tyrian, one of Bartol’s younger sons, was not expected to amount to much when he was young. He was a bit of a weakling and not particularly bright, and life in The Gates did not suit him well. His mother sent him to live with her family in Iriaebor, where he grew up in the splendour and comfort of nobility, reading and being read stories of the great warriors of old.

When he was of the right age he began pestering his father to allow him to serve as a page in Cormyr. The boy had plenty of admiration for the old King Azoun IV and his favourite stories were of the old king’s crusade against the Horde. The boy had spent his childhood on the towers of Iriaebor being prepared and educated for the priesthood, but mostly being pampered and indulged by his mother’s relations while he daydreamed of following in the armoured footsteps of his ancestors. He was not fit for the task, but it was clear that the boy’s heart was pure and his ambition strong, and Old Bartol could not bring himself to break any of his children’s hearts.

In exchange for a large sum of gold he was sent not to Cormyr, but to Elturel, where he would be a page in the household of a cousin who was a fairly new Tormite knight and needed the money to complete his tower.

===Purity and Doubt Rewarded===

Over the next six years, Tyrian surprised and amazed all. What he lacked in wits and strength of arm, he more than made up for in courage, in loyalty, in obedience, in plain common sense and most importantly, in strength of heart. There had never been a young boy the likes of Tyrian, and he became the favourite of all the Eltugardian knights.

When the time came for him to receive his spurs and become a proper squire something very peculiar happened. Tyrian had become a favourite, yes, and not just with the knights and other pages. He had even caught the eye of the High Observer himself. The High Observer awarded Tyrian an audience prior to his vigil, and Tyrian confessed two things to the ruler of Eltugard. One, that his faith in Torm was not strong enough to join the Order of the Golden Cup; and two, that he had always dreamt of serving the King of Cormyr and that is where he hoped to serve.

If it had been anyone else but Tyrian, things would have turned out different. But the young boy whom everyone liked had spoken plainly and honestly to the ruler of the nation, and the High Observer agreed with both things he had said.

It was announced that Tyrian would not receive his spurs from Torm, and the High Observer himself would arrange with King Florin of Cormyr to have Tyrian receive his spurs as a Purple Knight in that realm.

Old Lord Bartol, proud of his son whom he had once deemed unfit for knighthood arranged an honour guard to escort the young man from Elturel to Suzail, with a great feast planned at The Gates. Twenty knights of Torm from Eltugard, and twenty Purple Knights from Cormyr, all cousins of the House of Achres, along with twice that number in squires, pages and men-at-arms rode out of Elturel and down the Trade Way in glorious splendour.

At The Gates, Bartol spared no expense in his feast.

And there is where our story of young Tyrian takes its dark turn.

===The Fall of the House of Achres===

The House of Achres primarily kept the Trade Way safe from the forces of Darkhold. The Gates stood as a counterbalance to that evil fortress to the north. Ever watchful, Bartol and his household guards rode out to meet the threats whenever they came.

This time, not only did Bartol have his household, but he had a veritable small army of veteran knights at his disposal. All of them kin, all of them riding high not just on the sheer joy and celebratory air of the enterprise, but also on a good measure of wine. So when the word came that a small party of Darkhold brigands had been spotted trying to make its way down the mountains, every man present rode out to meet the threat.

The brigands were easy to find, for Bartol was experienced at this... and of course, they were meant to be found. It was a well planned ambush, and no sooner had Bartol’s war party engaged the brigands than the trap was sprung and the full forces of Darkhold fell upon them.

At the Battle of Bartol’s Folly the House of Achres was destroyed. All the knights of renown were killed, and many of their attendants as well. Of the nobility, only Bartol himself and Tyrian made it out alive, and Tyrian barely so. He had been struck by several arrows, and had suffered a tremendous blow that had broken his back. Without proper clerical aid, it was all Bartol could do to get his young son out alive.

In the aftermath, the House of Achres lost all of its splendour, its wealth and its stature. Restitution had to be paid to the families of all those knights that were killed, and both the courts of Eltugard and Cormyr turned their backs on them. Old Bartol became a broken man. He aged rapidly, the weight of his dishonour crushing his spirit. Within the year, he was dead, The Gates abandoned, and the Trade Way now unpatrolled and wild.

===A Bitter Convalescence===

Now Tyrian, he’d been wounded almost mortally. He was set to Iriaebor to be cared for once again by his mother‘s family in his childhood home. There an Ilmatari monk named Lucion slowly healed the young man and taught him to walk again. He spent the next five years with Lucion, who was under instructions to prepare the boy for a quiet monastic life. As Bartol’s sole surviving male direct heir he was entitled to inherit the Barony and the lordship of the House of Achres. However, because of the great dishonour, most of the fortune was paid off to Cormyr and Eltugard, and the lords of Iriabeor asserted their legal right and reclaimed the barony for the city. The House of Achres was no more.

Tyrian’s mother and Lucion had hoped the brutal reality of war and his brush with death would’ve shown him the futility and grimness of a life lead by the sword. They were preparing him for a more suitable, quiet life as a priest of Ilmater. But Tyrian had other plans. As soon as he was strong and able enough, he secreted himself out of Iriaebor and rode alone all the way to Suzail where, with the old letter of introduction from the High Observer which he had managed to keep, he was able to obtain an audience with the King.

The King was not happy about this. The name of Achres is worthless in Cormyr thanks to Bartol, and Tyrian was considered much too old. But the agreement was lawful, and his clerks agreed that as Tyrian still lived and had properly presented himself before the King, albeit five years late, it was still a binding agreement. Grudgingly he accepted, and Tyrian entered into the King’s service as a squire in the Order of the Purple Dragon.

The next three years were very difficult for Tyrian. The stigma of his father’s dishonour lay heavily upon his shoulders, and the Purple Dragons would not let him forget. He was also much older than all the other pages and squires and was treated in a manner that some would call torture.

But the same incredible courage, the same purity of heart and spirit, the same dogged obedience and loyalty pulled him through. Tyrian not only survived his training and initiation, but he won over all the knights he interacted with. He would, on his 23rd birthday, be once again hailed as everyone’s favourite. The Crown Prince Irvel himself stood as nominator before the King to secure Tyrian his knighthood.

But the King was the one person Tyrian could not win over. The King would not let the grievance be forgotten. The agreement with the High Observer of Eltugard had been for service as a squire and training for the knighthood, it had not been for the awarding of the knighthood itself. He decreed that no more scions of Achres would ever serve in the Order of the Purple Dragon.

===Knight-Errant===

With his training complete, Tyrian was expelled from Cormyr, and sent back to Iriaebor, to his mother’s family.

After a year spent brooding in the towers of Iriaebor he was reluctantly on the move again. Lucion, the Ilmatari monk who’d healed his wounds came to visit him, and seeing him depressed and wasting away, devised a plan. He arranged for Tyrian to deliver some letters to a friend of his, a man named Reynard, at the Temple of Ilmater in Westgate. Lucion insisted that the change of scenery and short journey to the cost of the Inner Sea would do him good. After some cajoling, Tyrian relented.

In Westgate, Tyrian easily found the Temple of Ilmater, and was surprised to find that “Reynard” was no simple Ilmatari priest. Reynard was actually Reynard the Boar, Grand-Master of the Order of the Noble Heart of Ilmater, one of the most highly regarded and well known knights in Faerûn. The letters Tyrian delivered were actually letters of support for Tyrian’s induction as a knight into the Order of the Noble Heart.

After the initial surprise wore off, Tyrian spent several months working at the Temple under the tutelage and guidance of Reynard. He shared his life’s story with the Grand Master, and Reynard in turn offered his counsel.

===The Three Conditions===

Eventually, when Reynard was satisfied with the young man’s character, he made Tyrian an offer. Tyrian would be welcome as a Knight-Companion in the Order of the Noble Heart if he fulfilled three conditions:

First, he was to shed his name. A name is earned, a name is made, a name is not inherited. If he was to follow this path, he would no longer be a scion of the House of Achres. If he wanted to restore that name and that inheritance to himself, he must do so through honourable deeds. He would henceforth be known as Tyrian Grey.

Second, he would serve Ilmater in all things. He would pledge his loyalty, his sword, his honour and his life and his faith to The Crying God.

And third, he would have one year to prove himself worthy of the Noble Heart in deed and action.

If he met these conditions, he would be welcomed with open arms as a brother knight.

With final words of guidance, Reynard instructed Tyrian to ride north, beyond Cormyr to Castle Radiance. Ilmater’s work needs doing in Daggerdale.
