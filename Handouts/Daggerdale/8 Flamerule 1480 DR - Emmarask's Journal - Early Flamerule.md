8 Flamerule, 1480: Quick Axes, Dagger Falls

We reached Dagger Falls late this afternoon, and soon found an inn for the night. It is civilized enough, I suppose. I have contracted with a Lieutenant Marshall of the Sun Blades to join a wagon train leaving for Castle Radiance on the morrow. Tyrian tells me the girl will continue with us to the Castle. At long last, this tiresome journey is nearly complete.

That bloody forest. I thought these Dalesmen knew their business, but the few farms we’ve seen almost seem to have been under siege from the wood, rather than the other way around. There shouldn’t be any reason for them to be falling behind on their lumber shipments. You could build a city using the resources to be gleaned simply from making the Gond-damned roads passable. We will have to put more pressure on these hicks at the next trade council meeting. If they can’t manage their lands, I’m sure a few well-placed words will make their way to the ears of someone who can.

In two days’ time I will be in my chambers in Boraskyr Tower and may even get a decent night’s sleep. I know the Ambassador likes his comfort, and from what I’ve heard, the Tower, if not the Castle, is a little slice of Suzail in the middle of nowhere. Not that this inn doesn’t seem propitious to a well-appointed slumber, but the Silver Lady is full-bosomed tonight, and tongues will be on the wag. My time will be better spent in the taproom, as many of the men here spent their winter on the frontier. I’ve heard some talk of the Winter War, and it appears that what we’ve heard in Suzail is true, if tavern tales be any gauge. The war seems to have ended before it’s even begun. Castle Radiance appears to have held against the Shadovar better than we could have expected. I must speak with the ambassador about this, as it goes against our expectations. If these sorry farmers and lumberjacks can beat back the Shadow from their door, imagine what the Third Royal Legion could do. This peace won't last I'm sure. In the words of the Raven, \"There is no avoiding war; it can only be postponed to the advantage of others.\"

A half-dozen elves joined us in the hall an hour ago – or rather, I should say, more correctly, two trios of elves. The older thee, two males and a female, possess much more of that eladrin gravitas than the younger group, whom they seem to be watching (safeguarding? In custody of?). The younger three are more sullen than serious, and covered in ridiculous tattoos. None of them look happy to be in this above-ground warren of an inn, but of the lot of them, the younger sprites look positively fearful of these stone walls. Even the elves in these parts are yokels, it seems. Only the she-elf seems unperturbed, and she is assuredly their leader. That one wouldn’t be out of place at court. I saw her talking to Marshall not long after I did. Will they be joining us? There is a tale here, and not a happy one, I’ll warrant. I’ll learn more tonight.

My travelling companions, Tyrian and Carpathia, have retired to their separate chambers. I saw Tyrian knocking on the wounded dove’s door not ten minutes ago, but she either ignored him or didn’t hear him. Such a polite knock I’d not be surprised if it was the latter. The boy is not what I would have expected, given his history. One might suspect that to travel openly bearing the King’s disfavour is the mark of either a simpleton or a braggart, but since he joined me in Arabel, I find myself warming to what seems to be a genuine desire to… well, help out. A pity he’ll never be a Dragon. Of course, his connection to Reynard is a much more salient point here. It will be a delicate matter to cultivate that connection without taking on the taint of Achres. Fortunately few in these benighted hinterlands will know or care who he is. Maybe the lad will even make a name for himself out here. If so, the boy himself may have his own part to play when old Florin (Long May He Reign, indeed) shuffles off.

And now, I think, a pipe, and a bowl of that fine Arabel claret master Balak mentioned.

9 Flamerule, 1480: Foothills

We are camped somewhere on the road to Castle Radiance. The younger elves have joined us, against their volition, from what I can see. They have been walking at the end of the wagon train, doing their level best to look as though they are not with us, though they are never farther than a hundred paces from the last mule. Now they are camped at the edge of the firelight, pretending to be aloof, though I can see them staring at our merry blaze. I can't tell whether they are afraid of it, or jealous, or both. They have no fire of their own, though I’ve no idea why. We’ve gained some altitude and it’s quite nippy for a summer’s night. Luckily these mountain pines burn quite well. As for the elves' story, I learned nothing more last night, as they kept to themeselves, but I was able to talk to Marshall about them earlier today.

“What do you think is the story with that elf?” I asked.

“Which elf is that again?”

“The less effeminate one.”

The lieutenant chuckled, and then cleared his throat, holding up a finger to me in gentle admonishment. “No disrespect intended, sir, but you’d best be careful with such talk. These wild elves aren’t the same as the civilized eladrin you’ll have seen at court in Suzail,” he told me. “No fancy manners, no subtlety or subterfuge hereabouts. A fey doesn’t like you, he’ll be plain about it. Plain as a dagger in your ear. These three seem tame enough – not domesticated, but more like, whipped, mebbe. Still, there have been some strange troubles lately, and I reckon I wouldn’t turn my back on an elf if I could help it. I’m told they’re being assigned to Ambassador Miritar’s delegation, and that’s all I know. They ain't spoken word one since that lady-elf told me they'd be joining us, so there’s nothing I can say about any of them – and even if I did know, it would be worth more than my commission to tell you without authorization, with respect. Now, if you’ll excuse me.”

As he spurred his horse to the front of the train, I reflected on what he’d said. Tension between the Dales and Cormanthor. Well, well, well. I will have to observe these sprites more closely, it seems.
