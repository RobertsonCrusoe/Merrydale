10 Mirtul 1371 - Repaying Debts
The characters have much to think about during the following tenday. Disillusioned with honest work after Serpentil’s Folios, Garth becomes a Dockward landlord. His first tenant is a recently arrived Calishite scholar named Jafar. A wizard by trade, Jafar is sponsored to join the Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors by a local mage, Orilar Thanas, and assigned to the City Firewatch. Esperel also finds sponsorship into the local Finesmith’s Guild under Master Halthor Duzmund.

On the afternoon of the tenth, Ellyth, Esperel, Garth and Tomas receive invitations from Thagdul to meet at the Thirsty Throat Tavern, to discuss their outstanding ‘debt’.Jafar is also summoned, being in the trader’s debt for reasons of his own. After a splendid repast, Thagdul lays out his proposal. To clear the debt they owe him, the five are to burn down a house in Northward. As an added incentive, Thagdul offers ‘salvage rights’ on the contents of the building. He also indicates that Gilthanas has made amends independently, and has since left Waterdeep.

Despite Ellyth’s odd suspicion that Thagdul may be a dragon in human guise, the party reluctantly agrees. A ‘diversion’ has been arranged to occupy the City Firewatch and Guard at half past ten bells that evening. Thagdul directs them to [[Dalael Safehouse|a large four-story building in Northward]], on the block bounded by the High Road and Sulmor, Delzorin and Vhezoar Streets, indicating that further instructions await in a nearby sewer entrance.

The party splits up to prepare. While Tomas returns to Dockward to retrieve his armour, Esperel and Garth reconnoitre the target. They detect movement on the upper stories, indicating that the house is not abandoned. Meanwhile, Ellyth discreetly makes enquiries about Thagdul among her contacts at a local gambling den, The Gentle Mermaid. Unfortunately, her mark, a noble named Arsten Thunderstaff II, has no apparent knowledge of the Moonsea trader or his operation. To soothe her disappointment in his ignorance, Ellyth convinces Thunderstaff to cut her in on his winnings.

At ten bells, the party rendezvous at the sewer entrance, where they find a crate containing five detonation devices and a map of the building. Esperel locates a hidden passage that leads to a chamber directly below the building. The room contains the ruins of several statues, upon which Ellyth senses residual traces of an enchantment. There are no obvious doors, but Esperel soon uncovers a secret entrance to the building above. As it is opened, however, three figures burst in on the party, just as two spider-like creatures attack from behind. At least one attacker is a drow. 

Esperel wedges the door shut against the drow, confronting the two remaining men with Ellyth and Jafar, while Garth and Tomas attack the watchspiders Tomas suffers a poisonous bite, but is not severely hurt, and he and Garth soon dispatch the spiders. Seeing this, the men surrender and are knocked unconscious and bound.

Esperel finds the door blocked from the other side, but Tomas is able to bash it open, revealing a storeroom of crates and kegs, scattered about with books and scrolls. Ellyth and Jafar set to examining the room’s contents while the others pursue the fleeing drow. Tomas forces open a second blocked door, revealing a room containing the smashed remains of a jade spider statue. As they enter, the drow darts out the opposite door, and Esperel and Tomas give chase up a flight of stairs, leaving Garth to inspect the room. Upstairs, however, the pursuers encounter a group of surprised-looking men, prompting a hasty retreat. Luckily, only one man pursues – attacking Esperel as Tomas sets an explosive charge on the stairwell. Esperel is soon able to force his opponent back upstairs, just as Garth emerges from the cellar. Together, the three climb the staircase, splitting up to set the remaining charges – Garth on the second floor, Esperel on the third, and Tomas pursuing the retreating man to the fourth.

Garth finds the second storey to have been recently vacated or ransacked, and he sets his charge then escapes out a window into a nearby tree. Searching the third storey, similarly ransacked, Esperel finds two rune-covered porcelain cases, oddly untouched. Sensing danger, he leaves them, sets his charge and retreats downstairs. Upstairs, Tomas finds his quarry pounding on a door, yelling “Vuldan!”. Seeing Tomas, the man flees around a corner, but as Tomas gives chase, the door opens and a strangely calm voice asks what is going on. Turning, Tomas is horrified to see what looks like a beholder floating in mid-air behind him. With unnerving politeness, the creature requests that there be no violence. Tomas, fear-stricken, warns of the impending immolation, but the creature appears unconcerned, drifting nonchalantly back into its room. The man Tomas had been chasing has, meanwhile, dashed out onto a balcony, but Tomas leaves him, sets his charge and returns downstairs to rejoin his comrades.

Hoping that Garth has made his own way out, the remaining party members move what they can into the sewer entrance before the bombs detonate. Garth rejoins the party at the sewer entrance, and while he and the others take inventory of their ‘salvage’, Tomas returns home for a cart to transport the haul, which consists of: 

* 1 exotic dagger (upon which Jafar identifies a drow enchantment)
* 2 hand crossbows
* 2 stone heads (identified by Jafar as former golem heads)
* 88 lbs. of jade (lightly enchanted – likely a former golem)
* A sealed scroll, written in the drow tongue
* 2 journals, also in drow
* 40 books on the subject of Cormyr
* Selections from The Draconomicon concerning ‘Khaldun, the Deeping Dragon’, annotated in drow
