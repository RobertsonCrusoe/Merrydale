In the aftermath of the encounter, Lucan watches quietly as Jasper moves to recover Coventina’s remains. He instinctively clenches his loins as his mind replays the horrific scene—the girl’s body falling onto the cruciform blades, quartering before tumbling into the cages below. There is some mercy, he reflects, in the fact that no one else had witnessed what he had seen—especially not Jasper. That Shadovar witch had murdered Coventina with calculated cruelty. Despite Jasper being several years his senior, Lucan feels a surge of protectiveness toward his friend, mingled with a cold, crystallizing rage at the Shadovar for inflicting such a devastating blow.

As he ruminates, a recovered Jesra flits over to his shoulder and throws her tiny arms around his neck. "Oh Stone Tree! The false-face daughter fell to your sharp-singing arrow!" She twirls in excitement, her glow pulsing erratically. "So brave! Like lightning. Strong-armed mortal mine!" She strokes his cheek with miniature fingers. "Poor flower-lady Coventina, thorns hidden beneath petals she had. Nasty thorns, yes? But maybe she didn’t know they were thorns at all! Not like sister-elf, who wears her sharp edges where all can see and admire!"

She looks over at Jeska, who stands methodically sawing away at Charrah's corpse, severing the changeling’s head to display it from the parapets as a warning to the goblin hordes infesting Thistle Ridge. Jesra's wings hum a discordant tune as she leans into Lucan's ear, her voice shifting between singsong and whisper. "Dragon-slayer she is! Hero of old tales now walking! You've hero-light too, my Stone Tree, but different stars guide your path." The sprite's glow deepens to a sunset hue. "Sister-elf's heart beats like war drums. Like forbidden dances. She makes my wings warm. Such delicious danger! Oh! Look now, Stone Tree! The changeling’s tale ends with a tale still to tell! Sister-elf severs the story at its sweetest chapter! Charrah will speak to the yellow-teethed scuttlers without saying a word!"

At that, she launches herself off Lucan’s shoulder and flies over to join Embra and Masra, the trio darting this way and that as they appreciate Jeska’s grisly work.

Moving over to the pit, beside which Jasper sits with Coventina’s wrapped remains, Lucan looks at his friend. Jasper’s body is limp, but he is as still as the package beside him. Lucan isn’t quite sure that he’s even breathing. No – there is some movement. Jasper’s eyes are darting around the room, and Lucan suddenly realizes that the wizard is replaying the events of their encounter in his mind. He sighs and sits down beside his friend, his legs dangling over the edge of the deathtrap. Once settled, he pulls out a silver flask and takes a deep swallow before waving it in Jasper’s direction. Jasper doesn’t even look at the young Sembian.

"By all the gods and their mistresses," Lucan mutters, his usual bombast deflated as he stares into the pit where Coventina fell. He takes another small sip from the flask.

“Jas,” he begins “We did what we could. You did what you could. Coventina’s gone, but she’ll not be forgotten, eh? And, you know…” he trails off until he sees Jasper turn his head his way. “It falls to us to see how she’s remembered.

“We’ve only got Charrah’s word that Coventina served the Mistress of Night. And even if we entertain the notion that that one single detail—alone out of the cross-eyed skank’s entire lying monologue, mind you—was actually true, what benefit is there to telling anyone? There’s no profit in it. Cassimar won’t thank us for impugning her dead niece’s memory. So why tell her at all? Better she doesn’t know. Just like I don’t tell you every time Jesra and I watch you and Masra through that hole in your door.  Let Coventina remain what she truly was—a victim of forces beyond her control.”
His voice takes on a thoughtful tone, refreshingly free of self-congratulation.
“Did she betray us? Maybe! But what of it? We have circumstantial evidence at best, and only Charrah's word as to what her motivation was. In Saerloon, you’d barely even have to pay off a magistrate to acquit her. For all we know, she acted under magical compulsion. Or maybe she was threatened. Or maybe that witch used some arcane fetish to eavesdrop on her every whispered word."

"We’ll tell Cassimar that Charrah intended to use Coventina as leverage—to extort influence in Dagger Falls and force the Council to relinquish Thistle Ridge. We’ll tell her that Charrah never intended to honour any agreement, which is why she attempted to sacrifice Coventina to the Orb. We’ll tell her we arrived too late to save her, but we were able to end the witch who captured her and, as seems likely, reclaim Thistle Ridge in the process."
He glances at Jasper's devastated expression and softens his tone further.
"I won't insult you by claiming to understand exactly what she meant to you. But I know you had affection for her. By Sune's smallclothes, what man with eyes wouldn't? A touch clingy for my tastes, but there you go. One doesn’t let personality get in the way of soft … right. Sorry." He catches himself. "Look. It may well be that she played you false. But I've known enough lovestruck maidens to recognize adulation. Her feelings for you were true, as sure as Chaunteans shovel shit. It’s a tragedy, I know, but you’re not alone in this. You’ll find Masra can be very comforting. And maybe Jesra can help out with that. I wouldn’t mind at all…”

Lucan trails off, his mind wandering. Then, shaking his head with a smile, his voice grows colder, and he places a hand on Jasper's shoulder.


"Look, my friend—don't let this harden your heart. The Realms have enough cynical bastards lounging about, drowning their sorrows in wine and bedding other men's wives. You keep the rest of us honest."  Lucan glances furtively at Mari, Jeska, and Ser Rowan, then shrugs.
His voice drops to a deadly serious tone, all pretense now gone.
"We got Charrah. That’s an unalloyed good. But this conspiracy started well before her. The Shadovar were as deeply entangled in the plot to dishonor my family name as that hag and her misbegotten whelp. If that shadow-witch was indeed Loki—and I'd wager every crate in Waukeen’s warehouse it was—then this Alavandor's vendetta is far from over. I swear to you, brother, by my father's name and the wealth of my house, we’ll find Loki. And we’ll stab at Shar right through the bitch’s heart.”
