== Darkness Falls ==

Iolus was following his friends through the gloom, and, like Tuugan, noticed that what little light there was in this realm was fading.  Before he, or any of his companions could react, he was in the deepest black, alone, and unable to find his friends.

Then, his limbs began to feel heavy, and soon, he was unable to stand.  He slouched against something that moved slightly, and then settled.  He called out once more for his friends, but his voice was only a choking noise, and the air itself seemed to draw out of his lungs, his head swam, and he collapsed.

== In Dreams ==

Some time later, perhaps hours, perhaps days, the twilight of the Shadowfell had returned, and Iolus stirred to life.  Looking around, he found he was quite alone; there was no sign of his friends at all.  In fact, he wasn't sure he recognized the place he was in.  He thought better of calling out; who knows what he might attract?  He tried his best to take a bearing in the gloom, and headed east.

As soon as he set off, he had that feeling at the nape of his neck that something was right behind him.  Turning, to face his stalker, he found only more gloom.  He let a flash of light go into the trees behind him, and his skin crawled when he saw a few shadowy forms shrink back from the light.

\"Run, you fool!\" a voice inside his head commanded.  It was not his own voice, but that of a woman.  And it sounded half like advice, and half like mockery.  No matter.  He ran.

== The Farm ==

Iolus ran for all he was worth.  The gloom did not give him enough light to dodge smaller reaching sticks and thorns, and he was terribly cut up before long.  He started to get a feel for the path he was on after some time, though.  It was familiar.  He ran and instinctively jumped over a crumbled portion in an old field-stone wall that he had jumped over a hundred times as a youth; he landed in the old apple orchard of Warburton Farm, where the gloom had let up just enough to illume the small cottage on the other side of the orchard he grew up in.

Now, behind the small cottage are the ruins of the greater estate that was built by his forebears, and brought to ruin and shame by Gode.  He played in these ruins as a child, but he grew up with his mother in a small cottage on the edge of that ruin, in the apple orchard, and it was this very cottage that he was creeping up to now, drawn towards the pale flickering light in the single window.

Through familiar trees he passed, his hands finding branches that he had ducked past countless times as a child and young man.  He crept ever nearer to the window, until he found himself crouched beneath it, listening to a rocking chair creaking back and forth inside.  He could hear the tender voice of his mother humming softly, and he was disarmed for a moment.  The gloom seemed to reside momentarily, and he worked up the nerve to stand and look in.

== The Ravens' Feast ==

He instantly wished he hadn't.  The humming stopped before he could look, and what he saw froze him in place; his mother sat in the chair, but she was naked and pale, and her guts were spilled onto the ground in front of her in a slick and black oozing mess at which three black ravens were pulling furiously, their attack at the festering entrails causing the chair to rock.  The black dead eyes of his mother were fixed at the window, staring lifelessly into his, and he was unable to look away for hours.  

Iolus was completely transfixed.  He was only able to look at his mother's face, and down at the feasting birds.  The birds devoured her spilled entrails completely, and they were so fat and bloated with her in their bellies that they just sat on their haunches and started to laugh a cacophony of cackling laughter.

The terrible din of the sated ravens shook Iolus from his paralysis.  That, and his mother's head slumped down as the crows settled on her feet and lap, and the chair stopped rocking.  He shrank back down from the window, and with his back to the wall, he covered his ears and looked out across the apple orchard.

== The Mob ==

The gloom had again settled on the place, and the light from the window went dim, and the ravens went silent.  From across the apple orchard, Iolus could see a number of flickering lights weaving through the trees.  There were a dozen or more of them, and as they drew nearer, he could hear the noise of many people coming through the orchard, and then voices.

\"We'll kill every last one of those Zhent bastards!\"
\"Let's hang 'em!  The boy and the bitch!\"
\"Hangin's too good!  Let's tie 'em to ol'Darius and light em up!\"

Pale dalesmen; nightmarish Shadowfell copies of real people he knew back in Dagger Falls were coming at him, with torches and weapons.  They seemed to be in a long row, fanned out to encompass the entire estate, leaving nowhere to run, but deeper into the ruined estate.

Iolus ducked through a hole in an old wall and hid behind some rubble, and some of the pale lynch mob passed by him without noticing.  There were more behind those, however, and one small girl in a white dress, clutching a stuffed bear in one hand, pointed at him after making eye contact.  She wore an expressionless face, and Iolus put finger to mouth to instruct her to 'shush', but she closed her eyes, rolled back her head, and her mouth opened impossibly large and let out a deafening screech.  Iolus fell back from the violent sound, and then scrambled to his feet and began to run. 

The Shadowfell dalesmen gave chase.  Though he used every trick, short cut and hiding place he knew from his childhood, he could not hide from them, and often, the little girl would spot him and laugh.  Finally, he found himself the family graveyard, surrounded on all sides by an angry mob that was closing in, weapons and torches brandished menacingly.  Of a sudden, the mob stopped.  Collectively, they gasped, and then a moment later the recoiled in unison. At that very moment, Iolus felt the ground under his feet move, just a bit.  Then, the ground moved more, and the villagers shrieked and stepped back several paces.

== Darius Returned ==

From between the legs of Iolus, a hand emerged, clawing its way free.  In a second's time, an arm was free, then another, then a man was emerging, sputtering dirt from a skeletal face with only a thin sheet of dried skin stretched across it.  Iolus looked at the headstone, which read \"Darius Warburton\", and at that moment, the huge tattered skeletal thing was free, and was pulling a huge rusty sword from the soil.  The dalesmen were drawing back as one, and a child began to cry.  The hulking skeletal remains of Darius, who's face Iolus had looked at every day of his life on the statue of the very same man at the entrance to the ruin, was then standing tall and straight, and it wore a grin on its thin lips, and a light flickered in the hollow eye sockets.

\"Stand aside, coward!\" the monster bellowed as it gave Iolus a shove which knocked him to the ground.  \"I'll show these cowardly shits what happens when they come to Warburton Farm without an invitation!\"

With that, two young men dashed forward.  Their faces now were no longer pale and twisted, but looked for all the world like a pair of young men he had served with in the Sun Blades.  The two men were cut in half with one great swing of the rusted blade, their faces struck frozen in disbelief before falling to the rich soil.

\"No!\" cried Iolus, as he got to his feet.  He grabbed the sword with his left hand, and the jawbone of his ancestor with his right.  He looked over his shoulder to see that some of the young men were now closing in, emboldened by his restraint of his undead forebear.  Through his grappled jaw, the skeleton grumbled curses at Iolus.  \"Damned cowardly whelp!  Let's face them together, and show them what the Warburton name really means!\" The skeletal hand of Darius Warburton began to draw the blade slowly through Iolus' hand, leaving a deep and jagged cut, and eventually Iolus had to let go.  

== The Statue Pyre ==

As Iolus relinquished the blade and the jawbone of Darius, the huge skeletal thing cast him off, and kicked him away with one huge boot.  Iolus fell into the midst of the dalesmen, and they immediately set upon him.  Confused and injured, Iolus didn't realize what was happening until he was bound hand and foot on the ground, with several pairs of boots holding him down.  There had been a brief battle between Darius and some of the young men, and though a dozen bisected corpses now littered the ground, Iolus could see man men with hammers and shovels obliterating the bones of his ancestor.  He tried to call out and to resist, but was struck on the head and blacked out.

When Iolus came to, he was bound to the statue of Darius in the ruins.  All about him, the gloom of the Shadowfell was punctuated by fires; this time, rather than torches, the timbers of the ruins were alight, and the derelict walls were now starting to crumble violently.  Through the flickering flames, Iolus could see a trio of black birds flying through the smoke and ruin.

Iolus struggled to get free, but he was held fast.

After an eternity of watching the fires grow and the walls crumble, one of the ravens flew to his feet.  It had a long thin burning match in its beak, and it dropped it at his feet, into the waiting tinder.

Flames slowly crept up Iolus Warburton, and the agony of it, which lasted for what seemed hours, made him roll his head back; he died looking up at the stony jaw of the statue of Darius.
