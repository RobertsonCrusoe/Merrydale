Fiona Scarlette 
Aide-de-camp & cleric of Sune 
She is short and curvy, at 5'1", and not particularly athletic.  She has naturally blonde hair, but it is dyed black today, and she favours wearing lots of makeup to match the colour of her hair.  She goes on and on about the dressing table she had at The Old Fort in Charity, and how she could apply any colour to her face or hair, and you can see that she misses it.  

She is an orphan, and never knew her parents.  The surname 'Scarlett' in Charity denotes her status as an orphan, just like the surname 'Dale' in the rest of the Dalelands.  In Charity, it is a sign that you are likely not free, and is a stigma, whereas many boast of their surname of Dale, as being emblematic of their self-reliance. 

Fiona was found on the steps of Charnel Priory 16 years ago, and was raised by the Sisters until the age of 8.  She showed no promise as a Sister, and was assigned to live with the Keystone family.  Her adopted father, Walter Keystone, was the Merchants' Guildmaster, as well as Speaker of the Common Assembly.  He was one of the most influential people of his caste ('Middlemen'), but was revealed recently to be part of a cult known as The Gideon Cult, and is at-large as a wanted man.  The family descended quickly into penury; Fiona was turned out, her 'papers' (you think this is something of an ownership contract or somesuch) signed over to Medea Rowan at The Old Fort. 

She had been a novitiate in the church of Sharess since the age of 12, and was already well known to Medea, and was a promising girl with enthusiasm and a natural affinity for people and her goddess that made her stand apart from other girls her age.  However, she never got accustomed to the rougher men of the Old Fort, and she knew that her time would soon be coming to minister to her patrons in a more intimate manner. Some of the older girls told her that there were many men asking when she would be available, and that she should be careful.  It was well known that the dilettante Godwin Ferguson asked after her all the time, and that Mme. Medea had refused great inducements to turn her out early from Godwin.

"Merry", as he prefers to be called, is a middle-aged halfling with shaggy grey hair and a kind face under some reading glasses perched on his nose.  He wears sensible clothes, and has his sleeves rolled up and some gardening tools hanging out of his pockets as he smiles and greets you.
