14 Kythorn The sand is everywhere. In my boots, in my beard, in the very water we drink. The "Golden Death" nearly had us yesterday. If I hadn't seen the shift in the wind and ordered the horses hobbled in the lee of that ridge, we'd be buried under ten feet of Anauroch right now.

Maturin is a rock. He stood out in the gale for three hours, shielding the pack animals with his own body. I've never seen a man with such endurance. Dorian, on the other hand, spent the storm complaining about the "dryness" of the air. I told him if he wanted moisture, he could go find a cactus, but I think the sarcasm was lost on him.

We've made a terrible mistake in our navigation. The sun in this place plays tricks on the eyes. We’ve spent four days walking in a circle. We are back near the trade road, or what’s left of it. I need to keep a closer eye on the stars tonight, provided the sky clears.
