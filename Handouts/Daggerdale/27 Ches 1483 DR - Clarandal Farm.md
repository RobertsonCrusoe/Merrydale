27 Ches, 1483 DR - Clarandal Farm 

After a perilous trek through Cormanthor, you finally come to a small hill about a ¼ mile away from your objective.  From here you can see sitting atop a small hill like an island in the shallow valley before you, Clarandal Farm.  From this distance you can make out that there is one large building intact, with several other outbuildings and barns having been reduced to ruins.  The main house seems to be in relatively good condition, and smoke is rising from some of the many chimneys of the fine, 2-story Sembian-styled home.  It is mostly stone, with strong stone-reinforced terraces about the building which are likely responsible for it's current condition, having kept out the aggressive forest reasonably well. 

Around the hill in the middle of the valley there are 2 fires giving off a thick smoke that lazily drifts through the valley.  "They're reburning the fields" Mari says, taking it all in and guessing what is happening.  "You see that spot there; a creek is feeding it, and there was likely a big vegetable farm where that fire is.  And across the valley on that far west-facing slope is likely where they planted barley.  There's a fire there too.  The goblins are resettling this farm.'  The words hang heavy in the air like the smoke which presently dominates your sense of smell. 

As you get a bit closer to the surviving stone house, other smells compete with the smoke.  You can smell the filth of the goblin camp; feces and unwashed bodies, rotten meat, tanning hides and other foulness.  You can see that surrounding the large stately ruin are a number of goblin camps, with their crude tents, fires giving off an oily smoke, crude tables, benches and other camp accoutrements scattered disorderly about, and skulls and bones decorating just about all of it. 
The Bedroom of Lilly Clarandal 
Outside the room are several hanging fetishes of crude manufacture.  Runes and warnings in the goblin tongue have been carved into the door, and a string of goblin skulls, some still with desiccated flesh hanging from them, hangs across the door 2' off the ground. 

Inside, the room is in reasonably good condition, relative to the rest of the house.  The furniture is intact, and it looks as though the room hasn't been ransacked.  The furniture coverings and rugs have been damaged by moisture and neglect, but other than that, this room appears to be in good order, and is clearly the bedroom of a little girl. 

The dolls were of very fine workmanship, and have survived the ages and depredations of goblins and decades of other intruders remarkably well.  There are 4 of them, all of similar make, and are clearly a set. One appears to be a human girl with olive skin, and wears a red dress and cape, but also has a leather belt and scabbard, and a small wooden shield is affixed to her arm.  Another olive-skinned girl wears a green dress and has green wrappings on her wrists.  The last human-sized girl has very pale skin, and wears a grey dress and a leather scabbard in which is little silver kukri is affixed.  Finally, there is a halfling girl with curly blonde hair wearing a brown dress and apron with an oak leaf embroidered on it.
