DDC – 1481 – 13: Note from Phinneas Hoetmer to Basil Muloch 
Written on 27 Flamerule, 1480, Hoetmer once again displays his prescience, if not his ability to make others take it seriously. 
GFD Notes: 
This note was in the archives in the Historic Wing of the Yellow Abbey, and was, I think a copy. The original must be at Loedegan Close with the rest of Gale's things, but Reks didn't disclose it. From Paros' journal, we do know that this was recovered in the kitchen at the Muloch Estate, where Basil liked to do most of his reading, when the White Cloaks investigated his home. 

I am forced to wonder what it was that Hoetmer didn't send to Muloch. I never found out, so others will have to discover that secret. 
-GFD 
My Old Friend; 
There will be a Blue Moon in a couple of nights, and when these fall in Flamerule, the whole world seems to go crazy. I certainly do; I have been pouring over old records like there won't be a tomorrow, and I found something that immediately put me in mind of your 'sleepy little towne'. This is a copy of some passages of the journal of Loedegan Whitegate that I found in the Yellow Abbey. When I read the one enclosed, I immediately thought of you and yours. I know you are tired of hearing me say that it is Him guiding my hand, and in this case, my pen, so I'll save all that this time. Please do take consideration of this transcript, however. I feel it will touch upon events in the near future in your part of the dale. 

I nearly enclosed one other thing as well. It was a locket that I thought somehow connected to the journal. I will keep it a while and ruminate upon it. I trust my gut, and I trust Him, but sometimes a slow approach is best. 

I believe this missive puts me in an awkwardly advantageous position for two old correspondents. I look forward to your next letter, hopefully this decade. If not, I will be happy to have tea with you Hereafter. 
Until we meet again, Phin Hoetmer. 
27 Flamerule, 1480 DR.
