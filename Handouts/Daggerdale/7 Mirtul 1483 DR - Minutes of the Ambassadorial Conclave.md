7 Mirtul, 1483 DR - Minutes of the Ambassadorial Conclave

These being the notes that I, Master Meriadoc Burgundy, Steward of the Embassy of the Elven Court in Dagger Falls, did assemble during and shortly after Ambassador Monarch met in the Mythral'Telanor, at the gracious request of the Ambassador, and stored herein for posterity.

Around five bells of the evening, Ambassador Monarch ('Monarch' or 'Jeska') summoned me to the basement, where she had opened the doors to the Mythral'Telanor. I have only been in this chambre once before, and paused. She gave a slight nod, acknowledging my hesitation and reverence for the place. "It is time I reported in" she said with solemnity. She was right of course; the Elven Court had summoned her, as evidenced by the ghost-like purple butterflies that fluttered about the Embassy, alerting us to their attention at the other end.

The Mythral'Telanor is a small chambre in the deepest part of the Embassy, and is austere, save for a couple of comfortable leather chairs and a fine birch table in the middle of the room, upon which a large stone bowl of water sits. The room is the Mythral, and the bowl the Telanor, or so I surmise. These are elvish things, and though I have a good grasp of their tongue for every-day matters, strong magics and such are a little out of my reconning.

At any rate, there we were, sat in the chairs. Me with a board and some paper and pens, and Jeska sat cross legged and, forgive me, a bit on edge if I'm right honest. I don't think she likes this part of the job, and she's told me to keep this in. I'll never fully understand the eldar...

After some time, the butterflies gathered quick about us, and of a sudden vanished, and then she was there after the dazzle in my eyes subsided; Lady Miratar, Princess of the Elven Court, and Monarch's liege lord. Miratar is one of the stateliest creatures anyone could see; she is beautiful and imperious like Jeska, but in an intangible way. Almost like a god, forgive me, but Jeska says I'm very near the truth on that matter, when it comes to the Family of the Elven Court; they are now old even by eldar standards, and their wisdom exceeds that of many gods.

I found myself weeping of a sudden after taking her in. Miratar smiled at me, and reached out a hand as though to reassure me with a touch of my shoulder. But she is only the image of the Princess, sitting in a room just like this a thousand miles away. I asked forgiveness for the unexpected show of emotion, but Jeska was understanding and kind, and said "you weep, though you don't know it, because you look upon the Princess of the Last Household on this continent. The Miratars have remained when all others answered the summons to Evermeet, far across the Trackless Sea, and beyond the Curtain. The Princess and the Miratars, and maybe even myself, will someday have to answer this summons too, when we are done dealing with Shadow. Your tears are not misplaced, but do let's continue. Even an elven princess can run out of time..."

"Indeed, Ms. Monarch" was the reply of the patient princess. Her voice came into the chambre like a four-piece band of minstrels of the greatest skill; music and feeling reverberating through my ears and body, filling me with the meaning of her words, more so than the words themselves. And it's a good thing too, because what followed was a discussion exclusively in the eldar tongue. And not the crude 'elvish' you might hear a clever merchant or hoity scholar use. This was elves talking through a Telanor, and they spoke with their hearts as much as their tongues, though now an hour later, I find it all a little hard to describe. I will stick to what Monarch has asked me to record.

I'll start with the short list of things she said I didn't need to write down, because Lucan and Jasper can't put down pens even when it's time to pick up a blade and practice. She gave the full reporting of all her activities since the 19th of Ches. This is a date less than fifty days past, yet the list sounded like it might take years to accomplish;

- Forming team Chrysalis
- Winning a grand competition which included several tournaments
- Establishing allies, trading partners and military alliances
- Reconnaissance of the hinterlands around Thistle Ridge
- The reconstituting of Greenwood Farm
- The capture of Thistle Ridge Tower and the eradication of a nightmare enemy
- Discovery of a plot that goes back decades or more, and is intertwined with...
- The fey courts of the region that Chrysalis has become inextricably connected with
- The mustering of soldiers to defend these holdings
- The hiring of tradesmen to begin reconstruction of the various holdings
- The final sacking of Thistle Ridge Town Hall and the elimination of an even greater enemy
- The discovery of the Slave Orbs...

This last bit began the exchange of information, rather than a unidirectional report. The Princess was very interested in these orbs, and the involvement of the Shadow. Here she had good intelligence for Monarch.

"Your description does indeed match that of a Slave Orb, and we know full well that the Orbis Imperiosis are in Shade, in the tower of Prince Melegaunt, and have been for a few years now. The Slave Orbs function to bring some of the power of the parent orbs (which are quite large and unwieldy) to a localized region. You're familiar now with some of the mischief they can cause, and the cost to sew it. Let me clarify some facts for you, so you can better know our enemy;

"There are indeed three Slave Orbs, and according to your report, two remain in the Historic Wing. This would be a good place for them, if not for the inconvenient escape of one from interment there already. A better place for them I know not. Not here, indeed, for our struggles intensify just as yours. They are connected. What is good is that we know the location, more or less, of the orbs. What is bad is that one remains in the hands of Mouvranesh, and she is the greatest of Melegaunt's agents. She has operated in the Dales for over 50 years, and is not to be trifled with. She plays only pawns, though she is but a bishop. If you can corner and eliminate her, you must. But be wary; if she appears vulnerable, your eyes are mistaken, and your feet are close to a snare.

"Now I will update you on the politics of our great enemy, as they impact your assignment. You are chiefly engaged with Melegaunt, and he is on the back-foot. His losses the last several years to the tenacious and oft-underestimated Dalesmen, and their new dwarven allies, has jeopardized his standing in Shade. Princes of Shade do not love one another; they see the next as an obstacle to their ambitions. Melegaunt can feel his brothers' relish for his failures, and he is desperate to prevail over Daggerdale.

"His chief rivals in Shade are the neighbouring princes Brennus and Yder.

"Brennus you know well enough by now; his portfolio includes the subjugation of Sembia, which he failed at mostly, and gaining entry into our very halls in Myth Drannor. Like Melegaunt, he is anxious for a victory that he can show his father, after loosing so much ground in Sembia. Having heard all that you have to say about Sembians and their migration to Thistle Ridge, and the odd entanglement they have with Jannath, I expect that Brennus had a role to play and may have another role yet. One wonders if the fate of Thistle Ridge might not straddle the fates of both Brennus and Melegaunt. There may be some opportunity here...

"Yder has been struggling to subjugate Cormyr for twenty years now, and we have intelligence that he may have committed one of the few crimes the Netherese won't tolerate; allying with Devils. We are sure that they are allied with, if not entirely compelling, the Sons of Bel in Cormyr and beyond. If we had clear evidence of this that could be shown to the other princes, or better yet, Telamont himself, Yder would unravel. We have been seeking this evidence, as the Sons of Bel have been a nuisance for us here in the Elven Court, as you already know.

"If you can sort out how the interests of these three princes are at cross purposes, and shine a light on this discord, they may do our work for us. This may in fact be the only way to draw them out; we must acknowledge that the myriad battles against Shade in every part of Faerun happen everywhere except Anauroch. In fact, though they may not know it, the men of Daggerdale and the dwarves of Tethyamar together are the only to march against Shade. It is to their great credit that they have not only endured this enemy, but are again growing the strength to face the enemy in the West, on the plains of our enemy.

"We will do our part in the East, to ensure that those that cross blades in the Plains of Netheril don't have to look over their shoulders."

"We will talk again soon, Ms. Miratar. Corellon Larethian strengthen your bow and lighten your heart, sister." The princess began to fade, but she took one last moment to smile upon me, and nod approval at my work. This again brought me to tears, which have since subsided, allowing me to make this record, the quality of which I hope Monarch will approve.

-Master Burgundy, Servant of the Last Household
