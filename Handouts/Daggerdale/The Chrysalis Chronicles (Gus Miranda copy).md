## Prologue

# **A Call for Bravery, Endurance, and Honour\!** {#a-call-for-bravery,-endurance,-and-honour!}

Alturiak 17, 1483 DR.

By Order of the Governors’ Assembly, at its Seventh Sitting, Alturiak 21-29, 1483 DR, the establishment of the Thistle Ridge Reclamation and the creation of an Adjudicating Committee with authorization to oversee the Reclamation is hereby announced to all citizens of Daggerdale, and all her allies notified forthwith.

It is the will of the Assembly that a Company of suitable capability and virtue be Chartered to Reclaim the settlement known as Thistle Ridge Farm, to secure its walls and other properties, to hold and defend those properties from enemies current and future, and to see to the growth and prosperity of that settlement.

The Adjudicating Committee, which is composed of i) Bishop Whitegate, ii) High Cultivar Oakdale, iii) Mayor Cassimar, iv) General Thunderstorm, and v) Ambassador Whitmore (Charity), will receive applications from organizations with a Charter Militant, and those seeking a Temporary Charter Militant for the purposes of this endeavor. They will select the five most suitable Companies and pit them in competition to test all the skills needed to carry out the Reclamation.

## The Dragon’s Last Watch {#the-dragon’s-last-watch}

Sunrise. 1st day of the month of Ches in the Year of the Tasked Weasel, 1483 Dale Reckoning.

Zephrin had opened the gate a few hours earlier, but only now did the first signs of the rising sun begin to stretch on the eastern horizon. He had been waiting for this moment all night.

“So that’s that, eh?” came the tired voice from just inside the gate, where some semblance of warmth and shelter could be found. Sergeant Templeton and the other two sentries had been sitting around a brazier on the other side of the open portcullis, trying to keep warm as best they could. Coutts and Decker stood and stretched, their minds already on the ration of oatmeal and tea waiting for them at the barracks, not to mention their bunks.

“Be seeing ya, then,” said Coutts, with a wave of his hand towards Zephrin. Decker had never quite warmed to the Dragonborn, and simply gave a small nod before following his fellow Sunblade up into the town.

The morning squad hadn’t arrived yet, a fact that caused Templeton to curse them to freeze at Umberlee’s tits \- though he said it beneath his breath lest the Frost Queen be actually listening on a morning such as this.

Zephrin’s gaze was still on the horizon. Those first rays of sun meant he was now a free man. He couldn’t remember the last time he'd been free. Had he ever been?

He’d belonged to the Sunblades for the last 4 years. The True Zhents before that. The Last Spike and The First Spike both, before that. The Church of Oghma even before that...

Running fast into the woods with the twins, Robbie and Ricky Hounslow, when the three of them had each stolen one of Auntie Farah’s “award-winning” apple pies\! She’d been baking those all day for the Midsummer Fair…

Zephrin cracked a small smile at this one fleeting memory of his childhood amongst the halflings of Deepingdale.

Cousins Patty and Fat Vic had found them, and Robbie couldn’t sit down for a tenday after that\! He wondered if Auntie Farah still lived. He wondered if he showed up in Deepingdale this Midsummer if she’d bake him an apple pie.

Zephrin’s smile faded.

It wasn’t long after that day that everyone decided that Zephrin should spend more time at the Leaves of Learning and less time out on the Hounslow farm.

The last time he saw Ricky, she was on the ground, crying. She’d tried to stop him from coming north, and Zephrin had shoved her to the ground, pushing past her. How many years has it been? How many decades?

If he went home, would Ricky forgive him? Would Robbie or anyone else? Was it even home anymore? Or was Rockland right and all that waited for him at home in Deepingdale was a jail cell.

Zephrin took out the paper Captain Rockland had handed him last night: “A Call for Bravery, Endurance and Honour\!”

Sergeant Templeton cleared his throat, bringing Zephrin back from his reverie.

“Well, you big ol’ Lizard: this is the end of it, is it?” He said with a glint of affection in his eye. Like most of the Sunblades in town, Templeton had grown to like and respect the Dragonborn. He was a good soldier, sure. More importantly, as far as Templeton was concerned, he was a good thief and he always shared his spoils with his squadmates.

Almost as if reading the Sergeant’s mind, Zephrin reached into his satchel and pulled out a bottle of Cormyrean brandy, handing it over, and giving the speechless Templeton a pat on the shoulder as he walked away.

Later that morning, having collected his belongings from the barracks and gone through the final bureaucratic trials of officially leaving the Sunblades, Zephrin found himself approaching The Tomb.

Captain Rockland had told him to seek out an elf woman named Jeska here.

The two Redjacks standing guard at the door of the Myth Drannor Embassy had eyed him with curiosity, more than anything, as he approached. Most of the Daggerdalesmen had heard of Zephrin: it was hard to miss the only Dragonborn in town; much less one that had worn the Sunblade uniform and often stood guard at the gates of Dagger Falls.

But Zephrin mostly kept to himself, and neither Redjack sentry could think of any reason why the Dragonborn would be approaching them now. They tightened the grip on their weapons as he approached.

“What’s your business with…um, uh, Myth Drannor… friend?” Said the braver of the two.

Zephrin could see the knuckles whiten on the man’s grip on his halberd. He could see the sweat on their brow, despite the deep winter cold.

Deep inside him, unbidden, his blood rumbled, roiled and churned. Zephrin's throat filled with a metallic tingle, his teeth crackled with electricity.

Lightning. Claws. Thunder. Wings. Wind. Teeth. Waves. Death.

It would be a trifle to end these insignificant mammalian lives. These barely elevated apes. How dare they stand in his way\! How dare they question him?\! You are a Dragon\! Teach them their insignificance\! Let the storm loose and show them their place\!

His grip tightened around the handle of the axe at his waist.

Mammals...

A small tug on his sleeve.

It was almost imperceptible.

Another small tug, somehow enough to bring Zephrin back from the ledge he found himself on.

With a blink, he was back in Dagger Falls. He swallowed hard, the lightning doused and dissipated. The grip on his weapon relaxed.

*What was that?*

He was aware of the two sentries before him, stinking of fear and confusion.

Again, a small tug on his sleeve.

Zephrin looked down at a pile of rags pulling on his sleeve, a child’s eyes suddenly appearing from within. A small hand, steady with confidence and determination emerged from the rags and offered a small, folded piece of paper.

As soon as the paper had left its hand, the pile ran, disappearing deep into the town.

Zephrin looked to the sentries, still quavering, and down to the piece of paper and read: “Tonight. 8pm. Quick Axes. Don’t be late.”

He gave a look back at The Tomb, his gaze no longer on the guards but rather on the window above where a halfling was looking down at the scene. The halfling met Zephrin’s gaze, and gave him a small nod then drew the curtains shut.

Tonight, then.

## Three No Good Days In Tesh Valley {#three-no-good-days-in-tesh-valley}

19th of Alturiak, 1483

![][image1]

Within the walls of the Tesh Valley Farm, a pair of halflings wrestled with unloading a weighty trunk from a wagon.

"Your trunk is a right heavy one, Mum. What've you stashed in there?" inquired the stout halfling man.

Standing beside the wagon, a halfling woman with a mop of greying hair adorned with hints of orange streaks graced her head. Her once youthfully pretty visage now bore the gentle creases of age, etched beneath her eyes and cheeks. Marigold Thickwickle, known as Mari, smiled warmly as she replied.

"If you're sendin' me packin' from my own home, dear, then I'll be takin' my dishes with me," Mari replied.

In that moment, Mari's son, Pim, grunted as his grip faltered on the trunk's corner. He retorted, "Come on, Mum. You know it ain't safe for you here no more. It's gettin' worse with each passing day."

"As you wish, my sweetie. You and your brothers are old enough to make such decisions," Mari replied in her gentle, loving tone.

Just then the door swung open anda cascade of light spilled onto the street from inside the apartment. A youthful halfling lady, bearing a striking resemblance to a younger Mari, her hair auburn and figure untouched by motherhood, stood at the threshold.

"Hurry up and bring that inside. It's too dark to be dilly-dallyin' out here," she admonished, hands placed firmly on her hips.

"Won't take long if we each lend a hand and take a side, Bella," Mari suggested.

Together, the quartet efficiently finished unloading, leaving the wagon sheltered beneath the archway. The two men bid their farewells with brisk hugs and slipped away into the shadows of the night on barefoot.

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

19th of Alturiak, 1483

A distant dog's bark pierced the stillness, rousing Bella and Mari from their shared bed. Swiftly, they left their cozy haven and moved into the adjacent room. Bella cautiously cracked open a window, surveyed the surroundings, and then withdrew her head, firmly closing the window behind her.

"It's them," Bella reported.

"All right, daughter. This time, you'll handle it on your own, just as I know you can. Yondalla blesses all halfling women who keep a good home," Mari encouraged.

Bella briefly glanced at the black scarf gracing her neck, then sighed, nodding in acknowledgment. Mari retreated to the bedroom, leaving the door impercebtibly ajar.

Before long, a pounding echoed at the door, accompanied by a barking dog. Bella promptly swung the door open and whispered, "Quick, come inside. We don't want to wake Mother."

A dog promptly darted inside, making a beeline for the bedroom door, sniffing and scratching. Unseen by all, a treat slipped through the door's crack, which Mari deftly closed.

Standing at the threshold were Mari's sons from the previous day, supporting a third halfling between them. He appeared roughly bandaged and exhibited signs of severe bleeding underneath. His face and shins bore scrapes and wounds.

To a human observer, the lad might seem like a mere child. However, to halflings, it was evident that he was in his twenties—still youthful, on the cusp of adulthood.

"Argh, it hurts\!" the young man exclaimed.

"Hush, Doddy, or you'll wake Mum," Pim snapped.

“Not to worry, “ said Bella, “Mother has taken the pipe tonight. She won’t be waking up till the morrow.”

"I want Mummy\!" Doddy wailed through the pain.

"Well, you'll have to settle for your sister\! Now, get him up on the table," Bella instructed.

Bella went to work, tending to Doddy's wounds in a proficient but somewhat brusque manner.

"Argh, Bella\! Not so hard," Doddy protested.

"Quit your bellyachin', you big baby. I won't be a moment longer," Bella retorted.

With the wounds dressed, Bella placed her hand on a small brooch she wore, quietly invoking, "Yondalla, heal my family." She kissed her hand and tenderly touched it to her brother's injuries.

Suddenly, Doddy's body tensed, arching before swiftly relaxing.

"Thank you, my dear sister," Doddy expressed, his voice steadier and more composed. "Could you rustle us up something to eat?"

Bella offered a warm smile to her brothers and replied, "Of course. Have a seat, and I'll put the kettle on."

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

20th of Alturiak, 1483

The following morning, Bella, having stayed up late the previous night to see her brothers off, awoke later than usual. After offering her morning prayers, she ventured into the kitchen. The table was set for her breakfast, and Mari appeared to be packing her belongings.

"What are you up to, Mother? You can't just leave right after you've just arrived," Bella protested.

Mari straightened up and approached her daughter. "I'm afraid I must, sweetie."

"But why? We need you here."

"Oh, Bella, my dear. You're a Thickwickle through and through—kind-hearted and caring. You'll do just fine looking after the family. You and your brothers need to forge your own paths for a time."

"But you're a Thickwickle too, for Yondalla's sake\!" Bella exclaimed, her emotions held at bay, but tears glistening in her eyes.

Mari gently but firmly placed her hands on Bella's shoulders. "Aye, I am indeed a Thickwickle through and through, and for most of my days, that's been true\! Yet—I'm also a Sweetberry, and Sweetberrys are cut from a different cloth. We must tend to our kin, but we've a duty to our kind too."

Bella sighed in resignation. "I ought to know by now there's no changing your mind."

"You've tugged at my heartstrings your entire life, my dear. But this is a path I must walk. Come, let me show you something."

Mari led Bella over to the trunk they had moved just two days prior, carefully unwrapping a cloth that concealed a small metal shield beneath. The aged shield bore the marks of time but was meticulously preserved, catching the sunlight with a subtle shimmer of oil. Embossed on its front was the image of a cornucopia, bursting with the harvest of all seasons.[^1]

"A shield of Yondalla..." Bella whispered, her voice trailing off in reverence.

"This belonged to your Thrice Great-Grandmother. She gifted it to me when I was scarcely older than you. I tucked it away to start this family, but now, I am called to bear it."

Beneath the shield rested a humble set of leather armor.

"Is that Father's armor?" Bella inquired.

"Aye, though I believe it will suit me well with a few adjustments to the buckles. Here, help me don it." Mari and her daughter shared a quiet moment, their emotions unspoken yet evident in their eyes, brimming with unshed tears.

"Alright, my dear. The wagon's nearly loaded. Lend me a hand with the last few items from the trunk, and then I must be on my way."

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Outside, Mari climbed into the wagon, taking up the reins of the petite pony that pulled it. Bella stood beside, looking up at her mother.

"You know, Mother, you may have objected to Pim sending you away for the full tenday, but I'm beginning to suspect it was your idea all along," Bella remarked, a faint smile touching her lips.

Mari smiled softly and replied, "Perhaps, my darling. Perhaps." With that, she gently tugged the reins and called out to the pony, "Alright, Peony, let's be on our way\!"

The pony neighed and began its slow journey down the alley toward the south gate. The alleyway lay empty, except for the halfling and her wagon. Unbeknownst to her, the dog from the previous night trailed behind at a safe distance, whimpering softly but never daring to bark.

Mari rode in relative silence, a hopeful smile gracing her lips. She retrieved a piece of parchment sized for humans and unfurled it. Her smile widened as she held aloft the tabloid and whispered to herself, "Perhaps this will bring enduring peace to us hobbits of the Dale."

At the top of the parchment, in bold letters, read:

**A Call for Bravery, Endurance, and Honor\!**

Weave... just as good as any of those university educated gods-damned elitist hacks\!”

The young man is somewhat riled up now, exuding the confidence that had escaped him earlier.

“Look, I can be useful to you\! I can help you win this contest, and then you can help me get to Thistle Ridge Farm where I can study them up close\! What do you have to lose?”

He looks at all of you expectantly, desperation in his eyes.

“What do you say?”

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Jeska lowers and sheathes Eclose.

She reaches into her satchel and draws forth a contract. She looks down at the torn contract, then down the street where the mighty Drgaonborn walked away.

“It appears we are in need of a Scribe. If you can fulfill this role, sign on the line Jasper of Shadowdale.”

She hands him the contract.

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Before Jeska is even finished speaking, the young man leaps and takes the contract from her hand, an enormous smile of gratitude on his face. He wheels around and kneels at an enormous backpack that had been at his feet; as he opens it and begins scrounging around, you catch a glimpse of bottles and jars that hold a variety of "specimens" and liquids, books ans scrolls, and bits of brass and pewter equipment \- but before your curiosity can fully be satisfied, the stench hits you like a wall and makes you all take an involuntary step back.

Unable to find what he is looking for, he grabs one of the jars which contains some greenish liquid, and what looks like a dead toad floating inside. Jasper opens it and dips his index finger into it, and swiftly signs his name on the contract with the goo. The paper sizzles and smokes a little, as does the tip of his finger, but both are otherwise unharmed.

With an enormous smile of gratitude, and a great look of relief in his eyes, he hands the paper back: “You won't regret this\!”

He notices all eyes are still on the open jar he is holding, where a very dead, and mostly desicated toad is attempting to crawl out.

“What...?”

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Mari looks back and forth between the wizard and her friends, mouth agape. She takes a moment to collect her thoughts before turning to address Jeska.

“I think we should take dinner at the embassy tonight, all of us. We have much to do before tomorrow and must make haste. Let us each go off and find out what we can today and make a plan together tonight.”

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Jeska is also horrified. She looks at the contract. “Agreed”

She looks at Jasper.

“My Brains ... and my... Death?.... So be it.”

She turns and begins walking again.

“Yes Mari, Supper at the Embassy, I am suddenly in the mood for Frogs Legs.”

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Mari exclaimed, "Hold that thought\!" her voice laced with urgency as she sprinted after Zephrin.

Zephrin, with his head start and long strides, moved swiftly ahead. Mari, pausing briefly, raised her holy symbol and opened her mouth to shout...

...yet to the baffled onlookers around her, not a single sound escaped her lips.[^3]

Zephrin, unwavering, continued his march, seemingly bound by an invisible force. Abruptly, Mari's voice, infused with desperation, pierced his ears.

“Mr. Zephrin Dragon Son\! A word, if you would be so kind\!”

“There's no need for such volume. Your words reach me clearly,” Zephrin responded, his voice a deep, tranquil rumble.

“Oh\! Of course,” Mari blurted out, flustered.

“My apologies, young sir. It's a habit from corralling my little ones.”

Mari's tone shifted, laden with sincerity. “I'm not here to plead for your return. It seems to me you're in search of something far more profound.”

“You've walked paths few can fathom. We, hobbits and dragons alike, find ourselves navigating realms not entirely our own.”

Her voice softened further, brimming with empathy. “Your whole life, these adventures, these journies, this quest, whatever fanciful name we give it, it was thrust upon you. You had no choice. The burden that darkens your spirit—it's not your doing.”

Her voice wavered, barely holding back tears. “It pains me that I never shared with you... every hobbit in the Dale knows of your father's valor in Deepingdale. Every single one, I assure you\!”

Looking back, Zephrin can see Mari running after him, screaming like a lunatic.

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

As Mari approaches, she slows down.

Zephrin says, “We will meet again, grandmother. Of this I am sure. We will be free.”

“Zephrin, my son. Grant me a favor, please. Journey back to my homestead in the Tesh Valley. Deliver the news to my kin that I am in good spirits. Let them know I cannot come back just yet.”

Her voice trembles as she utters those final words.

“If you ever speak these words to any Thickwickle, 'the nettle stings but often sustains,' they will recognize you as my kin, and you will be embraced with warmth and hospitality.”

“For you, I will do as you ask.” Zephrin comes down on one knee, and bows low. Tumble comes up and gives Mari a lick on her hand, and a small whine.

The Dragonborn stands, and walks out, heading north towards the Tesh Valley rather than south towards Deepingdale.

With one final look at Mari, Rumble turns and follows.

“Farewell, my son,” Mari's voice concludes, and with those parting words, Zephrin is gone..

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

As Mari trails Zephrin, and Jeska stares in curious horror at the young wizard as he begins the laborious process of re-stowing his portable laboratory, Lucan looks again at Jeska's now-resheathed rapier.

“Waukeen whip me,” he reflects, "but that elf is either mad or... well, an elf. Fool me once... When things are too good to be true, well,  you know the truth is not good. Let's see how that rapier serves her in battle before we take her up on any offers like that, eh, Lucan, milad?"

Out loud, he approaches Jeska, and speaks to her quietly. “Governess, tell Mari I'm going to find out what I can about this Ferguson. I have an inkling that if we turn down this offer, he might make another we can't refuse. If I have a chance, I'll also scope out the competition. I'll meet you and....”

He glances down at the mage, sidestepping a stoppered copper flask that slips away from Jasper's nervous rustling.

“... the team back at the embassy this afternoon.”

Addressing the mage, he says more loudly. “I'm Lucan, friend. Oh \- mind that spill…”

Jasper looks over his shoulder to see a black powdery substance spilling from a jar onto a large cobble. Gasping in horror, he quickly reaches into his pack, retrieving a dustpan and a very delicate-looking brush with which he begins very gingerly to sweep up the grains of powder, lips muttering in what seems to be prayer.

After conferring briefly with Fiona, Lucan walks off into the market.

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Addressing the mage, he says more loudly. “I'm Lucan, friend. Oh \- mind that spill…”

“Well, yes\! Of course\! I mean, er… Hello?” Jasper suddenly realizes that Lucan has no recollection of him whatsoever.

“We came in on the same caravan… I think… well… yes, of course… Didn’t we? Yes. We did. I think?”

Lucan can hardly be expected to remember every anonymous peasant that walked with the trade caravan.

Jasper’s gear properly stowed away, his attention finally unfocused from obtaining the means to reach Thistle Ridge, lands on the smell and sight of the food being prepared by the nearby Chaunteans.

“Um… we will have supper later, was it? That is, I mean…” an audible growl comes from Jasper’s stomach. His attention snaps back to the others.

“Wait\! I have a question\! Where? No… I mean, what? No\! Wait\! That is to say: First… um… when? And also… yes, what where? And, also… what? And I guess also, who? And perhaps… what, as well…?”

Jasper looks around expectantly, but the others have largely moved on.

“Master Lucan\! Perhaps I can be of help to you\!” He rallies, and brings himself together to focus on the Sembian youth and catch his attention before he walks away, “you see I spent the last tenday \- more…? \-(He teeters dangerously on the edge of distraction as he begins to count days in his mind)- in any case, since I’ve been here, I’ve been trying to join any company that would take me to Thistle Ridge. White Cloaks, Standing Stones, Freedom Coins \- the Freedom Coins, incidentally, were very keen to take my deposit, but I can’t seem to find them anywhere anymore to get my money back… \- anyway… I’ve had chance to, well…’meet’ might be too strong a word; but I can help you ‘scope out the competition’ (he says this with an awkward conspiratorial smile). I think I know who will be on each team. If you want to, we ca-…” 

Jasper’s voice fades and his gaze turns away from Lucan, his eyes locked on Mari walking away. His sunburnt face manages to turn an even brighter red.

Jasper has noticed Fiona.

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Mari comes walking back to the group, slightly out of breath.

“Pardon my mascara,” she says with a fond smile, “I was just bidding farewell to my son.”

She turns her attention towards Jasper, the young wizard, her eyes twinkling with motherly concern.

“Well hello, my young wizard friend. It is a most fortuitous pleasure to meet you," she greets him warmly, her gaze assessing his appearance. "Are you hungry, my boy? You are practically skin and bones\! Here, let me see if I have something on me. I usually carry something to nibble on.”

Reaching into her pack, Mari retrieves one of the pastries she had purchased at the morning market.

“Here you go, love. Have a bite to eat while I get your things tidied up a bit.”

She kisses her hand and touches her holy symbol. As she leans in, the messy liquids on the ground begin to dissolve. Her attention remains focused on her work as she crooks her head to speak.

“My two bright boys," she begins, her voice brimming with maternal affection, "I am hoping you can help us out.”

“Now, we must learn more of this Ferguson fellow, which you have offered to do," she continues. "But we also have a fight with some White Cloaks tomorrow, and I'm hoping you can help us find out some information about that.”

“Firstly, I would like to see if we can find these White Cloaks and try to judge their capabilities some. I would like to know if we are meant to win or meant to lose. If we lose, not all is lost, but I should like to know ahead of time just the same.”

“Secondly,” Mari goes on, “I want to learn the rules of this damn fight and scout out the grounds we will be fighting on. Perhaps Jeska and I can do that?”

“Thirdly,” she adds, her tone turning inquisitive, “pardon my unlearned ways, Master Wizard, my boy, but it is my understanding that you wizards can do practically anything. Why, we have a beloved hobbit tale where an old wizard visits a young hobbit one day, and he ends up defeating a dragon in the end\!”

“Now, you seem to be a bit short on silver, but perhaps we can all pool our coin some and purchase you a scroll or two that might turn the tide of battle tomorrow. Do you think that's a possibility, son?”

❖ ❖ ❖ ❖ ❖

Jasper snaps out of it, and graciously accepts the proffered pastry, his eyes still stuck on Fiona.

“Thaumph youth\!” He manages to get out while gorging on the pastry.

As soon as Mari mentions wizardry, he blinks, Fiona all but forgotten, and he swivels his attention on the halfling woman.

“Well, yes… I mean… a dragon, huh?” He stares off into the sky scratching the back of his ear, mouthing to himself what seem to be random numbers.

“It would have to be a very old wizard indeed… or perhaps… hmm.. How fat would you say the hobbit was? I mean… (a quick count on his fingers) Five stone at least\! I mean if we’re talking a standard chromatic Great Wyrm… Yes\! You’d want a properly well-fed hobbit\! Er…?”

Jasper’s mind comes back to the real world.

“Scrolls\! Of course\!” He pats his torso as if looking for something that isn’t there. “Right\! Well yes… I may be a bit short on that at the moment, but I think you’ve got the right idea\!”

He pauses, thinking to himself.

“Is he ginger-haired? The hobbit, I mean. Hmm… I guess chestnut or auburn is fine, but a ginger’d have to be very fat indeed if he was going up against a dragon of any color\! Ha\! Er… What?”
