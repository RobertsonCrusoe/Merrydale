Be creative lads.  I'd suggest making two characters each and then we can

Source: Gmail thread 13652256d8210935
From: gus.miranda@gmail.com

Actually... I had this idea last night for a reluctant priest-type.
Someone who is a little touched, and forcibly recruited into the divine
service of a particular god by the voices/apparitions in his head.  Might
be some minor, or possibly dead god, calling the shots.  I don't even know
who the gods are in the 4E Realms.  The character might be a cleric, oracle
or even witch(?).  The idea is that he's just a Daggerdale lad being
compelled to do what the voices in his head tell him to do, just to get
them to shut the hell up.

2nd character is definitely a Fighter, possibly Barbarian.  Been thinking a
long time of a Warrior-type with a Spear.  Like Blacky's useless Old Man,
I've had this dude in mind that can fight with both ends of a spear
(two-weapon fighting) really effectively:  knocking down and tripping with
the blunt end, then skewering or slicing with the sharp end.  Chucking it
at the enemy when necessary.  He'd have a bit of Barbarian thrown into the
mix.  Possibly he'd be one of those xenophobic wild elves, or some other
allowed race.

Might be interesting to combine both concepts into a Paladin build.  I
never got to play my Paladin after spending so much time building him, and
I'd still like to try.  But I think any Paladin I make now would be
against-type. Not a knight at all, but some form of noble savage, or
low-born whelp.  It would be all about the concepts of morality and honour,
and not about knighthood at all.  I like the idea of a peasant lad with
nothing but a spear and a solid sense of moral righteousness showing the
armoured knights of the world what honour really means.

Though I'd still love to play my pal Erevan the Friendly Drow in Daggerdale.
