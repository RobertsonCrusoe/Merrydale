Memorable Moments in Capture the Flag - Todd's Notes and the Making of the Ballad

by Todd Burry (as Ser Rowan/Mari) and Stephen Robertson (as Beliard Emmarask/Lucan)
Email thread: "Memorable moments in capture the flag", 18-19 Feb 2024

The behind-the-scenes exchange in which Todd Burry laid out his narrative vision for the first round of the Thistle Ridge Competition (Capture the Flag on the Mountain, 20 Ches 1483 DR), and Stephen Robertson composed the ballad "The Monkey On the Mountain" in response. The thread captures Todd's character-by-character notes, the composition process, Todd's corrections (longbow not crossbow; first victory, not overall), Stephen's explanation of working with ChatGPT, and an alternate Icelandic Saga version. Les Blackwell joined at the end with his reaction.

The final ballad was later shared with the full group in the "Mountain of Mayhem" thread (16-23 Feb 2024) and is preserved in the songbook "Songs of the Thistle."

---

TODD'S NARRATIVE NOTES (18 Feb 2024)

Todd to Stephen:

Hey Dixon. Here are some narratives I want to see recanted for the first challenge of the competition.

One thing I discovered while making some pictures is that you should take artistic license a bit. If you want to change the order of some things or condense the timeline, please go for it.

To this end, one of my regrets is not talking or describing stuff during the combat. The podcasts always have their character narrate their action briefly before rolling dice, and it makes the combat more memorable. So, I'm hoping artistic license can bring that out this time around.

Anyway, here is how I think of some of the stuff happening.

Opening Moments
Ser Rowan starts off jumping towards the flag. Mari scurries forward on her little halfling legs. Lucan gets shot. Ser Rowan's heels dig into the dirt in hesitation when he sees that. Mari yells, "Just go for the flag! I have Lucan!". Mari runs back and calls on Yondalla to save Lucan, and he is up. Starting off the battle on a dire note will make for a great song.

Ser Rowan's Arc
Ser Rowan bouncing is going to be pretty easy. Really, he went for the flag, got filled with arrows and spells, and then grabbed the flag and bounced up the mountain. He was close to being downed many times but managed to stay up. When he was on the mountain, he swung his hammer at the Dale brothers. Feel free to call Ser Rowan anything with the word "Ser" in front of it. He's heard it all.

Jeska's Arc
Jeska tumbled around to protect the flag valiantly, all the while hurling funny insults at the Dales. In the end she ended up in the water. I would make her crossbow grappler get the flag and emphasize how hard it was for the Dale brothers to get it to the top. I think it would be nice flavor if Jeska's banter emphasized the Dale brothers being handsome.

Jasper's Antics
This will be one of the good ones. Jasper uncorked his decanter, sending the Dale brother flying backward and dropping the flag. I think there was also a big gust of wind. Then, of course, the monkey is the star of the show. If you can have him leap off the mountain with the flag, that would be fun (And their flag was never seen again).

The magical counterspell with their wizard could be something, but whatever.

Lucan's bow
You know what you did, so I'll leave it at that. You occupied 3/5's of their team at one point, which deserves high praise.

Mari
Mari earned her keep, I think, but didn't do anything else of note. I would leave her out of everything about the opening.

Jeska's last shot
I imagine Ser Rowan was blocking the shot on top of the mountain, and Jeska was leaning, trying to get the shot. Maybe she even yells out at Rowan to get out of the way. Finally, she jumps on the fence to get a better shot and says to herself, "Show me your heart, stag," before loosing the perfect shot to end the combat.

These are just some thoughts to help jog your memory.

STEPHEN'S FIRST DRAFT OF THE BALLAD (18 Feb 2024, evening)

Stephen to Todd:

Here's a bit of bardery to consider:

In Dagger Falls, as snowflakes whirled, two teams of valor met,
Chrysalis bold and Whitecloaks proud, in a challenge fiercely set.
The field prepared, the flags were placed, excitement ruled the day,
For boasting rights, and victory, the gods would have their say.

The bell was rung, Ser Rowan took flight and towards the flag did bound,
While Mari, quick and small and brave, ran out to find her ground.
Lucan, laid low by Oakbough's sting, fell tangled, in dismay,
But healed by Mari's fervent prayers, he rose once more to join the fray.

Zelia's arrows flew like hawks, at Ser Rowan aiming true,
Yet he with heart and girth and ire, the flag from her withdrew.
Then up the mountain's lofty peak, with flag in hand, he climbed,
'Midst magic spells and arrows' flight, his valor brightly shined.

Jeska, elf of wit and blade, danced 'round her flag with grace,
Her sharp and stinging verbal barbs led the Dales a spritely chase.
But Tyrell Dale, through melee's din, advanced with purpose strong,
To grasp the flag in eager hands, amidst the battle's song.

He hurled it to his kin in stride, a toss both bold and fleet,
Jannus caught it on the rise, their triumph seeming sweet.
Into the water Jeska plunged, 'twas by Tyrell's hand she fell,
Yet her spirit, bold and undimmed, rose with stories yet to tell.

Meanwhile, Jannus held the flag, and to the mountain path he raced,
Dodging slicks of Jasper's grease, with a skillful, artful pace.
Up he climbed, the peak in sight, the summit his to claim,
But Jasper's wind, with forceful might, soon brought him low again.

Then 'midst clash of steel, spell and brawn, a monkey took the stage,
A creature small, yet bold and brave, a true hero for this age.
The primate, swift and nimble, grabbed the flag with cheeky glee,
Then leapt from the mountain's lofty height, as cunning as could be.

Lucan, with his bow in hand, at Zelia and Alexandus aimed,
But Hilltop's healing thwarted him, their vigor she reclaimed.
So he seized the flag anew, and towards his goal, he ran,
He threw it back to Chrysalis' zone, a shrewd and clever plan.

Alexandus, the Whitecloak mage, strode towards the arena's heart,
To thwart the hedge wizard's simple ploys, to block his arcane art.
Yet Jasper, friend to bird and beast, sought the monkey's sly assist,
To disrupt the mage's eldritch plans, and his pride's fall to enlist.

The monkey, quick with jest and jape, caught the war mage unawares,
Its arms were strong, its aim was true, it flung dung into his hair.
Cormyr's son, a wizard proud, was thus by rustic mageling bested,
His pride was scarred by the monkey's jibe, his temper sorely tested.

Ser Rowan, up atop the peak, with hammer swung in might,
Faced the Brothers Dale in a fearsome fray, a truly gallant sight.
Then Jeska, with her crossbow poised, found just the perfect shot,
"Show me your heart, stag," she whispered, her aim forgot her not.

At last, the Whitecloaks, waning, their wills by war were worn,
Tara Hilltop hailed the end, her team's defeat was sworn.
Thus, Chrysalis, with heart and might, claimed victory that day,
Their deeds of bravery and skill, in my song I do convey.

THE CHATGPT QUESTION (19 Feb 2024)

Todd: Dixon, you truly are a talented writer. Is this all you or did ChatGPT help you out?

Stephen: Oh, I worked with ChatGPT on this for sure, but there were a lot of iterations until I was happy enough with it. In the end, I came up with a detailed chronology of the fight and then had it craft the song verse by verse. Sometimes I liked only one line and then used that as a seed to regenerate the verse. I edited a lot of them by hand too. ChatGPT is good at generating rhyming doggerel like this (I played around with different meters, but it wasn't very good at differentiating styles.

That said, I asked it to rewrite the final product as an Icelandic Saga, and got this:

THE ICELANDIC SAGA VERSION (19 Feb 2024)

In the days of yore, in Dagger Falls where snow swirled like spirits in the night, two bands of warriors met: the Chrysalis, brave and unyielding, and the Whitecloaks, proud as the northern winds. The field of battle was set, flags fluttering like the wings of ravens, under the watchful eyes of the gods, for glory and honor were at stake.

The clash commenced with the sound of the bell, echoing through the frost-laden air. Ser Rowan, bold as a winter storm, charged forth, his eyes fixed on the prize. Mari, swift as a river and brave as a she-wolf, darted forth to seek her fate. Lucan, felled by Oakbough's cruel hand, lay ensnared, a warrior defeated, yet through Mari's prayers, as powerful as the song of the Valkyries, he rose again to join the battle.

Zelia, with her arrows swift as falcons, aimed for Ser Rowan, the foe as sturdy as an ancient oak. Yet, he, with the fury of a tempest, wrested the flag from her grasp. Up the mountain's spine he ascended, with the flag as his charge, amidst the storm of spells and arrows, his bravery shining like the Northern Star.

Jeska, an elf as cunning as Loki and as sharp as Freyja's blade, danced around her banner, her taunts as piercing as thorns. But Tyrell Dale, through the chaos of battle, advanced with the determination of Thor, seizing the flag with hands eager for triumph. He cast it to his kin, a throw as mighty as Thor's hammer, and Jannus, seizing it mid-air, thought victory near. Yet Jeska, betrayed by Tyrell's hand, plunged into the waters below, her spirit undimmed, rising like a phoenix with tales of valor yet to be sung.

Jannus, with the flag in his grasp, raced to the mountain's path, evading Jasper's slippery traps with the grace of a stag. But Jasper's winds, fierce as a dragon's breath, brought him low once more. In this maelstrom of steel, spell, and might, a monkey, small but brave as Tyr, emerged. This cunning creature seized the flag with glee, a trickster's grin on its face, and leapt from the mountain, nimble as a fox.

Lucan, with bow in hand, aimed at Zelia and Alexandus, but Hilltop's healing magic, as rejuvenating as Idunn's apples, foiled his plans. So he, with a mind as sharp as Odin's, hurled the flag back to Chrysalis' realm, a clever ruse. Alexandus, the Whitecloak sorcerer, strode forth, his heart set on defeating Jasper's rustic magic. But Jasper, friend to all creatures, enlisted the monkey's aid. The monkey, with a jest as sly as Loki's, caught Alexandus off guard, pelting him with dung, a humbling blow to the proud mage.

Atop the mountain, Ser Rowan, wielding his hammer like Thor himself, clashed with the Brothers Dale, a spectacle worthy of the sagas. Jeska, with her longbow ready, whispered to the winds, "Show me your heart, stag." Her aim was true, her arrow unerring. As the battle waned, the Whitecloaks, weary and worn, faced their defeat. Tara Hilltop, with a heart as heavy as the setting sun, conceded their loss. Thus, the Chrysalis, with valor and might, triumphed, their deeds of bravery and cunning to be sung through the ages, a saga for the hearth and hall.
