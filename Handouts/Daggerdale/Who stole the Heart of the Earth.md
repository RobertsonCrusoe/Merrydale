The following notes and speculations were composed by Rufus of Selûne during his brief tenure with the Company of the Red Herring, between the events of Harvestide and his expedition to the North with Raven in search of Hob during the winter of 1371-2.
----

==Chronology==
=== Mirtul 25, 1371 ===
Thagdul sends the Company of the Red Herring to \"prevent an ambush\" being arranged by evil lycanthropes in the employ of Ersgul. A cargo of some value to the mysterious mage is at risk, and he wants to \"make sure he has a fair chance to buy it\" from the Company of the Southern Star, newly returned from the far jungles of Chult.

This scenario indicates two things

#Thagdul knows the timing and location of the planned ambush
#Thagdul has some knowledge of the cargo in possession of the Company of the Southern Star

The first item could only have come from someone close to, or within, Company Ersgul. While the second item may have come from some unrelated source, there seems no reason not to believe it came from the same source within Ersgul. It is interesting to me that in what I have been told of his briefing of the Red Herrings, Thagdul appears to have no knowledge or interest in the Heart of the Earth, which we have heretofore presumed to be the most valuable item in the cargo. In hindsight then, we can propose the following corollaries to the first two items then:

#Thagdul had a mole within Company Ersgul
#Thagdul was not being upfront with the Red Herrings on the matter of the Southern Star

=== Mirtul 26, 1371 ===
The Red Herrings secure the cargo, though the Southern Star perishes in the battle with Ersgul's agents, the Brothers Adard. Thagdul takes possession of the potions he was after, and leaves the rest of the spoils, including an ornate wooden box, to the Red Herrings.

=== Mirtul 27, 1371 ===

Kappiyan Flurmastyr, a purveyor of enchantments, scrolls and potions, informs the Red Herrings that the box contains none other than shavings from the Heart of the Earth, a Chultan rock of mystical properties that scholars believe is a fallen meteor or asteroid. Choosing profit over haste (and thereby demonstrating their inexperience in the art of larceny), the Red Herrings decide that to sit on their acquisition, rather than sell it straightaway at a discount.

Over the next tenday, news filters back to the Red Herrings that underworld rumour has it that it was the Red Herrings themselves who ambushed and slew the Southern Star, and not Ersgul. This is presumed to be disinformation spread by agents of Ersgul, sore from their defeat. While we know this to be untrue, this gossip reveals that anyone who knew that the Heart of the Earth was part of the Southern Star cargo would now assume it to be in our possession.

Without unjustifiable speculation, we can assume this knowledge to be limited to the following parties:

*Thagdul and his agents
*Members of the Company of the Red Herring
*Kappiyan Flurmastyr
*Villains within the Ersgul network.

=== Kythorn 4, 1371 ===

Thagdul puts a bounty on the Reprobates, a party of Ersgul enforcers (who count among their members those notorious Brothers Adard). The Red Herrings arrange their own ambush for the Reprobates, and the ensuing battle accounts for all known members of both the Reprobates and the Red Herring, with the exception of Jafar – whom neither Garth nor Tomas could account for when they related these events to me. During the battle, the Heart of the Earth is stolen from the Red Herrings' safehouse.

===Kythorn 6, 1371===

Thagdul reveals information that Haskar Hunabar the Younger has tried to move the Heart of the Earth through a fence frequented by Company Drurginz, Alek Lenter.

=== Later that year ===

During the Harvestide celebrations of 1371, Thagdul terminated any contractual obligations between the Company of the Red Herring and Company Drurginz.

=== Eleint 25, 1371 ===

Intelligence suggests that a death threat has been made against Huskar Hunabar, resulting in increased security at the Hunabar Villa. A mage named Kalindos has been retained as a bodyguard, as have a complement of mercenaries in the employ of the Silmerhelve noble family.

=== Eleint 26, 1371 ===

The Red Herrings, their Harper Allies, as well as Rychel Voordan and Stuart Bold raid the Hunabar Villa, and the following revelations occur:
#The Hunabars are a family of lycanthropes (werewolves)
#The family worships an unknown, likely evil, god of lycanthropes
#The family was in possession of a third Beholder Staff
#The family was in possession of the box that formerly contained the Heart of the Earth, and now contained a locket and a note indicating that the locket was a gift to Joldara Hunabar from Istolil Dalael.

=== Eleint 27, 1371 ===

I return to the Hunabar Villa as a messenger of Selûne, but am violently attacked when Haskar the Elder learns (from Rychel's letter) that Thagdul is involved with the Red Herrings. Haskar also indicates that his son's affairs are his own, and nothing to do with him. Later that day, the new lining of the box is traced through arcane means to a safehouse in the sewers where Rychel is fooled by the drow Rubikawn into believing that he is conversing with \"Istolil\" Dalael. The encounter leads the Red Herrings into a drow ambush when the intelligence about Raven's whereabouts turns out to be a trap.

=== Eleint 28, 1371 ===
The staff recovered from the Hunabar Villa is stolen from the headquarters of the Red Herrings by an unknown thief during a bloody battle at the Dripping Dagger.

Following Harvestide, a story is sold to a popular Waterdhavian broadsheet by Raven and Rychel, but the story is never published.

== Conjecture ==

The history related above raises some interesting points for conjecture.

=== The Role of Istolil Dalael ===

I will start with the note from “Istolil”, who we have every reason to believe is actually Rubikawn, since a) the box was traced to his safehouse, and b) he used the charade to fool Rychel, a Red Herring, and no stranger to drow himself.

If Rubikawn, then in the employ of Company Ersgul, an ally of the Dalael family, was indeed charading as a Dalael suitor for Joldara’s hand, then such a charade, if not born of suicidal madness, must surely have had the Firstboy's blessing. To corroborate this, we saw that Rubikawn was working in concert with the silver-sashed drow who ambushed us in Dockward, undoubtedly members of Company Dalael. Having left the Reprobates, it is a good bet that Rubikawn had found a new master in Quevin Dalael. Given this hypothesis, we can propose that the Dalael, through Rubikawn, were trying to infiltrate yet another Waterdhavian House, as they have with the Houses of Eltorchul, Dezlentyr and Anteos. There are many connections by which this budding alliance may have been facilitated:

*Horth Hunabar, whom I suppose to be a close relative, though I know not his position in the family, is a member of the Company of the Deep Delvers, and thus a comrade of Corinna Dezlentyr, who we know to be Quellar Dalael’s wife.
*Rubikawn, a member of the Reprobate mercenary crew, was a colleague of the evil lycanthrope brothers Balkine and Lucien. No children of Selûne these, I wonder if they had similar religious convictions to the Hunabars. Conjecture, surely, but also plausible.

There seems no question that, whatever circumstance had come to pass to bring them together, Joldara Hunabar was being wooed by a drow. But if Rubikawn was indeed “Istolil”, then the Hunabars were being negotiated with under false pretences. This leads me to believe that it is unlikely that the Dalael were committed to a long-term charade, and that any benefits from the alliance would be realized in the short term. I believe this is an important insight, and will return to it shortly, but my thoughts these past hours have revealed to me the importance of another matter.

=== Why does Haskar hate Thagdul? ===

In my nightmares, I still see Haskar's frothing jaws as that lupine fiend leapt at me from the balcony in his House. That his rage at the name “Thagdul”, so fatefully mentioned in Rychel’s letter, was so immediate and so complete, that he so unreservedly cast aside his prior ultimatum to us to return the staff by noon, Haskar must have known of Thagdul and his connection to the staff. It must have been clear to the patriarch that the staff was lost utterly. Why would Haskar so desperately want to keep the staff from Thagdul personally?

Several options:
#Thagdul was involved, somehow, during the events that surrounded the acquisition of the staff by the Hunabars. Regardless of their association at that time, the two were now at odds.
#Haskar had an unrelated vendetta against Thagdul, and vice versa, and the staff was merely a point of honour between sworn enemies.
#Haskar had designs at profiting by the staff that he knew could never be realized without it. 
#Another party, to which Haskar had some obligation, had made known their opposition to Thagdul possessing the staff.

Of these explanations, the first cannot be confirmed, and so brings me to a dead end. The second is rather whimsical, irreconcilable with what I know of Thagdul’s activities, and leaves the problem open to conjecture and speculation as much as the first. And so I am left with the latter two options, which invite the relevance of other facts in the matter.

As the director of Company Drurginz, Thagdul was in open competition with the Dalael (through their association with Ersgul) with whom I suspect the Hunabars to have been negotiating an alliance, possibly to be cemented by a marriage between Joldara and “Istolil”. With this in mind, Haskar’s actions can be seen as the result of seeing a valuable resource go to the enemy of a possible ally - of potential family. But the alliance never came to fruition. Rubikawn was slain, and we presume the Dalael to have fled to the Underdark after securing the Phaulkon cargo. But what if the alliance was mooted even before the events on damnable night? I have said before that the Dalael were only pretending to the Hunabars, and a house built on a foundation of lies will easily topple. Why the charade if not to quickly acquire something from the Hunabars without creating a powerful enemy? If “Istolil” and Joldara were indeed to be married, then perhaps the dowry was to be the staff itself, a gesture between the Hunabars and the Dalael. That he would lose such a key strategic tool to the very enemy of an ally he hoped to gain would surely be a painful thorn in Huskar’s paw.

That both the Dalael and Thagdul were after the Hunabar Staff is very telling. If my suspicions about “Belduran” are correct, then Thagdul, like Malkor, is confirmed as a Zhentarim pawn of Zantiid, just as he was supposed to be by the Dalael, themselves associates of Xulla. If the beholders Xulla and Zantiid were the true seekers after the staff, then the events of last Harvestide can be seen much more clearly.

If the above interpretation is correct, then my writings so far on this matter would seem to be a narration of the machinations of Xulla to secure the third staff for the Dalael. As Ersgul failed, and Yarkul became a liability, perhaps Xulla sought to establish an independent link between the Dalael and himself, and so choose a new lieutenant to its cause. In the end, these machinations were utterly foiled, and Harvestide saw Xulla lose both Yarkul and Quevin.

=== Path of the Staff ===

But what of Zantiid and Thagdul? If the information we have uncovered on Malkor Githeas is accurate, then I believe that he is now in possession of the staff recovered from the Hunabar villa. A necessary condition for the above interpretation of events is that both Zantiid and Xulla, and their agents, knew where the third staff was. Thagdul’s narrative of the origins of the third staff (which he revealed after the theft of the staff, and thus when he had no reason to lie) tells us that it disappeared during a conflict between Malthoon’s High Road Shippers (Malkor again!) and the Dalael. We can presume that during the conflict the Dalael knew who had secured the staff, even if Malthoon’s remained in the dark. Indeed the note Garth showed me from Quellar Dalael mentions a brush with Zantiid by the drow. I wonder if it may relate to this matter. If so then it may be that the competition for the staff may have been more of a long-term project than I suspected.

A final matter remains. It seems very odd that the Red Herrings should have stumbled into this alliance, as it would have been very much in the interests of the parties involved to keep them as far away as possible. It would seem this involvement guaranteed both Xulla’s failure and Zantiid’s victory. The circumstances of the staff’s discovery, theft and reappearance seem to be overly coincidental. I believe the Red Herrings were set up.

=== The Setup ===

Information that the Hunabars had stolen the Heart of the Earth was the motivating factor in the involvement of the Red Herrings. Little evidence concerning the identity of the thief has been uncovered. True, the box formerly containing the Heart of the Earth was found in the Hunabar villa, but we have already determined that it was previously in the possession of Rubikawn. It seems unlikely that Haskar would have stolen the box, sold the Heart of the Earth, and then given the box to Rubikawn only to have it regifted containing a pledge for the love of Joldara. Such a series of events is too complex to be likely. Given equal evidence, the simplest explanation is best. How then, did Rubikawn come to possess the box that contained the Heart of the Earth?

#He got it from the thief that stole it from the Red Herrings.
#He never had it.

While Rubikawn himself has an alibi for the theft of the Heart of the Earth, it seems probable that an ally of his is responsible, or, more accurately, someone that Rubikawn trusted. As I have said, it seems likely that the theft was arranged in order to guarantee the involvement of the Red Herrings with the Hunabar family, and thus hamper with the Dalael. The information about Sal Worthingham revealed by Dragon, Yarkul's daughter, during her short-lived captivity keeps tugging at my mind. Was this man Teskul? If so, then we have an identity for Thagdul's agent within Ersgul. Could he also be the thief? As an agent of Thagdul, he would know that the Red Herrings were in possession of the Heart of the Earth, and as a double agent within Ersgul, he had access to Rubikawn. A plausible explanation, surely, but the question is of probability.

A taste for conspiracy invites a taste for complexity, and I wonder if this theory is not too twisted to be true. If Teskul was responsible for the theft, then he could easily have given the box to the Rubikawn or the Dalael for their negotiations for the staff, and ensured that Thagdul could then frame the Hunabars for the theft, knowing that the evidence had been planted. Other information tempers this possibility though. In the information she revealed to us after her capture, Dragon also professed that Ersgul didn't have a relationship with the Hunabars, and there seems to be little reason to distrust her. It is quite possible, even likely, that the play for the staff was unknown to Yarkul, and strictly a project of the Dalael, who have been Xulla's agents behind Yarkul's back before. If this is the case, then it seems unlikely that Teskul would have been close enough to the negotiations to ensure that the evidence was planted. Could he have been a triple agent?

== A Theory ==

In the end, I don't suppose it matters who the thief was. Considering the facts coolly, one simply must ask: who benefits? I am convinced that Zantiid, through Thagdul, and Xulla, through the Dalael, were maneuvering for the staff. These base assumptions, which I have already explored, will serve as the foundation for my argument.

Even if Haskar the Younger did indeed steal the Heart of the Earth against his family's interests, it is very unlikely that this would have happened by coincidence, as I have already said how it was this theft that guaranteed the involvement of the Red Herrings and thwarted the play by the Dalael for the staff. Even if Haskar did in fact steal it, then I would bet my moonblade that he did it acting on information from Thagdul. 

The scenario is thus: an agent of Thagdul's, Teskul, makes contact with Haskar the Younger, offering to sell intelligence on the location of some valuable illicit property, which Thagdul had previously arranged for the Red Herrings to acquire. Haskar takes the bait, paying who knows what in return? Impossible to say. The theft occurs, and two days later, Thagdul is able to report the intelligence from Alek Lenter to the Red Herrings without any hint of subterfuge, guaranteeing that his hounds have been put on the scent. Note that in any case, it is Thagdul who sets us after the Hunabars, and thus, the staff.

This, to me, seems to be the simplest explanation, which does not assume the unlikely connection between Sal Worthingham and the Dalael. Unfortunately, it does not explain why the box led us back to Rubikawn, and thus into the ambush, nor does it explain how Thagdul learned the location of the staff.

Both explanations lead me to the conclusion that Thagdul knew all along about the Heart of the Earth, and used it to guarantee the involvement of the Red Herrings in the affairs of the Hunabars. Through his contact within Ersgul, presumably Teskul, Thagdul arranged for us to acquire the Heart of the Earth, knowing that it would be useful for his own purposes.
