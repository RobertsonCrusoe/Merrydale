A conversation between an old pirate and a guardsman at the Thirsty Throat Tavern, shortly after Harvestide, 1372. Zechary Mudge arrives at the Thirsty Throat,  looks around, grabs a beer, looks around again, checks the door, looks around yet again, then sits quietly for long enough to down the brew. He walks to the bar and has a glass of rum (or rum-like substance) then heads over to a table where the Guard is sitting.

----

“Well met, old man. Join us for a beer.”

“Arr.”

“What brings you here today?”

“I hear you be looking fer young Garth Shanks. I be looking for him too. D’ye know aught of his whereabouts?”

“I was told to look here. Me and Garth have had our dealings. Are you familiar with the man named Rychel? Rumour has it that they’re in a bit of trouble, but I don’t believe it. I want to find them to get their side of the story before my pals in the Guard and I get involved in the hunt. Lord Piergieron’s upset. And if the big guy’s upset and calls us in over the Watch it’s because things are really fucked.”

“Sounds like me boy’s ‘re in the process of being keelhauled by someone who don’t like the cut of their jib. Garth’s a good lad, and whatever he’s got into got me sister’s boy Tomas sent to the Bitch Queen’s terlet. You tell Piergieron that fer me and keep yer pals from casting their nets too far out to sea.”

“Aye old man. I knew your nephew Tomas too. Never let his blade go dull. Drank like a dwarf the day before battling orcs. I’m sorry for your loss. Well how do we go about finding the lads?”

“Nobody ‘round the Ward knows where the boys is at. But if I know Garthy, he’s either sailing into a whirlpool in the middle of a hurricane or laying low in calm waters. What’s been happening around town lately?”

“Well, word on the street is that he was involved in the slaughter of many Watchmen last night. He’s hiding out for sure. But where?”

“That’s shark piss. I saw Garthy yesterday at Tomas’ funeral. Welll... Sayin’ yer speakin’ the right of it, and I’m not implicatin’ young Shanks here, but their Company seems to be workin’ fer someone, near’s I can tell (they seem to be doin’ okay, any row). Maybe they’re layin’ low with whoever sends ‘em on their jobs.”

“Well, I’ll ask the barkeep about their boss. There’s a foul smell over this whole business, old man. A foul smell. Word has it that the Magistrate looking for them ... Richter’s his name, is as crooked as a wizard with a sword. I’d take Garth’s word over Richter’s any day, I tell you what.”
