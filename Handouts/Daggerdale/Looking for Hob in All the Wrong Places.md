== Hob Sets Out ==
thumb
During Harvestide of 1371, Hob departed for Neverwinter to seek out Malkor. It was not difficult to find the man. Hob traveled with a Red Shields caravan, and learned that only Malthoon’s High Road Shippers managed to get along on the High Road unhindered all last year. That had changed ever since ‘those Harpers’ cleaned up the mess. Hob decided to start his searching at Malthoon’s.

There, his searching ended. Malkor, a talented diviner, saw Hob coming and ambushed him in the warehouse with dozens of darkenbeasts. Hob was put in a barrel and taken to Dekanter. There, he was let out, and found that he was one of dozens of slaves. Most of the slaves would have died if not for the efforts of the stalwart Halfling. He clothed and fed the slaves in the exposed pen (it was the beginning of winter when they arrived in Dekanter) and built up their strength for the long march that was ahead of them.

Soon enough, they were marching across Anauroch. Again, Hob kept up the strength of the other slaves by walking the lines and administering drinks to them. After the months-long march, Hob and his comrades were sent to work in the mines west of Dagger Falls, and would not be rescued until the coming of the Heroes from the Sands.

That does not mean that attempts were not made to find him before that…

== A Rescue Mission ==

By late Marpenoth of 1371, Raven and Rufus decided that Hob was long overdue. The autumn was tremendously cold already, and so the two decided to get moving before the snows closed the roads for winter. The Raventree family supplied horses, and the journey to Neverwinter to locate Hob began.

Four days of riding brought the two to the mining town of Leilon. The single inn of the town, the Sword of Leilon, is poorly organized, and expensive for these parts, but it was a welcome change from the cold they had been sleeping in for the last three nights. The inn was quiet, and there were no travelers to make inquiries of. The proprietors did recognize Hob’s description, though; apparently he had come through weeks ago with a caravan of Red Shields (a trading company which operates along the Sword Coast, and in the northern interior).

==A New Companion==

Two more days on horseback, and the pair came to Neverwinter. This expensive city frustrated both of them it is difficult just to get through the winding streets, let alone find anything out. However, after a couple days of looking around, they found a group of Red Shields in the Shining Serpent Inn. Inside, the place is absolutely full of men from this company, and after some well-worded inquiries, they found themselves speaking to a man named Agamemnon, in his room upstairs.

“I do know your friend, Hob,” explains the scholarly looking man. “He took passage from Waterdeep with us a couple of tendays ago, and I have not heard from him since. I suspect that he was going to the shipping house of Malthoon’s High Road Shippers, as he made some inquiries about them. It does not bode well that you have not heard from him after all this time.

“I should like to help you find him, as I may be partially responsible for his fate. It was I that pointed him in the direction of Malthoon’s. Come, let us set out now.”

==Malthoon's High Road Shippers==

Shortly, they found themselves being led through the streets with Agamemnon. Just as the sun was vanishing behind the roof-line of the city, the three turn up outside the office of the shipping house.

Upon approaching, a man was stealing out the door, and looking altogether very suspicious. This prompts action from Agamemnon, who yells ‘Hold there!” The person is startled and bolts. He is quickly caught in an alley by the three, and looked altogether frightened. The man appeared to be in his mid-thirties, had short black hair, brown eyes, and was dressed in leather armour with the sign of the Red Shields on his breast.

“Bartelos, you cur!” exclaims Agamemnon, who is clutching the prisoner. “What are you doing sneaking around here?” and he throws the man into a pile of trash, stands and faces the two to speak lowly to them. “Look up his left sleeve, Raven…” he implores. Pulling back his sleeve Raven finds the black mark of the Zhentarim tattooed on his left shoulder. “Hold him, while I cast a spell to see what is going on,” he asks, and then casts a short spell, then concentrates, eyes closed.

Thud! Thud! Agamemnon gives the Zhent two quick boots to the ribs, and the picture of rage is observed on his face. “This Zhent villain has come here looking for his master. I have read his surface thoughts. His master is not here, but he is worried that we will find out about a man named Malkor, who has traveled to Luskan. He is also afraid that we will find out about the slave caravan that Malkor is leading there.” This being said, he asks “What shall we do with this piece of trash?”

It that time that the Watch came by and interrupted. After a quick exchange, they end up hauling off the Zhent, leaving the three empty handed. “I have enough to go on; there are some friends of mine that I fear may also have gone to Luskan on that slave caravan, and I aim to free them. What say you?” That night, Raven and Rufus find themselves making preparations in Agamemnon’s room in the Shining Serpent for travel north, and the next day, the three set off to Luskan.

==To Luskan==
It was bitter cold the morning they left, and a slight snow flurry erupts part way through the day. However, after a hard day of riding, the three pull into Port Llast. The only inn in this village of 700 is The Alliance Arms, and it is in this quiet place that an attempt is made on their lives. While sleeping that night, someone set fire outside their room. The smoke quickly filled the place, and the three found themselves bailing out of the second-floor window. Below, a flock of assassins was waiting with poisoned crossbow bolts. Through a little bit of luck, most of the bolts struck Rufus, and had little effect. The five assassins were quickly overcome by the abilities of the three, and four were captured alive (Raven having killed one). Once again, the mage cast a spell for interrogation, and learned that the assassins were regular army from Luskan, hired by an unknown man to kill the three of them. Little else was discovered, and the local authorities hung the men the next morning.

===An Uncanny Likeness===
thumb|600px|The northern port of Luskan
That morning Agamemnon donned a suit of leather armour and bow. The bow had a symbol of Mielikki on it, and for Rufus, the resemblance of this man in this gear was far too reminiscent of the road-mage Zaltim. Throughout the day Rufus managed to bring up the subject obliquely and learned that Agamemnon had been a very close friend to Zaltim and Ansal Bloodshoulder. He explains that he had come here to help them, but he could not find them. The news of their deaths, conveyed by Rufus, brought the man into a rage, and the rest of the journey to Luskan was made shorter by his determined pace.

===Too Late===

Reaching Luskan the three are all treated like spies and thieves as soon as they enter the place, and there are certainly people following them at all times. Luckily, after a little investigation, they manage to learn that a suspicious-looking caravan headed to Mirabar a couple weeks ago. A couple witnesses claimed that they heard moaning coming from barrels that were supposed to be delivering salted fish to the miners of Mirabar.

By then, winter was near.
