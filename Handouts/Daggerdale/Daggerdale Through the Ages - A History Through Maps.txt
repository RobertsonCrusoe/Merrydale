Daggerdale Through the Ages  A History Through Maps 
The Volo Map (1367 DR) 
From Volos Guide to the Dalelands, this is the first known map with any distribution of Dagger Falls. This is during towards the end of the Randal Morn and Freedom Riders era of the dale. 

The Harper Map (c. 1371 DR) 
This is a rare version of a map that depicts the substantial improvements made to Dagger Falls, mostly with the application of hundreds of slaves, by the Zhents from 1367-1372 DR.  The walls expand considerably, and the beautiful Lords Bridge is completed. The Eastern Bailey is constructed, mostly for the corralling of animals and slaves.  The Tower has been completely restored and expanded, and, significantly, Brotherhood Tower and The Dark Sun Temple are constructed. 
Attribution for this map is difficult, but it is likely the work Harper agents who operated within the Falls during the final stage of the Zhent occupation. I found this map in Steeles folio. 

The Yellow Abbeye Map (1482 DR) 
There are a few very good copies of this map in circulation, and I was lucky enough to be able to purchase and reproduce this one. 
The most significant change to the town is the construction of the Dagger Falls Cathedral, is attenuated structures, and the Cathedral Bailey around it.  Interestingly, this map shows work in progress, including the expansion to the tower (Tethyamar Stone Masons Camp) and a new smaller bailey in the north west corner of the town.
