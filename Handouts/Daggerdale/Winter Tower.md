==Headquarters for the Free Defense League==

thumb|Frame|450px|Bridge across a narrowing in the Middle Bailey

The Tower is found on the outer wall of the outer bailey.  It is at the south end of the wall, nearest the south-gate.  This is a heavy burden, as any attack against the Castle would fall hardest here, as the southern approach is the easiest and most open.

The Tower is an irregular-shaped thing that makes up part of the wall, but juts out sharply into the outer bailey, its many corners, balconies, windows and crenulations making it difficult to focus on any one feature.  The Tower is 60’ high, has 6 levels (plus a dungeon-level), with a number of battlements on the upper 3 levels facing both inward and outward.  Entry into the Tower is through a huge oak door that sits on a 10’ x 15’ porch that is 4’ higher than the outer bailey floor.  The foot print of the Tower, though irregular in shape and convoluted, is roughly 80’ x 80’.

Access to most of the levels of the building is by one spiral stair which runs in one of the convoluted outcroppings from the main building, a sort of sub-tower.

Inside, visitors are first welcomed into the spacious hall that makes up most of the first floor.  This is a huge room, with a 20’ ceiling and a balcony running all the way around the upper 10’ of the room.  The room is full of every kind of trophy that the mountains nearby can provide.  There are stuffed wild animals of every type and size, and there are weapons, shields, armour and banners from dozens of defeated opponents decorating the walls.  There are also racks of serviceable weapons, including practice weapons of wood, and padded shields and helmets on racks nearby.  These devices, and the wide-open and bare stone floor, suggest that this hall is used more for practicing than anything else.  

The second level is made up mostly of rooms surrounding the balcony which looks down into the great hall below.  There are a dozen good sized rooms, and it is on this level that the hired garrison of 8 men stay, as well as members of the new team.  There are also kitchens and a large dining room on this level.

The third level, which begins to narrow in size from the huge base of the Tower, is where the members of Gryphon Company have their rooms and personal armoury, as well as another practice room.

The fourth level runs parallel with the upper battlements of the outer wall, and half of this level is compartmentalized to allow access from the battlements on one side of the tower, to those on the other side, while thick reinforced doors and locks keep this thoroughfare separate from the rest of the tower.  The other half of the fourth level is a large war room in which Bargle conducts his meetings.

The fifth level is the home of Dragon Company.  This level has a private armoury for this company, and the fine rooms that these adventurers have earned.  There is a private dining room, a servants quarters, practice room and fine lounge.  This level also has a small, but public library that is accessible to anyone in the League.

The sixth level is off-limits to anyone but Bargle and his assistant, Mr. Acorn.
