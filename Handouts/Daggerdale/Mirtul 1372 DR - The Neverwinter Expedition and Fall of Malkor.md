Summer, 1372, Decisions at Phaulkonmere 

A group of adventurers and heroes comes together at Phaulkonmere to discuss the Zhent and Drow threat to the City and to points beyond.  Many present were there because they had been involved in this mess for so long.  Others were there through a sense of duty to their fellow man. Some were there because they were seeking such things as adventure, fame and fortune.  Some were there for even more selfish reasons.  And some were there for the simple reason of pursuing vengeance. 

Present at Phaulkonmere that day were the following; 

Thagdul was conspicuously absent from this meeting, but Jafar insisted that he represented Thagdul.  This did nothing if not make worse the CRH member's opinion of this wayward mage. 

Events current and past were discussed.  From the East, Boregar brought a report most grim.  The Zhents had taken over Daggerfalls once again, and Randall Mourn himself had been hung from the new bridge over the Tesh River.  Yarkul had been put in place as ruler of Daggerfalls and Daggerdale, and his rule was enforced by an elite mercenary company known as The Fist.  Further, churches to Bane and Iyachtu Xvim had been built, and the town appeared to be taken for good. 

Further, the Zhents had hundreds of slaves toiling in a number of mines.  It was suspected that Hobb was imprisoned in one of these slave camps.  Outposts had been built in Anauroch, and Zhent caravans were coming and going through the Great Sand Sea with little difficulty, and the town of Daggerfalls was growing rich off the trade through its gates, as it is the gateway to Anauroch from the East. 

All trade through the more traditional routes (such as through the gap at Tilverton and then through Cormyr) had ceased because of a couple reasons.  Tilverton had been overrun by goblinoid hordes again, and a Zhent banner was flying from the city's walls.  Cormyr was involved in a costly war with the Sembians over a border-mountain that Mithral had been found in.  The resurrection of Bane had much to do with the problems in the East, but the Zhents had seemed also to have played the hand that was dealt them in a most cunning manner there. 

In summary, almost all trade between the Western Heartlands, The North, Amn, Tethyr and Calimshan was coming through the desert upon Zhent cargo trains, or paying huge fees to use their roads and buy safe passage.  The Zhents were growing wealthy, and most of this wealth seemed to be pouring into Daggerfalls, where extensive fortifications were being constructed.  Clearly, an expedition would have to be sent east to set things straight.  Yarkul, at least, would die.  Or so was the pledge of Sparrow. 

In the North, a second objective was set.  Malkor, too, would have to be brought down and the roads made safe once again.  The work of the Road Mage was not complete, and Beljuril and Rufus had made a pledge that they would honor. 

Finally, the City of Splendours itself became the last objective.  It was clear that the Zhents had too much influence in this city, and indeed, much of their wealth and power structure was now located here.  With the Company Dalael as another huge problem to be fixed, perhaps the group should have assigned a more capable task force to this objective.  Be that as it may, here are the stories of these three teams 

To set things straight in Neverwinter, and to repay their oath to the Road Mage, Rufus and Beljuril chose this path.  Along with them came a band of adventurers that had recently traveled from the Dalelands.  This group (though the whole group was sundered to send men with certain talents on certain missions) was comprised of May, a Witch from Rashemen, Oberon, an ancient rogue who had been rescued from a 3000 year stasis in an ancient dungeon, and Oliver, Master of Human Nature. 

It was decided that secrecy was needed on both expeditions.  To defend against the divining eyes of Malkor, Jafar sent Yarkul's staff along with this party.  The staff is supposed to provide protection from divination, but the NC finds that they are always one step behind Malkor anyway 

This crew was plagued during their journey by a portion of the headhunting company known as Grimwald's Rangers.  These were men who had fame and fear in many lands, and were well known by Oliver, himself from the same Dale as the leader of the outfit, 'Sheriff' Grimwald.  The Neverwinter Crew and the Daggerfalls Crew all traveled as far as Roarke's Tavern together, where their roads would part.  It was here that the Rangers were first encountered.  Their number was 7 at that point, and all were men who whore grim faces, fell and stern.  The newest member of the Rangers, and the only one to say a word was Stone Man, a huge man with a huge axe.  He tried to pick a fight with the adventurers, but none ensued that day.  

Days later, the Neverwinter Crew (NC) would actually get the drop on the Rangers.  Suspecting that they were being followed, Rufus stayed hidden in the forest, allowing his companions to pass, and the Rangers as well. 

Rufus found that three Rangers were following them;   

Shellwynd Thann, A Dalesman and old partner of the Sheriff.  He was a crack shot with a musket. 

Tuugan Voun, a large man with a large two handed sword 

Derrick Swift, a half-elf, and one of the sneakier members of the Rangers.  Fights with paired short swords. 

With the help of an invisibility spell from Beljuril, Rufus managed to follow the three for the day.  They were all riding mounts of the best quality, but stopped often to check the trails, and were wary of ambushes.  When they stopped to camp, Rufus hit them with a Call Lightning spell.  He managed to kill two of the horses, and bought the NC some valuable time. 

On the 17th of Mirtul, The NC arrives in Leilon.  They had run the horses hard to get here, hoping to have some time to prepare to fight the Rangers, or, even better, make their way for Neverwinter unabated. 

However, that would prove to be the longest night the NC ever had to spend.  They were harassed all night by sniper-shots through windows (obviously from Shellwynd).  What spells they cast to defend themselves are wasted, as they cannot rest to recover.  The Rangers seemingly know this, and do not relent.   

When the NC goes to the authorities to ask for help, they are put in jail 'for their own good'.  The jail itself is attacked as well, and the NC is nearly incinerated in their cell. 

Towards the end of the long night, while orcs mounted a raid outside the city, Leilon became a chaotic mess.  The NC decided to hole up in a temple to Lathander, but the Rangers pursued them there as well.  The result of this encounter was the death of Tuugan by May's familiar-snake.  The other two made good their escape. 

The NC shared harsh words with the Ruler of Leilon, who insists that the NC is trouble makers and that they are not welcome to return.  The next morning, a pair of notes is delivered to the NC.  The first is from the Lord of Leilon, and the second from Atemnon. 

My apologies for the complicated and trying events of yesterday.  All of our tempers were not held properly in check, and I suspect this was mostly due to the efforts of some evil enchanter. 

Here is the sword that you tried to acquire from me through deceit.  You have gained it merely through sympathy.  Although I worry that you will be vulnerable out on the road, I still must turn you out, and request that you never show your faces in my city again. 

If you turn around as soon as you leave the city, and head back to Waterdeep, I will give you a chance to live.  Never come north of Waterdeep again, or you shall surely perish. 

While these notes anger the NC tremendously, they can do not but bear it, as a more important goal lies ahead. 

On the High Road once again, The NC catch up with a Red Shields caravan and travel with them to the city.  As the caravan nears the city, it is attacked by an enormous throng of darkenbeasts.  The NC and the caravaners all took pause.  Then, Rufus invoked his moonblade and lead a charge into the throng.  After a drawn out battle with the unflinching creatures, the caravan and the NC were victorious. 

On the 26th of Mirtul, the NC arrives in Neverwinter.  The crew finds the city difficult to navigate through.  The streets are all deliberately winding, and the place does not seem to make sense.  They have to hire guides (young boys, who are paid one cp) to take them through the city.  One guide explains that years of warring with Luskan and goblinoid hordes in the North has necessitated the confounding urban planning of the place. 

While lodging the first night, Beljuril scries upon the last two Rangers, and learns that they are heading south through the mountains.  Seemingly they have been routed. 

The first lead that the NC follows is that of Malthoon's High Road Shippers.  This proved to be a bit of a cold trail however.  The mage Githias (Malkor) had sold the company to a legitimate merchant at an exorbitant price.  This is perhaps the last profit gained from his villainy the year before. 

The NC is able to track down the house that Githias lived in.  They speak to his neighbors, one being an upstanding priest of Oghma, who tell them that they think Githias left town for 'more fertile waters'.  They say that while the house was occupied, that the servants had to take loads of dung out of the place every day.  The house is then searched, but appears abandoned. 

Tracking down every lead they have, all lead to the same information; Githias left days ago.  Some say by caravan, but many accounts put him on a boat.  They also learn that he has a new staff, this one with an ugly beholder head atop it.  All attempts by Beljuril to scry upon the mage fail, likely because of the staff he bears. 

Finally, after much digging, the NC learns of a company called the Moon Wheel Trading Coster.  This Coster has an office in Waterdeep, but also here in Neverwinter, just off of the harbour.  It is learned that barrel loads of dung are also carted out of this warehouse daily.  Further investigation about ships reveals that there are only a few ships in the entire merchant fleet that have been able to navigate on the Sea of Swords.  These are the Myrmidon, the Dolphin Star and Waukeen's Wyvern.  They Myrmidon is registered to the Moon Wheel and the Dolphin Star to Emuldan's Carters (a lead they had forgotten about).   

The NC approaches the problem of dealing with the Zhents in a more lawful manner than the CRH.  They approach the Neverwinter Militia to disclose that they are in town to rid it of Zhent spies, and are quickly given audience with Lord Nasher.  Lord Nasher is quick to give thanks for their efforts, and assures them that they will not encounter the same corruption found in other cities of the North. 

On the 27th of Myrtul, the NC goes to the Moon Wheel trading house.  This place is an office with a large warehouse behind.  It is adjacent to the board walk on the harbour.  There, they talk to a clerk who unexpectedly tells the NC that Githias will be meeting with a business associate at the Gilded Serpent that afternoon. 

The NC show up at the Serpent and case it out.  They wait for either Malkor or his 'associate' to show up, and after a while Atemnon makes an appearance.  The NC hatch a ridiculous plan to have Atemnon arrested for the 'poisoning' of Oberon, but the whole thing fails miserably, and Atemnon escapes out the back. 

Further research that night reveals that Githias is living on the Myrmidon, which is moored in the harbour beside The Moon Wheel office.  Investigation of this boat reveals that no one is living on it, however.  The NC also learns that flying creatures are seen flying in the harbour at nights, and that they disappear under the boardwalk somewhere in the north western corner of the harbour (which is where the Moon Wheel office is). 

The NC also learns of a spy named Onyx, who seems to be some sort of mediary between Luskan and the Zhents.  They intercept this note; 

The Dolphin Star and The Myrmidon will be heading north in no more than two tenday.  Please consult your man and make the proper arrangements.  He can expect about 20 units of cargo, for which I will expect 150 gp each. 

On the 28th, things start to happen quickly.  The NC follows up their lead on Emuldan's Carters.  Making a forcible entry to the place, a skirmish ensues.  The NC manages to take down a cagey Zhent rogue named Ryerson, and recover the following notes from the desk in the office Emuldan office; 

You are to be in Dekanter by no later than Kythorn 20.  Please load up lots 22, 23, 24, 25 and 42.  You will meet up with a man named Gildon, who you will negotiate a trade with.  Do not trade for less than 100 gp/unit.

This does not give you much time, but Malkor is worried about something.  That worrisome old mage. 

It is later discovered that Gaskar and Ezmail were the two caravan masters for Emuldan's Carters.  Neither is found. 

With Ryerson captive, the NC interrogates him on the spot, and through the use of Beljuril's divinations, learns that Malkor is on Waukeen's Wyvern.  A group of Nasher's Paladins shows up at this point, and they execute Ryerson for being a slaver and a spy. 

An examination of the Moon Wheel warehouse office turns up this note below; 

Here is the scroll you requested.  Please have the message I have also enclosed delivered to Gilthzan, and send him to face the troublesome lot that is plaguing our cause.   

This note gave the NC much to think about 

Waukeen's Wyvern is quickly found, moored at the docks, and a battle is quickly joined by the NC, Malkor and the few men he had around him.  Were the fight at night, there may have been problems from his numerous darken beasts, the battle is in the middle of the day, and the NC has the advantage. 

The battle goes sour, however.  Malkor, recklessly wielding the powerful Hunnabar beholder staff, casts numerous fire balls wantonly at the NC, and also into the civilians on the docks.  Rufus bears the brunt of several of these spells.  To his credit, he absorbed more damage than a dozen men, but in the end it was too much, and he succumbed to the powerful destructive magics of the staff and the cruel will of the madman wielding it.  Rufus was slain. 

Malkor managed to make good his escape at that point.  His ship puts out, and is soon found to be in the escort of cogs from Luskan.  A fleet comprised of Neverwinter military vessels is put together as quickly as possible, and they give chase.  The NC takes passage aboard one of the vessels, the captain happy to have them along. 

The chase lasts long into the night, and it isn't until the pre-dawn hours of the 29th that the naval engagement begins.  The night found the pursuing Neverwinter fleet plagued by darken beasts, but they finally caught the slower, if larger, Luskan Cogs. 

Several ships were sunk in the engagement, and, regrettably, many men on both sides died.  But in the end, Malkor was slain by the efforts of the NC.  An archer aboard the Neverwinter warship was responsible for the killing arrow that took the villain down, but all were quick to give credit to the NC, who gave more than they asked. 

The Hunnabar staff was reclaimed by the heroic and dashing efforts of Oliver. 

Back on land, Lord Nasher throws a great feast in honour of the heroes, and Rufus is given an appropriate ceremony.  The Zhent threat to Neverwinter had been eliminated, and a statue of the heroes was begun.  To this day, high atop the Southern High Road Gate, by which the heroes first entered the city, a 25' high statue sits atop the 40' keystone of the gate.  The statue depicts the heroes, including Rufus and the archer that killed Malkor.  All are standing in a circle atop a mound of dead darken beasts, holding the captured staff up high in the air.   

The house that Malkor had owned was given as a reward to the heroes.  Oberon and May stayed on at the house, and dropped out of this story.  Their story does not end here, but their part in this tale does.  They bid their farewells to their friends, especially to Oliver, whom the two had traveled so far with, and to whom Oberon owes the debt of his freedom. 

Beljuril and Oliver returned to Waterdeep with the staff, and arrived in good time.  Beljuril's tale continues in a later chapter, but for Oliver, his part in this story, too, had come to an end.  Oliver found his friend Arkos Thunderstaff (Falcon) who was putting together an expedition to the south, and he joined on.  Oliver bid farewell to Beljuril, and here he leaves this story.  Like May and Oberon, his tale is not here told to it's end, but his pivotal involvement in this tale here is concluded. 

What became of Beljuril in the City of Splendours is told in a later chapter.
