Journal of Tergus Clarandal
Winter Solstice, 1439

Today marks the Winter Solstice, a day of great cosmological significance, and I am filled with gratitude as I write from my cherished library at the Farm. The sun shines brightly, casting long shadows that dance in the brief warmth of the day. It brings me joy to see so many friends and family gathered here, choosing to celebrate this momentous day with us at the Farm rather than in Thistle Ridge.

My aversion to Thistle Ridge has only grown stronger. The few who worship Amaunator will undoubtedly put on a grand service today, led by the ever-dutiful Clerk, Daphne Dale. Ms. Dale, in my eyes, is nothing more than an agent for the man I hold in deepest contempt: Magistrate Invictus. Despite his ostensible devotion to Amaunator, and his claim to bring all things into the light, Invictus is, in truth, a keeper of secrets. His new "Historic Wing" in the Cathedral at Dagger Falls is nothing but a vault for his clandestine hoard of knowledge, locking away truths that should be shared.

In stark contrast, my own library at the Farm is open to all who seek it. I delight in seeing my kin and friends exploring the shelves, the laughter and lively conversations filling the air. Yet, amidst this celebration, a new concern has taken root in my mind.

The four girls—Lilac Alavandor, my Lilly, Poppy Sweetberry, and Alaventa Alavaxi—play together as they always do. However, today, Alaventa seems different, her usual joy subdued, a faint shadow dimming her spirit. This worries me deeply. Senthia once shared a memory from her childhood, recalling the year the Alavaxi first came to Thistle Ridge. She overheard the eldermen discussing their arrival from the caves of the Iron Hole.

The eldermen speculated that the Alavaxi may have traversed deeper and more obscure depths than the Underdark to get here, and for what purpose they knew not, nor have I ever learned it.

These thoughts haunt me now as I observe Alaventa. Could there be some Shadow inside her? Is there a connection between her current demeanor and the dark origins of her ancestors' arrival? The fertile soils, the shadowy history of my family, the Alavaxi elders who disappeared, and now this—everything seems to converge on that hidden passage and the shadows it harbors.

As the day progresses and the festivities continue, I cannot shake these thoughts. The interplay of light and shadow, so prominent on this solstice day, mirrors the dualities I grapple with. My mission now is clear: to uncover the truth that Magistrate Invictus seeks to hide and to protect my family from whatever darkness lies beneath.

The Solstice may symbolize the promise of growing light and life, but it also casts the longest shadows. Today, I resolve to face those shadows head-on, to illuminate the secrets of our past, and to safeguard our future. The answers lie within reach, and I will not rest until they are brought into the light.
