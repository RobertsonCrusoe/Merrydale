DDC – 1482 – 1: Current State of the White Cloaks 
An inventory of licenses, team members and real estate held by the famous heroes of Daggerdale. 
GFD Notes: 
Even in Waterdeep, so far away from the Dales, I have heard tales of the White Cloaks sung in taverns by itinerant bards.  Not that it happens every evening, mind you.  But when it does, I take note.  The efforts of these brave men and women are legendary, and continue to mount.  The people of Daggerdale, as one curmudgeon once told me in a taproom in Shadowdale, "never knew peace up there.  Probably never will."  And so, heroes must grow their songs, or evil will prevail. 

As I hope you've read so far, fair reader, evil has not prevailed, and in our times, we have the White Cloaks to sing of; descendants by some degree of earlier heroes, tied together with the bonds of bravery, victory and loss, their songs will grow as their enemies diminish. 

Now I should say that the account below was given to me mostly by Administrator Reks, with some modifications that were required by my hand.  I feel it is fairly accurate, wanting only for intelligence on selected members, for whom many others also desire closure. 
Perhaps the next generation of heroes will provide this for us 
-GFD 
Charters 
The White Cloaks have operated under Charter of the Castle since 1479.  This has allowed them to hold property in Castle Radiance (White Cloak Tower), and to pursue bounties as 'Preferred Agents' (they get their pick, along with the Redjacks and some other smaller groups). 

In Elesias of 1482, Daggerdale underwent formal reorganization during a major summit called the Governors' Assembly (c.f. DDC – 1482 – 10).  The Assembly is dealt with in greater detail later in this chronicle, but here it is important to note that the White Cloaks were granted a Charter Militant, recognizing them as a formal part of the defense of the dale, and granting them a seat on the General Assembly.

Loedegan Close (Daggerfalls) 
This is one of the ancestral homes of the Whitegate Family and was gifted to the White Cloaks by Luminous Whitegate, for their efforts in ending the Dark Garden threat in 1481.  'The Close', as it is called, is a stately home in Daggerfalls, and is the de facto headquarters of the White Cloaks, now that there are three teams operating in different arenas. 

The Close serves as an orphanage, there usually being 10-20 children of various ages living and learning there.  The Close is currently presided over by Lady Hasting Blackmane, Steward of the Close.  She educates the children and overseas the general operation of the estate. 

It should be noted that the estate previous to the Dark Garden and the escalation of the war with Netheril in 1480/81 was quite opulent.  However, in order to pay for the mercenaries needed to fight Netheril, the Whitegate family sold off nearly everything of value in the house.  While still a large and stately home, it is austere inside, usually dark and cold. 

The Heartland Team calls the Close 'home' and can sometimes be found here.  Administrator Reks lives in the Close and is rumoured to never leave the gates of the estate.  Despite the austerity of the place, it is rumoured that a great store of treasures and magic, accumulated by the White Cloaks, is hidden deep within the estate somewhere. 

The estate is made up of three buildings; the main house, a fine brick stables, and a large servants' house.  These three buildings, along with one brick wall form a courtyard.  Most of the White Cloaks keep a room in the guest building, while only the Administrator stays in the main house (which functions as the orphanage and school).  The main house has a fine tower at the top of which is an aviary for messenger doves. 
White Cloak Tower (Castle Radiance) 
This is one of the main defensive structures of Castle Radiance, being on the Western Bailey exterior wall, facing west towards the plains of Netheril.  It is a squat tower of 4 stories, thick-walled and serviceable.  The White Cloaks have had a lease on the tower since receiving their Charter of the Castle in 1479. 

This tower is the home of the Mountain Team, and is where the captain of the White Cloaks can be found when not ranging in the mountains and plains. 

The first floor serves as a hostel and public kitchen.  Newcomers to the Castle can stay and eat here for free, though donations are accepted.  This space has functioned as a recruiting centre for the teams as well, with many notable members starting their career as a White Cloak here.  Troia can often be found cooking and serving food here when she is at the Castle. 

The second and third floors are the quarters for the teams, which (I am told) include a training room, a small library, a small workshop, and several bedrooms. 

The fourth floor is a mainly defensive structure that can also be accessed by the battlements on either side of it, as well as an aviary for messenger doves. 

The steward of the tower is a young lady named Lesia Goering, the daughter of the former mayor of Liberty Hill.  Her mother was one of the main conspirators with the Dark Garden, herself a daughter of Belladonna.  Lesia appears to be free of the taint of Shar but is happy to be cloistered up on the Castle, away from her ancestral home.  Undoubtedly, Luminous is happy to have her close and under observation. 
Arwencroft (Charity Hills Farm) 
This is a stately row-home in Charity.  It was gifted to the White Cloaks even before their arrival at Charity by Basil Muloch, who registered it as being 'Assigned for the usage of the White Cloaks as they see fit, until such times as they deem their efforts in Charity are no longer needed, even if that be to the end of days'. 

The home itself is in the style of Charity, which mimics that of Westgate; it is narrow, deep and tall, and finely appointed with leaded glass, light fixtures, wrought iron and fine millwork.  The ground floor on the street-side has a small office in which their steward, Flora Scarlett (a local woman in her early 20's) can usually be found.  Flora does triple-duty as the steward of the estate, the land-lord for some renters, and a gatherer of intelligence for the White Cloaks. 

This property comes with a tax liability that is accounted for by 4 small apartments on the first floor that are rented out to Freemen of the town.  The 2nd and 3rd floors are kept by the White Cloaks, but are usually empty.  It is rumoured that they keep emergency supplies there.  There are no teams headquartered at Arwencroft, but there is room for a dozen people if needed. 
Yarl Orgen's Hall (Tethyamar) 
A good-size hall adjacent to Thorbardson's Hall, this space has been granted to the White Cloaks by the Silver Council of Tethyamar at the Governor's Assembly, as a reward for the efforts of the team in the depths.  Though I have not seen it myself, I have seen a map that Reks keeps posted on the wall of his study (he seems somewhat obsessed with the place, but tells me he's never been).  It is about 200' long, and is much like a street with rooms and mezzanines on both sides.  It looks as though you could accommodate a small army in the space.  It has been granted completely free of encumbrances by the Silver Council in perpetuity. 

This of course is the headquarters for the Tethyamar Team, currently lead by Sgt. Creed.  It is widely rumoured that a great store of treasure is hidden in Yarl Orgen's Hall, the legal spoils of exploration and re-conquest of the lower halls.  When asked about this, Reks replied 'I don't know that I'd use the word 'great' to describe the treasure'.
