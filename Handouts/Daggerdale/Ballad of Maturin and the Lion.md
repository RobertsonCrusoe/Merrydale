A Harper composition sung in the Dalelands c. 1373 DR

== I ==
thumb|400px

Maturin was a Paladin who rode out of the East<br>
An exile from Damara to secure his nation's peace<br>
A soldier of Ilmater and a healer of the poor,<br>
He made his way to Waterdeep, on Faerûn's western shore.<br>

'Gainst slavery and suffering, Ilmater's son was called,<br>
With five brave friends from Waterdeep, to ride for Dagger Falls.<br>
But a villain's bounty on their heads brought Grimwald and his men,<br>
'Til a bitter night in Secomber, where the Sheriff met his end.<br>

House Raventree there lost a son, and Hanibal was gone,<br>
But with Dorian and Dalak now, the party soldiered on.<br>
The road they took grew grimmer still, through Loudwater and Llorkh,<br>
Towards destiny and danger, towards wailing Anauroch.<br>

:I will endure. I will endure.<br>
:Maturin and the Lion.<br>

== II ==

The desert burned away their flesh, their horses died of thirst,<br>
They were poisoned by the Zhentarim, they were hunted, they were cursed.<br>
The Grandfather of Lions came upon them in the dunes,<br>
And dread grew on them daily, with the waxing of the moon.<br>

The full moon rose, all Hell broke loose, and in the dying light<br>
From heroes in the desert's sun, came lions in the night.<br>
The Green-Eyed God grinned on the scene of murder, strife and gore<br>
As hapless slaves were hunted down, as Zhents were rent and torn.<br>

Maturin woke beneath the sun; his wounds had all been healed.<br>
A night beyond remembrance, the corpse-strewn ground revealed.<br>
His noble steed fled from his touch into the barren sands.<br>
Ilmater had forsaken him; he knew then that he was damned.<br>

:I will endure. I will endure.<br>
:Maturin and the Lion.<br>

== III ==

thumb|200px|Shildevan
In that bloody night of carnage, two more friends had disappeared,<br>
Three men and a raging dwarf pressed forward, through their fears<br>
'Til mad and roaming Shildevan, a prophet and a saint,<br>
Came to them at the next full moon, to cleanse the lion's taint.<br>

'You will hearken to your duty, you will go into the East,<br>
But when Dagger Falls is free then you must answer to my geis.<br>
Boregar, you'll set to rights the wrongs you've left behind.<br>
Dorian, you'll a pauper be, 'til you've foiled Beholder-kind.<br>

Maturin, Ilmater weeps to see your low disgrace,<br>
But all may be redeemed and if you would once more take your place,<br>
Return unto the desert, for alone to face your foe<br>
In single combat slay the Lion, and thereby save your soul.\"<br>

:I will endure. I will endure.<br>
:Maturin and the Lion.<br>

<br>

== IV ==
thumb|400px|Cyric's spectral army
Two months in the desert, then another two of war<br>
But none of these brave heroes forgot what they were fighting for.<br>
The mines were closed, the slaves were freed, and Yarkul put to death.<br>
But yet there lurked in Dagger Falls an unseen, silent threat.<br>

Maturin's dreams were clouded then, the God of Lies appeared,<br>
\"Go into the desert, now, your fate awaits you there.\"<br>
His prayers went still unanswered, and he was plagued anew with doubt,<br>
But Daggerdale could not be free 'til the evil was cast out.<br>

The walking dead and demon-knights and all things dark and weird,<br>
Came boiling from dark temple crypts, but the Heroes persevered.<br>
Victory at Harvestide was the conclusion of their quest.<br>
But a desert wind rose up that night, and Maturin could not rest.<br>

:I will endure. I will endure.<br>
:Maturin and the Lion.<br>

== V ==

thumb|400px|Companions of the Eastern Storm
They rode into the desert then, five brothers of the sword:<br>
Boregar and Garrik, Count Dorian the Lord,<br>
Gaebril the Crusader and Maturin the Grey<br>
No foe could stand before them; no blade could block their way.<br>

That Eastern Storm whipped through the sands, a purifying gale.<br>
It swept through Zhentish citadels, it cleansed the desert trails.<br>
The disciples of the God of Lies were slaughtered to a man,<br>
But their quarry still eluded them, the Tyrant's caravan.<br>

At length they reached the ruined place where they had fought and killed,<br>
Midwinter was upon them then and they sheltered from the chill.<br>
Maturin stared into the dunes, hand clenched upon his sword,<br>
And somewhere in the desert night, they heard a lion roar...<br>

:I will endure. I will endure.<br>
:Maturin and the Lion.<br>

<br>

== VI ==

thumb|400px
The Lion came into the keep, the winter stormed without<br>
Maturin stood before it and he gave a mighty shout<br>
\"I've sworn by all that's holy to end your foul plague.<br>
Forsake your Green-Eyed Master now and answer to my rage.\"<br>

The Lion roared, its claws lashed out, its fierce teeth opened wide<br>
Maturin raged with passion, and he fought with all his might<br>
And in that clash of silver, muscle, steel, tooth, and blood,<br>
A man warred with a lion; god made war with god.<br>

Abruptly then, as silence fell, both bodies fell to ground.<br>
Maturin's friends rushed to his side, and on seeing him, they found<br>
A small grey tear beneath one eye, and a smile 'pon his face, <br>
He whispered out a prayer then, and I swear they heard him say...<br>

:I will endure. I will endure.<br>
:Maturin and the Lion.<br>

:He will endure. He will endure<br>
:Maturin and the Lion.<br>

:We will endure. We will endure!!<br>
:Maturin and the Lion.<br>
