DDC – 1482 – 10: Excerpts from The Daggerdale Concordat, 1482 DR. 
The decree crafted and agreed upon at the Governors' Assembly of Elesias, 1482, wherein the current political entity of Daggerdale was established. 
GFD Notes: 
This document is quite easy to find, but I include a copy of it herein for posterity, in the event that it is not easy to find in subsequent ages.  This is a legal document that lays out the relationship between the various settlements in Daggerdale, entrenches religious pluralism, and defines the common defence strategy and obligations of the signatories. 

If anyone wants my opinion on the matter, and you are reading it, after all, this was driven by the church of Amaunator, who desired more than anything else, a strategy for the common defense, and a budget which they didn't carry the lion's share of.  Their Sunblades and the Order of the Lambent Shield had protected the dale at their own cost for nearly 80 years, but the war with Netheril and others had been costly, and they now required partners to share that cost.   

I was fortunate to have been invited to the Tower to watch the proceedings from the Gallery, and for the most part, this Governors' Assembly went smoothly.  There were some raised concerns and voices over the shared cost of the troops and their command structure, and some small boundary disputes, but relative to similar proceedings in other regions, there seemed to be something near to consensus on almost all issues. 

I suppose this amiable governance is the result of having so many determined enemies for so long, and there being little prospect for true peace in the near future, though I do wish it for them. 
-GFD  

Preamble & Superseding Rights 
In any matters addressed by and prescribed for in this Concordat, The Concordat shall be held to be the governing document, superseding any provision in other agreements of any variety, on those specific matters.  The Concordat is not a prescription for how the Signatories and their people are to carry out their daily lives, but rather, what obligations and limit to obligations they owe to each other.  In all respects save Religious Pluralism, the Concordat neither prescribes nor proscribes specific behaviours.

Consul of the Governors' Assembly 
The Voting Signatories will elect a Consul of the Governors' Assembly at the beginning of this agreement, and every Shieldmeet thereafter.  The Consul shall be chosen from any citizen of the dale, but shall not be a Voting Signatory or Non Voting Signatory to the Concordat.  The Consul shall call the Assembly and maintain order during proceedings and act as the head of the Sun Blades.   

The first Consul of the Governors' Assembly, elected by unanimous agreement by all Voting Signatories, shall be Cpt. Kesh Oakdale (ret.) late of the 16th Company Sunblades, of Dwimmerholme Farm, whose first term shall commence on 15 Elesias, 1482 DR, and end on Shieldmeet, 1484 DR. 

For his service as Consul, Cpt. Oakdale will be provided with apartments in Governor's Hall (Dagger Falls), a yearly salary of 300 gp, 100 gp in discretionary administrative funds each year, and one aide to be paid 182 gp 5 sp per year, of his choosing.

36 gp/year in lodging (payable in-kind by the settlement that soldier is stationed at, if possible) 

8 gp/year in stabling for his or her mount-share (with one mount for every other soldier, payable in-kind by the settlement that solider is stationed at, if possible)

Provisions for the Common Defense – Order of the Lambent Shield & Other Holy Orders 

For the greatest clarity, the holy orders of knights, paladins, and other warriors, priests and staff will be paid for entirely by their Churches, and will extract no mandatory payments from the people of Daggerdale.  Holy orders may be granted a Charter Militant by four-fifths majority vote in the Governors' Assembly as needed from time-to-time.  The following Holy Orders are hereby granted a Charter Militant at the outset of the Concordat: 
The Order of the Lambent Shield (Amaunator) 
The Order of the Golden Lion (Torm) 
The Order of the Lambent Rose (Ilmater) 
The Knights of Justice (Tyr) 
Provisions for the Common Defense – The Charters Militant 
For the greatest clarity, the companies of independent soldiers, scholars, adventurers, and others who would commit themselves to the defense of the dale, at their own expense and peril, will extract no mandatory payments from the people of Daggerdale.  However, they shall be granted by four-fifths majority vote of the Governors' Assembly, their Charter Militant, which provides them with the following rights and privileges, so long as they keep their Charter Militant in good order: 

To recruit, arm and train individuals whom the Governors' Assembly finds to be of suitable character and fibre, for the purposes of defending the dale and of making a living and a profit 

To hold such properties needed to conduct their business as the Governors Assembly finds suitable, and declares the specifics of such properties in the Charter Militant 
To hold a seat on the General Assembly 
Each Charter Militant will be reviewed in advance of each Shieldmeet, whereupon it shall require approval by three-fifths majority of the Governors' Assembly for the next 4 years, until the next Shieldmeet. 
Provisions for the Common Defense – The Militia 
It being in the best interest of all Signatories to the Concordat that every Signatory provides defense in adequate measure to avoid becoming a liability to the Common Defense, all Signatories agree that: 

All fighting-aged and capable people of their settlements are trained in their defense, with drills and training and other such suitable preparations to be conducted at least 4 times per year for 2 full days, and further that, 

Each farm will conscript or have volunteers to form a Militia that will be composed of fighting-aged and capable people with the following considerations; 

That one-fifth of all fighting-aged and capable people will be enrolled into the Militia at any one time for a period of not less than 4 years per person 

That members of the Militia drill and train for at least 2 hours per tenday either under local martial leaders or alongside the Sunblades 

That the militia may engage in their vocational duties with a minimum of disruption caused by their martial duties 

That at least one full-time Captain of Militia is chosen to lead the Militia, whose duties are exclusively martial rather than vocational, and paid for by the settlement 

The signatories of Daggerfalls, Castle Radiance, Charity, and all ally-nations (those currently being Tethyamar, Cormyr, Myth Drannor and Cormanthor) agree to maintain such suitable defenses, standing armies and militia required for their own defense, and for the provision of mutual-defense of the dale 

An attack on one Signatory is to be considered an attack on all Signatories, with due consequences, which may include the deployment of Milia to neighbouring Signatories to aid in the Common Defense
