== Mule Company's First Sight of Purple Eye Village ==

After winding your way along the rough, rocky road for some time, you come to an intersection of sorts; the road continues east-west, and here it runs along a small, perhaps 80’ high bluff which forms a sort of northern wall.  You can see that a fissure in this wall leads to a well-trod path winding its way north.  Just about 100’ into the winding path, there are two way-markers; a pair of stacks of long bones which support a few skulls atop them haphazardly.  The skulls appear to have been blackened with soot, and around the empty sockets, a purple pigment has been applied like a sort of macabre makeup.

The fissure winds its way through the rock in a convoluted switch-back fasion, and you have to travel several hundred yards before it opens up again.  

As it opens, you see that a sturdy wooden pallisade has been erected across the mouth, and rough burlap banners hang from the sharpened logs with a crude purple symbol of shar painted upon them.

The fissure finally opens into a small canyon, open at one end.  The ground in here is fairly level, and against the stone walls, simple huts made of boulder-and-stone-walls with thatched roofs provide shelters and homes for what might be a hundred or so creatures.

The centre-piece to this village is a large stone altar, 3’ high, with a 4’ wall of stone all the way around it.  The altar has been dyed black and purple, and the outer wall is clearly under construction.  There are some large blocks of stone that might be pillars when they are done, at each of the 4 compass points, and there is dressed stone laying about the place, cut to about the same degree of precision as the stones you saw at Shepherd's Crook.

The place is buzzing with activity; there are many orcs of all ages living here, and dozens of potential combatants.  There are also a number of dwarven slaves working on the stone here whom you have come looking for.
