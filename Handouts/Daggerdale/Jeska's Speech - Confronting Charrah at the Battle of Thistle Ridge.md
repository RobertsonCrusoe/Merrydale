Jeska's Speech — Confronting Charrah at the Battle of Thistle Ridge
Stephen Robertson (ghostwriter) and Scott Mclaren (as Jeska Monarch)
Email thread: "Jeska's speech", 8-10 May 2025
Thread ID: 196ae11fd3e6dba8

A collaborative writing process in which Stephen drafts multiple versions of Jeska's pre-battle speech confronting Charrah, the Daughter of Shadows, and Scott provides feedback and direction. The final version incorporates Scott's tactical preferences (the whip with cold iron, the tonic, the Bon Mot attempt) and Stephen's literary touches (the Eclose/chrysalis metaphor). This is a DIFFERENT speech from the one already archived as "Jeska's Speech - Confronting the Daughter of Shadows.txt" — this thread shows the drafting process and contains the full final version.

---

STEPHEN'S FIRST DRAFT (8 May 2025, 00:09):

[How old is Jeska again?]

Jeska absently brushes goblin gore from her hair with elegant fingers, her ageless eyes finding Charrah's gaze with cool amusement.

"I pray you'll forgive my momentary distraction, daughter of shadows. When one has witnessed the fall of empires, it becomes difficult to attend to every... theatrical flourish. I was contemplating the curious similarity between goblin viscera and the ink used in the original Nether Scrolls. Both possess a certain... ephemeral quality."

She traces Eclose's tip in lazy, arcane patterns across the floor.

"Your visions betray your lineage as surely as that smoky eye. A thousand years before your grandmother drew her first breath in her swamp-sodden cradle, I walked beneath the mythallar of Myth Drannor. I have seen true illusions crafted by masters who studied under Windsong herself — not these pale shadows of truths half-twisted."

Her voice sharpens like a blade being drawn.

"Boxfray, fallen? I've seen that man endure wounds that would fell a dragon. And speaking of dragons — really, my dear? Such elaborate embellishments suggest desperation rather than confidence."

With fluid grace, she rises to her full height.

"What stands before you is no illusion, no glamour to be dispelled. We are Chrysalis — forged in battle and tempered by trials you cannot fathom. Your time in this valley has ended. The question that remains is whether you possess the wisdom to concede with dignity, or if you prefer we demonstrate exactly how thoroughly we've claimed these lands."

Her hand drifts casually to her weapon.

"I find myself rather hoping for the latter. Shall we dance, child of twilight?"

STEPHEN'S SECOND DRAFT (9 May 2025) — more Jeska-flavoured, age-appropriate:

"Oh, pardon me, daughter of shadows — can you repeat all that nonsense? I was busy picking goblin bits from my hair and missed your little speech."

Jeska flicks something imaginary from her sleeve while drawing circles on the floor with Eclose.

"You made some obvious mistakes there. That image of Boxfray dying? Please. That bellowing mountain of muscle brought down one of your purple worms single-handedly. The man is literally unkillable — I've tried myself! He eats your kind for breakfast and spits out the toenails."

She snorts dismissively.

"And a dragon? Really? Even the Zhents would call that heavy-handed. I see your pet Shadovar friends lurking in the darkness. Tell them we've danced with their kind before — we're still here and they're not."

She straightens, her casual posture deliberately disrespectful.

"We, on the other hand, are no illusion, no glamour to be dispelled. We are Chrysalis — the new owners of your little goblin farm. You can either accept that your time here is done, or find out why Myth Drannor doesn't send emissaries to places it intends to lose."

She tests Eclose's edge with her thumb.

"So which is it going to be? More melodramatic threats, or shall we dance? Fair warning — I step on toes. Deliberately."

STEPHEN'S FINAL DRAFT (10 May 2025):

[Despite what I just said about the whip, I tailored the speech to Eclose — especially the end, since the name means the moment the metamorphosed insect crawls out of the chrysalis.]

Jeska brushes goblin gore from her hair with casual elegance, her ancient elven eyes finding Charrah's gaze with something between amusement and contempt.

"Pardon the distraction — after two and a half centuries, one develops the habit of multi-tasking. Was there something important in all that theatrical nonsense you were spouting?"

She traces Eclose's tip in lazy, deliberate patterns across the floor, the scratching sound punctuating her words.

"Your visions have some... creative liberties. That image of Boxfray dying? That bellowing mountain of muscle brought down one of your purple worms single-handedly while complaining it didn't put up enough of a fight. The man is literally unkillable — I've tried myself. He eats your kind for breakfast and spits out the toenails."

She snorts dismissively, a sound perfected through centuries of diplomatic encounters.

"And a dragon? Please. I walked through the smoldering ruins of cities that fell to real ones. Your parlor tricks might frighten your pet goblins, but when you've survived two centuries of Myth Drannor politics, you develop an eye for fabrications."

Her voice sharpens like a blade being drawn.

"I see your Shadovar pets skulking in the darkness. You can tell them that I was already hunting their kind before you were even born. The difference? I'm still here."

With fluid grace, she straightens to her full height, her casual posture deliberately disrespectful.

"We are Chrysalis — not some illusion to be dispelled with a wave of your hand. We claim this valley from those who have no right to it. You have no right to anything you offer. After what you've done to Morganna, to Jendwendle, to the Drurginz coster families — your time here is done. You can either slink away while you still can, or witness the transformation my blade Eclose brings — the beautiful metamorphosis from injustice to justice, nourished by your blood."

She tests Eclose's edge with her thumb, a centuries-old warrior's habit.

"So, which is it going to be? More melodramatic threats, or shall we dance? Fair warning — I've been perfecting my footwork since before your grandmother crawled out of her bog."

KEY LORE POINTS:

- Jeska is 274 years old at this point in the campaign
- Eclose is named for the moment a metamorphosed insect emerges from the chrysalis — Stephen uses this as the climax of the speech
- Charrah is vulnerable to cold iron (Fey heritage) — Scott wanted to use the whip with cold iron core
- Lucan's tonic is mentioned as a tactical element
- Scott wanted a Performance check bonus from the speech — the theatrical element is deliberate game mechanics
- Charrah controls Shadovar allies
- Morganna, Jendwendle, and the Drurginz coster families are named as victims of Charrah's regime
- Boxfray killed a purple worm single-handedly
- The "Hit it Dolly" / "9 to 5" joke suggests Scott's sense of humour in approaching the confrontation
