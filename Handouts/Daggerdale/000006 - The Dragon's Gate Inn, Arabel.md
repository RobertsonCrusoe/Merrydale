[2011-01-26 09:54:58] DM (OP)
It is in a large road-house outside the walls on the northern side of Arabel, that Beliard and Tyrian first meet. This should be a busy place; 100 years ago, this 'road-house', which more resembles a small fortress built outside the walls of the great Cormyrian city, would have been packed with traders, this time of year especially. These days, the  world such as it is, the enormous tap-room of this inn is 90% empty. It would have been nothing for merchants to exchange thousands of gold a  day in handshakes in this place, but now, the roads are a dangerous place, and travel up the road and into the dales is not what it once was.

Despite that, the world is waking up again, from its long and troubled nightmare. It is recovering from the Spellplague, and trade is one of the first healthy signs of that recovery. And so it is that a few merchants are gathered together in one lonely corner of this place, arguing about when they should set off. A dozen retainers, followers and other caravan add-ons wait sitting or standing at tables nearby, where the merchants, who in this age more resemble warlords, discuss their fate.

At one of these tables sit an interesting trio.
1. Beliard Emmarask, envoy from Cormyr, on his way to Castle Radiance to replace a low-level bureaucrat there, who's service obligations are concluded.

2. Tyrian Grey, the paladin of Ilmater, who has likewise been tasked with going to Daggerdale, where he is to earn his knighthood.

3. Carpathia Twist, guide and hunter, who is leaving The King's Forest for her own reasons, and likewise heading north, to the dales.

Beliard, who is quite astute, has noticed that Tyrian has the vocabulary and mannerisms of someone who has spent much time between various polities, and is drawn to him. It isn't long before he figures out that this is one of the Scions of the ill-fated Achres family.

The lot of them will be traveling to Tilverton Gap together, with this larger caravan, which will then likely break up into smaller units to head into the various dales. You are all accompanying a man named Denelius Ormath, a merchant-warlord from Suzail, who is bringing goods all the way to Castle Radiance. He has a contract with the Whitegates, apparently, and has been to the Castle 4 times in the last 2 years. He is bickering with 2 other merchants who want to wait a few days before going. Ormath wants to leave right away so that he can fit in 2 more trips this year, but it is late in the season already for this hope. And besides, as the discussion is focused, there is war raging between Netheril and Daggerdale, so what's the rush?

[2011-01-27 04:37:28] Beliard
"Yeah, what's the rush, Ormath?"

Unable to help overhearing the semi-private conversation happening at the next table between Denelius and his colleagues, Beliard clears his throat and begins ruminating, seemingly to himself, but projecting his voice loudly enough that he can easily be heard throughout the room.

"What's the rush, indeed? Why help the Dalesmen with their little war when we could wait for them to succumb to the Shadow? Why, they'd be doubly grateful for our commerce. Nay - triply! What's that you say? They wouldn't then want these swords and shields - not even good Cormyrean steel? I'll grant it you have a point. But you're too focussed on the short term. Take the long view, my friends - it's a seller's market! So what if Netheril takes the Dales? We might sell north! To... Netheril! Ah, yes - yes, my mistake. Well, let us sell east then, to Sembia! What's that you say? Sembia is naught but a Netherese puppet-regime? So it is! Politics - one forgets the little things. So be it. Those rascally Netherese. They seem to be just everywhere, don't they? In every shadow you might say. Well, as the halfling harlot says, if you can't beat 'em, bill 'em! Netheril is a growing market! After a war, they'll need to resupply - and, why, seeing as they'll have eliminated our competition, we'll have them by the short and shadies! We can sell them anything! If they're going to keep up this expansionist policy, they'll need all the materiel they can get, from swords to siege engines! Once we share three borders with them, we'll be their favourite neighbours! They couldn't NOT buy from us. Yes, sir - we can just sit back and let the jack roll in as the Netherese do their thing. What's the rush indeed?"

Turning to address Ormath directly, Beliard's voice hardens. "Now, Master Ormath. Though it pains me to encourage what we can all see to be the less profitable option [this last now dripping with sarcasm], I am on His Majesty's business, and intend to be at Castle Radiance by Flamerule's first ride. Sorry scrollpusher that I am, I should prefer to ride with the strength of numbers, and though I do not command the royal purse, I can extend His Majesty's gratitude for any haste you might be able to muster on my account."

He pauses for a second, then his eyes brighten. Turning to gesture at a wide-eyed Tyrian, with whom he has exchanged only a few words so far, he continues: "In payment, I can offer the axe and strong arm of this fine young knight, for he tells me that he, too, is bound for Castle Radiance." He claps Tyrian on the shoulder. "This young man, whom I know to be a fine and noble fellow, has sworn an oath of anonymity, and may not name himself until he has found Azoun's Lost Crown!"

(gasps from the room, now fully enthralled)

In a stage whisper, he adds, "I can, however, tell you, good master, that this man is a favourite of the White Prince. His glorious future is assured, and the favour of your service is sure to pay off in spades!"

During this exchange, the knight in question appears to be trying to shrink under his own breastplate. Clapping his aghast companion on the back, Beliard whispers to him, sotto voce, "No fear, my friend - I know exactly what I'm doing", then smiles brightly at the room - all eyes upon him, now - and stands and lifts his glass.
"Ladies and Gentlemen - the King!"

[2011-01-27 04:52:15] Arkadios_Solaris
[Follows "The Knight's Journey"]

Arriving at the The Dragon's Gate Inn in Arabel, the road-house built outside the fortress city as an extension of the old landmark Pride of Arabel housed inside the city walls, Tyrian bade his friends farewell and prepared himself for the next stage in his journey.

The road north from here, the Moonsea Ride, lay ahead. Tyrian had taken his turn patrolling that stretch a number of times. He did not know it as well as Calantar's Way, but he knew it well enough to be sure of two things: first, he should no longer travel with the Purple Dragons, even if they were his friends. The knights were on duty and the dangers of the road ahead obligated him to let them do their appointed tasks without having to worry about him. His sword and lance were peace-bound until he left the kingdom. Second, many of the caravans and pilgrims headed north to Daggerdale would be congregating here, and he would do well to make arrangements to join with one of the groups now.

Opening the doors of the ‘Gate, Tyrian was stunned to discover the tap room nearly empty. In truth, this had been his first entry into this place despite the many times he had been to Arabel. In the past he had always been housed in the garrison within the walls and had had no reason to mingle with the merchants, pilgrims and assorted other travellers that congregated here. He knew this was the place where all voyages north began, and he had expected a rollicking and rowdy tavern. Even the refectory at the House of Ilmater in Westgate was more boisterous than this room.

After quickly arranging stabling for his horse and securing a private room and a bath, Tyrian retired without a glance back at the tap room, ashamedly longing for his first sleep in a real bed in almost a year.

Very early the next morning, cleansed and refreshed, Tyrian set about exercising himself and his horse, watching carefully as the sun-worshiping patrons of the New Pride went about their morning adorations. Tyrian had never quite understood Amaunator and his worshipers. He had been well schooled in theology when he had trained for the priesthood, and knew about all the faiths of Faerûn. He knew enough about Amaunator to know that he did not really trust him or his clergy. While their devotion to the fight against the world's evil was admirable, and while he had found the pilgrims he had met on the road to be genial and affable on the whole, there was a certain element of trickery and misdirection at play in the faith's history, and its clergy's dealings with the people which had always given him pause. The heavy-handedness he saw in Marsember and that he had heard existed in Daggerdale added to his skepticism. It may be the same god as Lathander, as they claim, but whatever the case may be, Tyrian was sure that the truth was one thing Amaunator did not shine too brightly on.

Re-entering the roadhouse, Tyrian made his way to one of the empty tables for breakfast. Before he could get there, an impeccably dressed, middle-aged man made a discreet attempt to get his attention. Before long, Beliard Emmarask, newly appointed Cormyrean envoy to Castle Radiance, was sharing pleasantries of tales of life at the Court of the Purple Dragon, and trading names of mutual acquaintances and friends.

Beliard told Tyrian that the best way to get to Castle Radiance would be accompanying a merchant by the name of Denelius Ormath, but it was difficult to know when he would be leaving. Negotiations were in progress, and it was just as likely that they would depart tomorrow as next week or next year. A little patience was all that was needed. With that, Tyrian headed off to the garrison in Arabel, and then to replenish his supplies.

Upon his return in the evening, he found Beliard sharing a table with the most peculiar looking woman he had ever seen. Tyrian had spent a long time in the slums of Westgate, and had seen people of just about every race and culture there is to be found. But this woman? This one was odd.

Tyrian approached the table and was about to open his mouth in greeting to Beliard and his unusual companion, when Beliard turned to the table next to him and spoke:

"What's the rush, indeed? Why help the Dalesmen with their little war when we could wait for them to succumb to the Shadow...?"
Tyrian listened to the orator's words enthralled.

"...He pauses for a second, then his eyes brighten. Turning to gesture at a wide-eyed Tyrian, with whom he has exchanged only some pleasantries so far, he continues: "In payment, I can offer the sword and strong heart of this fine young knight, for he tells me that he, too, is bound for Castle Radiance." He claps Tyrian on the shoulder. "This young man, whom I know to be a fine and noble man, has sworn an oath of anonymity, and may not name himself until he has found Azoun's Lost Crown!"

Too perplexed from this last statement, Tyrian lets it go, mesmerized by the demagogue before him.

In a stage whisper, he adds, "I can, however, tell you, good master, that this man is a favourite of the White Prince. His glorious future is assured, and the favour of your service is sure to pay off in spades!"

Clapping his confused companion on the back, Beliard smiles brightly at the room - all eyes upon him - stands, and lifts his glass.

"Ladies and Gentlemen - the King!"
Tyrian simply sat, overwhelmed by the spectacle he had just witnessed.

"Mister Emmarask?" he eventually asked in a whisper to his tablemate, "I was not aware Azoun had lost his crown."

Carpathia sat with the two men she had just met, and without provocation, began softly to laugh.
