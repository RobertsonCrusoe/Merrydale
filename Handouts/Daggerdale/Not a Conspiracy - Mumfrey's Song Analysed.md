Not a Conspiracy - Mumfrey's Song Analysed
by Stephen Robertson (as Lucan) and Gus Miranda (as Jasper)
13-24 Aug 2024

This thread began as a meta-discussion about using AI to analyse the campaign's historical documents, then evolved into one of the campaign's richest in-character exchanges: Lucan and Jasper dissecting Merri Mumfrey's cryptic song over a bottle of Arabellan Dry.

---

OPENING - "THE TRUTH ABOUT THEM WHITEGATES" (Stephen, 13 Aug):

So I've been working my way through Blacky's amazing historical narrative, which I thought I knew most of. He left one key document out though.

"The Truth About Them Whitegates"
As told by Jebediah Greenthumb to Scribe Elmira Quill

Listen here, and listen good. I'm gonna tell you 'bout them Whitegates and how they ain't what they seem. My granpappy told me stories, and his granpappy before him, about how we Greenthumbs used to be somebody in this here dale. But then them Whitegates came along with their shiny sun god, and suddenly we're nobodies.

First off, where'd they even come from? One day there's no Whitegates, next day they're everywhere. That Ardelusk fella shows up outta nowhere, claims he fought in some battle, and suddenly he's a big hero? Mighty convenient, I say.

And don't get me started on that "Paladin's Grave" business. Why'd Ardelusk move them bodies to his farm? I'll tell you why - it's dark magic, that's what it is. Them bones got power, and the Whitegates are using it.

Remember when the Lathanderites split? That was when we Chaunteans were still strong. But right after, suddenly Amaunator's the big dog in town. Coincidence? I think not!

And what about that Dorian fella coming back from the dead? Mark my words, them Whitegates had something to do with it. Probably made a deal with some devil or another.

Look at how they're always in the thick of things. Dark Garden? Whitegates. Sons of Bel? Whitegates again. They're creating problems just so they can solve 'em and look like heroes.

And now they've got their hooks in everything - the Church, the Sunblades, even them White Cloaks. It's a conspiracy, I tell you!

Why, just t'other day, I heard tell that the Whitegates have got some kinda magic book hidden away in that fancy Yellow Abbey of theirs. Probably full of secrets they don't want us common folk knowing.

I'm telling you, something ain't right with them Whitegates. They come outta nowhere, push out good honest Chauntean folk like us, and now they're running everything. It's high time somebody looked into what's really going on with that family. But you didn't hear it from me, you hear?

*(Stephen's note: "Disclaimer: I did not write that article. I used the following prompt with claude.ai: 'In the voice of a simple, uneducated person, draft a conspiracy theory article about the Whitegate family based on the following information...'")*

LUCAN ANALYSES THE SONG (Stephen, 16 Aug):

Lucan sits down with Jasper to talk about Merri Mumfrey's song.

Jasper, my friend. You've got to stop singing that song under your breath like some kind of insane chant. You'll be madder than Mumfrey if you keep this up. Or maybe I will... It'd be a close race at any rate. But, tada! I've just acquired this bottle of Arabellan Dry, and I need someone to drink it with. Let's see if we can get to the bottom of the song before we get to the bottom of the bottle, eh? And I know Mumfrey's supposed to only be right half the time, but for the purposes of this discussion, we'll imagine the song isn't meaningless. Well? I know you've got a copy of the song. Let's get to it!


"There are spirits spirits spirits one-two-three;
Small but great in wrath and sad betrayal,
Their tragic story is the key to set them all and others free,
And expose for good an ancient false portrayal."

No points for figuring this one out, now. It's the girls haunting the ruins of Thistle Ridge: Poppy Sweetberry (b. ?), Lily Clarandal (b. 1435), Lilac Alavandor (b. 1432). We can assume that their deaths - or their un-deaths, I suppose - triggered the 1441 tragedy. But remember what Nemia said? Of course you do! And how she said it!! Those full Vaasan lips parting, her husky voice: "Tenebrioom Ostioom" "Pure souls zat haf done eempure deeds."

Easy now - that's the good stuff.

She said they'd taken part in some kind of ritual in which they were tricked into doing something terrible, that they would have considered a betrayal. So betrayers and betrayed at the same time. So - small? Check. Wrathful? Check. Sad and betrayed? Check check. So it's the last two lines that we need to figure out.

What's the tragic story? (Well, we know the gist, but I guess we need details.)
Who are the "others" referred to here?
What do you think "ancient" means here? Like, Netheril ancient? Or my-aged-granny ancient?


"There are maidens maidens maidens one-two-three;
Their warm breath in the frosty air a haze,
They sing and dance and tempt the boys with all they have and little poise,
To consummate in less than ten and fifty days."

I know you thought this was bawdy, but let's think about it a bit more symbolically. Maidens - so maybe something inviolate? A virgin forest? Untapped mine? Hard to say. Let's think about that one some more. You said maybe it's the story of the final days of Thistle Ridge? Not sure I follow. But ten and fifty days... well, he sang the song on the 19th of Ches. Sixty days - oh, well, fifty-nine if we take "less than" as important - puts us at, what, Mirtul 17th or 18th? Yes, I accounted for Greengrass. I just don't know if the day of the song counts. But who waits two months? As my uncle once told me, "To consummate, why hesitate? Don't make her wait - Penetrate!"


"There are shadows shadows shadows one-two-three;
They are strongest in the west and bend to thee
These shadows cast a form that stirs a mighty storm
That darkens river, ridge and road and sea."

Could be the shadows in Thistle Ridge, but this might be a bit more all-encompassing. I mean, there's no sea around here, so we're talking on the scale of nations. I liked your other idea about the Princes of Shade. Strongest in the West - that's where Netheril is. Shadows bending to thee, well the shadow of those mountains gets pretty close when the sun goes down in the west. Netheril's shadow looming over Dagger Falls? And they're putting themselves on a war footing, from what Jeska tells us. Now that's a storm that will cast a long shadow. Makes you want to have a nice long pull to warm your belly while you can.


"There are mothers mothers mothers one-two-three
Whose husbands toil in the field and the bough.
They have welcoming rear-ends and sons with lonely friends,
Who come and go before the husbands' turn to plough."

Plough! Ha! How's that for bawdy! Farmers aren't a subtle bunch. It's enough to make you blush. Or is that the wine? It was a good one. Sad that it's gone. But don't worry - I didn't say it was my only bottle. I've got another ... here! Looks like it's time to switch to Moonshae whisky, friend! So, the song...  Yes, you've told me about Amos's mother several times. And like I always tell you, "When the candle's out, they're all the same age." On a serious note here, this makes me wonder about how Nemia said that none of the ghosts share my blood. I'm pretty sure that old Alvund was my great-great-uncle, and let's assume for a minute that I've got bona fide Alavandor blood in me. So if there was some extracurricular ploughing going on in Thistle Ridge, it could be that Andarl wasn't Alvund's true son, or Lilac wasn't Andarl's daughter. It's an idea, anyway.


"There are devils devils devils one-two-three;
Men think that they have got them by the tail.
We laugh while they are weeping but they dance while we are sleeping,
And in the cold light of day they may prevail."

I'm hoping this is just about the bugbears, or the goblin-bugbear-hobgoblin trifecta. We're not up for fighting actual devils. YET! AM I RIGHT?


"There are nanas nanas nanas one-two-three;
Whose granddaughters have husbands tall and strong.
These men blow out the candles, more fun than broom handles,
Before returning to their young wives' legs so lithe and long."

Even bawdier. What did I just say about blowing out the candles? And with your wife's granny, too. Spicy. Well, what else could it mean? Look at High Cultivar Oakdale. Are you saying you wouldn't? Hah. That's not a no, Jasper. I don't know what it has to do with Thistle Ridge though. I don't think there were enough generations for those kinds of shenanigans. None of the third generation lived long enough.


"There are monsters monsters monsters one-two-three;
Who dreamt a man who doesn't know exactly how to seam,
There's an eye that doesn't close while all the others doze,
And I think we all might be but a monster's dream"

Did you know you misspelled "seam" here? Or did you? Well, anyway, so there are three monsters that dream up imperfect men. Sounds a bit like what Magistrate Winter described in his account. And that eye that doesn't close. I read enough of that doorstopper of a history book to know that there were beholders in "them thar hills" just over a hundred years ago, and that they pulled something out of the Tyrant's Mine. Reminds me of the old saw about the Beholder's autobiography, "I, Tyrant." And boring enough to turn you to stone! Whew. Okay, let's call it a night. Jasper?

JASPER'S VIGIL (Gus, 16 Aug):

"zzz.... zzz.... huh?! What? Oh... umm... yes?" Jasper sits up and rubs the sleep from his eyes, the open "Daggerdale Chronicles" slipping off his chest and landing with a heavy thud on the floor, amidst a slew of other papers, books and scrolls the wizard had been poring over.  "Uh... hmm?"

As Lucan leaves, taking the bottle of whisky with him, Jasper gets up from his chair and begins to gather all the literature from the floor, quietly singing Mumfrey's song to himself.  As he picks up the copy of "Botanycals and Biologycks of the Dayleslund", he sits back down and begins to flip the pages... Poppy ... Lilac ... Lily ... Lotus... Lotus ...

"Did they ever catch her?" Jasper says to himself as he drops the book back to the floor, and picks up "The Daggerdale Chronicles", flipping through the pages... "I mean, they sold her off to a dragon, but did they ever confirm her death?"

Jasper sits quietly reading for a while.

"Deadnettle.... dead.  Lotus... I guess no one is willing to go poking through some dragon scat to check... Belladonna... why...?!  Is she... still alive, then?... And of course, being 'questioned' by Invictus... No nefarious Shadow plot in this Dale without the Good Magister Himself right at the very heart of it!"

Jasper looks around to see anyone's reactions to what he has just said, only to be met by the silence of the room.  Not only has Lucan now long gone off to bed, but the silence also provides a painful reminder of having (temporarily...?) killed Mortimer in the course of his experiments.

Jasper closes his eyes, and leans back in his chair, once again singing Mumfrey's song quietly to himself.

"There are devils devils devils one-two-three;
Men think that they have got them by the tail.
We laugh while they are weeping but they dance while we are sleeping,
And in the cold light of day they may prevail..."

Jasper's face seems to harden, and drains of colour.   "You're wrong Lucan.  There is devilry here, and plenty of it..."

He stands, letting the heavy book fall to the floor.  He begins his habitual figure-8 pacing around the room.

"'And in the cold light of day...' if I were a gambling man, I would bet that these devils are hiding amongst the ranks of the Amaunatori now.  We will not find them in the shadows, but in the cold light of day that administers this Dale.  You are right that we are not up to fighting any such creature outright ourselves!"

The pacing continues, in unusual silence.

"Alright... so, once upon a time, an alliance of beholders and zhents came together to dig up some enormous religious tablets that had been buried here.  They did this under the guise of competing trade companies attempting to monopolize the route between the Moonsea and Waterdeep across the desert.  The beholders succeeded in retrieving their tablets and abandoned their zhent partners to infighting and destruction by some petty Waterdhavian adventurers.  Is the beholder interest in Daggerdale done?  Is their part in this story complete?"

Jasper pauses a moment to look out the window toward the mountains in the west.

"But Netheril returns and cuts off any true communication between east and west across Anauroch.  The zhents are gone and so are the beholders.  But now the shades want something from Daggerdale.  Tethyamar, sure.  Myth Drannor, undoubtedly.  Sembia...?  Hmm... But why are they so committed to this war with Daggerdale? Liberty Hill, Dwimmerholme, Thistle Ridge...? Rule of Three. Three Sharite Cult leaders, three devils, three little girls, three farms... three families...."

He continues pacing in silence.

"'There are maidens, maidens, maidens...' Lucan, I think we might have to start taking cold baths and thinking of Elminster, my friend.  We are surrounded and tempted by ginger-haired maidens at every turn... Fiona, Coventina..."

He stops again and looks out the window.

"Coventina.  'Promise me you'll kill her'... the Ritual of Corruption... This contest was a distraction.  The Redjacks knew it, and someone paid Hastle Redwater."

More pacing.  Singing the song again.

"What happens on the 17th of Mirtul?  Close enough to the anniversary of the Saint's Battle.  The Ritual of Corruption... The Ritual of Transcendence... The Ritual of Founding... three rituals... three mothers... three illegitimate children...?"

Jasper looks out towards the East now, the silver light of dawn is beginning to push back the shadows.

"Nanas... granddaughters with husbands who blow out the candles... well, at least we know there's a coven of Sharite witches still out there, in league with devils, possibly three generations of the women, with 'husbands' out killing Amaunatori... I don't think any of that is new to these people, and yet..."

Jasper watches the sun come up from the horizon, the sounds of the town coming to life drifting up towards the window.

"'Who dreamt a man who doesn't know exactly how to seam'... It wasn't my misspelling, but Vessuvian's.  And I don't think he meant 'imperfect'.  I think he's talking about crossing through the seams between worlds: material, shadow, fey..."

Jasper lets out a big yawn, and rubs his eyes.

He casts Unseen Servant and the shadows in the room seem to peel off from the walls and floor, swirling together to coalesce into a humanoid shape that then fades away into invisibility.  The books, papers and scrolls strewn around the floor begin to be stacked and collected into a neat pile floating just around waist level.

Jasper remains rooted in thought, looking out the window, as his phantom servant takes the collection of material it picked up from the floor and table back to the wizard's room.

He opens his mouth as if to say one last thing, but he changes his mind, and shakes his head instead.

The wizard turns, and leaves the room.
