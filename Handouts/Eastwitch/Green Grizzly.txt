This tavern/inn has is the largest building in the village. It is run by the Orlschuk family, and the proprietor is Gemedun Orlschuk, the patriarch of the family. He stays at the tavern most nights, and keeps 4-10 employees, depending on the season and how busy he has been. The building was constructed in 1556 by the Mason family, as indicated by the large keystone above the entrance. This keystone made of a particularly green piece of limestone, and has an engraved bear emblem, and thus names the place the Green Grizzly. The building is also solid stone, and it is rumored that the Mason family is still living well off what the Orschuk family paid for this place. Indeed, it is the largest building in all of Eastwitch, at three stories in height, and being over 100’ in length. Further detail of this building can be found on it’s own map, and in the commerce section of the Eastwitch Summary.

The taproom is busy most nights; many of the town villagers converge here nightly, and year round, and are met by an assorted crew of whatever ranger-types may happen to be in town at the time. There are also many adventurers and hunters who find their way here, to drink and engage with the locals.

Goods and services as follows:

*Gemedun’s Winter Ale: 2 cp/tankard, 2 sp/hand keg
*Gemedun’s Camp Wine (potato whiskey): 1 cp/glass, 2 sp/gallon jug
*Orlschuk Winter Wine: 5 cp/glass, 5 sp/bottle
*Orlschuk Table Wine (white or red): 3 cp/glass, 3sp/bottle
*Sagewind’s Cider: 5 cp/tankard

==Meal==
Typically the main course includes either a thick stew of rabbit, elk, bear and/or deer, with potatoes, carrots, and a bit of horseradish. This comes with a loaf of rye bread and garlic butter sauce for dipping, and a small bowl of farmer’s cheese. This also includes a tankard of Gemedun’s Winter Ale, and costs 1 sp.

==Accommodations==
This fine stone building has many large (if not a little drafty) rooms for rent. There are 12 rooms which each contain two large beds (with straw mattresses), a wooden table and four chairs and a single couch. The furniture is rustic, but not crude. Each room goes for 2 sp/night, plus 1 sp/person above the first. Stabling of horses is free for anyone who is staying in the Inn (there aren’t any huge caravans coming through town, which would make stabling of many mounts an issue), or 2 cp/mount otherwise.
