==Bear Hunter [General]==
The character is an elite hunter who prefers to use the knife as his or her primary weapon. The hunter engages large opponents by repeatedly stabbing them whilst grappling. This fighting style is very dangerous and dramatic, and only very few hunters actually hunt this way. This feat may only be chosen by characters who have had a great deal of hunting experience with large size creatures such as bears (though it is just as effective with trolls, ogres and the like). The character must have killed at least one large creature in single combat with the knife in order to take this feat, and even then only after the DM’s approval.
;*Prerequisite: Dex 13+, Str 13+, base attack bonus +5, Jump 5 ranks, weapon focus (knife).
;*Benefit: The character can initiate a grapple with a creature the same size, or two size categories larger, using Dex as the modifying ability score to the grapple contest. The character must be able to attack it’s opponent’s flank or back, which usually involves surprising it (though not always). Thus, the grapple check is worked out as follows;

*Base attack bonus + Dexterity modifier

=== A Note on Size ===
There is no special consideration for size, but the DM may give the creature being attacked a bonus if it has a particularly difficult hide to cling to (thick fur is ideal for this style of fighting, but a dragon’s scaly hide might offer it a +2 to +4 bonus). Note; the defending creature also adds it’s dexterity modifier to the grapple check, even though it may have been surprised; this attack is not a quick, instantaneous attack; the hunter is trying to stay holding onto the creature.

Once the hunter has successfully initiated the grapple, he may begin attacking with his knife (including the round that he initiates the grapple). He need not make an opposed grapple check to make an attack with his knife. Rather, he simply makes a regular attack, with a +2 bonus to hit.

The creature he is attacking may attempt an opposed grapple check each attack to get the hunter off his back. The creature must use the formula above to make this opposed check, as it’s strength will be of little use in such a conflict. If for some reason the creature is able to reach around and attack the hunter, then the regular grappling formula is used. Also, particularly clever creatures my attempt to brush the hunter off by slamming against a tree or other obstacle. In this case, a regular grapple check is made as well.

== Line Hunter [General] ==

Characters with this feat are patient hunters who are accustomed to sitting and waiting for game to come their way, whilst maintaining a low profile. This feat is available to any character from Eastwitch.
;*Benefit: +2 Wilderness Lore, +2 Hide.

== Stalker [General] ==

Characters with this feat are expert game-stalkers. This is a method of hunting that involves tracking prey and closing into closer range. It is available to any character from Eastwitch.
;*Benefit: +2 Wilderness Lore, +2 Move Silently.
