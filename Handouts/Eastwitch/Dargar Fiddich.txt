A miner in the hills near Eastwitch

==A Dwarf Reminisces...==

“Few knew young Dargar as well as I – and even fewer know what became of him after the Accident. Some say he went mad, and became lost in the depths of the Forgotten Colonies. Others say he exiled himself to the surface, forswearing the mines. Even I wam not sure what became of him, and I was his closest friend, almost 200 cycles ago.

“The Fiddichs were an industrious clan, and the patriarch Urgar was ever being sought after by the Thane’s council to advise on the expansions that we were going through in those years.

“Dargar was a bit of a dreamer, and had his heart set on leaving his home tunnels to make a fortune as a gem-cutter in the Core – a skill not in demand here in the Outer Quadrants. His father, Baldur, knew this and sent him on to the Core with his blessing. It was during this time that the Great Gate was opened, joining these tunnels with the Forgotten Colonies. You can see paintings of the battles that ensued with the duergar and their illithid masters in the Great Hall today. Three bloody days was all it took for us to scatter our enemies and drive them deep into the Bowels, but not before the assassins and wiped out almost of third of our clan, and most of the women and beardlings.

“When Dargar heard the news, he returned immediately, but found that of his entire family, only Urgar himself remained – a crippled and saddened old dwarf. He illogically and unfairly blamed the youth for his absence. Unable to reconcile with his elder, and, I think, secretly agreeing with him, he left the colonies. Perhaps he was unwilling to stay with his memories and the pity of his remaining kin, but regardless, he was not seen again. 

“I thought for ages that he had gone back to the Core, but when next I happened to make the journey, cycles later, no one had heard of young Dargar. In the last 170 cycles, I’ve heard of nothing relating to him, other than rumours, speculations, and innunendoes.”
