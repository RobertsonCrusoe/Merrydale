==Drinks for Weary Companions== 

At the door of the Green Grizzly stood Dargar the old Dwarf, Wyatt the ranger, and Zieg the magician. Dargar and Wyatt had arrived together, Zieg from a different location. All of the companions bore many deep cuts and bruises belying the nature of their relationship. They silently nodded to each other in hesitation as those who are slightly awkward with a new friendship often do, then Wyatt quietly ushered them all to a table near the back of the room.

The night was still fairly new, but with the short days and the crowd consisting of mostly farmers, the room was full. Niches of people littered the floor. The Hobbits and Humans were mostly separated, not out of spite, but rather out of table size. Dargar was the only Dwarf in the bar. Wyatt surveyed the room looking to see if something interesting was going on. Indeed the bar was quite as befit a rural tavern like the Green Grizzly. Just before Wyatt finished his survey the bar keep, old Gemedun rushed up to the table with three tankards.

“You lot finally showed up eh. He told me to give these to you as soon as you got in. He must be awful fond of you guys to shut up about himself for once. He hasn’t stopped rambling about yous all evening. Talked me into servin’ you with my finest tankards too.”

The mugs were crafted of silver-grey porcelain. The lips were all dipped in Mythril that matched the hinges that held the oaken handles on. On each face of the mugs was embossed the symbol of the bar with Mythril again filling the recesses of the intricate relief.

==The Benefactor Revealed ==

Wyatt wondered who the buyer of this most queer round was. Then his eyes swept past the last part of the room he hadn’t surveyed yet. There at the opposite corner of the room sat a young boy surrounded by a group of town girls. He was just talking up a storm; his bright eyes, excited expressions seemed to make the room a little lighter around him. The boy held a matching cup in his hand. Wyatt gave himself a slight smile and then turned to guide his companion’s eyes towards his quarry. Dargar was transfixed on his mug with a sort of drunken hypnotism that most Dwarves get when in close proximity to such finery. Zieg was slouching back in his seat looking as if he was waiting for the night to be over. Wyatt nudged his companions and pointed to the boy across the room. Dargar snapped out of his trance and looked over.

“Heh. Dumb kid. That boy probably doesn’t realize that those girls want more out of him then he is old enough to give. Just look at the way they are looking at him.” Dargar then laughed out loudly, at the boy’s innocent prattling.

“Why Dargar, I think that is the first time I’ve heard you laugh,” said Wyatt in happy astonishment. Catching himself in this accidental joy, Dargar sat back in his chair trying to put on his most grim face.

Zieg was still looking over at the table as the pretty bar maid was leaning over the table topping off the boy’s mug.

“Huh. He had barely taken two sips from that mug. She didn’t even need to fill it.” Zieg slumped back in his chair and pushed his mug away. He hadn’t taken one sip.

Over at the boy’s table the town girls were hanging on every word that the boy said.

“Dargar’s axe is so sharp he could shave his beard with it, but he wields it with such a terrible might, more than any man from here to Waldheim could do. And Wyatt makes his two spears sing with such beauty. He killed a giant wolf with one of them and skinned it with the other in one swoop! Now he wears that wolf’s hide as a token. And ... and Zieg is so brave; I think he and I will be best friends forever.

\"Oh look, there are my friends now!” The boy stood up and gave a big wave to the companions across the room. Wyatt gave a half wave, Dargar gave a slight nod; Zieg didn’t move. The boy was about to join the companions when one of the girls took his arm and sat him back down again.

“They look like they’ve been through a lot. You don’t even have a scratch on you. You must have fought so bravely” It was true that the boy didn’t have a scratch on him neither seen nor unseen, but not because he hadn’t gone through as much as the others. Indeed he may have been through even more, but for some inexplicable whimsy true magical powers had began to manifest themselves in the boy the night before enabling him to heal his wounds and mend his clothes without leaving a trace even as small as a table scratch. This fact was not even known to his companions yet and his powers were just a small trickle.

“Tell us of your adventures. You said that you would tell us once your friends got here,” one of the other girls said.

“Yes, tell us your stories, tell us of your adventures Bin.” The rest of the girls took up with the first two and soon they were all mooning over Bin to perform for them.

== Bin Tunes his Lute ==

Bin really didn’t need as much coaxing to tell his story. In fact, he had already started tuning his lute before the girls had even started their clucking. He produced his lute from under the table and started playing.

The first notes that came out were nothing more than a little folk tune that most of the townsfolk knew from their childhoods. Even this simple music quietly worked its way through the room. People seemed to be minding their own business, but even the old dwarves could see mouths starting widen into smiles one-by-one. Bin got up off of his chair and walked over to the bar still playing his lute, but a little louder now. As he walked over chairs scraped across the floor as people started to turn and watch the young Bin with his lute.

Bin approached the middle of the bar where two of the Greendale men who were sitting on a couple of the higher stools stood up. One of them took off his cap and buffed the stool while the other gave a small flourish to let Bin in. Bin stepped up to the stool and sat on the bar just as Gemedun moved a tankard out of Bin’s way. Bin finished his little tune and the room gave way to modest applause. He lowered his instrument with a smile at least twice as wide as anyone’s in the room.

“Thank you, but I cannot in good conscience play any tune with this little drinking going on.” The room let out an audience’s sympathetic “Ahh” and then laughed as the bar maid presented Bin’s own tankard to him. No one seemed to mind that such a young boy was such an advocate of drinking. Truth be told: Bin never drank as much as anyone thought. Bin took his drink in one hand and raised it to the crowd and started to sing.

:So raise your tankard, raise your cup, raise your mug to young Bin
:’Cause I want to see the bottom of them before I even begin

Everyone in the room raised their drinks with a merry “Huzzah” and took a hearty draught. Zeig absolutely hated this kind of thing, but he knew that he couldn’t sit out of this little game either. So Zieg gave a fatalistic sigh, squeezed his eyes shut and raised his drink with everyone else.

== Raise Your Cups to Zieg! ==

Zieg slammed his tankard to the table with the exact look on his face of someone who drank a full mug of Gemedun’s Winter Ale and Gemedun’s Camp Wine mixed together. No one seemed to notice Zieg’s queer expression except for Bin because he was the only one in the room looking right at Zieg. At that moment Zieg realized that Bin was not staring at Zieg, but rather his tankard that he must have smashed when he slammed it down on the table. At that moment Bin picked up his singing again.

:Now raise your cups again because you know I really don’t believe
:That those drinks are really empty now raise your cups to Zieg!

Zieg was utterly horrified. All eyes on the room were upon him and he had broken a tankard that was worth more money than he thought he would ever see in his life. All he could do was raise the remains of the tankard to the crowd and wait for his life to be ruined. Zieg lifted what was left of the cup with a rubbery arm cursing this cruel, cruel world and Bin for being its master of fate.

The crowd’s reaction was not the one that Zieg had expected. They didn’t gasp, but rather erupted in roaring applause for Zieg himself. He looked at his mug and there was no trace of even a hairline crack. The mug had been repaired and he had inadvertently showed the mug to the crowd so that everyone could see that it was empty. Like it or not Zieg had drank twice as much, twice as fast, of a twice as awful drink as anyone else in the bar and everyone was cheering this extraordinary ability that he didn’t even know he had. The pretty bar maid came around and refilled his tankard, giving him a wink on her way back to the bar. As Zieg raised his tankard to his lips he felt that implacable crackle that only a magic user can feel.

“That Bin’s magic does work in strange ways doesn’t it,” he said to himself as he gave in to the merriment of the evening.

== Tale of the Fletcher Expedition ==

Soon, Bin’s verse came to the part where the new Fletcher expedition, consisting of Bin and his companions, had discovered the fate of the old Fletcher expedition.

:And the Orcs who had taken our arrows where giving them back to bring
:But to our dismay they were giving them back from the twang of their bow strings

:My arrows were ready to send right back through the skill of my great longbow
:The craftsmanship of the Fletcher arrows and the Fletcher behind them dropped many a foe

:Dargar’s axe cut down many Orcs, his might so terrible and bold
:If I could I’d fill his cap with gems and dip his beard in gold

:Oh Wyatt whirled and swung his spears as fast as he is brave
:Oh Wyatt cut down twenty Orcs and sent a Troll or two to its grave

:Zieg did all right that battle that night, that is definitely very true
:But not nearly as good as he’s doing tonight at the bottom of his brew

:We fought back hard, and we fought back long and the battle raged on
:And the bravery of Bin and his companions soon saw their foes gone

:As you can see by my companions over there we didn’t come out without a scratch
:But an inspiring whistle and song from Bin got us through that dangerous match

:So raise your drinks again and again raise them through the highs and lows
:And raise your drinks to the time that Bin and his friends became Eastwitch heroes

Bin sang on into the night and answered many questions afterwards. He and his companions were heroes that night and by the end of it all Bin’s companions thought that maybe they would let Bin tag along a little longer.
