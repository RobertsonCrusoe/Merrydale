The industrious and militant independents of the Greathorn family  run a very high quality smithy at their fort outside Eastwitch. They only produce a few types of weapons, but they are all of fine quality (+1 to hit or damage). The customer must travel out to the Greathorn fort, and endure many words from the smiths and the patriarch before any discussion of a purchase can begin. A meal usually comes before any business discussion, and a good long discussion about who’s-married-to-who, and the resulting offspring, and many other familial details must be brought to light, before Elrach Greathorn will have a mind for business.

At any rate, once the purchase is made, the customer is almost always pleased. Their weapons are very fair to behold, and very enduring. They hold a fantastic edge, which the customer will not likely be able to reproduce on his own, after dulling (something the smiths at the fort will rectify for free).

The produce short swords, hand axes, arrow heads (which are purchased by the Fletchers), long swords, and long knifes. Each item must be individually discussed, for price.  But for example, Judge Beorl recently purchased a long sword for Sheriff Hansig Miller. This purchase took 3 evenings of discussion, and a final price of 60 gold, which, it is reported, was a discount price given for a man of Office. The Sword, named Leirardor (meaning 'cunning ray of merciful death' in Old Imperial, or so it's said) took four full months to forge; it’s blade is of mithral, and the hilt has been fashioned from rare Black Oak from (or so it's said) the Valdenwail Hedge.  Two long ribbons of silver material, akin to silk, flow from the pommel, and are said to have bound the locks of a fair warrior maiden from the Archipelago.

Such is the detail that they put into their craft.  The blades all bear the family crest, near to the hilt, upon the blade, and also the name of the blade, and the name of its smith, and the date of it’s making.
