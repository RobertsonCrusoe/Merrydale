“I thank you for the food. This should settle my debt.” The young man dropped a small pouch onto the table and headed away from the table, towards the door. He looked back as he opened the door. “Next time, carry extra bowstrings,” said he, quietly.

I started, and peered at the strange young man. I had a mind to ask him how he’d known, but I saw the old barkeep watching us closely, and held my tongue. When I looked to the door again, he had gone.

Marlon guffawed and grabbed the pouch, “You see, boys?” he cried to the crowd. “Even the Warden won’t eat on a hero’s tab!” He laughed and upended it on the table between us. I gasped, and Marlon’s amusement turned into a choking sound. Lying on the table for all to see were eight still-bloody scraps of tattooed green skin.
 
At the bar, the barkeep snorted and shook his head. “A fine story, milords. A fine story indeed. Drink up.”
