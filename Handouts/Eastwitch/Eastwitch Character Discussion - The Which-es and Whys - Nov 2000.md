CASEY BAUMAN-WIKE (23 Nov 2000):

I just finished downloading and hardcopy-ing your Eastwitch (Revised Nov 2000) files and I have to say that this is your best work yet. Better than the colony even, and that is saying a lot. I am looking forward to playing in such a simple and elegant setting. Some questions though...

This I don't understand. There is nothing inherent in the 3rd ed. PHB discription of a druid that is offensive to an Eastwitch mindset. Perhaps you think that a druid would oppose hunting or cultivating land, but I do not think that is true. Druids make use of nature at nature's expense all the time. Their favorite armor is *hide* armor, which must be made from hunted game. They also frequently employ weapons (daggers, spears, scimitars) made of mined and forged metals, which must can only be fashioned at Faerun's expense.

All that aside, the people of Eastwitch would value and respect the right druid. A far cry from the despised city folk, druids have much to lend a rural community. A small amount of religion might be tolerated from any character who once fixed the leg of old Hamstead's prized breeding mare. And who could forget when my character brought sheriff Hansig Miller back from death's door by casting cure light wounds on his grizzly maulings. And then there was the time the infant Boughberry twins took fever...

Dude, druids can have 5 different alignments. This is *way* more tolerable than a paladin, and we love those guys. (What's more fun than a paladin of mystra taunting a paladin of illmater? Few things, I'm afraid.) In the realms, different priests (and druids) can worship different aspects of the same god. This is why some gods have both good *and* evil priests. A neutral good follower of Malar that was all about the hunt would go over quite well in Eastwitch. Similarily, a druid of the farm god would be all over cutting down wilderness and planting seeds everywhere. This too seems amicable with an Eastwitch mindset. Gods in the realms are hurting for love, and will take it wherever they can, it seems.

TODD BURRY (23 Nov 2000):

Casey, don't listen to Blacky about the no Druid/Cleric thing. We need someone with more healing powers, and although Bin kicks ass, he only has one cure light wounds a day. This is enough for Bin, maybe. The people of Eastwitch will learn to accept a non-preachy type of cleric (and they do exist, just watch 7th heaven).

Blacky realizes that he cannot create such a narrowly focused setting that allows only fighters and rangers and one wizard. Very few people will use a campaign setting in this way. My suggestion, invent a distant, implacable, mysterious, enigmatic force that some mysterious forest dwellers turn to to help protect the town from this deeper creeping evil that the Warders alone cannot defeat. The Warders, knowing that they need help in this supernatural evil will collaborate and protect such hidden priests, but at the same time protect the people from things they ought not know about.

The missionary who came to the town over a decade ago now. He gave up trying to convert the people, but realizes that their beliefs really aren't that different from his own. He knows that his god gives the townspeople his blessing as much as he gives it to his loyal follower.

The kindly hermit that lives in the woods. People can rarely specifically find him, but his little cottage often turns up through a clearing when someone is in the most desperate need. His kindly face comforts even the most superstitious of the townsfolk.

The mysterious cloaked man who sometimes visits the old wizard's tower. He seems to be battling with some inner turmoil. Those who don't know him look on him with suspicion, but when he removes his hood all they see is a man, nothing more. Those who know him see that he is unsure if his power comes from a force of good or a force of grave evil, but they also know that he uses his power for good always. Can this man be tricking an evil force into using its power against itself or are there more than one force in the world? Indeed, these powers are much older than the Gods and no one knows their true nature.

The naive boy performer. Nothing seems to phase him and the world is there for his personal enjoyment. He is too young, to un-wise to realize what else in the world. But there is something more to him too: Why does his father never speak to him of his dead mother? Why does the old wizard look at him so? He may save the world, but he probably would never realize it. The truth is that the town needs people like him to help them forget the cruel world that they live in. Right on Bin.
