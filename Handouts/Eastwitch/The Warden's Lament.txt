The wind through the Iron Mountains does not just blow; it mourns. I have sat upon the Watcher's Crag for twenty winters now, and I have heard the names of every man who fell in the Valley of Mist.

They come for the ore, or the glory, or the secrets of the Old King. They do not understand that the mountains do not keep secrets; they are the secrets. The stone remembers the weight of the dragons; the ice remembers the heat of the first fires.

Yesterday, a young man from the coast arrived. He carried a sword that had never seen blood and a heart full of songs. He asked me the way to the Sunless Grove. I told him to follow the stream that flows backward. He laughed and called me a madman.

I am not mad. I am simply the only one left who remembers that the stream only flows backward when the mountain is hungry. I will hear his name in the wind by morning.
