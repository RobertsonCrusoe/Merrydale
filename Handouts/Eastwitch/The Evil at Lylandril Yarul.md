Bin Fletcher, Turik Grimwood and a group from the Lord’s University had set out on an expedition to an Elder ruin known as Lylandril Yarul in early April. It is now the middle June and they have not returned. This is not an uncommon occurrence, however, for the wood around Eastwitch are a predator that claims most that dare to enter them.

On June 19, however, a letter is found, left by Bin for the Doctor.
----

== Visitors from Waldheim ==

Dear Ezekiel,

Two months is a long time to have been away, but I am alive and so is Turik. Two of our companions also survived, but only one has returned with us. That is the purpose of this letter as you will read, but let me start from the beginning.

As you know you did some research for me in the winter regarding the Elder ruins of Lylandril Yarul to aid in the expedition from your University that you would not be able to make. Well you may not have known that Turik came into town a couple of days later with a dagger for me. He said that it was “in repayment for my services at the Dunlogan Farm.” I replied that there was no repayment necessary, but that I would consider the dagger a great gift. When I looked upon the dagger I saw that this was perhaps the greatest gift I had ever received. The dagger was ornately crafted and had a masterful curve to the blade with runes damasked on to it. Now I have never seen such a thing in my life, but I know a few old ballads that tell of such blades and I knew at once that it was of Elder design.

This gift was an excellent lead, but Turik was not so willing to help with the expedition as I’m sure you could have guessed. It took much pleading on my part and a long list of demands on his part to get him to agree to come along on the expedition. I cannot remember what his demands were, but I am sure they were of little real importance.

So it came on the 3rd of April when the company from the University arrived. They were lead by Professor Niles Winthorp, the academic who came with high recommendation from your friend at the University. His bodyguard was a large half-ogre of few words named Mr. Trundles. With them was a girl not much older than myself named Georgia McBride. She was apparently the Professor’s best student and quite beautiful I must say. Finally there was a man by the name of Timothy Weydenham. He carried a rapier and a pistol and was very loud and jolly; I liked him immediately. The whole lot, except for maybe Timothy, certainly had the city look to them, but why hold to such prejudices, that’s my philosophy.

=== That Grimwood Hospitality ===

That night we gathered in the Green Grizzly to plan the expedition. Turik showed up, and by way of introduction, he straight away said something about about the ruins being the deaths of the city folk. He really is tough to be around sometimes. The good Professor was polite about the matter, but Turik pressed the matter and took some sort of infusion that caused his appearance to be terribly frightening to everyone in the bar — frightening enough to empty the entire bar of everyone but us in fact. In the empty bar Turik pressed his warning again and even threatened not to guide us there. It really wasn’t clear what his concern was though. Luckily the Profess or was polite enough to see past Turik’s abrasive way and everything was talked out. At this Turik left for the evening.

After Turik left we finished up the details of the plan for the expedition. This didn’t take much doing so we decided to have some drinks and turn the conversation to lighter matters. Mr. Trundles drank about as much as he spoke which is to say not so much. This is understandable as I surmise that it would take many tankards to soak his large frame. The Professor and Georgia seemed to keep to themselves although Georgia kept on looking at me the whole evening for some reason. Timothy and I exchanged tales of adventure the entire evening. What grand expeditions he had been on! Anyway, I did not drink that much because I knew that we would have to leave early the next day. So a few hours later I called a toast to the expedition and then bid everyone a good night.

When I left the Grizzly Turik was there waiting for me. He seemed to be angry at me for not being more distrustful of the party and for not finding out more about them when he left. I think that this was just due to his own distrustful nature though. We went back to my place to discuss more plans for the expedition should we have to fight any wolves or orcs. It was then that Turik explained that the ruins where in the territory of a clan of orcs known as the Maugrym.

== The Expedition ==

=== First Day - Spider ===
The next day, on the fifth of April we set out towards the ruins. We were to travel on foot with a team of mules to carry the vast amount of equipment that the Professor and his party had. The day’s journey went well with no delays until we encountered a large spider that had been stalking us. Turik was scouting back when the thing was spotted so he had to deal with it for the most part until Timothy and I finally came in to help. The spider was large, but much smaller than the one that was at the Dunlogan farm and we dispatched it without injury to ourselves. Turik was going to harvest the poison from the spider when a bear came, attracted by the corpse. At that we decided to continue on and avoid a needless encounter.

As we set up camp that evening Turik went back to harvest the poison from the spider that we'd felled earlier that day. This seemed like a needless risk to me, but we hadn’t made camp that far from the encounter and Turik managed to carry out his task without incident. That night also passed without incident. Georgia and I spent the last watch together on her own insistence. She was quite talkative and very pleasant to listen to. At the end of the watch she gave me a kiss. I think that she fancies me.

=== Second Day - Ambushed ===

The next day’s journey went as well as the first's. Turik spotted another bear, larger than the first, but we decided to skirt it. Timothy wholeheartedly wanted to hunt the bear exclaiming that he had never seen such a bear of magnificent size. This was kind of amusing as to me the bear was quite a bit smaller than many bears in our forest.

Towards the end of the evening Turik announced that we were now entering Maugrym territory, and this is when the journey took a turn for the worse.

That night I was awoken by Turik’s alarm that we were being attacked. Me and the rest of the sleeping party had been sleeping in a large tent that had been set up by the city folk. By the time I emerged from the tent the perimeter of our camp had nearly been breached from all sides by our attackers. In the darkness I could see a host of orc footmen moving towards us. Further away, there were three orc rangers guiding terribly large cat-like beasts on long leashes. Just as I took my first step out of the tents the grizzly cats were let loose and began their bound towards us. I managed to get one, maybe two shots off before we were surrounded.

There we were, surrounded by charging orcs and these terrifying cats. Timothy was on the northern flank, the Professor was at the east, Mr. Trundles the south, and I was to the west at the foot of the tent. Georgia was still in the tent gathering her gear and Turik was up a tree firing arrows at our attackers. I decided to call out some encouraging words to our party to keep everyone’s morale up or else we might fall to ourselves at this terrible ambush.

Timothy had to take on two of the cats, while another started to make its way up Turik’s tree. Mr. Trundles ended up holding off most of the footmen. It was here that I first saw the terrible strength of Mr. Trundles at work. He cut through orc after orc with a furious ease that made me grimly thankful of his presence in our party. Niles was not much of a fighter so he just defended himself as best as he could.

Now we have been in battles with worse foes before, but in this case our enemies had outmaneuvered us and we were fighting in unfavorable conditions with some companions that were not suited to defending themselves. I hadn’t even seen Georgia yet, but I hoped that she would not be noticed inside the tent. I had just gotten off all of the shots with my bow that I could before two orcs were upon me. I was in too close quarters to be useful with my bow so I called out to Niles to watch himself and dove past him to an open patch of forest where I could better maneuver with my bow. This seemed to buy me some time as the two orcs that were attacking me changed their targets to the Professor and Mr. Trundles, while two of the orcs on Mr. Trundles broke off and started towards me. The same number of foes was upon me, but I had bought enough time to magic myself some expeditious speed.

With my new maneuverability I was able to shoot arrows at my assailants and then move to prevent myself from getting surrounded. Turik was still up in the tree, shooting arrow after arrow after the beast that was stalking up the tree after him. Mr. Trundles was cutting down orc after orc with his massive great axe. Timothy was barely holding his own against the grizzly cats on him even though his dodged with skillful speed and struck back with precision that showed true finesse. At this point a new host of orcs cut their way through the rear of the tent. I almost forgot my own assailants to bound to her side when I heard her voice make the sounds of arcane sorcery and sounds of the orcs thudding to the ground.

The combat continued on like this for a few more moments. The orc rangers that had guided the grizzly cats had now taken up bows and were shooting the targets that best presented themselves, mostly myself and Turik as we were the only ones that were also using bows. No one on our side was taking out any foe though except for Mr. Trundles. Then I managed to drop one of the orc on me when a terrible thing happened. Timothy slipped back slightly and flung his arms out to catch his balance. At this point one of the grizzly cats sunk both of its front claws into his stomach and tore it open, leaving Timothy dead at its feet. The next moment I saw the Professor go down at the hands of one of the orc footmen as he was coming towards me to give aid.

My heart did not have the time to feel the loss of our two companions because I was too busy thinking that this encounter would be the end of us all. However, it turned out not to be. Mr. Trundles had managed to make his way through the rest of the footmen and was now attacking the grizzly attacks that were now on him. To Timothy’s credit the cats were now worn down enough that they were easily dispatched by Mr. Trundles. Soon later the last cat fell out of the Tree as Turik finally dispatched it. At that the rest of the orcs fled. We managed to dispatch one of the fleeing orcs, but the rest got away.

I immediately went to our fallen comrades and was able to revive the Professor, but Timothy had been torn apart too badly to be saved. And so with heavy hearts we broke camp and began a forced march to a different locale because we knew that our location was not safe anymore.

=== Third Day - The Ruse ===

The party had been dealt a terrible blow, but we decided to go on, figuring that Timothy would have wanted it that way. We had decided that we would have to march through the rest of the night. However, even this would not be enough to ensure our safety as the orcs rangers would be able to track us to our new location. To this end we came up with a plan to split up the party. Turik would take half the mules and hide our trail to try and fool our pursuers.  Not much of a plan, but it was our only hope.

We did a quick search of the orc bodies and found that two of them were carrying elvish weapons similar to my dagger: another dagger and a short sword.

We force marched through the night, burying Timothy a small distance out of our camp. The Professor said that he thought that this was a good place for the burial because this was a good forest.  We marched until we came in sight of a large hill where Turik explained was where the ruins were. From atop the hill, apparently in a large bowl we could make out about ten or so camp fires. Turik whispered to me that this was many more than when he was here last time.

It was almost day break and we decided to make camp where we were. Turik took his leave as planned and we set up a hidden camp. I could barely sleep in fear that our plan would not work, but a few hours later I was woken up—Turik had returned and said that he had bought us some time, but we would be set upon soon.

We managed to go another night and then another day with out incident. Turik’s ruse had worked! But as the next day began to dim Turik spotted a small host of orc rangers about a thousand yards away. This was good for us as we managed to escape an ambush. With this advantage we managed to dispatch the company with arrows without injury to ourselves. Indeed the fight even seemed too easy until we came upon the fallen orcs. They were not dead, but rather had fallen because they were pressing so fast to find us that they were too tired to stand up to our assault.

Since it was now night and our party was fresh we decided that there was nothing else to do then to set out for the ruins. At night was the safest time because most of the orcs would be out hunting. We made our way up the hill, tethering the mules about half way up. When we rounded the top of the bowl I gazed at last on the Elders' ruins.

== The Ruins of Lylandril Yarul ==

The ruins were mostly a gnarled and thicketed forest now with corners of buildings and the odd statue here and there throughout the area. In the center of the bowl was a small lake. The buildings around it continued on under water so I don’t think that the lake was always there.

We decided to stalk or way around the ruins through the forest so that we could avoid detection if at all possible. This worked fine as Turik seemed to know where the main trail was and therefore where to avoid it. We made our way around for a while until we came upon an opening in the forest. Georgia stumbled out too fast and had to be pulled back into the forest by Mr. Trundles. What we saw in the clearing was strange indeed. There was a circle of stones, some toppled inwards, some toppled outwards, all covered in what must have been Elder script. In the middle of the circle was a tall black walnut, not as big as the hanging tree, but close. Sitting around the circle was a small group of orcs, all of them with pictures of trees painted on their faces. They were all staring at the tree as if hypnotized by it.

The Professor leaned over to Turik and whispered, “Do you think that’s it? Could that be the White Tree of Lylandril?”

He was of course referring to the book of Elder lore that you had researched giving us the only fact we knew about the ruins. About how the ruins contained the White Tree of Lylandril and that it is a token of the last inhabitance of the edgewood.

To this question Turik gave a very strange answer. He said, “I would like nothing more than to stand in the middle of those stones.” Then he paused for a moment and said, “No, that is not the White Tree. It is not safe down there. Let’s continue.”

=== The White Tree ===

And so we continued. We made our way to what we surmised was the heart of the ruin. From near the heart we saw the top of what appeared to be a large white tree. As we worked our way towards it the thicket became thicker and thicker. We had to claw our way through some of the most tangled and twisted trees that I have ever encountered in the Great Wood to get at this tree. The trees were so tangled and interwoven that it seemed as if the wood itself was trying to keep casual visitors at bay. However, being determined we soon found ourselves at the base of the tree.

This tree is a Walnut, and there is no mistaking it. At over 80’ in height, it would be one of the largest naturally occurring trees of that height. But it is most certainly not natural. The tree has been carved of some pure alabaster stone, and is flawless in its presentation. The tree is menacing, to say the least. It looms high over head, and spans out easily twice as broad as it is tall. Thus, it made a great canopy over its base. At all angles, shadows play tricks with the eyes.  This branch, or that, will appear to move out the corner of the eye, but then appear quite fixed as I focused on the branch. It was quite unsettling.

Then I looked towards the tree’s base which I did not notice at first. I found this very strange and asked the rest of the party if they had also failed to notice the base and they agreed. Perhaps some magic is at work, or perhaps it is just a trick of the clever workmanship and artistry of the Fey artisans who created this statue. For at the base, noticeable long after the I had been caught in the menacing deceits and tricks of the upper boughs, the spirits of several fey creatures have been captured in stone. The wavering, distorted forms of elves and good creatures of the wood cast themselves on the ground at the base of the great tree, recoiling from its ominous boughs overhead as if they were emitting a great light or tremendous heat. Such is the workmanship of these statues, that the first-time viewer is given a great fright, thinking these spirits actually present. But a moment of calm follows, and it is observed that these are but the stony forms of some remembered or imagined horrible fate.

Mimicking the arboreal habits of the live Walnuts of the Wood, the great white tree has permitted nothing to grow at its base. The impossibly dense and tangled wood that was previously fought through to arrive here thins immediately under the boughs of the great statue, and one can clearly see the great alabaster pedestal upon which the tree and its terrified spirits rest. The pedestal is 80’ in diameter and 3’ high. Ancient Elder writings, which have not aged a day since they were formed, wrap around the pedestal. They are ancient and obscure, but even the uneducated can see after a quick walk around the base, that the writings are repeated four times, oriented to the points of the compass.

=== The Translation ===

The Professor and Georgia knew something of these writings and set to work in translation. This took quite some time and I thought nothing of it of the time, but I occasionally glanced up towards the sky and saw daylight, only to see night when I looked up again to confirm my suspicions. After a few hours the translations were complete and read aloud:

Know this, you who stand beneath the Accursed Tree; after countless ages since the first pine or birch took hold on this soil, and grew strong and multiplied, and gave an Earthly home to Good, all Good things have left it. Far have they traveled in the hopes of forgetting the loss of the beautiful home that has been taken from them, though far from adequate shall Earthly distances be to purge such bitterness. All good things have left the Great Wood, and their works shall wither, though this statue shall be the last to do so. For the curse that is placed upon the Evil Spirit of this wood by the last Good Creatures here dwelling, shall endure as long as the leaves of the Walnuts grow in the Spring. For when these foul trees fail to grow, the need for their curse will end. The Ravenous Spirit of the Wood will have no Earthly minion, and then shall Good People return to the wood. Until that day, the Spirit of the Wood will have dominion, and even the trees may walk and make war upon the unfortunate Living who must call the Blood Wood their home.}}

I think that there is no need to write of our reactions at this message since you are probably drawing the same conclusions that I did. At this point we all agreed that we have found what we came to find and that we should leave.

== The Sudden Fortress ==

We made our way out of the thicket. The leaving was much easier then the entering; only there was nothing different to my eyes about the thicket that we were leaving. When we emerged it was daylight which did not make sense. Furthermore our surroundings revealed that it was no longer spring like it should be, but rather summer had seemed to set in.

Figuring we did not have time to discuss such things during the day in an orc encampment we decided to make our way out of the ruins as quickly as possible. We walked about a hundred yards and came to a clearing — a large clearing that hadn't been there when we entered the ruin. The clearing formed a semicircle from where we stood to the edge of the former ruins. The walls at the edges of the ruins had been partially repaired and wooden towers had been built at regular intervals along the walls. Atop each tower were orc archers. To our right some distance away marched what I can only say was an Orc Phalanx. Never before have I seen or heard of orcs being organized enough to even resemble a military formation. When we entered these ruins they were no more than an orc encampment, and now it was a full orc fort.

Suddenly we were spotted and an orcish horn sounded as the Phalanx charged our company. Without hesitating Turik called out, “Towards that breech, run!” Turik pointed towards a gap in the fort’s wall and began running as did the others.

=== Battle at the Fort ===

I held back and cast mage speed about myself to aid my retreat. Just as I caught up with the others the Phalanx clashed with the company. None of us fought, but kept on running as we could hear several other Phalanxes charging at us from the rear. Suddenly another host of orcs was upon us and we realized that we could never make it over the wall without being cut down as we climbed. Mr. Trundles spoke up, “Go towards that tower. I’ll hold off these orcs at the door. You secure the platform.”

We switched directions, but Georgia caught a few unlucky strikes as we retreated and was knocked unconscious. I managed to quickly heal her and pull her to her feet and in the right direction as we made our way to the tower. It this point we started getting shot at with a flurry of bolts from two of the towers. We managed to make our way to the tower. Mr. Trundles set up at the door and the rest of us except Turik dove in. Turik held back and cast a spell and then began stalking up the side of the tower.

In the room in the tower was a bare room with a single table and two chairs. Two ladders lead up to the roof where orc archers where starting to fire at us from. The professor dove under the table and I rolled under after him and started firing arrows at the orcs above. I looked over at Georgia an said, “Come over to me, there is room under here.”

I had to roll out into the open to let Georgia in, but I got a peck on my cheek for the trouble. I loosed a few arrows and managed to dispatch enough of the orcs above to make it up one of the ladders before they were replaced. When I reached the top, Turik was near the edge of the tower fighting with his two hand axes. He had already dispatched a number of the orcs up and there were only a few left. I started firing arrows and stood guard over the breech in order to give the others time to make their way up.

=== Piles and Piles of Orcs ===

By the time the Professor and Georgia got up to the top the whole fort had been alerted to our presence. Mr. Trundles was defending the door against easily fifty orcs, their bodies piling up at his feet. Our path of escape had been cut off by a company of orcs and grizzly cats that were now emerging from the forest. Orc footmen were piling atop of lesser orc workers in a sort of pyramid to scale the sides of the pyramids.

From the edge of the forest I saw a giant of a troll emerge. This troll could easily be a match for even Mr. Trundles and I was worried. Suddenly Georgia aimed her finger at the troll and muttered an incantation and the troll turned around and walked away.

By now the orc pyramid had piled up to where it was reaching the top of the tower. I took an arrow and aimed it at one of the workers, killing it with one shot. I managed to hit the worker in his side so that he flailed in pain before dying, toppling the entire pyramid to the ground and wounding several of the other orcs. A one in ten shot maybe, but it was what we needed and it bought us time.

At this cue Mr. Trundles saw his opening and broke away from the door to make his way up the tower. Georgia and I began pulling up the other ladder. We almost didn’t make it as an orc took a jump to grab the bottom rung of the ladder, but missed. When we finally got the ladder up Mr. Trundles was up with his ladder. The amazing Mr. Trundles had managed to climb up and pull up his ladder by himself.

By this point I saw a terrible sight. The grizzly cats were climbing up the outer edge of the tower with their handlers in tow. Mr. Trundles was at the ready with his great axe and I had an arrow knocked, waiting for the first grizzly cat to round the edge of the tower.

I looked over at Turik, but he was engaged with his bow towards some foe in the next tower over. He was on one knee perched against an arrow slit for maximum cover muttering quite enchantments on his arrows before letting them loose at his foe on the other side. I saw him take an almost impossible hit in the rib right below his outstretched arm. I looked over at the other tower and caught a glimpse of an orc captain firing arrows from an elven bow. I left Turik to deal with his nemesis and turned to wait for my own target.

Suddenly a cat rounded the top of the tower wall and I put an arrow in its shoulder and rolled aside as it leaped over top of me. In another instant the rest of the orcs were up the tower and the fight was renewed. Turik had stowed his bow and was engaging the new foes in melee.

=== Georgia Taken ===

Georgia cast another spell over the other wall and I heard another orc pyramid fall. The pyramids, I had forgotten! Too late, act against the other side of the wall orcs started pouring over from the orc pyramid that had rebuilt itself. Turik turned to deal with these orcs, easily cutting through his quarry that were now battered and bruised from the first fall.

The tower battle raged on. The roof of the tower was crowded almost to the point of collapsing under the weight of too many attackers. It was all I could do to find openings to fire my bow without being cut down while my defense was down.

My muscles aced and my lungs gasped for air as we fought. I felt like I would pass out from exhaustion. Georgia had been using her magic to great effect, but it seemed to be running out as she began to cast simple cantrips to hold off only one foe. Mr. Trundles though was hacking foes down. Without him we would have been lost. With him we were beginning to open up a hole in the opposition until there were only a handful of opponents left and they were beginning to break. Then Mr. Trundles fell.

Before I could grasp the turn of the tide the Professor fell and was tossed off the side of the tower. Then two orcs tackled Georgia. I pulled out my dagger and tried to stop the orcs, but I couldn’t. My frantic stabs did nothing to stop the orcs as they heaved her over the side of the tower into the heart of the tower screaming as she fell.

As if their objective was achieved the rest of the orcs bounded off the tower, laughing as they went. I immediately went to Mr. Trundles and brought him to his feet. I yelled at him to get the ladder down the other side of the tower, that we must flee, and that the others are dead.

I was the last to leave the tower, and just before I went to the ladder I saw Georgia being carried away by the other orcs. The savages were laughing and groping her as they fled. Seeing that sight made a hate grow in me, a hate more fiery than I have ever felt before. The tower too was now on fire and flames were beginning to lick the edges of the roof. We had lost this day, our victory stolen from us by a pack of savages that deserve nothing more than death.

== Back to Eastwitch ==

We made our way back to Eastwitch with ease even though we were all injured and tired. While licking our wounds I implored Mr. Trundles and Turik to come back with me to rescue Georgia. Mr. Trundles agreed at once, but Turik said that he was under no obligation and that Georgia is probably dead anyway. He agreed to consult with his family on the matter and left for his part of the woods.

Three days later Turik returned saying that he would join us, his tune changed. I can only think that he has his own agenda in this. Perhaps one of his own kin has been taken or his family is troubled by the growing orc encampment and he was too weak to see that on his own. Sometimes his hypocrisy makes me so angry but I cannot stay that way for I consider him a good friend. I have considered him thus from the first time I met him and ask you to as well, even though his ways are strange. I hope you and Clemend will both see that.

I have now departed back east with Turik and Mr. Trundles to rescue Georgia if we can. I am asking that you and Clemend will come to our aid as I know that you will. I don’t think that we will be able to do it without you guys.

We will be heading out east and give Clemend directions as we pass his ranch. We will tell him to wait for you so that you may both depart together. Please hurry though.

— Bin

PS. I have a new suspicion that I must speak to you about.
