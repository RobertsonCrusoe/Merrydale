This building is a large place which can be mistaken for a barn at first glance. Closer inspection reveals that it has been built from thick planks of planed walnut, 4” thick. It has been framed in huge beams of pine, with every joint carefully laid in a double dove-tail. The walnut planks have all been tongue-and-grooved jointed, and the whole structure sits atop a sturdy stone foundation, from which arches rise up to surround doors and windows. Though the Greendales are a short family, it has been built with larger people in mind; the doors are 8’ high, and the spacious interior has 12’ ceilings. It is a two-story building.

It is run by Sandy Greendale, a niece of Ned’s, as well as her 2 daughters. These tiny women will buy just about anything of value that the people of Eastwitch can catch, skin, or produce. They give generous amounts of money/trade for most pelts that the hunters and guides bring into town. They also buy up surplus amounts of alcohol from anyone who will sell or trade it to them. They have long had a reliable trade agreement with the Fletcher family, who provides them with some of the most valuable arrows in the North, as well as many clothing items. They buy up manufactured clothing from anyone who will trade or sell it. This business offers a little less for goods than their actual value, but they will buy just about anything; this is a great convenience for woodsmen who don’t have time to sell each of their pelts individually, and also to many women who turn furs and pelts into clothing. Merchants coming into Eastwitch will usually unload their goods in bulk upon Mason’s, and then load up their wagons again at Greendale’s. Below is a list of the types of things that they will buy, and with a typical value. They will sell any of these items (if they are in stock) for about 30-50% more. They take trade or coin, at equal values.
 
==Clothing==

They will buy clothing at about 1/3 the price listed for Mason’s.

==Weapons==

For any weapon that Garrison can manufacture (or did manufacture), Sandy will pay 50% of what he is selling it for. For items that are more rare to Eastwitch, she will pay up to 100% of their list value (in the PHB).

==Armour==

No one in Eastwitch manufactures armour of any kind, with the exception of poor quality shields. Sandy will thus purchase most kinds of armour at nearly 100% of their list value (in the PHB), and will keep them for resale to the locals, or to adventurers in need, for almost 200%.
