Eugenia's Lament (sometimes known as Woman's Scorn)
An Eastwitch Ballad
Adapted by Stephen Robertson, January 2001
Source: Email thread e3e014be0a05594

[Note from Stephen: "I adapted an old ballad for Eastwitch over the holidays... much of it is the same as the traditional (it's originally about a Blacksmith who ditches some girl) but I've adapted it to reflect a bit of the frontier imagery from Eastwitch. Imagine her sorrow turning to a hatred for all Wardens."]

---

A Warden courted me
Nine months and longer
He fairly won my heart
His heart was stronger
With his axe-blade in his hand
He looked quite clever
And if I was with my love
I'd live forever.

But where is my love gone
With his eyes like weather
And his strange brown token on
Decked out in wolf's leather
I'm afraid the darkling Wild
Has robbed me of his beauty
And if I was with my love
I'd do my duty.

Strange news is come to town
Strange news is carried
Strange news flies up and down
That my love is buried.
I wish his brethren joy
Though they can't hear me
And may the Wild reward them well
For the slighting of me.

They don't remember when
He lay beside me
And he said he'd marry me
And not deny me.
No witness have I none
Save the Wild murky
And may It reward them all
For the slighting of me.

Her lips grew pale and wan
It made a poor heart wonder
To see the Wildwood yon
Twist her love asunder.
