Loved by the ladies, admired by the lords, few in Waterdeep haven't heard of the celebrated adventurer, brawler and gentleman entrepreneur, Arkos Thunderstaff, better known as Falcon.

Falcon spent most of 1371 accompanying a crew of adventurers on their journey from Elmwood to Waterdeep by way of the Dales and swamps of the central heartlands. He then became one of the canniest traders on the Sword Coast, outclassed only by one Nathaniel Thorp - his business partner and friend. Falcon and Nate, along with a third errant nobleman by the name of Valgard, plied a roaring trade in swords and sundries for many years before their destinies changed forever when ... but that is another tale.

13
\"Let's see here... if I buy 100 suits of Elturelian leather at 10gp each, and take them down to Irieabor I could sell them for 25gp each.  But Iriaebor has a 20% customs levy on Elturelian goods, and they charge 50gp per day for the berth in the docks, and the Carters guild's rates are exorbitant.  So, if I take the goods downriver to Baldur's Gate instead, I can save myself the taxes, and its cheaper to berth the boat, but I can only get 15gp per suit.  Maybe I can take them up to Waterdeep, where they'll sell for 30gp each... but the operating costs of the boat on that long voyage might not make it worthwhile.  Still, I can take a bit of a loss in Waterdeep if I can manage to get a good shipment of Mirabar steel to bring back down to Amn.  But, I've been given a mission to find the source of that narcotic and bring a shipment back to Waterdeep as soon as I can, so backtracking to the north will get me off-schedule.\"

To Falcon's regret - one of many, it must be said - he fears he will never build that inn on the East Road. Still, who knows?
