26th of Mirtul, 1479 DR

It is only a handful of hours past sunrise and the last day and a half has been fraught with peril.  We have done battle with an army of hobgoblins and some manner of orc warband that is far past the typical orcs one might encounter.

Lucretius has been injured terribly. A stone tower collapsed atop him while defending the horse breeding settlement of Kohr’Murron.  He took a serious blow to his head and although breathing, he is asleep and none of my medical expertise or assistance from the Gray Lord has helped him in the manner of having a speedy recovery.

Also injured in the fall of the tower is Kethoth Sepret.  How such a hardy and stalwart individual such as Lucretius could succumb to such a terrible blow, yet Kethoth is conscious still baffles me.  He did not escape from the tower unscathed however.  His knee has been twisted at a terrible angle and it took divine power from myself as well as a dwarven priest by the name of Hrogan to simply make it functional once more.  Considering that he might walk for the rest of his days with a discernible limp, he has handled the whole thing with no fuss at all.  The tiefling is utterly mercurial in his nature.  What would cause most to rail, he simply smiles politely at.  What causes him to quite literally burst into flames are matters that most simply coulnd not comprehend or bother to be bothered with.

This is the very least of what has happened here however.  Much as I failed Baird, another of our fellowship has fallen and in a far worse manner than simply having a stone tower fall upon them.  Arya is lost.  The vampire known as Barthus has somehow followed us, and while we were battling with the hobgoblins and orcs, Barthus snuck into the settlement, took control of Arya’s mind and had her begin killing the women and children of the settlement that she had been charged with protecting.  She outright murdered three women when screams of the women and children began drawing people towards the great hall.

Barthus and a dominated Arya tried to make their escape and with so much going on, the last I saw of Arya was her sunlight-kissed hair bouncing in the air as Barthus threw her over his shoulder before running up the wall, leaping over it and disappearing into the darkness with our eladrin companion.

There was no way to leave with the battle from the orcs and hobgoblins converging on us the way they did.  Ontop of all of that, there was some manner of drama between Kethoth, Cayd; a halfling of the settlement as well as Kohr, the father of the missing wife and children following Arya’s assault.

The fellowship is currently fighting amidst themselves.  A foul abomination has fled into the shadows with one of our own, and the people that have battled and bled with Arya are trying to decide if we’re going to rescue her, or track down Kohr’s missing wife and children that might have been abducted by a slaver from Llorkh.

Kethoth has somehow managed to covince Kohr that this halfling Cayd, is somehow responsible for his wife and children being abducted.  Kethoth is making a remarkably convincing argument which only serves to make Cayd look more and more guilty.  Cayd is half the size of Kethoth and and a tenth as articulate and now Cayd is locked away,  confined in a room and awaiting certain death whether Kohr’s family is found or not.  

I do not understand the logic of Kethoth’s argument no matter how convincing it sounds and I find myself missing Baird as I know he would not tolerate any of it, or possibly some recalcitrant Tormite that would get to the bottom of things while we went to the aid of Arya.  It has been nearly two and a half days since some of us have slept and Kethoth believes that we need to rest and then put our efforts into finding Kohr’s family before beginning our search for Arya.

If Kethoth is lying about Cayd, why?  Why would he lie?  I am sure that he is doing what he does best, stirring truth and lie together until both look and taste the same.  Beyond that, why would he choose to rescue people that he has called fools and whores and not Arya whom I believe he actually felt some manner of kinship with as well as responsibility for?

Kethoth is a capable spellcaster and controls flame as easily as he controls the slick words that fall from his lips.  He is a capable quartermaster and fleet of thought but he is no leader.  He brought the Mercykillers upon us and that cost us Baird.  He now has chosen rest and the safety of strangers over Arya; leaving her to be victimized by Barthus.  He cannot carry the burden of leadership and struggles to make the right decision at times and much of it seems driven by pride and pride alone.  A leader needs more than pride and while he leads this fellowship, I fear that tragedy will continue to be heaped upon us. Perhaps if I can awaken Lucretius, he and I can make a legitimate bid for control once more.  

I find myself envious of Baird and Lucretius, both blissfully unaware of these dark times.

The debate and posturing has concluded and whether I agree with the decision or not, sleep beckons.  We must find Kohr’s family as quickly as possible so that we might begin our search for Arya and her captor, Barthus.

I fear what we will find when we find Arya...if we find Arya.

Brother Mattrick Lychaeus, Voice of the Grey Lord
