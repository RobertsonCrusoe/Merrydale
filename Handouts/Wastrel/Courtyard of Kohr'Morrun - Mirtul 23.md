== Mirtul 23rd, 7 PM ==

The remaining wardens were keeping busy. Those not keeping watch for probing strikes from the Red Hand hobgoblins were busy with clearing aside dead bodies and fighting a losing battle against putting out the fires that were blazing on various parts of the Kohr'Morrun stables.

There just didn't seem to be enough hands to help with everything. Two wardens, Curran Feldegar (human male) and Sheel Parin (half-elf female) are struggling with keeping up with the fire brigade, weary from the rushed journey from Falcon's Roost in the northern Graypeaks, the panicked fortifying of the Khor'Murron homestead and the battle following that.

There is little left regarding stamina and it seemed right when Curran had dropped the four buckets he was carrying on a wooden pole across his shoulders and Sheel had stumbled to the ground, her foot slipping off a lower rung of the ladder she had been using to climb to the top of the wooden roof of the main hall...the skies opened up. There was a brief rumble of distant thunder and then the rain began to pour.

Although cold and certainly unpleasant to fight in, it worked wonders on washing away the blood and putting out the fires.

===Kethoth===

An exhausted and limping Kethoth seeks out Kohr, after using his magic to extinguish fires before the rain began.

Kethoth claps the big man in his muscled back, and shoots him a toothy smile. \"It was a quite a dust-up, eh? Looks like there will be another round. We need some reinforcements. Is there any way you can have one of your ranch-hands ride out to track down those deserters? Maybe Tyriol could go with them to convince them to come back. Hope yet remains...\"

At some point soon after the battle, Kethoth will lead an effort to get any casualties on their feet. Hopefully some of the men who were 'downed' during the battle can be brought to health with the aid of our healers.

Kohr takes a moment to give Kethoth a hard look, his extraordinarily tall frame looming over the tiefling's ordinarily tall frame before looking back at the sight of the ruined tower that once marked the entrance of his home.

\"When I climb on top of Suriy after being in the cups too long, THAT's a dust up. This...this mage, was a battle.\"

There's a small, almost secret smile on Kohr's lips as he says it, as if saying such a thing pleases him to no end.

The disapproving look he normally reserved for Kethoth was gone at the moment as he turned back to look at the tiefling.

\"Not often do I particularly enjoy working shoulder to shoulder with spellcasters but I can admit that were you and your companions not with us, this evening would have gone decidedly different.\"

Winding his way through the smoke of the besieged ranch, Kethoth seeks out each and every one of his men, making sure they are all accounted for and in passable health. He makes sure that all have a task to do; he makes sure that our muscle sets about pulling people from the wrecked tower, in the hopes of finding some survivors that might be healed by Mattrick. He tries to muster some help from Khor and his men for the grisly task of looking for survivors, and suggests bringing all casualties to the main lodge.

He then sets about looking for Victra, and finding her, asks;

\"Can you ride, girl?! If you can, and you know these hills, then let us find those deserters and bring them back. We'll need them before too long! Listen; the drums are booming again...\"

There was little work to be done regarding the seeking out of people in the collapsed tower. Everyone that should have gotten out of there before it collapsed did, other than Arya, Lucretius and Kethoth. The organizing was of value however because injured people were being seen to and small, tired groups were working on reinforcing the holes that had been put in the walls. Abrom was busy dragging large loads of timber to the workers at the walls while Hrogan and Abrom both advised on how to best reinforce the walls. Little could be done with the pile of stone where the gate tower once stood. The rain was falling hard and it made the stones slick. If anyone was still in the rubble and ruin, they were staying there. The women of the homestead were busy with tending to the wounded and preparing hot meals and directing survivors to where they could go to get some rest, even if it was a short one. Kuzma was busy cooking and Suriy had set up a camp of sorts in the main hall, imperiously shouting at people to do her will.

Mattrick was busy with tending to the still-injured Diay with Pima close by, scowling in fear and worry over what would become of her daughter.

<nowiki>* * * *</nowiki>

Victra was quick to nod her head in affirmation when Kethoth asked her if she could ride. She was already moving towards the barn, motioning for Kethoth to follow. 

\"Of course, knowing my father, he'll only want them brought back so he can kill them himself but the extra hands will be of use.\"

There was no hesitation in the way she moved and two horses were quickly saddled. She worked that suggested she was confident in herself physically as well as confident in her knowledge of what was asked of her. it was easy to confuse the beauty for a woman and it was only when she turned to tell Kethoth that his mount was ready, that she appeared as he true age once more of seventeen.

She gave the tiefling an awkward, almost shy smile for a moment before quickly mounting the large horse, wheeling it about and heading out of the barn when her forward progress was impeded by the halfling, Cayd.

He stood boldly in the way of the horse and gave the impression that he was taller than the horse that towered over him as he leaned slightly to the side and arched an eyebrow inquisitively in Kethoth's direction. He spoke, addressing Victra even as he watched Kethoth.

\"No one leaves. Kohr said. I've seen things in the woods to the north...strange things. Kohr agrees that no one leaves...and although he hasn't exactly been specific, I'm sure that a tiefling mage leaving with his favourite to seek out assistance from a group of witless cowards in a rain storm are not an exception to his wishes.\"

Victra sidestepped with the large horse, looking a bit confused and unsure how to respond to the halfling. Her head turning and her blue eyes falling to the tiefling behind her, unsure what to say while between the two \"men\".

Kethoth edges his horse right up to the halfling, looking down at him and smiling the most sincere smile he can muster. \"You're a brave little soul, and I don't doubt that you are committed to your master. But we are in need of any hands we can get, and if you can't find something more productive to do than get in the way of people with a plan, I fear for the fate of this place.

\"Now, I know those men that left have many rotten souls among them; none were as brave as you, mighty halfling, to stay in this palisaded crucible. However, if we can find but a few brave souls among them, the sight of survivors, this young flower I have with me especially, might convince them to test their mettle shoulder to shoulder with the likes of you and I, halfling.\"

Kethoth gives the halfling an intent stare, nodding and smiling almost imperceptibly.

Cayd gives Kethoth a look that is one part amusement and another part puzzlement before smoothly stepping to the side and out of the way of the horse that Kethoth has approached him with.

\"If you feel that strongly about it, mage; you are more than welcome to join the men that fled earlier.\"

The halfling's attention shifts to Victra for a moment as he continues.

\"You know where your father stands on such things. I'm sure your mother or Pima have something to yell at you about, or you can look for your brother...again.\"

Victra gives Cayd a disheartened look before quietly slipping down off of her horse and leaving the barn but not before looking back at Kethoth and giving him an apologetic moue. The young blonde covered her head against the falling rain as best she could and ran off in the direction towards the hall.

Cayd continued to move away from Kethoth and lean casually against the side of a stall with his arms crossed over his chest.

\"Some advice, mage. If you do leave of your own accord take one of your own horses and not Kohr's because if you don't, it's best if you don't return at all.

Also, you're a mage, which means you're most likely well-read and if you are indeed well-read then it most likely means you have a broader understanding of the world. Your superior airs would suggest otherwise. Stop pretending to be something you're not.\" 

Shrugging his shoulders nonchalantly as he continues...

\"...or at least pretend to be something more interesting.\"

Pushing off the wall and turning his back on Kethoth as he moves towards a large rack full of gear.

\"Are you going to need a saddle for YOUR horse? THAT, I can hide from Kohr.\"

<nowiki>* * * *</nowiki>

First making sure there is no one else in the barn first, Kethoth takes two quick steps towards the halfling, and his smile changes to a sneer. \"You want me to pretend to be something more interesting, do you? Well then, I shall pretend to be a cook, and I shall further pretend that you are a cony, and this damnable barn the oven!\"

Kethoth takes two more steps towards the halfling, and as he does, he seems to grow a foot taller with each step. A black and sinister flame erupts, its flames slowly ululating and enshrouding the tiefling, and his eyes grow so deep-black that they seem to siphon all light around him. He addresses the halfling in an unearthly, booming voice [Kethoth used 'Daunting Presence' utility power; +5 to Intimidate, +14 total].

\"I have sent over a hundred souls screaming to hell today with my flames! And yet, I have not burnt my fill! If you interfere with me again, or even deign to address me, I will add your corpse to the smouldering heaps of them I have decorated this little ranch with. Get thee from my sight, rabbit!\"

Kethoth points an impossibly long, black-flame-covered arm towards the door, and scowls down at the halfling, waiting for him to leave...

Cayd's eyes widen a great deal and he takes two steps back, stumbling for a moment before catching his balance and scrambling out of the barn and out of sight, leaving only Kethoth in the quiet of the barn, rain falling heavily outside with the sounds of faint shouting as people struggle to mend the damage done to the walls of Kohr'Murron.

Kethoth returns to his normal size and demeanour, and mutters to himself \"Honestly...if there is something I like less than halflings in these Realms, i haven't found it yet...\"

Kethoth saddles up a pair of horses, and leads them out to the courtyard, and seeks out Tyriol.

===Tyriol===

Tyriol brushed past Sepret and Kohr, heavily favouring his left leg - he had no time to search for deserters, or whatever the nine-hells that tiefling was rambling about. The ranger's eyes fell upon the ruined wagons that had been left unhitched near the armoury...or what was left of them, that is.

\"Gods,\" he thought to himself, \"...where was he?\" 

Frantically he searched, digging through piles of debris, tossing timber and stone aside in a panic. Stomach knotted tight, hands shaking, the ranger's wide eyes finally fell upon a small patch of ruined white cloth jutting out of the earth. As quick as he could, he made his way over and scratched at the mud and stone until he caught glimpse of what he was looking for. Relief washed over him as he excavated the rest of the messily wrapped bundle.

Gingerly, the half-elf got to his feet, muddy bundle in hand. Hesitantly, he pulled a corner of the cloth aside and rested his eyes on the ravaged face of his childhood friend. Arlyn's empty eyelids gazed back at him, and Tyriol quickly covered it up again, both relieved and disgusted at the same time. 

\"You'll be home before you know it, friend. Just awhile longer.\" Tyriol whispered, assuring both himself and Arlyn's remains. Looking sidelong at the main hall, past Kethoth and Kohr, he caught sight of the wardens and slowly made his way over. Tyriol recognized them both, but their names escaped him. Quietly, he bent over and scooped up one of empty buckets Curran had dropped and spoke.

\"I'm glad to see you two made it.\" He held the bucket out to the warden, trying hard to smile as he did so. \"What are your names?\"

Curran gave a cursory look over the bundle that Tyriol held in one arm, his nostrils flaring as if he caught an odd, foul scent even over the smell of smoke and blood that was quickly being washed away by the downpour.

He looked up to meet Tyriol's eyes and gave him an appraising smile before dropping the buckets that now would be of little use in the rain and extending a hand to Tyriol in greeting.

\"Curran Feldegar and this here is Sheel,\", motioning to the drenched and miserable looking half-elf behind him.

\"You might actually be a better shot than your mother.\"

Sheel drew up alongside Curran and gave him a disapproving look, only to have Curran shrug his shoulders and speak to Sheel.

\"What? He's in it now as deep as any of us. I overheard Ghaleon telling him nearly everything in the hall before the battle.\"

Turning back to Tyriol before continuing.

\"She's just a bit jumpy. None of us are supposed to let anyone know about The Roost. I'm willing to make an exception for the Captain's son.\"

Sheel, a female half-elf, seemingly older than Tyriol but perhaps it is simply a harder life that she has lived might be attractive were it not for a cruel looking, and awkwardly bandaged cut along her right jaw lifted her chin slightly in the direction of Tyriol.

\"Seems like you're injured as well. You do that leaping off the tower? Can't say I've ever seen anything so...\"

\"Brilliant!\" , grinned Curran.

\"...I was going to say, reckless...\" , muttered Sheel

Tucking the bundle nonchalantly into his cloak, Tyriol dropped the bucket he was pointlessly grasping and took a firm hold of Curran's hand.

\"Reckless fits the bill, alright. I am not a clever man, but I know full well when the ground is giving beneath my feet. Well met Curran, Sheel.\" He nodded to both of them.

A sensation of warmth had welled up inside of the ranger. Seeing these two in high spirits had a lifting effect on the half-elf, his leg felt a bit better, the rain a bit less cold. Tyriol had vague memories of these two - in his youth, he’d roved the streets of Loudwater with a large company of children. Back then he was just one of many, but now he got the feeling that they looked at him with a twinge of admiration. An awkward feeling welled up in his stomach as he realized this was probably just spill-over from their respect for his mother.

Clearing his throat, Tyriol organized his thoughts and calmly asked the question that had been burning the back of his mind for the last few hours.

\"The Roost, you and the rest of the men have made constant mention of it. I need to know where it is and what state you last saw my mother in.\"

Curran fell quiet after Tyriol's greeting and he took a step back to stand beneath the eaves of the hall in order to get even partly out of the rain. Sheel followed and motioned for a moment with a tilt of her head for Tyriol to follow as she moved to try to get out of the rain as well.

Sheel gave him another wary look before finally speaking.

\"Your mother...\"

Frowning a moment before correcting herself.

\"The Captain is as well as she always is. When we left, she and the remaining wardens were bolstering the defenses at The Roost. The real army of the Red Hand is on its way and The Roost is the only true defense standing between them and the lowlands. She sent us to warn Loudwater...we had no idea that we would be attacked by these hobgoblins. I just don't understand where they came from.\"

Tyriol's half-smile faltered at the seemingly contrived answer he recieved, but he understood that the two might be in a bit of an awkward position. Unmoving, the half-elf stroked his chin. Rain hammered against his neck and shoulders, soothing his sore back and spurring his thoughts - Loudwater indeed needed warning. This Red Hand vanguard had nearly decimated the defenses of Kohr'Morrun...his home would not last long against the full might of the hobgoblin army that even now marched upon them. 

\"Much thanks for you candour, Sheel. I am somewhat relieved to hear my mother is in good health...but you didn't answer the whole question.\" He said pointedly.

\"Where is The Roost? It is important that I know.\"

Sheel looks back over her shoulder at Curran who is busy with picking at a scabbed scrape along his forearm and a somewhat defeated look passes across her features before looking back to Tyriol.

\"The Roost is north by north-east of here about five days journey on horseback if you know the mountains. It's at the southern end of a valley we call The Noose. It's the fastest way out of the central Graypeaks. You could hold off an army with just a small force at The Roost.\"

Curran decided to join the conversation once more.

\"Not 'could' Sheel. It's been done before and it'll be done again.\"

Sheel scowls at Curran a moment before retorting.

\"They had the three heroes. We do not.\"

Curran gives Sheel a smug grin as he speaks again.

\"The four heroes. You're wrong...again.\"

Sheel shakes her head and rolls her eyes.

\"Not everything you hear around a campfire is truth, Curran.\"

Satisfied for the moment, Tyriol clapped his hands together and gave them a quick rub, warming his palms through the rain-soaked leather that covered them. He observed the pair's bickering with a bemused expression on his face - Tyriol had no trouble picturing the two as a couple. After the two had finished, the ranger spoke.

\"By the end of this siege, we will be making new tales to tell around the campfire, believe you me.\"

It was at that moment that Kethoth arrived, leading two horses

\"Ranger! I have need of you! Let us ride!

\"We will seek out the 10 men who fled, and inspire them to return. I want your face with me to remind them of your mother, who we will be marching to aid once we are done saving these horse-breeders from what passes as an army in these hills.\"

The rain beckoned wisps of steam from his robes, and he wore a somewhat annoyed expression on his face. Tyriol, in a much improved mood, was in complete agreement with Kethoth's proposed plan.

\"I like the way you think, wizard. The sooner we're finished here, the sooner we can be making our way to the Roost to assist the captain.\" 

In the presence of the tiefling, Tyriol felt somewhat awkward referring to his mother. He wasn't sure why, perhaps the ranger wanted avoid sounding overly fond of her, or some nonsense. He took ahold of his horses reins and began leading it alongside the wizard, but not before looking aside to his comrades and saluting.

\"Be well, you two. We will return in due time, hopefully with those cowards in tow.\"

=== The Warforged ===

As Kethoth and Tyriol ride behind the back of the hall and make their way towards the concealed entrance, they can see Abrom, Doan and a few of the Loudwater wardens finishing up reinforcing the hole that was battered in the wall close to the forge.

A few had noticed the two riding towards the concealed entrance and even through the fall of the rain, the clicking, clanking and whirring of Abrom could be heard as the tracks that made up his lower body began to move. Even as he rolled closer to Kethoth and Tyriol, he was still facing towards the men working by the wall, giving them orders on how to place the reinforcing supports in his strange, metallic, echo-sounding tone.

The tank treads whirred to a stop and with a low hum, Abrom's torso rotated around to address the two riders. Abrom's eyes glowing with an amber light and flaring brighter as he speaks in the same tone as he has previously done.

\"No one leaves through the concealed northern entrance. If you wish for an exception to this command, then you must speak to Kohr.\"

Kethoth looks back over his shoulder at Tyriol. \"Is this contraption addressing me?!\" Kethoth sneers at the machine. \"Kohr should never have given this thing means of locomotion. It must be dreadfully frustrating for the kitchen-maids to grind beef when they are told to do so.\" Kethoth sneers at the machine again.

\"All the same, I don't know that I can reason with either Kohr or his vocalizing egg-whipper. Let us find another way out.\" To the machine; \"Go... tell... Kohr... that... we... have... left... period... we... will... be... coming... back... in... haste... period... prepare... to... open... the... back... door... upon... our... arrival... period.

Any impact that Kethoth's words might have are lost on the lifeless features of the Warforged's face. Abrom; nearly as tall as Kethoth and Tyriol are while mounted on horseback simply watches Kethoth with its amber-hued eyes glowing softly for a few moments before the sound of things whirring and grinding occurs once more. Abrom's torso rotates away from Kethoth and its attention seems focused on the individuals still reinforcing the wall. Its voice can be heard, speaking far louder so that the men by the wall can hear.

\"No Doan, not there. To the side, closer to the foundry. More... more... now up... up... up... there. Secure the board.\"

Abrom stays close by to Tyriol and Kethoth as well as the concealed door, unmoving as it continues to direct the men twenty feet away with their work.

===Volund===

Feeling completely drained, Volund slowly walked over to where the west wall gave way to thunderous magics of the goblin warcasters. The shouts and cries for aid from the other defenders fell upon deaf ears as he made his way through the debris, he simply wanted a moment to reflect on what just happened. His every foot fall froze the wet ground and turned it into a muddy slush and the falling rain turned to sleet while the cold air swirled with tiny flakes of snow, his carapace of ice carved a frozen trail as he walked out to the edge of the river. 

Staring out passed the polluted water, he saw the path of the destruction the goblins visited upon the western forest. Fires and smoke were still burning in the distance and he felt a pang of remorse for his part in betraying nature for the protection of this settlement. He knelt and cleaned his blade, Shyr'rach on the frozen grass and mouthed a prayer to the Lord of Forests. 

Volund rarely saw eye to eye with the Cerulean Circle, but their raising him meant he could not escape their philosophies and partial indoctrination. He was obligated to protect nature and rain vengeance upon those who transgressed it. He stood up, blade in hand, and stared westward - eyes smouldering through his tomb of ice that spiralled outward as those feelings of sorrow quickly turned to unfettered anger and hatred of the Red Claws.

He shouted over the sound of the rain and the river, \"I CURSE YOU IN THE NAME OF SHYR'RACH, THE EARTH WILL NOT SHELTER YOU. THE WIND WILL NOT GRANT YOU FAVOUR. THE FOREST WILL CONFUSE YOUR PATHES. THE DREAD COLD WILL CLOSE IN AROUND YOU. EVERY PASSING HOUR HASTENS YOUR DEMISE.\"

By now the thick spires of ice had grown over the outer side of the walls and had etched their way across the river. His thoughts turned to the blade and what these coming days will hold. He lingered a minute or two longer and then sheathed Shyr'rach. The icy shell dissolved and the surrounding landscape began to thaw as he walked back into the fortress to assist the others.

== Mirtul 23rd, 9 PM ==

=== Tyriol and Kethoth ===
It took some coaxing from Tyriol and some exercising of patience from Kethoth to get the horses to navigate past the rain-slick rubble of the gate tower but after a short while, they were both on the charred and rickety, steel-shod \"bridge\" that the Red Hand had placed after the initial battle.

They had mounted and were just wheeling about to leave when they heard someone shouting above the constant drumming of the rain upon the ground and their heads.

Hrogan was seen clambering over the pile of rubble, his rain-soaked beard thrown over one shoulder to keep from tripping him up and he drew up alongside the tiefling and the half-elf.

\"You do know that there's an army of hobgoblins out there right? Is it true you're going to look for the deserters? Kohr seems to think the two of you are simply looking for an excuse to flee and not return. That line of thought would make sense to me, but I've seen what you have brought in your wagons. I doubt you would simply flee and leave such artifacts behind so then the logical answer that you're trying to flee does not seem quite so logical. The line of thought that says you are genuinely going out into a battlefield during a rainstorm to track down a bunch of deserters and bring them back...well that seems the opposite of logical, in fact, excuse me for saying so but it sounds downright foolhardy. \" 

The dwarf blows rain out of his moustache before taking a step away from the horses and shrugs his shoulders.

\"Whatever your course of thought is, whatever the motivations are behind it...I hope Tymora watches over you.\"

=== Volund ===

Kohr approaches Volund shortly after Volund is done shouting and gives the swordmage a speculative look. For having taken part in the defending of a siege and a battle after that; for having just spent the last hour working non-stop to help reinforce and plan defenses for his home, the older man seems to be as fresh as a morning flower.

\"That was quite the oath. You're an odd one, Heimdall but nearly all of your companions are, but you certainly know how to defend a breach. Your swordsmanship at the gate during the siege and here at the wall when the shaman blasted their way through ... fine work.\"

The tall, broad man shifts, seemingly unfazed by the heavy rain and motions with his chin in the direction of Hrogan speaking to Kethoth and Tyriol on the bridge.

\"Those two are hard to trust. What are they up to?\"

Volund's jaw muscles clench as he glances at the half-elf and tiefling. Turning back to the large man he says, \"I know very little about these seven as I've only been travelling with them for a few days. Cerulean Circle business... they're here, in part, to pay penance for crimes against nature.\" Resting his hand on his scimitar, he finishes, \"For now, I'm here to witness their atonement.\"

\"This much I do know, the demon, Kethoth, is quick in thought, sharp in speech and would sooner convince you to fall upon your blade than have you raise it against him. He is cunning and his motives remain shrouded to me and if I should guess the other six as well.\"

Volund lets out a sigh and says, \"Not to speak ill of the man, I just have a hard time trusting anyone who sees fit to raze a forest for one - and secondly, destabilize the entire countryside. Now tell me, dragon-slayer, is there anything pressing that I can assist with while we wait for the Red Hand's return?\"

\"I can't say I know of this Cerulean Circle. So they...and you are here because of something they did? Are they the reason why hobgoblins are knocking on my front door?\" This is all said while Kohr continues to scowl at the gathering on the crudely-built bridge that the three stand upon in the distance.

His scowl fades though upon Volund referring to him as \"dragonslayer\" and he gives Volund another speculative look.

\"Get inside and get dry. I've been here for over twenty years and I've never seen it rain like this. There's something ill brewing and now would be a time for you to pray to your forest god or your sword or whatever brings you peace because that peace will be short-lived. They're not as numerous as they were before they attacked us...\"

His attention turns away from Kethoth, Tyriol and Hrogan and turns to look out at the forest that had been cut down in the name tactics and defense and the countless corpses that littered the ground.

\"...but they're out there and they're plotting and planning. We'll hear their drums again.\"

Placing a large, calloused hand on Volund's shoulder and giving him a toothy smile.

\"No matter my misgivings about the tiefling and the half-elf, and no matter the reasons why you're here, your presence here is welcome. The grim priest and the dragonborn might need your assistance. They asked if they would be allowed to bury their brother in arms behind the hall. The priest said that he could no longer wait to speak the rites of passage for his brother. It's a strange group you travel with. Some look to bury their dead and others look to parlay with cowards. Strange times swordsman, strange times...\"

\"Some in my order would have these seven blamed for every broken stick or fallen leaf in the region, but that is their nature - protective of the forests and self-righteous to a fault. It is not my place to excuse the Seven of their wrong doings, but I am level-headed enough to understand they are not entirely to blame for what has transpired in recent days. Something is afoul in those mountains.\"

He looks up at the sky and says, \"Druids of my order have been manipulating the weather of late to deal with a forest fire to the south-east. This could be another ritual of theirs or just a side effect of the first one.\" 

Volund nods to Kohr and wanders off to find Lucretius and Mattrick.

===Kethoth and Tyriol===

Kethoth mounts up after leading the horses across the rubble. He takes a long look at the edges of the forest, looking for close and dangerous enemies. \"Take a good look before we head out, Tyriol; your eyes are younger than mine. Make sure we are safe to leave.\" 

Kethoth looks down at Hrogan, and puts a finger alongside his nose. \"I am about to play a small ruse; I am sure that there are spies nearby that can hear or see us. Just go along with me, and make sure the gates are open at the back when we return.

With this, Kethoth rears up his horse, and shakes a fist at the dwarf. Shouting as loud as he possibly can \"The devil take you all, if you can't see that you are all doomed! Defend this festering barn and the whores and numb-skulls that sleep upon the dung heaps herein! Kethoth will not die a fools' death here!\" (though bluffing, Kethoth’s ruse was aided by a sincere belief in what he said about whores, numb-skulls and dung heaps).

With this, he gives the dwarf a wink, then bolts off, towards the deserters.

Even in the downpour, fires can be seen to the south where the forest wasn't cut down, and clearly the hobgoblins have managed to cobble together some crude structures in the forest in order to shelter them from some of the rain.

Even though he was \"in\" on Kethoth's ruse, Hrogan's eyes still widened in surprise at the false conviction Kethoth managed to put in his tone.

Both Tyriol and Kethoth had to strain to see if there's anything ill waiting for them in the shadows to the south of the settlement and after a few moments, things look \"relatively\" clear as it's hard to see much of anything in the dark as well as the rain.

Both riders needed to follow the river to the east, back towards the Graypeaks and the foothills that the settlement was nestled in. The rain hammered down on them mercilessly and they needed to ride slower than they would have wished for fear of their horses tripping over something in the dark. They had to ride for close to an hour until they were able to come to a part of the river that was shallow enough for them to cross so that they could head north again and hopefully pick up the trail that was left by the deserters as they fled north.

The rain hadn't helped with the temperature and not only was the night cold, but the water was freezing as the tiefling and half-elf carefully rode their horses through the shallower part of the river before emerging upon the other side.

=== Volund ===

Volund needed to only walk around the back of the hall to find two brawny figures toiling in the mud with heavy spades. Having never fully dealt with the disappearance of his own family has hardened his heart somewhat and caused it to callus in the face of personal loss. Volund found he was indifferent toward death, and he did all he could to muster a silent stoicism for the dead man.

He foolishly asked them, \"Is there anything I can help with?\" Anticipating a proud and stern, \"No\" from either, if not both of them.

To Volund's eyes the body's wrappings appeared the finest white, even with the weather the way it’s been and how far they have travelled he never really paid attention to just how clean it has managed to stay. He suspected form of ritual or divine aid, having grown up in a sheltered and paranoid community the doings of a cleric are a curiosity.

Volund's thoughts wandered for a moment, \"I wonder if their God of Death was delivered my family all those years ago.\"

The rain continued to fall and Mattrick looked to Volud for a moment as Volund spoke. His mud-spattered face clouded with anger for a moment before Lucretius laid a heavy hand on his shoulder. So great was my bloodlust that I chose to assault a helpless opponent rather than defend my charge of nearly two decades.
