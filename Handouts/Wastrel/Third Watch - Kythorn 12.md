As usual, the tiefling and the sorcerer are on third watch together, and most of the party is used to falling to sleep to the hushed voices of these two, as they discuss matters of many sorts into the smallest hours of the morning.

The last couple of nights, they have been quieter than usual, however. Kethoth has his nose stuck in the book that he brought out of the vault in the basement of the Monastery, and Radha studies the writings of Barthas, to whom she still seems inextricably connected. Kethoth mutters arcane words under his breath, and closes his eyes, trying to recite them verbatim. Once in a while, your rest is interrupted as a ball of fire the size of a head dances between Kethoth's hands, and the wizard chuckles with delight and satisfaction.

Camped outside the doors of the Nexus tonight, Kethoth puts down the book, concentrates, and the ball of fire appears. He smiles and the fire fades. He then turns to Radha, who has not yet taken her nose out of the book of Barthas'. \"Well, my plucky little plum\" he whispers to Radha. \"Have you discovered anything in his writing? He still has a hold on you, even in death. I can see it. What are we going to do about your...condition?\"

Kethoth looks at Radha with a mix of sympathy and mischief playing across his face, his eyes shimmering in the candlelight by which Radha explores the heavy book in her lap.

==Radha Ruminates==

On third watch that night, Radha's mind was wide awake, eager to continue where she last left off in 'Courting Death'. The other nights had usually been spent recounting the events of the previous day or in casual chatter (although 'casual chatter' with Kethoth is never full of mindless topics like the weather or the quality of travel rations), but this night, there were serious matters at hand.

\"-- to do about your...condition?\"

Kethoth's hushed question draws Radha's focus back from sea of words on her lap to the hard stone floor on which she sat. There is a moment's pause as she finishes the sentence that had sat patiently waiting under her finger while her mind churned. \"I am not sure what can be done,\" she says, sitting up straighter give her back a stretch.

Radha looks over at the tiefling. \"This book is a journal or log of sorts for his thoughts and... experiments, as you probably well know, having read it already.\" Radha's face twists into a quick scowl, a little galled that this tome of information had been in the Company's possession for quite some time and that she had not known of it nor would have been able to read it.

\"Barthas makes a point of saying that his prize specimen was Arya. He was able to do more with her, and I was only a half-success. His efforts were directed toward making his kind more hardy, more difficult to exterminate. By linking their... essences, I suppose, with phylacteries, Barthas wanted to ensure that a vampire couldn't be killed without its phylactery being destroyed or compromised first.\"

Radha pauses, looking over the last paragraph she read and thinking back to her nightmare at Kohr'Morrun. There wasn't a lot of information on magical pottery or enchanted earthenware in the Monastery's library, but what she could find in the hours and hours she spent looking with middling success were hints that the Vase from her dream could be one such phylactery. But whose? Barthas' awkwardly translated writings suggested that it was Arya, not herself or even himself who was the experiment. So, as far as Radha could surmise on her own, the phylactery was most likely the eladrin's. She wasn't sure if the tiefling had come to the same conclusion or any conclusion at all, so Radha decided to keep her suspicions to herself.

\"If Barthas is truly dead, this may be a lingering condition that I have will have to work around for the rest of my days; however, if Barthas had a phylactery made for himself -- which he does not openly state in this book, but the possibility cannot be disregarded -- then we will see him again.\" Radha gives a quick smirk, trying to hide the growing unease in the pit of her stomach. \"He'll be back for his Waterdhavian.\"

==Sepret Seethes==

Kethoth swallows hard as he listens to Radha's words. He squints and focuses intently on her as she finishes her speculations, and his face is visibly angry as she concludes.

\"If this monster yet lives, as it were, it is only through one of these Phylacteries.\" His face turns into a scowl and he shakes his head, and a low rumble from the base of his throat issues. This is the first time any of you have heard this noise or seen this face.

\"If he's not destroyed, he'll soon wish he were. I will spend my days learning to torment these monsters; I will summon a host of demons to flay Barthas over and over for a thousand years, until his own torment...\" Kethoth stops in mid-sentence; his words had been becoming growls, but he clears the angry expression from his face and smiles warmly at Radha, seemingly coming out of a spell.

\"We will put things right, my dear. You'll see. We have so many more questions to have answered, and we shall find the answers, you and I.\" Kethoth sits down next to Radha. He pulls back one silk sleeve, and his shadowy familiar, the cobra that he calls 'Herrutep', uncoils from his forearm and drops to the ground, and slithers off in the direction Kethoth is supposed to be watching. Kethoth then leans heavily against the stone wall.

\"Did I ever tell you about Baron Maskil, my dear sweet Radha? The year was 1373 and...\" the tiefling drifts off into an old story as he massages the knee of his damaged leg.

\"So you see, my fangling, that in times past, these monsters were easier to destroy. I should reconcile with the fact that the Spellplague has made the world a mysterious place again, even to the most erudite\" he winks at Radha with this. \"However, I just can't believe that Barthas yet exists. We both watched him burn. His very ashes are in one of my trunks, by Thoth's third eye! And yet, his hold on you is not relinquished. So, we must accept that he may yet stalk the Realms.

\"We shall find the phylactery of your dreams, and free you. Mark my words!\"
