Following the delivery of the last two \"keys\" by Brother Turnagall and Arya's conversation with Brother Allendi a day and a half prior.

Kethoth addresses the troops; \"We have a few things to discuss, my friends, not least of which is our plan of approach for the 'Mercykillers'\" Kethoth rolls his eyes a little, then smiles warmly after mention of your adversaries.

\"However, let me start with a little disclosure, to put some of you at ease. When we arrived from our subterranean adventure the other day, we found two notes waiting for us; one was the odd skull, which we will discuss further in a moment, and the other was addressed to myself, from Curavar. Curavar, if some of you don't know, is the colourful mage of Loudwater.

\"Now, the two of us have yet to meet, but we have common friends and associates. His letter to me indicates that he has been asked to investigate me, as I appear to have some dubiuos qualities. Certainly, I am of devilish lineage, and my past is long and varied. I am sure that Curavar has only the best interest of his fair town in mind, and it seems that he has been given the task of investigating me, and should be credited for carrying out his charge so diligently that he tracked us down here, and delivered his note in person.

\"However, the wizard, and his employer, missunderstand me. I am sure that in the days to come, when we are all put to the test, that you will all see that Kethoth's character and motivations are not a reflection of his devilish countenance.

\"Anyway, friends, let us discuss this business with the Mercykillers...\"

\"We need to discuss our plan of action and position on the Mercykillers. These villains have threatened us with death more than once, and just recently presented us with this tangilbe evidence of their intentions towards us\" with this, Kethoth holds up the curious halfling skull with the metalic mandible. He investigates it with a look of seroious contempt and pitty on his face.

\"This 'message' was delivered by the two newest additions to their ranks, which brings their number to seven. However, before we start counting heads and such, let us back up a bit and assess our relationship.

\"At the best of times, these black-clad villains have been a seroius nuisance and obstacle to our objective, keeping us unhorsed the entire way. The worst of times is yet to come, but they have shown their intentions more than once with thinly vieled threats, which have now escalated into an unmistakeably clear promise of violence and the attempted extermination of the lot of us.

\"Some of you may think that Kethoth has brought some of this upon us with my actions against their scout the other day, in the Broken Tower. I have said little on this matter since then, but now is the time to address it.

\"When we left the lower chambre of the Broken Tower, where the 'Killers were found unearthing an alabaster pillar similar to our own, they threatened us with violence then, and we backed down, for better or for worse. That some of you decided to temper violence with a diplomatic retreat is a credit to your character and cool thinking, and I'll not claim otherwise.

\"However, as we mounted the steps, the mind of Kethoth was working as fast as it can, and put together the following facts;

\"1. The Mercykillers are after the 8 alabaster pillars, of which we have read about in the analects, of which we have found in our delvings in the ruin, and of which we have in our very possession one of their limited number.

\"2. The Mercykillers have accepted no help when it was offered by us, and have in fact hindered all those that they have encounted on the way here, and have further threatened us with violence if we interfere in their activities again.

\"3. That we have in our possession one of the alabaster pillars, a fact which won't stay secret indefinately, puts is squarely in the path of those villains. They have threatened to do us harm if we cross paths again, and our paths must needs cross.

\"This all added up to the conclusion that we have made enemies of this group, through no fault of our own, though admittedly, they might not have realized it at the time, if indeed they did not know about our pillar. When we found their scout lurking about in the Tower, much as I expected we would, I thought 'now is the time to strike, and fudge the odds in our favour a bit'. At that time, we were at full sthrength, in full numbers, and were facing off against a single adversary who we could have taken prisoner quickly and without cost to ourselves. I did not have the time to convey this plan to the rest of you, Baird included\" Kethoth puts a gentle hand on Baird's shoulder \"and so I found myself acting quickly, and without explanation. 

\"Unfortunatly, the rest of you did not see things then as I did, and the villain was allowed to escape, by the Baird granting the villain the benefit of the doubt. It is a credit to you, Baird, that you acted such as you did; your mercy and benevolence are some of the very qualities for which you were chosen for this endeavour, and your temperance and cool head are things that I will never ask you to abandon. In fact, though I was angry about your physical intervention then, I have since thought about it, and putting myself in your position, I can't but say that I would have done precisely the same thing as you.

\"However, things are different now. The Killers have escalated both their promise of violence and their numbers, and we are likely to cross paths with them again. When this happens, we need to have to present a unified front, and have a plan of action.

\"If there are any that disagree, now is the time to speak your mind...\"

Mattrick seems to be deep in thought for a moment as he chews upon his morning meal. Lifting his fork and holding it between his index finger and thumb, he slightly wags it in Kethoth's direction as if doing so allows him to better facilitate turning his thoughts into words.

\"I have a few concerns. The first being this mage, Curuvar. I have little faith in mages and although my opinion of you was not the best at first, I have bled along side of you. Despite your inclination to sling arcane energies and burn things into piles of ashes I believe you to be a man...err...being of merit. I have no knowledge of this Curuvar but I would certainly like to know if this mage has chosen to threaten you; a companion of mine and a comrade in arms.\"

Kethoth replies; \"He hasn't threatened so far. He has only informed me, and in a mannerly fashion, that he is 'investigating' me, at the request of an unnamed employer. I doubt he had to even let me know about his investigation, but rather told me as a professional courtesy. I have no reason to think Curavar a foe just now. Mercykillers, on the other hand...\"

Baird; \"We don't know where the Mercykillers are, how many pillars they have, or what they're planning on doing with them. From what I can see they are seeking to accomplish one of two things. Either they seek the Saharel Oracle like everyone else who travels here, or they are trying to stop anyone from speaking with her. While it would be safe to assume they seek the question, there may be a greater plan to remove the opportunity for any to even question the omniscient ghost.

\"Their threats of death only came after your bullheaded assault on their person. Do you know for certain their evil intent? Because I'm sure the rest of us don't. If anything they have displayed patience with our collective arrogance and nosing about. The ranger and rogue ran off to scout their camp, they were asked to leave. The dragon and yourself, while bold, foolishly exchanged threat for threat back in that broken tower.

\"While I am aware that our two groups are competing for the acquisition of those pillars, I will not engage them unless I have reason to. Which brings me to that metal skull of yours, which I'm sure is addressed to you personally. When next we do meet them we'll have to fight them and rest assured it will be a difficult fight especially with the addition of two more.

\"We are more or less evenly matched in numbers and fighting style. We have a half-elf who couldn't shoot the ground if he wanted to. They have an orc with a great bow. Your magics, Kethoth, will hopefully outmatch that woman in Eladrin attire. That scout they have could be in this very room and we would never no it, and the rest can hopefully be equally met by Luc, Mattrick, Arya and myself.

\"Rest assured, silver-tongue, their attitude toward our being here was neutral before you made the mistake of thinking you know better than the rest of us. While you may be quick to forgive I will save my judgement until after we all leave this place alive. Should any of us fall to these Mercykillers, know that your death will not be. You'll see yourself caste back into the Abyss and reborn into whatever lowly demonic lifeform best suits you. I think you refuse to acknowledge that there are consequences to rash behavior. Why don't you ask you friend with the metal jaw, I'm sure he has some stories to tell.\"

Mattrick was about to respond to Kethoth when Baird spoke up. He grimaced slightly and reached out to rest his hand against Baird's upper arm and giving the younger man a look as if to simply ask for patience. Turning to give Kethoth and the rest of the companions at the table he gives an amused look.

Mattrick says; \"Baird deals with people much as he deals with undead incursions while on the Watchwall. He likes to charge right down the gullet.\"

Pausing to give Baird a speculative and possibly warning look before picking up where he left off.

\"I have little desire to get embroiled in the intrigues of mages. If this Curuvar simply meant to inform you of his actions and nothing more...then so be it.\"

His voice taking a firmer tone.

\"We have no idea what The Mercykillers are here for or as to their reason why. I was here when Sister Charra presented the letter and the skull. She clearly said the letter was for Kethoth and the skull was for...us. Bandying words over what has transpired is womanish.\"

Pausing and giving Arya an awkward look before muttering.

\"I meant no disrespect. I simply want to state my feelings on the matter. What happened, happened. Clearly they wish to do us harm and to that, I say they need to get in line. Their bullying tactics are unwanted but I grew up in a land where children's stories about monsters under your bed are not stories. It's simply fact. I have seen what we are capable of and if we need to meet these individuals in battle then so be it. What's done is done and looking for blame only delays us from settling upon a course of action.\"

Lucretius adds; \"Do you not see what your schemes and petty hatreds have done here Kethoth? 

\"You awaken the ire of these Mercykillers before it is strategic to do so. You turn Baird, who has displayed only reason and mettle, into a near foe. And you try and make things right by lapping at his boot?

Our goal lies singularly with Lady Saharel until she speaks her truths. Your untimely grudges will wait until after that, or you will not have my sword to skulk behind.\"

Lucretius is speaking in a cold measured voice, but his anger is evident in the traces of smoke emanating between his words. 

And then the smoke thins, wafting into nothing like a snuffed candle. He turns to Baird, eyes pleading.

\"I am truly sorry if I played any part in this game with the Mercykillers. In my mind I meant only to reason with my kin in the group, but I see it was not so.

\"Growing up in Tymanther, my Grandfather told me 'all Dragonborn Sons of Aebir must stand together to honor the struggles of those not yet free of their Shackles.' And it tears at my heart to see those Dragonborn be as they are. My brothers are not my brothers in this land.

Baird, you are lucky your brother is with you, and I hope to earn your trust as he has.\"

Lucretius finishes with his own possibly warning look towards Mattrick...and Kethoth.

\"Kethoth has this to say, to answer questions of initiation;

\"1. We scouted their camp just as they scouted ours.

\"2. I offered a diplomatic solution to this problem in the base of the Broken Tower. I asked if there wasn't something we could do together, to help each other out. They refused bluntly, and in fact, at that very moment, made their first threats before I had even finished smiling. They continued to threaten as I continued to sue for peace and an alliance.

\"3. In the end, most of our company backed away, and up the stairs. I alone stood in the doorway of that chambre to watch Lucretius stride into that room, and I alone was ready to defend him if something happened, which I thought it might.

\"4. It was Lucretius, and not I, that advanced on our enemy, though I offer Luc no ill-will for it. He didn't roll over like the rest of us.

\"So let us dispense with calling this some sort of 'scheme' or 'petty hatred' of mine. I would be happiest of all if we never saw these people again. I don't want to fight them any more than the rest of you. I think we have the means to deal with them, but I don't want to spill blood over misunderstandings and paranoia. 

\"However, if they feel things must come to blows, and they must if we both continue to search these ruins, then we must present a unified front, and not divide ourselves before our breakfast is even done.

\"All I ask is this; are we all in agreement that if we cross paths with these 'Mercykillers' again, that we will defend ourselves as best we can, and as a team, as we have been doing? If not, we can head back to Loudwater presently, because we will be putting ourselves in great peril.\"

Kethoth rolls his fingertips rhythmically on the table, looking at you all for answer...

Baird responds first; \"The Mercykillers made that decision for us back at the broken tower. And as aggravated as I am by these circumstances, I will agree that if we have any hope of surviving our next encounter with them our in-fighting must be put behind us. 

\"I ask in return that you spare us your rash behaviour in the coming days, lest you bite off more than we all can chew.\"

With that said, Baird shakes his head and calms down some as he begins to eat his now cold meal. After a couple bites, he looks over to Mattrick first and then Kethoth and says, \"What of that cretin, Barthas? I for one could never outlive the shame of leaving such a soulless killer to stalk these lands.\"

Mattrick frowns at the mention of Barthas and finishes what he was chewing on before speaking.

\"Methinks it will be some time before Barthas decides to come as close to the surface as he did when last we met with him. Perhaps having an opportunity to look upon the face of Amaunator for the first time in what I would wager was quite some time will be enough to keep the fiend in it's crypt.\"

Taking a long drink of water before continuing.

\"I have little desire to leave him be, but if we happen to find the apparition of Lady Saharel, her answers might very well lead us in another direction. The motives behind our questions might be of greater import than seeking out a coward that seems to have no other wish than to be left alone in it's crypt.\"

Nestled near the end of the table, hidden past Baird's imposing frame and a heafty plate of untouched eggs lay the ranger, weary head tucked into folded arms.

Blatantly unattentive, Tyriol had not been paying much heed to the earlier conversation. Nothing was being said that the half-elf was not already aware of...afterall, that lot of mercernaries had made it transparently clear as to what would happen the next time their groups were to encounter eachother. As far as he was concerned, Tyriol was in full accordance with whatever it was Baird was saying, and expressed this approval by sliding a tired arm out from under his face and patting the paladin on the shoulder.

Groggily, he craned his face level with the meal before him.

\"Yes, less bickering you lot. We all know it's the wizard's fault that we've got ourselves a pack of ravenous mercernaries riled up at us, so what's the point in arguing about it..?\"

The half-elf picked a piece of egg off his plate and gingerly placed it on his tongue. He swallowed the morsel with a grimace and cleared his throat to speak once more. 

\"More of my concern would be that vampire, as Baird mentioned. Good rest has eluded me every night since we encountered Barthas - what we did in his lair...I doubt that withered thing will rest until he's gutted the lot of us. I'd feel much relieved if we could put him down before we leave with our questions answered. I do not want him following me home.\"

Kethoth smiles at Tyriol. \"You needn't worry about the thing stalking you more than half a night's journey, my capable ranger. These creatures are as territorial as the bobcats in the hills of the Grey Vale. It is too much effort for them to relocate. Though they can move at great speed, it is only over short distances. It would take him months to find his way to Loudwater, and only then with a host of minions to help him.\" 

Kethoth frowns as he thinks about what he has said.

\"Don't get me wrong; I can't abide a vampire. I think I might hate them more than our punchy paladin over here. Did I ever tell you about my dance with Baron Maskil...another day perhaps...But I want to do things in an orderly fashion. Tomorrow, we will seek out Saharel, and ask our questions. I have a suspicion that her answers will only come with some silly task, and I begin now to speculate in my mind about her task...\"

Kethoth trails off for a second, and pushes some bacon around his plate with a fork, then nods agreement to his own thoughts, and continues.

\"Answer me this, my stout friends; why should the Lady be so hard to get at? And let us put the patently clear answer of Thoran and his lackeys behind us. They are a recent development here.

\"There has been so much traffic in and out of the ruins the last few decades that this very monastery has grown up adjacent to it to accommodate the endless seekers. So many have gotten close enough that Brother Turnigal can fabricate the alabaster keys to her tower from memory! An amazing feat, to be sure, and one we can be thankful of.

\"No, I don't think she is difficult to find. The analects of Kuryon nearly paint a map of how to find her. But they suggest something else too, don't they?\"

Kethoth continues;

\"The very last line of Analect 2 states this exactly; 'Yet first a task I crave of thee...'

\"In that analect, Kuryon, the gentleman-adventurer who has provided us with so fine a hall in which to eat our breakfast, has met Saharel, and he asks her a question, which she, in her ghostly and round'bout way suggests she can answer, if only he does something for her.

\"In analect 7, and let us suppose that this analect falls chronologically after the second, Kuryon describes the vexing ascent to the Lady, the puzzle, and mentions the 8 pillars; 2 left standing, and 6 absent. He knows what the 6 pillars represent, and I suppose we can speculate on that, but let's do so in a moment.

\"In analect 12, we find the labouring Kuryon being watched over by the ghost as he puts each pillar back in it's place. He is not talking about putting them in the 6 empty holes though. In the final stanza, he describes a 'dusty toil' in ancient Netherese soil. This might mean several things, but what it doesn't mean is the top of a stone tower.\"

Kethoth rolls his head back a moment, and drums his fingertips on the table top and thinks.

\"Kethoth, that is me, thinks that one must put all the pillars (or perhaps only six of them) into some specific hiding place in order to have the Lady answer your question. The pillars must then be sought out by the next group of seekers who must place them in their holes, in order to ask a question, then hide them again, and so on.\"

\"Now, I am sure some of you are thinking 'Why, Kethoth, why would a ghost make such repetitive requests of the living?' and to this I respond 'It is what they do!' Have I ever told you about the month I spent at sea with the ghost of Captain Murderboard? It is a long and troubling story, but the crux of it is this; every night he asked me to do the same things for him, and he told me the same story over and over again. He did not have the wisdom of Lady Saharel, but he shares with her the spectral predisposition towards the repetition of a particular day or event.

\"The Lady may yet prove me wrong, but I suspect that we will have to acquire the pillars to ask our question, and then redistribute them in order to have it answered. The redistribution of those pillars will bring us squarely into the path of Barthas again, and so all trajectories will be realized.

\"So speculates Kethoth.\"

Reading over the Analects again, Baird recites the first paragraph of Analect 7 in a quieter tone. 

*No looter I, no thief of tombs
*For learned men have called me sage.
*And yet within these maddening rooms
*Perplexed I was by puzzle strange.

Baird continues; \"We have yet to encounter any kind of puzzle other than the stone shapes that fit into the surface of that door. He turns to Mattrick and asks, \"You saw more of these sockets on the doors of the upper floor, correct? When we stormed the upper reaches of the tower? Perhaps these are the rooms Kuryon wrote about and the reason Thoran is still up there, trying to solve the puzzles.\" 

\"This can be supported by the next few lines as follows: 'Many trials would reveal / The pattern I must master.' We have seen one of these trials, in the mindflayer room, perhaps the rooms at the top of the tower hold the other trials.\"

\"This part I can't quite grasp, 'I write these words with quiv'ring hand... / I know just what these pillars mark.' What does he mean by 'mark'? The site of Saharel? Does each pillar have some ancient Netherese representation or location?\"

\"Perhaps I am reaching for meaning here, but could there be some form of magic that will send us back into ancient Netheril? This is from the 7th Analect,

*Six holes where pillars do not dwell
*So hope remains and yet I fear
*I've seen the doom of Saharel.

Baird; \"He could be returning to the past to reclaim or place the 6 pillars and he knows he still has time, because Saharel hasn't been destroyed at the time of its writing.\"

Stopping a second to make sure everyone is following his line of thought. \"In the 12th Analect he writes, 'My path, though twisted, is assured,' as if locked by the marches of time. And once he reclaimed the stones he writes,

*I set each stone into it's place
*Casting back to ancient days.

Baird; \"Casting what back? Himself? The restored presence of the unbroken circle of stones? 

\"I don't know for sure ... but this part of the Analects speaks loudly, 'My footsteps left within the soil / Of Netheril left long alone.' Maybe I'm interpreting this too literally, but to me this is a clear indication that Kuryon at least believes he travelled backward into the past. If this is what is required to save Aglarond, then so be it.\"

Kethoth listens and nods encouragingly here and there, then adds;

\"The seventh analect is about the claw-puzzle. 'The claw within the silver seal...

The squares of alabaster...' That is clearly about the puzzle we already figured out.

\"You may yet be right about a temporal translocation. This would explain the disappearance of that elven seeker, Tyriols' friend, not long ago.

\"That part we will have to wait to find out. I think we have put together as much as we can. We have now only to test our theories and our blades.

\"So! Finish your breakfast, my friends! We have a ghost to interrogate!\"
