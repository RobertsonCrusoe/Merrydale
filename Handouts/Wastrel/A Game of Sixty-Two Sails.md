In this fateful tale, Zechary wins a treasure map in a game of sixty-two sails (a card game that pirates play). The treasure map takes the party to the mysterious “Isle of the Lich” where they must descend into an underwater tomb. In order to get to the best of the treasure the adventurers must rob the body of a greater mummy and possibly set this terror upon the world.

== The Wages of Heroism ==
After saving Freeport from the madness of the Yellow Sign, we find that Zech has moved up in Life and has changed his base of operations from Scurvytown to the Docks, where all of the best pirates hang out when not at sea. Resting on his current good fame Zech stays in a different inn or tavern every night insisting that it is the least that anyone can do for the hero of Freeport.

== Fine Companions ==

On this night we find Zech at “The Creeping Tide,” an inn and tavern that is right on the water with easy access to the sea just in case anyone needs a good dunking. Zech is seated around a table that is made out of a large ship’s wheel that looks as though it was salvaged from a ship built for Ogres or Hill Giants. At the table are five other sailors:

* Black Gum: The self-proclaimed meanest first mate from here to the Dragon Coast. He has lived with scurvy for the past twenty-one years.

* Displacer Pete: An elven rigging man from the Wavefront — an enormous man-o-war from the Waterdeep navy that has stopped in for supplies. He is a slight fellow with long limbs who will start a toast at the drop of a hat.

* Wilver Mastmin: A deeply sunburned dwarf with a thick black beard, bald head and intricate designs tattooed around his eyes. He is a ship’s carpenter and he just got off of a voyage from to the Nelanther Isles were half the crew was lost and he was sold into slavery. Luckily he had an insurance policy at Rhode’s of Freeport to buy his freedom.

* Manny Mithril: So named for his Mithril peg leg and hook. He brags that he is the only salt to survive a run-in with a dire shark and live to tell about it. Mithril is an old retired campaigner and he is living off his earnings from his adventuring days.

And,

* Seabrim Waterwing: He claims that he is a “sailor” from out “East.” By the look of him he would probably get sea sick in a puddle on Greengrass. And with a name like that he’s not fooling anyone. Luckily Sailors aren’t known for their fuss about who they sit with.

== The Game of 62 Sails ==

At the table the salts are all playing a game known as Sixty-Two Sails which is a card game using sixty-two cards, a favorite among seafaring men. The reason for this is two-fold. One, the game uses all the convoluted terminology used aboard a ship, so that the salts are easily separated from the land-lubbers. Two, the game involves betting, and no pirate is going to give up a chance to lose all of his swag so that he can go back to sea and win another fortune. The game also has a set of rules that allows for the betting of virtually anything so the sailor that is down on his luck can usually find something to play with.

A typical round goes like this: Each player is dealt between five and nine cards depending on a set of circumstances that are difficult to explain, but most sailors pick it up after observing a few hands. Everyone tosses in on opening bet simultaneously and the highest bet goes to Umberlee’s Ante, which gives that player both good luck and an additional pillage or two that can be spent in many ways throughout the round. The player with the fewest cards goes first. Ties are broken either by throwing in an additional bet or racing to see who can drain their tankard first. The first player then plays between three and two short of his hand to set the course for the round. Depending on the course and the position of each players’ compass which is a marker that each player brings to the table with them and positions when the betting opens. The compass is usually some sort of good luck charm. The positioning of the compass is very important in the game and you must look at the positions of the other players’ compasses for the best strategy. Now like I said, the play goes in order depending on the opening bet, the number of cards, and the position of each player’s compass. Each player plays a number of cards to match the first player’s cards, and then play continues. If a player does not have enough cards then he can purchase them from the remaining deck for a gold each or he can pillage them from other players if they have remaining cards. Pillaging is done a lot in this game and works with pillagers that are counters that are usually made out of finger bones. Each player has seven of them and uses them throughout the course of the round. Some say that pillaging is more difficult to understand than the rest of the game, but most Pirates love it because it gives them a chance to show off the bones of the foes that they have sacked. Now that the opening cards have been played the round begins and the game gets really complicated. Maybe I should just get on with the story...

== A Toast to Waterwing ==

As we join the game the night is almost through and most of the gold has gone. Some of it has gone into the drinks. They always seem to get more expensive the drunker you get. A lot of it has gone to Valkur’s and Umberlee’s Antes. Valkur’s Ante is optional and Valkur doesn’t ever expect a lot, but if you ante to him he will protect the game from foul magic; his ante usually takes the form of buying a lass a drink and having her sit on your lap for a round. However, Umberlee’s Ante is another story; woe to the sailor that doesn’t ante up to Umberlee. As I said before, Umberlee’s ante goes straight to the sea and is never seen again by any sane or living sailor. The rest of the gold, however, seems to be at Waterwing’s end of the table. Despite his lack of knowledge for the terminology he seems to be winning most of the hands. Most people obviously suspect him of cheating, but that is to be expected as cheating is built right in to the game of Sixty-Two Sails. Many a Pirate has retired a rich and famous man for discovering a new way to cheat at this particular game.

At this point Waterwing has won all of the gold and is scraping it off the table into a bag where it seems to disappear.

“I would love to stay fellow sailormen, but the night is almost over and it looks like I have won.” Waterwing looks as though he might be stifling a laugh, but his constant sour expression makes it hard to tell.

“Just a bleeding moment you bloody daft cow of a land-lubber!” shouts Black Gum at the top of his lung.

“A toast! A toast! A most glorious toast to the daftness of Waterwing!” sings Displacer Pete as he raises his tankard.

“To the daftness of Waterwing!” booms in Wilver raising two tankards, one in each hand.

At that the rest of the men, except Waterwing raise their cups and join in a merry chorus:

“To the daftness of Waterwing! To the daftness of Waterwing! It really is quite legendary, just thinking of it makes me merry! So I’ll raise my cup and drink it back to the daftness of Waterwing!”

What is a toast good for if you can’t insult someone in the process? But I digress. The reason for this toast involves one of the best rules of the game of sixty-two sails, and the rule is this: Once all of the gold has gone to one end of the table the game gets interesting. That is to sya, once all of the gold has gone to one end of the table the winning player must pack up all of his gold and then all of the players must start getting creative with what they bet. And everyone knows that the better the bets the better the cards that Valkur will deal you and even Umberlee’s Trumps will be favorable. Sixty-Two Sails is not a game that you can just get up and leave from - indeed, once the game reaches this point you can’t get up for any reason - and many a pirates has pissed his breeches for the fault of it. Many a sailor has also died for just this reason. The rules for ending a game are very specific. However, every sailor knows that Valkur reserves a special place for those that die in the service of the game of Sixty-Two Sails.

== Round One ==

The cards are dealt.

Black Gum throws in a platinum ring with a sapphire carved in the shape of a fish. “This ring will turn any salt water into drinkable fresh water. Will get you out of any mess.”

Pete throws in a small crystal vial. “This is one of the most potent aphrodisiacs from Evermeet. Casually mention it to one of my kind and see what happens.”

Wilver pulls out a box and opens it revealing a large brush. “This brush contains an endless supply of tar. It can be used to waterproof any ship’s bottom.”

Mithril pushes his Mithril hook onto the table. It had already been detached from his arm to make room for a tankard attachment. “This is me bloody hook Selûne dammit!”

Zech takes an amulet from around his neck. “This be a magical amulet. It will be protecting ye from sword an poison both.”

Finally, Waterwing pulls out a silver symbol of some sort. “This symbol allows me to cast spells from the gods.”

The rest of the table looks at Waterwing in puzzlement. Pete sweeps the symbol into the hole, “Well, I guess the symbol is worth the most. You get the pillage.”

Waterwing looks at Pete in disgust. “What!? That amulet was definitely worth the most at the table.”

“Oh no! All the amulet does is deflect a few swords and quite some bad grog. That symbol gives you the power of the very gods themselves. Obviously the symbol was the best choice.”

Play begins.

Wilver; Gum; Waterwing; Zech; Pillage; Pillage; Mithril; Pillage; Pillage; Pillage Again; Pete; and so on until Waterwing takes the round.

== Round Two ==

The cards are dealt.

Wilver throws in some folded up cloth. “It may not look it, but this can fit the main sail of most any ship. And when unfurls it will catch the best wind and carry your ship as fast as can be.”

Waterwing pulls out a piece of folded paper. ”This is a map to untold wealth and treasure.”

Pete throws in a handkerchief. “This is a placeholder for a date with my fair sister. Here beauty is as staggering as a ship in a sunny storm, her breath as sweet as the finest Mytul breeze, and the things she can do with her body...”

Black Gum takes a small ring off his toe. “This ring enables its wearer to swim the roughest oceans wide.”

Mithril reaches under the table and produces his Mithril peg leg. “This is me bloody leg Shar dammit!”

And Finally Zech reaches into his mouth and pulls out two gold teeth. Normally these teeth would be impossible to remove, but Valkur himself loosens any swag for the sake of the game. “I bet these teeth and the very name Zechary Mudge! With these teeth in yer maw you will be getting out of most any trouble. And with the name of Zechary Mudge ye will be the hero of Freeport. Every Inn will take ye for free. Every Tavern will give ye a bottomless tankard of the finest grog! And everyone will toast ye to the end of your days.”

At that everyone raises a toast to Zech. Mithril then slumps forward at the table and says: “I know, I know” and pushes his leg into Umberlee’s Ante. At that moment, as the leg makes a splash in the water the first ray of morning slices through the window and on to the table. (This, my friend is one of the conditions that can end a game, but not before the round is done. However in this situation all cards get thrown down and a winner is determined right away.)

Mithril plays his hand. It is a strong one: the Sailor's Swag.

Black Gum plays his hand next. It is also strong, but doesn’t beat Mithril’s: a Barrel of Fruit.

Then plays Pete: a Shanty and a Bottle of Rum. Not even close.

Waterwing - wouldn’t you know it - the Kraken. A virtually unbeatable hand and currently the leader.

Wilver doesn’t beat it with his Unsunken Ship.

And Zech? Well, Zech plays his cards one by one onto the table, his hand: the Waves of Umberlee. Nothing.

Waterwing begins to scrape in his winnings. “Ha, ha. As you can see I have won all of your swag. I do think I shall enjoy your sister. Just call me Zechary Mudge!”

The rest of the sailors all star at each other dumfounded. But then, simultaneously they all look down at the floor. “Just wait a moment there Waterwing! Look at the floor! The tide has come in and that makes the waves of Umberlee unbeatable! Ha! Zechary - the real Zechary, that is - wins after all!”

At that Waterwing slams a morning star on to the table. It sticks right in to the map that he bet. “I will not honor that bet! That isn’t a rule of this game!”

The whole bar lets out a simultaneous gasp, many of the sailors spitting over their shoulders or making signs to ward off the wrath of the gods. For every sailor knows that \"he who does not honor the bet of sixty two sails be swallowed by Umberlee herself with Valkur’s boot in his arse.\"

Waterwing looks around the room and realizes that he has lost. “Alright, Zechary Mudge! You win! But you’ll never find that treasure. It is on the Isle of the Lich. And no one knows where that is!”
