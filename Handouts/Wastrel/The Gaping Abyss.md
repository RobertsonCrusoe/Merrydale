Though Barthas is destroyed, Kethoth wears a solemn face, and for the first time since you've known him, he seems troubled.

\"If I could breathe life back into that monster myself, I'd gladly suffer him to walk this earth if it meant I could have Arya or Mattrick back. We have not won the day, here, friends. I apologize for the loss of our companions...\"

Kethoth just stares at the piles of ashes that represent both the monster and the elf that was the soul of his company. All at once, though, his eyes brighten, and he heads for his bags.

\"There is some small hope that Mattrick has not perished in that Abyss; until I see the body of my friend, I will not count him dead. And while we cannot descend there just now, we shall offer what aid we can, praying for his well being.\" 

Kethoth stuffs a pair of sacks full of provisions, ropes, sun rods, and some other gear as hastily as he can. He quickly writes a pair of notes, putting one in each bag. He ties them off, fixing a lit sun rod to each, and then ventures back into the cave, a magical light floating ahead of him.

He goes to the edge of the Abyss, and whispers to himself, if anyone \"There may yet be hope for you, and I will give you every chance I can, friend. I shall not call out, because if you live, there is no point attracting the denizens of the Dark Wastes to you. Kelemvor keep you...\" and he tosses in the two bags, each on it's own slightly different course.

Leaving the cave a moment later, he explains \"The notes were in his native Damarran tongue; they say that we have triumphed over Barthas, but that we must tend to the welfare of the many before the few; we will not be back for him soon, if at all...\" Kethoth says the last words with flagging conviction. Clearly, he is troubled at the fate of his friend.

Much as the previous sun rod that had been tossed down the abyss, the two sacks fall into the darkness until all that can be seen is the small light coming from the sun rods attached to them. The lights grow smaller and smaller; more and more faint until finally the darkness is all that waits within the abyss once more.
