== A Year of Adventure ==
It has been said that there are only two seasons in Freeport: summer and hurricane season. With hurricane season fast approaching you will have spent nearly a year in this city. A year you say? How could time have gone so fast? Yes my ever faithful friends—when a year’s worth of events have passed then surely a year must pass too. But if you are still unconvinced let us look back on this year and see what our dear heros have accomplished.

== Diamonds in the Rough ==
thumb|300px|The Nefarious Captain Scarbelly
Just at the beginning of summer it was when our heros entered Freeport harbor, a lowly bunch of deck scoundrels with barely enough salt in ‘em to spit. It was these meek qualities that surely made them easy prey for the press gang that took interest. But the spark that we call hero was there even at that tender time and life aboard a slave ship was not our characters’ fate that day.

Then there was the time that Salazar and Zech attempted to raid the Bloody Vengeance and failed. This lackluster encounter nearly resulted in Zech’s doom at the sword of the orc pirate Scarbelly, but a chance encounter with a skeleton by the name of Smiling Jack propped Zech back on the trail of adventure. Brave Salazar decided to run away to fight another day, but forgot that one’s location is not safe when you leave a friend to the machinations of a wizard. And this wizard did come into Salazar’s story, burning all of his possessions. It seems though that Salazar was little matter to this wizard named Kenzel for we never heard from him again. Well, there is one lesson that should have been learned from this encounter: Don’t do your pirating at port.

Swagfest, remember that? Remember Lendin winning the competition of One Eyed Jack’s Last Stand in an amazing display of fisticuffs? Remember that jolly salt that ran the event? You don’t? Why it was captain Lydon, and I do believe that our heroes saved his life from an assassination attempt. Who would have known back then that Captain Lydon would become a good and long lasting friend of our adventurers?

== The Isle of the Lich ==
thumb|300px|The Blasphemous Tomb
And it was Captain Lydon that brought the adventurers to Parakeet Island and then to the Isle of the Lich to hunt down the Bloody Vengeance for a bloody revenge. That butchering of Scarbelly and his crew proved to be a success. However, there was a piece of unassuming treasure brought back that was missed. That piece of treasure was filled with magic most potent. Oh well, easy come easy go.

That ship of Lydon’s would bring our adventurers back to the Isle of the Lich on a treasure hunt following a map won by dear Zech in an epic game of Sixty-Two Sails — a treasure hunt leading down into the depths of a foul and alien tomb. What unimaginable scenes were displayed on the reliefs of the main tomb hall—depictions of a repulsive insect race melding into a slave race of man. What evil sorcery could have conjured such an abomination? Surely that blasphemous magic came from some place a billion years ago and a billion miles away. Well, the hunt proved fruitful as four golden statues were hauled up from the tomb before it sank to the depths of the sea. And any horrific curses that our adventures may have caught were easily done away with at the local temple before they could manifest themselves, or were they?

== Heroes of Freeport ==
right|thumb|300px|City of Adventure

But what else did our heroes accomplish this year? These deeds may be modestly mighty, but surely not enough to earn the title known as hero. Oh yes, there was that deed of saving Freeport and even Toril itself from the creeping terror known as the Yellow Sign. How that adventure turned from a simple investigation of a lost cleric to an epic battle atop the spire of the wondrous lighthouse now known as Milton’s Folly is truly a tale for the bards. And I suppose that this deed, with the others will allow me to use the word hero with some credibility.

So let’s reflect on where we are now. Our heroes have gained some ability—indeed they are a match for many a man and monster in many a fight. Our heroes have gained some gold — indeed they could easily retire now and live as rich men for the rest of their lives.
Not bad for almost a year in the city of Freeport. However, look around you: the city doesn’t seem as big as it once seemed on that day you first stepped on its port, does it now? The people walking around the streets are not as mysterious anymore, nor are they as intimidating. Being the flavor of the month known as the “Heroes of Freeport” has started to wear off, indeed thanks in part to Salazar’s, um “colorful” disposition. The heroes have been “trimmed” of their seat on the Captain’s Council, a feat that Lydon assures you he will get back at the Council for. But council seat aside being the “Heroes of Freeport” does not seem like such a great thing anymore. It seems as though in the scope of nearly one year you have outgrown this city.

So where do we go from here? More power and fame, more gold? Possibly, but somehow those motives don’t seem to befit the life of a true adventurer — a side effect maybe, but not a motive. However, it is not my job to give our heroes motives, for I am just a humble narrator and that is not my job. However, I will be watching with much anticipation to see where things lead for our heroes. So, without further ado let’s set the scene for this adventure.

== Raidfest Explained ==

With the hurricane season fast approaching the window of opportunity to leave the seas around Freeport is beginning to close. Missing this opportunity means that one must remain cooped up in the city when it is at its most disgusting worst: when it is boring.
What does one need to leave Freeport? Why a boat of course. Being a city of sailors there are usually plenty of boats around, one can usually buy a decent boat if they shop around for a month or two. Being a city of pirates people usually don’t plan that far in advanced, living off the cuff as that is the only way to truly live at all.

To this end the city has come up with a solution: Raidfest. Raidifest is Freeport’s annual festival that commemorates the end of Freeport’s sailing season where all of the pirates sail off to safer seas to raid other ships in other lands. It is also a festival about getting the pirates ready to sail by making it convenient for them to shop for anything and everything that they may need and is a great way for merchants to unload their merchandise before their market drops out dramatically.

This three-day festival begins with Freeport’s largest auction of the year. In this auction mostly large merchandise is sold such as ships, ship accessories, and magic items. Since the merchandise is usually plunder from some raid or another the sky is the limit as to what one can find at this auction. Suffice it to say it is usually interesting.

The next two days see general merrymaking marketing in the streets, followed by a ceremony on the evening of the third day to see all of the ships away. The ceremony also carries blessings from both Valker and Umberlee to see the ships sail safely to the waters beyond.
