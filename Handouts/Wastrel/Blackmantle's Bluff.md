== Whispers on the Wind ==

It all started somewhere on the open Sea of Swords. Somewhere between Baldur’s Gate and Waterdeep, there was a whispering on the Wind, or so Brenaen would say. At those sullen moments at dusk and at dawn, the Windwalker could be seen riding the bow of the ‘‘Wastrel’’, leaning out as if to replace the bowsprit with his nose, and his long moustache blowing in the wind. One evening, after a couple of mornings and evenings of this peculiar behaviour, he ran back to the poop deck, and up to the wheel, where the Captain himself was steering the ship.

“We have to turn about, Captain” said the Windwalker, in such a tone that conveyed not a command, but a fact. The stern Captain sized up his crew member and friend, and made the only reply that he could to such a statement.

“Yar!”

Days later, the ‘‘Wastrel’’ was back where it last departed; docked in Baldur’s Gate. A quick provisioning was once again carried out in this port by the Master, while the rest of the crew went about the City to find what entertainment they could. But it was not entertainment that some sought. After two days in port, both Mae and the Captain came back to the ship with news.

On the second night in port, the Captain called the entire crew to the Ready Room, in the rear castle of the ship. All gathered around the great Sarlin Table to hear what the news was. It was May that spoke first.

“There is a great work happening on the lake of Steam. I would go there to see what the news is, and assess the situation.”

“What the cryptic little jade hints at” Zech offers, when all faces seem blank “Is that there is some sort of work on the Lake of Steam that is supposed to change the Realms, for good or ill. We make for the north shore of the Lake, if all hands are agreed.”

There is a short and non-illuminating conversation, followed by a show of hands. All save Kethoth show their hands in favour of travel to that destination, but even he does not make any argument against going.

The next morning, the ‘‘Wastrel’’ heads out of the harbour at Baldur’s Gate, and heads south. It is past Harvestide, and a fine time to sail south, by all accounts. The ‘‘Wastrel’’ needs little time to make Calimport, heading south with the Trades this time of year. Indeed, less than a tenday finds her back in the sheltered Port of Calim, and the crew are once again abroad in the City looking for news.

That first night finds the crew assembled in a private dining room in a fine inn called the Tuugan’s Head, where sailor’s mouths find something more palatable than the dried pork and hard tack that the Master seems to favour.

“Yar!” is the expected salutation of the Captain. “The Master here, and me, see, we been sniffing about. And we turned up a thing or two. I had to purposefully loose a few hands in order to get some old saltes to talk” he says, and Salazar, the most seditious Master on any boat on this coast, gives him an incredulous look.

“Yar! There is indeed something happening at a place called Blackmantle's Port. It’s a place neither me nor the Master here ever been to, or heard of, or can even find on a chart, and by Umberlee’s Cunny...”

“You need to look a little further than those lines that separate land from water, my able and inscrutable Captain” is the venomous interjection of the Ship’s Navigator and Cartographer, Kethoth. “There is a very important piece of land here” he says, indicating on the main charts, which he has brought with him. “This narrowing in the land is all that separates the Sea of Fallen Stars, by way of the Nagaflow, from the Lake of Steam, and the West Coast. There is a rocky bluff there called by many names over many ages, but today it is known as ‘Blackmantle’s Bluff’, for the self-important man-lord of that region. There are two miles of stone there that keep the Nagaflow from the Lake of Steam. I think I know what is that is going on there, but I shall spare you all my speculations.”

“Yar! Sounds like ye know yer maps, me Abyssal Cartographer! Good job!”

“I know a thing or two about the nearest City” says the usually-quiet Lendin. “Innarlith is as wicked a place as they come. The laws there are known only to the rulers, but they are many, and easy to run afoul of, and quickly enforced. It was there that I was ‘enlisted in the navy’ and became a crewman aboard the Shylock. I know there are Red Wizards in that City.”

“And there will be Red Wizards where we travel as well” says Mae. “The latest is that a new town has sprung up where Kethoth has indicated, and a great canal is being dug. I aim to investigate this, and to see what dominion the Red Wizards hold over such valuable Real Estate.”

“I met a wonderful girl this afternoon” offers Cedric with a wry smile “who knows other men of the Sea that are bound for this new Port. I think things will be lively there.”

“Yar! Then we must make haste towards this new Port. We leave before first light this night. Don’t fall asleep in the greasy sheets of whatever whores you might find tonight, lads, and pardon me, Miss. Make your way back aboard ships. The Master’ll be chomping at the bit, I suppose, and no doubt a late boarder will be a lubber!”

A few hours later, the ‘‘Wastrel’’ is indeed ready to make sail again, and all hands wait above decks, surveying the array of activities that happen in the wee hours of this busy port City. But they are not interested in the goings-on of the coopers and joiners of this place. They are searching the gloom for their last crewmember, who, expectantly, is late. Salazar is livid, and just as he is untethering the ‘‘Wastrel’’ from the docks, there is a cry of from the deck.

“Hold that line, Master” cries out Brenaen. “Our ‘‘Wastrel’’ returns from his wastrelry, to the ‘‘Wastrel’’ once more!”

“Yar. He sees ‘im sure, Whiskers. He’s just putting to, so’s our man put’s to, and put’s them broads to, too. Har har!”

Sure enough, Cedric comes rolling up the dark dock, wreathed in smoke, a shapely wench on each arm, and a smile from ear to ear. Just out of earshot, he dismisses the ladies, and then hustles the rest of the way to the boat, just in time to make the gang. The ‘‘Wastrel’’ begins to float away as Salazar takes all the lines in. As she drifts out, the gang is brought in by the Captain, and the Master makes an unnatural leap aboard, carrying the lines.

Most of the tired faces retire below decks, seeing their friend safely aboard. The Captain is having a good laugh with the vagrant sorcerer over his most recent conquests, but the Master is still red in the face, and he works at storing the lines, then striking the sails, with no humour.

At length, the Master joins the two laughing revellers aboard the poop deck. The Captain has given Cedric the wheel, and both are now wreathed in smoke, Cedric burning one of his cigarettes and the Captain his pipe. Salazar stares Cedric in the face, and offers a short statement.

“You were late. Again.”

In response, Cedric shrugs his shoulders, offers his palms to the sky and makes a short verbal retort of a single syllable, and then offers a pull off of his cigarette. The surly Master takes in the smoke deeply, and seems peaceful of a sudden.

“Forgiven” says the Master, and things are under way.

== Across the Shining Sea ==

The trip across the Shining Sea is a fast one, and the ‘‘Wastrel’’ overtakes a good many ships heading east as well. The wind is from the north, but the clever rigging of the ‘‘Wastrel’’ captures every knot out of those winds, and she makes five knots all the way from the Port of Calim to the Strait of Storms. This is a distance of some 600 miles, and she makes it in under seven days. The weather for the travel couldn’t be better; a warm wind keeps the sails full, and the weather is warm enough that most of the men aren’t wearing much more than leggings.

Towards the end of those seven days, Captain and Master are heard discussing the approach to the Strait of Storms.

“Yar! She’s the most turbulent bit of water on the face of the globe, Master Salazar! When last I passed her, I was headed the other direction, and we still had to wait for the wind. Too much one direction and too little the other, and you can swirl about in those eddies ‘til your hull rots!”

“This boat needs little wind, Zech. I say we just charge the Straight. Leto Cook’s ship will not falter nor founder. And I don’t mean nothing about the celestial promise in some old log book; I mean wind and canvas, Captain. Mast and rigging! She’d fly right up the Dessarin all the way to Arabel, I’d wager. Let’s just run’er!”

“Yar!”

A day later, the discussion about the Strait of Storms was understood by the crew. As the ‘‘Wastrel’’ once again drew close to land, a veritable armada of ships was found, dead in the water, nearby the mouth of the Straight. Ships of all varieties and with flags of all colours are found waiting. Zech explains that the Strait that leads from the Shining Sea to the Lake of Steam is very shallow, and turbulent, owing to the fact that the Lake lets into the Sea, and pours over the shallows. For 40 miles, the waves are so violent and destructive that the Strait of Storms also has such names as the Strait of Talos, Umberlee's Pass, and the Strait of Broken Beams.

It is known by all sailors who have made the pass and lived to tell of it, that to go from the Sea to the Lake, you need as much wind behind you as possible. The violent waves and current will attempt to wash any ship back out into the Sea, or onto the bleak rocky shoals that enclose the straight. It is likely the most treacherous and challenging bit of water known to sailors of any sea, in all of Faerûn. And it is the first real naval challenge that the crew of the ‘‘Wastrel’’ faces.

== Running the Strait of Storms ==

The ‘‘Wastrel’’ is running proud as she plows through the warm waters of the Shining Sea, and navigates quickly and with great agility through the throng of ships waiting for better wind. Many men wave from other ships, and many are spotted shaking their heads and giving incredulous stares towards the ‘‘Wastrel’’. She glides up the approach to the Straight with no resistance. The wind is from the North, so the Captain takes her on the North East tack, starting close to the western Shoal, and cutting an angular slice through the Straight. As soon as she is in the Straight itself, and both the east and west shores are visible, the waters begin to live up to their name. As soon as both shores are visible, the waves coming down from the Lake begin to rise and show their foamy heads.

The ‘‘Wastrel’’ slams into the first of the waves with a tremendous boom, and many are the crew aboard her who begin to doubt that even Leto Cook’s ship will hold up against such a pounding. And this at the first, and smallest of the waves!

But, any old Salte will tell you that the worst moments of any storm are the first few. Once your legs and your guts get used to being tossed around like so much chattel and grain, and you begin to trust to your beams and sails again, things are fine. But in those first few minutes, as the ship rolls up and down, and lists nearly to the wheel, all but the saltiest doubt that there can be survival in such madness. And on this day, as the ‘‘Wastrel’’ plows violently into the head of the Straight and the impossibly huge and seemingly random waves, there is doubt enough to go around aboard the ship. The Captain is at the wheel, and he bellows and laughs in the face of it, but many can detect a little worry even in his jocularity. The Master is quiet, and does his work amongst the ceaseless crashing and violent spray of the waves, and the chaotic rolling of the ship, without any of the defiant jest found on the lips of the Captain. Lendin helps with the rigging, and Brenaen plies his muscle where it is needed, but the other hands stay below decks; the waves threaten to wash any but the stout and strongest overboard, and the winds themselves, funnelled down the Straight as they are, are enough to carry off a man if he is unwary.

For 14 long hours, these 4 men work the rigging and sails, and drive the ‘‘Wastrel’’ through the maw of the turbulent straight. Though the shoals to either side are covered in the remains of dozens of ships that attempted this same passage, these four are not daunted. After the initial shock of hitting such terrible and murderous waters after such a calm journey, they steel themselves against the waters and the winds, and remind themselves once again that they are sailors, they wanderers, they are adventurers, and
fear has no place in their hearts.

== The Lake of Steam ==

In the grey dawn of the next day, the ‘‘Wastrel’’ emerges from the Strait of Storms into the warm, calm waters of the Lake of Steam. There are two things that the first time visitor will never forget about the Lake of Steam, on the first morning they are there. The first is the fog for which the Lake takes her name, and the second is the smell and taste of that fog, for it is bitter and sharp, the profound smell and taste of sulphur.

“This ‘Lake’” explains Kethoth, “is actually no such thing. This is a large, shallow, saltwater bay of the Shining Sea. It’s waters you will notice have a yellow tinge, and the fog that it produces on cool mornings have the taste of sulphur. This is because the Lake sits atop scores of small but active volcanoes, and there is a ceaseless supply of sulphur and other volcanic minerals deposited into the Lake year round.

“Though the waters of this Lake are poisonous to us, even more so than regular salt water, they are fertile enough for the scores of things that live in them. The minerals feed an abundance of plant life both in the water and on the shores. These plants feed small animals, which feed progressively larger animals, as you can appreciate. In fact, I am told that the largest of sharks live in these waters, which, ironically, are the shallowest of the salt waters known to sailors. The deepest sounding ever taken from these waters was scarcely over two hundreds in feet, yet the largest shark taken, about half that measure. We are in an amazing part of the world, gentlemen and lady!”

It is indeed an amazing slice of Faerûn. For the most part, the weather has been sunny and warm. It is breezy on the Lake, and there is no humidity to speak of. The place seems the Nest of Paradise. When the sun is bright, the bottom of the lake is usually visible through the yellow waters. At many points, the waters are scarcely more than 20 feet deep, and thousands of varieties of fish, plants and all manner of ocean creatures can be seen. Schools of bright orange fish over 2 yards long, thousands strong, follow the ‘‘Wastrel’’ one day, leaving her wake a turbulent playground for the jumping and diving fish.

== Umberlee's Handmaiden ==

But there is some worry in the Captain’s face at the presence of such a school, and his worries are soon realized. Running in about 100 feet of water, with the school all about the ship, a great shadow is seen beneath the boat, running from her port, then underneath, then out from starboard. The shadow is larger than the ‘‘Wastrel’’, and no mistake. And it glides menacingly under the water twice as fast as the ‘‘Wastrel’’ can sail in fair wind. But as it turns out, it is only the large orange fish the monster is interested in. The turbulent waters behind the ship become a bloody, chaotic roil, as a shark over 80 feet long goes into a feeding frenzy the delivers fright to all but Kethoth, who seems, somehow, to delight in the spectacle.

Small islands in the Lake are plenty, and the charts are not accurate at all. Some islands have vanished, and new ones seem to crop up, unexpected. Kethoth explains that this is due to volcanic activity. The many islands that are passed are as green and verdant as any you have seen. The growth is thick and covers all but the highest and blackest peaks of the rugged cliffs of the volcanic islands. Flocks of colourful birds fly from one island to the next, and huge migrations of turtles and other water faring creatures are seen with even greater frequency among the green isles.

When mainland is spotted, the same volcanic cliffs, covered in voracious life are often present, usually with the whitest of sandy beaches. Off in the distance, rolling green hills can be seen. The vegetation is not large, for the most part. Some trees are palm-like species that stand no taller than 50 feet. Everywhere, there is abundant life in a pleasing variety of colours, shapes and sizes, and the weather is a constant warm breeziness.

== The Folk of the Arnrock ==

Halfway through the journey, a huge mountain is espied to the North. It is a few thousand feet high, and has a flat top. The mountain and island are completely covered in the bright greens of this tropical paradise, and smoke from many small fires rises up from the beach, the mountain side, and from the mountain top itself. With the spyglass, little huts can be seen on the beaches of this mountain island, and little bronze skinned halflings are at work and play close by. At one point, a group of long canoes with outriggers on each side, and carrying 15-20 of these nearly naked, little bronze halflings row out to the ‘‘Wastrel’’. They are covered in little bits of bronze jewellery of a local manufacture, much of it set with pearls of green, lavender and blue.

It isn’t long before scores of the nimble little halflings are aboard ship and crowding the crew. They give many of the more threatening looking crew members a wide berth, but they close in around such charismatic ones as Mae, Zeke and Cedric, attempting to trade their bits of jewellery and fresh fruits for any number of things.

At length, Zech has May tell them to go ashore, and that a trading party will be sent to deal with them. They are brushed off the deck, with a little less tenderness than they deserve by the likes of Kethoth.

“Yar! Proper little pirates, aren’t they? Well, we’ve made good enough speed the last couple days. There’s no reason why a few crew couldn’t go ashore for a few hours, re-supply and maybe do a bit of trading. I need some volunteers to fill the casks!”

== The Port of Blackmantle ==
thumb|450px
After the brief pause to visit with the Arnfolk, the ‘‘Wastrel’’ is off again. Well supplied with fresh water, meat, fruits and vegetables, the rest of the journey is not only pleasant of wind and weather and surveyance, but also of provender.

It is about another 500 miles from the Arnrock to Blackmantle's Port, and from here on, the going is slow. The volcanic islands are more numerous on the east end of the Lake, and many of them peak right beneath the waves, making for treacherous sailing. Sailing at night is out of the question, and even under the broad light of the sun, a fast pace is too dangerous. Further adding to the length and complication of the trip is the fact that the course must meander like the strings of islands, and a straight path becomes impossible to follow. The one improvement to the journey in this leg is that many nights are spent ashore tiny little islands that are full of game and fruit and fresh clean water, and the tedium of the trip is duly broken in this tropical paradise.

On this leg of the journey, the ‘‘Wastrel’’ continues to overtake more and more vessels. Many are the local boats which do a brisk business in trade contained to the lake and its surroundings; these are cogs similar to those found in the northern waters, with a single mast and lots of oars. But many larger vessels, ocean crossing vessels are also found among the many ships all heading for the new Port of Blackmantle.

As you draw nearer the Port, the boats are thicker and thicker, until, as you reach the new Port, there are boats everywhere. There are easily more than one hundred boats laying off port, sheltered in a large cove formed by old volcanic rock. There is room for only a few vessels at the few large docks at the new settlement, and boats are unloading there quickly, and putting back out to moor.

The settlement itself is clearly new. There are almost no permanent structures there at all, but several large ones being built presently. A few large warehouse-looking structures have already been erected, and there are, of course, hundreds of tents of all sizes choking the little river-valley that the settlement is built in.

From the water, three great canals can be seen. They are all about a mile apart, with the closest being about a ½ mile from the settlement itself. It is the middle of the day, and it is clear to see that two of the canals are being worked by hundreds of labourers, with the third seemingly abandoned.

The Port of Blackmantle itself is alive with an intense bustle; there are wagons, horses and men filling up the impromptu streets, and you can tell, even from your great distance away, that there is brisk trade happening here already.

There is one other structure of note that is hard to miss. Perched high atop a rocky bluff is a fortress that seems to be brand new. The rock itself is not covered by the voracious growth common to the rest of the region, and the flags wagging proudly in the wind say exactly what you have expected; the Red Wizards of Thay have already entrenched themselves here in the Port of Blackmantle.
