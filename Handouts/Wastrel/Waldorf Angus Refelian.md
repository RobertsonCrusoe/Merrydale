---------------------------------------

The modern story of the Refelians begins in the youth of its patriarch, Waldorf. As a young lad, Waldorf was appreticed to a silversmith in Trades Ward, whose name and reputation has long since been forgotten. It was through this trade that Waldorf first made his name in the city, and eventually, the connections that would make his fortune. A skilled and proficient smith, Waldorf specialised in creating fine goblets and dishes for the elites of the city. As the years passed, he, along with all the other human silversmiths in the city, began to face stiff competition in his craft from the dwarven immigrants settling in Waterdeep seeking their own fortunes. The competition was not only a matter of skill (for Waldorf was equally, if not more skilled, than his dwarven peers) but of cost. The dwarven smiths were being supplied their silver at a lower price by the Clans of Mirabar, while most human smiths had to settle for the much higher prices of Amnian-imported Maztican silver. Thus could the dwarves lure Waldorf's customers away with promise of lower prices.

It was at this time that Waldorf made the first, only, and yet, most significant journey of his life. He set off to the north from Waterdeep, determined to negotiate with the Clans, not only for supply of the metal, but for an equal price as that being given to the dwarves.

Waldorf's negotiations with the Chieftains of the Clans of Mirabar were short. The Chiefs would not relent in their support of their dwarven bretheren by allowing the Privilege of the Deep to be extended to an topsider. Waldorf, had seemingly made the trip for naught.

It was at this time that he heard tell that the son of one of the Chieftains, a young dwarf named Grim Fitzhammer, was to be married in but a week's time. Upon hearing this information, he set upon a plan. He purchased a measure of silver, and set about crafting a pair of matrimonial goblets for the bride and groom. He believed that he would be able to sway the Chiefs with his skill and art, where his words had failed.

Indeed, when the gift was presented to the Fitzhammer Chief's son, it is said that all the dwarves present were suitably impressed. Never before had they seen such mastery in the medium of silver outside their own race. Waldorf was hailed as a true artist. However, this was not enough to sway the Chiefs in their decision. As was pointed out to Waldorf, thousands of years of tradition cannot be swept aside by a pair of fancy goblets. But, the Chiefs were prepared to offer Waldorf supplies of tin at a rate just above that charged to fellow dwarves, as a token of good faith. Realising that accepting their offer of tin might be a stepping stone to supplies of inexpensive Mirabarian silver in future years, Waldorf graciously accepted, and set off for his return to Waterdeep. And with this, the Refelian fortune was born.

It is said that when Waldorf crested Sunset Hill, he had a magnificent vantage of the entire city, sprawled before him to the south. And it is said that at that moment, he came upon the idea that would make his empire: Waterdeep was a city of nearly a million people, and all of them had to eat and drink.

Within the week, High Road Metal Works was born. Within a year, the company was supplying cheap, yet durable plates, tankards, goblets, and cutlery to every tavern, inn, bar, festhall, guild house and caravan house south of Julthoon Street and Traders Way. All of it made of tin, and later, pewter. By the end of the second year, the company had consumed its adjoining buildings, sprawling into one of the largest factory and warehouse compounds in Trades Ward. Within 5 years of its inception, High Road tin goods had found their way into the packs of many adventurers and soldiers on the Sword Coast. Their inexpensive plates were being used in taverns as far south as Athkatla, and as far north as Luskan. And, perhaps to Waldorf, most importantly, High Road tin had found its way to many of the homes of the regular people of Waterdeep.

Although, to date, Waldorf has yet to receive a lower price for silver from the Chiefs of Mirabar, his association with them, particularly with Chief Grim Fitzhammer, has been one of the most enduring and profitable in the city's mercantile history. Not only did the dwarves provide the tin, but many a young Fitzhammer dwarf seeking his fortune has been sent to Waldorf's factory in Waterdeep. In return, Waldorf has always personally taken it upon himself to look after the welfare of the Mirabar dwarves who arrive in Waterdeep, has never turned away a Fitzhammer in search of work, and has been an outspoken defender of the rights of dwarves within the City of Splendour.
