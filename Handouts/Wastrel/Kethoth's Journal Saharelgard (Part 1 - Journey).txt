==4 Mirtul, 1479 DR - The Ranks are Joined==

It has been a few days of searching, but I now have a full roster of seemingly capable ‘adventurers’ to assist me in the fairy tale errand of the Shapeless Mayor.

The first two to join my ranks were a pair of Aglarondans, and I should say that I would never have taken them, if it wasn’t for the very fact of their origin. I have a soft spot for Aglarond, for a number of reasons. First of all, my friend [[Cedric]] is from there, and I hoped to get some news of his family from these two human Aglarondans, but they knew nothing of the Blackchalicess. Aglarond is also positioned in a strategically crucial spot, relative to my home and Thay, and so the welfare of Aglarond is always in the back of my mind.

But let me say a thing or two about this pair. They are [[Baird of Kelemvor]], and Brother [[Mattrick Lychaeus]], also of Kelemvor. Baird is a Knight of Kelemvor, and it is against my better judgement to have a fanatic in my midst, but he was so enthusiastic about going, and seemed young and impressionable enough that I grudgingly agreed. His friend, Mattrick, is also of the faith of Kelemvor, being a sanctified priest. I should say that I presently have no quarrel with Kelemvor, though I should think that all the unceremonious crimes against corpses I have inflicted over the years might not endear me to the Lord of Funerals. At any rate, the two Aglarondans seem like a stout and capable pair of humans, and I am sure they will return on the [[Mayor]]’s investment. 

[[Lucretius]] the dragonborn is one of the two of that noble race that I met in the Tankard the other night. He seems every bit the natural born leader of fighting men (or whatever), though he is quiet in times of peace. I wouldn’t have it otherwise. He is a fearsome creature of such a size that I think, pound for pound, the Mayor’s money is well spent.

[[Ender]]. One can never undertake a nebulous and scrutable adventure without having a thief in his back pocket, and so like the carpenter brings along a burdensome tool which he hopes he won’t need, I too have brought along this little thief. Ender is a Halfling, and given to the lawless and roaming lifestyle that so many of his people cherish. I don’t like to perpetuate notions of racial proclivity for this bad behaviour or that, given especially because I am of an oft-maligned and misunderstood race of people who can act in any way, good or bad, but this short fellow looks as though he emerged directly from the pages of a school-boy’s pulp novel. I will give weight to common racist wisdom, however, and assume this creature a fit little thief. 

[[Tyriol Grimbold]] is our scout. I should never have taken this boy along, and I know I will regret his company, not for the sake of the pup himself, but for his dour-faced mother, [[Maeghuen]]. Maeghuen is a scout of no small accomplishment, and a significant personality in this insignificant town, and she hates me intensely. I recall the first time I met her and her son a month ago. Maeghuen is constantly giving reports of wilderness affairs to the Mayor, who quite foolishly disregards Maeghuen’s capable counsel. Maeghuen knows her business, if the [[Lady Moonfire]] does not. As it happens, she came to the manor home of the Mayor a month ago with her son in tow, and gave a troubling report about activity that was perilously close to the walls of town. In her usual remissive manner of leadership, the Lord Mayor smiled and thanked Maeghuen for her valuable service, and promptly changed the subject to that of the scout’s hair and shoes, and the upcoming soiree later in the week. 

[[Maeghuen]] is no fool. She knows that the Lord Mayor does an insufficient job of putting the scout’s intelligence to work for the town, and I don’t doubt that Maeghuen goes through great pains and efforts to do what must be done, despite the Mayor. In fact, I told Grimbold just this a month ago, after the meeting to which she brought her half-elven boy, Tyriol. She retorted to my panegyric by asking me bitterly and contemptuously what I knew of either scouting or mayoral duties. She had already decided that she didn’t trust me, and rightly so I suppose. However, I said to her then that I knew enough of scouting to know that it doesn’t involve bedding the first randy elf that crosses a whore’s path, and bringing a bastard son into the world as a constant reminder of a lack of commitment to one’s duty. That, I suspect, was the final doom for any relationship we may ever have enjoyed. My lingering anxiety about this woman is that we may have crossed paths during my wit’s absence as the bandit Kossur, but if we did, she is keeping that card close to her chest.

So today, Tyriol has been paid to be our scout, and I can only hope that we get away from town before his mother finds out.

The lucky seventh in my merry little band is my favourite. Arya. [[Aryaluystrinne Miritar]]. If I needed any further evidence that Set still guides my fate in his serpentine way, Arya is it. This lovely creature, a shapely and taught gold elf, hasn’t been in Toril for more than a week, having just recently absconded from her comfortable court life in the fey, to seek adventure here in this crude realm. She approached Loudwater like a rabbit approaches an emerald patch of aromatic clover; hungry and interested, but slowly and cautiously. I had the good fortune of being embroiled with some of the potato-brained ‘guards’ at the gate over some small matter, when we all noticed her approaching. The guards went immediately and awkwardly to arms, not knowing what to expect, but Kethoth knows the difference between predator and prey in an instant, and I extended her salutations. It was not long before I had her before the Lord Mayor, whom I knew would take to her immediately.

Arya is a capable-seeming blade-mage of remarkable talent, and a full-sensory pleasure to be in proximity to. She is adapting quickly to the crude society of these Heartlands, and though I know she has come seeking the both the fortunes and hardships of adventure, I suspect that she appreciates the comforts that Lady Moonfire has to offer.

Now, here is the interesting thing, and a thing I have kept secret from Arya. Her family name is known to me, by way of her remarkable uncle, [[Seiveril]]. If I have the dates correct, I met her uncle in 1373 DR, as he was organizing an historically peerless assault on Myth Drannor. It was [[Maevri]] the Witch that knew Seiveril, as she had been sent by the Simbul to give him assistance, and she dragged us with her. I’m glad she did. I met in those days the greatest assembly of elven heroes that had been mustered in over 3000 years, and it was a marked honour to offer what little assistance was ours to give. Seiveril was a natural leader and I understand that his efforts resulted in the retaking of that most salient landmark in the Realms, though he did not live to see the fruit of his labour. It is a credit to Arya that her uncle was such an elf as he was, and I shall keep her close to me because of it.

So these six are my companions on this errand of fancy for the skinny Mayor. I was reluctant to tell Moonfire that I had assembled what I thought was an adequate team for her job, because I knew it would make her so excited and anxious that her lust would grow and need slaking, and I would be forced into my phallic duties, and I was right. In fact, as I write this she is sleeping next to me, and though she is as pleased as you may, if I never penetrate so gaunt and bony a vixen again, it will be too soon.

To get back on track, I should note now that we seven first met as a team at supper time tonight at the Green Tankard, where I paid them out a stone each, and treated them to food and some drink. I told Ender to find out anything he could about our objective, guessing correctly that the halfling was as gregarious as he was diminutive. Tyriol too I gave a task; to find out anything he could of the road ahead of us, and the best path to take to get to Saharelgard. 

I told my little band that we would leave in two mornings, but that we would meet for breakfast at the Tankard tomorrow morning.

I took the humans and the lizard to help me procure some horses, and this is when I learned that we are not alone in our easterly pursuits. There are few enough horses available in this economically insignificant bywater, and the Dwarven brothers that run the stables here had the bad sense to sell off the whole lot to a group of ‘serious-looking’ villains in black armour. Set’s Blood! It will be a long walk if I can’t think of something by morning. I could easily creep off into the night and come back with some horses, and perhaps some farmer’s virginal daughter, but I had better start playing things pretty straight from here on in. My present company is not comprised entirely of dimwits. 

We shall see what tomorrow brings. For now, I have the displeasure of a waking lover, who is presently rolling over and tugging at my shirt tails. Set grant me strength…

==5 Mirtul - The Mysterious Obelisk==

As I collect my thoughts tonight, and record them herein, I am seated at the desk of the Lady Moonfire, and I can see from this window, across the courtyard and in through the coachhouse window, the straw-covered cargo of our recently procured conveyance illuminated by a wan and pale sliver of moon. 

Who would have guessed that I would end the day with a Netherese obelisk in my possession?

But let me start at the beginning.

In the morning our company met for breakfast at the Tankard, and I was happy to note that five out of six of them seemed completely sober and well rested; exactly as I suspected. If the investigations that I sent my little bumblebees to carry out uncovered anything, they can’t recall a relevant detail, so we are heading out largely uniformed. 

I was determined to fetch some horses for our merry band, and canvassed the town once again first thing in the morning. Tyriol suggested going to Nistral’s farm to see if the old man had some horses to offer us, and we decided to check in with Nistral’s daughter, Megana, who is the Loudwater smith. Megana falls quite short of the ‘farmer’s daughter’ ideal; she is neither comely or charming, but she finds Baird pleasing enough. She took to the Paladin almost immediately, likely because he was the only one of us who was both taller than her, and not of reptilian ancestry. I was about to make a whispered suggestion to Baird that he might be able to satisfy both our need for horse and her need for a ride, but held my tongue; you may as well ask a horse to write you a treatise on celestial movements as ask a Paladin to accomplish any task with some means other than brute violence or pietistical vociferations. At any rate, she suggested that her dad might be willing to rent us a couple of horses and a wagon, and presently we were off to see to it.

Nistrils farm is a half day away from Loudwater, and we got there in good time. However, we arrived at a quiet and suspicious scene; the stead and outbuildings of the farm had doors ajar, and there was no sign of Nistril or his workers. 

I sent off our scout and thief to investigate things, and instructed Baird to keep an eye to our left where the open door of a large shed waved ominously in the wind on squeaky hinges. Baird must have thought that I told him to keep a boot on the shed, though, as he made for it. Most of the party sensed some danger, and they fanned out a little bit as our reconnaissance element approached the stead. I put a hand on Arya’s arm and whispered for her to stay close.

As our scouts came close to the stead, they were indeed ambushed by a pair of ettercaps, and a pair of creatures we would later learn to be called ‘dark creepers’; an unimaginative name to be sure. This was our first challenge as a team, and though we had a couple of stumbles, we overcame our opponents with none of our party slain. An auspicious occasion certainly!

A quick search of the farm revealed the mutilated corpse of Nistril in the barn. Our servants of Kelemvor saw to the fit and proper burial of the deceased, and we had also to free up the two horses we had come seeking from the encapsulating webs of the ettercaps. The beasts were terrified nearly beyond reason, but Tyriol was able to calm them and to hook them up to the huge lorry that Nistril kept.

We also found a Netherese obelisk in the barn. 

The thing evidently had been unearthed very recently, and seemingly nearby. There was no doubt a story to be uncovered with but a bit of detective work, but frankly, we lacked the time to put it all together. In hindsight, I think we should have gone out looking for the two farmhands that Nistril was known to keep, but by all accounts they were a pair of vagrants who as likely got Nistril into the early grave he now rests in as not.

I will admit that I find it a bit coincidental that we are headed to a Netherese ruin, and on a side-errand to a remote farm, we turn up some monsters, a dead famer, and a Netherese artifact. However, farms are always ‘growing rocks’, or so I’m told, and perhaps it is no unusual circumstance for a farm in valley that once held a Netherese garrison to uncover the odd old carved-up stone, but I am leaning towards ruling out coincidence. 

To be safe, we are bringing the obelisk with us, though we have told not a soul, least of all Lady Moonfire.

==6 Mirtul - Departure for the Grey Vale==

Today was mostly uneventful. We were stopped at the gates when we set out. The guards there insisted that Captain Harrowleaf wanted to talk to us before we left, but I told the guards that we were too small and unimportant to take up much of Harrowleaf’s time, but that if he were close by and would not be delayed more than ten minutes of his inestimably valuable time, that we would wait. He could not be produced in the allotted time, and I pressed us onwards.

Tyriol says it is two days to Llorkh. He’s worth every penny so far. I could walk to Llorkh backwards and blindfolded, and do it in a day and half, but I won’t let on about the extent of my travels in the Grey Vale. What he does offer is that it is eight days beyond Llorkh to Saharelgard, and he seems to know a couple ways to get there, though only one offers expedient conveyance for an obelisk-laden wagon.

==7 Mirtul - A Mother's Love==

We made good time today, and forced our march a little to make up time to catch the Waterdhavians up a little.

A meeting that I was hoping to avoid happened today. I guess I should never have planned on making off with the son of a seasoned scout and hope not to be found. At noon hour today, we were met by Maeghuen and some of her scouts some distance from Llorkh.  

The scout pup offered his mom a guilty ‘hello’, but she made directly for me, reminding me that I was to stay away from her boy. 

“Your son is a capable ranger, my good woman, and he is on official business for the Lord Mayor Moonfire. You should be proud of the boy!”

“I don’t trust you, Sepret, and I don’t approve of you making off with my boy on your idiotic errand for…her!”

“Your deliberate and protracted detour from your usual business and duty is certainly a testimony to your peerless maternal instincts and concerns, Miss Grimbold. I applaud your unilateral and solitary commitment to your child, my good scout, and I assure you that I shall see to his safety, though surely, his capable aim will see more to mine, and I am thankful of it!” 

“You return him unharmed and intact, Sepret, or we will have words...”

Yes, yes. Why the humans of the Heartlands can’t simply say ‘do this or that or I will disembowel you and roast your children alive’ rather than politely saying ‘we’ll have words’ is completely beyond me, though I have learned to speak in their terms. Truth be told, of all the members of this band that I worry for, Tyriol is not one. He is in his home element, and the only thing we have to fear for the pup is that his voice will change mid-battle in some day to come, and we will all put ourselves at risk, prostrating ourselves in front of our enemies in laughter.

While I made a game of calling Grimbold a slut and an inadequate and single mother, Lucretius ([[Luc]]) broached the subject of the five riders in black and azure raiment who had purchased all the horses in Loudwater. Maeghuen blanched at the mention of the riders, and told us to steer clear of them. She suggested that they worked for a powerful man in Waterdeep, and that they were not to be trifled with.

Bearing in mind that small-minded locals are always frightened by determined looking men from ‘the city’, especially when they wear black and say very little, I dismissed Maeghuen’s warnings. I was smiling at her and giving her a look up and down as I might at a remarkable breed of dog, or perhaps a fine painting, but I caught something in her eye that told me she was being something rather more than a simple and paranoid local. Maeghuen knows her business. While looking her up and down I noticed the fine line of her thigh and calves, and thought it might be fun to spawn a little brother or sister for young Tyriol. I’ll make that coupling my last night in Loudwater if I am lucky and patient enough.

We parted company with Grimbold, and I couldn’t resist adding that if she was quite through delivering a bag lunch for her baby boy, that we had dragons to slay and treasures to claim. I know I’ll never be this woman’s friend, and damned if I can’t make a game of it, even when I shouldn’t, which is in the presence of finer creatures such as Arya.

Anyway, we kept along the road to Llorkh, and a couple miles from the nefarious settlement, encountered some outriders. I was delighted to notice the face of Gavrin, a decidedly corrupt and self-serving villain who masquerades as a road warden. He had a few others of his sort in tow, and though the thought of killing the four of them for their horses crossed my mind, I quickly ruled it out. There is no use killing for anything less than six horses, and anyway, I’ve a pair of zealots in tow who wouldn’t approve of the spontaneous slaughter of these figures of ‘law and order’. 

I decided to split the difference and bribe them for safe passage through Llorkh and back again, whenever that might be, and secured the deal with one of the precious stones that Moonfire had pursed. This was probably something more than six or seven times the bribe he was expecting, but I wanted no trouble in Llorkh, and I knew that there would be more than just a quick run through the town proper before we were ready to leave.

Indeed, we stretched Gavrin’s patience thin with various stops and errands in the town, looking for horses and information, and found an equal supply of both. I did manage to make an arrangement with the dodgiest stablemaster this side of Zhentil keep for the sale of any horses that we might chance upon in our travels. A crook named Ephram will buy any serviceable mount for 100g/head, and of course, no questions asked. 

Tonight we are free and clear of Llorkh, and any semblance of civilization. The next walls that we can expect to see will be those of the Monastery of the Precipice, on the edge of the ruins of Saharelgard. Tonight we sleep in the open, in the lee of the looming and ominous Grey Peake Mountains.

==11 Mirtul - The Borders of Netheril Restored ==

The road has treated us well. We came through Weathercote Woods and The Smear (a malodorous bog) in one piece and largely unhindered. The whole time we did our best to catch up with the Waterdhavians whose only slight against us thus far has been to buy up all of the horses in our path, and to head in the same direction as us. All the same, I had intended to catch them up when we could, to at least compare notes with the lot of them, or perhaps to murder them outright, make masks of their faces, wine of their blood, and burden my purse with their coin. Well, tonight we have caught them, but I don’t think we’ll murder them just yet.

But let me back up a bit.

The narrow pass through the Grey Peakes was serviceable enough, and our passage there through was unhindered, though we marched through a dozen murderous ambush spots. If I were a bandit (again), that pass would pay out, I don’t doubt. Anyway, on the other side of the pass is the Smear, and it was here that we first encountered any interesting inhabitants. We were watched by bog-dwelling creatures for much of the day, and during the night, just on the edge of the Wood and Smear, we camped and were set upon by kobolds.

Now, we took care of them quite handily, and I should suggest that Kethoth had much to do with this. I was able to illuminate our attackers so that our fighters could engage them, and I managed to burn quite a few to death. However, the fire used in the fight became something of an issue. Between the kobolds hurling pots of fire at us, and my raining it down on them, we started a pretty decent fire that we watched burn behind us for two days. I have been given numerous reprimands by various elements in my cadre, though nothing approaching a serious confrontation; Tyriol is the chief instigator of these grumblings, and no surprise; I might have burned his father to death in that kobold-scorching conflagration behind us. But Baird and Mattrick also seem put off by the whole thing.

I was going to explain to them yesterday that while on a ‘nature survey’ of Chult (many long years ago now), I saw the locals there burn swaths of forest in order to cultivate. I was going to explain that fire is just part of the life cycle of a forest, and I would be half right. But I would be half jabbing them all in the ribs too, and I don’t know if they would take that well just now.

Anyway, after our trek through Weathercote, on a very good trail, we came to the Blasted Lands, which I will confess presently that I enjoy immensely. Now, it is not that I am a fiend of Hell, who only likes things bleak and ominous. No; the climate here is nice; it is warm and dry like home, and there are no flies. There isn’t much of anything alive, which suits me just fine.

And the landscape is dotted with relics of many varieties. We passed by a very conspicuous Dwarven mausoleum, which I would very much liked to have broken into and plundered, or at least investigated. It’s markedly difficult to get a pair of zealots of Kelemvor to agree to such an undertaking, profitable or entertaining though it might be. It has occurred to me more than once over the centuries that the types of endeavours I would most like to undertake are those that the company I tend to keep are pre-eminently occupied in stopping. “Why don’t you just hang around with evil folk that would like to put villages to the torch, and dig up every grave in 100 miles, then, Kethoth?” you ask? I have struggled with this, but I think the short answer is that I tend to pick the winners, and I will keep it that way until the world is such a terrible place that my kind run the show.

Anyway, we passed by some inverted obelisks that were clearly Netherese in origin. This had the dual-effect of giving us a sense that we were in another world of sorts, and that we were nearing our objective. We will soon be in Saharelgard, in the very midst of what remains of an ancient Netherese ruin.

I will have to make some speculations at this point, for lack of proper intelligence. The world has changed, and I am only just waking up to this fact. I know that the Spellplague wrought a good many disasters, but it also made some constructive changes. I have heard, and heard only, mark it, that Netheril is again come to Faerun, and that she is expanding her borders. I can attest to one thing at least; 100 years ago I would have been standing in the desert. In fact, I was within 50 miles of this very road, and there was sand as far as the eye could see back then. The sands have now retreated, and they have opened up these previously sand-shrouded ruins of Netheril for folk of my kind and inclinations, but the Netherese too are returned just as the sand has retreated. I suspect that they, like the Kossurian, would like nothing more than to take back some of what was theirs. I am forced to wonder if we won’t encounter some agents of Netheril, or if, indeed, we haven’t already. 

I say this because the obelisks in the distance seem to hold great weight in the minds of my companions, Kethoth included. Most of them suggested keeping well away from them. “Not on the way, so why bother?” was what the Son of Bahamut counselled, and pragmatically so. Still, I think these things scare some of my company. Not Tyriol, though. He was all for investigating them, and it was this very fact that made me side with the dour lizard, and choose the straighter path. On the way back we should investigate them, if time permits.

In the wee hours this morning, we caught up to the Waterdhavians. We packed up our camp, and crested a rise and spied them about a quarter mile ahead. I didn’t doubt for a second that if we saw them, that they certainly saw us. Our reconnaissance has left much to be desired on this journey, and I shall have to instruct mother Grimbold in the proper rearing of children and the teaching of a trade. I don’t doubt that happy [[Mae]] would have saw those guys a week before the rest of us, and hopefully a little while before they saw us, but I think it was the lovely Arya who first saw them and alerted the rest of us to their presence across the valley, and for all her delicious qualities and skill at-arms, the lovely thing goes about with her head in the clouds much of the time.

Anyway, one must go through the motions. I instructed Ender and Tyriol to go up and gather some intelligence for us. “Just sneak up there, count heads of both men and beasts, and see how they are armed, and such” I told them. “Whatever you do, do not engage them” I clearly instructed. I also told them to expect some counter-recon, which they did indeed find, or were found by, rather.

The two ‘scouts’ managed to get to and from our quarry’s camp unkilled, though quite shaken up over the encounter they had with the scout that they found half way there. Ender tried to put on a stalwart face when he came back, and was questioned about their scout. However, if he made a partway decent attempt at lying to us about how squarely and manly were his dealings with the scout, Tyriol blew it. He was visibly shaken, and had no compunctions about telling us he was straight away terrified by the man, who did threaten them off.

Again, some minds are easy to convince into fear, and Tyriol exemplifies this. Tonight I am sitting here wondering if it might not be best just to continue to exploit this trait in the pup, or if I would get more return on the bony mayor’s coin if I instilled a little belligerence and confidence in the boy. I can’t have him running away from these fellows if it comes to a fight, just because he’s more afraid of them than Kethoth. I shall have to ruminate upon it.

At any rate, our Urbane counterparts took off at great pace ahead of us, and put some distance between our two groups, and we have not spied them the rest of the day. Tomorrow we shall be in Saharelgard, and I don’t doubt we’ll run into them there, for good or for (hopefully) ill.

I had an unpleasant conversation with the dragonborn tonight. He asked me a very good question about why I wasn’t as dismayed about what has happened to Faerun in the last 100 years. I wanted to say, just to yank at his chain and spit in his eye in the most polite way possible that “now that the world is full of your kind, noble Son of Bahamut, how could I possibly complain?” But I can’t tip my hand. I hate his people, and will someday head back home to slay a quarter of them, and enslave the rest, but until then I will say nothing of them.

I did give him an honest enough answer though, which I don’t regret. The world is still dangerous no matter what happens to it, and I think this is as fine a time as any to live, especially for folk such as ourselves that choose to live by the blade, so to speak. However, he didn’t buy it, and dismissed me with a plaintive insult which I am not entirely sure he didn’t mean.

==12 Mirtul - The Monastery of the Precipice==

I have just come in from watching the nocturnal activities of the ruins of Saharelgard as best I could from the Monastery. This is a lively place at night; I watched several conflicts in the distance, and heard the sound of battles throughout the night. We shall have a lively day tomorrow…

Anyway, there is much to record, so I shall begin with some facts, recorded in no particular order, other than that in which I recall them.

*We arrived at the monastery at about six bells of the evening tonight.

*We have not seen the Waterdhavians, which we now know are called the Free Company of the Crescent (what an interesting name; I suspect at least one of them was a slave or other low-born sort; only those kinds of creatures feel the need to advertise their freedom). They are lead by a man (I think) named Devlin.

*The Monastery of the Precipice is a newer structure that was built by Kuryon about 50 years ago. He too was seeking the Lady Saharel, and it is his analects which both Baird and I have come here with. 

*The Monastery is run by a small sect of Oghma. I should not have been surprised that a monastery catering to people seeking knowledge should be committed to the librarian of the spheres.

*The senior monk is a man named Allendy. He is quite agreeable, and neither too interested or too aloof in his business dealings with guests.

*There are a only a couple other monks here, one of which is named Chara; a young human female who catches my eye in the right light. She is also quite professional in her dealings here, and has treated us all well. The other is a man named Turnigal, who seems to be in charge of running the place. It was he who saw to our lodgings and took our coin and such. I have no reason to dislike the man, but I do think that he has more understanding of commerce than of more academic matters that Oghma might endorse.

*The monastery appears to have been built new, but out of the remains of the old ruins. This isn’t surprising really; there are dozens of cities in Faerun just like this, but given the stark contrast between the blasted ruins and the cozy, friendly, happy keep, it seems that some of the people coming here to seek out the Lady have taken to using the stones of the ruins to build their own strange constructs. I don’t quite understand what they are on about…

*This place manages to turn a profit through the sale of beer and whiskey that some dwarven brothers distil in the basement here. They have gone missing, though, and I have not yet had time to figure out how to use this to my economic advantage, but I will. 

*There is something of a rogue’s gallery dwelling in the taproom of the Monastery. 
*There is a female halfling named Milek, who seemed imminently to swindle Ender, though he might get a night’s warm bed out of it. 
*There is an aged man named Yannick who is something of a curmudgeon. He has been here for some time, and has not seemingly met the Lady or any other success yet.
*There is a dwarf whose name escapes me just now. It’s probably Dour Ironbeard or something like that.
*There is a despondent tiefling who I immediately attempted to befriend named Rallio Devore here. He is a sad case, and I think I’ve seen his kind before. He’s one of those self-loathing half-devils who go about this world asking why he has to be the way he is, or I miss my mark. I don’t doubt that he is here trying to find some familial absolution via the wisdom of the Lady.

*We heard a good many rumours, but the two most interesting include a mention of wererats in the ruins, and the tale of an elven woman named Hannah who apparently found the Lady last month, but then disappeared. I speculate, based upon this rumour, and also upon the analects, that she did indeed find the Lady, but that she was forced to carry out some errand of the Lady, perhaps including some sort of magical journey. She could just easily have slipped away in the night too. This journey and this place are giving me childish and fantastic ideas…

*The Monastery is very comfortable, especially after a long journey. There is a library here that Baird and Arya made use of, uncovering something of the history of this very Monastery, and Kuryon himself. Now that I think of it, in the morning we shall have to see if there is a more complete collection of his analects found therein. 

To sum up my feelings about the Monastery, I should say that it is comfortable and intriguing. This place lives on the very edge of a battle ground, and it is only through the intervention of a mythal that it is allowed to do so in relative peace. I like the Monastery, and though I think the monks are doing their best to gouge the many travelers to this ruin, I can’t fault them for it. People are always going to seek out places like Spellgard, and there are hundreds of them all over the Realms. I just hope that we are not here because of a very clever rumour that they have spread throughout the Grey Vale. That’s what I would have done…

But I digress. Tonight I am going to sleep in a soft bed, under a dry, warm roof, on the very edge of a Netherese ruin, if I can in fact sleep at all, for the excitement of tomorrow’s adventure.
