==A Pirate's Progress==
Captain Zechary Mudge is known, at least in the streets and shanties of Waterdeep’s Dock Ward, as the elder brother of the widow Zsarah Mudge, and uncle to her son, the late Tomas Quoyle. To be sure, 'tis true that he was a brother-soul to the poor widow, and acted in some small way as an guardian to young Tomas; after all, was it not Zeke who got the boy started with the Phull Family, apprenticing him with a captain who sailed the Calishite waters of the near south? Sure, Tomas’ destiny in this land was played out ere Zechary would see him again, and his grief at the young fool’s doom spoke of a kinship that few ever questioned, but the truth of the matter is less certain than 'twould seem, and ‘tis likely that it shall never be moreso.

For not even Zeke himself can separate fact from phantom, for the memories of his life and labours are as murky as Deepwater Harbour, and twice as deep. Wherever the winds take him – in every fishing village or smuggler’s den, every island colony or naval outpost, every pier in every harbour in every port  – names come to his lips, and faces to his memory, and none call him stranger. In each such place, it seems, he is canny to a few stray strands of lore, of some salted wisdom which locals know to be common sense, but which are yet strange or fantastic to the foreign mind. Yet to Zeke they are not so. Have his sea travels taken him there before? Or has he perhaps once known a sea dog who hailed from that place ... or was it a woman? Or perhaps ... Yet, any attempts to chart the waters of how and why or who or what are soon lost in a tangle of conflicting and confused tales whose dates and details have been mixed as seawater with rum, and whose effect is just as befuddling.

‘In any case, speak ye not of riddles, me boys, lest ye speak the Riddle of the Sea!’

== The tale is told ==

The old sea dog arrived in Waterdeep in a beaten dinghy, blown into the harbour during the Great Gale of the Year of the Banner – the worst storm in the memory of that great Western city which men call the City of Splendours. Alone in the dilapidated boat, he was one half drowned, and one half drunk, dead to the world and naked as a newborn babe. His clothing and possessions, he later claimed, had been stripped clean away by the lusty wrath of the Bitch Queen herself. At the time, however, he could not name from where he had sailed, nor how long he had been at sea. His report, made to the Office of the Port Watch, was such a dreadfully incomprehensible brew of tall tales and unbelievable claims about how he had been personally responsible for calming the storm, or causing it, or both, that he was taken for a madman and the question of his identity given up. When he had sobered and dried, and been roughly clothed, he was thrown to the streets of Dock Ward with a few pieces of silver in his pocket – a charitable recompense for the scrap salvaged from his sorry craft. The rotten dinghy itself had no identifying markings, though it was sure enough unlike any built by the men of the Western Coast. One old stevedore claimed that the rigging was reminiscent of a style practiced in Yaulazna, far to the south in the Bay of Pirates on the Great Sea, but few took heed of his musings. Not even Valkur the Mighty could have sailed such a sorry vessel from so far a port.

So came Zechary to Waterdeep, and not for the first time, he was sure. The smells and sounds of the harbour were much like those of any other port city, but when he turned his mind to plotting a course, he found that he knew exactly where to find the grungiest gambling houses, the scummiest taverns, and the scurviest wenches. Perhaps inevitably, however, his luck at the first precluded the latter two, for within the half of an hour, his few silvers were gone, as were the old boots and hat he’d nicked from the Watch house. Thereupon he wandered for some hours before his hunger drove him to seek sustenance for his wasted belly.

Now, Zeke was enough of a vagrant to know what he was looking for – the poor man finds the widow’s table as a rich man finds the landlord’s – and it took only a few inquiries before he found himself knocking on the door of the late Thom Quoyle, lost at sea two months previous to the sorrow of wife and boy. He could not say what fortunate wind blew him to that door, but when the widow opened the door, she gave a cry of recognition for which he was quite unprepared. Was he not her old brother, returned after an absence of thirty years? For his own part, Zeke had his doubts. True, he was a good deal older than the woman, but their resemblance began and ended with their pasty skin. Yet as he listened, her tale of how he had gone his own way when she was barely a girl took on at least the guise of truth. Sure enough, his name was Zechary, and sure enough she did recognize him. In the end, truth or not, Zeke knew an opportunity when he saw one, and so he obliged his ‘sister’ by staying on to do odd jobs around the tumbledown home. He soon took her boy Tomas under his scruffy wing, and on the lad’s fourteenth birthday, set him up with the crew of Captain Elgar Phull, who sailed for the sardine fields off Calimshan.

During that year, Zeke made some coin as a lubber, putting his carpentry skills to work by making coffins for the corpses turned up each week around Dock Ward by the Watch. There was violence in Waterdeep in those days, and business was brisk, if ill-rewarded. Before long, though, the wily old sea dog had found a way to make things a bit more lucrative. The corpses of the Ward were delivered to the City of the Dead in nailed coffins which were closed with the Seal of the Watch, there to lie forever in the City's great tombs. From a spell spent in a Luskan jail with a wizard's apprentice, and his knowledge of the character of many a ship’s surgeon, Zeke knew that there was money to be made in corpses, so long as they were fresh. Before long he and a partner in the Carters and Coachmen had worked out a quiet arrangement for their cargo to holiday at the Waterdeep School of Anatomy and Medicine before moving on to their final destination. The resurrectionist business being much more profitable than mere undertaking, he was soon able to set himself up with a serviceable craft to replace his old dinghy. This, too, he put to use, discreetly ferrying cargo to and from Skullport for those who, for various bureaucratic reasons, found it more convenient to handle their transactions out of sight of the already overworked port authorities. Such men needed the services of an inconspicuous soul, and among the miscreants and sinners of Skullport, a fellow like Zeke would find it hard to stand out were his very life to depend on it.

Alas, the tide that surges needs must ebb in time, and Zeke’s luck turned in the Year of the Gauntlet, when a pair of bounty hunters tracked him down at the Thirsty Throat Tavern. The pair – a one-legged Lantanese gnome with a face covered in burn scars and a false eye of obsidian glass, and a heavily tattooed and even more heavily muscled cyclops dressed in richly tailored robes of the fashion favoured by the merchant princes of Calimshan – approached the old pirate at a game of Sixty-Two Sails. Much to Zeke's chagrin and protest (he was winning for once), the larger of the pair reached across the table and yanked the old rogue from his chair, to the bewilderment and surprise of the other players. His voluble protestations, however, were cut as short as the card game by the cyclops’ oversize grip, which wrapped around his mouth and most of his jaw besides. “Our apologies,” smirked the gnome to the other gamblers as he threw a platinum piece on the table. “It’s just that we have business with your colleague. I trust this will cover his next round?” Looking at each other hesitantly, Zeke’s erstwhile sail mates slowly nodded, shrugged their agreement to the diminutive stranger, then turned away, calling for the barman, and dealing a new hand.

Zeke Mudge don’t much care where he keelhauls the barnacle-blasted squid-jiggers, be it the Realms or some uncharted shore in a backwater crystal sphere beyond the phlogiston, so long as there’s booty and beauties ta be had.  Be prepared to hear the salty tale of Zeke’s young life as a legendary ladykillin’ freebooter.  The Whip of the Sea, they called ‘im, long before his simpleton nephew Tomas ever laid eyes on a bottle.  ‘Twas aboard a spelljamming dhow out of the Rock of Bral that he was level-drained before making his way back to Faerûn’s western shore to meet the Red Herrings.  But that’s another story…  And far in the future… (or the past?)
