==17 Mirtul Continued==
 
===Unlikely Company===

I should note at this point that we found two more pieces of the alabaster puzzle key among the hobgoblins that we had just slain in the base of the Sceptre Tower, and that [[Tyriol Grimbold|Tyriol]], who rarely shows his value, was able to return a bit of investment by way of notifying us that the hobgoblins had markings of the Red Hand of Doom, a tribe not local to this area. It seems that Saharelgard draws more than just heroes like us from remote parts.

We needed some time to recover from our protracted escape from the dungeons beneath Saharelgard, but we weren’t out of hot water yet, I surmised. I was right, but I shall come to that in due course.

Once we had taken enough of a break to see to our now-multitudinous wounds (the [[Ender|halfling]] and fair Aryaluystrinne will have more than a few interesting scars to remember this day by), I convinced the team that it was time to move. There was a brief discussion about holing up in the basement of the Tower, but I was determined to keep going. We had stirred up a nest of hornets that included not only a Vampire Lord, but a host of humanoids who might have comrades in the many warrens we left behind us, unexplored. And anyway, I knew we were close to making either an escape, or a visit with our objective. 

We continued up the next flight of steps then, and had a humorous confrontation with a nest of hedge wizards. They were on guard in two rooms to either side of a hall that we emerged from on the next level of the tower, and though I attempted to fool them into granting us admittance to the next region of the tower, I failed horribly. I couldn’t have chosen a better time to blunder and sputter on my attempted trickery; the four mages that came to oppose us made a very feeble effort to stop us, and before any of us were even excited about this ‘battle’, our opponents were begging for mercy, which we granted.

The four mages, whose names were [[Dalagio]], [[Tebyn]], [[Grimsby]] and [[Lokus]], had a very defensible position, and though they were incapable of keeping us out, I figured that this was as good a place as we were likely to find to stop for a rest, and so we held the four hostage, and camped out in their quarters. 

Those four tricksters had little in the way of arcane talent, but were rich in information for us. We stayed with them for about eight or ten hours, and managed to learn the following facts from them;

*they were hired for 5 gold/month by a fellow named [[Thoran]]
*they have been here about 2-3 months
*Thoran has been here almost a year
*Thoran is searching for the 8 alabaster pillars, of which he may have 5
*Thoran is not working with the [[The Mercykillers|Mercy Killers]]
*The Vampire in the basement is named [[Barthas]], and he has an uneasy peace with Thoran and his minions, though we have now disturbed that peace
*There are more pieces to the alabaster puzzle, and the parts, when properly assembled, will open two doors; one below that leads to a treasure chamber, and one above, which leads to [[Lady Saharel]]
*The hedge wizards had more pieces of the puzzle, but it looks as though we need more

After spending some little time with these pathetic men, we were able to buy off their services cheaply. In fact, we got them at the best possible price in the end... but let me not get ahead of myself. We offered to pay them 100 gold to guard our rest in their apartments and then to lead us out of the Tower, at which time they would be free of us, and us, them. They attempted to negotiate for a place in our cadre, hoping to fight with us to the top of the Tower, and they had the gall to demand the second question of the Lady Saharel, but I would no more grant them any question, much less the second, than I would give a rat a precious bit from my plate, and so I bluntly refused them. In the end they didn’t quite keep up their part of the bargain, but they were properly paid.

I should say that many of us were happy to find that the hedge wizards had beds in their apartments, my lovely Eladrin not the least. I, however, fell asleep sitting in a chair, much like my father used to do. It was a couple hours after we had settled in, and some of my henchmen were having amiable, quiet conversations with the hedge wizards, the whispers from the corner of the room slowly became muted by the sound of my own breath which I could hear inside my head. My head started to bow down, and I was acutely aware of the movements of my chest and my pulse, which was slowing down, and starting to sing to me, in its low comforting voice. Each breath came fuller and slower, and my cheeks warmed, my head swam, and the world slowly drifted into a dream. Perhaps I was just tired beyond reckoning, but I think I could hear Set himself singing me to sleep, and despite being trapped in a tower somewhere between an angry Vampire Lord and a small army of monsters, I was able to rest fully.

After our much needed rest in their comfortable chambers, the hedge wizards did in fact lead us to the main entrance of the Tower, which was a floor above us. They deactivated a silly magical curtain which gave us access beyond their chambers, and up we went, into a room full of bugbears, ettercaps and dark creepers. The room was the main entry hall, and the light of day could be seen through the cracks of the great doors that they guarded. There large room also had a warning bell and a rope, and it was this objective towards which most of my fighters scrambled.

I should say that we absolutely dominated the battle. With everyone well rested, and with a few extra spells being tossed into the fray by our new hired help, we weren’t long in the dispatching of most of the creatures in the room. I had warned my cadre before this battle that we needed to save some of our energy for a possible confrontation with Barthas, and sure enough, as we neared slaughtering the entire room, Barthas came up the steps from which we had come, with a small host of undead constructs with him.

=== Round Two with Barthas ===

I will say this about Barthas; he is damned lucky to not be sunbathing on the steps of the Sceptre Tower this very moment, but he is also a cagey creature that knows when he’s beat, and he doesn’t let pride govern him, which explains his longevity. We were well prepared for Barthas this time, and though he seemed to have learned a lesson or two about how to deal with us, and though our four hired-hedge-wizards fled at the first sight of him, he was no match for us. Our Faithful of Kelemvor filled that room so full of radiant energy that I myself was uncomfortable, and the undead minions that came with him didn’t last more than 10 seconds in the fray.

The most desperate moments of the battle came right at the end; we were a little taxed from the fight with the door wardens, and now a lengthy fight with a Vampire Lord who had vengeance in his cold, unbeating heart had challenged us somewhat, but Barthas too had taken his lumps from our bruisers. We then conceived of a plan to drag him by any means we could muster, into the morning light which was just now rising above the hills to the east, and was creeping into the massive doorway through which the wizards had fled. The bruisers tried to man-handle him into the light, and the Eladrin did her usual tricks of translocation, and they brought him to the verge of that light. In fact, light finally fell across his forearm in the desperate struggle, and it sizzled and gave off a vapour, and I saw Barthas scowl, though he didn’t cry out, and he again played his trump card, and turned to mist, which we were unable to catch or destroy.

If [[Baird of Kelemvor|Baird]] was mad the first time Barthas did this, he was positively livid and exasperated at his second escape. My hounds gave him chase as best they could, and Baird chased the Vampire long past the point of sense and safety, but he could not be caught. He came back furious, and I stayed out of his way for a time, while I looked at the dead forms of two of the mages on the steps of the Tower; they had been killed by the defenders on the ramparts during their flight, the fools, and I made myself busy yanking their corpses back into the safety of the entry chamber to be looted, while my holy chums and their supporters spouted curses and oaths.

At this point, we had a decision to make, and I ruminated aloud on the subject to invite input from the hired help.

“We are in the Tower, our quarry, the Lady Saharel is somewhere above us, no doubt guarded by creatures that know we are here; an alarm sounded whilst we battled with Barthas. There is sunlight and freedom before us, and our warm and safe beds in the monastery not far beyond. Which route to choose...?”

It was Baird who first chimed in, and I’m not surprised. “Hardly worth thinking about, tiefling; let us head up those stairs and find Saharel, and be done with this!”

“Indeed, those stairs resemble precisely the description in the analects” I agreed. “And we are here now, and might have a difficult time gaining entry again if turn to the safety of the monastery just now. You might be right, Paladin.”

He wasn’t. We made a short-lived sally up the steps, where one level up we were forced to beat a retreat in the face of a prepared and numerous foe in a room that was just too difficult to breach. In fact, we barely made it out of there with our lovely [[Arya Nightweaver|Arya]], but we did in the end retreat back to the kitchens behind the main entry room, and though I tried to call out Thoran to deal with him, or at least have some sort of parlay, he could not be summoned. I shall have to remember to use some magic next time to speak Netherese to him.

We made a half-hearted attempt at a siege, stoking a great fire at the base of the steps which sent fire and smoke into our foes above, and though the sky was soon streaking with inky smoke from our vandalism, which reached the very heights of the Sceptre Tower and found some egress at the top, our foes found clean air and respite somewhere, and they did not give in.

We thoroughly sapped the front doors so that they could not be used again, and we set fire to nearly everything in the kitchens and much of the furniture on the lower levels, but we had the foresight to preserve two large, thick tables which we used as shields for our final escape from the Tower. The pulpy bodies of the mages that tried the entrance earlier were all the reminder we needed that the upper ramparts were well manned, but their missiles could not penetrate the excellent shields we had found, and before long we found ourselves returning to the monastery, our ragged company giving away our tales of heroism by the many wounds, tattered clothing and armour, and damaged gear which we bore from the place.

=== Rest ===

Returning to the monastery we were met with some strange looks and given some small praise when people learned it was us who had put the Sceptre Tower to smoke. The old monk of the house, [[Allendy]], gave us a nod, saying he had never seen that sort of thing before, and Brother [[Turnigal]] seemed overtly impressed at our return, though I didn’t wonder if was a bit unhappy to see us, the owners of much property left in the monastery that he would have become the new owner of.

Sister [[Chara]] had a pair of messages to deliver, one to us, and one to me.

The ‘message’ that was intended for our happy cadre was a clean halfling skull with a metal mandible. Chara described the men that delivered this thing; a colourful pair, certainly, including one tattooed giant kin, and one fellow who is described as possibly spellscarred. These two men, she was told, are new recruits or reinforcements to the Mercy Killers, whose name we heard for the first time upon our arrival. The message seems clear enough to me, but I shall have to have a discussion with the troops about our policy on dealing with these villains as soon as the chance presents itself.

The other note was for me alone, and it was from [[Curavar]]. This note is troubling, and it seems that some of the anxiety I have harboured about my days as the Bandit Kossur were well founded. This note was addressed to ‘Chadrazzan Kossur’, which is troubling. The flamboyant mage of Loudwater indicated that he had been hired by an unnamed party to investigate me, and he certainly knows about the baseness and wickedness of my days in the Grey Vale. However, he offered a professional courtesy to me, offering to keep my secrets safe, if only I keep on the straight and narrow path that I am attempting to now walk down.

I think what most enrages me about this note is not that he Names me, but that he makes a friendly threat to me, indicating that if I behave, our next meeting “will be a pleasant one”. Now, one doesn’t say “pleasant” without implying the opposite, and I should know, because I pioneered that game back in Loudwater, where the usual threats of “we’ll have words” was the sort of thing that the simple folk there prefer. 

I should add that Curavar had the professional courtesy to deliver this note by his own hand, and he does the sensible thing by following up his “I know who and what you are” with a “I’ll give you a chance to fly right”, and all this amounts to what is, for the most part, a pretty decent and fair letter, if you can overlook his chiding tone and his shameless usurpation of my own style. But that matters little to this Son of Set. I am going to wear his face as a mask, while I eviscerate him and feed him his own guts.

Though he is going to cause me many sleepless nights now, Curavar will have to wait.

Other news came to us while we lounged about the monastery for the last couple of days. The most interesting bit is this; when we asked about the two brunettes found on the Vampiric dinner table, we were told that they belong to a group known as the [[Blades of Korombos]]. That is a name I have not heard in a long time, and I am surprised to hear it at all in this age. The Blades had a very poor career in Waterdeep, getting tangled up in the affairs of some friends of friends of mine, but they did play a key role in the events surrounding the [[Saga of the Red Herring|Occular Ascendancy]]. I will have to investigate this further when time permits.

Arya, who seems to be the singular soul to whom Allendy will have open discourse with, the dirty old man, told her a bit about Barthas. He apparently knows a bit about this old Vampire Lord, and indicated that he has been skulking about the ruins for decades, but that he actually keeps mostly to himself, tending to spend his time on his constructs. 

It is too damned bad that Barthas is a vampire. If there is one school of magic that I have always loved but never seemed to have the time to get around to, it’s Necromancy. Barthas has been a busy little blood sucker, creating not only the usual minions, but original and dangerous undead killing machines, and I would love to have the chance to spend some time discussing his Art. But, he is a vampire, and I can’t abide a vampire. I’d rather have tea with the devil himself, and share cakes made of baby fat, than keep company with a vampire. I guess old prejudices die hard.

Anyway, of Barthas and his introspective and benevolent rule of the dungeons of Saharelgard, I must say this; we have brought an end to any unsteady peace that has been known in these parts. Barthas has been causing no end of torment and slaughter the last couple of nights. I have stayed up watching the grounds and listening from the balcony of the monastery, and it sounds as though he is inflicting his rage, that we stoked, upon the many other seekers who are without the walls.

I should say presently that I don’t care a fig for the fate of the seekers outside the walls of the monastery. I haven’t met one worth mentioning, and maybe becoming a servant to Barthas will actually add some character and purpose to their lives. However, I must feign terror and indignation at the unleashing of this thing upon the people of the area, even though the screams at night are a soothing song to Kethoth. We have been avoiding the topic at breakfast, but I think all of my minions know that we have awakened a terrible and powerful evil in Barthas, and we will have to answer for that, with one more visit with him.
