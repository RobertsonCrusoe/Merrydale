\"That thing is a coward.  I will not leave Spellgard until this damned creature and all of it's minions are cleansed.\" ~ Baird of Kelemvor

== Background ==

Barthallomos Brax was a talented and skilled nercromancer in the region of Thay around 730 DR.  There is some speculation that Brax was a contributor to the vast preparation that went into the demon Eltab being trapped beneath the city of Eltabbar.  Something that was finally accomplished in 923 DR, The Year of the Cracked Turtle.

As Barthallomos was growing in knowledge and power, he was also growing in age.  He felt death growing closer and closer to him and although he had surrounded himself with all manner of undead creations and experiments, he struggled with learning of a process to achieve lichdom.  Attempts at correspondence with and entreaties to Aesclipius, The King in Copper; an ancient lich of great power had failed and Barthallomos was growing more and more anxious and restless.

Barthallomos had always worshipped Orcus, but it had always been little more than lip service.  Barthallomos took it upon himself to become more devout, setting aside his focus on necromantic experimentation as well as the work going on beneath the elaborate channels of Eltabbar and sought the answers he desired through the worship of and service to Orcus.

While a priest to Orcus, Barthallomos found himself associating in higher circles of Thay society and while he was able to accumulate wealth, as well as power, he also accumulated no small amount of enemies.  It was 764 DR, The Year of Mist Maidens when Orcus deigned to gift Barthallomos with the unlife that he so greatly desired.

Vampirism, although exceedingly better than simply being 'alive' was nowhere near lichdom in power.  Barthallomos although appreciative of the gift his Lord bestowed upon him was also keenly aware of the vulnerabilities that came part and parcel with vampirism.  With political enemies growing more and more interested in Barthallomos' current affliction, the newly created vampire left Thay, taking as much of his wealth and knowledge with him.

It was a lengthy journey, and it allowed Barthallomos an opportunity to grow into his new abilities.  He found that he took no greater pleasure than turning females into his minions, particularly elven females.  It caused his cold, shrivelled heart to positively hum with excitement when he looked upon the absolute crime against nature itself; an elf, turned to something dark, selfish and ever-hungry.  The journey he took taught him much in the way of managing his vulnerabilites and over the years, he was even able to devise ways to travel during the day, despite the fatal effects sunlight could have on someone of his nature.

The Year of the Bloody Stone, 775 DR when Barthallomos, now simply calling himself Barthas arrived in a pleasant, simple area at the foothills of the Desertsmouth Mountains called Merrydale.  Cormanthor was close enough to allow him access to plenty of elven maiden playthings but not so close as to be a concern.  Merrydale was a quiet place, and Barthas was able to set up a residence of sorts in the mountains, allowing him greater opportunities to explore the limits of his own vampirism as well as begin to get a better understanding of vampirism in general.  Although accumulating undead minions over time as he studied, he found himself in need of an intelligent assistant, and certainly not a female one that he would be tempted to abuse and toy with.

He settled on a handsome fellow in Merrydale by the name of Cassiar Sukiskyn.  Things went well for a while but Cassiar enjoyed his newly acquired vampiric abilites a little too much and had no small amount of dislike for spending days and days in Barthas' underground laboratory and library.  Cassiar was spending more and more time away from Barthas and filled as many nights as possible with rampaging through Merrydale until it became widely known that Merrydale was no longer the merry and quiet place that earned it its name.  Merrydale was swimming in blood and overrun with vampires.  Barthas was content to stay in the mountains, observing Cassiar as well as the vampires that now ruled Merrydale.  He knew it was only a matter of time that evil such as he had brought to this place we begin attracting the type of people that wished to do something about it.

As much trouble as he had controlling Cassiar, he took no small amount of pleasure in the trouble Cassiar had with an elf that he had sired; one Janden Sunstar.  Janden fought Cassiar on every command given to him and even resorted to feeding on cattle and other livestock, simply to further irritate and anger his sire, Cassiar.  Despite the trouble Cassiar had with the undead gold elf, Cassiar, Janden and the other vampires managed to terrorize Merrydale for well over a century until Janden managed to destroy Cassiar in 894 DR, The Year of Howling Winds.  Cassiar for many decades had presented himself as the vampire that initiated the overrunning of Merrydale.  Barthas had moved past such things as pride and desire for title.  Cassiar claiming to be the head vampire of the coven that controlled Merrydale, allowed Barthas to stay in the shadows.  Eventually Cassiar simply stopped visiting Barthas, perhaps as a way to trick himself into thinking that he truly was in control of Merrydale, or perhaps simply too proud to admit that he was beholdened unto another.  Whatever the reasons, Barthas found them unimportant but upon the destruction of Cassiar, Barthas took that as a sign of the beginning of the end of his time in Merrydale.  Once more, he arranged his caravan but unconcerned about the wealth he had acquired since coming to Merrydale a little over a century ago.  He simply took along all of his notes, tomes and anything related to his experiments and research he had done.  Knowledge was the only wealth he cared about now.

The vampire known as Barthas was lost track of for a great length of time after he left Merrydale and there is no record of where he had been, or what he had been doing until word of a vampire residing beneath the ancient Netherese ruins of Spellgard in 1329 DR, The Year of the Lost Helm.

It was in these ruins that Barthas was content to simply continue his research and experiments; preying on the odd unwitting group of adventurers that would stumble upon his lair just so that he might have more materia with which to experiment upon.

Barthas' encountering of the adventurers eventually known as The Seven in his lair beneath Spellgard eventually led to Barthas' destruction.  Barthas, his minions and The Seven battled one another on three seperate occasions before Barthas was destroyed in a cave at the southern foothills of the Graypeak Mountains.  The Seven did battle with Barthas, a summoned Goristro demon and a recently turned Arya Nightweaver, an eladrin and founding member of The Seven.  Barthas had captured Arya Nightweaver on the twenty-seventh of Mirtul, 1479 and upon The Seven tracking Barthas to a cave on the twenty-eighth of Mirtul, they encountered Arya Nightweaver in her vampiric form.  It is currently unknown how Barthas was able to turn Arya into a vampire so quickly.

Barthas, seemingly trapped in the cave as the sun was still setting was unable to flee as he had done twice previously and was cut down by The Seven, but not before he had dominated Tyriol Grimbold and nearly threw the half-elf into a seemingly bottomless crevice and fail at the attempt but succeeding at dominating Mattrick Lychaeus, priest of Kelemvor and throwing him into the crevice in the same manner he had tried earlier with Tyriol.

Kethoth Sepret is currently in possession of a green crystal skull, and three large tomes; all possessions of Barthas that they had found in the cave.

Titles of the tomes and descriptions are as follows.

Lexicon of Inestimable Darkness – Written in a modified demonic language with a cipher that will be quite difficult to solve.  It is a thick tome, thirteen inches deep, twenty-four inches long, and eighteen inches wide.  The covers consist of the hide of a black dragon, with the spine and lock upon it consisting of finely wrought brass.  The lock itself consists of the front half of a small skull (a child’s), magically protected and preserved with it’s jaws spread open as if in a scream.   The tome has six hundred-sixty six pages, consisting of the flayed skin of children, and written in the blood of slain priests of Selûne, Lathander and Amaunator.

Status of tome - Currently unopened and unread.

Courting Death by Barthallomos Brax – The covers and pages are made of thin sheets of hammered brass, totalling two hundred in all, yet the book is only a few inches thick.  The binding consists of a series of adamantine rings that each page of brass is attached to.  The book is sixteen inches long and five inches wide; made to be opened up and read lengthwise.  The book is full of finely etched drawings of undead creatures, detailing their anatomy, instructions on creation as well as sustaining them and all manner of study that Barthallomos (Barthas) managed to collect and come up with over the seven centuries he was alive.  The cover of the tome is written in a common tongue from Thay in small, neatly organized and square script.  There is also a a small etched wizard’s sigil on the cover (Barthas’ sigil) almost appearing to be three unevenly sized diamonds floating over a mountain range.

Status of tome - Original reviewed by Kethoth Sepret - Arcane copy written in \"common\" (NOT the same language that the original is in), obtained from the Monastery of Sundered Faith and read extensively by Radha Leveaux.

By Night & By Wing - This book contains all of Barthas’ research, learnings and musings of vampirism, containing a detailed history of the fall of Merrydale as well as a great deal of information about vampires he had learned of and from.  He has an extensive list of different types of vampirism as well.  The book seems to be the least elaborately crafted of the three, with a crude binding of silk attaching two well-crafted sheets of oak.  The pages within are crafted from a much more sinister material, as investigation of it will show that the pages are made of skin; of the female, elven variety.  The entire tome is written in a common tongue from Thay.

Status of tome - Original reviewed by Kethoth Sepret

== Further Discoveries ==

Documentation found in the Monastery of Sundered Faith by The Seven reveals that Bathallomos Brax played a role (possibly minor) in the design of Eltabbar's canals that eventually lead to the imprisoning of the demon known as Eltab.
