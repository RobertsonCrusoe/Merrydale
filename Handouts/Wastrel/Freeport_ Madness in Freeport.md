== Summary of Parts 2 & 3 ==

When we last left our heroes they had just emerged from Black Dog’s caves and the Sunken Temple of Yig with the mysterious artifact known as the Jade Serpent. Upon boarding Captain Lydon’s ship, they find out that the good Captain has taken on a new passenger, Tarmon, high wizard of Freeport and ally to the adventurers.

Unfortunately Tarmon has been wounded while inspecting the lighthouse of Drac the night before and his familiar has been killed. Tarmon explains that the upper floors of the lighthouse while not outwardly radiating magic seem to be magically protected beyond even his means. Tarmon has suffered the greatest loss that any mage has suffered and his injury is quite obvious to our heroes. However, Tarmon tells our heroes that he will leave his grieving until later. Now, they will have much work to do before the night is through.

== Introduction to Part 4 ==

The time is now at the crack of daybreak and as the first light of dawn creeps over the horizon you are sailing into Freeport. As you get closer to the harbor you see an amazing number of ships and boats of every kind. Never in its history has Freeport’s harbour been this crowded with vessels.

And in the distance about is the reason for the sudden influx of tourists into Freeport. About a mile away on Freeport’s closest island stands the Lighthouse of Drac. For ten years has this legacy of the Sea Lord Drac been under construction. Labeled by the general public “Milton’s Folly” only you and a handful of others know that this lighthouse could very well doom the world into madness this very night.

The lighthouse never seemed to be visible from Freeport’s harbor. Maybe the scaffolding that was used during the lighthouse’s construction obscured the entire structure. Now the scaffolding is gone, and the white marble of the lighthouse gleams in the sunlight. The waters around it are kept clear by a patrol of four Sea Lord cutters.

Tarmon hobbles above deck with his hand grasped over his left side. He limps to the aft of the ship and stares at the lighthouse with the rest of you.

“We’ll not be able to get close to it in the daytime, but the cover of night should get us there unseen. There is a new moon this night and the good Captain Lydon should be able to out sail any of those cutters.”

“Now we must gather all of our strength and resources together and plan for this evening. The fate of the world stands on the edge of a knife.”

== At the Docks ==

At the docks Captain Lydon’s faithful shiphand Pippin is waiting. He notices Tarmon limping and immediately jumps aboard ship to help the old mage.

“Now no need for that boy.” Tarmon reaches into a fold of his rob and hands Pippin one of Lydon’s signaling crystals.

“Thank you Pippin my boy. That crystal saved my life last night.”

Captain Lydon looks at the mage and the boy with a bit of happy exasperation. He shakes his head an says, “Argh, so you two have been conspiring behind my back have ye? I had been wondering how ye got one of my signaling crystals. I knew ye had potential Pippin my lad!”

“We had to conspire behind your back. You’ve been doing nothing but stewing in your gambling for the past fifteen years. You weren’t worth even one grain of your old self. I’m glad to see that you’ve finally come to your senses Captain. Now enough of this talk, I recommend that we adjourn to my place and straighten out our plan of action.”

“Pippin, go and fetch Brother Thuron we may need his help tonight too.” Tarmon looks Lendin and Zech over and says to Pippin, ”You had better get Lady Elise as well. I think she may be the only person in Freeport who will be able to heal these two. Go now!”

== Tarmon’s Tower ==

In the Eastern District near the Old City is Tarmon’s tower. Being the high wizard of Freeport affords Tarmon a residence in its own small park right in the middle of the city. It looks as though Tarmon’s tower was here before the city was born and the entire city was built around his tower.

Tarmon leads you to the door, whispers a few quick words of magic and the door swings open. Beyond the door is a large sitting room that is lavishly furnished with dark wood furniture of the finest craftsmanship. To the left is a granite staircase that leads up to the second floor.

“We will spend the day in my study. We will be safe from divination and most other magery down there.”

Tarmon grabs a walking stick that is leaning against a wall and taps it on the wall while saying some ancient words. An opening appears in the wall revealing a set of stairs down.

The stairs descend to a door that opens and turns out to be the backside of a large bookcase. The study below the tower has the look of an eccentric’s domicile. Bookcases line every wall, filled with books of and trinkets of every kind. Three desks are set near one edge of the room covered with stacks of parchment. The rest of the room is completely cluttered with various things: mismatched chairs and stools, chests, baskets, a stack of sacks, tapestries, a skeleton of some prodigious creature from places unknown. There is a cot in one corner of the room and. Apparently Tarmon spends much time in this study.

== Tarmon’s Gifts ==

The old mage goes over to one of the lower bookshelves and pulls out a chest that is decorated like a set of books. He opens up the chest to reveal a small treasury of weapons and items.

“These belonged to companions of mine from a long while ago. They perished in a battle to protect a dying kingdom that fell only a year later. I’ve held on to these things for too long. Now I impart then on to you.”

== The Tower Stairs of Milton’s Folly ==

Opening the trapdoor releases millions of pin-prick shafts of pecular yellow light that causes your features to glow while your metallic items are encased in shadow. The room beyond is a square tower, 50 feet long on either side. A five-foot-wide, railed staircase hugs the walls, rising all the way to the summit of the tower, over 100 feet above your heads. The staircase is lined with thousands of ritual candles like the ones you’ve seen in the temples of the Yellow Sign. They are all burning an eerie violet light that no fire should produce.

The ceiling of the room has an opening in the middle that reveals the apex of the tower as a large dome of glass pentagonal facets. Mounted in the middle of them between beams joined to a small catwalk is the crystal that is seemingly the source of the lighthouse’s energy. It is shooting a beam of yellow light into the sky. And in the sky is a most terrible sight. Moving halfway into the light’s radius is a planet that is roughly two harvest moons in diameter. This is no planet known to any astronomer on this earth. As the light hits the planet it diffuses and reflects its terrible light onto the entire skyline beyond and out to sea. And as the light illuminates the sky it reveals countless upon countless stars forming impossible constellations in the heavens. These stars continue on to the lighthouse walls making the inside of the tower seem to almost disappear except for the yellow signs illuminating from the bricks within. The planet is slowly moving into full view of the crystal and you can see the mountains and craters on the planet writhing and churning as if alive.

Around the opening of the ceiling you can see six figures looking. Five of the figures are dressed in feathered robes that have an almost tribal appearance. They are wearing masks in the guise of the serpent men turned upside-down. The last figure is clearly Milton Drac. He is dressed in a black robe that is decorated like the terrible stars in the new sky. His face is glowing white and his teeth are glowing a dull yellow while his eyes are the deepest black.

You have maybe ten minutes until the planet is in full view of the crystal.

== The Aftermath ==

The days immediately following the destruction of the Yellow Sign are both strange and chaotic. Many seemingly unrelated yet influential people have mysteriously vanished. Without certain key positions filled the city falls into a bit of chaos. Many of the city’s guards find themselves without jobs as their former employers have now vanished. Riots break out, homes are looted, but all of this only lasts a few days.

The Captain’s Council members that had been loyal to Drac have now vanished, withdrawn from the council or changed their tunes. Lady Elise spearheads a campaign that abolishes the Drac blood succession law and begins a new chapter in Freeport history. Captain Lydon easily walks his way onto the council and together the new members work towards finding a new leader for Freeport.

A large ceremony is held in the center of the old city to honour you as heroes. You are collectively given a special voting position on the Council. Most businesses in the city have put up signs offering free meals and boarding to the “Heroes of Freeport.” The shadow that once fell over Freeport has definitely vanished.

== Wrap-up ==

So our adventurers have stopped the insidious Milton Drac and have destroyed the cult of the Yellow Sign. Oh, they may have come close to their mutual demise, but in the end they persevered to save the world for good and profit.

From their meager beginnings on the docs of Freeport against a rag-tag press gang to the final battle at the summit of the lighthouse of Drac, our faithful adventurers have shown us that they were the heroes of Freeport all along.

But is their work done? Have all of the loose ends been tied up? What about the mysterious dragon mage who nearly ended Salazar’s career and life in a fiery grave? What ever happened to the Bard Finien and his retinue of rogues? Will our heroes ever find the answers to these questions? Will our heroes retire in Freeport or will they set sail to see more of the world? Surely there are more adventures ahead of our heroes of Freeport. But, those are other stories that will not be told this day. The book of fate has many pages and wherever our adventurers go you can be certain that they will be dancing across those pages wrapped up in the fate this world and worlds beyond.
