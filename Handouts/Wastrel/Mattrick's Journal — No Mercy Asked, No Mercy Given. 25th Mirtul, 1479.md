1479, 25th of Mirtul

It has been nearly two days since Baird has been lost while in battle with the Mercykillers.  I have had very little time to understand what I am thinking and feeling and even commune with my Lord has done little to soothe the turmoil I am feeling inside.

I feel as if I have fallen from the path that I should be on.  I have some possible ideas regarding what I have done to fail the Gray Lord as well as my brother-in-arms and I am hoping that I might find some solace and peace for my soul by writing it down.

Baird is dead.  Killed.  He was my charge and I have known him since he was a small child.  I know that he is on a better path, serving our Lord faithfully but I still feel that I have failed him, my Lord and myself in our battle with the Mercykillers.

The skies had been cracked open and emptying rain upon our heads.  We were still puffed up after our success in the tower and upon leaving it, obscured within the grey curtain of the rain were the Mercykillers.  Seven of them.  We were similar in numbers and although a number of spirited arguments were held between Kethoth and Baird upon encountering the group of mercenaries; I feel as if they are not that different from us, if a bit less compromising.

Kethoth’s earlier actions all but guaranteed there would be a confrontation and before any words could be spoken, the seven mercenaries had faded into the rain and the ancient ruins around us.

It had begun and the tension was palpable.  These seven individuals were waiting in the ruins for us.  Waiting to kill us.  Ender slipped away without any word whatsoever, leaving the six of us to move further into the ruins.  It had not occurred to me that Ender was fleeing.  We had battled and bled together.  I saw the joy he took in combat and I assumed he was moving off on his own to wreak havoc amongst the Mercykillers.

The remaining six of us moved at a crawl, cautiously moving past ancient stone and through the worst rain I have ever experienced.  A shadow was seen, and the half-giant with a sword the size of a large man charged towards Tyriol and the battle had begun.  Tyriol fell back from the dangerous swordsman and we were all moving to assist him.  I stepped around the corner and was immediately dazzled by green motes of arcane energy.  My skin burned and as I struggled to move away from the source of the arcane assault, a cross bow bolt pierced my chain mail and with a startling velocity, punched straight through my abdomen and out my back.  I collapsed on the ground, my blood spilling into the mud and I couldn’t feel anything except the rain pounding onto my face.  Baird was above me, shouting and pointing with his sword even as he dragged me behind the cover of a ruined wall.  Lucretius was tending to my wound and with a number of my companions rallying around me, the Mercykillers had the opportunity they needed.

The rest was a blur.  Lucretius hoisted me to my feet and with a snarl, pressed me to the wall with a massive forearm and even then, towered over me.  He looked down into my eyes and said, “Fight well battle-priest for it is upon us.”

The dragonborn was not wrong.

Not even on the wall, have I seen such ruthless fighting.  Spells were cast and weapons clattered against one another.  There was a point where it felt as if we were all going to die there in the rain.  Kethoth stepped up as he had done in the past and where things seemed lost, suddenly three Mercykillers fell to the ground, seemingly asleep.

This was my first step off the path.  A ferocious dragonborn that wielded a halberd and commanded the Mercykillers masterfully was one of the three that had been put to the ground with the tiefling’s enchantments.  Some manner of battle lust was upon me, and the same could be said for Lucretius.  Rather than assisting Baird as he battled two other Mercykillers, Lucretius and I assaulted the incapacitated leader of the Mercykillers.  It was my blow that split open the skull of the dragonborn upon the ground.  The death of their leader did the opposite of what we had hoped.  They were far from demoralized and instead attacked us with even greater fury.  The giantkin struck Baird with a mighty blow and knocked him to the ground and as Baird moved to pull himself from the mud, a crossbow bolt smashed through his skull and sprayed us with blood and brains.

I had forsaken my charge to kill an incapacitated combatant.  This was not battling on the wall against Tam’s undead legions.  I would never have done such a thing ordinarily and by being swayed by the order from the tiefling, I abandoned Baird in order to kill a helpless opponent.  The rest of the battle seemed to go quickly from that point.  Although it was not easy, the loss of Baird spurred us onto greater acts of ruthlessness and violence.  The tiefling refused to let any escape, and even those that were on the verge of surrendering were cut down with seemingly no remorse.

I am now at a settlement in the southern foothills of the Graypeaks.  A horde of hobgoblins that we are seemingly responsible for are terrorizing the land because of a fire that we started nearly a month ago is readying to destroy this small settlement.  I have helped create defenses for the settlement. It seems as if I have had no time to deal with the loss of my brother and the selfish action I committed that resulted in his death but in my soul, I know I have failed.

I buried Baird shortly ago in a corner of this very settlement with Lucretius helping to dig.  Arya stood nearby, wracked with sorrow and I hope to never see such a lovely creature caused so much pain again.  Lucretius and I both understand how we failed.  We do not need to speak of it.  We could see it in each other’s eyes as we dug in the rain and the mud.

There were seven of us when we started this journey, yet only four attended the brief ceremony that was had for Baird.  They will have their own reasons for not attending but I cannot help but feel anger towards them, particularly the tiefling.  I should not have relied upon these adventurers.  There is much more to do regarding the task Baird and I were given and I cannot help but doubt the souls of these people I have aligned myself with.

I have failed my Lord and my brother and I cannot help but think the forthcoming days will be dark ones. 

My brother; Baird.  I failed you.  I miss you.  Should the Gray Lord grant it, I will apologize to you when next we meet.  I will spend the remainder of my days doing what I can to prove my worth in the service of Kelemvor.

Brother Mattrick Lychaeus, Voice of the Grey Lord
