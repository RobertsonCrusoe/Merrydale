The days after the siege of the Temple of the Unspeakable One
----

After a brief argument with Brother Egil and Lucius everyone agrees that it would be impossible to keep the events of the past few days secret from the Temple and the city authorities. Milos was a high ranking official at the Temple and an advisor to the city. Keeping his death a secret would be practically impossible and it would be better that our adventurers are labelled heroes rather than murderers. All of this excitement coupled with a less than discreet hostage situation has worn out our heroes' welcome at the Scholar's Quill. Brother Egil offers to arrange accommodations for a few days at the Temple, \"...until you can find something else. Its the least we can do and besides you could all use the rest.\" The last few days have indeed proven dangerous, but our adventurers also have more swag than they've ever had in their lives. Freeport sees a lot of wealth pass through its clutches and all of it is there for the taking. Perhaps our heroes will try to find more permanent accommodations in the near future.

Over the next couple of days things are fairly quite. Lendin spends his time strengthening his mind and body, Rahzial and Zech spend their time rooting around the city, and Salazar spends his days locked in a private chamber reading some mysterious tomb. The nights are spent in a larger reading room speaking with Brother Egil and Lucius.


== A conversation with Brother Egil ==

\"Indeed we will at least keep the Brotherhood's real name a secret. I can't even say the full name, and I would advise you all to do the same. This is most grievous news to find that the mere utterance of its name causes such a reaction. You will have to do some research into this, Lucius. I just hope that the cult does not come closer to discovering their God's true name.\"

\"But I thought that there Yellow Sign be its true name.\"

\"Please refrain from saying those words within these walls Zechary! No, by Hedrada no that is not its true name or else we probably wouldn't be here right now. The Gods can be recognized by many names; some are closer to their true names than others. Many believe that it would take many a man's lifetimes to utter a God's true name. Some elves believe that they could do it, but they would have to be born with the knowledge and start speaking its name from birth to death. Others believe that dragons say the names of the Gods when they sleep. I cannot say what is true.\"

\"What happens when one speaks the bloody true name?\"

\"Ah... an astute question Salazar, but I cannot answer that one either. I do know this though: through the knowledge of our God we gain a closer relationship with him. Through our relationship with our God we gain his power. When the world was old no one knew about the Gods and their power was unavailable to us. The Gods must not have had the power to reveal themselves to us because many lifetimes passed before we discovered them. You see it was us who had to discover the Gods and it all started with a name. Our first Prophet opened us up to a relationship with Hedrada, but he discovered him as Hea. This name only gave us a spark of Hedrada's power. Through many generations of clergy many more Prophets came along who discovered other, more true, forms of Hedrada's name and our clerical power has grown through this knowledge. Those of the faith can feel Hedrada's embrace with each utterance of his name. Those who are not of the faith will probably feel nothing. This is the way things should be; those who do not feel belief for a God's ontology should not be forced beneath its presence. However, the unspeakable one seems to be different, and this is very bad news indeed. We must keep its name secret and pray that a truer form of it is not found.\"

== Zechary's Dream ==

You find yourself walking down a dark underground hallway. You are being led by four cowled figures, one in front, one behind, and one on either side of you. You notice that your peripheral vision is being obscured by a similar cowl over your head. You are led into an octagonal room with black walls and the ceiling shimmers with stars like the sky at night. There is a circular depression in the centre of the room and an altar at the far end. You know this room as one you've been in before. You are lead into the circular depression and stripped of your clothing. A hooded figure stands behind the alter lighting black candles. He removes his hood and looks up chanting at you:

:\"You accept the cult of the Unspeakable One.\"

He strikes a gong and repeats his phrase.

:\"You accept the cult of the Unspeakable One.\"

He strikes his gong again.

:\"You accept the Brotherhood of the Yellow Sign.\"

He strikes the gong again and his last words reverberate with the final clang of the ritual.

:\"Yellow Sign, Yellow Sign, Yellow Sign...\"

Above you the stars seem to twinkle as if you really are looking at the sky. Then you feel a presence - and the circular depression erupts with black tentacles all around you. The tentacles lick your flesh violently. Inhuman voices fill your head, uttering terrible words and evoking images of horror. The voices join together forming a single voice.

:14\"Know that I am more ancient than you can ever imagine. Know that I will destroy you...\"

You wake up sweat soaked surrounded by Brother Egil and several other Clerics praying so vigorously that they hardly notice you come to. Brother Egil looks up at you.

:\"We almost lost you this night. You were very lucky.\"
