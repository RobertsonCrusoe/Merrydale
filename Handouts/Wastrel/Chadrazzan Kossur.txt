An archaeological and literary scholar without equal, Chadrazzan Kossur is a man (well...) determined to preserve the heritage of the ancients. Though a man (well...) of fell passions, he is determined to make good, refusing to be simply a tool of the base blood and dark history that have forged him.

== Kethoth Reminisces ==

Just the other night, I found myself and three adventuring companions in some deep Chultan tomb. In the last encounter in the tomb, we disturbed a rather potent ghoul of large size who laid low the entire party, minus old Kethoth (of course). While everyone was either paralyzed or unconscious, Kethoth fought on, summoning a veritable hoard of demonic rats and undead to combat the lethal undead foe. In the end, of course, my magics won the day (again).

So there I was, in this tomb with the whole party at my mercy. I did not let on my delight at that moment, and it is only now, in defense of my character that I confess this. I fancied that I could easily bash the brains out of the head of the paralyzed npc with us (some nameless, useless pirate that I have no love for), thereby making myself a little extra money, getting a little bit of xp (maybe) and gratifying myself immensely. But do you know what I did? I dragged his prostrate form from that dank dungeon to the salvation and light of outdoors.  The same thing I did for all the characters (with the help of some rather skeletal friends that I had summoned).

So there you have it. Kethoth is nearly a reformed man...man-like being, rather. If I can just quit killing prostitutes and children...

== Significant Moments ==

While travelling with The Seven and upon returning from Spellgard, Chadrazzan (while under the guise of Kethoth) suffered a devastating injury during the battle with The Red Hand at Kohr'Murron Stables.  While standing atop the stone tower that made up the main gate, raining down firey death upon the hordes of hobgoblins that were crashing up against the settlement in howling waves, the tower collpased while the mage was still upon it.  The large tower fell down around the mage, burying him in stone and debris and it was only the arcane energies that he wielded that kept him alive.  After being pulled free from the ruin, injured but still alive, the mage found himself walking with a distinct limp.  One leg too badly injured from the disastrous fall and subsequent raining down of stone and timber upon him. The injury seemed to be beyond the means of the healers he kept company with and it was entirely possible that he would walk with a limp for the rest of his days... (and a -1 penalty to movement thereafter :D )
