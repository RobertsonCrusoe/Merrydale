After momentarily looking inside the Nexus, the party turned back into the tunnel they had just used and closed the door to the nexus behind them.

The large, thick 15' square door closed suddenly and violently after a hiss of steam and gave the impression that you don't really want to be near the door let alone under it when it closes.

With the 15\" by 15\" square tunnel that heads north, back from where you've come, the party prepares to set camp and rest for the next stage of the journey.

==Kethoth Calls A Meeting==

After camp is set up, Kethoth addresses the troops.

\"Friends, it is time for us to discuss some of our goals. With what we have learned in the Library, we are now on a course to find and retrieve the Horns of Eltab. This, of course, was always the goal of our fallen comrades Baird and Mattrick. It is now my goal as well; I will honour a promise made to them, and do the people of their homeland the service they deserve for sacrificing their young men in our lands.

\"There are questions we must ask ourselves though, in the event we do recover the Horns.

\"The first and most obvious is, do we surrender them to Zammomel Stass? I was very unsure of this man when I met him, and remain a little sceptical even now. However, after our research in the Library, I believe that Zam Stass, whether he is a man or an institution, is reliable enough to be trusted with the Horns.

\"When next we meet Zam, if ever we do, I will make one last assessment of his nature. If I think he is who he says he is, I will surrender the Horns to him, and wish his people well.

\"The main question is this; should we surrender the Horns only if a reward is offered? I know many of you have no ties to the fallen Aglarondans, and I don't ask you to risk your necks for their sake. Our endeavours have been profitable every step of the way so far; many of us now have a fortune stowed away, and we are poised to take a bit more plunder before we are through.

\"However, I know that the value of the Horns is tremendous, and that if we are able to recover them, they could realize some additional profit for us. I am sure that Zam could come up with a reward. I will ask you all presently who would like a reward from Zam. Do not be ashamed to ask your heart's desire. This is the last time we will discuss this, so speak your mind now.

\"Kethoth Sepret, for one, will ask no reward. But if more than half of you wish a reward, I will demand reward for all of you.

\"So now, please do cast your lot...\"

==Paetr Pipes Up==

Arousing from what seems to be sleep Paetr replies:

\"Aye my ancient friend, a reward indeed shall be requested. I hold no allegiance to this journey, save my word that I would accompany you. The keeping of the Roost is costly indeed, and although we receive much, I would be negligent in my duty were I to continue on this path for nothing but the satisfaction of another. Now perhaps this mission serves well the succession of hostility at the roost, but, perhaps threats appear from other directions. I accompany you to aid the Roost, and the Roost has done much to preserve the lives of all free citizens of the realms. Should a citizen profit by my deeds, said citizen should thus pay for my service. I ask this not for me, but for the young soldiers defending your borders.\"

With that the old dwarf drifts back into sleep...

==Volund is Dubious==

\"I'm sceptical of the wizard as well and I'm not comfortable handing the artefact over to him just yet. The least we could do is take it to Aglarond's ruling council with Zam as an escort. And a reward? That would be appreciated and I'm sure they would be more than willing if the horns could prevent an invasion from Thay.\"

==Ender is Tired==

Leaning up against a wall and sliding down to touch the floor, Ender sighs. \"The horns can go to whoever offers the biggest reward in my opinion. Kethoth is right our endeavours have been profitable thus far but profit is not of much use if you are dead or constantly on the run with no place nor time to spend it. I am tired. I am sure we all are. Nothing against the lot of you but the only good that has come of this little adventure of ours is that I got my flesh and blood brother tossed out of the place he called home, so if any of you don’t want a reward I will be glad to take it on your behalf.\"
