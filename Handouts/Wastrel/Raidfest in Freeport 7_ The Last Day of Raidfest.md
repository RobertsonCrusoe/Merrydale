As the sun begins to set and the full moon begins to rise the final celebration of Raidfest begins.

The ceremony takes place on an eastern beach of Freeport’s island where a coral reef comes almost up to the shore. The waters here are shallow, usually too shallow for most of the boats that are gathered here, but the cleric of Umberlee makes sure that no ship is beached.

Maybe two hundred boats are anchored around here starting almost at shore and receding off almost to the hurricane waves that have gathered around the island. Performers on long stilts walk around the shallow waters holding torches and dropping bait poles into the water here and there coaxing the luminous guppies to jump out of the water. Lights on the water seem to be a theme of this ceremony as the light of the moon hits the special fabric that has been attached to the rigging of most of the ships it begins to burn slowly emitting soft colours in the night sky.

This all goes on for about two hours. Those staying on the island huddle with their loved ones on the beach and watch the beautiful lights. Ships jostle in the water, having conversations from ship to ship: arranging to meet up in this port or that harbor, figuring out which flotilla to join, getting rumors of flotillas to plunder, and even making new friends on this last day in Freeport.

Even though all of this does not appear to be an intended part of Raidfest it is one of the most important parts of the festival and illustrates how the last day of Raidfest is a quiet foil to the previous two days, indeed the entire year at Freeport.

Around midnight when the moon is at its apex the ceremony begins. Illuminated by star blue torches, the beautiful Valkurea, maiden of Valkur and sailors of Freeport steps out of the forest and onto the beach. She is barefoot and wearing a light gown that flutters like sails on calm day. Walking on the beach she begins to sing in her beautiful echoing voice, unaccompanied by instrument for she needn’t any artifact to sully her natural voice. Reaching the shore she steps onto a tiny boat with a single triangle sail. The boat begins to sail and the maiden of Freeport stands atop it singing her song while a channel of boats that you never knew were there lets her past, almost bowing to her beauty and perfect sailing.

She sings “Farwell to Freeport”

As the song ends and Valkurea’s boat reaches the ridge of the calm water she raises her arms and the water before her calms into a smooth pathway. She sails out into that path and disappears off into the horizon, but the calm water stays.

One by one, then many by many the ships begin to leave through the calm water, some veering off north, some south, while still others continue on east. At first you have many ships around you and you even continue your ship-to-ship conversations for a while. But eventually, as the night wears on those ships begin to drop off from you as you all dropped off from Freeport’s horizon.

You and many other ships have left Freeport’s shores today and cannot return for another season. You have left Freeport’s shores today and may not return ever.

Why is it worth sailing at all? That is the riddle of the sea. Is there an answer to this riddle? That I cannot tell for I am just the narrator. But do sail my friends. Sail where the wind takes you and sail where it guards against. Learn from Valkur, that one man can challenge the Bitch Queen and win. Learn that any who chooses to brave the seas is Valkur’s Chosen and will always be guarded by fate. For the sea is terrible yes, but is also wondrous beyond imagination. Sail my friends and see what you find.
