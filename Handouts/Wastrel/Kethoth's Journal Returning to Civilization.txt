==Tarsakh, 1479==

I am now living in comfort and fashion, such as can be found in Loudwater.

I came her only a couple of weeks ago, wearing the finest clothes that the copper merchant had to offer; they are decent and presentable, though I wondered at the fit; the man was 100 lbs greater than I, and some of his things hung off of me. However, I was able to spin a quick tale about suffering the flux on my long journey from Calimshan to Loudwater, and I have done a decent enough job of passing myself off as something I’m not, that I am currently living in the rich quarters of the Lord Mayor of the Town of Loudwater.

Loudwater is a place much changed since my last visit, which was more than 100 years ago. It is not as populous, but yet it seems more prosperous. The place is abustle with various resource based oeconomies, and there is much coin changing hand. Here, I am Kethoth Sepret, Royal Surveyor of the Pasha Riminsk of Calimshan, and I am on official business seeking trade contacts. I like this lie well enough because it affords me the chance to lament for the finer things that I so enjoyed in Calimport many years ago. I am told that Calimshan is as prosperous and as civilized as ever, and I eagerly anticipate a return there at some point. It is there that I am most likely to find my old friend, [[Cedric]]. 

In Loudwater I have discovered that I have indeed developed the silver tongue that I used to envy in my father. I have been able to turn my devilish countenance into a social focus in my favour, rather than against it. However, I should say that the world has changed a bit, and likewise in my favour; when last I was in this part of the Heartlands, those with my looks were at the least scorned, and often met with overt hostility. However, the people of Loudwater do not all recoil from me like I would have expected, and indeed there are stranger folk than me afoot here. This has helped me to sharpen my social weapons, such as they are, and to thrive in this odd little enclave of civilization nestled in the wilds.

Shortly after I arrived in Loudwater, I immediately liquidated the goods I had taken from the copper merchant, and I now wear custom-tailored clothes of respectable linen that are both fashionable and serviceable. I have washed away the countenance of the bandit Kossur, and am thoroughly submerged in the role of Kethoth the Royal Surveyor, and it is a comfortable role. It has been made all the more so by the skinny halfelven Lord Mayor of this town, Illustryel Moonfire.

I met Illustryel in the trade-quarter of Loudwater, on the street happenstance. Fortune found me wearing my new robes, fresh from the tailor, and I was looking as good as I had been in untold decades. Illustryel stopped me then and there and introduced herself. She’s a gregarious thing, to be sure, and though I was taken aback at the forthright friendliness of this nearly attractive halfelven lady, I had wits enough about me to bow deeply and make a cordial greeting. It was 2 nights later that I found myself on top of her in her plush bed in her fine home, heaving and sweating and trying to imagine her resisting.

I should elaborate just a little on our relationship, and the curious complication that I have with it. Kethoth has had many lovers, but not a single one of them was willing. Not even the well paid and peerlessly-professional escorts of Calimport could conceal their contempt for this devil, and I wouldn’t have it any other way. What is the value of something freely given? Nothing! I do not come like some poet, on bended knee and with presents and charming words for those I would take to my bed. I am no beggar of love. It is only when a lover resists with all her strength and soul, and when I am only able to take her, inch by clawing inch, that I am satisfied. It has always been this way. Illustryel, however, is a willing participant in our copulations, and I have to own that this makes things difficult for me. I must needs resort to memories and imagination whilst taking her, in order to muster the needed assets to finish the job, and I am sure she knows it, for she is most happy and satisfied when she is atop me, and I beneath her, though I can scarcely stand for it, as it were. At any rate, she has never boiled my lust; I have only ever been half fond of elves and their like, and she is somewhat too skinny. She’s a handsome woman, but her skinny figure seems to Kethoth something akin to a pig’s foot, crammed into a fancy dress many degrees too small for her prepubescent-sized breasts. She dresses and speaks and acts well, but none of this will ever be enough for me, so long as she keeps me as her consort.

Now, I am Illustryel’s plaything, and I harbour no other misconceptions. She seems almost simple in her uncommonly voracious taste for me, but I will not twice make the mistake of thinking any elven lady simple. Illustryel wears the guise of the gregarious politician, with a charming and optimistic disposition that is perfectly suited for her damnably frequent parties and social gatherings. Set’s Blood! But how I have grown to hate those parties. I am paraded about like some sort of flashy bauble on her wrist; always a thing accompanying the great lady, and I think she delights in it, though I don’t quite know her mind yet. She does enjoy her parties, and she does enjoy mounting me with debilitating frequency, but I don’t think I hold any more important role than mountable accessory.

At least, I didn’t think so until last week. As I have said, there are no simple elven ladies, or halfelven for that matter. Illustryel is a warlock of some stamp, and though I have tried to piece together the source of her power, and I am privy to her nocturnal ramblings in some tongue unknown to me, I am as yet unsure of the nature of her power. I have prepared a spell of tongues to help me interpret her alien ramblings the next night that we spend together. Though she usually talks in her alien language most nights, one night she even bolted upright, and scrawled some cryptic message on her chamber wall. I have recorded it is best I can in the back of this very book, and shall endeavour to translate it as soon as possible. Illustryel certainly has some deep secret, and perhaps a potent, yet unharnessed power about her.

The lady Illustryel also has the romantic interest in adventure that one might find in children in any part of the Realms. In fact, it is for this very reason that I think she has kept me on; there is little doubt that her sexual proclivities drew her at first to me, and that my new-found guile kept me in her chambers for some time, but the fact that I am a wizard of some small power is known to her, and I don’t doubt that she knows that I am something rather a little more than the Royal Surveyor that I claim to be. In fact, she has recently given me a task, a little quest involving a ghost or a demon in some ruins to the east. She has heard that this ruins are called Spellgard, and that the demon therein will grant a wish or some other fantastic and ridiculous boon to those that can brave the pits of snakes and riddle-slinging-trolls that guard its secret lair. I have a mind to dismiss this nonsense completely, but she has given me a sizeable sum of money to find out what I can about this legend, and to hire the muscle needed to complete this noble quest.

Illustryel insists that the demon has a vast and inestimable knowledge, and that it will grant us some secret knowledge when we confront it, as our reward for braving the silly adventure. What is amusing to Kethoth is that she will have me ask of the thing where a great and ancient treasure is buried. I can understand the lady’s desire for treasure, but Kethoth would be inclined to ask the creature, if such is my right and boon, what the nature of his limitless knowledge is. We shall see what becomes of this child’s gambit.

Kethoth is flush with means just now, and there is work to do.
