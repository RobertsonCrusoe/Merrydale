===Software Changes and Upgrades===

We used the 4ed pluggin from the Maptool website, which made for a lot of work up front, but has already returned some dividends.  The 'framework' is basically an imported campaign with all the coding needed for 4ed rules.  We still had to import characters and code each power individually, but after a while Gus and I both had the hang of it.

The actual game-play with the 4ed framework was buggy at first, but by the end of the session, it was taking care of all the math that usually drags out our sessions into the morning hours.  This will be a really fast interface and a great way to play 4ed once the bugs are mostly worked out.

We also went to Skype instead of Vent, and this was the best possible communication tool short of actually sitting at the table together.

===Game On: A Lack of Focus?===
While talking to the locals in Winterhaven, it was discovered that an archaeologist who had been digging up some dragon bones at a site south of the town had not been heard from in a few days.  This was unusual, and the location of his dig-site was in between two known locations of kobold ambushes.

Rahter than sticking to the plan to investigate the kobold threat and prepare the people of Winterhaven for a fight, the party went to investigate the dig site.  There, they met an overhwhelming and deceptive group of foes, lead by some shadowy apparition.  The adventurers were outclassed from the get-go; the deceptive boss of the dig harrassed Shepherd relentlessly with a cross bow, while a group of slave like rabble and lizardly guard dogs attacked in force.

During the chaos of the combat, the dig boss and the shadowy form made off with some artifact that had been uncovered only moments before the adventurers showed up, leaving thier minions to take care of their foes.  In the end, the adventurers were able to set up a good line of defense, and fought back their attackers using the confounding swarms of the druid and the resolute arms and armoured Tormites to hold the line.
