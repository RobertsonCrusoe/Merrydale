# **Noncombat:**

* Malmotte:
  * party spokesman and currently quartermaster;
  * Also good an intelligence-gathering, vis high trained diplomacy and defacto-decent other social skills.  Can also identify magic items.  Can also speed up healing outside of combat.
* Harrigan
  * Great fisherman who can supplement our supplies.  He is a local, though long-time removed from the area.  One of the best scouts in the party.
* Tarr:
  * he is the only True local in the party. He has also lost everything. He is trusted and folk can empathize with him.
* Huntmaster:
  * he is a straight talker. Some in town will appreciate that more than malmottes "social skill", some people simply distrust a smooth talker.
* Khel:
  * volunteers for Information Gathering
* Ford:
  * volunteers for Quartermaster duty (also a Thief in case something needs to be "retrieved"...)

# **In Combat:**

* Malmotte:
  * Pretty useless right now.  He has 4 spells which are all crowd-control spells; sleep (which can be fucking brilliant with a bit of luck), fear, which has a very high DC and will send enemies running away full speed for 1d4 rounds, and lots of other small wizard tricks.  He really isn't much use just yet..
* Harrigan
  * Excellent at tripping guys; he can make 2 trip attacks/round at +4 each.  He can also stun opponents for a round.  He doesn't deal much damage, but can really tie up the enemy on the front line, and make it easier for our true strikers to deal damage.
* Tarr:
  * like, well tar. Get too close and you are stuck. A control fighter, can be fairly damaging.
* Huntmaster
  * a close/medium range shooter.
* Khel
  * best at spells that cause distractions and incapacitate opponents
* Ford
  * Medium-range artillery, melee striker/flanker

# **Exploration:**

* Malmotte
  * He is a generally skilled guy, though at first level he is a bit dim.  At second level he will be trained in Knowledge:Local and Knowledge:Geography.  Currently, the woods scare him.  Dungeons are even scarier.
* Harrigan
  * Harrigan is an expert outdoors-man in every sense.  He is a great tracker, great at stealth, keen eyes, great forager/fisherman, you name it.
* Tarr
  * can climb and swim
* Huntmaster
  * spot, sneak and track are all pretty good
* Khel
  * little value, other than a few divination spells
* Ford
  * excellent Stealth skills, medium Perception, will serve as a solid party scout

Scun's suggestions:

* I suggest Ford scout for us. 150- 200 feet out ahead. Looking for creatures.
* Harrigan, for now, will be 50-75 feet behind Ford. He will track. Ford is the spotter so Harrigan can watch exclusively for tracks.
* Huntmaster will stay about 100 feet behind the party, covering tracks and watching our asses.
* When there is trouble Ford and Harrigan sneak back to within 30 feet of the party and signal them. Huntmaster will then close the distance he is keeping as well.
* Tarr will stay with the weezard and malmotte.
