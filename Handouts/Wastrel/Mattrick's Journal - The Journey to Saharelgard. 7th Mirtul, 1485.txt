1479, 9th of Mirtul

I had always been under the impression that [[Aglarond]] was different from the rest of realms in the fact that it was fraught with danger. After traveling with this group of individuals for nearly a tenday; I now see that my impressions of the world were quite limited. Danger is everywhere but where enthusiasm and optimism has been all but squashed in Aglarond it has not seemed to happen here in the west. Some manner of fell creature seems to be lurking in every other shadow but the people here remain optimistic about their future and view each skulking fiend as an opportunity.

Although the difference in morale is refreshing and even encouraging in some respects, the cynic side of me thinks that perhaps these people are simply in denial. There appears to be no small amount of danger gathering at the gates of Loudwater yet the guards of the settlement seem oblivious to much of it and the mayor of the town is more concerned with throwing galas and sending her tiefling lackey off on wayward adventures.

Strange times…

…and strange company.

Saharelgard; or Spellgard as it is referred to by the less learned in the area is drawing closer with each passing day and it seems we are a little more than halfway there. I have had a chance to interact more with those I am traveling with and I have insight that I would like to write.

I wrote at length about the tiefling mage that leads our band, one Kethoth Sepret. Sepret has an unshakable sense of self-worth that some might admire and I find a bit tedious. He can be quite handy in a fight however, although I’m concerned about his reckless displays of power.

He lit a forest on fire a day and a half ago defending us from kobolds. The ranger, Tyriol informed me that the name of the forest is [[Weathercote Wood]] and has a history of being the home of a hobgoblin tribe and other less than pleasant things. Smoke from the fire still stains the sky and I fear it is still burning with us nearly two days away from where it started. We’re close to clearing the northern edge of Weathercote and I worry about three things.

What will the fire attract?
What will the fire drive out of the forest?
Are we actually going to be responsible for the destruction of an entire forest?

Although my Lord’s domain is death and all that is associated with it, I will ask Him for rain once more upon sunset of this day and pray for forgiveness when the final number of those that perish in the fire is decided upon.

Were this Aglarond. Were this the [[Yuirwood]] on fire; we’d already be dead and our heads would be left on the spears of elves at the edge of the forest as a cautionary tale for those that are reckless with the power they wield.

Even now, I watch the shadows of the Weathercote Wood to the East of us and wonder when a storm of arrows and spears will pour forth upon us.

Tyriol, the young ranger with us seems unconcerned and he seems to know this area. He has told me that there are elves in the area but they do not seem to be as ferocious as their kin in Aglarond by his account. Perhaps I am letting my previous experiences cloud my ability to see the larger picture, much as I was predisposed to disliking Sepret due to his fiendish lineage and appearance.

I must exercise serenity.

Something of which never seems to evade one member of our band. Lucretius, a large, ferocious looking “dragonkin”. It would be a mistake to think that this large, ferocious looking creature is like most large and ferocious looking things; stupid. I am unable to ascertain whether or not Lucretius is old or young for his kind but he shows a keen mind for combat and is quite valuable in a fight. He never appears to be rattled in combat and I find myself leaning towards asking him to join Baird and I when we return to Aglarond. His presence on the WatchWall would be of great benefit to our cause.

At first, I thought the term “dragonkin” was simply a lazy way of identifying the creatures but it seems the name could not be more accurate. I am unsure of the relationship between them and actual dragons but Lucretius actually breathes fire. I have seen it. As natural as I might draw a breath or shout my displeasure, Lucretius can spit forth fire. He is a formidable creature and I am thankful to have him in our band. Having met him has imparted the realization that I hope to never meet a dragon. I cannot imagine how dangerous and terrifying a massive, savage, animalistic and evil version of Lucretius would be. All I can hope for is that should we encounter such a thing, that I die well.

The day is drawing to a close and I need to begin preparation to speak to my Lord.

The next time I write in this, we should be in Saharelgard and if Kelemvor grants us his boon, we will be preparing for the journey home with the information we were seeking.

Honour to the dead.

Brother Mattrick Lychaeus, Voice of the Grey Lord
