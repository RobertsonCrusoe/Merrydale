"THAT WAS A GREAT WEEKEND, TODD. THANKS FOR THE FIRST-RATE ADVENTURES.

FIRST, I WANT TO SAY THAT I AM STILL SORTING OUT MY EQUIPMENT LIST AND
FIGURING OUT WHAT I WANT TO DO WITH THE LOOT. I'LL FORWARD MY THOUGHTS

I HAVE BEEN THINKING THAT SALAZAR WOULD REALLY LIKE A BOAT. THIS IS
FREEPORT, AFTER ALL, AND A BOAT IS A PRETTY MUCH ESSENTIAL PIECE OF
EQUIPMENT FOR ANY RESPECTABLE DENIZEN OF FREEPORT THAT IS INTERESTED IN
MAKING MONEY, OR FURTHERING HIS INTERESTS ABROAD.

I HAVE BEEN READING LOTS OF BOOKS ABOUT SAILING AND PIRATES LATELY, SO

TAKE A SMALL CREW UP AND DOWN THE COAST SAFELY. I ALSO FIGURE THAT A
GOOD WIZARD SHOULD BE ABLE TO LEND SOME ADVANTAGES TO A BOAT... STUFF
LIKE UNSEEN SERVANT AND OTHER USEFUL SPELLS."


PART 2: TODD — SHIP RULES AND SMOKEPOWDER — TODD BURRY (14 May 2002)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Source: Message eddf00dfb38cdb4
From: tburry@dymaxium.com

"Thank you sir. I had a really great time this weekend too. This was the
first time I've had a group play like a 7th level group. Everyone seemed
to be working together, and people were actually choosing their actions
based on the situation. I really enjoyed that.

As for a boat, I've been working on ship rules that I mentioned and I
think a boat will be a lot of fun. I've worked up some speed charts with
variable speeds based on wind direction. I also want to work smokepowder
guns into the campaign. The guns in the DMG 'Suck.' In reality (and in
my campaign), it should take about three full rounds to reload a
matchlock or flintlock. Now, they also did tremendous damage and I will
have to work out some rules for that.

What I really need is a map of the Realms coastal areas with wind and
current patterns. I'll have to look into that.

Also, Casey's campaign is coming up so we should start thinking about
characters for that too.

Do you have any good recommendations for pirate books? I've been meaning
to read some but never got around to it."
