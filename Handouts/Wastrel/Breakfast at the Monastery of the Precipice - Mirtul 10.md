Everyone is gathered around the breakfast table in the main room of the Inn.

Lucretius says \"Well, we are half way through our task. This Lady Moonfire has paid us some small token, but we have agreed to share in equal treasure for equal blood risked.

\"However, there seems to be one treasure that may not be shared so easily, and that is the council of Lady Saharel. I have my question I wish to ask, but there may be room for only one.

\"Let me say this though: I do not make any claim to this prize before others have made their case. By my thinking, there are two main interests at stake here.

\"Kethoth has completed most of the planning for this journey and seems to have some task at hand. However, he was just the plaything of this Lady Moonfire and I'm not so sure her whims should be considered too highly here.

The dragonborn continues; \"Baird and Mattrick have traveled a great distance risking much danger for just this one task. For this there must be something great at stake. However, I am at a disadvantage now. Neither of you have volunteered any information on exactly what you seek from Lady Saharel. Perhaps now is the time.\"

\"Kethoth agrees with the dragonborn completely.

\"This is precisely the reason I wanted to meet with all of you this morning. Baird and I did in fact discuss this matter of questions for the Lady last night, and I think we have come to some small understanding.

\"I must confess that it was not until last night that I did in fact truly believe that the Lady Saharel in fact existed. This seemed like a fantastic tale to me, but enough evidence in belief of her and her power exists; there are scores of people pursuing the same quest we are on, and the very...ahem...monastery...that we now break our fast in is evidence enough for me. We will find the Lady Saharel, and we will ask her our questions.

\"Kethoth must disclose a bit of information for you benefit, before we decide on anything.\"

\"Here is a portion of the Analects of Kuryon that I am privy to...\"

Kethoth closes his eyes and begins to recite;

*No looter I, no thief of tombs
*For learned men have called me sage.
*And yet within these madding rooms
*Perplexed was I by puzzle strange.
*The claw within the silver seal . . .
*The squares of alabaster . . .
*Many trials would reveal
*The pattern I must master.
*And then, enticed by crystal glow
*Ascend I did the central stair
*Beyond the shim’ring curtain, so
*Vexed I was by things not there.
*Two alabaster pillars stand
*A silent watch in chamber dark.
*I write these words with quiv’ring hand . . .
*I know just what these pillars mark.
*A timid step will bring me near
*Six holes where pillars do not dwell
*So hope remains, and yet I fear
*I’ve seen the doom of Saharel.\"

\"I suppose the urgency of our quest has been revealed by crossing the continent to these ruins. We have been sent by Aglarond to help secure the safety of our nation from the hordes of the forsaken plateau.

\"Kethoth and I discussed the Analects for some time last night and failed to decipher their meanings.\" Baird nods at Mattrick, \"We were sworn to secrecy and to be honest I am still somewhat reluctant in sharing this information ... but in the past few days I have grown to trust in our fellowship. In the spirit of cooperation here are the second and twelfth Analects of Kuryon.

Baird produced a pair of writings, the 2nd and 12th Analects, and laid them on the table.

\"Maybe our combined effort can figure out what these texts are saying.\" Baird said.

Kethoth has this to say; \"Kethoth has this to say; I have been sent on this task by the Lord Mayor, and have hired the lot of you to assist. However, fate has changed the nature of our mission.

\"I cannot deny the fact that Baird and Mattrick have come for greater purpose than the petty...no...selfish...pardon me...the small matter to which the Lord Mayor wishes me to inquire.

\"It could be that when we meet the Lady Saharel, that we will all get to ask her a question, and it could be that the 'great hidden treasure' that the Lord Mayor wants me to find might lead us in the same direction as Baird and Mattick are bound for. 

\"All that said, when we meet the Lady Saharel, it will be Baird that talks to her first. He can ask his question of her first, because he has come further than any of us to ask it, and the fate of his noble nation rests upon it.

\"I suspect that we will all get to ask a question of her, and if the tales be true, then each of us will recieve an answer, though I must warn you that they will likely be crypic answers at best, and should be treated with caution. 

\"In the event that she answers more than one person, I will ask mine next. All of you will get a turn after that, and I encourage you to think out very carefully what you would ask her. What little I know of this type of divination warns against asking paroxisms and other attempts at trickery. Ask a single, plain question, and don't be overly greedy for wealth or power, is my counsel.

\"At any rate, we have some work to do before then. Let us talk about these analects a bit, but after we have had some breakfast, and have found a bit more privacy...\"

\"It says in this one that he found the Lady above some tombs, perhaps we should look for the remains of a cemetery. This other Analect is entitled The Walls of Spellgard, perhaps we should search the perimeter of these ruins.

Nay, scratch that. I think we should wander around aimlessly within the ruins and dig a hole somewhere.\"

Kethoth continues; \"I am pretty sure that our objective is at the top of that tower, and that we must find a way to the top of it, or inside at least.

\"I have a pair of schemes to do just this. Firstly, we could try to go underground to find a passage up; as we have heard, this was a Netherese ruin, and I don't doubt that there were lots of passages beneath the place.

\"My other plan is a little more colourful. We know that the tower is controlled by something or someone, and that it is both unnaproachable and inviolable from the outside. I surmise this, and lend you some patience to your ear and my tongue; there is a potent power in that tower that had the strength to wrest control of it from myriad horrors and oponents. I am sure that whatever motivated that power is the same thing motivating everyone else; the Lady Saharel. 

\"I surmise that they want more than a single answer from the Lady, however, and seek to either imprison her, or threaten her with doom, as per the las line of the Analects of Kuryon. If this is so, then we can assume, also from the Analects, that in order to accomplish their goal, that they need 8 alabaster pillars to accomplish this. If this is so, then two things become true;

\"First, that we have a piece of the puzzle, in the form of one providence-sent alabaster Netherese pillar, and secondly, that the agents in the tower are closely associated with the Dark Creepers that have been seeking them out.

\"Here is where my 'colourful' plan comes in; we have both a pillar and dark creeper disguises. What if we were just to ride brazenly up to the tower, 2 of us dressed as Creepers, the rest in hiding, and the pillar in plain sight, perhaps the doors would open to us, and we could storm the place? I'll have to think on that a bit longer...

\"At any rate, we know of some ways to get down, and should recon those today.\"

Tyriol pulled a spoonful of porridge away from his lips and sighed,\"What makes you think Lady Saharel is at the top of that tower? I mean, how many people have come and gone through here claiming to have had their questions answered by the ghost lady?\"

Taking a quick gulp of his meal, he leaned close and wagged the empty spoon at the Tiefling across from him,\"I seriously doubt that tower would still be occupied by Creepers, or whatever it is up there, if so many adventurous folk had managed to work their ways to the top and survived to return with the tale.\"

Seating himself properly in his chair, the ranger shrugged,\"Besides, that second plan is nothing short of ridiculous anyways. I mean, that tower is Netherese in origin, correct? Gods forbid there's any manner of warding or spellwork surrounding it, which could promptly fry the halfling and obelisk in a moment...and even then, if they do need the pillars to imprison the Lady or destroy her or what have you, we'd just be handing it over to them.\"

\"I say we follow the dragonborn's advice and search the area for a graveyard or something. Find Lady Saharel, get our answers, and leave before we run into those black-armoured fellows again...or something worse.\"

Kethoth smiles politely at Tyriol and makes encouraging gestures at him as he speaks. Afterwards, Kethoth says \"Your input is important to me, half elf. I shall keep it close.

\"Let me say this though; that tower is the only place here that is occupied; it is of some value, and I don't doubt that someone is holding it specifically because the Lady Saharel is in there, and they want to monopolize her insight. 

\"Further evidence comes from the Analects themselves; they speak of a central stair, which I don't doubt we'll find in a tower. Perhaps one that is open to the light of the moon, or perhaps one that just plain glows itself.

\"At any rate, Luc did offer one useful suggestion; some portions of the outer walls seem intact too. We should go and check those out. There might be some old tower portions extant that might offer us descent.

\"Today, we shall see what we find. But think you all on what you would like to ask the Lady. Most of you probably have thought about this very thing for much of our journey, but I haven't heard too many of you mention what it is you would ask if given the chance.

\"I don't doubt Tyriol will use this chance to learn the name of the rutting fey who bred him...no offense, of course my good ranger...but I wonder what the rest of you would ask?\"

Tyriol shifted in his chair, mouth pulled into a nearly imperceptible frown. 

Something about this Kethoth fellow rubbed him the wrong way, although the half-elf couldn't be sure as to why this was. His overly-formal behaviour came off as heavy at times, but he was always polite and, outside of battle, was the very soul of civility.

Maybe it was all just a clever contrivance, a facade of high breeding this tiefling had brewed up to trick them all. Tyriol had been under the impression that Kethoth Sepret was merely the newest playing Lady Moonfire had found to dangle off her arm, but it was steadily becoming more and more apparent to the half-elf that this wizard had a plans of his own that probably didn't involve the Lord Mayor of Loudwater at all.

Then again, it could all be in his mind. Tyriol bit his lip. He was a tolerant enough fellow and commonly judged people by their behaviour and not the shape of their ears, but those glowing eyes hovering above that sharp, proper smile just made him feel uneasy. Devils were not to be trusted, afterall, and Kethoth certainly looked the part.

Luc;\"I would guess our destination is in the spire as well. However, the area around the place is not accessible to us. Since the Analects mention tombs perhaps there are catacombs beneath the ruins. Just finding some way below ground might be the best we can do. We can search for tombs from there.

\"I am also beginning to think this pillar does not represent anything good towards our quest. The analects seem to think they are the doom of Lady Saharel. I don't think they are necessarily from ancient Netheral either, but rather contain Netherese symbols carved by some wicked mage. Perhaps we should ensure they don't fall into other hands either.\"

\"Exactly,\" Tyriol nodded, gesturing towards the Dragonborn with both hands.

\"As far as I'm concerned,\" the ranger continued \"it'd be terribly counter-productive for us to drag that stone all the way to Lady Saharel if it could in anyway harm our chances of getting our questions answered.\" He pushed the analects that he had just finished perusing and tapped his index finger against the oak table.

\"I say we dump that pillar down the first spider hole we come across and just do what we came here to do. It was a mistake to bring it this far, right into the lion's den.\"

\"It was right of us to keep it close and under our noses. That way we know that who ever wants to use it for evil won't have it.

\"At any rate, there are enough holes to go down, out there. Let's go find one and be on with it. I think we have to go down to go up!\"
