The Great Dale

Battle Upon The Sea of Fallen Stars

It is the stormy season in the early spring, and no time to be upon the Sea of Fallen Stars for many mariners.  But, for the Wastrel and her crew, no storm has proved her master, and she claws her way through wave after wave upon this relentless, tumultuous sea.  And she is not alone.

For a day and a half, a black-sailed three-mast vessel has been on her tail.  The Wastrel is captained by a stout man known to his crew as "Stonekeeper", and he knows the signs of a hunter well. The ship that hunts them is a sleek and fearsome thing, with black sails and a crimson figurehead that cuts through the water like blood through milk. The crew of the Wastrel, some fifteen men and women strong, are gathered on the deck, armed, wary, and ready.

The Captain stands at the helm, watching the dark storm clouds gather on the horizon like a gathering army. The wind is fierce and the sea is angry. The crew is tense but disciplined. They have weathered many storms together, and many fights as well.

As the sun begins to set, painting the sky in shades of blood red and purple, the black ship closes in. Her crew lines the deck, weapons at the ready. The hunters and the hunted face each other across the churning sea.

The Wastrel's crew is a motley bunch: a ranger who prefers the sea to the forest, a rogue who has more lives than a cat, a wizard who mutters to herself constantly, and a cleric of Tempus who has seen more battle than most men see in a lifetime. Beside them stand the common sailors who have crewed this ship for years, loyal, brave, and true.

The black ship fires a warning shot across the bow. The Wastrel's captain curses and grips the wheel tighter. The wind howls like a living thing, and the sea rises up in waves tall as castle walls.
