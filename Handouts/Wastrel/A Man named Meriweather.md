==The \"Lady's” Man==
 
Meriweather Tarsk stood before the five of you inside the rundown tavern called The High Lady Walking, or as most from Secomber and the Gray Vale called it; The Lady. The smell of sawdust on the floor mingled with the sweet smell of vomit, and the sour smell of sweat, on top of plenty of spilled, cheap ale (if you could even call it ale). Tarsk was a tall man, and as gray and grizzled as he was tall ... and old; although by the way his reputation went, you wouldn't see his fifty plus years in the way he moved.
 
Tarsk was one of the few remaining caravan masters that knew the Delimbiyr Way. One of the few because he'd been both lucky enough to not be killed and crazy enough to stay with it when there were clearly more profitable routes to run.
 
He looked at the five of you over the rim of the crude, chipped tankard he was drinking out of and although you couldn't see his entire face, the man seemingly radiated disapproval. He lowered the tankard, not bothering to wipe as foam clung to and dripped from his whiskers and began to speak and a low growl.
 
“Can't say I likes the look o' you lot. Beggars can't be choosers so'z it's youse or nuthin' though so'z I s'pose I can't hold you lot responsible if'n we all get kilt on the way. Thunderstaff Mercantile and Sundries outta the 'Deep arrived here a fortnight ago with three wagons full'o supplies fer Loudwater. They pay well for this part o' Vale...the best actually. If things round 'ere weren't so arse over tea-kettle, there'd be a line up for caravan guards and I'd have my pick o' the best. Instead... I got you lot.”
 
He pointed a thick, gnarled finger at Artagan.
 
“You then, you're from 'round here. You know what's goin' on 'round 'ere even if you might not be worth a damn as a guard.”
 
He walked closer, standing directly in front of Otrallia, his nostrils flaring as he sniffed the air once, and then once more, drawing her perfume into his lungs before giving Otrallia a withering look.
 
“The rest o' you chaps will 'ave ta do. Thunderstaff pays thirty gold each way, and if you don't get my 'orses kilt, I pay two gold per horse that makes it to where we're 'eaded.”
 
Turning his head and giving Ciratto and Bigglesby suspicious looks, he continues,
 
“...those of you not good wit' figures, that's an extra twelve gold. If 'n ya don't make me angry or get me kilt, there might be more in it for ya depending on my mood. We leave at daybreak on the 'morrow. Do we have an understanding then?”

==Done Deal==
 
Artagan listens to the man, making little eye contact with him. While the unsavoury aged scab of a man spews out his offer, Artagan simply looks around at the 4 other people at the table. He then does a bit of counting on his fingers, as the numbers start to come in.
 
\"That's 42 gold to get to Loudwater, by my reckonin'. Each, I s'pose. 42 don't go splittin' 5 ways none too good. It is each, ain't it, ol'timer?\"
 
He looks up from under broad-brimmed weather-worn leather hat, squinting his eyes to take in the face of the man, awaiting the answer.
 
The wiry, scruffy man at the table with you, (Ciratto you believe the barkeep called him), merely raps the table twice and nods. Mr. Tarsk taps his hip twice (where a coin bag may usually hang) and nods in return, with a very brief appraising pause on Ciratto's eyes. He almost seems surprised that Cirrato knows a rather customary acceptance of terms for these parts.
 
\"Well if you don't have the looks and the brains of the bunch. Forty-two gold splits fine between five if'n ya know yer figures but you don't need to know no figures. I said that Thunderstaff Mercantile pays the best in the Vale and tha's what I meant. Forty-two for each o' yas that doesn't get themselves kilt or me kilt, or any o' my 'orses. Been sittin' on this for too long and the Thunderstaffs are ready to kill the whole deal. Bandits have everyone acting womanish 'round here of late and the Thunderstaffs prefer to throw gold crowns at problems now rather'n do it theyselves liken they used ta.\"
 
He gives Ciratto an appraising look, as if adjusting his initial opinion of the man, however so slightly.
 
\"Don't get lost in the gulletfire they serve here. We leave at first light.” Turning to look at Edward and Otrallia, he adds in a menacing tone. \"...and by leave, I mean just that. You two princesses will need to wake up BEFORE first light.\"
 
==Biggs Knuckles Down==
 
Bigglesby remains quiet for the man’s speech and just nods in acceptance of the offer. Though his wits are questioned he remains calm throughout, but when the man starts insulting his new friends he met on the way here … Well, there has to be a line.
 
\"I may not be too smart, but I do know – like you said, given the state around here, we are your only choice. Might be smart to not kick the help when they are down.”
 
\"You can keep your extra gold for me not making you angry, I’ll propose a deal of my own. Talk down to me and my friends again and we may just find out the terms of it.\"
 
Meriweather took a long look at Bigglesby. Where he had been slightly impressed with Ciratto's handling of local custom, he looked genuinely pleased at Bigglesby's not-so-subtle threat.
 
==Ciratto’s Assessment==
 
The Lady kel Tannith was known to him, as were most people of stature on the coast.
 
He’d not heard she had travel plans, and judging by her somewhat nervous state, he could only assume her departure had been a choice she’d made alone. A solid looker, though, one had to admit. Her arrival in town this morning had simplified things.
 
Her ‘pretty’ young companion, Edward, was new to him. Perhaps he was the reason for her running away. Eloping was not unheard of in noble circles.
 
How these two had made the acquaintance of “The White Knuckle” of Luskan was a mystery he would have to pursue. Oh yes, he readily recognized Bigglesby. Not only did his mother have no small measure of infamy – twenty-four children – all boys, but ‘Biggs’ himself had a reputation. The man had, albeit unwittingly, foiled many a dark treasonous plot in Luskan. Hence the title. Ciratto was fairly certain Biggs did not even know it was a title he held.
 
The final member of the group, Artagan, was also unknown to Ciratto, but he had learned something of him over the past few hours. The man was en route to Loudwater to deliver ‘silver flakes’ purchased here in Secomber. A worthy fifth, his axes are quite respected in the area.
 
Yes, Ciratto sits and studies his four companions and the Caravan Master, noting their pleasure at how good fortune had smiled upon them all.
 
==Edward and Otra== 
 
Edward counted himself amongst the numerically-challenged, mulling over the division in his head, eyeing Artagan's thick fingers, internalizing his own feeble calculations. He had been as polite and attentive as ever, this being a job and all, but the young Cormyrean could not help but shake the feeling that he and Otra just did not belong amongst these hard-looking men, with their confident smiles and casual airs, selling out their muscle for cash – albeit cash he and the woman found themselves in desperate need of.
 
\"Fair enough,\" was what Otra had said after hearing Biggs' proposal, only after moments of consideration. They had just met the man, and despite his honest face, Edward had trouble trusting the offer. He had tried to persuade her to turn back – they had only been on the upstream ferry for a few days, the paymaster at the storehouse still had all her things tucked safely away (probably) still intact… it would just take a bit of riding, maybe another ferry to get back to Daggerford, and so there was no reason to be sad or upset about anything – but Otra wouldn't have it.
 
\"No, what's done is done. We don't have the money. We're...I'm not going back.\" Not hasty, or evasive. Edward didn't get the feeling that she was running away from something (at least not something that was pursuing). The young woman just seemed to want to move on, and he could respect that, oddly enough. Her mood had improved considerably over the last few hours, even as his own seemed to wane. Was it her fault? Her fault that Edward had been so skewed off his original intent, his purpose? He had found Daggerford quaint, a place where he could do what he needed to do, and yet again she had pulled him along by sheer coincidence, and now he was even further away from home, from his goal, from the woman he told himself he loved, yet thoughts of whom he had little time for between worried considerations of where the push and pull of fate would lead him while leashed to the unyielding Otrallia kel Tannith. Or perhaps he was too harsh. What had occurred in Daggerford was not by the woman's intent, not her fault. Edward rubbed his face wearily. Perhaps this was his fate.
 
==Desperate Pockets==
 
So there they were, crowded around a filthy table in a filthy bar, halfway upstream to nowhere at all, ready to set out into the dangerous northern frontier to line their desperate pockets.
 
Edward glanced aside, still idly lost in thought. Otra was not paying attention to what anyone had been saying, a slight downturn of her mouth indicating her distaste with their current accommodations, casually braiding a section of her hair with two pairs of slender fingers. Edward leaned in close, catching her eyes and a faint smile, as though she had forgotten he was sitting next to her. \"I think that fellow's math is off,\" Edward whispered, gesturing between Artagan and the scruffy Mr. Meriweather. Otra replied with a withering stare, smile upturned into a smirk. She didn't say anything, deigning instead to tickle the side of his nose with the soft tip of the hair bunched in her hand. Otra laughed as he settled back into his seat as he scratched at his face, all the more bothered by the condescending gesture.
 
Fair enough. Just sit there pretty, let him keep track of the arrangements. It wasn't as though he didn’t already take care of most of their shared duties anyway. Cooking, lifting, chartering fares, setting up the camp, repairing what broke, and whatever else he wasn't thinking about. Otra was just there to hang off him and watch things get done. Wouldn't want to break one of those nails, get those hands dirty. Edward considered saying something to the woman, before remembering where he was and what they were here for. Mr. Meriweather spoke on, and Edward quickly became aware of the apparent lack of faith and regard the man held for Otra and himself. He glanced at Otra, who merely rolled her eyes.
 
Biggs took his turn to speak, in a surprisingly even voice, that Edward would not immediately match the big man with. He relaxed and smiled somewhat at the fellow's honest words, although Edward found the man's ultimatum a bit unsettling. His eyes wandered towards Biggs' hands, flat and rough, knuckles scarred and ground down and mottled with calluses. He looked every inch a man who could back up his words with action, the Edward judged, and was silently thankful that he had taken so swimmingly to their recent companionship. Better a friend than an enemy, that went well without saying.
 
Smoothly, Otra stepped in before Biggs threat could fester the air between them and the caravan master. Laying a gentle hand on the brawler's forearm, she fixed Meriweather with a petite, charming smile.
 
\"We won't be problematic, I assure you. I'm always up before the dawn,\" she delicately traced the side of her chin with one finger. \"Surprisingly, this requires little sleep to maintain. As for Edward... well, he's always... energetic, no matter how much sleep I let him have.\" She followed up with a languid stare that gradually shifted from Meriweather to Edward, silent laughter prodding at the corners of her mouth.
 
\"What? No, she doesn't mean that, we don't even... we keep separate beds!\" Edward sputtered uncomfortably, eyes darting between the accumulated faces lining the table.
 
Otra laid a hand on his shoulder, cooing at him softly, seemingly apathetic to anyone watching. \"Oh, but yours always looks so much warmer than mine does, Edward... the nights up north are far colder than I'm used to.\"
 
\"Please! You're embarrassing both of us.\"
 
\"Oh my, I embarrass you Edward?\" She leaned closer, lifting the hand from his shoulder to his cheek, smile broadening as she noted the budding warmth that raged uncontrollably beneath the surface of his skin.
 
\"Stop it.\" He muttered, breaking contact with an angry shrug. Otra cackled, mischief in her eyes, and settled back into her seat, resuming the casual twiddling of her hair.
 
He stared guiltily at Meriweather, pinching the bridge of his nose as though the expression of exasperation was something Edward considered an apology for their crass behaviour.
 
Meriweather let out a loud peal of laughter as he watched Otrallia torment Edward and he motioned towards Edward with his tankard, accompanied by a generous amount of ale spilling over the lip of it.
 
\"Well don't she have a way about her! A Cormyrean would have no idea what to do with a woman like that. I can make yer life a better one by takin' her off yer hands. She won't be complainin' of cold nights after that, I can tell yeh. It's been but a moment and I'm already rivvim with that one.\"
 
His attention moved back to Bigglesby and he gave him a simple nod of respect.
 
\"...and you got me feelin' better about all this business\"
 
The sound of Ciratto’s chair scraping back alerts you that he has risen from the table.
 
\"If we are to depart in the morning, I have work to do tonight. A good evening to you all. See you on the road.\"
 
With that he turns smoothly on his heel and heads out of the bar.
 
==Artagan’s Adieu==
 
Artagan watches the suggestive spectacle that Otra puts on from beneath the brim of his hat, looking down any time her eyes begin to turn his way. As she lets the poor Cormyrean off the hook, he takes a deep breath and scratches his stubbly face. From beneath his hat, one can hear the anxious noise of his fingers scratching away, and after she finishes, a single loud cluck of his tongue issues from the corner of his mouth, and his hat shakes in the slightest gesture of a 'no'.
 
He looks up when the chair of the slender Ciratto reports its occupant’s departure. Artagan just nods slightly at the man’s 'good evening', but his eyes narrow and follow him as he leaves the bar. He looks back to see that some of you have noticed his scrutiny of the man, to which he simply raises his eyebrows in a silent gesticulation that might read something like \"I dunno\" or \"strange feller\", but it is hard to say.
 
After a minute or so, Artagan himself stands, and shoulders the weather-beaten leather back pack that has been at his feet the entire time. His hands instinctively go down to his belt, where they gently touch the heads of the two axes tucked into the belt; the gesture is not threatenting, but rather seems like he needed to reassure himself that they were still there. With this, he pulls at the brim of his hat, and says \"g'night\". He looks at Otra with a profound look of deference, pulls his hat again and says \"M'Lady\", nearly as a question, and then turns to leave.
 
As he gets to the door of the inn, you see him touch the axes again. Satisfied, he opens the door and heads out into the night
