==Mirtul 25==

I will have to write quickly.  We have just repelled one regiment of Red Hand orcs, and have another yet to deal with.

Yesterday afternoon, as we were picking our way along an uncertain trail, lead by our inscrutable scout, Tyriol, we were nearly overrun by a fleeing horse carrying two children, Diay and Murron, residents of the settlement in in which we are now trapped and besieged.

These two youths, who have no business being out of doors by themselves in peaceable times, were fleeing from a small group of Red Hand orcs and barhgasts.  Their horse took a horrible tumble as it raced past us, and the children were both terribly hurt in the crash.  The younger of the two, Diay, has yet to recover.

After quickly dispatching their pursuers, and tending to the wounded dear little children, Murron lead us on the best track possible directly for Koh’Murron stables.

There isn’t time just not for my usual narrative and commentary, though I have much to say.  I will summarize quickly;

*The leader of this place is a barbarian named Kohr.  He dislikes me tremendously, and I him.  I am considering making him my vassal, as I really do like this place, and think I could profit by it.  He is a fiend with a sword, but his lack of wits will be his undoing.

*As we raced into the settlement, the drums of a great host of Red Hand were beating to the south, and we knew we would be set upon in a matter of hours.  Though fatigued from our journey, we made hasty greetings with the residents of the stables, and set about fortifying the place for the oncoming assault.

*There are a number of Loudwater Wardens here, and Tyriol knows one or two of them really well, and they all know his mother.  They say that the host of orcs assailing us is but the van of a much greater army that is on the other side of Falcon's Roost.  They report that there is a small group of wardens, including the lovely Maeghuen, holding the Roost, but that they are unsure if such a small group can hold it.

*There are two dwarves here of interest; a priest of Ohgma from the very monastery we are headed to named Hrogan Goringrimm, and an old soldier named Paetr Helgorod from the Roost.  Hrogan is, not surprisingly, a knowledgeable creature who was able to recite the entirety of the ballad that Tyriol started the other day.

*In the first wave, there were perhaps four or five hundred orcs, and we managed to repel them.  They did bring down the gate tower with me in it, and nearly cost me one of my lives.  I don’t know if Mattrick is just punishing me for some perceived fault, but he says he is unable to mend my leg and that I will be walking with a limp for the rest of my days.  We are still hedged in on two sides by a thousand or more orcs, seemingly united under the Red Hand of Doom banner.

*The ceaseless rains, which we have found out are in fact being maintained by unnatural means by the Cerulean Circle, can be thanked for the settlement not being burned to the ground.

*After the first wave, I took Tyriol into the woods to track down about a dozen men who had fled before the attack.  These are wardens that lost their courage, and I hope that I am able to compel them to return, and if not, to make some sport of them.  We need every set of hands we can get just now, however.  The drums are rolling, and the next wave might be…Set only knows what is coming.

*We never found the wardens, but did run into the Cerulean Circle villains again.  That was quite a meeting.  Of all things, the plant-aberration Bogrun recognized me, and called me ‘Kossur’ of all damned things.  He looks flammable enough, but I have more than a few loose ends to take care of now.  Thankfully, the awkward meeting was interrupted by a wyvern-riding orc, who seems to be the general or other high-ranking officer in this orcish rabble.  He scattered us pretty good, and we returned to the stables as fast as we cool.  Tyriol was not eaten.

While I think of it, I will inventory the mentionable residents of this settlement.
*Kohr, the leader of the place, and a mindless barbarian
*Suriy, Kohr’s stunning and regal wife, who hails from, if I am not mistaken, Chult.  I shall have to dig through my memories for the proper greeting for nobility from Chult from my expedition there over a century ago.  I wonder if they still speak the same language?
*Pima, the oldest daughter of the two above, who is the mother of the injured Diay that we rescued; I have yet to receive suitable thanks for rescuing her wayward brat, and shall surely take it some day
*Victra, the youngest daughter, and most comestible bit of flesh within 100 miles
*Murron, Kohr's only boy and a troublesome one at that.  He has shown an interest in magic and in Radha; he pays close attention to her when she is trying to instruct him on the use of his burgeoning art, and even closer attention when she turns around or walks away
*Cayd, Ender (ions?!) brother.  Another halfling in my midst.  This one has all of Ender’s malevolence in his eye, but none of the adventure.  He is a career-thug, I surmise, and though he tried to get in my way once, I warned him that to do so again would spell his death.  He has tripped over himself getting out of my path ever since.
*Zita, a hired stable-hand who avoided us as best she could

The drums are getting louder.  It is time to take arms.
