Speculation on Zamomel Stass by Loremaster Sigellius Marbrandt in 1321 DR, The Year of Chains – Marked as argumentative and speculative by Loremaster Tindral Narwok in 1345 DR, The Year of the Saddle.

thumb

Much like the west has their Elminster, the east is no different and where society feels the need to bolster themselves with legends of a powerful mage that always manages to appear on the eve of destruction and save them all. Zamomel Stass fits that role.  I would like to theorize that a number of mages have taken up the mantle of Zamomel Stass over the centuries with references as early as 1121 DR.  Zamomel Stass has been “killed” , “destroyed”, “burnt to cinders”, “banished” and “devoured” as many times as their are drunken tongues to be found in a tavern.  Clearly Aglarond needs a Zamomel Stass much like the Sword Coast needs its Elminster.  I have reviewed as many notes from Brother Broca as I can locate and  I currently possess documentation that supports Zamomel Stass was not present at the battle that involved the destruction of Szass Tam and the eventual creation of Tam becoming the all-powerful lich that he is today.  There are far too many tales and far too many questions with no answers to be had to suggest that Zamomel Stass is little more than legend or a number of foolhardy but well-meaning individuals that fancy themselves heroes.}}
