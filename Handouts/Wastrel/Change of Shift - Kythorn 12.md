As Radha and Kethoth are about to spell off Volund and Tyriol for watch, outside the doors to the Nexus, the tiefling approaches Volund as he is organizing his gear.

\"Mr. Heimdall! It has been a while since we discussed your plans. With the army of the Hand of doom waylaid and probably sundered, the Monastery free of the clutches of Huu’rom ak Murul, I wonder what you suppose your organization's position on mine will be. Do you suppose they feel that our obligations in these mountains are completely fulfilled?

\"I do not ask to see if or when we will be free of your company. We have benefited greatly from your expertise with your blade and your magic, and I would have you to the very end of this task. I do often wonder why you leave such a wake of frost periodically. We could discuss your condition, and perhaps I could lend some insight.\"

==Volund Plays Coy==

Volund glances up at the wizard while he continues to fix up his gear and says,

\"The Cerulean Circle - and more specifically, Ghedorh - can at times be unpredictable and harsh, Kethoth. The High Circle sees the world in a different light than you or I, what their verdict will be is beyond me. However, if my opinion holds any weight with them, I feel that the recent events of the Saharel Seven have redeemed yourselves and I consider your debt to nature, and to the region, repaid. We have done all that we could, more than what was asked in fact, in way-laying the orc invasion and saving the monastery - the rest will be up to the Circle and the peoples of the Gray Vale.

\"My current plans are to see this quest to the end, I have gone this far with the company and have grown fond of them. I have even come to not despise you so much that I say, rather than spit, your name, Kethoth. After this quest is over I intend to leave the Circle first and foremost. I have other familial matters to attend to that may keep me with the company if our paths stay the same course.\"

With his packs in order he stands up and addresses Kethoth's question,

\"I won't go in-depth into my history, Kethoth, but to sate your curiosity, know that I made a rash choice in my youth and I am paying the consequences for it.
