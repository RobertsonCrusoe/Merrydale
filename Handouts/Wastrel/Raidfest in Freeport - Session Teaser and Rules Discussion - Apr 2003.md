================================================================================
PART 1 — Todd Burry, 18 Apr 2003
================================================================================

Todd teases the upcoming "Raidfest in Freeport" adventure — the annual festival
where pirates shop for ships and supplies before hurricane season. The three-day
festival features: auction day, merry-making, and a ceremony with blessings from
the deities Valker and Umberlee.

Todd introduces an unconventional XP mechanic: "come to the table with a short
legend of the seas." He explicitly asks Stephen Robertson to contribute even
though Robertson cannot play (Robertson is at University of Toronto at the time).

================================================================================
PART 2 — Casey Bauman-Wike
================================================================================

Casey calls the XP-for-legends idea "great" and comments that it's "very
Amber-ish."

================================================================================
PART 3 — Les Blackwell (ALL CAPS as always)
================================================================================

Les responds with a massive 8-point rules manifesto covering campaign mechanics
and player concerns:

1. CROSS-CLASS SKILLS — Agrees with KC (Casey).

2. BOAT ADVENTURES — Will not disadvantage armor-clad characters; leaves "hard-
   core tales of the sea" to Todd and Robertson.

3. SORCERERS AND METAMAGIC FEATS — [Position stated but not elaborated].

4. SCROLLS — Detailed analysis of scroll economics at different character levels.
   Mentions two wizards he has played: Salazar and Kethoth, "both good guys."
   Notes that Kethoth carries approximately 20 scrolls at all times.

5. NEUTRALITY — Notes that Todd is apparently annoyed about neutral characters.

6. MYSTIC THEURGE — States: "this character looks more like Beljuril than any I
   have seen yet."

7. WAR IN EASTWITCH — Asks whether people didn't like the Greyshield Lodge
   battle against "a hoarde of Oruk."

8. HASTE AND MAGES — Emphatic statement: "I will be MADDER'N'FUCK if there is
   some rule that says a hasted mage can't cast two spells/round."

Les closes with a longer statement about group dynamics and fairness:

"DND MAIL IS FUN, BUT SOMETIMES FRUSTRATING. SCUN AND KC SEEM TO SPEAK A
DIFFERENT LANGUAGE AT TIMES, AND TODD IS JUST PLAIN ECCENTRIC AND ALWAYS MAKING
SPECIAL INTEREST CASES. I AGREE, THOUGH, THAT BLACK FEMALE BARDS SHOULD BE PAID
JUST AS MUCH XP AND GOLD AS WHITE MALE BARDS."

NOTES
================================================================================

This thread captures a moment when the Freeport and Eastwitch campaigns were
running concurrently. Stephen Robertson was enrolled at University of Toronto
(Stephen.Robertson@utoronto.ca); despite being unable to attend sessions, he
remained part of the campaign correspondence and contributed ideas.

Key character references: "Beljuril" is Casey Bauman-Wike's character/handle.
Salazar and Kethoth are both Les Blackwell's wizard characters. "The Gaffer"
and "the Wastrel" are Freeport campaign elements that appear in later
narratives.

Les's ALL-CAPS communication style is preserved verbatim throughout, reflecting
his characteristic voice in the campaign email archive. The tone shows the group
managing rules questions and campaign tone collaboratively, with clear
personalities and preferences.

The thread reveals that by April 2003, the group had accumulated enough campaign
mechanics discussions to warrant explicit rule-setting — particularly around
class balance, magical item economics, and character alignment.
