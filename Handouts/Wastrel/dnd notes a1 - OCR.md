Battle Upon The Sea of Fallen Stars

It is the stormy season in the early spring, and no time to be upon the Sea of Fallen

Stars for many mariners. But, for the Wastrel and her crew, no storm has proved her
master, and she claws her way through wave after’ wave upon. this. relentless,
tumultuous sea, And she is not alone.

For a day and a half, a black-sailed three-masted ship has been pursuing the Wastrel,

and it seems that a captain of great skill is at her command. The Wastrel can outrace

any ship ever made in a straight-out run in almost any waters, But the stormy waters

that she sails this day require a constant effort of navigation and piloting, and here,

her crew finds that she has met her betters. The black sails find a few cable’s gain

with every masterful tack, and it is not long before she is only 100 cables behind the

Wastrel, and the anxious pirates aboard her can be seen through the rain and foam,
slavering upon the decks, awaiting their next prize.

But the Wastrel is no easy prize. She is manned by some of the most notable men and

women of the Realms, few though they be. The crew is all above decks, and look on at

the frustrated faces of the Captain and Master. The faces of these men are stern, but

betray something of the failure they feel in their hearts. They have been unable to

pilot this vessel, the very ship of Leto Cook, better than the antiquated Galleon that

gains on her. And now the time for running is at an end, and the Captain lets loose
his crew to fight in the manner they know best.

of the North East. They speak mostly the language of the Damarans, which was the
language that prospered after the destruction of Raumathar and Narfell. They speak,

too, a little of the Rashemi language, which you have seen them do with May. None of

them speak Ancient Nar. They do not speak Ancient Nar in Narfell today, in fact! But,
these girls speak it. Or, rather, Aurora speaks it for them.

“And she calls Arcane in ancient Nar, though not many would recognize it. Not even

fellow sorcerers would have recognized it, as the repertoire of the verbal arcane is a

bricollage of a hundred cultures. But Kethoth noticed, because none now call Arcane
in Ancient Nar. None save those three girls.”
