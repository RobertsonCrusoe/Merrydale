Taking place in the library of the Monastery of the Precipice. Shortly after arriving at the monastery and when people had separated and \"did their own thing\" of sorts in the evening.

In the seclusion of the library, Mattrick speaks in the calm, strong voice that he's known for. Either he's confident in the fact that he and Baird are alone or he is simply unconcerned with who may or may not be listening. Giving Baird a direct look as he speaks...

\"You have been keeping council with the tiefling of late. Do I need to be concerned?\"

\"You needn't worry, just keeping tabs on the fiend. We discussed our reasons for travelling to these ruins and neither of us told our true intention, just vague generalities. He ceded that my question would be of 'greater importance' and showed me the seventh analect then told me how he came of it. I spent some time trying to decipher the riddles, but could not.

\"I am more worried about his inevitable treachery. He could not be so inept and naive as to the purpose of the obelisk we carry. I had no idea how smooth-tongued the abyss had become, and the five of them are eating it up. He is well enough to have at your back in a fight but I just can't trust him in that room with the Lady Saharel.

\"Perhaps he will prove himself, I hope he will, but I am prepared to do what I must to ensure that the glory of Aglarond does not fall to that damned Lich.\" Baird finishes with a curse at that unholy name.

\"Well...I worry. That's what I do. Have you told Sepret specifically of what we seek? Have you told him we seek the Horns of Eltab?\"

\"No\", replied Baird \"the reason we're here is to 'help save Aglarond', I made sure not to go into specifics.\"

\"Good...good. I am afraid that his odd appearance has me predisposed to expecting the worst when it comes to him. Despite the fact that he comes off as a bit pompous and clearly has little regard for putting an entire forest to flame, he seems to be pleasant enough and has been more than generous with coin and supplies.\"

Mattrick Continues; \"I must admit that I was concerned. I was worried you were becoming too focused on simply completing the task and had begun to forget about who and what you are. As much as we are on a task for our homeland we are also here to exert Kelemvor's influence on those around us. Your disinclination to assist with the funerary rites of the farmer that was tortured and unjustly killed disappointed me, Baird. I had that slip of a girl that knows nothing of our ways or world for that matter assist with the burial. An eladrin, assisting with the burial of a murdered innocent when a paladin of Kelemvor is too busy to assist with such.\"

\"Your concern is understandable, brother ... but the burden of our task has not, nor ever will cause me to forget our teachings or who I am. I have respect for the dead, but I have never been comfortable with reading over the fallen and you know that. If that has disappointed you, so be it. I chose to become the arm of the Lord of Death, cleansing our world of those arrogant enough to control life and death. Not his mouth.

\"If you don't remember, that dragonborn and I dug the farmer's grave. You cared for the bereaved, so do not lecture me on my duties and spare me the burden of yours. Should either fall on this quest the other's mantle will be taken up. But until that day comes I will display the tenants of our faith with my hammer, not with your prayers.

\"If I have sated your concerns, brother, lets see what books can tell us of Saharelgard.\"

Smiling and clapping his hand on Baird's shoulder ruggedly, Mattrick continues;

\"Ever the arrow of our God, aye? Stick to swinging your weapon and I will continue to be the stage that the Grey Lord stands upon and lets his message roll forth.

\"...and yes. Let us seek more information about this place. This monastary seems like no monastary I have ever been a part of and I am concerned there is far more to these ruins than any of us suspect.\"
