“But, please, Jalael,” he says holding up a hand, “I mean no disrespect, but how on earth do you think that sounds to us? You would lead us into a place you have never been? To potentially interact with a culture you know nothing about? While the sentiment is valiant, the idea is laughable. No, the blind will not lead the blind into the darkness. It is your right, though, to volunteer your own life to keep Kethoth company in the Underdark. The rest of us will decide on our own, after we have secured the horns and received what is rightfully ours by the agreement we brokered with Zamomel Stass. An agreement Kethoth feels he can augment after we have risked our lives.” Turning back to Kethoth, “And for the third time, that promise stands, wizard. Do not forget it and spare me your petty threats, I do not fear you. If it comes down to it, the seven will stand behind me before the yield to your extortion.”

Continuing to speak at Kethoth, “I will not put on airs for your sake or anyone else's. This matter will be resolved one way or the other. You will change your ways or you will be made to change your ways. While I will continue to fight alongside you, know that my back will never be toward you.”

===But Volund Holds Back===

The look on Volund's face is one of disgust. His thoughts wander as he tries to piece all of this together. He has grown tired of these half-truths and outright lies. He has grown tired of those who compromise their morality out of ignorance, naivety, and oaths. He half wants to wash his hands of these fools. To leave them to their fate at the whims of this tiefling.

'This tiefling', He thinks to himself, clenching his jaws, 'the one who cannot understand how much his actions influence those around him. The one who burns acres of forest, helping to destabilize an entire region, only to gnash his teeth at this who would call for justice. The one who, as I hear it, cast the first stone at the Mercykillers resulting in the death of Baird and the near-death of Tyriol. The one who would seek to press on, further into the echoing halls of the nexus into the veritable den of those gibbering creatures and certainly to our deaths. And now, he is the one demanding we venture with him into the Underdark. The audacity!'

'No,' is the answer his mind settles on. He would not be able to forgive himself for leaving the party to the wizard. 'How the party has managed to survive this long is simply beyond me, but I believe Kethoth's actions have finally taxed even the grace of the gods. We are doomed.'

==Jalael is Just Saying...==

The drow does not move this time.. he merely speaks.

“Volund, we DO have books, we DO have supplies. And a Cormyrian who has never been to Cormyr is a better guide than a Sembian who grew up there.”

“That being said, the Moonstars actions have never been a captains choice. The Lady decides. And where she sends you, you WILL go. The Moonstars is not a 'cloak on - cloak off' organization. I trust you in this, or I would not have been permitted to make you the offer.”

“As for the Wizard, to me he is nothing. He may lead a few of this party, and to that party the Moonstars are obliged, but he alone decides nothing. Dismiss his irrationality for the flippant meanderings of a too old tiefling and have done with it, BUT please accept he will be of value in the battle to come.”

His tone was conversational, neither bravado nor sarcasm could be sensed.

==Kethoth is Amused==

Kethoth gives a bemused look of surprise as Volund continues. As Volund is stating \"And for the third time, that promise stands, wizard. Do not forget it and spare me your petty threats, I do not fear you. If it comes down to it, the seven will stand behind me before the yield to your extortion.\" Kethoth is nearly laughing silently, and saying \"Oh, indeed indeed indeed! Do carry on, sir...\"

Continuing to speak at Kethoth, Volund says \"I will not put on airs for your sake or anyone else's. This matter will be resolved one way or the other. You will change your ways or you will be made to change your ways. While I will continue to fight alongside you, know that my back will never be toward you.\"

Kethoth continues to smile and laugh silently, nodding encouragement. \"It is good you aren't afraid of me, Volund. We need stout hearts in the battles to come, and you are well-advised never to trust either a wizard, nor a tiefling, nor a tiefling wizard least of all.\" Kethoth nods assent to his own declaration of his untrustworthiness, and continues.

\"Well, I suppose I have a good deal of that coming, and even though you have crossed the only line I have ever drawn for you, yet I cannot hold to my threats, Volund. You've proven yourself too useful and true so far, even if you say you don't trust me. I would be a poor leader indeed, if I let fights happen amongst us before the battle of our lives, so I'll indulge your frustrations, and even encourage you to speak your mind, if only to get the blood in your cheeks and make you ready for the upcoming fight.

\"Anyway, perhaps more of the men need to speak their minds, eh? I might not get so fiery when they do, if I were more accustomed to it.

\"To that end, let me address your concerns. Some of this I have already said, but I will make sure your inventory of displeasure is accounted for.

\"First of all, it is my chief goal to get everyone out of here alive. However, we are going to attempt to retrieve the two artefacts we have come for, but not to the reckless extent of using up our fixed and limited time.

\"Secondly, the matter of Mattrick. We will exploit the resources of the Lady and the Tower to inquire about my lost friend. If he is alive and well, we are going after him. Perhaps not you, Volund, but I am sure that I will not be alone going to look for him. Nor will we be unprepared. Goodness no!\" Kethoth chuckles and points to the stack of chests that have accompanied him through this entire adventure.

\"I have dragged a block and tackle through hell, to the ire of many. What makes you think that I would be unprepared for a descent to the Underdark? We will not be jumping blindly into a chasm and crossing our fingers. I am not going to dive into the minutiae of that endeavour, but suffice it to say, we will prepare as best we can.

\"Finally, I must address your questioning the value of Mattrick as a strategic goal. Let us say that Mattrick was nothing more than another object we were after; no flesh, soul, friendship or loyalty. Just a...thing. He is a thing that will help us assess the value of this man, Zammomel Stass, and provide one more layer of insurance that the Horns are not delivered unto the wrong hands. For this value alone, I would go after him. But to be level and honest, that is a distant second-rated concern behind getting my friend, a man who has kept alive nearly every one of the people sleeping around us, out of the grave.

\"Now, you can continue to argue that he is no importance, and that somehow your share in this endeavour is compromised by this mission. And for the last time, I free you of any obligation to assist us in rescuing him, if indeed he is still alive. We will even leave the Horns top-side so that in the event of our failure and demise, you can continue the journey to Waterdeep and cash out the Horns yourself.

\"But, make no mistake; if I think he is alive, I am going after him. I won't be alone.\"

\"Now, do you have any other grievances that cannot wait until after we have slain Moghus?\"

==Volund Finally Relaxes==

Volund finally relaxes after listening to Kethoth's reasoning. Finally, some headway. He speaks to the wizard, \"I find we are now in agreement on many of these issues. If the Lady of the Tower has the means to scry Mattrick's current state and provide us with the necessities and knowledge needed for the rescue, I will accompany you into those who go into the chasm. If, however, she does not, I will not be following you to my death unless we travel to back to civilization to find the equipment and supplies we need first.\"

He continues, \"It would also be prudent to leave the horns top side with those who remain behind. Or in the event we all go down there, someone we can all agree on. That way the horns are not lost to Aglarond should those who go into the Underdark stay there.\"

\"Even though I only knew him a short while, I do value Mattrick. If he is alive and our provisions in order I will not hesitate to help the man. I would be less inclined to risk so much in order to retrieve a corpse, even of one such as he. But my primary argument was one of preparedness and seeing how you have assured me that the provisions will not be an issue, then I have no reason not to accompany you. Provided what you say is true when the time comes.\"

Volund stands and walks over to one of the ooze-covered walls and draws Shyr'rach. With a thought frost begins to climb the steel until it is entombed in ice. A cloud of freezing vapour can be seen with every breathe, \"I may have some ideas about what we can do about this Moghus.\" He says, touching the blade to the wall and watches to see if the arctic temperatures have any effect on the black ooze.

Kethoth nods his head in solemn approval.

\"Everything seems to be in order then. Let's set about planning on how we are going to skin this devil, Moghus...\"
