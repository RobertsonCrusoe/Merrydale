\"Young, lady\" Kethoth says through a smile as he approaches this woman in the furnace room. \"Kethoth Sepret, at your service\" and he bows deeply.

\"Come, do let us hear how you fit into this colourful story. You have an intriguing surname, and if you tell me something about yourself, I shall tell you something of yourself as well.\"

Miri is a slight woman, possibly more girl than woman but carries herself as if she were a barbarian warlord out of Narfell. She has black hair, closely cropped and resting just above her ears. Her eyes are pale blue, verging on silvery-white and as black as her hair is, her skin is pale. Despite her pale skin, she seems healthy and moves with a sense of purpose and confidence. Out of everyone rescued from the torture, she seems to be the least affected by the entire experience.

Miri is quiet for quite some time as she looks at Kethoth's face without meeting his eyes. A strange sound comes from her, almost as if she's sucking on her teeth in contemplation before she finally meets the wizard's gaze and she speaks in a less than enthusiastic tone. Her upper lip curled just slightly enough to look like a sneer.

\"I live here...tiefling. Now; your turn.\"

Kethoth either does not notice the acrimony in this new acquaintance, or, more likely, pretends not to notice, but you would never know the difference.

\"Young lady, the last man I met with your surname is tied closely to this very monastery. I don't suppose Gaebrel is a relation of yours, is he? He and I had some common friends many years ago, and I met him twice. To be honest, he liked me about as much as you at first, but he warmed to me eventually. He was a good, if somewhat untethered man.\"

She lifted a single dark eyebrow in surprise as Kethoth spoke. Clearly taken aback by how old Kethoth would have to be to have been an acquaintance of her great, great grandfather. The gears turning in her head could be easily seen by the likes of Kethoth and he could see the moment where she decided internally that stranger things had happened during and after the Spellplague, the least of which being a tiefling with an unusually long lifespan.

Her pale eyes narrowed slightly before taking a step closer to Kethoth. Her voice lowering to give the conversation a more intimate atmosphere.

\"Aye...he is...was. A grandfather far removed, and I know he did some good things and I know he did what some think are terrible things.\"

She raised her right hand, palm facing upward and as close as Kethoth was standing, he heard a faint sound, like air being sucked out of a small area. Accompanying the \"whooshing sound\", was a fist-sized ball of dark energy coalescing in her palm. She slowly clenched her fist and the dark ball began to swirl around her fist and form into an eight inch triangular shape at the end of her fist. She held up her hand, sheathed in dark energy leaving the whispy, shadowy blade hovering in the air between herself and Kethoth as she spoke.

\"What I know of him, I've learned from the library here. Some of it good, and some of it bad but I've been told that this...this here is his legacy.\"

Kethoth gives the slightest smile as he watches the shadowy blade form on Miri's hand. He gives a sly wink to her, and whispers \"You're a fortunate girl. Let us keep your wonderful gift to ourselves, though, shall we?\"

She scowls momentarily, turning the delicate features of her face into something still as delicate but darker.

\"There is no secret to keep, mage. The ones left...\"

Jerking her head to the side and over her shoulder in the direction of the surviving priests.

\"They all know. It's why the Selûnite is here and why I'm given the illusion of freedom.\"

\"Ahh. Well, we'll have to talk about your gifts and your freedom when all this is done, Ms. Tremalkin. I find you as captivating as you are mysterious. 

\"Are you planning on staying here with the wounded? You are welcome to accompany me if you desire it.\"

Miri gives Kethoth a somewhat nervous look but the air of over-confidence that she was displaying only moments before, faltered a moment as she simply nodded her head and gave Kethoth a hopeful but conflicted look.

\"I...I do not know. This...this monastery is all I know. I will not be going with you into the monastery. I will stay here and protect the brothers and sisters. They are the only family I have, even if they are also my keepers.\"

\"I laud your decisions, Ms. Tremalkin. Stay. Protect these people. Who knows what dangers might visit this place once we begin our assent? When all this business is completed, and I am set to leave, I will ask you again if you would like to come. Take some time to think about your answer to that question.\"

With that, Kethoth gives her a warm toothy smile, and begins to check his gear, tightening straps on bags, and checking his pockets. He then begins a short ritual, at the end of which a glowing disc of energy appears, upon which he throws some of the heavier bags and gear that seem to accompany him into every dungeon. He gives it all one last check, grabs his staff, taps it twice on the ground and looks around the room at all of you.

\"Everyone just about ready to go?\" he says, less like a question and more like an order.
