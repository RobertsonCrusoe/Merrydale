==A Sort of Vacation ==

A long pier stretched out into the shallow waters of the sea, ending just before the island’s shelf dropped off into deep dark waters. Lined with boathouses of all sorts of shapes and sizes, the pier was home to maybe a hundred private yachts. Today the pier was empty except for three men standing by one of the houses.

“What’s Lydon got in store for us today?” said a rather ugly spiky haired man with perhaps a trace of orc blood as he puffed on a hand-rolled cigar.

“He’s takin us to see his ol’ friend — a salt by the name of Gaffer” replied a shortish man. If it weren’t for the countless charms around his neck and the fine sword at his side this man would look just like a castaway with his unkempt scraggily black hair and beard.

“A sort of vacation, Salazar,” said the last man. He was leaning up against the boat house with a posture that was anything but leisurely. It was no wonder that he was referred to as the Mysterious Amnyard by many around the city.

== A Mysterious Woman ==

At that moment a middle-aged man strolled up two armfuls of supplies. He was definitely an old sailor — his grey curly bear with no moustache and single gold earring were a dead giveaway. He was wearing a fine red jacket that was open at the front to allow his generous belly to poke through.

Accompanying him was a woman. She looked as though she was a native Rashemi, but she had no wildness about her at all. Instead she was an example of pure refinement from her attire right down to her gait. As enigma as she was there was no denying that she was one of the most beautiful women that a man could ever lay eyes on.

“How are we today me hearties!” belted the old sailor.

“Not good Lydon ya old salt; it’s well past morning,” replied the ugly man with the cigar.

“Yarr, what’s the point in getting up earlie when ther be no world ta save lad? Anyway take these supplies off me hands and we can have us some introductions. This be Maevreal, a new friend o’ mine. She’ll be coming wit us.”

“Salazar, “said the man with the cigar in a manner that had a forced coolness about it. This man had a hard time dealing with people in general let alone a woman as beautiful as this.

“Lendon at your service senora,“ said the Mysterious Amnyard, always the professional.

“Zechary Mudge m’am, and might I say ye be as pretty as the settin' sun herself,” said the scraggily castaway.

“Charmed to meet you all,” replied the lady in a delicate, relaxing voice.

“Let us be underway lads and lass,” cried Lydon as he unlocked the boat at got everyone aboard.

== Underway ==

Getting everyone ready to sail when there is no world to save takes more time than when there is a prodigious task at hand. Indeed leaving at noon may be the gloss that a sailor gives to this word we call vacation. But frankly who cares? The adventurers all had enough gold in their purses to buy a hearty enough breakfast to pay for the night before. Indeed they all had enough gold in their purses to buy a hearty enough night before; to sample in the arcane caterings of nobles that have kept many a wizard from crafting items of war and protection that adventurers prefer. Besides asks Lydon, “What are ye, a bunch of farmers!?”

And so they set off around noon someone would note if there were any proper clocks around. One last voyage aboard Lydon’s rickety little dinghy with a single mast. Of course it has brand new brass railings and a new mithril nameplate, new silk sails and a new wheel of antediluvian oak, but it is still a rickety old lugger.

One could draw comparisons to the curator of Lady Pict’s bordello in Innarlith. Of course she wears enchanted gem dust around her eyes to make them glitter with endless beauty. She colours her lips with dye distilled from the blood of virgin nymphets that makes her very words curl the toes of man and woman alike. She anoints her breasts with oils extracted from celestial unicorn whales found only on the plane of Avandor that make her breasts perk and dance in generous rituals of lavish fertility. But alas Lady Pict is still a lich and no sane person would spend even a copper to look between her rancid hams.

Well, perhaps that comparison is a little to unfond, for the Dinghy sails well in its own sort of way, especially at the hands of its long time master Captain Lydon. He handles the dipping lines for the single lugsail with ease as he chats and points out scenes and creatures all the while tending to this task and that.

== The Salty Dog's Tale ==

During the voyage he tells the adventurer’s a little more about this Gaffer:

“His name is Bollyswag Gaffer, but everyone calls him 'the Gaffer'. He and I had a few adventures together back in the day.
Now he’s retired and owns a small archipelago about a half days sail away. Should be a fun time and is a great weekend trip out to his neck of the sea.

He was as gleeful a sea dog as any in his day but navigation was what he was used fer. It was him that showed me how to sail to the Isle of the Lich, an island that not many a salt can get to.

Now he uses his divinations to locate wrecks at the bottom of the sea. He raises ‘em and restores them as a sort of hobby. He’s good at his trade too, being an old patient fart with nothing else to do in his retirement. He always enters one into the Raidfest auction. I’m wondering what he’ll give us this year.

He’s a bit salty these days, but no more salty than Salazar I’d say.”

== The Gaffer's Archipelago ==

Noon turns into afternoon and then begins to turn into sunset and the sky lights up in brilliant hues of reds, oranges, and pinks. The dinghy sails towards a distantly cloudy sky. It’s funny how these clouds that are so beautiful now will bring in the hurricanes later. The Dinghy glides across the perfect mirrored glass surface of the water as the adventurers approach the Gaffer's archipelago.
At first, small protrusions of volcanic rock begin to poke out of the water in the distance. Then those protrusions become small rocky islands that begin to form a maze for the channels of water between them. Captain Lydon winks in and says, “Watch closely if ye want ter find ye way out me hearties.”

As he guides the Dinghy through the channels the small rocky islets give way to larger islets with beaches and then still bigger ones with lush tropical trees. Large colourful birds and small tropical mammals watch the boat as they lounge in their topical paradise beneath the setting sun.

== The Musty-Bottomed Flop ==

Just before twilight a light shines in the distance. “There she be,” says Lydon. As the boat gets closer there is a smaller flashing light closer to shore and one soon makes out the outline of a small villa and shipyard. Approaching one of the docks there is a small flashing light being reflected to the boat from a mirror being held by a man on the dock. He is rotating the mirror back and forth so that it flashes as the angle between the boat and the lighthouse hits the right spot. The man starts laughing and hollering, giving gestures that may be construed as rude if he didn’t appear to have such a friendly disposition. On his shoulder is a strange bird that has the colouring and size of a parrot, but the shape of an owl. It flaps its wings and lifts off the man’s shoulder as he waves his arm too frantically for a stable perch.

“Yer late little Lydon. Did ya get lost or were ya busy flappin yer pecker,” says the man in a very geezerly voice.

“Ahoy to ye too Gaffer, ye old musty-bottomed flop!”

Lydon gives the introductions as the boat pulls up and disembarks.

“These are me friends that I’ve told ye bout.”

The Gaffer grasps each of the adventurers hand with both of his, giving them a hearty warm handshake, all the while shaking frantically himself on his old brittle frame.

The Gaffer is a little old human of around 90 years of age. His eyes bulge out of his face a little because his old skin has lost the elasticity to keep them in. His hair is white and wiry; some of it is pulled back into a tarred queue while shorter bits stick out every which way. He smiles a lot showing that he has few teeth. He has skinny legs, a pot belly, a rather pronounced hunching posture and large boney hands. The Gaffer wears an indigo jacket showing wear around the sleeves and collar and white breeches and waistcoat. He has a small linen brimless cap atop his head, the kind of under cap that captains usually wear beneath their tricorns. He wears some jewelry after the pirate fashion, but not as much as Zech.

“Aye, did ya bring the feckin' thing lad?”

“I did, I did,” says Lydon as he pulls out a bottle of what looks to be a bottle of twelve hundred year old whiskey.
Gaffer belts a small cantrip and the cork pops off and lands in his breast pocket. He then grabs a small cup from beneath his cap and pours himself a shot. Taking the shot he has a small coughing fit.

“That’s the good piss. We’ll all be having some tonight. I may even have a gem left somewhere that will duplicate this cum. Anyway, common inside for a bit you lot.”

== The Gaffer's Ships ==

The Gaffer owns a small port where the Dinghy has been docked. It looks sort of like one of those boom ports that poked up around the Whalebones during the big adamantine boom of some years past. After the precious metal had gone away so did the industry and the ports were left to the poor indigenous communities that made the ports into small shanty towns.

There are plenty of misshapen and odd angled floating docks around. Moored up around them are many old boats, some without masts, some without castles, all not seaworthy.

“They’re bloody parts ships!” says the Gaffer as he sees Salazar scanning his cluttered port. He points him over to a couple of giant barges that are in better shape. One floats atop two giant pontoons. It has a hoist like device over a tall arch in the middle, a well suited machine for pulling wrecks up from the ocean floor.

== The Bachelor's Box ==

He leads the group up a small hill toward the villa stepping on scattered stones that appear to be stairs. The villa consists of a stout lighthouse and a small mansion, both made of marble. The door to the house contains no lock, nor a door knob. This old bachelor wizard has no worry of inconveniencing anyone with a mage lock for his own exclusive use.

The door opens into a small sitting room that is littered with crates covered in canvas. He leads the group through this room into a large open room that is equally cluttered. Crates and whatnot pile up the sides of the wall, the place looks like a ship’s hold after a storm. However it smells pleasant and is free of dust and any grime and interesting knick knacks are set atop those boxes. The place isn’t messy but rather decorated by an eccentric old sailor. Large throne like chairs dot the room, each one different but extravagant and tucked away in its own nook or cranny. A room this large has never looked so cozy.

“Have a seat while I look for my box, mates!”

Salazar sits on a twisted vine-framed thing with absolutely no symmetry in any dimension. Its cushions are thick patches of moss.
May perches atop a narrow phandar wood chair with navy velvet cushions riveted to the chair with duskwood rivets.

Zech jumps back onto a pudgy silken hammock swinging between two statues of naked breasted mermaids.

Lendon leans against a solid gold throne about the size of one of the statues from the Isle of the Lich. Its cushions are made from intricate layers of peacock-like feathers.

Lydon sits in a semi-sphere with its concave side facing out and titled on an angle. It is about fifteen feet in diameter and is covered with all manners of cushions.

While the adventurers are getting themselves comfortable the Gaffer putters around the room lifting up the odd box and swearing to himself. Finally he looks atop a large shelf and smiles.

“Yarr! Fuck yeh! Lendon, you’re standing; why don’t you fetch me that box atop that shelf.”

Lendon gives a slight nod and runs clear up the wall and brings the box down for the Gaffer.

“Yarr, that’s a good lad. Let’s see that box.”

The Gaffer opens the box and pulls out small tumblers, each differently shaped but more or less the same sized crystal. He hands out the tumblers and pulls out a large decanter and a small pouch. He shakes the pouch into the decanter and out pop several gems into the decanter. When the whiskey is poured into the decanter it doubles in sized for each gem that was there.

“Right then tip your cups and I’ll pour you all some of this tits great grog. Let’s have a toast then: To pussy!” belts the Gaffer.

“To plunder!” Salazar shouts.

“To gold!” Lendon yells.

“To manners...” Mae says.

“To the wind at me back!” Lydon roars.

“Ta a smooth sea and a clear night!” Zech belts to end the toast and everyone drinks.

The whiskey singes as it floods down the holy throat. The burning proceeds to branch out and make its way to the brain, fingertips, and areas of most unmentionable affairs. In one way the drink is like liquid balefire, but in another way it is like the first time one makes passionate love with the nymph of his dreams. It is a good drink.

The drink is welcomed by a dry coughing fit from the group while Zechary yells out, “I’m blind! I’m blind!\"

“That’s alright lad,” claims the Gaffer. “Some people have that reaction when getting their cherry popped on this piss.\"

The Gaffer claps Zech on the back and Zech begins to blink, testing out his recovered eyesight.

“I figure we’d go out on my latest project and make a night of it. Why, did you want to head up into the hills and bugger the feral cows again?”
