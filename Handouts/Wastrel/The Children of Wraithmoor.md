Yar! Salazar is an olde salte, make ye no mistake. Motherless son of a pirate, he has learned many a harsh tale in his day, and here be one.
----

Know you of the Isle of Wraithmoor, and the cursed ship SS Craven? 

Let me tell you. 

In the Year of the Burnt Banner, the SS Craven of the Wavesilver Family of Waterdeep set out from Calimport laden heavily with spices and breadfruit trees.

She wasn't long out of port when she was confronted by the Luskan pirate house Branigan aboard the dreaded Venomspray. Captain Branigan himself was captaining the ‘Spray and if you knew Branigan you knew that the souls upon the Craven were in great peril.

The ‘Spray harried the Craven along the sword coast halfway to Waterdeep when Talos himself blew up such a storm that they say even soaked the sands of Anauroch. Well, the tempest blew the two ships together, and they collided with such a din that Talos himself took notice of the tremendous sound of that collision amidst the tempest's wail.

Well, the sturdy cog of the shipwrights’ guild of Waterdeep design was more than a match for the fast but light Luskan raker. The heavily laden Craven had only 2 feet of beam, she was that low. The ‘Spray's bow struck the Craven's beam solid and low, and the Craven may have been a rocky shoal to the ‘Spray, she shivered so.

The two ships were locked, the ‘Spray broken and awaiting her scuttle. Her sundered bow planks and bowsprit were a wooden mangled mess that had the look of a broken-toothed pike weakly biting into a big old sea turtle.

The pirates of the ‘Spray knew she would founder, and they scurried, undisciplined, to the bow to amble onto the deck of the Craven like so many mice. But the crew of the Craven were a pack of cats to those mice, and while the pirates were funnelled one at a time over the broken bowsprit on the deck of the Craven, the Craven's crew formed a gauntlet and cut them down piecemeal while Talos made the seas roil and tore at the skies.

As a great streak of lightning rent the blackened skies, all saw the illuminated form of the maddened captain Branigan leap into the tempestuous seas, and all thought him to be swimming straight to Umberlee.

Before long, the crew of the pirate ship was slain or cast into the seas, and the Craven was free to limp along through the tempest.

With the damage done by the collision, and the few casualties inflicted by the pirates of the ‘Spray, the Craven was at the whim of the tempest. The crew hunkered down to weather the storm.

But, before long, men started vanishing. Many rumours about the fate of the missing men began, but the most likely is this; that captain Branigan himself boarded the Craven after a swim of heroic efforts. In fact, it *is* said that he was the son of murderous great white, and that he could outswim a school of dolphins, were there blood or gold in the deal.

At any rate, the men of the Craven were killed and vanished over the course of a few days while the tempest continued and the Craven remained a plaything for Talos.

When the storm abated, all that was left of the Craven's crew was a trio of young girls; the daughters of the noble Wavesilver captain who had himself vanished during the besiegement of the Craven. They found themselves still afloat, but dead in the water and with a strange island just 5 cables to port. The young Wavesilver girls, taking the chance given them by the recent calm, scoured the crippled ship for survivors. However, they found only one, or rather, they were found.

On the sundered pilot's deck it was, where captain Branigan revealed himself to the girls. They were struck with terror upon meeting the legendary butcher of the sword coast, but they bonded together and quickly attacked the man.

Branigan was noticeably distracted by the nearby island during the brief melee, but he did indeed fight back, and the resolution of the noble girls was no match for the dreaded pirate. He laid them low with blows from his huge, hard hands, and stood poised over their prostrate forms at the last, a cutlass in his hand, ready to do his butcher’s work, at his leisure.

What happened next is uncertain, but what is known is that the girls cowered and hid their faces, and in the next instant the pirate vanished into thin air.

The shipwrecked girls then noticed that they had drifted to within a cable of the shore of the shadowy island. On the exotic beach they saw the spectral forms of many a lost soul, and quickly they surmised that they had been killed by the pirate, and had entered the spirit world.

The ghosts of the island drifted out to the boat, and among them were the spirits of many a legendary and noble mariner of the home city of the three girls. Accepting that they were dead, they further accepted seven of the noble mariner's spirits aboard to keep their company. The mariners explained that they had been shipwrecked and marooned upon Wraithmoor Island since gods-know-when, and that this was their one chance to escape.

Thus, three noblewomen and seven noble mariners’ spirits set ghostly sails and were the first to sail away from that island. 

What happened on the ship next is uncertain, but from three earthly wombs twenty-and-one children came forth – a strange thing indeed. 

Many years later, the Craven was discovered by a small armada of the Holy Trinity. Righteous paladins and priests of Torm, Tyr and Ilmater boarded the Craven and quickly they sent the spirits of the noble mariners to their peace.

The score of children, now men and women, were driven into the hold by a throng of divine assailants, and the three women were rounded up by a paladin of each faith.

When the women were told they were still alive, beings of flesh and bone, they all turned white as ghosts and wailed like banshees. The horror of their years-long cavorts with the spectral mariners drove them all mad, and they cast themselves into the sea, and were never heard from again.

The senior officers of the armada debated the fate of the twenty-one children at length. But they had no power to destroy them; their faith had no dominion over these spawn of aether and flesh.

And so, they set them free, and the twenty-one children of the Craven sail the sword coast today upon on an earthly ship with ghostly sails.

Fin.
