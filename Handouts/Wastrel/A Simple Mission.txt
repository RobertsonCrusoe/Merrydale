...Being the tale of Salazar’s Solo Adventure

==Summary==

Salazar is contacted by his fixer, Tin Brenin, to repay a favour that Salazar owes Tin. The mission turns out to be the assassination of one Hannah Lumgin, who turns out to be the bookkeeper for Finn, the head of the largest crime syndicate in Freeport.

==Tin Contacts Salazar==

One day while at the Black Gull, Bill Mackey the barkeep pulls Salazar aside.

“The Tin does be wantin' to contact yer arse for some reason. He'll be at Benjamin Hall this eve.”

Benjamin Hall is located in the old city. It is a gentleman’s club of sorts and offers admission by membership only. Salazar is on the guest list this night and is admitted without a problem.

==Benjamin Hall==

The main foyer of the hall is a small room. There is a doorkeep there by the name of Wen who makes sure that no unauthorized people are allowed in. He has a key at his desk that disables an arcane lock on the doors. There is also a coat check girl (the only girl that works here, in fact) named Terri. A few members walk come and check there coats are given robes or smoking type jackets before they go in. There is a crystal tip vase on her desk that is filled with mostly silver and the occasional gold.

The foyer opens up into a 60 foot square gathering room with an intricately designed throw rug that runs from wall to wall. There are also a few leather wing back chairs and low tables interspersed with small exotic potted trees. Smells of intermixed tobaccos permeate the room coming from the pipes and cigars of the various few members sitting and standing around the room. A few of them are clustered around a low table arguing over some sort of open book. Many well-dressed staff members stride in and out of the many ground level doors that adjoin the room attending to the various people around the room; fetching drinks, removing used goblets, bringing pipes and leaf, removing cigar butts, and cleaning up those glasses and cigar butts that have been tossed on the floor because the staff took too long to bus it. Across the room is a wide staircase that climbs up to a surrounding balcony that borders this room on three sides. The upper floor is bordered by many large rooms, each with two large wood sliding doors. Some of the doors are open while some are closed. One of the staff members approaches you moments after you take in the scene of this room and asks you, “Can I get you anything sir?”

==The Octopilius Room==

If Salazar asks for Tin he finds that he is in the Octopilius Room upstairs. When Salazar gets up there he finds that Tin is sitting around a table gambling with four others at the table.

One of the sliding doors to the Octopilius Room is open showing a square room that is about 25 by 25 feet. The room is windowed on two sides covered by thick green velvet drapes that hang from floor to ceiling. Around the corners of the room are some of the same wing-backed chairs that were found downstairs. In the middle of the room is a pentagonal table. The chairs around it are each unique and look as though they could be thrones in plundered from castles around the world.

In the chairs sit Tin and four others. They are playing some sort of card game and are using wooden tokens as betting chits. Tin is the only one who isn’t smoking or drinking. Tin notices you and says “Ah Salazar, take a seat. We are almost finished.” You notice the other people at the table sneer when Tin says that they “are almost finished.”

The game goes on for a few more minutes with the people at the table repositioning cards in their hands and the table in a seemingly nonsensical way all the while announcing their moves. All of the sudden one-by-one everyone except Tin says “I’m out,” “No more for me,” “That’s it,” “I’m out for the night.” They all start shuffling out of the room. Tin pats the last one on the back as he is leaving and says, “and remember, you owe me a favour.” After the last one leaves Tin slides the door shut and starts putting the wooden chits into the side pockets of his jacket. “Valkur be damned, Tymora is behind me tonight.”

===People Who Were With Tin:=== 
*Captain Hector Torian, Captain of the Silver Ghost and on the Captains Council (pg 27) 
*Patch Carty (Human Rog10), Leader of the Buccaneers (pg 36)
*Poppy Brag (Human Com8), Head of the Longshoreman’s Union (pg 41)
*Torsten Roth (Elf Ari8), Head of the Merchant’s Guildhouse (pg 64) 

Once Tin and Salazar get down to business Tin explains that he is calling in his favour and needs Salazar to do something for him. Tin tells Salazar that he is actually acting as a middle man for someone who goes by “Waynewater\". Salazar is not to ask him his real name. Tin does not know what the job is and he does not want to know. The man is to be contacted at The Last Port (29) on the following day at noon. The man will be wearing a red hood and a ring with a mithril rose. Tin hands Salazar a scroll of Comprehend Languages and says that he was told to use this just before he enters the bar.

==An Encounter with Waynewater==

Salazar finds the man easily enough. And makes a deal with him.

You find a man wearing a red hood that covers his eyes. He is sitting a table in a secluded nook of the tavern and is tapping his fingers on the table making his rose adorned ring obvious to spot. The man looks as though he is from far the far east, however there is a touch of Sword Coast blood in him there somewhere. He reaches up to pull down his hood revealing fine bracers around his wrists. He looks up at you and speaks in a low Rashemi dialect.

Waynewater gets right to the point and says that he needs a man assassinated. The man is Hannah Lumgin and he is a Barrister who runs his business out of the Eastern District. Salazar is to assassinate him by whatever means possible and return to Waynewater any documents that Hannah keeps on his person.

Waynewater knows some information about Hannah that he is willing to tell Salazar.

*Hannah keeps the guards in the Eastern District bribed so there is usually a patrol of at least two guards near his house.
*Hannah has two bodyguards that he usually travels with.
*There is a sewer entrance that can probably be used to access Hannah’s residence.

==The Real Story==

Waynewater is really Delvin Grandbank and one of Finn’s top earners. Hannah is Finn’s bookkeeper and has discovered that Delvin has been severely short-changing his boss. Hannah knows this because he has acted as Barrister for several of the businesses that Delvin gets money from. Hannah has been blackmailing Delvin for some time now, but has decided to raise the ransom to keep his silence.

Recently Delvin has Discovered Tin asking questions about Finnian, with whom Delvin has been doing some business. Delvin finds out about this and approaches Tin in the guise of Finn himself and pus a halt on Tin’s nosing around. Furthermore, Delvin threatens Tin saying that he now owes Finn a favor and must find him a capable person to do a “job” for him, but that this must be kept strictly secret.

It is not widely known that Hannah also has an unsavory business where he gets the daughters of the well-to-do families hooked on a strange narcotic and has them wound up in a prostitution ring, some of which he runs out of his own house. Delvin suspects that Hannah has some other source of income and wants to take this once Hannah is dead.
