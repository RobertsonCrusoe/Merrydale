==\"Things Have Changed\"==
The great hall that has been used as an infirmary is even more packed after the night's events. Wounded men and women lay moaning while the few hands that are available to help them hurry from bed to bed, trying to offer what aid they can. 

Amid this chaos, Kethoth stands at the bedside of Quintus Lucretius Cato. He has a bowl of steaming soup in one hand, and a large wooden spoon in the other. The mess about Lucretius' face is evidence of Kethoth's partially successful attempt to bring some health back into the barrel-chested warlord. Kethoth seems to be taking a break from his ministrations, and is looking at the chest of Lucretius rise and fall slowly. He seems deep in thought, but the sight of Tyriol rushing through the hall on some errand makes him look up. 

\"Come here, Ranger!\" Kethoth shouts across the room. Tyriol is coaxed from his errand by the Wizard with some difficulty, but comes to hear what he has to say.

\"Boy, we have some decisions to make,\" Kethoth says. He taps the wooden spoon on Lucretius' chest with the emphasis and punctuation of his words. \"I have lead us to Spellgard and back...well, to here rather. I need to know the mind of the men, Ranger.

\"Things have changed. I sometimes regret that our course took us into these hills. So many have perished that might have fled if we hadn't bolstered them for a hopeless defense. And yet, these walls still stand, and there is life in this hall. More of it by the minute too.\" Kethoth gives a few more thumps on Lucretius' chest with the spoon as though this act illustrates his point better than words.

\"I don't doubt you want to go to the aid of your mother. I too would like to continue down this path; aiding the people of Loudwater and these hills, and doing my part to keep the hordes at bay. But I also aim to go to the Monastery of Sundered Faith. I will continue to pursue the Horns of Eltab, to honour the men who have brought me this far. 

\"I am going to retrieve these horns that are so valuable that Aglarond sent two of her sons so far afield after them. There is some desperate hope wrapped up in them, and I will do my best to preserve that hope.

\"My question is this; if I go as far as Falcon's Roost to help you help your mother, will you come with me all the way to the Monastery, and to help me retrieve the Horns?\"

Kethoth, who has been tapping the chest of the Dragonborn, ceases his percussions, as Lucretius' nostrils flare and his huge chest expands. He lets out a smoky snort, and Kethoth smiles. \"More hope is kindled...\"
==Tyriol Puts on a Brave Face==

Interesting, such words coming from Kethoth Sepret. Since their first meeting, Tyriol had never held the wizard in especially high regard. Something in Kethoth's very nature seemed to rub the young half-elf entirely the wrong way, but after all the turmoil and chaos they had been through over the last few nights, Tyriol was somewhat surprised to find that he now trusted the tiefling.

He wanted to speak, but Kethoth's mention of lost comrades put the ranger at a loss for words. Tyriol merely put on a brave face and nodded. He would be glad to have the wizard along.
== The women of Kohr'Murron==
The women of Kohr'Murron are a whirlwind of activity. Where Kuzma had seemingly run the domestic side of the settlement which allowed Kohr's wife, Suriy to...do whatever it was the Suriy wished to do to fill her day, with Kuzma gone, Suriy appears to give the impression that she has been running the settlement for decades and that she's been seeing to the tending of wounded and dying all her life. Suriy and Pima; Suriy and Kohr's eldest daughter and just as gloriously beautiful as her dark haired mother; once slightly contemptuous of the party is now patient and accommodating with you if a bit aloof. Pima's daughter, Diay, appears to have recovered from the head injury she sustained when she first happened upon the party and has been intent on sitting next to Lucretius as the large dragonborn laid incapacitated with his own head injury. She said nothing while Kethoth tended to Lucretius, simply sitting quietly and watching Kethoth with her large, dark eyes. There was mourning to be done and funeral rites to be exercised but Kohr's wife and the daughter's of the ferocious warrior seemed to recognize that there was still fighting of sorts to be done. They had to fight to keep sorrow from crushing them. They had to fight to clean up the awful remains of the battle that had taken place. If there was any joy to be felt from surviving the assault of the Red Hand, none of it was displayed by the women. Simply a grim, ferocious, determined look upon each of their beautiful faces. While Suriy, Pima and tiny Diay seem to be busy with tending to those recovering and those not long for this world, Victra and Murron do not appear to be inside the hall.
==Kethoth Checks In==
Kethoth returns to the infirmary to check on Lucretius, but spends much of his time there watching Suriy. Eventually, he approaches her, bringing her a pile of bandages that she seems to be looking for.

\"Glad to be of some help to the Lady of the Estate, madam!\" he says warmly.

\"I have been thinking ahead, to the rebuilding of this peerless ranch, and I can see by the alacrity of your deeds here, that you are really the one who will be keeping this place going, leading into a new and prosperous era.

\"I wonder if I can't offer some help to you, that your husband will not listen to, on account of his intense, but prudent, focus on defense and retribution and such? There are over 1000 dead orcs in the fields about this estate, and most of them are in possession, or at least the mud beside them is, of some weapons and armour.

\"I propose to you that I could turn this to some profit, if you'll work with me. My band is heading deeper into the mountains tomorrow, and so I cannot play a role in the 'heavy lifting' of what I propose to you.

\"If we could gather up all that crude orcish stuff, and reduce it to its constituent form; that is to say, have your steel golem return it all into ingots, I could bring them down out of the mountains, and make some money for the reconstruction effort that is needed here.

\"If you could work with me on this project, we will both be better off; I will certainly retain some profit for the shipping and trading of the goods, but I will dutifully surrender half of the profits to this ranch, so that you might hire the stone masons and woodsmen needed to set about reconstruction here.

\"What say you?\"
