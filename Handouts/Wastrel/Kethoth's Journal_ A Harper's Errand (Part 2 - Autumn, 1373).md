==Autumnal Equinox, Westgate==

As one approaches the great City of Westgate, the farms and villages become a constant ribbon along the road, rather than breaking up bits of forest. The road is also wider and hard packed. But, for the size and quality of the road, there are still many arguments over ‘right of way’, and caravan traffic is a little slower. Cedric and I were not hindered though. We raced around on exhausted mounts, and made excellent time to the City of Westgate.

About a mile out of the City, caravans are moving at a crawl, and they are all being harassed by toll collectors. The less savvy caravan masters often pay tolls to more than one collector, and I heard many complaining of just that. Cedric and I were not bothered by collectors of any variety, and we made the West Gate of Westgate without trouble. Just before the gates, to the northwest of the City, is a great fortress sitting atop what is clearly, to my trained eye, a rocky plateau of magical origin. The fortress flies the crimson banners of Thay from every parapet, and I have learned today that this is the ‘Enclave’. More and more Cities are finding Enclaves in their peripheries these days, I note.

Before I could say anything about the fortress, Cedric took a knowing tone and indicated “They are here now, too. I knew it would not be long before the Thayvians were thick in Westgate”. He seems to know a thing or two about Thayvians...

The City is rimmed by the Sea, and by a fifty-foot wall on land. Though the wall is sturdy and tall, it does not hide half the roofs of the buildings of the City. The more common town homes are all five, six and more stories tall, and any building of import is much larger. This is a City that has grown tall, to stay inside its sturdy and protective walls. The tallest structures in the City are a pair of lighthouses that face each other across an inlet that leads to the inner harbour of the City. These towers both exceed two hundred and fifty feet, which is a height not duplicated in many Cities I have been in throughout the Realms.

Inside the walls, space is at a premium. The tall town homes are built close together, and very small roads run choked with people and conveyances of all sorts between them. The pace of the City is unlike any City I have been in. The whole place has the feel of a Calishite Bazaar; people are in constant motion, even when they are standing still!

=== The Market ===

Of the people, I should say that this is the most cosmopolitan place I have visited. There are faces of every colour and shape here, though the dusky Sembians and the olive people of my homelands are plenty. This of course, puts me on edge, and Cedric notices.

Inside the West Gate, one travels along the congested Silver Street West, where there is more business taking place than in the markets of any other three Cities added together. The sheer volume of warehouses, clearing houses, factories, shipping offices and the like is enough to befuddle the mind. And there are merchants and caravaners to match.

After a frustrating trip down the Silver Street, we entered the Golden Square. This vast market square is one of the hearts of Westgate and is home to its commodity trade. Merchants of all sorts bellowed as Cedric and I walked through the market.

“Tapestries from Turmish, last lot left.”

“Mulhorandi Masonry, selling at a thou per ton.”

“Next auction in an hour. I have thirteen Calishite slave girls.”

“Sugar cane from Waterdeep, Selling at a hun per crate.”

At the Northern end of the square is a tall building with two grand doors. The doors are now open and people are spilling out into the square followed by the sound of an incessant dinging from within. The engraved title over the doors reads “Westgate Trade and Stocks.”

Truly, this is a modern place. I would call the place civilized, but just a little too modern for my tastes. There are things going on in the Trade and Stocks building that I don’t quite understand, but it is clear that it is making the garishly dressed native businessmen very wealthy.

=== The Free Thinker ===

The most interesting thing we found in the Market though, and the thing that made both Cedric and I stare in amazement and then let out a chuckle were the words of a boy standing atop a crate and shouting as loud as he could:

“Take hear, Take hear ye all! Secret diamond shipment from Cimbar sunk by Pirates! Octogenarians blame Raillery agents! Read more in the Free Thinker!”

Cedric promptly purchased one of the newspapers, and asked the boy for directions to the printing house, which he was politely furnished with. I suppose that we should have gotten down to business right away, but we were a little overwhelmed, and not a little saddle-sore, and decided we would find some accommodation and wash the road from ourselves.

=== Casino Platinum ===

We found ourselves drawn to the most expensive hotel in town, which was perched atop what is known as the Casino Platinum. Though traffic in and out of the Casino was tremendous, it was handled quickly and courteously, and we found ourselves checked in promptly. We were forced to change our currency to the house platinum coins, though, and lost very little in the exchange. The little that was lost was well worth it for admission to such a singular place. The floor of the Casino is a bustling place where there is more gambling happening in this single room than there is in the entire Busker’s Borough of Unthalass, I am sure. And the focal point of all this gambling, found in the centre of the great oval floor, is a huge and regal table for a game known as ‘roolet’.

Roolet is a handsome game, and no mistake. A huge red and black chequered board accommodates bets of a great variety, all staked upon the result of a steel ball’s travels through a spinning, numbered wheel. An elegant game, it drew the finest gamblers and the retinues that you expect from such excellent sport. I was drawn immediately to this table like a vulture to carrion, but I had to wait to play; Cedric and I were both in dire need of a bath and change of clothes.

If I have been in a better appointed room in any inn or hotel in all of my journeys, I cannot remember it. The room where I now write this from is without a doubt the finest of accommodations. But, at twenty platinum per night, it should be. It is quite a trick that the Casino plays on its guests; turning all your money to platinum, you begin to treat one of their coins as if they were a gold or a silver, not a five-piece! But, here we are, in a stateroom on the twelfth floor, enjoying life to its fullest.

=== A Splash in the Tub with Kethoth ===

Cedric and I spent a few hours in the room recovering from our travels. I took to the tub room first, and had a bit of sport there. In each room there is a platinum bell that you can ring, and servants will come to you as quickly as they can, from some place outside the rooms. I wasted no time in conjuring, with bell, a small host of bath attendants. In no time, I had a tub that was nearly as hot as I like it (though not quite; most servants cannot bear the temperature of water that I prefer). The host stood by, and I singled out one particularly comely young wench to help me with my robes. When I was disrobed, I playfully pushed her into the tub, and she made quite a splash. The rest of the host looked on and laughed, some uncomfortably. I was about to proceed into the tub after her, but the small host was aghast, and protested as politely as their horror would allow. For, these youngsters had seen the naked body of Chadrazzan Kossur, and knew me to be of an infernal lineage.

For a moment, I thought I would conjure up a few pets to hold the host of servants captive audience to my romantic intentions towards this young lady, and even conceived of summoning forth a goodly sized infernal octopus, or some such thing, in the tub in order to hold her down. But, I thought of my gentle friend in the next room, and thought what a shame it would be if I were to spoil our accommodation by spoiling the flesh of this girl, and I relented with a smile and some jest. The host departed quickly and awkwardly, and they are the only people in this Casino that did not hold out waiting palms to me, before departing.

=== What Happens in Westgate... ===

Another perk of the Casino is our private balcony overlooking the very active coliseum next door. Cedric and I spent quite some time seated upon this balcony as the sun went down, wrapped in fine bath gowns from the hotel, and drinking fine drinks, toasting this and that. Life is good.

We both agreed that tonight we would have some fun, and that tomorrow we would get down to business. Cedric was well into his cups by the time we set out for the gambling hall again, but I kept my sobriety. I wished to test that roolet table with the best chances I had. These proved not good enough.

Every man has his vices, and I have several. The feel of a struggling lover, the taste of a thick red wine, the coppery smell of spilled blood; these are all things that are fond to me. But roolet, now there is a real diversion!

While there were many men playing the table, and most betting heavily (with platinum being the only currency bet, of course), I guess I got a little caught up in my habit, and drew more attention to myself than I should have. I played fifteen coins each round, and lost, in merciless unbroken succession, nine rounds. Had I the coin, I would have played a tenth, but, as I write this, the Casino has every coin I have come to the City with. The funny thing is, I don’t feel the least bit upset about it. Easy come, easy go.

==Westgate, Day Two==

Today we got down to business, and it was a long day indeed. Longer for Cedric, who is lying here, still in quite a bit of pain from the nearly fatal skewering he received today.

=== The Classified Column ===

Our first order of business was to pay a visit to this paper known as the “Free Thinker”. We hired a boy named Prit to guide us through the City, and the boy has earned almost as much as we are paying him. He brought us to the paper without delay.

At the paper, we find a very busy place where men are cranking out papers on some modern machine, and excited writers shout and hurry about in the nonsense of their vocation. He met the ‘Associate Editor’, one Mr. Daniel Thompson. He was a little put off by me, but Cedric effortlessly convinced the man to tell us of the whereabouts of the editor, Mr. William Spedicut. As it turns out, Mr. Spedicut is attending some form of political debate at the parliament house presently.

Last night, Cedric and I talked at length about taking out some letters in tomorrow’s paper. We suspected that we could draw out who ever the contact in this city was, by printing some vague, yet hinting message. We decided on this;

“Comely Rashemi woman seeks dashing wastrel for entertainments. Please leave correspondence for Mr. Cook at the Platinum Casino.”

We left the message with the Assistant Editor, Mr. Thompson, and we had no idea how quickly this would get us drawn deeper into what is going on here in the City.

=== The Parliament of Westgate ===

We made our way to the Parliament house, and did indeed find a debate raging. I shall insert a little bit on the politics of the City here. Westgate came into its present political quandary over one hundred years ago, when by some means, the ruling family was deposed, and elected governments were put in place! Westgate has remained in this political limbo ever since. My father used to talk about the politics of that day, and he would always spit and roar, and curse out oaths about the politics of his realm. If only he knew...

Anyway, the voting process and all the murder, deceit, and wickedness that it bred didn’t actually change the City much. But, three years ago, for whatever reason, it was declared that the voting population would include many people of common blood, but uncommon purse, and thus the population became too large to influence with murder, and the candidates for succession turned in their assassins for orators, and the modern age of the City began.

I have clipped an article from the archive of the paper, which describes best, the present political situation.

After the political ramblings in the Parliament building, Cedric approached Mr. Spedicut, and though I missed the nuances of their conversation, the gist of it was that we would be meeting him for supper later this evening.

With that settled, we decided it was time to go to the Enclave, and do a little merchandising.

=== The Long Red Hand ===

The Enclave is a capital place, and we were both well received. The place treats its visitors with wise caution, and the Devil and Shadow Walker were both treated with the utmost respect and dignity. This is the first place I have been since the University in Skuld that I perceive will give me no grief for my simply acting upon my whims, and speaking plainly.

Indeed, an inscrutable merchant-mage did some brisk business with me, and batted not an eyelash when I requested such scrolls as Enervation, Contagion, and Horizaukul’s Boom. These I acquired with ease and comfort, as well as Magic Missile, Detect Secret Doors and Know Protections. I also purchased a number of potions.

We pawned the protective ring that we took off the body of the would-be-assassin back in Calimport, and this financed not only my scroll acquisitions, but also supplies for transcription and scroll production.

Until later in the day, I confess that I wished there was an Enclave in every City in Faerûn. That was before Cedric found himself with two more holes in him than he started the day with...

But I am getting ahead of myself.

=== An Exchange of Opinions ===

By the time we were done at the Enclave, it was time for our meeting with Mr. Spedicut. We went to what the locals call a ‘public house’, but which the rest of the world calls a tavern, and found the Associate Editor and the Secretary from the Free Thinker, having drinks and engaging other seemingly educated men in political banter. There was no sign of Mr. Spedicut, and indeed, we did not cross paths with him until much later in the night.

We decided to wait for a while, though, and had the misfortune of making conversation with these lowborn men of letters. To say the least of it, Mr. Thompson and I disagreed tremendously on politics, and I found myself to be the lone defendant of the more traditional styles of government and rule. This bar was packed tight as Luskan Slave ship with ‘intelligent’ men who thought that everyone should have a say in the laws of the land he lives in. They supported this ‘Raillery’ party which I previously mentioned, and the ludicrous ideals that it purports. In every City I have been in, there are lowborn educated men who all sing the same anthem as these bastards; that educated and wealthy men, not just men of high birth, should rule. They do not see the world as men such as I. They do not see that there are simply those that are born to rule, and those that are born to be ruled. There are also Freemen, who may wander and be free of rule, but these are not men intended to Rule, themselves, unless they happen to be the last living son of Kossur.

Well, Mr. Thompson is a man who wields his tongue like a sabre, and to be as honest and humble as ever I am, my own wit was of no match for the man. He brought his wit fully to bear on me, and the only proper retort I could think to muster would certainly have landed me in trouble. A public house is no place for an Abyssal Hound.

So, I took my leave of the place, leaving the cheap and offensive swill they had been serving me on the table. I waited patiently in the street, to let my fires cool. I was very close to killing this man, and perhaps the whole bar, and now that I think about it, I regret not taking any action bloodier than leaving. But the day turned out well.

=== Two Letters ===

Cedric came out on the street to meet me. I was a little put off by him at that moment. I thought he should have cast his lot with me, and assist me in illustrating the folly of common rule, that the Raillery pups were pressing, but he was quiet. Cedric is not a boat rocker, but I suppose he thinks I rock every boat a little too much.

“Let’s get out of here,” he said. “I am thirsty for real beer”.

So, it was about 9 bells in the evening, when we returned to the Casino Platinum, and to our great surprise, found two letters for 'Mr. Cook’, and this before our letters had even been printed!

The first read thusly:
Regarding your add for tomorrow’s paper. Platinum Card Room presently...
— -a friend}}

The second said this:
Sorry I couldn’t make our meeting today. My business has kept me detained. Meet me at Thalavar Park tonight at Midnight.
— -William

PS. Watch out for the Assistant Editor}}

Had I been warned to ‘Watch out for the Assistant Editor’ a little earlier, I should think the Free Thinker would be posting for wanted help on the morrow. Be that as it may, our more proximal engagement was in the Platinum Card Room, so we went there presently.

=== The Libeler Boss ===

When I saw the rolling jowls of the man known as Heathcliffe Randolf Arnold sitting at the card table in this private room, I immediately knew that we had been drawn into a political struggle that we otherwise had no business in. Mr. Arnold is the Editor-in-Chief of the Libeler, and we had observed him hotly debating with Mr. Spedicut in the Parliament building earlier in the day. He shot us a politician’s smile, and bid us take seats at the table.

Around the table sat a couple of the fatted orators hired hands; a revolting couple of Cormyreans, who if I read properly, are both brother and sister, and lovers. That would surprise me not at all.

Mr. Arnold suggested that ‘we can be of benefit to each other’, and after bandying words for some time, I spoke plainly with the man.

“What would you have us do, fleshy one?”

“I need to have William ... silenced’ replied he.

“Do not speak roundly. Do you wish for him to have his tongue out of his mouth, or his head off his shoulders, or simply for the man to live in such fear as to change his vocation?”

“Whatever means you see fit to employ to silence the man, I am sure are quite appropriate, and I have every confidence that you are just the pair to do your business quickly, quietly and professionally” was his reply. He still made no commitment as to the actions he would like taken, but from his tone seemed to impart that he would not grieve a moment if Mr. Spedicut was to turn up dead, or vanish altogether.

I commented that I am just the man for the job, but that I would need to be handsomely rewarded for my quick, quiet, and professional work. To this, Mr. Arnold replied that he would be a very powerful man in two week’s time, and that we would be rewarded tremendously at that time.

Cedric and I pardoned ourselves for a moment to talk. We both agreed that we would help this man, even though we did not intend to do so. Clearly, this man wanted to hire us out as assassins, which did nothing to further our goal of finding information related to the Wastrel. Mr. Arnold simply wants Mr. Spedicut killed, because there is an election in a couple of weeks, and he must know something that would derail the Octogenarians, who Mr. Arnold publicly supports. With this decision made, we went back into the Card Room.

“We shall endeavour to assist you, Sir, as we discussed. The price we shall have to negotiate after I have researched the job a little further, and I shamelessly confess that it will certainly be gauged by the measure of success of the job. That is to say, what is good for you shall be proportionally good for us, if you understand me.” I tried speaking in his round terms, but it is not my style. Still, he caught my meaning, offered his doughy hand to seal the deal, and made to depart.

“One other thing, Mr. Arnold” cut in Cedric. “What is it that worries you about Mr. Spedicut?”

“What worries me most is his silence, gentle Sirs” replied the fatted Faerûn-born Dretch. “He has not written a thing in ages, and he is no doubt up to something that I do not want to read about in his paper.”

And that was that.

=== A Simple Trick ===

Cedric and I made our leave, and discussed matters briefly and hastily. We are both worried about this matter of the assistant editor of the Free Thinker. Mr. Thompson is the only one who knew about our advertisement from ‘Mr. Cook’. This information somehow got to Mr. Arnold, and given that Mr. Spedicut himself warned us about this man, it is clear he can’t be trusted.

The real problem is this: Mr. Thompson knew that we would be meeting with Mr. Arnold tonight. He also knew that we have had contact with the elusive Mr. Spedicut, and may have guessed that we will have contact with him later tonight. Given Mr. Thompson’s readiness to sell his boss to his boss’ adversary, Cedric and I both agreed that there is a very good chance that we are being used as bait to draw out Mr. Spedicut, and agreed further that we would try to hamper this if possible.

And so, the talented Mr. Blackchalice used a variety of illusions and such to make it appear as though we had gone the other direction up the street (we even had Prit lead the illusions away, for the sake of authenticity), whilst we proceeded, invisible, to Thalavar Park.

=== Shadows in Thalavar Park ===

If I ever again have need of setting up an ambush, I hope I find a place as convenient and suited to the job as Thalavar Park. The place is a small triangular court bordered by tall buildings. It is adequately illuminated by three magical globes, but the lighting leaves a good many shadows to hide in (in fact, Cedric and I took to these very shadows). For escape routes, there are but three narrow alleys. A perfect trap, indeed. But not perfect enough to close around the neck of Kethoth!

Cedric and I made the court quite early, and decided to take separate positions in the shadows, and wait and see. As the many clocks in this modern City began to ring twelve bells, Mr. Spedicut made his timely arrival in the court, by way of a back door from one of the tall buildings.

Nothing much happened, and Cedric approached William. They had a brief conversation about the character of Leto Cook, what he was doing on the sea, the riddle of the sea, and a good bit about a ‘Metatext’, when trouble sprang.

Just as William was saying “The Octogenarians want to sell the...” he was cut off by an arrow in his chest. Well, William is built of stern stuff, well weathered, and this arrow was not enough to send him to the next world, I am happy to report.

=== Wolves in the Night ===

The battle that ensued was made brief by my best efforts. We had been set upon by three very skilled and professional assassins who had been hiding, invisible, in the court. One went after each of Cedric Mr. Spedicut and myself. Cedric will be some time recovering from his attacker’s best efforts, for he was nearly killed in the first few seconds of that melee by his assassin, and would not be drawing breath presently, if not for Kethoth.

The assassin that came after me fired an arrow and closed into melee with his dagger before I had a chance to react, but the man was powerless to harm to me. I managed to avoid the man’s first surprising blows with great fortune, and then it was time for me to act. By the time I had assessed what was happening, I noticed that a brace of villains was also running down each of the three alleys, to compound matters. I was standing very close to one such alley, and had my assassin between the advancing brigands and myself. I took the situation to be dire, with Cedric being down so early in the fight, and a dozen enemies to slay, so I did not spare my spells. I began with a giant Abyssal Wolf, summoning it between my nearby assassin and the advancing thugs.

Though the sheer number of thugs managed some harm to the daunting wolf, it mattered little in the end. The assassin closest me stayed focused on skewering me with his poisoned dagger, and managed a nick or two. The poison was too weak for the blood in these noble veins though, and Kethoth stayed his ground, and summoned forth another, and then another wolf.

To summarize things quickly, my three wolves turned the three assassins and nine of the dozen thugs into so much kibble, in very short order. There was one tender moment when one brace of thugs snatched up the prostrate form of my companion, and began to run off with him, trailing blood as he was!

When one wolf closed in on the pack, their ‘leader’ held a knife to Cedric’s throat and threatened that he would open it, if I did not call off my demons. Well, I could not have Cedric killed in such a manner; he is fated for a better death, and so I relented, but promised them a swift death if they did not leave Cedric in peace, and depart quickly enough for my liking. They did indeed leave Cedric in the alley, and they fled, but not fast enough for my liking. I sent one of my other two wolves after them, and I am happy to report that two of the four thugs were found dead towards the end of the alley. That is good enough.

=== Spedicut's Quality ===

I should note one other moment in the battle. Immediately after Cedric was felled by his assassin, Mr. Spedicut found himself engaged with two of the bastards, and he had a chance to show his worth. Though the two assassins engaged the man expertly, William showed that he was much more than just a man of words, and put his hanger to expert use in defending himself. With such a tight melee between the two assassins and the Editor, I contemplated solving the whole confounded mess with a fireball. I could then claim the prize from the corpulent editor of the Libeler, and quite this City. I really do not care which paper or which political party gets their way in this City; they are both expounding nonsense in equal measure. But, I had a moment where I admired the man, fighting for his life, but with dignity and poise, and decided that Kethoth would not kill William Spedicut this day. Besides, I had to talk to him again about this ‘Metatext’, and try to glean some greater understanding as to why it was important enough for us to travel all this way to learn of it.

After all the villains were slain, and Cedric stabilized, I had a moment to talk with Mr. Spedicut, commend him on his swordplay and cool head, and ask his assistance in stripping our attackers of their valuables. A man of the world, and certainly an adventurer from days past, he saw his duty and our opportunity plainly and we gathered what loot we could.

It was then that we had a good chance to look at the faces of our attackers, and I confess that my blood boiled for a moment. For our attackers had the look of men from my homeland, and I thought, arrogantly but briefly, that perhaps I was the chief target here. I hope I did not let on about it. At any rate, we also found that these men had masks concealed in their vestments, which are supposedly used as calling cards to mark their victims. These are the markings of the organization known as the Night Masks. This is an organization that I have heard much of, but of which I know little. I know enough to stay out of their way, however, and Mr. Spedicut and I agreed that it was time to make our hasty leave.

=== The Plot Unfolds ===

We returned to our opulent suite at the Casino to take enough time to sort things out, and get Cedric back on his feet. William explained that he had learned of a great conspiracy plotted by the Octogenarians that would have the City fall into Thayvian hands, and that he aimed to print an article to that effect. The details of this conspiracy and its implications I will preserve in the form of that very article, which I have pasted here, for we managed to convince William to print the article and have it on the street for the next day.

Thus, we made our way, as safely, secretly and quickly as possible, to the printing house of the Free Thinker, and William gathered his men and indeed put to publishing the story. We found the treacherous Mr. Thompson there as well. It is fortunate for him that Cedric was left with more Art to deal with him than I, after that last battle, for my solution to his quandary was base and bloody. But Cedric used his magics to detain and interrogate the man, and he insists that Mr. Thompson got caught up in something bigger than himself, and should be spared. It seems I have done a good bit of sparing this evening.

Well, as I write this, we have just returned from the press. We have already passed boys on the street who are selling today’s Free Thinker, with the story that Mr. Spedicut has written, which will make both the Libeler and the Octogenarians founder. I suppose these are great political dealings, and this is a historic moment, but Kethoth sees it all as folly. I have said this before; this City is civilized, but a little too modern.

We are tired.

== Day Three, Departure from Westgate ==

Our minds were made up quickly once we awakened. The whole City is in an uproar over what William has written, and it is time to leave. William would like to come with us. He wishes to talk to May about the ‘‘Wastrel’’, Leto Cook and the Metatext, and we have decided to take him with us.

William Spedicut has proven himself a versatile man to me already; he is a skilled orator, writer, investigator and fencer, and I will take what I can of the company of such a skilled man, while I may.

We made great haste out of the roiling City today, and we all ride like winged devils with the wind at their backs for Baldur’s Gate. We will ride hard for many days, and even ride from Ireiabor to Baldur’s Gate, down the river. No river barge will keep pace with our expendable mounts.

== Early Marpenoth, Baldur’s Gate ==

We rode into the City today like a tempest. He have made excellent time getting here, but were forlorn when the unique masts of the ‘‘Wastrel’’ could not be found in harbour.

That was yesterday. As I sit here in my room, overlooking the harbour, I see the efficient sails of our noble ship coming to. I can’t imagine what delay they might have experienced that brought them here as late as us. I am sure they have a story just as interesting...
