==Another Gmail Interlude==

===From Gussy===

600px

After your victorious rout of the little Kobold party, you set off back up the road.

It isn't long before you come out of the woods, and a quaint, gentle valley shaped like a bowl stretches before you.  (See attachment #1)  Farmland has now replaced the thick forest and mountain walls at either side of the road.

Soon afterwards, you begin to take notice of the small hill, sitting all by itself near the centre of this bowl shaped valley.  The hill isn't very high, but its smll elevation is enough to afford an unobstructed view of most of the surrounding lands, and provide protection to the denizens of this place.  It is no wonder, then, that at the crown of this little hill you find the fortifications of what is the village of Winterhaven proper.  Small, thatched houses line the fortifications and spread themselves all down the hill, and most are attached to small plots of cultivated land.

The village itself is no more than a collection of about a dozen wood and stone structures surrounded by the fortifications.

As you approach the hill, you notice a small group of men standing at the crossroads arguing passionately about something.  Two of the men seem to be trying to calm the other four down.  As you come closer you notice that the group of four is armed and armoured, albeit in a rag-tag piecemeal sort of way.  At this point, Kaily gives out a scream of joy and runs off ahead of you towards the group of men.  You notice the men stop their arguments and look over in your direction, with the man you perceived to be the leader of the armed quartet by dint of being the loudest and most animated, drops his axe and battered shield and runs to meet Kaily in a deep embrace.

Shortly afterwards, introductions are made and much rejoicing ensues.  The four armed men were composed of Kaily's father, uncle and two cousins who had been on their way to search for Kaily.  The other two men, trying to calm the search party down, were Lord Ernest Padraig, ruler of this valley, and Valthrun, a retired scholar and resident of the village.

Kaily calmly explains her ordeal to her family members, and the men are clearly overjoyed at having he back safely.  They express this to your small little party with offers of much food and drink to be bought in your honour at the Inn tonight, and a guarantee of always having a place to sleep and rest while you're in Winterhaven.

The younger men (Kaily's cousins) are dispatched to escort the young girl down to her farm to see her mother and sisters.  The elder men, Caius (Kaily's father) and Wilfred (her uncle) along with Lord Pardraig and Valthrun escort you up into the village and directly to the common room at Wrafton's Inn.  Two Rooms are arranged, baths are ordered, food is cooked, and drinks are served.

Sitting comfortably in worn armchairs near the fire, you begin to notice that a crowd has formed in the Inn this evening, and you find yourselves fielding questions from curious farmers for much of the evening.  You lose count of how many times you told the tale of how you found Kaily, and how it was nothing to deal with that band of 20 Kobolds.

As the evening wears on, and all the farmers excuse themselves with complaints about needing to be up at sunrise, you find yourselves in a much quieter room sharing a final drink with Lord Padraig.

Lord Padraig insists you call him Ernest, as everyone else does.  It seems that the House of Padraig had once been quite prominent in these lands.  Long ago, when the last empire fell, the Padraigs lost most of their standing and wealth.  In reality, Ernest tells you, he is more of a mayor than a Lord.  The villagers only keep him running the place because his father had done it before him, and done it well, and his father had done it before him and done a good job also.  It followed then that Ernest should be Lord simply out of tradition.  A Padraig had always ruled, so a Padraig rules now.

In truth, though, Ernest confesses he has little power.  This is a closeknit community, and people pay their taxes (light as they are) because everyone would know if you didn't.  There isn't a military or any form of defence force other than what would constitute a citizens' militia, and even then, he can barely convince anyone over the age of 14 to come out for training.  This is a quiet valley full of quiet hard working people who just want to be left alone to farm and enjoy their lives.

This kobold problem, though, worries Ernest.  Usually theres a pair or two of them that come out every year, but they are easily scared off and have never really harmed anyone.  a few stolen sheep, a mutilated cow, that's about it.

This year, though, the koblods are appearing in greater numbers  And they seemed organised and well armed.  In fact, Caius and Wilfird and their party were only able to frighten them away during their ambush on the other road (where Kaily was lost) by their sheer numbers.  They only had 6 enemies to contend with, and their party was made up of nearly 30 villagers returning from the Midsummer market in Fallcrest.  It was a small scuffle, and they were frightened off, but it was nothing quite like your fight.

No, Ernest has been trying to convince the old farmers that something must be done.  They are getting bolder and coming in larger groups, and are well organised.  Your fight, for example, happened only a mile from the valley's edge.  Who is to say they won't raid one of the outlying farms tomorrow?  No, what Ernest wants is to convince these old farmers to at least come out and train together and get a proper militia established.

With a brooding look into his drink, you get the sense that he is waiting for one of you, who easily killed an entire war-band of the things earlier, to say something...

===Blacky Responds===

Brash just sort of looks at the face of his old friend, Shepherd, and at his two new traveling companions, waiting for someone to say something.  He isn't actually the quiet type, but this decision making business is not for him, and what's more, he is not the type to commit to anything but his friend, and one-night commitments to local girls.  In fact, until Lord Ernest stopped talking and it went quiet, Brash was occupied with eyeing up whatever young ladies might be in the inn.

Shepherd, on the other hand, stands up, puts a hand on his friend's shoulder, and makes an awkward but heart-felt bow to Ernest.  \"Lord Padraig\" which he insists on calling the big man of the town, despite having permission to call him familiar.  \"I couldn't turn away from Winterhaven now, even if I wanted.  I have been guided here for a purpose, I am certain.  Torm,\" and with this he grips the weathered copper holy symbol about his neck in a clenched fist, and continues on; \"He has brought me here to test me.  He directed young Kaily to us to test our resolve, and to measure us against the monsters in this fair land.  Saving her life, I am now duty-bound to preserve it the best I can, and that means I cannot walk away from Winterhaven while there is trouble lurking 'round.\"

Under his breath, Brash curses, and then drains his tankard.  A stout slap on his back the armoured cleric beside him makes him choke on the last mouthful.  Brash elbows his friend, and the pair chuckle.  \"I'm with him\" he finally says, raising his tankard in the air to indicate it's emptiness.

Shepherd continues on.  \"This town has good walls, though some of the gates and battlements are wanting for use and upkeep, like the militia here.  This is a defensible place, though, and there are many stout lads and leathery men here.  There would be no problem organizing a defense that could hold against a small division here.\"  Shepherd tears off a crust of bread and dips it in his beer, chews it down, and continues.

\"There are two things we must do to prepare for conflict; we must first assess the threat, and then build our defenses based on that.  It sounds like kobolds are nothing new around here, though their numbers and bravery are now increased, if I understand it properly.  Perhaps we need to count their numbers, and find out the reason why they are growing in both numbers and courage.\"
