PART 1 — Les Blackwell (as Salazar) — Journal Entry — 27 Nov 2003
================================================================================

[OPENING MEDITATION ON THE SEA]

Salazar reflects on his return to the sea after years leaving to study magic in
Luskan. He meditates on the Riddle of the Sea and invokes the memory of Leto
Cook (the Gaffer), the ship's original builder. The passage includes verse:

"Often I've suffered in foreign seas
Yet still, even now, my spirit within me
Drives me seaward..."

[RETURN TO FREEPORT]

The Wastrel, captained by Zechary "Zeke" Mudge, returns to Freeport at the
Gaffer's summons for repairs and maintenance. Salazar notes that Freeport looks
cleaner than before and sees more guards patrolling the docks. He takes the new
companion Gaul MacMorn (an enormous, violent barbarian) ashore with Captain
Zeke.

[VISITING CAPTAIN LYDON]

They visit Captain Lydon, an old friend living in an Old City Estate. Lydon has
aged and gained weight. He tells them of "The Ripper" — a murderer carving up
people in the dock borough, creating a climate of fear.

[HUNTING THE RIPPER]

Salazar takes to the rooftops alone to investigate. He is spotted by the City
Watch. Gaul answers the guard's summons aggressively, threatening: "Take a step
closer, and I will gut you where you stand." Gaul kills two guards and flees to
the jungle, complicating the situation.

[ENCOUNTERING THE CREATURE]

The Ripper is revealed to be a nightmare chain devil — "shaped roughly like a
large man, though a man made of writhing evil chains." It wears the face of
Salazar's father ("the first man I ever killed!"). The revelation is profound:
Salazar's past crimes manifest in physical form. He pursues the creature to the
sea and attacks it with arrows.

[SETTING THE TRAP]

Working with Zendor Williams (captain of the guard, a devotee of Tyr), Salazar
helps set up a curfew to restrict the Ripper's hunting ground. They plan to use
Zendor as bait.

[THE FINAL BATTLE]

On the third night of the curfew, the creature appears. All three — Salazar,
Zendor, and Zeke — engage it in combat. Salazar casts transmutation magic to
enlarge himself and boost his strength, then grapples the creature. A silver
dagger ultimately destroys it.

[AFTERMATH AND REWARD]

Captain Zeke receives public glory for the kill. Salazar trades his monetary
reward to secure Gaul's freedom from legal jeopardy. The Captain's Council,
impressed with Salazar's contribution, pays him 1,000 gold crowns regardless.

NOTES
================================================================================

This is one of the most polished pieces of in-character writing in the Wastrel
campaign archive. The narrative demonstrates the literary ambitions that would
later drive the elaborate Chapter 7 Chronicle for the current Daggerdale
campaign. Les's commitment to character development and emotional resonance is
evident in the confrontation with Salazar's past — the Ripper wearing his
father's face is a profound personal horror, not merely a mechanical encounter.

The "Wastrel" itself is the ship that gives the entire campaign its name. This
thread marks a moment where the campaign's mythology was being actively written
by players, not just experienced in play sessions.

LITERARY REFERENCES: The Riddle of the Sea poetry combines elements from the
Anglo-Saxon "The Seafarer" with John Masefield's "Sea-Fever," suggesting that
Les was drawing on maritime literature to set the campaign's tone.
