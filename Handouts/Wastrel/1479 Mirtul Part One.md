== Mirtul 1, 1479 ==
thumb|450px|Kethoth enjoys the Mayor's charms

The party had begun to take form in the small town of Loudwater around the 1st of Mirtul, 1479. They were a group of individuals as different in their motivations as they were in their appearances from one another.

*Baird of Kelemvor – A paladin of Kelemvor, hailing from Aglarond sent on a “fool’s errand” to locate the means with which to discover an artifact of great power. He has been led to believe that finding this artifact would assist in the defense of his homeland against the vast armies of Thay.

*Brother Mattrick Lychaeus – The ward and friend of Baird as well as a priest of Kelemvor. Mattrick accompanied Baird out of a sense of duty as well as hoping to temper some of Baird’s more impetuous behaviour with his own deliberate and methodical ways.

*Tyriol Grimbold – A half-elf ranger hailing from the Loudwater area. Tyriol’s human mother, Maeghuen Grimbold is a ranger and scout of renown in the Loudwater area. Tyriol’s elven father is unknown to him save his first name, \"Leirran.\" Tyriol is growing older and growing more and more bored with the lifestyle that Loudwater presents. When given an opportunity to take part in an “adventure” to ruins he has heard stories about since his childhood, he jumped at the chance.

*Kethoth Sepret – A tiefling mage with a past that is so long and colourful that it would make some dragons envious. Kethoth was commissioned by his lover, Lady Moonfire; the mayor of Loudwater.  Lady Moonfire is obsessed with adventurers, the more unique looking the better and she commissioned her lover, Kethoth to form a party and seek out a treasure of great power. Kethoth would glean such information by having this party accompany him on the way to the fabled ruins of Spellgard.

*Quintus Lucretius Cato – A dragonkin educated on the ways of combat and warfare hailing from Tymanther. Lucretius found himself unemployed and looking for a source of income after having had his employment terminated with the caravan he had been previously hired to protect found out that dragonkin turned out to be more “kin” than “dragon”. He was spotted by Kethoth and immediately put on retainer.

*Arya Nightweaver – Royalty from the Feywild; eager to find an adventure and make a name for herself that was based upon the merit of her own actions and not those of her forebearers. She was a rare sight in Loudwater and was immediately put on retainer upon having been noticed by Kethoth.

*Ender – A Halfling hailing from Llorkh. He has a questionable background and similarly matched morals to go along with it. As much as those can be questioned, few can question his ferocity or skill with a blade. Kethoth having found need for someone with a keen eye and a sharp street-sense brought Ender under his employ before the party decided to start planning the expedition to Spellgard.

== Mirtul 3, 1479 ==

It took no small amount of effort on Kethoth's part but he was eventually able to arrange a time and place for his recently paid for companions to meet. Upon have gathered at the Green Tankard Tavern and taken care of learning one another's names Kethoth believed that the very best way to travel to Spellgard would be on horseback as it would expedite the journey there and back.  Inquiries as to where horses could be purchased led the party to speak to one Wilkes Tamblin.  Wilkes is the sole source of horses in Loudwater who in turn gets his horses from a breeder that is northeast of Llorkh and resides at the foothills of the Graypeak mountains.  Wilkes informed them that he had been bought out by a group of grim-faced, black armoured individuals hailing from Waterdeep.  Not only did they purchase all of his remaining horses but they also bought his two wagons that he also had available but no longer.  The Waterdhavians had left five days prior to the party inquiring and Wilkes was not able to say when more horses would arrive.  This led the party to looking for other means with which to find transport.

The local blacksmith Megana Nistral had taken a shine to Baird and during an \"innocent\" conversation Baird was having with the broad-shouldered and big-boned blacksmith, she revealed that her father might at least have a wagon and some workhorses to sell to the party should they find themselves in need of such.  Much of the day had been spent preparing for the moderately far trip to Spellgard as well as seeking out what Kethoth deemed was a suitable conveyance so it was decided that the party would head south to Nistral's farm at first light the following day.

== Mirtul 4, 1479 ==

With a desire to get moving towards Spellgard, after having their morning meal and setting on a plan, the party left for Nistral's farm that day and upon arriving there it was obvious that something was amiss.  Baird, clearly not a man of subtlety shouted out a greeting for Nistral and when the only greeting to be returned was the wind blowing open door of the farmhouse against it's doorframe, the party decided to act.  A plan was devised for the more quiet members of the group to approach and gather information about the current situation which turned about to be Ender and Tyriol.  As they drew closer to the old man's farmhouse Ender took note that not only was the door to the farmhouse hanging on one hinge, clearly damage but windows on one side of the farmhouse appeared to have the shutters smashed.  Tyriol and Ender both approached, moving as quietly as they could while Tyriol tried to discern what manner of strange tracks were near the damaged windows.  Seperated from the rest of the party, they were over-extended and the opponents that were observing the party showed no hesitation in attacking the sneaking individuals.

Three monstrous looking things leapt from the roof of the barn, seemingly unbothered by the thirty foot drop with two landing in close proximity to Ender and Tyriol with the third landing at the front of the barn and immediately charging Baird, who seemed to be struggling with the concept of stealth or tactical positioning.

The battle had barely begun when two small creatures came charging out of the barn itself, like pale, twisted, sinister looking halflings wearing heavy black cloaks and robes, they in turn assisted the monsters who were later to be discovered were ettercaps.

Although there was a complete lack of tactics and battle-planning shown by the party initially due to their unfamiliarity with one another, instinct quickly took hold.  Lucretius, Kethoth and Mattrick began to exercise their knowledge of combat as well as show their experience and although the group was badly injured, no one perished and soon the ettercaps were on the ground with their blackish blood spilling out across the grass.  There were no corpses for the strange \"halflings\". (It was learned later that they are referred to as \"dark creepers\" by the wardens of Loudwater; a decidely unimpressive name, so deemed by Kethoth.)  As it turns out, when a Dark Creeper is killed, they collapse into themselves and then quickly burst in an explosion of \"darkness\" cloaking the nearby area and blinding those close to the creature's death.  All that was left behind by the dark creepers were their distinctly foul-smelling clothing.

Upon investigating the barn, it was discovered that more had been left behind by the dark creepers.  The body of Albus Nistral was found staked to the dirt floor of the barn, spread-eagled and clearly tortured to death.  Also in the barn was a wild-eyed and terrified horse and an old but well cared for wagon.  In the wagon was an alabaster obelisk appearing to be recently excavated from the soil.  While Tyriol did an expert job of calming the frightened horse, Kethoth and Arya focused on the obelisk, learning that it indeed was quite magical and Netherese in origin.

The party surmised that the dark creepers must have been seeking out the obelisk and logic pointed to them torturing and killing Albus Nistral for the location of the obelisk.  Their visit to the Nistral farm left them with more questions than answers but they did get what they came looking for to begin with.  They had a wagon and the means with which to pull it although the acquired it in a manner that none of them could have expected.

When they arrived back at Loudwater, it was quite late in the day. Baird and Mattrick shared the grim task of informing the blacksmith that her father had been killed while Kethoth took it upon himself to inform Captain Harrowleaf of the Loudwater wardens of the murder at Nistral's farm while leaving out the acquisition of Nistral's horse and wagon; as well as the obelisk.

Over the course of the evening while the party prepared to leave for Spellgard the next day, they learned the following.

* Baird although a paladin of Kelemvor is clearly better tasked with the dispatching of undead and not assisting thick-thewed, big-boned daughters with their grieving.

* Both Tyriol and Kethoth learned that the appearance of dark creepers was not as unusual as they first thought as patrols of wardens have been dealing with seemingly random groups of them in the area.

* Clearly these dark creepers were seeking out the obelisk.

* Ender learned through some shadier contacts that \"old man Nistral\" had two young and dim-witted farm hands.  Bor Grollen and Terman Dalus.  Bor and Terman had been seen a month ago in the local tavern, bragging about how it wouldn't be long until they were living in a noble's house in Waterdeep and living the high life.  When asked why they were sure of such things, they simply guffawed loudly and said they found a \"great treasure\".  Coincidentally, Bor and Terman; regulars at the tavern, had not been seen in nearly a tenday.
