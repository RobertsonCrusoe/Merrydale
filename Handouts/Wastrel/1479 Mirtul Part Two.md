== Mirtul 7, 1479 ==

The journey to Spellgard had been relatively uneventful for the first day.  The Seven, encountering Maeghuen Grimbold on patrol was not by chance and she made no effort to present it as such.  She gave explicit instructions to avoid the bandit village of Llorkh on the way to Spellgard as well as a tersely worded reminder to Kethoth that should something happen to her son, Tyriol; worse might very well happen to him.

When the party made mention of the armed and black armour-clad individuals that had previously been in Loudwater and were heading east, roughly a day and a half ahead of the party, Maeghuen faltered in her usually calm and competent demeanour and in a distressing fashion, she told them to stay away from the group of serious-looking individuals.  They hail from Waterdeep and their leader is a man that needs to be respected and feared.

The party heeded two of the three warnings she gave.  Tyriol remained in good health, while they travelled straight through Llorkh, paying off an unscrupulous bandit with a significant bribe to lead them through the dangerous village.  They also often discussed the Waterdhavians and what should be done about them should they encounter them in the future.

The party continued east until they reached the south-eastern foothills of the Graypeak Mountains.  They pushed their mounts hard and travelled through the swampy region known as The Smear and witnessed telltale signs of lizardmen watching them from a distance but observation seemed to be the only thing the lizardmen wished.

The evening of the 7th of Mirtul left the party setting up camp in the southern edge of the Weathercote Woods.  Late in the night, they were set upon by kobolds and the night was lit up brightly by fire generated from Kethoth's arcane energies and Lucretius' fiery breath.  After the battle, a number of the trees in the area were sheathed in flame.  Fearing to draw further attention from the denizens of Weathercote Woods, the Seven broke camp and headed north along the road that ran alongside the old forest.  The party travelled north by north-west for two full days and for the entirety of that time, black smoke drifted upwards into the sky like dark tendrils, only to grow into large clouds of smoke as the fire grew and consumed more and more of Weathercote Woods.

== Mirtul 9, 1479 ==

As much as the party was pushing hard to leave behind the forest fire they had caused, they were also pushing hard to catch up to the Waterdhavians ahead of them.  It had taken some time but the group of five riders and wagons could be seen in the distance and the party was indeed drawing closer.  So much so that when it got dark on the evening of the 9th, the party sent out their two stealthiest individuals, Ender and Tyriol to scout out the Waterdhavians.  It was a long, stressful approach but the duo eventually drew close enough to make out that the Waterdhavians were well-geared and the \"five\" consisted of two fierce looking dragonkin, an orc that wielded an impossibly large bow and a slender looking female dressed in eladrin gowns.  That made four individuals and the fifth could not be located.

That issue was resolved during Ender and Tyriol's return trip to the party's camp.  They met a cloaked and hooded human male and through the use of confidence and threats, the two were quickly hurried off and returned to their own camp with the information they had gained but also visibly shaken from running into the man that had nearly appeared out of nowhere.

== Mirtul 11, 1479 ==

The rest of the journey through the far eastern foothills of the Graypeak Mountains went without incident.  Although the fire continued in Weathercote Woods from a distance, the ruins of Spellgard could be seen.  It was situated on the edge of a cliff upon remarkably flat land.  A place that was once nearly entirely buried beneath the hot sand of Anauroch, now rested upon the dry, cracked surface that the party travelled upon.

Upon reaching Spellgard, the party could see that the large citadel was all but reduced to ruins, with a number of structures that could be seen, skeletal and ancient, protruding from the chaotic surface of the ground around them.  What immediately caught their attention was a modestly sized, well-cared for inn at the far edge of the ruins.  Although they had heard that The  was indeed recognized as a safe place in the ruins, the party was not prepared to find something quite so organized in the middle of all the treasure-seekers and creatures that called the ruins their home.

The party had arrived late in the day and rather than begin exploring the ruins in the twilight, they spent the remainder of their energy arranging costly lodgings at the monastery and becoming familiar with its residents as well as the monks of Oghma that tended to the monastery and the adventurers that stayed there.

The Oghmen consists of the following...

Brother Allendy – An elderly man with the appearance of being well into his seventh or eighth decade.  Although he moved like a man of that age, his bright blue eyes do not seem to miss much and he proves himself to be as inquisitive as he is old.  Allendy appears to be the individual that the other Oghmen answer to.

Brother Turnigal - A man in his late forties with an altogether normal appearance.  Turnigal appears to be the man that runs things in the monastery, negotiating all the fees for staying at the monastery and acquiring whatever items or gear is necessary for adventurers that wish it.  Sharp of mind, and keen on coin, Turnigal is a friendly man but makes sure business is always taken care of first.

Sister Chara - Chara is the youngest of the three, clearly in her early twenties and runs the taproom and kitchen.  She also acts as the first line of defense for any and everyone that wishes to speak to Turnigal and Allendy.

Residents of the monastery that do not work there consist of the following.

A female halfling named Milek. Attractive and charming, she worked her way into conversation quickly with the party and a few hours thereafter worked her way into Ender's bed chamber.

An older, human male named Yannick whom Tyriol tried to strike up conversation with and failed to make any progress with the curmudgeon.

A male dwarf by the name of Gietel Helgorod and seemingly ignored by the party.

A young, male tiefling by the name of Rallio Devore.  He had a few conversations with Kethoth and it was revealed that where Kethoth revels in who and what he is, Rallio despises himself.  Rallio is in the ruins, seeking the Lady of Saharelgard and hoping to ask her who his father is.

Plans were made for the following day and the party took its rest where and when it could.  It's also speculated that this day was when Baird and Arya's brief romance began.
