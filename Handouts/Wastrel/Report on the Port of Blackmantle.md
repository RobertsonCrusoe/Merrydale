This report was intercepted in Waterdeep by the Rooken Network, and forwarded to the Master of Vauxborough's. It is unclear just who it was intended to be delivered to, but there is some suspicion that it was intended for the now-defunct Aldebarran Company, or one of its agents.
----

Hammer, 1373 DR.

T;

You were wise to send me to watch over the fool, Hazzearth. As you expected, he turned a fantastic opportunity into ruin. To make a long story short, Hazzearth used old and worn tactics in the Canal Project, and his treachery was quickly and duly discovered. Any protections afforded him by the faceless Lord Blackmantle quickly ceased after his deepspawn were found in both of his rival’s Canals, and it was not long before the Thayans pressed the bulk of Hazzearth’s men into their service. And it was not long after that, that Hazzearth and Razzamon, the Khazarch of the local Thayan Enclave, had heated words. To this day, the stony form of Hazzearth decorates the foyer of the Enclave, for any visitor to hang his hat upon. It should be noted that the terrified form of Hazzearth has had a crucial bit of stone removed from that portion of his chest that should contain his heart, making the prospect of reversing the transmutation a grisly, if somewhat entertaining endeavour.

In short, our efforts in the region to win the Levies have come to a dead and certain halt, but I remained behind to see which company would ultimately win Blackmantle’s little contest; the enterprising people of Yhep, or the Red Wizards of Thay. I should have said the people of Yhep would have won without contest. They marched 50 miles from their small city, 1200 strong, and began their work with vigour. I found out that the governor of that city, one Anglemar Farstone, founded an organization called the Braeriefae Canal Company, so named after his dearly departed wife. Anyone in Yhep with means purchased shares in the company, and those with no means were given the chance to work for their share, with pick and shovel. Of course, Blackmantle’s promise of enormous levies gave the workers enough fire in their arms to swing fast and hard. For only the first company to complete their canal would reap it’s benefit. The losers would forfeit their dearly-purchased canal-lands, and to the victor, the spoils. For a time, it looked as though honest work, and the promise of capital gain, gave an honest and capital edge. But, wizards will be wizards.

It was not long before people in the Yhep work camps began to disappear at night, and the numbers in the Thayan slave camps soared. No doubt, the Thayans bolstered their ranks with the multitudinous vagrants and wanderers who found their way to this new and bustling port, and adding these numbers to the captured Yhep workers, and their original number of 600, they quickly numbered over three thousands! Now make no mistake; slavery is quite illegal in each and every one of the scattered City-States that perch on the shores of the Lake of Steam. To abide such behaviour here is to commit economical suicide, in such a closed economy. But the faceless Blackmantle has seemingly thumbed his nose at his neighbours, by turning a blind-eye on the activities of the Thayans. At no time has he ever declared slavery legal, and to be sure, there are postings against it everywhere. Yet, the Thayans seem to have thousands of them toiling in their canal, and the local guards aren’t doing anything about it. In fact, the rumour is that over 200 of Blackmantle’s newly acquired mercenary-guard vehemently guarded the Thayan Canal, and that no one can approach to within a quarter mile of the place to investigate the claims of open slavery.

To further hone their edge, the Thayans began to employ their magics to clear the canal at break-neck speed. The last mile of the canal, terminating just outside of the new Port, is a ditch-digger’s nightmare of lava-rock. This rock had slowed the depleted work force of the Braeriefae Canal Project significantly, but it was of little hindrance to the Red Wizard. Starting before dawn each morning, Razzamon would gather his initiates to himself, and begin the casting of powerful transmutations, calling on the power of his circle of mages. He would then proceed to the work site, where he would turn huge tracts of the stone to mud. His slaves were able to readily remove the mud, while the remaining Yhep workers chipped and chiselled at a snail’s pace.

And so things would have gone, if not for the intervention of an outside party. Some adventurers aboard a ship known as the Wastrel came to Port, and did not find things to their liking. Well, most people that come here find something to their disliking; this is a new and ugly place full of treachery and danger. But it is a place full of profit, and so most abide its ugliness. But not these men. Not these brave fools who have made an enemy of an empire!

One morning, the dim fools openly attacked the Enclave, and actually managed to kill a pair of Black Mages (apprentices to Razzamon). They were put on the run readily enough, but managed to hide in the hills until the local Trinity was able to get to them and harbour them.

The next day, they made an even more daring attack, upon Razzamon himself! Whilst casting his rock-to-mud transmutation, they attacked from above, both employing magical flight, or so the story goes (and there are many versions of this story). At any rate, the short version of this story, and the one that agrees with all other accounts, is that Razzamon was ultimately killed by, of all things, a priest of Waukeen (I wonder if the Zulkir of Divination foresaw such irony?!) With the death of the tyrant, the Red Wizards were ultimately broken. Later in the day, the few remaining wizard-lackeys were killed by parties unknown. While none claim responsibility for the three heads that came to ends as decorations on the outer walls of the Enclave, there can be little doubt that our ‘Heroes’ were in collusion with the local Eternal Flame of Kossuth. An ebon flame burns atop the Spires Of Purgation, and there is a fell-handed servant of the Ebon Flame that parades about this Port like a fox amidst the coop, who knows the farmer is away.

A great exodus of crooked Blackmantle mercenary guard took place after Razzamon’s death. As it turns out, the Red Wizards have been buying these fellows off with the many retail magics at their disposal. It was at this time that I acted, and in the heat. Having the local administrator’s ear, I was able to arrange for purchase of the Enclave, and have taken legal possession of this remarkable fortress.

Three days after the death of Razzamon, the people of Yhep completed the Braeriefae canal. A ceremony of great pomp was planned, and all expected the faceless Lord Blackmantle to finally show himself. The ceremony was indeed rich; there were banquet tables full of every delight to be found within two thousands in miles of the Port, and entertainments of many varieties. Indeed, the best that could be brought to such a rough, new, settlement was found. At length, the final bit of stone that kept out the yellow waters of the Lake of Steam was blown with smoke powder, and the waters rolled down her right away, in a triumphant and violent sluice.

All ships stood many cables off, to witness the spectacle of the connection of the two great bodies of water that was about to happen; they were afraid of being sucked into that rushing water. But one boat stood close by, at the ready. The Wastrel, her sails trimmed and her sparse but remarkable crew on the decks, watched from scarcely 100 yards out, and as the waters began to rush, she sailed right into the rapidly-filling canal, on the crest of the wave! The opening of the Canal was lined with the workers from Yhep, and all called a warm welcome, thank you, and goodbye all at once, as that proud ship shot into the canal, past their grateful waves, and up and out of sight in a matter of moments, racing with that anxious tidal rush.

The Wastrel was the first boat to make that navigation. She was first in the Lake of Steam end of the Braeriefae Canal, and she rode the first waters in that canal all the way to the Nagaflow, where a carefully timed charge of Smokepowder opened that end of the canal. And so, the Wastrel became the first ship to go from the Salt Waters of the West, and into the Salt Waters of the Inner Sea. That thirty mile canal was lined with many people at many points, and all give the same account; of a handful of crew members making merry upon the deck of that small, but remarkable vessel, while the Captain and the Master kept her on the crest of a violent wave which washed, for the first time, the bottom of the canal that has become the most important thirty miles in the Realms.

One footnote of interest; I am told that the Wastrel was actually levied for its passage, of all things! I would have thought that Farstone would have granted the very people that made his capital gamble a success, a free ride, but it did not seem so. Not at first at any rate.

Today, I have noticed a new structure being built in the Port of Darkmantle, on a very nice piece of real estate overlooking the beach, perched comfortably on the side of one of the small volcanic mountains that offer such an agreeable ecological foundation for the area. It is a villa, by the looks of things, of generous size and appointment. And it flies the standards of the Refalian Family, of which I am sure you know much.

As it turns out, the very man that killed Razzamon was one Aldous Refelian, a notable priest of Waukeen. And, as it turns out, the man was able to procure some very valuable stock in the Braeriefae Canal Company. Indeed, Farstone signed over his shares to the Coin Counter, before he went to join his wife. Farstone jumped into those rushing waters just after the Wastrel charged ahead, and he has not washed up since.

Those that knew and loved the successful administrator of Yhep, and the capitalist responsible for this remarkable project all shake their heads, and curse themselves for fools, though smiling the whole time. As it turns out, Braeriefae, the departed spouse of Farstone, was herself a wanderer and Follower of the Wind. In a vision, Farstone saw her beneath the waves of a nameless ocean, and despaired that he could never join her, not knowing which ocean she might be laying in. But, his is the first body that can find its way to any sea on the face of Toril, and those that knew and loved him, and his wife, say they are together.

…

A moving tale to be sure. Things have gone poorly here, but not all is lost. We have our foot in the door, in the very door that every foot worth mentioning is trying to jab itself just now. I will stay on for a while longer, and meet up with you when your ‘pressing and inescapable’ business, as you have termed it, is complete.

I remain, Sir,

Your humble Servant,

— H
