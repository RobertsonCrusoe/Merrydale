''Significant Entries from the Journal of [[Chadrazzan Kossur]]...''
----


{{journal|Hammer, 1485}}

I have just killed my retinue and the girl that they were holding for me. The smell of their still-smouldering flesh fills this rotten little hovel in which I hide, and it is a pleasing smell that confirms for me that I am happy to have incinerated these pitiful sycophants, though I wish the girl was spared.

Perhaps I should start at the beginning, though...

Today for the first time in decades I am able to write. I have awoken now fully from the half-dream through which I have been stumbling for many years. Today for the first time, memories came flooding back to me, including my name, and where I came from, and what happened to me. I think I have been in and out of my mind several times over the last century, this confounding Spellplague taking root in me periodically. I have some very muddy memories filling some voids between the end of the 14th Century and this, but I shall try my best to record what I now recall. I have, many decades ago, hidden some of my precious books in a safe spot that I shall endeavour to repatriate in the near future, and my adventures aboard the Wastrel are well documented therein, and so, being of pitifully limited means just now, and short of ink and paper, I shall start where those surreptitious records leave off.

Sometime towards the end of 1385, my time aboard the Wastrel came to an end. The [[Zechary Mudge|foolhardy Captain]] decided that he wanted to sail to Sespech; we were close by the place, and had heard horrifying tales of what was transpiring there, and Mudge, in his limited wisdom, decided we had some role to play there. All I knew was this; whilst aboard the Wastrel, [[Maevri]] and I were spared from the afflictions that were destroying Practitioners and driving them insane all over Faerun, and I didn’t want to leave. However, I also knew that the terrible dweomerstorms were something of an epicentre for the affliction, and I didn’t want to sail a fathom closer.

{{page|2|3}}

A heated debate on our course of action was joined, and all hands took part. I sued for reason, and for the others to understand that sailing right into one of those storms was beyond even the remotest limits of safety and sanity, but the others would not listen. Mavri, for once was silent; she knew that our well-being was tied to this vessel, but that it would be seriously compromised if we approached the storm in Sespech. However, I think she might have already been loosing her mind, or perhaps was just confronted with a palatable threat to her power for the first time in her remarkable adventuring career, and could not cope with it, and would not take my side. Cedric said that he felt safe because the Shadowdweamor had not been affected the same way (though he was wrong), and sided also with the crew, who he never ceased trying to please. In the end, the mundane crew won the argument, and I burnt then some bridges that I will never again cross. I left the Wastrel that day, after cursing and damning them all for fools, and I have never seen them or the Wastrel again.

Now, I suppose that most of them would be dead by now anyway; the Captain didn’t have more than a dozen years left in his liver if the rest of him survived whatever befell them in Sespech, and anyway a century has passed. I don’t miss many of them, but to be honest with myself, in my own mind it only seems like a couple years since we parted ways, though in truth, most of them are dust and bones by now.

Cedric perhaps still lives. He is of a long-lived race, and if Mudge didn’t drive them right into their doom, I suppose he may yet walk this corporeal realm, no doubt wreathed in tobacco smoke and whores. I hope to cross paths with him again some day in this world.

At any rate, I quit the Wastrel and was left to my own means in a forgettable fishing village on a forgettable shore, and I immediately thought of home. I was alone in the world again, and I longed to see what was happening at home. After long years in the wilderness of the West, I felt that I could again confront all which I had fled from back in Unther. My skill as a summoner was rivaled by few in those days, and at that point in my career, and I knew that I would be able to gather a host about me and make my return and claim what was rightfully mine; my father’s kingdom, the ancient and immutable Kossurrian. 

The details of my return could fill two of these pitiably small books, so I will have to leave out most of it. But suffice it to say that after a couple years I did in fact find my way back to the Kossurrian with a sizeable retinue, even though my Art was unstable, and my command of it untenable. 

I returned to find my father’s kingdom (rightfully mine) completely destroyed; The Kossurian had been razed completely, and there were bands of slaves-cum-marauders roaming the lands like so many jackals. And one recognized me as the Son of Kossur, and things went poorly. My Art failed that day, and my retinue followed suit. I was able to make my escape, but found myself once again on the run, submerged again in the nightmare that I lived decades before, when I escaped my home the first time. Deciding that hiding in Skuld worked so well the first time, I again made my way there.

Now, I have seen many kingdoms rise and fall in my time; I have seen the slow death of entire civilizations, and the constant changing of the political landscape, and the ruinous visitations upon the cities of the mutable west that are concomitant with it, and I have never given it more thought than an academic curiosity. I even saw the end of my own family’s kingdom, and that was somewhat difficult to bear, though I half expected it for much of my life. However, when I came to the ruins of Skuld, I was crestfallen. 

{{page|3|3}}

Ancient Skuld, who was old when even Myth Drannor was young, and who had relations with Netheril herself, and many ancient and forgotten cultures from the Unapproachable East. Ancient Skuld, where I was harboured and nurtured and became the Wizard I was in those days before the Spellplague. Skuld was the first place that I respected that I had seen brought to ruin by those dangerous times, and something in me changed that day. The last clear memory I have of my life was standing in the blasted wastes before her quondam peerless gates, now so much rubble, and shrinking, for the first time in all my days, to my knees, to shake my head at tragedy. At that very moment, I fell fully to the affliction that lurked deep inside me, and lost command of myself. My story fades then for a while.

I think that sometime a few decades later I came to my senses, and perhaps I was still somewhere in what was left of Unther or Mulhorrand, which I understand is now called High Imaskar. I am sure I was a slave, but I am sure I escaped, and fled to the West again. I have vague memories of robbing and killing and stealing aboard ships, and running and fighting for my life, all with not so much as a cantrip to help me along. But those memories turn again into a malevolent and lurking madness from within, and I know that I became a wild thing for many decades following.

There are some periods between now and then that I awoke, and in those times I was more and more aware that I had been something greater in the past, but never did I realize I had been a great Conjuror, much less a Son of Kossur, and always I resorted to the basest means of survival; hiding at the fringe of this small place or that, lurking as some sort of devilish hedge-mage, sometimes with a companion, sometimes as a lone and despised thing.

However, some few years ago, I awoke again from my beastial madness, and this time I must have remembered that I had previously owned some power, great or small. I think I managed to kill some lonesome fledgling mage in a town not far from here, and started to teach myself command of the Art, such as it is these days. I was still a hated and feared thing that had to hide and lurk, but I was able to muster not only some small power, but a small retinue as well. I was surrounded by half-bred nightmares who lacked any cohesive plans or wills of their own, and we became something of a marauding band of brigands in the wilder places here in the Grey Vale, staying well away from the larger towns and the busy road.

I think we made a good career of it, but that changed today. It all changed because of a girl. It always has to be a girl…but here I have to back track a little.

I will never forget the day that I was cast out of Kossurian; it was, in the calendar of the West, Greengrass of 1215 DR, and though I was already more than a century old, I was just then developing the desires and lusts of the springtime of life for a devil such as myself, and this came with trouble. My father always told me to keep out of the slave pens, and specifically, that I should stay out of the thighs of the slave girls. Kossurian was in those days a kingdom of many tens of thousands of slaves, and maintaining peace and order among such numbers requires that certain rules be followed by both slave and slaver alike, and the raping of slave girls by their masters topped the list of transgressions to avoid. Until that spring, I had been properly raised, reared and educated, and I am sure there were no signs of my uncontrollable lusts.

{{page|4|3}}

However, just previous to Greengrass that year, that changed. I recall with the most vivid clarity the daughter of my father’s groom. She was deliciously small, perhaps 5’ tall and not an inch taller, though she had the endowments of full womanhood. Often she accompanied him in the stone carriage house in our summer estate, learning his proscribed trade, and that spring I could not take my eyes off of her, as she helped her father; she would have to stand tip-toe and stretch as high as she could to reach the back of an 18-hand charger with the brush, and her simple, clean chemise would ride just north of her navel, and my blood would boil. One night I arranged for her father to take some of the horses out for a walk, and then had my personal servant fetch her to the carriage house, insisting that one of the horses needed tending to, and that in her father’s absence, she would have to be the one to take care of things.

Of course, the only mount she dealt with that night was me. I cornered her alone in an empty stall, and attempted to seize her. The terror on the girl’s face when she realized what I intended made my mouth water and my blood boil all the more, and though she broke a burning lantern over my head in an attempt to escape, I eventually held her down in the straw, and had my way with her. Unfortunately, though I got what I wanted out of her, the lantern that she broke caused something of a fire which I was completely oblivious to. Whilst I forced myself upon her, our bed of straw became a pyre, and she was burned most terribly during the slaking of my lust.

When I was done, I rolled off of her and enjoyed the moment, but her father returned that very instant, no doubt alarmed by the growing fire and the screams, and he managed to pull his horribly burnt and disfigured daughter from our consummatory bed-pyre, and left me there laughing in the midst of it. I then had that post-coital moment of clarity that I am told men of every race experience, and decided it was best that I made my way out.

Now, even though I was the Son of Kossur, and she was but a slave’s daughter, I had committed a terrible crime, and I knew it well. I had quickly to devise a wicked plot to keep my black behind out of hotter water, and had the father framed for the burning of the barn, with accusations made of his incestuous rape of his own daughter, who was too traumatized and terrified to dispute the charges. He was killed for his crimes, and all seemed well.

However, I learned then one thing about my race and the nuances of our procreation; though only a couple of weeks passed by after my intercourse with the groom’s daughter, she came quickly to full bearing, despite her nearly-fatal burns. I suppose my plot to kill her father was only partially successful; in the weeks that followed his death, I kept my place in my family and on the estate, but the slaves knew what had happened, and further, they knew that I had impregnated the girl. Now, no landed lord of Unther keeps slaves without many spies amongst them, and so my father learned of my transgression, and he instructed me to make things right. He knew also that the girl would soon be delivering our child unto the world, and he said that would never do. So, I set out with a number of our most trusted slave-guards into the camps to find the girl. It wasn’t long before we found her in a large tent, and we had to force our way in through protesting senior women who exclaimed that she was delivering the child that very moment.
What followed was a nightmare that shaped the rest of my days.

As I forced my way into the tent, I did indeed find the mutilated and deflowered thing in the midst of her throes, surrounded by the usual throng of crones and maidens that one finds at the birthings in the slave pens, and they recoiled in superstitious terror as I approached the scene. I guess I should have done some immediate and gruesome and definitive violence upon the girl before she delivered, but I was struck with wonder that she was about to deliver unto the world something that was of my loins. So I stood there and watched intently, and her helpers backed away slowly and cautiously from me, the devil who had visited this nightmare upon the slaves, as she writhed and suffered, suddenly alone.

{{page|5|3}}

Before long, my child erupted into the world; it was a disfigured thing that was all bulbous, wiry haired and toothy, and it tore and gouged its way into this world in a manner that even I found disturbingly gruesome and uncommonly bloody and noisy. I cannot to this day tell you the fate of the poor girl, whether she lived another day or not, but she certainly would not have use of her organs of progeny after that violent eruption of my angry spawn into this world.

As the creature tore its way free of the groom’s daughter, the slave women in the tent began to scream in terror, and a great chaos was suddenly upon all of us. I commanded my men to kill the creature, which already was bounding around the tent and harassing the other women, with the strength and agility of a hunting hound. The gruesome aberration seemed intent on feeding on some flesh, and it made several terrible wounds with its misshapen claws and mouth on both women and guards alike, and when it seemed clear that the thing could not be easily caught, I gave the signal for a prearranged course of action that I had plotted. Outside the tent, some of my most trusted slaves doused the tent in oil and put it to the torch. The tent filled with thick smoke immediately, and before long was completely engulfed in flame. In the confusion, I made my way out of the conflagration, and back to the palace.

My plan had not worked, and I did nothing to help clear up the matter that my father had insisted I take care of. In fact, rumour got back to us quickly that a monster was loose in the slave camps, and that many slaves whispered that it was in fact my child, born of the poor groom’s daughter. My father was left with no other course of action than to banish me from the kingdom at that point; the slaves demanded blood for what I had done; not only had I brought this terrible monster into the world (and I merrily report that the thing harassed the slave camps for months to come, though I cannot say what happened to it in the end), but over a dozen slaves were dead because of me, many of them valuable maidens and learned midwives. They rightly demanded justice, and though my father would not turn me over to them, I was forced to leave home, and it was then that I fled to Skuld. There is much history that comes after that, but let me return to the events of today.

This morning I was awoken by my motley retinue who had brought me a fetching young girl of some 15 or 16 winters. Where they got her I never learned, because the sight of her had the same impact on me as the groom’s daughter centuries before. She was small and curvy and terrified, and what little sense I had been slowly acquiring during my awakening from my madness went presently out the window, and I veritably threw myself upon the girl, and dragged her to my bed, her screams and crying a delight to my ears. However, I had a moment of epiphany, perhaps caused by the nearness of the situation to that carriage house rape of bygone centuries, or perhaps just because of the waking of my springtime urges and the turbulent flow of blood through my partially dormant mind. Whatever the cause, a flood of memory gave me pause.

I stopped my assault upon the prostrate and pinned creature beneath me, and looked up at my detestable minions, who looked on with drooling lust and anticipation. I realized that they likely thought I would share the spoils after my turn, and it was then that I realized that everything was wrong. These mongrels should not have been allowed to touch her bare flesh, much less witness me in my violent lust, and I was repulsed by the entire situation. I looked around, and saw that I was in the dirtiest of hovels, with skins hanging on the walls, a mattress of greasy straw, and other vile trappings of a marginal life in the wilds, and I was thoroughly repulsed. In one moment of rage, I stood up, naked as the day I was born, and I roared at them; “I am Chadrazzan Kossur, and you are beneath contempt!” I unleashed a terrible burst of flame that consumed everything and everyone in that damnable hovel, this devil excluded.

Fortunately, some of my things have been spared; I had one crude chest that preserved my most valued possessions; those mean things that I have pilfered and stolen in the last Set-knows-how-many years since I again half woke out of madness. I know not what name the creatures that worshiped and followed me knew me by, but that matters not today. Today I again take up the name Kethoth Sepret, and today I again set off into the world to see where the fickle Tides of Fortune may lead.
