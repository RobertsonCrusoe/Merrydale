Courting Death by Barthallomos Brax

*Item Description:
:The covers and pages are made of thin sheets of hammered brass, totalling two hundred in all, yet the book is only a few inches thick. The binding consists of a series of adamantine rings that each page of brass is attached to. The book is sixteen inches long and five inches wide; made to be opened up and read lengthwise. The book is full of finely etched drawings of undead creatures, detailing their anatomy, instructions on creation as well as sustaining them and all manner of study that Barthallomos (Barthas) managed to collect and come up with over the seven centuries he was alive. The cover of the tome is written in a common tongue from Thay in small, neatly organized and square script. There is also a a small etched wizard’s sigil on the cover (Barthas’s sigil) almost appearing to be three unevenly sized diamonds floating over a mountain range.
