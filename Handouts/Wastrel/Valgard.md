But back to the second, who was similar to Logan in many ways, but completely different.  His name was Valgard, and he was the greatest Achilles Heel for Blacky I ever came up with.  He was another who was rolled up as a PC, but there was no place for him in any of the current campaigns going, so he was demoted to minor NPC in my campaign.  Basically, Valgard was a drunken fop of a noble.  He had the Noble Warrior kit, which, if you dust off the old Fighter's Handbook, you will recall, has a very significant hindrance:  he must pay 10% per level more for any goods and services being bought.

This was fucking great!

Picture this:  Falcon and Nathaniel on their very own boat, trying to establish a trading empire along the Sword Coast.  Along the way they pick up a nobleman named Valgard.  Falcon, being a noble himself, is not one to turn another blue blood out, so he agress to let Valgard cruise the high seas on his boat as a drinking companion, and when needed, a little extra muscle (he may have been a womanising drunk, but get him on a horse with a lance, and the man was unbeatable.)  So, Falcon, who, since he is played by Blacky, always spent more money than he should in accomodation, drinks, meals etc, suddenly found himself with a drinking buddy that was automatically adding 10% per level on top of that bill.

I think there was oone time where Blacky had sunk all his available capital into a shipment of swords, and was left with something like 20gp to his name (temporarily, since he had like 20,000gp in illicit weapons he was about to set sail with.)  while he and Nate were out securing the deal with the weapons, Valgard was left in charge of finding accomodations for the evening.  Well, Falcon came back to find that good old Val had run up something like 1000gp on Falcon's tab at the Inn with his gambling, drinking and whoring.  That was before the food, and not counting the cost of the best suite in the place.  I thought Blacky was going to have a heart-attack for real right there at the fucking table.  Valgard the Albatross.  Falcon never got a chance, but I'm sure, fellow blue-blood or not, he was about ready to dump Val at the earliest possible moment.

Given that in 3ed that Noble Warrior hindrance has been removed, I might resucitate Valgard to out on this boat.  The world doesn't have enough foppish nobles drinking and whoring their way across the Realms. Plus, he was a kick ass fighter.  If he was on a horse.  And had a lance.  And was reasonably sober.  And his opponent was at least 2 levels lower than him.  And wasn't of any of the warrior classes.  And was on foot.  And unarmed.  Well, not unarmed, because that would have been dishonourable, so perhaps armed with a small knife.
