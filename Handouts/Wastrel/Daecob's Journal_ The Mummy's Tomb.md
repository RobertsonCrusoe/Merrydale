...We came upon a small tomb of savage nature and unveiled a mummy of some sort. According to my childhood stories and Kethoth's alleged tomb raiding expertise, we all agreed that this creature of the supernatural would probably come to unlife if disturbed too much. Further, despite this alleged extensive tomb raiding expertise Kethoth agreed that this creature was more than a match for us.

To this end we gathered two bundles of tinder and spread them at the door of the tomb, setting them alight as we left. This catalyst did indeed affect the mummy as we expected, but further it started to attack us which was wholly unjustified and legally actionable I am sure. Since the thing stumbled quite slowly we were able to fill it with a few arrows and bolts as it came at us. Unfortunately, these had no affect on the thing so Kethoth threw a couple of arcane spheres of lightning at it which seemed to nick it a touch. After that the mummy emerged from its tomb and let out such an unnatural shriek that Kethoth and myself were paralyzed for the sake of it.

It was at this time that Ratsel took hold of the situation and went at the creature with a whip in one hand and a torch in the other. Seeing the foolhardy elf attempting to trip that thing was a discouraging sight, for myself. However, this despair turned into sheer amazement as the elf succeeded in his plan and tripped the thing not once, but twice, and managed to finish it off with his torch against all odds.

I will swear to any oath that it was Ratsel that ended that mummy through shear audacity and foolishness, and neither I nor Kethoth would be alive today if it wasn't for his whip. You know, every elf I have met has never hesitated in telling me of the superior wisdom of their race as tautologed through their amazing life span. Yet, I have never encountered a situation in which any elf took even the easiest opportunity to cease in acting as foolish as any human, hobbit, dwarf, gnome, or otherwise.
