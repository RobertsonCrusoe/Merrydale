==Heroes ==

*Priya Moonstar – Wielding a Selunite holy artefact – Tremalkin’s Bow. A Waterdhavian priestess of Selune of elven decent and adopted into the Moonstar noble family at a young age. Once; studying at The Monastery of Sundered Faith, she joined The Seven after assisting them with the liberation of the Monastery and it was decided that The Seven would be trying to seek out The Spire (a possible secret location for Selunites on the western edge of the Graypeak mountains.

*Volund Heimdall – Enigmatic warrior, seemingly empowered by the strange and unusual looking blade that he wields that he recently revealed to have the name of Shyr’rach. Volund is a member of the “Cerulean Circle”. An aggressive and somewhat militant group situated in the High Forest that seeks out those that wrong the land and forces those individuals into “making things right” or dying. Volund was assigned to “watch” The Seven while they were attempting to make amends for the burning of Weathercote Wood but upon the successful defending of Kohr’Murron Stables, Volund had elected to accompany The Seven. His reasons for doing so remain his and his alone.

*Enderion Gateward – Better known as “Ender”; a halfling rogue hailing from Sembia. Little is known about the past of Ender or what caused his relationship with his brother “Cayd” to sour but upon running into one another once more at Kohr’Murron Stables, Ender did little to assist his brother when Cayd was accused of planning to kidnap Kohr’s family and sell them to slavers. In the end, with the prompting of Kethoth Sepret, Cayd was forced to leave Kohr’Murron; a place he had been a part of for three years and Ender said little about what was going on inside his head. Ender continues to travel with The Seven, seeking out adventure, battle and a chance to lay his hands on exotic treasures.

*Tyriol Grimbold – A half-elf ranger hailing from Loudwater. A member of The Seven that started out as somewhat of a bumbler and seemingly harmless has now emerged to be an interesting member of the group. There is reason to believe that he somehow has ties to the ruthless group of Waterdhavian thugs called The Mercykillers. There is definitely more information to be had regarding this, but it is information that Tyriol and Kethoth Sepret are keeping to themselves. As Tyriol grows more and more comfortable with himself, he is becoming a more and more effective member of The Seven and becoming prone to possibly foolhardy but definitely notable acts of heroism.

*Paetr Helgorod – Dwarven warrior and long-time soldier stationed at the mountain pass known as Falcon’s Roost. Paetr would have been made Captain of the ‘Roost decades ago were it not for his adamant refusal to being “burdened” with leadership. Paetr was one of the few survivors of the legendary battle that caused the defensive structure once known as Murtaw’s Point to now be referred to as Falcon’s Roost. Paetr first encountered The Seven during the defense of Korh’Murron Stables when he and a small group of Loudwater Wardens were sent from Falcon’s Roost to give warning that the orcish army known as the Hand of Doom had gathered once more and was pushing south and trying to leave the Graypeaks. Paetr was convinced to stay with The Seven by Maeghuen Grimbold; Captain of Falcon’s Roost and a well known person in the town of Loudwater. Maeghuen was concerned about her son Tyriol and particularly concerned about him associating with Kethoth Sepret. She asked Paetr to protect Tyriol as best he could and Paetr grudgingly obliged.

*Radha Leveaux – A human sorceress hailing from Waterdeep. Radha belonged to an adventuring company that was named “The Blades of Korombos” by her once-boyfriend, Dantilar Bleth. The Blades of Korombos were captured and then turned into experimental subjects by a vampire named Barthas in the ruins of Spellgard. Radha was able to escape a horrible death at the hands of Barthas when the vampire became enraged and obsessed with the destruction of The Seven and particularly Aryalustrinne Miritar. Radha encountered The Seven while in the catacombs beneath the Tower of Spellgard and has been a contributing member since being rescued by them. Radha has been bitten by Barthas and exhibits very minor vampiric powers. While in the presence of staunch worshippers of Kelemvor, Radha rightly kept this hidden. With the death of Baird and Mattrick Lychaeus, Radha still keeps her minor vampirism a secret and recently has been suffering from traumatic dreams. Radha has spent a great deal of time reading a copy of a book in Kethoth Sepret’s possession by the name of “Courting Death by Barthallomos Brax”. The copy she has consists of a number of high quality parchment pages, written in common that she was able to acquire while in the “copy room” at The Monastery of Sundered Faith.

*Jalael Do’Ridan – A drow rogue that seemingly has a solution for whatever problem he encounters. Jalael has been trapped inside the secret Selunite structure known as The Spire for well over one hundred years although it feels like to him that it has been roughly a little more than a year. Jalael is the sole surviving member of the Selunite group known as The Moonstars and raised from a small age by the high priestess of Selune by the name of Shyruil Talandros. Jalael encountered The Seven during a brief but anxiety-filled battle with an eye-tyrant outside of The Spire. Jalael was assigned by the Steward of the Spire to travel with The Seven to find an artefact called “Mhogus’ Construct”. The Steward revealed that at the time, the Shade Lords seek “Mhogus’ Construct” and if the Shade Lords want it...then so do the Moonstars. Jalael current uses an enchanted necklace that he must wear in order to counter the effects of the time anomaly that he suffers from while in the Spire. Were he to not have it, upon leaving the time anomaly, he would suffer from excruciating pain and simply disintegrate. He needs to insert a very expensive sapphire into the necklace roughly once a day.

*Kethoth Sepret – A tiefling and learned mage, one-time historian and expert on ancient cultures and antiquities; one-time bandit lord and self-proclaimed scourge of the High Forest and High Moor and now, self-appointed leader of The Seven. What was once an opportunity to be free of the amorous intentions of his lover, the Mayor of Loudwater and simply lead an expedition to the fabled ruins of Spellgard has become a quest to find an artefact of power by the name of The Horns of Eltab. Kethoth is rarely content where he is. When in ancient ruins, he longs for the open road, and when on the open road, he longs for ruins all while longing for an opportunity to settle in a “civilized” settlement where one might be able to buy things and sit in something that more closely resembles a chair as opposed to a stool. Kethoth has used his arcane and historical knowledge for not only personal gain but to the gain of the company of individuals that he leads in order to track down where the Horns of Eltab are and has possibly brought them to a location where they might indeed lay their hands on the artefact...if they can survive.

===Lost Along The Way===

*Baird of Kelemvor – Human paladin of Kelemvor – Tasked to seek out the Horns of Eltab by Seriadne. Seriadne was under the guise of a trusted advisor but in reality was a malaugrym. A creature from the Far Realm. This ruse was revealed by Zamomel Stass. Baird was killed during a battle with the Mercykillers.

*Aryalustrinne Miritar – Known to the majority of the party as Arya Nightweave, the eladrin swordmage joined up simply seeking adventure. Instead, she found death at the hands of Barthas, a vampire and in time was destroyed by her companions when she had been dragged into the sunlight and attacked, her body burning into ashes.

*Mattrick Lychaeus – A human priest of Kelemvor. Mattrick was the assigned protector and adopted older-brother of Baird. Mattrick is presumed dead after being controlled by Barthas and made to walk off a cliff into a seemingly bottomless chasm in a cave in the southern foothills of the Graypeaks.

*Hrogan Goringrimm – Dwarven priest of Oghma. First encountered at Kohr’Murron Stables, it was revealed that Hrogan was from the Monastery of Sundered Faith and after battling with the Hand of Doom army, Hrogan agreed to escort The Seven back to the monastery where they could research the Towers Tyrannous. Upon liberating the monastery from the Hand of Doom, Hrogan decided to stay at the Monastery and rebuild his home. Hrogan attained the rank of First at the monastery and is currently there trying to undo the damage that was done by the Hand of Doom.

*Quintus Lucretius Cato – Dragonborn warlord and stalwart friend of Mattrick Lychaeus. “Luc” as he was known was becoming more and more dissatisfied with the leadership of The Seven and the decisions being made. Upon liberating the Monastery of Sundered Faith, Luc took the opportunity to part ways with the company and stay at the monastery to help the survivors rebuild.

==In the Tower==

The party is at the Towers Tyrannous because they are looking for the Horns of Eltab. Baird of Kelemvor and Mattrick Lychaeus were sent from Aglarond to seek out the Horns of Eltab and return the artefact to the Simbarch known at the time as Seriadne. (Now revealed to be a malaugrym in disguise.) 

Even after the deaths of both Aglarondans, the Seven, driven along the path by Kethoth Sepret drew closer and closer to their goal of finding the Horns of Eltab and possibly returning it to Aglarond. There was some concern about making the long journey to Aglarond but a mage by the name of Zamomel Stass, and mage of renown in Aglarond and the far east happened upon the party and requested that they deliver the Horns to him should they find it. If they leave word for him at the Dripping Dagger in Waterdeep, he will meet them there and he will reward them handsomely for the role they played in Aglarond’s defense against the ever-encroaching forces of Thay.

Through a lengthy journey, the party found themselves in The Spire. A secretive staging point for Selunites that used the arcane nature of The Spire to instantly transport people from The Spire to a desire location anywhere else in Faerun. When arriving at The Spire, it was discovered that a temporal anomaly surrounds the Spire. The anomaly is some manner of defensive mechanism that went awry and while within the Spire and a small area around the Spire time is slowed by an incredible rate.

The ratio is as follows.

*Every minute in the Spire is 1.5 hours outside

*Every hour in the Spire is 3.75 days outside

*Every day in the Spire is 3 months outside

*Every Year in the Spire is 90 years outside

The party entered the time anomaly around noon on Kythorn the 16th.

The party had spent in total, roughly 12 hours within the time anomaly of the Spire before entering the portal that lead them to The Towers Tyrannous. Hanahlaos; the Steward of the Spire let the party know that they had 3 days to find what they were seeking as well as acquire the construct of Mhogus and return to the portal before it closed, trapping the adventurers in the Towers Tyrannous.

For the portal to be activated, a specific type of ore was needed. The ore is watched over and distributed by a Silver Dragon by the name of Zhan’Batol. Hanahlaos informed the adventurers that Zhan’Batol has been tasked with guarding a ‘bleed’ from the Shadowfell deep within a cave in the Graypeak mountains nearby. The dragon, once young when first assigned has grown over the centuries and is now said that it rarely steps outside of the cave and relies on its servants (a tribe of Kenku referred to as The Stone Crows) to serve its needs.

Zhan’Batol stayed deep within the shadows of its lair when the party encountered it and all the party could see was the menacing darkness from which it spoke from. Zhan’Batol expressed a desire to know the reason why the party needed to use the portal in the Spire and in an unusual display of openness, Kethoth Sepret shared everything that the dragon requested, including the name of the artefact that they were seeking; The Horns of Eltab. The ore was given; rewarding Kethoth for his honesty and the party returned to the Spire and prepared for their journey to the Towers Tyrannous while Hanahlaos prepared for the ceremony to activate the portal.

While in the ‘stream’ as Hanahlaos called it, the party was disoriented by a rapid and rhythmic pattern of coloured rings. At some point during the ‘journey’ there was some manner of ‘schism’ that shattered the rhythm of the coloured rings, further disorienting the party only to return once more to the original pattern they saw moments before; albeit moving more quickly. Jalael confirmed that the ‘schism’ did not occur the last time he had used the portal.

The party is now within an ancient temple of Bane with walls that seep necrotic slime. They appear to have explored nearly the entire complex, or at least what is currently accessible to them and now have seemingly only two goals left. Retrieve the construct of Mhogus for Jalael and Hanahlaos, and find the Horns of Eltab.

The party has made no attempt to seek out an exit from temple that they are currently in.

While using the ancient dwarven construct called The Nexus, the party sabotaged the mechanism to open and close the door from the ‘Spoke’ side of Nexus. In essence, they prevented the hoard of grimlocks from following them but also ensured that there would be no chance of them returning from whence they came.

Kethoth surmises that it is the 14th of Flamerule.
