Battle upon the Sea of Fallen Stars
It is the stormy season in the early spring, and no time to be upon the Sea of Fallen Stars for many mariners.  But, for the Wastrel and her crew, no storm has proved her master, and she claws her way through wave after wave upon this relentless, tumultuous sea.  And she is not alone.
For a day and a half, a black-sailed three-masted ship has been pursuing the Wastrel, and it seems that a captain of great skill is at her command.  The Wastrel can outrace any ship ever made in a straight-out run in almost any waters.  But the stormy waters that she sails this day require a constant effort of navigation and piloting, and here, her crew finds that she has met her betters.  The black sails find a few cables
gain with every masterful tack, and it is not long before she is only 100 cables behind the Wastrel, and the anxious pirates aboard her can be seen through the rain and foam, slavering upon the decks, awaiting their next prize.
But the Wastrel is no easy prize.  She is manned by some of the most notable men and women of the Realms, few though they be.  The crew is all above decks and look on at the frustrated faces of the Captain and Master.  The faces of these men are stern, but betray something of the failure they feel in their hearts.  They have been unable to pilot this vessel, the very ship of Leto Cook, better than the antiquated Galleon that gains on her.  And now the time for running is at an end, and the Captain lets loose his crew to fight in the manner they know best.
It is Maevri of Rashemen that opens the battle with her direct and deadly magic.  But aboard the pursuing vessel, unseen, is a mage of some ability who seems to be able to dispel every flash of lightning that May hurls.  Though the stormy conditions make any missile engagement futile, the Master is able to send a few magically-aimed arrows into the crew aboard the gaining vessel, but her crew are many, and a few dead men do not keep her from the chase.
When the ship gets a little closer, Kethoth begins his assault; he sends balls of fire that meet the same anemic results as May
s lightning, sputtering out half-way to the pirate ship.  He then calls forth a number of huge sharks to assail the vessel, but as they race towards the vessel, a man in the bow of the ship holds aloft a set of black antlers that give off a great red light, and the sharks are kept at bay.
