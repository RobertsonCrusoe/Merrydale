==Helgorod Valley==

Kythorn 2

This morning I was up long before any of my men. I went to the mess hall and found only a handful of soldiers eating a breakfast of salted pork, and a hard rye bread that they dipped in their beer. Captain Grimbold was sitting among them, and I approached her table with a tin plate of the rough fare in hand, and a smile on my face which made her grimace. She pushed her plate away which had the effect of dismissing the men around her without a word. I sat and wished her a good morning.

“There is something else concerning the parentage of my scout, your son, which troubles me. What dalliances do you have with the Mercykillers? You do know that these maniacs have sworn to kill us, don’t you?” I looked earnestly at her, then let out a harsh laugh and a small snort escaped me.

“I have no ‘dalliances’ with these people, devil” she was saying. I raised an eyebrow at her in her own interrogatory and derogatory fashion, and it was her this time that had to disqualify her own words quickly. “There was one, of course, but I do not cavort with this lot. I do see one of them from time to time, on a professional basis. He is a fellow soldier named Leosferis Duo’din, and I think he is a high ranking member of the organization.” She went on to explain that this man-hunter was often in the Grey Vale pursuing his quarry, and that they helped each other when it served them to do so.

I knew all of this, of course. The night before we left, and again last night, I asked some of the soldiers here at the Roost if they knew of the Mercykillers, and a few knew of the comings and goings of this man, Leosferis. There was some speculation that he and the Captain were romantically involved, and while I supported those base rumours for the sake of humour and the deliberate besmirching of the name Grimbold, I doubt very much that she is involved in any conjugal relation with this man.

“So you haven’t seen this contemptuous murderer recently, then?” I asked.

“No. I have no idea where he might be.” She lied to me. The captain always crosses her arms when she is dealing straight with me, which is most of the time. She uncrossed her arms and drummed the table with her fingers when she answered. Deceit is not her game.

“I am sure your son will be safe then! We depart today for the Monastery of Sundered Faiths, as soon as the troops are up and ready to go. You have some time to talk to your boy; I don’t think Ender will be ready much before the sun is high in the sky.” What I meant to say was “You will have a chance to run to your boy and warn him that Leosferis is nearby and hunting for him or me.”

Before we set out, which wasn’t indeed a minute before high sun, I had learned that Leosferis travels with a giant named Q’Hrog. Encouraging.

We travelled up the fair Helgorod Valley all day, and are now camped out in the pines on the south-west face of the valley. Several times today, we have seen the giant behind us, usually standing sentinel on an impossibly difficult bit of rock. The monster is thrice the height of a man, and he wears a gauntlet the size of a soup-cauldron on his right hand. He is dressed in the black and azure garb of the Mercykillers, which means that this is not merely an associate of the Mercykillers, but one of their members.

What the devil do these villains want from us?

Certainly revenge is not worth pursuing to the death of half of their membership. Then again, Radha, our lovely lifelong Waterdeep resident, states that they are indeed a pack of crazy zealots who might sacrifice any number of souls for their agenda. I would be flattered if I were this important, but I’m not. Not to them, at any rate. Tyriol is another matter, however.

===Hunted Once More===

Kythorn 3

Today was another pleasant journey up the valley. Things are quiet now; I think my guess that the Hand of Doom would break up was correct.

Twice today, we saw Q’Horg. He is now ahead of us, and seems to have guessed our route. This is not good news. I am not looking forward to a confrontation between any more Mercykillers. At the very least, it will provoke another round of seditious accusations from the troops about me being responsible for this quarrel, but it could cost us man-power. This might be a dangerous pair we’re up against.

I have considered that they might just be after Tyriol, and if a conflict does happen, I shall watch how they deal with him closely. I suspect that I will be the primary target of any aggression, and rightly so; I am the captain here, and at range, I am as dangerous as anyone else. Further, the Mercykillers do hate me, whether their goal is to kill me or take Tyriol alive.

I had a short talk with the troops tonight about strategy. I am certain we will face these villains, and I worry that they might be clever bastards who will use probing strikes to wear us down, and then strike hard when we are fatigued. I have informed them to pace themselves in any battle from now on, and that Lucretius and I will give orders to increase our tempo and aggression in any battles in the future. There will be many, and we must prepare for them all.

===Leosferis Duo'din===

Kythorn 4

It seems my anxiety about the Mercykillers is unfounded, though my question about their motives will have to remain unanswered for a while. We have killed Leosferis and his giant, and as I write this, the troops are congratulating themselves, and picking through their gear.

From the beginning;

Today, we had to pick a new course up a steep chute and along a treacherous path that took us ever further away from Helgorod Valley. I regretted the loss of that scenic valley and the easy mode of travel one enjoys therein. Even when it rained, the journey was as pleasant as strangling a cat; easy and fast. The path to the monastery is challenging.

===A Carrion Scene===
At one point in the day we lighted upon a carrion scene; a dozen or more Hand scouts lay dead and butchered on the ground, seemingly unpillaged. The birds were already at them, and they protested our interruption of their feast, but I had to have a close look at this scene. The fact that no weapons or supplies were rounded up made me wonder what had taken these creatures. In truth, I was surprised to find any scouts here; the Hand of Doom, such as it is now, is many days behind us. These troops have been here for a while, and I am forced to wonder if we haven’t found some special detachment of the army that was being held in reserve, or perhaps was given some other task.

The orcs seemed to have been carved up by a large, heavy blade, and there wasn’t a sign of any fallen enemies. My capable scout wasn’t able to read the ground of the battle to provide any illumination, and so I resorted to a costly but effective ritual, and channelled a necromantic divination which allowed me to observe the last moments of one dead orc’s life. To my astonishment, I witnessed a single man, clad in trademark Mercykiller black and azure, fighting like a hurricane with a long-hafted pole-axe. He moved with blinding speed, but at the same time didn’t seem to be exerting himself enough to bring the wind into his chest. This dangerous man is no doubt Leosferis, and the fact that he is able to dispatch a score and half of orcs on his own is not reassuring. There will be a reckoning with him soon.

I was forced to ruminate upon our relationship with the Mercykillers after I watched that Necromantic vision, and considered the threat of Leosferis and his bodyguard.

Although I had some hand in the provocation of the Mercykillers, I still stand by my conviction that we were fated to cross blades, unless I was willing to abandon the mission to see the Lady. Their leader, Ithrax Mal Turik, a petulant dragon man, made it abundantly clear that if we got in their way that there would be a fight. When I seized the initiative I was vilified, but in the end the score was 6-1 in our favour. While this might be enough of a thumbed nose towards the management of the Mercykillers for them to pursue our destruction, I am now convinced that a certain half-elf in my company might have just as much to do with their relentless hunt for us. Perhaps the quest for the alabaster pillars and the council of Lady Saharel was all just a ruse (for the Mercykillers anyway), and their primary objective has been Tyriol all along.

Something to consider. Perhaps I should just hand the pup over to his father’s thugs and see if they cool their heels a bit.

At any rate, while we were conducting our investigation of Leosferis’ most recent victims, we were set upon by a trio of ettins, and were duly reminded that we are now a good piece into the mountains. Our brushes with the local fauna are becoming more dangerous by the mile.

In this particular encounter, I witnessed again the amazing healing powers of Hrogan Goringrim. Though these ettins were able to launch our front-line fighters fifteen feet with each easy swing of their bull-sized arms, each time they stood up as though nothing had touched them, as the prayers of the dwarf were continuously canted. Ettins are a tricky thing to manage; they move about erratically and much quicker than one would guess, and their two heads don’t always pull in the same direction. In the end, we finally were able to coral the monsters, and I held them in place with some powerful necromancy. Our strikers did the rest, and there are now three less ettins in the mountains to bother us.

We did not tarry long at the scene. The slaughter of the orcs by Leosferis no doubt drew these ettins in the first place, and I did not want to face that which comes hunting for those that hunt ettin. We forced a march along the difficult trail, and as the sun vanished behind the mountains, Hrogan announced it was time to stop.

===Death of Leosferis===
We are camped in that same spot presently; it is a deep ravine between two nearly vertical walls of stone that form the bases of two opposed mountains. Hrogan says we are heading up and into the further of the two, and that the trail was now too dangerous to follow in the dark.

The area we are in now was a bridge-head. Two plateaus of rock face each other from across the abyss, and where they are most proximal to each other, a sturdy rope bridge joined them. I say ‘joined’ in the past-tense, because we cut that bridge down in a frantic effort to trap Leosferis and Q’Horg on the other side. We didn’t succeed.

Before first watch was concluded, the Mercykillers struck. We had decided to camp on the far side of the ravine, and so it was that Leosferis and the giant approached from the other side. This bought us time, as they had to close the distance, and that is well; we could buy no time with words, as the pair came at us as straight and quickly as they could.

Before we could let a volley fly, Leosferis ran towards the bridge along the narrow rocky path, and jumped when he was about twenty feet or so away; he was able to hang in the air like a cloud it seemed, as his jump was of such an extraordinary distance and height, and as he landed on the bridge, nearly halfway across, he closed the rest of the distance in blinding speed. He was already in our midst, and we didn’t even get a shot off!

The giant was closing somewhat slower, and I ordered the men to cut the bridge. I had ordered this done before we laid camp, but Hrogan simply said “Haven’t you destroyed enough bridges lately, wizard?” Fair enough. Now, we were left to frantically hew at the ropes with what men we could spare, in order to keep the number of Mercykillers in our midst down to a manageable size. Fortunately, Leosferis came straight for me, and left several of my thugs behind to do just the job needed.

Those that attempted to stand up to the dervish and his long-hafted blade did not fare well; he was able to slice with inhuman speed, and every stroke sent his opponent flying back, which is a dangerous prospect on the edge of a deadly drop. Fortunately, he only got one swipe at me before I took to the sky. Levitating out of his reach, and favouring a wound to my abdomen that nearly eviscerated me in one back-handed swipe, I started to rain arcane missiles on the villain for all I was worth. My own advice about pacing ourselves in battle was sent to the wayside as quickly as my waistcoat had been tailored by the halberd of my new friend.

I will say this; if I had gotten out of that madman’s reach part of a second later, I would not be writing this presently. One more stroke like the one he had given me would have sent me directly to Set, and then who knows what would have happened after that? As it stands, I don’t think he planned on me giving him the slip so easily. By the time I was aloft and had rained down my third or fourth missile at the man, the bridge was cut free, and the noise of it crashing into the wall of the opposite side of the ravine caught Leosferis’ attention; he looked away only for a second, but that was long enough for Lucretius and Paetr to slam into him from behind, and pin him to a wall. I sent one more blast into the face of the man, and he fell dead.

The giant was only slowed by the cutting of the bridge, and not waylaid entirely. He actually found a way across further down the path, by way of jumping onto a stony protrusion from the far wall, and then leaping a tremendous leap onto our side. The battle with the giant went quickly, with our front-line able to drive him off and over the cliff with relative ease. The noise of the enormous sack of meat and bones coming apart at the bottom of the ravine was as satisfying as the indignant look on Leosferis’ face as he watched the last bolt of arcane death hit him between the eyes.

One more nest Mercykiller assassins taken care of.

There is no hope for plunder from the giant, but Leosferis did serve as a reasonable recompense for the nasty cut he gave me; his gear is fine and valuable, his halberd being perhaps the most powerful magic we have captured thus far. All of his gear has been shared out to the group, except the halberd, which is now under lock and key in the company coffer.

Hrogan says we’ll make the monastery the day after next.

==Back Door to the Monastery==

Kythorn 7

We have gained entry through a back door of sorts, into the monastery, and have found a terrible mess that needs cleaning up. We are now saddled with many wounded and dying priests and priestesses, and have a difficult climb to the upper reaches of the monastery ahead of us.

Yesterday, after two days of mountaineering without incident, winding our way through a hidden chasm at the base of the great mountain upon which the Monastery is settled, Hrogan brought us to the back door. This is a dwarven door that only knowing dwarven eyes can find, and only the crinkle of a dwarven nose and the secret whistle of dwarven lips can open. Beyond the door was an unused passage that we followed for the better part of a day. The tunnel is straight for the most part, and just barely high enough to allow a human-sized visitor to walk comfortably along.

After numerous hours in the monotonous corridor, I reckoned it was time for a rest. We put down our gear and made camp. As we were rolling out bags and getting ready to settle, Tyriol stopped us, claiming he had heard screaming coming from up ahead. “Nonsense, ranger” I replied. “That is just the inner voice of your ego you hear.” I was in no spirit to continue. Ever since my fall at Kor’Murron, I walk with one bad knee, and the odd step I take with it is now punishing to my feet when I must beat a course along stone for too very long. I was happy to sit for a while.

Most of us continued to unpack our things, but Tyriol was now joined in his auscultation of the hall by Lucretius, whom I knew would not let this business rest. They were right of course; a terrible torture was happening about half a mile up the tunnel. I could hear it clear as a bell, but I was not inclined to go running up the hall just yet.

Let me explain myself. Aching feet and throbbing knee notwithstanding, the would-be-hero must hone the fine art of showing up just a wee bit after he is needed. Certainly, there were some terrible violations and torments being inflicted upon someone up the hall, and it was most probably not the resident priests (Hrogan assured us that even the Ilmatari do not self-flagellate). The only answer came to me immediately; elements of the Hand of Doom were here, causing more mischief, and I couldn’t have been happier. If we let things go for a while, and the victims ahead got a good chance to see just how terrible things can get, then it would be time to show up and bail them out of hot water. It does no good to save the day before the damsels-in-distress have had a good dose of terror, violation and torment.

In our case, things worked out perfectly.

I continued to play deaf to the delicious noises up the hall, while more and more of the men added their ears to the concerned camp. I slumped down onto my bedroll, and sat cross-legged for a moment, when one particularly shrill scream carried down the hall clear as you like. “By Orcus’ prick, these fools will want to rush in now, Kethoth” I said to myself. I feigned a concerned look, and hushed the men.

It was Lucretius that acted first. “I’ll not stand about while you tuck yourself in, wizard!” he belched at me. Damn that lizard’s hide. I was just about to make myself look good when he seized the initiative from me. They were off like a shot, and I got to my feet against the crunching protest of my knee, and hobbled after them. I was happy to observe that Radha, throughout this worrisome eavesdropping episode merely unpacked her things with a detached indifference, and went straight for the book I have loaned her. She may prove to be even more useful than I thought, despite having one lovely leg planted in the realm of undeath. Her presence in my company is a trial of some sort, and I pray for the insight to figure out the rules and win the game.

Back to rescuing damsels, though.

===The Forge===
We ran down the hall and forced our way into a forge. The room isn’t overly large, especially for dwarven construction; about fifty feet on a side. In one corner is a great fire that was glowing hot when we got there; it was being stoked by a cruel group of orcs who were roasting the feet of some of the uglier priests, making some sport for themselves, while the prettier ones were being held against a wall and tied up on various stone work benches on the far side of the room.

We had to fight our way into the room, as we made a very loud arrival; the armoured boots of Lucretius make more noise than a stone golem in a bellwright’s shop, and the orcs in the forge mounted a stiff resistance against us. With only a small opening to fight from, and that partially obstructed by large crates of supplies stacked twice as high as a man, it was a bit of a slog. However, as Lucretius and Paetr slowly pushed back the odorous defenders of the forge-cum-torture-chamber, I managed to clear the door, and levitated above the crates immediately.

I surveyed the situation from my advantageous position, and saw that things were dire, for the hostages at least. There was no way we were going to be able to save them all; some of the orcs in the rear with the hostages, seeing their front-line falter under the press of my door-crashers, began to throw their victims into the forge. Two were tossed in before we could do much; the orcs that were pitching the prisoners into the fire began to run to the back corner of the room where the comelier morsels were locked up, clearly intending to kill them in the event that we gain the room. We were gaining the room quickly.

We were doing what we do best in a tight spot; our heavy front line gave no ground, and when a foot of ground was offered, they took it, and so the dauntless press of our might opened the room to us. Tyriol and Ender managed to work their craft, both getting into positions to make opportunistic strikes at our foes, but I could see they would not get to the lovely prisoners on the far side of the room in time. It was Kethoth’s time to wear the hero’s boots.

There was still a chaotic fray of enemies below me, which was slowly roiling and mixing with my troops every second, so that I could not find a good spot to blast away a clear landing site and take action. So, I unleashed the most powerful spell I had prepared, Grasp of the Grave, which bogged down a huge a portion of the room with spectral tendrils that clawed at and incapacitated most of our foes, while leaving my men free to mop them up.

I landed amidst the ghostly assault, and immediately used my arcane retreat in a manner that goes against my better judgment; I charged through the zone of necromantic assault, through most of the enemies in the room, and straight to the far side, where I faced off against a half-dozen of the cruel green brutes alone. As the world caught up with me, at the profound moment just before real time again intersects that of the arcane retreat, and the distorted faces of my foes again start to slowly move, I realized I could spare no effort; the hostages’ lives certainly depended on me, but my skin was also in jeopardy, so I fought hard. At that moment when the eyes of my prey again catch mine, and the sound of the world returned, I unleashed what hellfire is mine to command.

The ejaculation of flames that emanated from me seemed to protract the surreal feel of the arcane retreat. Many of the foes set against me were just starting to move again as the flames engulfed them, and they recoiled and screamed their last. A few larger ones were only a few feet away, and these were not the sort that a gout of flame will take out of the fight. They raised their weapons to face me, even while they burned from my hellfire. There were three of these left; two appeared to guarding a third who had all the usual orcish markings of rank. I was still under the effect of my Wizard’s Fury spell, and crumpled their resolve with a pair of arcane missiles that burned into the face of one and the neck of another.

I snarled and affected the most fearsome countenance that my nature and heritage will allow, and dressed it up with same illusion that I used to scare the hair off of Cayd’s feet. In the eyes of those three orcs, I appeared as a demon of pure fire, and my words burned their resolve. “Fall at my feet, rats, and beg my mercy!”

The two bodyguards did just that, though they might have just been dropping and rolling to put out the fires I had blanketed them in. The boss took flight though, to my irritation. There was a second passage out of the room, and he was only ten feet or so from it. He growled in defiance, and took off as fast as he could.

I was spent, and was not about to chase this brute down a hall by myself. He was easily a foot taller than me and thrice my weight and probably equal in nastiness and a sense of self-preservation. Fortunately, Lucretius wasn’t far away. I slowed the fleeing boss with a bolt of frost from the wicked sceptre that I have come to rely upon, and this was enough for Luc to slam into the beast and take him down. I looked on in satisfaction as Lucretius kneeled on the back of the now prostrate orcish prisoner, while my hellfire still burned the thing and licked at Luc’s armoured bulk.

I turned around to look at the priestesses I had just saved, and found I had made an honest mistake. I shall have to back up a bit to explain myself.

===Priya Moonstar===
As I was levitating above the crates before my ill-advised rush across the room, I espied what I thought must be a priestess of Sune; she was the one that the boss seemed most interested in, and indeed, he was calling for his souse-chefs to haul her off to the fire. From across the room I might have mistaken her for Sune herself, such was the appeal of the line of her hip and the mouth-watering angle of her jaw. “If there is one priestess worth saving” I said to myself “Certainly it is one of those strumpets. She might even be fun as a willing lover.” So, I made the assault detailed above.

When the room was ours, and the orc boss was pinned beneath the immense weight of our lizardly warlord, I turned to greet this woman, and noticed immediately that I mistook her; for starters, she is Eladrin. Elves don’t normally catch my eye like human women do, but she is a little juicier than most of her emaciated kind. The mistake was honest enough. However, she is also a priestess of Selûne, and for all their scampering about under the moon, they are as prudish as any other holy order, and I knew a willing fleshy reward was off the menu. Just the same, I bowed deeply at the waist, and flourished a greeting.

“Kethoth Sepret, your servant, m’lady”

Through a chattering jaw and between choking tears, she replied “Priya Moonstar, sir. Priya of Selûne...”

There was more than one beautiful woman liberated by Kethoth in the forge, but Priya proved to be the most useful.  She has accompanied us all day, and we are glad of her help.

But let me put down a few words about the thirty minutes we spent in the Forge, because it impacted our roster tremendously.

First of all, here is a list of the survivors that I managed to free;

*Kulda Murghom, dwarven priestess of Ohgma
*Mendel Krioy, priest of Ohgma
*Darmol Rugan, priest of Berronar Truesilver
*Iria Mapress, priestess of Selûne
*Regnal Dantrol, priest of Illmaetry
*Priya Moonstar, priestess of Selûne
*Miri Tremalkin, delicious descendant of the Miserable Knight

From these survivors, we learned that the Hand of Doom came here five days ago.  This puts their arrival at the 2nd of Kythorn, two days after we destroyed Warklegnaw’s Span.  Clearly, they were across the divide long before we destroyed the bridge, and after the events of the day, I am forced to wonder if this Hand of Doom business wasn’t more about causing a diversion and keeping forces held down at the Roost, rather than helping to liberate the main objective, which has, all along, been the Monastery.  That is just a thought.

So, the rabble I liberated were the only survivors.  They didn’t know it at the time, but we have now been through the entire Monastery, and there is no one else alive.  The other scores of priests and priestesses were slaughtered for sport, and even I found the treatment of the souls here in slightly poor taste.  More on that, below.

We got much information from Mendel, who is a dour little man.  If I could do things over, I would have withheld my heroic charge across the room until the orcs threw this simpering wretch into the flames.  Mendel seemed to have an air of authority, although as I understand it he has no legitimate claim above Hrogan. I pressed Mendel for information about the attackers on the upper levels, and he delivered information like a petulant child.

===Miri Tremalkin===
The other notable woman here is the young descendant of Gaebril Tremalkin.  Miri Tremalkin is a pale little plum that is just barely ready to be plucked, but my interest in her goes beyond her alabaster thighs and bad-girl disposition; she has some mastery over shadow and darkness, which has likewise made her interesting to the Selûnites.  In fact, Ms. Moonstar is here primarily to study this girl and her family.

I had a brief conversation with Miri which only served to pique my interest even more, though time did not permit my further examination of her.

===Interrogation Results and Dilemmas===
I interrogated the boss-orc that Lucretius had taken prisoner, and he was able to tell us about the leadership and structure of the orcs that have come here;

*Rahn’ohk is the name of some literate orcish who is tasked to gain access to the library
*Morja is the name of the wyvern-rider who led the Red Hand of Doom against us at Kor’Murron
*Huu’rom ak Murul, ‘The Blue’, is the chieftain who has assembled this army, and is looking for something in the archives.  I don’t doubt that he is here looking for the legendary weapons that may or may not be found in the vault of this place

This orc says that The Blue will be quite angry if Morja and Rahn’ohk are not able to uncover the archives and whatever it is he is looking for.  Morja’s life depends on it, in fact, after his botched attack of Kor’Murron.  I might be able to use this as a bit of leverage later…

The fate of the orcish prisoner was a sticky subject.  I assured this prisoner that I would not kill him if he answered my questions, but as usual, the more blood-thirsty members of my retinue were all for disemboweling the captive brute.  I commanded that the orc be sent back the way we had come, down the tunnel to the back door.  There was no way he would be able to get out, and he would die a hungry, maddening death, no doubt wearing his fingers to the bones trying to claw his way out of stone door at the end.  We sent him on his way, and sealed up the entrance behind him.  No one seemed pleased with this outcome.  Why these simple souls don’t see the aesthetic value of a creative sentence is beyond me.  At any rate, so long as Kethoth is doing the interrogations, I will decide what happens to the prisoners.

===Preparing to Assault the Monastery===
As we sorted out the details of what was going on upstairs, and had the priests and priestesses healed up as well as time would permit, we started putting together the team for the assault of the upper levels.  This is when Hrogan decided to part company.  He stated that he felt his most proximal charge was here in the forge, looking after the survivors, and that he had carried out his duty to bring us here, as promised. He was correct on all counts, and I don’t begrudge him his decision.  We needed a small force to watch the rear, and I wanted those freed monastery inhabitants looked after.

I should add presently that I find myself with a dose of respect for the dwarven-kind that I have not hitherto had.  They are durable and determined, and do as they say they will do.  A reliable lot.  I might keep more of them in my company in the future.

Paetr, we were happy to hear, agreed to seeing the upper reaches of the monastery cleared of the green-skinned brutes.

Lucretius agreed to stay behind and guard the forge.  He is really the only able-bodied creature, besides Hrogan, so it is a logical choice.  Still, I know he wants to head upstairs and face the orcs.  There are more of those noxious Tiamat-worshipers up there, and we are both spoiling to kill as many of those as we can.

The Selûnite, Priya, has been with us all day, and she has proven to be as capable a healer as Hrogan.  This strange priestess wields a magical bow of no small power, and her healing abilities are woven into her divine attacks.  She is a boon to our party.

So with the new roster complete, we set out for the upper reaches.
