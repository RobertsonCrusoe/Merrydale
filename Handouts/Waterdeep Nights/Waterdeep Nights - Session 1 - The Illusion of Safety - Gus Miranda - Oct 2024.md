SESSION 1 RECAP

Thank you guys very much for the session this morning. That was lots of fun, for me as a DM.

As you figured out while traipsing through Falrik's house, he is a man that has relied quite heavily on illusion and enchantment traps to keep himself safe over the decades. Add to that a couple of esoteric monsters, and some mechanical traps, as well as the entire vibe of being an abandoned house, and you might even say he's been successful in keeping himself hidden for the past 40 years.

But, as the saying goes, you've only scratched the literal surface, and we left off with the promise of plunging into the depths underground!

DM NOTES:

- You guys rolled remarkably well in the initial attempt to get over the fence. Not in climbing, as that was meant to be easy, but in whether you'd be spotted by passers-by or any other bystander.

- The well had an enchantment to draw you in with the radius being right at the corner of the house where Steadypost stood and failed his save. I figured you'd just wave it off, but then as you got closer the Magic Mouth trap kicked in and I was so hoping that gnome would fall into the freezing water. But it was not meant to be.

- I had planned for you to go in through the kitchen and then be weakened by the Ooze and seriously hurt by the spider's surprise round (that spider's poison attack was no joke). But you guys went into it ass-backward to your great benefit. It also helped having a half-giant that can kill my 3HD spider in one shot.

- It was also infuriating that you not only put half of my 13 rats to sleep, but Sewer Druid talked them into being non-hostile. The skeleton trap worked perfectly as it was meant to spook the rats, not the PC's. The rats, though only 1/2HD each were diseased, which were also meant to cause major havoc. But no, he pied-pipered them back under the bed and into their nest.

- The trap in the living room came from the Dragon adventure I loosely based this dungeon around. The well is an enchantment that draws you in, followed by an illusion to get you to go drown in the cold water. The spectral man is an illusion meant to draw you in so you can be frightened by the fear effect and run away. It was effective, but I think you are meant to use the Dispel Magic scroll that you found in the library to get rid of this trap, and I left it that way to get you to do that. Alas, like a good party of adventurers, you guys decided to hoard your scrolls and leave the trap in place, and go around it.

- The final fight with the Animated Dagger was a little stunted because I knew everyone had to go. The dagger was meant to attack twice per round for 6 rounds and then fall inert. But we didn't have time, so I just had it attack each of you once and then fall inert only cause we ran out of time. I think the end result would've been roughly about the same.

  Animated Dagger: AC 4; HD n/a; HP n/a; THAC0 14; DAM 1-4; MOR n/a; EXP 0

  The magic missile attack should've done something, but the thing has no HD or HP, and is a Dagger+1, so... The spell was lost, but the party gained a Dagger +1.

- By my time-keeping, you guys have spent about 3 hours exploring the house. You came into the house at 18h00, and are now at 21h00. You still have the rest of the night, as Zula wants the package delivered before sunrise, which is expected around 07h00.

I just want to clarify that I ran the half-goliath and sewer druid by our magnanimous DM before any of this retro-gaming started.

Gudrun has missed a lot of her hits, or else the DM would be even less happy about her. Also, she didn't actually crit that spider - that was just a middling hit: (6 damage on a d10). A max damage crit against that thing would have been 36 HP of damage, I think (1d10+8)x2. Lucky for the baddies that we need natural 20s in 2ed for that."

Gus (20 Oct 2024):
"And I want to clarify that I have no issue with Half-goliath or sewer druid or gnome illusionist/thief, etc. It's all fun!

But one of the things that I am learning is that classic AD&D as designed by Gygax et al is multiple small encounters, building up to one big bloody fight.

So, this scenario that is composed of 1/4 from an adventure found in the pages of Dragon Magazine, 1/4 from a different adventure found in the pages of Dungeon Magazine, and 1/2 my own invention, I'm skewing to the Gygax model of small encounters with traps and puzzles and monsters meant to test you and cause attrition before you reach the big bad guy.

So it's ok that Gudrun killed my spider in one shot, because Gorm has been down twice and I know Fishook's already cast his magic missile for the day. And I really liked that Gorm dealt with my rats without violence! It was a clever move that saved a lot of rolls vs disease. (And Cure Disease is a 3rd Level spell.)

You're moving down into the 2nd level of the dungeon, so the difficulty is about to be ramped up!

Or you can cut your losses with what you've plundered and make a run for it..."
