SESSION 0 RECAP

Following the narrative I had sent you previously, the action began at the heart of the Docks, near the intersection of Dock Street and Odd. Zula, a Blue Dragonborn from Halruaa stood gravely injured, having barely survived an assassination attempt, and he grimly watched his skyship lift into the air and out of the Waterdhavian harbour, abandoning him in this city.

With bloody hands, he pointed to a group of rough-looking individuals who had stood as bystanders and promised to make them wealthy beyond their dreams, should they be willing to assist him in his time of need. If they agreed, then they should meet him at the Inn of the Dripping Dagger in the Trades two hours hence. With this, he leapt aboard a rickshaw and disappeared north into the city.

Without even the time to survey the scene or introduce themselves to one another, they were set upon by the City Watch, made scapegoats for the violence that had just occurred - perhaps as cover for the real assassins to make their escape.

Some of you recognised the notorious Corporal Dent as the leader of this squad. Dent is well known for his corruption and brutality, especially when it came to the weaker and poorer citizens of the Docks.

And here, what was meant to be an exciting re-introduction to the AD&D 2E combat system, degenerated into a lopsided rout of the City Watch by the PC's. Helped in part by the three Natural 1 rolls I made at the outset, the PC's were able to subdue the entire squad before the end of the first round. You put 5 of them to sleep, and successfully enchanted Corporal Dent and ordered him to flee. However, just to be sure, your brutes chased down the already fleeing Dent, and grabbed his arm in such a way as to break his elbow and render him unconscious from the pain.

You then all ignored the first useful magical item of the campaign: the Paralysis Mancatcher that was immediately dropped at the Half-Goliath's feet when I rolled my first 1.

At this, it was a trip down memory lane as the local duo of the Ratcatcher and Priest of Mask decided that it would be prudent to dive into the alleyways and shadows of the city, ignoring the obvious traps set by muggers and conmen alike, and then successfully making their way to the Dripping Dagger Inn, adopting a helpless little kitten along the way.

Meanwhile, the three foreigners opted for the more direct approach of leaving a trail of broken, bruised, and unconscious bodies along the main roads of Waterdeep. This included a mad Red Wizard and a gnomish child who was just out having fun playing pretend with his friends.

Eventually both groups arrived at the Dripping Dagger Inn, where the proprietor Filiare was expecting them and delivered the news that their patron, Zula, had quite generously hired the entire Inn out for the next 48 hours so as to be able for him and you to conduct your business here without worry of interruption.

Zula arrived and laid out the parameters of the work he requires for you to do: a crazed and paranoid half-elf fugitive thief has been holed up in a villa just north of here. This thief, by the name of Falrik, is renowned for his ability to know the comings and goings of Halruaans that visit Waterdeep, as he is paranoid that those Halruaans who hunt him will one day show up to take his head. Zula believes that Falrik knows something about the assassination attempt on his life, and the hijacking of the Southern Star Skyship. And more importantly, of the kidnapping of his charge, Cassio son of the Halruaan Archmage he serves.

Zula believes time is of the essence, as the longer he waits the more likely that Falrik will deepen his defences, and that the trail made by his would-be-assassins will fade.

Since Zula is grievously wounded, he is in no shape to undertake any action. Thus he has hired you, for the sum of 100gp each plus whatever mundane equipment you require, and whatever booty you can help yourselves to while in Falrik's villa. In return you are to deliver Falrik to him alive and as unharmed as possible, as soon as possible - meaning he expects it done before the sun rises tomorrow.

Zula also provided a small box containing 12 Potions of Healing to help see you through the deadly traps you are likely to encounter, and a Scroll of Whispering Wind to be used in order to send word that the target has been acquired and a Rickshaw or similar vehicle will come to collect the prize.

DM NOTES:
- All spellcasters can rest up and prepare fresh spells at the Dripping Dagger before the mission.
- Gorm rolled 94% on his sewer check and, as a guild member, he can find a sewage entrance to the villa.
- Fishook identified the grabbed staff as a Thayan Fire Staff: warm to the touch, does 1d6 bludgeoning + 1 fire damage as melee weapon, also casts Affect Normal Fires and Burning Hands each once per day as 1st level spells.

GORM'S RATCATCHER MONOLOGUE (Stephen Robertson, 6 Oct 2024)

"If'n there weren't no rats, there'd be no ratcatchers! So's you see, we ratcatchers are more ratcatch-and-releasers. We be keep'n the rats under control. Our control that is. Not in our interest to get rid of 'em, ye see. Well, I suppose, more technical-like, it's not in the guild's interest. We rat'chers could find other work I've not a doubt. But if'n we did, then the guild wouldn't get our fees, ye see."

He jams a finger down to emphasize his point.

"Sellars'n Plumm'rs learned long ago that the real money lies in keepin' the rats just the right amount of mean. That's the sweet spot where the City will pay ye to fix a problem that they'll consider maintaining the modus operandi - that's Netherese for sta'us quo, by the by - as a win. So, technically, you just don't let the problem get worse. One of our guildmistresses - the Iron Lady we calls 'er - she 'splained it to me like what the ol' thieves' guilds used to do - a protection racket, she called it. 'Cept we were doin' the opposite, like, since we were the good'uns. Didn't follow meself, but then, that's why she's guildmistress and I'm not, I suppose...

He gets a sly look in his eye, sits up in his chair and with an exaggerated nonchalance continues.

"Anyway, won't never not be rats in Waterdeep."

He stares at everyone expectantly, then suddenly slams his hand on the table with a feral grin.

"Couldn't spell it, like!"

He howls with laughter at his own joke.

PRE-SESSION 1 BRIEFING: FALRIK'S VILLA (Gus Miranda, 17 Oct 2024)

A little bit of time skipping, in order to expedite the next session as you have plenty of time to prepare for the mission ahead.

To recap: Falrik is a Halruaan half-elf fugitive who has been hiding in Waterdeep for the past 40 years. Zula, the blue dragonborn, would like to question him on the matter of his attempted assassination in the Docks, but it is unlikely Falrik will come willingly. You have been hired to quickly enter into Falrik's villa and bring him back alive (and "mostly unharmed") to the Dripping Dagger. Time is of the essence.

The villa is at the corner of Delzorin and Copper Streets in the North Ward.

RECON RESULTS:

- Despite the Waterdeep map making it look like the entire property is the house, the actual building only takes up the north-eastern third of the property, with a garden wrapping around the southern and western sides. The entire property is surrounded by a 10 foot wall, and the house itself is only a single story with three vaulted roofs in parallel running north-south, and containing a variety of chimneys.

- Copper Street is paved and cleared of snow regularly by the city making it a rather busy thoroughfare. Across the villa is the Holyhands House, an "All Faiths" Hostel that receives travellers and pilgrims at all hours of the day and night. Delzorin Street, on the other hand, is unpaved and mostly left to the residents themselves to clear the snow, making the street an icy, muddy, frozen mess.

- None of the chimneys are emitting smoke despite the terrible freeze this winter, and the main door — a rather large and sturdy oak door measuring nearly 10 feet wide — on Delzorin Street is blocked by a build up of ice and snow that reaches nearly halfway up the door. It does not seem like this door has been opened all winter, and possibly not in years.

- There is a park-like alley that wraps around the property along the south and west. Normally a pleasant and busy place to escape the bustle of the city, but at the moment it is covered in snow. There are footpaths worn into the snow as shortcuts. A sturdy metal gate is located in the south western corner of the property. Through the gate you can see something of the grounds and main building. Snow is piled high all around the property, and there is no sign this back gate has been used either.

- Gorm the Sewer Rat found an old schematic showing the villa has indoor plumbing and a garderobe somewhere near the north east corner of the house which falls directly into the sewer. If this is still there, it may provide a direct entrance into the house via the sewer. The bad news is that it is just a narrow garderobe pipe, which means you will have to put in some work and time if you want to Shawshank it.

- Overall, the sense is that this is an abandoned house. But Zula has warned you that Falrik is a fugitive that has spent 40 years hiding, and Zula himself has it on good authority that the half-elf is in there.
