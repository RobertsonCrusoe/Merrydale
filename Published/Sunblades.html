⚔ THE SUN BLADES OF DAGGERDALE ⚔

Part I of The Military Establishment of Daggerdale – A report prepared for His Majesty’s Embassy at Castle Radiance by Beliard Kathklan Emmarask, Secretary to the Ambassador

I. The Sun Blades

The Sun Blades are the standing army of Daggerdale, founded in 1410 DR by the Whitegate family and the Church of Amaunator to replace the patchwork militia that had previously defended the dale. They are headquartered at Castle Radiance in the Desertsmouth Mountains and maintain garrisons throughout the dale – at Dagger Falls, the fortified farm settlements, and forward positions along the Netherese frontier. At any given time the force numbers in the hundreds of soldiers, organised into numbered companies with traditional names, under the overall command of a General. Names preceded by † in this document denote confirmed or presumed killed in action.

Conscription & Service

All men in the dale must serve a minimum of 2 years (raised to 3 years at the Greengrass 1480 Strategic Meeting), entering in their 18th year. New cohorts are inducted on Greengrass and train together. Women are not subject to conscription but may volunteer; they comprise approximately 9% of the force. Over 2,000 retirees in the dale keep their weapons and armour and can be summoned within 24–48 hours by any captain or higher officer. The last day of the Sun Blade contractual calendar is 19 Ches. The Muster occurs in the tenday following the Vernal Feast (19 Ches): 288 soldiers rotate out and 288 come in. During this period, over 500 additional young soldiers are present in Dagger Falls. The recruit training companies (11th–14th) absorb incoming conscripts under the Captain of the Recruits.

Soldiers live a near-monastic life in their first two years: no drinking, gambling, fornication, or bawdy songs. They receive 10 days off during the Summer Solstice Festival. More than half of soldiers stay on beyond the mandatory service.

Ranks & Pay

Rank Pay (per tenday) Notes
Footman 10 gp (5 gp if single, held in trust) Standard conscript. Families paid while soldier serves.
Corporal 15 gp Immediate promotion for those staying beyond 2-year minimum.
Sergeant 20 gp Promising soldiers promoted directly to Sgt. on re-enlistment.
Lieutenant 25 gp Company-level officer commanding a platoon.
Captain 30 gp Company commander.
General 40 gp Commander of all companies.

Paid at end of every other month. Soldiers may not carry more than 10 gp on their person while on duty.

Unit Structure

Unit Composition
Squad 11 footmen/corporals + 1 sergeant (12 total)
Platoon 3 squads under 1 lieutenant (36+ total)
Company 3 platoons (or more/less) under 1 captain
Army All companies under the General

Note: Some sources give a smaller “unit” of 1 sergeant + 7 soldiers for smaller farm garrisons, and “a company of 32” for larger farms – suggesting flexible garrison sizing distinct from the standard squad/platoon table. In practice, companies vary considerably in size: the 2nd “Mountaineers” field 5 platoons, the 7th “Bright Spears” had 10, while others maintain the standard 3.

Religious Command Structure

The Sun Blades maintain a parallel religious chain of command integrated with the military hierarchy. Each platoon has a Chaplain attached (a battle-trained priest who lives alongside the lieutenant). Each company has a Sunlord attached – a senior battle-priest, often a former soldier, who wears plate armour and shares decision-making with the Captain. The head of this religious structure is the Zenith Lord (Zenith Lord Argent Whitegate holds this title). Orders flow from the Zenith Lord through both chains simultaneously; conflicts between the two are described as “unknown.” About half of squads in the field also have a trainee priest attached. Separately, the Daggerdale Concordat mandates that each chartered company (White Cloaks, Redjacks, FDL, etc.) be assigned one Magistrate from the Yellow Abbey “to see to the moral rectitude of the Company and to provide aid as needed and appropriate to both the Company and the Sun Blades.” This is the provision under which Magistrate Madigara Dale serves with the Bright Spears' 4th Platoon at Thistle Ridge.

Weapons, Tactics & Standard Issue

All soldiers are trained in longsword, longspear, and longbow (issued on joining, kept for life). Standard armour: chain mail + light shield (army crest on shield, company crest on cloak). The yellow tabard is the distinguishing garment of the Sun Blades. Rank is indicated by chevrons on the tabard. Trained in shield walls and rampart defence. All receive some training in mounted combat; those excelling are mustered into the Sun Dragoons. Those showing talent for woodland scouting (especially elven speakers) join the Foresters. All captains and many officers carry a Radiant Blade – a magical sword forged at Castle Radiance, awarded to exceptional officers as a form of medal. Sun Blades “don't act without orders, though they usually have detailed orders with contingencies laid out” – a reliable but somewhat static force. Patrols actively maintain road clearances, cutting back encroaching forest to keep roads approximately 20 feet wide. Field communications include dispatch by carrier pigeon and dove from farm magistrates to Castle Radiance; bonded culvers (swifts) are also used by individual officers. Bandit justice is severe. The roads into the dale are lined with burned stakes, gibbets, and piles of severed hands—a grim deterrent. A caravan scout's account records the common belief that “the Sun Blades cut off the hands of all the bandits they catch; they burn one, hang half, and let the other half go”—though the arithmetic of this folk saying does not quite add up.

II. Command

The Sun Blades answer to a dual authority. Before the Daggerdale Concordat, they were a military arm of the Church of Amaunator, answering ultimately to the Whitegate family. After the Concordat (Elesias 1482), administrative and financial control passed to the Governors' Assembly, though the officer corps remained overwhelmingly Amaunatori.

Supreme Authority (Pre-Concordat)

Lord Luminous Whitegate

Lord of Castle Radiance, Grand Master of the Order of the Lambent Shield. Supreme military and political authority in the Dale pre-Concordat. Commands both the Sun Blades (through the General) and the Order of the Lambent Shield directly.

Zenith Lord Argent Whitegate

Head of the Church of Amaunator. Luminous's brother. Opens every Strategic Meeting with prayer. The Sun Blades were originally his church's military arm. Since the Concordat, Argent has become an increasingly figurehead authority—mostly resident at Castle Radiance, with Bishop Blaze Whitegate handling the daily administration of the church and the town.

Civilian Authority (Post-Concordat, 1482+)

Consul Kesh Oakdale

RANK
ret. Cpt., late of 16th Company Sun Blades

First Consul of the Governors' Assembly, elected unanimously. Calls the Assembly, maintains order, and serves as the civilian head of the Sun Blades. Term: 15 Elesias 1482 – Shieldmeet 1484. Based in Governor's Hall, Dagger Falls. Formerly Lt. of the 7th Bright Spears, 3rd Platoon (Dwimmerholme/Charity Hills).

Bishop Blaze Whitegate

Bishop of Dagger Falls. Half-nephew of Argent. Runs the Church of Amaunator in daily practice; with Argent increasingly resident at Castle Radiance, Blaze is the effective head of church and civil administration in Dagger Falls. Sits on the Adjudicating Committee—the joint civil-military-ecclesiastical body overseeing the Thistle Ridge Reclamation. Religious authority over Sun Blade chaplains. Veteran of the Order of the Lambent Shield.

General Command

General of the Sun Blades

General Dauntless Thunderstorm

ASSIGNMENT
Castle Radiance – Madigan's Hall (War Room)

Highest-ranking military commander of all Sun Blade companies, continuing post-Concordat. Son of Karrigan Thunderstorm and Radiance Whitegate (daughter of Loedegan); thus a member of “the other side” of the Whitegate family. On excellent terms with the Whitegates, which “has actually put him at odds with other members of the Thunderstorm Family, but he carries on his duties as though there is no conflict.” Many present-day Thunderstorms serve in the Sun Blades, though none have joined the Order of the Lambent Shield. He is notably a follower of Torm rather than Amaunator, despite commanding an overwhelmingly Amaunatori institution. Attends every Strategic Meeting. Reported to Lord Whitegate before the Concordat; now answers to the Consul. “The General of the Sun Blades makes no oath to the Magistrates” – the General operates with command autonomy from the church's judicial arm. Member of the Adjudicating Committee for the Thistle Ridge Reclamation (1483). Staff includes Quartermasters, Intelligence officers, and 9 attendants. Personally led ~100 soldiers of the 2nd Company Mountaineers in a dawn assault on the Sons of Bel cult at Foothills Farm (Kythorn 1480); the “Dalehearth Company” volunteered for this mission despite still recovering from Liberty Hill.

III. Companies

Garrison of Dagger Falls

1st Company – “Dalehearth”

ASSIGNMENT
Dagger Falls – Town walls, Tower, gates
STRENGTH
4 platoons

Primary garrison of the town; “usually deployed in the town and patrolling nearby.” Provides wall guards, gate security, and customs support. Captain Lucius ordered new lights mounted on towers and lantern-bearer patrols against night-flying threats (1480). The “Dalehearth Company” won renown at the Battle of Liberty Hill.

Captain Lucius – Captain of the 1st Company as of 1479. Responded to rumours of things flying overhead at night by commissioning new lights and lantern patrols.

Captain Hazel Haverstone (retired) – Captain of the 1st Company over 30 years ago. The first soldier in the Sun Blades to achieve the rank of captain without any battlefield experience, having served mostly in charge of logistics at the Castle. Now a Master Baker running Haverstone's Bakery in the Old Market of Dagger Falls. Many of her children and grandchildren have served in the Sun Blades; their spears, shields and cloaks adorn the wall behind the bakery's main counter.

Sgt. Buck Thistledown (retired) – Veteran of the Battle at Liberty Hill. Close comrade of Alen Reks. Organised the logistics for the White Cloak Bravo Tourney (Marpenoth 1480).

NOTE: Dorian Dale (Corporal, aged 21, Tarsakh 1479) and Lumina Whitegate both served their 2-year conscription in the 1st Company. Dorian's diary records their journey from Dagger Falls to the Castle in Tarsakh 1479, where they met Warburton and Troia Trustar. Named platoons: The 2nd Dalehearth Platoon was “first over the wall” at Liberty Hill, gaining the rampart after driving off Lotus. The 3rd Dalehearth Platoon was led down the main concourse of the village as the battle closed. During the Saints' Battle at Liberty Hill (1480), White Cloaks stormed the walls “as leaders of platoons of Sun Blades, Militia, and volunteers”—an instance of Sun Blade platoons operating under chartered company command during a crisis.

1st Company, Escort Platoon – Cormanthor (1483)

A half-platoon of Sun Blades from the 1st Company (“Dalehearth”), escorting parties through Cormanthor to the Thistle Ridge area. Core: 8 men with spears and shields in single file, each responsible for a sector. 4 women foresters: two ride ahead scouting, one tails at distance, one stays with the main body. The platoon's average age is about 23; well-trained and disciplined. Lt. Riverstone is deeply knowledgeable about Cormanthor, warning that the forest “doesn't want us here” and has been “angry about our presence here since we erected the Standing Stone.”

Lt. Maddy Riverstone – Commander of 1st & 2nd Platoons, 1st Company (“Dalehearth”). Accompanied Chrysalis on the initial reconnaissance of Greenwood Farm (12 Tarsakh 1483). Also escorted parties from Gribbons' in Ches 1483 with 12 Sun Blades.

Sgt. Brendle Vale – Second most senior officer in the escort platoon.

Sephany – Forester, ~20 years old. Curly brown hair, kind round face. Skilled in wilderness survival; stays with the main group and provides instruction. Calm and confident.

Zelia Oakbough – Forester, formerly of the 16th Company, 3rd Platoon. Born and raised near Lostholme. Retired from the Sun Blades; now rides ahead of the escort column as a scout. Author of a field message to Lt. Riverstone. Recruited into the White Cloaks (Ches 1483).

NOTE: The platoon includes at least 4 female foresters: two ride ahead scouting, one tails the party at distance, and Sephany stays with the main body.

Mountain Operations

2nd Company – “Mountaineers”

ASSIGNMENT
Castle Radiance and mountain approaches
STRENGTH
5 platoons; “always deployed to Castle Radiance, and on patrol in the Desertsmouth Mountains.” Typically 120 Sun Blades garrisoned at the Castle (in Loedegan's Hall, alongside 60 Knights of the Order of the Lambent Shield). Wagons carrying approximately 30 coffins are dispatched from the Castle once per tenday—a measure of the attrition rate on the Netherese frontier.

Responsible for mountain patrols and the defence of the approaches to Castle Radiance. Captain Rainyerd attended Strategic Meetings and reported intelligence directly. The company had colour-coded platoons (e.g. “Red Platoon”). Elements of the 2nd Mountaineers also garrison Fort Last Sun (2 platoons permanently stationed, alongside 30 paladins of the Order of the Lambent Shield) and Fort Sansum at the edge of the Netherese Plaguelands.

Captain Rainyerd – Company Captain, as of 1479. Veteran mountain fighter. Noted that Sun Blades needed to adapt tactics for ranged combat against sophisticated orc threats near Old Man's Tooth.

Lt. Northman (promoted) – Led the Red Platoon. Promoted to Captain of the newly re-mustered 3rd Company “Iron Backs” (winter 1481).

Jannus Dale (retired) – Served under Lt. Hilltop in the Mountaineers. “Lived most of his life at the Castle.” After service, competed for the White Cloaks at the Thistle Ridge tournament (1483).

Tyrell Dale (retired) – Brother of Jannus. Served under Lt. Hilltop in the Mountaineers. “Their spears having found the hearts of orcs, ogres and Shadowvar alike.” Competed for the White Cloaks at Thistle Ridge (1483).

Chester Hawkford – Veteran Sun Blade, 12 years of service. Renewing his term during the Muster. Has been at the Castle in the 2nd Company for the last 4 years. “Much older” than a typical conscript. Married a girl from the Falls; wife Abigail. Children: twins Lionel and Lavinia (9), Reynard (5). Brother of Jasper Hawkford.

Lt. Tara Hilltop (KIA, 2 Tarsakh 1483) – Former Lieutenant of the Mountaineers. Left the Sun Blades to lead the White Cloaks; killed as a White Cloak during the gargoyle attack at the Thistle Ridge tournament. Also a healer associated with the Church of Amaunator.

Lt. Hardigan (defected – Sharran convert) – Sun Blade lieutenant who converted to the worship of Shar and betrayed the Dale. In Mirtul 1480, Hardigan and Priestess/Mayor Goering led the White Cloaks into a trap at Liberty Hill, where the two were operating as Sharran agents. One of the “evil Sharrans” responsible for the Liberty Hill conspiracy.

Cormanthor Forest Operations

4th Company – “Foresters”

ASSIGNMENT
Cormanthor – deep forest patrols and counter-insurgency
STRENGTH
3 platoons

Specialised in fighting within Cormanthor forest against orcs, goblins, and other threats. “A specialised company that is deployed to regions of recent conflict. All officers speak elven, and the company has an attached adviser from Myth Drannor who can requisition elven reinforcements.” Long-service veterans were common; Alaric Swift had 10 years as a Forester before his promotion.

Sgt. Leedo Forester – Sergeant, 4th Company.

Cpt. Darius Winters (retired) – Served 10 years as a Forester. Retired circa 1480, elected Mayor of Dwimmerholme Farm. Stout, well-muscled, many scars on arms and head; Chondathan features (brown hair, blue eyes, fair skin). Quiet, speaks in short succinct sentences. Age “perhaps 28 or 29” by physical description. Note: the Yellow Abbey Booke records his age as 44, but this contradicts the physical description and is likely a clerical error.

Cpt. Alaric Swift (transferred) – 10 years as a Forester. Older twin brother of Tuugan Swift (White Cloak/adventurer). Harsh but effective captain and trainer. Promoted out to command the new 9th Company “Deadly Rain.”

Farm Settlement Garrisons & Thistle Ridge Reclamation

7th Company – “Bright Spears”

ASSIGNMENT
Distributed across dale farms: Crossed Blades, Dwimmerholme, Charity Hills, Tesh Valley, and others
STRENGTH
10 platoons, 1 at each farm

The most widely dispersed company, responsible for garrisoning the fortified farming settlements across the heartland of the dale. Platoons were deployed to individual farms under local lieutenant command. After the Concordat, a 4th Platoon was formed specifically for the Thistle Ridge Reclamation (1483).

Lt. Kesh Oakdale (promoted) – Commander of the 3rd Platoon (bolstered to 5 squads), stationed at Dwimmerholme Farm. Aged 30. Cooperative with Alystair Dretch's investigations (1479), helping round up Sharran converts. In Marpenoth 1481, assisted the White Cloaks Bravo Team, going so far as to send men into the field against standing orders. Provided a 6-soldier escort detachment to Sgt. Hennigan Paros for the Sons of Bel cave assault (Marpenoth 1481); one unnamed Sun Blade killed in action during the engagement. Later promoted to Captain (16th Company), then retired and elected first Consul of the Governors' Assembly.

Lt. Ander Millstone – Stationed at Tesh Valley Farm. 3rd lieutenant in 3 years at that posting—Mayor Blackmane is always at odds with the Sun Blades and tends to go through them quickly. Patient, has done a good job ingratiating himself with the locals. Knows the militia often acts outside his chain of command; blames Sgt. Fortune Swift for recent problems but is not accusative. Actually appealed to the White Cloaks for help.

Sgt. Randall Eversmoore (formerly Lt.) – Officer in charge of the Sun Blades at Crossed Blades Farm. Young, perhaps 24; big with a square jaw. “Whose family are successful farmers and land owners at the Dwimmerholme Farm, north of Dagger Falls. He has a respect and appreciation for the lifestyle of the people here, and has had a spotless record of service here for 2 years.” (when still Lt.) Wears plate chest piece (not chain mail) and carries a large axe and a pair of daggers. “A friendly and unassuming way of asking you where you have been”—a natural interrogator. Conducts militia drills every tenday. Organised the pilgrim escort with Sun Blades and White Cloaks (Eleint 1479). Initially held the rank of Lieutenant, but demoted to Sergeant by Eleint 1479. Cause of demotion unknown.

Chaplain Hammerstone – Platoon chaplain garrisoned at Crossed Blades Farm. Veteran of many mountain campaigns. Getting older; enjoying the warmer valley posting.

NOTE: Crossed Blades Farm maintains a dual-platoon garrison: Eversmoore's infantry platoon (a full-strength platoon of 3 squads and a lieutenant) plus a second specialised cavalry platoon of 37 soldiers that patrols the roads. The cavalry platoon's commander is not recorded. Approximately 40 retired Sun Blades veterans and ~40 young militia also contribute to the settlement's defence.

7th Company, 4th Platoon – Thistle Ridge Reclamation (1483)

Newly formed platoon raised by order of the Governors' Assembly (Alturiak 1483) to support the Chrysalis expedition to reclaim Thistle Ridge. Understrength: 3 squads plus command element. Saw heavy action at the siege of The Tower and the Battle of Thistle Ridge (1–2 Mirtul 1483). Suffered 7 KIA during the siege.

Command:

Lt. I Forge Farmountain – Platoon commander. Authorised to choose his own junior officers. Marched his exhausted platoon to relieve Chrysalis at Thistle Ridge rather than resting after the siege of The Tower.

Pt. Fletcher Farmountain – Brother of Lt. Forge Farmountain. Wounded at the Tower when his ankle triggered a poisoned trap in the basements; the poison was beyond Magistrate Dale's skill to cure, though “his life is not in danger, we are assured.” In the infirmary as of 20 Tarsakh 1483.

Magistrate Madigara Dale – Church magistrate attached to the platoon command. Delivers Prime prayers from the eastern balcony of the Tower each dawn; the Yellow Tooth attacks cease and the enemy vanishes into the shadows when she begins her prayer. “She has challenged the faith of some of the non-Amaunatori with her prayers.”

Sgt. III Phoebe Birchgrove – Aide-de-camp to Lt. Farmountain. Author of the Thistle Ridge Expeditionary Record. Travels with her bonded culver, Mareeseah (a Swift, not a dove).

Sgt. I Ivy Thickwickle – Cook and Quartermaster. Halfling. Her spiced venison stew is “nightly enjoyed with great anticipation” at the Tower. Organised First Breakfast before the expedition's departure from Gribbons'. Spirited personality; when Lt. Farmountain called it “last breakfast,” she replied: “dear, that is at the very least a pessimistic outlook on these trays of bacon, and at worst an indictment of my ability in the field.”

1st Squad – Mounted:

Sgt. II Chastity Dale (KIA) – A Selûnite from Shadowdale. Killed during the siege of The Tower. 3 Spearmen – 2 killed in siege.

2nd Squad – Mounted:

Sgt. II Buck Harrowbridge – A Selûnite from Mistledale. Remains of his kin were discovered in the Tower basement (the Beast's lair); “the discovery of the troubling remains of kin of Sgt. Harrowbridge in the basement was enough for him to walk out against orders.” Lt. Farmountain was fair with the men on this account. 3 Spearmen – 2 killed in siege. 2 Deepingdale Mastiffs (Bull and Newt).

3rd Squad – Foresters:

Sgt. I Illya Elderbough – A Chauntean from Gribbons', Ranger 1st Class.

Pt. Sanza Swift – While on patrol with 3rd Squad, repelled a Yellow Tooth skirmish party and captured a goblin prisoner, which was interrogated for intelligence on the enemy's plans. 3 Foresters (women).

Pvt. III Dunlodan Millstone (KIA) – First of Tethyamar's folk to serve in the Sun Blades. Both spearmen in this squad killed in the siege.

4th Squad – Reserve:

Sgt. III Kelya Cordoza – A Vaasan, Ranger 2nd Class. Her 4th Squad took over the Tower basement cleanup when 1st and 2nd Squads could not stomach the work; “the Vaasan Sgt and the Newharvest girls have set a fine example of courage.”

Violet Newharvest – Forester, from Daggerford.

Daffodil Newharvest – Forester, from Daggerford. Sister of Violet. 2 Spearmen – 1 killed in siege. The Newharvest Hounds, Manny & Chuck (Lowstead Wolfhounds).

Storied Veterans (c. 1420s–1440s DR)

The following are drawn from the historical record of Thistle Ridge (c. 1420s–1440s DR). Their names endure in the journals and parish records recovered during the 1483 reclamation campaign. The Thistle Ridge Tragedy occurred on 21 Eleint, 1441 DR.

Lt. Tergus Clarandal – Born 1408, Thistle Ridge Farm. Rose from Sergeant in the 2nd Company (Mountaineers) over five Terms of Service. Commissioned as Lieutenant commanding the 7th Company Bright Spears, 4th Platoon—the original Thistle Ridge garrison (c. 1436 DR). His extensive journals (1423–1439 DR) were recovered by the Chrysalis expedition from the ruins of Clarandal Farm in 1483. These journals reveal a thoughtful, observant soldier who harboured doubts about his family's claimed Sembian origins and sensed “hidden truths” within the community. Fate uncertain after the Thistle Ridge Tragedy of 1441.

Sgt. Claudius Clarandal – Born 1410, Thistle Ridge Farm. Brother of Tergus. Served as Sergeant in the Mountaineers over five Terms of Service. After retiring from the Sun Blades, became the militia captain of Thistle Ridge, leading one of the largest and best-trained militias in the dale (106 capable men and women). Presumed dead in the Thistle Ridge Tragedy of 1441 DR, if not already deceased by that date.

NOTE: Sources describe Claudius as “of the 3rd Company, Mountaineers” rather than the 2nd, which is the usual Mountaineers numbering. This may reflect an older numbering convention or a reorganisation.

Mounted Patrol & Rapid Response

8th Company – “Sun Dragoons”

ASSIGNMENT
Mobile – deployed as needed across the dale
STRENGTH
3 platoons; “all mounted and expert fighters from horseback”

The Sun Dragoons are the mounted arm of the Sun Blades. Units are deployed to hot spots for rapid reinforcement and aggressive patrolling. A full squad was ambushed by ~200 gnolls on the Moonsea Road near Gribbon's Farm (1480), with half killed.

Lt. Grid Hammerfall – Commander of a mounted unit (2nd Sun Dragoons, as of 1479). Stoic, aloof leader. Deployed to Tesh Valley Farm to step up patrols. After orc raiders seized the farm's cattle, personally tracked the raiders' trail 8 miles north along Old Zhent Road and marked the turn-off with three stones spaced 100 yards apart; ordered his men to keep silent, fearing the local militia would pursue recklessly. Escorted the White Cloaks north with a platoon of Sun Dragoons.

Captain Alaric Swift – Older twin brother of Tuugan Swift (White Cloak/adventurer). Formerly a 10-year veteran Forester (4th Company). Not as gentle or patient as his brother. Harsh but effective captain and trainer.

The “Dale” surname convention for bastards and orphans makes shared names likely.

IV. The Daggerdale Concordat

In Elesias 1482, the Daggerdale Concordat transferred administrative and financial control of the Sun Blades from the Church of Amaunator to the newly formed Governors' Assembly. This was not a disbandment or reconstitution of the force – the General remained in command, and the existing companies continued to operate under their traditional names and numbers. What changed was the governance, the funding model, and the imposition of a new divisional superstructure intended to organise the expanded force.

The Concordat expanded the Sun Blades from a force of roughly 400 to a planned Standard Order of 1,296 soldiers plus officers, organised into 18 companies, 54 platoons, and 8 Divisions. Each Division is commanded by a Captain answering to the General. Costs are shared across all settlements through a system of 20 “shares” apportioned among Castle Radiance (4), Dagger Falls (4), each farm (1 each × 9), Charity (1), Tethyamar (1), and Cormyr (1). Settlements seeking Concordat membership must “establish the infrastructure and means to host a company of Sunblades, in accordance with the Provisions for the Common Defense.”

Concordat Unit Structure

The Concordat specifies a standardised unit structure: each Platoon comprises 24 soldiers and 1 Sergeant; each Company comprises 3 Platoons and 1 Lieutenant; each Division comprises 1–4 Companies under 1 Captain. Whether this standard has been imposed on the existing companies or merely represents the template for new ones is unclear. In practice, company sizes continue to vary – the 2nd “Mountaineers” maintain 5 platoons, the 7th “Bright Spears” had 10 before the Concordat, and the 8th “Sun Dragoons” have 3. The mapping of existing named companies to their Concordat divisional assignments is not specified in any available source. Division 4 (Recruits) is the only division with an explicit company-to-division link in the archive material.

Concordat Pay & Costs

NOTE: Cost per soldier: 183 gp/year (80 gp payable in-kind). Cost per platoon: ~4,527 gp/year. Cost per company: ~13,826 gp/year. Common companies (Rides, Recruits, Reserve = 7 companies) funded by 20 shares across all signatories at ~4,839 gp/share/year.

Whether the Concordat altered the existing rank-based pay scale (see Section I) is not recorded. The per-soldier cost of 183 gp/year exceeds the apparent pay of a Footman (10 gp/tenday ≈ 360 gp/year), suggesting that the Concordat figure includes provisioning, equipment, and overhead rather than wages alone – or that pay scales were revised downward as part of the expansion.

Related Military Organisations

The Concordat formalised four categories of military institution in Daggerdale: the Standing Army (Sun Blades), the Holy Orders (Charters Militant), the Chartered Companies, and the Militia. The following are the non–Sun Blade organisations drawn from various sources.

Holy Orders – Charters Militant

Order of the Lambent Shield (Amaunator)

~120 paladins and holy warriors of Amaunator. Led by Grand Master Luminous Whitegate, with Knight Commander Kassius as a senior field officer. Deployed from Castle Radiance against Netheril and the Plaguelands. Funded entirely by the Church. Granted Charter Militant by the Concordat.

NOTE: Iolus Warburton (Captain of the White Cloaks) is described as a “candidate for the Order of the Lambent Shield, which he regularly turns down.” See Part II of this report for full coverage.

Order of the Golden Lion (Torm)

Holy order dedicated to Torm, the Loyal Fury. Led by Sir Gethard Soulbright (killed at the Second Battle of Anathar's Dell, Eleint 7, 1372 DR). Known members include Daniel Overcourt (squire to Soulbright; wounded at Anathar's Dell but survived) and Corran Altair (former Sun Blade, now Paladin of Torm; listed as Sergeant of the Order). See Part II.

Order of the Lambent Rose (Ilmater)

Holy order dedicated to Ilmater, the Crying God. Led by Sir Lexinauth (killed by the Baron at the Second Battle of Anathar's Dell, Eleint 7, 1372 DR) and Father Eisley of St. Collin's Abbey. Reverend Brother Turan, a Damaran monk-priest and friend of Lord Maturin Horgath, is also a member (last seen working in the medicine tents in Shadowdale, 1372 DR). Involved in refugee care during the 1372 crisis. See Part II.

Knights of Justice (Tyr)

Holy order dedicated to Tyr, the Even-Handed. Led by Sir Wildermot Silverlance (killed by the Baron at the Second Battle of Anathar's Dell, Eleint 7, 1372 DR). Sir Durin Saddlemoor survived Anathar's Dell but lost his shield arm. Smallest of the four orders. Twenty-one paladins from the assembled orders were slain at Anathar's Dell and buried there, earning it the name “Paladin's Grave.” See Part II.

Chartered Companies

White Cloaks

The most prominent chartered adventuring company in Daggerdale. Founded by a group that included Lumina Whitegate. Led by Captain Iolus Warburton, a Soldier of Amaunator. Organised into operational sub-teams (Alpha, Bravo, etc.) with letters patent from the Governors' Assembly. The White Cloaks fielded a large tournament force (the Bravo Tourney, Marpenoth 1480) and have extensive operations across the dale. Lt. Tara Hilltop left the Sun Blades to join their leadership. Numerous members including Vanion Creed (Sergeant, Tethyamar Team Leader & Quartermaster), Alystair Dretch (Sergeant), Sgt. Hennigan Paros, Imogen Mystral, and Sammus Weaver. The Captain of the White Cloaks holds a seat on the Governors' Assembly.

Redjacks

A large chartered company of veteran soldiers and adventurers, numbering more than 60. Led by Captain Hastle Blackwater (also rendered “Redwater” in one source). Described as “very capable, if somewhat self-interested.” The Captain of the Redjacks holds a seat on the Governors' Assembly.

Free Defence League (FDL)

An independent bounty-hunter and volunteer force, headquartered at Castle Radiance. Led by Captain Bargle Battamond (also “Battlemaster”), with nearly 40 members who take bounties posted at the castle. “Ser” Reginald Boxfray commands the 14th Company, 1st Platoon “Dawnshields” (4 squads). The FDL fought alongside the Sun Blades at Thistle Ridge (1483). The Captain of the Free Defence League holds a seat on the Governors' Assembly.

Militia & Other

Local Militia

Each farm maintains its own militia of fighting-aged people (1/5 enrolled at any time, 4-year terms, minimum 2 hrs/tenday training). Tesh Valley's militia under Sgt. Fortune Swift (Tuugan Swift's uncle) is the largest and best, with ~40 active men, mostly mounted. The historical Thistle Ridge militia under Claudius Clarandal was one of the dale's largest, with 106 capable fighters. Militia captains are paid by their settlements. The Concordat requires common militia-readiness drills 4× per year.

Wonderbringer Warsmiths

Engineers and artificers based at Fort Luminous. Operate and develop experimental weapons including the great bronze gun. Include gnomish specialists. Not part of the Sun Blades chain of command.
