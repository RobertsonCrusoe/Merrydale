The Whitegate, Thunderstorm & Blackmane Families

Pedigrees, Titles & Notes on Three Intertwined Houses of Daggerdale

Compiled from the Yellow Abbeye Booke of Surveys and Measures (1481 DR edition), the Daggerdale Chronicle ch. 7, and corroborating records

I. The House of Whitegate

The Whitegate family descends from Ardelusk Whitegate, a former Zhentarim slave liberated by Saint Maturin’s Liberators. Ardelusk led the famous Whitegate Epiphany that established the church of Amaunator in the dale, and founded the settlement at Liberty Hill. The family has held the highest ecclesiastical and civil offices in Daggerdale ever since, though by the early 1480s the surname is nearing extinction—the surviving bloodline has dispersed into the Thunderstorm family (through Radiance) and the Blackmane family (through Aestus).

II. The House of Thunderstorm

The Thunderstorm family saw a decline after the Whitegate Epiphany and the rise of Amaunator, but Karrigan’s marriage to Radiance Whitegate in 1425 reversed their fortunes. The union produced a large number of family members that have multiplied to a couple of dozen in the present generation. Many still live at Foothills Farm, while others have entered the Church of Amaunator and military service. The Thunderstorms have produced three Sunlords. Some members of the Whitegate family resent the Thunderstorm side, whom they perceive as ambitious and scheming. No Thunderstorm has ever been admitted to the Order of the Lambent Shield.

Editorial note – Maddrick vs. Maddread: Several sources (the “Thunderstorm Family” article, the “Church of Amaunator” article, “The Journey to Provisioner’s Hall”) attribute the role of Dean of the Yellow Abbey and the title of Sunlord to “Maddrick.” The Crossed Blades Farm article spells his name “Madrick” (single ‘d’), adding a further variant. However, the Whitegate & Thunderstorm Pedigrees (DDC 1480–12) distinguish two brothers: Maddrick (b. 1430, Magistrate of Crossed Blades Farm) and Maddread (b. 1433, Magistrate and Dean of the Yellow Abbey). The pedigree is the more precise source for distinguishing similarly-named family members. The Dean is most likely Maddread, not Maddrick. In “The Journey to Provisioner’s Hall,” Helm refers to “his uncle, Maddrick, who is the Dean of the Yellow Abbey”—but since both Maddrick and Maddread are Helm’s uncles, this may simply be a misnaming.
Editorial note – Karrigan’s parentage: The “Thunderstorm Family” article describes Karrigan as “the son of Rewel Thunderstorm.” The pedigree, however, places an intervening generation: Rewel → Riven → Karrigan. Karrigan is Rewel’s grandson, not his son. The pedigree is the more detailed and authoritative source.
Editorial note – Incomplete pedigree (DDC 1480–12 vs. Chronicle ch. 7): The DDC 1480–12 Whitegate & Thunderstorm Pedigrees—the standalone document from the Yellow Abbey—omits several family members that appear in the fuller pedigree reprinted in the Daggerdale Chronicle ch. 7. The omissions are: Lumen Whitegate (1457–1480) and Luminosus Whitegate (1458–1480), children of Perspicuus; Radiance (b. 1452) and Sola (b. 1455), daughters of Helm Thunderstorm; and Garnagle (b. 1452) and Bethany (b. 1453), children of Laurel Thunderstorm and Castle Dunvegan. All six are confirmed by narrative sources. The DDC 1480–12 pedigree appears to be an abridgement or summary; the Chronicle ch. 7 version is the more complete record.
Editorial note – Aestus / Aestis spelling: The DDC 1480–12 pedigree and the Chronicle ch. 7 both spell the name Aestus. However, the dedicated character file is titled “Aestis Blackmane (née Whitegate),” and the Hasting Blackmane article uses both forms: “Aestus (Whitegate)” in one passage and “Aestis” by implication in the filename. The correct form is uncertain; both are attested.

Editorial note – Lanya Forester and Madigan Whitegate: In Decker Blackmane’s account of the Kythorn 1 massacre (Kythorn 1, 1455 DR), the bride Lanya is referred to as Loedegan’s “favourite niece” and Madigan’s “grand daughter.” Madigan is recorded as having “only one illegitimate daughter” with no surviving members of his line. If Lanya is Madigan’s granddaughter, his illegitimate daughter must have married into the Forester family and produced Lanya. This would make the marriage of Perspicuus (Loedegan’s grandson through Argent) and Lanya (Loedegan’s grand-niece through Madigan) an endogamous union within the extended Whitegate family. The Forester/Forster surname is prominent across all three families: Maira Forester married Rewel Thunderstorm; Lanya Forester married Perspicuus Whitegate; Sarah Forster married Lucidus Whitegate.

III. The Blackmane Family

The Blackmanes are an old Daggerdale family—Decker’s grandfather designed and built the settlement at Tesh Valley Farm. They have long been worshippers of Chauntea and Torm, placing them outside the Amaunatorite establishment. Decker’s marriage to Aestus Whitegate (daughter of Zenith Lord Argent) created a blood link to the ruling house, but Decker has been openly hostile to the Whitegate family, resenting what he calls the “Whitegate Entitlement.” The pedigree notes that “the Blackmane side of the family is also prolific,” but only Decker and Hasting are named in the archive. The other children have “tried to infiltrate the power structure of the Dale, mostly unsuccessful.”

Refuses to use the Blackmane surname, calling herself by her mother’s name: “I am my mother’s daughter, am I not?” In the narrative “The Lady of Loedegan Close,” she introduces herself as “Ms. Hasting Whitegate, cousin to your dear Lumina.” Referred to as “Lady Blackmane” by the White Cloaks.

IV. Cross-Family Connections

The three families are bound together through two marriages in the generation of Loedegan Whitegate’s children and grandchildren. Together, these unions mean that by the 1480s, the Whitegate bloodline persists almost entirely through the Thunderstorm and Blackmane surnames.

V. The Extinction of the Whitegate Name

By the early 1480s, the Whitegate surname is nearing extinction. Two catastrophes, a generation apart, destroyed the family.

The first was the massacre of Kythorn 1, 1455 DR—the attack on the wedding of Perspicuus and Lanya—which killed Loedegan, Madigan, Solis, Ardelusk II, Dorabell, Taryara, and many others. This left only four Whitegate men: Luminous (widowed since 1429), Argent, Blaze (widowed since 1455), and the young Perspicuus, who was left in charge of Liberty Hill.

The second was the Ritual of Corruption at Liberty Hill in Mirtul 1480. “Every member of the Whitegate family that resided at the farm perished.” Perspicuus had died in 1477; his wife Lanya was tortured to death in the chapel. Their children Lumen (b. 1457) and Luminosus (b. 1458) were “fed to the Ritual of Corruption, dying unthinkable deaths,” alongside their cousin Lucidus and his three small children (unnamed, ages 2, 3, and 5). Only Lumina survived—she was in Dagger Falls at the time.

Luminous’s grand-nephews and grand-nieces—“Lumen, Luminosus, Lucidus, and the small children of Lucidus—had been killed in unspeakable ways.” The living Whitegate men—Luminous (elderly), Argent (elderly, widowed since 1475), and Blaze—have no further heirs. Lumina, granddaughter of Argent, is the only Whitegate of child-bearing age, but she has no children as of 1481.

The Whitegate bloodline persists through the Thunderstorm family (via Radiance) and the Blackmane family (via Aestus), but no one in either line bears the Whitegate surname. Hasting Blackmane has claimed her mother’s name, but she remains officially a Blackmane. Luminous, the patriarch, “is the patriarch of a family that is almost gone, ruling from an inner keep he rarely leaves.”
